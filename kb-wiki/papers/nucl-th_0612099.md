# qqqbar to qqqbar and qqbarqbar to qqbarqbar Elastic Scatterings and Thermalization of Quark Matter and Antiquark Matter

> Thermalization of quark matter and antiquark matter is studied with quark-quark-antiquark as well as quark-antiquark-antiquark elastic scatterings. Squared amplitudes of qqqbar to qqqbar and qqbarqbar to qqbarqbar at order alpha_s^4 are derived in perturbative QCD. Solved by a new technique, solutions of transport equations with the squared amplitudes indicate that the scatterings qqqbar to qqqbar and qqbarqbar to qqbarqbar shorten the thermalization time of quark matter and antiquark matter. It is emphasized that three-parton and other multi-parton scatterings become important at the high parton number density achieved in RHIC Au-Au collisions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of quark-quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-antiquark elastic scatterings at order alpha_s^4 shortens the thermalization time of quark matter and antiquark matter to about 1.55 fm/c, but this result depends on the use of a screening mass to regulate Coulomb divergences and a particle sphere technique with a fixed sphere radius of 1.6 fm, and the authors note that the gluon propagators in Feynman gauge lead to some gauge dependence.

## Method

The authors derived the spin- and color-averaged squared amplitudes for the 3-to-3 elastic scatterings qqbar-q to qqbar-q and qbarqbar-q to qbarqbar-q at tree level in perturbative QCD using Fortran codes that implement QCD Feynman rules. They solved transport equations for quarks and antiquarks using a "particle sphere" technique, where each parton is assigned a sphere of fixed radius 1.6 fm and only partons within the same sphere are allowed to scatter, with the interaction range for 3-to-3 scatterings determined by an integration over Lorentz-invariant momentum variables in equilateral triangle regions. The transport equations included both 2-to-2 and 3-to-3 scattering terms, with the squared amplitudes for 2-to-2 scatterings taken from Cutler and Sivers (1978) and Combridge, Kripfganz, and Ranft (1977), and the squared amplitudes for triple-quark elastic scatterings from Xu, Peng, and Weber (2005).

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time of quark matter and antiquark matter: 1.55 fm/c, obtained from the solution of transport equations including qqbar-q to qqbar-q and qbarqbar-q to qbarqbar-q elastic scatterings at alpha_s = 0.5
- Temperature of quark matter at thermalization: T = 0.27 GeV, with fugacity lambda = 0.31, from fitting the momentum distribution to a Juettner distribution
- Parton number density used in the Monte Carlo test: 19.4 fm^{-3}, generated from HIJING for central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV within a volume of -0.3 fm < z < 0.3 fm and r < 6.4 fm
- At an interaction range of 0.62 fm, the occurrence probabilities for n-parton scatterings are: 2-parton 30%, 3-parton 20%, 4-parton 14.6%, and 7-parton 7.5%
- Particle sphere radius fixed at 1.6 fm, with maximum particles per sphere m_ps = 75 for 1000 partons
- The particle subdivision technique used a factor of ell = 20, with the solution taken as the average of 20 runs

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of triple-quark elastic scatterings (Xu, Peng, and Weber, 2005) which gave a thermalization time of about 1.8 fm/c, and addresses the discrepancy between early parton dynamics calculations giving thermalization times greater than 1 fm/c (Shin and Mueller, 2003; Xu and Greiner, 2005; Wong, 1996) and hydrodynamic calculations requiring thermalization within 1 fm/c (Kolb, Huovinen, Heinz, and Heiselberg, 2001; Teaney, Lauret, and Shuryak, 2001; Hirano, 2001; Eskola, Niemi, Ruuskanen, and Rasanen, 2003). The results suggest that multi-parton scatterings become important at the high parton number densities achieved in RHIC Au-Au collisions, and the particle sphere technique provides a computationally efficient method for simulating such scatterings. The work also enables future studies of three-body elastic scattering effects on heavy quark momentum degradation in quark-gluon plasma, as noted by Liu and Ko (2006).
