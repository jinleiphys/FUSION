# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors and electroexcitation of low lying nucleon resonances in a light-front relativistic quark model

> We utilize a light-front relativistic quark model (LF RQM) to predict the 3q core contribution to the electroexcitation amplitudes for the Delta(1232)P33, N(1440)P11, N(1520)D13, and N(1535)S11 up to Q2= 12GeV2. The parameters of the model have been specified via description of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the approach that combines 3q and pion-cloud contributions in the LF dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the pure three-quark (3q) core contribution to the electroexcitation amplitudes of four low-lying nucleon resonances up to Q² = 12 GeV², but only after fitting a single free parameter (the weight of the 3q component in each resonance) to experimental data at high Q² where meson-cloud contributions are assumed negligible.

## Method

The authors employ a light-front relativistic quark model (LF RQM) formulated in the infinite momentum frame, using SU(6)×O(3) wave functions with Melosh rotations. They first fix the model parameters (quark mass m_q and oscillator parameters α₁, α₂) by fitting the nucleon electromagnetic form factors G_Ep, G_Mp, G_En, and G_Mn from Q² = 0 to 16 GeV², combining the 3q core with a pion-cloud contribution from Miller (2002) and introducing a Q²-dependent constituent quark mass. They then predict the 3q core contribution to the γ*N → N* transition amplitudes for the Δ(1232)P33, N(1440)P11, N(1520)D13, and N(1535)S11, determining the 3q weight factor c_N* for each resonance by fitting experimental amplitudes at Q² > 4 GeV² for the Δ and at Q² = 2.5–4.5 GeV² for the other three resonances.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon magnetic moments: μ_p = 2.86 e/(2m_N), μ_n = -1.86 e/(2m_N), obtained with quark mass m_q(0) = 0.22 GeV and oscillator parameters α₁ = 0.37 GeV, α₂ = 0.41 GeV
- Mean invariant mass of the 3-quark system at Q² = 0: ⟨M₀²⟩ ≈ 1.35 GeV²; mean squared quark momentum: ⟨q_i²⟩ ≈ 0.1 GeV²
- Two parameterizations of the running quark mass: m_q^(1)(Q²) = 0.22 GeV / (1 + Q²/56 GeV²) for wave function Φ₁; m_q^(2)(Q²) = 0.22 GeV / (1 + Q²/18 GeV²) for wave function Φ₂
- Nucleon wave function composition: |N> = 0.95|3q> + 0.313|πN>, giving proton charge F_1p(0) = 1
- 3q weight factors for resonances: c_N* ≈ 0.53 ± 0.04 for Δ(1232)P33; c_N* = 0.73 ± 0.05 (Φ₁) and 0.77 ± 0.05 (Φ₂) for N(1440)P11; c_N* = 0.78 ± 0.06 (Φ₁) and 0.82 ± 0.06 (Φ₂) for N(1520)D13; c_N* = 0.88 ± 0.03 (Φ₁) and 0.94 ± 0.03 (Φ₂) for N(1535)S11
- Pion-cloud contribution to nucleon form factors is negligible at Q² > 2 GeV²
- Mixing angle for N(1535)S11: θ_S = -31° from hadronic decays

## Context

This work extends the light-front relativistic quark model approach previously developed by Aznauryan (1985, 1987) and applied to nucleon form factors and resonance electroexcitation, now incorporating a Q²-dependent constituent quark mass motivated by lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger equation results from Bowman (2005) and Bhagwat (2003, 2004). The predictions for the 3q core contribution to resonance transition amplitudes are intended to guide interpretation of data from the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade, where the quark core is expected to be more fully exposed. For the Δ(1232)P33, the meson-cloud contribution from the dynamical model of Sato and Lee (2001) is included at low Q²; for the N(1440)P11, the σN contribution from Obukhovsky (2011) is added. The results contradict the assumption that meson-cloud contributions are negligible for the Δ(1232)P33 at Q² = 2–4 GeV², unlike for the other three resonances where they become negligible above Q² ≈ 2 GeV².
