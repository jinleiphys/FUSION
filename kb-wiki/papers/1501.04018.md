# Quasielastic electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering in a continuum random phase approximation approach

> We present a continuum random phase approximation approach to study electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections, in the kinematic region where quasielastic scattering is the dominant process. We show the validity of the formalism by confronting inclusive ($e,e'$) cross sections with the available data. We calculate flux-folded cross sections for charged-current quasielastic antineutrino scattering off $^{12}$C and compare them with the MiniBooNE cross-section measurements. We pay special emphasis to the contribution of low-energy nuclear excitations in the signal of accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

The continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) formalism successfully describes the gross features of quasielastic electron- and antineutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections, but the predictions tend to underestimate the MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic antineutrino data, suggesting that multinucleon contributions not included in this approach are essential for a more complete reproduction.

## Method

The authors solve Hartree-Fock equations with a Skyrme (SkE2) two-body interaction to obtain a mean-field description of the nucleus, then obtain continuum wave functions by integrating the positive-energy Schrodinger equation with appropriate boundary conditions to account for final-state interactions. Long-range correlations are implemented via a CRPA Green's function formalism that iterates the first-order polarization propagator to include one-particle one-hole excitations out of the correlated ground state. Relativistic kinematic corrections and a dipole hadronic form factor at nucleon-nucleon interaction vertices are included, and the modified effective momentum approximation accounts for the nuclear Coulomb field on the ejected lepton.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass value of M_A = 1.03 GeV used in the dipole axial form factor for antineutrino calculations.
- For neutrino energies of 150 MeV, low-lying nuclear excitations appear at all scattering angles; for 800 MeV (near the mean energy of MiniBooNE and T2K fluxes), forward scatterings still show sizable low-energy excitations.
- For momentum transfer q less than or approximately equal to 300 MeV/c, nuclear effects are prominent and the relativistic Fermi gas description becomes poor; for q approximately equal to 500 MeV/c, the relativistic Fermi gas is sufficiently accurate.
- For antineutrino energies E_nu up to 0.4 GeV, HF and CRPA total cross sections essentially coincide; for E_nu greater than or approximately equal to 0.4 GeV, CRPA results are slightly smaller than HF ones.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CRPA formalism used for exclusive photo-induced and electron-induced quasielastic scattering (Jan, 1988; Jan, 1989), inclusive neutrino scattering at supernova energies (Natalie, 1999-2012), and charged-current quasielastic antineutrino scattering at intermediate energies (Vishvas, 2014). The main update from the 2014 work includes relativistic corrections and suppression of RPA quenching at high Q^2. The results are compared with MiniBooNE antineutrino data, and the authors note that multinucleon contributions, as suggested by Martini (2009, 2010, 2013) and Nieves (2011, 2013), are needed for a more complete reproduction of the data. The formalism enables improved treatment of low-energy nuclear excitations that cannot be accounted for in relativistic Fermi gas based simulation codes used by MiniBooNE and T2K.
