# Spontaneous breaking of rotational symmetry in superconductors

> We show that homogeneous superconductors with broken spin/isospin symmetry lower their energy via a transition to a novel superconducting state where the Fermi-surfaces are deformed to a quasi-ellipsoidal form at zero total momentum of Cooper pairs. In this state, the gain in the condensation energy of the pairs dominates over the loss in the kinetic energy caused by the lowest order (quadrupole) deformation of Fermi-surfaces from the spherically symmetric form. There are two energy minima in general, corresponding to the deformations of the Fermi-spheres into either prolate or oblate forms. The phase transition from spherically symmetric state to the superconducting state with broken rotational symmetry is of the first order.

## Key claim

Homogeneous superconductors with broken spin/isospin symmetry can lower their energy by transitioning to a state where Fermi surfaces deform into quasi-ellipsoidal forms at zero total momentum of Cooper pairs, with the gain in condensation energy dominating the loss in kinetic energy from the quadrupole deformation.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS-Gorkov equations in energy-momentum representation for a two-species fermion system with S-wave pairing, parameterizing the quasiparticle spectrum with a quadrupole (l=2, m=0) deformation term. They compute the gap equation, density normalization condition, and free energy self-consistently, then minimize the free energy with respect to the deformation parameters epsilon (conformal deformation) and delta-epsilon (relative deformation) at fixed density asymmetry alpha.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap in the isospin-symmetric case (neutron-proton pairing in nuclear matter using the Argonne potential) is Delta_00 = 12 MeV
- The density used in calculations is rho = 0.16 fm^{-3} at temperature T = 3 MeV
- The critical relative deformation delta-epsilon where the gap is maximal in the large alpha limit is approximately 0.5
- The superconducting phase becomes unstable for density asymmetry alpha > 0.4
- For alpha = 0.35, the free-energy minimizing relative deformation is delta-epsilon = 0.64

## Context

This work extends the BCS theory of bulk superconductivity (Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer, 1957) and the LOFF phase (Larkin and Ovchinnikov, 1964; Fulde and Ferrell, 1964) which breaks translational symmetry. The paper proposes an alternative mechanism where rotational symmetry is broken via Fermi-surface deformation rather than finite-momentum Cooper pairs, and applies this to isospin-singlet neutron-proton pairing in nuclear matter, building on prior studies of homogeneous pairing under isospin asymmetry (Nuclear BCS studies) and the nuclear LOFF phase. The results enable investigation of anisotropic dynamic properties such as sound attenuation, infrared absorption, and the Meissner effect in the deformed superconducting state.
