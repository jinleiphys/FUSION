# Production of Strange and Charm Hadrons in Pb+Pb Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 5.02 TeV

> Using a quark combination model with the equal-velocity combination approximation, we study the production of hadrons with strangeness and charm flavor quantum numbers in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$5.02 TeV. We present analytical expressions and numerical results for these hadrons' transverse momentum spectra and yield ratios. Our numerical results agree well with the experimental data available. The features of strange and charm hadron production in the quark--gluon plasma at the early stage of heavy ion collisions are also discussed.

## Key claim

Using a quark combination model with the equal-velocity combination approximation, the authors show that strange and charm hadron production in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV can be described simultaneously, with the condition that the model inputs (quark transverse momentum spectra) are fixed by fitting only a subset of hadron species (phi mesons and Lambda baryons for strange quarks; D0 mesons for charm quarks).

## Method

The authors apply a quark combination model (QCM) in momentum space with an equal-velocity combination (EVC) approximation, where quarks and antiquarks with the same velocity combine into hadrons. The normalized transverse momentum distributions of light (up/down) and strange quarks are extracted by fitting experimental data on phi meson and Lambda baryon spectra, while the charm quark distribution is obtained by fitting D0 meson data using a hybrid thermal-plus-power-law parameterization. The model then predicts the spectra and yield ratios for other strange hadrons (K0s, Xi-, Omega-) and charm hadrons (D+, Ds+, D*+, Lambda_c+, Sigma_c, Xi_c, Omega_c) across multiple centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 0.3 GeV, m_s = 0.5 GeV, m_c = 1.5 GeV
- Strangeness suppression factor lambda_s gives N_u:N_d:N_s = 1:1:lambda_s
- Production ratio of vector to pseudo-scalar mesons set to 1.5
- Production ratio of J^P=(1/2)+ to J^P=(3/2)+ single-charm baryons set to 2
- Rapidity density of d quarks in 0-5% centrality: dN_d/dy = 840; in 70-80% centrality: dN_d/dy = 27
- Rapidity density of s quarks in 0-5% centrality: dN_s/dy = 370; in 70-80% centrality: dN_s/dy = 10
- Rapidity density of charm quarks in 0-10% centrality: dN_c/dy = 23.07; in 60-80% centrality: dN_c/dy = 0.417
- Lambda/K0s ratio peak height increases from about 0.8 in peripheral (70-80% centrality) to about 1.5 in most central (0-5% centrality) collisions
- QCM yield density for D0 in 0-10% centrality: 8.438 (experimental data: 6.819 +/- 0.457 +0.912/-0.936 +/- 0.054)
- QCM yield density for Lambda_c+ in 0-10% centrality: 5.983 (no experimental data available for comparison)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quark combination model studies from pp, pA, and AA collisions at various energies (Shao 2004, Wang 2013, Song 2013) to simultaneously describe strange and charm hadron production in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy. The model builds on the earlier works of Xie (1984, 1988) on quark combination in e+e- and pp collisions. The equal-velocity combination approximation was inspired by constituent quark number scaling observed in p+Pb collisions (Song 2017). The model's prediction for the Lambda_c+/D0 ratio was previously verified by ALICE measurements (ALICE 2021, 2022). This work enables predictions for unmeasured charm baryons (Sigma_c, Xi_c, Omega_c) that can be tested by future ALICE measurements.
