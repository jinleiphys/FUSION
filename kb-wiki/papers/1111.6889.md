# Compact Stars, Heavy Ion Collisions, and Possible Lessons For QCD at Finite Densities

> Large neutron star masses as the recently measured $1.97\pm0.04$ M$_\odot$ for PSR J1614-2230 provide a valuable lower limit on the stiffness of the equation of state of dense, nuclear and quark matter. Complementary, the analysis of the elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions suggests an upper limit on the EoS stiffness. We illustrate how this dichotomy permits to constrain parameters of effective EoS models which otherwise could not be derived unambiguously from first principles.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the combined application of a lower limit on equation-of-state stiffness from the 1.97 solar mass neutron star PSR J1614-2230 and an upper limit from elliptic flow data in heavy ion collisions can constrain the vector and diquark coupling parameters of a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type quark matter model, but that the specific choice of scalar coupling parametrization (which determines the dynamical quark mass) significantly changes whether the resulting hybrid equation of state can simultaneously satisfy both constraints.

## Method

The authors perform a systematic scan over the vector coupling ratio η_V (0.0 to 0.7) and diquark coupling ratio η_D (0.8 to 1.15) in a three-flavor color-superconducting NJL model with selfconsistently determined quark masses and diquark gaps. They construct hybrid neutron star equations of state by joining the NJL quark matter phase to a Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock nuclear matter equation of state via a Maxwell phase transition construction, imposing electric charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium for neutron star matter. For each parameter pair they compute the maximum neutron star mass and the onset density for quark matter, and compare the symmetric-matter equation of state to the elliptic flow constraint from heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

* The neutron star PSR J1614-2230 has mass M = 1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙, providing a lower limit on equation-of-state stiffness.
* For the NJL model with the chosen scalar parametrization, the transition density in symmetric matter along the red band (configurations with maximum masses matching PSR J1614) is approximately n_onset^sym ≈ 4 n_S, where n_S is nuclear saturation density.
* For parameter pairs within the red band, the density jump Δn between quark matter and nuclear matter at the phase transition ranges from 0.144 fm^{-3} (η_V=0.1, η_D=0.92) down to 0.017 fm^{-3} (η_V=0.6, η_D=1.15).
* The vector coupling value obtained from Fierz transformation of one-gluon exchange is η_V^F = 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the constraint scheme of Klahn (2006) which simultaneously applied neutron star mass, cooling, mass-radius, baryon number, and heavy-ion flow constraints to equations of state. It directly responds to the high-precision mass measurement of PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest (2010) and the elliptic flow analysis of Danielewicz (2002). The paper shows that the same NJL model which previously resolved the conflict between the DBHF nuclear equation of state and the flow constraint (Klahn 2006) fails to do so with a different scalar coupling parametrization, highlighting the sensitivity of such analyses to the treatment of chiral symmetry breaking. This enables future work to distinguish between different parametrizations of the scalar channel using combined neutron star and heavy-ion collision data, and motivates development of equations of state that incorporate the Mott dissociation of baryons into quarks.
