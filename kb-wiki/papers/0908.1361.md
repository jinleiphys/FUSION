# Enhancement of quark number susceptibility with an alternative pattern of chiral symmetry breaking in dense matter

> We explore a possible phase where chiral $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ symmetry is spontaneously broken while its center $Z_2$ symmetry remains unbroken and its consequence on thermal quantities. In this phase, chiral symmetry breaking is driven by a quartic quark condensate although a bilinear quark condensate vanishes. A Ginzburg-Landau free energy leads to a new tricritical point (TCP) between the $Z_2$ broken and unbroken phases. Furthermore, a critical point can appear even in the chiral limit where explicit breaking is turned off, instead of a TCP at which restoration of chiral and its center symmetries takes place simultaneously. The net quark number density exhibits an abrupt change near the restoration of the center symmetry rather than that of the chiral symmetry. Hadron masses in possible phases are also studied in a linear sigma model. We show that, in the $Z_2$ symmetric phase, the $\bar{q}q$-type scalar meson with zero isospin I=0 splits from the $\bar{q}q$-type pseudoscalar meson with I=1.

# Enhancement of quark number susceptibility with an alternative pattern of chiral symmetry breaking in dense matter

## Key claim

The paper proposes that in dense baryonic matter, an intermediate phase can exist where chiral symmetry remains spontaneously broken (driven by a quartic quark condensate) while the center $Z_2$ symmetry of the chiral group is restored, leading to a strong enhancement of quark number susceptibility near the center symmetry restoration rather than near the chiral phase transition. This possibility is not ruled out by the no-go theorem that forbids this pattern at zero density, and the authors explicitly hedge that this phase may provide a theoretical description of the quarkyonic transition in QCD with $N_c=3$.

## Method

The authors construct a Ginzburg-Landau effective potential for two-flavor QCD using two order parameters: a 2-quark condensate $\sigma$ (bilinear quark condensate) and a 4-quark condensate $\chi$ (quartic quark condensate), with the 4-quark field $\Sigma$ transforming as an adjoint representation under chiral symmetry. They classify possible phases by analyzing the potential's minima as functions of the coefficients $A$ and $B$ (related to the squared masses of the $\sigma$ and $\chi$ fields), and compute the critical exponents of the quark number susceptibility $\chi_q$ at tricritical points and critical points. They also study hadron mass spectra in a linear sigma model by diagonalizing the mass matrices for scalar and pseudoscalar mesons in the different phases.

## Key numbers

- Two tricritical points (TCPs) appear in the phase diagram for $D=F=h=0$: TCP$_1$ at $(A,B)=(0,1/4)$ and TCP$_2$ at $(A,B)=(1/4,-1/8)$, with a triple point $T$ at $(A,B)=(1/8,0)$.
- Near TCP$_1$, the quark number susceptibility diverges as $\chi_q \sim t^{-2/3}$, where $t = |\mu-\mu_c|/\mu_c$ is the reduced chemical potential.
- Near TCP$_2$, the quark number susceptibility diverges as $\chi_q \sim t^{-1/2}$, a different critical exponent from TCP$_1$.
- For $D < 0$ (negative cubic term coefficient), the critical exponent of $\chi_q$ at the critical point CP$_1$ is $2/3$, while at TCP$_2$ it remains $1/2$.
- For $D > 0$, the critical exponent at TCP$_1$ becomes $1/2$ and TCP$_2$ disappears.
- In the $Z_2$ symmetric phase (phase II), the pion decay constant remains finite: $F_\pi \to \sqrt{8/3}\,\chi_0$, with $\chi_0 = \sqrt{3\bar m^2/\bar\lambda^2 + (\sqrt{3}g_3/(2\bar\lambda^2))^2} - \sqrt{3}g_3/(2\bar\lambda^2)$.
- In the $Z_2$ symmetric phase, the pure 4-quark pseudoscalar state $\psi$ is massless ($m_\psi = 0$) as the Nambu-Goldstone boson, while the $\sigma$ and $\phi$ states are not degenerate in mass.

## Context

This work extends the concept of an alternative chiral symmetry breaking pattern originally suggested by Stern (1998) and studied in QCD at zero density by Shifman (2000), who showed it is ruled out at zero density by QCD inequalities. The authors connect their findings to the quarkyonic phase proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) in the large $N_c$ limit, and to the Skyrme crystal phase with half-skyrmions found numerically. The paper provides a chiral Lagrangian framework with two distinct order parameters (2-quark and 4-quark condensates) that can describe a phase reminiscent of the quarkyonic transition in $N_c=3$ QCD, and enables future microscopic calculations of dynamical center symmetry breaking in dense matter.
