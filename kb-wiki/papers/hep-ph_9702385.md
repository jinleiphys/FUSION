# Charmonium Production at ELFE Energies

> I discuss issues related to charmonium production, in view of physics possibilities at a 15 ... 30 GeV continuous beam electron facility. High energy photo- and hadroproduction of heavy quarkonia presents several challenges to QCD models concerning cross sections, polarization and nuclear target dependence. Theoretical approaches based on color evaporation as well as on color singlet and color octet mechanisms have met with both successes and failures, indicating that charmonium production is a sensitive probe of color dynamics. Experiments close to charm kinematic threshold will be sensitive also to target substructure since only unusual, compact target configurations contribute. In particular, subthreshold production on nuclei should identify nuclear hot spots of high energy density. At low energies, charmonium will form inside the target nucleus, allowing a determination of c cbar bound state interactions in nuclear matter.

# Charmonium Production at ELFE Energies

## Key claim

Charmonium production near kinematic threshold will be sensitive to target substructure because only compact, unusual target configurations contribute, but the exact energy at which these higher-twist effects become dominant is not yet determined and can only be settled by experiment.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares three theoretical approaches to charmonium production, the Color Evaporation Model (CEM), the Color Singlet Model (CSM), and the Color Octet Model (COM), against existing photoproduction and hadroproduction data, then discusses generic features of near-threshold and subthreshold production on nuclear targets using arguments about proton Fock state lifetimes and effective transverse size.

## Key numbers

- The elastic photoproduction cross section near threshold is approximately 1 nb for $\gamma N \to J/\psi N$, measured by SLAC and Cornell in 1975.
- At ELFE luminosity of $10^{35} \text{ cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}$, the expected rate is about 5 $J/\psi$ dimuon decays per second.
- The fraction $f_{J/\psi}$ in the Color Evaporation Model takes values of 0.025 in $pp$ reactions and 0.034 in $\pi p$ reactions, and ranges from 0.005 to 0.025 in photoproduction after excluding the diffractive peak.
- The CSM prediction for the $\psi'/\psi$ direct production ratio is $0.24 \pm 0.03$, while the experimental uncorrected ratio is $0.20 \pm 0.05 \pm 0.07$.
- The CSM fails for absolute hadroproduction cross sections by factors up to 30 to 50.
- For $J/\psi$ and $\psi'$ hadroproduction on nuclear targets, $\alpha = 0.92 \pm 0.01$ for $0.1 \lesssim x_F \lesssim 0.3$, and $\alpha(x_F=0.6) \simeq 0.8$.
- In inelastic photoproduction, a nuclear enhancement is observed with $\alpha = 1.05 \pm 0.03$ for $x_F < 0.85$ and $p_\perp^2 > 0.4 \text{ GeV}^2$.
- For coherent photoproduction, E691 finds $\alpha_{coh} = 1.40 \pm 0.06 \pm 0.04$ and NMC gives $\alpha_{coh} = 1.19 \pm 0.02$.
- The effective proton transverse size near threshold scales as $r_\perp^2 \simeq (\zeta - 1)/\Lambda_{QCD}^2$, reaching $r_\perp \simeq 1/m_c$ at threshold where $\zeta - 1 \simeq \Lambda_{QCD}^2/m_c^2$.

## Context

This paper reviews the status of charmonium production puzzles in the context of the proposed ELFE (Electron Laboratory for Europe) facility, which would operate in the 15 to 30 GeV energy range. It extends discussions from earlier reviews by Schuler (1994), Sansoni (1996), and Mangano (1996) on the failures of the Color Singlet Model and the successes and limitations of the Color Evaporation Model and Color Octet Model. The paper enables future experimental tests of higher-twist effects near charm threshold, subthreshold production as a probe of nuclear hot spots, and measurements of charmonium-nucleon interactions in nuclear matter, which are relevant for interpreting quark-gluon plasma signals in heavy ion collisions as discussed by Satz (1996).
