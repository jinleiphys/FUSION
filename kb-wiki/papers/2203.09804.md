# Prediction of five-flavored pentaquarks

> We analyze the possibility of the existence of a fully exotic pentaquark state $udsc\bar{b}$, which is made from the five different flavors participarting in the strong interactions. We investigate the coupled channel effects of the $B^{(*)} Ξ_c-B^{(*)} Ξ_c^\prime$ system through $t$-channel vector meson exchange to search for such states. A $B Ξ_c$ and a $B Ξ_c^\prime$ bound state are found to have binding energies of about $10-30$~MeV with $B_sΛ_c$ and $B_cΛ$ being the possible decay channels. Similarly, a bound state is formed in the $B^* Ξ_c$ and $B^* Ξ_c^\prime$ channels, respectively. These states could be searched for through the $pp\to B_cΛX$ or $pp\to B_s^{(*)}Λ_c X$ processes by the LHCb collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of fully exotic pentaquark states with quark content \(udsc\bar{b}\) (five different flavors) as \(B^{(*)}\Xi_c\) and \(B^{(*)}\Xi_c^\prime\) bound states, but the existence depends on the isospin being \(I=0\) and on the choice of the subtraction constant \(a(\mu)\) in the loop function; for \(I=1\) the potential is repulsive and no bound states form.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in the on-shell factorization approach, using \(t\)-channel vector meson exchange (\(\rho, \omega, \bar{K}^*, D^*, D_s^*\)) as the driving interaction. For the pseudoscalar meson-baryon (\(PB\)) sector they consider four channels: \(B\Xi_c\), \(B\Xi_c^\prime\), \(B_s\Lambda_c\), and \(B_c\Lambda\); for the vector meson-baryon (\(VB\)) sector they consider three channels: \(B^*\Xi_c\), \(B^*\Xi_c^\prime\), and \(B_s^*\Lambda_c\). Bound states are identified as poles in the complex energy plane of the scattering amplitude \(T = [1-VG]^{-1}V\), with couplings extracted from the residues.

## Key numbers

- \(B\Xi_c\) bound state pole at \(z_0 = 7720.12 - 3.7i\) MeV, with binding energy \(30.1\) MeV and decay width to \(B_s\Lambda_c\) and \(B_c\Lambda\) of about \(7.4\) MeV.
- \(B\Xi_c^\prime\) bound state pole at \(z_0 = 7847.60 - 2.2i\) MeV, with binding energy \(10.5\) MeV and decay width of about \(4.4\) MeV.
- \(B^*\Xi_c\) bound state pole at \(z_0 = 7768.81 - 2.5i\) MeV, with binding energy \(26.7\) MeV and decay width to \(B_s^*\Lambda_c\) of about \(5\) MeV.
- \(B^*\Xi_c^\prime\) bound state pole at \(z_0 = 7894.43\) MeV (on the real axis), with binding energy about \(9\) MeV.
- Thresholds: \(B\Xi_c\) at \(7750.19\) MeV, \(B\Xi_c^\prime\) at \(7858.12\) MeV, \(B^*\Xi_c\) at \(7795.52\) MeV, \(B^*\Xi_c^\prime\) at \(7903.45\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the vector-meson exchange model previously applied to hidden-charm pentaquarks by Wu et al. (2010) and to hidden-beauty systems by Wu et al. (2010) and Shen et al. (2017), and is inspired by the observation of hidden-charm pentaquarks by LHCb (2015, 2019) and a strangeness-containing pentaquark candidate by LHCb (2020). It also builds on predictions of charm-antibeauty pentaquarks by Peng et al. (2019). The predicted \(\Lambda_{c\bar{b}}\) states with five different quark flavors would be the most exotic pentaquarks to date; they could be searched for by LHCb in the \(pp \to B_c\Lambda X\) or \(pp \to B_s^{(*)}\Lambda_c X\) processes.
