# Rotational damping in a multi-$j$ shell particles-rotor model

> The damping of collective rotational motion is investigated by means of particles-rotor model in which the angular momentum coupling is treated exactly and the valence nucleons are in a multi-$j$ shell mean-field. It is found that the onset energy of rotational damping is around 1.1 MeV above yrast line, and the number of states which form rotational band structure is thus limited. The number of calculated rotational bands around 30 at a given angular momentum agrees qualitatively with experimental data. The onset of rotational damping takes place gradually as a function of excitation energy. It is shown that the pairing correlation between valence nucleons has a significant effect on the appearance of rotational damping.

# Rotational damping in a multi-$j$ shell particles-rotor model

## Key claim
Rotational damping sets in at an excitation energy of approximately 1.1 MeV above the yrast line, but the transition from discrete rotational bands to damped motion is gradual rather than sharp, with some band structure persisting even above the onset energy. The pairing correlation between valence nucleons has a significant effect on the appearance of rotational damping, and the calculated number of rotational bands (around 30 at a given angular momentum) agrees qualitatively with experimental data for the rare-earth nucleus $^{168}$Yb.

## Method
The authors use a particles-rotor model (PRM) with eight valence particles in a multi-$j$ shell mean-field (including $h_{9/2}$, $i_{13/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, $f_{5/2}$, and $p_{1/2}$ shells) coupled to a deformed core, with angular momentum treated as a good quantum number and pairing correlation included explicitly. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized exactly for spins $I = 0$–$60$ $\hbar$ using the lowest 2000 many-particle many-hole configurations (up to $6p$–$6h$), and stretched E2 transition probabilities are computed to identify rotational band structure versus damped motion, quantified by the branch number $n_{\text{branch}}$.

## Key numbers
- Onset energy of rotational damping: approximately 1.1 MeV above the yrast line, defined by the criterion $n_{\text{branch}} = 2$.
- Number of calculated rotational bands: around 30 at a given angular momentum, in qualitative agreement with experimental data for $^{168}$Yb.
- Pairing strength used: $G = 0.45$ MeV; moment of inertia: $\mathcal{J} = 76.0$ $\hbar^2$ MeV$^{-1}$; deformation parameter $\kappa = 2.5$ MeV for $i_{13/2}$ shell and $\kappa = 2.2$ MeV for other shells.
- Branch number at excitation energy $U \sim 2.3$ MeV: about 16 branches; at $U \sim 3.1$ MeV: about 42 branches.
- Configuration truncation: excitation energy $\sim 0.6$ $\hbar\omega_0$, corresponding to the lowest 2000 many-particle many-hole configurations.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of rotational damping that used the cranked Nilsson mean-field combined with two-body residual interaction (e.g., Matsuo and collaborators, 1993–1999) and random-matrix approaches (e.g., Gu and Weidenmüller, 1982; Broglia and collaborators, 1986). Unlike those semi-classical cranking models where angular momentum is not a good quantum number, the present particles-rotor model treats angular momentum coupling exactly and includes pairing correlation explicitly. The calculated onset energy of approximately 1.1 MeV is similar to the theoretical prediction of $U \sim 0.8$ MeV from cranked Nilsson calculations. The results enable future work to better understand the mechanism of rotational damping, particularly the role of pairing and the exact treatment of angular momentum coupling, which the authors note are still schematic in the present model.
