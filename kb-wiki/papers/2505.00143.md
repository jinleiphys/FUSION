# Enhanced Nuclear Binding Near the Proton Dripline Opens Possible Bypass of the $^{64}{\rm Ge}$ rp-process Waiting Point

> We performed astrophysics model calculations with updated nuclear data to identify a possible bypass of the $^{64}{\rm Ge}$ waiting-point, a defining feature of the rapid-proton capture (rp-) process that powers type-I x-ray bursts on accreting neutron stars. We find that the rp-process flow through the $^{64}{\rm Ge}$ bypass could be up to 36\% for astrophysically relevant conditions. Our results call for new studies of $^{65}{\rm Se}$, including the nuclear mass, $β$-delayed proton emission branching, and nuclear structure as it pertains to the $^{64}{\rm As}(p,γ)$ reaction rate at x-ray burst temperatures.

## Key claim

Enhanced nuclear binding of the neutron-deficient isotope \(^{65}{\rm Se}\), which is within current theoretical estimates, could allow up to 36% of the rp-process flow to bypass the \(^{64}{\rm Ge}\) waiting point under the most favorable nuclear physics assumptions and astrophysical conditions, though this bypass fraction is limited by the \(\beta\)-delayed proton emission branching of \(^{65}{\rm Se}\).

## Method

The authors performed flow calculations using a limited nuclear reaction network initialized with all non-hydrogen abundance concentrated in \(^{63}{\rm Ge}\), solved at constant temperature and hydrogen density across the phase space relevant for x-ray burst conditions. Steady-state abundances were determined by propagating reactions for 120 seconds, and the bypass fraction was measured as the ratio of the total flux of \(^{66}{\rm Se}(\beta^{+})\) to the summed total flux of \(^{66}{\rm Se}(\beta^{+})\), \(^{65}{\rm As}(\beta^{+})\), and \(^{64}{\rm Ge}(\beta^{+})\). Nuclear data included the latest REACLIB snapshot updated with recently measured nuclear masses and \(\beta p\)-branchings, with the authors investigating the impact of uncertainties by modifying the \(\beta\)-delayed proton branching of \(^{65}{\rm Se}\), the mass excess of \(^{65}{\rm Se}\), and the \(^{64}{\rm As}(p,\gamma)\) reaction rate.

## Key numbers

* The rp-process flow through the \(^{64}{\rm Ge}\) bypass could be up to 36% for maximally favorable nuclear physics assumptions.
* The \(\beta\)-delayed proton emission branching probability for \(^{65}{\rm Se}\) is \(P_{\beta p}(^{65}{\rm Se}) = 88^{+12}_{-13}\)%, with a 2\(\sigma\) reduction to 62% used in the calculations.
* The mass excess of \(^{65}{\rm Se}\) is ME(\(^{65}{\rm Se}) = -33020 \pm 300\) keV, with favorable values of \(-33320\) keV (1\(\sigma\) enhancement) and maximally favorable values of \(-33620\) keV (2\(\sigma\) enhancement).
* For a \(\times\)10 enhancement of the \(^{64}{\rm As}(p,\gamma)\) reaction rate, the bypass fraction achieves 14%; for a \(\times\)100 enhancement, it reaches 36%.
* The \(^{64}{\rm Ge}\) waiting-point bypass is potentially twice as strong as the \(^{56}{\rm Ni}\) waiting-point bypass studied by Saxena (2022).
* Nearby nuclides in the same isospin group have \(P_{\beta p}\) values of 84.7\(\pm\)1.4% for \(^{57}{\rm Zn}\), 62\(\pm\)4% for \(^{61}{\rm Ge}\), 99\(^{+1}_{-11}\)% for \(^{69}{\rm Kr}\), and \(\sim\)100% for \(^{73}{\rm Sr}\).

## Context

This work extends the recent finding by Zhou (2023) that \(^{66}{\rm Se}\) is more bound than previous theoretical predictions, applying the same assumption of increased binding to the neighboring isotope \(^{65}{\rm Se}\) to open a possible bypass of the \(^{64}{\rm Ge}\) waiting point. The bypass mechanism is analogous to that recently established for the \(^{56}{\rm Ni}\) waiting point by Forstner (2001), Langer (2014), Ong (2017), Valverde (2018), and Saxena (2022). The results call for new experimental studies of \(^{65}{\rm Se}\), including nuclear mass measurements with precision of order 100 keV, percent-level measurements of the \(\beta\)-delayed proton branching, and nuclear structure studies relevant to the \(^{64}{\rm As}(p,\gamma)\) reaction rate at x-ray burst temperatures around 0.8 GK.
