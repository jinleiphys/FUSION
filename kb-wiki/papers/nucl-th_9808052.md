# Dirac Sea Effects on Superfluidity in Nuclear Matter

> We study two kinds of Dirac sea effects on the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in nuclear matter based on the relativistic Hartree approximation to quantum hadrodynamics and the Gor'kov formalism. We show that the vacuum fluctuation effect on the nucleon effective mass is more important than the direct coupling between the Fermi sea and the Dirac sea due to the pairing interaction. The effects of the high-momentum cutoff are also discussed.

## Key claim
The vacuum fluctuation effect on the nucleon effective mass (a bulk property) is more important for the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in nuclear matter than the direct coupling between the Fermi sea and the Dirac sea due to the pairing interaction; the latter decouples from the Fermi sea pairing with an accuracy of $10^{-7}$ MeV.

## Method
The author formulates the pairing problem in nuclear matter using the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) to quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) with the $\sigma$-$\omega$ model, combined with the Gor'kov formalism for superfluidity. The Bogoliubov transformation amplitudes are derived from the normal and anomalous Green's functions, and the gap equation is solved self-consistently together with the equation for the nucleon effective mass $M^\star$, including the renormalized vacuum fluctuation contribution. The calculation is performed for symmetric nuclear matter in the $^1S_0$ channel, with the $\Delta N=0$ pairing (direct Dirac sea coupling) shown to be negligible.

## Key numbers
- The $\Delta N=0$ pairing gap $\delta(k)$ is of order $10^{-6}$ MeV or less in both the RHA and the mean-field theory (MFT), leading to decoupling of Fermi sea and Dirac sea pairing with an accuracy of $10^{-7}$.
- The maximum pairing gap occurs at Fermi momentum $k_{\rm F} = 0.9$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The cutoff momentum $\Lambda_{\rm c}$ is taken as 15 fm$^{-1}$ for numerical convergence; plateaus in the gap as a function of cutoff appear around $\Lambda_{\rm c} = 2$–3 fm$^{-1}$ in the RHA and $\Lambda_{\rm c} = 1$–2 fm$^{-1}$ in the MFT.
- Parameters used: $M = 939$ MeV, $m_\sigma = 550$ MeV, $m_\omega = 783$ MeV, $g_\sigma^2 = 62.89$ (RHA) or 91.64 (MFT), $g_\omega^2 = 79.78$ (RHA) or 136.2 (MFT).

## Context
This work extends the relativistic treatment of pairing in nuclear matter by Kucharek and Ring (1991), who first used a one-boson-exchange interaction in the gap equation and found gaps about three times larger than non-relativistic calculations. It also builds on the work of Guimarães et al. (1995), who included antinucleon degrees of freedom in the OBE step. The author shows that the vacuum fluctuation effect on $M^\star$ (the bulk Dirac sea effect) is more significant than the direct Fermi-Dirac sea coupling, and that the MFT result depends more strongly on the high-momentum cutoff than the RHA result. This enables future studies of nucleon-antinucleon polarization in the particle-particle channel as a possible improvement.
