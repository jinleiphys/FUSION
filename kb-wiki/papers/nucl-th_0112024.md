# Singularities of ultra-relativistic expanding quark-gluon plasma within Quark-gluon transport theory

> We follow Quark-gluon transport theory and analyse singularities of the ultra-relativistic and spherical expanding quark-gluon plasma. Within the linearized QCD oscillations and instabilities of the ultra-relativistic and spherical symmetric expanding quark-gluon plasma near global thermodynamical equilibrium are investigated in dependence on a chemical potential mu(T) of non-strange light quarks and antiquarks, a strange quark mass m_s, a temperature T and a hydrodynamical velocity $u$. We calculate the chromoelectric permeability tensor for the quark-gluon plasma at rest and ultra-relativistic moving. We show that the contribution of chemical potential of light quarks and antiquarks can be neglected to the chromoelectric permeability. We show that the account for the non-zero mass of strange quarks and antiquarks diminishes the value of the plasma frequency. We show that the plasma frequency of the ultra-relativistic and spherical symmetric expanding quark-gluon plasma is enhanced by a Lorentz factor compared with the plasma frequency of the quark-gluon plasma at rest. We find that the ultra-relativistic and spherical symmetric expanding quark-gluon plasma behaves like a collisionless thermalized plasma.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for an ultra-relativistic and spherically symmetric expanding quark-gluon plasma near global thermodynamic equilibrium, the chromoelectric permeability tensor retains the same form as for the plasma at rest, except that the plasma frequency is enhanced by a Lorentz factor; the contribution of the chemical potential of light quarks and antiquarks can be neglected, and the non-zero mass of strange quarks and antiquarks diminishes the plasma frequency.

## Method

The authors use the linearized quark-gluon transport (QGT) theory, based on relativistic kinetic equations with Vlasov-type mean-field terms and collision terms in the relaxation-time approximation, to compute the colour conductivity tensor and then the chromoelectric permeability tensor for the quark-gluon plasma. They evaluate the tensor components analytically for the plasma at rest (with a chemical potential for light quarks and a strange quark mass of 135 MeV) and then generalize to the ultra-relativistic expanding case by boosting to a frame with hydrodynamical four-velocity \(u^\mu = (\gamma, \gamma \vec{v})\) where \(\gamma \gg 1\). The dispersion relations for oscillations and instabilities are then analyzed via the longitudinal and transverse parts of the chromoelectric permeability.

## Key numbers

* For the plasma at rest, the contribution of strange quarks and antiquarks to the chromoelectric permeability is suppressed by a factor less than 0.11, evaluated at \(m_s = 135\ \text{MeV}\) and \(T = 175\ \text{MeV}\) with \(N_C = 3\).
* The chemical potential of light quarks at freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 175\ \text{MeV}\) satisfies \(\mu(T)/T \simeq 0.29\), which the authors deem insignificant for the chromoelectric permeability.
* The plasma frequency of the ultra-relativistic expanding quark-gluon plasma is enhanced by a factor of the Lorentz factor \(\gamma\) (specifically, by twice the Lorentz factor) compared to the plasma frequency of the plasma at rest.

## Context

This work extends the linearized QCD analysis of oscillations and instabilities in the quark-gluon plasma by Elze and Heinz (1990s) and Mrowczynski (1990s), who previously calculated the chromoelectric permeability for a plasma at rest with massless quarks. The paper introduces the effects of a non-zero strange quark mass and a chemical potential for light quarks, and it generalizes the calculation to an ultra-relativistically expanding plasma, showing that the plasma frequency is boosted by a Lorentz factor. This enables more realistic modeling of the QGP produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the plasma expands spherically at near-light speeds.
