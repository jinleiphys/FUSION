# Understanding the Possible Proton Antiproton Bound State Observed by BES Collaboration

> We comment on the quantum numbers and decay channels of the possible proton antiproton bound state observed by BES Collaboration. Based on the general symmetry consideration and available experimental information, we conclude that the quantum number of this state is very likely to be $J^{PC}=0^{-+}, I^G=0^+$, which can not decay into final states $π^+π^-, 2π^0, K{\bar K}, 3π$. Besides its dissociation into $p\bar p$, the other important mesonic decay modes could be $ηππ$, $η'ππ$, $ηηη$, $4π$, $K{\bar K}π$, $ηK{\bar K}$, $K\bar K ππ$, 6$π$. Experimental search of this signal in these meson final states is strongly called for.

# Understanding the Possible Proton Antiproton Bound State Observed by BES Collaboration

## Key claim
Based on general symmetry considerations and the absence of a signal in BES searches for the state in two-pion, three-pion, and two-kaon final states, the quantum numbers of the possible proton-antiproton bound state near threshold are very likely to be \(J^{PC}=0^{-+}, I^G=0^+\), which forbids decay into \(\pi^+\pi^-\), \(2\pi^0\), \(K\bar K\), and \(3\pi\).

## Method
The authors applied conservation laws for angular momentum, parity, C-parity, isospin, and G-parity to constrain the possible quantum numbers of the near-threshold enhancement observed by BES in \(J/\psi \to \gamma p\bar p\). They enumerated the allowed mesonic decay channels for each candidate quantum number assignment and compared these predictions with the null results from BES searches in \(\gamma 2\pi\), \(\gamma 3\pi\), and \(\gamma K\bar K\) final states to eliminate all assignments except \(0^{-+}0^+\).

## Key numbers
- The S-wave Breit-Wigner fit to the near-threshold enhancement gives a peak mass \(M=1859^{+3}_{-10}(\text{stat})^{+5}_{-25}(\text{sys})\) MeV below \(2m_p\), with total width less than 30 MeV.
- The P-wave fit gives a peak mass \(M=1876.4\pm 0.9\) MeV and total width \(\Gamma =4.6\pm 1.8\) MeV.
- The photon polar angle distribution is consistent with \(1+\cos^2\theta_\gamma\), favoring total angular momentum \(J=0\).

## Context
This work extends the long history of theoretical studies of nucleon-antinucleon bound states, reviewed extensively in Ref. pr, and follows the experimental observation by the BES Collaboration (2003) of a narrow enhancement in \(J/\psi \to \gamma p\bar p\). The analysis builds on earlier potential model work by Datta and O'Donnell, who described a deuteron-like singlet state, and on Rosner's discussion of low-mass baryon-antibaryon enhancements in B meson decays. The paper provides specific mesonic decay channels for experimental confirmation, which would help distinguish between a baryonium, a proton-antiproton molecule, or a six-quark state.
