# Gauge invariant formulation of 3$γ$ decay of particle-antiparticle bound states

> We construct the gauge invariant three-photon decay amplitude of particle-antiparticle bound states modeled by the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. Application to the quark-antiquark ($q\bar{q}$) bound states is emphasized. An essential aspect of our formulation is that it applies to any underlying quantum field theoretic model of the $q\bar{q}$ system, and not just to models, like exact QCD, where the quark self-energy $Σ$ couples to the electromagnetic field solely via dressed quark propagators. In this way, applications to effective field theories and other QCD motivated models are envisioned. The three-photon decay amplitude is constructed by attaching currents to all possible places in the Feynman diagrams contributing to the dressed quark propagator. The gauge invariance of our construction is thus a direct consequence of respecting the underlying structure of the quantum field theory determining the dynamics. In the resultant expression for the three-photon decay amplitude, all the basic ingredients consisting of the bound state wave function, the final-state interaction $q\bar{q}$ $t$ matrix, the dressed quark propagator, and dressed quark currents, are determined by a universal Bethe-Salpeter kernel.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a gauge-invariant three-photon decay amplitude for particle-antiparticle bound states that applies to any quantum field theoretic model of the quark-antiquark system, not just to models where the quark self-energy couples to the electromagnetic field solely via dressed quark propagators; this generality is essential because the widely used Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and other effective theories would otherwise violate gauge invariance.

## Method

The authors use the "gauging of equations method" to attach electromagnetic currents to all possible places in the Feynman diagrams contributing to the dressed quark propagator, starting from the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark self-energy. They then extract the one-photon decay amplitude from the residue of the resulting three-point Green function at the bound state pole, and iterate this procedure to obtain the two-photon and three-photon decay amplitudes. All basic ingredients (bound state wave function, final-state interaction t-matrix, dressed quark propagator, and dressed quark currents) are determined by a universal Bethe-Salpeter kernel.

## Key numbers

* The three-photon decay rate is proportional to alpha^3, making it very small for quarkonia compared to orthopositronium.
* For J/psi to 3gamma decay, the lowest order prediction is "several times greater than the experimental result" and including known O(alpha_s) and O(v^2) corrections worsens the situation (no explicit numerical branching fraction given in the truncated text).
* The bound state pole is located at P^2 = M_Phi^2, where M_Phi is the meson mass.

## Context

This work extends the gauging of equations method previously developed by Haberzettl (1997) and by Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (KB1-KB4) to the specific case of three-photon decays of bound states. It addresses a known problem where Low's theorem (Low, 1958) is violated for all currently popular orthopositronium decay amplitudes (Smith, Smith1), and where nonrelativistic QCD calculations for J/psi to 3gamma (Feng, 2012) struggle to reconcile theory with experiment. The formulation enables applications to continuum QCD approaches based on Dyson-Schwinger equations (Alkofer, 2000; Maris, 2003; Fischer, 2006; Roberts, 2007; Eichmann, 2009-2011) and to effective field theories like the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Klevansky, 1992).
