# Quantum chaos in the perfect fluid: spectrum of initial fluctuations in the little bang

> We outline how unstable quantum fluctuations decohere classical fields in heavy ion collisions, leading to an equation of state and hydrodynamics. Explicit numerical realization of this framework in a scalar $ϕ^4$ theory demonstrates that anomalously low values of $η/s$ can be generated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that anomalously low values of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) can be generated in heavy ion collisions through the decoherence of unstable quantum fluctuations into classical fields, but this result is demonstrated only in a scalar φ⁴ theory as an analogy, not in full QCD.

## Method

The authors formulate a weak-coupling approach within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, where quantum fluctuations are resummed and expressed as a Gaussian spectrum of initial fluctuations on a Cauchy surface at proper time τ=0⁺. They then solve the classical Yang-Mills equations repeatedly with these initial conditions, and test the framework numerically in a massless scalar φ⁴ theory in a fixed box and in an expanding 1+1-D geometry, extracting the stress-energy tensor and matching it to first-order viscous hydrodynamics to obtain η/s.

## Key numbers

- The extracted η/s ≈ 0.26 from matching to first-order viscous hydrodynamics in the scalar φ⁴ theory
- This value is two orders of magnitude lower than the perturbative value η/s ∼ 10⁴/g⁴ ∼ 40 for g=4
- The extracted η/s is about three times the conjectured AdS/CFT universal lower bound η/s = 1/4π
- For spatially independent fields and fluctuations in φ⁴ theory, the period is T_period = 18.2/gΔφ_max
- The parametric time scale for instability growth in gauge theories is τ ∼ ln²(1/α_S)/Q_S

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous resummation of leading temporal instabilities (Dusling et al., 2011) and provides a concrete numerical realization in scalar φ⁴ theory as a testbed for the gauge theory case. The results relate to work in the AdS/CFT framework on universal features of relaxation to hydrodynamics (Heller et al.) and aim to improve state-of-the-art Glasma+hydro computations (Schenke et al.) by providing non-equilibrium initial conditions that can be matched to viscous hydrodynamics without ad hoc assumptions.
