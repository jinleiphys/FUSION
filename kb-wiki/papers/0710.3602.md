# Multichannel chiral approach for kaonic hydrogen

> We present an exact solution to the $K^{-}$-proton bound state problem formulated in the momentum space. The 1s level characteristics of the kaonic hydrogen are described together with the available low energy $\bar{K}N$ data.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact momentum-space solution for the kaonic hydrogen 1s level, claiming that the strong-interaction energy shift is reproduced well but the width is significantly larger than the experimental value; however, the authors note that within three standard deviations and considering older KEK results, the kaonic hydrogen measurements do not necessarily contradict other low-energy \(\bar{K}N\) data.

## Method

The authors constructed a separable potential matrix for ten coupled meson-baryon channels (\(K^-p\), \(\bar{K}^{0}n\), \(\pi^{0}\Lambda\), \(\pi^{+}\Sigma^{-}\), \(\pi^{0}\Sigma^{0}\), \(\pi^{-}\Sigma^{+}\), \(\eta \Lambda\), \(\eta \Sigma^0\), \(K^+\Xi^-\), and \(K^0 \Xi^0\)) based on chiral SU(3) symmetry up to second order in meson center-of-mass kinetic energies. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for transition amplitudes and the bound state problem via Jost matrix construction, fitting 11 free parameters (five inverse range radii, the pseudoscalar meson decay constant \(f\), and five second-order chiral couplings) to 15 data points including threshold branching ratios, total cross sections at kaon laboratory momenta of 110 MeV and 200 MeV, and the DEAR results for kaonic hydrogen.

## Key numbers

- Strong interaction shift \(\Delta E_N\) of the 1s level in kaonic hydrogen: 232 eV (for \(\sigma_{\pi N}=20\) MeV), 272 eV (for \(\sigma_{\pi N}=30\) MeV), 257 eV (for \(\sigma_{\pi N}=40\) MeV), 266 eV (for \(\sigma_{\pi N}=50\) MeV); experimental value is 193(43) eV.
- Width \(\Gamma\) of the 1s level in kaonic hydrogen: 725 eV (for \(\sigma_{\pi N}=20\) MeV), 683 eV (for \(\sigma_{\pi N}=30\) MeV), 713 eV (for \(\sigma_{\pi N}=40\) MeV), 708 eV (for \(\sigma_{\pi N}=50\) MeV); experimental value is 249(150) eV.
- Threshold branching ratios from fits: \(\gamma = 2.366\)–2.370, \(R_c = 0.657\)–0.658, \(R_n = 0.190\)–0.191; experimental values are \(\gamma = 2.36(4)\), \(R_c = 0.664(11)\), \(R_n = 0.189(15)\).
- \(\chi^2\) per data point ranges from 1.33 to 1.40 across the four \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) values.
- Exact solution for the 1s level complex energy (for \(\sigma_{\pi N}=20\) MeV): \(232 - (i/2)725\) eV, compared to the modified Deser-Trueman formula result of \(251 - (i/2)714\) eV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel chiral approach of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) by including all ten meson-baryon channels open at the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold rather than only six, and applies the exact momentum-space bound state method previously developed for pionic atoms by Cieply and Smejkal (1996). The exact solution is compared to the standard Deser-Trueman formula and the modified version by Meissner, Raha, and Rusetsky (2004), showing that the modified formula is sufficient for current experimental precision but may need replacement for the upcoming SIDDHARTA experiment.
