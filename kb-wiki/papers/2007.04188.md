# Properties of the QCD thermal transition with $N_f=2+1$ flavours of Wilson quark

> We study properties of the thermal transition in QCD, using anisotropic, fixed-scale lattice simulations with $N_f = 2+1$ flavours of Wilson fermion. Observables are compared for two values of the pion mass, focusing on chiral properties. Results are presented for the Polyakov loop, various susceptibilities, the chiral condensate and its susceptibility, and the onset of parity doubling in the light and strange baryonic sector.

## Key claim

The paper characterizes the QCD thermal crossover using anisotropic lattice simulations with Wilson fermions at two pion masses (384 MeV and 236 MeV), finding that the pseudocritical temperature decreases as the light quark mass is reduced, but the precise value depends on which observable is used to define the transition.

## Method

The authors performed fixed-scale lattice QCD simulations with \(N_f = 2+1\) flavours of Wilson fermion on anisotropic lattices, using the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration's action and parameter tuning. They generated ensembles at eleven different temperatures for the lighter pion mass (Gen2L, \(m_\pi = 236\) MeV) and compared results with previous ensembles at a heavier pion mass (Gen2, \(m_\pi = 384\) MeV), measuring the Polyakov loop, quark number susceptibilities, chiral condensate and susceptibility, and parity doubling in baryonic correlators.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass for Gen2 ensembles: \(m_\pi = 384(4)\) MeV; for Gen2L ensembles: \(m_\pi = 236(2)\) MeV
- Temporal lattice spacing: \(a_\tau = 0.0350(2)\) fm (Gen2) and \(a_\tau = 0.0330(2)\) fm (Gen2L)
- Renormalised anisotropy: \(\xi = 3.444(6)\) (Gen2) and \(\xi = 3.453(6)\) (Gen2L)
- Pseudocritical temperature from renormalised Polyakov loop: \(183^{+5}_{-8}\) MeV (Gen2) and \(183^{+6}_{-3}\) MeV (Gen2L)
- Pseudocritical temperature from single heavy-quark entropy peak: 168(5) MeV (Gen2) and 144(8) MeV (Gen2L)
- Pseudocritical temperature from renormalised chiral condensate: 181(2) MeV (Gen2) and 164(2) MeV (Gen2L)
- Pseudocritical temperature from chiral susceptibility: 170(3) MeV (Gen2) and 165(2) MeV (Gen2L)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of QCD thermodynamics and spectroscopy using Wilson fermions (Aarts 2014, Aarts 2015, Aarts 2017, Aarts 2018) by introducing lighter quark masses while keeping the lattice spacing fixed. It provides a systematic comparison with results from the Budapest-Wuppertal group (Borsanyi 2012, 2015) and the WHOT collaboration (Umeda 2012, Taniguchi 2016, 2020), who used staggered or twisted-mass fermions, and enables future spectroscopic analyses of hadron properties across the crossover region with lighter quarks.
