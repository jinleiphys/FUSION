# Dilepton production spectrum above Tc with a lattice quark propagator

> The dilepton production rate from the deconfined medium is analyzed with the photon self-energies constructed from quark propagators obtained by lattice numerical simulation for two values of temperature $T=1.5T_{\rm c}$ and $3T_{\rm c}$ above the critical temperature $T_{\rm c}$. The photon self-energy is calculated by the Schwinger-Dyson equation with the lattice quark propagtor and a vertex function determined so as to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity. The obtained dilepton production rate at zero momentum exhibits divergences reflecting van Hove singularity, and is significantly enhanced around $ω\simeq T$ compared with the rate obtained by the perturbative analysis.

## Key claim

The dilepton production rate at zero momentum, calculated non-perturbatively using lattice quark propagators and a vertex function satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity, exhibits van Hove singularities and is significantly enhanced around $\omega \simeq T$ compared to perturbative results, but the calculation relies on a two-pole ansatz for the quark spectral function and an approximation for the spatial components of the vertex function.

## Method

The photon self-energy was computed via the Schwinger-Dyson equation using full quark propagators obtained from quenched lattice QCD simulations in Landau gauge at $T=1.5T_{\rm c}$ and $3T_{\rm c}$, with the quark spectral function parametrized by a two-pole ansatz representing normal and plasmino modes. The vertex function was constructed to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity, with the temporal component determined exactly and the spatial components obtained under the approximation that the $\bm{q}$-derivative of $\Gamma_0$ vanishes at $\bm{q}=0$. The dilepton production rate at zero total three-momentum was then extracted from the imaginary part of the retarded photon self-energy.

## Key numbers

- Thermal mass $m_{\rm T}/T = 0.768(11)$ at $T=1.5T_{\rm c}$ and $0.725(14)$ at $T=3T_{\rm c}$, obtained from lattice data after extrapolation to infinite volume
- Lattice volume $128^3 \times 16$ used for the quark propagator analysis
- Two temperatures studied: $T=1.5T_{\rm c}$ and $T=3T_{\rm c}$
- The dilepton production rate shows enhancement of "one order or more" compared to the free quark gas result
- The rate at $m\simeq 500$ MeV from PHENIX is "about one order larger than the cocktail" (cited experimental context)

## Context

This work extends non-perturbative analyses of dilepton production using lattice QCD, addressing the difficulty of analytic continuation from imaginary-time correlators noted by Karsch (2001) and Ding, where the maximum entropy method has limited sensitivity to low-energy structure. The approach uses the lattice quark propagator from quenched studies by the same authors (2014-2015) and constructs a vertex satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity, differing from the vertex used in earlier Schwinger-Dyson studies by Harada, Gao, and Fischer. The results enable comparison with hard thermal loop resummed perturbation theory calculations by Braaten and Pisarski (1990) and by Thoma (1995), and provide input for interpreting the dilepton enhancement observed by PHENIX at RHIC.
