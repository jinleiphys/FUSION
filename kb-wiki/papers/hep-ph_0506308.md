# The Color Glass Condensate and hadron production in the forward region

> We consider one loop corrections to single inclusive particle production in parton-nucleus scattering at high energies, treating the target nucleus as a Color Glass Condensate. We prove by explicit computation that in the leading log Q^2 approximation, these corrections lead to collinear factorization and DGLAP evolution of the projectile parton distribution and hadron fragmentation functions. At leading logarithmic level, and in single-inclusive cross sections, only two-point functions of Wilson lines in the adjoint and fundamental representations (Mueller's dipoles) arise, which can be obtained from the solution of the JIMWLK equations. The application of our results to forward-rapidity production at RHIC energy shows that, in general, recoil effects are large and that the forward rapidity region at RHIC is rather different from the central region at LHC, despite comparable gluon densities in the target. We show that both the quantum x-evolution of the high-density target as well as the DGLAP Q^2-evolution of the parton distribution and fragmentation functions are clearly seen in the BRAHMS data. This provides additional strong evidence for the Color Glass Condensate at RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors prove that one-loop corrections to single inclusive particle production in parton-nucleus scattering, treating the target as a Color Glass Condensate, lead to collinear factorization and DGLAP evolution of the projectile parton distribution and hadron fragmentation functions in the leading log Q² approximation; however, they note that at RHIC energy, incoherent leading-twist interactions with partons from the nucleus at x_A ~ 0.01 contribute significantly and may require additional suppression mechanisms not constructed in this paper.

## Method

The authors compute one-loop corrections to quark-nucleus scattering due to gluon radiation, isolating diagrams with collinear singularities that yield logarithms of Q². They square the amplitudes, integrate over the outgoing quark transverse momentum, and reorganize the terms to show that the cross section factorizes into DGLAP-evolved parton distribution functions, dipole cross sections (two-point functions of Wilson lines in the fundamental and adjoint representations), and DGLAP-evolved fragmentation functions. The final result for hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions is applied to deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC using the Kharzeev-Kovchegov-Tuchin phenomenological parametrization for the dipole profile.

## Key numbers

* The saturation momentum of the nucleus at rapidity y is given by Q_s(y) = Q_0 exp[λ(y − y_0)/2] with Q_0 = 1 GeV, y_0 = 0.6, and λ ≈ 0.3.
* The BRAHMS minimum-bias data for h⁻ production at forward rapidity is reproduced with a transverse-momentum-independent K-factor of 1.6 (for Q² = P_t²).
* For hadrons produced at rapidity Y ≈ 3.2 (BRAHMS), the average momentum fraction probed in the target nucleus is ⟨x_A⟩ ~ 10⁻³; for Y ≈ 4 (STAR), ⟨x_A⟩ ~ 10⁻⁴.
* The saturation momentum at y = 4 increases by ~13% compared to the BRAHMS kinematics.
* For central collisions, the saturation momentum of the nucleus increases by ~50% relative to minimum-bias collisions.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate formalism for hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions by including recoil effects from DGLAP evolution, which were neglected in prior approaches such as those by Kovchegov and collaborators (2001) and by Blaizot, Gelis, and Venugopalan (2004) that treated the proton as a dilute CGC subject to BFKL evolution. The authors show that both the quantum x-evolution of the target (anomalous dimension of its gluon distribution) and the DGLAP Q²-evolution of the projectile parton distribution and fragmentation functions are required to describe the BRAHMS data, providing additional evidence for the Color Glass Condensate at RHIC. The framework enables future comparisons with STAR π⁰ data at y ~ 4 and with ab initio solutions of the JIMWLK equations for the dipole profiles.
