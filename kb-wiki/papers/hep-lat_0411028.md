# Lattice Computations of the Pion Form Factor

> We report on a program to compute the electromagnetic form factors of mesons. We discuss the techniques used to compute the pion form factor and present results computed with domain wall valence fermions on MILC asqtad lattices, as well as with Wilson fermions on quenched lattices. The methods can easily be extended to rho-to-gamma-pi transition form factors.

## Key claim

The paper reports on a lattice QCD program to compute the pion electromagnetic form factor, but the results are preliminary and the extracted VMD pole mass from dynamical domain-wall fermion data at the lightest valence quark mass (0.01) with sink momentum (1,1,0) yields a mean square charge radius of 0.310(46) fm², which is significantly below the experimental value of 0.439(8) fm², indicating that more dynamical quark masses and a proper chiral extrapolation are needed.

## Method

The form factor was computed using two methods: a fitting method that simultaneously extracts the form factor, pion energies, and amplitudes from two- and three-point correlation functions, and a ratio method that constructs a ratio of correlators independent of amplitudes and Euclidean time exponentials. Quenched Wilson fermion calculations were performed at β=6.0 (a⁻¹ ≈ 2 GeV) on 16³×32 and 24³×32 lattices, while dynamical calculations used domain-wall valence fermions on MILC asqtad lattices at a⁻¹ ≈ 1.6 GeV with 20³×64 volume. The sequential source method was employed to compute three-point functions with local and point-split vector currents.

## Key numbers

- Quenched Wilson fermion calculations reached pion masses as low as 300 MeV (κ=0.1563, mπ=0.1921(29) in lattice units) without observing exceptional configurations
- For dynamical domain-wall fermions, the lightest pion mass achieved was 318(3) MeV (am_val=0.01, mπL=3.97(4))
- VMD fit to domain-wall data with m_val=0.01 and sink momentum (1,0,0) gave mVMD = 0.888(56) GeV with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.37(72)
- Unconstrained linear extrapolation of m_val=0.01 and 0.081 data with sink momentum (1,1,0) gave mVMD = 0.868(65) GeV in the zero quark mass limit
- Extracted mean square charge radius ⟨r²⟩_VMD = 0.310(46) fm², compared to experimental value ⟨r²⟩_expt = 0.439(8) fm²
- For m_val=0.081 with sink momentum (1,1,0), VMD fit gave mVMD = 1.022(87) GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations that validated vector meson dominance at low Q² (Martinelli 1988, Draper 1989) and more recent results extending to Q² up to 2 GeV² (van der Heide 2003, Nemoto 2003, Abdel-Rehim 2004). The paper enables future calculations of the ρ⁺→γπ⁺ transition form factor using the same propagators and sequential propagators already computed. The results highlight the need for lighter dynamical quark masses and proper chiral extrapolation to match experimental data, and demonstrate that local axial vector operators are preferable to pseudoscalar operators for higher momentum states due to better signal-to-noise ratios.
