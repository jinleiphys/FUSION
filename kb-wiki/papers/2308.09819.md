# Modified Urca neutrino emissivity at finite temperature

> Charged current neutrino-nucleon reactions, generally called Urca processes, are crucial actors of a neutron star's thermal evolution. The so-called direct processes show a pronounced threshold under which the reaction is kinematically suppressed. This suppression does not apply to "modified" Urca processes which involve interaction with an additional nucleon. Calculations of the modified Urca neutrino rates were established for cold neutron star matter and for dilute hot matter, in both cases under strong assumptions. In this paper, we revise the calculations of the modified Urca neutrino rates for dense and hot matter, and for different compositions. We study the influence of different approximations used in previous computations. We derive expressions for the rates of modified Urca neutrino emissivity within thermal field theory and perform the phase space integration numerically using mainly importance sampling Monte-Carlo techniques. The neutrino emissivity of modified and direct processes are established and compared. We find in particular that the modified Urca process is not necessarily suppressed with respect to the direct process above the threshold of the latter at moderate densities and temperatures, in contrast to what is generally assumed. Numerical results are confirmed by an estimation of the ratio of modified to direct Urca rates with a simple analytic approximation, thereby showing the regimes of suppression for the modified processes depending on temperature and density. These results show that modified Urca rates have to be considered carefully upon evaluating neutrino opacities in dense and warm matter.

## Key claim

The authors find that the modified Urca process is not necessarily suppressed relative to the direct Urca process above the direct Urca threshold at moderate densities and temperatures, contrary to what is generally assumed; this result is confirmed by a simple analytic approximation for the ratio of modified to direct Urca rates, showing regimes of suppression depending on temperature and density.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for the modified Urca neutrino emissivity within thermal field theory, treating nucleons at the mean-field level with effective masses and chemical potentials from the SRO(APR) equation of state, and using the one-pion exchange approximation for the strong interaction between nucleons. They perform the phase space integration numerically using importance sampling Monte Carlo techniques, and compare the neutrino emissivity of modified and direct Urca processes for dense and hot matter with different compositions.

## Key numbers

* The pion-nucleon-nucleon coupling constant is taken as \(f_{\pi NN} = 1.01\) and the pion mass as \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV for numerical applications.
* The neutrino emissivity and mean free path are computed for matter temperatures up to several tens of MeV, with the zero-temperature limit treated at approximately 1 MeV for mature neutron stars.
* The SRO(APR) equation of state from the CompOSE online database provides nucleon effective chemical potentials and effective masses as functions of temperature, density, and lepton fraction.

## Context

This work revises calculations of modified Urca neutrino rates for dense and hot matter, extending prior studies by Friman (1979) for cold beta-equilibrated matter and by Yakovlev (1995, 2001) for the proton branch and nonzero electron momenta, as well as finite-temperature treatments by Alford (2018, 2021) that kept the Fermi surface approximation. The results enable more accurate evaluation of neutrino opacities in dense and warm matter, relevant for proto-neutron stars, binary neutron star merger remnants, and core-collapse supernova simulations.
