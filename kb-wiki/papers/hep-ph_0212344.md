# Comparison of relativistic bound-state calculations in Front-Form and Instant-Form Dynamics

> Using the Wick-Cutkosky model and an extended version (massive exchange) of it, we have calculated the bound states in a quantum field theoretical approach. In the light-front formalism we have calculated the bound-state mass spectrum and wave functions. Using the Terent'ev transformation we can write down an approximation for the angular dependence of the wave function. After calculating the bound-state spectra we characterized all states found. Similarly, we have calculated the bound-state spectrum and wave functions in the instant-form formalism. We compare the spectra found in both forms of dynamics in the ladder approximation and show that in both forms of dynamics the O(4) symmetry is broken.

## Key claim

Using the Wick-Cutkosky model and its massive-exchange extension, the authors show that in the ladder approximation both light-front dynamics (LFD) and instant-form dynamics (IFD) break the O(4) symmetry of the bound-state spectrum, contradicting the expectation that such symmetry breaking might occur only in LFD due to its broken manifest rotational invariance.

## Method

The authors derived bound-state equations in both LFD and IFD by truncating Fock space to the two- and three-particle sectors, eliminating the three-particle sector, and keeping only ladder-exchange terms (no self-energy contributions). In LFD, the wave functions were expanded in cubic splines for the plus-momentum variable and in Jacobi-polynomial basis functions for the transverse momentum, while in IFD the wave functions were expanded in spherical harmonics and Olsson-Weniger basis functions. The resulting integral equations were solved numerically for the mass spectra and wave functions, and states in LFD were assigned orbital angular momentum quantum numbers by applying the Terent'ev transformation and projecting onto spherical harmonics, accepting assignments only when the overlap exceeded 85%.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state mass in LFD for massless exchange at coupling \(\alpha = 6.0\) (g = 17.36): 0.811 (in units of the boson mass m), compared to 0.820 from Mangin-Brinet et al. (2000).
* Ground-state mass in LFD for massless exchange at \(\alpha = 1.0\) (g = 7.09): 1.922, matching the value 1.923 from Mangin-Brinet et al. (2000).
* Ground-state mass in IFD for massless exchange at g = 17.36 (\(\alpha = 6\)): 1.4931, versus 0.811 in LFD for the same coupling.
* For massive exchange (\(\mu = 0.15\) m) at g = 17.36, the LFD 1S mass is 0.923 and the IFD 1S mass is 1.5384.
* For massless exchange at g = 10.03 (\(\alpha = 2\)), the LFD 1S mass is 1.788 and the IFD 1S mass is 1.9366 (from Table not fully shown but referenced in text).
* Numerical accuracy: LFD masses for the lowest state for any l are accurate to at least three decimal places; states with principal quantum number n = 3 have an estimated absolute error of 0.004; IFD masses are accurate to four decimal places.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of LFD and IFD bound-state calculations in the Wick-Cutkosky model, previously studied by Mangin-Brinet et al. (2000) for ground states in covariant LFD, and by Ji and Furnstahl (1986) using variational methods for 1S and 2P states. The authors show for the first time that O(4) symmetry breaking occurs in IFD as well as LFD, and that LFD states generally have more binding (lower masses) than IFD states at the same coupling. This enables future comparisons with more realistic models such as the Yukawa model, where Glazek et al. (1993) and Fuda et al. have done pioneering work, and provides a benchmark for assessing the accuracy of the ladder approximation in different forms of relativistic dynamics.
