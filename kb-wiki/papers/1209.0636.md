# Disentangling the EMC Effect

> The deep inelastic scattering cross section for scattering from bound nucleons differs from that of free nucleons.This phenomena, first discovered 30 years ago, is known as the EMC effect and is still not fully understood. Recent analysis of world data showed that the strength of the EMC effect is linearly correlated with the relative amount of Two-Nucleon Short Range Correlated pairs (2N-SRC) in nuclei. The latter are pairs of nucleons whose wave functions overlap, giving them large relative momentum and low center of mass momentum, where high and low is relative to the Fermi momentum of the nucleus. The observed correlation indicates that the EMC effect, like 2N-SRC pairs, is related to high momentum nucleons in the nucleus. This paper reviews previous studies of the EMC-SRC correlation and studies its robustness. It also presents a planned experiment aimed at studying the origin of this EMC-SRC correlation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed linear correlation between the strength of the EMC effect and the relative amount of Two-Nucleon Short Range Correlated (2N-SRC) pairs in nuclei is robust and does not depend on the specific theoretical corrections applied in extracting the SRC scaling factors, though the slope of the correlation changes slightly when center-of-mass motion corrections are included.

## Method

The authors reviewed previous global analyses of world data on deep inelastic scattering (DIS) cross section ratios for nuclei relative to deuterium at moderate to large four-momentum transfer (Q² ≥ 2 (GeV/c)²) and 0.3 ≤ x_B ≤ 0.7, comparing the strength of the EMC effect (defined as the slope of the per-nucleon DIS cross section ratio for 0.35 ≤ x_B ≤ 0.7) with SRC scaling factors a₂(A/d) extracted using different sets of theoretical corrections. They tested the robustness of the correlation by comparing three correction schemes: (1) radiative and isoscalar corrections as used by Egiyan et al., (2) radiative, Coulomb, inelastic, and center-of-mass motion corrections as used by Fomin et al., and (3) radiative, Coulomb, and inelastic corrections without the center-of-mass motion correction. The paper also presents a planned experiment (E12-11-107) at Jefferson Lab's 12 GeV program that will study the structure function of high-momentum nucleons in deuterium by performing DIS and tagging backward recoil nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The EMC effect is observed at Q² ≥ 2 (GeV/c)² and 0.3 ≤ x_B ≤ 0.7
- The SRC scaling factor extraction uses data at Q² ≥ 1.4 (GeV/c)² and 1 ≤ x_B ≤ 2
- For medium nuclei, the Fermi momentum k_F ≈ 250 MeV/c
- In medium and heavy nuclei, 2N-SRC account for about 20-25% of the nucleons and approximately 80% of the kinetic energy carried by nucleons
- The center-of-mass motion correction is on the order of ~20% for medium and heavy nuclei (A ≥ 12)
- The linear fit of the EMC-SRC correlation gives a χ² per degree of freedom in the range of 0.8-1 for all sets of corrections
- The d/u parton distribution ratio at the limit of x_B → 1 was constrained to 0.23 ± 0.09
- The nucleon swelling level in deuterium was constrained to 0.8 ± 0.6%
- The proposed experiment will use a Large Acceptance Detector (LAD) consisting of 132 Time of Flight scintillators covering a solid angle of 1 Sr at backward angles of 90°-180° at 4 meters from the target

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Weinstein et al. (2011) of a linear correlation between the EMC effect and 2N-SRC pairs, and tests its robustness against different theoretical corrections used in extracting SRC scaling factors from the data of Egiyan et al. and Fomin et al. The paper contradicts the earlier assumption that the EMC effect scales linearly with average nuclear density (Gomez et al., 1994), noting that Beryllium is an outlier with an anomalously large EMC effect compared to its low average density. The results enable extraction of the free neutron structure function corrected for the deuteron In Medium Correction (IMC) effect, which was incorporated into the CTEQ-JLab (CJ) global QCD analysis to constrain off-shell nucleon structure in deuterium and the d/u parton distribution ratio. The planned experiment E12-11-107 will test whether the internal structure of high-momentum nucleons is modified, which could explain the EMC-SRC correlation.
