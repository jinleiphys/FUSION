# The form factor expansion in the precision $β$ decay era

> Precision tests of the Standard Model using $β$ decay have always relied on a careful choice of transition to minimize residual nuclear structure uncertainties. Following breakthroughs in nucleon-level radiative corrections in the last decade, however, corrections due to nuclear structure are once more a limiting factor in several scenarios. Progress in ab initio nuclear theory provides a path forward, but common recoil-order approximations in traditional formalisms often go unnoticed. Here, we critically examine their origin and address recently resolved and identify open questions.

## Key claim

While precision tests of the Standard Model using beta decay have historically minimized nuclear structure uncertainties through careful transition selection, and recent breakthroughs in nucleon-level radiative corrections have made nuclear structure corrections once again a limiting factor, the paper argues that common recoil-order approximations in traditional multipole formalisms often go unnoticed and must be critically re-examined to avoid double-counting and other errors that can significantly impact Beyond Standard Model searches.

## Method

The paper critically examines the two main formalisms for describing nuclear electroweak response in beta decay: the elementary particle (covariant) approach developed by Kim, Primakoff, and Holstein, and the relativistic multipole decomposition approach developed by Donnelly, Walecka, Behrens, and Buhring. It traces the origin of recoil-order approximations in each formalism, identifies where the Breit frame requirement for multipole decomposition is violated in common practice, and documents specific cases where formalism mixing has led to errors, including a double-counting mistake in mirror V_ud extraction that was resolved only recently.

## Key numbers

- The double-counting mistake in mirror V_ud extraction resulted in a shift of the extracted value by 3 standard deviations, resolving a long-standing disagreement with neutron and superallowed extractions
- Coulomb-recoil induced corrections can be at the level of Z x 0.1%, i.e. not negligible with respect to current precision
- Recoil-order corrections in radiative corrections due to momentum differences between initial and final states are typically dismissed as order O(10^{-6} x q MeV^{-1})
- The multipole responses in the recent 10C work are calculated explicitly until q approximately 500 MeV, while true quasielastic processes can probe even higher momenta

## Context

This work extends the critical examination of nuclear structure corrections following the 2018 breakthroughs in dispersion approach to radiative corrections by Seng, Gorchtein, and others, which elucidated the importance of the quasielastic regime in the nuclear gamma-W box diagram. The paper identifies open questions including the validity of recoil-free radiative corrections in loop calculations where no well-defined Breit frame exists for virtual loop momentum, and calls for a clear unified prescription within the community as ab initio methods (such as the No Core Shell Model work by Gennari (2025) on 10C) begin to provide first-principles evaluations of nuclear structure corrections.
