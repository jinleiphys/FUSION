# Quasifermion spectrum at finite temperature from coupled Schwinger-Dyson equations for a fermion-boson system

> We nonperturbatively investigate a fermion spectrum at finite temperature in a chiral invariant linear sigma model. Coupled Schwinger-Dyson equations for fermion and boson are developed in the real time formalism and solved numerically. From the coupling of a massless fermion with a massive boson, the fermion spectrum shows a three-peak structure at some temperatures even for the strong coupling region. This means that the three-peak structure which was originally found in the one-loop calculation is stable against higher order corrections even in the strong coupling region.

## Key claim

The three-peak structure in the fermion spectral function, previously found in one-loop calculations, persists when nonperturbative higher-order corrections are included via coupled Schwinger-Dyson equations, even in the strong coupling region. However, the central peak acquires a finite width (rather than being a delta function as at one loop) due to multiple scattering effects that allow fermion decay at zero energy and momentum.

## Method

The authors formulated coupled Schwinger-Dyson equations for fermion and boson propagators in a chiral U(1)L × U(1)R invariant linear sigma model using the real-time formalism at finite temperature. They employed the ladder approximation with bare vertices, evaluated the imaginary parts of self-energies through momentum integrals, and obtained the real parts via Kramers-Kronig dispersion relations. The boson mass was renormalized using an on-shell condition, while an ultraviolet cutoff Λ/m₀ ≈ 10 was retained for logarithmic divergences.

## Key numbers

- For g² = 1, the three-peak structure appears in the fermion spectral function at zero momentum for temperatures T/m = 1.0 and 1.2, with clear separation of three peaks at T/m = 1.6
- At T/m = 2.0 with g² = 1, the central peak nearly disappears, leaving only two peaks corresponding to normal quasifermion and antiplasmino excitations
- The peak position of the normal quasifermion at p = 0 and T/m = 1 saturates around g² = 3 in the Schwinger-Dyson approach, while the one-loop result continues to increase with coupling
- The imaginary part of the fermion self-energy at zero energy and momentum, |ImΣ₊(0,0)|, increases with temperature for g² = 1, giving the central peak a finite width that broadens as T increases

## Context

This work extends the one-loop analysis of the three-peak structure in Yukawa models by Kitazawa et al. (2006, 2007) to include nonperturbative effects through coupled Schwinger-Dyson equations. It provides the first numerical solution of coupled fermion-boson Schwinger-Dyson equations at finite temperature, going beyond previous approaches where the boson propagator was fixed rather than solved self-consistently (Barducci 1989, Kondo 1993, Harada 2007). The results enable future studies of fermion spectra in gauge theories and near phase transitions where soft bosonic modes arise dynamically.
