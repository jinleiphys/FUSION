# Scalar field correlator in de Sitter space at next-to-leading order in a 1/N expansion

> We study the dynamics of light quantum scalar fields in de Sitter space on superhorizon scales. We compute the self-energy of an O(N) symmetric theory at next-to-leading order in a 1/N expansion in the regime of superhorizon momenta, and we obtain an exact analytical solution of the corresponding Dyson-Schwinger equations for the two-point correlator. This amounts to resumming the infinite series of nonlocal self-energy insertions, which typically generate spurious infrared and/or secular divergences. The potentially large de Sitter logarithms resum into well-behaved power laws from which we extract the field strength and mass renormalization. The nonperturbative 1/N expansion allows us to discuss the case of vanishing and negative tree-level square mass, which both correspond to strongly coupled effective theories in the infrared.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the infinite series of nonlocal self-energy insertions in the O(N) scalar field theory on de Sitter space, which generate spurious infrared and secular divergences in perturbation theory, can be resummed at next-to-leading order in a 1/N expansion to yield well-behaved power laws for the two-point correlator on superhorizon scales, provided one works within the regime of superhorizon momenta and restricts convolution integrals to a scale μ ≲ 1.

## Method

The authors compute the self-energy of an O(N) symmetric scalar field theory on the expanding Poincaré patch of de Sitter space at next-to-leading order in a 1/N expansion, using the physical momentum (p-)representation of de Sitter correlators. They solve the corresponding Kadanoff-Baym equations (the nonequilibrium version of Dyson-Schwinger equations) for the two-point correlator in the infrared regime, where the dominant contributions come from superhorizon fluctuations. The solution involves evaluating the one-loop bubble integral and the infinite series of bubble diagrams resummed into a closed form, then using these to obtain the infrared behavior of the self-energy and subsequently solving the integro-differential equations for the statistical and spectral components of the propagator.

## Key numbers

* The effective coupling parameter λ_eff = λ/(6Ω_{D+1} M_0^4), where M_0^2 is the leading-order tadpole mass given by M_0^2 = m_dS^2/2 + sqrt((m_dS^2)^2/4 + λ/(6Ω_{D+1}))
* The infrared mass renormalization at NLO: m_IR^2/M_0^2 = 1 + (1/N) × λ_eff(2 - λ_eff)/(1 + λ_eff)^2
* The dynamical mass (from field variance) at NLO: m_dyn^2/M_0^2 = 1 + (2/N) × λ_eff/(1 + λ_eff)^2
* The field strength renormalization factor Z = 1 - (1/(2N)) × λ_eff^2(3 + 2λ_eff)/(1 + λ_eff)^3
* The parameter γ = (ε + ε̄)/2, where ε ≈ M^2/d and ε̄ ≈ ε + π_ρ/d, with π_ρ ≈ λ/(3M^2Ω_{D+1})

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-loop perturbative solution of the Kadanoff-Baym equations (Gautier and Serreau, 2013) to the nonperturbative 1/N expansion at next-to-leading order, which allows treatment of vanishing and negative tree-level square mass cases that were inaccessible perturbatively. The results agree with the stochastic effective theory approach of Starobinsky (1986, 1994) for the local field variance at the same approximation order, and the method enables computation of the complete spacetime structure of the propagator for superhorizon momenta, including field strength and mass renormalization that receive nonperturbative corrections as functions of the self-coupling.
