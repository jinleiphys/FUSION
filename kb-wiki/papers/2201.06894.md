# Current-induced inverse symmetry breaking and asymmetric critical phenomena at current-driven tricritical point

> We study critical phenomena associated with a spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in current-driven non-equilibrium steady states by using holography. We find that the critical exponents $(γ, ν)$ at the tricritical point are asymmetric between the chiral symmetry restored phase and the broken phase. Their values in the broken phase are different from those of the mean-field theory, whereas other critical exponents are the mean-field values. The phase diagram with respect to temperature and current density shows a re-entrant structure: the broken chiral symmetry is restored again at low temperatures in the presence of current density.

## Key claim

The authors find that at the current-driven tricritical point (TCP), the critical exponents (γ, ν) are asymmetric between the chiral symmetry restored (χSR) phase and the chiral symmetry broken (χSB) phase, with values in the broken phase (γ₋ ≈ 0.58, ν₋ ≈ 0.29) differing from mean-field theory, whereas all other critical exponents take mean-field values; this asymmetry appears only at the TCP and not on the critical line (CL).

## Method

The authors use the gauge/gravity duality (holography) to study a strongly-coupled SU(N) N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with N=2 hypermultiplet in the large-N limit, with zero mass and zero charge density, subject to external electric and magnetic fields. The holographic dual is the D3-D7 model in the probe limit with an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole geometry; the D7-brane dynamics are governed by the Dirac-Born-Infeld action. The authors solve the equations of motion for the D7-brane embedding field θ(u) and compute the retarded Green's function for fluctuations of the order parameter (chiral condensate) to extract critical exponents (γ, ν, η, z) and δ, using the current density J as the control parameter in the non-equilibrium steady state.

## Key numbers

- At the critical line (CL), the critical exponents are mean-field values: (γ₊, γ₋) = (1, 1), (ν₊, ν₋) = (0.5, 0.5), η = 0, z = 2, β = 0.5, δ = 3.
- At the tricritical point (TCP) in the χSB phase, γ₋ ≈ 0.58 and ν₋ ≈ 0.29, while in the χSR phase γ₊ = 1 and ν₊ = 0.5; η = 0 and z = 2 at the TCP.
- At the TCP, β = 0.25 and δ = 5.
- The critical line at small T/B^(1/2) and J/B^(3/2) is described by (T_c / B_c^(1/2))^κ ≈ 1.2 (J_c / B_c^(3/2)), with κ ≈ 3/2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Imaizumi and Nakamura, 2019) which first identified the phase transition and tricritical point in a current-driven system, but did not investigate the full phase structure or critical phenomena. The re-entrant phase structure (inverse symmetry breaking at low temperatures) is characteristic of the current-driven system and contrasts with the equilibrium phase diagram studied by Evans et al. (2011) using charge density instead of current density. The asymmetric critical exponents at the TCP are a novel finding not previously reported; asymmetric critical behavior has been found in the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev model (Ferrari, 2019; Cao, 2021), but not specifically at a tricritical point. The results enable experimental tests in systems with gapless chiral fermions such as Dirac semimetals under electric and magnetic fields.
