# Comment on "Electromagnetic form factors for nucleons in short-range correlations": arXiv:2305.13666

> The relationship between medium modifications of nuclear quark structure functions and medium modifications of elastic electromagnetic form factors is shown to be model dependent.

## Key claim

The relationship between medium modifications of nuclear quark structure functions (the EMC effect) and medium modifications of elastic electromagnetic form factors is model dependent; a large suppression of the structure function for nucleons in short-range correlations does not necessarily imply a large suppression of elastic form factors, contrary to the claim of Xing et al. (2023).

## Method

The authors present a counter-example using a previously published AdS/QCD motivated light-front quark-diquark model (Kim et al., 2022) that reproduces nuclear deep inelastic scattering data by requiring bound nucleons to be significantly off the mass shell. They compute the free and medium-modified elastic form factor \(F_1\) for a proton in a Pb nucleus, where the EMC effect is largest, and extend the calculations to show the \(Q^2\) dependence of the in-medium form factors. The model uses an average virtuality and predicts no medium modification in the form factor \(F_2\).

## Key numbers

- The model predicts medium modifications of elastic form factors that are smaller than existing experimental uncertainties, with an upper limit between 3 and 6% for values of \(Q^2\) between 1 and 4 GeV\(^2\) for missing momentum less than about 150 MeV/c (citing Sick, 1985).
- The measured EMC effect is no more than 10-15%, and within the model the probability that a given bound nucleon has an excited state component is proportional to the square of the size of the EMC effect.
- The model of Xing et al. (2023) claims a suppression of nucleon elastic form factors by about 50% at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This comment directly contradicts the claim of Xing et al. (2023) that a large suppression of the nucleon structure function from short-range correlations leads to a strong suppression of elastic form factors. The authors show that their own model (Kim et al., 2022), which also uses an AdS/QCD motivated light-front quark-diquark model and reproduces the EMC effect, yields very modest form factor modifications. The work extends the discussion of the EMC effect beyond the mean-field versus short-range correlation dichotomy noted by Hen et al. (2013), arguing that both effects contribute to the average virtuality in roughly a 1:2 ratio (Ciofi degli Atti, 2007). The paper calls for direct experimental measurements of medium-modified form factors at kinematics where nucleon virtuality is large.
