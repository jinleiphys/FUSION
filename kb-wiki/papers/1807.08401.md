# Quarkonium tomography of heavy ion collisions at the LHC

> Quarkonium production in high-energy hadronic collisions provides a fundamental test of QCD. Its modification in a nuclear medium is a sensitive probe of the space-time temperature profile and transport properties of the QGP, yielding constraints complementary to the ones obtained from the quenching of light hadrons and jets, and open heavy flavor. In these proceedings, we report new results for the suppression of high transverse momentum charmonium [$J/ψ,\, ψ(2S)$] and bottomonium [$Υ(1S),\, Υ(2S),\, Υ(3S)$] states in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Our theoretical formalism combines the collisional dissociation of quarkonia, as they propagate in the quark-gluon plasma, with the thermal wavefunction effects due to the screening of the $Q\bar{Q}$ attractive potential in the medium. We find that a good description of the relative suppression of the ground and higher excited quarkonium states, transverse momentum and centrality distributions is achieved, when comparison to measurements at a center-of-mass energy of 2.76 TeV is performed. Theoretical predictions for the highest Pb+Pb center-of-mass energy of 5.02 TeV at the LHC, where new experimental results are being finalized are presented. Preliminary calculations for smaller systems, such as Xe+Xe are also shown. Last but not least, the potential of jet substructure to shed light in the mechanisms of heavy flavor production is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper reports that a theoretical formalism combining collisional dissociation of quarkonia with thermal wavefunction effects due to screening of the QCD attractive potential in the quark-gluon plasma achieves a good description of the relative suppression of ground and excited quarkonium states, transverse momentum and centrality distributions when compared to measurements at 2.76 TeV, but the results carry combined uncertainty from the interaction onset time and the broadening parameter.

## Method
The author uses non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) to compute quarkonium production cross sections in proton-proton collisions, then propagates proto-quarkonium states through a quark-gluon plasma modeled by the EBE-VISHNU (2+1)-dimensional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic package. The survival probability is obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation at zero and finite temperature, computing the overlap between the evolved initial wavefunction (which accumulates transverse momentum broadening from collisional interactions) and the thermal wavefunction, and then describing production and dissociation by coupled rate equations.

## Key numbers
- The NRQCD calculation uses long distance matrix elements (LDMEs) to describe cross sections for bottomonia in pp and p-pbar collisions for pT in the range of 5 to 30 GeV.
- The coupling between heavy quarks and the medium is fixed at g = 1.85, with the broadening parameter xi varied; the upper edge of the uncertainty band corresponds to formation time t_form = 1.5 fm and xi = 1.
- Theoretical results are presented for Pb+Pb collisions at center-of-mass energies of 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV at the LHC.
- Preliminary calculations are shown for smaller systems such as Xe+Xe.
- Numerical results for heavy flavor tagged jet substructure are presented for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the formalism of Sharma (2012) for quarkonium suppression in heavy ion collisions, which failed to quantitatively reproduce the large J/psi suppression in central Pb+Pb reactions at the LHC and the larger attenuation of excited Upsilon states. The new approach incorporates thermal wavefunction effects due to screening of the Q-Qbar potential, as originally proposed by Matsui (1986). The results enable testing of the model against upcoming experimental measurements at 5.02 TeV and in smaller systems such as Xe+Xe, and the heavy flavor jet substructure method builds upon Chien (2016) to provide a new way to study heavy quark QCD dynamics.
