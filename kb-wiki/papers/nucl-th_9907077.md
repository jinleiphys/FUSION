# Quartet S Wave Neutron Deuteron Scattering in Effective Field Theory

> The real and imaginary part of the quartet S wave phase shift in nd scattering (^4 S_{3/2}) for centre-of-mass momenta of up to 300 MeV (E_cm \approx 70 MeV) is presented in effective field theory, using both perturbative pions and a theory in which pions are integrated out. As available, the calculation agrees with both experimental data and potential model calculations, but extends to a higher, so far untested momentum régime above the deuteron breakup point. A Lagrangean more feasible for numerical computations is derived.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first systematic effective field theory calculation of three-body properties with explicit pions, computing the quartet S-wave neutron-deuteron phase shift for center-of-mass momenta up to 300 MeV (about 70 MeV kinetic energy), extending above the deuteron breakup threshold where no experimental data or potential model calculations previously existed.

## Method

The authors use the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) effective field theory scheme with power divergence subtraction (PDS) at renormalization scale mu = m_pi, treating pions perturbatively and summing contact interactions to all orders to produce the deuteron bound state. They reformulate the Lagrangian to include an auxiliary "deuteron" field d^i that removes four-nucleon contact interactions, then solve the three-body scattering equations numerically for the quartet S-wave channel where Pauli principle forbids three-body forces at the orders considered.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass momenta up to 300 MeV (E_cm approximately 70 MeV) are covered, extending above the deuteron breakup point
- The deuteron binding energy is B = 2.225 MeV, corresponding to a binding momentum gamma = sqrt(M B) approximately 46 MeV
- The quartet scattering length satisfies 1/a^(4S_3/2) = 31 MeV
- The triplet channel scattering length parameters are 1/a^(3S_1) = 36 MeV and 1/a^(1S_0) = -8.3 MeV
- The pion decay constant is f_pi = 130 MeV
- The expansion parameter is estimated to be about 1/3, so NLO calculations are accurate to about 10% and NNLO to about 3%

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory calculations of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998-1999) for three-body observables, which were limited to energies below the deuteron breakup threshold, by including pions explicitly and computing above threshold. The calculation enables systematic improvement through higher-order corrections and provides a parameter-free test of the KSW scheme's range of validity in the three-nucleon system, with the quartet channel chosen because it avoids Coulomb complications and three-body forces at leading orders.
