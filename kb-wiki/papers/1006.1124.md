# Entropy current for the relativistic Kadanoff-Baym equation and H-theorem in $O(N)$ theory with NLO self-energy of $1/N$ expansion

> We derive an expression of the kinetic entropy current in the nonequilibrium $O(N)$ scalar theory from the Schwinger-Dyson (Kadanoff-Baym) equation with the 1st order gradient expansion. We show that our kinetic entropy satisfies the H-theorem for the leading order of the gradient expansion with the next-to-leading order self-energy of the $1/N$ expansion in the symmetric phase, and that entropy production occurs as the Green's function evolves with an nonzero collision term. Entropy production stops at local thermal equilibrium where the collision term contribution vanishes and the maximal entropy state is realized. Next we also compare our entropy density with that in thermal equilibrium which is given from thermodynamic potential or equivalently 2 particle irreducible effective action. We find that our entropy density corresponds to that in thermal equilibrium with the next-to-leading order skeletons of the $1/N$ expansion if skeletons with energy denominators in momentum integral can be regularized appropriately. We have a possibility that memory correction terms remain in entropy current if not regularized.

## Key claim

The authors derive an expression for the kinetic entropy current from the Kadanoff-Baym equation in nonequilibrium O(N) scalar theory and prove that the H-theorem holds at the level of Green's functions for the next-to-leading order self-energy of the 1/N expansion, but only under the leading order of the gradient expansion and in the symmetric phase; memory correction terms may remain in the entropy current if singularities in skeleton diagrams are not regularized.

## Method

The authors start from the Schwinger-Dyson (Kadanoff-Baym) equation for the two-point Green's function in O(N) scalar theory with vanishing mean field, using the 2PI effective action formalism. They apply a first-order gradient expansion in the center-of-mass coordinate and Wigner transform the equations to obtain kinetic equations for the statistical function F and spectral function ρ. The self-energy is taken at next-to-leading order of the 1/N expansion, which includes resummed chain diagrams, and the collision term C = i(Σ_ρ F − Σ_F ρ) is evaluated in the local approximation of the gradient expansion.

## Key numbers

* The effective coupling after resummation is λ_eff(X,p) = λ / [(1 − λ/(3N) Π_R(X,p)) (1 − λ/(3N) Π_A(X,p))], where Π_R and Π_A are the retarded and advanced polarization functions.
* The collision term C(X,p_1) is proportional to (λ/(18N)) times an integral over momenta with a δ-function δ^(d+1)(p_1+p_2−p_3−p_4) and the effective coupling λ_eff.
* In the quasiparticle limit (Σ_nonl → 0), the entropy current reduces to s^μ = ∫ d^d p/(2π)^d (p^μ/ε_p) [−n_p ln n_p + (1+n_p) ln(1+n_p)], where ε_p = sqrt(p^2 + m^2 + (λ(N+2)/(6N))∫ d^d k/(2π)^d F).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous H-theorem proof for φ^4 theory (Nishiyama, 2010) to O(N) scalar theory with NLO self-energy of the 1/N expansion. It builds on the 2PI effective action approach developed by Berges (2004) and Berges and Serreau (2002), and on numerical simulations of thermalization in O(N) models by Berges (2002) and Aarts and Tranberg (2006). The result provides an analytic criterion for thermalization in off-shell propagation and may enable qualitative studies of entropy production in theories where numerical simulations are difficult, such as non-Abelian gauge theories.
