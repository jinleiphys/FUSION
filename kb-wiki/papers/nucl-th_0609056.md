# Superscaling analysis of inclusive electron scattering and its extension to charge-changing neutrino-nucleus cross sections beyond the relativistic Fermi gas approach

> Superscaling analyses of inclusive electron scattering from nuclei are extended from the quasielastic processes to the delta excitation region. The calculations of $(e,e^\prime)$ cross sections for the target nucleus $^{12}$C at various incident electron energies are performed using scaling functions $f(ψ^{\prime})$ obtained in approaches going beyond the mean-field approximation, such as the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) and the one based on the light-front dynamics (LFD) method. The results are compared with those obtained using the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model and the extended RFG model (ERFG). Our method utilizes in an equivalent way both basic nuclear quantities, density and momentum distributions, showing their role for the scaling and superscaling phenomena. The approach is extended to consider scaling function for medium and heavy nuclei with $Z\neq N$ for which the proton and neutron densities are not similar. The asymmetry of the CDFM quasielastic scaling function is introduced, simulating in a phenomenological way the effects which violate the symmetry for $ψ^{\prime}\geq 0$ including the role of the final-state interaction (FSI). The superscaling properties of the electron scattering are used to predict charge-changing neutrino-nucleus cross sections at energies from 1 to 2 GeV. A comparison with the results of the ERFG model is made. The analyses make it possible to gain information about the nucleon correlation effects on both local density and nucleon momentum distributions.

## Key claim

The paper extends superscaling analyses of inclusive electron scattering from the quasielastic region to the delta excitation region using scaling functions derived from the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) and light-front dynamics (LFD) method, going beyond the mean-field approximation; however, the asymmetric shape and reduced maximum value of the quasielastic scaling function are introduced phenomenologically through a parameter \(c_1\) to simulate final-state interaction effects, rather than being derived from first principles.

## Method

The quasielastic scaling function \(f^{QE}(\psi')\) was calculated within the CDFM by averaging the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) scaling function over either the nuclear density distribution \(\rho(r)\) or the nucleon momentum distribution \(n(k)\), using weight functions derived from the derivatives of these distributions. For nuclei with \(Z \neq N\), the total scaling function was expressed as the sum of separate proton and neutron scaling functions obtained from their respective densities or momentum distributions. The LFD method was used to compute \(n(k)\) from a combination of hole-state contributions (using Woods-Saxon wave functions) and particle-state contributions from deuteron light-front wave functions, with a parameter \(\beta\) controlling the particle-state admixture. The asymmetry of the CDFM quasielastic scaling function was introduced by modifying the RFG scaling function with parameters \(c_1\) (controlling the maximum value) and \(c_2\) (controlling the width for \(\psi' \geq 0\)), with \(c_2 = 3/(2c_1) - 1\) from normalization.

## Key numbers

- The maximum value of the empirical quasielastic scaling function \(f^{QE}(\psi')\) is about 0.6, compared to 3/4 in the RFG model.
- The CDFM scaling function for \(^{12}\)C was calculated for different values of \(c_1\): 0.75, 0.72, 0.60, and 0.50.
- The LFD nucleon momentum distribution for \(^{12}\)C was calculated using \(\beta = 0.80\) in the particle-state contribution.
- The CDFM scaling functions were calculated for \(^{4}\)He, \(^{12}\)C, \(^{27}\)Al, \(^{56}\)Fe, and \(^{197}\)Au at momentum transfers \(q = 500, 1000, 1650\) MeV/c and \(q = 1560\) MeV/c.
- The LFD scaling function for \(^{56}\)Fe was calculated at \(q = 1000\) MeV/c.
- The charge-changing neutrino-nucleus cross sections were predicted for energies from 1 to 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analyses of Donnelly and Sick (1999) and the phenomenological scaling functions of Amaro et al. (2005) by providing microscopic calculations of scaling functions from nuclear density and momentum distributions, rather than relying solely on empirical fits. It builds on the authors' previous CDFM studies (Antonov et al., 2004, 2005) and LFD momentum distribution calculations (Antonov et al., 2002) to include asymmetric scaling functions for the quasielastic region and to extend the formalism to the delta excitation region. The results enable predictions of charge-changing neutrino-nucleus cross sections for neutrino oscillation experiments, using the superscaling properties of electron scattering data as a foundation.
