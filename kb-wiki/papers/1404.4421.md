# Shear viscosity of hot nuclear matter by the mean free path method

> The shear viscosity of hot nuclear matter is investigated by using the mean free path method within the framework of IQMD model. Finite size nuclear sources at different density and temperature are initialized based on the Fermi-Dirac distribution. The results show that shear viscosity to entropy density ratio decreases with the increase of temperature and tends toward a constant value for $ρ\simρ_0$, which is consistent with the previous studies on nuclear matter formed during heavy-ion collisions. At $ρ\sim\frac{1}{2}ρ_0$, a minimum of $η/s$ is seen at around $T=10$ MeV and a maximum of the multiplicity of intermediate mass fragment ($M_{\text{IMF}}$) is also observed at the same temperature which is an indication of the liquid-gas phase transition.

# Shear viscosity of hot nuclear matter by the mean free path method

## Key claim
The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) for hot nuclear matter exhibits a minimum at around T = 10 MeV when the density is approximately half of normal nuclear matter density (ρ ∼ ½ρ₀), and this minimum coincides with a maximum in intermediate mass fragment multiplicity, suggesting a connection to the liquid-gas phase transition, but only at low densities, not at ρ ∼ ρ₀ where η/s instead saturates to a constant value at high temperature.

## Method
The authors initialized finite-size nuclear sources (¹¹²Sn, with 50 protons and 62 neutrons) at temperatures 0 < T < 30 MeV using a Fermi-Dirac momentum distribution within the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. The shear viscosity was extracted from the mean free path of nucleons, calculated from the real nucleon-nucleon collision frequency per fm/c in the early-time evolution (1 < t < 5 fm/c), using the kinetic theory relation η = (1/3)ρmvλ. Fragment distributions were obtained by evolving the system without boundary conditions and applying a coalescence model (relative momentum < 300 MeV/c, relative distance < 3.5 fm) to identify intermediate mass fragments (IMF, charge 3 ≤ Z ≤ Z_src).

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- At ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, η/s decreases with temperature and saturates to approximately 0.75 (about 7–8 times the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound of 1/4π) for T > 20 MeV
- At ρ = 0.07 fm⁻³ (∼½ρ₀), a minimum of η/s is observed at T ≈ 10 MeV
- At ρ = 0.07 fm⁻³, a maximum of intermediate mass fragment multiplicity (M_IMF) is observed at T ≈ 10 MeV, coinciding with the η/s minimum
- At ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the shear viscosity η saturates to approximately 50 MeV fm⁻² at high temperature
- The Gaussian wave packet width used in IQMD: L = 2.16 fm²
- Symmetry energy strength: C_sym = 32 MeV
- Soft equation of state parameters: α = −356 MeV, β = 303 MeV, γ = 1.17 (incompressibility K = 200 MeV)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nuclear matter viscosity using the Green-Kubo formula and the parameterized function of Danielewicz (1994), as applied by Li and Zhou (2012) and Zhou et al. (2013) to heavy-ion collision systems. The observation of a η/s minimum at the liquid-gas phase transition temperature for ρ ∼ ½ρ₀ parallels the empirical behavior seen in other substances such as H₂O, He, and Ne₂, where η/s has a minimum at or near the critical point of phase transition. The results enable future investigations of η and η/s for infinite uniform nuclear matter to fully understand the relation between shear viscosity and the liquid-gas phase transition.
