# Dressed Quark Mass Dependence of Pion and Kaon Form Factors

> The structure of hadrons is described well by the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, which is a chiral effective quark theory of QCD. In this work we explore the electromagnetic structure of the pion and kaon using the three-flavor NJL model, including effects of confinement and a pion cloud at the quark level. In the calculation there is only one free parameter, which we take as the dressed light quark ($u$ and $d$) mass. In the regime where the dressed light quark mass is approximately $0.25\,$GeV, we find that the calculated values of the kaon decay constant, current quark masses, and quark condensates are consistent with experiment and QCD based analyses. We also investigate the dressed light quark mass dependence of the pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors, where comparison with empirical data and QCD predictions also favors a dressed light quark mass near $0.25\,$GeV.

## Key claim

The authors argue that using a dressed light quark mass of approximately 0.25 GeV in the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, rather than the more conventional 0.3–0.4 GeV, yields improved agreement with experimental data and QCD-based analyses for pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors, charge radii, and several other observables, provided pion cloud and vector meson corrections are included.

## Method

The authors use the three-flavor NJL model with four-fermion interactions, regularized via the proper-time scheme with an infrared cutoff to simulate confinement. They compute the pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors at three levels of sophistication: (1) with point-like dressed quarks, (2) including pion loop corrections to the quark-photon vertex, and (3) also including vector meson dominance contributions from ρ and ω mesons. The model has one free parameter, the dressed light quark mass M, with all other parameters fixed by the physical pion mass, pion decay constant, ρ meson mass, and kaon mass.

## Key numbers

- Dressed light quark mass M ≈ 0.25 GeV is favored by comparison with empirical data and QCD predictions.
- Pion mass fixed to m_π = 0.140 GeV and pion leptonic decay constant to f_π = 0.0934 GeV.
- Kaon mass fixed to m_K = 0.494 GeV and ρ meson mass to m_ρ = 0.776 GeV.
- Infrared cutoff chosen as Λ_IR = 0.2 GeV.
- For M ≈ 0.25 GeV, the calculated ratios m_s/m, f_K/f_π, and ⟨s̄ s⟩/⟨ℓ̄ ℓ⟩ agree with experimental and lattice QCD results: m_s/m = 27.5 ± 1.0 (experiment), f_K/f_π = 1.197 ± 0.002 ± 0.006 ± 0.001 (experiment), and ⟨s̄ s⟩/⟨ℓ̄ ℓ⟩ = 1.08 ± 0.16 (lattice QCD).

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model calculations of meson form factors, which typically used a dressed light quark mass of 0.4 GeV (Horikawa 2005, Cloët 2014), by systematically exploring the dependence on M and including pion cloud and vector meson corrections. The proper-time regularization scheme with an infrared cutoff follows the approach of Bentz (2001) and others to avoid unphysical decay thresholds. The results enable future extensions to studies of the ρ meson and nucleon structure within the same framework.
