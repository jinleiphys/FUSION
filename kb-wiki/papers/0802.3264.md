# Energy-Dependence of the Nucleon Self-Energies in Off-Mass-Shell Energy Region and the Gamow-Teller Sum-Rule in the Relativistic Hartree-Fock Approach

> The relativistic Hartree approximation predicts a deep attractive potential for antinucleon, which largely reduce the threshold energies of the nucleon-antinucleon (N-Nbar) production. This effect has played an important role to explain the quenching of the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength because the quenched strength in the particle-hole excitation is partially taken by the nucleon-antinucleon production. On the other hand antiproton experiments do not reveal deep attractive potential for antinucleon. In this paper we study energy-dependence of the nucleon self-energies in the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation in off-mass-shell states. Then we demonstrate that the antinucleon appearing in low energy observables is in the off-mass-shell energy region and its properties are quite different from that at the on-mass-shell state. Furthermore we show that the quenched amount of the GT strength is not taken only by the \NNbar production but also by the meson production through the imaginary part of the nucleon self-energy in the RHF approximation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the energy dependence of nucleon self-energies in the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation resolves an apparent contradiction between relativistic Hartree (RH) predictions of a deep attractive antinucleon potential and antiproton experiments that do not reveal such a potential, because the antinucleon appearing in low-energy observables is in an off-mass-shell energy region where its properties differ from the on-mass-shell state.

## Method

The author calculates the nucleon self-energies in the RHF approximation within one-meson exchange, using two parameter sets (HF(C) and Bonn-A) and fixing the Hartree parts to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties. The energy dependence of the self-energies is examined at fixed momentum, and the Gamow-Teller (GT) response function is computed in the impulse approximation at zero momentum transfer, separating contributions from particle-hole excitation, nucleon-antinucleon production, and meson production through the imaginary part of the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- In the RH calculation, the nucleon-antinucleon production threshold energy is about 1250 MeV; in the RHF calculation it is about 1630 MeV, compared to the vacuum value of 2M approximately 1880 MeV.
- With HF(C) parameters, the particle-hole contribution to the GT sum-rule is S_ph/S_IFF approximately 0.84 in both RHF and RH, while the nucleon-antinucleon contribution is S_NNbar/S_IFF approximately 0.27 in RHF versus 0.16 in RH.
- With Bonn-A parameters, S_ph/S_IFF is approximately 0.88 in RHF versus 0.87 in RH, and S_NNbar/S_IFF is approximately 0.249 in RHF versus 0.13 in RH.
- The sum S_ph + S_NNbar accounts for only about 1.11 of the Ikeda-Fujii-Fujita sum-rule value in the RHF calculation with both parameter sets, indicating the remaining strength goes to meson production.
- The binding energy used for saturation is 16 MeV, the incompressibility is 250 MeV, the effective mass is 0.6M, and the Fermi momentum is 1.36 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kurasawa and Suzuki (1990s), who showed that the reduced nucleon-antinucleon threshold energy in the RH approximation explains the quenching of the Coulomb sum-rule and the Gamow-Teller sum-rule, with 88 percent of the Ikeda-Fujii-Fujita sum-rule coming from particle-hole excitation and 12 percent from nucleon-antinucleon production. The paper addresses the contradiction with antiproton experiments analyzed by Teis et al. (1990s) and the KEK experiments, which did not reveal the deep attractive antinucleon potential predicted by RH. The results enable future quantitative studies incorporating random phase approximation contributions, delta excitation, and multi-particle multi-hole excitations to fully resolve the GT strength distribution.
