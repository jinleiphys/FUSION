# Dynamical Body Frames, Orientation-Shape Variables and Canonical Spin Bases for the Non-Relativistic N-Body Problem

> After the separation of the center-of-mass motion, a new privileged class of canonical Darboux bases is proposed for the non-relativistic N-body problem by exploiting a geometrical and group theoretical approach to the definition of {\it body frame} for deformable bodies. This basis is adapted to the rotation group SO(3), whose canonical realization is associated with a symmetry Hamiltonian {\it left action}. The analysis of the SO(3) coadjoint orbits contained in the N-body phase space implies the existence of a {\it spin frame} for the N-body system. Then, the existence of appropriate non-symmetry Hamiltonian {\it right actions} for non-rigid systems leads to the construction of a N-dependent discrete number of {\it dynamical body frames} for the N-body system, hence to the associated notions of {\it dynamical} and {\it measurable} orientation and shape variables, angular velocity, rotational and vibrational configurations. For N=3 the dynamical body frame turns out to be unique and our approach reproduces the {\it xxzz gauge} of the gauge theory associated with the {\it orientation-shape} SO(3) principal bundle approach of Littlejohn and Reinsch. For $N \geq 4$ our description is different, since the dynamical body frames turn out to be {\it momentum dependent}. The resulting Darboux bases for $N\geq 4$ are connected to the coupling of the {\it spins} of particle clusters rather than the coupling of the {\it centers of mass} (based on Jacobi relative normal coordinates). One of the advantages of the spin coupling is that, unlike the center-of-mass coupling, it admits a relativistic generalization.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new class of canonical Darboux bases for the non-relativistic N-body problem that are adapted to the SO(3) rotation group, where for N=3 the construction reproduces the xxzz gauge of the orientation-shape principal bundle approach of Littlejohn and Reinsch, but for N≥4 the resulting dynamical body frames are momentum dependent and differ from the static, velocity-independent construction of that earlier work.

## Method

The authors exploit the theory of canonical realizations of Lie groups, specifically SO(3), to construct Darboux bases in the (6N-6)-dimensional relative phase space after separating center-of-mass motion. They analyze the SO(3) coadjoint orbits contained in the N-body phase space to identify a spin frame, then search for Hamiltonian right actions of SO(3) by solving equations for orthonormal triads that yield canonical generators satisfying the required Poisson bracket relations. For N=3 the construction yields a unique dynamical body frame determined solely by configuration variables, while for N≥4 the solutions necessarily depend on momenta, leading to multiple dynamical body frames corresponding to different spin clusterings.

## Key numbers

* For N=3, the dynamical body frame is unique and reproduces the xxzz gauge of the orientation-shape SO(3) principal bundle approach.
* For N=4, three sets of spin frames and dynamical body frames exist, corresponding to the hierarchy of clusterings ((12)3), ((23)1), and ((31)2) of the relative spins.
* For N=5, there are K=15 independent spin clusterings: 12 of pattern (((ab)c)d) and 3 of pattern ((ab)(cd)).
* For N=6, there are K=105 spin clusterings: 60 of pattern ((((ab)c)d)e), 15 of pattern (((ab)(cd)e), and 30 of pattern (((ab)c)(de)).
* For N≥4, the relation between spin and angular velocity takes the form ${\check S}^r = {\cal I}^{rs}(q^{\mu}_{(A)})\, {\check \omega}^s_{(A)} + f^{\mu}(q^{\nu}_{(A)}) p_{(A)\mu}$.

## Context

This work extends the orientation-shape SO(3) principal bundle approach of Littlejohn and Reinsch, which was formulated in configuration space and required gauge conventions for defining body frames, by providing a Hamiltonian construction that yields dynamical body frames with operational meaning in phase space. The spin clustering approach used here differs from the Jacobi center-of-mass clustering and, unlike the center-of-mass coupling, admits a relativistic generalization to the rest-frame Wigner covariant instant form of dynamics developed by Lusanna and collaborators. The results enable a dynamical definition of vibration based on vanishing angular velocity components in the dynamical body frame, which replaces the static C-horizontal cross section of the earlier approach and may be applicable to spinning stars in astrophysics.
