# Double Charge Exchange And Configuration Mixing

> The energy dependence of forward pion double charge exchange reactions on light nuclei is studied for both the Ground State transition and the Double-Isobaric-Analog-State transitions. A common characteristic of these double reactions is a resonance-like peak around 50 MeV pion lab energy. This peak arises naturally in a two-step process in the conventional pion-nucleon system with proper handling of nuclear structure and pion distortion. A comparison among the results of different nuclear structure models demonstrates the effects of configuration mixing. The angular distribution is used to fix the single particle wave function.

## Key claim

The resonance-like peak around 50 MeV in forward pion double charge exchange reactions arises naturally from a conventional two-step sequential process with proper handling of nuclear structure and pion distortion, without invoking dibaryon degrees of freedom; however, the height of this peak is sensitive to the configuration mixing ratios in the nuclear wave function, and the second peak near the delta resonance is not reproduced for all nuclei within the same framework.

## Method

The authors calculated forward-angle differential cross sections for pion double charge exchange (DCX) reactions on 16O, 40Ca, and 18O using a two-step sequential process in coordinate space, with pion wave distortion described by an optical potential extended to the multi-j case. They compared results from three nuclear structure models: the SU(4) ⊗ SU(3) model (extreme L-S coupling), the single particle shell model (extreme j-j coupling), and the shell model (intermediate mixing), using single particle wave functions from Woods-Saxon potentials adjusted to match separation energies, rms radii, and angular distribution data.

## Key numbers

- The forward DCX cross section on 16O shows a peak around 50 MeV pion lab energy; the shell model reproduces both the low-energy peak and a second peak at around 180 MeV (experimental second peak at around 160 MeV).
- For 40Ca, the forward DCX cross section at 49 MeV and 56 MeV is calculated; the shell model gives a peak lower than experiment, while adding 5% of (d5/2)^{-2} configuration improves agreement.
- For 18O, the angular distribution at 164 MeV is fitted with valence neutron rms radii: r_{1d} = 4.66 fm and r_{2s} = 4.42 fm for the WS1 wave function, compared to r_{1d} = 3.68 fm and r_{2s} = 3.91 fm for the standard WS wave function.
- The forward DCX cross section on 18O shows a peak around 50 MeV; the height decreases with increasing valence neutron radius (r_{1d} ranging from 3.22 fm to 5.69 fm across HO0, HO, HO1, and HO2 wave functions).

## Context

This work extends the sequential-process framework of Nussinov (1998) to demonstrate that nuclear structure configuration mixing significantly affects the energy dependence of forward DCX reactions, contrary to the claim by Bilger (1993) that the low-energy peak indicates a dibaryon. The results enable future studies of DCX on medium-mass nuclei (e.g., 56Fe and 93Nb) using the pseudo-SU(4) model of Isacker (1999), and highlight the need for improved treatment of valence neutron radii in 18O as suggested by Gibbs (1992) for 42Ca.
