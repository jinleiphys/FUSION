# Chaos in chiral condensates in gauge theories

> Assigning a chaos index for dynamics of generic quantum field theories is a challenging problem, because the notion of Lyapunov exponent, which is useful for singling out chaotic behaviors, works only in classical systems. We address the issue by using the AdS/CFT correspondence, as the large $N_c$ limit provides a classicalization (other than the standard $\hbar \to 0$) while keeping nontrivial quantum condensation. We demonstrate the chaos in the dynamics of quantum gauge theories: Time evolution of homogeneous quark condensates $\langle \bar{q}q\rangle$ and $\langle \bar{q} γ_5 q\rangle$ in an ${\cal N}=2$ supersymmetric QCD with the $SU(N_c)$ gauge group at large $N_c$ and at large 't Hooft coupling $λ\equiv N_c g_{\rm YM}^2$ exhibits a positive Lyapunov exponent. The chaos dominates the phase space for energy density $E \gtrsim (6\times 10^2)\times m_q^4(N_c/λ^2) $ where $m_q$ is the quark mass. We evaluate the largest Lyapunov exponent as a function of $(N_c,λ,E)$ and find that the ${\cal N}=2$ supersymmetric QCD is more chaotic for smaller $N_c$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chaos in the dynamics of quantum gauge theories can be quantified via Lyapunov exponents by using the AdS/CFT correspondence, where the large N_c limit provides a classicalization while keeping nontrivial quantum condensation. The chaos in the time evolution of homogeneous quark condensates appears only above a critical energy density threshold, and the system is more chaotic for smaller N_c or larger 't Hooft coupling lambda.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to map the strongly coupled N=2 supersymmetric QCD with SU(N_c) gauge group at large N_c and large 't Hooft coupling to a classical D7-brane action in AdS_5 x S^5 geometry. They expand the non-Abelian D-brane action around a static classical solution up to quadratic order in derivatives and a single commutator term, then reduce to the lowest meson eigenstates to obtain an effective action for spatially homogeneous meson fields phi_8 and phi_9, which are proportional to the quark condensates <q-bar q> and <q-bar gamma_5 q>. For N_f=2 flavors, the system reduces to a quartic oscillator model with action S_0 = integral dt [1/2 (x-dot^2 + y-dot^2) - m^2/2 (x^2 + y^2) - g x^2 y^2], where m = 4 pi m_q / sqrt(lambda) and g = 72 pi^2 / (5 N_c), and they compute Lyapunov exponents from numerical solutions of the equations of motion.

## Key numbers

* The chaos-order phase transition occurs at critical energy density E_chaos ~ (6 x 10^2) x m_q^4 (N_c / lambda^2), where m_q is the quark mass.
* For the linear sigma model toy case, the chaotic phase appears at intermediate energy densities: 1.3 x 10^2 MeV < E^{1/4} < 1.7 x 10^2 MeV, with parameters m_pi = 135 MeV, m_sigma = 500 MeV, and f_pi = 93 MeV.
* The Lyapunov exponent L for the N=2 supersymmetric QCD is approximated by L ~ (E/N_c)^{1/4} [0.90 log{(E/N_c)^{1/2} lambda} - 2.03] for 10 < (E/N_c)^{1/2} lambda < 200.
* The Lyapunov exponent reaches a maximum value L ~ 1.6 x (E/N_c)^{1/4} at lambda ~ 200 x (N_c/E)^{1/2}.
* For (E/N_c)^{1/2} lambda less than about 10, there is no chaos (L = 0).

## Context

This work extends the classical chaos analysis of Yang-Mills theory initiated by Matinyan (1981) and Savvidy (1982) into the quantum domain by using the AdS/CFT correspondence, building on the D7-brane probe construction of Karch (2002) for N=2 supersymmetric QCD. The paper connects to the recent development of out-of-time-ordered correlators by Maldacena (2015) as a quantum analog of the Lyapunov exponent, and the results enable assigning a chaos index to quantum gauge theories, with potential applications to entropy production in heavy ion collisions, neutrino mass anarchy, and Higgs criticality.
