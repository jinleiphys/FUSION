# Neutron Transfer Reactions for Deformed Nuclei Using Sturmian Basis

> We study the spin-parity distribution P(J$^π$,E) of $^{156}$Gd excited states above the neutron separation energy that are expected to be populated via the neutron pickup reaction $^{157}$Gd($^{3}$He,$^{4}$He)$^{156}$Gd. In general, modeling of the spin-parity distribution is important for the applicability of the surrogate reaction technique as a method of deducing reaction cross sections. We model excited states in $^{156}$Gd as rotational states built on intrinsic states consisting of a hole in the core where the hole represents neutron removal form a deformed single particle state. The reaction cross section to each excited state is calculated using standard reaction code that uses spherical reaction form-factor input. The spectroscopic factor associated with each form-factor is the expansion coefficient of the deformed neutron state in a spherical Sturmian basis consisting of the spherical reaction form-factors.

## Key claim

The paper models the spin-parity distribution of excited states in \(^{156}\)Gd populated via the neutron pickup reaction \(^{157}\)Gd(\(^{3}\)He,\(^{4}\)He)\(^{156}\)Gd using a Sturmian basis expansion of deformed single-particle states, but the model neglects Coriolis band mixing and pairing effects in the spectroscopic factors, assuming these adjustments are small perturbations.

## Method

The authors construct excited states in \(^{156}\)Gd as rotational bands built on intrinsic hole states, where the hole represents neutron removal from a deformed single-particle state in the \(^{157}\)Gd core. They calculate the deformed single-particle wave functions using the WSBETA code with a Woods-Saxon potential and expand these states in a spherical Sturmian basis to obtain expansion coefficients \(c_{\nu}^{nlj}\). The reaction cross sections are then computed using the coupled-channel code CHUCK3 with DWBA, using optical model potentials from the RIPL-2 library for the incoming \(^{3}\)He and outgoing \(^{4}\)He channels.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy for \(^{156}\)Gd: \(S_n = 8.536\) MeV, defining the excitation energy region of interest (\(E_{ex} > 8.5\) MeV).
- Neutron separation energy for \(^{157}\)Gd: \(S_n = 6.3598\) MeV, used to calibrate the 47th neutron single-particle state binding energy at \(\epsilon_{47} = -6.361\) MeV.
- Deformation parameters for \(^{157}\)Gd: \(\beta_2 = 0.29\) and \(\beta_4 = 0\), with Woods-Saxon parameters \(V_0 = -45.1776\) MeV, \(r_0 = 1.25\) fm, \(a_0 = 0.65\) fm, \(V_{ls} = 19.2015\) MeV.
- BCS pairing parameters: Fermi energy \(\mu = -6.169\) MeV, pairing gap \(\Delta = 1.307\) MeV, pairing strength \(g = 0.15\) MeV.
- Moment of inertia for rotational bands in \(^{156}\)Gd: \(\hbar^2 / 2\mathcal{I} = 13.59\) keV, fitted to the first four excited states of the \(\Omega = 0^+\) ground state band.
- Energy shift coefficient for \(\Omega = 3^+\) band: \(c = 17.741\), chosen to reproduce the \(3_1^+\) state excitation energy at 1.248 MeV (experimental) vs. 1.255 MeV (theoretical).
- Theoretical ground state energy of \(^{156}\)Gd: 0.004 MeV (not exactly zero because \(\epsilon_{47}\) is not exactly equal to \(S_n\) in \(^{157}\)Gd).

## Context

This work extends the surrogate reaction technique for deducing neutron capture cross sections on unstable nuclei, specifically testing the method for the reaction \(^{155}\)Gd(n,\(\gamma\))\(^{156}\)Gd by modeling the surrogate reaction \(^{157}\)Gd(\(^{3}\)He,\(^{4}\)He)\(^{156}\)Gd. It builds on prior approaches using linear deformation approximations (Hird and Huang, 1975) and the Rost coupled-channel code (1967), but improves the treatment of deformed single-particle states by using a Sturmian basis expansion that maintains correct asymptotic tails for strongly deformed nuclei. The results enable future calculations of spin-parity distributions \(P(J^\pi, E)\) for surrogate method applications, though the model currently neglects Coriolis band mixing and pairing effects in the spectroscopic factors.
