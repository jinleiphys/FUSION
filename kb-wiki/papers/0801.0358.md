# Signature of deconfinement with spin down compression in cooling hybrid stars

> The thermal evolution of neutron stars is coupled to their spin down and the resulting changes in structure and chemical composition. This coupling correlates stellar surface temperatures with rotational state as well as time. We report an extensive investigation of the coupling between spin down and cooling for hybrid stars which undergo a phase transition to deconfined quark matter at the high densities present in stars at low rotation frequencies. The thermal balance of neutron stars is re-analyzed to incorporate phase transitions and the related latent heat self-consistently, and numerical calculations are undertaken to simultaneously evolve the stellar structure and temperature distribution. We find that the changes in stellar structure and chemical composition with the introduction of a pure quark matter phase in the core delay the cooling and produce a period of increasing surface temperature for strongly superfluid stars of strong and intermediate magnetic field strength. The latent heat of deconfinement is found to reinforce this signature if quark matter is superfluid and it can dominate the thermal balance during the formation of a pure quark matter core. At other times it is less important and does not significantly change the thermal evolution.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the coupling between spin down and cooling in hybrid stars can produce a period of increasing surface temperature for strongly superfluid stars of strong and intermediate magnetic field strength, but this signature depends on the presence of a pure quark matter core and is reinforced by latent heat only if quark matter is superfluid; at other times the latent heat is less important and does not significantly change the thermal evolution.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed the thermal balance equations for neutron stars to incorporate phase transitions and latent heat self-consistently, then performed numerical calculations using the G$^{300}_{180}$ equation of state from Glendenning (1992) to simultaneously evolve the stellar structure and temperature distribution of rotating hybrid stars as they spin down. They used the perturbative method of Hartle (1967, 1968) to model rotating stars with constant total baryon number and solved the general relativistic structure equations coupled to the thermal evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- Total baryon number of the stellar sequence: $A = 1.87 \times 10^{57}$
- Nonrotating total gravitational mass: $M = 1.42$ M$_\odot$
- Kepler frequency: $\Omega_\mathrm{K} = 6168$ rad s$^{-1}$, corresponding to a period of 1.02 ms
- Pure quark matter core appears below $\Omega = 1400$ rad s$^{-1}$ and grows to comprise 30% of the stellar gravitational mass and 26% of the stellar baryon number
- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.153$ fm$^{-3}$
- Mixed phase appears at densities around $2-5\rho_0$
- Estimated latent heat contribution: $10^{33} T_9^2$ erg s$^{-1}$ for a star converting steadily over $10^7$ years, where $T_9 = T/10^9$ K

## Context

This work extends the study of coupling between spin down and thermal evolution in neutron stars, building on the roto-chemical heating analysis of Reisenegger (1995) and Fernandez (2005). It contrasts with the latent heat calculations of Miao (2007, 2007a) and Xiaoping (2008), who found very significant heating terms that the present authors do not recover. The results are relevant to interpreting the correlation between neutron star temperatures and magnetic fields discovered by Pons (2007), and complement the work of Aguilera (2007, 2008) on magnetic field decay and Drago (2008) on r-mode instability constraints for hybrid stars.
