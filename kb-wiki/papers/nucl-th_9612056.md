# The Flavor Asymmetry of the Nucleon Sea

> We re-examine the effects of anti-symmetry on the anti-quarks in the nucleon sea arising from gluon exchange and pion exchange between confined quarks. While the effect is primarily to suppress anti-down relative to anti-up quarks, this is numerically insignificant for the pion terms.

## Key claim

The authors re-examine the effect of quark antisymmetry on the flavor asymmetry of the nucleon sea, finding that for gluon exchange the interference terms from antisymmetrization overcome the naive Pauli principle expectation, favoring \(\overline{u}\) over \(\overline{d}\), the opposite of the experimental result, but that for pion exchange the effect of antisymmetry is numerically insignificant, leaving the pion-driven \(\overline{d} > \overline{u}\) asymmetry intact.

## Method

The authors calculate the spin-flavor-color wave function overlaps for gluon-exchange pair creation in the proton, extending the earlier work of Donoghue and Golowich, and compute the probabilities for \(u\overline{u}\) versus \(d\overline{d}\) sea pairs including antisymmetrization between sea and valence quarks. For the pion case, they use the Cloudy Bag Model to evaluate one-loop (pion emission) and two-loop (pion emission followed by decay into \(q\overline{q}\)) diagrams, computing the expectation values of the relevant operators between bare proton states to determine the flavor dependence of the sea.

## Key numbers

- The NMC measured Gottfried sum rule: \(S_G(0.004 \leq x \leq 0.8) = 0.221 \pm 0.008(\text{stat}) \pm 0.019(\text{syst})\), with full extrapolation \(S_G = 0.235 \pm 0.026\).
- The NMC result yields \(\int_0^1 dx [\overline{u}(x) - \overline{d}(x)] = -0.147 \pm 0.039\).
- For the gluon-exchange case with \(s=g, v\neq g\), the probability amplitude for \(\overline{u}\) is larger than for \(\overline{d}\) (e.g., \(\langle \psi_s|\psi_s\rangle = 320\) for graph (e) of Fig. 2 for \(u\overline{u}\), with no analogous graph for \(d\overline{d}\)).
- For the one-loop pion probability, the expectation value between bare proton states is 57, which decomposes as \(22\delta_{\pi^+} + 19\delta_{\pi^0} + 16\delta_{\pi^-}\), favoring \(\overline{d}\) over \(\overline{u}\).
- For the two-loop pion graph, the expectation values are \(684 - 110\delta_{\tilde{s}\overline{u}} - 181\delta_{\tilde{s}\overline{d}}\), indicating the antisymmetry effect on the pion sea is small.

## Context

This work extends the earlier gluon-exchange calculation of Donoghue and Golowich and the pion-cloud model of Thomas (1983) for the nucleon sea asymmetry. It addresses the NMC (1991) measurement of the Gottfried sum rule and the NA51 (1996) result showing strong flavor symmetry breaking at \(x=0.18\). The finding that antisymmetrization does not significantly alter the pion-driven asymmetry preserves the meson-cloud interpretation of the sea, while the gluon result warns that disconnected and connected insertions in lattice QCD (Leinweber, 1996) must be treated together to obtain physically meaningful results.
