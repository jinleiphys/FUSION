# Gamow Shell Model description of ${ { }^{ 40 } }$Ca(d,p) transfer reaction

> Transfer reactions are essential to determine spectroscopic factors and astrophysical reaction rates. However, their theoretical evaluation is typically effected using standard reaction theory, from which structure degrees of freedom are absent. While reaction cross sections have been implemented in the frame of the no-core shell model with continuum, this model can be applied in practice only to the lightest nuclei. The use of the core + valence nucleon picture is then necessary to include inter-nucleon correlations in reaction cross sections involving medium nuclei. For this, we will use the recently developed coupled-channel Gamow Shell Model (GSM-CC) for direct reactions and extend it to the evaluation of transfer cross sections. As an example, we will study the ${ { }^{ 40 } }$Ca(d,p) transfer reaction with GSM-CC. Experimental data can be successfully reproduced, but at the price of the use of a very phenomenological Hamiltonian.

# Gamow Shell Model description of 40Ca(d,p) transfer reaction

## Key claim

Experimental data for the 40Ca(d,p) transfer reaction can be successfully reproduced using the coupled-channel Gamow Shell Model (GSM-CC), but only at the price of very precise fine-tuning of both the Hamiltonian parameters and the Woods-Saxon basis-generating potentials that describe the continuum wave functions and their asymptotes.

## Method

The authors extended the coupled-channel Gamow Shell Model (GSM-CC), previously developed for direct reactions, to evaluate transfer cross sections. They used a 40Ca inert core with one or two valence nucleons, expanding target and projectile wave functions in a harmonic oscillator basis and solving coupled-channel equations using a Berggren basis expansion of the Green's function. The Hamiltonian employed a Woods-Saxon core potential and the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) residual interaction, with deuteron projectile states generated from a no-core GSM calculation using the N3LO interaction.

## Key numbers

- The 40Ca(d,p) transfer cross section was calculated at a center-of-mass energy of 1.853 MeV and successfully reproduced experimental data from Fodor (1965) in both form and magnitude.
- The calculated neutron separation energy for 41Ca is 8.38 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 8.363 MeV.
- The calculated proton separation energy for 41Sc is 1.11 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.09 MeV.
- The calculated deuteron separation energy for 42Sc is 12.53 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 12.48 MeV.
- The calculated two-neutron separation energy for 42Ca is 19.74 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 19.8 MeV.
- The calculated two-proton separation energy for 42Ti is 4.68 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 4.83 MeV.
- Excited states of A=42 nuclei were reproduced with a typical precision of approximately 50 keV.
- The harmonic oscillator energy truncation for composite basis states was set at Emax(HO) = 8 hbar-omega.
- Corrective factors in positive-parity channels ranged from 0.99 (1+ channel) to 1.14 (0+ channel).

## Context

This work extends the GSM-CC framework, previously applied to 6Li via deuteron induced elastic scattering on 4He (Mercenne, 2019), to the calculation of transfer reaction cross sections in medium-mass nuclei. It represents the first calculation combining shell model and coupled-channel equation approaches for transfer reactions in heavier nuclei, going beyond the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) which is practically applicable only to the lightest nuclei. The approach enables future systematic studies of transfer reactions relevant for astrophysical reaction rates, though the authors note that renormalization of neglected reaction channels using complex coupling potentials will be necessary for practical systematic application.
