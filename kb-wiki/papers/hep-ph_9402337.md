# Chiral invariant renormalization of the pion--nucleon interaction

> The leading divergences of the generating functional for Green functions of quark currents between one--nucleon states are calculated with heat kernel techniques. The results allow for a chiral invariant renormalization of all two--nucleon Green functions of the pion--nucleon system to $O(p^3)$ in the low--energy expansion.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the leading divergences of the generating functional for Green functions of quark currents between one-nucleon states using heat kernel techniques, enabling a chiral invariant renormalization of all two-nucleon Green functions of the pion-nucleon system to O(p^3) in the low-energy expansion. The results are limited to two light flavours (SU(2) chiral symmetry) and the extension to chiral SU(3) is deferred to a later publication.

## Method

The author starts from the effective chiral Lagrangian for pions and nucleons and uses heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) to perform a systematic low-energy expansion. The heavy components of the nucleon field are integrated out, reducing the functional integral to a mesonic integral, and the generating functional is expanded around the classical solution of the lowest-order equation of motion. The divergences of the one-loop self-energy functionals are extracted using heat kernel representations for both the meson propagator and the nucleon propagator in the presence of external fields, with the divergences appearing as simple poles in epsilon = (4-d)/2 in dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The divergences are extracted in d-dimensional Euclidean space with epsilon = (4-d)/2, and the counterterm Lagrangian is expressed in terms of dimensionless coupling constants B_i with beta-functions beta_i that are functions of the axial coupling g_A.
- The counterterm Lagrangian is given by L_ct = (1/(4*pi*F)^2) * sum_i B_i * bar_N_v * O_i * N_v, where the beta_i for 22 operators are tabulated; for example, beta_15 = -3*g_A^2 for the operator i(v*gradient)^3, and beta_10 = -(1+5*g_A^2)/6 for the operator gradient^mu*f_{+mu*nu}*v^nu.
- The renormalized low-energy constants B_i^r(mu) satisfy the renormalization group equation mu*d/dmu B_i^r(mu) = -beta_i, implying B_i^r(mu) = B_i^r(mu_0) - beta_i * log(mu/mu_0).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering application of chiral perturbation theory to the pion-nucleon system by Gasser, Sainio and Svarc (1991), who applied CHPT methods to the pi-N system but were hampered by the nucleon mass staying finite in the chiral limit. The paper builds on the heavy baryon CHPT formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991), which allows a systematic low-energy expansion of baryonic Green functions analogous to the meson sector. The results enable the complete renormalization of all two-nucleon Green functions to O(p^3), including nucleon form factors and processes such as pi N -> pi ... pi N and gamma* N -> pi ... pi N, and provide a foundation for extensions to chiral SU(3), inclusion of higher baryon states, and renormalization at O(p^4).
