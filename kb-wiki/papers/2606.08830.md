# Nuclear parton distributions and nuclear shadowing

> In this contribution, we review the physics and phenomenology of nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs), with a particular focus on the small-x region of nuclear shadowing. We summarise experimental evidence for nuclear modifications of PDFs and discuss their theoretical explanations; in particular, we contrast different models of nuclear shadowing. We also overview model-agnostic extractions of nuclear PDFs from global data on hard processes with nuclei, emphasizing the validity of collinear factorization and the dominance of leading-twist nuclear PDFs. Finally, we discuss perspectives for present and future precision studies of nuclear PDFs and small-x QCD dynamics, including photonuclear reactions in heavy-ion ultraperipheral collisions at the Large Hadron Collider and lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

This review argues that nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) provide a universal, leading-twist description of hard processes with nuclei across a wide kinematic range, but that the interpretation of nuclear shadowing at small $x$ admits multiple competing theoretical explanations, including the Gribov-Glauber theory, the leading twist approximation, the dipole model, Color Glass Condensate-based approaches, and resummation of nucleus-enhanced higher-twist contributions.

## Method

The authors review the collinear factorization framework for nuclear PDFs, presenting formal operator definitions and the connection between nuclear and bound-nucleon PDFs via nuclear modification ratios. They survey experimental evidence from fixed-target deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and Drell-Yan (DY) measurements, as well as from hadron, jet, electroweak boson, and top-quark pair production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC, and from photonuclear processes in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs). The review contrasts theoretical models for nuclear modifications at large $x$ (Fermi motion, EMC effect) and small $x$ (nuclear shadowing), and critically examines global analysis approaches for extracting nuclear PDFs from data.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear modification ratio $R^A_{F_2}(x,Q^2) = F_2^A(x,Q^2)/F_2^d(x,Q^2)$ shows suppression (EMC effect) for $0.3 < x < 0.7$, enhancement (antishadowing) for $0.1 < x < 0.3$, and suppression (shadowing) for $x < 0.1$.
- Coherent $J/\psi$ photoproduction in Pb-Pb UPCs at the LHC probes the gluon density of the target for $10^{-5} < x_N < 0.05$ at $Q^2 = \mathcal{O}(M^2_{J/\psi})$, providing direct evidence of gluon nuclear shadowing.
- Fixed-target DY measurements with proton beams on nuclear targets probe nuclear PDFs in the range $0.01 < x_N < 0.3$, while charged-pion beam DY measurements probe $0.1 < x_N < 0.4$.
- The LHCb measurement of D$^0$ production in $p+$Pb collisions shows strong suppression in the forward (proton-going) direction and moderate enhancement in the backward (nucleus-going) direction.
- The CMS dijet measurement in $p+$Pb collisions shows suppression at negative dijet pseudorapidity (EMC effect), enhancement at midrapidity (antishadowing), and suppression at large positive pseudorapidity (shadowing).

## Context

This review extends the extensive experimental and theoretical work on nuclear PDFs that began with the discovery of the EMC effect by the European Muon Collaboration (1983) and continued through fixed-target experiments at CERN, SLAC, FNAL, and JLab, as well as RHIC and LHC measurements. It contrasts different theoretical approaches to nuclear shadowing, including the Gribov-Glauber theory, the leading twist approximation, the dipole model, and Color Glass Condensate-based calculations. The review enables future precision studies of nuclear PDFs and small-$x$ QCD dynamics at the Electron-Ion Collider and through photonuclear reactions in heavy-ion UPCs at the LHC.
