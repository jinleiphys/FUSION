# Translationally invariant shell model calculation of the quasielastic $(p,2p)$ process at intermediate relativistic energies

> Relativistic beams of heavy ions interacting with various nuclear targets allow to study a broad range of problems starting from nuclear equation of state to the traditional nuclear structure. Some questions which were impossible to answer heretofore -- can be addressed nowadays by using inverse kinematics. These includes the structure of short-lived nuclei and the precision study of exclusive channels with production of residual nuclei in certain quantum states. Theoretical understanding such processes is so far based on factorization models which combine the single-step amplitude of the reaction on a bound nucleon or nuclear cluster with a certain wave function of its relative motion with respect to the residual nucleus. The nuclear structure information is encoded in the spectroscopic amplitude, calculable within nuclear many-body theories. In this work, we use for this purpose the translationally-invariant shell model with configuration mixing and demonstrate that it successfully reproduces the single-differential and integrated cross sections of the quasielastic proton knockout, $^{12}\mbox{C}(p,2p)^{11}\mbox{B}$, with outgoing $^{11}$B in the ground state and low-lying excited states measured at GSI at 400 MeV/nucleon.

## Key claim

The translationally-invariant shell model (TISM) with configuration mixing successfully reproduces the single-differential and integrated cross sections of the quasielastic proton knockout reaction $^{12}\mbox{C}(p,2p)^{11}\mbox{B}$ at 400 MeV/nucleon, but only when initial- and final-state interactions (ISI/FSI) are included via eikonal absorption factors, which reduce all partial cross sections by a factor of 5.1.

## Method

The authors calculated the spectroscopic amplitudes for the virtual decay $^{12}\mbox{C} \to p\, ^{11}\mbox{B}$ using the TISM in a harmonic oscillator basis with configuration mixing from the intermediate coupling model of Boyarkina (1973). The reaction amplitude was constructed in the impulse approximation and then modified by including initial- and final-state interactions through eikonal absorption factors derived from optical potentials proportional to the total $NN$ cross section and nuclear density. The fully differential cross section was integrated over phase space in the rest frame of the initial nucleus and the center-of-mass frame of the two outgoing protons.

## Key numbers

- Absorption reduces all partial cross sections by a factor of 5.1 for the $^{12}\mbox{C}(p,2p)^{11}\mbox{B}$ reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon beam energy.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter used is $r_0 = 1.581$ fm for the $^{12}\mbox{C}$ nucleus.
- The spin-orbit parameter $a = -5$ MeV and radial integral $K = -1.2$ MeV were used for $A=12$ and $A=11$ nuclei, with $L/K = 6$ fixed.
- The reconstructed two-body potential parameters are $d = r_0$ and $V_0 = -56.12$ MeV.
- The beam momentum per nucleon is $p_{\rm beam}/A = 0.951$ GeV/c in the laboratory frame.

## Context

This work extends the TISM approach previously applied by Larionov and Uzikov (2023) to proton knockout from short-range correlated $pN$ pairs in $^{12}\mbox{C}$, now applied to single-proton knockout. It tests the reaction model against data from Panin (2016) measured at GSI, serving as a necessary benchmark before extending the framework to exotic nuclei studied in inverse kinematics at RIKEN (Kondo, 2009) and GSI (R3B collaboration, 2019). The approach contrasts with simpler factorization models that combine single-step amplitudes with spectroscopic amplitudes from conventional shell models, which contain spurious center-of-mass excitations that the TISM avoids.
