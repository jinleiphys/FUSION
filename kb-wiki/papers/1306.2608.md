# Limits on tensor type weak currents from nuclear and neutron $β$~decays

> The limits on time-reversal invariant tensor-type weak currents from nuclear and neutron $β$ decays are evaluated including most recent experimental data. We find that $ -0.14 \times 10^{-2} < (C_T + C^\prime_T)/C_A < 1.4 \times 10^{-2} $ and $ -0.16 < (C_T - C^\prime_T)/C_A < 0.16$ (90% C.L.), while for the case $C_T = C^\prime_T$ the limits are $ | C_T/C_A | < 3.7 \times 10^{-3} $. These limits are shown to be more stringent than those from recent measurements of the radiative pion decay. In addition, the sensitivity of future $10^{-3}$-level correlation measurements is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper finds that combined limits from nuclear and neutron β decays on time-reversal invariant tensor-type weak currents are more stringent than those from recent radiative pion decay measurements, contrary to earlier claims in the Effective Field Theory literature (Bhattacharya 2012, Cirigliano 2013) that pion decays gave tighter constraints.

## Method

The authors construct a χ² function using the formalism of Jackson, Treiman, and Wyld (1957) parameterizing the β-decay Hamiltonian with coupling constants C_V, C_A, C_S, C'_S, C_T, and C'_T. They fix C_V using corrected ft-values from 0⁺→0⁺ super-allowed Fermi transitions (Hardy 2009), then step through values of the tensor couplings while letting C_A, C_S, and C'_S vary to minimize χ², producing 2-D confidence contours and 1-D projected probability distributions.

## Key numbers

- Combined 90% C.L. interval: -0.14 × 10⁻² < (C_T + C'_T)/C_A < 1.4 × 10⁻²
- Combined 90% C.L. interval: -0.16 < (C_T - C'_T)/C_A < 0.16
- For the case C_T = C'_T (left-handed neutrinos only): |C_T/C_A| < 3.7 × 10⁻³ at 90% C.L.
- For the 3-parameter fit with C'_T = C_T and C'_S = C_S: -0.0 × 10⁻² < C_T/C_A < 0.4 × 10⁻² at 90% C.L.
- The value of λ = C_A/C_V at minimum χ² is -1.2753(6), consistent with the PDG2012 recommended value of -1.2701(25)
- The β-asymmetry parameter A₀ for neutron decay from UCNA (Mendenhall 2013) is -0.11952 ± 0.00110; from PERKEOII (Mund 2013) is -0.11926 ± 0.00050
- The neutron lifetime values used range from 878.5 ± 0.76 s (Serebrov 2005) to 889.2 ± 4.8 s (Byrne 1996)

## Context

This work extends previous evaluations by Boothroyd (1984) and Severijns (2006) using the most recent experimental data including UCNA (Mendenhall 2013) and PERKEOII (Mund 2013) neutron asymmetry measurements. The results contradict the conclusion of Bhattacharya (2012) and Cirigliano (2013) that radiative pion decay limits were more stringent than nuclear β-decay limits. The paper enables future 10⁻³-level correlation measurements in neutron or selected nuclear decays to surpass the sensitivity of current and future LHC experiments for new physics at higher energy scales, provided theoretical uncertainties on nucleon and pion form factors are reduced.
