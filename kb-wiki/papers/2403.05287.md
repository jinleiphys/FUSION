# Shape of 12C

> We have examined the hypothesis by Bijker and Iachello who asserted that 12C has an internal structure with three alpha particles arranged in a triangular shape, leading to the formation of the ground rotational band consisting of 0+, 2+, 3-, 4$^\pm$ and 5- states. Following this idea, we reconstructed the intrinsic shape of 12C using experimental electron scattering data with minimal theoretical assumption. Our sole assumption was that the observed 0+, 2+, 3-, and 4+ states share a common internal structure, forming a rotational spectrum. The reconstructed intrinsic density showed a beautiful triangular shape with three peaks implying alpha cluster formation in the ground band.

## Key claim
The authors reconstruct the intrinsic shape of 12C from experimental electron scattering data under the sole assumption that the 0+1, 2+1, 3-1, and 4+2 states share a common internal structure forming a rotational band, as hypothesized by Bijker and Iachello. The reconstructed density shows a triangular shape with three peaks, but the authors note that the 3-1 state's membership in the ground rotational band currently lacks clear experimental evidence.

## Method
The authors fitted experimental charge and transition form factors (for 0+, 2+, 3-, and 4+ states) using a sum of Gaussian functions to extract reduced transition densities. They then applied a rigid-rotor model assuming the states belong to a common rotational band, using Eq. ## Key numbers
- The 4+2 state at 14.1 MeV and the 4-1 state at 13.3 MeV are almost degenerate, supporting the D3h symmetry prediction
- The 5-1 state at 22.4 MeV (discovered by Marin-Lambarri 2014) completes the rotational spectrum
- B(E2) = 4.65 Wu (Weisskopf units) for the 2+1 state, from Pritychenko (2016)
- Electric quadrupole moment of the 2+1 state: Q = +6 ± 3 e fm2, from Vermeer (1983)
- The reconstructed matter density at the triangle vertices reaches approximately 0.22 fm-3, extremely higher than normal density
- The central density of the L=0 component is approximately 0.07 fm-3 (proton density only)

## Context
This work extends the earlier shape reconstruction approach of Nakada (1971) and Kamimura (1981) to non-axial and parity-asymmetric cases, testing the algebraic cluster model of Bijker and Iachello (2000, 2020). The results support the interpretation that the ground band of 12C arises from rotation of a triangular alpha-cluster configuration, complementing the Bose-Einstein condensate description of the Hoyle state by Tohsaki (2001) and Funaki (2009, 2015). The reconstructed shape contrasts sharply with the moderate deformation seen in heavy nuclei like 152Sm (Cardman 1978), where transition densities are significant only near the nuclear surface.
