# Thermodynamics of the strongly interacting gluon plasma in the large-N limit

> We report on our recent study of equilibrium thermodynamic observables in SU(N) gauge theories with N=3, 4, 5, 6 and 8 colors at temperatures T in the range from 0.8 T_c to 3.4 T_c (where T_c denotes the critical deconfinement temperature). The results, which show a very weak dependence on the number of colors, are compared with gauge/gravity models of the QCD plasma, including the improved holographic QCD model proposed by Kiritsis and collaborators, and with the supergravity prediction for the entropy density deficit. Furthermore, we investigate the possibility that the trace anomaly may receive contributions proportional to T^2 at temperatures close to T_c. Finally, we present the extrapolated results for the pressure, trace anomaly, energy and entropy densities in the limit for N going to infinity.

## Key claim

The equilibrium thermodynamic observables of SU(N) gauge theories show a very weak dependence on the number of colors for N=3 through N=8, making it plausible that the QCD plasma could be described by models based on the large-N limit; however, the trace anomaly data may be compatible with a T^2 contribution near T_c, but the precision does not rule out more complicated functional forms involving perturbative logarithms.

## Method

Lattice simulations of SU(N) gauge theories with N=3, 4, 5, 6, and 8 colors were performed on isotropic hypercubic lattices using the standard unimproved Wilson gauge action, with heat-bath updates for SU(2) subgroups and full-SU(N) overrelaxation. The trace anomaly was extracted from the difference of plaquette expectation values at zero and finite temperature, and the pressure was determined via the integral method. The physical scale was set using Sommer's parameter r0 for SU(3) and by interpolating string tension values for SU(N>3).

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 0.8 T_c to 3.4 T_c for all gauge groups (N=3,4,5,6,8)
- Lattice sizes: N_s=20 for N=3, N_s=16 for N>3, with N_t=5
- The SU(N) plasma approaches approximate scale invariance only at temperatures about 3 T_c
- Comparison of N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills and full-QCD lattice results for drag force yields lambda approximately 5.5 (Gubser 2006)
- The trace anomaly data appear compatible with a T^2 contribution in the range (T_c/T)^2 less than or equal to 0.9

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of SU(N) gauge theory thermodynamics to larger N values (up to N=8) and provides extrapolations to the N to infinity limit, building on prior lattice thermodynamics reviews and the large-N framework of 't Hooft (1973). The results are compared with the improved holographic QCD model of Kiritsis and collaborators (2007-2008), showing good agreement, and with the AdS/CFT prediction for N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills entropy density from Gubser (1998). The study enables future comparisons with gauge/gravity predictions for other observables and renormalized Polyakov loops, and motivates similar high-precision thermodynamics studies in lower-dimensional models or with smaller gauge groups.
