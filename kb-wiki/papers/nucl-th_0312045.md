# Polarization of Lambda^0 hyperons as a signature for the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The momentum distribution of Lambda^0 hyperons produced from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is calculated in dependence on their polarization. The momentum distribution of Lambda^0 hyperons is defined by matrix elements of relativistic quark Wigner operators, which are calculated within the Effective quark model with chiral U(3)xU(3) symmetry and the Quark-Gluon transport theory. We show that the polarization of the Lambda^0 hyperon depends of the spin of the strange quark that agrees well with the DeGrand-Miettinen model. We show that Lambda^0 hyperons, produced from the QGP, are fully unpolarized. This means that a detection of unpolarized Lambda^0 hyperons, produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, should serve as one of the signatures for the existence of the QGP in intermediate states of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors show that $\Lambda^0$ hyperons produced from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) are fully unpolarized, meaning that detection of unpolarized $\Lambda^0$ hyperons in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions should serve as a signature for the existence of the QGP in intermediate states, provided the polarization properties are fully defined by the strange quark, which agrees with the DeGrand-Miettinen model.

## Method

The momentum distribution of $\Lambda^0$ hyperons produced from the QGP was calculated using matrix elements of relativistic quark Wigner operators, evaluated within the Effective quark model with chiral $U(3)\times U(3)$ symmetry and the Quark-Gluon transport theory. The calculation employed the reduction technique to express the matrix elements in terms of two-point quark Green functions at finite temperature, with the strange quark treated as massive and the $u$ and $d$ quarks as massless, leading to the result that only the strange quark contributes to the polarization properties.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the $\Lambda^0$ hyperon: $M_{\Lambda^0} = 1116\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- Phenomenological coupling constant of the low-lying baryon octet: $g_{\rm B} = 1.34 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-2}$.
- Experimental polarization values from the E896 Collaboration for Au+Au collisions at 11.6$A\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ at AGS: for $p_{\perp} \ge 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ and $x_f \ge 0.5$, the Silicon Drift Detector Array gave $P_{\Lambda^0} = (-25.7 \pm 7.5)\%$ and the Distributed Drift Chamber gave $P_{\Lambda^0} = (-23 \pm 11)\%$.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Stock (1982) that phase transitions to a QGP could lead to complete depolarization of $\Lambda^0$ hyperons, and addresses the open question raised by Bellwied (2002) regarding whether $\Lambda^0$ hyperons from a QGP are depolarized. The authors contrast their quark-level approach with earlier semi-classical di-quark, s-quark recombination models (Ayala et al., 2002) and Regge-type models (M. Anselmino, 2001), which did not necessarily lead to complete depolarization; the present calculation shows that within the Effective quark model and Quark-Gluon transport theory, $\Lambda^0$ hyperons from the QGP are fully unpolarized.
