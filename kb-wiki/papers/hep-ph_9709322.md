# Interference Fragmentation Functions and the Nucleon's Transversity

> We introduce twist-two quark interference fragmentation functions in helicity density matrix formalism and study their physical implications. We show how the nucleon's transversity distribution can be probed through the final state interaction between two mesons ($π^+π^-$, $K\bar K$, or $πK$) produced in the current fragmentation region in deep inelastic scattering on a transversely polarized nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper introduces twist-two interference fragmentation functions for two-meson production and shows that the nucleon's transversity distribution can be probed at leading twist through the final state interaction between two mesons (π⁺π⁻, K anti-K, or πK) produced in the current fragmentation region of deep inelastic scattering on a transversely polarized nucleon, provided the data are kept differential in the two-meson invariant mass and the azimuthal angle of the decay plane.

## Method

The authors use a helicity density matrix formalism to factor the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering process e N_perp → e' π⁺π⁻ X into a nucleon-to-quark distribution function, a hard partonic eq → e'q' cross section, a q → (σ, ρ) fragmentation step, and a (σ, ρ) → ππ decay, all expressed as density matrices in helicity basis. The final state interaction between the two pions is incorporated explicitly using measured ππ phase shifts δ₀ and δ₁, and the s-wave (σ) and p-wave (ρ) interference is isolated by integrating over the decay polar angle Θ to eliminate the spin-independent fragmentation function contribution.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry A_⊥⊤ is proportional to sinδ₀ sinδ₁ sin(δ₀−δ₁), which is shown as a function of the two-pion invariant mass m using ππ phase shift data from Estabrooks and Martin (1974).
- The asymmetry contains a factor (1−y)/[1+(1−y)²] from the hard scattering, where y = P·q/P·k is the standard DIS inelasticity variable.
- The cross section for transversely polarized targets includes a factor √6 cosφ, where φ is the azimuthal angle of the normal to the two-pion plane relative to the nucleon transverse spin vector S_⊥.
- The interference fragmentation function δq̂_I satisfies the Schwartz inequality bound δq̂_I² ≤ 4q̂₀q̂₁/3 for each flavor.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Collins, Heppelmann, and Ladinsky (1994) to use two-meson production for accessing transversity, but replaces their unphysical assumption of a narrow s-wave resonance interfering with a real continuum p-wave with a treatment based on actual measured ππ phase shifts. The approach avoids the drawbacks of other proposed methods: it is leading twist (unlike twist-three pion production from Jaffe and Ji (1991) which is suppressed by O(1/Q)), does not require both transversely polarized quarks and antiquarks as in the Drell-Yan process from Ralston and Soper (1979), and does not rely on the uncertain final state interaction phase needed for the Collins effect in single particle fragmentation. The technique can be extended to longitudinally polarized nucleons, e⁺e⁻ → (π⁺π⁻ X)(π⁺π⁻ X), and single polarized nucleon-nucleon collisions.
