# Target-mass corrections and the Bloom-Gilman duality of the nucleon structure function

> The occurrence of the Bloom-Gilman local duality in the low-order moments of the nucleon structure function is investigated for values of the squared four-momentum transfer Q**2 between ~ 0.5 and 10 (GeV/c)**2. At variance with previous analyses truncated Cornwall-Norton moments, limited to the nucleon-resonance production regions, are considered. The role played by target-mass corrections is illustrated, showing that target-mass effects are necessary (but not sufficient) for producing the observed Bloom-Gilman duality of the nucleon structure function. The possibility of a local duality between the unphysical region at large values of the Nachtmann variable and the nucleon elastic peak contribution is analyzed. It is found that the proton magnetic form factor extracted assuming local duality is significantly below the experimental data at low and intermediate values of Q**2.

## Key claim

Target-mass corrections are necessary but not sufficient for producing the observed Bloom-Gilman local duality of the nucleon structure function; the duality also requires small dynamical higher-twist effects when locally averaged, and it cannot be extended to a super-local version involving only the nucleon elastic peak.

## Method

The author computed truncated Cornwall-Norton moments of the nucleon structure function, limited to the nucleon-resonance production region (invariant mass W* = 2.5 GeV), for Q² between ~0.5 and 10 (GeV/c)². The target-mass-corrected leading-twist structure function F₂^TM(ξ, Q²) was calculated via the operator product expansion using the GRV parton distribution functions evolved at next-to-leading order, and compared with pseudo-data from the SLAC interpolation formula. The elastic peak contribution was isolated and tested against a proposed super-local duality with the unphysical region of the Nachtmann variable.

## Key numbers

- The onset of Bloom-Gilman duality occurs at Q² ≈ Q²_BG ~ 1.5 (GeV/c)², almost independent of the moment order n.
- For 1.5 ≲ Q² (GeV/c)² ≲ 10, target-mass corrections almost compensate pQCD evolution effects, yielding F₂^TM(ξ, Q²) ~ F₂^S(ξ, Q²_high) with Q²_high ~ 30 (GeV/c)² for ξ ≳ 0.2.
- The truncated target-mass-corrected moments deviate from pseudo-experimental moments by only ~20–30% for Q² ≥ Q²_BG ~ 1.5 (GeV/c)².
- The proton magnetic form factor G_M^p(Q²) extracted assuming local duality in the elastic peak region is below experimental data by a factor of 2–3 at low and intermediate Q².

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bloom-Gilman duality by De Rujula, Georgi and Politzer (1977) and the subsequent studies of duality in nucleon and nuclear data (Refs. duality, elastic), but uses truncated Cornwall-Norton moments limited to the resonance region rather than full moments. The finding that target-mass corrections are necessary but not sufficient clarifies the role of kinematical versus dynamical higher twists. The failure of a super-local duality for the elastic peak, consistent with the earlier analysis of Ref. elastic using untruncated Nachtmann moments, points to the need for a non-trivial compensation among dynamical higher-twist effects in resonance bumps and background, as suggested by the relation between the background and the N–Δ(1232) transition peak noted by Carlson and Mukhopadhyay (1993). The results are relevant for interpreting forthcoming high-precision data from Jefferson Lab.
