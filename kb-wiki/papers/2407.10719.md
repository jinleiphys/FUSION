# Global analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions

> We review the theoretical foundations, methodological approaches and current status of the determination of nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs). A large variety of measurements in fixed-target and collider experiments provide increasingly precise constraints on various aspects of nuclear PDFs, including shadowing, antishadowing, the EMC effect, Fermi motion, flavour separation, deuteron binding, target-mass and other higher-twist effects. We give particular emphasis to measurements carried out in proton-lead collisions at the Large Hadron Collider, which have revolutionised the global analysis during the past decade. These measurements include electroweak boson, isolated photon, single inclusive hadron, jet, and heavy-flavour observables.

# Global analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions

## Key claim
Nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) can be determined with increasing precision from global fits to experimental data, but significant differences remain among the three major global analyses (EPPS21, nCTEQ15HQ, nNNPDF3.0) in both central values and uncertainty band widths across several parton species and kinematic regions, even at 90% confidence level.

## Method
The author reviews the theoretical foundations and methodological approaches for determining nuclear PDFs, including the factorisation of nuclear structure functions into perturbative Wilson coefficients and nuclear PDFs, DGLAP evolution equations, and the fitting of experimental data by optimising a chi-squared figure-of-merit function with Hessian or Monte Carlo uncertainty propagation. The review emphasises measurements from proton-lead collisions at the LHC, including electroweak boson, isolated photon, single inclusive hadron, jet, and heavy-flavour observables, and compares the three current global NLO analyses (nCTEQ15HQ, EPPS21, nNNPDF3.0) as well as the NNLO analyses KSASG20 and TUJU21.

## Key numbers
- CMS Z-boson data at sqrt(s) = 8.16 TeV: NNLO corrections are around 5% for the high-mass bin (60 < M < 120 GeV) but reach 20% at the largest absolute rapidities for the low-mass bin (15 < M < 60 GeV)
- ATLAS prompt photon data at sqrt(s) = 8.16 TeV: pPb/pp ratios can be described at NLO, but absolute cross sections are underestimated at NLO, suggesting a need for NNLO precision
- Underlying event in nuclear collisions: estimated to involve on average 7 +/- 5 pN interactions
- Top quark production in pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 8.16 TeV: measured cross sections show impressive agreement with PDF predictions even though these data were not included in the fits
- Nuclear modifications compared at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 for lead (208Pb) across three global analyses at 90% CL

## Context
This review extends the author's recent comprehensive review of nuclear PDFs (Klasen, 2023) and provides a concise summary with complementary information presented at the 31st International Workshop on Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS2024). It covers the evolution from earlier analyses such as EPPS16 and nCTEQ15 to the current state-of-the-art global fits EPPS21 (Eskola et al., 2021), nCTEQ15HQ (Duwentaster et al., 2022), and nNNPDF3.0 (AbdulKhalek et al., 2022). The work enables future progress by identifying where LHC data have already revolutionised nuclear PDF determinations and where tensions remain, particularly in the forward dijet region and in the description of absolute prompt photon cross sections, while also highlighting the anticipated impact of the Electron-Ion Collider.
