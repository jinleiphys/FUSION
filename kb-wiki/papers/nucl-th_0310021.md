# Indication of anomalous exchange in exclusive charmonium photoproduction

> We find that available data on $J/Ψ$ photoproduction at $|t|{\ge}$1 GeV are energy independent over the range $22{\le}\sqrt{s}{\le}170$ GeV and show a very soft $t$ dependence. Through a systematic analysis of the data we detect a new trajectory, which manifests itself at threshold and is energy independent. The trajectory couples to the axial form factor of the nucleon and can reproduce the $J/Ψ$ exclusive photoproduction data at large -$t$.

## Key claim

The paper finds that exclusive $J/\Psi$ photoproduction data at $|t| \ge 1$ GeV are energy independent over $\sqrt{s} = 22$ to $170$ GeV, indicating a new exchange trajectory beyond soft and hard pomeron exchanges; however, the authors caution that the reanalysis of ZEUS data cannot be considered definitive because there are only two data points at $|t| \ge 1$ GeV.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic analysis of existing $J/\Psi$ photoproduction data from Cornell, SLAC, EMC, E687, and ZEUS collaborations, fitting differential cross sections as $d\sigma/dt = A \exp(bt)$ to extract forward cross sections and slopes. They compared these results to calculations using the Regge model with soft and hard pomeron exchanges and to perturbative QCD two-gluon exchange with MRST2001 parton distribution functions, attributing the discrepancy between data and pomeron-only calculations to an additional soft contribution.

## Key numbers

- The forward $J/\Psi$ photoproduction cross section from the new trajectory is of order 20 nb·GeV$^{-2}$ at $t=0$, starting from threshold.
- The new trajectory has a slope $b < 3$ GeV$^{-2}$, indicating it does not involve the proton electromagnetic form factor.
- The cut-off parameter for the new trajectory form factor is $\Lambda \simeq 1.2$ GeV.
- The soft pomeron trajectory is $\alpha(t) = 1.08 + 0.25t$; the hard pomeron trajectory is $\alpha(t) = 1.44 + 0.1t$.
- The proton isoscalar electromagnetic form factor gives a local slope $b \simeq 4.6$ at $t=0$ and $b \simeq 3.7$ at $t=1$ GeV$^2$.
- The data are energy independent over the range $22 \le \sqrt{s} \le 170$ GeV at $|t| \ge 1$ GeV.
- The $J/\Psi$ threshold corresponds to a photon energy of $\simeq 8.15$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Regge model analysis of charmonium photoproduction by Sibirtsev et al. (2003) and challenges the assumption that only soft and hard pomeron exchanges (Donnachie and Landshoff, 1992; Donnachie and Landshoff, 1998) describe the data at all $t$. The proposed new trajectory, possibly corresponding to $f_1$ exchange with odd signature, was previously suggested by Kochelev (1999) and Oh (2001) for $\rho$ photoproduction at large $|t|$, but the $J/\Psi$ data rule out $f_1$ as a substitute for the hard pomeron. The results motivate low-energy measurements at Jefferson Lab's HALL D to confirm the energy-independent forward cross section of order 20 nb·GeV$^{-2}$ and to determine the quantum numbers of the new trajectory via polarization measurements.
