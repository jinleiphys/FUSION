# Neutron-proton effective mass splitting in neutron-rich matter

> Nucleon effective masses in neutron-rich matter are studied with the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory in the full Dirac space. The neutron and proton effective masses for symmetric nuclear matter are 0.80 times rest mass, which agrees well with the empirical values. In neutron-rich matter, the effective mass of the neutron is found larger than that of the proton, and the neutron-proton effective mass splittings at the empirical saturation density are predicted as $0.187α$ with $α$ being the isospin asymmetry parameter. The result is compared to other ab initio calculations and is consistent with the constraints from the nuclear reaction and structure measurements, such as the nucleon-nucleus scattering, the giant resonances of $^{208}$Pb, and the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem with systematics of nuclear symmetry energy and its slope. The predictions of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting from the RBHF theory in the full Dirac space might be helpful to constrain the isovector parameters in phenomenological density functionals.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton effective mass splitting in neutron-rich matter at empirical saturation density is predicted as \(0.187\alpha\) (where \(\alpha\) is the isospin asymmetry parameter) by the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory in the full Dirac space, but this linear relation is only valid for \(0 \leq \alpha \leq 0.6\); for larger asymmetry the splitting shows a non-monotonical behavior due to an up-bending of the proton effective mass.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations in the full Dirac space, including both positive-energy and negative-energy states of nucleons, to obtain the \(G\) matrix from the Thompson equation using the Bonn A, B, and C nucleon-nucleon potentials. From the \(G\) matrix elements, the scalar and vector components of the single-particle potential operator were extracted uniquely, and the nonrelativistic effective mass at the Fermi momentum was calculated via the Schrodinger-equivalent potential. The effective mass splitting was then fitted as a linear function of the isospin asymmetry parameter \(\alpha\) for the range \(0 \leq \alpha \leq 0.6\).

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter (\(\alpha=0\)), neutron and proton effective masses are \(0.80\) times rest mass, consistent with the empirical value \(0.8 \pm 0.1\) from isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance in \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The neutron-proton effective mass splitting at saturation density \(\rho = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) is \((M^*_{NR,n}-M^*_{NR,p})/M = 0.187\alpha\) from linear fitting (Pearson's \(r = 0.999\)).
- Using the Bonn A potential, the effective mass splitting increases from \(0.136\) at \(\rho = 0.10\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) to \(0.236\) at \(\rho = 0.24\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- At \(\rho = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), the splitting values for Bonn A, B, and C are \(0.187\), \(0.193\), and \(0.197\) respectively, with differences less than \(5\%\) of the averaged value.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by solving in the full Dirac space, which avoids ambiguities from the projection method (Gross-Boelting, 1999) and the momentum-independence approximation method (Brockmann, 1990) that neglected negative-energy states. The predicted positive splitting \((0.187\alpha)\) is consistent with constraints from nucleon-nucleus scattering analyses (Li, 2015), the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem with symmetry energy systematics (Li, 2013), and giant resonances of \(^{208}\)Pb (Xu, 2020), but disagrees with the negative splitting favored by some Skyrme functionals and covariant density functionals (Margueron, 2018). The results may help constrain isovector parameters in phenomenological density functionals.
