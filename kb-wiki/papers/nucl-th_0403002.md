# Some Recent Topics on Possible Chiral Restoration in Nuclear Medium

> Some topics are introduced on possible evidences of chiral restoration in nuclei and related ones. The topics include the $σ$-mesonic mode in nuclei, the vector mesons in a nuclear matter, deeply bound pionic and Kaonic nuclei with a discussion on the nature of $Λ(1405)$. Other related topics are briefly mentioned. An emphasis is put on that phenomena even in finite nuclei with normal and sub-normal nuclear density, as will be explored in the new project in GSI and possibly in J-PARC, are interesting for physics of finite-density QCD.

# Some Recent Topics on Possible Chiral Restoration in Nuclear Medium

## Key claim

Phenomena in finite nuclei at normal and sub-normal nuclear density (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³) may already show precursory signs of partial chiral restoration, even though complete restoration is expected only at higher densities or temperatures; the paper argues that the chiral condensate is reduced by 30–50% already at normal nuclear matter density, based on a Hellman-Feynman theorem estimate.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical and experimental evidence for partial chiral restoration in nuclear matter by examining the spectral functions of scalar (σ) and vector (ρ) mesons in the medium, using the O(4) linear σ-model and unitarized π-π scattering amplitudes constructed via the N/D method. The in-medium modification is implemented by replacing the vacuum pion decay constant fπ with a density-dependent fπ*(ρ) at the mean-field level, and the resulting pole trajectories and cross sections are computed for four chiral models (ρ model, σ model, degenerate σ-ρ model, and leading chiral Lagrangian). Experimental data from the CHAOS collaboration (π⁺π⁺ invariant mass), TAPS group (γ, π⁰π⁰ reactions), CERES collaboration (e⁺e⁻ from heavy-ion collisions), and E325 experiment at KEK (p-A collisions) are compared with the theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ is reduced by 30–50% relative to the vacuum value, based on the π-N sigma term ΣπN = 40–60 MeV.
- The σ meson pole in free space has real part Re mσ = 500–800 MeV and imaginary part Im mσ ∼ Re mσ.
- The TAPS experiment used incident photons in the energy range 400–460 MeV on targets of H, ¹²C, and Pb, observing a dramatic softening of the spectral function in the σ channel for heavier nuclei.
- The CERES experiment measured e⁺e⁻ from Pb-Au collisions at 158 GeV/A and 40 GeV/A, showing enhancement below the ρ-meson peak.
- The E325 experiment at KEK measured p-A collisions at 12 GeV, finding enhancement about 200 MeV below the ρ-ω peak for Cu targets.
- The anti-symmetrized molecular dynamics calculation by Dote et al. showed that kaonic nuclei may have densities up to 8 times normal nuclear density.

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on chiral restoration by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1985, 1995) and the finite-temperature calculations by Chiku and Hatsuda (1998), applying similar ideas to finite-density nuclear matter. The paper connects theoretical predictions of in-medium spectral changes to experimental observations from the CHAOS collaboration (π-π correlations), TAPS group (2π⁰ production), CERES collaboration (dilepton production), and the E325 experiment at KEK. The work enables future experimental searches at GSI and J-PARC for precursory chiral restoration phenomena in finite nuclei, and provides a framework for understanding dense matter relevant to neutron star interiors.
