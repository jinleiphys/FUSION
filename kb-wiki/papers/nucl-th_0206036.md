# Erratum: Neutron and proton spectra from the decay of $Λ$ hypernuclei [Phys. Rev. C55, 735 (1997)]

> An error in the computer program which evaluated nucleon spectra following the decay of $Λ$ hypernuclei has been detected. The error affected the final state interaction (FSI) of the nucleons, reducing by a factor ten the nucleon-nucleon (NN) collision probability. When correcting the error, some results and conclusions of Ref. [1] have changed substantially.

## Key claim
The original 1997 paper's nucleon spectra calculations contained a programming error that reduced the nucleon-nucleon collision probability by a factor of ten; after correction, the final state interaction (FSI) of nucleons is much more important, substantially changing the shape of spectra and the sensitivity of proton/neutron yields to the ratio \(\Gamma_n / \Gamma_p\).

## Method
The authors corrected the computer code that evaluated nucleon spectra following \(\Lambda\) hypernucleus decay, which had previously underestimated the NN collision probability by a factor of ten. They then recalculated the nucleon spectra for \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C and \(^{56}_\Lambda\)Fe, including the corrected FSI treatment, and compared the new results to the original figures and to experimental spectra.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C, removing protons with energies smaller than 30 MeV reduces \(N_p\) by a factor about three; for \(^{56}_\Lambda\)Fe, the reduction is more than four.
- In \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C, the number of emitted protons increased by 45 percent when \(\Gamma_n / \Gamma_p\) changed from 0 to 2 in the old results, but now the increase is only 33 percent.
- Low values of \(\Gamma_n / \Gamma_p\) around or below unity are preferred by the corrected spectrum of Fig. 15.

## Context
This erratum corrects the earlier work by Ramos, Vicente-Vacas, and Oset (1997) on nucleon spectra from \(\Lambda\) hypernucleus decay. The corrected results show much stronger FSI effects, making the spectra more similar for neutrons and protons and weakening the sensitivity of nucleon yields to \(\Gamma_n / \Gamma_p\). The new results may affect experimental analyses that used the original 1997 results as input, and the corrected spectra now show better agreement with experimental data for a wide range of \(\Gamma_n / \Gamma_p\) values.
