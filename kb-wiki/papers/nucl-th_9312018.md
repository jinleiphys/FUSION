# On the nature of the large-$q$ expansion of the response for a non-relativistic confined system

> We show the equivalence of a previously derived exact expression for the response of a non-relativistic system with harmonic forces and an infinite sum of weighted $δ$-functions corresponding to the spectrum. We forward arguments, indicating that the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith $1/q$-expansion of the response does not converge and prove that this expansion is an asymptotic series.

## Key claim

The Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) \(1/q\) expansion of the non-relativistic response for confined systems does not converge; the authors prove that for the harmonic oscillator this expansion is an asymptotic series, meaning a finite number of terms may still describe the response at high momentum transfer to any desired accuracy.

## Method

The authors analyze the equivalence of three exact forms of the response for a non-relativistic system with harmonic confining forces, using the two-particle harmonic oscillator as an explicit example. They demonstrate that the exact response can be expressed as an infinite sum of weighted delta-functions corresponding to the harmonic oscillator spectrum, and then examine the GRS expansion by studying the Fourier representation of the reduced response. By bounding the remainder term of the series expansion and applying the Lebesgue dominated convergence theorem, they prove that the GRS series satisfies the definition of an asymptotic expansion for the harmonic oscillator case.

## Key numbers

* For the harmonic oscillator, the exact incoherent response is given by \(S^{incoh}(q,\omega) = (m/q) e^{-(q^2/8\beta^2)} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (1/n!) (q^2/8\beta^2)^n \delta(\omega - q^2/4m - n\Omega)\), where \(\beta = (m\Omega/2)^{1/2}\) is the inverse oscillator length.
* The expansion coefficients \(d_n(q,\beta)\) in the Fourier series of \(D(q,s)\) are given by \(d_n(q,\beta) = (1/2\pi i) (q^2/8\beta^2)^n \xi_{n+1}\), with \(\xi_{n+1} = 2\pi i / n!\).
* The bound on the partial sums of the Fourier expansion is \(\left|\sum_{n=-N}^N d_n(q,\beta) e^{-2in\beta^2 s/q}\right| \leq 2 e^{(q^2/8\beta^2)}\), independent of \(N\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) expansion (Gersch, Rodriguez, and Smith, 1972), which was formally derived for large momentum transfer \(q\) as a \(1/q\) expansion of the incoherent part of the non-relativistic response. The authors address the lack of a detailed convergence analysis for systems with confining interactions, building on their own exact solution for harmonic confining forces (Pace, Salme, and Rinat, 1993). The proof that the GRS expansion is asymptotic for the harmonic oscillator provides a rigorous foundation for using a finite number of terms at high \(q\), and opens the question of whether similar asymptotic behavior holds for other confining interactions.
