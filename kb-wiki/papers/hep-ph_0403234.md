# Prethermalization

> Prethermalization of the equation of state and the kinetic temperature to their equilibrium values occurs on time scales dramatically shorter than the thermal equilibration time. This is a crucial ingredient for the understanding of collisions of heavy nuclei or other nonequilibrium phenomena in complex quantum and classical many body systems. We also compare the chemical equilibration time with other characteristic time scales.

# Prethermalization

## Key claim

Prethermalization describes the very rapid establishment of an almost constant ratio of pressure over energy density (equation of state) and a kinetic temperature on time scales dramatically shorter than the thermal equilibration time, even though the occupation numbers of individual momentum modes still show strong deviations from the late-time Bose-Einstein or Fermi-Dirac distribution. This phenomenon is based on dephasing and is independent of the interaction details, unlike the scattering-driven process of thermalization.

## Method

The authors performed a nonequilibrium quantum field theory calculation using the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action to two-loop order for a low-energy quark-meson model (linear sigma model) with two quark flavors, a Yukawa coupling to a scalar sigma-field and a triplet of pseudoscalar pions. The dynamics was solved numerically without further approximations, allowing the study of both far-from-equilibrium dynamics and the late-time approach to quantum thermal equilibrium. Two different initial conditions with the same energy density were compared: one with high initial particle number density in a narrow momentum range (reminiscent of colliding wave packets) and one closer to a thermal distribution.

## Key numbers

- Damping time for fermions: \(t_{\rm damp}^{(f)} \simeq 25\, m^{-1}\) for \(p/m\simeq 1\)
- Damping time for scalars: \(t_{\rm damp}^{(s)} \simeq 28\, m^{-1}\) for \(p/m\simeq 1\)
- Thermal equilibration time: \(t_{\rm eq} \simeq 95\, m^{-1}\), approximately the same for fermions and scalars
- Prethermalization time \(t_{\rm pt}\) is of order the characteristic inverse mass scale \(m^{-1}\)
- Product of temperature and prethermalization time: \(T\, t_{\rm pt} \simeq 2 - 2.5\) (defined by \(|w(t_{\rm pt}) - w_{\rm eq}|/w_{\rm eq} < 0.2\) for \(t > t_{\rm pt}\))
- For \(T \gtrsim 400 - 500\,\)MeV, prethermalization time is somewhat less than \(1\,\)fm
- Momentum cutoff used: \(\Lambda/m = 2.86\)

## Context

This work extends earlier observations of rapid equipartition between bulk kinetic and potential energy in classical field theories (Bonini 1999) and rapid approach to a slow evolution of the equation of state in expanding space-time geometries (Borsanyi 2003). It provides a quantitative quantum field theory demonstration that prethermalization of bulk quantities occurs on time scales much shorter than thermal equilibration, resolving the apparent contradiction between successful hydrodynamic descriptions applied less than 1 fm after heavy-ion collisions and the much longer thermal equilibration times expected from QCD theory. The results enable a framework where quasi-thermal descriptions using prethermalized quantities can be applied to far-from-equilibrium situations, and suggest that chemical freeze-out temperatures measured in heavy-ion collisions (Braun-Munzinger 2003) can be meaningfully compared to the critical temperature of QCD.
