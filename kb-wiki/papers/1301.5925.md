# Quadrupole moments of rotating neutron stars and strange stars

> We present results for models of neutron stars and strange stars constructed using the Hartle-Thorne slow-rotation method with a wide range of equations of state, focusing on the values obtained for the angular momentum $J$ and the quadrupole moment $Q$, when the gravitational mass $M$ and the rotational frequency $Ω$ are specified. Building on previous work, which showed surprising uniformity in the behaviour of the moment of inertia for neutron-star models constructed with widely-different equations of state, we find similar uniformity for the quadrupole moment. These two quantities, together with the mass, are fundamental for determining the vacuum space-time outside neutron stars. We study particularly the dimensionless combination of parameters $QM/J^2$ (using units for which $c=G=1$). This quantity goes to 1 in the case of a Kerr-metric black hole and deviations away from 1 then characterize the difference between neutron-star and black-hole space-times. It is found that $QM/J^2$ for both neutron stars and strange stars decreases with increasing mass, for a given equation of state, reaching a value of around 2 (or even less) for maximum-mass models, meaning that their external space-time is then rather well approximated by the Kerr metric. If $QM/J^2$ is plotter against compactness $R/2M$ (where $R$ is the radius), it is found that the relationship is nearly unique for neutron-star models, independent of the equation of state, while it is significantly different for strange stars. This gives a new way of possibly distinguishing between them.

# Quadrupole moments of rotating neutron stars and strange stars

## Key claim

The dimensionless combination $QM/J^2$ (the "Kerr factor") for both neutron stars and strange stars decreases with increasing mass, reaching values around 2 or even less for maximum-mass models, meaning their external spacetime is then rather well approximated by the Kerr metric. When plotted against compactness $R/2M$, the relationship is nearly unique for neutron-star models independent of the equation of state, while it is significantly different for strange stars, offering a new way of possibly distinguishing between them.

## Method

The authors constructed models of rotating neutron stars and strange stars using the Hartle-Thorne slow-rotation method, which is a perturbative expansion to second order in angular velocity $\Omega$ around a non-rotating comparison object. They used a wide range of equations of state for neutron-star matter (APR, UBS, BBB2, SLy4, BPAL12, Gandolfi, BALBN1H1, GLENDH3) and the MIT Bag Model for strange stars with two values of the bag constant. The perturbation equations were solved to obtain the mass $M$, angular momentum $J$, and quadrupole moment $Q$ for each model.

## Key numbers

- The Kerr factor $\tilde{q} = QM_0/J^2$ for neutron stars ranges from $\tilde{q} \sim 1.5$ for the most extreme objects close to maximal mass up to $\tilde{q} \sim 9$ for low mass objects.
- For compact stars with the canonical mass $M = 1.4 M_\odot$, the value of $\tilde{q}$ ranges between $\tilde{q} \simeq 2$ and $\tilde{q} \simeq 7-8$, depending on the equation of state.
- The angular momentum parameter $j = J/M_0^2$ for objects rotating at 300 Hz is plotted as a function of compactness; for the maximum observed rotation speed of 716 Hz, $j$ would lie in the interval 0.2-0.5.
- For neutron stars with masses greater than about $1.25 M_\odot$, the compactness parameter $x = R_0/2M_0$ is less than about 4; for strange stars it is less than about 3.
- The analytic fit for the universal relation of $\tilde{q}$ versus compactness $x$ uses parameters $a_1 = 3.64$ and $a_0 = -5.3$ for the linear branch ($x > x_0$), with the matching point $x_0$ determined by continuity.
- The moment of inertia factor $I/M_0R_0^2$ for strange stars at large $x$ tends toward the classical value $2/5$ for uniform density spheres.

## Context

This work extends previous findings by Lattimer and Prakash (2001) and Bejger and Haensel (2002), who showed that the moment of inertia factor $I/MR^2$ follows an almost unique relation in terms of $R/2M$ for most neutron-star equations of state, while strange stars show different behavior. The authors demonstrate that the same uniformity holds for the quadrupole moment, quantified by the Kerr factor $\tilde{q} = QM/J^2$, which characterizes deviations from the Kerr black hole metric (where $\tilde{q} = 1$). This result enables a new observational discriminant between neutron stars and strange stars if both $\tilde{q}$ and compactness can be constrained independently. The quadrupole moment also plays a key role in determining the position of the innermost stable circular orbit (ISCO), affecting models of quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) as discussed by Urbanec et al. (2010).
