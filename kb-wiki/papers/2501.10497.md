# Constituent Quark-model for BaryonsTh. Harmonic confinement and Two-body Meson-exchange Potentials

> Soft Two-body potentials between the constituent quarks of the nucleon are derived using harmonic oscillator, i.e. gaussian, quark wave-functions. The gaussian wave-functions are very suited for applications with the ESC soft-core interactions, which employ gaussian form factors. In these notes using the Fourier transformation to momentum space the local and non-local contributions of the potentials based on the ESC meson-quark-quark vertices are evaluated. Using the ESC16 parameters translated to the quark-level leads to parameter free two-body and three-body diquark and triquark meson-exchange interactions. Applications to the SU(3) baryon-octet states and the $Δ_{33}$ resonance are performed., within the CQM using a harmonic confinement potential, leading to a satisfactory picture with relativistic constituent quarks. We present two versioins for the $N-Δ$ splitting: (i) model A with the instanton interaction, and (ii) model B with a large color-magnetic interaction from an almost point like OGE. The size of the baryons $\approx$ 1 fm.

## Key claim

Using the ESC16 meson-exchange parameters translated to the quark level, the constituent quark model (CQM) with harmonic confinement yields a satisfactory description of the SU(3) baryon-octet and the Δ33 resonance, but the contributions from one-boson-exchange (OBE) and one-gluon-exchange (OGE) potentials are marginal, with large cancellations between the confinement potential and the relativistic kinetic energies of the quarks.

## Method

The authors evaluate matrix elements for the proton and neutron of one-boson-exchange (OBE) quark-quark potentials using the Dalitz-Downs (D&D) model for the three-quark wave function, which is a Gaussian in the Jacobi coordinates ρ and λ. The masses for the SU(3) octet baryons (P, Λ, Σ, Ξ) and the Δ33(1236) resonance are computed within the CQM using a harmonic confinement potential of the form Vconf = -C0 + C2 r², including OBE and OGE potentials in Born approximation. Two versions for the N-Δ splitting are presented: model A with the instanton interaction, and model B with a large color-magnetic interaction from an almost point-like OGE.

## Key numbers

- Baryon size is approximately 1 fm.
- The instanton density is n_c = 8 × 10⁻⁴ GeV⁻⁴ and the instanton radius is ρ_c ≈ 0.3 fm.
- The calculated effective low-momentum quark mass in the instanton vacuum is m_Q(p=0) = 345 MeV, and the gluon mass is m_G(p=0) = 420 MeV.
- The non-perturbative vacuum expectation value for the quark fields is ⟨vac|ψ̄ψ|vac⟩ ≈ -10⁻² GeV³.
- The Δ33(1236) resonance mass is 1236 MeV, and the sum of the constituent quark masses is approximately M_p, which is about 300 MeV less than the Δ33 mass.

## Context

This work extends the Extended-Soft-Core (ESC) model approach to the quark level, building on the constituent quark model (CQM) connection to the instanton-anti-instanton liquid structure of the QCD vacuum as described by Diakonov and Petrov (1984). The formalism generalizes the Dalitz-Downs (1958, 1964) three-quark wave function approach and is designed to be compatible with the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) of Hiyama and Kamimura (2003). The results enable future applications to quark-matter studies and provide a framework for parameter-free two-body and three-body diquark and triquark meson-exchange interactions.
