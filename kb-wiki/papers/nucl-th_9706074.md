# Possible solution to basic problems regarding the coupling constants $G_V$ and $G_A$

> The existing experimental evidence on the vector and axial-vector coupling constants, $G_V$ and $G_A$, for neutron $β$-decay exhibits two prominent problems: (i) the unitarity for the first row of the CKM matrix seems violated; (ii) one obtains different values of $G_A/G_V$ according to whether one uses as input the neutron lifetime or neutron-decay correlation observables. We show that the in-medium modification of $G_A$ can influence both the inner and outer radiative corrections used in deducing $G_V$ from the super-allowed Fermi transitions and that this effect may resolve the long-standing problems (i) and (ii) simultaneously.

# Possible solution to basic problems regarding the coupling constants $G_V$ and $G_A$

## Key claim
The in-medium modification of the axial-vector coupling constant $G_A$ in nuclear matter can affect both the inner and outer radiative corrections used to extract $G_V$ from super-allowed Fermi transitions, potentially resolving simultaneously the apparent CKM unitarity violation and the discrepancy between $\lambda = G_A/G_V$ values extracted from neutron lifetime versus neutron-decay correlation measurements. The authors emphasize this is an exploratory schematic estimate, not a definitive calculation.

## Method
The authors calculate the $\lambda$-dependent part of the $O(\alpha)$ radiative corrections to $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ nuclear beta decays using a nucleonic weak current approach rather than the standard quark-current description, employing two simplified models: a single common cut-off parameter $\Lambda_0$ and a common form factor $\Lambda^2/(q^2+\Lambda^2)$ at the weak and electromagnetic vertices. They introduce an in-medium renormalization parameter $\rho = \lambda^*/\lambda$ (fixed at 0.8) to represent the nuclear medium effect on the axial coupling, and compute the resulting change $\Delta$ in the radiative correction between free-nucleon and in-medium cases.

## Key numbers
- $V_{ud} = 0.9740 \pm 0.0005$ (existing empirical value from super-allowed Fermi transitions)
- $|V_{ud}|^2 + |V_{us}|^2 + |V_{ub}|^2 = 0.9972 \pm 0.0013$ (existing CKM unitarity sum, indicating violation at 98% confidence level)
- $\lambda_A = 1.2601 \pm 0.0025$ (from neutron-decay angular distribution experiments)
- $\lambda_\tau = 1.2681 \pm 0.0033$ (from neutron lifetime measurements using $\tau_n = 888 \pm 3$ sec)
- $\Delta = 0.0044 \pm 0.0029$ (computed change in radiative correction due to in-medium modification, using $J = 15 \pm 10$ and $\rho = 0.8$)
- Corrected $V_{ud} = 0.9761 \pm 0.0015$ (after applying in-medium correction)
- Corrected unitarity sum $|V_{ud}|^2 + |V_{us}|^2 + |V_{ub}|^2 = 1.0013 \pm 0.0031$ (consistent with unitarity)
- Corrected $\lambda_\tau = 1.2655 \pm 0.0034$ (after applying in-medium correction, now consistent with $\lambda_A$)

## Context
This work extends the analysis of radiative corrections to super-allowed Fermi transitions by Marciano and Sirlin (1986), who established the standard framework using quark currents. It builds on earlier studies of two-nucleon contributions to the long-distance correction term $C$ by Jaus and Rasche (1990), Towner (1992), and Barker et al. (1992), and on Towner's (1994) estimate of in-medium $\lambda$ effects limited to the $C$ term. The authors propose that including the in-medium modification of $G_A$ in the entire radiative correction (not just the $C$ term) can simultaneously resolve the CKM unitarity problem and the neutron lifetime versus correlation observable discrepancy, though they stress their schematic treatment requires refinement.
