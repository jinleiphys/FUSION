# Bound H-dibaryon in Flavor SU(3) Limit of Lattice QCD

> The flavor-singlet H-dibaryon, which has strangeness -2 and baryon number 2, is studied by the approach recently developed for the baryon-baryon interactions in lattice QCD. The flavor-singlet central potential is derived from the spatial and imaginary-time dependence of the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function measured in N_f=3 full QCD simulations with the lattice size of L = 2, 3, 4 fm. The potential is found to be insensitive to the volume, and it leads to a bound H-dibaryon with the binding energy of 30 - 40 MeV for the pseudo-scalar meson mass of 673 - 1015 MeV.

## Key claim

The flavor-singlet H-dibaryon is bound in the flavor SU(3) limit of lattice QCD with a binding energy of 30–40 MeV, but only for the range of pseudo-scalar meson masses from 673 to 1015 MeV studied; the authors note that the binding energy has weak quark mass dependence and may appear as a weakly bound state or resonant state near the Lambda-Lambda threshold under realistic SU(3) breaking.

## Method

The HAL QCD Collaboration extracted the flavor-singlet central potential from the spatial and imaginary-time dependence of the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function measured in N_f=3 full QCD simulations on lattices with spatial sizes L approximately 2, 3, and 4 fm. They used the renormalization group improved Iwasaki gauge action and non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson quark action, with quark propagators calculated from spatial wall sources. The potential was fitted to an analytic function composed of an attractive Gaussian core plus a long-range (Yukawa)^2 attraction, and the Schrodinger equation was then solved in infinite volume to obtain binding energies and wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the H-dibaryon at m_ps = 1015 MeV: 32.9 (4.5)(6.6) MeV, with rms distance 0.823(33)(40) fm
- Binding energy at m_ps = 837 MeV: 37.4 (4.4)(7.3) MeV, with rms distance 0.855(29)(61) fm
- Binding energy at m_ps = 673 MeV: 35.6 (7.4)(4.0) MeV, with rms distance 1.011(63)(68) fm
- Lattice spacing a = 0.121(2) fm for the 32^3 x 32 lattice
- Octet baryon masses: 2030(2) MeV at m_ps = 1015 MeV, 1748(1) MeV at m_ps = 837 MeV, 1485(2) MeV at m_ps = 673 MeV
- Non-interacting two-baryon energy splitting W_1 - W_0 approximately 50 MeV for L = 4 fm and baryon mass approximately 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method for extracting baryon-baryon potentials from lattice QCD (Ishii 2007, Inoue 2010) to the flavor-singlet H-dibaryon channel, where previous lattice calculations faced difficulties with finite volume effects and excited state contamination. The approach avoids the need for large imaginary-time separations required in conventional Luscher-type analyses, and the potential method shows negligible volume dependence. The results enable future (2+1)-flavor simulations with coupled-channel analysis of Lambda-Lambda, N-Xi, and Sigma-Sigma systems to determine whether the H-dibaryon is a bound state or resonance at physical quark masses.
