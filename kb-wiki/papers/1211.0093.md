# Investigation of target in C-ADS and IAEA ADS benchmark

> The spatial and energy distribution of spallation neutrons have an effect on the performance of Accelerator Driven Subcritical systems. In this work, the spatial, energy distribution of spallation neutrons and the effect of these factors on proton efficiency was studied. When the radius of spallation region increases, backward neutrons were found to have a rather big ratio and have a positive effect on proton efficiency. By making better use of these neutrons, we may increase the radius of target to satisfy some other requirements in the design of subcritical core.

## Key claim
The spatial and energy distribution of spallation neutrons, particularly backward neutrons, can have a positive effect on proton efficiency when the radius of the spallation region increases, potentially allowing larger target radii without severely degrading system performance. The authors hedge that while this result appears to contradict prior work (Seltborg) which found that larger target radius decreases proton efficiency, the trade-off between softer radial neutrons and increased survival neutrons makes it difficult to definitively conclude that larger radii are beneficial.

## Method
The authors used the FLUKA Monte Carlo code to simulate spallation neutron production, spatial distribution, and energy distribution for targets in the China ADS (C-ADS) design and the IAEA ADS benchmark. Neutron multiplicity, neutron current surface density, and the partitioning of neutrons into forward, radial, and backward components were recorded. Proton efficiency was then computed using the RMC (Reactor Monte Carlo code) by transporting the recorded source neutrons through the subcritical core geometry.

## Key numbers
- For the IAEA ADS benchmark with 1.0 GeV protons, the effective multiplication factor is \(k_{\rm{eff}} = 0.96455 \pm 0.00062\).
- As the spallation region radius \(R_{\rm{s}}\) increases from 12 cm to 30 cm, the number of radial neutrons changes slightly (from 20.665 to 20.199 per source proton), while backward neutrons increase from 3.729 to 10.767 per source proton.
- The ratio of radial neutrons with kinetic energy above 1 MeV decreases from 59.62% at \(R_{\rm{s}} = 12\) cm to 31.04% at \(R_{\rm{s}} = 30\) cm.
- Proton efficiency \(\psi^{*}\) increases from 26.803 at \(R_{\rm{s}} = 12\) cm to 28.002 at \(R_{\rm{s}} = 30\) cm.
- For the C-ADS target (1.5 GeV protons, \(R_{\rm{s}} = 25.0\) cm, \(L_{\rm{s}} = 100.0\) cm), the relative variation in average kinetic energy of radial neutrons with position is \((E_{\rm{rmax}} - E_{\rm{rmin}})/E_{\rm{raverage}} \approx 0.141\).

## Context
This work extends studies of spallation target design for Accelerator Driven Subcritical (ADS) systems, specifically the China ADS (C-ADS) project and the IAEA ADS benchmark (Slessarev). It directly addresses the prior conclusion by Seltborg that proton efficiency decreases when target radius increases due to neutron softening and leakage. The authors show that backward neutrons (survival neutrons) can compensate for this effect, enabling larger target radii to meet other design requirements such as reducing structural damage from high-energy spallation neutrons. The work enables future optimization of target position using the spatial distribution of radial neutrons and suggests design modifications such as annular targets or replacing moderator with fuel to better utilize backward neutrons.
