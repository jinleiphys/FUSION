# Field theoretic study of light hypernuclei

> A nonperturbative field theoretic calculation has been made for the $s$-shell hypernuclei. Here we dress the $Λ$- and $Σ$- hypernuclei with off-mass shell pion pairs. The analysis replaces the scalar isoscalar potential by quantum coherent states. The binding energies of $^{4+nΛ}$He $(n=0,1,2)$ agree quite well with the RMF result of Greiner. The experimental binding energies of $^4$He and $^3$He are reasonably well reproduced in our calculation. A satisfactory description of the relevant experimental $Λ$- and $Σ$- separation energies has been obtained.

## Key claim

A nonperturbative field-theoretic calculation that dresses s-shell hypernuclei with off-mass-shell pion pairs (replacing the scalar isoscalar sigma meson by quantum coherent states) reproduces binding energies of $^{4+n\Lambda}$He reasonably well compared to the RMF results of Greiner, but the double hyperon separation energy for $^{4+2\Lambda}$He is underestimated by a factor of about three, suggesting additional hyperon-hyperon interactions are needed.

## Method

The authors construct a translationally invariant state for the hypernucleus containing nucleons, hyperons, and a coherent state of off-mass-shell pion pairs. They calculate the energy expectation value from an effective Hamiltonian that includes kinetic terms, pion-baryon interaction terms (with $\pi NN$ and $\pi\Lambda\Lambda$ couplings), baryon repulsion from omega exchange, Coulomb repulsion, and a phenomenological meson repulsion term. The variational parameters (Gaussian widths for nucleon, hyperon, and pion distributions, plus the pion-pair amplitude) are determined by minimizing the total energy for each nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $^4$He: -28.96 MeV (agrees with earlier calculation and experiment)
- Binding energy of $^3$He: -7.09 MeV (agrees well with experiment)
- Binding energy of $^{4+\Lambda}$He: -31.42 MeV
- Binding energy of $^{4+2\Lambda}$He: -32.42 MeV
- Binding energy of $^{4+\Sigma}$He: -29.92 MeV
- Binding energy of $^{4+2\Sigma}$He: -29.84 MeV
- Binding energy of $^{3+\Lambda}$He: -12.30 MeV
- Binding energy of $^{3+2\Lambda}$He: -15.8 MeV
- Binding energy of $^{3+\Sigma}$He: -10.61 MeV
- Binding energy of $^{3+2\Sigma}$He: -12.85 MeV
- $\Lambda$ separation energy for $^{4+\Lambda}$He: 2.46 MeV (experiment: 3.12 MeV)
- $\Lambda$ separation energy for $^{3+\Lambda}$He: 5.21 MeV (experiment: 2.39 MeV)
- $\Sigma$ separation energy for $^{3+\Sigma}$He: 3.52 MeV (experiment: 3.2 MeV)
- Double $\Lambda$ separation energy for $^{4+2\Lambda}$He: 3.46 MeV (experiment: 10.6 MeV)
- Variational parameter $R_\Lambda$ for $^{4+\Lambda}$He: 2.258 fm; for $^{4+2\Lambda}$He: 2.426 fm
- Variational parameter $R_\Sigma$ for $^{4+\Sigma}$He: 2.605 fm; for $^{4+2\Sigma}$He: 2.87 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier nonperturbative field-theoretic approach (Panda, Misra, and Sahu, 1992; Panda et al., 1996) from ordinary light nuclei (deuteron, $^4$He) and nuclear matter to s-shell hypernuclei. It directly compares with the relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations of Greiner (1996) for multi-lambda hypernuclei, finding good agreement for binding energies of $^{4+n\Lambda}$He. The model fails to reproduce the experimental double hyperon separation energy for $^{4+2\Lambda}$He (3.46 MeV vs. 10.6 MeV), which Greiner attributed to the need for additional hyperon-hyperon interactions from strange meson exchange. The calculation predicts the formation of $\Lambda$- and $\Sigma$-halos around the nucleus, which could be observable in peripheral collisions.
