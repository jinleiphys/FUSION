# Charged current cross section for massive cosmological neutrinos impinging on radioactive nuclei

> We discuss the cross section formula both for massless and massive neutrinos on stable and radioactive nuclei. The latter could be of interest for the detection of cosmological neutrinos whose observation is one of the main challenges of modern cosmology. We analyze the signal to background ratio as a function of the ratio of the neutrino mass over the detector resolution and show that an energy resolution less than 0.5 eV would be required for sub-eV neutrino masses, independently of the gravitational neutrino clustering. Finally we mention the non-resonant character of neutrino capture on radioactive nuclei.

## Key claim

The charged-current cross section for nonrelativistic neutrinos scales as 1/v_nu, causing the capture rate on radioactive nuclei to converge to a finite value as v_nu approaches zero; however, detecting cosmological neutrinos requires an energy resolution below 0.5 eV for sub-eV neutrino masses, and this requirement is independent of gravitational neutrino clustering.

## Method

The authors derived the charged-current cross section formula for massive neutrinos by modifying the standard massless expression, replacing (s - M_p^2)^2 with 4 M_p^2 p_nu^2 and the Jacobian with 2 p_nu p_e, yielding a 1/v_nu dependence. They applied this to neutrino capture on tritium, using the ft value of the inverse beta decay to eliminate nuclear matrix elements, and computed the signal-to-background ratio as a function of m_nu/Delta, where Delta is the detector energy resolution. They also examined whether resonance enhancement could occur for neutrino capture on radioactive nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Cross section for T=1.9 K nonrelativistic neutrinos on tritium: sigma = 1.5 x 10^{-41} (m_nu/eV) cm^2, or sigma v_nu/c = 7.6 x 10^{-45} cm^2
- Capture rate per tritium atom: R = 10^{-32} x (n_nu / <n_nu>) s^{-1}
- Expected event rate: N_nu_capt = 6.5 x (n_nu / <n_nu>) year^{-1} Mcu^{-1} for a Megacurie tritium source
- Signal-to-background ratio for m_nu < Delta: lambda_nu / lambda_beta = 2.5 x 10^{-11} x (n_nu / <n_nu>) x 1/(Delta (eV))^3
- For m_nu >= Delta, signal-to-background ratio of order unity requires m_nu/Delta ~ 2, implying Delta <= 0.5 eV for sub-eV neutrino masses
- Average cosmological neutrino density: <n_nu> = 55 cm^{-3} per flavor at T_nu = 1.9 K
- Gravitational clustering enhancement: n_nu / <n_nu> ~ 10^3 - 10^4 for m_nu = 1 eV in galaxy clusters

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Weinberg (1962) and the recent proposal by Cocco et al. (2007) for detecting cosmological neutrinos via capture on beta-decaying nuclei, providing a corrected cross section formula with the proper 1/v_nu dependence. The authors show that the signal-to-background ratio depends critically on m_nu/Delta and is essentially independent of gravitational clustering, contradicting earlier optimistic assumptions. The paper also clarifies that neutrino capture on radioactive nuclei is non-resonant, addressing speculation by Raghavan (2007) about possible resonance enhancement, and enables future experimental design by quantifying the extreme energy resolution requirements for relic neutrino detection.
