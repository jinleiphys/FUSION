# Is $Z_{cs}(3982)$ a molecular partner of $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ states?

> We perform an effective-field-theory-based coupled-channel analysis of the recent BES III data on the $e^+e^-$ annihilation into the final state $K^+(D_s^-D^{*0}+D_s^{*-}D^0)$ in a wide energy range and extract the poles responsible for the formation of the $Z_{cs}(3982)$. We identify two scenarios which provide a similar description of the experimental mass distributions but result in utterly different predictions for the spin partners of the $Z_{cs}(3982)$: although both scenarios are consistent with the $Z_{cs}$ as a $SU(3)$ partner of the $Z_c(3900)$, the $Z_c(4020)$ appears naturally as a spin partner of these states only in one of them (fit 1) while in the other (fit 2) its nature has to be different. Also, the $Z_{cs}(3982)$ has a $J^{P}=1^+$ spin partner near the $\bar D_s^* D^*$ threshold in fit 1, while no such state exists in fit 2. We predict the $\bar{D}_s^*D^*$ invariant mass distribution in the $J^{P}=1^+$ channel for the reaction $e^+e^-\to K^+D_s^{*-}D^{*0}$ and argue that this line shape can be used to distinguish between the two scenarios once data in this channel are available.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the BES III data on the $Z_{cs}(3982)$ can be described equally well by two different dynamical scenarios (fit 1 and fit 2), but these scenarios make starkly different predictions for the spin partners of the $Z_{cs}(3982)$, only one scenario (fit 1) naturally accommodates the $Z_c(4020)$ as a spin partner, while the other (fit 2) requires a different nature for that state.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel analysis using an effective field theory framework with leading-order contact interactions derived from heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) and light-quark $SU(3)$ flavour symmetry. They solved coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the $J^P=1^+$ system including the three elastic channels $\{D_s^-D^{*0}, D_s^{*-}D^0, D_s^{*-}D^{*0}\}$, and incorporated production amplitudes from tree-level diagrams via $D_{s2}(2573)$ and $D_{s1}(2536)$ intermediate states, triangle loop diagrams, and point-like production mechanisms, fitting the resulting line shapes to the BES III invariant mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- The $Z_{cs}(3982)$ mass from the BES III Breit-Wigner fit is $(3982.5^{+1.8}_{-2.6}\pm 2.1)$ MeV and width is $(12.8_{-4.4}^{+5.3}\pm 3.0)$ MeV.
- The BES III data sample had an integrated luminosity of $3.7$ fb$^{-1}$ in the energy range from $4.628$ to $4.698$ GeV.
- The spin-averaged $D$ meson mass is $\bar{M}=(3m_*+m)/4$ with $\delta=m_*-m\approx 140$ MeV.
- The ultraviolet cutoff was chosen as $\Lambda=1$ GeV and varied from 0.8 to 1.3 GeV to verify cutoff independence.
- The $D_{s1}$ decay branching fractions used were $\text{Br}(D_{s1}^+\to D^*K^+)_{S\text{-wave}}=0.61$ and $\text{Br}(D_{s1}^+\to D^*K^+)_{D\text{-wave}}=0.39$, giving $\lambda\approx 53$ MeV.
- The $D_{s2}(2573)\bar{D}_s^*$ and $D_{s1}(2536)\bar{D}_s^*$ thresholds are at 4.681 GeV and 4.647 GeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Yang et al. (2020) by incorporating the full energy range of the BES III data, including additional coupled channels ($\bar{D}_s^* D^{*0}$) and triangle singularities from $D_{s2}D_s^*D^{*0}$ and $D_{s1}D_s^*D^{*0}$ loops that were previously neglected. The results enable future experimental discrimination between the two scenarios through measurement of the $\bar{D}_s^*D^*$ invariant mass distribution in $e^+e^-\to K^+ D_s^{*-} D^{*0}$, and provide a framework for understanding the relationship between the $Z_{cs}(3982)$ observed by BES III and the $Z_{cs}(4000)$ recently observed by LHCb.
