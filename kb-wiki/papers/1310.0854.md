# Skyrme functional from a three-body pseudo-potential of second-order in gradients. Formalism for central terms

> In one way or the other, all modern parametrizations of the nuclear energy density functional (EDF) do not respect the exchange symmetry associated with Pauli's principle. It has been recently shown that this practice jeopardizes multi-reference (MR) EDF calculations by contaminating the energy with spurious self-interactions that, for example, lead to finite steps or even divergences when plotting it as a function of collective coordinates. As of today, the only viable option to bypass these pathologies is to rely on EDF kernels that enforce Pauli's principle from the outset by strictly and exactly deriving from a genuine, i.e. density-independent, Hamilton operator. We wish to develop the most general Skyrme-like EDF parametrization containing linear, bilinear and trilinear terms in the density matrices with up to two gradients, under the key constraint that it derives strictly from an effective Hamilton operator. The most general three-body Skyrme-like pseudo-potential containing up to two gradient operators is constructed to generate the trilinear part. The present study is limited to central terms. Spin-orbit and tensor will be addressed in a forthcoming paper. (See paper for full abstract)

## Key claim

The paper constructs the most general central three-body Skyrme-like pseudo-potential containing up to two gradient operators, under the strict constraint that the resulting energy density functional (EDF) kernel derives exactly from a genuine, density-independent Hamilton operator, thereby enforcing Pauli's principle from the outset and avoiding the spurious self-interactions that plague multi-reference EDF calculations.

## Method

The authors systematically write down all possible central three-body contact operator structures consistent with the symmetries of the nuclear Hamiltonian, then derive the corresponding EDF kernel and perform a singular-value decomposition of the matrix expressing the coupling constants to determine the number of independent parameters. A formal algebra code was developed to carry out these tasks, deriving the complete trilinear EDF kernel (including time-odd and T=1 pairing parts), the corresponding normal and anomalous one-body fields entering the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations, and expressions for bulk properties of homogeneous nuclear matter and associated Landau parameters.

## Key numbers

* The central three-body pseudo-potential is defined out of six independent parameters.
* The three-body interaction is built up to second order in derivatives (gradient operators).
* The study is limited to central terms; spin-orbit and tensor terms are deferred to a forthcoming paper.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts by Sadoudi et al. (2013) to construct a Skyrme pseudo-potential without density dependence using gradient-less three- and four-body contact terms, which proved unable to simultaneously reproduce empirical nuclear matter properties, attractive pairing, and stability. It supersedes previous unsystematic studies of three-body Skyrme-type interactions by Blaizot (1975), Liu (1975), Onishi (1978), Arima (1986), Zheng (1990), Waroquier (1976, 1979, 1983), and Liu (1991) by providing the most general central three-body pseudo-potential with complete EDF kernel derivation suitable for symmetry-unrestricted single-reference and multi-reference calculations. The work establishes a first step toward a new generation of nuclear EDFs that respect Pauli's principle and can be safely used in predictive, spuriousity-free symmetry restoration and configuration mixing calculations.
