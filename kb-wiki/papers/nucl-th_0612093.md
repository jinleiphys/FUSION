# Momentum conservation and correlation analyses in heavy-ion collisions at ultrarelativistic energies

> Global transverse-momentum conservation induces correlations between any number of particles, which contribute in particular to the two- and three-particle correlations measured in heavy-ion collisions. These correlations are examined in detail, and their importance for studies of jets and their interaction with the medium is discussed.

## Key claim

Global transverse-momentum conservation induces correlations between any number of particles that contribute to two- and three-particle correlations measured in heavy-ion collisions, and these correlations are sizable enough that they must be accounted for in experimental studies of jets and their interaction with the medium, particularly because the assumption that jet and background are uncorrelated is invalidated by momentum conservation.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for the two- and three-particle cumulants arising from global transverse-momentum conservation using a generating function of multiparticle probability distributions and a saddle-point calculation to leading order in 1/N, where N is the total number of emitted particles. The resulting cumulants are then analyzed for specific kinematic configurations relevant to RHIC heavy-ion collisions, with the trigger particle at high transverse momentum and associated particles at lower transverse momenta, to map out the angular dependence of the correlations.

## Key numbers

- The M-particle cumulant from global transverse-momentum conservation scales like 1/N^(M-1), where N is the total number of emitted particles.
- For a central Au-Au collision at RHIC with N=8000 particles per event and rms transverse momentum ⟨p_T^2⟩^(1/2)=0.45 GeV/c, the three-particle cumulant from transverse-momentum conservation is of order 10^(-5) for trigger and associated particle transverse momenta of 3 and 1 GeV/c respectively.
- The minimum transverse momentum for associated particles to produce a maximum at Δφ_12=0 in the symmetric three-particle cumulant is p_T_min ≃ ⟨p_T^2⟩/(2 p_T_1), which for high-p_T studies is always satisfied since p_T_1 ≫ ⟨p_T^2⟩^(1/2).
- When the associated particle transverse momentum p_T_2 = p_T_3 equals the trigger transverse momentum p_T_1, the minimum in the three-particle cumulant along the symmetric configuration occurs at Δφ_12 = arccos(1/3 + ⟨p_T^2⟩/6 p_T_1^2) ≃ 1.23 rad (70.5°).
- The threshold for a second local maximum to appear in the three-particle cumulant is p'_T_min ≃ p_T_1/5 when the trigger transverse momentum is large.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies showing that momentum conservation biases anisotropic flow measurements at SPS energies (Borghini 2000, Borghini 2002) and addresses the role of momentum conservation in femtoscopy analyses (Chajecki 2006). The paper provides the analytic framework needed to correct three-particle correlation analyses developed by Pruneau (2006) and others (Ajitanand 2005, Ulery 2006) for the unavoidable contribution of global transverse-momentum conservation, which produces correlations of the same magnitude as anisotropic collective flow in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC. The results enable experimental studies to properly account for momentum conservation effects before searching for jet-medium interaction signatures such as Mach cones or Cherenkov-like radiation.
