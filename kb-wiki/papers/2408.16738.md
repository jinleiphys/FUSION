# Bounds on the minimum sound speed above neutron star densities

> We show that the existence of massive neutron stars and asymptotic freedom of QCD place robust upper bounds on the lowest sound speed of the ultra-dense matter unattainable in neutron stars. Centered on worst-case scenarios, our limits are the most conservative among physical equations of state in the density range $\sim 2-40 n_0$. Discovery of $\gtrsim 2.6 M_\odot$ neutron stars, in combination with current multimessenger astrophysical constraints on the equation of state, would strongly support first-order phase transitions at high baryon densities.

# Bounds on the minimum sound speed above neutron star densities

## Key claim
The existence of massive neutron stars combined with the asymptotic freedom of QCD places robust upper bounds on the lowest possible sound speed in ultra-dense matter at densities beyond those reached inside neutron stars. These bounds are the most conservative among all physical equations of state in the density range approximately 2 to 40 times nuclear saturation density, and discovery of neutron stars with masses at or above 2.6 solar masses would strongly support first-order phase transitions at high baryon densities.

## Method
The author applies the mean value theorem to the relation between baryon density and chemical potential, connecting low-density constraints from chiral effective field theory at 2 times nuclear saturation density to high-density perturbative QCD predictions at a chemical potential of 2.4 GeV. A constrained optimization procedure is then performed over all possible neutron star inner-core equations of state, using the maximally stiff and maximally soft constructions to derive upper limits on the minimum sound speed from both the mean value bound and a pressure-based bound relating the central pressure of the most massive neutron star to the perturbative QCD pressure.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The mean value of sound speed squared between chiral EFT at 2n0 and pQCD at mu_B = 2.4 GeV is 0.308_{-0.011}^{+0.014} (for renormalization scale factor X=1)
- For the APR* neutron star model, M_TOV = 2.2 solar masses, mu_TOV = 2.2 GeV, n_TOV = 7.1 n0, yielding a mean value bound on minimum sound speed squared of 0.070^{+0.002}_{-0.003}
- The secondary component of GW190814 has mass 2.59^{+0.08}_{-0.09} solar masses
- With n_c = 3n0 and assuming the GW190814 secondary is a neutron star, the minimum sound speed squared cannot exceed 0.2

## Context
This work extends the model-independent framework of Zhou (2023) to derive bounds on the minimum sound speed at densities beyond those realized in neutron stars, complementing previous studies by Bedaque (2014), Tews (2018), and Drischler (2020, 2021) that showed the maximum sound speed likely exceeds 1/3 inside massive neutron stars. The results enable a new observational pathway to study first-order phase transitions and non-perturbative phenomena in dense quark matter, particularly in light of ongoing and forthcoming gravitational wave observations from KAGRA and the broader gravitational wave network.
