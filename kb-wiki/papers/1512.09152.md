# Indications for a critical point in the phase diagram for hot and dense nuclear matter

> Two-pion interferometry measurements are studied for a broad range of collision centralities in Au+Au (Root_s = 7.7 - 200 GeV) and Pb+Pb (Root_s = 2.76 TeV) collisions. They indicate non-monotonic excitation functions for the Gaussian emission source radii difference [(R_out)^2 - (R_side)^2], suggestive of reaction trajectories which spend a fair amount of time near a "soft point" in the equation of state (EOS) that coincides with the critical end point (CEP). A Finite-Size Scaling (FSS) analysis of these excitation functions, provides further validation tests for the CEP. It also indicates a second order phase transition at the CEP, and the values T^{cep} ~ 165 MeV and mu_B^{cep} ~ 95 MeV for its location in the (T, mu_B)-plane of the phase diagram. The static critical exponents (nu ~ 0.66 and gamma ~ 1.2) extracted via the same FSS analysis, place this CEP in the 3D Ising model universality class. A Dynamic Finite-Size Scaling analysis of the excitation functions, gives the estimate z ~ 0.87 for the dynamic critical exponent, suggesting that the associated critical expansion dynamics is dominated by the hydrodynamic sound mode.

## Key claim

Non-monotonic excitation functions for the Gaussian emission source radii difference (R_out^2 - R_side^2) in Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions, analyzed via Finite-Size Scaling, indicate a second-order phase transition at a critical end point (CEP) located at T^cep ~ 165 MeV and mu_B^cep ~ 95 MeV, with the caveat that these values are extracted from a parametrization for chemical freeze-out rather than directly measured.

## Method

Two-pion interferometry (HBT) measurements were performed for multiple centrality selections in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 - 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. The Gaussian emission source radii difference (R_out^2 - R_side^2) was extracted as a function of collision energy and centrality, and its peak positions, widths, and magnitudes were analyzed using Finite-Size Scaling (FSS) with the transverse size estimate R_bar from Monte Carlo Glauber model calculations as the system size parameter. A Dynamic Finite-Size Scaling analysis was also performed using R_long as a proxy for the emission duration tau.

## Key numbers

- CEP location: T^cep ~ 165 MeV and mu_B^cep ~ 95 MeV in the (T, mu_B)-plane
- Static critical exponent nu = 0.67 +/- 0.05 from FSS analysis of peak positions
- Static critical exponent gamma = 1.15 +/- 0.065 from FSS analysis of excitation function magnitudes
- Infinite volume sqrt(s_NN)(infinity) = 47.5 +/- 1.5 GeV from FSS fit
- Dynamic critical exponent z ~ 0.87 from Dynamic Finite-Size Scaling analysis
- Energy range studied: Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 - 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical predictions that the CEP belongs to the 3D Ising model static universality class (Stephanov, 1998) and addresses the broad range of predicted CEP locations in the (T, mu_B)-plane. The study provides experimental validation using HBT radii as a proxy for the compressibility susceptibility, following the approach of Lacey (2014). The extracted dynamic critical exponent z ~ 0.87 is incompatible with the commonly assumed Model H dynamic universality class (z ~ 3) and instead suggests critical dynamics dominated by the hydrodynamic sound mode, consistent with predictions by Minami (2011) for a sound mode with z_s ~ -0.8. This enables future experimental searches for the CEP to account for critical speeding-up rather than critical slowing-down in the sound mode.
