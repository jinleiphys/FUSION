# Neutrino spectra from stellar electron capture

> Using the recent shell model evaluation of stellar weak interaction rates we have calculated the neutrino spectra arising from electron capture on pf-shell nuclei under presupernova conditions. We present a simple parametrization of the spectra which allows for an easy implementation into collapse simulations. We discuss that the explicit consideration of thermal ensembles in the parent nucleus broadens the neutrino spectra and results in larger average neutrino energies. The capture rates and neutrino spectra can be easily modified to account for phase space blocking by neutrinos which becomes increasingly important during the final stellar collapse.

## Key claim

The neutrino spectra from stellar electron capture on pf-shell nuclei, calculated using large-scale shell model weak interaction rates, are broader and have larger average energies than previously assumed, and can be parametrized simply for implementation in collapse simulations; however, the double-peaked structure observed for odd-odd nuclei under certain presupernova conditions (e.g., low electron chemical potential) is not captured by the simple parametrization.

## Method

The authors computed neutrino spectra from electron capture on pf-shell nuclei by summing over all Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions between thermally populated parent states and daughter states, using nuclear matrix elements from large-scale shell model calculations. The phase space integral included Fermi-Dirac electron distributions, Coulomb distortion via the Fermi function, and the reaction Q-values for each transition. The total normalized neutrino spectrum was obtained by dividing the partial rate per energy interval by the total electron capture rate, and a simple parametrization of the form \( n(E_\nu) = E_\nu^2 (E_\nu - q)^2 N / [1 + \exp((E_\nu - q - \mu_e)/kT)] \) was proposed, with \( q \) adjusted to match the average neutrino energy from the shell model.

## Key numbers

- For the A=56 isobars under typical silicon shell burning conditions (T=4×10⁹ K, ρ=3×10⁸ g cm⁻³, Yₑ=0.45, μₑ=2.5 MeV), the neutrino spectra for ⁵⁶V, ⁵⁶Cr, ⁵⁶Mn, ⁵⁶Fe, and ⁵⁶Ni are peaked around 1–2 MeV with a width of 1.4–1.8 MeV.
- For ⁵⁶Co under the same conditions, the spectrum shows a double-bump structure with a high-energy peak around E_ν ≈ 8 MeV from capture on the excited J=1 state at 1.7 MeV excitation energy.
- At presupernova conditions for a 15 M_⊙ star (T=7.2×10⁹ K, ρ=9.1×10⁹ g cm⁻³, Yₑ=0.43, μₑ=8.1 MeV), the average neutrino energy from capture on nuclei is about 3 MeV, while for capture on free protons it is 6.25 MeV.
- For ⁵⁹Fe, the ground state Q-value is Q₀ = -5.696 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the shell model evaluation of stellar weak interaction rates by Langanke et al. (2000) to include neutrino spectra, which were previously unavailable for the Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN) rate compilation. The spectra are intended for use in collapse simulations with explicit neutrino Boltzmann transport, such as those by Bruenn (1985), Mezzacappa (1993), and Janka (1996), which previously used a simplified independent particle model with a fixed 3 MeV energy splitting. The parametrization enables consistent implementation of shell-model-based neutrino spectra into these codes, and the spectra can be corrected for neutrino blocking during collapse by folding with a blocking factor (1 - S_ν(E_ν)).
