# Molecular dynamics description of an expanding $q$/$\bar{q}$ plasma with the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model and applications to heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies

> We present a relativistic molecular dynamics approach based on the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Lagrangian. We derive the relativistic time evolution equations for an expanding plasma, discuss the hadronization cross section and how they act in such a scenario. We present in detail how one can transform the time evolution equation to a simulation program and apply this program to study the expansion of a plasma created in experiments at RHIC and LHC. We present first results on the centrality dependence of $v_2$ and of the transverse momentum spectra of pions and kaons and discuss in detail the hadronisation mechanism.

## Key claim

The authors present a relativistic molecular dynamics approach based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Lagrangian that can describe an expanding quark/antiquark plasma from creation through hadronization without assuming local equilibrium, but the approach requires the Todorov-Komar (KT) condition to be fulfilled for the equations of motion to be well-behaved for systems with more than two particles.

## Method

The authors derive relativistic Hamilton equations of motion for an N-body system using the Dirac constraint formalism, starting from the NJL Lagrangian. They compute the matrix of constraints and the resulting equations of motion, then implement these in a simulation program that evolves quark and antiquark positions and momenta through an expanding plasma. The hadronization cross sections are derived from the NJL Lagrangian, and the local temperature for each particle is defined via a Gaussian-weighted density of neighboring particles.

## Key numbers

- The prefactor for the local temperature relation is Pi = (hbar c) (pi^2 / (ell g))^(1/3) approximately 190 MeV, where the degeneracy factor g = 36 (spin 2 x parity 2 x color 3 x flavor 3).
- The local temperature is given by T_i = (hbar c) (pi^2 / (ell g))^(1/3) rho_F^(1/3), where rho_F is the fermion density.
- The chemical potential is given by mu_i = (hbar c) (6 pi^2 / g)^(1/3) rho_B^(1/3), with rho_B the baryon density.
- For the low-order approximation, the baryon density rho_B is approximately (g / (6 pi^2)) (mu / (hbar c))^3.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) formalism originally advanced by Sorge (1989) but never practically implemented, and applies it to the NJL model which was previously used in a nonrelativistic approach by Scherer (2001) and in the parton hadron string dynamics (PHSD) model by Cassing (2009) and Bratkovskaya (2011). The approach enables the study of correlations and fluctuations built up during plasma expansion without assuming local equilibrium, complementing hydrodynamical models such as those by Luzum (2008) and the core-corona model by Aichelin (2010).
