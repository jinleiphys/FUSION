# Four-Body Scale in Universal Few-Boson Systems

> The role of an intrinsic four-body scale in universal few-boson systems is the subject of active debate. We study these systems within the framework of effective field theory. For systems of up to six bosons we establish that no four-body scale appears at leading order (LO). However, we find that at next-to-leading (NLO) order a four-body force is needed to obtain renormalized results for binding energies. With the associated parameter fixed to the binding energy of the four-boson system, this force is shown to renormalize the five- and six-body systems as well. We present an original ansatz for the short-distance limit of the bosonic $A$-body wave function from which we conjecture that new $A$-body scales appear at N$^{A-3}$LO. As a specific example, calculations are presented for clusters of helium atoms. Our results apply more generally to other few-body systems governed by a large scattering length, such as light nuclei and halo states, the low-energy properties of which are independent of the detailed internal structure of the constituents.

## Key claim

At next-to-leading order (NLO) in the effective field theory expansion, a four-body contact force is required to obtain renormalized binding energies for systems of up to six bosons, contrary to the leading-order (LO) behavior where no four-body scale appears. The authors conjecture that new A-body scales appear at N^(A-3)LO, meaning the four-body force is promoted to NLO rather than appearing at a higher order as naive dimensional analysis would suggest.

## Method

The authors study few-boson systems using effective field theory (EFT) with contact interactions, regularized by a separable Gaussian regulator with cutoff Λ. They solve the Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for A=3 and A=4 systems, and use the stochastic variational method (SVM) with correlated Gaussian basis functions for systems up to A=6. NLO corrections from two-body range terms are treated perturbatively, and the four-body force is fixed by demanding the tetramer ground-state energy matches the value calculated from the PCKLJS potential for ⁴He clusters.

## Key numbers

- The ⁴He dimer binding energy from the PCKLJS potential is B₂ = 1.615 mK; the EFT gives B₂^LO = 0.918 B₂ and B₂^NLO = 0.991 B₂
- The trimer ground-state binding energy: B₃^LO = 98.1 B₂ and B₃^NLO = 73.1 B₂, compared to the potential-model result B₃ = 81.6 B₂ (Hiyama and Kamimura, 2012)
- Tetramer binding energy ratio: B₄/B₃ = 4.8(1) at LO and 4.35(*) at NLO (the latter fixed to the PCKLJS value of 4.35)
- Pentamer binding energy ratio: B₅/B₃ = 10.8(5) at LO and 11.3(3) at NLO
- Hexamer binding energy ratio: B₆/B₃ = 18(2) at LO and 22(3) at NLO

## Context

This work extends earlier EFT studies by Platter et al. (2004), Platter (2005), and Hammer and Platter (2007) which found no four-body force needed at LO, and confirms the LO results of Bazak et al. (2016) for up to six particles. It resolves a debate in the literature about the existence of a four-body scale by showing that such a scale appears at NLO rather than LO, explaining why zero-range models (Yamashita et al., 2006; Hadizadeh et al., 2011) found a four-body parameter while EFT studies did not. The results enable predictions for larger helium clusters and suggest that correlations between cluster binding energies (the atomic Tjon line) survive at NLO, with planned extensions to light nuclei.
