# Transport coefficients of Relativistic Causal Hydrodynamics for Hadrons

> We investigate coefficients in the Israel-Stewart's causal hydrodynamics and discuss the way to calculate them with a microscopic theory. Based on the hadro-molecular simulation based on an event generator URASiMA, we evaluate the coefficients for a hot and dense hadronic fluid.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the additional coefficients (β relaxation times) in Israel-Stewart causal hydrodynamics can be extracted from hadro-molecular simulations, and that for a hadronic gas these relaxation times are approximately 2 fm and depend only weakly on temperature and baryon density, in contrast to the strong temperature dependence of the transport coefficients themselves.

## Method

The author used the event generator URASiMA to perform hadro-molecular simulations of a hadron gas in a periodic box, initially populated with baryons only, allowing collisions to produce mesons and resonances until a stationary state with a common slope parameter (temperature) was reached. From these stationary configurations, the shear viscosity and heat conductivity were computed via Kubo formulas from current-current correlations, and the relaxation times β were extracted from the exponential decay of those correlations. The analysis focused on the β coefficients (neglecting α terms) and assumed a particular situation with only a temperature gradient to relate β to the relaxation time τ.

## Key numbers

- Relaxation time of the stress-shear tensor and heat current for the hadron gas is about 2 fm.
- Shear viscosity and heat conductivity of the hadron gas show temperature dependence approximately as T⁵ (from prior work by the author, Muroya (2007)).
- Relaxation times of the currents show "very mild" changes with temperature (no explicit numerical values given for the variation).
- The square expectation values of the heat current and viscous-shear tensor (figures 6 and 7) are shown to account for most of the temperature dependence of the transport coefficients (no explicit numbers provided in the text for these quantities).

## Context

This work extends the Israel-Stewart causal hydrodynamics framework (Israel and Stewart, 1970s) by providing a microscopic calculation of the relaxation coefficients β for a hadronic gas, which had previously been treated as phenomenological parameters. The results contrast with earlier findings on relaxation in diffusion processes (referenced as PRC and EPL) where the relaxation time changes clearly with temperature. The paper enables more realistic applications of causal hydrodynamics to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where perfect fluid models (Euler equations neglecting viscosity and heat conductivity) had been commonly used.
