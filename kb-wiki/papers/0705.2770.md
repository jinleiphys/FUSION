# Schwinger Mechanism for Quark-Antiquark Production in the Presence of Arbitrary Time Dependent Chromo-Electric Field

> We study the Schwinger mechanism in QCD in the presence of an arbitrary time-dependent chromo-electric background field $E^a(t)$ with arbitrary color index $a$=1,2,...8 in SU(3). We obtain an exact result for the non-perturbative quark (antiquark) production from an arbitrary $E^a(t)$ by directly evaluating the path integral. We find that the exact result is independent of all the time derivatives $\frac{d^nE^a(t)}{dt^n}$ where $n=1,2,...\infty$. This result has the same functional dependence on two Casimir invariants $[E^a(t)E^a(t)]$ and $[d_{abc}E^a(t)E^b(t)E^c(t)]^2$ as the constant chromo-electric field $E^a$ result with the replacement: $E^a \rightarrow E^a(t)$. This result relies crucially on the validity of the shift conjecture, which has not yet been established.

## Key claim

The paper claims an exact non-perturbative result for quark-antiquark production from an arbitrary time-dependent chromo-electric field \(E^a(t)\) in SU(3) QCD, which is independent of all time derivatives of the field and has the same functional form as the constant-field result. However, this result relies crucially on the validity of the shift conjecture, which has not yet been established.

## Method

The author directly evaluates the path integral for the vacuum-to-vacuum transition amplitude of a quark in an arbitrary time-dependent chromo-electric background field \(E^a(t)\) in SU(3), using the axial gauge \(A^a_3=0\). The calculation proceeds by expressing the effective action in terms of a trace over Dirac and color indices, inserting complete sets of states, and applying the shift conjecture to handle the time dependence. The color trace is performed by diagonalizing the matrix \(M_{jk}(t)=T^a_{jk}E^a(t)\) to obtain its eigenvalues \(\Lambda_j(t)\), and the remaining operator reduces to a harmonic oscillator with time-dependent frequency, whose spectrum yields the final result.

## Key numbers

- The probability of quark-antiquark production per unit time, per unit volume, and per unit transverse momentum is given by \(\frac{dW_{q (\bar q)}}{dt d^3x d^2p_T} = -\frac{1}{4\pi^3} \sum_{j=1}^3 |g\Lambda_j(t)| \ln[1 - e^{-\frac{\pi (p_T^2+m^2)}{|g\Lambda_j(t)|}}]\), where \(m\) is the quark mass and \(\Lambda_j(t)\) are eigenvalues of the color matrix.
- The eigenvalues \(\Lambda_j(t)\) are expressed in terms of two time-dependent Casimir invariants: \(C_1(t)=[E^a(t)E^a(t)]\) and \(C_2(t)=[d_{abc}E^a(t)E^b(t)E^c(t)]^2\), with \(\Lambda_1(t)=\sqrt{C_1(t)/3}\cos\theta(t)\) and \(\Lambda_{2,3}(t)=\sqrt{C_1(t)/3}\cos(2\pi/3 \pm \theta(t))\), where \(\cos^2 3\theta(t)=3C_2(t)/C_1^3(t)\).
- The result is independent of all time derivatives \(\frac{d^nE^a(t)}{dt^n}\) for \(n=1,2,\ldots\infty\).

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger mechanism for quark-antiquark production from a constant chromo-electric field, previously studied by Nayak (2005, 2006), to arbitrary time-dependent fields in SU(3). It parallels the author's earlier treatment of gluon pair production in time-dependent fields (Nayak, 2007) and relies on the same shift conjecture introduced in Nayak (2006). The result enables non-perturbative calculations of quark production in time-varying chromo-electric fields relevant to heavy-ion collisions, but its validity depends on establishing the shift conjecture.
