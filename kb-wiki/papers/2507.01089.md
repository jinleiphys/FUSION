# Quantum Simulation of QED in Coulomb Gauge

> A recent work considered quantum simulation of Quantum Electrodynamics on a lattice in the Coulomb gauge with gauge degrees of freedom represented in the occupation basis in momentum space. Here we consider the more efficient representation of the gauge degrees of freedom in field basis in position space and develop a quantum algorithm for real-time simulation. We show that the continuum Coulomb gauge Hamiltonian is equivalent to the temporal gauge Hamiltonian when acting on physical states consisting of fermion and transverse gauge fields. The Coulomb gauge Hamiltonian is discretized by using the Green's function of the discrete Laplacian operator under the Dirichlet boundary conditions. Both the continuum Coulomb gauge Hamiltonian and the discretized one proposed here guarantee that the unphysical longitudinal gauge fields are decoupled and commute with the corresponding Hamiltonian. Thus there is no need to impose any constraint. The local gauge field basis and the canonically conjugate variable basis are swapped efficiently using the quantum Fourier transform. We prove that the qubit cost to represent physical states and the gate count for real-time simulation scale polynomially with the lattice size, energy, time, accuracy, and Hamiltonian parameters in lattice units. The gate cost here for implementing the time evolution of the gauge field is reduced at least by a factor on the order of $10^8$ for modest lattice size and accuracy level compared with the previous work.

## Key claim

The paper claims that representing gauge degrees of freedom in the field basis in position space, rather than the occupation basis in momentum space used in prior work (Li 2024), yields a quantum algorithm for real-time simulation of QED in Coulomb gauge whose gate cost for implementing gauge field time evolution is reduced by at least a factor on the order of $10^8$ for modest lattice size and accuracy level, while maintaining polynomial scaling of qubit and gate costs with lattice size, energy, time, and accuracy.

## Method

The continuum Coulomb gauge Hamiltonian for QED is discretized on a 3D cubic spatial lattice with Dirichlet boundary conditions, using the Green's function of the discrete Laplacian operator to define the Coulomb interaction and the transverse projection. Gauge fields are represented in the local field basis in position space, and the quantum Fourier transform is used to efficiently swap between the field basis and the canonically conjugate variable basis at each lattice site. The qubit cost to represent physical states and the gate count for real-time simulation are proven to scale polynomially with lattice size, energy, time, accuracy, and Hamiltonian parameters in lattice units.

## Key numbers

- The gate cost for implementing the time evolution of the gauge field is reduced at least by a factor on the order of $10^8$ for modest lattice size and accuracy level compared with the previous work (Li 2024).
- The lattice has volume $V = L^3$ with lattice spacing $a$, giving $\hat{V} = \hat{L}^3$ sites where $\hat{L} = L/a$.
- Discrete momenta under Dirichlet boundary conditions are given by $\boldsymbol p = \frac{\pi}{(\hat{L}+1)a}(k_1,k_2,k_3)$ with $k_i \in \{1,2,3,\cdots,\hat{L}\}$.
- The eigenvalues of the discrete Laplacian operator are $\lambda_{\hat{p}_i} = 2\cos\frac{k_i\pi}{\hat{L}+1} - 2$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Li (2024), which first proposed quantum simulation of QED in Coulomb gauge using the occupation basis in momentum space, by adopting a more efficient field basis in position space. It also builds on the systematic resource estimation framework of Jordan, Lee, and Preskill (2011, 2012) for scalar field theories, proving analogous polynomial scaling for QED with fermions. The paper contrasts with the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian approach (Kogut 1974) in temporal gauge, where the Gauss law constraint must be imposed on physical states; here the Coulomb gauge formulation decouples unphysical longitudinal gauge fields, eliminating the need for such constraints. This enables next steps toward rigorous resource estimates for lattice gauge theory simulations and potential BQP-completeness claims.
