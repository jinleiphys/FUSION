# X-ray bounds on the r-mode amplitude in millisecond pulsars

> r-mode astroseismology provides a unique way to study the internal composition of compact stars. Due to their precise timing, recycled millisecond radio pulsars present a particularly promising class of sources. Although their thermal properties are still poorly constrained, X-ray data is very useful for astroseismology since r-modes could strongly heat a star. Using known and new upper bounds on the temperatures and luminosities of several non-accreting millisecond radio pulsars we derive bounds on the r-mode amplitude as low as $α\lesssim10^{-8}$ and discuss the impact on scenarios for their internal composition.

# X-ray bounds on the r-mode amplitude in millisecond pulsars

## Key claim
Using upper bounds on the surface temperatures and luminosities of several non-accreting millisecond radio pulsars, the authors derive robust upper limits on the r-mode amplitude as low as α ≲ 4×10⁻⁹, but note that these bounds are conservative and could be more than a factor of two smaller for typical neutron star models, potentially reaching α ≲ 10⁻⁹.

## Method
The authors compiled X-ray data from the literature for millisecond pulsars with spin frequencies above 100 Hz, including both spectral fits and total luminosity bounds, and performed a detailed re-analysis of archival Chandra, XMM-Newton, and SUZAKU observations of PSR J1231−1411 to obtain an improved temperature upper limit. They then used the thermal steady-state condition where r-mode heating balances radiative cooling to convert temperature and luminosity upper bounds into upper limits on the r-mode saturation amplitude, taking into account uncertainties in mass, radius, compactness, and the presence or absence of fast neutrino cooling mechanisms.

## Key numbers
- Most restrictive r-mode amplitude bound: α_sat ≲ 4×10⁻⁹ for the transitional binary PSR J1023+0038, partly due to a mass measurement for this source.
- Rigorous upper bound formula (no fast cooling): α_sat < 1.40×10⁻⁹ × (T_∞/10⁵ K)² × (500 Hz/f)⁴, using conservative assumptions M > M_⊙, R > 10 km, and J̃ > 1/(20π).
- For a standard 2 M_⊙ neutron star model with APR equation of state, the lowest bound becomes α_sat ≲ 10⁻⁹.
- Temperature upper limit for PSR J1231−1411 from new X-ray analysis: kT = 0.015 keV (approximately 1.74×10⁵ K) for a 10 km emitting area at 0.4 kpc distance.
- Spin-down limit from timing data gives α ≲ 10⁻⁷ for the most constraining source, which is less restrictive than the thermal bounds.

## Context
This work extends the thermal r-mode amplitude bounds previously derived for LMXBs by Mahmoodifar and Strohmayer (2013) and Alford et al. (2013) to the colder, non-accreting millisecond pulsar population, yielding systematically lower amplitude limits. The results show that the obtained amplitudes (α ≲ 10⁻⁸ to 10⁻⁹) are many orders of magnitude below those that well-established saturation mechanisms like mode-coupling (Arras, 2002; Bondarescu, 2013) can provide, suggesting that neutron stars with minimal damping require another strong non-linear dissipation mechanism or point to new physics such as exotic forms of matter. The bounds approach the regime α ≲ 10⁻¹⁰ where r-modes could be completely ruled out in these sources, as even the fastest millisecond pulsars could cool out of the instability region.
