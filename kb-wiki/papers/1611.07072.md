# Electric dipole polarizability of $^{48}$Ca and implications for the neutron skin

> The electric dipole strength distribution in Ca-48 between 5 and 25 MeV has been determined at RCNP, Osaka, from proton inelastic scattering experiments at forward angles. Combined with photoabsorption data at higher excitation energy, this enables for the first time the extraction of the electric dipole polarizability alpha_D(Ca-48) = 2.07(22) fm^3. Remarkably, the dipole response of Ca-48 is found to be very similar to that of Ca-40, consistent with a small neutron skin in Ca-48. The experimental results are in good agreement with ab initio calculations based on chiral effective field theory interactions and with state-of-the-art density-functional calculations, implying a neutron skin in Ca-48 of 0.14 - 0.20 fm.

# Electric dipole polarizability of $^{48}$Ca and implications for the neutron skin

## Key claim
The electric dipole polarizability of $^{48}$Ca is measured as 2.07(22) fm³, and the dipole response is very similar to that of $^{40}$Ca, consistent with a small neutron skin. The experimental result agrees with both ab initio calculations based on chiral effective field theory and density-functional calculations, implying a neutron skin of 0.14–0.20 fm, but the authors note that the theoretical uncertainty band does not include possible shifts from coupled-cluster truncation errors that could affect the GDR position by about 2 MeV.

## Method
The electric dipole strength distribution in $^{48}$Ca between 5 and 25 MeV was determined from proton inelastic scattering at 295 MeV at RCNP, Osaka, using the Grand Raiden spectrometer at forward angles (0°–5.5°). A multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) of angular distributions separated the relativistic Coulomb excitation cross sections from nuclear contributions, and the resulting cross sections were converted into equivalent photoabsorption cross sections and B(E1) strength using the virtual photon method in an eikonal approach. These data were combined with photoabsorption data from the literature at higher excitation energies (25–60 MeV) to extract the full electric dipole polarizability.

## Key numbers
- Electric dipole polarizability of $^{48}$Ca: $\alpha_D = 2.07(22)$ fm³
- GDR centroid energy in $^{48}$Ca: $E_C = 18.9(2)$ MeV with width $\Gamma = 3.9(4)$ MeV
- Integrated E1 strength in the measured range (10–25 MeV) corresponds to 85% of the E1 energy-weighted sum rule
- Contribution to $\alpha_D$ from 10–25 MeV: $1.73(18)$ fm³ for $^{48}$Ca; $1.50(2)$ fm³ for $^{40}$Ca
- Low-energy E1 strength below neutron threshold ($S_n = 9.9$ MeV) contributes only $0.0101(6)$ fm³
- Electric dipole polarizability of $^{40}$Ca from combined data: $1.87(3)$ fm³
- Neutron skin in $^{48}$Ca implied by the data: $0.14 – 0.20$ fm
- Symmetry energy parameters from ab initio calculations: $J = 28.5 – 33.3$ MeV, $L = 43.8 – 48.6$ MeV

## Context
This work provides the first measurement of the electric dipole polarizability of $^{48}$Ca, enabling a direct comparison with ab initio calculations from Hagen et al. (2016) based on chiral effective field theory interactions and with state-of-the-art energy density functional (EDF) calculations. The result extends previous measurements of $\alpha_D$ in $^{208}$Pb (Tamii et al., 2011) and $^{120}$Sn (Hashimoto et al., 2015) to a lighter, doubly magic nucleus where ab initio calculations are feasible. The extracted neutron skin range is relevant for the proposed CREX parity-violating electron scattering experiment on $^{48}$Ca and provides constraints on the symmetry energy and its slope $L$, which govern the equation of state of neutron-rich matter relevant for neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae.
