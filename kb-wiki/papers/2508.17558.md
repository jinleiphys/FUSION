# Assessing Convergence Patterns Across Modern Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

> The BUQEYE model for correlated effective field theory (EFT) truncation errors assumes a regular pattern of dimensionless coefficients extracted from order-by-order observable calculations. This enables results from lower orders to inform statistical predictions for error estimates of omitted higher orders. We test the model for multiple chiral EFT ($χ$EFT) nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials using a suite of six common NN scattering observables represented as functions of relative momentum and scattering angle. First, we flag irregularity in the convergence patterns of potentials with so-called "soft" regulator scales, namely that the sizes of the coefficients in the observables' expansions are mismatched between the even and odd orders. Second, we test the BUQEYE model's assumption of Gaussian process (GP) stationarity against the data and find that the GP's correlation structure as encoded in its length scale is nonstationary: The GP length scale in the angular dimension $\ell_θ$ is approximately inversely proportional to the relative momentum of the scattering process. After we remediate this issue by allowing $\ell_θ$ to vary with momentum, diagnostics show significant improvement, validating the application of the BUQEYE model after this modification. Third, we find that good performance of the BUQEYE model relies on properly choosing the $χ$EFT breakdown scale $Λ_{b}$. With $m_{\text{eff}}$ held fixed at the physical pion mass we find $Λ_{b}=600$--$750$ MeV for various N$^{3}$LO interactions. Statistically consistent distributions for $Λ_{b}$ across orders are only found for the SMS potential at a regulator scale of 450 or 500 MeV. All our results can be reproduced using a publicly available Jupyter notebook, which can be straightforwardly modified to analyze other $χ$EFT NN potentials.

## Key claim

The BUQEYE model's assumption of Gaussian process stationarity fails for nucleon-nucleon scattering observables because the angular correlation length scale ℓθ is approximately inversely proportional to the relative momentum, but this nonstationarity can be remediated by allowing ℓθ to vary with momentum, after which the model performs well for potentials that do not exhibit even-odd coefficient staggering.

## Method

The authors extracted dimensionless coefficient functions cn(prel, −cos θ) from order-by-order chiral EFT predictions of six common neutron-proton scattering observables (dσ/dΩ, D, Axx, Ayy, A, Ay) for multiple potential families (SMS, SCS, EMN, GT+) at various regulator scales. They tested the BUQEYE model's assumption that these coefficients are independent and identically distributed draws from a stationary Gaussian process by examining the root-mean-square sizes of coefficients across orders and by inferring the GP length scale ℓθ at fixed momentum slices. They then modified the model to allow ℓθ to vary with momentum according to a power law and assessed improvement using diagnostic checks.

## Key numbers

- Λb = 600–750 MeV for various N³LO interactions with meff held fixed at the physical pion mass (138 MeV)
- Statistically consistent distributions for Λb across orders were found only for the SMS potential at regulator scales of 450 or 500 MeV
- For SMS 500 MeV, the rms coefficient values across orders were: c2 = 0.7 ± 0.1, c3 = 0.4 ± 0.2, c4 = 0.5 ± 0.2, c5 = 0.6 ± 0.1, c6 = 0.5 ± 0.1
- For SMS 400 MeV, the rms coefficient values showed even-odd staggering: c2 = 0.40 ± 0.04, c3 = 0.14 ± 0.04, c4 = 0.9 ± 0.3, c5 = 0.35 ± 0.04, c6 = 0.87 ± 0.07
- For SCS 0.9 fm, rms values: c2 = 0.6 ± 0.2, c3 = 0.5 ± 0.2, c4 = 0.6 ± 0.1, c5 = 0.38 ± 0.07
- For SCS 1.2 fm, rms values: c2 = 0.5 ± 0.2, c3 = 0.2 ± 0.1, c4 = 1.7 ± 0.4, c5 = 0.36 ± 0.05
- ℓθ was determined independently at fixed prel in intervals of 25 MeV from 25 to 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the BUQEYE model analysis of Millican (2024) from a single chiral EFT potential (SMS 500 MeV) to multiple potential families including SMS, SCS, EMN, and GT+. It confirms earlier observations by Melendez (2017) that soft potentials exhibit irregular even-odd convergence patterns, and provides a physical explanation: softer regulators suppress mid-range pion-exchange contributions, forcing even-order contact terms to compensate. The nonstationarity remediation enables application of the BUQEYE model to a wider range of chiral EFT potentials, with the publicly available Jupyter notebook allowing straightforward extension to other potentials not tested here, such as those including Δ isobars from Piarulli (2014) or Ekström (2017).
