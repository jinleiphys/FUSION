# Revealing the black-body regime of small-x deep-inelastic scattering through final-state signals

> We derive the major characteristics of inclusive and diffractive final states in deep-inelastic scattering off heavy nuclei for the-high energy (small-x) kinematics in which the limit of complete absorption is reached for the dominant hadronic fluctuations in the virtual photon (the black-body limit of the process). Both the longitudinal and transverse distributions of the leading hadrons are found to be strikingly different from the corresponding ones within the leading-twist approximation, and hence provide unambiguous signals for the onset of the black-body limit.

## Key claim

The paper argues that final-state observables in deep-inelastic scattering off heavy nuclei provide unambiguous signals for the onset of the black-body limit (BBL), whereas inclusive structure functions do not contain "smoking gun" signatures due to uncertainties in input gluon densities and leading-twist shadowing. The BBL is reached when the inelastic cross section for a small dipole-nucleus interaction saturates the geometrical limit of \(\pi R_{\text{target}}^2\).

## Method

The authors work within the \(q\bar{q}\) dipole approximation for the virtual photon wave function, assuming that in the BBL the interaction is diagonal in the dipole variables (longitudinal momentum fraction \(z\) and transverse momentum \(p_\perp\)) and black for each configuration. They derive expressions for the transverse and longitudinal nuclear structure functions \(F_T^A\) and \(F_L^A\) as integrals over the mass \(M^2\) of the produced \(q\bar{q}\) system, using the ratio \(\rho(M^2) = \sigma(e^+e^- \to \text{hadrons})/\sigma(e^+e^- \to \mu^+\mu^-)\). The diffractive mass spectrum and the inclusive spectrum of leading hadrons are then obtained by exploiting the diagonality of the BBL interaction and the equality of elastic and inelastic contributions.

## Key numbers

* For DIS on Pb at \(x \sim 10^{-3}\) and small impact parameters, the BBL may be reached for \(Q^2 \le 7\) GeV\(^2\) in the quark channel and \(Q^2 \le 15\) GeV\(^2\) in the gluon channel.
* In the BBL, the average squared transverse momentum of jets in diffractive production is \(\langle p_\perp^2(\text{jet})\rangle_T = 3M^2/20\) for transverse photons and \(\langle p_\perp^2(\text{jet})\rangle_L = M^2/5\) for longitudinal photons.
* The diffractive cross section for jet production in the BBL is proportional to \(M^2\), in contrast to the leading-twist approximation where it is proportional to \(\ln Q^2\).
* For vector meson production in the BBL at large \(Q^2\), the cross section behaves as \(1/Q^2\), whereas in perturbative QCD the asymptotic behavior is \(1/Q^6\).
* The total cross section in the BBL is given by \(\sigma_{\gamma^*A} = 2 \int dz\,d^2p_\perp\, |\psi_{\gamma^*}^{q\bar{q}}(z,p_\perp,Q^2)|^2 \pi R_A^2 / [2(2\pi)^3]\).

## Context

This work extends the original concept of the black-body limit in deep-inelastic scattering from heavy nuclei, first considered by Gribov (1969) before the discovery of perturbative QCD. It contrasts the BBL predictions with the standard leading-twist approximation (LTA) of perturbative QCD, which describes HERA data via DGLAP evolution equations. The paper enables experimental searches for the BBL at future facilities such as the LHC, RHIC in pA collisions, and eA mode at HERA/THERA, by providing specific final-state signatures that are more reliable than inclusive observables.
