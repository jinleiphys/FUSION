# Constraining the Radii of Neutron Stars with Terrestrial Nuclear Laboratory Data

> Neutron star radii are primarily determined by the pressure of isospin asymmetric matter which is proportional to the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy. Available terrestrial laboratory data on the isospin diffusion in heavy-ion reactions at intermediate energies constrain the slope of the symmetry energy. Using this constraint, we show that the radius (radiation radius) of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is between 11.5 (14.4) and 13.6 (16.3) km.

## Key claim

Using terrestrial laboratory data on isospin diffusion in heavy-ion reactions to constrain the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy, the authors show that the radius (radiation radius) of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is between 11.5 (14.4) and 13.6 (16.3) km, but this range depends on the assumption that the neutron star consists of npeμ matter with no exotic components such as hyperons, quarks, or Bose condensates.

## Method

The authors used an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model to simulate heavy-ion reactions and calculate the strength of isospin diffusion (1-Ri) in the 124Sn+112Sn reaction, comparing it to NSCL/MSU data. They also computed the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb using Skyrme Hartree-Fock with interaction parameters adjusted to match the same equation of state. The equation of state was parameterized by a variable x that controls the density dependence of the symmetry energy while keeping the isospin-symmetric part fixed, and neutron star structure was obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation for spherically-symmetric, non-rotating, non-magnetized stars at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter L (≡ 3ρ₀(∂E_sym/∂ρ) at ρ₀) is constrained between 62.1 MeV (x=0) and 107.4 MeV (x=-1) by the isospin diffusion data 1-Ri = 0.525 ± 0.05.
- The radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is constrained between 11.5 km and 13.6 km.
- The radiation radius R∞ = R/√(1-2GM/Rc²) of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is constrained between 14.4 km and 16.3 km.
- The incompressibility K₀ of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is set to 211 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density E_sym(ρ₀) is 32 MeV.
- For the x=0 EOS, neutron stars with masses above 1.39 solar masses have central densities above the threshold for the direct URCA process.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to determine the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy using isospin diffusion in heavy-ion reactions (Shi, 2004; Betty, 2004; Chen, 2005), flow observables (Pawel, 2004), and neutron skin measurements (Chuck, 2004; Steiner, 2005). The results are consistent with the range of radii from the extensive analysis in Steiner (2005) and with the predicted radius of 12.66 km from a new effective interaction calibrated by reproducing collective modes of 90Zr and 208Pb (Jorge, 2005). The constraint enables tighter predictions for neutron star radii and suggests that the direct URCA process is likely for stars above 1.39 solar masses, while a future radius measurement outside this range may indicate non-standard physics such as hyperons, quarks, or Bose condensates.
