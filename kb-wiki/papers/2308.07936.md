# The spin alignment of vector mesons with light front quarks

> The global spin alignment of the vector meson has been observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but is still on hot debates in the theoretical community. Here we propose to apply the light front framework to explain this phenomenon since the light front form explicitly describes the hadron spin including both the quark spin and the orbital angular momentum. After applying the light front spinor, we find that the spin alignment in the polarization of vector mesons with $ρ_{00}>1/3$ can be naturally manifested and in particular, the obtained spin alignment for $ϕ$ meson is in good agreement with the experimental data. This implies that to explain the spin alignment it is important to properly include the contribution from the gluon interactions that are presented in terms of the orbital angular momentum of the hadron bound state.

## Key claim

The light front framework naturally produces a spin alignment of vector mesons with ρ₀₀ > 1/3, and the obtained spin alignment for the φ meson agrees with experimental data, but this result depends on properly including the gluon interactions that are represented as orbital angular momentum of the hadron bound state.

## Method

The authors apply the light front spinor for quarks in the Dirac representation to compute the spin density matrix ρ_{λλ'} of vector mesons, starting from the Wigner function and the meson wave function expressed in Fock space. They derive a transition matrix that transforms the spin density matrix from the quark rest frame into the light front frame, which incorporates the orbital angular momentum of the hadron through terms like σ·p σ³. The resulting ρ₀₀ is then integrated over the quark momentum distribution using a Brodsky-Huang-Lepage parameterization of the light front wave function for φ, J/Ψ, and Υ mesons.

## Key numbers

- For the φ meson at √s_{NN} = 200 GeV, the effective temperature is set to T_eff = 300 MeV.
- In the equal quark mass case with x=1/2 and neglecting masses, ρ₀₀ reaches a limit of 1/3 when P⁺ >> p⊥, and decreases from 0.43 to 1/3 as P⁺ goes to infinity (with p⊥ = 0.5 GeV).
- The integrated ρ₀₀ for φ, J/Ψ, and Υ mesons is larger than 1/3 at lower collision energies, with heavier mesons showing suppressed spin alignment.
- An approximate upper limit for the spin alignment is given as ρ₀₀ = 1/2 from the large N_c limit result f^T_V/f_V = 1/√2.
- The parameter κ for the light front wave function is 0 for φ, 1.5 for J/Ψ, and 3.0 for Υ; the respective quark masses are 0.1 GeV (s), 1.2 GeV (c), and 4.2 GeV (b); the meson masses are 1.0 GeV (φ), 3.1 GeV (J/Ψ), and 9.5 GeV (Υ).

## Context

This work addresses the unexpectedly large global spin alignment of vector mesons observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions by the STAR collaboration, which could not be explained by conventional mechanisms including vorticity, electromagnetic field, local vorticity loops, quark fragmentation, helicity polarization, and axial charge current fluctuations as studied by Liang (2004), Yang (2017), Xia (2020), Becattini (2013), Sheng (2019), Gao (2021), and Muller (2021). The light front approach provides an alternative to including gluon fluctuations explicitly as in Sheng (2022) and Sheng (2019), and it connects to the longstanding "spin puzzle" of the proton where gluon interactions contribute a large fraction of hadron spin through orbital angular momentum. The results enable future incorporation with hydrodynamic simulations and chiral kinetic theories to fully describe the polarization and spin alignment of hadrons in heavy ion collisions.
