# Deformed Boson Scheme including Conventional q-Deformation in Time-Dependent Variational Method. IV

> Basic idea presented in Parts (I)-(III) for the deformed boson scheme is applied to the case of the su(2)- and su(1,1)-algebras for describing many-body systems consisting of four kinds of boson operators. A possible form of the coherent state given by the present authors is generalized and the su(2)_q- and the su(1,1)_q-algebras are obtained in the form expressed in terms of four kinds of boson operators. As an illustrative example of the application, the framework for describing thermal effects observed in two-level shell model under the pairing correlation is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper extends the deformed boson scheme, previously formulated for one, two, and three kinds of boson operators, to systems described by four kinds of boson operators, thereby constructing the su(2)_q- and su(1,1)_q-algebras in this expanded framework; the authors propose that this formalism can serve as a basis for describing thermal effects in a two-level shell model under pairing correlation, but they present this only as an illustrative example of the application, not as a fully developed physical model.

## Method

The authors generalize the coherent state previously defined for four-boson systems by introducing deformation functions that depend on the su(2)-spin quantum numbers (i, j, s, s_0) and su(1,1)-spin quantum numbers (k, l, t, t_0). They define two types of deformation, called S-type and T-type, by applying operator-valued functions Omega_S and Omega_T to the undeformed coherent state, and then derive the corresponding q-deformed commutation relations for the su(2)_q and su(1,1)_q algebras in terms of four kinds of boson operators.

## Key numbers

* The normalization constant for the orthogonal basis states is given as N_{m_R m_S m_T; n} = [n!/(n-m_R)!] * [n!/(n-m_S)!] * [(n+1+m_T)!/(n+1)!].
* The normalization constant for the undeformed coherent state is Gamma^0 = (1 - |gamma_T|^2) * exp( (1 + |gamma_R|^2)(1 + |gamma_S|^2)(1 - |gamma_T|^2)^{-1} |delta|^2 ).
* The eigenvalue of the operator hat(R) = hat(S) = hat(T) - 1 is n/2, where n is a non-negative integer.
* The su(2)-spin s takes values s = s_m, s_m+1, s_m+2, ..., (i+j) with s_m = |i-j|, and s_0 ranges from -s to s.
* The su(1,1)-spin t takes values t = t_m, t_m+1, t_m+2, ... with t_m = |k-1/2| + |l-1/2| + 1, and t_0 ranges from t upward.

## Context

This work is the fourth in a series (Parts I-III) by the same authors, extending the deformed boson scheme from one, two, and three kinds of boson operators to four kinds. It builds on the Schwinger boson representation of su(2) and su(1,1) algebras and on the authors' prior investigation of coherent states for four-boson systems (Kuriyama et al., 2000). The formalism is intended to enable the description of thermal and dissipative properties in su(2)-boson models, specifically the two-level shell model under pairing correlation, which requires four boson operators when combining the Holstein-Primakoff representation with phase-space doubling for thermal effects.
