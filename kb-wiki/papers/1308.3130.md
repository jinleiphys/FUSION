# Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Approach to Pseudoscalar Mesons

> Light pseudoscalar mesons are Janus-type particles: Within quantum chromodynamics, they must be described as bound states of its fundamental degrees of freedom and as the (pseudo-) Goldstone bosons of its spontaneously broken chiral symmetry. This janiform nature of pions and kaons may be easily accommodated by the Bethe-Salpeter formalism in its instantaneous limit: Starting from the general behaviour of the Bethe-Salpeter solutions for light pseudoscalar mesons at large Euclidean momenta, we provide the exact relationship between the solutions of our bound-state equation and the underlying interactions, boiled down to a configuration-space potential depending on the interquark distance: the potential exhibits a logarithmically softened Coulomb singularity at the origin and, at large interquark distances, rises beyond bounds for massless quarks but approaches a non-positive finite limit for too heavy quarks.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter (Salpeter) formalism can accommodate the dual nature of light pseudoscalar mesons as both quark-antiquark bound states and (pseudo-)Goldstone bosons, but only if the interquark potential exhibits a logarithmically softened Coulomb singularity at the origin and, for massless quarks, rises without bound at large distances; for too-heavy quarks, the confining character is lost and the potential approaches a non-positive finite limit.

## Method
The author starts from the known large-Euclidean-momentum behavior of Bethe-Salpeter solutions for light pseudoscalar mesons in the chiral limit, which fall off as the inverse fourth power of the relative four-momentum. Assuming spherical symmetry and instantaneous interactions, the Salpeter equation is reduced to radial eigenvalue equations in configuration space. By inverting the procedure, pre-selecting analytic solutions and then determining the central potentials that produce them, the exact relationship between the bound-state solutions and the underlying interactions is derived analytically for both massless and massive quarks.

## Key numbers
- For light pseudoscalar mesons in the chiral limit, the Bethe-Salpeter solutions fall off for large Euclidean momenta like the inverse fourth power of the quarks’ relative four-momentum.
- For massless quarks, the potential \(V(r)\) exhibits a logarithmically softened Coulomb singularity at the origin and rises to infinity for large interquark distance \(r\).
- For massive quarks, \(V(r)\) still has a logarithmically softened Coulomb singularity at the origin, but for quark masses too large, the potential approaches a non-positive finite limit at large \(r\) rather than rising without bound.

## Context
This work extends the author’s prior program of finding exact analytic solutions of the Salpeter equation (Lucha, 2013) by inverting the bound-state problem to determine the potential from a pre-selected solution. It specifically applies that method to light pseudoscalar mesons, building on the known chiral-limit behavior of Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes from Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1998). The results enable future studies of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in a single relativistic framework, and they clarify the conditions under which the Salpeter equation yields confining versus non-confining potentials depending on quark mass.
