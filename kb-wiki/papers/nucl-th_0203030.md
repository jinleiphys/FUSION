# Unlike particle correlations and the strange quark matter distillation process

> We present a new technique for observing the strange quark matter distillation process based on unlike particle correlations. A simulation is presented based on the scenario of a two-phase thermodynamical evolution model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a measurable asymmetry in the \(K^+K^-\) correlation function ratio \((1+R^+)/(1+R^-)\) can serve as a signal of the strange quark matter distillation process, but only if the deviation from unity is large (e.g., \(-26\%\) at \(q \rightarrow 0\) for a low bag constant); weaker deviations (e.g., \(-4.7\%\) or \(-2.3\%\)) cannot be distinguished from ordinary collision mechanisms without further study.

## Method

The authors simulate the dynamical evolution of a mixed quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas phase using a two-phase thermodynamical model that assumes Gibbs equilibrium at the interface and non-equilibrium hadron evaporation from the surface. They then compute the \(K^+K^-\) correlation functions for pairs with \(\vec{k}\cdot\vec{v}>0\) (\(R^+\)) and \(\vec{k}\cdot\vec{v}<0\) (\(R^-\)) using a quantum formalism that is sensitive to the sign of the relative emission time, and extract the ratio \((1+R^+)/(1+R^-)\) as a function of relative momentum \(q\). Three parameter sets are studied, corresponding to Au+Au at AGS energies (with low or high bag constant) and S+Au at SPS energies (with high bag constant).

## Key numbers

- For set 1 (Au+Au, \(B^{1/4}=160\) MeV, low bag constant): the predicted deviation of \((1+R^+)/(1+R^-)\) from unity at \(q \rightarrow 0\) is \(-26\%\); the effect extends up to about \(30\) MeV/c.
- For set 2 (Au+Au, \(B^{1/4}=235\) MeV, high bag constant): the predicted deviation at \(q \rightarrow 0\) is \(-4.7\%\); the effect extends up to about \(60\) MeV/c.
- For set 3 (S+Au, \(B^{1/4}=235\) MeV, high bag constant): the predicted deviation at \(q \rightarrow 0\) is \(-2.3\%\); the effect extends up to about \(60\) MeV/c.
- For the \(K^+K^-\) system, the Bohr radius is \(a = -111\) fm; each fm in the asymmetry \(\langle r_L^* \rangle\) or \(\langle \gamma v t \rangle\) transforms into a \(2\%\) change of the \((1+R^+)/(1+R^-)\) ratio at \(q \rightarrow 0\).
- For set 1, the mean separation \(\langle r^* \rangle \approx 18\) fm; for sets 2 and 3, \(\langle r^* \rangle \approx 6\) fm in both cases.

## Context

This work extends the unlike-particle correlation method developed by Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1994, 1996) and applied to low-energy nuclear collisions at GANIL, to the study of transient strange quark matter in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the strangeness distillation scenario proposed by Greiner (1987) and the two-phase dynamical model of Greiner (1991) and Spieles (1996, 1997). The technique offers a way to distinguish between different bag constant scenarios for the QCD phase transition at finite net baryon density, complementing earlier work by Gyulassy (1992) using \(K_s^0K_s^0\) correlations (which cannot determine the sign of time delays).
