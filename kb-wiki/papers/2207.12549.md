# Neutrino-induced single pion production and the reanalyzed bubble chamber data

> In this work we report the calculation of the charged current total and differential cross sections for weak pion-production with neutrinos and antineutrinos, with the final pion-nucleon pair invariant mass $W_{πN}\lesssim2$GeV. Our results are compared with the recent reanalyzed data from the old bubble chamber experiments, that solved the discrepancy between the ANL and BNL data. We implement a model previously tested for the cuts $W_{πN}<1.4,1.6$ Gev which includes explicitly resonances in the first and second resonance regions, within different approaches for the resonances self energy and $\frac{3}{2}$-vertexes and propagators, a fact not usually analyzed. Our model leans on consistent effective Lagrangians that generate resonant amplitudes together non resonant plus resonant backgrounds. Effects of hadrons finite extension and more energetic resonances corresponding to the emitted pion-nucleon invariant mass in the $1.6<W_{πN}<2$ GeV region, are taking into account using appropiate form factors in consistency with previous results on neutral current pion production calculations. Our results reproduce well the reanalysed data without cuts and are compared with another models.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously tested model for neutrino-induced single pion production, which was valid for final pion-nucleon invariant mass cuts \(W_{\pi N}<1.4,1.6\) GeV, to describe the reanalyzed bubble chamber data without cuts up to \(W_{\pi N}\lesssim2\) GeV by including a global hadronic form factor that accounts for the finite extension of hadrons and the effect of more energetic resonances not explicitly introduced.

## Method

The authors calculate charged-current total and differential cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino single pion production on nucleons using a model based on effective Lagrangians that generate resonant amplitudes together with non-resonant and resonant background terms. They explicitly include the \(\Delta(1232)\) and the second resonance region resonances \(N^{*}(1440), N^{*}(1520), N^{*}(1535)\), treating the spin-\(\frac{3}{2}\) fields with consistent contact invariance and a \(Z=\frac{1}{2}\) parameter, and implement both the complex-mass scheme (CMS) for the \(\Delta(1232)\) and a constant mass-width (CMW) approach for the other resonances. To extend the model to higher \(W_{\pi N}\) up to 2 GeV, they multiply the total amplitude by a global regulating form factor that becomes active above \(W_{\pi N}=1.6\) GeV, modeling the deviations from pointlike couplings due to hadron structure.

## Key numbers

* The total cross section for \(\nu p\rightarrow\mu^{-}p\pi^{+}\) is calculated for neutrino energies up to \(W_{\pi N}\lesssim2\) GeV, and the model reproduces the reanalyzed ANL and BNL data without cuts.
* The Fermi constant used is \(G_{F}=1.16637\times10^{-5}\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and the CKM matrix element is \(|V_{ud}|=0.9740\).
* The form factor used to regularize the amplitude above the second resonance region is \(F({\rm k},W_{\pi N}) = \Lambda^{4} / [\Lambda^{4} + {\rm k}(W_{\pi N})^{2} (W_{\pi N}-W_{\pi N}^{th})^{2} \theta(W_{\pi N}-1.6\ \text{GeV})]\), with the threshold invariant mass \(W_{\pi N}^{th}=m_{\pi}+m_{N}\).
* The isospin coefficients for the direct resonant \(\Delta(1232)\) contribution in the \(\nu p\rightarrow\mu^{-}p\pi^{+}\) channel are \(\mathcal{T}_{h}^{\Delta}=1\) for the direct term and \(\mathcal{T}_{g}^{\Delta}=-1/3\) for the crossed term.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Tamayo 2022) that described ANL data for \(W_{\pi N}<1.6\) GeV to higher invariant masses, addressing the long-standing normalization discrepancy between the ANL (Rad 1982) and BNL (Kitagi 1986) bubble chamber experiments that was resolved by Wilkinson (2014) and Rodriguez (2016) using a ratio method with CCQE event rates. The model is compared with the GENIE pion production model (Rein 1981) and provides a more consistent treatment of spin-\(\frac{3}{2}\) resonances and their self energies, enabling improved predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments that rely on accurate single pion production cross sections.
