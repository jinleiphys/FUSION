# Nuclear Physics with Chiral Effective Field Theory: State of the Art and Open Challenges

> Understanding the properties of atomic nuclei and nuclear dynamics from QCD remains a major challenge. Complementary to first attempts along these lines based on lattice QCD, an effective field theory approach has been developed in the past two decades and applied to a variety of nuclear bound states and reactions. I outline the foundations of this method, review selected applications and address some open challenges in this field.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) provides a systematic framework for describing nuclear forces and dynamics from QCD, but the renormalization of the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude with non-perturbative pions remains under debate, and the convergence of the chiral expansion for three-nucleon forces beyond leading order is not yet fully established.

## Method

The paper reviews the foundations of chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces, where nucleons interact by exchanging pions and short-range forces are parameterized by contact interactions with an increasing number of derivatives. The chiral power counting organizes contributions to two-nucleon, three-nucleon, and four-nucleon forces in powers of the soft scale Q relative to the chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi. Applications include solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering, solving Faddeev equations for three-nucleon scattering, and performing partial wave decompositions of three-nucleon force contributions up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) and next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N4LO).

## Key numbers

- S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths: a_{1S0} ~ (8 MeV)^{-1} and a_{3S1} ~ (40 MeV)^{-1}, indicating the breakdown of perturbation theory for two- and many-nucleon observables at low energy.
- Nucleon-nucleon phase shifts are accurately described up to laboratory energies E_lab ~ 200 MeV at N3LO (Q^4) in the chiral expansion.
- The deuteron binding energy and S-wave scattering lengths show a quark mass dependence that yields a limit on light quark mass variation: delta m_q / m_q = 0.2 +/- 0.04.
- Pionic hydrogen 1s level shift: epsilon_{1s} = (-7.120 +/- 0.012) eV and width Gamma_{1s} = (0.823 +/- 0.019) eV (Gotta 2008).
- Pionic deuterium 1s level shift: epsilon_{1s}^D = (2.356 +/- 0.031) eV (Strauch 2010).
- The N2LO analysis of Ref. Berengut (2013) used lattice-QCD results at pion masses M_pi > 353.7 MeV to constrain the quark mass dependence of nucleon-nucleon observables.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work by Weinberg (1990) on applying effective field theory to nuclear forces and summarizes developments up to 2013, including the N3LO nucleon-nucleon potentials of Entem (2003) and Epelbaum (2004). It addresses open challenges such as the renormalization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with non-perturbative pions, where a new manifestly Lorentz invariant framework was proposed by Epelbaum (2012), and the convergence of three-nucleon forces beyond N2LO, where calculations by Krebs (2012) and Krebs (2013) extend the chiral expansion to N4LO. The work enables improved chiral extrapolations for lattice QCD comparisons and more precise determinations of pion-nucleon scattering lengths from pionic atom data.
