# The pion-nucleon sigma term from lattice QCD

> We present an analysis of the pion-nucleon $σ$-term, $σ_{πN}$, using six ensembles with 2+1+1-flavor highly improved staggered quark action generated by the MILC collaboration. The most serious systematic effect in lattice calculations of nucleon correlation functions is the contribution of excited states. We estimate these using chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT), and show that the leading contribution to the isoscalar scalar charge comes from N$π$ and N$ππ$ states. Therefore, we carry out two analyses of lattice data to remove excited-state contamination, the standard one and a new one including N$π$ and N$ππ$ states. We find that the standard analysis gives $σ_{πN} = 41.9(4.9)$ MeV, consistent with previous lattice calculations, while our preferred $χ$PT-motivated analysis gives $σ_{πN} = 59.6(7.4)$ MeV, which is consistent with phenomenological values obtained using $π$N scattering data. Our data on one physical pion mass ensemble was crucial for exposing this difference, therefore, calculations on additional physical mass ensembles are needed to confirm our result and resolve the tension between lattice QCD and phenomenology.

## Key claim

The authors find that including low-lying Nπ and Nππ excited states in the analysis of lattice data raises the pion-nucleon sigma term from 41.9(4.9) MeV (standard analysis) to 59.6(7.4) MeV (χPT-motivated analysis), reconciling the long-standing tension between lattice QCD and phenomenological determinations, but they caution that this result relies on a single physical pion mass ensemble and requires validation on additional ensembles.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of the isoscalar scalar charge g_S^{u+d} using six 2+1+1-flavor highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) ensembles generated by the MILC collaboration, with lattice spacings a ≈ 0.12, 0.09, and 0.06 fm and pion masses Mπ ≈ 315, 230, and 138 MeV. They extracted the ground-state matrix element from simultaneous fits to nucleon 2-point and 3-point correlation functions using two strategies: a standard fit with four states in the 2-point function and three in the 3-point function ({4,3*}), and a new fit where the first excited state mass was constrained to the noninteracting Nπ/Nππ energy ({4^{Nπ},3*}), motivated by heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- Standard analysis ({4,3*}): σπN = 41.9(4.9) MeV
- χPT-motivated analysis ({4^{Nπ},3*}): σπN = 59.6(7.4) MeV
- Phenomenological value from Roy-Steiner equations updated with pionic hydrogen width data: σπN = 59.0(3.5) MeV (Hoferichter 2015)
- Global analysis of low-energy πN data in Roy-Steiner framework: σπN = 58(5) MeV (Ruiz de Elvira 2017)
- For the physical pion mass ensemble a09m130 (Mπ = 138 MeV), the {4,3*} fit gave σπN = 40(3) MeV while the {4^{Nπ},3*} fit gave σπN = 55(8) MeV
- Previous lattice results: ETM 2019 found σπN = 41.6(3.8) MeV; BMW 2020 found σπN = 37.4(5.1) MeV

## Context

This work addresses the persistent tension between lattice QCD calculations, which historically yielded σπN ≈ 40 MeV, and phenomenological determinations from πN scattering data and pionic-atom measurements, which give σπN ≈ 59 MeV (Gasser 1991, Pavan 2002, Alarcon 2011, Hoferichter 2015, Ruiz de Elvira 2017). The authors show that including Nπ and Nππ excited states, which were omitted in previous lattice calculations such as ETM 2019 and BMW 2020, raises the lattice result to agree with phenomenology. This enables future lattice calculations on additional physical mass ensembles to confirm the result and resolve the tension, while also providing improved input for dark matter direct detection and searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.
