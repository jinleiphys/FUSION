# Slope parameter of the symmetry energy and the structure of three-particle interactions in nuclear matter

> In the first part of this paper, we present a study of the symmetry energy ($a_s$) and its slope parameter ($L$) for nuclear matter in the framework of the Fermi liquid theory of Landau and Migdal. We derive an exact relation between $a_s$ and $L$, which involves the nucleon effective masses and three-particle Landau-Migdal parameters. We present simple estimates which suggest that there are two main mechanisms to explain the empirical values of $L$: The proton-neutron effective mass difference in isospin asymmetric matter and the $\ell=0$ moment of the isovector in-medium three-particle scattering amplitude. In the second part of this paper, we discuss the general structure of three-particle interactions in nuclear matter in the framework of the Fermi liquid theory. The connections to the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone theory and other approaches are also discussed. We show explicitly how the first few terms in the Faddeev series, together with medium induced three-particle interactions, emerge naturally in the Fermi liquid theory.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact, model-independent relation between the symmetry energy slope parameter \(L\) and the symmetry energy \(a_s\) in nuclear matter using Landau-Migdal Fermi liquid theory, showing that the empirical value of \(L\) can be explained by two main mechanisms: the proton-neutron effective mass difference in isospin-asymmetric matter and the \(\ell=0\) moment of the isovector in-medium three-particle scattering amplitude, with the caveat that the three-particle contribution is necessary unless the effective mass difference parameter \(\mu\) is large.

## Method

The authors used Landau-Migdal Fermi liquid theory to expand the energy density of nuclear matter to third order in density variations, deriving an exact relation between the symmetry energy \(a_s\) and its slope parameter \(L\) that involves nucleon effective masses, the proton-neutron effective mass difference, and three-particle Landau-Migdal parameters. They then performed semi-quantitative estimates by approximating the three-particle amplitude using the two-particle correlation (driving term) of the in-medium Faddeev series, representing the two-particle \(t\)-matrix as an effective contact interaction and evaluating angular integrals analytically.

## Key numbers

- Empirical symmetry energy: \(a_s = 31.6 \pm 2.66\) MeV at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.155\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Empirical slope parameter: \(L = 59 \pm 16\) MeV at normal nuclear matter density
- Empirical effective mass difference parameter: \(\mu = 0.27 \pm 0.25\)
- The quantity \(3a_s - L\) lies between 12 and 60 MeV from empirical values
- Normal nuclear matter Fermi energy: \(p^2/2M = 36\) MeV
- The nucleon effective mass range considered: \(0.7 < M^*/M < 1.0\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of a similar relation between the skewness of nuclear matter and three-particle interaction parameters (Bentz 2019) to the isovector case. The relation connects to empirical analyses of the symmetry energy and its slope from nuclear structure and neutron star observations summarized by Li (2018), and to the broader framework of Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone theory for three-particle interactions in nuclear matter. The results enable a model-independent understanding of how three-particle forces contribute to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which is critical for predicting neutron star radii and neutron-skin thicknesses in nuclei like \(^{208}\)Pb currently under experimental investigation at Jefferson Lab.
