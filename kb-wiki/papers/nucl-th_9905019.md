# Ground-state properties of deformed proton emitters in the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

> The Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) model is applied in the description of ground-state properties of proton-rich odd-Z nuclei in the region $53 \leq Z \leq 69$. The NL3 effective interaction is used in the mean-field Lagrangian, and pairing correlations are described by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction D1S. The model predicts the location of the proton drip-line, the ground-state quadrupole deformations and one-proton separation energies at and beyond the drip-line, the deformed single-particle orbitals occupied by the odd valence proton, and the corresponding spectroscopic factors. The results of fully self-consistent RHB calculations are compared with available experimental data, and with predictions of the macroscopic-microscopic mass model.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model, using the NL3 effective interaction for the mean field and the Gogny D1S interaction for pairing, can predict ground-state properties of deformed proton-rich odd-Z nuclei in the region 53 ≤ Z ≤ 69, including the location of the proton drip-line, but the model's accuracy for nuclei at the drip-line depends on the use of a large oscillator basis expansion rather than a coordinate-space solution, which may limit precision for properties sensitive to spatial extension.

## Method

The RHB equations were solved self-consistently by expanding the nucleon spinors and meson fields in the eigenfunctions of a deformed axially symmetric oscillator potential, assuming axial symmetry and neglecting time-odd components of the meson fields. The NL3 parameter set was used for the relativistic mean-field Lagrangian, and pairing correlations were described by the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S in the particle-particle channel. For odd-Z nuclei, the blocking procedure was applied by selecting the negative quasiparticle eigenvalue for the blocked level, and the canonical basis was used to determine single-particle energies and occupation probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The model was applied to proton-rich odd-Z nuclei in the region 53 ≤ Z ≤ 69.
- The NL3 effective interaction was used for the mean-field Lagrangian, and the Gogny D1S interaction for pairing.
- The proton drip-line was predicted for nuclei in this region, with one-proton separation energies calculated at and beyond the drip-line.
- Ground-state quadrupole deformations and spectroscopic factors for the odd valence proton were computed.
- Results were compared with available experimental data and with predictions of the macroscopic-microscopic mass model.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Vretenar et al. (1998) to a broader region of deformed proton-rich odd-Z nuclei (53 ≤ Z ≤ 69), building on previous RHB studies of spherical even-even proton-rich nuclei (Vretenar et al., 1998) and of the two-proton drip-line using the RMF+BCS model (Lalazissis and Ring, 1998). The results enable comparison with experimental data on ground-state proton radioactivity from deformed systems, as reported by Davids et al. (1998), and provide predictions for the Nilsson configurations of odd protons that can be tested by future measurements of proton decay rates.
