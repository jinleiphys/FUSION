# Potential resonant screening effects on stellar C12+C12 reaction rates

> The C12+C12 fusion cross sections show resonant behavior down to the lowest energies accessible so far in the laboratory. If this tendency continues into the astrophysical energy range, the stellar C12+C12 reaction rates have to be corrected for resonant screening effects, in addition to the conventional screening corrections. We estimate the resonant screening effects in the weak electron screening limit for hydrostatic burning and white dwarf environments.

# Potential resonant screening effects on stellar C12+C12 reaction rates

## Key claim

If the resonant structure observed in 12C+12C fusion cross sections at laboratory energies down to ~2.4 MeV continues into the astrophysically relevant energy range below 2.4 MeV, the stellar reaction rate must be corrected for resonant screening effects in addition to conventional global screening. This resonant screening correction reduces the cross section, counteracting the overall enhancement from global screening, but only applies if the reaction proceeds via narrow resonances and if the screening potential varies significantly under the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors apply the formalism of Salpeter and van Horn (1969) for narrow, isolated resonances to estimate the resonant screening correction factor f_r = Gamma'(E_r')/Gamma(E_r) for the 12C+12C reaction. They assume the weak screening limit for hydrostatic burning environments (where V_sc(r) is approximately constant under the barrier, yielding no resonant correction) and the cold, dense plasma formalism for white dwarf conditions (where V_sc(r) decreases under the barrier, reducing the resonance width). The correction factor is evaluated hypothetically for resonance energies in the interval 0.4–2 MeV, using the analytical expression derived by Salpeter and van Horn (1969) for dense plasmas.

## Key numbers

- For a carbon white dwarf environment at T = 5 × 10^7 K and ρ = 2 × 10^9 g/cm^3, the resonant screening correction can reduce the reaction rate by more than 11 orders of magnitude if the resonance energy is as low as E_r = 400 keV.
- Global screening in the same white dwarf environment enhances the rate by 12 orders of magnitude (based on Table 1 of Ichimaru (1993)), so the net effect depends on the balance between these two corrections.
- The observed resonant structures in laboratory data exist down to E ~ 2.4 MeV (Kettner et al., 1980), with total resonance widths of order ~100 keV.
- For hydrostatic carbon burning in a massive star (~20 M_sun) at T ~ 10^9 K and ρ = 2 × 10^5 g/cm^3, no resonant screening correction is expected because the weak screening limit holds and V_sc(r) is approximately constant under the barrier.

## Context

This work extends the general theory of resonant screening corrections developed by Salpeter and van Horn (1969) to the specific case of 12C+12C fusion, which had previously been treated only with global screening enhancement (Salpeter, 1954). The paper identifies that previous astrophysical applications ignored the resonant structure of the 12C+12C cross section and used a smooth average rate. If narrow resonances exist at astrophysical energies, the resonant screening correction could significantly affect carbon ignition densities in white dwarfs, motivating experimental searches for resonances below 2.4 MeV and further theoretical study of plasma enhancement factors including nuclear potentials that support low-energy resonances (Ogata et al., 1991).
