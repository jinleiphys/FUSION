# Overscreened Kondo effect, (color) superconductivity and Shiba states in Dirac metals and quark matter

> We study the interplay between the Kondo effect and (color) superconductivity in doped Dirac metals with magnetic impurities and in quark matter with colorful impurities. We first point out that the overscreened Kondo effect arises in the normal state of these systems. Next the (color) superconducting gap is incorporated as a mean field and the phase diagram for a varying gap and temperature is constructed nonperturbatively. A rich phase structure emerges from a competition of effects unique to a multichannel system. The Kondo-screened phase is shown to disappear for a sufficiently large gap. Peculiarity of quark matter due to the confining property of non-Abelian gauge fields is noted. We also investigate the spectrum of sub-gap excited states, called Shiba states. Based on a model calculation and physical reasoning we predict that, as the coupling of the impurity to the bulk is increased, there will be more than one quantum phase transition due to level crossing among overscreened states.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in doped Dirac metals and quark matter, the Kondo effect is actually the *overscreened* Kondo effect (rather than the exactly screened type) due to the multiplicity of channel degrees of freedom (chirality, color, flavor, spin), and that this overscreened phase is suppressed by a sufficiently large (color) superconducting gap, giving way to Shiba states and multiple quantum phase transitions.

## Method

The authors perform a two-loop renormalization-group (poor man's scaling) analysis of relativistic Coqblin-Schrieffer-type models with SU(N_c) color symmetry and N_f flavors to derive the beta function and identify intermediate fixed points. They then incorporate a (color) superconducting gap as a mean field and construct the phase diagram as a function of gap and temperature using scaling arguments and existing numerical renormalization group (NRG) results, supplemented by a slave-boson mean-field theory and T-matrix calculations for Shiba states in Dirac superconductors.

## Key numbers

* The beta function for the model with N_c colors and N_f flavors is β(Ĝ) = (1/2) N_c Ĝ²(-1 + N_f Ĝ), with a nontrivial fixed point at Ĝ = 1/N_f.
* The beta function for the spin-only model (no color) is β(Ĝ) = Ĝ²(-1 + (N_f/2) Ĝ), with a fixed point at Ĝ = 2/N_f.
* The Kondo temperature for the color model scales as T_K ~ D [Ĝ(D)]^(2N_f/N_c) exp(-2/(N_c Ĝ(D))).
* The Kondo temperature for the spin-only model scales as T_K ~ D exp(-1/Ĝ) Ĝ^(N_f/2).
* In the single-channel spin-1/2 Kondo model, the zero-temperature transition between screened and unscreened states occurs at Δ ≃ 3.3 T_K (citing Satori (1992)).
* The Kondo scale for QCD quark matter is Λ_K ∝ μ exp(-8π²/(N_c g²)), where g is the renormalized gauge coupling.

## Context

This work extends the study of Kondo impurities in gapped superconductors (Shiba (1968), Sakurai (1970), Satori (1992)) and in pseudogap Fermi systems (Withoff and Fradkin (1990), Ingersent (1996, 1998)) to the multichannel overscreened case in relativistic Dirac systems and quark matter. It also builds on recent proposals for the QCD Kondo effect (Yasui (2013), Hattori (2015)) by incorporating the competition with color superconductivity, which was neglected in those prior studies. The results enable future experimental searches for overscreened Kondo physics in Dirac/Weyl semimetals and theoretical studies of Shiba states in color superconductors.
