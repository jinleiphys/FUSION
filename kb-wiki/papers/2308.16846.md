# Upper Limit on the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Isobar Collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is a phenomenon that arises from the QCD anomaly in the presence of an external magnetic field. The experimental search for its evidence has been one of the key goals of the physics program of the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider. The STAR collaboration has previously presented the results of a blind analysis of isobar collisions (${^{96}_{44}\text{Ru}}+{^{96}_{44}\text{Ru}}$, ${^{96}_{40}\text{Zr}}+{^{96}_{40}\text{Zr}}$) in the search for the CME. The isobar ratio ($Y$) of CME-sensitive observable, charge separation scaled by elliptic anisotropy, is close to but systematically larger than the inverse multiplicity ratio, the naive background baseline. This indicates the potential existence of a CME signal and the presence of remaining nonflow background due to two- and three-particle correlations, which are different between the isobars. In this post-blind analysis, we estimate the contributions from those nonflow correlations as a background baseline to $Y$, utilizing the isobar data as well as Heavy Ion Jet Interaction Generator simulations. This baseline is found consistent with the isobar ratio measurement, and an upper limit of 10% at 95% confidence level is extracted for the CME fraction in the charge separation measurement in isobar collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Key claim
The paper reports that the isobar ratio of the CME-sensitive observable is consistent with a refined background baseline, and extracts an upper limit of 10% at 95% confidence level for the CME fraction in the charge separation measurement, rather than claiming a definitive detection.

## Method
The STAR Collaboration used isobar collisions of Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV to search for the chiral magnetic effect. They estimated nonflow background contributions from two-particle and three-particle correlations using both the isobar data and HIJING simulations, decomposing the isobar ratio Y into components from flowing clusters, nonflow contamination in elliptic flow, and genuine three-particle correlations.

## Key numbers
- Upper limit of 10% at 95% confidence level for the CME fraction in the charge separation measurement in isobar collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- The two isobars differ by up to 4.4% in produced charged particle multiplicities and 1.4% in elliptic flows
- The nonflow contamination parameter epsilon_nf ranges from 18-34% depending on analysis methods
- The relative difference of the fitted true v2 parameter between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions is approximately 2.2% in the 20-50% centrality range
- The delta(C_2p/N)/(C_2p/N) value averaged over 20-50% centrality is on the order of -3%
- The contribution to Y_bkgd from three-particle correlation backgrounds in 20-50% centrality is around -1.3%

## Context
This work extends the STAR blind analysis of isobar collisions (STAR 2021) that found the isobar ratio Y was systematically larger than the inverse multiplicity ratio, suggesting potential CME signal. The present analysis provides a more rigorous background baseline using two-particle and three-particle correlations, finding consistency with the data and enabling an upper limit extraction. This addresses the need for proper background assessment raised by the earlier isobar results and provides a framework for future CME searches at RHIC and the LHC.
