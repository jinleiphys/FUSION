# Path Integral Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations of Light Nuclei

> We describe a path-integral ground-state quantum Monte Carlo method for light nuclei in continuous space. We show how to efficiently update and sample the paths with spin-isospin dependent and spin-orbit interactions. We apply the method to the triton and alpha particle using both local chiral interactions with next-to-next-to-leading-order %(N$^2$LO) and the Argonne interactions. For operators, like the total energy, that commute with the Hamiltonian, our results agree with Green's function Monte Carlo and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations. For operators that do not commute with the Hamiltonian and for Euclidean response functions, the path-integral formulation allows straightforward calculation without forward walking or the increased variance typical of diffusion methods. We demonstrate this by calculating density distributions, root mean square radii, and Euclidean response functions for single-nucleon couplings.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that path-integral quantum Monte Carlo (PIMC) can calculate ground-state properties of light nuclei (A ≤ 4) with spin-isospin dependent and spin-orbit interactions, but only for s-wave nuclei where the fermion sign or phase problem is weak enough that calculations converge before substantial sign problems manifest.

## Method

The authors implement a real-space path-integral ground-state quantum Monte Carlo method using a Trotter breakup of the short-time propagator, with multilevel Metropolis sampling of bead positions and separate sampling of correlation operator orders in the trial wave function. They apply the method to the triton (³H) and alpha particle (⁴He) using local chiral interactions at next-to-next-to-leading-order (N²LO) and the Argonne AV6' and AV8' interactions, calculating ground-state energies, density distributions, root-mean-square radii, and Euclidean response functions for single-nucleon couplings.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy for ⁴He: calculated with τ = 0.1 MeV⁻¹ total imaginary time, with energy gap to excited states of about 20 MeV and variational bound of about -23 MeV from the trial wave function
- The free-particle propagator uses ħ²/(2m) = 20.375 fm²
- ⁴He has 96 spin-isospin basis states (6 × 16 = 96) from (A!/(Z!(A-Z)!)) × 2^A = 6 × 16
- Multilevel sampling uses about 80% of moves with n₁ ≈ N/3 beads (acceptance rate ~20%) and about 20% with n₂ ≈ N/6 beads (acceptance rate ~40%)
- For AV6' interactions, time-step error scales as Δτ²; for N²LO and AV8' interactions with spin-orbit, time-step error scales linearly as C₁(Δτ)

## Context

This work extends path-integral Monte Carlo methods (Ceperley 1995, Schmidt 2000) from spin-independent interactions to realistic nuclear Hamiltonians with spin-isospin and spin-orbit dependence. It provides an alternative to Green's function Monte Carlo (Carlson 1987) and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (Schmidt 1999) for calculating operators that do not commute with the Hamiltonian, such as density distributions and response functions, without requiring forward walking techniques (Runge 1992). The method enables straightforward calculation of Euclidean response functions for single-nucleon couplings (Carlson 1994) and can be extended to include three-body interactions in future work.
