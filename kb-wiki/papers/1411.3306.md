# Momentum distributions for $^2$H$(e,e'p)$

> Background: A primary goal of deuteron electrodisintegration is the possibility of extracting the deuteron momentum distribution. This extraction is inherently fraught with difficulty, as the momentum distribution is not an observable and the extraction relies on theoretical models dependent on other models as input. Purpose: We present a new method for extracting the momentum distribution which takes into account a wide variety of model inputs thus providing a theoretical uncertainty due to the various model constituents. Method: The calculations presented here are using a Bethe-Salpeter like formalism with a wide variety of bound state wave functions, form factors, and final state interactions. We present a method to extract the momentum distributions from experimental cross sections, which takes into account the theoretical uncertainty from the various model constituents entering the calculation. Results: In order to test the extraction pseudo-data was generated, and the extracted "experimental" distribution, which has theoretical uncertainty from the various model inputs, was compared with the theoretical distribution used to generate the pseudo-data. Conclusions: In the examples we compared, the original distribution was typically within the error band of the extracted distribution. The input wave functions do contain some outliers which are discussed in the text, but at least this procedure can provide an upper bound on the deuteron momentum distribution. Due to the reliance on the theoretical calculation to obtain this quantity any extraction method should account for the theoretical error inherent in these calculations due to model inputs.

## Key claim

The authors present a new method for extracting the deuteron momentum distribution from $^2$H$(e,e'p)$ cross sections that explicitly accounts for theoretical uncertainty from model inputs (wave functions, form factors, and final state interactions), but caution that the extracted distribution provides only an upper bound for all but the softest potentials, and that the original theoretical distribution is typically within the error band of the extracted distribution.

## Method

The authors use a Bethe-Salpeter-like formalism in impulse approximation to calculate cross sections for deuteron electrodisintegration at the kinematics of Jefferson Lab experiment E1210003 (incident electron energy 12 GeV, $Q^2 = 4.25$ GeV$^2$, $x = 1.35$, $\phi_p = 180^\circ$). They generate 48 distinct calculations by combining 8 deuteron wave functions (IIB, WJC 1, WJC 2, AV18, CD Bonn, NIJM 1, NIJM 2, NIJM 3), 3 nucleon electromagnetic form factor parameterizations (GKex05, AMT, MMD), and 2 final state interaction models (SAID helicity amplitudes and Regge model). Pseudo-data are generated from selected calculations, and the extraction procedure subtracts the average difference between reduced cross sections and theoretical momentum distributions across all 48 models, with the standard deviation providing the theoretical error band.

## Key numbers

- The kinematics are chosen from experiment E1210003: $\epsilon = 12$ GeV, $Q^2 = 4.25$ GeV$^2$, $x = 1.35$, $\phi_p = 180^\circ$
- The square of the invariant mass of the final state is $s = 5.5568$ GeV$^2$, above the pion-production threshold
- SAID amplitudes are reliable up to about $T_{lab} = 1.3$ GeV or $s = 5.9675$ GeV$^2$; the Regge model covers from $s = 5.4$ GeV$^2$ to $s = 4000$ GeV$^2$
- The PWIA cross section envelope covers a range of more than an order of magnitude at $p_m = 1$ GeV
- Above approximately $p_m = 0.65$ GeV, the envelope for the full IA calculations with FSI begins to narrow significantly
- For the CD Bonn wave function (the softest used), the extracted momentum distribution is much larger than the calculated distribution, demonstrating the upper-bound nature of the method

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to extract the deuteron momentum distribution from electrodisintegration data, which have relied on searching for kinematic regions where final state interactions and two-body currents are small (Boeglin 2011). The authors note that prior approaches using the IIB wave function, GKex05 form factors, and Regge model FSI (Van Orden 2008, 2009; Ford 2013) showed that the choice of optimal kinematics is model dependent. The new method enables a statistical treatment of theoretical corrections with an error band, which the authors state will be helpful for analyzing data from Jefferson Lab's 12 GeV upgrade, specifically experiment E1210003. Similar calculations using different frameworks (Ciofi degli Atti 2004, Laget 2004, Sargsian 2009) could be combined with these results to establish possible variations in theoretical models.
