# The contact in the unitary Fermi gas across the superfluid phase transition

> A quantity known as the contact plays a fundamental role in quantum many-body systems with short-range interactions. The determination of the temperature dependence of the contact for the unitary Fermi gas of infinite scattering length has been a major challenge, with different calculations yielding qualitatively different results. Here we use finite-temperature auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) methods on the lattice within the canonical ensemble to calculate the temperature dependence of the contact for the homogeneous spin-balanced unitary Fermi gas. We extrapolate to the continuum limit for 40, 66, and 114 particles. We observe a dramatic decrease in the contact as the superfluid critical temperature is approached from below, followed by a gradual weak decrease as the temperature increases in the normal phase. Our results are in excellent agreement with the most recent precision ultracold atomic gas experiments. We also present results for the energy of the unitary gas as a function of temperature in the continuum limit.

## Key claim

The authors use canonical-ensemble auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) on a lattice with extrapolation to the continuum limit to calculate the temperature dependence of the contact for the homogeneous spin-balanced unitary Fermi gas, finding a dramatic decrease in the contact as the superfluid critical temperature is approached from below and a gradual weak decrease above it; the abstract does not specify that the results are for 40, 66, and 114 particles with extrapolations to zero filling factor and zero Trotter time step.

## Method

The authors discretize space on a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions and a zero-range interaction tuned to infinite scattering length. They perform canonical-ensemble auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) simulations for N=40, 66, and 114 particles on lattices of sizes N_L^3 from 5^3 to 15^3, using a quadratic single-particle dispersion relation. For each particle number, they extrapolate to the continuum limit by fitting linearly in the cube root of the filling factor nu = N/N_L^3, and also extrapolate to zero Trotter time step Delta beta using a quadratic dependence. The contact is computed from the thermal expectation value of the potential energy via C = (m^2 V_0 <V>)/hbar^4.

## Key numbers

- Contact C (in units of N k_F) shows a dramatic decrease as temperature approaches the superfluid critical temperature T_c ~ 0.15 T_F from below, followed by a gradual weak decrease above T_c.
- The Bertsch parameter xi = 0.367(7), extracted from the zero-temperature limit of the thermal energy for N=40 and N=66 particles.
- Thermal energy E (in units of the non-interacting Fermi gas ground-state energy E_FG = (3/5) N epsilon_F) is calculated as a function of T/T_F for N=40 and N=66 particles in the continuum limit.
- Simulations are limited to T less than or approximately 0.45 T_F.
- The contact results for N=66 and N=114 particles show little systematic difference from N=40 particle results, suggesting proximity to the thermodynamic limit.

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature AFMC calculations by Drut (2011) which were performed in the grand-canonical ensemble at finite filling factor and gave qualitatively different results for the contact above T_c. The results agree well with the recent precision ultracold atomic gas experiments of Carcy (2019) and Mukherjee (2019), and show similar qualitative behavior to the diagrammatic Monte Carlo approach of Goulko (2016) below T_c but different behavior above T_c, while being systematically below the bold diagrammatic Monte Carlo results of Rossi (2018) above T_c. The calculations enable quantitative comparison with experiment across the superfluid phase transition and provide the best agreement among available theoretical results.
