# Constraining the CME in AVFD-simulated heavy-ion collisions using the Sliding Dumbbell Method

> The Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) framework is utilized to generate $^{197}_{79}Au+^{197}_{79}Au$, $^{96}_{44}Ru+^{96}_{44}Ru$, and $^{96}_{40}Zr+^{96}_{40}Zr$ collision events at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV to investigate the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME). The CME signal is modulated through the axial charge per entropy density ($n_5/s$) in each event to produce data sets with varying CME signal strengths. Additionally, a 33$\%$ local charge conservation (LCC) is implemented in each event. These data sets are analyzed using CME-sensitive two- and three-particle correlators. Furthermore, the Sliding Dumbbell Method (SDM) is employed to identify potential CME-like events within each data set. The identified events selected using the SDM exhibit characteristics consistent with CME. The CME fraction in these events is quantified while accounting for background contributions.

## Key claim

The Sliding Dumbbell Method (SDM) can identify CME-like events in AVFD-simulated heavy-ion collisions, and the extracted CME fraction increases with injected CME signal strength; however, even at zero injected CME (n5/s=0.0), a positive CME fraction is observed due to the 33% local charge conservation (LCC) mimicking a CME signal, and in isobaric collisions the CME fraction does not increase detectably from n5/s=0.0 to n5/s=0.1 due to background from lower multiplicities.

## Method

The authors used the EBE-AVFD Beta1.0 framework to generate Au+Au, Ru+Ru, and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with axial charge per entropy density values n5/s = 0.0, 0.1, and 0.2, each with 33% local charge conservation, for 30-40% collision centrality. They applied the Sliding Dumbbell Method (SDM) to scan the azimuthal plane of each event and compute the fractional charge separation fDbCS, then divided events into ten percentile bins of fDbCS. The γ-correlator, δ-correlator, and Δγ were calculated for each fDbCS bin, with background contributions estimated using charge-shuffle and correlated background samples.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions in the top 20% fDbCS bins, fCME increases from 11.70±0.35% at n5/s=0.0 to 39.30±0.20% at n5/s=0.2
- For Ru+Ru collisions in the top 20% fDbCS bins, fCME increases from 5.39±0.53% at n5/s=0.0 to 9.43±0.56% at n5/s=0.2
- For Zr+Zr collisions in the top 20% fDbCS bins, fCME increases from 3.58±0.59% at n5/s=0.0 to 9.50±0.54% at n5/s=0.2
- Δγ for the top 10% fDbCS bin is approximately ten times greater than values observed for the overall centrality across all data sets
- The number of events analyzed: Au+Au ~95M (n5/s=0.0), ~58M (n5/s=0.1), ~77M (n5/s=0.2); Ru+Ru ~58M (n5/s=0.0), ~49M (n5/s=0.1), ~50M (n5/s=0.2); Zr+Zr ~48M (n5/s=0.0), ~71M (n5/s=0.1), ~56M (n5/s=0.2)

## Context

This work extends the AVFD framework studies of the Chiral Magnetic Effect by applying the Sliding Dumbbell Method, originally developed by Aggarwal (2022), to simulated Au+Au and isobaric collisions. The results are consistent with experimental observations from STAR (2022) that found no enhanced CME signal in Ru+Ru compared to Zr+Zr collisions. The analysis enables future application of the SDM to experimental data from Au+Au, Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, and Pb+Pb collisions to validate the CME signal, as the method can identify potential CME-like events on an event-by-event basis while accounting for background contributions from local charge conservation.
