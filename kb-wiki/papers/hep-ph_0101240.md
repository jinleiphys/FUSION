# Relativistic corrections to the electromagnetic polarizabilities of compound systems

> The low-energy amplitude of Compton scattering on the bound state of two charged particles of arbitrary masses, charges and spins is calculated. A case in which the bound state exists due to electromagnetic interaction (QED) is considered. The term, proportional to $ω^2$, is obtained taking into account the first relativistic correction. It is shown that the complete result for this correction differs essentially from the commonly used term $Δα$, proportional to the r.m.s. charge radius of the system. We propose that the same situation can take place in the more complicated case of hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the complete first relativistic correction to the electric polarizability of a two-particle bound state differs essentially from the commonly used term Δα proportional to the r.m.s. charge radius, and proposes that the same situation can occur in hadron systems.

## Method

The authors calculate the low-energy Compton scattering amplitude for a bound state of two charged particles of arbitrary masses, charges, and spins, where the binding is due to electromagnetic interaction (QED). They use nonrelativistic expansion and include the first relativistic correction (Breit Hamiltonian) to obtain the O(ω²) term in the amplitude, computing contributions from corrections to the current, propagator, wave function, ground state energy, and the relativistic correction to the center-of-mass coordinate.

## Key numbers

- The nonrelativistic electric polarizability αₒₙᵣ for two spin-0 particles is 9/(2μ g⁴) × (e₁/m₁ − e₂/m₂)², where g = −e₁e₂ > 0, μ is the reduced mass, and a = 1/(μg) is the Bohr radius.
- The relativistic correction αₒB to the electric polarizability for two spin-0 particles is −(1/g²)(e₁/m₁ − e₂/m₂)²(121/(6μ) − 113/(4M)) − ((e₁+e₂)/(Mg²))(e₁/m₁ − e₂/m₂)(m₁−m₂)/(2m₁m₂).
- The final electric polarizability for two spin-0 particles is (1/(μg⁴))(e₁/m₁ − e₂/m₂)²[9/2 − g²(121/6 − 113μ/(4M))] + ((e₁+e₂)/(Mg²))[3/2(e₁/m₁² + e₂/m₂²) − (e₁+e₂)/(2m₁m₂)].
- For two spin-1/2 particles, the tensor polarizability α_T = −47(1+κ₁)(1+κ₂)/(40Mg²) × (e₁/m₁ − e₂/m₂)².
- For positronium, the paramagnetic contribution to magnetic polarizability β₁ = ±3/7 a³, where the upper sign is for parapositronium (S=0) and the lower sign for orthopositronium (S=1).

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic treatment of electromagnetic polarizabilities by Petrunkin (1961, 1964) and Baldin (1960), who derived the expression for electric polarizability as a sum of a dipole term αₒ and a relativistic term Δα proportional to the r.m.s. charge radius. The authors show that the complete relativistic correction modifies both αₒ and Δα significantly, resolving a long-standing contradiction in the nonrelativistic quark model where the predicted electric polarizabilities of proton and neutron differ due to Δα while experimental values are close. This enables more consistent calculations of nucleon polarizabilities in quark models and hadron theories.
