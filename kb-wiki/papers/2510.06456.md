# Impact of relativistic corrections to high-pT prompt-psi(2S) production at hadron colliders

> We study relativistic corrections to prompt psi(2S) at high pT in hadron colliders. Our calculation employs leading-power factorization with Fragmentation Functions (FFs) computed in nonrelativistic QCD and evolved to next-to-leading-logarithmic accuracy. Relativistic corrections increase the cross section significantly for the gluon channel, but moderately for charm. We perform a full analysis of uncertainties. We observe a good agreement with both ATLAS and CMS cross sections without the need of higher-order color-octet contributions. The polar anisotropy is found to be close to CMS data.

## Key claim
Relativistic corrections of order v^2 to the gluon fragmentation channel increase the prompt psi(2S) cross section by factors of 3.8 to 7.5 depending on the value of ⟨v^2⟩, while charm contributions are moderate and stable; this allows NLO+NLL leading-power results to agree with ATLAS and CMS data without invoking higher-order color-octet contributions.

## Method
The calculation uses leading-power (LP) factorization with fragmentation functions (FFs) computed in nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) at the initial scale μ0 ~ M, evolved to next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) accuracy via DGLAP equations using APFEL++. Short-distance coefficients (SDCs) are evaluated at NLO (O(α_s^3)) using INCNLO1.4, with CT18NLO PDFs. Relativistic O(v^2) corrections are included for the gluon (g → ψ(2S) gg) and charm (c → ψ(2S) c) channels, using the Gremm-Kapustin relation to connect the v^2 LDME to the leading CS LDME, with ⟨v^2⟩ values of 0.25, 0.35, and 0.5.

## Key numbers
- O(v^2) corrections boost the gluon-to-ψ(2S) FF Mellin moment at N=6.2 and μ=100 GeV by a factor of O(15.6 ⟨v^2⟩), while the charm FF moment gets O(-0.12 ⟨v^2⟩).
- At P_T=100 GeV, the gluon channel cross section increases by factors ranging from 3.8 to 7.5 depending on ⟨v^2⟩ (0.25 to 0.5).
- Three values of the radial wavefunction at the origin |R(0)|^2 are considered: 0.50, 0.75, and 1.00 GeV^3.
- The LP cross section central value increases by a factor of four compared to 1990s calculations after updating perturbative accuracy and input parameters.
- The μ_{R0} uncertainty on D_{g/ψ(2S)} alone gives a factor close to four, since α_s(m_c)/α_s(4m_c) ≈ 1.6.

## Context
This work extends the leading-power fragmentation approach to psi(2S) production, which in the 1990s (Braaten 1994, Cacciari 1994, Kramer 2001) gave cross sections O(30) times too small compared to CDF Tevatron data (the "CDF psi(2S) anomaly"). By including NLO SDCs, NLL evolution, updated PDFs and parameters, and O(v^2) relativistic corrections, the authors achieve agreement with recent ATLAS and CMS data without color-octet contributions. The results underscore the need for NLO corrections to the polarized gluon FF and for next-to-leading-power (NLP) corrections below P_T=30 GeV to address Tevatron data, and they enable improved determinations of color-octet LDMEs at O(v^{3,4}).
