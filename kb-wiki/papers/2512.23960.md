# Towards a bottom-up formulation of spin kinetic theory

> We develop a bottom-up formulation of spin-kinetic theory for hot and/or dense plasmas. We introduce scalar and axial-vector phase-space functions as dynamical variables that parametrize both spin-averaged and spin-dependent distribution functions. Using spin-dependent Poisson brackets, we derive the corresponding kinetic equations and construct the associated Schwinger-Keldysh action. We further demonstrate how physical observables can be expressed in terms of these dynamical variables through constitutive relations. In the linear response regime, we establish a precise matching between the kinetic-theory and field-theory descriptions of vector and axial Wigner functions under electromagnetic and gravitational perturbations. Our framework provides a complementary approach to describing the dynamics of spin effects in a medium.

## Key claim

The authors propose a "bottom-up" formulation of spin kinetic theory (SKT) for hot and/or dense plasmas, where the dynamical variables, a scalar \(f\) and an axial vector \(\vg\), are introduced phenomenologically and their equations of motion are constructed using spin-dependent Poisson brackets, rather than derived from microscopic Schwinger-Dyson equations. The framework is claimed to be complementary to existing "top-down" approaches, but its validity depends on matching the constitutive relations to microscopic field-theory calculations in the linear response regime, and the authors explicitly state that the construction is "a proposal that requires testing through microscopic calculations."

## Method

The authors introduce scalar \(f\) and axial-vector \(\vg\) phase-space functions as dynamical variables parametrizing spin-averaged and spin-dependent distribution functions. They derive kinetic equations using spin-dependent Poisson brackets (generalized from Delacretaz (2022)) and construct the corresponding Schwinger-Keldysh action. Physical observables, including vector and axial Wigner functions, are expressed in terms of these variables through constitutive relations, and the linear response of the kinetic theory is matched to field-theory calculations of Wigner functions under electromagnetic and gravitational perturbations.

## Key numbers

* The domain of applicability requires \(\gamma_R, m_D \ll \omega, q \ll T_{\text{eff}}\), where \(\gamma_R\) is the mean damping rate, \(m_D\) is the thermal mass, and \(T_{\text{eff}}\) is the effective temperature of quasiparticles.
* For a weakly coupled plasma, the coupling constant satisfies \(g_C < 1\), with \(m_D \sim g_C T_{\text{eff}}\) and \(\gamma_R \sim g_C^2 T_{\text{eff}}\).
* The linearized kinetic equations yield solutions \(\delta f(Q) = (\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{q} / (v \cdot Q)) \, f_0' \, \delta\epsilon(Q)\) and \(\mathbf{g}(Q) = (\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{q} / (v \cdot Q)) \, f_0' \, \boldsymbol{\zeta}(Q)\) in Fourier space, where \(f_0'\) is the derivative of the background distribution with respect to energy.

## Context

This work extends the "bottom-up" approach previously applied to spin-averaged kinetic theory (e.g., Landau Fermi liquid theory and hard thermal loop effective theory) to include spin degrees of freedom. It contrasts with the "top-down" formulations of spin kinetic theory developed by Hattori (2019), Weickgenannt (2019), Gao (2019), and others, which start from microscopic Schwinger-Dyson equations for Wigner functions. The framework enables a systematic description of spin dynamics in hot QCD matter, relevant for understanding Lambda hyperon polarization (STAR Collaboration, 2017, 2019) and vector meson phenomena in heavy-ion collisions, and provides a basis for incorporating collisional effects and fluctuations via the Schwinger-Keldysh action.
