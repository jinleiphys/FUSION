# Model independent constraints from vacuum and in-medium QCD Sum Rules

> We discuss QCD sum rule constraints based on moments of vector meson spectral distributions in the vacuum and in a nuclear medium. Sum rules for the two lowest moments of these spectral distributions do not suffer from uncertainties related to QCD condensates of dimension higher than four. We exemplify these relations for the case of the omega meson and discuss the issue of in-medium mass shifts from this viewpoint.

# Model independent constraints from vacuum and in-medium QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim
The two lowest moments (n=0,1) of vector meson spectral distributions provide model-independent QCD sum rule constraints that do not suffer from uncertainties related to four-quark condensates of dimension six, unlike the second moment (n=2) which does involve such condensates. These constraints are demonstrated for the omega meson, but the authors note that a reliable extraction of an in-medium "mass shift" from such sum rules is only meaningful when the in-medium spectrum shows a reasonably narrow quasiparticle excitation.

## Method
The authors start from the current-current correlation function for the isoscalar vector current and apply a twice-subtracted dispersion relation. They perform a Borel transform of the operator product expansion and expand the exponential to derive moment sum rules for the spectral function R(s) integrated up to a continuum threshold s0. The first two sum rules (eqs. c1 and c2) are evaluated using a Vector Meson Dominance model and then tested against a more realistic chiral effective Lagrangian calculation of the omega meson spectrum in vacuum and in nuclear matter at saturation density.

## Key numbers
- Continuum threshold in vacuum from VMD: sqrt(s0) = 1.16 GeV, using alpha_S(s0) ~ 0.4 and m_omega = 0.78 GeV
- Continuum threshold in vacuum from chiral model: sqrt(s0) = 1.25 GeV, with integral of R(s) = 0.29 GeV^2
- First moment sum rule in vacuum: integral of s R(s) = 0.19 GeV^4, compared to right-hand side 0.22 GeV^4 (consistency at 10% level)
- Continuum threshold in nuclear matter at rho0 = 0.17 fm^-3: sqrt(s0) = 1.08 GeV, with integral of R(s,rho0) = 0.26 GeV^2
- First moment sum rule in medium: integral of s R(s,rho0) = 0.11 GeV^4, compared to right-hand side 0.12 GeV^4
- In-medium "mass" from moment ratio for omega: m_bar = 0.65 GeV at rho = rho0
- Density-dependent correction delta c2(rho0) ~ 4 x 10^-3 GeV^4, dominated by the A1 term (quark momentum fraction A1 ~ 1)
- Gluon condensate used: <(alpha_S/pi) G^2> = (0.36 GeV)^4
- Chiral condensate: <m_u ubar u + m_d dbar d> ~ -(0.11 GeV)^4 from Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation
- Nucleon sigma term: sigma_N ~ 45 MeV
- Nucleon mass in chiral limit: M_N^(0) ~ 0.75 GeV

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to vector mesons in nuclear matter, addressing criticisms by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and others regarding the reliability of four-quark condensate factorization assumptions. The authors build on their own chiral effective Lagrangian calculations (Klingl and Weise, 1998) for the omega meson spectrum. The results enable model-independent tests of calculated in-medium spectral functions and suggest that the spectral gap sqrt(s0) scales with the chiral condensate, decreasing by about 15% in nuclear matter, consistent with expectations for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking.
