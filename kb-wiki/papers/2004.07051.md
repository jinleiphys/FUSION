# Application of Boltzmann equation to investigate kinetic processes of meson and baryon production in the quark-gluon plasma

> We consider the meson and baryon production in the quark-gluon plasma as applied to the early Universe and ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions in collider. The essential feature of our investigation is to take into account a potential barrier for quarks under meson and baryon production. We have used the Boltzmann equation to describe kinetic processes of quark fusion. We have derived analytic expressions for solutions of kinetic equations. This permits to find the dependence of reaction rate on a time, temperature, mass and charge of quarks as well as on a string tension connected with the confining potential. Next we have considered in detail the meson and baryon production for some concrete kinetic processes in collisions of ultrarelativistic heavy ions. Exactly the same results will be as well true for processes in expanding Universe

## Key claim

The paper claims that analytic solutions to the Boltzmann equation for quark fusion can describe meson and baryon production in the quark-gluon plasma, but only under the assumption that reverse processes (hadron decay into quarks) are absent, which is justified for temperatures below 180 MeV.

## Method

The author solves the Boltzmann equation for quark fusion into mesons and baryons using a velocity-averaged collision integral, where the fusion cross-section is derived from a quasi-classical tunneling probability through a confining potential barrier modeled as a spherical well with string tension. For baryon production, a two-step process is considered: first two quarks form a pair, then the pair fuses with a third quark, leading to a Bernoulli-type differential equation. The solutions are applied to both ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions and the early Universe, with the Hubble expansion term added to the kinetic equation for the cosmological case.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for QGP-hadron phase transition: 180-200 MeV
- For meson production at T = 170 MeV: initial quark density n(0) = 1.8 × 10^38 cm^-3
- Time for quark density reduction by factor β = 10^9 for J/ψ meson: τ = 1.98 × 10^-11 s
- Time for quark density reduction by factor β = 10^9 for π^0 meson: τ = 2.63 × 10^-11 s
- For Σ^+ baryon production at τ = 10^-11 s: β = 5.3 × 10^12
- For proton production at τ = 10^-11 s: β = 4.92 × 10^12
- Hubble parameter at phase transition time θ = 10^-5 s in early Universe: H(θ) = 6.67 × 10^4 s^-1
- String tension used for mesons: K_m = 0.9 GeV fm^-1 = 1.44 × 10^10 erg/cm
- Meson radius used: R_m = 1 fm = 10^-13 cm

## Context

This work extends prior studies of QGP kinetics using the Boltzmann equation (Kuz, Let) and hadron production in heavy ion collisions (Bla, Chaud, Emel, Grish, Vogt, Wong, Kohs) by incorporating a potential barrier for quark confinement via quasi-classical tunneling. The results are claimed to apply equally to the early Universe, following the approach of transferring heavy ion collision results to cosmology (Kap, Kohs). The method enables analytic expressions for reaction rates as functions of time, temperature, quark mass and charge, and string tension, which could be used to study phase transitions and quark annihilation processes in QGP.
