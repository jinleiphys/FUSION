# Plasma-balls in large N gauge theories and localized black holes

> We argue for the existence of plasma-balls - meta-stable, nearly homogeneous lumps of gluon plasma at just above the deconfinement energy density - in a class of large N confining gauge theories that undergo first order deconfinement transitions. Plasma-balls decay over a time scale of order N^2 by thermally radiating hadrons at the deconfinement temperature. In gauge theories that have a dual description that is well approximated by a theory of gravity in a warped geometry, we propose that plasma-balls map to a family of classically stable finite energy black holes localized in the IR. We present a conjecture for the qualitative nature of large mass black holes in such backgrounds, and numerically construct these black holes in a particular class of warped geometries. These black holes have novel properties; in particular their temperature approaches a nonzero constant value at large mass. Black holes dual to plasma-balls shrink as they decay by Hawking radiation; towards the end of this process they resemble ten dimensional Schwarzschild black holes, which we propose are dual to small plasma-balls. Our work may find practical applications in the study of the physics of localized black holes from a dual viewpoint.

## Key claim

The authors argue that "plasma-balls", meta-stable, nearly homogeneous lumps of gluon plasma at just above the deconfinement energy density, exist in a class of large N confining gauge theories that undergo first order deconfinement transitions, provided the theory obeys an additional condition (that the pressure of the plasma vanishes at the critical energy density). In gauge theories with a dual gravity description, these plasma-balls map to a family of classically stable finite energy black holes localized in the IR region of a warped geometry.

## Method

The authors use large N counting arguments to analyze the decay rate of plasma-balls via hadron emission, showing that glueball production from gluon-gluon collisions is suppressed by a factor of 1/N^2. They also construct a conjecture for the qualitative nature of large mass black holes in warped backgrounds, and numerically construct these black holes in a particular class of warped geometries, specifically, a Scherk-Schwarz compactification of Anti-de Sitter space, solving Einstein's equations with a negative cosmological constant to find the domain wall solution that interpolates between the black brane and the background at the phase transition temperature.

## Key numbers

- Plasma-balls decay over a time scale of order N^2 (where N is the number of colors in the SU(N) gauge theory)
- The energy density of the plasma-ball is of order N^2 in the large N limit
- The rate of glueball production from gluon-gluon collisions is independent of N (order N^0)
- The lifetime of a plasma-ball of radius R (with R much greater than 1/Lambda_gap) is of order N^2 R
- The temperature of large mass black holes approaches a nonzero constant value T_d (the deconfinement temperature) in the limit of infinite mass

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the AdS/CFT correspondence by connecting meta-stable plasma configurations in confining gauge theories to localized black hole solutions in warped geometries, building on earlier work by Giddings (2003) and Nastase (2004-2005) on black holes in gauge theories. The paper enables the study of localized black hole physics from a dual gauge theory viewpoint, and suggests that small plasma-balls correspond to ten-dimensional Schwarzschild black holes, while also proposing that the information paradox in Hawking radiation could be studied through the dual plasma-ball description.
