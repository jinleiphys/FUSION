# Spectroscopy of 232U in the (p,t) reaction: More information on 0+ states

> The excitation spectra in the deformed nucleus 232U have been studied by means of the (p,t) reaction, using the Q3D spectrograph facility at the Munich Tandem accelerator. The angular distributions of tritons were measured for 162 excitations seen in the triton spectra up to 3.25 MeV. 0+ assignments are made for 13 excited states by comparison of experimental angular distributions with the calculated ones using the CHUCK3 code. Assignments up to spin 6+ are made for other states. Sequences of states are selected which can be treated as rotational bands. Moments of inertia have been derived from these sequences, whose values may be considered as evidence of the two- or one-phonon nature of these 0+ excitations. Experimental data are compared with interacting boson model (IBM) and quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) calculations.

## Key claim

The paper reports 13 excited 0+ states in 232U identified via the (p,t) reaction, but the assignment for one state (at 1947.2 keV) is tentative, and the state at 927.2 keV is interpreted as an unresolved doublet of 0+ and 2+ states rather than a pure 0+ state.

## Method

The 234U(p,t)232U reaction was studied using 25 MeV protons from the Munich Tandem accelerator and the Q3D magnetic spectrograph, with triton angular distributions measured at twelve laboratory angles from 5° to 50°. Spin assignments were made by comparing experimental angular distributions to DWBA calculations using the CHUCK3 code, employing one-step and multi-step transfer schemes for states up to spin 6+. The analysis identified 162 excited levels up to 3.25 MeV excitation energy.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of 0+ excitations in actinide nuclei performed by the same group using the Q3D spectrograph, following similar analyses for 228Th and 230Th (Levon 2009, 2013). The results are compared with interacting boson model (IBM) and quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) calculations, and the derived moments of inertia from rotational bands built on 0+ states provide evidence for their one- or two-phonon nature. The data enable further theoretical understanding of multiple 0+ excitations in deformed nuclei, which remain poorly understood (Sheline 2008).
