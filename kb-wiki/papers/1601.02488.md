# Nucleon Resonance Structure Studies Via Exclusive KY Electroproduction

> Studying the structure of excited nucleon states employing the electroproduction of exclusive reactions is an important avenue for exploring the nature of the non-perturbative strong interaction. The electrocouplings of $N^*$ states in the mass range below 1.8~GeV have been determined from analyses of CLAS $πN$, $ηN$, and $ππN$ data. This work has made it clear that consistent results from independent analyses of several exclusive channels with different couplings and non-resonant backgrounds but the same $N^*$ electro-excitation amplitudes, is essential to have confidence in the extracted results. In terms of hadronic coupling, many high-lying $N^*$ states preferentially decay through the $ππN$ channel instead of $πN$. Data from the $KY$ channels will therefore be critical to provide an independent analysis to compare the extracted electrocouplings for the high-lying $N^*$ states against those determined from the $πN$ and $ππN$ channels. A program to study excited $N^*$ state structure in both non-strange and strange exclusive electroproduction channels using CLAS12 will measure differential cross sections and polarization observables to be used as input to extract the $γ_vNN^*$ electrocoupling amplitudes for the most prominent $N^*$ states in the range of invariant energy $W$ up 3~GeV in the virtually unexplored domain of momentum transfers $Q^2$ up to 12~GeV$^2$.

# Nucleon Resonance Structure Studies Via Exclusive KY Electroproduction

## Key claim

The electrocouplings of excited nucleon states (N*) in the mass range below 1.8 GeV have been determined from CLAS πN, ηN, and ππN data, but the paper argues that independent analyses from the KY (kaon-hyperon) channels are "critical" to provide a cross-check of the extracted electrocouplings for high-lying N* states, particularly because many of these states preferentially decay through ππN rather than πN. The paper acknowledges that while the KY data exist, reliable extraction of N* electrocouplings from these channels requires the development of adequate reaction models that currently do not exist.

## Method

The CLAS program measured differential cross sections and polarization observables (including separated structure functions σ_T, σ_L, σ_LT, σ_TT, σ_LT' and recoil polarization) for the exclusive electroproduction reactions γ*p → K+Λ and γ*p → K+Σ0. These measurements span Q² from 0.5 to 4.5 GeV², invariant energy W from 1.6 to 3.0 GeV, and the full center-of-mass angular range of the K+. The CLAS12 program will extend these measurements to Q² up to 12 GeV² and W up to 3 GeV using an 11 GeV electron beam at a luminosity of 1×10³⁵ cm⁻²s⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- N* electrocouplings extracted for states below 1.8 GeV from CLAS πN data for Q² up to 5 GeV², from ηp data for Q² up to 4 GeV², and from π⁺π⁻p data for Q² up to 1.5 GeV²
- CLAS KY electroproduction data span Q² from 0.5 to 4.5 GeV² and W from 1.6 to 3.0 GeV
- CLAS12 will operate at a nominal beam-target luminosity of 1×10³⁵ cm⁻²s⁻¹, an order of magnitude increase over CLAS
- CLAS12 will measure at Q² up to 12 GeV², the highest photon virtualities ever probed in exclusive electroproduction reactions
- The PDG lists only four N* states (N(1650)1/2⁻, N(1710)1/2⁺, N(1720)3/2⁺, N(1900)3/2⁺) with known couplings to KΛ, and no N* states listed that couple to KΣ
- Branching ratios to KY for these states are typically less than 10% with uncertainties on the order of the measured coupling

## Context

This work extends the CLAS N* program that previously extracted electrocouplings from πN, ηN, and ππN channels (Mokeev 2013, Mokeev 2015), which revealed that meson-baryon cloud contributions dominate at Q² < 1 GeV² while quark degrees of freedom dominate for Q² > 5 GeV². The paper contrasts the successful description of photoproduction data by the Maxwell isobar model (Maxwell 2012) and the Ghent Regge-plus-Resonance models (Corthals 2007, De Cruz 2011) with their complete failure to describe the electroproduction data. The CLAS12 program (experiments E12-09-003 and E12-06-108A, approved for first running in 2017) will enable extraction of γ_vNN* electrocouplings at Q² up to 12 GeV², allowing direct comparison with Lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger equation predictions for the dressed quark core, and providing constraints on N→N* transition Generalized Parton Distributions.
