# Helium halo nuclei from low-momentum interactions

> We present ground-state energies of helium halo nuclei based on chiral low-momentum interactions, using the hyperspherical-harmonics method for 6He and coupled-cluster theory for 8He, with correct asymptotics for the extended halo structure.

# Helium halo nuclei from low-momentum interactions

## Key claim

The ground-state energies of the helium halo nuclei 6He and 8He can be reproduced using chiral low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions alone only within a cutoff variation that is significantly larger than the many-body convergence uncertainties; the experimental energies fall within this cutoff variation, indicating that three-nucleon (3N) interactions are crucial for quantitative agreement, especially for 8He which remains underbound at all studied cutoffs when only NN forces are used.

## Method

The authors evolved chiral N3LO NN potentials (Entem-Machleidt with 500 MeV cutoff and Epelbaum et al. with 450–600 MeV cutoffs) via the renormalization group to low-momentum interactions V_lowk at cutoffs Λ = 1.8, 2.0, and 2.4 fm⁻¹ using a smooth regulator. For 6He, they employed the hyperspherical-harmonics (HH) method with a translationally-invariant Jacobi basis that provides correct asymptotic behavior for the halo wave function; for 8He, they used coupled-cluster theory at the CCSD and Λ-CCSD(T) levels in a Hartree-Fock basis. Both methods were benchmarked against exact Faddeev-Yakubovsky results for 4He.

## Key numbers

- 4He ground-state energy from HH method: −28.65(2) MeV for Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹, compared to exact Faddeev-Yakubovsky result −28.65(5) MeV (benchmark agreement)
- 4He ground-state energy from Λ-CCSD(T): −28.63 MeV for Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹, compared to exact FY result −28.65(5) MeV
- 6He extrapolated ground-state energy for Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹: −29.35(13) MeV, with experimental value −29.268 MeV
- 8He ground-state energy from Λ-CCSD(T) for Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹: −29.84 MeV, with experimental value −31.395 MeV (underbound by about 1.56 MeV)
- 8He ground-state energy from Λ-CCSD(T) for Λ = 1.8 fm⁻¹: −31.21 MeV, closest to experiment among NN-only results
- 6He HH calculation at largest basis (K_max = 14) included three million basis states

## Context

This work extends prior ab-initio calculations of helium isotopes using Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) methods with traditional potentials, and goes beyond a previous coupled-cluster study of helium isotopes (Hagen 2006) by including the exact hyperspherical-harmonics expansion for 6He and studying cutoff variation as a probe of many-body force effects. The results demonstrate that low-momentum interactions enable converged calculations for halo nuclei with correct asymptotic wave functions, and that the helium isotopes probe 3N effects beyond the overall repulsion seen in infinite nuclear and neutron matter. The work enables future inclusion of chiral 3N interactions in both HH and CC approaches to achieve quantitative agreement with experimental ground-state energies.
