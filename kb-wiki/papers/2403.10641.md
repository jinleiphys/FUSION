# Quark anomalous magnetic moment in an extreme magnetic background from perturbative QCD

> We compute the one-loop QCD correction to the photon-quark-antiquark vertex in an extremely strong magnetic background, i.e., one in which $\sqrt{eB}$ is much larger than all other mass scales. We resort to the lowest-Landau level approximation, and consider on shell fermions. We find that the total magnetic moment is such that the anomalous magnetic moment contributes to the electric part $\sim iE_3σ_3$, the other contributions to $\boldsymbol{B}\cdot\boldsymbolσ$ and $i\boldsymbol{E}\cdot\boldsymbolσ_\perp$ being completely suppressed. We show results for the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks up and down, scaled by their values in the vacuum, as a function of the external magnetic field, considering dressed gluons and a renormalization scale $μ_\mathrm{\tiny \overline{ MS}}=\sqrt{|eB|}$.

## Key claim

The one-loop QCD correction to the quark anomalous magnetic moment in an extremely strong magnetic background (where \(\sqrt{eB}\) is much larger than all other mass scales) produces a total magnetic moment where the anomalous contribution only appears in the electric part \(\sim iE_3\sigma_3\), while contributions to \(\boldsymbol{B}\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}\) and \(i\boldsymbol{E}\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}_\perp\) are completely suppressed; the result depends on the choice of gluon mass (constant or magnetically dressed) and the renormalization scale.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop QCD correction to the photon-quark-antiquark vertex in an extreme magnetic background using the lowest-Landau-level (LLL) approximation for on-shell fermions, with the magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis. They evaluated the triangular diagram with a gluon propagator, introducing an effective gluon mass \(m_g\) to regulate infrared divergences, and considered two scenarios: a fixed gluon mass \(m_g = 0.3\) GeV and a magnetically-dressed gluon mass derived from the one-loop gluon polarization tensor in the LLL approximation.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum one-loop QCD correction to the anomalous magnetic moment is given by \(a_q^\mathrm{vac} = \frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi} \frac{N_c^2-1}{2N_c}\).
- For a fixed gluon mass \(m_g = 0.3\) GeV and renormalization scale \(\mu_\mathrm{\overline{MS}} = \sqrt{|eB|}\), \(|a_u^\pm|\) exceeds its vacuum value for \(eB \gtrsim 2\) GeV\(^2\), and \(|a_d^\pm|\) exceeds its vacuum value for \(eB \gtrsim 4\) GeV\(^2\).
- The two polarization components satisfy \(a_q^- \approx -a_q^+\) to within \(\sim 1\%\) for the fixed gluon mass scenario.
- For a constituent quark mass \(m_q = 0.35\) GeV with fixed gluon mass \(m_g = 0.3\) GeV, the results show a shift in the positive direction with no remarkable qualitative modification.
- The magnetically-dressed gluon mass is given by \(\Pi_g(k_\parallel^2,k_\perp^2) = \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} N_c \sum_q \frac{e^{-\frac{1}{2}\ell_q^2 \boldsymbol{k}_\perp^2}}{\pi^2 \ell_q^2} g(m_q^2/k_\parallel^2)\), taken in the chiral limit \(g(0)=1\).

## Context

This work extends the calculation of the anomalous magnetic moment from radiative corrections to the fermion-photon vertex in strong magnetic fields, previously done for low magnetic fields using the Schwinger proper-time method by Lin (2021), to the regime of extremely strong magnetic fields relevant for heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. The results relate to the phenomenon of magnetic catalysis studied by Gusynin (1995), Shovkovy (2012), and Miransky (2015), and may inform studies of the chiral magnetic effect proposed by Fukushima (2008). The calculation enables future investigations of whether the quark anomalous magnetic moment becomes experimentally relevant in ultrahigh magnetic fields produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.
