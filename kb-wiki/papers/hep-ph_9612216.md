# Database on the Net for Particle Correlations and Spectra

> 9 pages, Latex, uses epsf.sty and sprocl.sty, to appear in the proceedings of International Workshop on Correlations and Fluctuations in Multiparticle Production, Nijmegen, The Netherlands, June 1996 (World Scientific, ed. W. Kittel et al.) Database is available at http://www.quark.lu.se/~janus/hbt_home.html

## Key claim

The authors present a fully interactive, web-based database for archiving and retrieving experimental data on single-particle spectra and two-particle correlations (HBT effect), but the paper is a description of the database's design and motivation rather than a report of new physics results. The database aims to make detailed measured distributions (not just fitted parameters) accessible to the community, with a clear separation between published and preliminary data.

## Method

The database was implemented using PHP/FI version 2.0 as a programming language inside HTML documents, together with the mSQL database program and the W3-mSQL interface, allowing the World-Wide-Web to serve as the user interface. The system supports uploading of ASCII data files and GIF picture files, with a recommended format for single-particle spectra (Table 1: momentum components, invariant distribution value, and error) and for correlation functions (Table 2: relative momentum components, actual and background pair distributions, uncorrected and fully corrected correlation functions with errors). Submitters must register to receive a password, while data retrieval requires no password; submitted data are sorted by beam energy, beam particle, and particle type.

## Key numbers

- The database was first reported at the 7th International Workshop on Multiparticle Production in Nijmegen, The Netherlands, June 30 – July 6, 1996.
- The database URL is given as: http://www.quark.lu.se/~janus/hbt_home.html
- The paper states that a Netscape browser was utilized in more than 80% of Internet explorations at the time of writing.
- The database separates data into four parts: HBT and IMD (invariant momentum distributions) for published and preliminary data, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the practice of publishing only fitted parameters (e.g., slope parameters of invariant momentum distributions or rapidity distributions) by providing a framework for archiving the full measured distributions. The authors note that recent theoretical developments (citing Csorgo and collaborators for the "halo" model and three-dimensional HBT analysis) and measurements (citing W.A. Zajc at HBT'96) show that spectra are not factorizable and that the rapidity and transverse-mass dependence is coupled, making two-dimensional distributions necessary. The database enables future re-analysis of data as new correction methods (e.g., Coulomb corrections) become available, and allows experimentalists to submit both published and preliminary data while maintaining clear separation between the two categories.
