# Resolving Ratio Redundancy in Chemical Freeze-out Studies with Principal Component Analysis and Bayesian Calibration

> We introduce a Principal Component Analysis (PCA)--Bayesian framework for extracting chemical freeze-out conditions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions that resolves long-standing ambiguities in hadron-ratio--based analyses. By constructing all possible hadron-yield ratios from a chosen set of species and transforming them into an orthogonal PCA basis, the method removes linear redundancies and eliminates the information loss and systematic uncertainties associated with ratio selection. Energy-wise Bayesian calibration of the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model is then performed directly in this decorrelated space, with a Gaussian Process emulator enabling fast and accurate model evaluations. A detailed Sobol sensitivity analysis, together with the PCA loading structure, identifies the most informative ratio combinations and reveals a transition from chemical-potential--dominated to temperature-controlled freeze-out with increasing $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. The calibrated model reproduces all measured ratios, and the extracted freeze-out parameters are consistent with previous HRG determinations.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a PCA-Bayesian framework that resolves the long-standing ambiguity in hadron-ratio-based chemical freeze-out analyses by constructing all possible ratios and transforming them into an orthogonal basis, thereby eliminating information loss and systematic uncertainties associated with ratio selection, though the method assumes independent Gaussian uncertainties for yields in the absence of published experimental covariance matrices.

## Method
The authors construct all distinct pairwise hadron-yield ratios from ten identified particle species (π⁺, π⁻, K⁺, K⁻, p, p̄, Λ, Λ̄, Ξ⁻, Ξ̄⁺) across collision energies from √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV, then apply Principal Component Analysis to the log-ratio ensemble to obtain an orthogonal basis. A Gaussian Process emulator is trained on 500 Latin Hypercube design points in the four-dimensional parameter space (T, μB, μS, μQ) to enable fast HRG model evaluations, and Bayesian calibration is performed using the affine-invariant ensemble MCMC algorithm in the decorrelated PC space.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy range: √sNN = 7.7 GeV (RHIC Beam Energy Scan) to 2.76 TeV (LHC)
- Number of hadron species used: 10 (π⁺, π⁻, K⁺, K⁻, p, p̄, Λ, Λ̄, Ξ⁻, Ξ̄⁺)
- Number of independent degrees of freedom recovered by PCA: N-1 = 9 principal components
- Monte Carlo sample size for ratio covariance: NMC = 5000 (doubling changes covariance eigenvalues by less than 0.1%)
- GP training design points: 500 total (450 training, 50 validation)
- Prior ranges: T [120, 180] MeV, μB [0, 450] MeV, μS [-10, 150] MeV, μQ [-20, 30] MeV
- MCMC: 100 walkers, 1000-step burn-in, 5000 production steps, yielding ~10⁶ posterior samples
- Gelman-Rubin statistic: R̂ ≈ 1.0 for all parameters

## Context
This work extends prior efforts by Bhattacharyya et al. (2019, 2020) who analyzed ratio selection uncertainties and proposed spanning-tree sampling, and addresses the correlation issues emphasized by Becattini (2007) regarding ratio-based χ² fitting. The method enables consistent freeze-out parameter extraction across the full RHIC Beam Energy Scan to LHC energy range, and can be extended to incorporate centrality dependence and more detailed systematic uncertainty treatment in future work.
