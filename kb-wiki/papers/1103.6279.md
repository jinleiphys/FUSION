# Thermal Relaxation of Charm in Hadronic Matter

> The thermal relaxation rate of open-charm ($D$) mesons in hot and dense hadronic matter is calculated using empirical elastic scattering amplitudes. $D$-meson interactions with thermal pions are approximated by $D^*$ resonances, while scattering off other hadrons ($K$, $η$, $ρ$, $ω$, $K^*$, $N$, $Δ$) is evaluated using vacuum scattering amplitudes as available in the literature based on effective Lagrangians and constrained by realistic spectroscopy. The thermal relaxation time of $D$-mesons in a hot $π$ gas is found to be around 25-50\,fm/$c$ for temperatures $T$=150-180\,MeV, which reduces to 10-25\,fm/$c$ in a hadron-resonance gas. The latter values, argued to be conservative estimates, imply significant modifications of $D$-meson spectra in heavy-ion collisions. Close to the critical temperature ($T_c$), the spatial diffusion coefficient ($D_s$) is surprisingly similar to recent calculations for charm quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma using non-perturbative $T$-matrix interactions. This suggests a possibly continuous minimum structure of $D_s$ around $T_c$.

# Thermal Relaxation of Charm in Hadronic Matter

## Key claim

The thermal relaxation time of D-mesons in a full hadron-resonance gas is 10-25 fm/c at T=150-180 MeV, which the authors argue are *conservative* (lower-limit) estimates; the spatial diffusion coefficient near T_c is surprisingly similar to non-perturbative T-matrix calculations for charm quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma, suggesting a possible continuous minimum structure of D_s around T_c.

## Method

The authors calculate D-meson elastic scattering amplitudes with the ten most abundant hadron species in a hot hadron gas. D-pion interactions are approximated via D* resonances in Breit-Wigner form, while scattering off other hadrons (K, eta, rho, omega, K*, N, Delta) is evaluated using vacuum scattering amplitudes from the literature based on effective Lagrangians constrained by charm-resonance spectroscopy. These amplitudes are then used in the standard Boltzmann-equation expression for the thermal relaxation rate A(p,T) of a D-meson in a heat bath.

## Key numbers

- Thermal relaxation time of D-mesons in a hot pion gas: 25-50 fm/c for T=150-180 MeV
- Thermal relaxation time of D-mesons in a hadron-resonance gas: 10-25 fm/c for T=150-180 MeV
- D-meson scattering width in a pion gas at T=150-180 MeV: Gamma_D = 20-40 MeV
- Total D-meson width in a hot meson gas reaches ~80 MeV around T=180 MeV
- D-meson relaxation rate at T=180 MeV: A ~ 0.1/fm, corresponding to tau_D ~ 10 fm
- Spatial diffusion coefficient D_s normalized to thermal wavelength: D_s ~ 5/(2 pi T) at T=180 MeV
- Baryon-induced D-meson width at T=180 MeV and mu_B=0: ~15 MeV
- Estimated modification of D-meson observables in RHIC hadronic phase: at least 20% for Delta_tau_had ~ 5 fm

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of D-meson spectral functions in nuclear matter using effective hadron Lagrangians by Lutz (2005) and Tolos (2007), and in a hot pion gas by Fuchs (2004). It provides the first systematic evaluation of D-meson diffusion in a full hadron-resonance gas including scattering off strange mesons, vector mesons, nucleons, and Deltas. The results enable quantitative modeling of charm transport through the hadronic phase in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and suggest a "quark-hadron duality" for charm diffusion across T_c, similar to what has been found for thermal dilepton emission rates. The authors note that two subsequently submitted papers by Abreu (2011) and Ghosh (2011) have also addressed hadronic D-meson diffusion, with the former finding close agreement and the latter finding significantly larger relaxation rates.
