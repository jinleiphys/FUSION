# Five-body calculation of $s$-wave $n$-$^4$He scattering at next-to-leading order pionless effective field theory

> We present the first five-body calculations of $s$-wave $n$-$^4$He scattering within leading order and next-to-leading order (NLO) pionless effective field theory. Using an harmonic oscillator trap technique and pionless effective field theory fitted to just six well-established experimental parameters, we predict the $s$-wave $n$-$^4$He phase shifts, scattering length $a^{1/2}_{n ^4\text{He}}(\text{NLO})=2.47(4\ \text{num.})~(17\ \text{theor.})~{\rm fm}$, and effective range $r^{1/2}_{n ^4\text{He}}(\text{NLO})=1.384(3\ \text{num.})~(211\ \text{theor.})~{\rm fm}$ in agreement with experiment. The apparent cutoff independence of our results is used to estimate the theoretical errors coming as an integral part of our final results.

## Key claim
The authors present the first five-body calculation of s-wave neutron-4He scattering within pionless effective field theory, including next-to-leading order corrections perturbatively. The abstract's claim of "agreement with experiment" is qualified by the fact that the NLO scattering length and effective range carry substantial theoretical errors (17 fm and 211 fm respectively) estimated from residual cutoff dependence, and the results are compared to R-matrix analyses rather than direct experimental data for the phase shifts themselves.

## Method
The authors solve the five-body Schrödinger equation using the stochastic variational method with correlated-Gaussian basis states, adding an harmonic oscillator trap potential to discretize the scattering continuum. The LO pionless EFT potential (two- and three-body contact terms) is iterated exactly, while NLO terms (momentum-dependent two-body, three-body, and four-body contact interactions) are included via first-order perturbation theory. Phase shifts are extracted from the trapped energy spectrum using the Busch formula, and the scattering length and effective range are obtained by fitting the effective range expansion to the calculated phase shifts.

## Key numbers
- NLO scattering length: a^{1/2}_{n^4He} = 2.47(4 num.)(17 theor.) fm
- NLO effective range: r^{1/2}_{n^4He} = 1.384(3 num.)(211 theor.) fm
- LO scattering length: a^{1/2}_{n^4He} = 1.76(4 num.)(62 theor.) fm
- LO effective range: r^{1/2}_{n^4He} = 0.70(2 num.)(45 theor.) fm
- The theory is fitted to six experimental parameters: neutron-neutron scattering length a_nn^0 = -18.95 fm, deuteron binding energy B(^2H) = 2.2246 MeV, triton binding energy B(^3H) = 8.482 MeV, neutron-neutron effective range r_nn^0 = 2.75 fm, neutron-proton effective range r_np^1 = 1.753 fm, and ^4He binding energy B(^4He) = 28.3 MeV
- Phase shifts are calculated for relative energies between 0.7 and 7.5 MeV
- Momentum cutoffs up to Lambda = 6 fm^{-1} are used
- The HO trap length b_HO ranges from 5 to 15 fm

## Context
This work extends the pionless EFT approach from three- and four-body systems (as previously studied by Kirscher et al. (2010) and Schäfer and Barnea (2023)) to the five-body n-^4He system. It contradicts the findings of chiral EFT calculations (e.g., Kravvaris et al. (2020)) which fail to reproduce the experimental scattering length, while the NLO pionless EFT result agrees with the 2020 neutron interferometry measurement by Hachmann et al. The calculation enables future extension to p-wave scattering and to reactions involving the ^5He system, and demonstrates that the four-body force required for renormalization-group invariance at NLO also yields correct predictions for five-body observables.
