# Generalised global symmetries in holography: magnetohydrodynamic waves in a strongly interacting plasma

> We begin the exploration of holographic duals to theories with generalised global (higher-form) symmetries. In particular, we focus on the case of magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) in strongly coupled plasmas by constructing and analysing a holographic dual to a recent, generalised global symmetry-based formulation of dissipative MHD. The simplest holographic dual to the effective theory of MHD that was proposed as a description of plasmas with any equation of state and transport coefficients contains dynamical graviton and two-form gauge field fluctuations in a magnetised black brane background. The dual field theory, which is closely related to the large-$N_c$, $\mathcal{N} = 4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at (infinitely) strong coupling, is, as we argue, in our setup coupled to a dynamical $U(1)$ gauge field with a renormalisation condition-dependent electromagnetic coupling. After constructing the holographic dictionary for gauge-gravity duals of field theories with higher-form symmetries, we compute the dual equation of state and transport coefficients, and for the first time analyse phenomenology of MHD waves in a strongly interacting, dense plasma with a (holographic) microscopic description. From weak to extremely strong magnetic fields, several predictions for the behaviour of Alfvén and magnetosonic waves are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a holographic dual to magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) formulated using generalised global (higher-form) symmetries, arguing that the simplest such dual contains dynamical graviton and two-form gauge field fluctuations in a magnetised black brane background; the dual field theory is closely related to large-\(N_c\), \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at infinite coupling but is coupled to a dynamical \(U(1)\) gauge field with a renormalisation condition-dependent electromagnetic coupling, meaning the precise microscopic definition is a deformation of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM rather than the theory itself.

## Method

The authors construct a five-dimensional holographic bulk action with Einstein-Hilbert gravity, a negative cosmological constant, and a two-form gauge field \(B_{ab}\) with three-form field strength \(H = dB\), which is dual to a boundary theory with a conserved stress-energy tensor \(T^{\mu\nu}\) and a conserved two-form current \(J^{\mu\nu}\) (the magnetic one-form symmetry current). They develop the holographic dictionary for higher-form symmetries, compute the dual equation of state and first-order transport coefficients from the magnetised black brane background, and then use these microscopic data to analyse the dispersion relations of Alfvén and magnetosonic waves in the strongly coupled plasma.

## Key numbers

- The trace anomaly of the dual field theory is \(\langle T^\mu_{\;\mu} \rangle = -\frac{N_c^2}{4\pi^2} \mathcal{B}^2\), where \(\mathcal{B}\) is the magnitude of the background magnetic field.
- The beta function for the inverse electromagnetic coupling is \(\beta(1/e^2) = \mu \frac{d e^{-2}}{d\mu} = -\frac{N_c^2}{2\pi^2}\), which is one-loop exact.
- The running electromagnetic coupling satisfies \(\frac{1}{e^2(\Lambda)} = \frac{1}{e^2(M)} - \frac{N_c^2}{2\pi^2} \ln(\Lambda / M)\), where \(M\) is an IR scale and \(\Lambda\) is the UV cut-off.
- The first-order dissipative MHD is controlled by seven transport coefficients: \(\eta_\perp\), \(\eta_\parallel\), \(\zeta_\perp\), \(\zeta_\parallel\), \(\zeta_\times\), \(r_\perp\) and \(r_\parallel\), with positivity constraints \(\eta_\perp \geq 0\), \(\eta_\parallel \geq 0\), \(r_\perp \geq 0\), \(r_\parallel \geq 0\), \(\zeta_\perp \geq 0\) and \(\zeta_\perp \zeta_\parallel \geq \zeta_\times^2\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory formulation of MHD based on generalised global symmetries developed by Grozdanov (2016) and provides the first holographic realisation of that framework. It enables the computation of microscopic transport coefficients and wave phenomenology for strongly coupled plasmas, complementing the concurrent work by Hofman (2017) on generalised global symmetries in holography. The results can be applied to systems ranging from fusion reactor plasmas to astrophysical plasmas and QCD quark-gluon plasma, where microscopic data from holography can now be used to predict the behaviour of Alfvén and magnetosonic waves.
