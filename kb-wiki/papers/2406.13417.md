# The study of $0νββ$ decay of $^{136}$Xe using nonclosure approach in nuclear shell model

> In this investigation, we compute the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) relevant to the light neutrino-exchange mechanism governing neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay in $^{136}$Xe. Our method is based on the nonclosure approach within the interacting nuclear shell model framework. This approach considers the genuine effects arising from the excitation energies of two hundred states for each spin-parity of the intermediary nucleus $^{136}$Cs. All computations are performed using the effective shell model Hamiltonian GCN5082. To understand the impact of nuclear structure on $0νββ$ decay, we explore the dependence of the NME on various factors, including the number of intermediate states and their spin-parity characteristics. We identify an optimal closure energy of approximately 3.7 MeV for the $0νββ$ decay of $^{136}$Xe that reproduces the nonclosure NME using the closure approach. The calculated total NME for the light neutrino-exchange $0νββ$ decay of $^{136}$Xe is 2.06 with the CD-Bonn short-range correlation (SRC). These results can be valuable for future experimental investigations into the $0νββ$ decay of $^{136}$Xe.

# The study of $0νββ$ decay of $¹³⁶Xe$ using nonclosure approach in nuclear shell model

## Key claim

The total nuclear matrix element (NME) for the light neutrino-exchange mechanism of $0νββ$ decay in $¹³⁶Xe$ is calculated as 2.06 using the nonclosure approach with CD-Bonn short-range correlations, but this value has not fully converged even with 200 intermediate states per spin-parity of $¹³⁶Cs$, and the NMEs show up to 18% variation compared to recent closure-approach calculations using different Hamiltonians.

## Method

The authors employed the interacting nuclear shell model with the GCN5082 effective Hamiltonian within the jj55 model space, using the KSHELL code to diagonalize wave functions for the initial ($¹³⁶Xe$), intermediate ($¹³⁶Cs$), and final ($¹³⁶Ba$) nuclei. They computed the lowest 200 energy states for each allowed spin-parity of $¹³⁶Cs$ and used the running nonclosure method, which explicitly incorporates the excitation energies of these intermediate states into the neutrino potential integral, rather than approximating them with a single constant closure energy.

## Key numbers

- Total NME $M^{0ν} = 2.06$ for $¹³⁶Xe$ using CD-Bonn short-range correlation (SRC) with the nonclosure approach
- Optimal closure energy of approximately 3.7 MeV for the total NME, where closure and nonclosure results intersect
- Fermi NME $M_F = -0.489$, Gamow-Teller NME $M_{GT} = 1.743$, and tensor NME $M_T = 0.012$ with CD-Bonn SRC
- Total NME varies with SRC type: 1.437 (Miller-Spencer), 2.058 (CD-Bonn), 1.903 (AV18), and 1.990 (none, including only finite nucleon size and higher-order current effects)
- The calculated NME of 2.06 is about 18% larger than the 1.74 reported in Ref. PhysRevC.98.035502 using closure with CD-Bonn SRC, and about 6% smaller than the 2.19 reported in Ref. Menendez:2008jp using UCOM SRC

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations of $0νββ$ decay NMEs for $¹³⁶Xe$ that used the closure approximation, such as those by Menendez (2008) and Coraggio (2020), by applying the more computationally demanding nonclosure approach that explicitly includes excitation energies of intermediate states. The nonclosure method has previously been applied to other nuclei including $⁴⁸Ca$, $⁷⁶Ge$, $⁸²Se$, and $¹²⁴Sn$. The calculated NME of 2.06 is notably smaller than values from other many-body models, which range from 2.35 (generator coordinate method) to 4.32 (relativistic energy density functional), highlighting the ongoing challenge of reconciling NMEs across different theoretical approaches. The identified optimal closure energy of 3.7 MeV enables future closure-approach calculations to reproduce nonclosure results with reduced computational cost, and the method can be extended to study non-standard $0νββ$ decay mechanisms.
