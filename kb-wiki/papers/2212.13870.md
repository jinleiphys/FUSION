# Forbidden beta decay properties of $^{135,137}$Te using shell-model

> In this work, the large-scale shell-model calculations for $β$-decay properties have been done. The $β$-delayed $γ$-ray spectroscopy has been performed recently at ILL, Grenoble [M. Si \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. C {\bf 106}, 014302 (2022)] to study $β$-decay properties corresponding to $^{137}$Te (($7/2^-))$ $\rightarrow$ $^{137}$I ($J_f$) transitions. We have done a systematic shell-model study for nuclear structure properties and compared the obtained results with the experimental data. Finally, the $β$-decay properties such as the $\log ft$ values and average shape factors have been reported. This is the first theoretical calculation for the $\log ft$ values corresponding to these new experimental data. In addition, we have also reported calculated $\log ft$ results for $^{135}$Te (($7/2^-))$ $\rightarrow$ $^{135}$I ($J_f$) transitions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical calculation of $\log ft$ values for the newly measured beta-decay transitions from $^{137}$Te to $^{137}$I, using large-scale shell-model calculations with the jj56pnb effective interaction and a quenched axial-vector coupling constant $g_A^{eff}=0.66\pm0.03$ and mesonic enhancement factor $\epsilon_{MEC}=2.0$; the agreement with experiment is reasonable but not perfect, and the shell-model is used to suggest spin-parity assignments for several unconfirmed states.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the jj56pnb effective interaction, derived from the N3LO interaction, with $^{132}$Sn as the inert core. The model space includes proton orbitals $0g_{7/2}, 1d_{5/2}, 1d_{3/2}, 2s_{1/2}, 0h_{11/2}$ and neutron orbitals $0h_{9/2}, 1f_{7/2}, 1f_{5/2}, 2p_{3/2}, 2p_{1/2}, 0i_{13/2}$. The NUSHELLX and KSHELL codes were used to compute wave functions, one-body transition densities, and beta-decay observables including $\log ft$ values and average shape factors for first-forbidden unique and non-unique transitions.

## Key numbers

- For $^{137}$Te($7/2^-$) $\rightarrow$ $^{137}$I($7/2^+_1$) ground-state transition: shell-model $\log ft = 5.712$, experimental $\log ft = 5.8(1)$.
- For $^{137}$Te($7/2^-$) $\rightarrow$ $^{137}$I($5/2^+_1$) transition at 243.6(8) keV: shell-model $\log ft = 6.045$, experimental $\log ft = 6.2(1)$.
- For $^{137}$Te($7/2^-$) $\rightarrow$ $^{137}$I($9/2^+_1$) transition at 554.2(10) keV: shell-model $\log ft = 6.976$, experimental $\log ft = 6.4(1)$.
- For $^{135}$Te($7/2^-$) $\rightarrow$ $^{135}$I($7/2^+_1$) ground-state transition: shell-model $\log ft = 6.276$, experimental $\log ft = 6.14(2)$.
- Magnetic moment of $^{135}$I($7/2^+_1$): shell-model value 2.372 $\mu_N$, experimental value 2.940(2) $\mu_N$.
- Quadrupole moment of $^{135}$I($7/2^+_1$): shell-model value -0.133 $eb$ (no experimental value available).
- $B(E2)$ for $^{135}$I($5/2^+_1 \rightarrow 7/2^+_1$): shell-model value 52.101 $e^2\text{fm}^4$ (no experimental value available).
- $B(E2)$ for $^{137}$I($5/2^+_1 \rightarrow 7/2^+_1$): shell-model value 1.930 $e^2\text{fm}^4$ (no experimental value available).

## Context

This work extends the shell-model study of beta-decay properties in the $^{132}$Sn region, following the recent experimental $\gamma$-ray spectroscopy of $^{137}$Te performed at ILL, Grenoble by Si et al. (2022). The calculations use the jj56pnb interaction and compare with the N3LOP interaction results reported in that same experimental paper. The authors previously applied similar large-scale shell-model calculations to beta-decay in the $^{208}$Pb region (Sharma et al., Anil Kumar et al. 2021). The results enable future experimental studies to confirm spin-parity assignments for several unconfirmed states in $^{137}$I and provide theoretical predictions for beta-decay properties where experimental data are lacking, which may be beneficial for $r$-process nucleosynthesis modeling.
