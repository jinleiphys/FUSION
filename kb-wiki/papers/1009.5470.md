# Experimental balance energies and isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross-sections

> The effect of different isospin-dependent cross-section on directed flow is studied for variety of systems(for which experimental balance energies are available) using an isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamic (IQMD) model. We show that balance energies are sensitive towards isospin-dependent cross-sections for light systems, while nearly no effect exist for heavier nuclei. A reduced cross-section $σ= 0.9σ_{NN}$ with stiff equation of state is able to explain experimental balance energies in most of systems. A power law behaviour is also given for the mass dependence of balance energy, which also follow N/Z dependence.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a reduced isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross-section of \(\sigma = 0.9\sigma_{NN}\) combined with a stiff (hard) equation of state can reproduce experimental balance energies for most systems, but this agreement holds only for heavier nuclei; for light systems the balance energy remains sensitive to the cross-section choice, and the \(^{12}C+^{12}C\) system specifically requires additional momentum-dependent interactions.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions for ten different systems ranging from \(^{12}C+^{12}C\) to \(^{197}Au+^{197}Au\) at incident energies between 45 and 200 MeV/nucleon. They varied the isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross-section from \(\sigma = 0.7\sigma_{NN}\) to \(\sigma = 1.3\sigma_{NN}\) while employing a hard (stiff) equation of state, and extracted the balance energy (energy of vanishing directed flow) from both \(\langle P_x/A \rangle\) rapidity distributions and the directed transverse flow \(\langle P_x^{dir}\rangle\) as a function of incident energy.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{86}Kr+^{93}Nb\) system, the balance energy is around 80 MeV/nucleon at \(\sigma = 0.7\sigma_{NN}\) and around 60 MeV/nucleon at \(\sigma = 0.9\sigma_{NN}\); the experimental balance energy for this system is in the range 55-60 MeV/nucleon.
- The mass dependence of balance energy follows a power law \(C(A_{TOT})^\tau\) with \(\tau = -0.29 \pm 0.06\) for the IQMD model with \(\sigma = 0.9\sigma_{NN}\), compared to the experimental \(\tau = -0.33 \pm 0.06\).
- The N/Z dependence of balance energy is parametrized with a power law \((N/Z)^\tau\) giving \(\tau = -2.39 \pm 0.40\).
- The shift in balance energy \(\Delta E_{bal} = (E_{bal})_{0.7\sigma_{NN}} - (E_{bal})_{0.9\sigma_{NN}}\) is maximum for light systems and decreases with increasing system mass, becoming nearly zero for the heaviest systems.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Sood et al. (2004) who used the QMD model with an enhanced constant cross-section of 40 mb and a stiff equation of state, but without isospin dependence. The present results show that the IQMD model, which includes isospin-dependent cross-sections (\(\sigma_{np} \approx 3\sigma_{pp} \approx 3\sigma_{nn}\)), requires a reduced cross-section (\(\sigma = 0.9\sigma_{NN}\)) rather than an enhanced one to match experimental data, because the isospin-dependent treatment introduces additional repulsion. The findings are consistent with earlier IQMD calculations by Gautam et al. (2010) and Zhang et al. (1999) who also demanded reduced cross-sections, and the work enables future studies of momentum-dependent interactions for light systems such as \(^{12}C+^{12}C\).
