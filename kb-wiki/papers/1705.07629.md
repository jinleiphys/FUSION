# Renormalized quark-antiquark Hamiltonian induced by a gluon mass ansatz in heavy-flavor QCD

> In response to the growing need for theoretical tools that can be used in QCD to describe and understand the dynamics of gluons in hadrons in the Minkowski space-time, the renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) is shown in the simplest available context of heavy quarkonia to exhibit a welcome degree of universality in the first approximation it yields once one assumes that beyond perturbation theory gluons obtain effective mass. Namely, in the second-order terms, the Coulomb potential with Breit-Fermi spin couplings in the effective quark-antiquark component of a heavy quarkonium, is corrected in one-flavor QCD by a spin-independent harmonic oscillator term that does not depend on the assumed effective gluon mass or the choice of the RGPEP generator. The new generator we use here is much simpler than the ones used before and has the advantage of being suitable for studies of the effective gluon dynamics at higher orders than the second and beyond the perturbative expansion.

## Key claim

The renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) yields a universal spin-independent harmonic oscillator correction to the Coulomb potential with Breit-Fermi spin couplings in the effective quark-antiquark component of heavy quarkonium, provided one assumes that gluons acquire an effective mass beyond perturbation theory; this correction does not depend on the assumed effective gluon mass value or the choice of the RGPEP generator.

## Method

The authors start from the canonical front-form Hamiltonian of one-flavor QCD in the gauge A⁺=0, regularized with counterterms, and apply the RGPEP to obtain a scale-dependent Hamiltonian H_s for effective particles of size s. They introduce a gluon mass ansatz in the Q_s \bar Q_s G_s Fock component as a minimal substitute for dropping all Fock components with more than one effective gluon, then project the eigenvalue problem onto the quark-antiquark sector to compute the effective two-body Hamiltonian up to second order in the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The effective quark mass squared contains a term proportional to g² with an integral over transverse momentum κ^⊥ and longitudinal momentum fraction x, regulated by the RGPEP form factor exp[-2(ℳ² - m²)² t] and the gluon mass ansatz μ² appearing in denominators such as (m² - ℳ² - μ²_{653}/x).
- The interaction kernel U_{t eff} includes terms F_Z and F_ȥ from massless-gluon exchange, with denominators containing (m² - ℳ²_{25})² + (m² - ℳ²_{35})² - (ℳ²_{13} - ℳ²_{24})².
- The non-relativistic limit yields a potential W(q) = (4/3) 4π α [1/q² - 1/q_z²] [μ²/(μ² + q²)] exp(-2 t m² |q|⁴ / q_z²), where α = g²/(4π).

## Context

This work extends the RGPEP approach previously applied to heavy quarkonia by Głazek and collaborators (QQ1, QQ2) using a simpler generator that avoids dependence on the derivative of the Hamiltonian with respect to the scale parameter, which had stalled earlier development. The new generator has already been verified to produce asymptotic freedom in QCD (AF) and to work beyond perturbation theory in simpler scalar and fermion theories (scalarmassmixng, fermionmassmixing). The resulting harmonic oscillator potential resembles the confining potential in the light-front holographic approach based on AdS/QCD duality (holography), though with a different frequency due to the heavy quark mass being much larger than Λ_QCD. This work prepares the ground for systematic renormalized Hamiltonian studies of gluon dynamics in heavy quarkonia, where the gluon mass ansatz will eventually be replaced by true QCD terms from higher Fock sectors.
