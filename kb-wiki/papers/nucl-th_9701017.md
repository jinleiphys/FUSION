# Ghost poles in the nucleon propagator in the linear-sigma model approach and its role in pion-nucleon low-energy theorems

> Complex mass poles, or ghost poles, are present in the Hartree-Fock solution of the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator in renormalizable models with Yukawa-type meson-nucleon couplings, as shown many years ago by Brown, Puff, and Wilets (BPW). These ghosts violate basic theorems of quantum field theory and their origin is related to the ultraviolet behavior of the model interactions. Recently, Krein et.al, proved that the ghosts disappear when vertex corrections are included in a self-consistent way, softening the interaction sufficiently in the ultraviolet region. In previous studies of pion-nucleon scattering using "dressed" nucleon propagator and bare vertices, did by Nutt and Wilets in the 70's (NW), it was found that if these poles are explicitly included, the value of the isospin-even amplitude A+ is satisfied within 20% at threshold. The absence of a theoretical explanation for the ghosts and the lack of chiral symmetry in these previous studies led us to re-investigate the subject using the approach of the linear-sigma model and study the interplay of low-energy theorems for pion-nucleon scattering and ghost poles. For bare interaction vertices we find that ghosts are present in this model as well and that the A+ value is badly described. As a first approach to remove these complex poles, we dress the vertices with phenomenological form factors and a reasonable agreement with experiment is achieved. In order to fix the two cutoff parameters, we use the A+ value for the chiral limit (m_pion -> 0) and the experimental value of the isoscalar scattering length. Finally, we test our model by calculating the phase shifts for the S waves and we find a good agreement at threshold.

## Key claim

Ghost poles appear in the Hartree-Fock solution of the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator in the linear-sigma model with bare interaction vertices, and their presence leads to a badly described isospin-even amplitude A(+); however, dressing the vertices with phenomenological form factors removes the ghosts and yields reasonable agreement with experiment, provided the two cutoff parameters are fixed using the chiral-limit value of A(+) and the experimental isoscalar scattering length.

## Method

The authors solved the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator in the linear-sigma model (augmented by the omega meson) using the Hartree-Fock approximation, first with bare vertices and then with phenomenological form factors on the meson and nucleon legs. The spectral function A_R(kappa) was obtained by iterating the coupled nonlinear integral equations for the self-energy and the spectral representation of the propagator. The resulting dressed propagator was then used to compute the isospin-even amplitude A(+) at threshold and the S-wave phase shifts for pion-nucleon scattering.

## Key numbers

- For the system with pi+omega+sigma(550 MeV) and bare vertices, the ghost pole position is at (0.2624 +/- i 0.4402) in units of the nucleon mass M, with residue (-0.6208 +/- i 1.1281).
- For the system with pi+omega+sigma(infinity) and bare vertices, the ghost pole position is at (1.1058 +/- i 1.1337) in units of M, with residue (-0.7410 +/- i 0.1788).
- The critical nucleon cutoff Lambda_c for the pi+omega+sigma(550) system is 1822 MeV; for pi+omega+sigma(770) it is 1958 MeV; for pi+omega+sigma(980) it is 2037 MeV; for pi+omega+sigma(infinity) it is 2085 MeV.
- The integral of the spectral function A_R(kappa) (related to the renormalization constant Z_2) for pi+omega+sigma(550) is 0.2324; for pi+omega+sigma(770) it is 0.1998; for pi+omega+sigma(980) it is 0.1943; for pi+omega+sigma(infinity) it is 0.1923.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant squared over 4pi is 14.6; the omega-nucleon coupling constant squared over 4pi is 6.36.
- The pion mass is 138.03 MeV; the omega mass is 783 MeV; sigma masses studied are 550, 770, 980 MeV and infinity.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Hartree-Fock study of ghost poles by Brown, Puff, and Wilets (BPW) and the pion-nucleon scattering calculation by Nutt and Wilets (NW) from the 1970s, which used a non-chiral model and found that including ghost poles gave A(+) within 20% of the Adler-Weisberger theorem. The present paper introduces chiral symmetry via the linear-sigma model of Gell-Mann and Levy and finds that ghosts persist with bare vertices, but can be removed by vertex form factors, following the approach of Krein, Nielsen, Puff, and Wilets (KNPW) who showed that vertex corrections soften the ultraviolet behavior. The results enable future work on constructing a ghost-free effective field theory of mesons and nucleons that respects chiral symmetry, with the caveat that the phenomenological form factors used here do not satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identities required by chiral symmetry.
