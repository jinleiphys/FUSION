# The Fermion Sign Problem and High Density Effective Theory

> We investigate the positivity of the Euclidean path integral measure for low-energy modes in dense fermionic matter. We show that the sign problem usually associated with fermions is absent if one considers only low-energy degrees of freedom. We describe a method for simulating dense QCD on the lattice and give a proof using rigorous inequalities that the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase is the true vacuum of three flavor, massless QCD.

## Key claim
The sign problem in dense QCD is absent when only low-energy degrees of freedom near the Fermi surface are considered, because the leading term in the high density effective theory (HDET) expansion yields a positive semi-definite Euclidean path integral measure; however, higher-order corrections in the 1/μ expansion may re-introduce complexity.

## Method
The authors construct a high density effective theory by decomposing quark momenta into a large Fermi momentum μv_F and a residual momentum l, then integrating out fast modes (ψ_-) far from the Fermi surface. They show that the leading effective Dirac operator M_eff = γ^E_∥·D(A) is anti-Hermitian and anti-commutes with γ_5, yielding a real positive determinant. For lattice simulation, they propose splitting the functional integral into modes within a shell of width Λ around the Fermi surface (region I, with positive determinant) and modes farther away (region II), whose complex contributions are suppressed by powers of 1/Λ.

## Key numbers
- The hierarchy required for the method is μ >> Λ >> Λ_QCD, where μ is the chemical potential and Λ is the cutoff of the effective theory.
- The Debye screening mass is M = √(N_f/(2π^2)) g_s μ, where N_f is the number of flavors.
- The logarithm of the Euclidean quark determinant scales as O(μ^4) (constant free energy) plus a non-local real term from region I, plus a local complex term of O(1/Λ) from region II.
- The propagator bound uses an IR regulator d, giving exponential decay as e^{-d R}/d where R = |x-y|.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous demonstration (Hong 2002) that QCD near a Fermi surface has positive semi-definite measure, using the high density effective theory previously introduced by Hong (2000). It provides a rigorous proof using inequalities that the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase is the true vacuum of three-flavor massless QCD at asymptotic density, complementing explicit calculations by Evans (1999), Hong (2000), and Schäfer (1999). The method enables lattice simulations of dense QCD that avoid the sign problem, and the positivity allows application of Vafa-Witten-type inequalities to constrain symmetry breaking patterns at high density.
