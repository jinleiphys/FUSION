# Is the eta Meson a Goldstone Boson?

> The decoupling of the eta meson from the nucleon, as recently deduced from analyzing \bar p p collisions and eta photoproduction off the proton at threshold, is shown to provide an argument against the octet Goldstone boson nature of the eta meson. This argument concerns the structure of the strong isoscalar axial vector current. A vanishing eta-N coupling means a vanishing contribution of the eta-pole term to the hypercharge nucleon axial vector current. Therefore, no partial conservation of the latter can be achieved. This new situation invalidates the octet Goldberger--Treiman relation for the eta-N coupling constant that is indicative of the octet Goldstone boson nature of the eta meson. In such a case there is no longer a compelling reason for the standard belief that the eta meson should be coupled to the hadronic vacuum through the hypercharge axial vector current. Rather, it will be coupled to it as any ordinary neutral pseudoscalar meson via the neutral axial vector current of the electroweak theory. As a result of the suggested universal structure of the weak and strong neutral axial vector currents the eta meson satisfies the strange analog of the Goldberger-Treiman relation so that it acquires features of a strange Goldstone boson. Hence the eta-NN vertex constant appears proportional to $Δs$, the fraction of nucleon spin carried by the strange quark sea, thus explaining its suppression naturally.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed suppression of the eta-nucleon coupling constant invalidates the standard octet Goldberger-Treiman relation for the eta meson, meaning the eta meson should not be considered an octet Goldstone boson. Instead, the authors propose that the eta meson couples to the hadronic vacuum through the neutral axial vector current of the electroweak theory, making it a "strange" Goldstone boson whose coupling to the nucleon is proportional to the strange quark spin fraction Δs.

## Method

The authors compare the eta-nucleon coupling constant predicted by the octet Goldberger-Treiman relation (using SU(3) flavor symmetry and measured proton spin fractions from deep inelastic scattering) with the experimentally extracted value from eta photoproduction off protons at threshold at MAMI. They then construct an alternative scheme where the eta meson decays via the purely strange axial vector current of the electroweak theory, applying Jaffe's flavor-diagonal coupling scheme to pseudoscalar mesons to derive a "strange" Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally extracted eta-nucleon coupling constant from MAMI data: |g_ηNN| = 0.16 g_πNN (Equation labeled MAMI_eta)
- The octet Goldberger-Treiman prediction using E143 spin fractions (Δu=0.84±0.05, Δd=-0.43±0.05, Δs=-0.08±0.05) gives g_ηNN = (0.26±0.11) g_π^0NN
- The octet Goldberger-Treiman prediction using spin fractions from EK (Δu=0.85±0.03, Δd=-0.41±0.03, Δs=-0.08±0.03) gives g_ηNN = (0.28±0.06) g_π^0NN
- The ordinary weak decay constant of the neutral pion: f_π^0 m_π^0 = 85±0.3 MeV
- The eta decay constant: f_η m_η = 94 MeV
- The ratio f_η m_η / f_π^0 m_π^0 ≈ 1.1
- The strange Goldberger-Treiman relation yields |g_ηNN / g_π^0NN| ≤ |Δs / (Δu - Δd)| ∼ 0.06, using Δs = -0.08 and g_A = 1.2573

## Context

This work extends the analysis of eta photoproduction data from the Mainz Microtron (MAMI) reported by Lothar and collaborators, and the dispersion relation analysis of p̅p collisions by GrKr. It contradicts the standard SU(3)_L ⊗ SU(3)_R chiral symmetry picture where the eta meson is the octet Goldstone boson coupled to the hypercharge axial vector current. The paper enables a reinterpretation of the eta meson as a strange Goldstone boson, with implications for the structure of the strong isoscalar axial vector current and the role of strange quarks in nucleon spin, opening the possibility of probing the strange content of hadrons using strongly interacting eta mesons.
