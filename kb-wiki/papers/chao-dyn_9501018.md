# Time Delay Correlations in Chaotic Scattering: Random Matrix Approach

> We study the correlations of time delays in a model of chaotic resonance scattering based on the random matrix approach. Analytical formulae which are valid for arbitrary number of open channels and arbitrary coupling strength between resonances and channels are obtained by the supersymmetry method. We demonstrate that the time delay correlation function, though being not a Lorentzian, is characterized, similar to that of the scattering matrix, by the gap between the cloud of complex poles of the $S$-matrix and the real energy axis.

# Time Delay Correlations in Chaotic Scattering: Random Matrix Approach

## Key claim
The time delay correlation function in chaotic resonance scattering is not a Lorentzian, but is characterized by the gap between the cloud of complex poles of the S-matrix and the real energy axis, rather than by the classical escape rate as previously conjectured.

## Method
The authors used a random matrix model where the internal Hamiltonian belongs to the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) and transition amplitudes are independent Gaussian variables. They applied the supersymmetry method to analytically calculate the two-point autocorrelation function of the time delay, valid for arbitrary number of open channels and arbitrary coupling strength between resonances and channels. The analysis was performed in the limit of a large number of resonances, with saddle-point approximations used for the supermatrix integrals.

## Key numbers
- The average time delay ⟨τ⟩ = Γ₀⁻¹, where Γ₀ = Γ(0) is the correlation length at zero energy separation.
- For isolated resonances (ρΓ_W ≪ 1), the normalized correlation function at zero energy separation is K_Q(0) = 1/(πρΓ_W) ≫ 1.
- For strongly overlapping resonances (ρΓ_W ≫ 1), K_Q(0) = 1/(π²ρ²Γ_W²) ≪ 1.
- The correlation function vanishes at an intermediate point ε₀ ≈ √(Γ_W/(πρ)) for isolated resonances.
- For strongly overlapping resonances, the correlation function takes the form K_Q(ε) ≈ (Γ_W² − ε²)/(π²ρ²(ε² + Γ_W²)²), vanishing at ε = Γ_W.
- The one-sided contribution to the correlation function at ε = 0 is approximately |K̃_Q(0)/K_Q(0)| ≈ (1/2)(πρΓ₀/(2N))², which is small when Γ₀ ≪ 1.

## Context
This work extends the random matrix approach to chaotic scattering, previously applied to S-matrix fluctuations by Verbaarschot, Weidenmüller, and Zirnbauer (1985) and by Lehmann et al. (1994), to the time delay correlation function. It contradicts the conjecture by Smilansky (1991) that the classical escape rate would be the semiclassical limit for the correlation length. The results are compared to the periodic orbit approach of Eckhardt (1993), which suggested a similar form for the correlation function but with the classical escape rate. The paper enables further study of time delay fluctuations in nuclear, atomic, and molecular chaotic scattering, as well as in microwave cavity experiments.
