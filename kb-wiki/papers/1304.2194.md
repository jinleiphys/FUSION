# Pion couplings of the Delta(1232)

> We calculate the strong couplings of pions to the Delta(1232) resonance using a QCD parameterization method that includes in addition to the usual one-quark also two-quark and previously uncalculated three-quark operators. We find that three-quark operators are necessary to obtain results consistent with the data and other QCD based baryon structure models. Our results are also in quantitative agreement with a model employing large D state admixtures to the nucleon and Delta wave functions indicating that the pion-nucleon and pion-Delta couplings are sensitive to the spatial shape of these baryons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including previously uncalculated three-quark operators in a QCD parameterization method is necessary to obtain results for the pion couplings of the Delta(1232) that are consistent with experimental data and other QCD-based baryon structure models, resolving a discrepancy where two-quark operators alone gave an unrealistically small value for the double Delta coupling.

## Method

The authors use a general parametrization method developed by Morpurgo to calculate strong pion couplings, expressing the axial vector operator as a sum of one-quark, two-quark, and three-quark operators in spin-flavor space multiplied by unknown constants that parametrize orbital and color space matrix elements. They evaluate these operators between SU(6) spin-flavor symmetric wave functions for the nucleon and Delta, then fix the unknown constants using the empirical pion-nucleon coupling, the empirical relation for the pion-nucleon-Delta coupling, and a QCD sum rule value for the pion-Delta-Delta coupling. The results are compared with a quark model employing large D-state admixtures in the nucleon and Delta wave functions.

## Key numbers

* The additive quark model gives f_pi N Delta = (6 sqrt(2)/5) f and f_pi Delta Delta = (4/5) f, where f = f_pi0 p p.
* Including two-quark operators with the empirical relation f_pi N Delta = 2.1 f gives f_pi0 Delta+ Delta+ = 0.04 f, which is unrealistically small.
* Including three-quark operators and using the QCD sum rule value f_pi0 Delta+ Delta+ = 0.666 f gives final results of f_pi0 p Delta+ = 2.10 f and f_pi0 Delta+ Delta+ = 0.67 f.
* The empirical value for the strong pion-nucleon coupling is f_pi0 p p^2/(4 pi) = 0.08.
* The Abbas model with D-state probability P_D = 0.34 gives f_pi N Delta = 2.1 f and f_pi Delta Delta = 0.67 f, in quantitative agreement with the three-quark operator results.

## Context

This work extends the QCD parameterization method previously applied by Henley et al. (2000) that included only one- and two-quark operators, which gave an unrealistically small double Delta coupling inconsistent with QCD sum rules by Zhu et al. (2000) and 1/N_c expansion calculations by Dashen et al. (1994). The results are in quantitative agreement with the model of Abbas (1991), who used large D-state admixtures in nucleon and Delta wave functions, indicating that nonspherical baryon shapes are necessary for understanding strong pion couplings. The findings enable future investigation of two- and three-quark operator effects on other axial vector quantities such as the N to Delta transition magnetic moment and weak axial transition.
