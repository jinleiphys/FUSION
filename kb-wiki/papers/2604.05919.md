# ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills thermodynamics to order $λ^{5/2}$

> We calculate the resummed perturbative free energy of ${\cal N} = 4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills in four spacetime dimensions (SYM$_{44}$) to order $λ^{5/2}$ in the 't Hooft coupling at finite temperature and zero chemical potential. All infrared divergences cancel when we include contributions from SYM$_{44}$ ring diagrams and the final result is both ultraviolet and infrared finite. Our result has special significance since order $λ^{5/2}$ is the highest order calculation that can be done with perturbation theory, because there are nonperturbative effects associated with the magnetic mass scale that come into play at order $λ^3$. We compare results obtained with regularization by dimensional reduction (RDR), which preserves supersymmetry, and canonical dimensional regularization (DR). We also compare with a generalized Padé approximant constructed by matching the weak coupling result at order $λ^2$ and the large $N_c$ strong coupling result at order $λ^{-3/2}$. Finally we make a comparison between our result and the QCD free energy and show that SYM$_{44}$ has better convergence properties.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the resummed perturbative free energy of ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory in four spacetime dimensions (SYM$_{44}$) to order $\lambda^{5/2}$ in the 't Hooft coupling, which they argue is the highest order accessible to perturbation theory because nonperturbative effects from the magnetic mass scale enter at order $\lambda^3$.

## Method

The calculation uses static resummation, a simplified version of Braaten-Pisarski resummation, to reorganize perturbation theory for static quantities. The authors separate hard and soft momentum regions, using resummed propagators with thermal masses for gluons and scalars, and compute all one-loop, two-loop, and three-loop diagrams (including counterterm contributions) using a Mathematica program that applies variable transformations and symmetrization to match terms to known finite-temperature sum-integrals. Regularization by dimensional reduction (RDR) is used to preserve supersymmetry, and results are compared with canonical dimensional regularization (DR).

## Key numbers

* The coefficient $a_2$ in the weak-coupling expansion of ${\cal F}/{\cal F}_{\rm ideal}$ is $-3/(2\pi^2)$.
* The coefficient $a_3$ is $(3+\sqrt{2})/\pi^3$.
* The coefficient $a_5$ (order $\lambda^{5/2}$) is given by $\left( \frac{33}{8} (\log 4 -1) + \frac{3}{128} (28+25\sqrt{2}) \log(1+\sqrt{2}) - \frac{1}{64} (6+\sqrt{2})\pi^2 - \frac{35+212\log 2}{128\sqrt{2}} \right) / \pi^5$ in the RDR scheme.
* The strong-coupling limit of the free energy ratio is $\frac{3}{4}[1 + \frac{15}{8}\zeta(3)\lambda^{-3/2} + {\cal O}(\lambda^{-3})]$.

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative calculations of the SYM$_{44}$ free energy, which were known to order $\lambda^2$ from Du et al. (2021) and Andersen et al. (2021) using static resummation and effective field theory methods respectively. The calculation also builds on the order $g^5$ QCD free energy calculation by Zhai (1995). The result enables improved interpolation between weak and strong coupling regimes via generalized Pade approximants, and comparison with QCD shows that SYM$_{44}$ has better convergence properties. The authors note that the generalized Pade approximant constructed by Blaizot et al. (2000) and extended by Du et al. (2021) cannot be updated with the new $\lambda^{5/2}$ result because two additional coefficients would be needed.
