# 3He structure and generalized parton distribution

> Nuclear Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs), a unique tool to access several crucial features of nuclear structure, could be measured in the coherent channel of hard exclusive processes, such as deep electroproduction of photons and mesons off nuclear targets. Here, a realistic microscopic calculation of the unpolarized quark GPD H_q^3 of the ^3He nucleus is described. In Impulse Approximation, H_q^3 is shown to be given by a convolution between the GPD of the internal nucleon and the non-diagonal spectral function, describing properly Fermi motion and binding effects. The obtained formula has the correct limits. Nuclear effects, evaluated by a modern realistic potential, are found to be larger than in the forward case. In particular, they increase with increasing the momentum transfer and the asymmetry of the process. Besides, it is found that the nuclear GPD cannot be factorized into a Δ^2-dependent and a Δ^2-independent term, as suggested in prescriptions proposed for finite nuclei. The dependence of the obtained GPDs on different realistic potentials used in the calculation shows that these quantities are sensitive to the details of nuclear structure at short distances.

## Key claim

The paper presents a realistic microscopic calculation of the unpolarized quark GPD \(H_q^3\) of the \(^3\)He nucleus in Impulse Approximation, but the result is valid only for \(\Delta^2 \ll Q^2, M^2\) and up to terms of order \(O(\xi^2)\), meaning the formalism is restricted to the kinematical range relevant for the coherent channel of deep exclusive processes.

## Method

The unpolarized quark GPD \(H_q^3\) of \(^3\)He was calculated in Impulse Approximation using a convolution formula between the GPD of the internal nucleon and the non-diagonal spectral function of \(^3\)He, which properly accounts for Fermi motion and binding effects. The non-diagonal spectral function was evaluated using overlap integrals between the \(^3\)He ground state wave function and eigenfunctions of the two-nucleon recoiling system, with realistic wave functions obtained from the AV18 interaction including Coulomb repulsion. The nucleon GPD \(H_q^N\) was modeled using the Double Distribution representation, with its \(\Delta^2\)-dependence given by the quark contribution to the nucleon Dirac form factor, parametrized according to the Gari-Krumpelmann model.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear effects in the ratio \(R_q^{(0)}\) for \(x_3 \leq 0.7\) are as large as 15% at most, for \(\Delta^2 = -0.15\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\Delta^2 = -0.25\) GeV\(^2\).
* The difference between the pointlike form factor for the proton and the neutron is 17% at \(\Delta^2 = -0.15\) GeV\(^2\) and 23% at \(\Delta^2 = -0.25\) GeV\(^2\).
* The removal energy \(E_{min} = |E_{^3He}| - |E_{^2H}| = 5.5\) MeV defines the delta function in the spectral function.
* The \(x\)-integral of the nuclear GPD reproduces \(^3\)He form factor data in the kinematical region \(-\Delta^2 \leq 0.25\) GeV\(^2\).
* The GPD \(H_u^3\) is shown for \(\Delta^2 = -0.15\) GeV\(^2\) as a function of \(x_3 = 3x\) and \(\xi_3 = 3\xi\) in the range \(0.05 \leq x_3 \leq 0.8\).

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear Generalized Parton Distributions, previously performed for the deuteron by Cano and Pire (2002) and for finite nuclei by Guzey and Strikman (2003), Kirchner and Mueller (2003), and Liuti and Taneja (2004), to the specific case of \(^3\)He. The calculation demonstrates that the nuclear GPD cannot be factorized into a \(\Delta^2\)-dependent and a \(\Delta^2\)-independent term, contradicting prescriptions proposed for finite nuclei by Kirchner and Mueller (2003). The results enable future calculations of Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering cross sections and asymmetries off \(^3\)He, and open the way to studies of polarized GPDs for extracting the angular momentum of the free neutron.
