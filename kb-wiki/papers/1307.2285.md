# Inflection point as a characteristic of the QCD critical point

> The appearance of the inflection point in the equation of state (EoS) is associated with the second order phase transition. The high cumulants of conserved quantities near the critical point are corresponding to the high derivatives of the EoS near the inflection point. The critical behavior of high cumulants of conserved charge near the QCD critical point, in particular, the sign change, is closely related to the appearance of inflection point. We show in general how the times of sign change of high cumulants relate to the order of derivative. We also demonstrate that the character of inflection point of EoS is as visible as the sign change of high cumulants in 3 systems, i.e., van der Waals equation of fluid, magnetization of spin models and the baryon number density of QCD matter. Therefore, we propose that the EoS, or the mean of baryon number density, should be measured and studied together with its higher cumulants in exploring QCD critical point in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The inflection point of the equation of state (EoS) is a characteristic of the QCD critical point, and the sign-change behavior of high cumulants of conserved charges near the critical point is fundamentally rooted in the existence of this inflection point; the authors propose that the EoS itself (or the mean baryon number density) should be measured alongside its higher cumulants in heavy-ion collision experiments, rather than relying solely on cumulant sign changes.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate analytically using a simple tanh function with an inflection point that the nth-order derivative changes sign (n-1) times in the neighborhood of the inflection point, establishing a general counting law. They then examine three physical systems: the van der Waals equation of fluid (using parameters for CO₂), magnetization in 3-dimensional O(1), O(2), and O(4) spin models from Monte Carlo simulations of PanX, and the baryon number density of QCD matter from lattice QCD and PQM model calculations. For each system, they compare the inflection point of the EoS with the sign-change behavior of the corresponding high-order cumulants or susceptibilities.

## Key numbers

* For the van der Waals fluid using CO₂ parameters (a=3.6 atm·l²·mol⁻², b=0.043 l·mol⁻¹), the critical temperature is T_c=302.305 K, critical volume v_c=0.129 l, and critical pressure p_c=72.111 atm; the inflection point appears exactly at T=T_c in the critical isotherm.
* In the example function f(x)=[1+tanh(5(x-1))]/2, the inflection point is at (1, 0.5); the 3rd derivative changes sign 2 times, the 4th derivative changes sign 3 times, and the 5th derivative changes sign 4 times in the neighborhood of the inflection point.
* For the van der Waals isotherms, at T=1.025T_c (=310 K) the peak in the first derivative (compression coefficient) shrinks compared to T_c, and at T=1.125T_c (=340 K) the peak nearly disappears.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of critical fluctuations in QCD matter by connecting the sign-change behavior of high cumulants (proposed by Asakawa, Stephanov, and others) to the more fundamental feature of an inflection point in the equation of state. The authors build on prior work showing oscillating behavior in χ₆ and χ₈ from universal scaling functions, PQM model calculations (Friman, Skokov), and lattice QCD calculations, as well as on the observation of inflection points in the quark number density by Schaefer (2007). The paper enables a combined experimental strategy where measurements of the mean baryon number density (the EoS) are used together with higher cumulant measurements to locate the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions, potentially providing a more robust signal than cumulant sign changes alone.
