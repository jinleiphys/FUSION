# Correlation measurements in high-multiplicity events

> Requirements for correlation measurements in high--multiplicity events are discussed. Attention is focussed on detection of so--called hot spots, two--particle rapidity correlations, two--particle momentum correlations (for quantum interferometry) and higher--order correlations. The signal--to--noise ratio may become large in the high--multiplicity limit, allowing meaningful single--event measurements, only if the correlations are due to collective behavior.

# Correlation measurements in high-multiplicity events

## Key claim
Meaningful single-event correlation measurements become possible in high-multiplicity events only if the correlations are due to collective behavior; if high-multiplicity events are merely superpositions of lower-multiplicity events, the signal-to-noise ratio decreases with increasing multiplicity, making accurate measurements more difficult rather than easier.

## Method
The author analytically derives signal-to-noise ratios and required event counts for four types of correlation measurements: hot spot searches using rapidity bin fluctuations, two-particle rapidity correlation functions (standard R2 and split-bin S2), two-particle momentum correlations for quantum interferometry (Hanbury-Brown-Twiss), and higher-order correlation functions including scaled factorial moments. The calculations assume independent superposition of lower-multiplicity events as a baseline model, with cluster decay parameters and thermal transverse momentum distributions used for numerical estimates.

## Key numbers
- For hot spot searches at RHIC/LHC with dN/dy = 1000 and δy = 1, hot spots occurring in 10% of bins must produce 75 excess charged pions to be clearly useful as a trigger; if occurring in 1% of bins, they must produce 100 excess pions.
- For two-particle rapidity correlations using R2 at RHIC/LHC with dN/dy = 1000 and cluster size nc = 10, approximately 830 events are needed for a 4σ measurement of the peak value; measuring the shape requires approximately 8,300 events.
- For the split-bin correlation function S2 with the same parameters (dN/dy = 1000, nc = 10, y* = 1.3, ΔY = 10), approximately 110 events are needed for a 4σ measurement.
- For quantum interferometry, a 4σ determination is possible with a single event for any spectrometer with ΔY ΔΦ > 256 (dN/dy)^{-1}; at dN/dy = 1000 this requires ΔY ΔΦ > 0.256.
- For quantum interference rapidity correlations, single-event measurement of S2 may be possible for U+U collisions at RHIC/LHC with r = 7 fm, but requires rapidity resolution to y* ≈ 0.06.

## Context
This paper extends the theoretical framework for correlation measurements in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions planned at RHIC and LHC, where multiplicities exceeding 1000 particles per unit rapidity are expected. The work contradicts earlier speculation that high multiplicity automatically enables single-event fluctuation measurements, showing instead that only collective correlations (not superpositions of independent collisions) yield favorable signal-to-noise. The analysis builds on prior work on hot spot searches, two-particle correlation functions (Author 1980s), split-bin correlation functions (Author 1990), and quantum interferometry techniques (Author 1950s, Author 1970s). The results enable experimenters to estimate required event statistics for various correlation measurements and identify which measurements are feasible at planned collider facilities.
