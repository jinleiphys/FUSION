# Spin Quantization in Heavy Ion Collision

> We analyzed recent experimental data on the disassembly of $^{28}$Si into 7$α$ in terms of a hybrid $α$-cluster model. We calculated the probability of breaking into several $α$-like fragments for high $l$-spin values for identical and non-identical spin zero nuclei. Resonant energies were found for each $l$-value and compared to the data and other theoretical models. Toroidal-like structures were revealed in coordinate and momentum space when averaging over many events at high $l$. The transition from quantum to classical mechanics is highlighted.

## Key claim

The hybrid α-cluster model (hαc) reproduces the binding energies of even-even N=Z nuclei up to mass 104 with less than 5% discrepancy to experimental data, and its dynamical simulations of rotating ²⁸Si reveal that the experimentally observed resonances at excitation energies 114, 126, and 138 MeV correspond to short-lived toroidal states, but the model's cross sections are overestimated because it does not include decay channels involving free fermions or other fragments.

## Method

The authors implemented a semi-classical hybrid α-cluster model (hαc) where α particles interact via the Bass potential for nuclear attraction and a monopole-monopole Coulomb repulsion, with Pauli blocking effects included through a density-dependent Fermi energy term. Classical Hamilton equations of motion were solved numerically using a Runge-Kutta method with relativistic kinematics, starting from ground state configurations obtained by adding a friction force until stable minima were reached. For dynamical simulations, the ²⁸Si nucleus was given an initial quantized angular momentum l along the z-axis, and the time evolution of the disassembly into α-like fragments was tracked using a simple fragment recognition algorithm based on a 5 fm distance threshold.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ²⁸Si: 236.9 MeV calculated vs. 236.5 MeV from experiment
- Moment of inertia I = 1.3 × 10⁵ MeV·fm², giving ħ²/(2I) = 0.15 MeV for r₀ = 1.15 fm
- Maximum repulsion in the two-body channel: 86.7 MeV per α particle at maximum overlap, compared to nuclear attraction Vαα(r=0) = -58 MeV
- Experimental resonances at excitation energies 114, 126, and 138 MeV from the ²⁸Si + ¹²C reaction at 35 MeV/A (Ecm = 294 MeV)
- Q-value for ²⁸Si decay into 7α: 33.6 MeV
- Model lowest excitation energy for breaking into 7α: 54 MeV at l = 16
- Integrated cross section for the 7α channel: 1.9 mb (experimental), model overestimates by a factor of 8

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Xiguang (2019), who observed resonances in the disassembly of ²⁸Si into 7α from ²⁸Si + ¹²C collisions at the Cyclotron Institute, Texas A&M University, and tentatively associated them with toroidal high-spin isomers predicted by models such as those of Wong and collaborators. The hαc model provides a computationally affordable semi-classical alternative to microscopic approaches like the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) model or Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model, and its results can be compared to the toroidal shell model predictions. The work enables future experiments at different beam energies and with different projectile-target combinations (e.g., ²⁸Si + ²⁸Si or ²⁹Si on identical targets) to test the predicted scaling of the energy-weighted excitation function and to search for additional resonances at higher excitation energies where statistics were low in the original experiment.
