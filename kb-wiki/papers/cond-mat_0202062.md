# Two-dimensional few electron systems in high magnetic fields: Composite-fermion or rotating-electron-molecule approach?

> A new class of analytic and parameter-free, strongly correlated wave functions of simple functional form is derived for few electrons in two-dimensional quantum dots under high magnetic fields. These wave functions are constructed through breaking and subsequent restoration of the circular symmetry, and they offer a natural alternative to the Laughlin and composite-fermion functions. Underlying our approach is a collectively-rotating-electron-molecule picture. The angular momenta allowed by molecular symmetry correspond to the filling-factors' hierarchy of the fractional quantum Hall effect.

# Two-dimensional few electron systems in high magnetic fields: Composite-fermion or rotating-electron-molecule approach?

## Key claim

The authors derive a new class of analytic, parameter-free wave functions for few electrons in two-dimensional quantum dots under high magnetic fields, constructed through breaking and subsequent restoration of circular symmetry. These rotating-electron-molecule wave functions (EMWFs) offer a natural alternative to Laughlin and composite-fermion wave functions, with the claim that the fractional quantum Hall effect hierarchy of filling factors may be viewed as an experimental signature of the yrast band of the rotating electron molecule.

## Method

The approach proceeds in two steps: first, breaking rotational symmetry at the unrestricted Hartree-Fock (UHF) level yields single-determinantal states representing electron molecules (Wigner molecules) with electrons arranged in concentric rings; second, circular symmetry is restored via projection techniques that act on the UHF determinant, yielding multi-determinantal wave functions with good total angular momentum. For the case of an infinite 2D electron gas, the UHF orbitals are approximated by analytic Gaussian functions centered at positions forming a hexagonal Wigner crystal, while for finite N-electron systems the UHF orbitals arrange in concentric rings with specific geometries denoted as (0,N) for a single ring and (1,N-1) for a ring with one central electron.

## Key numbers

- For N=3 electrons, EMWF overlaps with exact eigenstates range from 0.98347 (L=9) to 0.99839 (L=39), compared to JL overlaps from 0.99946 (L=9) to 0.99708 (L=39)
- For N=4 electrons, EMWF overlaps with exact eigenstates range from 0.92937 (L=18) to 0.97623 (L=54), compared to JL overlaps from 0.97880 (L=18) to 0.96815 (L=54)
- For N=4, L=10: EMWF energy = 1.78510, CF energy = 1.78537, exact energy = 1.78509 (in units of e^2/κl_B)
- For N=4, L=14: EMWF energy = 1.50955, CF energy = 1.50222, exact energy = 1.50066 (in units of e^2/κl_B)
- The EMWFs are nonzero only for special total angular momenta L = N(N-1)/2 + Nk for (0,N) configuration and L = N(N-1)/2 + (N-1)k for (1,N-1) configuration, with k=0,1,2,3,...

## Context

This work extends the rotating-electron-molecule picture previously developed by the same authors for zero magnetic field (Yannouleas and Landman, 2000) to the high magnetic field regime relevant to the fractional quantum Hall effect. The EMWFs reproduce the "magic angular momenta" found in exact diagonalization studies by Girvin and Jach (1983), Hawrylak and collaborators (1998), and Seki, Kuramoto, and Nishino (1996), while also addressing discrepancies where some larger magic angular momenta are not reproduced by the composite-fermion picture of Jain (1990). The approach enables a unified treatment of strongly correlated states in quantum dots over the whole magnetic field range and suggests that the FQHE filling factor hierarchy may be interpreted as the yrast band of a rotating electron molecule.
