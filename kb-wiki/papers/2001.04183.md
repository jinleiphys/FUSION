# Simulation of the wave neutron-nuclear burning of Th232 enriched with Pu239 for the thermal range of neutron energy

> At the initial stage, the development of wave reactors, which will operate in the mode of wave nuclear burning (WNC), requires the study of the kinetics of the WNC fuel mode when changing both external parameters (flux density of an external neutron source, thermal parameters of heat transfer) and internal parameters (fuel composition, reactor material parameter, delayed neutrons). In order to confirm the possibility of WNC with enrichment, we study its impact on the criterion of WNC for thorium fuel Th232 at different enrichments in Pu239) and its behavior during the "ignition" stage. To confirm the fulfillment of the criterion of slow WNC depending on the neutron energy, we perform a numerical simulation of the thorium fuel WNC mode dynamics, taking into account the delayed neutrons in the thermal and epithermal regions of the neutron energies (0.015-10 eV).

## Key claim

The paper claims that wave nuclear burning (WNC) of thorium-232 fuel enriched with plutonium-239 is possible in the thermal and epithermal neutron energy range (0.015–10 eV), but only if the initial enrichment of Pu-239 is sufficiently low (0.5% or less) to satisfy the WNC criterion; at higher enrichments (1–2%) the criterion fails for most of this energy range.

## Method

The authors performed a numerical simulation of the one-dimensional kinetics of wave nuclear burning in a thorium-232 fuel medium initially containing 99.5% Th-232 and 0.5% Pu-239, using a system of 19 coupled partial differential equations that included neutron diffusion, delayed neutrons (six groups for both U-233 and Pu-239), and the full Th-232 → Th-233 → Pa-233 → U-233 conversion chain with a beta-decay time constant of 39.5 days. The simulation was run for 360 days with a time step of 50 minutes and a spatial step of 0.01 cm, using averaged neutron cross sections over the energy range 0.015–10 eV, and solved with Mathematica 8.

## Key numbers

* The characteristic time for the two beta decays converting Th-233 to U-233 is τ = 39.53 days (calculated as 27.4 / ln 2).
* The simulation used an external neutron source flux of Φ₀ = 1.0 × 10¹⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ at the boundary x = 0.
* The initial fuel composition was 99.5% Th-232 (density 11.78 g/cm³) and 0.5% Pu-239 (density 19.84 g/cm³), corresponding to initial concentrations of approximately 0.995 × (11.78/232) × N_A and 0.005 × (19.84/239) × N_A, respectively.
* The simulation ran for 360 days with a time step Δt = 50 minutes and spatial step Δx = 0.01 cm.
* The neutron diffusion coefficient was D = 2 × 10⁻⁴ cm²/s, and neutron speed V_n = 10⁶ cm/s.
* The microscopic cross sections used (averaged over 0.015–10 eV) for Th-232 capture: 3.06 barn; for U-233 fission: 260.68 barn; for U-233 capture: 52.97 barn; for Pu-239 fission: 477.0 barn; for Pu-239 capture: 286.1 barn.
* The total delayed neutron fraction β for U-233 is 2.6 × 10⁻³, and for Pu-239 is 2.1 × 10⁻³.
* After 72 days, the initial Pu-239 concentration decreased by approximately 0.15%; after 360 days, the U-233 concentration reached approximately 1%.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for wave nuclear burning (WNC) originally developed for the uranium-plutonium fuel cycle by the authors' earlier works (Kakaev et al., prior references [1,2]) and by the Traveling-Wave Reactor (TWR) concept from TerraPower (references [6–14]). The key advance is demonstrating that WNC can be achieved in the thermal/epithermal neutron energy range using thorium fuel, which avoids the severe radiation damage problem (up to ~500 dpa) that plagues fast-neutron WNC designs, where current structural materials can withstand only ~100 dpa. The results enable further investigation of thorium-based molten-salt wave reactors, which could circumvent the problem of Pa-233 neutron capture losses by removing protactinium from the core during its 27-day half-life.
