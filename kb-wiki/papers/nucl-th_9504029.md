# Role of correlated two-pion exchange in $K^+ N$ scattering

> A dynamical model for S-- and P--wave correlated $2 π$ (and $K \bar K$) exchange between a kaon and a nucleon is presented, starting from corresponding $N \bar N \rightarrow K \bar K$ amplitudes in the pseudophysical region, which have been constructed from nucleon, $Δ$--isobar and hyperon ($Λ$, $Σ$) exchange Born terms and a realistic meson exchange model of the $ππ\rightarrow K \bar K$ and $K \bar K \rightarrow K \bar K$ amplitude. The contribution in the s--channel is then obtained by performing a dispersion relation over the unitarity cut. In the $ρ$--channel, considerable ambiguities exist, depending on how the dispersion integral is performed. Our model, supplemented by short range interaction terms, is able to describe empirical $K^+ N$ data below pion production threshold in a satisfactory way.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a microscopic model of correlated two-pion (and $K \bar K$) exchange, derived from dispersion relations over the unitarity cut, can describe empirical $K^+ N$ scattering data below pion production threshold, but only after supplementing with short-range interaction terms and with the caveat that considerable ambiguities exist in the $\rho$-channel depending on how the dispersion integral is performed.

## Method

The authors constructed a dynamical model for the $N \bar N \rightarrow K \bar K$ amplitude in the pseudophysical region using baryon exchange Born terms (nucleon, $\Delta$-isobar, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$) and a realistic coupled-channel meson exchange model for $\pi\pi \rightarrow K \bar K$ and $K \bar K \rightarrow K \bar K$. They then performed a dispersion relation over the unitarity cut to obtain the correlated $2\pi$ exchange contribution in the $s$-channel for $K^+ N$ scattering, isolating the scalar-isoscalar ($\sigma$) and vector-isovector ($\rho$) channels. The resulting potentials were extrapolated off-shell using a time-ordered propagator analogy and monopole form factors, then iterated in a scattering equation alongside other meson and baryon exchange diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The correlated $2\pi$ exchange in the $\sigma$-channel can be represented by an effective scalar mass of 0.75 GeV, compared to 0.55 GeV for the analogous $\pi N$ system.
- In the $\rho$-channel, the tensor-to-vector coupling ratio $g_T/g_V$ is approximately 2.5 when dispersing helicity amplitudes directly (method 1) and approximately 5 when dispersing vector/tensor combinations (method 2).
- The cutoff masses used for the correlated $2\pi$ potentials are $\Lambda_{\sigma,\rho} = 1850$ MeV for method 1 and 2400 MeV for method 2.
- The $K^+ N$ scattering lengths from the correlated exchange models are in overall agreement with empirical values (Table 3 in the paper).
- The $K^+$ nuclear mean free path is about 5-7 fm for $p_{lab} \le 0.8$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the meson exchange model for $K^+ N$ scattering previously presented by Bue et al. (1994), which treated $\sigma$ and $\rho$ exchange as sharp-mass particles with free parameters. The authors replace these phenomenological exchanges with a microscopic treatment of correlated $2\pi$ exchange, following a similar methodology to that used in $\pi N$ scattering by Schutz et al. (1994). The approach enables future investigation of medium modifications in kaon-nucleus scattering, including changes in meson masses and in the $N \bar N \rightarrow K \bar K$ interaction itself.
