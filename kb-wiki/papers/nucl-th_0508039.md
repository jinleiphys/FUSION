# A new analysis of 14O beta decay: branching ratios and CVC consistency

> The ground-state Gamow-Teller transition in the decay of 14O is strongly hindered and the electron spectrum deviates markedly from the allowed shape. A reanalysis of the only available data on this spectrum changes the branching ratio assigned to this transition by seven standard deviations: our new result is (0.54 \pm 0.02)%. The Kurie plot data from two earlier publications are also examined and a revision to their published branching ratios is recommended. The required nuclear matrix elements are calculated with the shell model and, for the first time, consistency is obtained between the M1 matrix element deduced from the analog gamma transition in 14N and that deduced from the slope of the shape-correction function in the beta transition, a requirement of the conserved vector current hypothesis. This consistency is only obtained, however, if renormalized rather than free-nucleon operators are used in the shell-model calculations. In the mirror decay of 14C a similar situation occurs. Consistency between the 14C lifetime, the slope of the shape-correction function and the M1 matrix element from gamma decay can only be achieved with renormalized operators in the shell-model calculation.

## Key claim

The ground-state Gamow-Teller branching ratio in 14O decay is revised to (0.54 ± 0.02)%, a shift of seven standard deviations from the previously published value of (0.61 ± 0.01)%, and consistency with the conserved vector current hypothesis is achieved only when renormalized rather than free-nucleon operators are used in shell-model calculations.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the original beta-spectrum data from Sidhu's Ph.D. thesis, applying modern screening, radiative, and shape-correction factors to extract the shape-correction function C(Z,W) for the hindered Gamow-Teller transition. They performed shell-model calculations using Cohen-Kurath (CK), Warburton-Brown (PWBT), and Millener-Kurath (MK) interactions, mixing 2-hole and 4-hole-2-particle configurations, and adjusted the wavefunction amplitude α to fit the experimental shape-correction function. The analysis was repeated with renormalized operators (effective axial-vector and M1 coupling constants derived from 15O decay and Towner-Khanna calculations) and applied to both 14O beta decay and the mirror 14C decay.

## Key numbers

- New ground-state Gamow-Teller branching ratio for 14O: (0.54 ± 0.02)%, compared to the original Sidhu-Gerhart value of (0.61 ± 0.01)%
- Gamow-Teller matrix element from the fit: M_GT = 0.0149 ± 0.0005, compared to Sidhu-Gerhart's 0.0164 ± 0.0004
- Experimental M1 matrix element from gamma decay in 14N: |M_M1^gamma|_expt = 0.442 ± 0.010, giving the CVC expectation |M_M1^beta| = 0.312 ± 0.007
- With renormalized operators, the calculated M_M1^beta values (0.300-0.346 across models) agree with the CVC expectation, whereas free-nucleon operators gave values (~0.52-0.57) that were too large by a factor of ~1.7
- For 14C decay, the calculated slope parameter a with renormalized operators is approximately -0.43 MeV^-1, agreeing with the experimental value of -0.45(4) MeV^-1 from Wietfeldt et al. (1995)
- The 14C half-life used for fitting is 5700(30) yr

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy noted by Garcia and Brown (1995), who could not fit the Sidhu-Gerhart beta spectrum with shell-model calculations using free-nucleon operators. The reanalysis extends the authors' previous systematic studies of superallowed Fermi transitions (Hardy and Towner, 2005) and demonstrates that the conserved vector current hypothesis is satisfied in the A=14 system when renormalized operators account for core polarization and meson-exchange currents. The revised branching ratio impacts the Fermi branching ratio for 14O used in superallowed decay surveys, and the authors recommend that the older data from Sherr et al. (1955) and Frick et al. (1963) be superseded, as their raw Kurie plots are in conflict with the Sidhu-Gerhart data by about 20%.
