# The two-nucleon problem in EFT reformulated: Pion and nucleon masses as soft and hard scales

> We outline the modified formulation of baryon chiral effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon scattering and discuss the issue of a possible power counting violation by the nucleon mass. We also present the results for the quark mass dependence of the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ scattering lengths and the deuteron binding energy at leading order.

# The two-nucleon problem in EFT reformulated: Pion and nucleon masses as soft and hard scales

## Key claim
The nucleon mass does not violate power counting in the modified formulation of baryon chiral effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon scattering, despite the naive interpretation that it acts as an ultraviolet cutoff; renormalization removes all nucleon-mass dependence that would violate the power counting, because the heavy-baryon expansion does not commute with the expansion in inverse powers of the cutoff parameter.

## Method
The authors formulate a manifestly Lorentz invariant effective Lagrangian and use time-ordered perturbation theory to derive a leading-order integral equation for the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude, where the leading-order potential includes contact terms and one-pion exchange. They analyze the nucleon-mass dependence of one-loop and two-loop tensor integrals arising from iterations of the one-pion exchange potential, expand these integrals in inverse powers of the nucleon mass using dimensional counting, and demonstrate that renormalization via counter-terms cancels all terms proportional to odd powers of the nucleon mass that would violate the standard power counting of the heavy-baryon approach.

## Key numbers
- The one-loop tensor integral I1, after expansion in inverse powers of the nucleon mass, yields a leading term proportional to m^2 m^(n-3) that is momentum and pion-mass independent and is cancelled by counter-terms from leading-order contact interactions; higher-order terms have either the same linear dependence on the nucleon mass (for spacetime dimension n=3) as in heavy-baryon expressions or are suppressed by additional inverse powers of m.
- For the two-loop tensor integral I2, all terms proportional to m^3 m^(n-3) cancel exactly when counter-term integrals are added; the remaining terms proportional to m^4 m^(2n-6) are momentum and pion-mass independent and are subtracted by two-loop counter-terms, leaving terms with either the same quadratic dependence on the nucleon mass as in the heavy-baryon approach or suppressed by additional inverse powers of m.

## Context
This work extends the pioneering approach of Weinberg (1990) for applying effective field theory to few-nucleon systems, which defined the nucleon-nucleon potential as a sum of two-nucleon-irreducible diagrams in non-relativistic EFT. The authors address the renormalization problem in Weinberg's approach by introducing a new framework based on a manifestly Lorentz invariant effective Lagrangian and time-ordered perturbation theory, as previously developed in Epelbaum and Gegelia (2012). The quark-mass dependence results are compared with the N^2LO calculation of Berengut et al. (2013), which relies on the resonance saturation hypothesis for contact interactions, and with earlier EFT calculations by Epelbaum (2002), Beane (2002), Chen (2010), and Soto (2011). This work enables systematic chiral extrapolations of two-nucleon observables order-by-order without implicit quark-mass dependence of contact-interaction coupling constants.
