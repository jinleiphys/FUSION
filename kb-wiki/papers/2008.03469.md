# Unveiling the correlations of tidal deformability with the nuclear symmetry energy parameters

> The chi-squared based covariance approach allows one to estimate the correlations among desired observables related to nuclear matter directly from a set of fit data without taking recourse to the distributions of the nuclear matter parameters (NMPs). Such an approach is applied to study the correlations of tidal deformability of neutron star with the slope and the curvature parameters of nuclear symmetry energy governed by an extensive set of fit data on the finite nuclei together with the maximum mass of the neutron star. The knowledge of the distributions of NMPs consistent with the fit data is implicitly inbuilt in the Hessian matrix which is central to this covariance approach. Comparing our results with those obtained with the explicit use of the distributions of NMPs, we show that the appropriate correlations among NMPs as induced by the fit data are instrumental in strengthening the correlations of the tidal deformability with the symmetry energy parameters, without it, the said correlations tend to disappear. The interplay between isoscalar and isovector NMPs is also emphasized.

## Key claim

The chi-squared based covariance approach reveals that the correlations of neutron star tidal deformability with the symmetry energy slope and curvature parameters are significantly strengthened when the correlations among nuclear matter parameters induced by finite-nuclei fit data are properly accounted for; without these correlations, the said correlations tend to disappear.

## Method

The authors employ a chi-squared based covariance approach within the Skyrme framework, using the Hessian matrix from fits to finite nuclei data (binding energies, charge radii, isoscalar giant monopole resonance energies, dipole polarizabilities) and the maximum neutron star mass to compute correlations between tidal deformability and symmetry energy parameters. They also construct equations of state using multivariate Gaussian distributions of nuclear matter parameters, both with independent variation and with the inclusion of the L0-Ksym,0 correlation, and compare these results to those from the covariance approach.

- The correlation coefficient between L0 and Ksym,0 for the SkLambda267 model is 0.9
- For Case I (independent NMPs), correlation of Lambda_1.4 with L0 is 0.56 and with Ksym,0 is 0.58
- For Case II (L0-Ksym,0 correlation included), correlation of Lambda_1.4 with L0 is 0.83 and with Ksym,0 is 0.86

## Context

This work extends the correlation systematics approach of Vidana (2009), Ducoin (2010, 2011), Newton (2011), Alam (2016), and Agrawal (2020) to specifically address the apparent discrepancy between studies using correlated nuclear models (Fattoyev 2018, Malik 2018) and those using independent variation of nuclear matter parameters (Carson 2019a) regarding the strength of correlations between tidal deformability and symmetry energy parameters. The results enable future Bayesian analyses to properly account for the information content of tidal deformability measurements from gravitational wave events like GW170817, and demonstrate that the distribution of nuclear matter parameters must be guided by realistic fit data to avoid masking correlations.
