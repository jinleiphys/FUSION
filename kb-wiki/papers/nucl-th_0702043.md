# Accelerating Solutions of Perfect Fluid Hydrodynamics for Initial Energy Density and Life-Time Measurements in Heavy Ion Collisions

> A new class of accelerating, exact, explicit and simple solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics is presented. Since these new solutions yield a finite rapidity distribution, they lead to an advanced estimate of the initial energy density and life-time of high energy heavy ion reactions. Accelerating solutions are also given for spherical expansions in arbitrary number of spatial dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new class of exact, accelerating solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics that yield finite rapidity distributions, enabling an improved estimate of initial energy density and lifetime in heavy ion collisions that corrects the Bjorken estimate by accounting for longitudinal acceleration effects.

## Method

The authors derived exact solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics for perfect fluids with an ideal gas equation of state (p = nT, ε = κp) in 1+1 and 1+d dimensions, introducing a parameter λ that controls the acceleration. They used Rindler coordinates (τ, η) and obtained analytic forms for velocity, pressure, number density, and temperature, then calculated rapidity distributions using a Boltzmann approximation with saddle-point integration at freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV: ε_c = 8.5–10 GeV/fm³, which is a factor of (1.7–2.0) times the Bjorken estimate of ε_B = 5 GeV/fm³
- Life-time correction factor: τ_c = λ τ_B, with λ ≈ 1.2 from BRAHMS data, implying a 20% increase in estimated life-time
- The rapidity distribution width formula: Δy² = α / (m/T_f – 1/2 + 1/α), where α = (2λ – 1)/(λ – 1)
- Valid solution parameters: λ = 2 with κ = d for arbitrary d; λ = 1/2 with κ = 1; λ = 3/2 with κ = 11/3 for d = 3; generalized κ = (4d – 1)/3 for any d

## Context

This work generalizes the Landau-Khalatnikov solution (Landau 1953, Khalatnikov) and the Hwa-Bjorken solution (Hwa 1974, Bjorken 1982) by introducing acceleration while maintaining exact solvability. It extends prior exact hydro solutions (Csorgo 2003, Biro 1999, Sinyukov 2004) to accelerating flows. The results enable improved estimates of initial energy density and reaction lifetime in heavy ion collisions, correcting the Bjorken formula and the Sinyukov-Makhlin longitudinal HBT formula (Makhlin 1987) for longitudinal acceleration effects.
