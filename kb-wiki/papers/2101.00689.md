# P-wave nucleon-pion scattering amplitude in the $Δ(1232)$ channel from lattice QCD

> We determine the $Δ(1232)$ resonance parameters using lattice QCD and the Lüscher method. The resonance occurs in elastic pion-nucleon scattering with $J^P=3/2^+$ in the isospin $I = 3/2$, $P$-wave channel. Our calculation is performed with $N_f=2+1$ flavors of clover fermions on a lattice with $L\approx 2.8$ fm. The pion and nucleon masses are $m_π=255.4(1.6)$ MeV and $m_N=1073(5)$ MeV, and the strong decay channel $Δ\rightarrow πN$ is found to be above the threshold. To thoroughly map out the energy-dependence of the nucleon-pion scattering amplitude, we compute the spectra in all relevant irreducible representations of the lattice symmetry groups for total momenta up to $\vec{P}=\frac{2π}{L}(1,1,1)$, including irreps that mix $S$ and $P$ waves. We perform global fits of the amplitude parameters to up to 21 energy levels, using a Breit-Wigner model for the $P$-wave phase shift and the effective-range expansion for the $S$-wave phase shift. From the location of the pole in the $P$-wave scattering amplitude, we obtain the resonance mass $m_Δ=1378(7)(9)$ MeV and the coupling $g_{Δ\text{-}πN}=23.8(2.7)(0.9)$.

## Key claim

The authors determine the Δ(1232) resonance parameters from lattice QCD using the Lüscher method at a pion mass of 255.4(1.6) MeV, where the Δ is an unstable resonance above the πN threshold, rather than a stable particle. The extracted resonance mass of 1378(7)(9) MeV is significantly higher than the physical Δ mass of approximately 1232 MeV due to the unphysical pion mass used in the calculation.

## Method

The authors compute finite-volume energy spectra for elastic πN scattering in the P-wave, J^P=3/2^+, I=3/2 channel using N_f=2+1 clover fermions on a 24^3×48 lattice with L≈2.8 fm. They construct correlation matrices from single-hadron Δ operators and multi-hadron Nπ operators projected onto irreducible representations of the lattice symmetry groups for total momenta up to (1,1,1) in units of 2π/L, including irreps that mix S and P waves. The energy levels are extracted via the generalized eigenvalue problem, and the scattering amplitude is determined by fitting up to 21 energy levels simultaneously using a Breit-Wigner model for the P-wave phase shift and the effective-range expansion for the S-wave phase shift.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_π = 255.4(1.6) MeV; nucleon mass: m_N = 1073(5) MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1163(4) fm; spatial extent: L = 2.791(9) fm
- Δ resonance mass from pole position: m_Δ = 1378(7)(9) MeV (first uncertainty statistical, second systematic)
- Δ-πN coupling: g_{Δ-πN} = 23.8(2.7)(0.9)
- 21 energy levels used in global fits across all irreps and momenta
- 600 gauge configurations with 9600 total measurements

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the Δ resonance (Alexandrou 2013, Lang 2017, Andersen 2018) by using multiple moving frames to obtain a dense energy spectrum and including irreps that mix S and P waves for a more complete determination of the scattering amplitude. The results enable future calculations of N→Nπ electroweak transition matrix elements using the formalism of Briceno (2014, 2015), and provide a first-principles benchmark for phenomenological approaches such as chiral perturbation theory (Pascalutsa 2006) and S-matrix analyses (Arndt 2006).
