# Two-Body Interaction. I. Inverse Scattering Problem, Nonlocal Potentials, and a Conception of Quantum Measurement Theory

> The present paper generalizes preceding papers of the author and opens a cycle of works concerning the general posing and solution in analytic form of the quantum-mechanical inverse scattering problem (for a given partial channel) in a nontraditional setting: the scattering wave function in the momentum representation is recovered immediately from the scattering data. The solution we find for the problem in question is given by a family of phase-equivalent wave functions with explicitly controlled functional-analytic arbitrariness. This arbitrariness corresponds to the arbitrariness in the choice of phase-equivalent nonlocal potentials and causes the necessary to include some additional concepts of quantum measurement theory to a complete description of quantum-mechanical systems. The mathematical apparatus of the approach thus developed is illustrated by the example of describing the nucleon-nucleon interaction in uncoupled partial channels.

## Key claim
The paper generalizes the author's prior work to solve the quantum-mechanical inverse scattering problem in a nontraditional setting: the scattering wave function in momentum representation is recovered directly from scattering data, yielding a family of phase-equivalent wave functions with explicitly controlled functional-analytic arbitrariness. This arbitrariness corresponds to the choice of phase-equivalent nonlocal potentials and necessitates including additional concepts from quantum measurement theory for a complete description of quantum-mechanical systems.

## Method
The author reformulates the inverse scattering problem for a fixed partial channel by working entirely in the momentum representation, avoiding the double Fourier transform that causes instability in traditional configuration-space methods (Gelfand-Levitan and Marchenko). The solution is constructed by recovering the scattering wave function in momentum space directly from the S-matrix, using analytic continuation properties and the Jost function formalism. The approach is illustrated for nucleon-nucleon interaction in uncoupled partial channels, with the mathematical apparatus applied to the example of describing NN interaction in uncoupled partial waves.

## Key numbers
- Experimental phase-shift data for NN scattering are available up to E_lab ≤ 3.0 GeV for pp scattering and E_lab ≤ 1.3 GeV for np scattering (reference [17]).
- The author notes that the SP40 energy-independent partial-wave analysis and the SP00 energy-dependent analysis (with 147 fit parameters) are not well-consistent with each other.
- The mixing angle ε₁ in the triplet ³S₁+³D₁ channel is refined for E_lab ≤ 80 MeV, with some indication of anomalously large tensor interaction.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier papers (references [1]–[6]) and opens a cycle of works on the general formulation and analytic solution of the quantum-mechanical inverse scattering problem. It contrasts with the classical Gelfand-Levitan (reference [13], [14]) and Marchenko (reference [15], [16]) methods, which reconstruct the local potential in configuration space and are unstable due to the double Fourier transform. The approach enables a controlled family of phase-equivalent nonlocal potentials and connects to quantum measurement theory, with implications for describing NN interactions and deuteron wave functions (the author's earlier SWF functions from 1981, reference [23], are noted as uniquely consistent with recent polarized ed-scattering data).
