# 3-D unrestricted TDHF fusion calculations using the full Skyrme interaction

> We present a study of fusion cross sections using a new generation Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) code which contains no approximations regarding collision geometry and uses the full Skyrme interaction, including all of the time-odd terms. In addition, the code uses the Basis-Spline collocation method for improved numerical accuracy. A comparative study of fusion cross sections for $^{16}O + ^{16,28}O$ is made with the older TDHF results and experiments. We present results using the modern Skyrme forces and discuss the influence of the new terms present in the interaction.

## Key claim

Using a new-generation TDHF code with no collision-geometry approximations and the full Skyrme interaction including all time-odd terms, the authors show that modern Skyrme parametrizations yield fusion cross sections substantially closer to experiment than older calculations, though the results still overestimate the experimental cross section by about 25%.

## Method

The authors performed unrestricted three-dimensional TDHF calculations using a basis-spline collocation lattice with 1.0 fm spacing, solving the static HF equations to an energy convergence of 1 part in 10^14 and evolving the system with an exponential expansion of the infinitesimal propagator. They computed fusion cross sections for 16O+16O at E_cm=34 MeV and for 16O+28O at E_cm=43 MeV using the sharp-cutoff approximation, searching for the maximum impact parameter that leads to fusion. The calculations included all time-odd terms (j, s, T) and the full spin-current pseudotensor J, which had been omitted in most earlier TDHF work.

## Key numbers

- Fusion threshold energy for 16O+16O: 56 MeV (SkM* with J^2 included), 56 MeV (SLy4), 53 MeV (SLy4 with J^2), 55 MeV (SLy5)
- Fusion cross section for 16O+16O at E_cm=34 MeV: 1368 mb (SkM*), 1347 mb (SLy5), compared to experimental value of 1075 mb
- Maximum impact parameter for 16O+16O fusion at E_cm=34 MeV: 6.65 fm (SkM*), 6.60 fm (SLy5)
- Maximum impact parameter for 16O+28O fusion at E_cm=43 MeV: 7.5 fm, yielding a cross section of approximately 1767 mb
- For 16O+28O at b=7.6 fm (no fusion), final translational energy of separating ions is about 20 MeV, indicating 23 MeV was converted to internal excitation

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF fusion calculations that used axial symmetry, the rotating-frame approximation, Yukawa finite-range approximations for the Skyrme interaction, and neglected time-odd terms beyond the j^2 contribution. The authors show that the "fusion window anomaly" (a non-zero lower orbital angular momentum limit for fusion) had already been resolved by including spin-orbit interaction (Umar et al., earlier work). The new calculations demonstrate that the J^2 term, while contributing only tens of keV to the binding energy of 16O, makes a significant dynamical contribution during fusion that is absent in non-fusing collisions. This suggests that future Skyrme parametrizations could be improved by incorporating dynamical features into the fitting process, as proposed by Dobaczewski and Dudek (1995) and Rutz and Fink (1995).
