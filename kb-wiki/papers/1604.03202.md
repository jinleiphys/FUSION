# Effects of realistic tensor force on nuclear quadrupole deformation around the shore of the island of inversion

> The M3Y-type semi-realistic interaction is applied to deformed nuclei for the first time. The constrained Hartree-Fock calculations assuming axial symmetry are implemented for the $N=20$ isotones $^{30}$Ne, $^{32}$Mg, $^{34}$Si and the $N=28$ isotones $^{40}$Mg, $^{42}$Si, $^{44}$S with the M3Y-P6 interaction. The results match the experimental data well. Effects of the realistic tensor force on the nuclear quadrupole deformation are investigated in relation to the loss of the $N=20$ and $28$ magic numbers. The tensor force is confirmed to favor the deformation for the $N=28$ nuclei owing to the closure of the $jj$-shell (\textit{i.e.}, $n0f_{7/2}$), while favoring the sphericity for the $N=20$ nuclei owing to the $\ell s$-closure of $N=20$.

## Key claim

The M3Y-type semi-realistic interaction is applied to deformed nuclei for the first time, and the tensor force is confirmed to favor deformation for N=28 nuclei due to the closure of the jj-shell (n0f7/2) while favoring sphericity for N=20 nuclei due to the ls-closure of N=20, though the authors note that the lack of pair and rotational correlations in their Hartree-Fock calculations prohibits a fully conclusive comparison with experimental data on the island of inversion.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock calculations assuming axial symmetry were performed for the N=20 isotones 30Ne, 32Mg, 34Si and the N=28 isotones 40Mg, 42Si, 44S using the M3Y-P6 semi-realistic interaction, which includes a realistic tensor force derived from the M3Y-Paris G-matrix. The Gaussian expansion method was employed for single-particle functions under axially deformed one-body fields, and energy curves E(q0) were obtained by adding a quadratic constraining term to the Hamiltonian. For comparison, calculations were also performed with the Gogny-D1M interaction (which does not include tensor force) and with the tensor force omitted from M3Y-P6.

## Key numbers

* 30Ne: prolate minimum at q0 = 158 fm^2 (beta = 0.57) with energy -204.7 MeV using M3Y-P6; experimental ground-state energy is -212.3 MeV
* 32Mg: nearly spherical minimum at q0 = 37 fm^2 (beta = 0.12) with energy -241.5 MeV and deformed minimum at q0 = 168 fm^2 (beta = 0.55) with energy -241.1 MeV using M3Y-P6; experimental ground-state energy is -249.7 MeV
* 34Si: spherical minimum at q0 = 0 fm^2 with energy -277.5 MeV using M3Y-P6; experimental ground-state energy is -283.4 MeV
* 40Mg: prolate minimum at q0 = 237 fm^2 (beta = 0.53) with energy -257.5 MeV using M3Y-P6; spherical HFB energy is -254.5 MeV
* 42Si: oblate minimum at q0 = -194 fm^2 (beta = -0.40) with energy -304.9 MeV using M3Y-P6; spherical HFB energy is -301.5 MeV
* 44S: minimum at q0 = 100 fm^2 (beta = 0.19) with energy -343.3 MeV using M3Y-P6; spherical HFB energy is -342.9 MeV; experimental ground-state energy is -351.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of M3Y-type semi-realistic interactions, previously used only for spherical nuclei by Nakada (2013) and Nakada and Suzuki (2014), to deformed nuclei for the first time. The results are compared with the Gogny-D1M interaction developed by Goriely et al. (2009), which is one of the most successful effective interactions without tensor force, and with earlier calculations by Terasaki et al. (1997), Peru et al. (2000), and Rodriguez-Guzman et al. (2002) that did not satisfactorily capture deformation for 30Ne and 32Mg. The study enables future work incorporating pair correlations via Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations and angular-momentum projection to fully resolve the shape coexistence and magicity questions in the island of inversion region.
