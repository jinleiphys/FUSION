# Local Chirality of Low-Lying Dirac Eigenmodes and the Instanton Liquid Model

> The reasons for using low-lying Dirac eigenmodes to probe the local structure of topological charge fluctuations in QCD are discussed, and it is pointed out that the qualitative double-peaked behavior of the local chiral orientation probability distribution in these modes is necessary, but not sufficient for dominance of instanton-like fluctuations. The results with overlap Dirac operator in Wilson gauge backgrounds at lattice spacings ranging from a~0.04 fm to a~0.12 fm are reported, and it is found that the size and density of local structures responsible for double-peaking of the distribution are in disagreement with the assumptions of the Instanton Liquid Model. More generally, our results suggest that vacuum fluctuations of topological charge are not effectively dominated by locally quantized (integer-valued) lumps in QCD.

## Key claim

The double-peaked behavior of the local chiral orientation probability distribution in low-lying Dirac eigenmodes is necessary but not sufficient for instanton dominance of topological charge fluctuations; the size and density of the local structures responsible for this double-peaking disagree with the assumptions of the Instanton Liquid Model (ILM), suggesting that vacuum fluctuations of topological charge in QCD are not effectively dominated by locally quantized (integer-valued) lumps.

## Method

The authors computed low-lying eigenmodes of the overlap Dirac operator (with Wilson-Dirac kernel, rho=1.368) on Wilson gauge ensembles at four lattice spacings from a=0.042 fm to a=0.123 fm, using the Ritz variational method to obtain eigenmodes of D^+ D. They analyzed the probability distribution of the local chiral orientation parameter X (defined via tan(pi/4 (1+X)) = |psi_L|/|psi_R|) over the top 10% of lattice sites ordered by eigenmode density, and extracted the average radius and density of the fermionic structures contributing to the peaks of the X-distribution.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings studied: a=0.123 fm (beta=5.85), a=0.093 fm (beta=6.00), a=0.068 fm (beta=6.20), a=0.042 fm (beta=6.55)
- The average radius of structures responsible for double-peaking extrapolates to a finite continuum limit value of R=0.15 fm
- The density of structures at a=0.042 fm is n=50 fm^{-4}, with the possibility that it diverges in the continuum limit
- The ILM assumes instanton radius rho=1/3 fm and density n=1 fm^{-4}, both in strong disagreement with the lattice results

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Horvath (2001) to use low-lying Dirac eigenmodes to probe local topological charge fluctuations, and addresses the ambiguity left by earlier studies (Horvath 2001, deGrand 2000, Gattringer 2001) that found qualitative double-peaking but could not distinguish instanton dominance from other mechanisms. The results contradict the Instanton Liquid Model of Diakonov and Petrov, which posits a dilute vacuum of (anti)instantons with radius 1/3 fm and density 1 fm^{-4}, and instead suggest that topological charge fluctuations are not dominated by locally quantized lumps, with implications for the microscopic explanation of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking.
