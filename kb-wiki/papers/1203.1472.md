# Spectral-statistics properties of the experimental and theoretical light meson spectra

> We present a robust analysis of the spectral fluctuations exhibited by the light meson spectrum. This analysis provides information about the degree of chaos in light mesons and may be useful to get some insight on the underlying interactions. Our analysis unveils that the statistical properties of the light meson spectrum are close, but not exactly equal, to those of chaotic systems. In addition, we have analyzed several theoretical spectra including the latest lattice QCD calculation. With a single exception, their statistical properties are close to those of a generic integrable system, and thus incompatible with the experimental spectrum.

## Key claim

The spectral fluctuations of the experimental light meson spectrum are close to, but not exactly equal to, those of chaotic systems; a Berry-Robnik distribution with 78% chaos provides the best description, while all theoretical spectra except one are incompatible with chaos and instead resemble integrable systems.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical analysis of nearest neighbor spacing distributions (NNSD) for 126 experimental light meson energy levels from the Review of Particle Physics (RPP) up to 2.5 GeV, separated into 23 sequences with fixed spin, isospin, parity and C-parity. They used a local unfolding procedure to remove the smooth level density and compared the experimental NNSD to distorted Poisson, Wigner, and Berry-Robnik distributions generated from 1000 realizations of synthetic spectra with the same sequence structure. The same analysis was applied to six theoretical spectra: five constituent quark models (Godfrey-Isgur, Koll et al. models A and B, Ebert et al., Vijande et al.) and one lattice QCD calculation by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Experimental spectrum: 126 energy levels from RPP up to 2.5 GeV, distributed in 23 sequences with lengths l_i >= 3.
- Berry-Robnik fit to experimental NNSD yields chaotic fraction f = 0.78 ± 0.13.
- K-S test p-values for experimental spectrum: p_DW = 0.47 (Wigner null hypothesis), p_DP = 0.13 (Poisson null hypothesis), p_DBR = 0.68 (Berry-Robnik with f=0.78).
- For 1000 realizations of experimental spectrum with error bars: mean p_DBR = 0.77 ± 0.07, mean p_DP = 0.28 ± 0.07, mean p_DW = 0.46 ± 0.09.
- K-S test p-values for theoretical spectra: GI (p_DW=0.84, p_DP=0.41), K1 (p_DW=0.038, p_DP=0.55), K2 (p_DW=0.005, p_DP=0.43), E (p_DW=0.083, p_DP=0.21), V (p_DW=0.51, p_DP=0.56), LQCD (p_DW=0.033, p_DP=0.44).
- Vijande et al. model NNSD fitted to Berry-Robnik with f = 0.63 ± 0.19.
- Lattice QCD NNSD at zero spacing: P_LQCD(s=0) ≈ 0.6.
- Lattice QCD calculation performed at pion mass of 396 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the spectral-statistics approach previously applied to baryons by Fernandez-Ramirez et al. (2007) to the light meson spectrum, where that earlier study found experimental baryon spectra to be chaotic while quark models produced integrable spectra. The present analysis confirms a similar pattern for mesons: the experimental spectrum shows near-chaotic behavior (78% chaos), while most theoretical models (Godfrey-Isgur 1985, Koll et al. 2000, Ebert et al. 2009) and the lattice QCD calculation by Dudek et al. (2011) produce integrable or nearly-integrable spectra. Only the model by Vijande et al. (2005) gives an intermediate spectrum with 63% chaos. The results suggest that current theoretical approaches miss essential ingredients needed to reproduce the dynamical regime of real mesons, and that the lattice QCD calculation at unphysical pion mass (396 MeV) requires both lower pion mass and careful examination of approximations.
