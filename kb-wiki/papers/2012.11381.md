# \boldmath{$╬ź$} and \boldmath{$╬Ę_b$} mass shifts in nuclear matter

> We estimate the $╬ź$, $╬Ę_b$ and $B^*$ meson mass shifts in symmetric nuclear matter. The interest is, whether the strengths of the bottomonium-(nuclear matter) and charmonium-(nuclear matter) interactions are similar or different. This is because, each ($J/╬©,╬ź$) and ($╬Ę_c,╬Ę_b$) meson group is usually assumed to have very similar properties based on the heavy charm and bottom quark masses. The estimate for the $╬ź$ is made using an SU(5) effective Lagrangian and the anomalous coupling one, by studying the $BB$, $BB^*$, and $B^*B^*$ meson loop contributions for the self-energy. As for the $╬Ę_b$, we include the $BB^*$ and $B^*B^*$ meson loop contributions in the self-energy. The in-medium masses of the $B$ and $B^*$ mesons appearing in the self-energy are calculated by the quark-meson coupling model. An analysis on the $BB$, $BB^*$, and $B^*B^*$ meson loops in the $╬ź$ mass shift is made by comparing with the corresponding $DD, DD^*$, and $D^*D^*$ meson loops for the $J/╬©$ mass shift. Our prediction for the $╬Ę_b$ mass shift is made including only the lowest order $BB^*$ meson loop. The $╬ź$ mass shift, with including only the $BB$ loop, is predicted to be -16 to -22 MeV at the nuclear matter saturation density using the $╬źBB$ coupling constant determined by the vector meson dominance model with the experimental data, while the $╬Ę_b$ mass shift is predicted to be -75 to -82 MeV with the SU(5) universal coupling constant determined by the $╬źBB$ coupling constant. Our results show an appreciable difference between the bottomonium-(nuclear matter) and charmonium-(nuclear matter) interaction strengths. We also study the $╬ź$ and $╬Ę_b$ mass shifts in a heavy quark (heavy meson) symmetry limit.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the bottomonium-nuclear matter interaction strengths are appreciably different from the charmonium-nuclear matter interaction strengths, contrary to the usual assumption that each (J/psi, Upsilon) and (eta_c, eta_b) meson group has very similar properties based on the heavy charm and bottom quark masses. The predictions are made with the caveat that only the lowest-order meson loop contributions are included for the final results, specifically the BB loop for Upsilon and the BB* loop for eta_b.

## Method

The Upsilon and eta_b mass shifts in symmetric nuclear matter were estimated using an SU(5) effective Lagrangian and an anomalous coupling Lagrangian to compute the self-energy contributions from BB, BB*, and B*B* meson loops. The in-medium masses of the B and B* mesons appearing in the self-energy loops were calculated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which describes the internal structure of hadrons in a nuclear medium via self-consistent couplings of confined light quarks to scalar and vector mean fields. Form factors with a wide range of cutoff mass values (2000-6000 MeV) were used to regularize the loop integrals, and a detailed comparison was made between the Upsilon and J/psi meson loop contributions.

## Key numbers

- Upsilon mass shift at nuclear matter saturation density: -16 to -22 MeV, using only the BB meson loop contribution with the Upsilon BB coupling constant determined by the vector meson dominance model and cutoff masses in the range 2000-6000 MeV.
- eta_b mass shift at nuclear matter saturation density: -75 to -82 MeV, using only the BB* meson loop contribution with the SU(5) universal coupling constant determined by the Upsilon BB coupling constant for the same cutoff mass range.
- In the heavy quark symmetry limit, the Upsilon mass shift is predicted to be -6 to -9 MeV at saturation density.
- In the heavy quark symmetry limit, the eta_b mass shift is predicted to be -31 to -38 MeV, and -8 to -11 MeV with a broken SU(5) symmetry.
- The B and B* meson mass shifts at saturation density are both -61 MeV, as calculated by the QMC model.
- The J/psi mass shift from earlier work using only the DD loop is -3.0 to -6.5 MeV at saturation density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by two of the authors on charmonium mass shifts in nuclear matter (Krein 2010, Krein 2017) to the bottomonium sector, estimating for the first time the Upsilon and eta_b mass shifts as well as the B* meson mass in medium. The analysis compares the bottomonium and charmonium loop contributions in detail, using the same QMC model framework that was previously applied to D and D* mesons. The results enable future experimental studies at facilities such as the 12 GeV CEBAF upgrade at Jefferson Lab and the FAIR facility in Germany, where heavy quarkonium production in nuclei can probe the gluonic properties of the nuclear medium.
