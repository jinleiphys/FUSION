# Symmetry-preserving contact interaction model for heavy-light mesons

> We use a symmetry-preserving regularization method of ultraviolet divergences in a vector-vector contact interac- tion model for low-energy QCD. The contact interaction is a representation of nonperturbative kernels used Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. The regularization method is based on a subtraction scheme that avoids standard steps in the evaluation of divergent integrals that invariably lead to symmetry violation. Aiming at the study of heavy-light mesons, we have implemented the method to the pseudoscalar pion and Kaon mesons. We have solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the u, d and s quark propagators, and obtained the bound-state Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes in a way that the Ward-Green-Takahashi identities reflecting global symmetries of the model are satisfied for arbitrary routing of the momenta running in loop integrals.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a symmetry-preserving subtraction scheme for regularizing ultraviolet divergences in a vector-vector contact interaction model can separate symmetry-offending terms in Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes independently of the arbitrary momentum routing in loop integrals, thereby preserving Ward-Green-Takahashi identities for any choice of the momentum partitioning parameters η±.

## Method

The authors implement a subtraction scheme based on repeated use of an algebraic identity to rewrite divergent integrals in the Dyson-Schwinger equation for quark propagators and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar mesons, avoiding momentum shifts in divergent integrals. They apply this scheme to the contact-interaction model of Gutierrez-Guerrero et al. (2010), solving the gap equation for u, d, and s quark propagators and obtaining bound-state Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for π and K mesons using proper-time regularization for the quadratic divergent integrals.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses from the gap equation: M_u = M_d = 0.367 GeV, M_s = 0.525 GeV, using input parameters α_IR = 0.93π, m_G = 0.8 GeV, m_u = m_d = 0.007 GeV, m_s = 0.160 GeV, τ²_IR = (0.24 GeV)², τ²_UV = (0.905 GeV)²
- π meson mass: 0.139 GeV, decay constant f_π = 0.106 GeV (compared to 0.139 GeV and 0.101 GeV from Chen et al. (2012))
- K meson mass: 0.494 GeV, decay constant f_K = 0.115 GeV (compared to 0.493 GeV and 0.107 GeV from Chen et al. (2012))
- Decay constants from the subtraction scheme are less than 5% larger than those of Chen et al. (2012)

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-preserving contact-interaction model of Gutierrez-Guerrero et al. (2010) and Roberts et al. (2010, 2011) by implementing a regularization scheme inspired by Battistel (1998) that was previously applied to Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models in vacuum (Battistel 2008, 2013) and at finite temperature (Farias 2005, 2006, 2007). The method enables systematic identification of symmetry-offending integrals A_μν, B_μν, and C_μνρσ, and the authors plan to apply it to heavy-light mesons (D and B mesons) where traditional cutoff schemes give incorrect ordering of decay constants (f_D < f_π instead of f_π < f_K < f_D).
