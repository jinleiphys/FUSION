# Dark matter scattering off ${}^4$He in chiral effective field theory

> We study dark matter scattering off ${}^4$He and other light nuclei using chiral effective field theory. We consider scalar DM interactions and include both one- and two-nucleon scattering processes. The DM interactions and nuclear wave functions are obtained from chiral effective field theory and we work up to fourth order in the chiral expansion for the latter to investigate the chiral convergence. The results for the scattering rates can be used to determine the sensitivity of planned experiments to detect relatively light dark matter particles using ${}^4$He. We find that next-to-leading-order scalar currents are smaller than expected from power counting for scattering off ${}^4$He confirming earlier work. However, the results for two-nucleon corrections exhibit a linear regulator dependence indicating potential problems in the applied power counting. We observe a linear correlation between the, in principle not observable, D-wave probability of various light nuclei and the scalar two-nucleon matrix elements, again pointing towards potentially missing contributions.

## Key claim

The paper finds that next-to-leading-order (NLO) scalar dark matter currents are smaller than expected from power counting for scattering off ⁴He, but the two-nucleon corrections exhibit a linear regulator dependence that indicates potential problems with the applied power counting, suggesting that short-range counter terms may need to be promoted to lower order.

## Method

The authors used chiral effective field theory (χEFT) to compute scalar dark matter scattering off ⁴He, ²H, and ³He nuclei, including both one-nucleon and two-nucleon scattering processes. Nuclear wave functions were obtained from chiral nuclear forces up to fourth order (N⁴LO) in the chiral expansion using Yakubovsky equations, and the scalar hadronic current was calculated up to N²LO (with no new corrections at N²LO). The response functions were evaluated for transfer momenta in the range q = (0–200) MeV using wave functions with different chiral orders and a range of cut-offs {R₂, R₃, R₄, R₅} = {0.9, 1.0, 1.1, 1.2} fm.

## Key numbers

* For ⁴He at q² = 0, the two-body correction Δ^(2b) ranges from (0.4 ± 1.9)% using N²LO wave functions, with the sign uncertain.
* For ³He at q² = 0, the two-body correction Δ^(2b) ranges from (−0.01 ± 0.13)% using N²LO wave functions.
* The maximum momentum transfer for ⁴He assuming m_χ ~ m_⁴He and escape velocity v_χ^esc ≈ 550 km/s is roughly |q| ≤ 12 MeV.
* The LO response function |F_0|² equals unity at zero momentum transfer due to normalization.
* For ²H, ³He, and ⁴He, NLO currents modify LO results by only a few percent despite larger power-counting expectations.
* The numerical uncertainty for expectation values of isoscalar quark-DM two-nucleon contributions at q = 0 is estimated to be below the 1% level with respect to the value of each matrix element.

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral EFT studies of dark matter scattering on light nuclei by Körber (2017) and Andreoli (2018), confirming that NLO corrections are small for ²H, ³He, and ⁴He. However, the observed linear regulator dependence of two-nucleon matrix elements for ⁴He suggests a potential renormalization problem similar to that found in neutrinoless double beta decay, where formally subleading contact terms must be promoted to leading order as shown by Cirigliano (2018, 2020), Wirth (2021), and Jokiniemi (2021). The results enable future work to determine whether scalar DM-nucleon-nucleon contact terms must be promoted to lower order, which would impact predictions for dark matter scattering on heavier nuclei and could be constrained by lattice QCD data on nuclear binding energies as a function of quark mass.
