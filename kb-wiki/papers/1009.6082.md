# On the nuclear stopping in asymmetric colliding nuclei

> Using an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, nuclear stopping is analyzed in asymmetric colliding channels by keeping the total mass fixed. The calculations have been carried by varying the asymmetry of the colliding pairs with different neutron-proton ratios in center of mass energy 250 MeV/nucleon and by switching off the effect of Coulomb interactions. We find sizable effect of asymmetry of colliding pairs on the stopping and therefore on the equilibrium reached in a reaction.

## Key claim
Using the IQMD model with Coulomb interactions switched off, nuclear stopping in asymmetric collisions depends sizably on the asymmetry of the colliding pairs at a fixed center-of-mass energy of 250 MeV/nucleon and fixed total mass, with maximum stopping obtained for systems having the largest neutron-to-proton ratio.

## Method
The authors employed the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, which treats different charge states of nucleons explicitly via both cross sections and mean field. They simulated collisions with projectile masses between 16 and 56 and target isotopes of Xe, Sn, and Ru such that the total system mass remained constant at 152, yielding asymmetries eta = |(A_T - A_P)/(A_T + A_P)| ranging from 0.2 to 0.7. Coulomb interactions were switched off, and all calculations were performed at a fixed center-of-mass energy of 250 MeV/nucleon; nuclear stopping was quantified using rapidity distributions, the anisotropy ratio R, and the quadrupole moment Q_zz.

## Key numbers
- Total system mass fixed at 152 for all reaction channels.
- Center-of-mass energy fixed at 250 MeV/nucleon.
- Asymmetry eta varied from 0.2 (nearly symmetric, e.g., Fe-56 + Ru-96) to 0.7 (most asymmetric, e.g., O-16 + Xe-136).
- Neutron-to-proton (N/Z) ratios ranged from 1.1 (eta = 0.2) to 1.4 (eta = 0.7).
- Gaussian width for nucleon density distributions: sigma = 0.16 N^(1/3) + 0.49 fm (tested at impact parameter b-hat = 0.1).
- Neutron-neutron or proton-proton cross-section is a factor of 3 lower than the neutron-proton cross-section.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of nuclear stopping that focused on symmetric colliding partners, such as those by Bauer (1988) linking stopping to mean-field and in-medium NN cross sections, and by Li et al. (1998) connecting isospin equilibrium to the equation of state. It also builds on the work of Kaur and co-workers (2010) who studied the effect of symmetry energy and isospin-dependent cross-section on stopping. The findings enable future investigations of how asymmetry affects thermalization and equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at radioactive beam facilities where neutron-rich or proton-rich nuclei are available.
