# Remark to ''On the Description of Fermion Systems in Boson Representations''

> The formulae of boson powers commutation relation used by B. Sorensen [Nucl. Phys. A, v. 119, No 1, (1968), 65] in his calculations is erroneous. We provide the correct formulae.

## Key claim

The boson-powers commutation relation formula (2.5) used by Sorensen (1968) in his calculations is erroneous; the authors provide the correct algebraic expressions for the commutator \([a^n, (a^\dagger)^m]\) for all integer \(n,m\).

## Method

The authors start from the identity \([A, B^n] = \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} B^k [A,B] B^{n-k-1}\) with \(A \to a\) and \(B \to a^\dagger\), then apply induction or the Leibniz rule for derivatives (mapping \(a \to d/dx\) and \(a^\dagger \to x\)) to derive the correct commutator. The corrected formula replaces the erroneous factor \((l+1)\) in Sorensen’s original expression with binomial coefficients \(\binom{n}{l}\) or \(\binom{m}{l}\) as appropriate.

## Key numbers

- For \(n=m=4\), the correct commutator is \([a^4, (a^\dagger)^4] = 24 + 96 a^\dagger a + 72 a^{\dagger 2} a^2 + 16 a^{\dagger 3} a^3\).
- Sorensen’s erroneous formula for \(n=m=4\) gives \([a^4, (a^\dagger)^4] = 24 + 48 a^\dagger a + 36 a^{\dagger 2} a^2 + 16 a^{\dagger 3} a^3\), which differs in the coefficients of the \(a^\dagger a\) and \(a^{\dagger 2} a^2\) terms.

## Context

This remark corrects a specific algebraic error in the boson-powers commutation relation published by Sorensen (1968) in *Nuclear Physics A*, which was used in calculations for describing fermion systems in boson representations. The corrected formula ensures that subsequent work relying on these commutation relations (e.g., in nuclear-structure mapping methods) uses the proper operator algebra. No new physical results or applications are presented; the paper is a purely formal correction.
