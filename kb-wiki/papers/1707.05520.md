# Tachyonic instability of the scalar mode prior to QCD critical point based on Functional renormalization-group method

> We establish and elucidate the physical meaning of the appearance of an acausal mode in the sigma mesonic channel, found in the previous work by the present authors, when the system approaches the $\mathrm{Z}_{2}$ critical point. The functional renormalization group method is applied to the two--flavor quark--meson model with varying current quark mass $m_q$ even away from the physical value at which the pion mass is reproduced. We first determine the whole phase structure in the three-dimensional space $(T, μ, m_q)$ consisting of temperature $T$, quark chemical potential $μ$ and $m_q$, with the tricritical point, $\mathrm{O}(4)$ and $\mathrm{Z}_{2}$ critical lines being located; they altogether make a wing-like shape quite reminiscent of those known in the condensed matters with a tricritical point. We then calculate the spectral functions $ρ_{σ, π}(ω, p)$ in the scalar and pseudoscalar channel around the critical points. We find that the sigma mesonic mode becomes tachyonic with a superluminal velocity at finite momenta before the system reaches the $\mathrm{Z}_{2}$ point from the lower density, even for $m_q$ smaller than the physical value. One of the possible implications of the appearance of such a tachyonic mode at finite momenta is that the assumed equilibrium state with a uniform chiral condensate is unstable toward a state with an inhomogeneous $σ$ condensate. No such an anomalous behavior is found in the pseudoscalar channel. We find that the $σ$-to-$2σ$ coupling due to finite $m_q$ play an essential role for the drastic modification of the spectral function.

## Key claim

The sigma mesonic mode becomes tachyonic with a superluminal group velocity at finite momenta before the system reaches the Z2 critical point from the lower density side, even for current quark masses smaller than the physical value; this anomalous behavior is attributed to the sigma-to-2sigma coupling arising from finite current quark mass, and may indicate instability of the uniform chiral condensate toward an inhomogeneous sigma condensate.

## Method

The functional renormalization-group (FRG) method was applied to the two-flavor quark-meson model, with the effective average action truncated at the local potential approximation level and using three-dimensional optimized regulators. The flow equations for the scale-dependent effective potential and for the retarded Green's functions of the sigma and pion mesons were solved numerically on a grid, with the analytic continuation performed by replacing the Matsubara frequency with ω + iε. The phase structure was mapped in the three-dimensional space of temperature T, quark chemical potential μ, and current quark mass mq, and the spectral functions ρσ(ω, p) and ρπ(ω, p) were computed around the O(4) and Z2 critical points.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point in the chiral limit lies between T = 44 MeV and T = 45 MeV.
- For the physical vacuum, the constituent quark mass is 286 MeV, the pion mass is 137 MeV, and the sigma mass is 496 MeV, with the UV cutoff Λ = 1000 MeV.
- The Z2 critical temperature Tc(mq) decreases from 8.6 MeV at mq = 1.27 MeV to 4.0 MeV at mq = 15.23 MeV; the corresponding critical chemical potential μc(mq) increases from 264.3059 MeV to 301.4115 MeV.
- The sigma screening mass vanishes at the Z2 critical point, defining its location with temperature intervals of ΔT = 0.1 MeV and chemical potential intervals of Δμ = 0.0001 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FRG study (Yokota, Kunihiro, Morita, 2016) which found an acausal sigma mode near the Z2 critical point at physical quark mass, by now varying the current quark mass to map the full wing-like phase diagram in (T, μ, mq) space. The results contradict the expectation from random phase approximation analyses (Fujii, 2004; Son, 2004) that the sigma meson becomes a hard mode at the Z2 critical point, and instead reveal a tachyonic instability that may precede the formation of an inhomogeneous chiral condensate. The wing-like phase structure is analogous to that seen in condensed-matter systems with a tricritical point, such as metamagnets and 3He-4He mixtures (PhysRevB.7.545; Gebhardt, 1980).
