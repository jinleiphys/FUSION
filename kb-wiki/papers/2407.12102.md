# BCS-BEC crossover of the strongly interacting $^6$Li-$^{40}$K mixture

> We present Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the properties of a two-component mass imbalanced Fermi gas, corresponding to the $^6$Li-$^{40}$K mixture. We compute the equation of state of the unpolarized system as a function of the scattering length with particular attention paid to the unitary limit, where the effect of the effective range of the interaction is explored. We have also computed the pair distribution function and the momentum distribution over a range of interaction strengths to provide information about the structure of the system. Finally, we have computed the heavy/light quasiparticle spectrum. Our theoretical predictions, based on Quantum Monte Carlo calculations, can be tested by future experiments with ultracold fermionic gases.

# BCS-BEC crossover of the strongly interacting $^6$Li-$^{40}$K mixture

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo calculations show that the Bertsch parameter $\xi$ for the mass-imbalanced $^6$Li-$^{40}$K mixture at unitarity ($\xi = 0.368(1)$ after extrapolation to zero effective range) is quantitatively different from the equal-mass case, while higher-order terms in the equation of state and the universal contact parameter remain similar; these results depend on careful treatment of finite effective-range effects and the fixed-node approximation.

## Method

The authors performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) simulations of 66 fermions (33 light $^6$Li and 33 heavy $^{40}$K atoms) in a periodic box, using a Poschl-Teller two-body potential tuned to reproduce desired scattering lengths and effective ranges. The trial wave function combined a Jastrow factor with a particle-projected BCS pairing function, and the ground state was projected via imaginary-time propagation within the fixed-node approximation. The equation of state, pair-distribution function, momentum distribution, and quasiparticle dispersion were computed across the BCS-BEC crossover by varying $1/ak_F$ from approximately $-4$ to $+2$.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter at unitarity after extrapolation to zero effective range: $\xi = 0.368(1)$ (from a linear fit $E/E_{FG} = \xi + c\,r_e k_F$ with $c = 0.13(1)$)
- Bertsch parameter from fit near unitarity (including $1/k_F a$ corrections): $\xi = 0.3726(6)$, with $\zeta = 0.900(2)$ and $\nu = 0.46(1)$ in the expansion $E/E_{FG} = \xi - \zeta/(k_F a) - 5\nu/[3(k_F a)^2] + \dots$
- Dimer-dimer scattering length ratio from fit to Bose gas mean-field expansion in the BEC regime ($1/ak_F \geq 2$): $a_{dd}/a = 0.886(4)$
- Effective range in most calculations: $r_e k_F \approx 0.03$ (with additional checks at $r_e k_F$ corresponding to $\mu/k_F = 24$ and $\mu/k_F = 60$)
- System size: 66 fermions (33 light, 33 heavy) with mass ratio $m_h/m_l = 6.5$

## Context

This work extends previous Quantum Monte Carlo studies of equal-mass unitary Fermi gases (Gandolfi, Schmidt, Carlson 2011) and earlier mass-imbalanced calculations (Gezerlis, Gandolfi, Schmidt et al. 2009) to the full BCS-BEC crossover for the $^6$Li-$^{40}$K mixture. The results provide equation-of-state parameters needed for density functional descriptions of trapped systems (Forbes, Gandolfi, Gezerlis 2011, 2012) and can be tested by ongoing ultracold atom experiments on mass-imbalanced Fermi mixtures (Kohstall, Zaccanti, Jag et al. 2012; Trenkwalder, Kohstall, Zaccanti et al. 2011). The findings enable future investigations into the role of nonzero total-momentum pairing and effective-range effects in mass-imbalanced systems, and they highlight both similarities (contact parameter, higher-order EOS terms) and differences (Bertsch parameter, quasiparticle dispersion, pair-distribution function tail) compared to the equal-mass case.
