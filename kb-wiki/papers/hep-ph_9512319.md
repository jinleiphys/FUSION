# QCD Equalities for Baryon Current Matrix Elements

> An examination of the symmetries manifest in the QCD path integral for current matrix elements reveals various equalities among the quark sector contributions. QCD equalities among octet baryon magnetic moments lead to a determination of the disconnected sea-quark contribution to nucleon magnetic moments, which is the most reliable determination in the literature. Matching QCD equalities to recent calculations of decuplet baryon magnetic moments in chiral perturbation theory (ChiPT) reveals an equivalence between ChiPT to order $p^2$ and the simple quark model with an explicit disconnected sea-quark contribution. New insights into SU(3)-flavor symmetry breaking, sea contributions and constituent quark composition are obtained. The strangeness contribution to nucleon magnetic moments is found to be large at $G_M^s(0) = -0.75 \pm 0.30\ μ_N$. The QCD equalities must be followed by any model which hopes to capture the essence of nonperturbative QCD. Not all models are in accord with these symmetries.

## Key claim

QCD path integral symmetries impose exact equalities among baryon current matrix elements that any model must satisfy to capture nonperturbative QCD, but these equalities depend on the approximation that u and d current quark masses are equal (isospin symmetry), which is excellent since the QCD scale parameter is much larger than the mass difference.

## Method

The author derived QCD equalities from the Euclidean path integral formulation by examining the detailed structure of three-point correlation functions for octet and decuplet baryons, separating connected current insertions on valence quarks from disconnected sea-quark loop contributions. These equalities were then matched to experimental magnetic moments and lattice QCD calculations of environment sensitivity ratios to extract the disconnected sea-quark contributions, and compared with chiral perturbation theory (ChiPT) predictions.

## Key numbers

- Disconnected sea-quark loop contribution to nucleon magnetic moment: mu_l^N = -0.17 +/- 0.07 mu_N (weighted average from two determinations)
- Strangeness contribution to nucleon magnetic moment: G_M^s(0) = -0.75 +/- 0.30 mu_N
- Ratio of doubly represented valence quark contributions: D_N / D_Sigma = 1.136 +/- 0.078
- Ratio of singly represented valence quark contributions: S_N / S_Xi = 0.721 +/- 0.457
- Ratio of singly represented valence quark contributions: S_Sigma / S_Xi = 0.390 +/- 0.244
- Ratio of doubly represented valence quark contributions: D_Xi / D_Sigma = 0.585 +/- 0.039
- Disconnected loop contribution from equation (mu_l^N1): -0.15 +/- 0.09 mu_N
- Disconnected loop contribution from equation (mu_l^N2): -0.20 +/- 0.09 mu_N
- Disconnected loop contribution for Sigma: mu_l^Sigma = -0.03 +/- 0.05 mu_N
- Disconnected loop contribution for Xi: mu_l^Xi = -0.14 +/- 0.05 mu_N
- Simple quark model estimate (taking ratio = 1) for nucleon disconnected loop: mu_l^N = -0.14 mu_N
- Previous estimate from Sachs sum rule violations: mu_l^N = -0.19 +/- 0.09 mu_N

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier lattice QCD studies of environment sensitivity in baryon magnetic moments (Leinweber 1991, 1992) and provides the most reliable determination of disconnected sea-quark contributions to nucleon magnetic moments in the literature. The QCD equalities reveal that chiral perturbation theory to order p^2 is equivalent to the simple quark model with an explicit disconnected sea-quark contribution, and that models absorbing loop contributions into constituent quark properties necessarily break isospin symmetry. The results enable improved lattice QCD predictions for baryon magnetic moments and provide a framework for testing any model claiming to capture nonperturbative QCD dynamics.
