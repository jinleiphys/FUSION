# Boltzmann equation with a nonlocal collision term and the resultant dissipative fluid dynamics

> Starting with the relativistic Boltzmann equation where the collision term was generalized to include gradients of the phase-space distribution function, we recently presented a new derivation of the equations for the relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics. We compared them with the corresponding equations obtained in the standard Israel-Stewart and related approaches. Our method generates all the second-order terms that are allowed by symmetry, some of which have been missed by the traditional approaches, and the coefficients of other terms are altered. The first-order or Navier-Stokes equation too receives a small correction. Here we outline this work for the general audience.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the standard derivation of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics from the Boltzmann equation misses some second-order terms allowed by symmetry, and that introducing a nonlocal collision term (including gradients of the distribution function) generates all such terms, while also altering the coefficients of existing terms and giving a small correction to the first-order Navier-Stokes equation.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation and generalize the collision term to include gradients of the phase-space distribution function, motivated by the fact that collisions within a fluid element occur at separated space-time points. They expand the distribution function in a Taylor series up to second order in the separation, obtaining modified collision terms with coefficients A^μ and B^{μν}. Using the second moment of this modified Boltzmann equation together with Grad's 14-moment approximation, they derive evolution equations for the bulk viscous pressure Π, heat current n^μ, and shear stress tensor π^{μν}.

## Key numbers

- The first-order (Navier-Stokes) equations receive a correction factor of (1-a), where a is a scalar coefficient from the nonlocal term A^μ = a(x) p^μ.
- The evolution equations for Π, n^μ, and π^{μν} each contain multiple second-order terms: the bulk equation has 8 additional terms beyond those shown explicitly, the heat current equation has 9 additional terms, and the shear equation includes terms proportional to χ_1, χ_2, and χ_3 involving derivatives of b_2.
- The numerical demonstration uses a massless Boltzmann gas with equation of state ε = 3P at vanishing net baryon number density in the Bjorken model.

## Context

This work extends the Israel-Stewart theory (Israel, 1979) and related kinetic-theory derivations of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics, showing that the standard approach misses some symmetry-allowed second-order terms. The authors compare with derivations based on the generalized second law of thermodynamics (Bhattacharyya, 2012), which also miss terms. The new equations enable more complete modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where dissipative fluid dynamics is used to calculate hadron spectra, anisotropic flows, and other observables.
