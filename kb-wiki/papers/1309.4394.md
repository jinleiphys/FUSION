# QCD in magnetic field, Landau levels and double-life of unbroken center-symmetry

> We study the thermal confinement/deconfinement and non-thermal quantum phase transitions or rapid cross-overs in QCD and QCD-like theories in external magnetic fields. At large magnetic fields, while the contribution of gauge fluctuations to Wilson-line potential remains unaltered at one-loop order, the contribution of fermions effectively becomes two lower dimensional and is enhanced by the density of states of the lowest Landau level (LLL). In a spatial compactification and for heavy adjoint fermions, this enhancement leads to a calculable zero temperature quantum phase transition on R^3*S^1 driven by a competition between the center-destabilizing gauge contribution and center-stabilizing LLL fermions. We also show that at a (formal) asymptotically large magnetic field, the adjoint fermions with arbitrarily large but fixed mass stabilize the center symmetry. This is an exotic case of simultaneous non-decoupling of large mass fermions (due to the enhancement by the LLL density of states) and decoupling from the low energy effective field theory. This observation has important implications for both Hosotani mechanism, for which gauge symmetry "breaking" occurs, and large-N volume independence (Eguchi-Kawai reduction), for which gauge structure is never "broken". Despite sounding almost self-contradictory, we carefully explain the physical scales entering the problem, double-meaning of unbroken center symmetry and how a clash is avoided. We also identify, for both thermal and spatial compactification, the jump in magnetic susceptibility as an order parameter for the deconfinement transition. The predictions of our analysis are testable by using current lattice techniques.

## Key claim

At asymptotically large magnetic fields, adjoint fermions with arbitrarily large but fixed mass stabilize the center symmetry of QCD on R^3 x S^1, even though they decouple from the low-energy effective field theory; this creates a "double-life" of unbroken center symmetry where the fermions simultaneously stabilize the symmetry and decouple from the dynamics.

## Method

The authors study QCD and QCD-like theories in external magnetic fields using one-loop analysis of the Wilson-line effective potential on a spatial compactification R^3 x S^1. They compute the separate contributions of gauge fluctuations and fermions to the effective potential, treating fermions in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation at large magnetic fields. The analysis covers both thermal (R^3 x S^1_beta) and non-thermal (R^3 x S^1) compactifications, and identifies the magnetic susceptibility jump as an order parameter for the deconfinement transition.

## Key numbers

- At large magnetic fields, the fermion contribution to the Wilson-line potential is enhanced by the density of states of the lowest Landau level (LLL), making it effectively two lower dimensional
- For heavy adjoint fermions on R^3 x S^1, a zero temperature quantum phase transition occurs driven by competition between center-destabilizing gauge contributions and center-stabilizing LLL fermions
- At a "formal asymptotically large magnetic field," adjoint fermions with arbitrarily large but fixed mass stabilize the center symmetry

## Context

This work extends the understanding of confinement/deconfinement transitions in QCD-like theories under external magnetic fields, building on the Hosotani mechanism for gauge symmetry breaking and the Eguchi-Kawai reduction for large-N volume independence. The analysis shows that the magnetic susceptibility jump serves as an order parameter for deconfinement in both thermal and spatial compactifications, making the predictions testable with current lattice techniques. The paper resolves an apparent contradiction between the non-decoupling of heavy fermions (due to LLL enhancement) and their decoupling from the low-energy effective theory, which has implications for both gauge symmetry breaking and volume independence scenarios.
