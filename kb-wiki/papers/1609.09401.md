# Tan's contact and the phase distribution of repulsive Fermi gases: Insights from QCD noise analyses

> Path-integral analyses originally pioneered in the study of the complex-phase problem afflicting lattice calculations of finite-density quantum chromodynamics are generalized to non-relativistic Fermi gases with repulsive interactions. Using arguments similar to those previously applied to relativistic theories, we show that the analogous problem in nonrelativistic systems manifests itself naturally in Tan's contact as a nontrivial cancellation between terms with varied dependence on extensive thermodynamic quantities. We analyze that case under the assumption of gaussian phase distribution, which is supported by our Monte Carlo calculations and perturbative considerations. We further generalize these results to observables other than the contact, as well as to polarized systems and systems with fixed particle number. Our results are quite general in that they apply to repulsive multi-component fermions, are independent of dimensionality or trapping potential, and hold in the ground state as well as at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The complex-phase problem in lattice Monte Carlo calculations of repulsive Fermi gases manifests itself in Tan's contact as a nontrivial cancellation between terms with varied dependence on extensive thermodynamic quantities, analogous to the signal-to-noise problem for baryon number in finite-density QCD. The analysis assumes a gaussian phase distribution, which is supported by Monte Carlo calculations and perturbative considerations, but the results are quite general in that they apply to repulsive multi-component fermions independent of dimensionality or trapping potential.

## Method

The authors generalize noise analysis techniques originally developed for finite-density QCD (Splittorff, 2009) to non-relativistic repulsive Fermi gases at finite temperature. They place the system on a discrete Euclidean spacetime lattice, implement a Trotter-Suzuki decomposition and Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, and analyze the characteristic functions of the phase of the fermion determinant. The analysis is extended to polarized systems with distinct chemical potentials and to systems at fixed particle number via Fourier projection to the canonical ensemble.

## Key numbers

* For a one-dimensional unpolarized system at finite temperature with chemical potential βμ ≈ -0.25 and lattice size Nx = 41, the phase distribution is roughly gaussian for physical dimensionless coupling λ = g√β values of 0.25, 0.5, 1.0, and 2.0, with width growing with increasing interaction strength.
* The temporal lattice extent is Nτ = ⌊β/τ⌋ ≫ 1, and the coupling enters through the parameter A² = 2(e^(-τg) - 1), where for repulsive interactions g > 0 implies A is purely imaginary.
* The characteristic function for the phase in the unpolarized case takes the form ln⟨e^(ipθ)⟩_(Nf) = -Σ_(j=1)^∞ X_j [p/2 (p/2 + Nf)]^j, where the leading term (j=1) captures the gaussian approximation.

## Context

This work generalizes the noise analysis of Splittorff (2009) from finite-density QCD to non-relativistic repulsive Fermi gases, showing that the complex-phase problem in these systems is analogous to that in QCD but arises from the signature of the interaction rather than breaking of time-reversal invariance. The results extend previous work on the sign problem in lattice calculations (de Forcrand, 2009; Gattringer, 2013) and provide a framework for understanding signal-to-noise issues in calculations of Tan's contact and other observables. The analysis enables future study of more general interactions and mixed Bose-Fermi ensembles, and the techniques apply to polarized systems and systems at fixed particle number.
