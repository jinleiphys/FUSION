# Impurities in Schrödinger field theories and s-wave resonance

> We study point impurities in non-relativistic quantum field theories, with a focus on scale-invariant fixed points. We establish the framework of conformal defects in Schrödinger field theories and their correspondence to many-body states in a harmonic trap. We discuss a multi-critical quantization of the one-body wave function induced by an impurity, with examples of free and unitary Fermi gases. Our results can potentially be tested in experimental setups.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that point impurities in non-relativistic Schrödinger field theories at scale-invariant fixed points can be described using defect conformal field theory (DCFT) formalism, and that the s-wave resonance fixed point for the impurity corresponds to an alternative quantization scheme for the one-body wave function, which exists unitarily only when the spatial dimension satisfies 2 < d < 4.

## Method

The authors develop the DCFT framework for line defects in Schrödinger field theories, deriving the bulk-to-defect operator product expansion (OPE) and the state/operator correspondence that maps defect operators to energy eigenstates in a harmonic trap with a delta-function potential at the center. They solve the equation of motion for the one-body sector to obtain the allowed scaling dimensions of defect primary operators, and compute exact two-point correlation functions for both standard and alternative quantization schemes. The analysis is applied to free Fermi gases and unitary Fermi gases, with renormalization group flows studied perturbatively near d=2 and d=3 dimensions using epsilon expansions.

## Key numbers

- The s-wave resonance fixed point exists unitarily for spatial dimensions 2 < d < 4, with the alternative quantization scaling dimension Δ(φ̂₀) = 2 − d/2
- For d=3, the exact two-point function for the s-wave resonance impurity is ⟨φ(τ₁,x₁)φ†(τ₂,x₂)⟩ = Θ(τ₁₂)(m/(2πτ₁₂))^(3/2)[exp(−m x₁₂²/(2τ₁₂)) + (τ₁₂/(m r₁ r₂))exp(−m(r₁+r₂)²/(2τ₁₂))]
- The defect chemical potential beta function near d=2 gives β(μ̂) = −ε̄μ̂ − μ̂²/π + O(ε̄³), with fixed point μ̂_f = −πε̄ + O(ε̄²)
- The defect quartic coupling beta function near d=3 gives β(λ̂) = −2ε̃λ̂ + λ̂²/(4π³) + O(ε̃³), with fixed point λ̂_f = 8π³ε̃
- In the free Fermi gas at the s-wave resonance fixed point, the lowest-lying two-body operator has scaling dimension Δ(Φ̂†₀) = 4 − d

## Context

This work extends the study of conformal defects from relativistic CFTs (Gaiotto 2014, Cuomo 2022) to non-relativistic Schrödinger field theories, which have been rarely explored in the QFT framework (Gupta 2022). The results apply to unitary Fermi gases at Feshbach resonance (Nishida 2007, Kaplan 2009) and to neutron-neutron scattering where the s-wave scattering length a_nn ≈ −18.5 fm is significantly larger than the nuclear interaction scale r_nn ≈ 2.8 fm (VSlaus 1989). The framework enables experimental tests in ultracold atomic gas systems with impurities such as neutral atoms in Bose-Einstein condensates (Cucchietti 2006) or Fermi superfluids (Massignan 2014), and provides a universal classification of impurity fixed points that can be characterized by relatively few conformal data.
