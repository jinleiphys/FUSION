# Confinement Phenomenology in the Bethe-Salpeter Equation

> We consider the solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Euclidean metric for a qbar-q vector meson in the circumstance where the dressed quark propagators have time-like complex conjugate mass poles. This approximates features encountered in recent QCD modeling via the Dyson-Schwinger equations; the absence of real mass poles simulates quark confinement. The analytic continuation in the total momentum necessary to reach the mass shell for a meson sufficiently heavier than 1 GeV leads to the quark poles being within the integration domain for two variables in the standard approach. Through Feynman integral techniques, we show how the analytic continuation can be implemented in a way suitable for a practical numerical solution. We show that the would-be qbar-q width to the meson generated from one quark pole is exactly cancelled by the effect of the conjugate partner pole; the meson mass remains real and there is no spurious qbar-q production threshold. The ladder kernel we employ is consistent with one-loop perturbative QCD and has a two-parameter infrared structure found to be successful in recent studies of the light SU(3) meson sector.

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter equation for a vector meson can be analytically continued to timelike momenta even when the dressed quark propagators contain complex conjugate mass poles (a feature simulating confinement). The would-be quark-antiquark decay width generated by one pole is exactly cancelled by its conjugate partner, ensuring the meson mass remains real and no spurious production threshold appears.

## Method
The authors adopt a parameterization of the dressed quark propagator as a sum of complex conjugate mass poles. They apply Feynman integral techniques to map the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter integration onto a one-dimensional domain, allowing the use of established numerical methods for Cauchy integrals. The method is demonstrated for a vector meson using a ladder kernel consistent with one-loop perturbative QCD and a two-parameter infrared structure.

## Key numbers
- The nearest non-analytic points for u- and d-quark propagators in a rainbow-ladder DSE model are at $q^2 = -0.207 \pm i 0.331$ GeV$^2$.
- The nearest non-analytic points for s-quark propagators in the same model are at $q^2 = -0.376 \pm i 0.602$ GeV$^2$.
- The critical meson mass above which these singularities enter the BSE integration domain is $M_c = 1.09$ GeV for $\bar{u}u$ mesons and $M_c = 1.47$ GeV for $\bar{s}s$ mesons.
- The pseudo-threshold for the onset of residue terms is $P^2 = -4\mu^2$, corresponding to a bound state mass $M_\rho > 2\mu$.

## Context
This work extends Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter studies of light mesons (Maris 1997, Maris 1999) to heavier mesons above 1 GeV, where complex singularities in quark propagators are encountered. It addresses a limitation noted in earlier work (Jain 1993) which used an Ansatz that precluded such singular behavior. The method enables future DSE-BSE studies of meson spectra beyond 1-2 GeV and applications to production processes at moderate momentum transfers (Ahlig 2000).
