# The Connection between the Stochastic Schrödinger Equation and Boltzmann Equation

> The heavy quarks present in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) can act as a probe of relativistic heavy ion collisions as they retain the memory of their interaction history. In a previous study, a stochastic Schrödinger equation (SSE) has been applied to describe the transport process of heavy quarks, where an external field with random phases is used to simulate the thermal medium. In this work, we study the connection between the SSE and the Boltzmann equation (BE) approach in the Keldysh Green's function formalism. By comparing the Green's function of the heavy quark from the SSE and the Keldysh Green's functions leading to the Boltzmann equation, we demonstrate that the SSE is consistent with the Boltzmann equation in the weak coupling limit. We subsequently confirm their consistency through numerical calculations.

## Key claim
The stochastic Schrödinger equation (SSE) is consistent with the Boltzmann equation (BE) in the weak coupling limit, as demonstrated through comparison of the heavy quark Green's function from the SSE with the Keldysh Green's functions that lead to the Boltzmann equation. The authors confirm this consistency through numerical calculations, but the connection is established only under the assumption of a thermal medium symmetric with respect to particles and antiparticles and using non-relativistic approximations for the heavy quark.

## Method
The authors derived the Boltzmann equation for fermions from the Keldysh Green's function formalism using the Wigner transformation, obtaining the drift term and scattering term. They then compared this formalism with the stochastic Schrödinger equation approach, where the thermal medium is modeled as an external gluon field with random phases following a Boltzmann distribution. The connection was established by showing that the SSE describes the process of an on-shell heavy quark absorbing or emitting an off-shell gluon and forming a final-state on-shell heavy quark, matching the scattering term structure of the Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers
- The thermalization time of QGP is typically within 5-10 fm/c, while light quarks (u and d) thermalize at around 0.5 fm/c.
- The gluon momentum distribution in the SSE follows a Boltzmann distribution: \( n(\vec{p}) = \exp(-E_{\vec{p}}/T) \), where \( E_{\vec{p}} = \sqrt{\vec{p}^2 + m_g^2} \).
- The gluon thermal mass is \( m_g = g_0 \sqrt{1 + N_f/6} \, T \) with \( N_f = 3 \) for light quark flavors.
- The color-spin degeneracy of the gluon is \( d_g = 16 \).

## Context
This work extends the SSE approach introduced by Wu et al. (preparing) for describing heavy quark transport in QGP, which previously had only been numerically compared to the Langevin equation. The authors establish the formal connection between the SSE and the Boltzmann equation using the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, building on the transport theory framework of Landau (1981) and the Keldysh Green's function approach. This connection enables future work to systematically include quantum effects beyond the Boltzmann equation through the SSE, which operates at the amplitude level rather than the probability level.
