# Harmonic flow correlations in Au+Au reactions at 1.23 AGeV: A new testing ground for the Equation-of-State and expansion geometry

> Correlations between the harmonic flow coefficients $v_1$, $v_2$, $v_3$ and $v_4$ of nucleons in semi-peripheral Au+Au collisions at a beam energy of 1.23~AGeV are investigated within the hadronic transport approach Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD). In contrast to ultra-relativistic collision energies (where the flow coefficients are evaluated with respect to the respective event plane), we predict strong correlations between the flow harmonics with respect to the reaction plane. Based on an event-by-event selection of the midrapidity final state elliptic flow of protons we show that as a function of rapidity, I) the sign of the triangular flow changes, II) that the shape of $v_4$ changes from convex to concave, and III) that $v_3\propto v_1v_2$ and $v_4\propto v_2^2$ for all different event classes, indicating strong correlations between all investigated harmonic flow coefficients.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that, in contrast to ultra-relativistic collisions where flow coefficients are evaluated with respect to the event plane, strong correlations between harmonic flow coefficients (with respect to the reaction plane) exist at 1.23 AGeV, and that these correlations are highly sensitive to the nuclear Equation-of-State (EoS). The claim is hedged by the fact that the results are obtained from a specific transport model (UrQMD v3.5) and that the correlations are attributed to the expansion geometry and time-dependent emission pattern, which are fundamentally different from high-energy collisions.

## Method

The authors used the hadronic transport model UrQMD (v3.5) to simulate 20-30% peripheral Au+Au collisions at a kinetic beam energy of 1.23 AGeV. They analyzed the event-by-event distribution of the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) at midrapidity and split the events into 14 classes based on the value of \(\langle v_2\rangle_{|y_\mathrm{cm}|\leq0.5}\). For each event class, they extracted the rapidity dependence of the first four harmonic flow coefficients (\(v_1\), \(v_2\), \(v_3\), \(v_4\)) with respect to the known reaction plane, and tested scaling relations \(v_3 \propto v_1 v_2\) and \(v_4 \propto 0.5 v_2^2\). They also computed Pearson correlation coefficients between the flow harmonics and compared results for a hard EoS, a soft EoS, and a cascade mode (no potential).

## Key numbers

- The mean elliptic flow at midrapidity for all events is \(\langle v_2\rangle_{|y_\mathrm{cm}|\leq0.5} = -0.05\), consistent with HADES data.
- The full width at half maximum (FWHM) of the \(v_2\) distribution at midrapidity is 0.15.
- The scaling relation for quadrangular flow is quantified as \(v_4/v_2^2 \approx 0.5\).
- The quadratic coefficients for \(v_4\) as a function of \(v_2\) trigger are 0.521 (hard EoS), 0.521 (soft EoS), and 0.522 (cascade).
- The Pearson correlation between \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) has a maximal value of -0.06.

## Context

This work extends the study of harmonic flow correlations, previously investigated at ultra-relativistic energies by ALICE (2016) and STAR (2018) and in hydrodynamic simulations by Niemi (2012), to the low-energy regime accessible at GSI. It builds on prior UrQMD studies of flow at HADES energies by Hillmann (2018, 2019) and on the experimental measurements of flow coefficients up to \(v_6\) by the HADES collaboration (2020). The results enable a new method to extract the nuclear Equation-of-State by using event-shape engineering (as done at high energies by Schukraft (2012)) and by analyzing the curvature and slope of flow harmonics as functions of the elliptic flow trigger, which are shown to be sensitive to the stiffness of the EoS.
