# Quarkonium production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions: CGC meets NRQCD

> We study the production of heavy quarkonium states in high energy proton-nucleus collisions. Following earlier work of Blaizot, Fujii, Gelis, and Venugopalan, we systematically include both small $x$ evolution and multiple scattering effects on heavy quark pair production within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework. We obtain for the first time expressions in the Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization formalism for heavy quarkonium differential cross sections as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity. We observe that the production of color singlet heavy quark pairs is sensitive to both "quadrupole" and "dipole" Wilson line correlators, whose energy evolution is described by the Balitsky-JIMWLK equations. In contrast, the color octet channel is sensitive to dipole correlators alone. In a quasi-classical approximation, our results for the color singlet channel reduce to those of Dominguez et. al. [1]. We compare our results to those obtained combining the CGC with the color evaporation model and point to qualitative differences in the two approaches.

## Key claim

The authors derive for the first time expressions within the NRQCD factorization formalism for heavy quarkonium differential cross sections in high energy proton-nucleus collisions, systematically including both small x evolution and multiple scattering effects from the Color Glass Condensate framework; the results are obtained under the assumption that the hadronization of heavy quark pairs into quarkonium states occurs well after the collision, so vacuum NRQCD matrix elements are applicable.

## Method

The heavy quark pair production amplitude from Blaizot et al. (2004) is projected onto color singlet and color octet configurations using NRQCD spin and color projection operators, with Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for LS coupling. The resulting short-distance cross sections are expressed in terms of Wilson line correlators (dipole and quadrupole) whose energy evolution is governed by the Balitsky-JIMWLK equations, and these are matched to long-distance NRQCD matrix elements to obtain cross sections for S-wave and P-wave quarkonium states.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_s characterizes the semi-hard scale separating highly occupied gluon modes from perturbative dynamics, with Q_s^2 >> Λ_QCD^2.
- For J/ψ production, there are four independent LDMEs: ⟨O^{J/ψ}(^3S_1^{[1]})⟩, ⟨O^{J/ψ}(^1S_0^{[8]})⟩, ⟨O^{J/ψ}(^3S_1^{[8]})⟩, and ⟨O^{J/ψ}(^3P_0^{[8]})⟩.
- The CS LDME ⟨O^{J/ψ}(^3S_1^{[1]})⟩ is largest in powers of v, while the three CO LDMEs are power suppressed by v^3, v^4, and v^4 respectively.
- For J/ψ production at large transverse momentum p_⊥, the CS channel at leading order in α_s is suppressed by m^2/p_⊥^2 compared to the ^1S_0^{[8]} and ^3P_J^{[8]} channels, and further suppressed by m^4/p_⊥^4 compared to the ^3S_1^{[8]} channel.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-classical results of Dominguez et al. (2011) for color singlet J/ψ production by including full small x evolution via the Balitsky-JIMWLK equations, and generalizes the heavy quark pair production formalism of Blaizot, Fujii, Gelis, and Venugopalan (2004) to specific quarkonium states. It contrasts with the color evaporation model approach of Fujii et al. (2013) for J/ψ and Υ production in proton-nucleus collisions, pointing to qualitative differences between the two frameworks. The results enable systematic comparisons to deuteron-nucleus data at RHIC and proton-nucleus data at the LHC for multiple quarkonium states.
