# Gauge-invariant momentum and angular momentum operators in quantum electrodynamics and chromodynamics

> Differences between vector potentials in different gauges contain no dynamics in both classical and quantum electrodynamics and chromodynamics. Consequently, once gauge invariance is established, results calculated in non-covariant gauges can be expected to agree with results obtained in covariant gauges in all Lorentz frames. We show in particular that canonical quantization in the Coulomb gauge can be used without giving up explicit gauge invariance. Quantization in the Coulomb gauge is particularly simple because it involves only the two transverse photons/gluons present in all gauges. These transverse photons/gluons reside on a 2-dimensional physical plane in momentum space perpendicular to the photon/gluon momentum {\bf k}. Explicit expressions are given for the basic momentum, spin and orbital angular momentum field operators of photons/gluons in the Coulomb gauge. Their properties are discussed in some detail. In particular, these field operators are shown to be more complicated than the corresponding operators in quantum mechanics which they also contain.

## Key claim

The paper argues that canonical quantization in the Coulomb gauge can be used without sacrificing explicit gauge invariance, and that the resulting nondynamical (canonical) momentum and angular momentum operators for electrons and photons/gluons are simpler and more practical for perturbative calculations than the dynamical operators advocated by Ji (1997) and Wakamatsu (2010), provided gauge invariance is verified afterward for all physical operators.

## Method

The authors construct gauge-invariant momentum and angular momentum operators in QED and QCD using Noether's theorem directly from the Lagrangian density, working in momentum space with a family of generalized velocity gauges that includes the Coulomb gauge as the "physical gauge" where the vector potential contains only the two transverse components on the physical plane perpendicular to the propagation vector k. They separate the photon momentum and angular momentum into gauge-independent physical parts (built from transverse fields) and nondynamical pure-gauge parts, then transfer the pure-gauge terms to the electron operators to achieve manifest gauge invariance. For QCD, they construct operators in complete analogy to QED but note that nonlinear interactions proportional to the color coupling constant g prevent complete removal of dynamical effects from the QCD operators.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb gauge condition is defined by α_C = 0 in the generalized velocity gauge condition A^(vg)_∥ = sα(ω/|k|) A^(0)(vg), where the gauge parameter α = 1/v_g^2
- For one-photon states on-shell (ω = |k|), the Lorenz gauge condition gives A_(L∥) = A_L^0, and the Lorentz scalar A_L^2 = -A_⊥^2
- The covariant light-cone gauge (s = -1, α = 1) gives A_(lc∥) = -A_lc^0, with A_lc^2 = -A_⊥^2
- The nondynamical electron momentum operator is P^e_nd = ∫ ψ^† (p̂ - eA_∥) ψ d^3x, where p̂ = ∇/i
- The photon spin operator S^γ_∥ = ∫ E_⊥ × A_⊥ d^3x has only the component along k, with helicity eigenstates |e_±⟩ = ∓|(e_1 ± i e_2)/√2⟩; the helicity 0 state |e_3⟩ is absent

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Chen 2008) for separating gluon orbital angular momentum and spin operators in the nucleon spin problem, providing a detailed and self-contained derivation of gauge-invariant operators in both QED and QCD. It contrasts with the manifestly gauge-invariant dynamical operators proposed by Ji (1997) and the dynamical version advocated by Wakamatsu (2010), arguing that the nondynamical canonical momentum operators are better matched to covariant perturbation theory and experimental measurements where particles are detected on-shell. The paper also responds to criticism by Ji (2010) of the authors' operators.
