# Cross effects in spin hydrodynamics: A revisit Entropy analysis and statistical operator

> We revisit the construction of first-order spin hydrodynamics and find that the constitution relations receive the corrections from the cross effects resulting from spin-orbit coupling. Starting from a routine entropy analysis, we show how to identify cross effects and new cross transport coefficients from the second law of thermodynamics. Interestingly, the conventional transport coefficient heat conductivity $κ$ is bounded from below by the product of cross transport coefficients, which means the threshold of heat conduction is changed. With recourse to Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator, we reproduce the construction of first-order spin hydrodynamics and identification of cross effects in a more rigorous way. By seeking the dispersion relations of normal modes, we find that these cross effects suppress the attenuation of sound modes and heat mode appearing in conventional hydrodynamics and also have impacts on the damping of non-hydrodynamic spin modes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that first-order spin hydrodynamics must include cross effects between the symmetric and antisymmetric sectors of the energy-momentum tensor, introducing two new transport coefficients (γ and ξ) that previous entropy analyses had omitted; these cross effects are bounded by the condition that heat conductivity κ is bounded from below by (γ+ξ)²/(4λ), meaning heat conduction has a modified threshold.

## Method

The authors performed a standard entropy analysis using the second law of thermodynamics to derive constitutive relations for first-order spin hydrodynamics, then verified their results using Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator method to obtain Kubo formulas for the transport coefficients. They subsequently performed a linear mode analysis by perturbing a quiescent equilibrium system and linearizing the hydrodynamic equations to study the dispersion relations of normal modes.

## Key numbers

- Heat conductivity κ is bounded from below by (γ+ξ)²/(4λ), where γ and ξ are the new cross transport coefficients and λ is the "boost heat conductivity"
- The cross transport coefficients γ and ξ satisfy the Onsager reciprocal relation γ = ξ
- Shear viscosity η ≥ 0, bulk viscosity ζ ≥ 0, and rotational viscosity η_s ≥ 0
- Heat conductivity κ ≥ 0 and boost heat conductivity λ ≥ 0
- The condition for semipositive entropy production in the vector sector is κλ ≥ (γ+ξ)²/4

## Context

This work extends the first-order spin hydrodynamics developed by Hattori (2019), Fukushima (2020), and Hu (2021) by identifying cross effects between the symmetric and antisymmetric parts of the energy-momentum tensor that those prior studies did not include. The cross effects are interpreted as a reflection of spin-orbit coupling through the equation ∂_λ S^{λμν} = T^{νμ} - T^{μν}, and the results enable the extraction of new transport coefficients from underlying field theories via the derived Kubo formulas, with implications for understanding spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions.
