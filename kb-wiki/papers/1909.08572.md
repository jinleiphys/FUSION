# Probing parton saturation with forward $Z^0$-boson production at small transverse momentum in p+p and p+A collisions

> We calculate and compare the differential cross sections for forward $Z^0$-boson production at small transverse momentum, in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, using both the collinear and dilute-dense factorization frameworks. In both cases, we implement a Sudakov resummation of the large logarithms generated by soft-gluon emissions, which is essential in order to describe the transverse momentum distribution of forward $Z^0$ bosons measured at the Tevatron and the LHC. We further compute the nuclear modification factor in the dilute-dense framework, hoping to single out signals of saturation effects at small values of $x$. Our predictions are compared with those obtained in the collinear factorization framework, using two different nuclear parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The suppression of low-\(q_T\) \(Z^0\)-boson production in proton-nucleus versus proton-proton collisions, predicted by saturation effects in the Color Glass Condensate framework, is still present after including Sudakov resummation but is significantly reduced; however, the same nuclear modification factor can also be reproduced by collinear factorization with nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) due to large uncertainties at small \(x\), so a measurement alone cannot distinguish saturation from collinear nuclear effects without more precise small-\(x\) gluon distributions.

## Method

The authors computed the differential cross section for forward \(Z^0\)-boson production at small transverse momentum using two frameworks: collinear factorization with Collins-Soper-Sterman transverse momentum resummation at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy, and dilute-dense factorization (Color Glass Condensate hybrid approach) with Sudakov resummation at leading logarithmic accuracy. In the CGC framework, the projectile proton is described by collinear PDFs (CTEQ14) and the dense target by small-\(x\) unintegrated quark distributions derived from the rcBK evolution equation, with the non-perturbative Sudakov factor taken from the BLNY parameterization. The nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}\) was computed for p+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{S_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV and compared with predictions using two different nPDF sets (nCTEQ15 and EPPS16).

## Key numbers

- The non-perturbative Sudakov factor parameters used are \(g_1 = 0.21\) GeV\(^2\), \(g_2 = 0.68\) GeV\(^2\), \(g_3 = -0.12\) GeV\(^2\), with \(b_{\rm max} = 0.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The CGC calculation for forward p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{S} = 7\) TeV and 13 TeV required a rescaling factor of 1.15 to match the absolute cross section measured by LHCb.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}\) was computed for forward rapidity windows \(1 < y < 2\) and \(3 < y < 4\) at \(\sqrt{S_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV; the CGC error band was obtained by varying the nuclear saturation scale from \(2Q_{sp}^2\) to \(3Q_{sp}^2\).
- The integrated cross section for the di-lepton channel includes a decay fraction factor of 0.03366 for a single lepton flavor.

## Context

This work extends the study of saturation effects from forward di-hadron and di-jet production (Marquet 2007, Stasto 2018) to the simpler process of forward \(Z^0\)-boson production, where non-perturbative fragmentation functions are absent. It implements the interplay between Sudakov resummation and small-\(x\) dynamics for the first time in this channel, following the theoretical framework of Mueller (2012, 2013). The predictions are compared with collinear factorization results using nPDFs from nCTEQ (Kovarik 2015) and EPPS16 (Eskola 2016), showing that current uncertainties in nuclear gluon distributions at small \(x\) prevent a clear discrimination of saturation effects; future LHC data on forward \(Z^0\) production could help constrain nPDFs and test nonlinear QCD evolution.
