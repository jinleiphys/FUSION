# Baryon Form Factors of Relativistic Constituent-Quark Models

> The electromagnetic and axial form factors of the nucleon and its lowest positive parity excitations, the Delta(1232) and the N*(1440), are calculated with constituent-quark models that are specified by simple algebraic representations of the mass-operator eigenstates. Poincaré covariant current operators are generated by the dynamics from single-quark currents that are covariant under a kinematic subgroup. The dependence of the calculated form factors on the choice of kinematics and on the gross features of the wave functions is illustrated for instant-form, point-form, and front-form kinematics. A simple algebraic form of the orbital ground state wave function, which depends on two parameters, allows a fair description of all the form factors over the empirically accessible range, although with widely different choices of the parameters, which determine the range and shape of the orbital wave function. The neutron electric form factor requires additional features, for instance the presence of mixed symmetry S-state component with 1 -- 2 % probability in the ground state wave function. Instant and front form kinematics demand a spatially extended wave function, whereas in point form kinematics the form factors may be described with a quite compact wave function.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic and axial form factors of the nucleon, Delta(1232), and N*(1440) can be described by a simple two-parameter algebraic wave function across instant-form, point-form, and front-form kinematics, but only with widely different parameter choices for each kinematic form, and the neutron electric form factor requires an additional mixed-symmetry S-state component with 1–2% probability.

## Method

The authors construct baryon states as eigenfunctions of a mass operator using a Bakamjian-Thomas representation, with Poincaré-covariant current operators generated from single-quark currents covariant under a kinematic subgroup. They evaluate form factors for instant-form, point-form, and front-form kinematics using a two-parameter algebraic orbital ground state wave function and a radially excited wave function for the N*(1440) constructed to be orthogonal to the ground state with a single node. The quark currents are taken as structureless spinor currents throughout, with quark masses of 350 MeV (point form), 250 MeV (front form), and 140 MeV (instant form).

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: 350 MeV for point form, 250 MeV for front form, 140 MeV for instant form
- Wave function parameter b: 640 MeV (point form), 500 MeV (front form), 600 MeV (instant form)
- Wave function exponent a: 9/4 (point form), 4 (front form), 6 (instant form)
- Matter radius r0: 0.19 fm (point form), 0.55 fm (front form), 0.63 fm (instant form)
- Mixed-symmetry S-state component required for neutron electric form factor: 1–2% probability in the ground state wave function

## Context

This work extends the relativistic constituent-quark model approach of Bakamjian and Thomas (1965) and Coester (1998, 2003) by systematically comparing three forms of kinematics (instant, point, front) for baryon form factor calculations. The results enable future studies of how the choice of kinematic subgroup affects form factor predictions and show that point-form kinematics can describe form factors with a much more compact wave function (r0 = 0.19 fm) than instant or front forms (r0 = 0.55–0.63 fm). The paper also confirms the finding of Boffi et al. that a small mixed-symmetry S-state admixture is needed for the neutron electric form factor.
