# Gluon production in the Color Glass Condensate model of collisions of ultrarelativistic finite nuclei

> We extend previous work on high energy nuclear collisions in the Color Glass Condensate model to study collisions of finite ultrarelativistic nuclei. The changes implemented include a) imposition of color neutrality at the nucleon level and b) realistic nuclear matter distributions of finite nuclei. The saturation scale characterizing the fields of color charge is explicitly position dependent, $Λ_s=Λ_s(x_T)$. We compute gluon distributions both before and after the collisions. The gluon distribution in the nuclear wavefunction before the collision is significantly suppressed below the saturation scale when compared to the simple McLerran-Venugopalan model prediction, while the behavior at large momentum $p_T\gg Λ_s$ remains unchanged. We study the centrality dependence of produced gluons and compare it to the centrality dependence of charged hadrons exhibited by the RHIC data. We demonstrate the geometrical scaling property of the initial gluon transverse momentum distributions for different centralities. Classical Yang-Mills results for $p_T < Λ_s$ are simply matched to perturbative QCD computations for $p_T > Λ_s$-the resulting energy per particle is significantly lower than the purely classical estimates. Our results for nuclear collisions can be used as initial conditions for quantitative studies of the further evolution and possible equilibration of hot and dense gluonic matter produced in heavy ion collisions. Finally, we study $pA$ collisions within the classical framework. Our results agree well with previously derived analytical results in the appropriate kinematical regions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) model from idealized infinite cylindrical nuclei to collisions of finite nuclei with realistic nuclear matter distributions, finding that imposing color neutrality at the nucleon level significantly suppresses the gluon distribution below the saturation scale compared to the simple McLerran-Venugopalan model, while the behavior at large momentum remains unchanged.

## Method

The authors implemented two improvements: (a) imposing color neutrality at the nucleon level using two prescriptions (Color Neutral I, which subtracts the spatial average of color charge per nucleon, and Color Neutral II, which additionally eliminates the dipole moment of each nucleon), and (b) using realistic Woods-Saxon nuclear density profiles to generate position-dependent saturation scales. They solved the classical Yang-Mills equations numerically on a lattice for SU(3) gauge theory, matching classical results for transverse momentum below the saturation scale to perturbative QCD computations above it, and computed gluon distributions both before and after nuclear collisions for various impact parameters.

## Key numbers

- For central collisions with saturation scale \(\Lambda_{s0}=1.41\) GeV: gluon multiplicity \(g^2 N_g = 1628.68\) and transverse energy \(g^2 E_g = 2725.40\) GeV, with the gluon multiplicity about half the final pion multiplicity at RHIC while the transverse energy is close to the experimentally observed value of about 600 GeV
- For central collisions with saturation scale \(\Lambda_{s0}=2.32\) GeV: gluon multiplicity \(g^2 N_g = 3768.00\) and transverse energy \(g^2 E_g = 9198.736\) GeV, with the gluon multiplicity close to the final pion multiplicity at RHIC but the transverse energy nearly four times larger than observed
- The gluon distribution before collision shows a \(1/p_T^2\) dependence at large momentum for all models, while color-neutral models suppress the distribution at momenta \(p_T < \Lambda_{s0}\)
- The color charge correlator for Color Neutral I scales as \(\tilde{\rho}(p_T) \sim p_T^2\) and for Color Neutral II as \(\tilde{\rho}(p_T) \sim p_T^3\) for small momenta \(p_T < \Lambda_{s0}\)

## Context

This work extends previous numerical CGC studies by Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan (1999-2001) that treated nuclear collisions as central collisions of infinite cylindrical nuclei with uniform nuclear matter distributions and only global color neutrality. The paper also builds on the analytical work of Lam and Mahlon (2000) on color neutrality in the MV model and Kovchegov and Mueller (1998) and Dumitru and McLerran (2002) on pA collisions. The results provide realistic initial conditions for subsequent studies of equilibration dynamics in heavy ion collisions, such as those by Baier et al. (2000-2001) and transport simulations, and enable quantitative comparisons with RHIC data on centrality dependence of charged hadron production.
