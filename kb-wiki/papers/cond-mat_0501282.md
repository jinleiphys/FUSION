# Finite-size scaling exponents and entanglement in the two-level BCS model

> We analyze the finite-size properties of the two-level BCS model. Using the continuous unitary transformation technique, we show that nontrivial scaling exponents arise at the quantum critical point for various observables such as the magnetization or the spin-spin correlation functions. We also discuss the entanglement properties of the ground state through the concurrence which appears to be singular at the transition.

## Key claim

Using the continuous unitary transformation technique combined with a 1/N expansion and a scaling argument, the authors show that the two-level BCS model exhibits nontrivial finite-size scaling exponents at the quantum critical point (h=1) for observables such as magnetization and spin-spin correlation functions, and that the concurrence displays singular behavior at the transition, though the specific scaling exponent for the concurrence differs from that of the observables.

## Method

The authors map the two-level reduced BCS Hamiltonian at half-filling onto a spin system of two coupled XX models with infinite-range interactions in opposite transverse magnetic fields. They perform a Holstein-Primakoff bosonic representation around the mean-field ground state in the symmetric phase (h >= 1), expand to order 1/N, and use continuous unitary transformations (CUTs) with a particle-conserving generator to diagonalize the Hamiltonian and compute the ground-state energy, gap, magnetization, and correlation functions. The finite-size scaling exponents are extracted by analyzing the singular part of the 1/N expansion in the vicinity of the critical point, and the results are verified by numerical diagonalization of systems with up to N=2^17 spins.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state energy per spin scales as e_0^sing(N) ~ N^{-4/3} at the critical point (h=1).
- The gap Delta scales as Delta^sing(N) ~ N^{-1/3} at the critical point.
- The magnetization 4⟨S_{1z}⟩/N scales as N^{-2/3} at the critical point.
- The correlation functions 16⟨S_{1z}^2⟩/N^2 and 16⟨S_{1x}S_{2x}⟩/N^2 both scale as N^{-2/3} at the critical point.
- The rescaled concurrence C_R^{1,2} has a finite-size scaling exponent of 1/3 at the critical point.
- In the thermodynamical limit, the rescaled concurrence for spins in distinct subsets is C_R^{1,2} = 2(1 - sqrt((h-1)/h)) in the symmetric phase (h >= 1) and C_R^{1,2} = 2 - sqrt(1 - h^2) in the broken phase (0 <= h <= 1).
- In the broken phase, the rescaled concurrence for spins in the same subset is C_R^{1,1} = max(0, 2 - 1/sqrt(1 - h^2)), which becomes zero for h >= sqrt(3)/2.

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling analysis previously applied to the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model by Dusuel and Vidal (2004) to the two-level BCS model, which displays a second-order quantum phase transition to a superconducting state. The nontrivial scaling exponent for the ground-state energy had been suggested numerically by Roman et al. (2002) and analytically derived using a spin coherent state representation by Roman et al. (2004), but the scaling exponents for the magnetization, correlation functions, and concurrence are new. The method is well suited for models where a semiclassical description of the thermodynamical limit is exact, and it enables future analysis of the von Neumann entropy in quantum critical systems, as studied by Latorre et al. (2003, 2004) and Its et al. (2004).
