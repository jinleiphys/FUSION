# The splitting of the one-body potential in spin-polarized isospin-symmetric nuclear matter

> Spin-polarized symmetric nuclear matter is studied within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. We pay particular attention to the difference between the one-body potentials of upward and downward polarized nucleons. This is formally analogous to the "Lane potential" for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. We point out the necessity for additional information on this fundamentally important quantity and suggest ways to constrain it.

## Key claim

The spin-dependent symmetry potential in spin-polarized symmetric nuclear matter is formally analogous to the Lane potential for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, and the DBHF predictions show that the spin-up and spin-down single-nucleon potentials become more repulsive and more attractive, respectively, with increasing spin asymmetry. The author emphasizes that empirical constraints on this spin-dependent potential are even scarcer than those on the isospin-dependent potential, and that additional information is needed to constrain the spin-dependent nuclear effective interaction.

## Method

The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach is applied to spin-polarized symmetric nuclear matter using the Bonn B one-boson-exchange potential as the realistic free-space nucleon-nucleon interaction. The single-particle potentials for spin-up and spin-down nucleons are obtained by solving a set of coupled self-consistent equations together with the two-nucleon G-matrix, where the Pauli operator is defined analogously to the isospin-asymmetric case with unequal Fermi momenta for the two spin species. The spin-dependent G-matrix elements are summed over partial waves and the resulting single-particle potentials are averaged over momentum magnitude and direction.

## Key numbers

- The average spin symmetry potential is defined as ⟨U_sym^S⟩ = (⟨U_u⟩ - ⟨U_d⟩)/(2α), where α = (ρ_u - ρ_d)/(ρ_u + ρ_d) is the spin-asymmetry parameter.
- Calculations are performed at average Fermi momenta of 1.1 fm⁻¹, 1.4 fm⁻¹, and 1.6 fm⁻¹.
- The momentum dependence of the spin symmetry potential is shown at a Fermi momentum of 1.4 fm⁻¹ for α = 0.6.
- The energy per particle in polarized SNM is shown at three fixed densities (in units of fm⁻³) as a function of α, demonstrating the parabolic form ⟨e(ρ,α)⟩ ≈ ⟨e₀(ρ)⟩ + ⟨e_sym^S(ρ)⟩α².

## Context

This work extends the author's previous DBHF study of spin-polarized pure neutron matter (Sammarruca and Krastev, 2007) to symmetric nuclear matter, and is formally parallel to the treatment of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter (Sammarruca, 2010). The spin symmetry potential is shown to be remarkably similar both qualitatively and quantitatively to the isospin symmetry potential for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, and the author notes that similar uncertainties to those found for the energy dependence of the isospin symmetry potential (Li, 2004; Bombaci, 2001; Rizzo et al., 2004) are to be expected for the spin-dependent case. The results enable future studies of spin-polarized phases of nuclear matter and provide theoretical predictions that could be constrained by heavy-ion collisions with polarized targets or by analyses of spin giant resonances.
