# Spin-triplet pairing in large nuclei

> The nuclear pairing condensate is expected to change character from spin-singlet to spin-triplet when the nucleus is very large and the neutron and proton numbers $Z,N$ are equal. We investigate the transition between these two phases within the framework of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations, using a zero-range interaction to generate the pairing. We confirm that extremely large nucleus would indeed favor triplet pairing condensates, with the Hamiltonian parameters taken from known systematics. The favored phase is found to depend on the specific orbitals at the Fermi energy. The smallest nuclei with a well-developed spin-triplet condensate are in the mass region A ~ 130-140.

# Spin-triplet pairing in large nuclei

## Key claim

The authors confirm that extremely large nuclei would indeed favor spin-triplet (J=1, T=0) pairing condensates over ordinary spin-singlet (J=0, T=1) pairing, but the transition depends sensitively on which specific orbitals lie at the Fermi energy. The smallest nuclei with a well-developed spin-triplet condensate are in the mass region A ~ 130-140, which is just beyond the proton dripline and thus not physically stable.

## Method

The authors applied Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential and a zero-range contact interaction to generate the pairing, using interaction strengths v_s = 300 MeV-fm^3 for spin-singlet and v_t = 450 MeV-fm^3 for spin-triplet with a cutoff E_B = 10 MeV. They calculated correlation energies for both pairing channels in spherical nuclei with equal neutron and proton numbers, scanning over nuclear radii from 4.0 to 12.0 fm (mass numbers A = 25-1000), and compared the ratio of spin-triplet to spin-singlet correlation energies.

## Key numbers

- Interaction strengths used: v_s = 300 MeV-fm^3 (spin-singlet), v_t = 450 MeV-fm^3 (spin-triplet), ratio 1.5
- Spin-orbit strength fitted to the f_7/2 - f_5/2 splitting in ^41Ca: V_so = 33 MeV-fm^2, with estimated uncertainty not more than 10%
- Chemical potential fixed at lambda = -13 MeV, corresponding to nuclear matter density rho = 0.16 fm^-3
- For ^48Cr with the full fp-shell space: spin-singlet correlation energy E_corr = 4.92 MeV, spin-triplet correlation energy E_corr = 3.45 MeV
- Spin-singlet pairing favored for R < 6.5 fm; spin-triplet favored for R > 9 fm
- Lightest nuclei predicted to have spin-triplet condensates: ^30P (E_corr = 1.4 MeV), ^76Sr (E_corr ~ 1 MeV), and ^136Er (E_corr ~ 13 MeV, the smallest with a well-developed condensate)
- For ^136Er at R = 6.50 fm: spin-singlet E_c = 7.0 MeV, spin-triplet E_c = 12.9 MeV, ratio 1.85

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Poves (1998), Baroni, Schwenk, and others that the strong nuclear spin-orbit field suppresses spin-triplet pairing more than spin-singlet pairing, and that this suppression should weaken in large nuclei due to the reduced surface-to-volume ratio. The study uses shell-model fitted interactions from USDB (Brown 2006) and GX1A (Honma 2005) to calibrate the pairing strengths. The results enable future investigations of whether two kinds of condensates can coexist, how deformation affects the competition, and how robust triplet pairing is against neutron excess.
