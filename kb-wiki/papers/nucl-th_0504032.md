# Aspects of particle production in isospin asymmetric matter

> The production/absorption rate of particles in compressed and heated asymmetric matter is studied using a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) transport model with an isospin dependent collision term. Just from energy conservation in the elementary production/absorption processes we expect to see a strong dependence of the yields on the basic Lorentz structure of the isovector effective interaction, due to isospin effects on the scalar and vector self-energies of the hadrons. This will be particularly evident for the ratio of the rates of particles produced with different charges: results are shown for π(+)/π(-), K(+)/K(0) yields. In order to simplify the analysis we perform RMF cascade simulations in a box with periodic boundary conditions. In this way we can better pin down all such fine relativistic effects in particle production, that could likely show up even in realistic heavy ion collisions. In particular, K(+,0) production is expected to be directly related to the high density behaviour of the symmetry energy, since kaons are produced very early during the high density stage of the collision and their mean free path is rather large. We show that the K(+)/K(0) ratio reflects important isospin contributions on the production rates just because of the large sensitivity around the threshold. The results are very promising for the possibility of a direct link between particle production data in exotic Heavy Ion Collisions and the isospin dependent part of the Equation of State at high baryon densities.

## Key claim

The production rates of pions and kaons in compressed, heated asymmetric nuclear matter depend strongly on the Lorentz structure of the isovector effective interaction, specifically whether only the vector rho meson or both the rho and scalar delta mesons are included in the relativistic mean field description, due to isospin effects on hadron self-energies that modify threshold conditions.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean field (RMF) cascade simulations in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, containing 100 nucleons at fixed baryon density 2.5 times saturation density and temperature 60 MeV, with asymmetry parameters ranging from 0.0 to 0.4. They implemented an isospin-dependent collision term that properly conserves total invariant collision energy using canonical momenta, accounting for changes in scalar and vector self-energies between incoming and outgoing channels for inelastic processes such as resonance excitation and kaon production. Kaon production was treated perturbatively via baryon-baryon and pion-baryon channels, while pions and kaons were assumed unaffected by the nuclear mean field.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density fixed at 2.5 times saturation density (rho_B = 2.5 rho_0) with rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Temperature set to T = 60 MeV, chosen to resemble conditions during the dense phase in heavy ion collisions at few AGeV energies
- Asymmetry parameter alpha = (N-Z)/(N+Z) varied from 0.0 (symmetric matter) to 0.4 (N/Z = 2.3)
- Symmetry energy parameter a_4 = 30.7 MeV for both NLrho and NLrhodelta models
- Saturation properties: E/A = -16.0 MeV, K = 240 MeV, m*/M = 0.75
- Kaon mean free path in nuclear matter quoted as lambda(K^{+,0}) ~ 7 fm
- Pion multiplicities saturated after approximately t = 30 fm/c; nucleon momenta distributed at Fermi-Dirac function with T = 56 MeV after 150 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isospin-dependent dynamics in relativistic heavy ion collisions, including collective flow observables by Danielewicz (2002), Larionov (2003), and Gaitanos (2001), as well as pion production studies by Gaitanos (2004). The authors build on earlier RMF calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter by Kubis (1997) and Liu (2002) that considered the role of the delta meson in the symmetry energy. The results suggest that the K+/K0 ratio from subthreshold kaon production in exotic heavy ion collisions could provide a direct probe of the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy, complementing isospin transparency studies by Gaitanos (2003) and Chen (2004) that favor a stiff symmetry energy at supra-normal densities.
