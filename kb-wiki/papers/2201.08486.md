# Critical point particle number fluctuations from molecular dynamics

> We study fluctuations of particle number in the presence of critical point by utilizing molecular dynamics simulations of the classical Lennard-Jones fluid in a periodic box. The numerical solution of the $N$-body problem naturally incorporates all correlations, exact conservation laws, and finite size effects, allowing us to study the fluctuation signatures of the critical point in a dynamical setup. We find that large fluctuations associated with the critical point are observed when measurements are performed in coordinate subspace, but, in the absence of collective flow and expansion, are essentially washed out when momentum cuts are imposed instead. We put our findings in the context of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Large particle number fluctuations associated with the critical point are observed when measurements are performed in coordinate space subvolumes, but are essentially washed out when momentum space cuts are imposed instead, in the absence of collective flow and expansion.

## Method

Molecular dynamics simulations of the classical Lennard-Jones fluid in a periodic box were performed by numerically integrating Newton's equations of motion using the Velocity Verlet method. The simulations were carried out for fixed particle number N and density, in both microcanonical and canonical-like ensembles, with particle number fluctuations studied in coordinate space subvolumes (defined by spatial cuts) and momentum space subvolumes (defined by cuts on longitudinal velocity). The scaled variance of particle number fluctuations was calculated as time averages during the production stage of the simulations.

## Key numbers

- Critical point of the Lennard-Jones fluid: reduced temperature T_c = 1.321 ± 0.007, reduced density n_c = 0.316 ± 0.005, reduced pressure p_c = 0.129 ± 0.005
- Compressibility factor at the critical point: Z_c ≈ 0.309
- Along the T = 1.4 isotherm at n = 0.3 (near the critical point), the grand-canonical scaled variance ω^gce ≈ 7-7.5, but MD simulations with N = 5000 particles in coordinate space subvolumes reach only about half that value, and even N = 25000 is insufficient to reach the thermodynamic limit
- At low density n = 0.02 along T = 1.4, the grand-canonical scaled variance ω^gce ≈ 1.126, close to the Poisson limit of 1
- At high density n = 0.6 along T = 1.4, the grand-canonical scaled variance ω^gce ≈ 0.30-0.31, showing suppression of fluctuations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of critical fluctuations in the Lennard-Jones fluid (Dorso 1988, Moretto 2011, Shuryak 2018) by using molecular dynamics to directly simulate particle number fluctuations near the critical point, incorporating all correlations and conservation laws without mean-field approximations. The finding that critical fluctuations are washed out in momentum space measurements, while present in coordinate space, directly challenges the interpretation of event-by-event proton number fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions as signatures of the QCD critical point (Stephanov 1999, Bzdak 2019). The results motivate future studies of expanding systems to better reflect heavy-ion collision conditions, and provide a testbed for methods to correct fluctuation measurements for global conservation laws (Vovchenko 2020, Vovchenko 2021).
