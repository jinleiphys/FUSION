# Folding model analysis of elastic and inelastic proton scattering on Sulfur isotopes

> The folding formalism for the nucleon-nucleus optical potential and inelastic form factor is applied to study elastic and inelastic proton scattering on 30-40S isotopes. A recently developed realistic density dependent M3Y interaction, well tested in the folding analysis of nucleus-nucleus elastic and inelastic scattering, is used as effective NN interaction. The nuclear ground state and transition densities (for the 2+ excitations in Sulfur isotopes) are obtained in the Hartree-Fock-BCS and QRPA approaches, respectively. The best fit ratios of transition moments Mn/Mp for the lowest 2+ states in Sulfur isotopes are compared to those obtained earlier in the DWBA analysis of the same data using the same structure model and inelastic form factors obtained with the JLM effective interaction. Our folding+DWBA analysis has shown quite a strong isovector mixing in the elastic and inelastic scattering channels for the neutron rich 38,40S nuclei. In particular, the relative strength of the isovector part of the transition potential required by the inelastic p+38S data is significantly stronger than that obtained with the corresponding QRPA transition density.

## Key claim

The folding model using the density-dependent CDM3Y6 interaction, combined with microscopic Hartree-Fock-BCS and QRPA densities, can describe elastic and inelastic proton scattering on Sulfur isotopes, but the isovector part of the transition potential required to fit the inelastic p+³⁸S data is significantly stronger than that predicted by the QRPA transition density alone.

## Method

The authors performed single-folding calculations of the real part of the nucleon-nucleus optical potential and inelastic form factors using the density-dependent CDM3Y6 effective NN interaction, with ground state densities from Hartree-Fock-BCS and transition densities for 2⁺ excitations from QRPA calculations using the SGII Skyrme interaction. The imaginary parts of the optical and transition potentials were taken from the CH89 global systematics and a collective vibration model, respectively, and the elastic and inelastic scattering data were analyzed within the optical model and DWBA using the code ECIS97.

## Key numbers

- The density dependence parameters of the CDM3Y6 interaction yield a nuclear incompressibility K = 252 MeV in the Hartree-Fock approximation.
- The elastic and inelastic scattering data for ³⁰,³²S+p were measured at E_lab = 53A MeV at GANIL, while data for ³⁴,³⁶,³⁸,⁴⁰S+p were measured earlier at E_lab = 30, 28, 39, and 30 A MeV, respectively.
- The pairing gap used in the HF+BCS calculations was taken as Δ = 12/√A MeV.
- The charge deformation lengths for the lowest 2⁺ states in Sulfur isotopes were obtained from the reduced electric transition rates B(E2↑) and are given in Table 1 of the paper.

## Context

This work extends the folding model approach previously applied to nucleus-nucleus scattering (Khoa 1993, 1995, 1997) to proton-nucleus scattering on exotic Sulfur isotopes, using the same density-dependent M3Y interaction. The results are compared to earlier DWBA analyses of the same data using the JLM effective interaction, and the study enables testing of microscopic QRPA transition densities for neutron-rich nuclei through comparison with inelastic proton scattering data.
