# Constructing phase space distributions with internal symmetries

> We discuss an ab initio world-line approach to constructing phase space distributions in systems with internal symmetries. Starting from the Schwinger-Keldysh real time path integral in quantum field theory, we derive the most general extension of the Wigner phase space distribution to include color and spin degrees of freedom in terms of dynamical Grassmann variables. The corresponding Liouville distribution for colored particles, which obey Wong's equation, has only singlet and octet components, while higher moments are fully constrained by the Grassmann algebra. The extension of phase space dynamics to spin is represented by a generalization of the Pauli-Lubanski vector; its time evolution via the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi equation also follows from the phase space trajectories of the underlying Grassmann coordinates. Our results for the Liouville phase space distribution in systems with both spin and color are of interest in fields as diverse as chiral fluids, finite temperature field theory and polarized parton distribution functions. We also comment on the role of the chiral anomaly in the phase space dynamics of spinning particles.

## Key claim

The resulting Liouville distribution for colored particles contains only singlet and octet components, with higher moments fully constrained by the Grassmann algebra rather than being independent.

## Method

The authors employ a world-line formalism to express one-loop effective actions as quantum mechanical point-particle path integrals, then generalize to the Schwinger-Keldysh real-time formalism for nonequilibrium processes. They perform a saddle-point expansion (truncated Wigner approximation) of the Keldysh path integral to obtain classical phase space distributions, where Grassmann variables encode internal symmetries. The classical color and spin phase space limits are obtained explicitly from the saddle point of the world-line effective action, with phase space dynamics derived from Grassmannian classical Poisson brackets and a Grassmann-valued phase space measure.

## Key numbers

- The classical color phase space measure satisfies: ∫ dQ = 0, ∫ dQ Q^a = 0, ∫ dQ Q^a Q^b = (1/2)δ^{ab}, ∫ dQ Q^a Q^b Q^c = (A_R/2)d^{abc}, where A_R is the anomaly coefficient of the representation
- The phase space distribution for color reduces to f(x,P,Q) = f(x,P)[1 + (2/(A_R d^2))d^{abc}Q^a Q^b Q^c] + 2f^a(x,P)Q^a, where d^2 ≡ d^{abc}d^{abc} = N_c^2 - 4
- Higher color moments are fully determined by lower moments: f^{ab}(x,P) = (1/2)δ^{ab}f(x,P) + A_R d^{abc}f^c(x,P) and f^{abc}(x,P) = (A_R/2)d^{abc}f(x,P)
- The spin phase space measure satisfies: ∫ dS = 0, ∫ dS S_{μν} = 0, ∫ dS S_{μν}S_{αβ} = iε_{μναβ}

## Context

This work extends the Grassmann variable approach to phase space distributions pioneered by Berezin and Marinov (1976) for spin, and by Balachandran (1976, 1977), Barducci (1976, 1982), and Brink (1976) for color degrees of freedom. The authors relate their construction to the Alekseev-Faddeev-Shatashvili (AFS) approach (1988) for symplectic orbits of compact Lie groups, but note that no topological Berry monopole term arises in their saddle-point derivation. The results enable further development of transport theory for color charges in nonequilibrium QCD processes, chiral fluids, finite temperature field theory, and polarized parton distribution functions.
