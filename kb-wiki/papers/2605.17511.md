# A flow-matching generative model for event-by-event jet-induced hydro response in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> In high-energy heavy-ion collisions, propagation of the energy deposited into the medium by energetic partons that traverse the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) leads to Mach-cone-like jet-induced medium response. Full simulations of such jet-induced medium responses require a complete model such as the coupled Linear Boltzmann Transport and hydrodynamic (CoLBT-hydro) model that can carry out the concurrent evolution of both hard partons and the medium. Such full simulations on parallelized computers, however, are very resource-intensive and alternative simulation methods will be useful for more extensive physics investigations. In this study, we train a Flow Matching generative model with $γ$-jet events in 0-10$\%$ Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 5.02 TeV from the CoLBT-hydro model to estimate the final-state hadron spectra $d^3N/dp_Tdηdϕ$ from jet-induced hydro response. With only the initial spatial and momentum information of the $γ$ and jets, the network is shown to conditionally generate the marginal final-state hadron spectra from the jet-induced hydro response that agree well with the training data. This generative model achieves a computational acceleration of approximately six orders of magnitude compared to the full CoLBT-hydro simulations, while faithfully preserving the statistical properties of the front and diffusion wake of the Mach-cone-like hydro response and their contributions to the hadron spectra.

## Key claim

The Flow Matching generative model can reproduce the final-state hadron spectra from jet-induced hydro response with "faithfully preserving the statistical properties of the front and diffusion wake of the Mach-cone-like hydro response," but only for the marginal spectra conditioned on initial gamma-jet information, not for the full coupled evolution of hard partons and medium.

## Method

The authors trained a Conditional Flow Matching model on 16,000 gamma-jet events from the CoLBT-hydro model in 0-10% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. The model takes as input 14 variables describing the initial photon and three leading jets (transverse momenta, pseudorapidities, azimuthal angles, and production vertex position) and generates the 50-dimensional PCA-reduced representation of the final hadron spectrum d^3N/dp_T d_eta d_phi from the jet-induced hydro response. The network learns a velocity field that transports samples from a 50-dimensional Gaussian distribution to the target distribution via linear interpolation, using a deep residual neural network with 6 residual blocks trained with the Adam optimizer.

## Key numbers

* Computational acceleration of approximately six orders of magnitude compared to full CoLBT-hydro simulations.
* Training dataset consists of 16,000 gamma-jet events in 0-10% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
* Photon transverse momentum cut: p_T^gamma > 100 GeV.
* Jet transverse momentum cut: p_T^jet > 10 GeV with pseudorapidity cut |eta_jet| <= 1.6.
* Final particle spectra generated in p_T range [0,4] GeV/c, eta range [-2.7,2.7], phi range [0,2pi].
* PCA compression to 50-dimensional feature vectors.
* Network uses 64-dimensional latent vectors and batch size 32 with learning rate 0.001.

## Context

This work extends the CoLBT-hydro model (Chen 2017, Chen 2020, Zhao 2021), which couples the Linear Boltzmann Transport model for hard partons with the CLVisc (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model for the bulk medium. The CoLBT-hydro model has successfully described jet suppression and modification of jet substructures, and predicted diffusion wake signals recently observed by CMS (2025) and ATLAS (2024) at LHC. The generative model enables rapid simulation of jet-induced medium response that would otherwise require resource-intensive parallelized GPU computations, making more extensive physics investigations feasible.
