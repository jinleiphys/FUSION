# Three-body Force Effects on the Properties of Neutron-rich Nuclear Matter

> We review our research work on the single-particle properties and the equation of state (EOS) of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter within the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach extended by including a microscopic three-body force (TBF). The TBF is shown to affect significantly the nuclear matter EOS and the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy at high densities above the normal nuclear matter density, and it is necessary for reproducing the empirical saturation property of symmetric nuclear matter in a nonrelativistic microscopic framework. The TBF-induced rearrangement effect and the ground state (g.s.) correlation effect on the s.p. properties in neutron-rich nuclear matter are investigated. Both effects turn out to be crucial for predicting reliably the s.p. properties within the Brueckner framework. The TBF effect on nucleon superfluidity in neutron star matter and neutron stars has also been discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the author's own body of work showing that including a microscopic three-body force (TBF) within the nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) framework is necessary to reproduce the empirical saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter and significantly stiffens the equation of state (EOS) and symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, but the specific quantitative predictions depend on the choice of the underlying two-body interaction (e.g., Argonne V18 versus Bonn B) and on the inclusion of both ground-state correlation and TBF-induced rearrangement effects.

## Method

The author applies the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) many-body theory extended to include a microscopic three-body force (TBF) constructed via the meson-exchange current approach, with parameters self-consistently determined to reproduce the Argonne V18 two-body interaction. The TBF is reduced to an equivalent density-dependent effective two-body interaction, and the single-particle potential is computed beyond the lowest-order BHF approximation by including both the ground-state (g.s.) correlation effect (Pauli rearrangement) and the TBF-induced rearrangement contribution. The calculations are performed for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter over a range of densities and asymmetries, and the resulting EOS, symmetry energy, single-particle potentials, effective masses, and nucleon pairing gaps are analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter improves from ~0.26 fm⁻³ (AV18 alone) to ~0.19 fm⁻³ (AV18 + TBF), and from ~0.33 fm⁻³ (Bonn B alone) to ~0.167 fm⁻³ (Bonn B + TBF).
- Saturation energy with Bonn B + TBF is ~ -15.9 MeV, in satisfactory agreement with empirical values.
- Without TBF, the symmetry energy follows approximately E_sym ∝ ρ^0.6 in the density region ρ ≤ 0.5 fm⁻³.
- The TBF rearrangement contribution to the single-particle potential is repulsive and increases monotonically with density and momentum; at high densities it becomes strongly repulsive and momentum-dependent.
- The neutron effective mass is larger than the proton effective mass in neutron-rich matter (m*_n > m*_p), and at a density of 0.5 fm⁻³ the TBF rearrangement effect may suppress the neutron-proton effective mass splitting almost completely.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous BHF studies of asymmetric nuclear matter (Zuo 1999, 2002b, 2005, 2006) by systematically reviewing the role of a microscopic three-body force. It addresses the well-known failure of nonrelativistic models using only realistic two-body forces (Coester 1970) to reproduce nuclear saturation, and it connects to relativistic Dirac-BHF approaches (Klahn 2006, Dalen 2005) by showing that the main relativistic correction can be reproduced quantitatively by the 2σ-N\(\bar{N}\) component of the microscopic TBF. The results enable more reliable predictions for the EOS of neutron star matter, the composition of β-stable matter, and the properties of nucleon superfluidity in neutron stars, which are relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision data (e.g., the FOPI π⁻/π⁺ ratio measurements by Xiao 2009 and the elliptic-flow studies by Russotto 2011).
