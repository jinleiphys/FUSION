# Kaon structure modifications in strange hadronic matter

> We present the valence quark distributions of the kaons in an isospin asymmetric dense strange medium consisting of nucleons and hyperons. The comparative analysis of in-medium parton distribution functions, electromagnetic form factors, and charge densities with respect to the free space distributions is studied in the light-cone quark model. The medium effects are incorporated in these distribution functions by using the effective quark masses, computed from the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model for finite values of baryonic density, isospin asymmetry, and strangeness fraction parameters. We observe a suppression of the kaon electromagnetic form factors and a redistribution of charge density in high-density strange matter.

# Kaon structure modifications in strange hadronic matter

## Key claim

The valence quark distributions, electromagnetic form factors, and charge densities of kaons are modified in an isospin-asymmetric dense strange medium containing both nucleons and hyperons, with the strangeness fraction of the medium producing a measurable but weaker effect than the baryonic density itself; the electromagnetic form factor of the strange antiquark shows a more significant reduction with increasing strangeness fraction than that of the up quark.

## Method

The authors employ a hybrid approach combining the light-cone quark model (LCQM) with the chiral SU(3) quark mean field (CQMF) model. The CQMF model computes effective in-medium quark masses by solving the thermodynamic potential for baryonic species (p, n, Λ, Σ, Ξ) at zero temperature, with scalar field couplings (σ, ζ, δ) and vector field couplings (ω, ρ, φ) that depend on baryonic density ρ_B, isospin asymmetry η, and strangeness fraction f_s. These effective masses are then used as inputs to the LCQM to calculate valence parton distribution functions (PDFs), electromagnetic form factors (EMFFs) via generalized parton distributions at zero skewness, and charge densities via two-dimensional Fourier transform of the EMFFs.

## Key numbers

- Baryonic density values studied: ρ_B/ρ_0 = 0, 1, 2, 3, where ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Strangeness fraction values studied: f_s = 0, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7
- Isospin asymmetry fixed at η = 0.5 for the strangeness-dependent results
- Harmonic scale parameter for kaon: β_K = 0.393 GeV, determined by fitting to the kaon decay constant
- Vacuum strange quark mass: m_s⁰ = 77 MeV; vacuum up and down quark masses: m_u,d⁰ = 0 MeV
- Coupling constants for u/d quarks: g_σ = 2.72, g_δ = 2.72, g_ω = 3.23, g_ρ = 3.23; for s quarks: g_ζ = 3.847, g_ω = 8.89, g_φ = 4.57

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium meson properties that were limited to symmetric or isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter without strangeness content. Prior work by Hutauruk et al. (2019) and Yabusaki et al. (2023) studied kaon EMFFs in symmetric nuclear matter using hybrid NJL-QMC and light-front constituent quark model approaches, respectively; the present authors note that Yabusaki et al. (2023) found the kaon EMFF unchanged with increasing nuclear density, whereas this work finds significant variation in strange matter due to direct interaction between the strange quark in the kaon and the surrounding strange medium. The results are relevant for interpreting future kaon measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider and J-PARC, and for understanding strange hadron production in heavy-ion collisions where the medium contains both nucleons and hyperons.
