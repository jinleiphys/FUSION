# Relativistic calculation of nuclear transparency in (e,e'p) reactions

> Nuclear transparency in (e,e'p) reactions is evaluated in a fully relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation model. The relativistic mean field theory is used for the bound state and the Pauli reduction for the scattering state, which is calculated from a relativistic optical potential. Results for selected nuclei are displayed in a Q^2 range between 0.3 and 1.8 (GeV/c)^2 and compared with recent electron scattering data. For Q^2 = 0.3 (GeV/c)^2 the results are lower than data; for higher Q^2 they are in reasonable agreement with data. The sensitivity of the model to different prescriptions for the one-body current operator is investigated. The off-shell ambiguities are rather large for the distorted cross sections and small for the plane wave cross sections.

## Key claim

Nuclear transparency in (e,e'p) reactions is evaluated using a fully relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) model, and the results show that at Q^2 = 0.3 (GeV/c)^2 the calculated values are lower than experimental data, while at higher Q^2 values they are in reasonable agreement with data.

## Method

The bound state wave functions were generated as solutions of a Dirac equation containing scalar and vector potentials obtained from relativistic mean field theory. The outgoing nucleon wave function was calculated using the direct Pauli reduction method, where a Schrodinger-like equation was solved for each partial wave starting from the complex phenomenological optical potential EDAD1. Nuclear transparency was defined as the ratio of the distorted wave cross section to the plane wave cross section, integrated over the same phase space volume, and calculations were performed for closed shell or subshell nuclei (12C, 16O, 28Si, 40Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb) in a Q^2 range between 0.3 and 1.8 (GeV/c)^2.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparency was calculated for Q^2 values of 0.3, 0.6, 1.3, and 1.8 (GeV/c)^2 for six nuclei: 12C, 16O, 28Si, 40Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb
- At Q^2 = 0.3 (GeV/c)^2, the RDWIA results lie below the experimental data for all nuclei
- The transparency data can be fitted with an exponential law of the form T = A^(-alpha), with alpha approximately 0.24
- The optical potential EDAD1 is valid for proton elastic scattering data in an energy range up to 1040 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier nonrelativistic distorted wave impulse approximation calculations (Oxford, Kelly1) that were successful for Q^2 less than 0.4 (GeV/c)^2, and builds upon recent RDWIA models developed for new data from TJNAF (gao, malov). The calculations are compared with experimental transparency measurements from Bates at Q^2 = 0.3 (GeV/c)^2 (garino), SLAC NE-18 experiment (oneill), and TJNAF E91-013 experiment (abbott, garrow), and the results enable further investigation of possible Color Transparency effects in (e,e'p) reactions.
