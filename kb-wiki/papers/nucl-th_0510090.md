# Can we distinguish energy loss from hadron absoprtion?

> Knowing whether a hadron is formed inside or outside the nuclear medium is very important for correctly interpreting jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions. The cleanest experimental environment to study the space-time evolution of hadronization is semi-inclusive DIS on nuclear targets. Two frameworks are presently competing to explain the observed attenuation of hadron production: quark energy loss, with hadron formation outside the nucleus, and nuclear absorption with hadronization starting inside the nucleus. I demonstrate that the observed approximate A^(2/3) scaling of experimental data cannot conclusively establish the correctness of either energy loss or absorption.

# Can we distinguish energy loss from hadron absorption?

## Key claim
The observed approximate A^(2/3) scaling of experimental data on hadron attenuation in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering on nuclear targets cannot conclusively establish the correctness of either quark energy loss or hadron absorption models, contrary to a naive argument that energy loss models give A^(2/3) scaling while absorption models give A^(1/3) scaling.

## Method
The author compared two competing frameworks for hadron attenuation in semi-inclusive DIS on nuclear targets: a hadron absorption model where the struck quark forms a prehadron inside the nucleus with a formation length computed from the Lund model, and an energy loss model where hadronization occurs outside the nucleus and attenuation is due to medium-induced gluon radiation. Both models were fitted to HERMES π+ production data on a Kr target at a beam energy of 27 GeV², then used to compute the multiplicity ratio R_M^h as a function of atomic number A, with a fit of the form 1 − R_M^h = c A^α performed for a set of targets {He, N, Ne, Kr}.

## Key numbers
- The hadron absorption model fits a prehadron-nucleon cross section σ*(ν) = 2/3 σ^h(ν), proportional to the experimental hadron-nucleon cross section, to π+ production data on Kr at E_beam = 27 GeV².
- The energy loss model uses a transport coefficient q̂(0,0) = 0.5 GeV²/fm, fitted to π+ production on a Kr target.
- Both models produce R_M^h ∝ A^(2/3) scaling, with breaking of the A^(2/3) law observed at A ≳ 80.
- The analysis uses 2σ confidence contours in the (c, α) plane for the fit parameters at z = 0.65 for targets {He, N, Ne, Kr}.

## Context
This work extends the debate between energy loss models (Wang, Arleo) and hadron absorption models (AGMP05, Falter et al. 2004, Kopeliovich) for interpreting hadron attenuation in semi-inclusive DIS, which is the cleanest environment for studying the space-time evolution of hadronization. The paper demonstrates that the A^(2/3) scaling argument often used in heavy-ion collision discussions is incorrect because nonzero prehadron formation lengths in absorption models introduce extra powers of A^(1/3), producing the same scaling as energy loss models. The author suggests that distinguishing the two mechanisms requires more exclusive observables such as hadron pT-broadening, the Cronin effect (Kopeliovich), or dihadron correlations (HERMES dihadron data).
