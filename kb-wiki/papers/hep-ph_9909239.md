# Hadronic electromagnetic form factors and color transparency

> We review the current status of electromagnetic form factor calculations in perturbative QCD. There is growing evidence that factorization prescriptions involving a transverse coordinate integration, such as that of Li and Sterman, are more appropriate than the prescription of LePage and Brodsky. Color transparency is naturally described within the formalism. We report the first explicit calculations of color transparency and nuclear filtering as perturbatively calculable phenomena.

# Hadronic Electromagnetic Form Factors and Color Transparency

## Key claim
The Li-Sterman factorization scheme, which retains transverse coordinate dependence and includes Sudakov suppression, is more appropriate for calculating hadronic form factors than the LePage-Brodsky prescription, and it naturally describes color transparency and nuclear filtering as perturbatively calculable phenomena, but the leading-order predictions for form factor normalization cannot be regarded as accurate, and higher-order corrections in alpha_s may not be negligible.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo integrations of up to 9 dimensions to calculate the proton Dirac form factor and nuclear transparency ratios, incorporating the full two-loop correction to Sudakov effects and a physical infrared cutoff that treats the proton as a quark-diquark configuration at extreme quark separation. For nuclear targets, they modeled the nuclear filtering amplitude using an eikonal form with the inelastic cross section parametrized as sigma = k b^2, and included short-range nuclear correlations using a correlation function with parameters alpha=1.1, beta=0.68 fm^{-2}, and Fermi momentum k_F=1.36 fm^{-1}.

## Key numbers
- For the pion form factor at Q^2 of a few GeV^2, nearly 50% of the form factor comes from a region where alpha_s/pi < 0.7
- The proton form factor calculation saturates at about b_c = 0.8/Lambda_QCD, with about 50% of the calculation coming from soft or large b regions
- Scaling of the proton form factor is postponed to beyond Q^2 = 10 GeV^2
- For a heavy nucleus (A=197) at Q^2 = 36 GeV^2, 90% of the contribution comes from a region where alpha_s/pi is less than 0.7
- The fixed-angle free space pp scattering shows oscillations from s=6 GeV^2 to s=40 GeV^2, making up roughly 50% of the 1/s^10 behavior

## Context
This work extends the transverse-position space factorization approach of Botts and Sterman (1990) and Li and Sterman (1992), which was developed to address the inability of the LePage-Brodsky factorization to describe independent scattering and to resolve the conflict between quark-counting scaling laws and the failure of hadronic helicity conservation. The calculations enable quantitative predictions for color transparency experiments in nuclear targets, showing that nuclear filtering reduces long-distance contamination and that the effective attenuation cross section sigma_eff(Q^2) decreases with increasing Q^2 to values well below the Glauber model attenuation cross section, which indicates color transparency.
