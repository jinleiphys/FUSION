# Efimov physics from a renormalization group perspective

> We discuss the physics of the Efimov effect from a renormalization group viewpoint using the concept of limit cycles. Furthermore, we discuss recent experiments providing evidence for the Efimov effect in ultracold gases and its relevance for nuclear systems.

## Key claim

The Efimov effect arises from an infrared renormalization group limit cycle, which breaks continuous scale invariance to a discrete scaling symmetry with a universal scaling factor of approximately 22.7 for three identical bosons; this discrete scaling is manifest in observables such as binding energies and scattering cross sections, but only when the two-body scattering length is much larger than the range of the underlying interaction.

## Method

The authors review the renormalization group (RG) framework for few-body systems, showing that the Efimov effect corresponds to an RG limit cycle in the space of coupling constants. They illustrate the concept using the quantum mechanical inverse-square potential, where the beta function yields complex conjugate fixed points for sufficiently attractive coupling, leading to log-periodic observables. They then connect this RG analysis to the hyperspherical coordinate formulation of the three-body problem, where the effective radial potential is proportional to \(1/R^2\), and discuss how a single three-body parameter (e.g., a short-range boundary condition or three-body force) is needed to renormalize the theory.

## Key numbers

* The discrete scaling factor for three identical bosons is \(\mathcal{S}_0 = e^{\pi/s_0} \approx 22.7\), where \(s_0 \approx 1.00624\) solves \(s_0 \cosh(\pi s_0/2) = (8/\sqrt{3}) \sinh(\pi s_0/6)\).
* The ratio of binding energies of successive Efimov trimers in the unitary limit approaches \(\mathcal{S}_0^2 \approx 515.03\).
* For the unitary limit, the deeper tetramer binding energy relative to the associated trimer is \(B_4^{(0)} \approx 4.6108\, B_3\) and the shallower tetramer is \(B_4^{(1)} \approx 1.00228\, B_3\) (Deltuva 2010).
* The scattering lengths at which tetramer binding energies vanish are \(a^*_{4,0} \approx 0.43\, a'_*\) and \(a^*_{4,1} \approx 0.92\, a'_*\) (von Stecher et al. 2009).
* The complex atom-trimer scattering length is \(a_T = (18.5 + i\,0.890)\,\kappa_*^{-1}\) and the effective range is \(r_T = (2.63 + i\,0.014)\,\kappa_*^{-1}\) (Deltuva 2010).

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Efimov (1970) on three-body bound states with large scattering length, and connects it to the renormalization group concept of limit cycles first discussed by Wilson (1971) and applied to the Efimov effect by Albeverio et al. (1981). The RG perspective unifies the description of Efimov physics across atomic, nuclear, and condensed-matter systems, and enables systematic effective field theory treatments (Bedaque et al. 1999; Braaten and Hammer 2006; Platter 2009). The review also summarizes experimental confirmations in ultracold gases (Kraemer et al. 2006; Ferlaino et al. 2009; Zaccanti et al. 2008; Gross et al. 2009) and discusses the role of Efimov states in nuclear systems such as the triton and halo nuclei (Hammer and Platter 2010).
