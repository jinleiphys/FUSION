# Test of time-reversal symmetry in the proton-deuteron scattering

> The integrated cross section $\widetildeσ$ for a special type of double polarized proton-deuteron scattering constitutes a null test forvtime-invariance violating but P-parity conserving effects. Using the Glauber theory for the $pd$ elastic scattering and different types of phenomenological T-odd P-even NN-interactions we show that the contribution of the lowest mass meson exchange, i.e. the $ρ$-meson, to the null-test signal $\widetildeσ$ vanishes. Variation of the cross section $\widetildeσ$ due to strong hadronic and Coulomb interaction is studied and its energy dependence is calculated in the GeV region.

## Key claim

The integrated cross section $\widetilde\sigma$ for double-polarized proton-deuteron scattering provides a null test for time-reversal-invariance-violating but parity-conserving (TVPC) effects; however, the contribution of the $\rho$-meson exchange, expected to dominate the TVPC NN interaction, vanishes in this observable, while the $h$ and $g$ terms give non-zero contributions.

## Method

The authors used the spin-dependent Glauber theory for elastic proton-deuteron scattering, including the full spin dependence of elementary proton-nucleon amplitudes and both S- and D-wave components of the deuteron wave function. They introduced phenomenological T-odd P-even NN interactions from meson exchange models (including $\rho$-meson, axial $h_1$ meson, and $g$-type terms) and applied the generalized optical theorem to compute the null-test cross section $\widetilde\sigma$. Coulomb effects were incorporated by adding the spin-independent Coulomb pp-scattering amplitude to the hadronic amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The $\rho$-meson exchange contribution to the null-test signal $\widetilde\sigma$ vanishes identically in the Glauber theory, even when D-wave components of the deuteron are included.
* For the $h$-term calculation, the axial meson mass $m_h = 1.17$ GeV and coupling $G_h^2/4\pi = 1.56$ were used, with results expressed in units of the unknown coupling constant ratio $\phi_h = \bar G_h/G_h$.
* Energies around 100 MeV are more preferable than energies around 1 GeV for searching for TVPC effects, based on the calculated energy dependence of $\widetilde\sigma$.
* The Glauber model calculations at 135 MeV and 250 MeV show reasonable agreement with experimental data for non-polarized differential cross section, vector $A_y$ and tensor $A_{ij}$ analyzing powers, and spin correlation parameters in the forward hemisphere ($\theta_{cm} \le 20^\circ - 30^\circ$).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of TVPC effects in proton-deuteron scattering beyond the impulse approximation used in the earlier study by Beyer (2005), which considered only the non-mesonic deuteron breakup channel $pd \to ppn$. The results are directly relevant to the TRIC experiment planned at COSY at 135 MeV. The finding that the $\rho$-meson exchange vanishes in this observable contradicts the expectation from Simonius (1997) that the $\rho$-meson would dominate TVPC NN interactions, and implies that the commonly used coupling constant $\bar g_\rho$ cannot be directly applied as a scale for TVPC effects in pd scattering.
