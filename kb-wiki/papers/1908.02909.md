# Neutron skins of heavy nuclei and tidal deformability of neutron star

> In this paper, I have discussed the numerical predictions for the neutron-skin thickness (NST) of various finite nuclei starting from $^{40}$Ca to $^{238}$U using recently developed effective relativistic mean-field models G3 and IOPB-I \cite{G3, IOPB}. The calculated results are compared with the PREX-II data, and the experiment has been done with antiprotons at CERN. Further, I have also calculated the dimensional tidal deformability of a canonical neutron star 1.4$M_\odot$ and compared it with the recent observation of GW1701817.

# Neutron skins of heavy nuclei and tidal deformability of neutron star

## Key claim
The relativistic mean-field models G3 and IOPB-I predict neutron-skin thicknesses for heavy nuclei that are consistent with the upper limit of Δr_np ≤ 0.25 fm derived from tidal deformability constraints, but the IOPB-I set slightly deviates from experimental antiproton data for some nuclei due to its smaller ω-ρ cross-coupling strength.

## Method
The author calibrated the extended relativistic mean-field (ERMF) energy density functional parameters using a simulated annealing method, fitting to properties of eight spherical nuclei and nuclear matter saturation constraints. Neutron-skin thickness was calculated as the difference between neutron and proton root-mean-square radii for 26 stable nuclei from ⁴⁰Ca to ²³⁸U without considering nuclear deformation or superfluidity. Tidal deformability was computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation with a perturbing metric using the nuclear equation of state as input.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.283 fm (NL3), 0.162 fm (FSUGarnet), 0.180 fm (G3), and 0.221 fm (IOPB-I)
- PREX-II experimental neutron-skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.33⁺⁰·¹⁶₋₀·₁₈ fm
- Upper limit from tidal deformability correlation: Δr_np ≤ 0.25 fm for ²⁰⁸Pb
- Tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: 680.79 (IOPB-I), 622.06 (FSUGarnet), 461.03 (G3)
- GW170817 re-analysis result: Λ₁.₄ = 190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀, with 90% confidence upper bound of Λ = 580
- First GW170817 analysis upper bound: Λ ≤ 800 at 90% confidence

## Context
This work extends the study of neutron-skin thickness using the ERMF models G3 and IOPB-I, comparing predictions with experimental antiproton data from CERN (Thick, year not specified) and the PREX-II measurement at JLab. The tidal deformability calculations are directly compared with the binary neutron star merger GW170817 observed by aLIGO-VIRGO, following the analysis by Fattoyev (year not specified) that correlated neutron-skin thickness with tidal deformability. The results enable further constraints on the neutron star equation of state and rule out the NL3 parameter set due to its overly stiff behavior.
