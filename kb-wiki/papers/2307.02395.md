# Collision integral with momentum-dependent potentials and its impact on pion production in heavy-ion collisions

> The momentum dependence of the nucleon mean-field potential in a wide momentum range can be an important factor to determine the $Δ$ resonance and pion production in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. In particular, in neutron-rich systems such as ${}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}$ collisions, we need to carefully treat the momentum dependence because the neutron and proton potentials can have different momentum dependence, as characterized at low momenta by effective masses. In the present work, we rigorously calculate the collision terms of $NN \leftrightarrow N Δ$ and $Δ\leftrightarrow N π$ processes with the precise conservation of energy and momentum under the presence of momentum-dependent potentials for the initial and final particles of the process. The potentials affect not only the threshold condition for the process but also the cross section in general as a function of the momenta of the initial particles, which is treated in a natural way in the present work. Calculations are performed by combining the nucleon dynamics obtained by the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model with a newly developed transport code which we call sJAM. The calculated results for central ${}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}$ collisions at 270 MeV/nucleon clearly show that the momentum dependence of the neutron and proton potentials has a significant impact on the $NN \to N Δ$ process, and this information is strongly reflected in the charged pion ratio ($π^-/π^+$). We also investigate the effects of the high-density symmetry energy and the isovector part of the potential of $Δ$ resonances on pion production, which we find are relatively small compared to the effect of the momentum dependence of the neutron and proton potentials.

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of neutron and proton mean-field potentials, particularly the effective mass splitting, has a significant impact on the $NN \to N\Delta$ process and the charged pion ratio ($\pi^-/\pi^+$) in neutron-rich heavy-ion collisions, while the effects of the high-density symmetry energy and the isovector part of the $\Delta$ resonance potential on pion production are relatively small compared to this momentum dependence.

## Method

The authors combined the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model with a newly developed transport code called sJAM, which rigorously calculates the collision terms for $NN \leftrightarrow N\Delta$ and $\Delta \leftrightarrow N\pi$ processes with precise energy and momentum conservation under momentum-dependent potentials. The potentials affect both the threshold condition and the cross section as a function of the initial particle momenta. The calculations used three different energy density functionals (SLy4, SLy4:L108, and SkM*) with a cutoff parameter $\Lambda_{\text{md}}/\hbar = 5.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}$ to modify the high-momentum behavior of the mean field.

## Key numbers

- Central ${}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}$ collisions at 270 MeV/nucleon incident energy
- Saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$ for all three functionals
- Effective mass $m^*/m_N = 0.70$ for SLy4 and SLy4:L108, and $0.79$ for SkM*
- Symmetry energy slope parameter $L = 46$ MeV for SLy4 and SkM*, and $L = 108$ MeV for SLy4:L108
- Neutron-proton effective mass difference $\Delta m^*_{np} = -34.4\ \text{MeV} = -0.18\, m_N \delta$ for SLy4 and SLy4:L108, and $\Delta m^*_{np} = 61.5\ \text{MeV} = 0.33\, m_N \delta$ for SkM*
- $\Delta$ potential parameters: $\alpha_\rho^\Delta = 15$ MeV and $\alpha_\tau^\Delta = 15$ MeV with $\gamma^\Delta = 1$ as default
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda_{\text{md}}/\hbar = 5.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}$ used in all calculations

## Context

This work extends the previous AMD+JAM model by Ikeno, Ono, Nara, and Ohnishi (2016, 2019) which lacked potentials for nucleons and $\Delta$ resonances in the collision terms and underestimated the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio compared to experimental data from the S$\pi$RIT collaboration at RIBF. The study addresses discrepancies identified by the Transport Model Evaluation Project (TMEP) regarding pion production predictions. The improved treatment enables more reliable extraction of symmetry energy information from heavy-ion collision observables, complementing analyses using the dcQMD model by Cozma (2021) that constrained the symmetry energy slope to $42 < L < 117$ MeV.
