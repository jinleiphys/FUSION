# Ab initio many-fermion structure calculations on a quantum computer

> To overcome the limitations of existing algorithms for solving self-bound quantum many-body problems -- such as those encountered in nuclear and particle physics -- that access only a restricted subset of energy levels and provide limited structural information, we introduce and demonstrate a novel quantum-classical approach capable of resolving the complete bound-state spectrum. This method also provides the total angular momentum $J$ associated with each eigenstate. Our approach is based on expressing the Hamiltonian in second-quantized form within a novel input model combined with a scan scheme, enabling broad applicability to configuration-interaction calculations across diverse fields. We apply this hybrid method to compute, for the first time, the bound-state spectrum together with corresponding $J$ values of ${^{20}O}$ using a realistic strong-interaction Hamiltonian. Our approach applies to hadron spectra and $J$ values solved in the relativistic Basis Light-Front Quantization approach.

# Ab initio many-fermion structure calculations on a quantum computer

## Key claim
The authors claim that their hybrid quantum-classical method can resolve the complete bound-state spectrum and total angular momentum J of self-bound many-fermion systems, demonstrated for the first time on 20O. However, the method is demonstrated only in a truncated single-particle basis (0d5/2 and 1s1/2 shells, excluding 0d3/2) using a noiseless quantum simulator, not on actual quantum hardware.

## Method
The authors block-encode the second-quantized many-fermion Hamiltonian using a novel circuit representation of fermion operators that avoids Jordan-Wigner or Bravyi-Kitaev overhead, based on direct basis encoding with validation registers. They compute Chebyshev moments of the Hamiltonian via quantum circuits on a statevector simulator, then classically construct the resolvent Green's function G(E) = 1/(E-H) using a discrete Fourier transform and Chebyshev polynomial expansion. A two-fold scan scheme (cascading-M scan and resolution scan) extracts eigenenergies and J values from the spectral function.

## Key numbers
- 110 few-body matrix elements were retained for the 20O calculation in the truncated basis.
- The number of grid points N used for the resolvent was 2000 for coarse resolution and 20000 for finer resolution.
- The total number of Chebyshev moments required scales as O(B * Xi / Delta), where Delta is the spectral gap to be resolved between neighboring states.
- The gate cost for inputting a general k-body Hamiltonian is O-tilde(N_sp^(2k+1)), where N_sp is the number of single-particle bases.

## Context
This work extends prior quantum algorithms for many-body structure (variational quantum eigensolver, imaginary time quantum eigensolver, quantum subspace expansion methods) that could only access a limited subset of energy levels, mostly the ground state. It introduces a practical circuit design that avoids the oracle design and ancilla uncomputation challenges of Child's quantum walk approaches (Childs 2010) and the Prepare oracle difficulties of standard linear combination of unitaries (Childs 2012). The method is directly applicable to hadron spectroscopy in the Basis Light-Front Quantization framework (Vary 2009) and to configuration-interaction calculations in quantum chemistry and condensed matter physics.
