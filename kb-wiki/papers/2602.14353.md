# Estimation of neutron star mass and radius of FRB 20240114A by identification of crustal oscillations

> By identifying quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) reported in FRB 20240114A (from the Five-hundred-meter Aperture Spherical Telescope) with neutron star crustal torsional oscillations, together with experimental constraints on the incompressibility $K_0$ of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, we constrain the mass and radius of an extragalactic neutron star at redshift $z\approx0.13$. Identifying the low-order QPO frequencies as fundamental oscillations, and frequencies of $567.7\,\mathrm{Hz}$ or $655.5\,\mathrm{Hz}$ (rest frame) as first overtone candidates, implies neutron star mass ranges of $1.00$--$1.55\,M_\odot$ or $1.17$--$1.76\,M_\odot$, respectively. The radius is also constrained, with a self-consistent value around $13$~km, consistent with the calculation of the NS structure within the low-mass/low-central density regime. Simultaneously, we also constrain another nuclear saturation parameter, namely the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density (i.e., the slope parameter), $L$, and determine it to be $L=59.5-96.8$ MeV with $\sim 10\%$ systematic uncertainty, which is broadly consistent with previous constraints on $L$ obtained from experiments and astronomical observations. Thus, a mapping of FRB QPOs to crustal torsional modes seems reasonable. This interpretation will be tested with the discovery of additional QPOs in upcoming FRB surveys.

## Key claim

By identifying quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) reported in FRB 20240114A with neutron star crustal torsional oscillations, the authors constrain the mass and radius of an extragalactic neutron star, but with the caveat that the QPOs may have low statistical significance and that consistency with crustal mode identification does not rule out alternative interpretations.

## Method

The authors constructed neutron star models by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using the phenomenological Oyamatsu-Iida equations of state, which are parameterized by the incompressibility K0 and the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density. They solved the eigenvalue problem for crustal torsional oscillations by linearizing the equation of motion with boundary conditions of vanishing traction at the base and top of the crust, identifying the lower-frequency QPOs (16.0-75.2 Hz observed) as fundamental oscillations and the higher-frequency QPOs (502.1 Hz and 579.8 Hz observed) as first overtone candidates. The observed frequencies were converted to rest-frame frequencies using the redshift z ≈ 0.13 of the host galaxy.

## Key numbers

- Redshift of FRB 20240114A host galaxy: z ≈ 0.130287 ± 0.000002
- Rest-frame QPO frequencies identified as fundamental oscillations: 18.1 Hz (ℓ=2), 23.2 Hz (ℓ=3), 36.9 Hz (ℓ=4), 85.0 Hz (ℓ=10)
- First overtone candidate rest-frame frequencies: 567.7 ± 7.0 Hz or 655.5 ± 6.3 Hz
- Neutron star mass range for 567.7 Hz first overtone candidate: 1.00–1.55 M⊙
- Neutron star mass range for 655.5 Hz first overtone candidate: 1.17–1.76 M⊙
- Self-consistent radius: approximately 13 km
- Constraint on slope parameter L: 59.5–96.8 MeV with approximately 10% systematic uncertainty
- Experimental constraint on incompressibility K0 adopted: 240 ± 20 MeV (from 2006EPJA...30...23S)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Sotani, Kokkotas, and Stergioulas (2023), who estimated the mass and radius of the neutron star associated with GRB 200415A using higher QPO frequencies identified as overtones of crustal torsional oscillations. Here, the availability of both low-frequency and high-frequency QPOs in FRB 20240114A allows simultaneous constraints on neutron star mass, radius, and the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter L. The results are broadly consistent with previous constraints on L from experiments and astronomical observations, and the interpretation will be tested with the discovery of additional QPOs in upcoming FRB surveys from CHORD and DSA.
