# Collisional Stark Transitions and Induced Annihilation of Cold Antiprotonic Helium Ions

> Stark transitions and induced annihilation of antiproton in the collisions of antiprotonic helium ions (\bar{p}He^{+2})_{nl} with He atoms at very low energy (~ 10 K) are considered in the framework of quantum coupled-channels method taking into account all the states with different l at given n~30, including the annihilating ns and np-states. Elastic scattering, Stark transitions and induced annihilation during collisions are produced by scalar and dipole terms in the interaction. It is shown that the most important contribution to the processes comes from the long-range polarization interaction. Admixtures of the ns and np-states to the states with higher l during collisions induce the effective annihilation cross sections for the initial l up to 15, but don't affect the Stark cross sections for the initial states nearly to circular orbits. Total rates of the Stark transitions from the circular orbits with n=28 - 32, averaged over the thermal motion, are compatible with the recent ASACUSA data. Isotope effect as well as the dependence on n are also qualitatively agree with the experiment.

# Collisional Stark Transitions and Induced Annihilation of Cold Antiprotonic Helium Ions

## Key claim

The authors show that Stark transitions from circular orbits of antiprotonic helium ions colliding with helium atoms at ~10 K are dominated by long-range polarization interaction, and that the calculated per-atom Stark transition rates are compatible with the ASACUSA experimental data on effective annihilation rates, though the calculated values are greater than but of the same order as the measured rates.

## Method

The authors use a quantum coupled-channels method with a basis including all states with different orbital angular momentum l at fixed principal quantum number n ~ 30, including the annihilating ns and np states. The interaction between the antiprotonic helium ion and the helium atom is treated using an adiabatic potential consisting of a Morse potential plus a polarization term, with dipole and quadrupole terms in the multipolar expansion. Boundary conditions at large distance are modified for channels with non-zero annihilation widths, and the S-matrix defect yields the induced annihilation cross section.

## Key numbers

- Polarizability of He atom: α = 1.383 a.u.
- Classical polarization capture cross section at E = 10 K: σ_p ≈ 930 a.u.
- Total Stark cross section from circular orbit with n = 30 at E = 10 K using only Morse potential: ~120 a.u.; including polarization term: ~620 a.u.
- Total Stark cross sections for circular orbits at E = 10 K for ⁴He: 573.5 a.u. (n=28), 598.2 a.u. (n=29), 619.6 a.u. (n=30), 636.5 a.u. (n=31), 649.0 a.u. (n=32)
- Total Stark cross sections for circular orbits at E = 10 K for ³He: 539.3 a.u. (n=28), 566.5 a.u. (n=29), 583.4 a.u. (n=30), 597.0 a.u. (n=31), 605.1 a.u. (n=32)
- Per-atom Stark transition rates at T = 10 K for ⁴He: 3.77 × 10⁻¹⁶ MHz·cm³ (n=28), 4.15 × 10⁻¹⁶ MHz·cm³ (n=30), 4.46 × 10⁻¹⁶ MHz·cm³ (n=32)
- Per-atom Stark transition rates at T = 10 K for ³He: 4.46 × 10⁻¹⁶ MHz·cm³ (n=28), 4.82 × 10⁻¹⁶ MHz·cm³ (n=30), 5.01 × 10⁻¹⁶ MHz·cm³ (n=32)
- Induced annihilation cross sections are appreciable for initial l up to 15 at n = 30 and E = 10 K
- Annihilation widths used: Γ₁s = 11 keV, Γ₂p = 36 eV for ⁴He

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical treatments of antiprotonic helium collisions that could not explain the n-dependence and isotope effect observed in the ASACUSA collaboration's measurements of cold long-lived antiprotonic helium ions (reported in 2004). The calculations address the specific low-temperature regime (~10 K) relevant to the ASACUSA experiments at CERN's Antiproton Decelerator. The results enable interpretation of the measured effective annihilation rates dγ/dρ ~ (1-3) × 10⁻¹⁶ MHz·cm³ as arising from a cascade process where Stark transitions from circular orbits are the first step, and the calculated rates show the same qualitative trends (increase with n and higher rates for ³He) as the experimental data.
