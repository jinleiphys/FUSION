# Improvement to Generalized Separable Expansion Method in Lippmann-Schwinger Equation

> Realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials are generally not in separable form, but there is a way to convert them into separable potentials, called the generalized separable expansion (GSE). When the separable potential is substituted into a three-body Faddeev equation, which generally has two Jacobi momenta, the integral equation is conveniently reduced to a one-variable integral equation. The two-body scattering t-matrix of the conventional GSE does not have an exact singularity at the energy threshold of the two-body bound state. The newly introduced GSE improves this by treating the singularity analytically.

## Key claim

The paper improves the Generalized Separable Expansion (GSE) method so that the resulting two-body scattering t-matrix has an exact first-order singularity at the bound-state energy threshold, which the conventional GSE method did not provide; the improvement is achieved by analytically separating out the bound-state contribution from the original potential before applying the standard GSE expansion.

## Method

The author defines a modified potential \(\tilde V\) by subtracting from the original nucleon-nucleon potential \(V\) a separable term built from the bound-state wave function \(\psi_d\) and its potential expectation value \(C\). The conventional GSE expansion is then applied to \(\tilde V\), and the subtracted separable term is added back, yielding an improved separable potential \(V_{GSE}\) of rank \(N+1\). The Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved with this improved potential, and the analytic structure of the resulting t-matrix is examined at the bound-state energy \(E_d\).

## Key numbers

* The bound-state energy \(E_d\) is negative and satisfies the Schrödinger equation \((E_d - p^2/m)\psi_d(p) = \int V(p,p')\psi_d(p') \, p'^2 dp'/(2\pi^2)\); no numerical value is given in the text.
* The constant \(C\) is defined as the potential expectation of the bound state: \(C = \langle V \rangle = \int_0^\infty \int_0^\infty \psi_d(p) V(p,p') \psi_d(p') \, (p^2 dp / 2\pi^2) (p'^2 dp' / 2\pi^2)\); no numerical value is given.
* The t-matrix \(\tau_{i,j}(E)\) is shown to be proportional to \(1/(E - E_d + i\epsilon)\) and thus diverges at \(E = E_d\); no numerical value is given for the proportionality constant.

## Context

This work extends the Generalized Separable Expansion method originally introduced by Oryu (1974) and tested on the Reid Soft Core potential (RSC). It addresses a known deficiency: the conventional GSE t-matrix lacked the correct analytic singularity at the two-body bound-state threshold. The improved GSE potential exactly reproduces the original bound-state wave function and t-matrix at the Bateman momentum points, and the author suggests that combining this method with low-momentum potentials (Bogner, 2002; Fujii, 2004) and the USE expansion (USE) could yield highly separable expansions for few-body calculations.
