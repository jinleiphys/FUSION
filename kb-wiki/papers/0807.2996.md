# Mach cones in the quark-gluon plasma: Viscosity, speed of sound, and effects of finite source structure

> I use the space-time distribution of energy and momentum deposited by a fast parton traversing a perturbative quark-gluon plasma as a source term for the linearized hydrodynamical equations of the medium. A method of solution for the medium response is presented in detail. Numerical results are given for different values of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s$, and speed of sound, $c_s$. Furthermore, I investigate the relevance of finite source structure by expanding the source term up to first order in gradients of a $δ$ function centered at the fast parton and comparing the resulting dynamics to that obtained with the full source. It is found that, for the source term used here, the medium response is sensitive to the finite source structure up to distances of several fm from the source parton.

## Key claim

The medium response to a fast parton traversing a perturbative quark-gluon plasma is sensitive to the finite structure of the energy-momentum deposition source up to distances of several fm from the source parton, meaning that a simple delta-function approximation for the source is insufficient at those distances.

## Method

The space-time distribution of energy and momentum deposited by a fast parton, derived from a Vlasov equation treatment of a gluonic medium, was used as a source term in the linearized first-order hydrodynamic equations with nonzero shear viscosity. The hydrodynamic equations were solved in Fourier space, and the resulting expressions for perturbed energy density and momentum density were transformed back to position space via contour integration and numerical integration over transverse momentum. The full source term was compared to a version expanded up to first order in gradients of a delta function to assess the importance of finite source structure.

## Key numbers

- The source parton speed was taken as \(u = 0.99955 c\) (\(\gamma \approx 33\)), with temperature \(T = 350\) MeV, strong coupling \(\alpha_s = 1/\pi\), and parton energy \(E_p = 16\) GeV.
- The full source and delta-function expanded source results converge at a distance of about 5-6 fm behind the source parton for the chosen values of \(\rho\).
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio was varied, with results shown for \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\).
- The speed of sound was varied, with results shown for \(c_s = c/\sqrt{3}\) (approximately 0.57).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior derivation of a hydrodynamic source term from a perturbative QGP (Neufeld, 2008) and builds on earlier studies of medium response to fast partons (Casalderrey-Solana, 2004; Stoecker, 2004; Satarov, 2005; Ruppert, 2005; Renk, 2005; Chaudhuri, 2005; Friess, 2006; Chesler, 2007; Betz, 2008). The results enable quantitative modeling of Mach cone formation in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, showing that the detailed structure of the energy deposition source matters for distances up to several fm, and that the Mach cone carries more total perturbed momentum than the diffusive wake.
