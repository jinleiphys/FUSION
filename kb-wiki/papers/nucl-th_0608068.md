# Recent advances in the theory of nuclear forces

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory (χPT) provides a model-independent, systematic expansion for nuclear forces that can achieve quantitative accuracy comparable to phenomenological potentials, but only when carried to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO); lower orders (NLO, NNLO) are insufficient for reliable nuclear structure calculations.

## Method
The authors construct nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials using chiral perturbation theory, where the NN amplitude is uniquely determined by contact terms and pion-exchange diagrams ordered by powers of (Q/Λχ)ν with Λχ ≈ 1 GeV. The potential is then applied in a Lippmann-Schwinger or Schrödinger equation to obtain the NN amplitude, with all expressions multiplied by a regulator function exp[-(p/Λ)2n - (p'/Λ)2n] where Λ ≪ Λχ.

## Key numbers
- χ²/datum for np data below 290 MeV: N³LO = 1.10, NNLO = 10.1, NLO = 36.2, AV18 = 1.04 (2402 data points)
- χ²/datum for pp data below 290 MeV: N³LO = 1.50, NNLO = 35.4, NLO = 80.1, AV18 = 1.38 (2057 data points)
- χ²/datum for np data 190–290 MeV: N³LO = 1.15, NNLO = 19.2, NLO = 68.3, AV18 = 1.11 (843 data points)
- χ²/datum for pp data 190–290 MeV: N³LO = 1.93, NNLO = 66.8, NLO = 111.6, AV18 = 1.82 (851 data points)
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale Λχ ≈ 1 GeV; regulator cutoff Λ = 500 MeV (and 600 MeV) used in chiral potentials
- Contact terms: 7 at O(Q²), 15 at O(Q⁴)

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994), Kaiser, Brockmann, and Weise (1997), and Epelbaum et al. (1998). The N³LO potential by Entem and Machleidt (2003) achieves accuracy comparable to the phenomenological Argonne V18 potential (Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla, 1995). This enables consistent nuclear structure calculations where two- and many-body forces are generated on the same footing, with first applications already producing promising results. The three-nucleon force at NNLO is known and has had successful applications, while three- and four-nucleon forces at N³LO are under construction.
