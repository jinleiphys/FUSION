# Volume Dependence of Bound States with Angular Momentum

> We derive general results for the mass shift of bound states with angular momentum l >= 1 in a finite periodic volume. Our results have direct applications to lattice simulations of hadronic molecules as well as atomic nuclei. While the binding of S-wave bound states increases at finite volume, we show that the binding of P-wave bound states decreases. The mass shift for D-wave bound states as well as higher partial waves depends on the representation of the cubic rotation group. Nevertheless, the multiplet-averaged mass shift for any angular momentum l can be expressed in a simple form, and the sign of the shift alternates for even and odd l. We verify our analytical results with explicit numerical calculations. We also show numerically that similar volume corrections appear in three-body bound states.

## Key claim

The binding of P-wave bound states decreases in a finite periodic volume (opposite to S-wave states, whose binding increases), while for D-waves and higher partial waves the mass shift depends on the representation of the cubic rotation group; the multiplet-averaged mass shift for any angular momentum l alternates sign for even and odd l.

## Method

The authors derived a general formula for the finite-volume mass shift of two-body bound states with angular momentum l >= 1 by inserting asymptotic wave functions (expressed via Riccati-Hankel functions and solid harmonics) into an overlap integral that accounts for periodic boundary conditions. They evaluated the resulting expressions analytically for S-waves, P-waves, and D-waves, and verified the predictions by numerically solving the Schrodinger equation for a lattice Gaussian potential. They also extended the numerical study to three-body bound states using a three-particle Gaussian model with different pairwise interaction strengths.

## Key numbers

* For S-waves (l=0, representation A1+), the mass shift coefficient alpha = -3, giving Delta m_B = -3|gamma|^2 exp(-kappa L)/(mu L) + O(exp(-sqrt(2) kappa L)).
* For P-waves (l=1, representation T1-), the mass shift coefficient alpha = +3, giving Delta m_B = +3|gamma|^2 exp(-kappa L)/(mu L) + O(exp(-sqrt(2) kappa L)).
* For D-waves (l=2), the coefficient alpha depends on the cubic representation: for T2+, alpha = 30x + 135x^2 + 315x^3 + 315x^4; for E+, alpha = -(1/2)(15 + 90x + 405x^2 + 945x^3 + 945x^4), where x = 1/(kappa L).
* The multiplet-averaged mass shift for any l is (-1)^(l+1) (2l+1) * 3 |gamma|^2 exp(-kappa L)/(mu L) + O(exp(-sqrt(2) kappa L)).
* From numerical fits to the Gaussian potential (R=1, V0=6, mu=1 in lattice units): S-wave binding momentum kappa = 2.198 +/- 0.005, asymptotic normalization |gamma| = 11.5 +/- 0.2; P-wave kappa = 1.501 +/- 0.004, |gamma| = 7.0 +/- 0.1.

## Context

This work extends Luscher's formula (1985) for S-wave bound state mass shifts in finite periodic volumes to states with nonzero orbital angular momentum, which had not been systematically treated. The results are directly applicable to lattice simulations of hadronic molecules, nuclear halo systems such as the P-wave 1/2- excited state in 11Be (Typel 2004, Hammer 2011), and P-wave Feshbach resonances in 6Li and 40K (Regal 2003, Schunk 2005, Gaebler 2007). The derived formulas enable future lattice studies to discern molecular structures in hadronic states and halo structures in nuclei from finite-volume dependence, and the authors note that similar volume corrections appear numerically in three-body bound states, suggesting a probe for radial distribution of angular momentum in complicated systems.
