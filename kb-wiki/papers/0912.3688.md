# From low-momentum interactions to nuclear structure

> We present an overview of low-momentum two-nucleon and many-body interactions and their use in calculations of nuclei and infinite matter. The softening of phenomenological and effective field theory (EFT) potentials by renormalization group (RG) transformations that decouple low and high momenta leads to greatly enhanced convergence in few- and many-body systems while maintaining a decreasing hierarchy of many-body forces. This review surveys the RG-based technology and results, discusses the connections to chiral EFT, and clarifies various misconceptions.

## Key claim

Low-momentum interactions constructed via renormalization group (RG) transformations decouple low and high momenta in nuclear potentials, making many-body calculations more convergent while maintaining a decreasing hierarchy of many-body forces; the authors emphasize that the potential is not an observable and that an infinite number of potentials can describe low-energy physics, so the failure of low-momentum NN interactions alone to reproduce nuclear saturation does not indicate they are wrong but rather that many-body forces are required.

## Method

The authors review two classes of RG transformations applied to phenomenological and chiral effective field theory (EFT) potentials: the Vlowk approach, which lowers a momentum cutoff Λ above which matrix elements are set to zero, and the similarity renormalization group (SRG) approach, which uses flow equations to evolve interactions toward the diagonal in momentum space by lowering a cutoff λ in energy differences. Both methods preserve low-energy observables while shifting contributions between interaction strengths and sums over intermediate states, thereby weakening sources of nonperturbative behavior such as short-range repulsion and tensor forces.

## Key numbers

- NN phase shifts are reproduced up to roughly 300–350 MeV laboratory energy by various high-precision potentials (Argonne v18, CD-Bonn, chiral N3LO)
- Chiral EFT cutoffs range from 450–600 MeV for N3LO potentials
- The elastic regime for NN scattering corresponds to relative momenta k ≲ 2 fm⁻¹
- Low-momentum interactions are defined as potentials that do not couple k ≲ 2 fm⁻¹ to larger momenta
- For cutoffs around 2 fm⁻¹, the Born series in nuclear matter is well converged at second order in the potential
- An exploratory calculation for ⁴He suggested a contribution of order 100 keV to the binding energy from the long-range four-body force at N³LO

## Context

This review extends the RG-based low-momentum interaction program (Bogner 2003, Bogner 2006) and connects it to chiral EFT developments (Epelbaum 2005, Epelbaum 2008, Machleidt 2007). The work contradicts the long-standing perspective summarized by Bethe (1971) that nuclear matter calculations must be highly nonperturbative and that soft potentials cannot give saturation. The approach enables controlled calculations across the nuclear landscape using many-body methods such as the No-Core Shell Model and coupled-cluster methods, with variable-cutoff interactions providing tools to assess theoretical errors from omitted higher-order contributions.
