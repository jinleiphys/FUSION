# The one-body and two-body density matrices of finite nuclei with an appropriate treatment of the center-of-mass motion

> The one-body and two-body density matrices in coordinate space and their Fourier transforms in momentum space are studied for a nucleus (a nonrelativistic, self-bound finite system). Unlike the usual procedure, suitable for infinite or externally bound systems, they are determined as expectation values of appropriate intrinsic operators, dependent on the relative coordinates and momenta (Jacobi variables) and acting on intrinsic wavefunctions of nuclear states. Thus, translational invariance (TI) is respected. When handling such intrinsic quantities, we use an algebraic technique based upon the Cartesian representation, in which the coordinate and momentum operators are linear combinations of the creation and annihilation operators a^+ and a for oscillator quanta. Each of the relevant multiplicative operators can then be reduced to the form: one exponential of the set {a^+} times other exponential of the set {a}. In the course of such a normal-ordering procedure we offer a fresh look at the appearance of "Tassie-Barker" factors, and point out other model-independent results. The intrinsic wavefunction of the nucleus in its ground state is constructed from a nontranslationally-invariant (nTI) one via existing projection techniques. As an illustration, the one-body and two-body momentum distributions (MDs) for the 4He nucleus are calculated with the Slater determinant of the harmonic-oscillator model as the trial, nTI wavefunction. We find that the TI introduces important effects in the MDs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that translational invariance (TI) introduces important effects in the one-body and two-body momentum distributions of finite nuclei, and that these effects can be calculated using an algebraic technique based on the Cartesian representation of creation and annihilation operators, with the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) prescription for constructing intrinsic wavefunctions.

## Method

The authors construct intrinsic wavefunctions from nontranslationally-invariant (nTI) Slater determinants using the EST projection technique, which fixes the center-of-mass coordinate to zero via a non-unitary operator. They then evaluate expectation values of intrinsic one-body and two-body operators (expressed in terms of Jacobi variables) using an algebraic normal-ordering procedure in the Cartesian representation, where coordinate and momentum operators are linear combinations of creation and annihilation operators for oscillator quanta. As an illustration, they calculate the one-body and two-body momentum distributions for the 4He nucleus using the harmonic-oscillator model Slater determinant as the trial nTI wavefunction.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of center-of-mass correlations from one-body quantities (previously studied by Tassie and Barker (1958), Gartenhaus and Schwartz (1957), Peierls and Yoccoz (1957), and Ernst, Shakin and Thaler (1973)) to two-body quantities, specifically the two-body momentum distribution. The algebraic technique based on Cartesian representation, introduced by Shebeko et al. (1974), avoids difficult multiple integrals and is particularly convenient for Slater determinants. The results enable comparison between center-of-mass correlations and short-range correlations in the same nucleus, and provide a foundation for calculating cross sections of electromagnetically induced two-nucleon emission reactions in finite nuclei.
