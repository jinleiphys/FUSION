# Expanded calculations of pn-QRPA electron capture rates on $^{55}$Co for presupernova and supernova physics

> Due to its abundance and its relatively high capture rates, $^{55}$Co is one of the key nuclide that can control the dynamics of core collapse of a massive star. Previously we introduced our microscopic calculations of capture rates on $^{55}$Co using the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory. Here we present for the first time an expanded calculation of the electron capture rates on $^{55}$Co on an extensive temperature-density scale. These type of scale is appropriate for interpolation purposes and of greater utility for simulation codes.

## Key claim
The paper presents an expanded calculation of electron capture rates on $^{55}$Co using the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory on a finer temperature-density grid than previously available, claiming these rates are enhanced up to two orders of magnitude compared to large-scale shell model calculations, though at very high temperatures and densities (logT > 9.4; density ≈ 10^11 g cm^-3) the earlier Fuller-Fowler-Newman (FFN) rates surpass the pn-QRPA rates.

## Method
The authors used the pn-QRPA theory with a separable interaction Hamiltonian including single-particle, pairing, particle-hole Gamow-Teller, and particle-particle Gamow-Teller forces, within a large model space of up to 7ħω. They considered 30 excited states in the parent nucleus $^{55}$Co and around 200 excited states in the daughter $^{55}$Fe, calculating allowed Gamow-Teller and superallowed Fermi transitions while assuming forbidden transitions were negligible. The calculations incorporated experimental data for excitation energies and GT transitions when available, and used Nilsson model single-particle energies, BCS pairing, and a quenching factor of (0.74)^2 for the GT strength.

## Key numbers
- Total GT strength from ground state of $^{55}$Co calculated to be 7.4, with a centroid in the energy range 7.1–7.4 MeV in daughter $^{55}$Fe.
- The pn-QRPA interaction constants were set to χ = 0.2 MeV and κ = 0.07 MeV.
- The deformation parameter for $^{55}$Co was taken as δ = 0.06, according to Möller and Nix.
- The pairing gaps were chosen as Δ_p = Δ_n = 12/√A MeV (approximately 1.62 MeV for A=55).
- The Nilsson oscillator constant was chosen as ħω = 41A^(-1/3) MeV (approximately 10.8 MeV for A=55).
- The value of D in the rate formula was taken as 6295 s, and the ratio g_A/g_V as -1.254.
- At high temperatures and densities (logT > 9.4; ρ ≈ 10^11 g cm^-3), the FFN rates surpass the pn-QRPA rates.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous pn-QRPA calculations of electron capture rates on $^{55}$Co (Nabi, Rahman, and Sajjad) by providing a more detailed temperature-density grid suitable for interpolation in core-collapse simulation codes. The paper builds on the foundational work of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN) who performed the first extensive calculation of stellar weak rates for 226 nuclei, and on Aufderheide et al. who identified $^{55}$Co among the top ten most important electron capture nuclei for presupernova evolution. The expanded grid addresses the need for finer interpolation intervals, as earlier compilations (e.g., FFN; Nabi et al.) did not present rates on a detailed enough grid, potentially leading to erroneous interpolated values for collapse simulators.
