# Nucleon Solution of the Faddeev Equation in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model

> Given the phenomenological success of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in describing the meson physics in the low energy limit, it is tempting to find the fully relativistically structured nucleon solution in the same model under the similar approximation employed in the mesonic sector. To achieve this goal we need to solve a relativistic Faddeev equation. The factorizability of the two-body T-matrix reduces the three-body Faddeev equation to a tractable two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation. The reduced equation is then solved numerically. Our result indicates that the nucleon consists of three loosely bound constituent quarks.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a nucleon-like bound state of three constituent quarks exists in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model under approximations similar to those used in the mesonic sector, but only if the scalar-isoscalar diquark channel itself supports a bound state below the two-quark threshold; the nucleon is found to be a loosely bound state of three constituent quarks.

## Method

The authors derived a relativistic three-body Faddeev equation for the NJL model, ignoring three-body irreducible graphs. By exploiting the factorizability (separability) of the two-body quark-quark T-matrix in the scalar-isoscalar diquark channel, they reduced the three-body Faddeev equation to an effective two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation with an energy-dependent interaction. This reduced equation was solved numerically using a Wick rotation to Euclidean space and a perturbation series iteration method with Gaussian quadratures, following the approach of Rupp and Tjon (1988).

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass m = 375 MeV for cutoff Lambda = 750 MeV, determined from the pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV.
* Constituent quark mass m = 400 MeV for cutoff Lambda = 739 MeV.
* For m = 375 MeV and Lambda = 750 MeV, the diquark mass needed to produce a nucleon at M_N = 939 MeV in the full two-channel calculation is M_qq = 576.8 MeV.
* For m = 400 MeV and Lambda = 739 MeV, the diquark mass needed to produce a nucleon at M_N = 939 MeV in the full two-channel calculation is M_qq = 564.7 MeV.
* For m = 450 MeV and Lambda = 728 MeV, the diquark mass needed to produce a nucleon at M_N = 939 MeV in the full two-channel calculation is M_qq = 547.8 MeV.
* In the static limit approximation with m = 375 MeV and M_qq = 576.8 MeV, the predicted nucleon mass is M_N = 936.4 MeV, compared to the full two-channel result of 939 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of NJL-type models, which had been phenomenologically successful in describing low-energy meson physics (reviewed by Vogl and Weise, 1991), into the baryonic sector by directly solving a three-body problem. It contrasts with earlier indirect approaches such as the non-topological soliton approach (Reinhardt and Wunsch, 1988; Meissner and Goeke, 1991) and the bosonization approach (Cahill, Roberts and Praschifka, 1989; Reinhardt, 1990), and with a prior direct three-body attempt by Buck, Alkofer and Reinhardt (1992) that used approximations different from those in the mesonic sector. The finding of diquark clustering with 150 to 300 MeV binding is consistent with instanton model calculations by Schafer, Shuryak and Verbaarschot (1992) and lattice cooling simulations by Chu, Grandy, Huang and Negele (in preparation). This work enables future studies of nucleon form factors, Delta isobar states, pion-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon scattering, and the role of anomalous singularities in loosely bound systems.
