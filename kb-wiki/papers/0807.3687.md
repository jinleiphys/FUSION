# Long Range Tensor Correlations in Charge and Parity Projected Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

> Within the framework of Fermionic Molecular Dynamics a method is developed to better account for long range tensor correlations in nuclei when working with a single Slater determinant. Single-particle states with mixed isospin and broken parity build up an intrinsic Slater determinant which is then charge and parity projected. By minimizing the energy of this many-body state with respect to the parameters of the single-particle states and projecting afterwards on angular momentum ground state energies are obtained that are systematically lower than corresponding Hartree-Fock results. The realistic Argonne V18 potential is used and short range correlations are treated with the Unitary Correlation Operator Method. Comparison with exact few-body calculations shows that in $^4$He about one fifth of the correlation energy due to long-range correlations are accounted for. These correlations which extend over the whole nucleus are visualized with the isospin and spin-isospin density of the intrinsic state. The divergence of the spin-isospin density, the source for pion fields, turns out to be of dipole nature.

## Key claim

Within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics, allowing single-particle states to mix proton and neutron components and break parity, followed by charge and parity projection, can account for about one fifth of the long-range tensor correlation energy missing in a mean-field picture for \(^4\)He, but the effect is smaller than anticipated from earlier work by Sugimoto, Ikeda and Toki because those authors used a tensor force about twice as strong at low momenta as the realistic interaction employed here.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations for \(^4\)He using a single Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets with variable spin orientation and generalized isospinors that allow arbitrary superpositions of proton and neutron states. They minimized the energy of the charge and parity projected intrinsic state with respect to all single-particle parameters, then projected onto good angular momentum and zero center-of-mass momentum. Short-range correlations were treated via the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) applied to the realistic Argonne V18 potential, and the resulting energies were compared to exact no-core shell model and hyperspherical harmonics calculations.

## Key numbers

- The charge and parity projected (CPP) ground state energy of \(^4\)He is \(-18.61\) MeV, compared to \(-16.42\) MeV for Hartree-Fock (HF) and \(-17.51\) MeV for parity projection only (PP), using \(V_{\text{UCOM}}\) with tensor correlator range \(I_\vartheta = 0.09\) fm\(^3\).
- The tensor contribution to the interaction energy is \(-4.59\) MeV in the CPP state, versus \(0.00\) MeV in HF and \(-1.35\) MeV in PP.
- The kinetic energy increases from \(50.25\) MeV (HF) to \(57.93\) MeV (CPP), while the total potential energy changes from \(-66.68\) MeV (HF) to \(-76.54\) MeV (CPP).
- For \(^4\)He, the difference between CPP and HF binding energy is \(0.5\) MeV; for \(^6\)Li and \(^{10}\)B the gain is \(3\)-\(4\) MeV.
- The ratio of the \(M_T = \pm 1\) projected norm to the \(M_T = 0\) norm is less than \(10^{-1}\), and for \(M_T = \pm 2\) less than \(10^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the Fermionic Molecular Dynamics approach by introducing charge-mixing and parity-breaking single-particle degrees of freedom, following ideas from a series of papers by Sugimoto, Ikeda and Toki (2003-2007). It contrasts with Hartree-Fock calculations where the tensor interaction gives zero expectation value for doubly magic nuclei like \(^4\)He. The method enables improved description of long-range tensor correlations within a single Slater determinant basis, but the authors note that the additional correlation energy is small for \(^4\)He and cannot be extrapolated to open-shell nuclei; future work should include variation after angular momentum projection and multi-configuration calculations.
