# The effect of cutoff dependence on heavy quark spin symmetry partners

> Hadron spectroscopy is revealed by observing heavy resonances. Among various explanations of the internal structure of these hadronic states, hadronic molecules play a unique role. For hadronic molecules, which are associated with meson-meson or meson-baryon interactions, the $Λ$ cutoff is a significant factor in determining the composite states' binding energies and overall properties. The cutoff becomes important when it comes to the location of hadronic molecules' masses because it influences the predictions. From this perspective, in the light of cutoff dependency, heavy quark spin partners of near-threshold the $χ_{c0}(3915)$, $χ_{c1}(3872)$, $P_c(4440)$, and $P_c(4457)$ resonances, which are considered as hadronic molecules, are examined.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that while the choice of the cutoff parameter Λ in effective field theories affects the predicted masses of heavy quark spin symmetry partner states, this dependence is absorbed by refitting low-energy constants and does not significantly influence pole positions at leading order, provided the cutoff remains within the EFT breakdown scale.

## Method
The author solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a contact-range potential regularized by a Gaussian cutoff function, using heavy quark spin symmetry to relate coupling constants across different channels. The low-energy constants are fitted to reproduce the experimental masses of χc1(3872) and χc0(3915) for meson-meson systems, and Pc(4440) and Pc(4457) for meson-baryon systems, then the masses of their HQSS partners are predicted for cutoff values ranging from 0.5 to 8.0 GeV.

## Key numbers
- χc1(3872) input mass: 3871.6 MeV; χc0(3915) input mass: 3921.7 MeV
- Pc(4440) input mass: 4440.3 MeV; Pc(4457) input mass: 4457.3 MeV
- Cutoff values tested: 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 4.0, 6.0, and 8.0 GeV
- D*D̅* 0+ partner (χc0(3915)) predicted mass ranges from 3956 MeV (Λ=0.5 GeV) to 3952 MeV (Λ=8.0 GeV), all below the 4017 MeV threshold
- D̅*Σc* 5/2- partner predicted mass ranges from 4524 MeV (Λ=0.5 GeV) to 4520 MeV (Λ=8.0 GeV), below the 4527 MeV threshold
- D̅Σc 1/2- partner (Pc(4312)) predicted mass ranges from 4312 MeV (Λ=0.5 GeV) to 4321 MeV (Λ=8.0 GeV), below the 4322 MeV threshold

## Context
This work extends the hadronic molecule interpretation of near-threshold resonances, following the molecular picture applied to states like χc1(3872) by Tornqvist (1993) and the Pc pentaquarks discovered by LHCb. The study uses heavy quark spin symmetry relations from Falk (1992) and Cho (1994) to predict partner states of χc0(3915), χc1(3872), Pc(4440), and Pc(4457). The results enable future next-to-leading order calculations including one-pion exchange to better determine the cutoff dependence and its limits.
