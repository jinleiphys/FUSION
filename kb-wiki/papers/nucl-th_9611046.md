# Vacuum Nucleon Loops and Naturalness

> Phenomenological studies support the applicability of naturalness and naive dimensional analysis to hadronic effective lagrangians for nuclei. However, one-baryon-loop vacuum contributions in renormalizable models give rise to unnatural coefficients, which indicates that the quantum vacuum is not described adequately. The effective lagrangian framework accommodates a more general characterization of vacuum contributions without reference to a Dirac sea of nucleons.

## Key claim

The one-baryon-loop vacuum contributions in renormalizable hadronic models (the relativistic Hartree approximation, or RHA) produce coefficients that are roughly two orders of magnitude larger than naturalness would require, indicating that the quantum vacuum is not described adequately by a Dirac sea of nucleons. The effective field theory framework accommodates a more general characterization of vacuum contributions without reference to a Dirac sea, and phenomenological fits to nuclear properties support natural coefficients.

## Method

The authors assess the naturalness of RHA vacuum contributions by matching the results from renormalizable models to an effective mean-field theory that excludes Dirac sea degrees of freedom by construction. They apply naive dimensional analysis (NDA) to the effective potential derived from the one-loop Dirac sea effective action, expanding it in powers of the scalar field and comparing the resulting coefficients to the naturalness expectation that dimensionless coefficients should be of order unity. They also examine the linear sigma model extended with a neutral vector meson to compare natural and unnatural coefficients explicitly.

## Key numbers

- The one-baryon-loop contribution to the vacuum energy for the leading $\phi^5$ term is a factor of 96 larger than naturalness requires: $96 \big( M^2 / (5! f_\pi^3) \phi^5 \big)$ compared to the natural estimate $M^2 / (5! f_\pi^3) \phi^5$, using $4\pi f_\pi \approx M$ and $g_s \approx M/f_\pi$.
- In the linear sigma model with RHA, the unnatural corrections to the cubic and quartic couplings are $\Delta\kappa_3 = 2 M^2 / \pi^2 f_\pi^2 \approx 20$ and $\Delta\kappa_4 = -8 M^2 / \pi^2 f_\pi^2 \approx -80$, compared to the natural tree-level values $\kappa_3 = -3$ and $\kappa_4 = -3$.
- In the RHA, the baryon effective mass at equilibrium in nuclear matter is $M^\ast / M \approx 0.73$, compared to $M^\ast / M \approx 0.60$ to $0.66$ in natural models that fit nuclear properties.
- The scalar potential changes from $\Phi = 430$ MeV in the mean-field theory to $\Phi = 250$ MeV in the RHA at equilibrium density (Fermi momentum $k_F^0 = 1.30$ fm$^{-1}$, binding energy 15.75 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear physics, building on the naive dimensional analysis of Georgi and Manohar (1984) and applying it to hadronic lagrangians for nuclei as done by Friar, Madland, and Lynn (1996) and Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1996). It contradicts the conventional relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) to renormalizable quantum hadrodynamics models as formulated by Serot and Walecka (1986), showing that the RHA vacuum contributions produce unnatural coefficients that disagree with phenomenological fits. The paper enables the use of truncated effective lagrangians with natural coefficients as a systematic framework for nuclear structure calculations, avoiding explicit baryon-loop calculations.
