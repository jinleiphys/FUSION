# Radiative Neutron Capture on Carbon-14 in Effective Field Theory

> The cross section for radiative capture of neutron on carbon-14 is calculated using the model-independent formalism of halo effective field theory. The dominant contribution from E1 transition is considered, and the cross section is expressed in terms of elastic scattering parameters of the effective range expansion. Contributions from both resonant and non-resonant interaction are calculated. Significant interference between these leads to a capture contribution that deviates from simple Breit-Wigner resonance form.

# Radiative Neutron Capture on Carbon-14 in Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
The cross section for ¹⁴C(n,γ)¹⁵C is calculated using halo effective field theory, with the dominant E1 transition expressed in terms of elastic scattering parameters from effective range expansion. The significant interference between resonant and non-resonant contributions leads to a capture cross section that deviates from a simple Breit-Wigner resonance form, contrary to what the abstract's mention of "dominant contribution from E1 transition" might suggest about the simplicity of the result.

## Method
The authors construct a halo effective field theory treating the neutron and ¹⁴C core as inert point-like particles, with the ¹⁵C ground state described as a ²S₁/₂ neutron halo bound state. The E1 capture cross section is calculated from initial p-wave continuum states (²P₁/₂ and ²P₃/₂) to the final s-wave ground state, with the photon coupling minimally to the ¹⁴C core charge. The EFT couplings are constrained by matching to effective range expansion parameters, with the ²P₁/₂ channel parameters determined from the known ¹/₂⁻ resonance state of ¹⁵C at E_r ≈ 1.885 MeV, and the ²P₃/₂ parameters estimated from direct capture and Coulomb dissociation data.

## Key numbers
- Neutron separation energy of ¹⁵C ground state: B = 1.218 MeV, corresponding to binding momentum γ = 46.21 MeV
- Resonance energy in ²P₁/₂ channel: E_r ≈ 1.885 MeV (center-of-mass frame)
- Resonance width in ²P₁/₂ channel: Γ_r ≈ 40 keV
- Tree-level (diagram a) contribution from both p-wave channels without effective range correction: approximately 5 μb
- Fine structure constant used: α = 1/137

## Context
This work extends the halo EFT formalism previously applied to one-neutron halo ¹¹Be (Hammer 2011, Phillips 2010) and radiative neutron capture on ⁷Li (Fernando 2011, Rupak 2011) to the ¹⁴C(n,γ)¹⁵C reaction important in the neutron-induced CNO cycle in asymptotic giant branch stars and inhomogeneous Big Bang Nucleosynthesis. The calculation provides a model-independent framework that can be systematically improved, enabling comparison between direct capture measurements (Beer 1992, Reifarth 2005, Reifarth 2008) and Coulomb dissociation data (Nakamura 2003, Nakamura 2009), and can be extended to include contributions from the excited ⁵/₂⁺ state of ¹⁵C and two-body currents.
