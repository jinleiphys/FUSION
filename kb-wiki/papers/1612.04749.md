# Muon bundles as a sign of strangelets from the Universe

> Recently the CERN ALICE experiment, in its dedicated cosmic ray run, observed muon bundles of very high multiplicities, thereby confirming similar findings from the LEP era at CERN (in the CosmoLEP project). Originally it was argued that they apparently stem from the primary cosmic rays with a heavy masses. We propose an alternative possibility arguing that muonic bundles of highest multiplicity are produced by strangelets, hypothetical stable lumps of strange quark matter infiltrating our Universe. We also address the possibility of additionally deducing their directionality which could be of astrophysical interest. Significant evidence for anisotropy of arrival directions of the observed high multiplicity muonic bundles is found. Estimated directionality suggests their possible extragalactic provenance.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the highest-multiplicity muon bundles observed by ALICE and earlier CosmoLEP experiments, which cannot be fully explained by conventional cosmic-ray models even with a pure iron primary component, may instead be produced by strangelets (hypothetical stable lumps of strange quark matter) comprising a minute fraction of the primary cosmic-ray flux.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations using a modified SHOWERSIM code, generating events for primary protons, iron nuclei, and strangelets with masses sampled from an A^{-7.5} distribution. They simulated extensive air showers with zenith angles 0-50 degrees and energies above 10^{14} eV, then propagated muons with energy thresholds of 16 GeV (for ALICE) or 70 GeV (for ALEPH) through the overburden to the detector. The simulated muon multiplicity distributions were compared directly to the ALICE and ALEPH data without arbitrary normalization, using 30.8 days and 20.2 days of effective live time respectively.

## Key numbers

- The abundance of strangelets needed to describe the high-multiplicity data is approximately 2.4 x 10^{-5} of the total primary cosmic-ray flux at the same energy per particle (at A = A_crit = 320).
- The expected flux of strangelets is F_S = 7 x 10^{-6} m^{-2} h^{-1} sr^{-1} for energies above 10 GeV per initial strangelet.
- The five highest-multiplicity ALICE events have muon multiplicities N_mu = 136, 136, 181, 225, and 276, with zenith angles ranging from 2.6 to 40.4 degrees.
- The centroid of the five ALICE events in equatorial coordinates is alpha_centr = 11 h 15 m, delta_centr = 39 degrees 29 arcminutes, with estimated positional errors of approximately 10 degrees.
- The energy density of strangelets is estimated as rho_SQM ~ 2 x 10^{-6} eV/cm^3, comparable to fluctuations in the cosmic microwave background.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Rybczynski et al., 2002, 2004, 2006) that strangelets could explain high-multiplicity muon bundles observed in the CosmoLEP project (ALEPH, DELPHI, L3 experiments), and applies it to the new ALICE cosmic-ray data (ALICE Collaboration, 2015). The paper contradicts the conventional interpretation that the highest-multiplicity events can be described by a pure iron component of primary cosmic rays, noting that extrapolation of the ALICE fits to the highest multiplicities shows a disagreement of about one order of magnitude. The directionality analysis enables future searches for extragalactic sources, with the blazar Mrk 421 identified as a candidate source near the centroid of the events.
