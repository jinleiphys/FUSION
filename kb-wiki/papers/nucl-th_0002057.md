# Radiative Corrections to Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering for Polarized Electrons

> We analyze the radiative correction to high energy elastic electron-proton scattering of polarized electrons. We show that if the approximations inherent in the calculations developed by Tsai and given in the work of Mo and Tsai, which have been used in the analysis of almost all experimental data pertaining to medium and high energy elastic electron scattering for the past three decades, are maintained, then the same radiative correction applies both in the case of initially polarized and unpolarized electrons.

# Radiative Corrections to Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering for Polarized Electrons

## Key claim
If the soft-photon approximations inherent in the calculations of Tsai (1961) and Mo and Tsai (1969) are maintained, then the same radiative correction factor applies to elastic electron-proton scattering for both initially polarized and unpolarized electrons, even when the polarization of the recoil proton is measured.

## Method
The authors analyzed each term contributing to the radiative correction (electron and proton vertex corrections, electron and proton self energies, box and crossed box diagrams, vacuum polarization, and soft bremsstrahlung emission) as treated in the standard Tsai/Mo and Tsai formalism. They demonstrated that under the soft-photon approximation, where integrands are peaked when virtual photons are soft and numerators are evaluated at k=0 or k=q, each higher-order matrix element reduces to the one-photon exchange matrix element multiplied by a spin-independent factor. The cross section including radiative corrections then takes the form dσ_corr = dσ (1 + δ), where δ is independent of particle spins.

## Key numbers
- Radiative corrections to elastic electron-proton scattering are generally of order 20%–30% for four-momentum transfer squared in the range 0.5 to 3.5 (GeV/c)².
- The fine structure constant is taken as α = e²/4π = 1/137.036.
- The analysis assumes high energies where initial and final electron energies satisfy ε₁, ε₃ >> m (electron rest mass) and large momentum transfers where -q² >> m².

## Context
This work extends the standard radiative correction formalism developed by Tsai (1961) and Mo and Tsai (1969), which has been used in the analysis of almost all experimental data on medium and high energy elastic electron scattering for three decades. The paper was motivated by recent Jefferson Lab Hall A experiments (Jones and Jefferson Lab Hall A Collaboration, 2000) using longitudinally polarized electrons scattered from unpolarized protons to measure the ratio G_Ep/G_Mp via recoil proton polarization. The result enables experimentalists to apply the same radiative correction procedures to polarized beam experiments that have been standard for unpolarized measurements, without needing to develop separate spin-dependent correction factors.
