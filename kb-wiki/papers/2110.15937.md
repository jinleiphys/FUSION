# CME search at STAR

> The hot and dense medium produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions has been conjectured to be accompanied by an axial charge asymmetry that may lead to a separation of electric charges in the direction of the extremely strong magnetic field, also known as the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME). The measurement of azimuthal correlator ($Δγ$) with respect to the spectator plane, gives us an opportunity to measure the possible CME fraction beyond the flow background. Preliminary results using this approach with combined Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 200 GeV and U+U at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 193 GeV show $f_{CME}$ at $(8\pm4\pm8)$ $\%$. Meanwhile, the observability of CME has been conjectured to be dependent on $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ due to changes in the lifetime of the magnetic field, the strengths of CME signal and non-CME background. At lower energies, the Event Plane Detector (EPD) installed in the year 2018 provides a unique capability for CME search. The background scenario test at Au+Au $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 27 GeV by using $Δγ$ with respect to TPC and the new installed EPD shows a consistency with no-CME scenario in the current statistics. The method of the ongoing isobar blind analysis, and the latest sensitivity check with the event-by-event AVFD model on the different observables between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports a preliminary combined measurement of the CME signal fraction in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and U+U collisions at 193 GeV, finding a value of (8±4±8)%, but this result carries large statistical and systematic uncertainties and is not yet a definitive observation; at lower energy (27 GeV), the data are consistent with a no-CME background scenario within current statistics.

## Method

The analysis uses the azimuthal correlator Δγ measured with respect to two different event planes: the participant plane reconstructed from charged particles in the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) and the spectator plane reconstructed from spectator neutrons in the Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDC). By assuming the background scales with elliptic flow (v₂) and the CME signal scales inversely with v₂, the four equations relating Δγ and v₂ for the two planes are solved to extract the CME fraction fCME. At lower energy (27 GeV), the newly installed Event Plane Detector (EPD) provides separate first-order (directed flow) and second-order (elliptic flow) event planes, and the ratio Δγ/v₂ is compared across planes to test the background-only hypothesis.

## Key numbers

- Combined fCME for Au+Au at 200 GeV and U+U at 193 GeV in 20%-50% centrality: (8±4±8)% (statistical and systematic uncertainties).
- For Au+Au at 27 GeV, the double ratio of Δγ/v₂ between TPC-EPD-inner and TPC-EPD-outer planes is consistent with unity within uncertainties, showing no significant deviation from the background-only scenario.
- The isobar program (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) collected two billion events per species, with a predicted B-field square difference of 10-18% between the two systems and a flow background difference up to 4%.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing search for the Chiral Magnetic Effect at RHIC, building on theoretical proposals by Kharzeev (1999, 2008) and McLerran (2013) and on the Δγ observable method developed by Haojie (2018) and Sergei (2018). The spectator-plane technique improves background separation compared to earlier participant-plane measurements. The isobar blind analysis, using Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions as proposed by Sergei (2010), aims to achieve a 5σ significance if the CME fraction is at the 14% level, based on model calculations by Weitian (2016), Schenke (2019), Haojie (2018), and Hanlin (2018). The event-by-event AVFD model (AVFD) provides sensitivity checks for the isobar observables. The results enable future definitive tests of CME with the isobar data, which are expected to be released soon by the STAR collaboration.
