# Consistent effective field-theoretic treatment of resonances with non zero width

> A systematic field theoretic formalism for treating Fermionic resonances with non zero width is given. The implication of unitarity to coupling constants of non local interactions is shown. The extension of the formalism to Bosonic resonance field operators is straight forward.

## Key claim
The paper presents a systematic field-theoretic formalism for treating fermionic resonances with nonzero width, where unitarity is preserved by introducing two distinct field operators ("left" and "right" eigen-fields) and allowing coupling constants to take arbitrary complex values, with the complex phases determined by the resonance width.

## Method
The authors construct an effective Lagrangian density using two distinct fermionic field operators \(\bar{\Psi}_L(x)\) and \(\Psi_R(x)\) and their complex conjugates to describe a single resonance degree of freedom, with the complex mass \(M = m_* - i\Gamma/2\). They derive generalized Dirac equations, momentum-space spinors, and Feynman propagators for this formalism, then extend the model to include nucleon and meson fields with a vertex matrix containing all interaction structures. The implications of unitarity on coupling constants are demonstrated by deriving relations between pseudoscalar and pseudovector couplings for specific resonances like the Roper \(P_{11}\) and the \(S_{11}(1535)\).

## Key numbers
- Resonance complex mass defined as \(M = m_* - i\Gamma/2\), where \(m_*\) is the real part and \(\Gamma\) is the resonance width
- For the Roper \(P_{11}\) resonance: pseudovector coupling relation \(\frac{f_{\pi NP^R_{11}}}{m_\pi} = \frac{f_{\pi NP^L_{11}}}{m_\pi} \cdot \frac{M_{P_{11}}+m_N}{M^\ast_{P_{11}}+m_N}\)
- For the \(S_{11}(1535)\) resonance: pseudovector coupling relation \(\frac{f_{\pi NS^R_{11}}}{m_\pi} = \frac{f_{\pi NS^L_{11}}}{m_\pi} \cdot \frac{M_{S_{11}}-m_N}{M^\ast_{S_{11}}-m_N}\)
- Pseudoscalar couplings constrained by \(g_{\pi NP^R_{11}} = g_{\pi NP^L_{11}}\) and \(g_{\pi NS^R_{11}} = g_{\pi NS^L_{11}}\)

## Context
This work extends the standard separable structure of resonance propagators \(G(E) \simeq |\psi\rangle(E-M)^{-1}\langle\psi|\) (as described in reference [1]) into a full effective field theory framework that maintains unitarity. The formalism enables consistent treatment of interference terms in threshold meson production processes at high momentum transfers, particularly proton-induced \(\eta\)-production at threshold where the \(S_{11}(1535)\) resonance dominates. The paper notes that a bosonization procedure applied to QCD might yield only effective degrees of freedom with real self-energies like the nucleon, and raises the open question of how to avoid double counting when resonances generated as S-matrix poles are represented by effective field operators.
