# Warm asymmetric matter in the Quark Meson Coupling Model

> In this work we study the warm equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter in the quark meson coupling model which incorporates explicitly quark degrees of freedom, with quarks coupled to scalar, vector and isovector mesons. Mechanical and chemical instabilities are discussed as a function of density and isospin asymmetry. The binodal section, essential in the study of the liquid-gas phase transition is also constructed and discussed. The main results for the equation of state are compared with two common parametrizations used in the non-linear Walecka model and the differences are outlined.

## Key claim

The Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model predicts a softer equation of state for warm asymmetric nuclear matter than the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) parametrizations NL-3 and TM-1, with the key difference arising from the explicit treatment of quark degrees of freedom which causes the nucleon effective mass to increase with temperature, contrary to the decrease seen in Walecka-type models.

## Method

The authors extended the QMC model to finite temperature and asymmetric nuclear matter by including isovector rho meson fields in addition to isoscalar sigma and omega mesons, with quarks confined in MIT bags coupled to these meson fields. The effective nucleon mass was obtained from the bag energy by minimizing with respect to the bag radius at each temperature and scalar field value, and the thermodynamic grand potential was minimized to determine the meson mean fields. Binodal sections for the liquid-gas phase transition were constructed by imposing Gibbs conditions (equal pressure and chemical potentials) between coexisting phases.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation properties used to fit couplings: binding energy per nucleon \(E_B = -15.7\) MeV at baryon density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), symmetry energy \(a_{sym} = 32.5\) MeV
- Free-space bag radius: \(R_0 = 0.6\) fm; free nucleon mass: \(M = 939\) MeV
- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter (\(y_p = 0.5\)): \(T_c = 17.7\) MeV
- Critical temperature decreases to \(T = 0\) MeV at proton fraction \(y_p = 0.041\)
- For TM-1 parametrization, the critical pressure is 10% larger than the QMC critical pressure; for NL-3, the proton fraction at the critical point is approximately 10% larger than the corresponding QMC value

## Context

This work extends the QMC model to finite temperature and asymmetric matter, building on prior zero-temperature QMC studies by Guichon (1988) and Saito and Thomas (1994), and on finite-temperature QMC work by Song and Su (1996) which introduced temperature only at the hadron level. The results are compared with the NL-3 parametrization by Lalazissis et al. (1997) and the TM-1 parametrization by Sugahara and Toki (1994) of the non-linear Walecka model. The findings enable further studies of liquid-gas phase transitions relevant to multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star crust composition, with extensions to droplet formation currently in progress.
