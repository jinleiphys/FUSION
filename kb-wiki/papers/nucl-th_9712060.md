# Relativistic Study of Medium Polarization Effects on Superfluidity in Nuclear Matter

> Effects of the Fermi-sea polarization on the superfluidity in nuclear matter are studied in a framework of quantum hadrodynamics. The particle-hole polarization due to $σ$ and $ω$ mesons enhances the peak value of the pairing gap contrary to the previous non-relativistic studies.

## Key claim

Contrary to previous non-relativistic studies, which found that particle-hole polarization reduces the pairing gap, the authors show that in a relativistic quantum hadrodynamics framework the Fermi-sea polarization due to sigma and omega mesons enhances the peak value of the pairing gap in neutron matter, although the density range of the superfluid phase is reduced.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dirac-HFB (BCS) equations for infinite neutron matter using a one-boson-exchange (OBE) particle-particle interaction with the QHD-I parameter set, then add one-ring particle-hole polarization diagrams from sigma and omega mesons (including sigma-omega mixing) under an instantaneous approximation (q^0=0). They use the Feynman-density decomposition and the particle-hole-antiparticle decomposition to isolate pure particle-hole effects, and apply a cut-off momentum Lambda = 19 fm^-1 for a full-range calculation.

## Key numbers

- The peak pairing gap from the OBE interaction alone is about three times larger than accepted values in non-relativistic studies.
- The OBE + pure particle-hole polarization enhances the peak value of the pairing gap relative to the OBE-only result, while reducing the density range of the superfluid phase.
- The particle-particle interaction matrix element from polarization, v_pp^(pol)(k,k_F), is shown for Fermi momenta k_F = 0.7, 1.0, and 1.3 fm^-1; it is attractive at low densities and repulsive at high densities due to cancellation between sigma attraction, omega attraction, and sigma-omega mixing repulsion.
- Numerical results converge around a cut-off Lambda approximately 10 fm^-1; the presented results use Lambda = 19 fm^-1.
- Meson parameters used: m_s = 520 MeV, m_v = 783 MeV, g_s = 9.051, g_v = 11.672.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic pairing calculation of Kucharek and Ring (1991), who first solved the gap equation with OBE interactions in QHD and found gaps about three times larger than non-relativistic values. It contrasts with non-relativistic studies such as those based on Babu-Brown's formalism (1973) and later works by Chen et al. (1993) and Wambach et al. (1993), all of which found that polarization reduces the gap. The authors note that further inclusion of other mesons (pi, rho) or Dirac-sea polarization could alter the result, and that a high-momentum cut-off related to nucleon size may be necessary for a fully realistic calculation.
