# Wilson Lines off the Light-cone in TMD PDFs

> Transverse Momentum Dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions (PDFs) also take into account the transverse momentum ($p_T$) of the partons. The $p_T$-integrated analogues can be linked directly to quark and gluon matrix elements using the operator product expansion in QCD, involving operators of definite twist. TMDs also involve operators of higher twist, which are not suppressed by powers of the hard scale, however. Taking into account gauge links that no longer are along the light-cone, one finds that new distribution functions arise. They appear at leading order in the description of azimuthal asymmetries in high-energy scattering processes. In analogy to the collinear operator expansion, we define a universal set of TMDs of definite rank and point out the importance for phenomenology.

## Key claim

Taking gauge links (Wilson lines) off the light-cone in transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions leads to new distribution functions that appear at leading order in azimuthal asymmetries, even though they involve higher-twist operators that are not suppressed by powers of the hard scale.

## Method

The authors analyze color-gauge-invariant definitions of TMD parton distribution functions by writing them as Fourier transforms of nonlocal operator combinations of quark and gluon fields, including gauge links (Wilson lines) that bridge the nonlocality. They extend the analysis beyond light-cone gauge links to generic paths, showing how multi-gluon interactions from multiple correlators become entangled in the color structure, producing symmetrized combinations of Wilson lines that prevent simple factorized expressions with universal correlators.

## Key numbers

- The hard scale satisfies $Q^2 \gg M^2$, where $M^2 \sim 1$ GeV$^2$ is the hadronic mass scale.
- The momentum fraction $x = p \cdot n$ is of order $\mathscr O(1)$, with $p_\st \sim M$ and $n \sim 1/Q$.
- In the Sudakov decomposition, the relation $q_\st \equiv q + x_B P - K/z_h = k_\st - p_\st$ identifies measurable transverse momentum up to order $1/Q$ corrections.
- The expansion of TMD correlators uses symmetric traceless rank $m$ tensors: $p_\st^{\alpha_1\ldots\alpha_m} = p_\st^{\alpha_1}\ldots p_\st^{\alpha_m} - \text{traces}$, with angular representation $p_\st^m(\varphi) = \frac{|p_\st|^m}{2^{m-1}} e^{\pm im\varphi}$.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of TMD parton distribution functions beyond the standard light-cone gauge link treatment, building on prior work by Buffing (2011) on multi-parton correlators and color entanglement. It connects to the operator product expansion approach for collinear PDFs and addresses the gauge link dependence first systematically studied by Bomhof (2006) and collaborators. The results enable phenomenological treatment of azimuthal asymmetries in processes like semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan, where the color entanglement from multiple hadrons produces more complex gauge link structures than the simple staple-like links found in single-hadron processes.
