# Quark mass effects on the topological susceptibility in QCD

> We investigate topology in lattice simulations of QCD with two flavours of dynamical Wilson fermions. At various sea quark masses we find reasonable agreement between results for the topological charge from fermionic and gluonic definitions, the latter with cooling. We study correlations of the topological charge with light hadronic observables and find effects in the flavour singlet pseudoscalar channel. The quark mass dependence of the topological susceptibility, $χ$, is in agreement with the leading order small m_pi expectation, chi=(f_pi m_pi)^2/4.

## Key claim

The topological susceptibility in QCD with two dynamical Wilson fermions is consistent with the leading-order chiral expectation \(\chi = f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2 / 4\) at small pion masses, but the data cannot unambiguously confirm the decrease reported by the UKQCD collaboration (Hart and Teper, 2000) and are also consistent with a mass-independent constant value.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavours of dynamical Wilson fermions at inverse lattice coupling \(\beta = 5.6\) on \(16^3 \times 32\) and \(24^3 \times 40\) lattices at five sea quark masses, corresponding to \(m_\pi/m_\rho\) ratios from 0.834(3) to 0.574(13). The topological charge was measured using both a gluonic definition (clover leaf field strength tensor after 60 cooling sweeps) and a fermionic definition via the Smit-Vink method, \(\tr \gamma_5 M^{-1}\), estimated with \(Z_2\) noisy sources. Topological susceptibilities were extracted from the variance of the charge distribution, and correlations with hadron masses were studied by splitting configurations into low-\(|Q|\) and high-\(|Q|\) subsamples.

## Key numbers

- Topological susceptibility at \(\kappa = 0.1560\) (heaviest quark): \(\chi^{1/4} = 183(4)(9)\) MeV
- Topological susceptibility at \(\kappa = 0.1580\) (lightest quark): \(\chi^{1/4} = 183(17)(9)\) MeV
- Quenched reference susceptibility: \(\chi^{1/4} = 199(9)(10)\) MeV
- Pion decay constant from lattice: \(f_\pi = (0.238 \pm 0.030) r_0^{-1} = (94 \pm 13)\) MeV at \(\kappa = \kappa_c\)
- Autocorrelation time at \(\kappa = 0.1575\) on \(16^3 \times 32\): \(\tau_{\text{int}} = 54(4)\)
- Leutwyler-Smilga parameter at \(\kappa = 0.1575\) on \(24^3 \times 40\): \(x = 26.1 \pm 6.6\)
- Linear fit to \(\chi = f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2 / 4\) excluding heaviest mass: \(f_\pi = 0.255(11) r_0^{-1}\) with \(\chi^2/N_{DF} = 3.93/4\)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of topological susceptibility with dynamical fermions, which had yielded conflicting results: the CP-PACS collaboration (Ali Khan et al., 2000) and the Pisa group (Alles, D'Elia and Di Giacomo, 2000) saw no evidence for the expected chiral decrease, while the UKQCD collaboration (Hart and Teper, 2000) did verify a decrease consistent with theoretical expectations. The present study uses Wilson fermions (rather than improved or staggered actions) and finds consistency with the leading-order chiral prediction but insufficient statistical power to distinguish between a decreasing and a constant susceptibility. The results enable future continuum extrapolations at multiple lattice spacings and simulations at even smaller sea quark masses to resolve the discrepancy.
