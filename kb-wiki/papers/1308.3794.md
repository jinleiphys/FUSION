# Two-photon exchange in electron-trinucleon elastic scattering

> We discuss two-photon exchange (TPE) in elastic electron scattering off the trinucleon systems, 3He and 3H. The calculations are done in the semirelativistic approximation with the trinucleon wave functions obtained with the Paris and CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potentials. An applicability area of the model is wide enough and includes the main part of kinematical domain where experimental data exist. All three TPE amplitudes (generalized form factors) for electron 3He elastic scattering are calculated. We find that the TPE amplitudes are few times more significant in the scattring of electrons off 3He then in the electron proton scattering.

## Key claim

Two-photon exchange (TPE) amplitudes in elastic electron scattering off trinucleon systems (³He and ³H) are calculated to be "few times more significant" than in electron-proton scattering, but the calculation is restricted to the semirelativistic approximation and valid only in the kinematic domain \(0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \ll Q^2 \ll 16 m^2\) (where \(m\) is the nucleon mass), which nonetheless covers the main part of the existing experimental data.

## Method

The TPE amplitude was computed in the Breit frame using a semirelativistic approximation with trinucleon wave functions obtained from the Paris and CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potentials, restricted to five partial waves. Two classes of diagrams were evaluated: type I, where both virtual photons interact with the same nucleon, and type II, where photons interact with different nucleons; nonnucleon degrees of freedom (mesons, isobars) were excluded. The nine-dimensional integrals for type II diagrams were reduced to four-dimensional integrals using an integral representation of the propagator and Fourier transforms of the wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The TPE amplitudes for electron-³He scattering are "few times more significant" than those in electron-proton scattering (no explicit numerical factor given).
- The model applicability condition is \(0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \ll Q^2 \ll 16 m^2\) (with \(m\) the nucleon mass), derived from the estimated value \(\langle\boldsymbol\mu^2\rangle \sim 3\times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) for both Paris and CD-Bonn potentials.
- The trinucleon magnetic moments used are \(\mu(^3\mathrm{He}) \approx -2.127\) and \(\mu(^3\mathrm{H}) \approx 2.978\) in nuclear magnetons.
- Numerical results for the ratios \(\mathrm{Re}\Delta\mathcal G_E(Q^2,\epsilon)/\sqrt{\widetilde\sigma_0^\mathrm{Born}}\), \(\mathrm{Re}\Delta\mathcal G_M(Q^2,\epsilon)/\sqrt{\widetilde\sigma_0^\mathrm{Born}}\), and \(\mathrm{Re}\mathcal F_3(Q^2,\epsilon)/\sqrt{\widetilde\sigma_0^\mathrm{Born}}\) are presented at \(Q^2 = 5, 10, 20, 30, 40\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}\) as functions of \(\epsilon\) (figures in the paper).

## Context

This work extends earlier TPE calculations in elastic electron scattering from protons (Guichon, 2003; Blunden et al., 2003; Chen et al., 2004), pions (Dong and Wang, 2006; Blunden et al., 2005), and deuterons (Kobushkin and Timoshenko, 2008) to the more complex trinucleon systems ³He and ³H. It goes beyond the rough estimations of TPE corrections for ³He by Blunden et al. (2003) (which considered only the elastic intermediate state) and by Boitsov (1975) (which used a multipole scattering model with a simple Gaussian density). The results enable future extraction of trinucleon electromagnetic form factors that properly account for TPE effects, beyond the Coulomb distortion corrections currently included.
