# On large mass $γγ$ and $γ$-meson photoproduction

> Enlarging the set of hard exclusive reactions to be studied in the framework of QCD collinear factorization opens new possibilities to access generalized parton distributions (GPDs). We studied the photoproduction of a large invariant mass photon-photon or photon-meson pair in the generalized Bjorken regime which may be accessible both at JLab and at the EIC.

## Key claim

The photoproduction of a large invariant mass photon-photon or photon-meson pair in the generalized Bjorken regime may be accessible both at JLab and at the EIC, although factorization of GPDs from a perturbatively calculable coefficient function has not yet been proven for these processes.

## Method

The authors calculated the Born-level coefficient functions for the photoproduction of a diphoton pair and a photon-meson pair on nucleon targets, using QCD collinear factorization. For the photon-meson case, they considered both transversely polarized rho mesons (sensitive to chiral-odd transversity GPDs) and pions (sensitive to axial GPDs). The unpolarized differential cross sections and integrated cross sections were computed as functions of invariant mass and energy, using different parametrizations of the relevant GPDs.

## Context

This work extends the study of timelike Compton scattering (TCS) (Berger 2001, Pire 2011, Muller 2012, Moutarde 2013) to the photoproduction of photon-photon and photon-meson pairs, enlarging the set of hard exclusive reactions for accessing GPDs. The diphoton channel selects charge parity-odd GPDs related to valence quark PDFs with no gluon GPD contribution, while the photon-rho channel provides access to chiral-odd transversity GPDs at leading twist, complementing earlier work on transversity GPDs (Ivanov 2002, Enberg 2006, Beiyad 2010, Cosyn 2019). The photon-pion channel tests whether higher twist contributions dominate exclusive amplitudes involving the pion distribution amplitude, as suggested by deep electroproduction of pions. The processes are expected to be measurable at JLab and may also be accessible at the EIC for the photon-pion channel at higher energies.
