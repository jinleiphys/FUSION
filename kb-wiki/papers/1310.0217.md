# Transverse Momentum Distributions at the LHC and Tsallis Thermodynamics

> An overview is presented of transverse momentum distributions of particles at the LHC using the Tsallis distribution. The use of a thermodynamically consistent form of this distribution leads to an excellent description of charged and identified particles. The values of the Tsallis parameter q are truly remarkably consistent.

## Key claim
The Tsallis distribution, when used in a thermodynamically consistent form (with the factor \(m_T\) included in the mid-rapidity expression), provides an excellent description of transverse momentum distributions of charged and identified particles at the LHC, and the extracted values of the Tsallis parameter \(q\) are remarkably consistent across different collision systems and energies, a consistency that is not apparent when using the alternative parametrization commonly employed by experimental collaborations.

## Method
The authors fit the thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution (Eq. 6 in the paper) to transverse momentum spectra of identified particles (pions, kaons, protons, and strange particles) from ALICE at 900 GeV in \(p-p\) collisions, and to charged particle spectra from CMS and ATLAS at various LHC energies, as well as to ALICE data for \(p-Pb\) collisions. For charged particles, a sum of three Tsallis distributions (for \(\pi^+\), \(K^+\), and protons) was used, with relative weights determined by degeneracy factors, and the fit parameters were the temperature \(T\), the Tsallis parameter \(q\), and the volume \(V\).

## Key numbers
- For positive pions in \(p-p\) collisions at 900 GeV (ALICE), the fit used Eq. (6) with \(q\), \(T\), and \(V\) as free parameters; the specific numerical values of these parameters are not given in the truncated text.
- The Tsallis parameter \(q\) is reported as "truly remarkably consistent" across fits to data from ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS at LHC energies, and also for \(p-Pb\) collisions; the actual numerical values of \(q\) are shown in Figure 5 but are not listed in the text.
- The fit for charged particles used a sum of three Tsallis distributions with degeneracy factors \(g_{\pi^+}=1\), \(g_{K^+}=1\), and \(g_{p}=2\), and a factor of 2 in front of the right-hand side to account for antiparticles.

## Context
This work extends the use of the Tsallis distribution to describe transverse momentum spectra at LHC energies, following earlier applications at RHIC by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations, and by the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations at the LHC. The paper specifically contrasts its thermodynamically consistent form (which includes an \(m_T\) factor) with the parametrization used by those collaborations (Eq. 7 in the paper), arguing that the latter breaks \(m_T\) scaling and leads to less consistent values of \(q\). The results enable a unified thermodynamic interpretation of particle production across different collision systems and energies, and the extracted \(q\) and \(T\) parameters can be compared with predictions from coalescence models (e.g., Refs. [coalesce1, coalesce2]) and other Tsallis-based analyses (e.g., Wong, Wibig, Deppman).
