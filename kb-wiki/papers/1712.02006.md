# Effects of ground-state correlations on dipole and quadrupole excitations of $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca

> The effects of ground-state correlations on the dipole and quadrupole excitations are studied for $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca using the extended random phase approximation (ERPA) derived from the time-dependent density-matrix theory. Large effects of the ground-state correlations are found in the fragmentation of the giant quadrupole resonance in $^{40}$Ca and in the low-lying dipole strength in $^{48}$Ca. It is discussed that the former is due to a mixing of different configurations in the ground state and the latter is from the partial occupation of the neutron single-particle states. The dipole and quadrupole strength distributions below 10 MeV calculated in ERPA are in qualitatively agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

Ground-state correlations, included via the extended random phase approximation (ERPA) derived from time-dependent density-matrix theory, produce large effects on the fragmentation of the giant quadrupole resonance in $^{40}$Ca and on the low-lying dipole strength in $^{48}$Ca, but the dipole and quadrupole strength distributions below 10 MeV are only in *qualitative* agreement with experiment, not quantitative.

## Method

The author uses the extended random phase approximation (ERPA), obtained as the small-amplitude limit of the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), which couples equations for the one-body density matrix (occupation probabilities) and the two-body correlation matrix. Ground states are stationary TDDM solutions found via the gradient method using a truncated single-particle basis with the Skyrme III force; excited states are calculated from the ERPA matrix equation including one-body and two-body transition amplitudes, with continuum states discretized in a 15 fm sphere.

## Key numbers

- Occupation probabilities in $^{40}$Ca deviate more than 10% from Hartree-Fock values; e.g., proton $1d_{5/2}$ occupation is 0.923, neutron $1f_{7/2}$ occupation is 0.154.
- Correlation energy $E_c$ is -21.7 MeV for $^{40}$Ca and -17.7 MeV for $^{48}$Ca; total energy gain from ground-state correlations (HF to TDDM) is 1.9 MeV for $^{40}$Ca and 2.2 MeV for $^{48}$Ca.
- In $^{40}$Ca, ERPA gives one dipole state at 8.4 MeV with strength $7.3 \times 10^{-3} e^2$fm$^2$; experimental summed strength between 5 and 10 MeV is $5.3 \pm 0.9 \times 10^{-3} e^2$fm$^2$.
- In $^{48}$Ca, ERPA gives 7 dipole states below 10 MeV with summed strength $103 \times 10^{-3} e^2$fm$^2$; experimental value is $61.5 \pm 7.8 \times 10^{-3} e^2$fm$^2$.
- For quadrupole excitation in $^{40}$Ca, ERPA gives 19 states below 10 MeV with summed strength $166 e^2$fm$^4$; experimental value excluding the first $2^+$ state is $263 \pm 46 e^2$fm$^4$.
- For quadrupole excitation in $^{48}$Ca, ERPA gives 12 states below 10 MeV with summed strength $28.4 e^2$fm$^4$ between 5 and 10 MeV; experimental value is $302 \pm 42 e^2$fm$^4$.
- Reduction factor for residual interaction strength to place spurious center-of-mass mode at zero energy is 0.66 for $^{40}$Ca and 0.69 for $^{48}$Ca.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous ERPA studies of oxygen isotopes (Tohyama, 2007) and $^{16}$O (Tohyama, 2013) to the heavier doubly-closed shell nuclei $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca. It contrasts with standard HF+RPA and second RPA (SRPA) approaches, which neglect ground-state correlations, and with renormalized RPA (Rowe, 1968) and self-consistent RPA (Dukelsky, 1998), which have limited applicability. The results enable comparison with experimental data from Hartmann (2004) and show that the two-body correlation matrix, missing in most extended RPA theories, plays a significant role in collective excitations.
