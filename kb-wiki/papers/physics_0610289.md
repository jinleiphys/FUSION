# New Evaluation of Proton Structure Corrections to Hydrogen Hyperfine Splitting

> We consider the proton structure corrections to hydrogen ground-state hyperfine structure, focusing on a state-of-the-art evaluation of the inelastic nucleon corrections--the polarizability corrections--using analytic fits to the most recent data. We find a value for the fractional correction Delta_{pol} of 1.3 \pm 0.3 ppm. This is 1--2 ppm smaller than the value of Delta_{pol}$ one would deduce using hyperfine splitting data and elastic proton structure corrections obtained from modern form factor fits. In addition, we discuss the derivations of the relevant formulas, paying attention to lepton mass effects and to questions surrounding the use of unsubtracted dispersion relations.

# New Evaluation of Proton Structure Corrections to Hydrogen Hyperfine Splitting

## Key claim

The polarizability correction to hydrogen ground-state hyperfine splitting is evaluated as 1.3 ± 0.3 ppm, which is 1–2 ppm smaller than the value one would deduce using hyperfine splitting data and elastic proton structure corrections obtained from modern form factor fits. This result leaves a persistent deficit of approximately 1.69 ± 0.57 ppm between the total calculated hyperfine splitting and the experimental value.

## Method

The author evaluates the inelastic (polarizability) contribution to the proton structure corrections using analytic fits to spin-dependent structure functions g1 and g2 obtained from electron-proton scattering data, including new data from the EG1 experiment at Jefferson Lab. For the low-Q² region below 0.05 GeV² where no data exist, a low-Q² expansion is used incorporating the forward spin polarizability γ0 and the slope parameter c1 from the integral of g1. The g2 contribution is estimated using the Wandzura-Wilczek relation, with conservative 100% error bars assigned due to the scarcity of g2 data.

## Key numbers

- Δpol = 1.3 ± 0.3 ppm (fractional polarizability correction to hydrogen ground-state hyperfine splitting)
- The experimental hydrogen hyperfine splitting is 1,420.405,751,766.7 (9) MHz (13 significant figures)
- The forward spin polarizability γ0 = [-1.01 ± 0.08 ± 0.10] × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- The slope parameter c1 = 2.95 ± 0.11 GeV⁻⁴
- The total theory deficit after including the new Δpol evaluation is 1.69 ± 0.57 ppm
- The Zemach correction ΔZ = -41.59 ± 0.46 ppm (using Friar and Sick form factor fits)
- The relativistic recoil correction ΔR^p = 5.84 ± 0.15 ppm
- The QED corrections total 1,136.19 ppm
- The weak interaction correction is 0.06 ppm

## Context

This work extends the earlier evaluations of the polarizability correction by Faustov and Martynenko (2002), who obtained 1.4 ± 0.6 ppm using only SLAC data, and by Simula, Osipenko, Ricco, and Taiuti (2002), whose fits would have given 0.4 ± 0.6 ppm. The new evaluation uses improved data from the EG1 experiment at Jefferson Lab, including data below Q² = 0.3 GeV² that were unavailable for the earlier fits. The result enables a clearer assessment of the remaining discrepancy between theory and experiment in hydrogen hyperfine splitting, and provides groundwork for evaluating the muonic hydrogen hyperfine splitting, which may be measured soon at the Paul Scherrer Institute. The author notes that the largest remaining uncertainty now comes from the Zemach radius rather than the polarizability corrections.
