# Computation of effective front form Hamiltonians for massive Abelian gauge theory

> Renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) is applied in terms of a second-order perturbative computation to an Abelian gauge theory, as an example of application worth studying on the way toward derivation of a dynamical connection between the spectroscopy of bound states and their parton-model picture in the front form of Hamiltonian dynamics. In addition to the ultraviolet transverse divergences that are handled using the RGPEP in previously known ways, the small-x divergences are handled by introducing a mass parameter and a third polarization state for gauge bosons using a mechanism analogous to spontaneous breaking of global gauge symmetry, in a special limit that simplifies the theory to Soper's front form of massive QED. The resulting orders of magnitude of scales involved in the dynamics of effective constituents or partons in the simplified theory are identified for the fermion and boson mass counter terms, effective masses and self-interactions, as well as for the Coulomb-like effective interactions in bound states of fermions. Computations in orders higher than second are mentioned but not described in this article.

## Key claim

The RGPEP is applied to an Abelian gauge theory in a second-order perturbative computation, showing that small-x divergences can be handled by introducing a mass parameter and a third polarization state for gauge bosons via a mechanism analogous to spontaneous breaking of global gauge symmetry, in a special limit that simplifies the theory to Soper's front form of massive QED; the paper identifies orders of magnitude of scales for fermion and boson mass counter terms, effective masses, self-interactions, and Coulomb-like effective interactions, but only for the simplified theory and only up to second order in perturbation theory.

## Method

The classical Lagrangian density for an Abelian gauge theory with a complex scalar field is written, and the massive limit is taken where g' goes to 0, v goes to infinity, and g'v = kappa is kept constant, yielding Soper's front form of massive QED. The canonical front-form Hamiltonian is derived from the energy-momentum tensor, quantized on the light front at x^+=0, and the RGPEP differential flow equation is solved perturbatively up to second order in the coupling constant g to compute effective Hamiltonians for effective particles of size s. The RGPEP form factors f_{t c.a} = exp[-t (M_c^2 - M_a^2)^2] suppress invariant mass changes exceeding 1/s, and counter terms are included to remove regularization dependence.

## Key numbers

- The gauge boson mass parameter kappa = g'v is kept constant in the massive limit, with g' going to 0 and v going to infinity
- The scalar field h acquires mass sqrt(2) * mu from the potential V_phi, where mu is the parameter in the scalar potential
- The photon mass is noted to be smaller than 10^{-18} eV/c^2 according to the Particle Data Group
- The RGPEP form factor is f_{t c.a} = exp[-t (M_c^2 - M_a^2)^2], where t = s^4 and s is the size of effective quanta
- The free fermion Hamiltonian term contains (p^{perp 2} + m^2)/p^+ for each fermion mode
- The free boson Hamiltonian terms contain (p^{perp 2} + kappa^2)/p^+ for both transverse gauge bosons and the B field

## Context

This work extends the RGPEP approach previously applied to Abelian gauge theory (Glazek 2019) by explicitly computing second-order effective Hamiltonians and identifying the scales of mass counter terms and interactions. It builds on Soper's front-form massive QED (Soper 1971) and the Stueckelberg formalism (Stueckelberg 1938), and is intended as a step toward deriving a dynamical connection between bound-state spectroscopy and parton-model pictures in front-form Hamiltonian dynamics, with the hope that the approach may eventually be extended to non-Abelian theories.
