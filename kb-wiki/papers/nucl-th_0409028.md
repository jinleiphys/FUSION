# Modern theory of nuclear forces

> Nuclear forces can be systematically derived using effective chiral Lagrangians consistent with the symmetries of QCD. I review the status of the calculations for two- and three-nucleon forces and their applications in few-nucleon systems. I also address issues like the quark mass dependence of the nuclear forces and resonance saturation for four-nucleon operators.

# Modern theory of nuclear forces

## Key claim
Nuclear forces can be systematically derived from effective chiral Lagrangians consistent with QCD symmetries, but the approach requires a non-perturbative treatment because the shallow few-nucleon bound states cause perturbation theory to fail already at low energy. The method works only when Weinberg's power counting is applied to the kernel of the scattering equation (the effective potential), not to the full amplitude.

## Method
The author reviews chiral effective field theory (CHPT) applied to nuclear forces, where the effective Lagrangian is constructed from pion and nucleon fields ordered by a parameter Δ = d + n/2 − 2 (d = derivatives, n = nucleon fields). Two-nucleon potentials are derived at orders from leading (LO) through N³LO, including one-pion exchange, two-pion exchange, three-pion exchange, and contact interactions with up to four derivatives. Three-nucleon forces appear at NNLO from three topologies (two-pion exchange, one-pion exchange, and six-nucleon contact terms). Spectral function regularization is introduced to remove unphysical short-distance attraction in the subleading two-pion exchange.

## Key numbers
- np scattering length in ¹S₀: |a(¹S₀)| ≈ 24 fm, much larger than the pion Compton wavelength 1/Mπ ≈ 1.4 fm
- Deuteron binding momentum: γ = √(Bd md/2) ≈ 45 MeV, much smaller than Mπ ≈ 140 MeV
- LECs from πN system: c₁ = −0.81 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = −4.70 GeV⁻¹ (central values from Paul00)
- Spectral function regularization cutoff: λ = 500 to 800 MeV (or 500 to 700 MeV for N³LO)
- nn scattering length: ann = −18.9 fm (used in N³LO fit)
- N³LO deuteron binding energy: −2.216 to −2.223 MeV (experiment: −2.2246 MeV)
- N³LO asymptotic S-wave normalization AS: 0.882 to 0.883 fm⁻¹/² (experiment: 0.8846(9) fm⁻¹/²)
- N³LO asymptotic D/S ratio η: 0.0254 to 0.0255 (experiment: 0.0256(4))
- NNLO ⁴He binding energy: −29.98 to −29.51 MeV (experimental synthetic value: −29.8 ± 0.1 MeV)

## Context
This review extends the program initiated by Weinberg (1990) and van Kolck (1994) to apply chiral effective field theory to nuclear forces, building on earlier work in pion and pion-nucleon CHPT. The N³LO two-nucleon results from Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2004) improve upon earlier NLO and NNLO calculations and show systematic convergence. The three-nucleon force calculations at NNLO from Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2002) are compared with conventional approaches using the AV18+URBANA IX and CD Bonn+TM' potentials. The resonance saturation analysis extends ideas from the meson sector (Ecker et al., 1989) to four-nucleon operators, showing that LECs from chiral EFT fits can be represented by heavy meson exchanges. This work enables consistent calculations of few-nucleon observables with controlled theoretical uncertainties and provides a framework for extending to external probes and heavier nuclei.
