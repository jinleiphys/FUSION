# Thermodynamics of baryonic matter with strangeness within non-relativistic energy density functional model

> We study the thermodynamical properties of compressed baryonic matter with strangeness within non-relativistic energy density functional models with a particular emphasis on possible phase transitions found earlier for a simple $n,p,e,Λ$-mixture. The aim of the paper is twofold: I) examining the phase structure of the complete system, including the full baryonic octet and II) testing the sensitivity of the results to the model parameters. We find that, associated to the onset of the different hyperonic families, up to three separate strangeness-driven phase transitions may occur. Consequently, a large fraction of the baryonic density domain is covered by phase coexistence with potential relevance for (proto)-neutron star evolution. It is shown that the presence of a phase transition is compatible both with the observational constraint on the maximal neutron star mass, and with the present experimental information on hypernuclei. In particular we show that two solar mass neutron stars are compatible with important hyperon content. Still, the parameter space is too large to give a definitive conclusion of the possible occurrence of a strangeness driven phase transition, and further constraints from multiple-hyperon nuclei and/or hyperon diffusion data are needed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that up to three separate strangeness-driven phase transitions may occur in compressed baryonic matter, associated with the onset of each hyperonic family (Λ, Σ, Ξ), but the parameter space is too large to give a definitive conclusion on the possible occurrence of such transitions, and further constraints from multiple-hyperon nuclei or hyperon diffusion data are needed.

## Method

The authors employed a non-relativistic Skyrme-type energy density functional (the Balberg and Gal BGI parameterization) to compute the thermodynamic properties of baryonic matter containing the full baryonic octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁰, Σ⁻, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) under strangeness equilibrium (μ_S=0). Phase coexistence regions were identified by analyzing convexity anomalies of the free energy, specifically back-bending in the baryonic chemical potential as a function of baryon density, and the phase diagram was mapped for both pure baryonic matter and charge-neutral matter including electrons.

## Key numbers

- The Λ hyperon appears at about 2.6 n₀ (n₀ is normal nuclear saturation density), the Σ hyperons appear starting from about 3.6 n₀, and the Ξ hyperons appear at about 5.5 n₀, for μ_S=0, μ_Q=0 at T=1 MeV using the BGI parameterization.
- The Λ-Λ bond energy from the Nagara event is ΔB_ΛΛ = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV, used as the experimental constraint for the Λ-Λ interaction.
- The Λ potential depth in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation is U_Λ^(N)(n₀) ≈ -30 MeV, with the BGI parameterization giving U_Λ^(N)(n₀) = -26.6 MeV.
- The Ξ potential depth in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation is U_Ξ^(N)(n₀) ≈ -14 MeV.
- The phase coexistence regions for Λ- and Σ-induced transitions extend over baryon densities 0.3 ≲ n_B ≲ 0.6 fm⁻³, with a Ξ-induced transition at higher densities.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Raduta, Gulminelli, and Oertel that considered only (n,Λ) and (n,p,Λ) mixtures (Raduta 2013, Raduta 2014), which found first- and second-order strangeness-driven phase transitions. The present paper generalizes to the full baryonic octet and tests sensitivity to model parameters, addressing limitations of the earlier work that assumed a strongly attractive hyperon-hyperon interaction. The results are relevant for (proto)-neutron star evolution and are compatible with the observational constraint of two solar mass neutron stars (PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432), but the authors note that further experimental constraints from multiple-hyperon nuclei and hyperon diffusion data are needed to resolve the large parameter space uncertainties.
