# Photon vortex generation from nonlinear Compton scattering in Feynman approach

> In the present study, we show calculation of nonlinear Compton scattering with circularly polarized photons in a cylindrical coordinate using Feynman diagram to calculate photon vortex generation in intermediate states considering conservation of angular momentum. We take two different vortex wave functions based on Bessel function for the emitted photon and the electron after emission of the photon, which are the eigenstate of z component of the total angular momentum (zTAM) when a particle propagates along z axis. The result shows that when an electron absorbs N photons a photon vortex with a zTAM = N is predominantly radiated, but there are still very small contributions of photons with a zTAM = (N-1) and (N +1) due to the spin flip of the initial electron. This means even when an electron absorbs only a single photon, the electron may emit a photon vortex of a zTAM of 2 h-bar. However, the numerical calculations show that the contribution of the spin flip is four orders of magnitude smaller than that of the dominant radiation. We also discuss the circular polarization for the generated photon vortices.

# Photon vortex generation from nonlinear Compton scattering in Feynman approach

## Key claim

When an electron absorbs N circularly polarized photons in nonlinear Compton scattering, a photon vortex with z-component of total angular momentum (zTAM) = N is predominantly radiated, but very small contributions with zTAM = (N-1) and (N+1) also exist due to spin flip of the initial electron; even for single-photon absorption, a photon vortex with zTAM = 2ℏ may be emitted, but the spin-flip contribution is four orders of magnitude smaller than the dominant radiation.

## Method

The authors performed a Feynman diagram calculation of nonlinear Compton scattering in cylindrical coordinates using Bessel-function-based vortex wave functions for both the emitted photon and the electron after photon emission, which are eigenstates of the z-component of total angular momentum (zTAM). They assumed an initial electron described by a plane wave absorbing N circularly polarized plane-wave photons in head-on collision, with the electron transitioning to a Bessel wave state after photon emission to conserve zTAM. The production probability was calculated by summing over intermediate states with matrix elements for photon absorption and emission, including contributions from both transverse magnetic (TM) and transverse electric (TE) modes of the photon vortex.

## Key numbers

- Initial photon energy: k = 1.2 eV; electron kinetic energy: 1 GeV
- Photon density: 1.5 × 10^6 nm^{-3}, corresponding to laser intensity of 0.9 × 10^19 W/cm^2
- For N = 2-3 photon absorption, the strength of K = N ± 1 contributions is four orders of magnitude smaller than that of K = N
- Photon densities used in calculations: 0.75 × 10^6, 1.5 × 10^6, and 3 × 10^6 nm^{-3}, corresponding to laser intensities of approximately 0.45 × 10^19, 0.9 × 10^19, and 1.8 × 10^19 W/cm^2, respectively
- Calculations performed for electron kinetic energies of 50 MeV and 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of photon vortex generation from nonlinear Compton scattering, which had been calculated using classical electrodynamics by Taira et al. (2017) and using Furry picture by Ababekri et al. (2024). The Feynman diagram approach allows calculation of each intermediate step with explicit angular momentum conservation, complementing particle-in-cell simulations by Chen et al. (2018), Zhu et al. (2018), and Wang et al. (2020) that predicted total angular momenta up to 10^18ℏ for laser intensities of 10^22 W/cm^2. The results enable understanding of the elementary process of photon vortex generation and suggest that circular polarization measurements near the maximum energy for a fixed K value could be used to observe these vortices, though mixing of different K values at a given energy would produce only weak circular polarization over a wide energy range.
