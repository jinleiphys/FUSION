# Noncongruence of phase transitions in strongly interacting matter

> First-order phase transitions (PTs) with more than one globally conserved charge, so-called noncongruent PTs, have characteristic differences compared to congruent PTs (e.g., dimensionality of phase diagrams and location of critical points and endpoints). Here we discuss the noncongruent features of the QCD PT and compare it with the nuclear liquid-gas (LG) PT, for symmetric and asymmetric matter in heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars. In addition, we have identified a principle difference between the LG and the QCD PT: they have opposite slopes in the pressure-temperature plane.

# Noncongruence of phase transitions in strongly interacting matter

## Key claim
First-order phase transitions with more than one globally conserved charge (noncongruent PTs) have qualitatively different phase diagrams than congruent PTs, and the QCD phase transition and the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition belong to different subclasses of first-order PTs (entropic vs. enthalpic) with opposite slopes in the pressure-temperature plane, contrary to the common impression that they are of the same kind.

## Method
The authors used the FSUgold relativistic mean-field model for nuclear matter and the Chiral SU(3) model for quark-hadron matter, solving thermal, mechanical, and chemical equilibrium conditions under different external constraints. They compared phase diagrams for symmetric and asymmetric matter under conditions relevant to heavy-ion collisions (with baryon number and isospin as conserved charges) and neutron stars (with local vs. global charge neutrality), neglecting Coulomb interactions throughout.

## Key numbers
- For heavy-ion collisions, two representative charge fractions were considered: Y_Q = 0.5 (isospin symmetric matter) and Y_Q = 0.3 (isospin asymmetric matter).
- The QCD PT in neutron stars under global charge neutrality (Y_Q = 0) produces a two-dimensional phase coexistence region in the mu_B-T plane, in contrast to the one-dimensional line under local charge neutrality.
- For the QCD PT around the critical point, noncongruent features are described as "vanishingly small" because high temperatures reduce the importance of conserved charge values.

## Context
This work extends the concept of noncongruent phase transitions, introduced to nuclear and astrophysics by Iosilevskiy (2010), from chemically reacting plasmas to strongly interacting matter. It builds on prior work by Glendenning (1992) on the quark-hadron transition in neutron stars and by Muller (1995, 1997) on isospin effects in heavy-ion collisions. The identification of the QCD PT as an entropic PT with negative latent heat, as opposed to the enthalpic liquid-gas PT, was previously noted by Iosilevskiy (2014, 2015) and has implications for proto-neutron star convection (Yudin 2015) and core-collapse supernova explosions (Hempel 2015).
