# Proton 3D reconstruction with T-odd TMD gluon densities

> We present studies aimed at probing the 3D gluon content of the proton through spin-dependent TMD gluon densities, computed by means of the spectator-model approach. Our formalism incorporates a fit-based modulation function for the spectator mass, designed to capture longitudinal-momentum effects across a broad kinematic range. Special emphasis is placed on the time-reversal even Boer-Mulders and the time-reversal odd Sivers functions. Accurate understanding of these functions is crucial for conducting precise 3D analyses of nucleons, highlighting the importance of collaborative efforts between the LHC and EIC Communities.

## Key claim

The paper presents a spectator-model calculation of the T-even gluon Boer-Mulders function and a preliminary analysis of the f-type T-odd gluon Sivers TMD PDF, where the T-odd densities acquire process dependence through one-gluon exchange in the eikonal approximation, but the results remain preliminary for the Sivers function.

## Method

The authors use an improved spectator-model approach where the proton remnant is treated as a single on-shell spin-1/2 spectator particle with mass M_X, and the nucleon-gluon-spectator vertex incorporates two dipolar form factors. A fit-based spectral modulation function for the spectator mass, weighted over a continuous range, is used to capture both small- and moderate-x dynamics, with parameters fixed by simultaneously fitting the |p_T|-integrated unpolarized and helicity gluon TMD PDFs to NNPDF collinear densities at Q_0 = 1.64 GeV. T-odd functions are constructed by superseding the tree-level approximation and including one-gluon exchange in the eikonal approximation to represent the first-order truncation of the full gauge-link operator.

## Key numbers

- The initial energy scale for the fit is Q_0 = 1.64 GeV
- The Boer-Mulders function is shown at x = 10^{-3}
- The |p_T|-weighted [+,+] Sivers function is shown at x = 10^{-1}
- The Sivers function peaks in the range p_T^2 less than or approximately 0.1 GeV^2 before exhibiting a larger, flattening tail

## Context

This work extends the spectator-model formalism previously applied to quark TMD PDFs by Bacchetta et al. (2008, 2010) and to T-even gluon TMDs by Bacchetta et al. (2020), providing the first spectator-model calculation of T-odd gluon TMDs. The results enable future phenomenological studies of transverse single-spin asymmetries at the Electron-Ion Collider and LHCspin, and the Boer-Mulders function can be accessed even in unpolarized collisions at the LHC, offering information about linearly polarized gluon distributions inside unpolarized protons.
