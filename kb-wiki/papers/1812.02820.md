# Initial transverse-momentum broadening of Breit-Wheeler process in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We calculate the cross section and transverse-momentum ($P_{\bot}$) distribution of the Breit-Wheeler process in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and their dependence on collision impact parameter ($b$). To accomplish this, the Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA) was generalized in a more differential way compared to the approach traditionally used for inclusive collisions. In addition, a lowest-order QED calculation with straightline assumption was performed as a standard baseline for comparison. The cross section as a function of $b$ is consistent with previous calculations using the equivalent one-photon distribution function. Most importantly, the $P_{\bot}$ shape from this model is strongly dependent on impact parameter and can quantitatively explain the $P_{\bot}$ broadening observed recently by RHIC and LHC experiments. This broadening effect from the initial QED field strength should be considered in studying possible trapped magnetic field and multiple scattering in a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). The impact-parameter sensitive observable also provides a controllable tool for studying extreme electromagnetic fields.

## Key claim

The observed transverse-momentum broadening of lepton pairs from the Breit-Wheeler process in hadronic heavy-ion collisions originates predominantly from the initial electromagnetic field strength that varies significantly with impact parameter, rather than solely from final-state effects such as trapped magnetic fields or multiple scattering in a Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Method

The authors generalized the Equivalent Photon Approximation (gEPA1) in a more differential way than the traditional approach used for inclusive collisions, retaining impact-parameter dependence in the cross-section calculation. They also performed a lowest-order QED calculation based on two Feynman diagrams with a straight-line assumption for the nuclei, extending it to all impact parameters. An empirical modification (gEPA2) was introduced by adding a phase term to the EPA formula to account for interference effects missing in the standard EPA approach.

## Key numbers

- The $\sqrt{\langle P_{\bot}^{2} \rangle}$ from STAR data in 60-80% Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} =$ 200 GeV requires an additional $\simeq40$ MeV/$c$ broadening compared to the STARLight result.
- The QED calculation in 60-80% centrality requires $\simeq30$ MeV/$c$ additional broadening compared to its ultra-peripheral collision (UPC) result.
- The $\sqrt{\langle P_{\bot}^{2} \rangle}$ from STAR data in 60-80% central collisions is about 15 MeV/$c$ broader than the QED calculation at the same centrality.
- The Woods-Saxon radius parameters used were 6.38 fm for Au and 6.62 fm for Pb, with skin depths of 0.535 fm and 0.546 fm respectively.
- The p+p inelastic cross sections employed were 42 mb for RHIC and 70 mb for LHC at their corresponding collision energies.

## Context

This work extends the external classical field approach of Baur and Hencken (1993) and the Equivalent Photon Approximation traditionally used in STARLight (Klein, 2016) to include impact-parameter dependence. It provides a baseline for extracting possible medium effects in the Breit-Wheeler process observed by STAR (2018) and ATLAS (2018) in hadronic heavy-ion collisions, and enables future precision measurements in ultra-peripheral collisions to validate the QED calculation and isolate any final-state broadening from the Quark-Gluon Plasma.
