# Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance in Spherical Nuclei as a Nuclear Matter Incompressibility Indicator

> The incompressibility of both nuclear matter and finite nuclei is estimated by the monopole compression modes in nuclei in the framework of a nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliyubov method and the coherent density fluctuation model. The monopole states originate from vibrations of the nuclear density. The calculations in the model for the incompressibility in finite nuclei are based on the Brueckner energy-density functional for nuclear matter. Results for the energies of the breathing vibrational states and finite nuclei incompressibilities are obtained for various nuclei and their values are compared with recent experimental data. The evolution of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) along Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains is discussed. This approach can be applied to analyses of neutron stars properties, such as incompressibility, symmetry energy, slope parameter, and other astrophysical quantities, as well as for modelling dynamical behaviors within stellar environments.

## Key claim
The incompressibility of nuclear matter and finite nuclei can be estimated from isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) data using a nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method combined with the coherent density fluctuation model, but the accuracy of this estimate depends on the specific energy-density functional (here, the Brueckner functional) and the isotopic chain studied.

## Method
The authors performed nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations to obtain ground-state densities for spherical nuclei. They then applied the coherent density fluctuation model, which uses these densities as input to the Brueckner energy-density functional for nuclear matter, to compute the energies of the breathing mode (ISGMR) and the associated finite-nucleus incompressibility. Results were obtained for several nuclei across the Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains and compared with recent experimental data.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nuclear incompressibility using the coherent density fluctuation model, which has been applied by Antonov and collaborators in earlier years. It provides a nonrelativistic framework for linking the ISGMR in finite nuclei to the equation of state of nuclear matter, a connection that is crucial for understanding neutron star properties.
