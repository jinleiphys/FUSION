# Neutron pick-up pion transfer reactions for the formation of deeply bound pionic atoms in $^{208}$Pb

> We study $(n,d)$ and (d,$^3$He) reactions on $^{208}Pb$ leading to deeply bound pionic atoms with one neutron hole being left. We develop a theoretical model to calculate cross sections of deeply bound pionic atoms and quasi-elastic pion production. The results are compared with data which have been obtained so far. We show that the theoretical model describes the data of both reactions well.

## Key claim
The theoretical model, based on the effective number approach, describes the measured (n,d) and (d,³He) reaction data on ²⁰⁸Pb well, but only after applying a normalization factor of 1.7 to the (n,d) calculations and assuming a flat non-pionic background.

## Method
The authors developed a theoretical model using the effective number approach to calculate cross sections for (n,d) and (d,³He) reactions leading to deeply bound pionic atoms and quasi-elastic pion production. The model incorporates pion wave functions from solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a realistic optical potential, neutron hole wave functions from a harmonic oscillator, and distorted waves treated in the eikonal approximation. The elementary cross sections were taken from (nn→dπ) and (dp→tπ⁺) reactions using charge conjugation.

## Key numbers
- For (n,d) reactions on ²⁰⁸Pb at T_n = 400 MeV, a normalization factor N = 1.7 was applied to the calculated results, with a flat background of 0.28 mb/sr/MeV assumed for non-pionic contributions.
- The (n,d) experiment had an energy resolution of about 1 MeV FWHM.
- For (d,³He) reactions on ²⁰⁸Pb at T_d = 600 MeV, a flat background of 20 μb/sr/MeV was assumed in the theoretical spectra.
- The (d,³He) spectra were calculated with 500 keV resolution for comparison with experiment and 200 keV resolution for predicting finer structure.
- The peak seen experimentally in (d,³He) at Q = -136 MeV corresponds to about 5 MeV binding energy, arising from [p ⊗ p_{1/2}⁻¹] and [p ⊗ p_{3/2}⁻¹] configurations with a cross section ratio of 2 (p_{3/2}⁻¹ to p_{1/2}⁻¹) due to neutron occupancy.
- The neutral pion threshold lies 2.7 MeV below the negative pion threshold in the (n,d) reaction.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical predictions of Toki and Yamazaki (1988, 1989) for forming deeply bound pionic atoms via direct reactions, following earlier failed attempts with (n,p) reactions at TRIUMF by Iwasaki et al. (1991) and (d,²He) reactions at SATURNE by Hayano et al. (1991). The model successfully describes the recent (d,³He) experiment at GSI by Yamazaki et al. (1996), which found a clear peak structure from deeply bound pionic atom formation. This enables systematic studies of pionic atom structure, including configuration mixing effects, nuclear deformation, and extension to other target nuclei and mesons such as η and ω.
