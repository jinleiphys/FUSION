# Approaching the conformal limit of quark matter with different chemical potentials

> We study in detail the influence of different chemical potentials (baryon, charged, strange, and neutrino) on how and how fast a free gas of quarks in the zero-temperature limit reaches the conformal limit. We discuss the influence of non-zero masses, the inclusion of leptons, and different constraints, such as charge neutrality, zero-net strangeness, and fixed lepton fraction. We also investigate for the first time how the symmetry energy of the system under some of these conditions approaches the conformal limit. Finally, we briefly discuss what kind of corrections are expected from perturbative QCD as one goes away from the conformal limit.

## Key claim

The paper systematically quantifies how the approach to the conformal limit (a free gas of massless quarks) depends on the specific combination of chemical potentials and constraints applied, showing that the baryon chemical potential at which the pressure deviates by less than 10% from the conformal limit can range from 77 MeV to 48,897 MeV depending on the chosen conditions.

## Method

The authors model quark matter as a free Fermi gas at zero temperature, varying the number of flavors (1, 2, or 3) and using realistic quark masses from the Particle Data Group (up: 2.3 MeV, down: 4.8 MeV, strange: 95 MeV). They compute thermodynamic quantities (pressure, baryon density, symmetry energy) under different combinations of baryon, electric charge, strange, and neutrino chemical potentials, imposing constraints such as charge neutrality, zero-net strangeness, fixed lepton fraction, and beta equilibrium with leptons.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark mass affects the density until a baryon chemical potential of 621 MeV and the pressure until 834 MeV (using a 10% deviation criterion from the massless conformal limit).
- The light quark masses (up and down) affect the density until 55 MeV and the pressure until 77 MeV.
- For 2-flavor quark matter with charge neutrality, the strange quark mass affects pressure until 839 MeV and light quark masses until 118 MeV.
- For 3-flavor quark matter with fixed mu_Q = -100 MeV and realistic masses, the 10% deviation from the conformal limit occurs at mu_B = 415 MeV (from above).
- For 3-flavor quark matter with mu_S = 100 MeV and mu_Q = 0, the 10% deviation occurs at mu_B = 4227 MeV.
- For 3-flavor quark matter with mu_S = mu_Q = 100 MeV, the 10% deviation occurs at mu_B = 4723 MeV.
- For large negative mu_Q (-381 MeV) and mu_S = 0, the pressure differs from the conformal limit by more than 10% until mu_B = 10,583 MeV.
- For large mu_S (1000 MeV) with mu_Q = 0, the pressure differs from the conformal limit by more than 10% until mu_B = 44,237 MeV.
- For combined large mu_S and mu_Q, the pressure differs from the conformal limit by more than 10% until mu_B = 48,897 MeV.
- The symmetry energy of 2-flavor quark matter is a monotonically increasing function of density and is independent of light quark masses.

## Context

This work extends studies of the conformal limit in dense quark matter by systematically isolating the effects of multiple chemical potentials and constraints relevant to different astrophysical and laboratory scenarios. It complements perturbative QCD calculations (Freedman 1977, Fraga 2001, Kurkela 2009, Gorda 2021) which show that interactions reduce pressure below the free gas value, by quantifying how much the free gas baseline itself varies with chemical potential conditions. The results enable more careful comparisons between theoretical predictions and observational constraints from neutron stars and neutron-star mergers, and highlight that statements about "the" conformal limit require specifying which chemical potentials are fixed or constrained.
