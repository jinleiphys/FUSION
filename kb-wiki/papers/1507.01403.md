# Appraising nuclear octupole moment contributions to the hyperfine structures in $^{211}$Fr

> Hyperfine structures of $^{211}$Fr due to the interactions of magnetic dipole ($μ$), electric quadrupole ($Q$) and magnetic octupole ($Ω$) moments with the electrons are investigated using the relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory with an approximation of singles, doubles and important valence triples excitations in the perturbative approach. Validity of our calculations are substantiated by comparing the results with their available experimental values. Its $Q$ value has also been elevated by combining the measured hyperfine structure constant of the $7p \ ^2P_{3/2}$ state with our improved calculation. Considering the preliminary value of $Ω$ from the nuclear shell-model, its contributions to the hyperfine structures up to the $7d \ ^2D_{5/2}$ low-lying states in $^{211}$Fr are estimated. Energy splittings of the hyperfine transitions in many states have been assessed to find out suitability to carry out their precise measurements so that $Ω$ of $^{211}$Fr can be inferred from them unambiguously.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that the magnetic octupole (M3) contribution to hyperfine splittings in $^{211}$Fr is at least four orders of magnitude smaller than the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole contributions, meaning that hyperfine splittings must be measured to fifth decimal place accuracy to infer the nuclear octupole moment $\Omega$ from these measurements, assuming the preliminary shell-model value of $\Omega_I = 0.229$ b is approximately correct.

## Method

The author applied the relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory truncated to singles, doubles, and important valence triple excitations treated perturbatively (CCSD$_{\text{t3}}$ method) to calculate hyperfine structure constants $A$, $B$, and $C$ for low-lying states of $^{211}$Fr up to the $7d \ ^2D_{5/2}$ state. The nuclear quadrupole moment $Q$ was revised by combining the calculated $B/Q$ ratio (259.73 MHz/b) with the experimentally measured $B$ value of the $7p \ ^2P_{3/2}$ state from Liberman et al. The nuclear octupole moment $\Omega$ was taken from the nuclear shell-model, and its contributions to hyperfine splittings were evaluated using the calculated $C/\Omega$ values.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear magnetic moment $\mu_I = 4.00(8)$ $\mu_N$ for $^{211}$Fr, from Ekstrom et al. using atomic-beam magnetic resonance.
- Revised nuclear quadrupole moment $Q = -0.21(2)$ b for $^{211}$Fr, obtained by combining the CCSD$_{\text{t3}}$ $B/Q$ value of 259.73 MHz/b with the measured $B = -55.3(3.4)$ MHz of the $7p \ ^2P_{3/2}$ state from Liberman et al.
- Nuclear shell-model value for the octupole moment $\Omega_I = 0.229$ b for $^{211}$Fr.
- Calculated $A$ value for the $7s \ ^2S_{1/2}$ state: 8786.20 MHz (CCSD$_{\text{t3}}$), compared to experimental value 8713.9(8) MHz from Coc et al.
- Calculated $B$ value for the $7p \ ^2P_{3/2}$ state: $-54.54$ MHz (CCSD$_{\text{t3}}$), compared to experimental value $-55.3(3.4)$ MHz from Liberman et al.
- Calculated $C$ value for the $7p \ ^2P_{3/2}$ state: $11.0 \times 10^{-4}$ MHz (CCSD$_{\text{t3}}$) using the shell-model $\Omega_I = 0.229$ b.
- Calculated $C$ value for the $6d \ ^2D_{5/2}$ state: $-3.26 \times 10^{-4}$ MHz (CCSD$_{\text{t3}}$) using the shell-model $\Omega_I = 0.229$ b.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous relativistic coupled-cluster calculations of hyperfine constants for $^{210}$Fr and $^{212}$Fr (Sahoo, 2015) to the isotope $^{211}$Fr, and revises the nuclear quadrupole moment value previously estimated by Heully and Martensson-Pendrill (using a lower-order relativistic many-body method) and by Coc et al. The paper provides the first theoretical estimates of magnetic octupole contributions to hyperfine splittings in $^{211}$Fr, enabling future experimental measurements to extract the nuclear octupole moment $\Omega$ from hyperfine transitions, specifically identifying the $F=6$ to $F=3$ transition in $np \ ^2P_{3/2}$ states and the $F=7$ to $F=2$ transition in $nd \ ^2D_{5/2}$ states as most suitable for unambiguous extraction.
