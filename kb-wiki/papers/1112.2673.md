# Nucleosynthesis of Nickel-56 from Gamma-Ray Burst Accretion Disks

> We examine the prospects for producing Nickel-56 from black hole accretion disks, by examining a range of steady state disk models. We focus on relatively slowly accreting disks in the range of 0.05 - 1 solar masses per second, as are thought to be appropriate for the central engines of long-duration gamma-ray bursts. We find that significant amounts of Nickel-56 are produced over a wide range of parameter space. We discuss the influence of entropy, outflow timescale and initial disk position on mass fraction of Nickel-56 which is produced. We keep careful track of the weak interactions to ensure reliable calculations of the electron fraction, and discuss the role of the neutrinos.

# Nucleosynthesis of Nickel-56 from Gamma-Ray Burst Accretion Disks

## Key claim

Significant amounts of Nickel-56 can be produced in outflows from black hole accretion disks with mass accretion rates between 0.05 and 1 solar masses per second, but only under specific combinations of outflow entropy, acceleration timescale, and starting disk position; the production is optimized when the outflow entropy is modestly increased above in-disk values (around s/k ~ 30 for disks with accretion rates above 0.2 solar masses per second) and when the outflow accelerates rapidly at early times but slowly during nuclear reassembly.

## Method

The authors used one-dimensional, steady-state black hole accretion disk models from Chen and Beloborodov (2007) with mass accretion rates from 0.05 to 1 solar masses per second, calculated the disk composition and neutrino fluxes as a post-processing step, then followed adiabatic outflow trajectories parameterized by a velocity profile with variable acceleration parameter beta (0.2 to 2.6), starting disk positions of 50 km and 100 km, and entropies from 10 to 100 k per baryon. The nuclear recombination in the outflow was tracked using a full nuclear network code, with careful accounting of weak interactions including neutrino captures.

## Key numbers

- Mass accretion rate range studied: 0.05 to 1 solar masses per second (specifically 0.05, 0.1, 0.15, 0.2, 0.5, and 1 solar masses per second)
- Outflow velocity asymptotic value: 10,000 km/s
- Starting disk positions investigated: 50 km and 100 km from the black hole
- Entropy range explored: 10 to 100 k per baryon
- Wind parameter beta range: 0.2 to 2.6
- For a disk with accretion rate 0.15 solar masses per second, neutrino heating changes entropy by 1 to 23 k per baryon; for a disk with accretion rate 1 solar mass per second, the range is 4 to 58 k per baryon
- Under optimal conditions, up to approximately 50% of the ejected material becomes Nickel-56 (mass fraction in excess of 0.5)
- For the spherical outflow model with accretion rate 0.5 solar masses per second, starting radius 50 km, entropy 40 k per baryon, the final mass fraction of Nickel-56 is approximately 0.4 when neutrino interactions are included, compared to approximately 0.5 when neutrino interactions are neglected

## Context

This work extends preliminary studies by Surman et al. (2005, 2006) and Pruet et al. (2004, 2005) on nucleosynthesis in GRB disk outflows, using the improved relativistic disk models of Chen and Beloborodov (2007) that include neutrino trapping and evolving neutron-to-proton ratios. The results provide a reference for interpreting observed Nickel-56 masses in GRB-associated supernovae such as SN2003dh (estimated 0.5 solar masses of Nickel-56), and can be used to constrain GRB central engine conditions when coupled with future wind model calculations and observations. The paper also identifies production sites for light p-process nuclei like Molybdenum-92 via the nu-p process, as discussed by Fröhlich et al. (2006) and Pruet et al. (2006), and notes that low-entropy trajectories can overproduce N=50 closed shell nuclei such as Strontium-88, Yttrium-89, and Zirconium-90 by factors exceeding 100,000 times solar.
