# Potential NRQED: The Positronium Case

> We discuss in detail potential NRQED (pNRQED), a previously proposed effective field theory for ultrasoft photons. The pNRQED lagrangian for the equal mass case is presented and it is shown that it correctly reproduces the positronium spectrum at order $mα^5$. The pNRQED lagrangian for the unequal mass case is also presented at the same order. Dimensional regularization is used throughout.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that potential NRQED (pNRQED), an effective field theory previously proposed for ultrasoft photons, correctly reproduces the positronium spectrum at order \(m\alpha^5\), including contributions from all momentum regions (hard, soft, and ultrasoft). The authors hedge that the matching procedure requires off-shell Green functions (not on-shell as in QED-to-NRQED matching) because bound-state particles are typically off-shell.

## Method
The authors match NRQED to pNRQED by equating two- and four-fermion Green functions with arbitrary numbers of ultrasoft photon legs, expanding about zero external fermion energy and ultrasoft photon energy and momentum. They use static (HQET) propagators for fermions and dimensional regularization throughout, integrating out fermions and photons with momenta of order \(m\alpha\) (soft) and photons with energies of order \(m\alpha^2\) and momenta of order \(m\alpha\) (potential). The resulting pNRQED Lagrangian contains local time-derivative terms and non-local spatial potential terms, and is projected onto the one-electron-one-positron subspace using a wave-function field with multipole-expanded ultrasoft photon fields about the center of mass.

## Key numbers
- The pNRQED Lagrangian reproduces the positronium spectrum at order \(m\alpha^5\), where \(\alpha\) is the fine-structure constant and \(m\) is the electron mass.
- The leading Coulomb potential is \(V^{(0)} = -\alpha / |\mathbf{x}|\).
- The one-loop matching contribution to the potential includes terms proportional to \(\alpha^2 / m^2\) with logarithmic factors \(\log(\mathbf{k}^2 / \mu^2)\).
- The ultrasoft photon contribution to the energy shift is \(\delta^{US} E_n = -\frac{8}{3} \frac{\alpha}{\pi} \sum_m |\langle n | \mathbf{p}/m | m \rangle|^2 (E_n - E_m) \left( \log(\mu / |E_n - E_m|) - \log 2 + 5/6 \right)\).
- The final energy shift \(\delta E_{n,l,j}\) at order \(m\alpha^5\) contains terms proportional to \(m\alpha^4 / 8\) multiplied by combinations of \(1/n^3\), \(1/n^4\), and \(\alpha/\pi\) corrections, including the Bethe logarithm \(\log R(n,l) = \log(2\langle E_{n,l}\rangle / m\alpha^2)\).
- The decay width at lowest order is \(\Gamma_n = \frac{m\alpha^5}{2 n^3} \delta_{l0}(1-\delta_{s1}) + \sum_{m<n} \frac{16}{3} \alpha |\langle n | \mathbf{p}/m | m \rangle|^2 (E_n - E_m)\).

## Context
This work extends the pNRQED formalism previously introduced by the same authors (Pineda and Soto, 1998) for positronium, following earlier development of NRQED by Lepage (1983) and others. It provides a systematic framework for bound-state calculations in QED that separates scales (hard, soft, ultrasoft) using two effective field theories, enabling efficient computation of higher-order corrections. The approach enables future calculations at order \(m\alpha^6\) by extending the matching to two loops in QED-to-NRQED matching and including higher-order terms in the NRQED Lagrangian, with the authors noting that no new ultrasoft photon contributions arise at that order due to angular momentum conservation.
