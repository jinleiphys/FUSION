# Lattice evidence that scalar glueballs are small

> This work reports the first calculation of the gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the scalar glueball, performed via lattice field theory in Yang-Mills theory at a single lattice spacing. The glueball GFFs are compared with those of other hadrons as determined in previous lattice calculations, providing strong indications that glueballs have a different gluonic structure than typical hadronic states. A mass radius of 0.263(31) fm is predicted, supporting previous suggestions that the scalar glueball is significantly smaller than other hadrons. These results point towards a potential smoking-gun characteristic to target by experimental glueball searches.

## Key claim

The scalar glueball's gravitational form factors (GFFs) decay more slowly than those of other hadrons, indicating a smaller mass radius of 0.263(31) fm; however, this result is obtained in pure Yang-Mills theory at a single lattice spacing, and the authors note that confirmation in full QCD is needed before this can serve as a definitive "smoking gun" for experimental glueball identification.

## Method

The authors performed lattice field theory calculations in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory using a single ensemble with the Wilson gauge action at β=5.95, corresponding to lattice spacing a=0.098 fm on a 24³×48 lattice. They computed two-point and three-point correlation functions using GEVP-optimized interpolating operators for the scalar glueball, extracted the gravitational form factors A(t) and D(t) from matrix elements of the gluon energy-momentum tensor, and determined the Breit-frame mass radius from the slope of A(t) at t=0.

## Key numbers

- Mass radius of the scalar glueball in the Breit frame: 0.263(31) fm
- Lattice spacing: a=0.098 fm (set using the Sommer parameter)
- Lattice volume: 24³×48
- Gauge coupling: β=5.95
- Momentum transfers analyzed: |Δ|² ≤ 10 (2π/L)² and |p'|² ≤ 6 (2π/L)²
- Number of configurations: O(10⁷), generated from O(10⁵) independent streams

## Context

This work provides the first lattice calculation of glueball gravitational form factors, extending earlier model-dependent Bethe-Salpeter studies (PhysRevD.43.2301, PhysRevLett.69.245, Loan 2006) and SU(2) Yang-Mills investigations (Tickle 1989) that estimated the scalar glueball size in the range 0.1-0.3 fm. The results are compared with gluon GFFs of the pion, ρ meson, nucleon, and Δ baryon from Pefkou (2021) and with total GFFs of the pion and nucleon from Hackett (2023), showing the glueball is more compact than these states. Future work could extend this analysis to other glueball quantum numbers (pseudoscalar, tensor) and to full QCD with dynamical quarks.
