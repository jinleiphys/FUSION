# Excitation function of elastic $pp$ scattering from a unitarily extended Bialas-Bzdak model

> The Bialas-Bzdak model of elastic proton-proton scattering assumes a purely imaginary forward scattering amplitude, which consequently vanishes at the diffractive minima. We extended the model to arbitrarily large real parts in a way that constraints from unitarity are satisfied. The resulting model is able to describe elastic $pp$ scattering not only at the lower ISR energies but also at $\sqrt{s}=$7~TeV in a statistically acceptable manner, both in the diffractive cone and in the region of the first diffractive minimum. The total cross-section as well as the differential cross-section of elastic proton-proton scattering is predicted for the future LHC energies of $\sqrt{s}=$13, 14, 15~TeV and also to 28~TeV. A non-trivial, significantly non-exponential feature of the differential cross-section of elastic proton-proton scattering is analyzed and the excitation function of the non-exponential behavior is predicted. The excitation function of the shadow profiles is discussed and related to saturation at small impact parameters.

## Key claim

The Bialas-Bzdak model is extended to include arbitrarily large real parts of the forward scattering amplitude while satisfying unitarity constraints, enabling statistically acceptable descriptions of elastic proton-proton scattering at both ISR energies and at LHC energy of 7 TeV, but only when the two TOTEM data sets (diffractive cone and dip region) are fitted separately rather than simultaneously.

## Method

The authors start from S-matrix unitarity in impact parameter space and derive a real extended Bialas-Bzdak (ReBB) model where the imaginary part of the opacity function is taken proportional to the inelastic scattering probability per unit area. The proton is described as a quark-diquark system with Gaussian distributions for the constituent positions and Gaussian profiles for binary inelastic collisions between quarks and diquarks, with the diquark assumed to be very weakly bound (mass ratio lambda = 0.5) and head-on quark-quark collisions assumed completely inelastic (A_qq = 1). The model is fitted to elastic differential cross-section data at ISR energies (23.5, 30.7, 52.8, 62.5 GeV) and at LHC energy of 7 TeV, using four free parameters: three scale parameters R_q, R_d, R_qd and the shape parameter alpha.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the fit to the diffractive cone region (|t| < 0.375 GeV^2) yields chi^2/NDF = 109.9/81 with CL = 1.8%
- At sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the fit to the dip region (0.375 < |t| < 2.5 GeV^2) yields chi^2/NDF = 120.4/73 with CL = 0.04%
- The calculated total cross-section from the low-|t| fit at 7 TeV is sigma_tot = 99.3 +/- 3.8 mb, compared to the TOTEM measured value of 98.0 +/- 2.5 mb
- The calculated rho parameter at 7 TeV is 0.09 +/- 0.03, consistent with the TOTEM measured value of 0.145 +/- 0.091
- At ISR energy of 23.5 GeV, the fit yields chi^2/NDF = 124.7/101 with CL = 5.5%
- The shape parameter alpha increases from 0.03 +/- 0.01 at 23.5 GeV to 0.12 +/- 0.01 at 7 TeV (dip region fit)
- The quark radius R_q increases from 0.27 +/- 0.01 fm at 23.5 GeV to 0.43 +/- 0.01 fm at 7 TeV (dip region fit)
- The diquark radius R_d increases from 0.72 +/- 0.01 fm at 23.5 GeV to 0.91 +/- 0.01 fm at 7 TeV (dip region fit)

## Context

This work extends the earlier Bialas-Bzdak model (Bialas, 2006) and the alpha-generalized Bialas-Bzdak model (Csorgo, 2013) by incorporating unitarity constraints explicitly, addressing the failure of the earlier models to describe the dip region at LHC energies. The approach is related to the geometric scaling method of Ferreira (2014) and Kohara (2014), but uses only constant and linear terms in ln(s) for parameter evolution. The results enable predictions for future LHC energies of 13, 14, 15 TeV and 28 TeV, and the analysis of the non-exponential behavior of the differential cross-section at low |t| connects to the preliminary TOTEM observation at 8 TeV and the t-channel unitarity approach of Jenkovszky (2014).
