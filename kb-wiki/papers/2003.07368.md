# Hydrodynamic attractors in phase space

> Hydrodynamic attractors have recently gained prominence in the context of early stages of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC and LHC. We critically examine the existing ideas on this subject from a phase space point of view. In this picture the hydrodynamic attractor can be seen as a special case of the more general phenomenon of dynamical dimensionality reduction of phase space regions. We quantify this using Principal Component Analysis. Furthermore, we adapt the well known slow-roll approximation to this setting. These techniques generalize easily to higher dimensional phase spaces, which we illustrate by a preliminary analysis of a dataset describing the evolution of a 5-dimensional manifold of initial conditions immersed in a 16-dimensional representation of the phase space of the Boltzmann kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation.

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic attractor, previously identified in specific observables like pressure anisotropy, is actually a special case of a more general phenomenon: dynamical dimensionality reduction of phase space regions, which can be quantified using Principal Component Analysis and generalized to higher-dimensional phase spaces without needing to know in advance which observable makes attractor behavior manifest.

## Method

The authors study the phase space of two hydrodynamic models (MIS/BRSSS and HJSW) under Bjorken flow, tracking clouds of initial conditions on constant-time slices and analyzing their evolution using Principal Component Analysis to quantify dimensionality reduction. They also adapt the slow-roll approximation from inflationary cosmology to identify attractor loci as regions where the phase space velocity is minimal, and extend the analysis to a 16-dimensional representation of the Boltzmann kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation.

## Key numbers

- In MIS/BRSSS, simulations used 10,000 random states with parameters C_eta = 0.75 and C_tau_pi = 1
- In HJSW, parameters were C_eta = 0.75, C_tau_pi = 1.16, and C_omega = 9.8
- For the Boltzmann kinetic theory analysis, 240 initial conditions spanned a 5-dimensional manifold embedded in a 16-dimensional phase space
- In the Boltzmann analysis, the second principal component stabilizes at tau approximately 22 tau_0, while exponential decay of principal components begins at tau approximately 15 tau_0
- PCA analysis of MIS/BRSSS used initial circles of radius 10^-4 in phase space

## Context

This work extends the concept of hydrodynamic attractors introduced by Heller (2015) and subsequent studies in MIS/BRSSS and holographic models, while critically examining the different notions of forward and pullback attractors distinguished by Behtash (2019). The phase space approach enables generalization beyond the highly-symmetric Bjorken flow to more realistic settings with less symmetry, and the PCA methodology connects to prior applications of dimensionality reduction in heavy-ion collisions by Bhalerao (2014), Mazeliauskas (2015), and others.
