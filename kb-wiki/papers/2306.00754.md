# Exclusive $η_c$ production by $γ^{*} γ$ interactions in electron-ion collisions

> One of the main goals of future electron-ion colliders is to improve our understanding of the structure of hadrons. In this letter, we study the exclusive $η_c$ production by $γ^{*} γ$ interactions in $eA$ collisions and demonstrate that future experimental analysis of this process can be used to improve the description of the $η_c$ transition form factor. The rapidity, transverse momentum and photon virtuality distributions are estimated considering the energy and target configurations expected to be present at the EIC, EicC and LHeC and assuming different predictions for the light-front wave function of the $η_c$ meson. Our results indicate that the electron-ion colliders can be considered an alternative to providing supplementary data to those obtained in $e^- e^+$ colliders.

## Key claim

The exclusive $\eta_c$ production by $\gamma^{*} \gamma$ interactions in electron-ion collisions can provide supplementary data to constrain the $\eta_c$ transition form factor, complementing measurements from $e^{-} e^{+}$ colliders, provided that the cross-section is dominated by the photon-photon subprocess and that the nuclear photon flux provides a $Z^2$ enhancement.

## Method

The total cross-section for $e A \rightarrow e \eta_c A$ is factorized into the electron photon flux, the nuclear photon flux, and the $\gamma^{*} \gamma \rightarrow \eta_c$ subprocess cross-section. The subprocess cross-section is expressed in terms of the $\eta_c$ transition form factor $F(Q^2,0)$, which is calculated using light-front wave functions derived from several potential models (harmonic oscillator, power-law, Cornell, Buchmuller-Tye, logarithmic) and from the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach. The rapidity, transverse momentum, and photon virtuality distributions are then estimated for the energy and target configurations of the EIC, EicC, and LHeC.

## Key numbers

- The $\gamma \gamma$ decay width of the $\eta_c$ used in calculations is $\Gamma_{\gamma \gamma} = (5.4 \pm 0.4)$ keV (PDG average).
- The cross sections for future electron-ion colliders are of the order of 0.1 to 60 nb.
- The predicted number of events per year is $\mathcal{O}(10^6)$ for the EicC and $\mathcal{O}(10^7)$ for the LHeC, and one order of magnitude larger for the EIC.
- The analysis assumes electron and ion energy benchmarks: LE-EIC ($E_e = 2.5$ GeV, $E_{Au} = 41$ GeV), HE-EIC ($E_e = 18$ GeV, $E_{Au} = 100$ GeV), EicC ($E_e = 3.5$ GeV, $E_{Au} = 10$ GeV), and LHeC ($E_e = 50$ GeV, $E_{Pb} = 2760$ GeV).
- The photon virtuality integration range used for rapidity distributions is $Q^2 > 0.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Babiarz et al. (2019, 2020, 2022, 2023) for the $\eta_c$ transition form factor in $e^{+} e^{-}$ collisions to the electron-ion collider environment. It contrasts with prior studies of exclusive $\eta_c$ production in $ep$ and ultraperipheral collisions that focused on Odderon exchange (e.g., Kilian et al. 1997, Czyzewski et al. 1996, Engel et al. 1997). The results enable future experimental analyses at the EIC, EicC, and LHeC to provide independent constraints on the $\eta_c$ wave function and validate data previously obtained by the BaBar Collaboration (2010) from the $e^{+} e^{-} \to e^{+} e^{-} \eta_c$ reaction.
