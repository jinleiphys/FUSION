# Level density of a Fermi gas: average growth and fluctuations

> We compute the level density of a two--component Fermi gas as a function of the number of particles, angular momentum and excitation energy. The result includes smooth low--energy corrections to the leading Bethe term (connected to a generalization of the partition problem and Hardy--Ramanujan formula) plus oscillatory corrections that describe shell effects. When applied to nuclear level densities, the theory provides a unified formulation valid from low--lying states up to levels entering the continuum. The comparison with experimental data from neutron resonances gives excellent results.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact expression for the many-body level density of a two-component Fermi gas that includes smooth low-energy corrections to Bethe's formula plus oscillatory shell-effect terms, but the authors note that for an arbitrary single-particle spectrum the higher-order smooth corrections (terms of order \(1/\sqrt{\rho_b Q}\) and beyond) are conjectured from the equidistant-spectrum case and their validity for arbitrary systems must be confirmed.

## Method

The authors compute the many-body density of states for a two-component Fermi gas (protons and neutrons) with fixed angular momentum projection by adapting the Hardy-Ramanujan-Rademacher circle method to the two-component partition problem \(p_2(K)\). For an arbitrary single-particle spectrum, they use a saddle-point approximation of the inverse Laplace transform of the many-body density, expressing the entropy as the sum of the equidistant-spectrum entropy plus a temperature-dependent term involving the fluctuating part of the energy. The fluctuating part of the ground-state energy is extracted from experimental nuclear binding energies by subtracting a liquid-drop parametrization, and the temperature-dependent suppression of shell effects is approximated by averaging over the shortest periodic orbits of a spherical cavity.

## Key numbers

- The leading Bethe term gives \(\rho_{\text{MB}}(Q) \sim \exp(2\sqrt{a Q})/Q^{5/4}\) with \(a = \pi^2 \rho_b(\epsilon_F)/6\).
- The smooth correction terms in the entropy for an equidistant spectrum are: \(\mathcal{S}_{eq} = 2\sqrt{(\pi^2/6)\rho_b Q} - (\pi/36 + 15/(16\pi))\sqrt{6}/\sqrt{\rho_b Q} + (35/96 + \pi^2/432)/(\rho_b Q) + \mathcal{O}((\rho_b Q)^{-3/2})\).
- The characteristic period of shell-effect oscillations as mass number \(A\) varies is \(\delta A \approx (\pi/3) A^{2/3}\), independent of excitation energy.
- The relevant temperature scale for shell-effect amplitude is \(T_c \approx 4/A^{1/3}\) MeV; the temperature associated with the single-particle mean level spacing is \(T_\delta \approx 1.3/A\) MeV, giving \(g = T_c/T_\delta \approx 3 A^{2/3}\).
- At low temperatures, the typical amplitude of shell effects \(\sigma_{\mathcal{S}} \approx \sqrt{T/T_\delta}\); at high temperatures \(T \gg T_c\), \(\sigma_{\mathcal{S}} \approx \sqrt{g/6} \, T_c/T \approx 2\sqrt{2}/T\) MeV.
- Fitting to 295 nuclei at neutron threshold yields \(a = A/10.42\) MeV\(^{-1}\), \(b = -0.019\) MeV, and \(c = 7.9\) MeV; the relative error in the logarithm of the density is \(\leq 10\%\) for most nuclei.

## Context

This work generalizes the single-component results of Leboeuf and collaborators (2005) to two-component Fermi gases with angular momentum conservation, extending Bethe's (1936) high-energy formula to include smooth low-energy corrections and shell fluctuations. The approach provides a unified theoretical framework that replaces composite models such as the Gilbert-Cameron (1965) formula by a single expression valid from low-lying states to the continuum. The results enable systematic extrapolations to unknown nuclear regions and improve upon phenomenological fits like those of Ignatyuk and collaborators (RIPL-2 database), with the main open question being the treatment of finite-size effects and the continuum at high excitation energies.
