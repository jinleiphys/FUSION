# Mechanisms for Direct Breakup Reactions

> We review some simple mechanisms of breakup in nuclear reactions. We mention the spectator breakup, which is described in the post-form DWBA. The relation to other formulations is also indicated. An especially important mechanism is Coulomb dissociation. It is a distinct advantage that the perturbation due to the electric field of the nucleus is exactly known. Therefore firm conclusions can be drawn from such measurements. Some new applications of Coulomb dissociation for nuclear astrophysics are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews simple mechanisms for direct breakup reactions, emphasizing that while the spectator mechanism (described in post-form DWBA) and the Coulomb dissociation mechanism are related, further clarification is needed to fully understand their connection, especially regarding the choice of Hamiltonian decomposition for the final state.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework for direct breakup reactions using a three-body model where a projectile \(a = b + x\) interacts with a target nucleus \(A\). They present the post-form DWBA T-matrix element for elastic breakup, discuss the spectator mechanism where one constituent interacts strongly with the target while the other remains undisturbed, and develop the Coulomb dissociation formalism using both semiclassical and quantum mechanical approaches, including the equivalent photon approximation and higher-order electromagnetic effects.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron breakup on \({}^{197}\)Au at \(E_d = 12\) MeV: coincidence cross sections measured at neutron angle \(\theta_n = 20^\circ\) and proton angles \(\theta_p = 50^\circ\) and \(\theta_p = -60^\circ\) show strong post-acceleration effect (proton average energy much higher than neutron average energy)
- Neutron separation energy of \({}^{19}\)C determined to be \(530 \pm 130\) keV from Coulomb dissociation experiments
- For the RIKEN experiments on \({}^{11}\)Be and \({}^{19}\)C, the adiabaticity parameter \(\xi\) is of order 0.1 for grazing collisions
- Coulomb dissociation of \({}^{8}\)B on \({}^{208}\)Pb studied at projectile energies of 46.5 \(A\cdot\)MeV and 250 \(A\cdot\)MeV for \({}^{7}\)Be-p relative energy of 0.3 MeV
- Higher order electromagnetic effects decrease strongly like \(1/b^4\) with impact parameter

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for breakup reactions originating from the work of Oppenheimer and Phillips (1935) on deuteron breakup in the Coulomb field of a nucleus. It builds upon the comprehensive review by Baur et al. (1984) on spectator breakup and the post-form DWBA formalism, and discusses recent applications of Coulomb dissociation for nuclear astrophysics, including the \({}^{7}\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\({}^{8}\)B reaction relevant to the solar neutrino problem and neutron-capture reactions for r-process nucleosynthesis. The work enables future studies using radioactive beam facilities to investigate photo-interactions with unstable nuclei and two-nucleon capture reactions in astrophysical environments.
