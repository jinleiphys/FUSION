# Partial restoration of chiral symmetry and pion in nuclear medium

> We discuss partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium, which is realized as an effective reduction of the quark condensate in nuclear medium. We derive the in-medium Weinberg-Tomozawa relation. We also give a brief calculation of the in-medium quark condensate based on chiral perturbation theory. We conclude that the density dependence of the quark condensate beyond the linear density comes from the vertex correction of the chiral field insertion and nucleon correlations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the density dependence of the in-medium quark condensate beyond the linear density approximation arises from vertex corrections of the chiral field insertion and nucleon correlations, but notes that realistic nuclear matter descriptions beyond chiral perturbation theory are necessary for quantitative results.

## Method

The authors derive an exact sum rule for the in-medium quark condensate in symmetric nuclear matter using current algebra in the chiral limit, and extend this to an in-medium Weinberg-Tomozawa relation for asymmetric nuclear matter. They then perform a direct calculation of the in-medium quark condensate using in-medium chiral perturbation theory with a non-interacting nucleon gas environment, expanding in Fermi momentum and chiral order counting.

## Key numbers

- The isovector π⁻-nucleus interaction repulsive enhancement was extracted as b₁^free/b₁ = 0.78 ± 0.05 at around ρ ∼ 0.6 ρ₀ from deeply bound pionic atom data (Suzuki 2002)
- With c₁ = -0.58 GeV⁻¹, f_π = 92.4 MeV, and normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the quark condensate is ⟨ūu + d̄d⟩* = ⟨ūu + d̄d⟩₀ (1 − 0.35 ρ/ρ₀), suggesting 30 percent reduction at nuclear density
- The leading order medium correction to the quark condensate starts at O(p⁴) of chiral expansion, but this order vanishes at the chiral limit; the leading non-vanishing contribution comes from O(p²) πPNN vertex diagrams

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework connecting experimental observations from deeply bound pionic atoms (Suzuki 2002, Friedman 2004) to the in-medium quark condensate, building on the sum rule derived by Jido (2008) that relates the in-medium pion decay constant to the quark condensate at linear density. The paper enables more quantitative determination of the in-medium quark condensate by showing that beyond-linear-density corrections require realistic nuclear matter descriptions incorporating multi-pion exchanges and nucleon-nucleon correlations, rather than relying solely on chiral perturbation theory expansions.
