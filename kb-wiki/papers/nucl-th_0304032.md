# A note on the time evolution of the fission decay width under the influence of dissipation

## Key claim
The claim by Jurado, Schmidt and Benlliure that the transient effect of nuclear fission can be described simply as a relaxation process in the upright oscillator around the potential minimum is refuted. The approximation of calculating the time-dependent pre-factor from a solution of the transport equation restricted to the upright oscillator (rather than from a global solution accounting for motion across the barrier) is fundamentally flawed because the upright oscillator yields a current that tends to zero exponentially, whereas the correct inverted oscillator yields the stationary Kramers current.

## Method
The author examines the formal structure of the Fokker-Planck equation solutions used by Jurado et al., specifically the replacement of the correct distribution \(W_n\) by the upright-oscillator distribution \(W^{\text{par}}\) in their eq.(6). The author demonstrates that the assumption in eq.(7) of Jurado et al., that at the barrier top the dependence of the distribution on coordinate and velocity is identical to that at infinite time, is incorrect for both under-damped and over-damped motion. The author derives the correct time-dependent width \(\sigma^2(t)\) from the Smoluchowski equation for the oscillator and shows that the relaxation time scale is \(\tau_{\text{ovd}} = \gamma/2C\), independent of initial fluctuation.

## Key numbers
- For \(\beta = 2 \times 10^{21} \text{s}^{-1}\), \(\hbar \omega_1 = 1\) MeV and \(T = 3\) MeV, the time shift \(t_0\) from eq.(12) of Jurado et al. is \(t_0 \simeq 0.06 \times 10^{-21}\) s, which is too small to explain why \(\Gamma_f(t)\) starts to become finite only at about \(0.7 \times 10^{-21}\) s.
- For the same parameters, the relaxation time \(\tau_{\text{ovd}}\) becomes \(\tau_{\text{ovd}} \simeq 0.36 \times 10^{-21}\) s using the correct relation of frequency to stiffness, and becomes very close to \(0.7 \times 10^{-21}\) s if the stiffness \(K\) is taken as half the correct value (as in eq.(13) of Jurado et al.).
- The Kramers decay time for over-damped motion is given by \(\tau_{\text{K}} = \frac{2\pi \gamma}{\sqrt{C_{\text{a}}|C_{\text{b}}|}} \exp(E_{\text{b}}/T)\), where \(C_{\text{a}}\) and \(C_{\text{b}}\) are the stiffnesses at minimum and barrier.

## Context
This note refutes the approach of Jurado, Schmidt and Benlliure (2003) who claimed a simple analytical approximation to the Fokker-Planck equation for fission transients. The author contrasts this with the mean first passage time (MFPT) concept applied to nuclear fission in Hofmann and Ivanyuk (PRL) and Hofmann and Muenchow (MFPT), which showed that Kramers' rate formula is valid only for simple potentials and favorable temperatures, and that for structured potentials the fission lifetime can be considerably longer than \(\tau_{\text{K}}\). The work extends earlier theoretical studies of nuclear dissipation (Hofmann review) and connects to experimental work by Paul and Thoennessen, Dioszegi, and theoretical work by Hirai and Yamaji. The key open question is whether the uncertainties in the reduced friction coefficient \(\beta\) can be resolved without properly accounting for the coordinate and temperature dependence of transport coefficients and the shape of the fission potential beyond the barrier top.
