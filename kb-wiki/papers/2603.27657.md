# Magnetic moments of open bottom--charm molecular pentaquark octets

> We present a comprehensive theoretical investigation of the magnetic moments of open heavy-flavor molecular pentaquarks with quark compositions $b\bar{c}qqq$ and $c\bar{b}qqq$ (where $q=u,d,s$). Employing a molecular picture in which the pentaquarks are treated as S-wave bound states of a heavy baryon and a meson, we systematically construct the complete spin--flavor wavefunctions for the two distinct SU(3)$_f$ octet representations, $8_{1f}$ and $8_{2f}$, arising from symmetric and antisymmetric light-diquark configurations, respectively. Within the framework of the constituent quark model, we calculate the magnetic moments of spin-parity configurations, $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-(\frac{1}{2}^+\otimes 0^-)$ and $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-, \frac{3}{2}^-(\frac{1}{2}^+\otimes 1^-)$, for each member of the $b\bar{c}$ and $c\bar{b}$ octets. Our results reveal a striking hierarchy: in the $8_{2f}$ representation, the $\frac{1}{2}^+\otimes 0^-$ states exhibit near-universal magnetic moments ($Î¼\approx -0.062\,Î¼_N$ for $b\bar{c}qqq$ and $Î¼\approx +0.362\,Î¼_N$ for $c\bar{b}qqq$), as a direct consequence of the spin-singlet light-diquark that suppresses light-quark contributions. In contrast, the $8_{1f}$ representation shows a broad spectrum of values with frequent sign changes, reflecting the active role of the symmetric light-diquark. The clear differences between the $b\bar{c}$ and $c\bar{b}$ families demonstrate explicit heavy-quark flavor symmetry breaking in electromagnetic observables. These predictions provide a detailed set of electromagnetic benchmarks that can serve as discriminants for the internal flavor structure and spin configuration of future experimentally observed open heavy-flavor pentaquarks, offering valuable guidance for ongoing and future searches at facilities such as LHCb and Belle II.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in the $8_{2f}$ SU(3)$_f$ octet representation, the $\frac{1}{2}^+\otimes 0^-$ states exhibit near-universal magnetic moments ($\mu\approx -0.062\,\mu_N$ for $b\bar{c}qqq$ and $\mu\approx +0.362\,\mu_N$ for $c\bar{b}qqq$) as a consequence of the spin-singlet light-diquark suppressing light-quark contributions, while the $8_{1f}$ representation shows a broad spectrum with frequent sign changes due to the active symmetric light-diquark.

## Method

The authors treat open bottom-charm pentaquarks as S-wave molecular bound states of a singly heavy baryon $(Qqq)$ and a heavy-light meson $(q\bar{Q}')$, with $(Q,Q')=(b,c)$ or $(c,b)$. They construct complete spin-flavor wavefunctions for the two SU(3)$_f$ octet representations ($8_{1f}$ from symmetric light-diquarks and $8_{2f}$ from antisymmetric light-diquarks) and compute magnetic moments within the constituent quark model using the operator $\hat{\mu}_{\mathrm{spin}} = \sum_i (Q_i/2M_i)\,\hat{\sigma}_i$ for spin-parity configurations $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-(\frac{1}{2}^+\otimes 0^-)$ and $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-, \frac{3}{2}^-(\frac{1}{2}^+\otimes 1^-)$.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses used: $m_u=m_d=361.8$ MeV, $m_s=540.4$ MeV, $m_c=1724.8$ MeV, $m_b=5052.9$ MeV (from Wu (2017))
- For the $8_{2f}$ representation in the $\frac{1}{2}^+\otimes 0^-$ configuration: every $b\bar{c}$ state has $\mu = -0.062\,\mu_N$ and every $c\bar{b}$ state has $\mu = +0.362\,\mu_N$
- The $\Lambda_b^{0}$ baryon magnetic moment is $\mu_{\Lambda_b^{0}} = \mu_b$, where $\mu_q \equiv Q_q/(2M_q)$
- For the $P_{b\bar c}^{2}(8_{1f})$ state in the $\frac{1}{2}^+\otimes 0^-$ configuration: $\mu = \frac{4}{3}\mu_d - \frac{1}{3}\mu_b + \frac{1}{3}\mu_d + \frac{2}{3}\mu_u + \mu_{\bar c}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetic moments in hidden-charm pentaquarks (Wang (2016), Ozdem (2021-2025), Mutuk (2024)) and hidden-bottom pentaquarks (Mutuk (2024)) to the relatively unexplored open bottom-charm sector. The molecular interpretation is supported by dynamical calculations from Lin (2023) and Wang (2025), who demonstrated that meson-baryon interactions in these systems can dynamically generate bound states near thresholds. The predictions provide electromagnetic benchmarks that could discriminate internal flavor structure and spin configuration for future experimental searches at LHCb and Belle II, building on the color-magnetic-interaction analysis and SU(3)$_f$ classification established by Wu (2017).
