# The nuclear equation of state probed by K+ production in heavy ion collisions

> The dependence of K+ production on the nuclear equation of state is investigated in heavy ion collisions. An increase of the excitation function of K+ multiplicities obtained in heavy (Au+Au) over light (C+C) systems when going far below threshold which has been observed by the KaoS Collaboration strongly favours a soft equation of state. This observation holds despite of the influence of an in-medium kaon potential predicted by effective chiral models which is necessary to reproduce the experimental K+ yields. Phase space effects are discussed with respect to the K+ excitation function.

# The nuclear equation of state probed by K+ production in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The increase of the excitation function of K+ multiplicities in heavy (Au+Au) over light (C+C) systems far below threshold, as observed by the KaoS Collaboration, strongly favours a soft equation of state (compression modulus K=200 MeV) over a hard one (K=380 MeV), even when the in-medium kaon potential predicted by effective chiral models is included. The conclusion holds despite the fact that the in-medium potential is itself necessary to reproduce the absolute experimental K+ yields.

## Method
The authors performed Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) transport calculations for Au+Au and C+C collisions at incident energies from 0.8 to 1.5 A·GeV, using soft and hard Skyrme forces with compression moduli of K=200 MeV and K=380 MeV respectively. They included baryonic resonances (Δ(1232) and N*(1440)), an in-medium kaon potential based on effective chiral models with a repulsive vector part and attractive scalar part, and elementary cross sections for kaon production channels (NN, πN, NΔ, πΔ, ΔΔ) from established parameterizations. The kaon production was treated perturbatively, and the ratio R of K+ multiplicities in Au+Au over C+C, normalized to mass numbers, was compared to KaoS Collaboration data.

## Key numbers
- Compression moduli used: K=200 MeV (soft EOS) and K=380 MeV (hard EOS), with saturation point at E_B = -16 MeV and ρ_sat = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Free threshold for K+ production: E_thr = 1.58 GeV; measurements taken from 0.8 A·GeV up to 1.5 A·GeV
- In the light C+C system, the K+ yield is reduced by about 50% due to the in-medium kaon potential
- Mean densities at which kaons are created in central collisions at 0.8 A·GeV: for C+C, <ρ/ρ_sat> = 1.46 (hard EOS) and 1.40 (soft EOS); for Au+Au, <ρ/ρ_sat> = 1.47 (hard EOS) and 1.57 (soft EOS)
- In Au+Au, kaons are created at densities up to three times saturation density; in C+C, densities above 2ρ_sat are rarely reached
- Impact parameter cuts: b_max = 11 fm for Au+Au, b_max = 5 fm for C+C

## Context
This work extends earlier transport model studies (Aichelin and Ko 1985; Li and Bauer 1994; Li and Brown 1995) that first noted K+ yield sensitivity to the nuclear equation of state, with yields a factor 2-3 larger for a soft EOS. It directly addresses the KaoS Collaboration data (Sturm 2000) on subthreshold K+ production, using improved elementary cross sections from COSY-11 measurements and from Sibirtsev (1995) and Tsushima (1999). The results are consistent with independent IQMD calculations (Hartnack 2001) that also include an in-medium kaon potential from relativistic mean field theory (Schaffner 1997). The analysis of K+ flow in Ni+Ni reactions at 1.93 A·GeV further confirms that the in-medium kaon potential is required to reproduce FOPI data (FOPI Collaboration 1995, 1999). The work enables future investigations into the remaining discrepancies between different transport models for the hard EOS case and the role of different in-medium potentials.
