# Calculation of nuclear matter in the presence of strong magnetic field using LOCV technique

> In the present work, we are interested in the properties of nuclear matter at zero temperature in the presence of strong magnetic fields using the lowest order constraint variational (LOCV) method employing $AV_{18}$ nuclear potential. Our results indicate that in the absence of a magnetic field, the energy per particle is a symmetric function of the spin polarization parameter. This shows that for the nuclear matter, the spontaneous phase transition to a ferromagnetic state does not occur. However, we have found that for the magnetic fields $ B\gtrsim 10 ^ {18}\ G$, the symmetry of energy is broken and the energy has a minimum at a positive value of the spin polarization parameter. We have also found that the effect of magnetic field on the value of energy is more significant at the low densities. Our calculations show that at lower densities, the spin polarization parameter is more sensitive to the magnetic field.

# Calculation of nuclear matter in the presence of strong magnetic field using LOCV technique

## Key claim

For magnetic fields B ≳ 10^18 G, the symmetry of the energy per particle as a function of spin polarization is broken and the energy develops a minimum at a positive spin polarization parameter, indicating that a strong magnetic field can induce spin polarization in nuclear matter even though no spontaneous ferromagnetic phase transition occurs in the absence of a field.

## Method

The authors used the lowest order constraint variational (LOCV) method with the AV18 two-body nuclear potential to calculate the energy of symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature. They included the magnetic energy contribution from the interaction of nucleon magnetic dipole moments with an external magnetic field oriented along the z-axis, and minimized the two-body cluster energy with respect to correlation functions under a normalization constraint to obtain the ground-state energy as a function of spin polarization parameter, density, and magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

- For B ≳ 10^18 G, the symmetry of the energy per particle as a function of spin polarization is broken, with a minimum appearing at a positive spin polarization parameter.
- At ρ = 0.05 fm^-3 and B = 10^19 G, the nuclear matter is polarized with spin polarization parameter ξ ≃ 0.69.
- At ρ = 0.5 fm^-3 and B = 10^19 G, the spin polarization parameter is less than 0.2.
- For magnetic fields B ≲ 10^17 G, the nuclear matter is nearly unpolarized; the spin polarization parameter begins to grow for B ≳ 3 × 10^17 G.
- At ρ = 0.05 fm^-3, the increasing rate of the spin polarization parameter raises at B ≃ 3 × 10^18 G.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LOCV studies of spin polarized neutron matter in strong magnetic fields (Bordbar 2010, 2011) to symmetric nuclear matter, and complements relativistic mean-field studies such as Dong (2013) which found similar density-dependent magnetic effects. The results show that magnetic fields above 10^18 G can significantly affect nuclear matter properties at low densities, which is relevant for understanding the interior of magnetars where surface fields can reach 10^15 G and core fields may reach 10^18-10^20 G as predicted by general relativity (Shapiro 1983) and inferred from observations (Yuan 2004).
