# Aspects of Rotating Anisotropic Dark Energy Stars

> By employing modified Chaplygin fluid prescription for the dark energy, we construct slowly rotating isotropic and anisotropic dark energy stars. The slow rotation is incorporated via general relativistic Hartle-Thorne formalism; whereas the anisotropy is introduced through Bowers-Liang prescription. We consider both the monopole and quadrupole deformations and present a complete analysis of rotating dark energy stars. By numerically solving the rotating stellar structure equations in presence of anisotropy, we analyse and quantify various properties of dark energy stars such as mass ($M$), radius, mass deformation, angular momentum ($J$), moment of inertia, and quadrupole moment ($Q$), for three different equation of state parameters. We find that anisotropic slow rotation results in significant deformation of stellar mass and thereby affects other global properties studied. For the values of angular frequencies considered, the effect of anisotropy on the stellar structure is found to be more prominent than that due to rotation. The dimensionless quadrupole moment $QM/J^2$ measuring deviation from a Kerr metric black hole was obtained for anisotropic dark energy stars. We observe that dark energy stars with higher anisotropic strength tend to approach the Kerr solution more closely. We report that our results have considerable agreement with various astrophysical observational measurements.

## Key claim

The authors find that for the specific angular frequencies considered (1500 and 2500 s⁻¹), the effect of pressure anisotropy on the stellar structure of slowly rotating dark energy stars is more prominent than the effect of rotation itself, and that dark energy stars with higher anisotropic strength tend to approach the Kerr black hole solution more closely in terms of their dimensionless quadrupole moment.

## Method

The authors construct slowly rotating isotropic and anisotropic dark energy stars using the modified Chaplygin fluid equation of state, incorporating slow rotation via the general relativistic Hartle-Thorne formalism and pressure anisotropy through the Bowers-Liang prescription. They numerically solve the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for the static case, then integrate the first-order (monopole) and second-order (quadrupole) perturbation equations to obtain stellar properties for three different sets of equation of state parameters, varying the anisotropy strength parameter λ_BL from -2 to 2.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses of rotating dark energy stars (Ω=1500 s⁻¹) for EoS Set I: approximately 2.07 M⊙ (λ_BL=-2), 2.48 M⊙ (λ_BL=0), and 3.09 M⊙ (λ_BL=2), with corresponding equatorial radii of 11.02 km, 11.78 km, and 12.89 km
- Mass correction δM due to rotation at central density ρ_c=8×10¹⁴ g cm⁻³: (3.07, 3.84, 4.90, 6.37, 8.45)×10⁻³ M⊙ for λ_BL = (-2, -1, 0, 1, 2) respectively
- Angular momentum at maximum mass (J_max) for EoS Set I with Ω=1500 s⁻¹: (3.61, 4.42, 5.47, 7.06, 9.26)×10⁴¹ kg m² s⁻¹ for λ_BL = (-2, -1, 0, 1, 2)
- Moment of inertia at maximum mass (I_max) for EoS Set I: (2.40, 2.94, 3.64, 4.69, 6.16)×10³⁸ kg m² for λ_BL = (-2, -1, 0, 1, 2)
- Dimensionless quadrupole moment at maximum mass (q̄_max) for EoS Set I: 2.01, 1.88, 1.74, 1.65, and 1.53 for λ_BL = (-2, -1, 0, 1, 2)
- Tidal deformability Λ at 1.40 M⊙: 49, 53, 59, 60, and 76 for λ_BL = (-2, -1, 0, 1, 2), all within the GW170817 limit (Λ_1.4 ≤ 800)
- Equatorial radii for a 1.5 M⊙ star rotating at Ω=1500 s⁻¹: 10.85 km (λ_BL=-2), 11.06 km (λ_BL=0), and 11.25 km (λ_BL=2)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of static anisotropic dark energy stars by Pretel (2023) and non-radial oscillations in dark energy stars by Jyothilakshmi (2024) to include the effects of slow rotation. It builds on the Hartle-Thorne formalism applied to anisotropic neutron stars by Pattersons (2021) and on earlier studies of rotating isotropic dark energy stars by Panotopoulos (2021). The results enable comparison with observational constraints from gravitational wave events (GW170817, GW190814) and pulsar mass measurements (PSR J2215+5135, PSR J0740+6620), showing that dark energy stars with certain anisotropy parameters can satisfy all current observational limits.
