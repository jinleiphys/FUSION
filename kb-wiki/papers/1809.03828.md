# Strangeness production via resonances in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies

> Production of strange hadrons in elementary and heavy-ion reactions is studied with the hadronic transport approach SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons). The poorly known branching ratios of the relevant hadronic resonances are constrained from the known elementary hadronic cross sections and from invariant mass spectra of dileptons. The constrained model is employed as a baseline to compare to heavy-ion-collision experiments at low energies ($E_\text{kin} = 1-2A\,\text{GeV}$) and to predict some of the upcoming pion-beam results by HADES, which are expected to be sensitive to the resonance properties. The employed vacuum-resonance approach proves to be viable for small systems at these energies, but for large systems additional in-medium effects might be required.

## Key claim

The vacuum-resonance approach implemented in SMASH can describe strangeness production in small systems at SIS energies (1-2A GeV), but for large systems additional in-medium effects might be required, and the model shows tension between simultaneously reproducing elementary pp and p-pion cross sections for both Lambda and Sigma production.

## Method

The authors used the hadronic transport code SMASH 1.3 to model strangeness production via hadronic resonances, constraining the poorly known branching ratios of nucleon resonances (N*, Delta*), hyperon resonances (Lambda*, Sigma*), and meson resonances against experimental data on elementary cross sections (pp, p-pion, antikaon-nucleon) and PDG values. They compared the resulting model to heavy-ion collision data from KaoS and HADES at kinetic energies of 1-2A GeV, and also used the model to predict pion-beam results for the HADES experiment.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio for N(1650) -> Lambda K is set to 4% in SMASH, compared to PDG range of 5-15% and HADES partial wave analysis value of 7±4%.
- The branching ratio for N(1710) -> Lambda K is set to 13% in SMASH, compared to PDG range of 5-25% and HADES value of 15±10%.
- The branching ratio for Lambda(1520) -> anti-K N is set to 46.2% in SMASH, compared to PDG value of 45±1%.
- The branching ratio for Lambda(1820) -> anti-K N is set to 65% in SMASH, compared to PDG range of 55-65%.
- The pp -> Lambda p K+ cross section is slightly overestimated at sqrt(s) > 3.3 GeV, while the p pi- -> Lambda K0 cross section is slightly underestimated at sqrt(s) ≈ 1.75 GeV.
- The p pi+ -> Sigma+ K+ cross section is overestimated at sqrt(s) = 1.75-1.95 GeV, while the p pi- -> Sigma- K+ cross section is underestimated at sqrt(s) < 2.05 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic transport approach SMASH (Weil 2016) by constraining resonance branching ratios for strangeness production, complementing earlier approaches that use kaon-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon potentials (GiBUU in Agakishiev 2014, IQMD and HSD in Hartnack 2011) or high-mass nucleon resonances and strangeness exchange reactions (UrQMD in Steinheimer 2015 and Graef 2014). The constrained model enables a vacuum baseline for future comparisons with upcoming HADES pion-beam data and experiments at FAIR, NICA, J-PARC, and RHIC Beam Energy Scan, and can be extended with in-medium effects for larger systems.
