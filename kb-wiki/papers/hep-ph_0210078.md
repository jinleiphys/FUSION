# Mixed phases of color superconducting quark matter

> We examine electrically and color neutral quark matter in beta-equilibrium focusing on the possibility of mixed phases between different color superconducting phases. To that end we apply the Gibbs criterion to ensure phase equilibrium and discuss the external conditions under which these mixed phases can occur. Neglecting surface and Coulomb effects we find a rich structure of different mixed phases with up to four components, including 2SC and CFL matter as well as more ``exotic'' components, like a phase with us- and ds-pairing but without ud-pairing. Preliminary estimates indicate, however, that the mixed phases become unstable if surface and Coulomb effects are included.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that electrically and color neutral mixed phases between different color superconducting phases (2SC, CFL, and more exotic pairings) can exist in bulk quark matter when surface and Coulomb effects are neglected, but preliminary estimates indicate these mixed phases become unstable once surface and Coulomb effects are included.

## Method
The authors apply the Gibbs criterion for phase equilibrium in the four-dimensional space of chemical potentials (μ, μ_Q, μ_3, μ_8) to construct electrically and color neutral mixed phases along first-order phase boundaries. They use a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with quark-antiquark and diquark interactions at mean-field level, including dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and color-superconducting condensates, and restrict the analysis to zero temperature and bulk matter without surface or Coulomb effects.

## Key numbers
- At the 2SC-CFL phase boundary with μ_8 = -32.5 MeV and μ = 465.7 MeV, the 2SC matter in equilibrium with neutral CFL matter carries electric charge density n_Q = 0.464 fm^{-3} and color charge density n_8 = -0.329 fm^{-3}.
- In the 2SC phase just below the phase boundary at μ = 390 MeV and μ_Q = -178.6 MeV, the ud pairing gap Δ_2 = 132.8 MeV, slightly larger than the critical value Δ_2^c = 127.4 MeV.
- The strange quark mass drops from above 460 MeV to 310 MeV at the 2SC–SC_{us+ds} phase transition point (μ = 390 MeV, μ_Q = -178.6 MeV).
- In the SC_{us+ds} phase, the ds pairing gap Δ_5 decreases from 50.8 MeV to 49.1 MeV while Δ_5^c increases from 48.2 MeV to 52.6 MeV, with the phase ending at μ_Q = -183.0 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of electrically and color neutral quark matter by Alford and Rajagopal (2002) and Steiner, Reddy, and Prakash (2002), who compared only homogeneous phases and found the CFL phase favored over 2SC under neutron star conditions. The authors generalize the mixed-phase construction of Glendenning (1992), originally applied to quark-hadron transitions, to handle simultaneous electric and color neutrality in color-superconducting matter. The results enable future studies of whether mixed phases can survive in realistic neutron star interiors when surface tension and Coulomb energy are fully accounted for, and suggest that crystalline color-superconducting phases or single charged interfaces may instead be realized.
