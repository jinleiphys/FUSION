# Pole structure and compositeness

> We present in this talk a series of new results on the nature of a bound state or resonance based on the calculation of the expectation values of the number operators of the free particles in the state of interest. In this way, a new universal criterion for the elementariness of a bound state emerges. In the case of large particle wavelengths compared to the range of their interaction, a new closed formula for the compositeness of a bound state in a two-particle continuum is obtained. The extension of these results to resonances with respect to the open channels can be given by making use in addition of suitable phase-factor transformations as also reviewed here. We end with a discussion on the $X(3872)$ as possible double- or triple-pole virtual state, which would be the first case in particle phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new universal criterion for distinguishing elementary from composite bound states or resonances based on expectation values of free-particle number operators, but the compositeness formula for bound states is strictly valid only when particle wavelengths are large compared to the range of their interaction, and for resonances the compositeness can become complex and requires phase-factor transformations to yield a physically interpretable result.

## Method

The author defines compositeness X as the expectation value of the number operator of free-particle constituents in the bound state, divided by the nominal number of constituents, using non-relativistic quantum field theory. For bound states, X is calculated from the half-off-shell T-matrix via Feynman diagrams, yielding an integral expression involving the coupling squared of the bound state. For resonances, the calculation requires analytical continuation to the unphysical Riemann sheet, with an extra term arising from contour deformation, and phase-factor transformations are introduced to obtain real and positive expectation values for open channels.

## Key numbers

- For an S-wave energy-independent potential with parameters v0 and v2, the two terms summing to X=1 are: (1-2γ̄²v2/v0)/(1-6γ̄²v2/v0) - (4γ̄²v2/v0)/(1-6γ̄²v2/v0), where γ̄ = √(-2μE_B)
- For a projected potential with orbital angular momentum ℓ, V(k',k)=vℓ k'^ℓ k^ℓ, the two terms summing to X=1 are: 1/(2ℓ+1) + 2ℓ/(2ℓ+1)
- The closed formula for X in the large-wavelength limit for bound states is: X = g²(ϰ²)μ²/(2πγ̄) + (μ²/(2π)) ∂g²(-γ²)/∂γ|γ=γ̄, where γ̄ = -iϰ and ϰ = √(-2μE_B)
- For resonances, the analogous closed formula is: XℓS = g²(ϰ²)iμ²/(2πϰ) + (iμ²ϰ/π) ∂g²(k²)/∂k²|k=ϰ

## Context

This work extends the foundational approach of Weinberg (1965) on compositeness of shallow bound states, providing a more general field-theoretic derivation and a new closed formula that includes subleading contributions beyond the standard Weinberg formula. The paper also connects to the Morgan pole-counting rule and to the work of Kang and Oller (2017) on near-threshold CDD poles, enabling a new interpretation of the X(3872) as a possible double- or triple-pole virtual state, which would be unprecedented in particle phenomenology.
