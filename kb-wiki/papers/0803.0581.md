# Quark Potential in a Quark-Meson Plasma

> We investigate quark potential by considering meson exchanges in the two flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model at finite temperature and density. There are two kinds of oscillations in the chiral restoration phase, one is the Friedel oscillation due to the sharp quark Fermi surface at high density, and the other is the Yukawa oscillation driven by the complex meson poles at high temperature. The quark-meson plasma is strongly coupled in the temperature region $1\le T/T_c \lesssim 3$ with $T_c$ being the critical temperature of chiral phase transition. The maximum coupling in this region is located at the critical point.

## Key claim
The quark-meson plasma is strongly coupled in the temperature region \(1 \le T/T_c \lesssim 3\), with the maximum coupling located at the critical point of chiral phase transition; however, the saturation temperature \(T/T_c = 3\) may be beyond the effective range of the NJL model, so the reliable conclusion is only that the plasma above and close to the critical point is strongly coupled.

## Method
The authors used the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with Pauli-Villars regularization to compute the static light quark potential mediated by pion and sigma exchanges in the random phase approximation (RPA). The potential was decomposed into vacuum cut, matter cut, and pole contributions, with meson masses and widths determined by solving the complex pole equation for the meson propagator. The ratio of quark potential to quark kinetic energy was then evaluated as a function of chemical potential at fixed temperature to locate the strongest coupling.

## Key numbers
- The critical temperature \(T_c\) for chiral phase transition in chiral limit is about 300 MeV; in a three-momentum non-covariant cutoff scheme, \(T_c = 170\) MeV.
- The vacuum quark mass is \(M = 376\) MeV, pion mass \(M_\pi = 138\) MeV, sigma mass \(M_\sigma = 760\) MeV, and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 94\) MeV.
- At high temperature \(T/T_c = 3\), the ratio of meson width to mass \(\Gamma_m/M_m\) is only about 10%.
- The critical chemical potential at \(T = 130\) MeV is \(\mu_c = 400\) MeV.
- The quark potential saturates for \(T/T_c \gtrsim 3\), indicating the upper bound of the strong coupling region.
- The ratio of quark potential to kinetic energy peaks at the critical point of chiral phase transition at \(T = 130\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of quark potential via meson exchanges in the NJL model, building on prior calculations of static meson correlation functions at finite temperature (Florkowski, 1997) and density (Florkowski, 1998). It contrasts with lattice QCD results for heavy quark potentials and the extracted dissociation temperature of \(J/\Psi\) at about \(2.7 T_c\) (Shuryak, 2004). The finding of a strong coupling region \(1 \le T/T_c \lesssim 3\) agrees with the widely accepted range for the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma from RHIC data, and the peak of the potential-to-kinetic-energy ratio at the critical point corresponds to the well-known minimum of the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio. The authors note that future work should include vector meson exchanges and the Polyakov loop extension (PNJL) to incorporate deconfinement effects.
