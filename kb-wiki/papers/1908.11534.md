# Review of predictions of hard probes in $p+$Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ and 8.16 TeV and comparison with data

> Predictions have been compiled for the $p+$Pb LHC runs, focusing on production of hard probes in cold nuclear matter. These predictions were first made for the $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV $p+$Pb run and were later compared to the available data. A similar set of predictions were published for the 8.16~TeV $p+$Pb run. A selection of the predictions are reviewed here.

## Key claim

This review compiles predictions for hard probe production in minimum-bias \(p+\)Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) and 8.16 TeV, comparing them to available data to assess cold nuclear matter effects; the abstract omits that the predictions are limited to a selection of results and that the comparisons often show large theoretical uncertainties, particularly from nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), which prevent definitive conclusions.

## Method

The paper reviews predictions for hard probes (quarkonium, heavy flavor, dijets, gauge bosons, top quarks) in \(p+\)Pb collisions at two LHC energies, comparing them to data from ALICE, LHCb, CMS, and ATLAS. The predictions employ collinear factorization with nPDF sets (EPS09, nCTEQ, EPPS16), a color evaporation model for \(\Upsilon\) production, data-driven parameterizations for \(B\) mesons, and an energy-loss model; dijet and gauge boson data are used to constrain nPDFs, while top quark production is assessed via a feasibility study and CMS measurements in lepton+jet channels.

## Key numbers

- \(\Upsilon\) nuclear modification factor \(R_{p\mathrm{Pb}}\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 8.16\) TeV: data from ALICE and LHCb show depletion at forward rapidity and low \(p_T\), with all nPDF calculations within the data uncertainties.
- \(B^+\) meson \(R_{p\mathrm{Pb}}\) at 8.16 TeV: LHCb data on non-prompt \(J/\psi\) and reconstructed \(B^+\) are most consistent with nPDF calculations (EPS09 LO, EPS09 NLO, nCTEQ), while Cronin and energy-loss models are inconsistent.
- Dijet data from CMS at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV: leading jet \(p_T > 120\) GeV, subleading jet \(p_T > 30\) GeV; the EPPS16 nPDF set gives a superior description over EPS09 when compared across five \(p_T^{\mathrm{ave}}\) bins (55–75, 75–95, 95–115, 115–150, >150 GeV).
- Gauge boson production: \(W^\pm\) and \(Z^0\) data at 5.02 TeV were included in the EPPS16 global analysis; new \(W^\pm\) measurements at 8.16 TeV show EPPS16 gives a better description than older sets like nCTEQ.
- Top quark production in \(p+\)Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.16\) TeV: CMS reported a total cross section in the lepton+jet channels (\(e+\)jets, \(\mu+\)jets, \(l+\)jets) with uncertainties larger than in \(p+p\) due to shorter run time and nPDF uncertainties.

## Context

This review extends the compilation work of Vogt and collaborators (Pred1, Pred2, Pred3) for \(p+\)Pb collisions at the LHC, comparing predictions to data from ALICE, LHCb, CMS, and ATLAS. It highlights the first inclusion of LHC data (dijets, gauge bosons) in the EPPS16 nPDF set by Eskola et al., which improves flavor separation and extends the fit range in \(x\) and \(Q^2\), but notes that gluon nPDFs remain poorly constrained at low \(x\). The results enable future global analyses with 8.16 TeV data and motivate higher-statistics measurements to reduce uncertainties, particularly for top quark production and gluon-dominated processes.
