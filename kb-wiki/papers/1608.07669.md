# Effects of proton angular momentum alignment on the two-shears-like mechanism in $^{101}$Pd

> The recently observed possible antimagnetic rotation band in $^{101}$Pd is investigated by the cranked shell model with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving method, in which the blocking effects are taken into account exactly. The experimental moments of inertia and reduced $B(E2)$ transition probabilities and their variations with the rotational frequency $ω$ are well reproduced. By analyzing the $ω$-dependence of the occupation probability of each cranked Nilsson orbital near the Fermi surface and the contributions of valence orbitals in each major shell to the total angular momentum alignment, the upbending mechanism of $νh_{11/2}$ in $^{101}$Pd is understood clearly. The proton angular momentum alignment and its influence on the two-shears-like mechanism are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the upbending in the possible antimagnetic rotation band of \(^{101}\)Pd, previously attributed to alignment of a neutron \(g_{7/2}\) pair, is instead mainly caused by the rearrangement of proton occupations in \(g_{9/2}\) orbitals, with the proton angular momentum alignment playing an important role in the two-shears-like mechanism.

## Method

The cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method was used, in which the Hamiltonian is solved directly in a truncated Fock space so that particle number is conserved and Pauli blocking effects are taken into account exactly. Adiabatic calculations were performed for the \(\nu 1/2^-[550]\) band in \(^{101}\)Pd using Nilsson parameters and deformation parameters \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.125\) and \(\varepsilon_4 = -0.02\) from the literature, with a CMPC truncation space of about 1000 dimensions for both protons and neutrons and effective pairing strengths \(G_{\rm p} = G_{\rm n} = 0.6\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic moments of inertia \(J^{(1)}\) for the \(\nu 1/2^-[550]\) band in \(^{101}\)Pd are well reproduced by PNC-CSM with pairing, except for the last two data points; the sharp backbending occurs at \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.65\) MeV.
- The upbending in the MOI's and the quick drop of \(B(E2)\) values occur around \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.45\) MeV.
- The calculated \(B(E2)\) values at higher rotational frequency \(\hbar\omega > 0.45\) MeV are reproduced well regardless of whether pairing is included.
- The occupation probability of the proton orbital \(\pi 9/2^+[404]\) drops from 1.0 to about 0.5, and \(\pi 7/2^+[413]\) drops from 0.5 to nearly zero, at \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.45\) MeV.
- The occupation probability of the neutron orbital \(\nu 3/2^+[422]\) decreases slowly from 0.5 to nearly 0.2 as \(\hbar\omega\) increases from about 0.3 MeV to 0.5 MeV.
- The CMPC truncation energies are about 1.0\(\hbar\omega_0\) for protons and 0.9\(\hbar\omega_0\) for neutrons, with \(\hbar\omega_{\rm 0p}=8.542\) MeV and \(\hbar\omega_{\rm 0n}=9.065\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM investigation of antimagnetic rotation bands previously applied to \(^{105}\)Cd and \(^{106}\)Cd (Zhang 2013) to the nucleus \(^{101}\)Pd, which has four proton \(g_{9/2}\) holes instead of two, leading to stronger pairing correlations. The results contradict the earlier interpretation by Sugawara (2012, 2015) that the upbending in this band is due to alignment of a \(\nu g_{7/2}\) neutron pair; instead, the present calculation shows the upbending is mainly from proton \(g_{9/2}\) orbitals. This enables a refined understanding of the two-shears-like mechanism in nuclei with more proton holes and stronger pairing.
