# The Complex Energy Method Applied to the Nd Scattering with a Model Three-Body Force

> Using the complex energy method, the problem of nucleon-deuteron scattering is solved with a simple three-body force having a separable form. Our results are compared with the results of modern direct two-variable calculations and a good agreement is found. This forms a firm base for other applications of the complex energy method.

## Key claim

The complex energy method applied to nucleon-deuteron scattering with a simple separable three-body force yields numerical results that agree with standard two-variable calculations to within 1%, provided the full three-body force t-matrix is used rather than the approximation \(t_4 \approx V_4\).

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equation for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering using a separable Yamaguchi potential for the two-nucleon interaction and a separable S-wave phenomenological three-body force. The three-body force t-matrix was obtained analytically via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and the resulting modified Amado-Lovelace equation was solved using the complex energy method. Results were compared with the standard Bochum-Krakow (BK) approach, which solves an auxiliary equation for the three-body operator \(T\) without explicitly computing the three-body force t-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy with the Yamaguchi potential alone: -13.0 MeV (experimental value: -8.48 MeV)
- Doublet scattering length without three-body force: \(^2a = -2.17\) fm (experimental value: \(0.65 \pm 0.04\) fm)
- Quartet scattering length without three-body force: \(^4a = 6.86\) fm (experimental value: \(6.35 \pm 0.02\) fm)
- Three-body force parameters used for calculations: \(\Lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(\lambda_4 = 16520\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\)
- Elastic nucleon-deuteron differential cross section calculated at \(E_{lab} = 14.1\) MeV
- The approximation \(t_4 \approx V_4\) gives elastic cross sections at forward angles 3-4% smaller than the full calculation with \(t_4\)
- The two calculational schemes agree with each other to within 1%

## Context

This work extends the complex energy method previously applied to realistic interactions like the CD Bonn and Argonne V14 potentials (Kamada) to a simple separable three-body force. It corrects an earlier calculation by Oryu that approximated the three-body force t-matrix as \(t_4 \approx V_4\). The results demonstrate that the complex energy method can be used reliably in few-nucleon calculations, enabling future applications to three-dimensional treatments of two- and three-nucleon systems (Golak).
