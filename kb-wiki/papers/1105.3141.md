# Spectral Singularities and Zero Energy Bound States

> Single particle scattering around zero energy is re-analysed in view of recent experiments with ultra-cold atoms, nano-structures and nuclei far from the stability valley. For non-zero orbital angular momentum the low energy scattering cross section exhibits dramatic changes depending on the occurrence of either a near resonance or a bound state or the situation in between, that is a bound state at zero energy. Such state is singular in that it has an infinite scattering length, behaves for the eigenvalues but not for the eigenfunctions as an exceptional point and has no pole in the scattering function. These results should be observable whenever the interaction or scattering length can be controlled.

# Spectral Singularities and Zero Energy Bound States

## Key claim

For non-zero orbital angular momentum, a bound state at exactly zero energy exhibits a spectral singularity that behaves like an exceptional point for the eigenvalues but not for the eigenfunctions or scattering function; the cross section becomes constant at low energy rather than following the usual power-law dependence, but this only occurs when the potential strength is tuned precisely to produce a zero-energy eigenvalue.

## Method

The authors analyze single-particle scattering for a radial potential that falls off faster than \(r^{-2}\), using the analytic properties of the scattering function \(\exp 2i\delta_l\) in the complex \(k\)-plane. They study the motion of resonance poles as the potential strength is varied through the critical value that produces a bound state at \(k=0\), and illustrate the general results with explicit expansions for a square well of width \(\pi\) with \(l=1\) (p-wave).

## Key numbers

- For \(l=1\) at the critical potential strength \(v_0=1\) (square well of width \(\pi\)), the scattering function expansion around \(k=0\) is \(\exp 2i\delta_1 = 1 - i\frac{4}{3}\pi k + O(k^2)\), giving a constant low-energy cross section.
- For \(\epsilon = \pm 10^{-2}\) away from the critical potential, the low-energy cross section rises as \(\sim E^2\) rather than remaining constant.
- The eigenvalue expansion for the square well with \(l=1\) gives \(k_1 = +i(\sqrt{\epsilon}/\sqrt{3} + \pi\epsilon/9 + O(\epsilon^{3/2}))\) and \(k_2 = -i(\sqrt{\epsilon}/\sqrt{3} - \pi\epsilon/9 + O(\epsilon^{3/2}))\).
- For \(l>1\), the leading power of \(\epsilon\) in the eigenvalue expansion persists, while the scattering function expansion becomes \(\sim k^{2l-1}\) instead of linear in \(k\).

## Context

This work extends the known fact that a zero-energy bound state has infinite scattering length (Newton, 1960) by identifying the square-root singularity in the potential strength as a spectral singularity distinct from a genuine exceptional point. The results connect to experiments with ultra-cold atoms using Feshbach resonance techniques (BEC), to halo nuclei at the nuclear drip line (Nazmitdinov, 2010), and to the behavior of Fano-Feshbach resonances in nano-structures (Flach, 2008). The findings enable interpretation of cross section measurements near threshold in trapped fermion and boson gases, and provide a framework for understanding weakly bound nuclei with larger angular momenta.
