# Exact-exchange density functional theory for neutron drops

> We compute the ground-state properties of finite systems of neutrons in an external harmonic trap, interacting via the Minnesota potential, using the "exact-exchange" form of orbital-dependent density functional theory. We compare our results with Hartree-Fock calculations and find very close agreement. Within the context of the interaction studied, we conclude that this simple orbital-dependent functional brings conventional nuclear density functional theory to the level of Hartree-Fock in an ab initio fashion. Our work is a first step towards higher-order ab initio nuclear functionals based on realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The authors show that the exact-exchange (EXX) form of orbital-dependent density functional theory reproduces Hartree-Fock results for neutron drops interacting via the Minnesota potential to within 0.1% or better, but they explicitly note that this agreement depends on the interaction and that HF itself is a poor description of real nuclei due to the non-perturbative nature of the nuclear interaction at short distances.

## Method

The authors implemented the Kohn-Sham (KS) density functional theory using the exact-exchange (EXX) functional, which consists of the Hartree-Fock energy expression evaluated with KS orbitals rather than HF orbitals. They solved the Optimized Effective Potential (OEP) integral equation using the iterative Kummel-Perdew algorithm to determine the local KS potential, expanding the KS orbitals in a harmonic oscillator basis. The method was applied to systems of 8 and 20 neutrons in an external harmonic trap interacting via the semi-realistic Minnesota potential, and results were compared with pure Hartree-Fock calculations.

## Key numbers

- For 8 neutrons at trap frequency hbar Omega = 10 MeV, HF total energy = 142.43 MeV and EXX total energy = 142.44 MeV, with r.m.s. radii of 2.7484 fm and 2.7478 fm respectively
- For 20 neutrons at trap frequency hbar Omega = 10 MeV, HF total energy = 456.25 MeV and EXX total energy = 456.26 MeV, with r.m.s. radii of 3.1246 fm and 3.1240 fm respectively
- For 8 neutrons at trap frequency hbar Omega = 3 MeV, HF total energy = 44.51 MeV and EXX total energy = 44.51 MeV, with r.m.s. radii of 4.8927 fm and 4.8921 fm respectively
- For 20 neutrons at trap frequency hbar Omega = 3 MeV, HF total energy = 143.52 MeV and EXX total energy = 143.52 MeV, with r.m.s. radii of 5.5958 fm and 5.5954 fm respectively
- The OEP method at the EXX-DFT level was a factor of 4-9 slower than HF for the basis sizes and particle numbers studied

## Context

This work extends the orbital-dependent DFT methods developed in quantum chemistry (Engel, 2003; Goerling, 1999; Kummel and Perdew, 2003) to the nuclear physics context, where most nuclear functionals are phenomenological Skyrme or Gogny forms fit to stable nuclei. The authors position this as a first step toward constructing ab initio nuclear energy density functionals based on realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions softened by renormalization group transformations (SRG, 2007), with the next steps being inclusion of three-body forces, non-central interactions, and pairing correlations.
