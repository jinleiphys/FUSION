# Wave Function Structure in Two-Body Random Matrix Ensembles

> We study the structure of eigenstates in two-body interaction random matrix ensembles and find significant deviations from random matrix theory expectations. The deviations are most prominent in the tails of the spectral density and indicate localization of the eigenstates in Fock space. Using ideas related to scar theory we derive an analytical formula that relates fluctuations in wave function intensities to fluctuations of the two-body interaction matrix elements. Numerical results for many-body fermion systems agree well with the theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

Wave function intensities in two-body random matrix ensembles deviate significantly from random matrix theory (RMT) expectations, with the strongest deviations occurring at the edges of the spectrum; these deviations indicate Fock-space localization and can be predicted analytically using ideas from scar theory, provided the spectral density is not too far from Gaussian.

## Method

The authors studied eigenstates of a Hamiltonian with random two-body interactions for fermions in a finite single-particle space, computing the inverse participation ratio (IPR) as a function of energy. They derived an analytical formula relating IPR fluctuations to fluctuations in the centroids and widths of the local strength functions of individual Fock states, using a separation of time scales argument adapted from scar theory. Numerical results for systems with and without spin degrees of freedom were compared to the theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers

- For a system of N=6 fermions distributed over M=13 orbitals, the fourth cumulant of the spectral density divided by the square of the second cumulant equals -0.7, closer to the semicircle value of -1 than to the Gaussian value of 0.
- In the dilute many-particle limit (M >> N >> 1), the IPR enhancement near the ground state is given by IPR(E_gs)/IPR_RMT - 1 = (4/M^2)[N ln(M/N) + 2 ln^2(M/N)].
- For the simple two-body model without spin, the squared width of the full spectrum is E_0^2 = 2 * C(N,2) * [1 + 2(M-N) + C(M-N,2)], where C denotes binomial coefficients.
- The variance in the centroid is given by <(delta c_b)^2> = C(N,2), and the variance in the width is <(delta v_b)^2> = 2 E_0^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of wave function structure in two-body random matrix ensembles, which had shown that ground states of shell model Hamiltonians with random two-body interactions favor certain quantum numbers (Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean, 1998). The results are in qualitative agreement with nuclear shell model calculations using realistic interactions (Zelevinsky, 1996; Whitehead et al., 1978; Verbaarschot and Brussaard, 1979; Brown and Bertsch, 1984) and with calculations in atomic physics (Flambaum et al., 1994). The analytical formula derived here enables quantitative predictions for wave function localization in many-body fermion systems without requiring explicit diagonalization, and can be applied to systems with spin degrees of freedom where the spectral density is well approximated by a Gaussian.
