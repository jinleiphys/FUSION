# A possible evidence of observation of two mixed phases in nuclear collisions

> Using an advanced version of the hadron resonance gas model we have found several remarkable irregularities at chemical freeze-out. The most prominent of them are two sets of highly correlated quasi-plateaus in the collision energy dependence of the entropy per baryon, total pion number per baryon, and thermal pion number per baryon which we found at center of mass energies 3.6-4.9 GeV and 7.6-10 GeV. The low energy set of quasi-plateaus was predicted a long time ago. On the basis of the generalized shock-adiabat model we demonstrate that the low energy correlated quasi-plateaus give evidence for the anomalous thermodynamic properties of the mixed phase at its boundary to the quark-gluon plasma. The question is whether the high energy correlated quasi-plateaus are also related to some kind of mixed phase. In order to answer this question we employ the results of a systematic meta-analysis of the quality of data description of 10 existing event generators of nucleus-nucleus collisions in the range of center of mass collision energies from 3.1 GeV to 17.3 GeV. These generators are divided into two groups: the first group includes the generators which account for the quark-gluon plasma formation during nuclear collisions, while the second group includes the generators which do not assume the quark-gluon plasma formation in such collisions. Comparing the quality of data description of more than a hundred of different data sets of strange hadrons by these two groups of generators, we find two regions of the equal quality of data description which are located at the center of mass collision energies 4.3-4.9 GeV and 10.-13.5 GeV. These two regions of equal quality of data description we interpret as regions of the hadron-quark-gluon mixed phase formation.

# A possible evidence of observation of two mixed phases in nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The paper claims that two distinct sets of correlated quasi-plateaus in the collision energy dependence of entropy per baryon and pion number per baryon, found at center-of-mass energies 3.6–4.9 GeV and 7.6–10 GeV, provide evidence for the formation of two separate hadron-quark-gluon mixed phases in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The authors hedge that it remains unclear whether these two regions belong to the same mixed phase or represent distinct phase transitions, and that the high-energy set requires further experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors used an advanced version of the hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) with multicomponent hard-core repulsion, assigning individual hard-core radii to pions, kaons, Lambda hyperons, other mesons, and baryons, to fit 111 independent hadron yield ratios measured at midrapidity in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at 14 collision energies from 2.7 to 200 GeV. They then performed a systematic meta-analysis of the quality of data description (QDD) of 10 existing event generators, divided into two groups (those assuming quark-gluon plasma formation and those not), comparing their ability to describe over a hundred data sets of strange hadrons across collision energies from 3.1 to 17.3 GeV. The QDD was quantified as the mean deviation squared per data point, arithmetically averaged over hadron species and model types.

## Key numbers

- The best global fit of 111 independent hadronic multiplicities was achieved with chi-squared per degree of freedom approximately 0.95, using hard-core radii: R_b = 0.355 fm, R_m = 0.4 fm, R_pi = 0.1 fm, R_K = 0.37 fm, R_Lambda = 0.11 fm.
- Two sets of correlated quasi-plateaus in entropy per baryon, total pion number per baryon, and thermal pion number per baryon were found at center-of-mass energies 3.6–4.9 GeV and 7.6–10 GeV.
- The meta-analysis identified two regions of equal quality of data description between hadron gas models and quark-gluon plasma models at center-of-mass energies 4.3–4.9 GeV and 10–13.5 GeV.
- A sharp peak of the trace anomaly delta = (epsilon - 3p)/T^4 was found at center-of-mass energy 4.9 GeV, with a second less pronounced peak at 9.2 GeV.
- The Lambda/p ratio shows a slope increase at 4.3 GeV and saturation above 8.8 GeV; the K+/Lambda ratio shows slope changes at the same energies.
- The Delta Lambda / Delta p ratio (Lambda - anti-Lambda)/(p - anti-p) shows a strong jump in the narrow interval 4.3–4.9 GeV and a slope change at 9.2 GeV.
- The most probable collision energy range to probe the QCD phase diagram (tri)critical endpoint is estimated as 12–14 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier prediction by Bugaev (1989–1991) that low-energy quasi-plateaus signal anomalous thermodynamic properties of the quark-gluon-hadron mixed phase. It builds on the multicomponent HRGM developed by Oliinychenko (2012), Sagun (2013), and colleagues, which first successfully described the problematic K+/pi+ and Lambda/pi- ratios. The meta-analysis method was introduced in a separate work by the same group (2015). The results enable future experiments at RHIC Beam Energy Scan, NICA, and FAIR to search for mixed phase signals, particularly using the newly proposed Delta Lambda / Delta p ratio as a sensitive observable. The paper contrasts with approaches that do not assume QGP formation, such as ARC, RQMD, HSD, and UrQMD models, versus those that do, such as the Quark Combination model, 3-fluid dynamics model, PHSD, and Core-Corona model.
