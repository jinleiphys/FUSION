# Photoproduction of eta-mesons on the deuteron above S11(1535) in the presence of a narrow P11(1670) resonance

> Incoherent photoproduction of eta-mesons on the deuteron is considered. The main attention is paid to the region above the S11(1535) resonance where rather narrow resonance like structure in the total cross section extracted for gamma n -> eta n has been reported. The corresponding experimental results are analyzed from the phenomenological standpoint within the model containing a baryon P11 with the mass about 1670 MeV and a width less than 30 MeV. This resonance was suggested in some recent works as a nonstrange member of the pentaquark antidecuplet with J^P=1/2^+. The calculation is also performed for the polarized and nonpolarized angular distributions of $η$ mesons. In addition, we present our predictions for the cross sections of the neutral kaons and double pion photoproduction, where the same narrow P11(1670) resonance is assumed to contribute through the decay into K^0 Lambda and pi Delta configuration.

# Photoproduction of eta-mesons on the deuteron above S11(1535) in the presence of a narrow P11(1670) resonance

## Key claim

The narrow resonance-like structure observed in the total cross section for gamma n -> eta n around W = 1670 MeV can be explained by a hypothetical narrow P11(1670) baryon with a width of 10-30 MeV, proposed as a nonstrange member of the pentaquark antidecuplet, but only if the Fermi motion in the deuteron broadens the observed peak by about a factor of 7 relative to the free nucleon width.

## Method

The authors calculated incoherent photoproduction of eta-mesons on the deuteron using a production operator valid in an arbitrary frame, with the elementary gamma N -> eta N amplitude taken from two variants of the EtaMAID model: model (i) with a strong D15(1675) resonance and model (ii) with a modified reggeized amplitude containing an additional narrow P11(1670) resonance. Final state interactions (FSI) in the NN and eta N subsystems were included up to first order in the two-body t-matrices, and the effects of Fermi motion were treated by folding the elementary amplitudes with the deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- The P11(1670) resonance is assigned a mass of 1670 MeV and a total width of either 10 MeV or 30 MeV, with branching ratios beta_etaN = 40%, beta_piDelta = 30%, and beta_KLambda = 30%
- The neutron photocoupling of P11(1670) is taken as _nA_{1/2} = 30 (or 35) x 10^{-3}/sqrt(GeV), while the proton photocoupling is _pA_{1/2} = 8 x 10^{-3}/sqrt(GeV)
- At the resonance position W = W_R, the estimated partial cross sections for the P11(1670) with Gamma_tot = 10 MeV are: sigma_eta n ≈ 15 microbarn, sigma_K0Lambda ≈ 11 microbarn, sigma_pi+pi-n ≈ 6.7 microbarn, sigma_pi-pi0n ≈ 2.7 microbarn
- The Fermi motion broadens the P11 peak by about a factor of 7 compared to the free nucleon width, while the peak position remains unchanged
- In the strong D15 model (i), the D15(1675) has an eta N branching ratio of 17% of Gamma_tot, while in the reggeized model (ii) this is reduced to 0.7%

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological analysis of eta photoproduction on neutrons, where preliminary data from GRAAL (Slava), CB-ELSA (Jaegle 2005, Krusche 2006), and LNS-Tohoku (kas) showed a peak around E_gamma = 1 GeV corresponding to W = 1670 MeV. The paper addresses the discrepancy that the standard EtaMAID2001 model (etaMAID) requires a D15(1675) eta N decay width of 17% of Gamma_tot to fit the data, which is an order of magnitude larger than the PDG listing and exceeds SU(3) symmetry bounds (Polyakov 2006). The narrow P11(1670) was suggested by VPI2 and DP03 as an alternative nonstrange member of the pentaquark antidecuplet, resolving the mass and width inconsistencies of the previously proposed P11(1710). The results enable future experimental searches for this resonance in eta, kaon, and double pion photoproduction channels, particularly on neutron targets.
