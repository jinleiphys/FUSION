# Shell structure and pairing for interacting fermions in a trap

> The shell structures for weakly interacting fermions in harmonic oscillator traps at zero temperature undergo several transitions depending on the number of particles in the trap and their interaction strength. Calculations of the one and two particle spectra give the pairing gaps, and the many particle state is constructed by the seniority scheme. For sufficiently few particles, $N\la 10^4$, the pairing field exceeds the mean-field splitting of single particle levels at the Fermi surface and the greater number of almost degenerate states due to the SU(3) symmetry in the harmonic oscillator shell lead to {\it supergaps}. Deformation and rotation are also discussed.

# Shell structure and pairing for interacting fermions in a trap

## Key claim
For weakly interacting fermions in harmonic oscillator traps at zero temperature, when the number of particles is sufficiently small (N ≲ 10³), the pairing field exceeds the mean-field splitting of single-particle levels at the Fermi surface, leading to enhanced "supergaps" due to the SU(3) symmetry of the harmonic oscillator shell; this condition is quantified as n_F ≲ 64/5.

## Method
The authors treat a dilute gas of N fermionic atoms in a spherical harmonic oscillator potential at zero temperature, interacting via an attractive s-wave scattering length a < 0. They calculate single-particle energies perturbatively from the mean-field potential, compute pairing gaps for two particles coupled to total angular momentum L using the delta-function interaction, and construct the many-particle state using the seniority scheme from nuclear pairing theory. The transition from pairing within a single l-multiplet to pairing across the full SU(3)-symmetric shell (superpairing) is determined by comparing the pairing field to the mean-field splitting and by solving the gap equation within a shell.

## Key numbers
- Fermi energy for non-interacting system: E_F ≈ (3N)^(1/3) ℏω
- Mean-field splitting of single-particle levels: ε_{n_F,l} - (n_F + 3/2)ℏω = (4√2/3π)(a/a_osc) n_F^(3/2) ℏω F(n_F,l), where F(n_F,l) ≈ 4/(3π) - (1/4π) l(l+1)/n_F²
- Supergap G = (32√2)/(15π²) n_F^(1/2) (|a|/a_osc) ℏω
- Condition for superpairing: n_F ≲ 64/5, corresponding to N ≲ 10³ trapped particles
- For n_F ≳ (64/5) ln(n_F), equivalent to n_F ≳ 50, the pairing gap approaches that for a single l level
- Pairing energies in two-dimensional traps: Δ^(2D)(S,M) = (|a|/a_3) ℏω_⊥ × (1/2^(2S)) × [(S-M)!(S+M)!] / [((S-M)/2)! ((S+M)/2)!]², independent of n_F

## Context
This work extends nuclear pairing theory (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975) to dilute fermionic atoms in harmonic traps, where the SU(3) symmetry of the oscillator potential creates larger degeneracies than in nuclei. The authors show that for sufficiently few particles, the pairing gaps are enhanced by a factor n_F compared to pairing within a single l-multiplet, a regime accessible in current cold-atom experiments with ⁶Li atoms near Feshbach resonances (Feshbach, 1993). The results enable predictions for experimental observation of superfluidity through response functions (Bruun, 2001), the scissors mode (Marago, 2001), or reduced moment of inertia in rotating deformed traps, and suggest superpairing favors insulator states over conductor states in optical lattices (optical, 2001).
