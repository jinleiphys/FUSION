# Pseudo-gauge invariant non-equilibrium density operator

> We obtain a form of the local thermodynamic equilibrium density operator which is invariant under pseudo-gauge transformations of the stress-energy and the spin tensors. This operator is an excellent candidate to describe the dynamics of a system which is assumed to achieve local equilibrium from a pseudo-gauge invariant quantum state, a situation which is believed to occur, for instance, in nuclear collisions at very high energy. As a consequence of pseudo-gauge invariance, the ambiguity affecting the predictions of mean values of observables from a local equilibrium state can be removed.

## Key claim

The authors derive a form of the local thermodynamic equilibrium density operator that is invariant under pseudo-gauge transformations of the stress-energy and spin tensors, thereby removing the ambiguity that previously affected predictions of mean values of observables from a local equilibrium state. The key condition is that the reduced spin potential must coincide with thermal vorticity, and an additional term involving thermal shear appears that was not previously considered.

## Method

The authors start from the most general linear form of the local equilibrium density operator in terms of the stress-energy and spin tensor operators, with unknown vector and tensor fields. They impose pseudo-gauge invariance by requiring that the variation of the operator under a pseudo-gauge transformation vanishes for arbitrary choices of the super-potential and spatial hypersurface, which yields conditions relating the unknown fields to derivatives of the four-temperature vector. The resulting operator is then compared with the previously known form and evaluated in the Belinfante pseudo-gauge.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-gauge invariant local equilibrium density operator is given by equation (solution): $\wrhol = \frac{1}{Z} \exp \left[ - \int_\Sigma \di \Sigma_\mu \left( \wT^{\mu\nu} \beta_\nu - \frac{1}{2} \varpi_{\lambda\nu} \wspt^{\mu,\lambda\nu} - \xi_{\lambda\nu} \wspt^{\lambda,\mu\nu} \right)\right]$, where $\varpi$ is thermal vorticity and $\xi$ is thermal shear.
- In the Belinfante pseudo-gauge, the operator simplifies to $\wrhol = \frac{1}{Z} \exp \left[ - \int_\Sigma \di \Sigma_\mu \; \wT_B^{\mu\nu} \beta_\nu \right]$, where $\wT_B$ is the Belinfante stress-energy tensor.
- At global equilibrium, where $\beta$ becomes a Killing vector, the thermal shear vanishes and the operator reduces to the global equilibrium form $\wrho_{\rm GE} = \frac{1}{Z} \exp \left[- b_\mu \wP^\mu + \frac{1}{2} \varpi_{\mu\nu} \wJ^{\mu\nu} \right]$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pseudo-gauge dependence in spin physics for relativistic heavy ion collisions, previously studied by Becattini (2018), Buzzegoli (2021), and others, who showed that different pseudo-gauge choices have quantitatively significant effects on spin polarization calculations. The result resolves the inconsistency that the local equilibrium density operator used in hydrodynamic models was pseudo-gauge dependent while the actual quantum state of colliding nuclei is not, and it implies that previously obtained results in the Belinfante pseudo-gauge are the correct pseudo-gauge invariant ones. The method does not extend to conserved charge currents, where the transformation involves an anti-symmetric tensor that only vanishes under restricted conditions.
