# Small-$x$ factorisation in the target fragmentation region

> We consider the differential cross-section for single-inclusive jet production with transverse momentum $P_\perp$ in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) at small Bjorken $x_{\rm Bj}$, mediated by a virtual photon with virtuality $Q^2$. Unlike most studies at small $x$, which focus on particle production in the current fragmentation region, we investigate the kinematic regime where the jet is produced in the target fragmentation hemisphere of the Breit frame, and with $P_\perp \ll Q$. For a longitudinally polarised photon, we demonstrate that this cross-section is not power suppressed in $P_\perp/Q$ and we derive a factorised expression in terms of extended quark and gluon jet fracture functions. Our formula, valid at next-to-leading order in $α_s$ at small $x$, is akin to the Altarelli-Martinelli identity for the longitudinal DIS structure function. Numerical estimates show that the extended quark jet fracture function is the most sensitive to saturation effects in large nuclei.

# Small-$x$ factorisation in the target fragmentation region

## Key claim

The single-inclusive jet cross-section in Deep Inelastic Scattering mediated by a longitudinally polarised photon is not power-suppressed by $P_\perp/Q$ when the jet is produced in the target fragmentation hemisphere of the Breit frame, contrary to the usual expectation that longitudinal photon contributions are power-suppressed. This leading-power contribution emerges only at next-to-leading order in $\alpha_s$ and factorises in terms of extended quark and gluon jet fracture functions, analogous to the Altarelli-Martinelli identity for the longitudinal structure function $F_L$.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross-section for single-inclusive jet production with transverse momentum $P_\perp$ in Deep Inelastic Scattering at small Bjorken $x_{\rm Bj}$, mediated by a virtual photon with virtuality $Q^2$, in the kinematic regime $P_\perp \ll Q$ and with the jet produced in the target fragmentation hemisphere. The calculation is performed in the Colour Glass Condensate effective field theory in the dipole frame, extracting the leading-power contribution from the next-to-leading order formula valid for general jet kinematics at small $x$, and deriving factorised expressions for the longitudinal structure function $F_{UU,L}$ in terms of extended quark and gluon jet fracture functions.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order contribution to $F_{UU,L}$ is $\mathcal{O}(P_\perp^2/Q^2)$, power-suppressed in the ratio $P_\perp/Q$.
- The next-to-leading order contribution to $F_{UU,L}$ for the quark-jet channel is given by Eq. (14): $F^{\textrm{NLO,LP-}q}_{UU,L} = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \sum_{i=q}e_i^2 \times \frac{N_c}{4\pi^4}\int_{\Bt,\qt}\mathcal{D}_F(x,\qt)\left[1-\frac{\Pt\cdot(\Pt-\qt)}{P_\perp^2-(\Pt-\qt)^2}\ln\left(\frac{P_\perp^2}{(\Pt-\qt)^2}\right)\right]$, which is leading-twist (not suppressed by $P_\perp/Q$).
- The gluon-jet contribution to $F_{UU,L}$ at next-to-leading order and leading power is $F_{UU,L}^{\textrm{NLO,LP-}g}= \frac{\alpha_s }{3\pi} \sum_{i=q}e_i^2 \times x\mathcal{F}_g(x,\Pt)$, where the gluon extended jet fracture function $x\mathcal{F}_g$ is given by Eq. (17) and contains a term $\ln(1/x) q_\perp^2/P_\perp^2$.
- In the dilute limit $P_\perp \gg q_\perp \sim Q_s$, the gluon extended fracture function reduces to $x\mathcal{F}_g(x,\Pt) \approx \frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi^2}\frac{1}{P_\perp^2}\int_{x}^1\der\xi P_{gg}(\xi)xg(x,P_\perp^2)$, up to $Q_s^4/P_\perp^4$ corrections.
- Numerical estimates use a McLerran-Venugopalan model with $Q_{s,p}^2=0.2$ GeV$^2$ for the bare proton saturation scale at $x=0.01$, $\Lambda=0.24$ GeV, and $A^{1/3}=5$ for a large nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the study of semi-inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering from the current fragmentation region, where transverse-momentum-dependent factorisation theorems have been established by Collins (1992), Ji (2004), and Collins (2011), into the target fragmentation region at small $x$. It builds on the fracture function formalism of Trentadue and Veneziano (1993), Grazzini (1997), and Collins (1997) for the Bjorken limit, and provides the first small-$x$ computation of these objects in the Colour Glass Condensate. The result is analogous to the Altarelli-Martinelli identity (1978) for $F_L$ but at the transverse-momentum-dependent level and at two loops in collinear power counting. This work enables new phenomenological studies of gluon saturation at the Electron-Ion Collider through jet production in the target fragmentation region, complementary to diffractive Deep Inelastic Scattering studies by Wusthoff (1997), Golec-Biernat (1999), and Hautmann (1999).
