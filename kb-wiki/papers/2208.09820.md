# Open Charm mesons in magnetized nuclear matter -- effects of (inverse) magnetic catalysis

> The in-medium masses of the open charm ($D$ and $\bar D$) mesons in magnetized isospin asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated within a chiral effective model, including the contributions of the Dirac sea (DS) to the self-energies of the nucleons. Within the model, the masses of these mesons are calculated from their interactions with the nucleons and the scalar ($σ\sim(\langle\bar u u\rangle+\langle \bar d d \rangle)$, $ζ\sim\langle {\bar s}s\rangle$ and $δ\sim(\langle{\bar u} u\rangle-\langle {\bar d} d\rangle)$) fields. The Dirac sea contributions are observed to lead to an enhancement (reduction) of the quark condensates with increase in magnetic field, an effect called `(inverse) magnetic catalysis'. These contributions are observed to appreciably modify the open charm meson masses calculated within the chiral effective model. In the presence of an external magnetic field, there is mixing of the pseudoscalar and the vector mesons (PV mixing), leading to as a drop (increase) in the mass of the pseudoscalar (longitudinal component of the vector) meson. The effects of the (inverse) magnetic catalysis, in addition to the PV mixing and Landau level contribtuions (for charged mesons) are observed to lead to significant modifications to the masses of the open charm mesons. These can modify the decay widths of the charmonium states to open charm mesons, e.g., $ψ(3770)\rightarrow D\bar D$, as well as $D^*\rightarrow Dπ$ (and $\bar {D^*}\rightarrow {\bar D}π$), which can affect the production of the open charm and charmonium states in ultra relativistic peripheral heavy ion collision experiments, where the created magnetic field is huge.

# Open Charm mesons in magnetized nuclear matter -- effects of (inverse) magnetic catalysis

## Key claim

The Dirac sea contributions to nucleon self-energies in magnetized nuclear matter lead to either magnetic catalysis (enhancement of quark condensates) or inverse magnetic catalysis (reduction of quark condensates) depending on whether the anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) of nucleons are neglected or included, and these effects dominate over pseudoscalar-vector mixing in modifying open charm meson masses.

## Method

The authors used a chiral effective model based on a nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry, incorporating scalar fields (sigma, zeta, delta) and vector fields (omega, rho), to calculate the in-medium masses of D and anti-D mesons in magnetized isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. The Dirac sea contributions to nucleon self-energies were included through summation of scalar and vector tadpole diagrams in the weak magnetic field approximation for the nucleon propagator, with and without accounting for the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons. Pseudoscalar-vector (PV) mixing was incorporated via a phenomenological Lagrangian interaction, and for charged mesons, lowest Landau level contributions were added.

## Key numbers

- At zero baryon density and zero magnetic field, the scalar field values are sigma = -93.3 MeV and zeta = -106.6 MeV
- At eB = 3 m_pi^2, sigma becomes -94.537 MeV (without AMMs) and -102.018 MeV (with AMMs); zeta becomes -107.19 MeV (without AMMs) and -109.9 MeV (with AMMs)
- Effective nucleon masses at eB = 3 m_pi^2 are 948 MeV (without AMMs) and 1026 MeV (with AMMs)
- For zero baryon density with AMMs included, solutions for scalar fields do not exist for eB higher than approximately 4 m_pi^2 in the weak field approximation
- At baryon density rho_B = rho_0 (nuclear matter saturation density), the Dirac sea contributions produce the most dominant effect on open charm meson masses, much larger than PV mixing

## Context

This work extends previous studies of open charm mesons in magnetized matter within the chiral effective model that used the "no sea" approximation (Dmeson_mag, 2017; Bmeson_mag, 2018; Charmonium_mag, 2019) by including Dirac sea contributions. It builds on the Walecka model calculations of Haber (2014) and Arghya (2017) that first demonstrated (inverse) magnetic catalysis from Dirac sea effects, and on lattice studies by Bali (2012) showing IMC. The results enable predictions for modifications to decay widths of charmonium states like psi(3770) to D anti-D and D* to D pi in ultra-relativistic peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
