# Dispersion relations for $γ^*γ^*\toππ$: helicity amplitudes, subtractions, and anomalous thresholds

> We present a comprehensive analysis of the dispersion relations for the doubly-virtual process $γ^*γ^*\toππ$. Starting from the Bardeen-Tung-Tarrach amplitudes, we first derive the kernel functions that define the system of Roy-Steiner equations for the partial-wave helicity amplitudes. We then formulate the solution of these partial-wave dispersion relations in terms of Omnès functions, with special attention paid to the role of subtraction constants as critical for the application to hadronic light-by-light scattering. In particular, we explain for the first time why for some amplitudes the standard Muskhelishvili-Omnès solution applies, while for others a modified approach based on their left-hand cut is required unless subtractions are introduced. In the doubly-virtual case, the analytic structure of the vector-resonance partial waves then gives rise to anomalous thresholds, even for space-like virtualities. We develop a strategy to account for these effects in the numerical solution, illustrated in terms of the $D$-waves in $γ^*γ^*\toππ$, which allows us to predict the doubly-virtual responses of the $f_2(1270)$ resonance. In general, our results form the basis for the incorporation of two-meson intermediate states into hadronic light-by-light scattering beyond the $S$-wave contribution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for certain helicity amplitudes of the doubly-virtual process γ*γ* → ππ, the standard Muskhelishvili–Omnès solution fails and a modified approach based on the left-hand cut is required unless subtractions are introduced; this explains why the D-wave resonance f₂(1270) can be understood as an effect of ππ final-state rescattering even when anomalous thresholds appear for space-like virtualities.

## Method

The authors start from the Bardeen–Tung–Tarrach scalar amplitudes and derive the kernel functions defining the system of Roy–Steiner equations for the partial-wave helicity amplitudes. They formulate the solution of these partial-wave dispersion relations in terms of Omnès functions, treating the left-hand cut contributions from vector-meson exchange in a narrow-width approximation. For the doubly-virtual case, they develop a strategy to account for anomalous thresholds in the numerical solution by splitting the anomalous integral into a finite part and a part treated via integration by parts, illustrated for the D-waves.

## Key numbers

- The f₂(1270) resonance appears in the D-waves of γ*γ* → ππ, with its doubly-virtual response predicted by the dispersive framework.
- The vector-meson exchange contributions involve the decay width Γ_{V→πγ} = (α/2) C_V² (M_V² − M_π²)³ / (3 M_V³), where C_V is the coupling in the Lagrangian model.
- The anomalous threshold treatment requires numerical integration over the range from s_cut⁻ to s₊, with the integrand vanishing as a square root for s′ → s₊.
- The exceptional point where the numerical method becomes unstable occurs at q₁² q₂² = (M_V² − M_π²)².

## Context

This work extends the dispersive treatment of γ*γ* → ππ beyond the S-wave contribution previously addressed by Colangelo et al. (2015, 2017), providing the basis for incorporating two-meson intermediate states into hadronic light-by-light scattering for the muon g−2. The results enable a model-independent evaluation of the f₂(1270) contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering, which had previously only been estimated within Lagrangian-based hadronic models as a narrow resonance by Pauk and Vanderhaeghen (2014) and Danilkin et al. (2016). The framework is applicable to data under study at BESIII and potentially at Belle II.
