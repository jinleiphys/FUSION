# Quark-Hadron Duality and Parity Violating Asymmetry of Electroweak Reactions in the Delta Region

> A dynamical model of electroweak pion production reactions in the Delta(1232) region has been extended to include the neutral current contributions for examining the local Quark-Hadron Duality in neutrino-induced reactions and for investigating how the axial N-Delta form factor can be determined by the parity violating asymmetry of N(\vec{e},e') reactions. We first show that the recent data of (e,e') structure functions F_1 and F_2, which exhibit the Quark-Hadron Duality, are in good agreement with our predictions. For possible future experimental tests, we then predict that the structure functions F_1, F_2, and F_3 for (ν,e) and (ν,ν') processes also show the similar Quark-Hadron Duality. The spin dependent structure functions g_1 and g_2 of (e,e') have also been calculated from our model. It is found that the local Quark-Hadron Duality is not seen in the calculated g_1 and g_2, while our results for g_1 and some polarization observables associated with the exclusive p(\vec{e},e' pi) and \vec{p}(\vec{e},e' pi) reactions are in reasonably good agreement with the recent data. In the investigation of parity violating asymmetry A of N(\vec{e},e') reactions, it is found that the non-resonant contribution is small at the Delta peak and a measurement of A can be used to distinguish two previously determined axial N-Delta transition form factors. The predicted asymmetry A are also compared with the Parton Model predictions for future experimental investigations of Quark-Hadron Duality.

## Key claim

The authors extend a dynamical model of electroweak pion production in the Delta(1232) region to include neutral currents, and predict that local Quark-Hadron Duality, previously observed in electron scattering structure functions F1 and F2, should also appear in neutrino-induced reactions, but is not seen in the spin-dependent structure functions g1 and g2. The parity violating asymmetry of N(e,e') reactions at the Delta peak is found to be sensitive to the axial N-Delta form factor, with non-resonant contributions being small at that kinematic point.

## Method

The SL dynamical model, previously developed for electromagnetic and charged-current pion production, was extended to include neutral currents by expressing the electroweak currents in terms of hadronic field operators (nucleon, Delta, pion, rho, and omega mesons) constrained by the isospin structure of the Standard Model. Inclusive structure functions F1, F2, F3, g1, and g2 were calculated by summing over all pion production channels in the Delta region (1.1 GeV ≤ W ≤ 1.4 GeV) using the hadronic amplitudes, and compared with Parton Model predictions at Q² = 10 (GeV/c)² using CTEQ6 parton distribution functions. The parity violating asymmetry A was computed from the interference between electromagnetic and neutral current contributions to the hadron tensor.

## Key numbers

- The axial N-Delta form factor parameters used are: d1(0) = 0.969 × (6/√2/5)g_A, d2(0) = -0.180 × (6/√2/5)g_A, d3(0) = 0.836 × (6/√2/5)g_A with g_A = 1.26, and d4 = 0.
- The axial form factor parametrization uses G^A_{N,Delta}(Q²) = (1 + aQ²)exp(-bQ²)G_A(Q²) with a = 0.154 (GeV/c)^{-2}, b = 0.166 (GeV/c)², and M_A = 1.02 GeV for the nucleon axial form factor.
- The Weinberg angle is sin²θ_W = 0.231 and the CKM coefficient V_ud = 0.974.
- The pion decay constant F = 93 MeV and the axial coupling strength of the nucleon g_A = 1.26.
- Structure functions are calculated at Q² values of 0.7, 1.5, 2.5, and 3.5 (GeV/c)² for the Delta region, and predictions extend to Q² = 20 (GeV/c)².
- The Delta peak (W = 1.232 GeV) at Q² = 1.5 (GeV)² occurs at Nachtmann variable ξ = 0.57.

## Context

This work extends the SL dynamical model of Sato and Lee (2001, 2003) for electromagnetic and charged-current pion production to include neutral currents, building on the observation of Quark-Hadron Duality first reported by Bloom and Gilman (1970) and later confirmed by JLab experiments (Niculescu, Liang). The predictions for neutrino-induced reactions are intended to guide experiments at new neutrino facilities, while the parity violating asymmetry calculations aim to distinguish between two previously determined axial N-Delta form factors from Kitagaki (1990) and Sato and Lee (2003). The results enable future quantitative tests of Quark-Hadron Duality including target mass corrections, and provide a framework for extracting the axial N-Delta form factor from parity violating electron scattering experiments planned at Jefferson Laboratory.
