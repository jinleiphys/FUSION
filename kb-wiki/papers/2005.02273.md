# Systematic study on the quark-hadron mixed phase in compact stars

> We investigate systematically the quark-hadron mixed phase in dense stellar matter, and its influence on compact star structures. The properties of quark matter and hadronic matter are fixed based on various model predictions. Beside adopting constant values, the surface tension $Σ$ for the quark-hadron interface is estimated with the multiple reflection expansion method and equivparticle model. To fix the structures of quark-hadron pasta phases, a continuous dimensionality of the structure is adopted as proposed by Ravenhall, Pethick, and Wilson. The corresponding properties of hybrid stars are then obtained and confronted with pulsar observations. It is found that the correlation between radius and tidal deformability in traditional neutron stars preserves in hybrid stars. For those permitted by pulsar observations, in almost all cases the quark phase persists inside the most massive compact stars. The quark-hadron interface plays an important role on hybrid star structures once quark matter emerges. The surface tension $Σ$ estimated with various methods increases with density, which predicts stiffer EOSs for the quark-hadron mixed phase and increases the maximum mass of hybrid stars. The EOSs of hybrid star matter are well constrained at densities $n\lesssim 0.8$ fm${}^{-3}$, while larger uncertainty is expected at higher densities.

## Key claim
The paper systematically investigates the quark-hadron mixed phase in compact stars using multiple model combinations, finding that the correlation between radius and tidal deformability seen in traditional neutron stars is preserved in hybrid stars, and that for models permitted by pulsar observations, quark matter persists inside the most massive compact stars in almost all cases.

## Method
The authors combined 10 hadronic matter equations of state (EOSs) from relativistic-mean-field models and variational methods with 46 quark matter EOSs from equivparticle, perturbation, and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models. They adopted a continuous dimensionality formalism for quark-hadron pasta phase structures as proposed by Ravenhall, Pethick, and Wilson, and estimated the surface tension for the quark-hadron interface using constant values, the multiple reflection expansion method, and the equivparticle model. Hybrid star properties were obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation and compared with pulsar observations including mass measurements and tidal deformability constraints from GW170817.

## Key numbers
- Maximum masses of hadronic neutron stars range from 2.08 to 2.49 solar masses across the 8 nuclear EOSs (TM1e: 2.13, TM1: 2.18, PKDD: 2.33, TW99: 2.08, DDME2: 2.49, DD2: 2.43, VM: 2.22, APR: 2.19 solar masses)
- Radii of 1.4 solar-mass neutron stars range from 11.4 km (APR) to 14.3 km (TM1)
- For hyperonic EOSs, maximum masses are reduced to 2.06 solar masses (TM1Lambda) and 2.16 solar masses (VMLambda)
- The EOSs of hybrid star matter are well constrained at densities n less than or equal to 0.8 fm^{-3}, with larger uncertainty at higher densities
- The surface tension Sigma estimated with various methods increases with density, predicting stiffer EOSs for the quark-hadron mixed phase

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Xia et al. (2019) that considered constraining surface tension from pulsar observations with a first-order deconfinement phase transition using specific models (TW99 for nuclear matter and perturbation model for quark matter). The present study systematically explores 10 hadronic EOSs and 46 quark EOSs with various surface tension treatments, enabling more robust constraints on hybrid star properties. The results enable future work to incorporate color superconductivity effects and further constrain dense matter EOSs using combined pulsar mass, radius, and tidal deformability measurements.
