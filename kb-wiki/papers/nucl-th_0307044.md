# Non-locality and Medium Effects in the Exclusive Photoproduction of Eta Mesons on Nuclei

> A relativistic model for the quasifree exclusive photoproduction of $η$ mesons on nuclei is extended to include both non-local and medium effects. The reaction is assumed to proceed via the dominant contribution of the S$_{11}$(1535) resonance. The complicated integrals resulting from the non-locality are simplified using a modified version of a method given by Cooper and Maxwell. The non-locality effects are found to affect the magnitude of the cross section. Some possibilities reflecting the effects of the medium on the propagation and properties of the intermediate S$_{11}$ resonance are studied. The effects of allowing the S$_{11}$ to interact with the medium via mean field scalar and vector potentials are considered. Both broadening of width and reduction in mass of the resonance lead to a suppression of the calculated cross sections.

# Non-locality and Medium Effects in the Exclusive Photoproduction of Eta Mesons on Nuclei

## Key claim

Non-locality effects increase the calculated cross section for quasifree exclusive eta photoproduction by up to 25%, while medium effects (mass reduction and width broadening of the S11(1535) resonance) suppress the cross section; the net result depends on the specific model for medium modifications, with the deep Hartree scalar potential causing a much larger suppression than the density-dependent quark-meson coupling model.

## Method

The authors extended a relativistic distorted-wave model for the A(gamma, eta p)B reaction, previously using a local approximation and free propagators, by solving the full non-local integral equations via a modified Cooper-Maxwell method that reduces the problem to a second-order radial differential equation solved by Gauss elimination. Medium effects were incorporated either by adding scalar and vector mean-field potentials to the S11 propagator (using Dirac-Hartree potentials from Horowitz and Serot) or by implementing density-dependent changes to the resonance mass (following Saito and Thomas) and width (following Lehr and Mosel). Calculations were performed for 12C and 40Ca targets at a photon energy of 750 MeV with symmetric detection of the outgoing proton and eta at 30 degrees on opposite sides of the beam.

## Key numbers

- Non-locality increases the cross section by up to 25% for 12C at E_gamma = 750 MeV (Figure 1, solid vs. dashed curves)
- Increasing the S11 width from 150 MeV to 208 MeV reduces the cross section by about 50% at the peaks (Figure 5)
- Density-dependent width broadening (Gamma(rho) = 150 MeV + 50 * rho/rho0, with rho0 = 0.17 fm^-3) reduces the cross section by only about 10% (Figure 5)
- The Hartree scalar potential is about 600 MeV deep at the nuclear center, leading to a large suppression when applied alone (Figure 4)
- The density-dependent mass reduction formula M(rho) = M_free - 0.14 * (rho/rho0) * M_free produces a substantial but much smaller suppression than the Hartree potential (Figure 6)
- Calculations were performed at E_gamma = 750 MeV and also at 900 MeV, with the same general features observed at both energies

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier relativistic model for exclusive and inclusive eta photoproduction (Hedayati-Poor and Sherif, 1997, 1998) by removing the local approximation and free-propagator assumptions. It complements the study of non-locality in coherent photoproduction by Peters, Lenske, and Mosel (1998), who found that non-local effects could enhance the S11 contribution that was strongly suppressed in local calculations. The medium effects investigated here are relevant to interpreting inclusive photoproduction data from MAMI (Roebig-Landau et al., 1996) and INS (Yorita et al., 2000), and the authors state they are analyzing existing 12C data to extract medium modifications of the S11 resonance.
