# In-medium Effects in the Eta-Photoproduction through the $S_{11}$ Resonance with the Relativistic Approach

> In-medium effects in photon excitation of the S$_{11}$ resonance from nucleon and its decay to $η$-meson using the RMF approach is studied. We examine the in-medium effects by varing the Dirac fields of the nucleon and the S$_{11}$ resonance separately. We obtained a conclusion that the Dirac fields of the nucleon, which reduces the nucleon effective mass, enlarge the width of S$_{11}$ resonance, and small Dirac-fields of the resonance shifts the peak position, and these combined effects can explain the in-medium properties observed in the experimental data for $^{12}$C($γ$,$η$) reaction.

## Key claim

The in-medium effects observed in the $^{12}$C($\gamma$,$\eta$) reaction can be explained by the combined effect of the nucleon Dirac fields (which reduce the nucleon effective mass and enlarge the $S_{11}$ resonance width) and small Dirac fields of the $S_{11}$ resonance itself (which shift the peak position), but the authors state that their treatment contains several ambiguities and they cannot give a perfectly quantitative conclusion at present.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach to calculate the eta photoproduction cross section in nuclear matter, employing a phenomenological Lagrangian with $\gamma_5 \vec{\gamma}$ coupling between the nucleon, $S_{11}$ resonance, and photon. They varied the Dirac fields of the nucleon and the $S_{11}$ resonance separately by introducing a ratio $u_r = U_s(R)/U_s(N) = U_0(R)/U_0(N)$ to examine in-medium effects on the peak position and width of the cross section.

## Key numbers

- The $S_{11}$ resonance parameters used: $M_R = 1.540$ GeV, $\Gamma_0 = 0.15$ GeV, $b_{\eta} = 0.55$, $c^2_{\pi} = c^2_{\eta} = 0.25$ GeV$^2$.
- The nucleon effective mass ratio $M^*/M = 0.7$ at nuclear saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ (from the PM1 parameter set with binding energy $BE = 16$ MeV).
- The absorption factor ${\cal F}_{abs} = 0.83$ was used to reproduce experimental data at the peak, compared to ${\cal F}_{abs} = 0.75$ from the QMD approach around $Q = 850$ MeV.
- The peak shift for $M^*_N/M_N = 0.7$ with $u_r = 0$ is about 80 MeV, while for $M^*_N/M_N = 1$ the peak shift is about 40 MeV.
- The elementary H($\gamma$,$\eta$)H cross section is described with a resonance width of 154 MeV, while the $^{12}$C target data required a width of 212 MeV (including collisional broadening) in the QMD analysis by Yorita et al.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field approach previously applied to exclusive eta photoproduction by Bolkland and Sherif (2001) and Peters et al. (1998), who could not compare with experimental data due to the lack of exclusive measurements. The study addresses the discrepancy found by Yorita et al. in their QMD analysis of $^{12}$C($\gamma$,$\eta$) data, where the theoretical peak position was about 40 MeV lower than observed even after including final state interactions and collisional broadening. The results suggest that the $S_{11}$(1535) resonance does not have large in-medium effects, contrasting with the more drastic effects needed to explain the disappearance of D$_{13}$(1520) and F$_{15}$(1680) resonance structures in heavier nuclei observed by Frois (1992).
