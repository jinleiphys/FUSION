# Nuclear threads

> The internal structure of deuterons weakly influences a motion of their center of mass (translational motion). The scenario can be different when the translational wave function has a formal singularity along the line (thread) connecting the deuteron and another Coulomb center. The singularity is cut off by fluctuations of nuclear internal degrees of freedom and the wave function becomes smooth with the narrow peak. The peak is associated with the certain potential well which is localized on the thread and dominates the Coulomb barrier. As a result, the deuteron is able to move along the thread with no classical reflection. Two deuterons can get in contact despite their energy is low. This is not an underbarrier motion but one along the region which became classically allowed due to the thread well. In experiments nuclear threads can be formed by the incident particle flux if to adjust its angular distribution at large distances.

## Key claim

The paper claims that when the translational wave function of a deuteron has a formal logarithmic singularity along a line (thread) connecting it to another Coulomb center, fluctuations of nuclear internal degrees of freedom cut off the singularity and produce a narrow peak associated with a potential well that dominates the Coulomb barrier; this allows deuterons to move along the thread with no classical reflection and get in contact even at low energy, but the effect depends on adjusting the incident particle flux angular distribution at large distances.

## Method

The author solves the Schrödinger equation for two deuterons in their center-of-mass frame using complex angular momenta, specifically choosing \(l = -1/2 - iBq\) where \(B\) is a large semiclassical parameter and \(q > 1/2\). The resulting Coulomb wave function is localized within a narrow cone of angle \(\theta \sim 1/B\) and has a logarithmic singularity on the \(z\) axis. The singularity is then smeared by considering the finite deuteron radius \(r_N \simeq 2.13 \times 10^{-13}\) cm and the coupling between translational and internal nuclear degrees of freedom, leading to a smooth wave function with a peak on the thread and an associated potential well of depth \(\sim 1\) MeV.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron radius: \(r_N \simeq 2.13 \times 10^{-13}\) cm
* Nuclear Compton length: \(\hbar/cM_D \simeq 1.05 \times 10^{-14}\) cm (less than deuteron radius)
* At energy \(E = 300\) Kelvin \(\simeq 2.58 \times 10^{-2}\) eV: semiclassical parameter \(B \simeq 1965\) and Coulomb scale \(2e^2/E \simeq 1114\) Å
* Nuclear Bohr radius: \(r_B = 2\hbar^2/M_D e^2 \simeq 2.9 \times 10^{-12}\) cm
* At room temperature (300 K): de Broglie wavelength \(\lambda \simeq 0.20\) Å and WKB tunneling probability \(10^{-2681}\)
* Deuteron mass energy: \(M_D c^2 \simeq 1.87 \times 10^3\) MeV
* Deuteron binding energy: \(E_b \simeq 2.225\) MeV
* Coulomb energy at nuclear distance: \(e^2/r_N \simeq 0.68\) MeV
* Typical energy inside thread: \(\hbar^2/(M_D r_N^2) \simeq 4.568\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of Coulomb scattering for point charges (Landau and Lifshitz, Newton) by considering complex angular momenta (Regge, 1959) and the finite size of the deuteron. It contradicts the usual WKB expectation that low-energy deuteron-deuteron tunneling through the Coulomb barrier is exponentially suppressed (probability \(10^{-2681}\) at room temperature). The paper enables the possibility of macroscopic nuclear states where translational and internal nuclear motions are hybridized, and suggests experimental creation of nuclear threads by adjusting the angular distribution of an incident deuteron flux at large distances, for example using a dielectric wall with mirror force interaction.
