# Quarkonium production at collider energies in Small-$x$ formalism

> 20 pages, 10 figures, uses svglov2.clo, svjour2.cls; Invited presentation at the workshop "New Observables In Quarkonium Production", ECT*, Italy, 28 February - 4 March 2016, v2: Grants info added, email address modified, v3: Published version

## Key claim

Quarkonium production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at collider energies can be described within the small-x formalism (Color-Glass-Condensate framework), but the effective factorization between heavy quark pair production and bound state formation is justified only at very forward rapidity where the condition \(y \gg \ln(Mv/Q_{sA})\) holds, except for the Color Singlet Model where \(v \to 0\) makes the factorization valid even at backward rapidity.

## Method

The paper reviews the small-x formalism for heavy quark pair production in pA collisions, starting from the classical Yang-Mills equation with color sources for projectile proton and target nucleus. The production amplitude is expressed in terms of Wilson lines in the fundamental and adjoint representations to account for multiple scattering, and the cross section is obtained by averaging over classical color source distributions. Quantum evolution at small x is incorporated via the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation with running coupling (rcBK) for the dipole amplitude, using initial conditions constrained by HERA DIS data. For forward rapidity, the unintegrated gluon distribution of the projectile proton is replaced by the collinear gluon PDF in the Hybrid approach.

## Key numbers

* Saturation scale for nucleus estimated as \(Q_{sA}^2 \sim A^{1/3} x^{-0.3}\) with \(A\) the atomic mass number.
* For \(J/\psi\) production at low transverse momentum at RHIC in forward rapidity: \(10^{-2} \lesssim x_A \lesssim 10^{-3}\).
* For \(J/\psi\) production at low transverse momentum at LHC in forward rapidity: \(10^{-4} \lesssim x_A \lesssim 10^{-5}\).
* The initial condition for the rcBK equation is set at \(x_0 = 0.01\) using the modified McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model.

## Context

This review extends the small-x formalism for quarkonium production developed in works by Blaizot et al. (2004) and Fujii et al. (2006, 2013), and incorporates NRQCD factorization approaches from Kang et al. (2013) and Ma et al. (2014, 2015). It contrasts with other cold nuclear matter effects such as nuclear shadowing (Eskola 2009, Hirai 2001, Vogt 2015), nuclear absorption (Ferreiro 2008, 2013), energy loss (Arleo 2012, 2013), and comover interaction (Gavin 1996, Ferreiro 2014), noting that a combination of saturation with these other effects has hardly been considered. The work enables quantitative comparison of saturation-based predictions with RHIC and LHC data for ground-state quarkonia like \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\), while identifying the need to incorporate final-state interactions for excited states like \(\psi(2S)\).
