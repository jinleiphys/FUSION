# Pairing in Asymmetrical Fermi Systems with Intra- and Inter-Species Correlations

> We consider inter- and intra-species pairing interactions in an asymmetrical Fermi system. Using equation of motion method, we obtain coupled mean-field equations for superfluid gap functions and population densities. We construct a phase diagram across BCS-BEC regimes. Due to intra-species correlations, the BCS singlet superfluid state can sustain finite polarizations, $P$. For larger $P$, we find phase separations in BCS and BEC regimes. A superfluid phase exists for all $P$ deep in BEC regime. Our results may apply to pairing in ultracold fermions, nuclear and quark matter physics.

## Key claim

Inclusion of intra-species p-wave correlations in an asymmetrical Fermi system can stabilize a gapped polarized superfluid phase (SFP) at low polarizations on both BCS and BEC sides of unitarity, a result that is dramatically different from calculations without such correlations where the superfluid state is unstable to phase separation.

## Method

The authors used an equation of motion method with imaginary-time Matsubara Green's functions to obtain coupled mean-field equations for three order parameters: an inter-species s-wave gap and two intra-species p-wave gaps. They solved these equations self-consistently at zero temperature for the gap functions and chemical potentials, then performed a stability analysis by constructing a 3x3 stability matrix from the second derivatives of the ground state energy and checking for positive definiteness, supplemented by analysis of the susceptibility ∂P/∂h.

## Key numbers

- For a representative intra-species correlation strength g₁ = 20, this corresponds to 1/(k_F³a_t) = 1.25 (triplet scattering volume parameter)
- The critical intra-species strength at which the slope of P vs h/μ becomes vertical is g₁^c ≈ 15 for inter-species coupling η = 0.25 on the BCS side
- One experiment (Partridge 2006) observed a transition from polarized superfluid to phase separation at a polarization ~10% near unitarity on the BEC side, which the authors' results appear to agree with
- The maximum polarization at unitarity is ~70%, in agreement with experiments

## Context

This work extends mean-field calculations by Pao (2006) and Sheehy (2006) that found the superfluid state unstable to phase separation in the BCS regime, by adding intra-species p-wave correlations that were previously ignored. It contrasts with the phenomenological approach of Ho (2006) for incorporating triplet correlations and with Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson (2005) that hinted at a polarized superfluid phase near unitarity. The results should apply broadly to ultracold fermions, nuclear matter, and quark matter, and the phase diagram indicates tricritical points on both BCS and BEC sides that could be explored at finite temperature.
