# Vacuum fluctuation effects on hyperonic neutron star matter

> The vacuum fluctuation (VF) effects on the properties of the hyperonic neutron star matter are investigated in the framework of the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. The VF corrections result in the density dependence of in-medium baryon and meson masses. We compare our results obtained by adopting three kinds of meson-hyperon couplings. The introduction of both hyperons and VF corrections soften the equation of state (EoS) for the hyperonic neutron star matter and hence reduce hyperonic neutron star masses. The presence of the $δ$ field enlarges the masses and radii of hyperonic neutron stars. Taking into account the uncertainty of meson-hyperon couplings, the obtained maximum masses of hyperonic neutron stars are in the range of $1.33M_{\odot}\sim1.55M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim

Vacuum fluctuation (VF) corrections, implemented via loop corrections to hadron self-energies in the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, soften the equation of state for hyperonic neutron star matter and reduce maximum neutron star masses, but the exact reduction depends on the choice of meson-hyperon couplings; the obtained maximum masses lie in the range of 1.33 to 1.55 solar masses when the uncertainty in those couplings is considered.

## Method

The authors extended their earlier VF-RMF model (Guo, 2009) to include the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰) and free leptons (e⁻, μ⁻) in the relativistic Lagrangian density. They computed the off-shell in-medium meson masses (σ, ω, ρ, δ) in the random-phase approximation and the in-medium baryon masses from tadpole-loop self-energy corrections, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations with the resulting density-dependent equation of state. Three sets of meson-hyperon couplings were compared: those fixed by hyperon potentials (U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = +30 MeV, U_Ξ = -18 MeV) with vector/isovector couplings from SU(6) quark symmetry, quark-counting couplings (x_jH = √(2/3)), and universal couplings (x_jH = 1).

## Key numbers

- Maximum hyperonic neutron star mass in the VF-RMF model with hyperon-potential couplings and no δ field: 1.33 M_⊙ (radius 12.37 km, central density 4.91 ρ₀).
- Maximum hyperonic neutron star mass in the VF-RMF model with hyperon-potential couplings and including the δ field: 1.43 M_⊙ (radius 13.13 km, central density 4.89 ρ₀).
- Maximum hyperonic neutron star mass in the VF-RMF model with quark-counting couplings and no δ field: 1.43 M_⊙ (radius 12.98 km, central density 4.71 ρ₀).
- Maximum hyperonic neutron star mass in the VF-RMF model with quark-counting couplings and including the δ field: 1.55 M_⊙ (radius 13.98 km, central density 3.98 ρ₀).
- Maximum hyperonic neutron star mass in the VF-RMF model with universal couplings and no δ field: 1.69 M_⊙ (radius 13.59 km, central density 4.48 ρ₀).
- Maximum hyperonic neutron star mass in the VF-RMF model with universal couplings and including the δ field: 1.80 M_⊙ (radius 14.50 km, central density 3.89 ρ₀).
- For comparison, maximum nucleonic (npeμ) neutron star mass in the VF-RMF model without δ field: 1.52 M_⊙; with δ field: 1.66 M_⊙.
- Hyperon potentials used: U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = +30 MeV, U_Ξ = -18 MeV.
- σ-meson–hyperon coupling constants (x_σH) from hyperon potentials in the VF-RMF model: x_σΛ = 0.62 (VFρ) / 0.64 (VFρδ), x_σΣ = 0.37 / 0.38, x_σΞ = 0.33 / 0.34.

## Context

This work extends the VF-RMF model previously developed by Guo (2009) for asymmetric nuclear matter and nucleonic neutron stars to include hyperonic degrees of freedom. It builds on the nonlinear Walecka model (NLWM) studies of hyperonic neutron stars by Glendenning (1982, 1985) and later work by Schaffner-Bielich (2003), Menezes (2006), and others, and on the inclusion of the δ field in asymmetric matter by Liu (2002, 2005). The results show that VF corrections soften the equation of state more than the standard NL-RMF model, and that the δ field increases maximum masses and radii, contrary to its effect in nucleonic matter. The sensitivity to meson-hyperon couplings highlights the need for better experimental constraints from hypernuclear data; the authors note that universal couplings are inappropriate because they yield hyperonic star masses exceeding nucleonic ones, violating the Pauli principle expectation that hyperon appearance lowers the energy. Future work could extend this approach to hyperon-hyperon interactions, kaon condensation, and quark deconfinement.
