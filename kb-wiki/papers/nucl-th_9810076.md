# One loop corrections to quantum hadrodynamics with vector mesons

> The renormalized elastic $ππ$ scattering amplitude to one loop is calculated in the chiral limit in the $σ$ model and in a Quantum Hadrodynamic model (QHD-III) with vector mesons. It is argued that QHD-III reduces to the linear $σ$ model in the limit that the vector meson masses become large. The pion decay constant is also calculated to 1-loop in the $σ$ model, and at tree level in QHD-III; it is shown that the coefficient of the tree level term in the scattering amplitude equals $F_π^{-2}$. The 1-loop correction of $F_π$ in QHD-III violates strong isospin current conservation. Thus,it is concluded that QHD-III can, at best, only describe the strongly interacting nuclear sector.

## Key claim

The paper argues that QHD-III can, at best, only describe the strongly interacting nuclear sector because the 1-loop correction to the pion decay constant in that model violates strong isospin current conservation, making the matrix element neither gauge-invariant nor renormalizable to that order.

## Method

The author calculates the renormalized elastic ππ scattering amplitude to 1-loop order in the chiral limit (mπ=0) in both the linear σ model and QHD-III, using dimensional regularization and assuming mσ² ≫ mρ² ≫ s,t,u. The pion decay constant Fπ is also computed to 1-loop in the σ model and at tree level in QHD-III, and the counterterms needed to cancel divergences are extracted from the σ 1-point, 2-point, and 3-point vertex functions.

## Key numbers

- The ππ scattering amplitude is calculated to O(gπ⁴) and to O(gπ²gρ²) in the limit mσ² ≫ mρ² ≫ s with mπ=0.
- The tree-level pion decay constant in QHD-III is given by 1/Fπ² = 1/σ∘² + gρ²/mρ², where σ∘ ≡ M/gπ.
- The 1-loop correction to Fπ in the σ model is: Fπ = σ∘ {1 + δz/2 + (3/(32π²))(mσ²/σ∘²)[Γ(ε/2) + ln4π - ln(mσ²/μ²) + 7/6] - (δμ + δλ)}.
- The counterterms for the σ model are: δμ ≐ -(3/2)(gπ²/M²)(mσ²/(16π²))Γ(ε/2) and δλ ≐ -2δμ.
- The counterterms for QHD-III are: δμ ≐ (3/2)(gρ² - mσ²/σ∘²)Γ(ε/2)/(16π²), δλ ≐ -2δμ, and δz ≐ 6gρ²Γ(ε/2)/(16π²).

## Context

This work extends the QHD series of models (based on {N; σ, ω} and {N; σ, ω, π} as in Serot and Walecka (1986)) by including vector mesons as gauge bosons of a local SU(2)L × SU(2)R symmetry, following the approach of Gasiorowicz and Geffen (1969). The paper shows that QHD-III reduces to the linear σ model in the limit gρ=0 and that gauge bosons decouple as mρ → ∞ via the decoupling theorem (Appelquist and Carazzone, 1975). The violation of current conservation at 1-loop in QHD-III limits the model to phenomenological descriptions of strongly interacting nuclear systems and prevents its use for processes like pion decay that involve isospin charge destruction.
