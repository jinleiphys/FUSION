# Hybrid neutron stars based on a modified PNJL model

> We discuss a three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type quantum field theoretical approach to the quark matter equation of state (EoS) with scalar diquark condensate, isoscalar vector mean field and Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) determinant interaction. While often the diquark and vector meson couplings are considered as free parameters, we will fix them here to their values according to the Fierz transformation of a one-gluon exchange interaction. In order to estimate the effect of a possible change in the vacuum pressure of the gluon sector at finite baryon density we exploit a recent modification of the Polyakov-loop NJL (mPNJL) model which introduces a parametric density dependence of the Polyakov-loop potential also at T=0, thus being relevant for compact star physics. We use a Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) EoS for the hadronic matter phase and discuss results for mass-radius relationships following from a solution of the TOV equations for such a hybrid EoS in the context of observational constraints from selected objects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a modified Polyakov-loop Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (mPNJL) model, with diquark and vector couplings fixed by Fierz transformation of one-gluon exchange rather than treated as free parameters, can produce hybrid neutron star sequences with stable quark matter cores, but only for sufficiently large values of the parameter \(c\) controlling the density dependence of the Polyakov-loop potential.

## Method

The authors construct a three-flavor NJL-type quark matter equation of state (EoS) including scalar diquark condensate, isoscalar vector mean field, and Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) determinant interaction, coupled to a modified Polyakov-loop potential that depends on chemical potential even at \(T=0\). They fix the ratios of vector and diquark couplings to the scalar coupling as \(G_V/G_S = 0.5\) and \(G_D/G_S = 0.75\) from Fierz transformation of one-gluon exchange, and use a Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) EoS for the hadronic phase. Mass-radius relationships are obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for the resulting hybrid EoS under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality constraints.

## Key numbers

- For \(\Delta B = -50\) MeV/fm\(^3\) (abridged mPNJL model), stable hybrid stars with 2SC quark core exist in the mass range \(1.74 < M/M_\odot < 2.04\).
- For \(\Delta B = -40\) MeV/fm\(^3\), stable hybrid stars with 2SC quark core exist in the mass range \(2.10 < M/M_\odot < 2.12\).
- The range of \(\Delta B\) values in the present model is limited to \(|\Delta B| < 50\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- The diquark coupling ratio is fixed to \(\eta_D = G_D/G_S = 0.75\) and the vector coupling ratio to \(\eta_V = G_V/G_S = 0.5\).
- The light quark constituent mass is set to 350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid star EoS using NJL-type models with color superconductivity (Ruester 2005, Blaschke 2005, Abuki 2005) by incorporating a modified Polyakov-loop potential that introduces density dependence at zero temperature, following the approach of Dexheimer (2009). It contrasts with traditional bag models (Baldo 2003) by providing a more microscopic treatment of the gluon sector's density-dependent vacuum pressure. The results enable future comparisons with high-precision mass-radius measurements from missions like the International X-Ray Observatory (IXO), and suggest that flavor-mixing interactions beyond the KMT term may be needed to fully describe the phase structure.
