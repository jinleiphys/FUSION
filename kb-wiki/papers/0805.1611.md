# High spin baryon in hot strongly coupled plasma

> We consider a strings-junction holographic model of probe baryon in the finite-temperature supersymmetric Yang-Mills dual of the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background. In particular, we investigate the screening length for high spin baryon composed of rotating N_c heavy quarks. To rotate quarks by finite force, we put hard infrared cutoff in the bulk and give quarks finite mass. We find that N_c microscopic strings are embedded reasonably in the bulk geometry when they have finite angular velocity ω, similar to the meson case. By defining the screening length as the critical separation of quarks, we compute the ωdependence of the baryon screening length numerically and obtain a reasonable result which shows that baryons with high spin dissociate more easily. Finally, we discuss the relation between J and E^2 for baryons.

## Key claim

Using a strings-junction holographic model in the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background, the authors find that the screening length of a high-spin baryon decreases with increasing angular velocity ω, indicating that baryons with higher spin dissociate more easily in a hot strongly coupled plasma, though the exact functional form differs from the velocity-dependence seen in the meson case.

## Method

The authors model a baryon as N_c fundamental strings with the same orientation, beginning at heavy quarks on the boundary and ending on a D5 brane (the junction vertex) in the AdS$_5$ bulk, with a hard infrared cutoff at r_Λ to give quarks finite mass. They impose constant angular velocity ω for all N_c strings rotating in the x_1-x_2 plane, solve the Nambu-Goto equation of motion for the string embedding function ρ(r) together with the radial force balance condition at the vertex, and compute the screening length numerically as the critical quark separation where the interaction potential changes behavior.

## Key numbers

- The screening length as a function of angular velocity is numerically fitted as l_s = 0.51/(0.14 ω + 0.22) - 0.85 (dimensionless units, with r_Λ = 100).
- The free quark mass is given by m_q = (1/(2πα'))(r_Λ - r_0), with the dimensionless form M_q = m_q/T = √λ (r_Λ - r_0)/(2r_0).
- The temperature is T = r_0/(πR^2), where r_0 is the black hole horizon position.
- The screening length shows negligible dependence on the cutoff r_Λ, confirming it is a property of the medium.

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of baryons in hot strongly coupled plasma, following the static baryon configuration of Witten (1998) and the moving baryon analysis of Liu (2008). It parallels the rotating meson studies of Peeters (2006) and Kruczenski (2003), but finds that the ω-dependence of the baryon screening length differs from the (1-v^2)^{1/4} behavior seen for moving mesons in Liu (2006) and Chernicoff (2006), because different parts of the hanging string have different linear velocities in the rotating case. The results enable future study of baryon dissociation in quark-gluon plasma as a function of spin.
