# Finite Formation Time in Electro-Disintegration of Few-Body Nuclei

> Inclusive and exclusive electro-disintegration of 2-H, 3-He and 4-He are calculated within an approach where, besides the final state interaction (FSI), also the finite formation time (FFT) of the hit hadron to its asymptotic physical state is taken into account.

# Finite Formation Time in Electro-Disintegration of Few-Body Nuclei

## Key claim

The finite formation time (FFT) of a hit nucleon to its asymptotic physical state, when included alongside final state interaction (FSI) in Glauber-type calculations, produces non-negligible effects in inclusive and exclusive electro-disintegration of few-body nuclei at moderate momentum transfers, but the quantitative size of these effects depends strongly on the details of the theoretical model used to treat FSI.

## Method

The authors calculate inclusive ($A(e,e')X$) and exclusive ($A(e,e')pB$) electro-disintegration cross sections for $^2H$, $^3He$, and $^4He$ using an eikonal Glauber approach for FSI, extended to incorporate FFT effects by replacing the standard Glauber operator with a modified operator containing a formation-time factor $\mathcal{J}(z) = \theta(z)(1-\exp(z x m M^2 / Q^2))$, where $M$ is the average virtuality of the struck nucleon. Realistic wave functions from the Reid Soft Core (RSC) and Argonne AV18 interactions are used, and the effective nucleon distorted momentum distributions $n_{eff}(\vec{p}_m)$ are computed.

## Key numbers

- For $^2H(e,e')X$ inclusive scattering at initial electron energy $E_e = 9.761$ GeV and scattering angle $\theta_e = 10^\circ$, calculations are performed at energy transfer $\nu = 1.3$ GeV (corresponding to Bjorken $x=1$) and $\nu = 0.83$ GeV (corresponding to $x=1.71$).
- The FFT-modified Glauber operator contains the factor $\mathcal{J}(z) = \theta(z)(1-\exp(z x m M^2 / Q^2))$, where $m$ is the nucleon mass and $M^2 = (\overline{m^*}^2 - m^2)$ is the average virtuality.
- For $^4He(e,e')p^3H$, the model predicts a dip in the cross section at missing momentum $p_m \simeq 2.2$ fm$^{-1}$ that would validate the FFT effects.
- Experimental data at $Q^2 \simeq 10$ (GeV/c)$^2$ would be needed to resolve quantitative differences between this model and the color transparency model of Omar (no year given).

## Context

This work extends the Glauber eikonal approach to FSI in quasi-elastic electron scattering by incorporating FFT effects previously introduced by Braun, Ciofi degli Atti, and Kaptari (no year given) and by Hiko (no year given). The results are compared with preliminary data from recent TJLab experiments (Ulmer, no year; experiment E-01020, no year). The predictions for $^4He(e,e')p^3H$ differ quantitatively from those of a color transparency model by Omar (no year given), and the authors note that experimental data at $Q^2 \simeq 10$ (GeV/c)$^2$ would resolve which model correctly describes hadron propagation in the nuclear medium.
