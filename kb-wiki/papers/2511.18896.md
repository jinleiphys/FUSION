# Impact of finite-range spin-orbit and tensor terms in Gogny EDF

> Energy Density Functionals are of major interest for the study of the atomic nucleus as, coupled with mean-field and beyond N-body approaches, they are applicable to the whole nuclear chart, including superheavy elements. On the one hand, the growing need for nuclear data and, on the other hand, the large amount of experimental data on exotic nuclei explain the work carried out on these phenomenological forms of the nucleon-nucleon interaction to analyze the richness of the nuclear phenomena. In this paper, we propose a fully finite-range extension of the Gogny EDF, including a short-range spin-orbit term and a long-range tensor term. The original fitting protocol of the Gogny interaction has been adapted to include both finite range spin-orbit and tensor terms, adding new constraints and filters linked to relevant data. Nuclear matter, spectroscopic and fission properties are discussed, highlighting ways of improving EDFs when all spin and isospin exchanges are introduced with finite-range terms.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully finite-range extension of the Gogny energy density functional (EDF) that includes both a short-range spin-orbit term and a long-range tensor term, with the claim that this extension improves the description of nuclear structure and fission properties compared to previous Gogny parameterizations, though the authors note that the improvement results from a combination of spin-orbit and neutron effective mass effects that are difficult to fully disentangle.

## Method

The authors generalized the existing D2 fitting protocol to produce a new parameterization called DG, which contains twenty-two parameters including five ranges and a density-dependent term with power alpha=1/3. The fitting procedure used a Restricted Hartree-Fock emulator with harmonic oscillator wave functions to generate equations from binding energies, charge radii, and two-body matrix elements, while new constraints from nuclear matter properties (including the slope of the symmetry energy and partial wave decomposition) and filters from finite nuclei (including the kink in Pb charge radii and first excited states in sd-shell nuclei) were added. The spin-orbit and tensor parameters were determined consistently with the other parameters for the first time, using a new set of four equations involving two-body matrix elements coupled to T=0 and T=1 channels for various total angular momentum values.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.163 fm^{-3} for DG (same as D2 and D1S)
- Energy per particle E/A = -16.01 MeV for DG
- Incompressibility K_infinity = 210 MeV for DG
- Effective mass m*/m = 0.74 for DG
- Symmetry energy a_tau = 31.3 MeV for DG
- Slope of symmetry energy L = 43.02 MeV for DG
- Kink angle in Pb charge radii: theta = 1.51 degrees for DG, compared to 0.87 degrees for D2 and 0.64 degrees for D1S
- 1i_{11/2}-2g_{9/2} gap in ^{208}Pb: 0.925 MeV for DG, compared to 1.514 MeV for D2 and 1.867 MeV for D1S
- Spin-orbit intensity W_4 - H_4 = 115.849 MeV fm^5 for DG, reduced from W_LS = 130 MeV fm^5 for D1S and D2
- Average difference between experimental and MPMH first excited states in odd sd-shell nuclei: 337 keV for DG, 659 keV for D1S, 821 keV for D2
- Standard deviation for odd sd-shell first excited states: 263 keV for DG, 698 keV for D1S, 673 keV for D2
- First GCM barrier height for ^{236}U: 7.3 MeV for DG, compared to 7.7 MeV for D1S and 7.1 MeV for D2
- First GCM barrier height for ^{240}Pu: 8.2 MeV for DG, compared to 9.3 MeV for D1S and 8.6 MeV for D2
- First GCM barrier height for ^{252}Cf: 7.6 MeV for DG, compared to 9.6 MeV for D1S and 9.0 MeV for D2

## Context

This work extends the Gogny EDF family by adding finite-range spin-orbit and tensor terms, building on previous extensions such as the D2 interaction (Chappert, 2008) which introduced a finite-range density-dependent term, and on earlier attempts to include tensor forces in Gogny interactions by Otsuka et al. and Anguiano et al. The new DG parameterization improves upon D1S and D2 in describing the kink in Pb charge radii and eliminates the monopole shift observed in sd-shell nuclei with D1S and D2, while providing comparable fission barrier heights. This enables future applications of the Gogny interaction in beyond-mean-field theories that include all types of nuclear long-range correlations, and suggests that further work on the residual multipole part of the interaction could improve predictions for excited states.
