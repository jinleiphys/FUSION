# Variational description of continuum states in terms of integral relations

> Two integral relations derived from the Kohn Variational Principle (KVP) are used for describing scattering states. In usual applications the KVP requires the explicit form of the asymptotic behavior of the scattering wave function. This is not the case when the integral relations are applied since, due to their short range nature, the only condition for the scattering wave function $Ψ$ is that it be the solution of $(H-E)Ψ=0$ in the internal region. Several examples are analyzed for the computation of phase-shifts from bound state type wave functions or, in the case of the scattering of charged particles, it is possible to obtain phase-shifts using free asymptotic conditions. As a final example we discuss the use of the integral relations in the case of the Hyperspherical Adiabatic method.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two integral relations derived from the Kohn Variational Principle (KVP) can compute scattering phase-shifts using trial wave functions that need only satisfy the Schrödinger equation in the interaction region, without requiring the correct asymptotic behavior, a condition the abstract states but does not quantify as a relaxation of the usual KVP requirement.

## Method

The authors derive two integral relations from the KVP that express the coefficients A and B (whose ratio gives tan δ) as short-range integrals of the trial wave function with regular and irregular asymptotic functions. They test these relations on two-body systems using a Gaussian potential, then extend to three-body n-d and p-d scattering below breakup using the pair hyperspherical harmonic (PHH) expansion with the MT I-III nucleon-nucleon interaction. For charged-particle scattering, they demonstrate that phase-shifts can be obtained by using wave functions computed with a screened Coulomb potential as input to the integral relations with the full unscreened Coulomb Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Two-body Gaussian potential: V₀ = −51.5 MeV, r₀ = 1.6 fm, ħ²/m = 41.4696 MeV·fm²; produces a bound state at E₂B = −0.397743 MeV.
- For M = 40 Laguerre polynomials, the relative difference between the variational [tan δ]²ⁿᵈ and the exact tan δ is about 10⁻⁶.
- MT I-III potential predictions: deuteron binding energy E_d = −2.23069 MeV, singlet n-d scattering length ¹a_{n-d} = −23.582 fm, triplet n-d scattering length ³a_{n-d} = 5.5132 fm.
- With M = 24 Laguerre polynomials and K = 16, three-nucleon bound state energies converge to better than 1 keV and scattering lengths to about 0.001 fm.
- For the screened Coulomb method with n ≥ 4 and r_sc > 30 fm, the second-order estimate [tan δ_c]²ⁿᵈ coincides with the exact Coulomb phase-shift.

## Context

This work extends the integral-relation approach previously introduced by the same group (Kievsky et al., 2009) by demonstrating its variational character and applicability to three-body systems. It contrasts with the standard KVP implementation, which requires explicit asymptotic forms, and with the Harris (1967) method, where the variational nature of the quotient was not recognized. The approach enables the use of bound-state techniques, such as the PHH expansion or potentially Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and No Core Shell Model (NCSM), to compute scattering observables in systems with A > 4, where accurate bound-state methods exist but scattering calculations remain limited.
