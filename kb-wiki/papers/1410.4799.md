# Simulation of Black Hole Collisions in Asymptotically AdS Spacetimes

> We present results from the evolution of spacetimes that describe the merger of asymptotically global AdS black holes in 5D with an SO(3) symmetry. Prompt scalar field collapse provides us with a mechanism for producing distinct trapped regions on the initial slice, associated with black holes initially at rest. We evolve these black holes towards a merger, and follow the subsequent ring-down. The boundary stress tensor of the dual CFT is conformally related to a stress tensor in Minkowski space which inherits an axial symmetry from the bulk SO(3). We compare this boundary stress tensor to its hydrodynamic counterpart with viscous corrections of up to second order, and compare the conformally related stress tensor to ideal hydrodynamic simulations in Minkowski space, initialized at various time slices of the boundary data. Our findings reveal far-from-hydrodynamic behavior at early times, with a transition to ideal hydrodynamics at late times.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first successful numerical simulation of black hole mergers in asymptotically AdS$_5$ spacetimes, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the simulations are limited to modest initial separations of the collision participants and require a regulator black hole at the origin to maintain numerical stability.

## Method
The authors solve the Einstein field equations with a negative cosmological constant using the generalized harmonic evolution scheme in 5D global AdS with an SO(3) symmetry. Two black holes are created via prompt scalar field collapse from Gaussian scalar field profiles centered at antipodal points on the axis, and a small regulator black hole is placed at the origin to avoid coordinate singularities. The boundary stress tensor of the dual CFT is extracted and compared to second-order viscous hydrodynamics on $\mathbb{R} \times S^3$ and to ideal 3+1 relativistic hydrodynamics on $\mathbb{R}^{3,1}$ using a conformal mapping.

## Key numbers
- The two physical black holes are initially separated by a distance of $2 L$ in the bulk (with the AdS length scale $L$ set to 1).
- The scalar field parameters used are $D=0.5$, $A_0=1.25$, $\delta=0.025$, $w_x=w_y=1$.
- The regulator black hole mass is kept below $10\%$ of the total spacetime mass to make its effect negligible.
- At early global times $t$, the mismatch between the boundary stress tensor and second-order viscous hydrodynamics is up to $\approx 20\%$.
- The energy density in Minkowski space falls off roughly as $\sim 1/(t^\prime)^4$ at late times.
- The maximum overlap of the two energy density lumps occurs at about $t^\prime \approx 0.5$.
- Ideal hydrodynamics initialized at $t^\prime=2.1$ recovers good agreement with the gravity data.

## Context
This work extends the program of holographic modeling of heavy-ion collisions, building on prior studies of colliding shockwaves in AdS$_5$ by Chesler (2010) and of distorted black hole ring-down by Bantilan (2012). It moves beyond earlier work where hydrodynamics dominated from the outset by dynamically generating the post-collision state from distinct trapped regions. The results enable future efforts to relax the bulk symmetry and make closer contact with the real-world flows observed at RHIC and the LHC, as well as to include viscous corrections in the Minkowski-space hydrodynamic comparison.
