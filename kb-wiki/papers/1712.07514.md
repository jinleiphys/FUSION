# Complex Langevin Simulation of a Random Matrix Model at Nonzero Chemical Potential

> In this paper we test the complex Langevin algorithm for numerical simulations of a random matrix model of QCD with a first order phase transition to a phase of finite baryon density. We observe that a naive implementation of the algorithm leads to phase quenched results, which were also derived analytically in this article. We test several fixes for the convergence issues of the algorithm, in particular the method of gauge cooling, the shifted representation, the deformation technique and reweighted complex Langevin, but only the latter method reproduces the correct analytical results in the region where the quark mass is inside the domain of the eigenvalues. In order to shed more light on the issues of the methods we also apply them to a similar random matrix model with a milder sign problem and no phase transition, and in that case gauge cooling cooling solves the convergence problems as was shown before in the literature.

## Key claim

The complex Langevin algorithm, when naively implemented for a random matrix model of QCD with a first-order phase transition to finite baryon density, converges to the phase-quenched result rather than the correct dynamical result; only the reweighted complex Langevin method reproduces the correct analytical results in the region where the quark mass lies inside the domain of the eigenvalues.

## Method

The authors performed complex Langevin simulations of the Stephanov random matrix model (1996), which exhibits a first-order phase transition to nonzero baryon density, using matrix sizes up to N=48 with a discretized Langevin equation and step size Δt=10⁻⁴. They tested four fixes for convergence issues: gauge cooling, the shifted representation, the deformation technique, and reweighted complex Langevin (RCL), comparing numerical results for the chiral condensate and baryon number density against analytical solutions obtained via bosonization and saddle-point analysis. The RCL method samples an auxiliary ensemble at parameters where CL converges (e.g., large mass or large chemical potential) and reweights to the target ensemble.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential in the chiral limit: μ_c = 0.527... for the Stephanov model with a first-order phase transition.
- Matrix size N=48 was sufficient for results independent of matrix size; step size Δt=10⁻⁴ eliminated discretization errors.
- For the RCL method with N=6, correct analytical results were reproduced across the full parameter range; for N=24, RCL reproduced results with growing error bars in the phase transition region.
- The phase diagram shows a pion condensation phase for μ > m_π/2, with a second phase transition to a chirally restored phase at larger μ.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of complex Langevin convergence in random matrix theory, particularly the Osborn model (2004) which lacks a phase transition to nonzero baryon density and has a milder sign problem. The authors build on convergence criteria established by Aarts (2009) and prior demonstrations that gauge cooling solves convergence problems for the Osborn model (Mollgaard, 2013; Nagata, 2016). The results enable systematic testing of complex Langevin fixes in a model with a realistic first-order phase transition, providing a benchmark for applying these methods to lattice QCD at finite baryon density.
