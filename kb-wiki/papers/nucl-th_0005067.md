# DSE Hadron Phenomenology

> A perspective on the contemporary use of Dyson-Schwinger equations, focusing on some recent phenomenological applications: a description and unification of light-meson observables using a one-parameter model of the effective quark-quark interaction, and studies of leptonic and nonleptonic nucleon form factors.

## Key claim
Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) can unify light-meson observables and nucleon electromagnetic form factors within a single framework, but the nucleon results rely on a product Ansatz that neglects axial-vector diquark correlations and the lower component of the nucleon spinor, with the latter improvement yielding preliminary results that are "too good" because those neglected contributions remain.

## Method
The authors solve the renormalised quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) and meson Bethe-Salpeter equations using a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation, with the effective quark-quark interaction specified by a single parameter D (the infrared mass-scale) plus a perturbative term. For nucleon observables, they employ a quark-diquark Faddeev-inspired product Ansatz for the nucleon amplitude, parametrising the scalar functions for the quark-diquark correlation, the diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and the diquark propagator, then fitting to the proton charge form factor on Q² ∈ [0,3] GeV².

## Key numbers
- Model parameter D = (1.12 GeV)², with current-quark masses m_{u,d}^{1 GeV} = 5.5 MeV and m_s^{1 GeV} = 124 MeV, yielding a root-mean-square error over predicted meson quantities of 3.6%.
- Calculated meson masses and decay constants (in GeV): m_π = 0.139 (fitted), m_K = 0.496 (fitted), m_ρ = 0.747, m_{K*} = 0.956, m_φ = 1.088; f_π = 0.130 (fitted), f_K = 0.154, f_ρ = 0.197, f_{K*} = 0.246, f_φ = 0.255.
- Pion charge radius squared: r_π² = (0.67 fm)², compared to experimental (0.663 ± 0.007 fm)².
- Kaon charge radii squared: r_{K+}² = (0.62 fm)² (expt. 0.583 ± 0.043 fm²), r_{K0}² = -(0.29 fm)² (expt. -(0.23 ± 0.06) fm²).
- Nucleon electromagnetic form factors (with ψ₂ = ψ₁): r_p² = (0.78 fm)², r_n² = -(0.33 fm)², μ_p = 2.81 μ_N, μ_n = -1.61 μ_N, |μ_n/μ_p| = 0.57 (expt. 0.68).
- Fitting parameters for nucleon: ω_ψ = 0.19 GeV, ω_Γ = 0.68 GeV, m_Δ = 0.64 GeV; corresponding length scales: 1/ω_ψ = 1.03 fm, 1/ω_Γ = 0.29 fm, 1/m_Δ = 0.31 fm.

## Context
This work extends the DSE phenomenology program of Roberts and collaborators, building on earlier studies of meson properties (Maris and Roberts, 1997; Maris and Tandy, 1999) and nucleon form factors (Bloch, Roberts, and collaborators, 1999). The meson sector provides a unified description of π, K, ρ, K*, and φ observables using a single-parameter interaction, while the nucleon sector improves upon pointlike scalar-diquark models by incorporating nonpointlike diquark correlations, yielding a better ratio |μ_n/μ_p| = 0.55 (old calculation) versus pointlike models which always give < 0.5. The results enable future inclusion of axial-vector diquark correlations and the lower component of the nucleon spinor, and motivate extending the approach to baryon transition form factors and weak interactions.
