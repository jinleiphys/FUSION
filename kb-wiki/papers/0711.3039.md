# Wilson line correlator in the MV model: relating the glasma to deep inelastic scattering

> In the color glass condensate framework the saturation scale measured in deep inelastic scattering of high energy hadrons and nuclei can be determined from the correlator of Wilson lines in the hadron wavefunction. These same Wilson lines give the initial condition of the classical field computation of the initial gluon multiplicity and energy density in a heavy ion collision. In this paper the Wilson line correlator in both adjoint and fundamental representations is computed using exactly the same numerical procedure that has been used to calculate gluon production in a heavy ion collision. In particular the discretization of the longitudinal coordinate has a large numerical effect on the relation between the color charge density parameter g^2 mu and the saturation scale Qs. Our result for this relation is Qs = 0.6 g^2 mu, which results in the classical Yang-Mills value for the "gluon liberation coefficient" c = 1.1.

# Wilson line correlator in the MV model: relating the glasma to deep inelastic scattering

## Key claim

The relation between the color charge density parameter $g^2\mu$ and the saturation scale $Q_s$ depends strongly on how the longitudinal (rapidity) coordinate is discretized in the numerical procedure, with approximately a factor of two difference between the $N_y=1$ prescription used in glasma computations and the $N_y \to \infty$ limit corresponding to the analytical calculation of the dipole cross section. The paper's result $Q_s = 0.6 g^2\mu$ is specific to the $N_y=1$ numerical procedure used in classical Yang-Mills simulations of heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The Wilson line correlator in both fundamental and adjoint representations was computed on a two-dimensional transverse lattice using the same numerical procedure previously employed for glasma field calculations, with SU(3) matrices constructed from Gaussian-distributed color charges. The calculation systematically varied the number of longitudinal discretization points $N_y$, the lattice spacing $a$, the system size $L$, and an infrared regulator mass $m$ to study their effects on the relation between $g^2\mu$ and $Q_s$, with the saturation scale defined from the maximum of $k_T^2 C(k_T)$ in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- $Q_s \approx 0.6 g^2\mu$ for the $N_y=1$ numerical CYM prescription (the value used in glasma computations)
- $Q_s \approx 1.15 g^2\mu$ for $N_y \to \infty$ (the analytical computation of the dipole cross section), giving approximately a factor of two difference
- For $N_y=1$ and $g^2\mu L \sim 100$ (the values used in numerical glasma computations), $Q_s \approx 0.57 g^2\mu$
- The classical Yang-Mills value for the gluon liberation coefficient $c \approx 1.1$, resolving the earlier interpretation that the CYM result would be $c \approx 0.5$
- The ratio of coordinate-space to momentum-space definitions of the saturation scale differs by approximately 10%, reflecting the non-Gaussian nature of the MV model correlator
- For RHIC central rapidity, estimated $g^2\mu \approx 2.0$ GeV from the IPsat model (Kowalski 2007), with other DIS extrapolations giving values from $2.0$ GeV to $3.0$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the analytical calculations of the Wilson line correlator in the McLerran-Venugopalan model by Jalilian-Marian (1997), Kovchegov (1996, 1998), McLerran (1998), Gelis (2001), and Blaizot (2004) by using the same numerical procedure as the glasma simulations of Krasnitz (1998-2003) and Lappi (2003-2006). The paper enables a more precise connection between the saturation scale $Q_s$ determined from DIS data (using fits such as Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998, 1999) or the IPsat model of Kowalski and Teaney (2003)) and the color charge density parameter $g^2\mu$ that sets initial conditions for heavy ion collision simulations. The results also reconcile the apparent discrepancy between the analytical expectation $c \approx 2\ln 2 \approx 1.4$ from Kovchegov (2000) and the numerical CYM results, showing that when the relation between $Q_s$ and $g^2\mu$ is computed consistently, the liberation coefficient is $c \approx 1.1$.
