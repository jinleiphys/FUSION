# Shell and cluster structures in $^{20}$Ne in the variation of multiple bases of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> We investigate the structures of $^{20}$Ne in the variation of the multiple bases of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). In this method, the multiple AMD bases are superposed and optimized simultaneously in the total-energy variation. This scheme is beneficial for describing the various configurations in $^{20}$Ne. In the results, we confirm the shell and cluster structures in the $K^π=0^+_{1-4}$ bands, such as the deformed states in the $K^π=0^+_{1,4}$ bands with the $α$ cluster development, and the spherical shell-like states in the $K^π=0^+_2$ band, the latter of which is difficult to describe in the previous AMD calculations imposing the quadrupole deformation. We evaluate the monopole and quadrupole transitions in these states. The negative parity states of $^{20}$Ne with $K^π=0^-$ and $2^-$ are discussed in relation to the shell and cluster structures. As a result, six kinds of the $K^π$ bands in $^{20}$Ne are described comprehensively in the microscopic framework of nuclei.

## Key claim

The multicool method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) can describe all six known \(K^\pi\) bands of \(^{20}\)Ne, including the spherical shell-like \(K^\pi=0^+_2\) band that previous AMD calculations imposing quadrupole deformation constraints could not obtain.

## Method

The authors performed a variation of multiple AMD basis states (the "multicool method") for \(^{20}\)Ne, where 16 intrinsic AMD basis states were simultaneously optimized in a total-energy variation without any constraints on deformation or radius. Excited-state configurations were generated using a pseudo-potential method to enforce orthogonality to the ground-state configurations, and the final wave functions were obtained by superposing all basis states with angular momentum projection and solving the Hill-Wheeler equation.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of the \(0^+_1\) ground state: \(-158.83\) MeV (experimental: \(-160.64\) MeV)
- Matter radius of the \(0^+_1\) state: 2.91 fm (experimental range: 2.84–2.87 fm)
- Charge radius of the \(0^+_1\) state: 3.03 fm (experimental: 3.0055(21) fm)
- Threshold energy difference for \(^{16}\)O+\(\alpha\) channel: 3.06 MeV (experimental: 4.73 MeV)
- Principal quantum number \(N\) for the \(0^+_2\) bandhead: 22.26, indicating dominant \(sd\)-shell configuration
- Principal quantum number \(N\) for the \(0^+_4\) bandhead: 40.38, with matter radius 3.84 fm, indicating a highly developed cluster structure
- \(B(E2)\) for \(2^+_1 \to 0^+_1\): 41.2 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (experimental: 65\(\pm\)3 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\))

## Context

This work extends previous AMD calculations by Kimura (2004), Isaka (2011), and Taniguchi (2004), which successfully described the \(K^\pi=0^+_{1,4}\), \(0^-\), and \(2^-\) bands but could not obtain the \(K^\pi=0^+_2\) band states. The multicool method also improves upon the mean-field model of Marevic (2018) and the shell-model calculations of Tomoda (1978), which described only the \(K^\pi=0^+_{1,2}\) bands. The results enable comprehensive microscopic description of all six \(K^\pi\) bands in \(^{20}\)Ne within a single framework, and the method can be applied to other light nuclei where both shell and cluster structures coexist.
