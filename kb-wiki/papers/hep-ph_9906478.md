# Schwinger-Dyson Approach to Color Superconductivity in Dense QCD

> The problem of color superconductivity in dense QCD is reconsidered in the improved rainbow approximation to the Schwinger-Dyson equation. The effect of the unscreened magnetic modes of gluons on the value of the color condensate is studied. In particular, it is shown that, at sufficiently large values of the chemical potential, these modes lead to the enhancement of the superconducting order parameter. The interplay between the instanton induced interaction and the one-gluon induced one in color superconductivity is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, at sufficiently large chemical potential, the unscreened magnetic modes of gluons enhance the superconducting order parameter in dense QCD, contrary to earlier approaches based on local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models or BCS-type analyses that neglected this long-range interaction.

## Method

The authors used the Schwinger-Dyson equation in the improved rainbow approximation, where the full vertices are replaced by bare ones but the gluon propagator includes one-loop polarization effects (hard dense loop approximation). They derived a gap equation for the diquark condensate, separating contributions from the three gluons of the unbroken SU(2) subgroup and the five gluons subject to the Meissner effect, and solved it analytically in the vicinity of the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- The Debye screening mass squared is \( M^2 = (g_s \mu \sqrt{N_f} / 2\pi)^2 \), where \( N_f = 2 \) for two light flavors.
- The Higgs-like gluon mass from the Meissner effect is estimated as \( M_0^2 \simeq (\alpha_s / \pi) \mu^2 \).
- The gap equation yields a solution with the ultraviolet cutoff \( \Lambda \equiv e^{3d/2} (4\pi)^{3/2} \mu / \alpha^{5/2} \), where \( d \) is the gauge fixing parameter.
- The expected baryon number density in neutron star interiors is between about \( n_0 \) and \( 10 n_0 \), with \( n_0 \simeq 0.17~\text{fm}^{-3} \), corresponding to chemical potentials between 200 and 700 MeV and coupling constants \( \alpha_s \) between about 0.4 and 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity beyond the instanton-based approaches of Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp et al. (1998), and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model studies. It directly addresses the observation by Pisarski and Rischke (1999) and Son (1999) that unscreened magnetic gluon modes could enhance the gap, providing a Schwinger-Dyson derivation that confirms their renormalization group results. The findings enable more reliable estimates of the superconducting gap for applications to neutron star phenomenology.
