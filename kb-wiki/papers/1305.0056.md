# Constraining Neutrino Mass from Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> We re-analyze the compatibility of the claimed observation of neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) in $^{76}$Ge with the new limits on the half-life of $^{136}$Xe from EXO-200 and KamLAND-Zen. Including recent calculations of the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs), we show that while the claim in $^{76}$Ge is still compatible with the individual limits from $^{136}$Xe, it is inconsistent with the KamLAND-Zen+EXO-200 combined limit for all but one NME calculations. After imposing the most stringent upper limit on the sum of light neutrino masses from Planck, we find that the canonical light neutrino contribution cannot satisfy the claimed $0νββ$ signature or saturate the current limit, irrespective of the NME uncertainties. However, inclusion of the heavy neutrino contributions, arising naturally in TeV-scale Left-Right symmetric models, can saturate the current limit of $0νββ$. In a type-II seesaw framework, this imposes a lower limit on the lightest neutrino mass. Depending on the mass hierarchy, we obtain this limit to be in the range of 0.07 - 4 meV for a typical choice of the right-handed (RH) gauge boson and RH neutrino masses relevant for their collider searches. Using the $0νββ$ bounds, we also derive correlated constraints in the RH sector, complimentary to those from the LHC.

## Key claim

The claimed observation of neutrinoless double beta decay in germanium-76 is still compatible with the individual limits from xenon-136 for some nuclear matrix element calculations, but is inconsistent with the combined KamLAND-Zen and EXO-200 limit for all but one NME calculation; the canonical light neutrino contribution alone cannot satisfy the claimed signature or saturate the current limit after imposing the Planck upper bound on the sum of light neutrino masses, regardless of NME uncertainties.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed the compatibility between the claimed 0νββ observation in 76Ge and the null results from 136Xe experiments by correlating their half-lives using updated phase space factors and nine different nuclear matrix element calculations from the literature (including EDF, ISM, IBM-2, pnQRPA, SRQRPA, QRPA, and SkM-HFB-QRPA methods). They then compared the canonical light neutrino contribution to 0νββ with the Planck cosmological bound on the sum of neutrino masses, and investigated whether a heavy neutrino contribution from TeV-scale Left-Right symmetric models with type-II seesaw dominance could saturate the current experimental limits, deriving correlated constraints in the right-handed sector.

## Key numbers

- Claimed half-life for 76Ge: T_{1/2}^{0ν}(76Ge) = 2.23^{+0.44}_{-0.31} × 10^{25} yr at 68% CL (Klapdor-Kleingrothaus 2006)
- KamLAND-Zen limit for 136Xe: T_{1/2}^{0ν}(136Xe) > 1.9 × 10^{25} yr at 90% CL
- EXO-200 limit for 136Xe: T_{1/2}^{0ν}(136Xe) > 1.6 × 10^{25} yr at 90% CL
- Combined KamLAND-Zen + EXO-200 limit for 136Xe: T_{1/2}^{0ν}(136Xe) > 3.4 × 10^{25} yr at 90% CL
- Planck upper limit on sum of light neutrino masses: Σ m_ν < 0.23 eV at 95% CL (with BAO data)
- For quasi-degenerate neutrinos, the Planck limit gives m_0 < 0.077 eV
- Predicted T_{1/2}^{0ν}(136Xe) from the 76Ge claim ranges from 0.33 × 10^{25} yr (EDF method) to 3.47 × 10^{25} yr (SkM-HFB-QRPA method) at 90% CL
- Lower limit on lightest neutrino mass from heavy neutrino contribution: (2-4) meV for normal hierarchy and (0.07-0.2) meV for inverted hierarchy
- For the KK claim with heavy neutrino contribution, lightest neutrino mass range: (1-3) meV for NH and (0.03-0.1) meV for IH
- Virtuality of exchanged neutrino: ⟨p²⟩ = -(157 to 185 MeV)² for 136Xe and -(153 to 184 MeV)² for 76Ge

## Context

This work extends the analysis of compatibility between the claimed 0νββ observation in 76Ge (Klapdor-Kleingrothaus 2006) and the null results from KamLAND-Zen (Gando 2012) and EXO-200 (Auger 2012), incorporating updated nuclear matrix element calculations from multiple groups including Rodriguez (2010), Menendez (2008), Barea (2013), Suhonen (2010), Meroni (2012), Simkovic (2013), and Mustonen (2013). The paper also incorporates the stringent Planck cosmological bound on neutrino masses (Ade 2013) and explores heavy neutrino contributions within TeV-scale Left-Right symmetric models, deriving constraints complementary to LHC searches for same-sign dilepton signals. The results enable future experimental comparisons and provide testable predictions for the lightest neutrino mass in type-II seesaw scenarios.
