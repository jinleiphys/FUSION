# The Influence Of Neutron Capture Rates On The Rare Earth Region Of The r-Process Abundance Pattern

> We study the sensitivity of the r-process abundance pattern to neutron capture rates along the rare earth region (A~150 to A~180). We introduce the concepts of large nuclear flow and flow saturation which determine the neutron capture rates that are influential in setting the rare earth abundances. We illustrate the value of the two concepts by considering high entropy conditions favorable for rare earth peak production and identifying important neutron capture rates among the rare earth isotopes. We also show how these rates influence nuclear flow and specific sections of the abundance pattern.

## Key claim

The paper shows that only a subset of neutron capture rates in the rare earth region (A~150 to A~180) significantly influence the final r-process abundance pattern, and that the magnitude of this influence depends on two conditions: large nuclear flow through the neutron capture channel and flow saturation (the degree to which the neutron capture output channel is limited by incoming flows). The abstract's claim that the paper "identifies important neutron capture rates" is qualified by the finding that the effect is local (not global) and depends on whether the nucleus is in or out of (n,γ)⇄(γ,n) equilibrium.

## Method

The authors used a one-dimensional r-process model following a single ejected mass zone, with parameterized density trajectories for both hot freeze-out (T≳1 GK) and cold freeze-out (T∼0.1 GK) conditions. They performed neutron capture rate studies by changing the neutron capture rate of a single isotope in the rare earth region by factors of K=5, 10, 50, or 100, then comparing the final abundance pattern to a baseline simulation using the F-measure (percent deviation summed over mass number). Nuclear flow was monitored by tracking individual terms in the abundance evolution equations, and the concepts of large nuclear flow (L≥0.2) and flow saturation (Ṡ) were introduced to predict which nuclei would show significant effects.

## Key numbers

- The rare earth region studied spans atomic mass A~150 to A~180.
- Neutron capture rates were changed by factors of K=5, 10, 50, or 100.
- The F-measure threshold for "no effect" was 0≤F≤100 (white shading); the darkest shading corresponded to F>400.
- For hot freeze-out conditions: entropy per baryon S=165, dynamical timescale τ_dyn=85 ms, electron fraction Y_e=0.30.
- For cold freeze-out conditions: S=105, τ_dyn=50 ms, Y_e=0.30.
- The unsaturated index S for ¹⁶⁸Gd was 6.73×10⁻³ in hot baseline and 1.27×10⁻³ in cold baseline.
- The unsaturated index S for ¹⁷¹Dy was 2.44×10⁻⁴ in cold baseline and 1.23×10⁻⁴ in hot baseline.
- The threshold for significant neutron capture flow was L≥0.2, where L is the integrated neutron capture flow divided by the abundance at maximal Ṡ.
- The neutron capture effect for ¹⁶⁵Eu in hot environment was exhausted near K=50; for ¹⁶⁵Pm in cold environment the effect was maximal near K=10.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Beun et al. (2009) and Surman et al. (2009), who demonstrated that neutron capture rates of single elements near the A=130 peak could produce global changes in the r-process abundance pattern. The present paper focuses specifically on the rare earth region (A~150-180) and introduces the concepts of large nuclear flow and flow saturation to explain why only certain neutron capture rates are influential. The results enable future experimental and theoretical work to prioritize measurements of neutron capture rates on specific rare earth isotopes (those with L≥0.2 and far from saturation) to improve the rare earth peak as a freeze-out diagnostic, as suggested by Mumpower et al. (2012).
