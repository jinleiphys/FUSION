# Quasiparticle properties and the dynamics of high-density nuclear matter

> The energy spectrum of nucleons in high-density nuclear matter is investigated in the framework of relativistic meson-nucleon many-body theory, employing the $1/N$ expansion method. The coupling of the nucleon with the particle-hole excitations in the medium flattens the spectra in the vicinity of the Fermi surface. The effect grows logarithmically for increasing density and eventually leads to instability of the normal state. The validity of the mean-field theory at high density is criticized.

## Key claim

The coupling of nucleons to particle-hole excitations in high-density nuclear matter flattens the quasiparticle energy spectrum near the Fermi surface, and this effect grows logarithmically with density, eventually leading to instability of the normal state. The author shows that the mean-field theory is not valid at high density because infrared singularities from long-wavelength fluctuations dominate over the naive dimension counting that would otherwise favor mean-field contributions.

## Method

The author employs the relativistic sigma-omega (Walecka) model of nuclear matter and uses the 1/N expansion method, where N is the extended number of nucleon species, to compute the quasiparticle energy spectrum beyond the Hartree approximation. The quasiparticle energy is obtained by taking the functional derivative of the energy density with respect to the Fermi distribution function following Landau theory of Fermi liquids, with the self-energy contributions separated into exchange and RPA-type correlation terms. The Fermi velocity is computed as the slope of the quasiparticle spectrum at the Fermi surface, and its high-density asymptotic behavior is analyzed by extracting infrared singularities from the angular integrals of the quasiparticle interaction functions.

## Key numbers

- The critical Fermi momentum where the Fermi velocity crosses zero is pF^c = 4.07 fm^-1 for parameter set B, implying a critical density for neutron matter of approximately 15 times normal nuclear matter density (15 rho_0).
- The leading high-density contribution to the Fermi velocity from the transverse (T) mode grows logarithmically as -(1/2)(g_omega(pF)/(2 pi))^2 ln(pF^2/m_omega^2), where g_omega(pF) is the running coupling constant.
- The Debye screening mass for the longitudinal (L) mode at high density is m_scL^2 = 2 g_omega^2 pF^2/pi^2, while the transverse screening mass m_scT^2 = 0 due to baryon current conservation.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Fermi liquid approach of Tanaka (1993) and Bentz (1993) to investigate the high-density behavior of nuclear matter beyond the mean-field theory of Serot and Walecka (1986). The logarithmic growth of the effective mass enhancement is analogous to the behavior of the nonrelativistic electron gas at high density studied by Gell-Mann and Brueckner (1957). The results suggest a possible first-order phase transition to a Fermi-gap state at high density, which was previously considered in the Hartree-Fock approximation by Horowitz (1985), but the mechanism here is beyond that approximation. The author notes that the critical density for this transition may be above those for quark matter or pion condensation, and that inclusion of pions, rho mesons, or higher orders in 1/N could change the result.
