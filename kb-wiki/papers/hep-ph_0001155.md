# Parametrization of polarized parton distribution functions

> Polarized parton distribution functions are determined by using asymmetry A_1 data from longitudinally polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments. From our χ^2 analysis, polarized u-valence, d-valence, antiquark, and gluon distributions are obtained. We propose one set of leading-order distributions and two sets of next-to-leading-order ones as the longitudinally-polarized parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a set of longitudinally-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) obtained from a χ² analysis of spin asymmetry A₁ data, but the antiquark and gluon distributions cannot be uniquely determined by the existing deep inelastic scattering data, particularly in the small-x region where no data exist below x ≈ 0.04.

## Method

The author performed leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) χ² fits to spin asymmetry A₁ data for the proton, neutron, and deuteron from experiments including E130, E143, EMC, SMC, HERMES, E142, E154, and E155. Polarized PDFs were parametrized at Q² = 1 GeV² using a functional form multiplied by unpolarized GRV PDFs, with the first moments of Δu_v and Δd_v fixed by hyperon and neutron β-decay constants (F = 0.463, D = 0.804). The DGLAP evolution equations were solved numerically to evolve distributions to experimental Q² points, using Λ_QCD⁽³⁾ = 204 MeV (LO) and 299 MeV (NLO) with flavor number N_f = 3.

## Key numbers

- χ²/d.o.f. = 322.6/360 for the LO fit and 300.4/360 for the NLO fit, a difference of about 7%
- First moments fixed from β-decay: η_{u_v} = 0.926 ± 0.014, η_{d_v} = -0.341 ± 0.018
- First moment of antiquark distribution: η_ḡ = -0.064 (LO) and -0.089 (NLO)
- Quark spin content ΔΣ = 0.201 (LO) and 0.051 (NLO) at Q² = 1 GeV²
- Parameter α_ḡ (NLO) = 0.32 ± 0.22, controlling small-x behavior of Δq̄(x)
- With α_ḡ fixed at 1.0, ΔΣ = 0.241; with α_ḡ fixed at 1.6, ΔΣ = 0.276; with α_ḡ fixed at 0.5, ΔΣ = 0.123
- χ² increases relative to free NLO fit by 0.1% (α_ḡ = 0.5), 1.8% (α_ḡ = 1.0), and 7.7% (α_ḡ = 1.6)

## Context

This work extends the Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration (AAC) effort to determine polarized PDFs from existing DIS data, complementing earlier parametrizations by SMC and Leader-Sidrov-Stamenov (LSS) which obtained ΔΣ = 0.19 and 0.28 respectively at Q² = 1 GeV². The analysis shows that the spin content cannot be uniquely determined without data in the small-x region (x < 0.04), and suggests that future RHIC-Spin experiments will provide valuable information on the antiquark and gluon distributions. The author proposes three sets of distributions (LO, NLO with free α_ḡ, and NLO with α_ḡ = 1.0) for practical applications.
