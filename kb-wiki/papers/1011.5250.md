# Modeling QCD for Hadron Physics

> We review the approach to modeling soft hadron physics observables based on the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD. The focus is on light quark mesons and in particular the pseudoscalar and vector ground states, their decays and electromagnetic couplings. We detail the wide variety of observables that can be correlated by a ladder-rainbow kernel with one infrared parameter fixed to the chiral quark condensate. A recently proposed novel perspective in which the quark condensate is contained within hadrons and not the vacuum is mentioned. The valence quark parton distributions, in the pion and kaon, as measured in the Drell Yan process, are investigated with the same ladder-rainbow truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach, using a ladder-rainbow truncation with one infrared parameter fixed to the chiral quark condensate, can correlate a wide variety of soft hadron observables for light quark mesons, but the infrared kernel component remains semi-phenomenological because efforts to solve truncated DSEs for the QCD gauge sector have been unable to produce sufficient kernel strength for an empirically acceptable amount of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors employ the ladder-rainbow truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, where the effective running coupling \(\alpha_{\rm eff}(k^2)\) consists of an infrared term that implements strong enhancement for \(0 < k^2 < 1\,{\rm GeV}^2\) and an ultraviolet term preserving one-loop renormalization group behavior with \(N_f=4\) and \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.234\,{\rm GeV}\). The infrared strength parameter along with two quark masses are fitted to the chiral quark condensate and the pion and kaon masses. For parton distribution functions, the Minkowski-space expression for the valence quark distribution is evaluated using Euclidean numerical solutions for dressed propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, with the distribution evolved to higher scales by leading order DGLAP evolution.

## Key numbers

* Chiral quark condensate: \(-\langle \bar q q \rangle^0_{\mu=1 {\rm GeV}} = (0.241\,{\rm GeV})^{3\dagger}\) (fitted), compared to experimental value \((0.24\,{\rm GeV})^3\)
* Current quark masses at \(\mu=1\,{\rm GeV}\): \(m^{u=d} = 5.5\,{\rm MeV}\) (fitted), \(m^{s} = 125\,{\rm MeV}\) (fitted)
* Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 0.138\,{\rm GeV}^\dagger\) (fitted), pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 0.131\,{\rm GeV}^\dagger\) (fitted)
* Kaon mass: \(m_K = 0.497\,{\rm GeV}^\dagger\) (fitted), kaon decay constant: \(f_K = 0.155\,{\rm GeV}\)
* Rho meson mass: \(m_\rho = 0.742\,{\rm GeV}\), rho decay constant: \(f_\rho = 0.207\,{\rm GeV}\)
* Pion charge radius: \(r_\pi^2 = 0.45\,{\rm fm}^2\) (experiment: \(0.44\,{\rm fm}^2\))
* Pion-two-photon coupling: \(g_{\pi \gamma \gamma} = 0.50\) (experiment: \(0.50\))
* Model scale for parton distributions: \(Q_0 = 0.57\,{\rm GeV}\), found by matching \(x^n\) moments for \(n=1,2,3\) to experimental values at \((2\,{\rm GeV})^2\)
* Momentum sum rule at model scale: \(\int_0^1 dx\, x\, (u_\alpha + \bar{d}_\alpha) = 0.74\) for pion, \(0.76\) for kaon
* Large-\(x\) exponent for pion valence quark distribution at model scale: \(\alpha = 2.08\)

## Context

This work reviews and extends the DSE-based approach to hadron physics developed by Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1997-2003), which successfully correlated pseudoscalar and vector meson masses, decay constants, and electromagnetic couplings. The parton distribution calculations significantly extend the exploratory study by Hecht et al. (2000) that used phenomenological parameterizations, and the results agree with the recent reanalysis of Drell-Yan data by Aicher et al. (2010) that accounted for soft gluon processes. The paper also discusses a novel perspective by Brodsky et al. (2010) in which the quark condensate is a property of hadron Bethe-Salpeter wavefunctions rather than the vacuum, which would eliminate the \(10^{45}\) overestimate of the cosmological constant from QCD vacuum energy density. The approach enables future calculations using modern lattice-QCD results for gluon propagators combined with generalized Bethe-Salpeter kernel constructions.
