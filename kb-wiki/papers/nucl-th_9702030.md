# Meson Exchange Currents in Kaon Scattering on the Lightest Nuclei

> The K^+ scattering on the lightest nuclei, d, 3He and 4He is studied in the framework of multiple scattering theory. Effects from MEC tied to the K^+N-->KNpi reaction are evaluated. We found that at momentum transfers Q^2<0.5 (GeV/c)^2 contributions from MEC are much smaller than kaon rescattering corrections. This makes the conventional multiple scattering picture a reliable tool to study these reactions in this kinematical domain and to extract the K^+n scattering amplitude from the K^+d data. At larger transferred momentum MEC can become more relevant.

## Key claim

The authors find that at momentum transfers \(Q^2 < 0.5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), contributions from meson exchange currents (MEC) in \(K^+\) scattering on the lightest nuclei are much smaller than kaon rescattering corrections, making the conventional multiple scattering picture reliable in this kinematical domain; however, at larger transferred momentum MEC can become more relevant.

## Method

The authors construct a MEC operator for finite nuclei using the \(K^+N \rightarrow KN\pi\) amplitude from chiral perturbation theory, including pion pole and contact terms. They evaluate the MEC contributions to \(K^+\) elastic scattering on deuteron, \(^3\)He, and \(^4\)He within the KMT version of multiple scattering theory, using realistic nuclear wave functions (Paris potential for deuteron, Faddeev solutions with Reid-Soft-Core for \(^3\)He, and a charge form factor from electron scattering for \(^4\)He). The spin-isospin matrix elements of the two-body MEC operator are computed explicitly in a basis of nucleon spin and isospin states.

## Key numbers

- At momentum transfers \(Q^2 < 0.5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), MEC contributions are much smaller than kaon rescattering corrections.
- For \(K^+d\) scattering, the simple PWIA approximation is good at momentum transfers \(Q^2 = -t < 0.2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), where most experimental data are concentrated.
- At backward angles for \(K^+d\) scattering at \(k_{lab} = 587\) MeV/c, MEC reduce the differential cross section by about a factor of two, but this effect is practically cancelled by kaon rescattering.
- For \(K^+\) scattering on deuteron and \(^3\)He at kaon momentum around 600 MeV/c and backward angles, MEC can reduce the cross section by about a factor of two, but the cross sections in that region are about four and five orders of magnitude smaller than at forward angles.
- At \(k_{lab} < 400\) MeV/c and \(Q^2 < 0.2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), corrections from the deuteron \(D\)-state and kaon rescattering are small.
- The \(\pi NN\) coupling constant used is \(f_{\pi}^2/4\pi = 0.08\).
- The off-shell form factor cutoff is \(\Lambda = 1300\) MeV.
- The chiral coupling constants are \(D = 0.85\), \(F = 0.52\), and \(f = 92.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(K^+\) scattering on light nuclei by Paez and Landau (1981) and earlier MEC calculations in nuclear matter by Jiang and Koltun (1992), Garcia-Recio, Nieves, and Oset (1995), and Meissner, Oset, and Pich (1995). The authors construct the MEC operator for finite nuclei for the first time, using the \(K^+N \rightarrow KN\pi\) amplitude from Oset and Vicente Vacas (1996). The results justify the conventional multiple scattering approach for extracting the \(K^+n\) amplitude from \(K^+d\) data and provide a foundation for extending such studies to heavier nuclei.
