# Unique forbidden beta decays at zero momentum transfer

> We report an exploratory study of the $\mathcal{O}(α)$ structure-dependent electromagnetic radiative corrections to unique first-forbidden nuclear beta decays. We show that the insertion of angular momentum into the nuclear matrix element by the virtual/real photon exchange opens up the decay at vanishing nuclear recoil momentum which was forbidden at tree level, leading to a dramatic change in the decay spectrum not anticipated in existing studies. We discuss its implications for precision tests on the Standard Model and searches for new physics.

# Unique forbidden beta decays at zero momentum transfer

## Key claim
The authors show that O(α) structure-dependent electromagnetic radiative corrections open up unique first-forbidden nuclear beta decays at vanishing nuclear recoil momentum (q→0), a kinematic region that was forbidden at tree level due to angular momentum selection rules. This leads to a dramatic change in the decay spectrum that has not been anticipated in existing studies, and the radiative correction amplitude can actually overtake the tree-level contribution at small momentum transfer.

## Method
The authors compute the O(α) γW-box diagram and bremsstrahlung diagram contributions to unique first-forbidden beta decays (specifically transitions with |J_i−J_f|=2, P_i=−P_f, such as 2^−↔0^+). They perform a multipole expansion of the generalized Compton tensor T^{μν}(k) in the ultrasoft photon region, retaining only the Z_f-enhanced ground-state contribution from the electromagnetic Coulomb operator. The ultraviolet-divergent integrals are regulated using a monopole form factor for the nuclear charge distribution, with Λ^2=6/R_C^2, to obtain a first rough estimate of the radiative corrections.

## Key numbers
- For ⁹⁰Sr→⁹⁰Y (Q_β = 545.9(14) keV, R_C ≈ 4.26 fm): the correction to the total decay rate is 1−Γ_tree/Γ_tot ≈ −7.7%, though this is partially due to accidental cancellation between the interference and squared radiative terms.
- The tree-level squared amplitude scales as |M_tree|² ∝ q², the tree-loop interference scales as q, while |M_γW|² approaches a constant as q→0, leading to radiative dominance at small momentum transfer.
- The squared radiative amplitude |M_γW|² contains logarithmic terms L_Λ ≡ ln(Λ/|p_e|) where Λ² = 6/R_C².

## Context
This work extends the study of radiative corrections from allowed beta decays (where they are central to V_ud extraction, e.g., Seng (2018), Cirigliano (2023, 2024)) to forbidden decays, which have recently gained attention for probing new physics through measurements of the Fierz term and electron-neutrino angular correlation (Glick-Magid (2016, 2023)). Existing theory analyses of forbidden decays focus on tree-level amplitudes, Coulomb effects, shape factors, recoil corrections, and outer radiative corrections (Wilkinson (1998)), but lack the full one-loop structure-dependent radiative correction studied here. The work enables new experimental opportunities: by focusing on the small-q region, one can evade large tree-level nuclear uncertainties and directly probe radiative correction physics and potentially light new degrees of freedom, relevant to anomalies such as the ATOMKI anomaly (Krasznahorkay (2015)). Planned experiments at Hebrew University (⁹⁰Sr, ⁹⁰Y, ¹⁶N aiming for 10⁻³ accuracy) and Oak Ridge National Laboratory (⁹⁰Y, ¹⁴⁴Pr aiming for 1-2% accuracy) will need these corrections included.
