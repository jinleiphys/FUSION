# Structure of near-threshold quasi-bound states

> We study the compositeness of near-threshold quasi-bound states in the framework of effective field theory. From the viewpoint of the low-energy universality, we revisit the model-independent relations between the structure of the bound state and the observables in the weak binding limit. The effective field theory is utilized to generalize the weak-binding relation of the stable bound states to unstable quasi-bound states with decay modes. We present the interpretation of the complex values of the compositeness for the unstable states. Combining the model-independent relation and the threshold observables extracted from the experimental data, we show that Lambda(1405) is dominated by the Kbar N molecular structure and that a_0(980) is dominated by the non-Kbar K component.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes Weinberg's model-independent weak-binding relation for stable bound states to unstable quasi-bound states with decay channels, showing that the compositeness (probability of composite structure) can be extracted from observables even when the state has a finite width, provided the binding is weak and the decay threshold is sufficiently separated.

## Method

The authors construct a nonrelativistic effective field theory (EFT) with contact interactions for two distinguishable particles, first for a single channel with a stable bound state, then extending to two channels by adding a decay channel. They solve the two-body problem exactly, derive the scattering amplitude, and expand the scattering length in powers of 1/|R| where R = 1/√(-2μE_QB) is the complex radius of the quasi-bound state. The compositeness X is expressed in terms of the scattering length a0 and eigenenergy E_QB, with correction terms suppressed by the ratio of the interaction range to |R| and by |l/R|^3 where l is the length scale from the decay threshold energy difference.

## Key numbers

- For Λ(1405): eigenenergies from five analyses range from (-4 - i8) MeV to (-13 - i20) MeV, with scattering lengths from (1.21 - i1.47) fm to (1.81 - i0.92) fm; the compositeness X̃_KN is 0.6–1.0, with uncertainty U = 0.0–0.6
- For Λ(1405): correction terms are |R_typ/R| ≤ 0.17 and |l/R|^3 ≤ 0.14, using the ρ meson exchange range for R_typ
- For a0(980): eigenenergies from five analyses range from (3 - i25) MeV to (31 - i70) MeV, with scattering lengths from (-0.13 - i0.52) fm to (-0.13 - i2.19) fm; the compositeness X̃_KK is 0.1–0.7, with U = 0.1–0.3
- For a0(980): |r_e/a0| ranges from 0.5 to 13, with most values much larger than 1.5
- For f0(980): eigenenergies from six analyses range from (-10 - i29) MeV to (19 - i30) MeV, with scattering lengths from (0.02 - i0.95) fm to (0.84 - i0.85) fm; the compositeness X̃_KK is 0.3–0.9, with U = 0.0–0.2

## Context

This work extends Weinberg's (1965) weak-binding relation for stable bound states to unstable quasi-bound states, which was previously limited to stable systems like the deuteron. The EFT formulation provides a clearer derivation than the original work and enables the generalization to states with decay width. The method is applied to Λ(1405) and a0(980), f0(980) using empirical threshold parameters from chiral effective theory analyses (Ikeda 2012, Mai 2012, Guo 2012, Mai 2014) and Flatte parametrizations (Adams 2011, Ambrosino 2009, Bugg 2008, Achasov 2000, Teige 1996, Aaltonen 2011, Ambrosino 2005, Garmash 2005, Ablikim 2004, Link 2004, Achasov 2000). The results agree with explicit model calculations by Sekihara (2014) and lattice QCD by Hall (2014), and the framework can be applied to other exotic hadrons once threshold parameters are determined.
