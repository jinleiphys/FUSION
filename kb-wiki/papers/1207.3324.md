# Spin and Pseudospin symmetries in the Dirac equation with central Coulomb potentials

> We analyze in detail the analytical solutions of the Dirac equation with scalar S and vector V Coulomb radial potentials near the limit of spin and pseudospin symmetries, i.e., when those potentials have the same magnitude and either the same sign or opposite signs, respectively. By performing an expansion of the relevant coefficients we also assess the perturbative nature of both symmetries and their relations the (pseudo)spin-orbit coupling. The former analysis is made for both positive and negative energy solutions and we reproduce the relations between spin and pseudospin symmetries found before for nuclear mean-field potentials. We discuss the node structure of the radial functions and the quantum numbers of the solutions when there is spin or pseudospin symmetry, which we find to be similar to the well-known solutions of hydrogenic atoms.

# Spin and Pseudospin symmetries in the Dirac equation with central Coulomb potentials

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for Dirac equation solutions with scalar and vector Coulomb potentials, spin symmetry is perturbative and pseudospin symmetry is non-perturbative for positive energy states, while the reverse holds for negative energy states, confirming earlier results obtained with Woods-Saxon nuclear mean-field potentials. The authors show that exact spin and pseudospin symmetries produce energy levels depending only on the principal quantum number, analogous to non-relativistic hydrogenic atoms.

## Method

The authors analytically solve the Dirac equation with scalar (S) and vector (V) Coulomb potentials, expressed in terms of sum Σ = V+S and difference Δ = V−S potentials. They perform expansions of the energy eigenvalue formula to next-to-next-to-leading order in small α_Δ (for spin symmetry) and small α_Σ (for pseudospin symmetry), and analyze the node structure of the radial wave functions g_κ(r) and f_κ(r) using generalized Laguerre polynomials. The analysis covers both positive (E⁺) and negative (E⁻) energy solutions, with the charge conjugation mapping between them.

## Key numbers

- For spin symmetry (α_Δ = 0), positive energy eigenvalues: E⁺_{n,κ} = mc²(1 − 2α_Σ²/(α_Σ² + 4n²))
- For pseudospin symmetry (α_Σ = 0), negative energy eigenvalues: E⁻_{n,κ} = mc²(−1 + 2α_Δ²/(α_Δ² + 4n²))
- The expansion for E⁺ near spin symmetry (small α_Δ) includes a first-order term: 4α_Σ³(κ² − 2n|κ|)/(κ²(α_Σ² + 4n²)²) · α_Δ
- The expansion for E⁺ near pseudospin symmetry (small α_Σ) includes a first-order term: α_Δ(κ² − 2n|κ|)/(4κ²n⁴) · α_Σ³
- For α_Δ = 0.5 and α_Σ = −0.8, the radial wave functions for E⁺ states 2s_{1/2} and 2p_{1/2} (κ = ∓1, n_r = 1) show n_g = n_f = n_r + 1 = 2 nodes
- For the same potential strengths, E⁻ states 4p_{3/2} and 4d_{3/2} (κ = ∓2, n_r = 2) show n_g = n_f = n_r + 1 = 3 nodes
- The non-relativistic limit yields E⁺ − mc² ∼ −mc²α_V²/(2n²) when α_Σ = α_Δ = α_V and α_V ≪ ξ, ξ ∼ n

## Context

This work extends the analysis of spin and pseudospin symmetries from nuclear mean-field potentials (Woods-Saxon type, studied by Marcos (2002), Zhou (2003), and Alberto (2005)) to Coulomb potentials, which have analytical solutions. The authors reproduce the relations between spin and pseudospin symmetries found earlier for nuclear mean-field potentials by Ginocchio (2005) and Leviatan (2004), and confirm that the node structure differs from the rule derived by Leviatan and Ginocchio for finite-range potentials. The results enable further study of how these symmetries manifest in atomic systems with mixed scalar-vector Coulomb fields, originally investigated by Greiner and Rafelski for atomic spectroscopy.
