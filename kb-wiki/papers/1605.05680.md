# Forward $J/ψ$ production at high energy: centrality dependence and mean transverse momentum

> Forward rapidity $J/ψ$ meson production in proton-nucleus collisions can be an important constraint of descriptions of the small-$x$ nuclear wavefunction. In an earlier work we studied this process using a dipole cross section satisfying the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, fit to HERA inclusive data and consistently extrapolated to the nuclear case using a standard Woods-Saxon distribution. In this paper we present further calculations of these cross sections, studying the mean transverse momentum of the meson and the dependence on collision centrality. We also extend the calculation to backward rapidities using nuclear parton distribution functions. We show that the parametrization is overall rather consistent with the available experimental data. However, there is a tendency towards a too strong centrality dependence. This can be traced back to the rather small transverse area occupied by small-$x$ gluons in the nucleon that is seen in the HERA data, compared to the total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Key claim

The authors show that their Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, using a dipole cross section fit to HERA inclusive data and extrapolated to nuclei via the optical Glauber model, is overall consistent with available experimental data for forward J/ψ production, but exhibits a tendency towards too strong centrality dependence, which they trace to the small transverse area occupied by small-x gluons in the nucleon compared to the total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Method

The authors compute J/ψ production cross sections in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions using the color evaporation model within the dilute-dense CGC framework, where the projectile gluon is described by collinear parton distribution functions and the target is described by a dipole cross section obtained from solving the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation. The nuclear dipole cross section is obtained by generalizing the proton case using the optical Glauber model with a Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile, and the calculation is extended to backward rapidities using nuclear parton distribution functions (EPS09). The centrality dependence is studied by relating impact parameter in the optical Glauber model to experimental centrality classes via the average number of binary collisions.

## Key numbers

- The best fit parameters from HERA inclusive DIS data (Q² < 50 GeV², x < 0.01) are: Q_s0² = 0.060 GeV², C² = 7.2, e_c = 18.9, and σ_0/2 = 16.36 mb, with χ²/d.o.f = 1.15
- The charm quark mass is varied between 1.2 and 1.5 GeV, and the D meson mass is taken as M_D = 1.864 GeV
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear density parameters are d = 0.54 fm and R_A = (1.12A^{1/3} - 0.86A^{-1/3}) fm
- The average number of binary collisions in the optical Glauber model ranges from ⟨N_coll⟩ = 14.7 (2-10% centrality) to ⟨N_coll⟩ = 1.5 (80-100% centrality)
- The saturation scale of the lead nucleus falls below that of the proton at impact parameter b ≈ 6.3 fm, corresponding to N_coll ≈ 4.3
- The effective proton transverse area σ_0/2 ≈ 0.3 σ_inel, where σ_inel is the total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CGC calculation of forward J/ψ production (Ducloue 2015) by studying mean transverse momentum, centrality dependence, and backward rapidity production, and compares with LHC data from ALICE and LHCb. The calculation differs from earlier CGC calculations such as Fujii (2013) by using a dipole cross section fit to HERA data and consistently extrapolated to nuclei, which yields smaller nuclear suppression for minimum bias events. The results enable future comparisons with higher-energy LHC runs and highlight the need for unbiased N_coll distributions to properly compare centrality-dependent calculations with experimental data.
