# Improved convergence of chiral effective field theory for 1S0 of NN scattering

> It is argued that the fine-tuning due to the unnaturally large, generalized effective range in the 1S0 channel of NN scattering must be incorporated in order for one to obtain satisfactory convergence for chiral effective field theory. Without the proposition of perturbative one-pion exchange, an effective field theory with the spin-0, isospin-1 dibaryon is developed to account for this fine-tuning, and is demonstrated up to O(Q) where the leading irreducible two-pion exchange arises. The approach shown in the paper results in rapid convergence of the 1S0 partial-wave amplitude.

## Key claim

The unnaturally large generalized effective range in the 1S0 channel of nucleon-nucleon scattering (2/˜r ~ 100 MeV, compared to a natural value around 600 MeV) must be resummed to all orders to achieve satisfactory convergence of chiral effective field theory, which is accomplished by introducing a spin-0, isospin-1 dibaryon field whose s-channel exchange brings the desired energy dependence into the leading-order potential.

## Method

The author introduces an auxiliary dibaryon field φ with the same quantum numbers as the 1S0 partial wave, whose s-channel exchange replaces the constant contact interaction at leading order, thereby resumming the generalized effective range contributions to all orders. The leading-order potential consists of one-pion exchange plus the dibaryon exchange, and subleading corrections including the leading irreducible two-pion exchange are treated perturbatively up to O(Q¹). The amplitude is regularized with a separable momentum-space regulator exp(-(p'⁴ + p⁴)/Λ⁴) and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved nonperturbatively for the leading order, with subleading corrections computed through perturbative insertions.

## Key numbers

- Generalized effective range: ˜r/2 = 1.55 fm = 1/(127 MeV) (from Steele (1998))
- For cutoff Λ = 800 MeV, the fitted ˜r/2 = 1/(115 MeV)
- Shape parameter ˜v₂ = (˜r²/4)/(550 MeV) from Steele (1998); for Λ = 800 MeV, ˜v₂ = (˜r²/4)/(693 MeV)
- Leading-order EFT predicts ~65° for k ~ mπ (k ~ 138 MeV), while Nijmegen partial-wave analysis gives ~40°
- Pion mass mπ = 138 MeV, axial coupling gA = 1.26, pion decay constant fπ = 92.4 MeV, nucleon mass mN = 939 MeV
- 1S0 scattering length a = -24 fm (unnaturally large compared to natural O(1) fm)

## Context

This work extends the dibaryon approach of Kaplan (1996) to systematically include higher-order corrections, particularly irreducible two-pion exchanges, as part of ongoing efforts by Long (2007, 2011, 2012) to modify Weinberg's original power counting for chiral nuclear forces. It addresses the slow convergence problem identified in comparisons between Weinberg's leading-order chiral effective field theory and Nijmegen partial-wave analysis (Stoks, 1993), and goes beyond renormalization-group-based modifications (Barford, 2002; Birse, 2005; Nogga, 2005; Valderrama, 2009) that did not specifically consider fine-tuning of momentum-dependent counterterms. The approach enables systematic inclusion of two-pion exchange contributions while maintaining rapid convergence, though at the cost of an additional parameter at each order.
