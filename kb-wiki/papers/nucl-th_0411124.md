# Nuclear Physics with electroweak probes

> The last few years activity of the Italian community concerning nuclear physics with electroweak probes is reviewed.Inclusive quasi-elastic electron-scattering, photon end electron induced one- and two-nucleon emission are considered. The scattering of neutrinos off nuclei in the quasi-elastic region is also discussed.

## Key claim

The Italian community's theoretical work on lepton scattering off medium and heavy nuclei (A>4) during 2002-2004 shows that mean-field models with one-body currents provide a good starting point for describing quasi-elastic excitation, but the paper hedges that the comparison with experimental data remains problematic, particularly for separated longitudinal and transverse responses where Saclay data are not well reproduced while MIT data are.

## Method

The paper reviews calculations using non-relativistic and relativistic mean-field models, random phase approximation (RPA), and local density approximation (LDA) to describe inclusive electron scattering (e,e'), one-nucleon emission (e,e'N) and (γ,N), two-nucleon emission (e,e'NN) and (γ,NN), and neutrino-nucleus scattering in the quasi-elastic region. Meson exchange currents (MEC), final state interactions (FSI), and short-range correlations (SRC) are included as perturbations to the mean-field results, with comparisons made between Fermi gas models and finite-nucleus calculations.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic effects reduce the peak height of the transverse response in Fermi gas by about 20% compared to non-relativistic calculations
- In finite nuclei, relativity lowers the maximum of the 16O(e,e'p)15N reduced cross section by about 10%
- The spectroscopic factor for 16O(e,e'p)15N is 0.7 in the relativistic mean-field model and 0.65 in the non-relativistic mean-field model
- FSI reduce the total neutrino-nucleus cross section by about 10%
- Correlations reduce the peak of the 16O(ν,e-)16F cross section for 1 GeV neutrinos by about 20%
- Fermi gas calculations show about a 25% difference between neutrino cross sections with and without strangeness in the nucleon form factor
- In electron scattering on 16O, the 1- multipole contributes 98% of the total cross section, while in neutrino scattering (ν,ν') the 1- contributes only 33% and the 2- contributes 58%

## Context

This review extends the work of the Italian community (Alb97, Pac01, Cen03, Cen01) on lepton-nucleus scattering, addressing uncertainties in reaction mechanisms and nuclear structure that were previously unresolved. The results enable improved predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for existing and planned neutrino experiments, and suggest that two-nucleon emission with real photons is the ideal tool to study short-range correlations, as shown by the Pavia and Lecce groups (Giu03b, Ang04). The paper also highlights the need for a major experimental program to investigate medium-heavy nuclei with electromagnetic probes, echoing Orlandini (2004).
