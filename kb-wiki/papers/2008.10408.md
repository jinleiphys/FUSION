# The total reaction cross section of heavy-ion reactions induced by stable and unstable exotic beams: The low-energy regime

> In this review paper we present a detailed account of the extraction and the calculation of the total reaction cross section of strongly bound and weakly bound, stable and unstable, exotic, nuclei. We discuss the optical model and the more general coupled channels model of direct reactions. The effect of long-range absorption due to the coupling to excited states in the target and to the breakup continuum in the projectile is also discussed. The generalized optical theorem for charged particle scattering and the resulting sum-of differences method is then discussed. The so-called "quarter-point recipe" is discussed next, and the quarter-point angle is introduced as a simple and rapid mean to obtain the total reaction cross section. The last topic discussed is the reduction of the total reaction cross section that would allow a large body of data to sit on a single universal function. Such a universal function exists in the case of the fusion data, and the aim of this last topic of the review is to extend the fusion case to the total reaction, by adding the direct reaction contribution. Also discussed is the inclusive breakup cross section and how it can be used to extract the total reaction cross section of the interacting fragment with the target. This method is also known as the Surrogate method and represents a case of hybrid reactions. The sum of the integrated inclusive breakup cross section with the complete fusion cross section supplies the total fusion cross section. The main experimental methods to determine the total reaction cross section are also discussed, with emphasis in recent techniques developed to deal with reactions induced by unstable beams.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that the total reaction cross section for heavy-ion reactions, including those induced by weakly bound stable and unstable exotic beams at low energies, can be extracted and calculated using established methods (optical model, coupled channels, CDCC) and that a universal reduction function for total reaction cross sections, analogous to the one existing for fusion data, can be constructed by adding the direct reaction contribution to the fusion case.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for calculating and extracting the total reaction cross section, starting from the single-channel optical model with a complex potential and extending to the coupled channels model and the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method for handling breakup. It discusses the semiclassical WKB approximation, the Hill-Wheeler transmission coefficient for fusion, and the Wong formula derived from a parabolic barrier approximation. The review also covers the generalized optical theorem, the sum-of-differences method, the quarter-point recipe for a rapid estimate from elastic scattering data, and the surrogate method for extracting the total reaction cross section of an interacting fragment with the target.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy for the Borromean halo nucleus $^6$He is $S_{2n} = 0.973$ MeV; for $^{11}$Li it is $S_{2n} = 0.369$ MeV; for $^{14}$Be it is $S_{2n} = 1.27$ MeV; for $^{22}$C it is $S_{2n} = 0.42 \pm 0.94$ MeV.
- The one-proton separation energy for the halo isotope $^8$B is $S_{p} = 0.14$ MeV.
- The one-neutron separation energy for $^{11}$Be is $S_{n} = 0.503$ MeV; for $^{15}$C it is $S_{n} = 1.218$ MeV; for $^{19}$C it is $S_{n} = 0.242 \pm 0.095$ MeV; for $^{23}$O it is $S_{n} = 2.74$ MeV.
- The breakup threshold for deuteron scattering is 2.2 MeV, compared to 0.369 MeV for $^{11}$Li and 0.973 MeV for $^6$He.
- Typical parameters for a Woods-Saxon imaginary potential simulating pure fusion absorption are $W_0 = 50$ MeV, $r_{\rm 0i} \sim 1.0$ fm, and $a_{\rm 0i} \sim 0.2$ fm.
- The Wong formula for fusion cross section is $\sigma^{\scr W}(E) = \frac{\hbar\omega\, R_{\scr B}^2}{2E}\ \ln{\left[1 + \exp{\left(\frac{2\pi}{\hbar\omega}(E - V_{\scr B})\right)}\right]}$, where $R_{\scr B}$, $V_{\scr B}$, and $\hbar\omega$ are the s-wave barrier radius, height, and curvature parameter.
- At energies few MeV above the Coulomb barrier, the Wong formula approximates to $\sigma_{\scr F}(E) \simeq \pi R_{\scr B}^2 \left[ 1 - \frac{V_{\scr B}}{E} \right]$.
- For the $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C system, the standard Wong cross section at 40 MeV is about 3/2 of the one obtained with energy-dependent barrier parameters.

## Context

This review extends the earlier comprehensive review by Hussein, Rego and Bertulani (1991) on the theory of the total reaction cross section, which was written when few data existed for exotic and weakly bound projectiles. The present work provides an updated account incorporating new elastic scattering data for exotic nuclear systems and discusses the CDCC method (SYK86, AIK87) as a basic tool for describing collisions of weakly bound projectiles. It enables future work by outlining a path to construct a universal function for total reaction cross sections, analogous to the one existing for fusion data, and by detailing experimental methods for unstable beams.
