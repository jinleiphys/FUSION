# Quarkonium inside Quark-Gluon Plasma: Diffusion, Dissociation, Recombination and Energy Loss

> We consider the quarkonium diffusion, dissociation and recombination inside quark-gluon plasma. We compute scattering amplitudes in potential nonrelativistic QCD for relevant processes. These processes include the gluon absorption/emission at the order $gr$, inelastic scattering at the order $g^2r$ and elastic scattering with medium constituents at the order $g^2r^2$. We show these amplitudes satisfy the Ward identity. We also consider one-loop corrections. The dipole interaction between the color singlet and octet is not running at the one-loop level. Interference between the tree-level gluon absorption/emission and its thermal loop corrections cancels the collinear divergence in the $t$-channel inelastic scattering. The inelastic scattering has no soft divergence because of the finite binding energy of quarkonium. We write out the diffusion, dissociation and recombination terms explicitly for a Boltzmann transport equation and define the dissociation and recombination rates. Furthermore, we calculate the diffusion coefficient of quarkonium. We find our result of diffusion coefficient differs from a previous calculation by two to three orders of magnitude. We explain this and can reproduce the previous result in a certain limit. Finally we discuss two mechanisms of quarkonium energy loss inside quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper computes quarkonium dissociation, recombination, and diffusion within a unified pNRQCD framework, finding that the diffusion coefficient differs from a previous calculation by two to three orders of magnitude, but can reproduce that earlier result in a certain limit.

## Method

The authors compute scattering amplitudes in potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) for gluon absorption/emission at order \(gr\), inelastic scattering at order \(g^2r\), and elastic scattering at order \(g^2r^2\), then verify the Ward identities for these amplitudes. They include one-loop corrections to the gluon absorption/emission process and write out explicit dissociation, recombination, and diffusion terms for a Boltzmann transport equation, also calculating the diffusion coefficient of quarkonium.

## Key numbers

* The diffusion coefficient of quarkonium differs from a previous calculation by two to three orders of magnitude.
* The inelastic scattering amplitude squared contains terms proportional to \( \frac{p_1p_2 + {\bs p}_1\cdot {\bs p}_2}{{\bs q}^2} \) and \( \frac{2(q^0)^2(p_1p_2-{\bs p}_1\cdot\hat{q} \cdot {\bs p}_2\cdot \hat{q})}{((q^0)^2-{\bs q}^2+i\epsilon)^2} \), with the binding energy \(|E_{nl}|\) serving as a soft regulator: \(p_1^2+p_2^2-2p_1p_2\cos\theta \geq (p_1-p_2)^2 \geq |E_{nl}|^2\).
* The summed squared amplitude for inelastic scattering with light quarks is \( \frac{16}{3}g^4V_A^2T_FC_F |\langle \Psi_{{\bs p}_\ma{rel}} | {\bs r} | \psi_{nl} \rangle|^2 \Big[ \frac{p_1p_2 + {\bs p}_1\cdot {\bs p}_2}{{\bs q}^2} + \frac{2(q^0)^2(p_1p_2-{\bs p}_1\cdot\hat{q} \cdot {\bs p}_2\cdot \hat{q})}{((q^0)^2-{\bs q}^2+i\epsilon)^2} \Big] \).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quarkonium dissociation rates computed via the optical theorem and loop corrections to forward amplitudes in pNRQCD (Brambilla 2010, 2011, 2013) by directly computing scattering amplitudes and explicitly verifying Ward identities. It also builds on prior work on quarkonium diffusion coefficients estimated in both weak and strong coupling limits (Dusling 2008) and on Boltzmann transport equations for quarkonium coupled with heavy quark distributions (Yao 2017). The results enable first-principles inclusion of diffusion in phenomenological transport studies of quarkonium in quark-gluon plasma, and clarify the relationship between different theoretical calculations of the diffusion coefficient.
