# Chiral expansion of nucleon PDF at $x\sim m_π/M_N$

> Based on the chiral perturbation theory, we investigate the low-energy dynamics of nucleon parton distributions. We show that in different regions of the momentum fraction $x$ the chiral expansion is significantly different. For nucleon parton distributions these regions are characterized by $x\sim1$, $x\sim m_π/M_N$ and $x\sim (m_π/M_N)^2$. We derive extended counting rules for each region and obtain model-independent results for the nucleon parton distributions down to $x\gtrsim m^2_π/M^2_N \approx 10^{-2}$.

## Key claim
The chiral expansion of nucleon parton distributions reorganizes differently in distinct regions of the momentum fraction \(x\), and the leading chiral correction is non-linear in the chiral expansion parameter \(a_\chi^2\) rather than linear, contrary to what a naive expansion would suggest. The paper derives model-independent results valid down to \(x \gtrsim m_\pi^2/M_N^2 \approx 10^{-2}\), but the actual expressions involve resummation of higher-order terms and depend on unknown generating functions for the analytical part.

## Method
The authors construct effective nonlocal operators in meson-baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) for nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and PDFs, using a counting scheme where the light-cone vector \(n_\mu\) scales as \(1/m_\pi\) for \(x > m_\pi^2/M_N^2\). They evaluate the leading nonanalytical chiral corrections to the isovector vector nucleon PDF at one-loop order using the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme, obtaining expressions that involve convolutions of chiral-limit PDFs with coefficient functions \(C\), \(\Delta C\), and \(C_\pi\).

## Key numbers
- The small parameter of the chiral expansion is \(a_\chi = m_\pi/(4\pi F_\pi) \ll 1\), with the pion mass \(m_\pi\) and pion decay constant \(F_\pi\).
- The ratio \(\alpha = m_\pi/M_N \approx 0.15\) defines the three \(x\)-regions: \(x \sim 1\), \(x \sim \alpha\), and \(x \sim \alpha^2\).
- The axial-vector coupling constant used is \(g_a \approx 1.27\).
- The model-independent result for the nucleon transverse size \(\langle b^2(x)\rangle\) is obtained for \(x \gtrsim \alpha^2 \approx 10^{-2}\).

## Context
This work extends the chiral perturbation theory analysis of nucleon parton distributions beyond the region \(x \sim 1\) previously studied by Chen (2001), Arndt (2001), and Diehl (2006), which gave the form-factor-like result \(q(x,\Delta^2) = q(x)F(\Delta^2)\). It resolves contradictions between the convolution model (Sullivan process) and straightforward ChPT calculations, as noted by Burkardt (2012), and provides a framework that can be compared with dispersion analyses such as Granados (2013). The results enable model-independent predictions for the transverse size of the nucleon and may help reconcile experimental data on the pion cloud with the Sullivan process description, as tested by Traini (2013).
