# A broad pseudovector glueball from holographic QCD

> We study the decay of a pseudovector glueball with quantum numbers $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$, the lightest glueball with spin different from zero or two, in the top-down holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model. This glueball is dual to the string-theoretic Kalb-Ramond tensor field, which completely fixes all its couplings by gauge invariance and $D$-brane anomaly inflow. The dominant decay channels are determined by a Chern-Simons-like action for the flavor branes; couplings from the Dirac-Born-Infeld action turn out to be subdominant. While the resulting width is parametrically of order $N_c^{-2}λ^{-1}$ for $N_c$ colors and 't Hooft coupling $λ$, when extrapolated to $N_c=3$ and with $λ$ matched to experimental data, the prediction is that the pseudovector glueball is an extremely broad resonance.

# A broad pseudovector glueball from holographic QCD

## Key claim

The pseudovector glueball with quantum numbers J^PC = 1^+-, the lightest glueball with spin different from zero or two, is predicted to be an extremely broad resonance with Γ/M ∼ 1 when extrapolated to N_c = 3 and with the 't Hooft coupling λ matched to experimental data, even though its decay width is parametrically of order N_c^{-2} λ^{-1}.

## Method

The authors study the decay of the pseudovector glueball in the top-down holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto (WSS) model, where this glueball is dual to the string-theoretic Kalb-Ramond two-form field. They derive the effective four-dimensional interaction Lagrangian from the Chern-Simons part of the flavor brane action, which completely fixes all couplings by gauge invariance and D-brane anomaly inflow, and evaluate decay rates into pseudoscalar and vector mesons by performing phase space integrals over the corresponding amplitudes. Couplings from the Dirac-Born-Infeld action are shown to be subdominant both parametrically and numerically.

## Key numbers

- Pseudovector glueball mass in the WSS model: M ≈ 2311 MeV (from the mode equation with lowest eigenvalue M ≈ 2.435 M_KK, where M_KK = 949 MeV is set by identifying the ρ meson mass)
- Total decay width to mass ratio: Γ/M = 0.9225 to 1.3685 (for λ = 16.63 to 12.55, N_c = 3)
- Two-body decay channel π ρ: Γ/M = 0.3624 to 0.4803
- Two-body decay channel K K*: Γ/M = 0.1945 to 0.2578
- Three-body decay channel π ρ ρ: Γ/M = 0.2595 to 0.4556
- Coupling constant for two-body decays: c_a = 56.027 N_c^{-1} λ^{-1/2}
- Coupling constant for three-body decays: c_b = 2571.72 N_c^{-3/2} λ^{-1}
- Parametric scaling of two-body decays: order N_c^{-2} λ^{-1}
- Parametric scaling of three-body decays: order N_c^{-3} λ^{-2}

## Context

This work extends previous holographic calculations of glueball decays in the WSS model for scalar and tensor glueballs (Brunner 2015, Rebhan 2016) and for the pseudoscalar glueball (Brunner 2016). The scalar glueball was found to match the experimental candidate f_0(1710), while the tensor glueball was predicted to be a very broad state with Γ/M ∼ 0.5 for M ∼ 2.4 GeV. The pseudovector glueball is predicted to be even broader, with Γ/M ∼ 1, suggesting it would be very difficult to identify as an isolated non-q\bar{q} isoscalar h_1 meson in the hadron spectrum. The results also indicate that phenomenological models of h_1 and f_2 mesons should consider possibly important glueball content of those states.
