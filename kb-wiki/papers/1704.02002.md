# Inclusive neutrino scattering off deuteron at low energies in chiral effective field theory

> Cross sections for inclusive neutrino scattering off deuteron induced by neutral and charge-changing weak currents are calculated from threshold up to 150 MeV energies in a chiral effective field theory including high orders in the power counting. Contributions beyond leading order (LO) in the weak current are found to be small, and increase the cross sections obtained with the LO transition operators by a couple of percent over the whole energy range (0--150) MeV. The cutoff dependence is negligible, and the predicted cross sections are within $\sim 2$\% of, albeit consistently larger than, corresponding predictions obtained in conventional meson-exchange frameworks.

## Key claim

Cross sections for inclusive neutrino scattering off deuteron calculated in chiral effective field theory (χEFT) at high orders in the power counting are consistently larger by about 1–2% than predictions from conventional meson-exchange frameworks, with contributions beyond leading order (LO) in the weak current increasing the LO cross sections by only a couple of percent over the entire energy range from threshold to 150 MeV.

## Method

The authors computed total cross sections for neutral-current (NC) and charge-changing (CC) neutrino-deuteron scattering using χEFT nuclear potentials up to order Q⁴ (Entem and Machleidt, 2003; Machleidt, 2011) and weak current and charge operators including one- and two-body terms up to N3LO in the vector current and axial charge, and up to N4LO in the axial current and vector charge. Matrix elements between the deuteron and two-nucleon scattering states were evaluated by direct numerical integration of the weak current operators, with final-state interactions accounted for in partial waves up to J_max = 5, using regulators with cutoff Λ = 500 and 600 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for ν_e-CC, \bar{ν}_e-CC, ν_l-NC, and \bar{ν}_l-NC processes increase by over two orders of magnitude from threshold to 150 MeV neutrino energy.
- The χEFT predictions are consistently larger than those of Nakamura et al. (2002) by 1–2% over the entire energy range (0–150) MeV.
- Contributions beyond LO in the weak current increase the LO cross sections by "a couple of percent" over the whole energy range (0–150) MeV.
- The cutoff dependence is negligible for Λ in the range 500–600 MeV.
- The predicted cross sections are within ~2% of corresponding predictions from conventional meson-exchange frameworks.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Nakamura et al. (2002) and Shen et al. (2012), which used conventional meson-exchange frameworks, by providing a fully consistent χEFT treatment of both the nuclear potential and weak currents at high orders in the power counting. The results confirm the earlier predictions at the 1–2% level while offering a more fundamental connection to quantum chromodynamics symmetries, and provide reliable benchmarks for studies of weak inclusive response in light nuclei such as ¹²C using Green's function Monte Carlo methods (Lovato et al., 2013–2016) and for analyses constraining the isovector axial form factor of the nucleon (Meyer et al., 2016).
