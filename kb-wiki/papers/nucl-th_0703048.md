# Partial Dynamical Symmetry at Critical-Points of Quantum Phase Transitions

> We show that partial dynamical symmetries (PDS) can occur at critical-points of quantum phase transitions, in which case, underlying competing symmetries are conserved exactly by a subset of states, and mix strongly in other states. Several types of PDS are demonstrated with the example of critical-point Hamiltonians for first- and second-order transitions in the framework of the interacting boson model, whose dynamical symmetries correspond to different shape-phases in nuclei.

# Partial Dynamical Symmetry at Critical-Points of Quantum Phase Transitions

## Key claim

Partial dynamical symmetries (PDS) can survive at the critical-points of quantum phase transitions, where underlying competing symmetries are conserved exactly by a subset of states while mixing strongly in other states; this is demonstrated for first- and second-order critical-point Hamiltonians in the interacting boson model (IBM), with the specific condition that the PDS notion applies to the more complicated case of a phase transition rather than only to stable limits of structure.

## Method

The author constructs critical-point Hamiltonians within the IBM framework, using a spectrum generating algebra U(6) and its dynamical symmetry chains (U(5), SU(3), O(6)) that correspond to spherical vibrator, axially-deformed rotor, and deformed gamma-soft rotor shapes respectively. For first-order transitions, a Hamiltonian H(beta_0) is built from a quadrupole operator P^dagger_2 such that its potential surface has degenerate spherical and deformed minima satisfying b^2=4ac; for second-order transitions, a Hamiltonian with epsilon n_d plus an A-term satisfies a=0, b=0, c>0. The eigenstates are analyzed for their decomposition into irreps of the competing symmetry chains to identify which states retain good quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- For the first-order critical Hamiltonian H(beta_0=sqrt(2)) with h_2=0.1 and N=10: solvable SU(3) states form bands with energies E=0 for the ground band (L=0,2,4,...,2N) and E=3h_2[2N+1-2k]k for gamma^k bands with k>0
- For the same Hamiltonian: spherical eigenstates have energies E=0 for |N,n_d=tau=L=0> and E=3h_2[beta_0^2(N-3)+5] for |N,n_d=tau=L=3>, which equals 3h_2[2(N-3)+5] for beta_0=sqrt(2)
- For the first-order critical Hamiltonian H(beta_0=1) with h_2=0.1 and N=10: solvable O(6) ground band states have E=0 with sigma=N and L=0,2,4,...,2N
- The second-order critical Hamiltonian satisfies epsilon=4(N-1)A, with the potential surface behaving as beta^4 for small beta

## Context

This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetries, previously demonstrated in stable structural limits (Leviatan 1996, Leviatan and Van Isacker 2002), to the critical-point region of quantum phase transitions. It builds on the analytic descriptions of critical-point nuclei proposed by Iachello (2000) and confirmed empirically by Casten and Zamfir (2000), as well as the emergence of quasi-dynamical symmetries near critical-points studied by Rowe (2004-2005). The results enable the construction of Hamiltonians with coexisting PDS of different types (type I, II, III) that can serve as tools for exploring symmetry aspects of critical behavior in nuclear and other dynamical systems undergoing quantum phase transitions.
