# $Ab~initio$ calculations for Gamow-Teller strengths in $sd$ shell

> In the present work we perform a systematic shell model study of Gamow-Teller transition strength distributions in $sd$ shell nuclei using $ab~initio$ effective interactions. The $ab~initio$ effective interactions are based on in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) and coupled-cluster effective interaction (CCEI) approaches. The aim of the present work is to test the predictive power of $ab~initio$ effective interactions by using the available experimental data of Gamow-Teller strength distributions in $sd$ shell nuclei. We perform calculations for $^{20}$Ne $\rightarrow$ $^{20}$F, $^{23}$Na $\rightarrow$ $^{23}$Mg, $^{23}$Na $\rightarrow$ $^{23}$Ne, $^{24}$Mg $\rightarrow$ $^{24}$Na, $^{24}$Mg $\rightarrow$ $^{24}$Al, $^{25}$Mg $\rightarrow$ $^{25}$Al, $^{26}$Mg $\rightarrow$ $^{26}$Na, $^{26}$Mg $\rightarrow$ $^{26}$Al, $^{26}$Si $\rightarrow$ $^{26}$Al, $^{27}$Al $\rightarrow$ $^{27}$Si, $^{28}$Si $\rightarrow$ $^{28}$P, $^{31}$P $\rightarrow$ $^{31}$Si, and $^{32}$S $\rightarrow$ $^{32}$P transitions. For comparison we also show the results obtained by using the phenomenological USDB Hamiltonian. The phenomenological USDB results of the Gamow-Teller (GT$_+$/GT$_-$) strength distributions show reasonable agreements with the experimental data in comparison to the $ab~initio$ interactions. We also calculate the electron capture reaction rates for $^{23}$Na(e$^-$, $ν$)$^{23}$Ne and $^{25}$Mg(e$^-$, $ν$)$^{25}$Na using $ab~initio$ and USDB interactions.

## Key claim

The authors test the predictive power of two ab initio effective interactions (IM-SRG and CCEI) for Gamow-Teller strength distributions in sd shell nuclei, finding that the phenomenological USDB Hamiltonian shows reasonable agreement with experimental data while the ab initio interactions systematically produce larger deviations, particularly for higher-mass nuclei.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed using the NuShellX code with two ab initio effective interactions: the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) approach based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions with a harmonic oscillator parameter of 24 MeV, and the coupled-cluster effective interaction (CCEI) approach using N3LO chiral NN and N2LO chiral 3N interactions with momentum cutoffs of 500 MeV and 400 MeV respectively. The Gamow-Teller strengths were calculated with a quenching factor of 0.77 applied uniformly across all three interactions (USDB, CCEI, and IM-SRG), and the Ikeda sum rule was verified for A = 23, 24, and 26 to ensure sufficient excited states were included.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factors obtained from chi-square fits: 0.79 for USDB, 0.78 for CCEI, and 0.81 for IM-SRG interactions for T=1/2 sd shell mirror nuclei with A=17-39 (USDB and CCEI) and A=17-33 (IM-SRG)
- Root-mean-square deviations from experimental values using f_q = 0.77: 0.088 for USDB, 0.177 for CCEI, and 0.149 for IM-SRG
- For the 20Ne to 20F transition, experimental errors are as large as 0.209 for the sum of B(GT)
- For 23Na to 23Mg, the first transition B(GT) strength is 0.09 after excluding Fermi transition strength, with the largest B(GT) strength at excitation energy 8.168 MeV
- For 23Na to 23Ne, the experimental B(GT) strength is dominated in the excitation energy range 3.432-3.458 MeV
- For 24Mg to 24Na, the strongest experimental peak appears at excitation energy 1.346 MeV from the (t,3He) reaction and 1.35 MeV from the (d,2He) reaction
- For 24Mg to 24Al, the strongest experimental peak appears at excitation energy 1.090 MeV from the (3He,t) reaction and 1.07 MeV from the (p,n) reaction
- For 26Mg to 26Na, the most intense experimental peak appears at excitation energy 0.08 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of Gamow-Teller strengths in sd shell nuclei by comparing two modern ab initio approaches (IM-SRG from Stroberg et al. and CCEI from Jansen et al.) against the phenomenological USDB interaction from Brown and Richter. The study covers 13 transitions across the A=20-32 mass region, adding to earlier B(GT) results obtained using phenomenological interactions, and also calculates electron capture rates for 23Na and 25Mg relevant to astrophysical applications. The results indicate that while ab initio interactions satisfy the Ikeda sum rule, they currently show larger deviations from experimental data than the phenomenological USDB interaction, particularly for higher-mass nuclei where neglected three-nucleon force contributions may become important.
