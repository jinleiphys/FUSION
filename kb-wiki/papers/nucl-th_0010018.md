# Ferromagnetism of Nuclear Matter in the Relativistic Approach

> We study the spin-polarization mechanism in the highly dense nuclear matter with the relativistic mean-field approach. In the relativistic Hartree-Fock framework we find that there are two kinds of spin-spin interaction channels, which are the axial-vector and tensor exchange ones. If each interaction is strong and different sign, the system loses the spherical symmetry and holds the spin-polarization in the high-density region. When the axial-vector interaction is negative enough, the system holds ferromagnetism.

## Key claim

The paper shows that ferromagnetism in nuclear matter can occur in the relativistic Hartree-Fock framework when the axial-vector interaction channel is sufficiently negative, but only if the spherical symmetry of the system is broken and the spin-vector is chosen appropriately (Choice-2), not the naive nonrelativistic extension (Choice-1). The authors emphasize that the spin-polarization mechanism depends critically on the choice of spin-vector configuration and on allowing axial deformation of the Fermi sphere.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach with zero-range approximation for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, incorporating scalar (σ), vector (ω), and pseudoscalar (η) meson exchanges. They take a variational approach rather than solving the full self-consistent RHF equation, fixing the baryon density and spin-polarization parameter x_s, then minimizing the total energy density with respect to the quadrupole deformation parameters λ_↑ and λ_↓ of the momentum distribution. Three different choices for the spin-vector a_μ (Ch1, Ch2, Ch3) are examined, with Ch2 (which maximizes |a_z|) being the most favorable for ferromagnetism.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for spin-polarization: ρ_c/ρ_0 = 8.74 for parameter-set SD2 (C_A = -50) and ρ_c/ρ_0 = 4.28 for SD3 (C_A = -100), using PM1 effective mass parameters
- Spin-symmetry energy at saturation density is fixed to ε_sp-sym = 25 MeV
- Three spin-dependent parameter-sets used: C_A = 0 (SD1), C_A = -50 (SD2), C_A = -100 (SD3)
- Two effective mass parameter-sets: PM1 (M*/M = 0.7) and PM4 (M*/M = 0.55)
- In the infinite density limit, total spin per nucleon converges to x_s/3 for Ch1 and x_s/2 for Ch2-S (spherical momentum distribution)
- For the HF-I parameter set from Ref. [RHF], the coupling strengths are C_A = -8.55 and C_T = 30.2

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of spin-polarization in nuclear matter beyond earlier studies by Niembro et al. (Refs. [Niem1,Niem2]), who used the RHF approach but kept spherical symmetry and used a naive spin-vector choice. It also builds on Tatsumi's earlier work on spin-polarization in quark matter via one-gluon-exchange interaction (Ref. [SP-QM]), which suggested that relativistic effects create a new mechanism for ferromagnetism absent in nonrelativistic treatments. The paper contrasts with Pandharipande et al. (Ref. [Pand]), who found no stable spin-polarization in nonrelativistic nuclear matter calculations. The results enable further study of whether ferromagnetic nuclear matter could explain the super-strong magnetic fields observed in magnetars.
