# Origin of the Proton Mass

> Atomic nuclei lie at the core of everything visible; and at the first level of approximation, their atomic weights are simply the sum of the masses of all the neutrons and protons (nucleons) they contain. Each nucleon has a mass $m_N \approx 1\,{\rm GeV}\approx 2000$-times the electron mass. The Higgs boson -- discovered at the large hadron collider in 2012, a decade ago -- produces the latter, but what generates the nucleon mass? This is a pivotal question. The answer is widely supposed to lie within quantum chromodynamics (QCD), the strong-interaction piece of the Standard Model. Yet, it is far from obvious. In fact, removing Higgs-boson couplings into QCD, one arrives at a scale invariant theory, which, classically, can't support any masses at all. This contribution sketches forty years of developments in QCD, which suggest a solution to the puzzle, and highlight some of the experiments that can validate the picture.

## Key claim

The proton mass (≈1 GeV) is generated almost entirely by emergent hadron mass (EHM) arising from quantum chromodynamics (QCD), with the Higgs boson contributing only about 1% (9 MeV) of the total; the pion remains anomalously light because its mass arises entirely from constructive interference between EHM and Higgs contributions, with no direct EHM-only component.

## Method

The paper reviews forty years of theoretical developments in QCD, focusing on the trace anomaly of the energy-momentum tensor, the Schwinger mechanism for dynamical gluon mass generation, and continuum Schwinger function methods (CSM) that simultaneously predict parton distribution functions (DFs) for both proton and pion. The analysis uses Poincaré-invariant decompositions of mass budgets and compares predictions for valence-quark, glue, and sea-quark DFs at the hadron scale ζ_H and evolved to ζ = m_J/ψ = 3.1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Proton mass m_N ≈ 1 GeV ≈ 2000 times the electron mass
- Higgs boson contributes only 9 MeV of the 939 MeV proton mass (≈1%); EHM alone is responsible for 94% of m_proton; the remaining 5% arises from constructive interference between these two sources
- Dynamical gluon mass scale m_0 = 0.43(1) GeV ≈ ½ m_Nature
- At the hadron scale ζ_H, valence quarks carry all hadron momentum: ⟨x⟩_{u_p}^{ζ_H} = 0.69, ⟨x⟩_{d_p}^{ζ_H} = 0.31, ⟨x⟩_{d_π}^{ζ_H} = ⟨x⟩_{u_π}^{ζ_H} = 0.5
- Large-x scaling exponents: proton valence DFs ∝ (1−x)^3, pion valence DFs ∝ (1−x)^2 at ζ_H
- Probability that proton data can be explained by a scalar-diquark-only wave function is 1/141,000

## Context

This review extends the line of work on emergent hadron mass (EHM) that began with Schwinger (1962) demonstrating dynamical gauge boson mass generation and Cornwall (1981) applying the Schwinger mechanism to QCD. It contradicts global fits to structure function data (Ball 2016, Hou 2019, Bailey 2020, Novikov 2020, Barry 2021) that ignore QCD constraints on large-x scaling behavior. The work enables future experimental validation through measurements of proton and pion structure functions at facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider, resonance electroexcitation measurements (Carman 2020, Mokeev 2021, Mokeev 2022), and electroweak transitions between heavy+light mesons and light final states.
