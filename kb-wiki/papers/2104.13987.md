# The Lowest Broad Alpha Cluster Resonances in $^{19}$F

> There is a deep astrophysical interest in the structure of $^{19}$F states close to the alpha decay threshold. The nuclear structure of these states is important for understanding of the development of $α$ clustering in the $^{20}$Ne region. Emergence of clustered states and generally states that favor coupling to reaction channels near the corresponding decay thresholds is currently of special interest in theoretical physics. Excitation function for $^{15}$N($α$,$α$) elastic scattering was measured by the TTIK method. These new data together with old, high energy resolution data, were analyzed using the R matrix approach. $^{19}$F nuclear structure was calculated using configuration interaction methods with the recently developed effective interaction Hamiltonian. The parameters of broad low spin $\ell = 0$ and 1 relative partial wave resonances close to the $α$ decay threshold in $^{19}$F were identified. Detailed theoretical analysis was carried out identifying all states coupled to the $\ell = 0$ and 1 alpha cluster channels. Considering hierarchy of states with different harmonic oscillator shell excitations allows to evaluate coupling to the alpha channels with different number of nodes in the relative wave function and helps to explain the distribution of the clustering strength and emergence of broad clustering resonances. Comparison of clustering in $^{20}$Ne into $^{16}$O+$α$ and consideration of spin-orbit splitting of the $^{15}$N+$α$ channel provides additional evidence. Detailed analysis of new and old experimental data allows to identify a series of $α$ clustering resonances in $^{19}$F and to assess the distribution of the clustering strength which is of importance to questions of astrophysics and for theoretical understanding of many-body physics and emergence of clustering in loosely bound or unstable nuclei.

# The Lowest Broad Alpha Cluster Resonances in $^{19}$F

## Key claim
The parameters of broad low-spin $\ell = 0$ and 1 resonances near the alpha decay threshold in $^{19}$F are identified through combined analysis of new TTIK measurements and reanalysis of 1961 data, but the theoretical configuration interaction approach using the FSU Hamiltonian still does not reproduce the broadest alpha resonances, indicating that mixing between different harmonic oscillator shells and coupling to the continuum must be included in future models.

## Method
Excitation functions for $^{15}$N($\alpha$,$\alpha$) elastic scattering were measured using the Thick Target Inverse Kinematic (TTIK) method at the DC-60 facility in Nur-Sultan, Kazakhstan with a $^{15}$N beam energy of 21 MeV. These new data were combined with the 1961 high-resolution data from Ref. [11] and analyzed using the R-matrix approach with the code AZURE. Nuclear structure calculations for $^{19}$F were performed using configuration interaction methods with the recently developed FSU effective interaction Hamiltonian, employing cluster center-of-mass boosting techniques to evaluate alpha spectroscopic factors for $\ell = 0$ and 1 partial waves.

## Key numbers
- $1/2^+$ state at excitation energy $E' = 5.333$ MeV (relative to alpha separation energy): total width $\Gamma = 1.4 \pm 0.4$ keV
- $3/2^+$ state at $E' = 5.488$ MeV: total width $\Gamma = 4.85 \pm 0.5$ keV (combined result giving $\Gamma = 5.4 \pm 0.4$ keV)
- $1/2^-$ state at $E' = 6.540$ MeV: total width $\Gamma = 220 \pm 40$ keV, reduced alpha width $\gamma_\alpha = 0.53$
- $1/2^-$ state at $E' = 7.048$ MeV: total width $\Gamma = 150 \pm 35$ keV, reduced alpha width $\gamma_\alpha = 0.12$
- $5/2^+$ state at $E' = 6.289$ MeV: $\Gamma_\alpha = 2.30 \pm 0.5$ keV
- $7/2^+$ state at $E' = 6.339$ MeV: $\Gamma_\alpha = 3.30 \pm 0.4$ keV
- Energy resolution of TTIK measurement determined as 33 keV from fit to narrow $5/2^+$ and $7/2^+$ resonances
- Theoretical $0^+_4$ state in $^{20}$Ne predicted at 10.121 MeV with $n=6$ nodes, proposed as candidate for broad experimental resonance at 8.7 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of alpha clustering in $^{20}$Ne and $^{21}$Ne (Volya et al., 2017, 2019) to the $^{19}$F nucleus, which is important for astrophysical fluorine production in AGB stars and for understanding mirror nucleus $^{19}$Ne in novae. The analysis corrects and extends the earlier R-matrix work of Ref. [03] (which was limited to data at a single angle, 169.1 degrees) by including all measured angles from the 1961 data of Ref. [11] and adding new TTIK measurements. The theoretical approach using the FSU interaction Hamiltonian, previously unexplored for clustering studies, reveals that states with different numbers of harmonic oscillator shell excitations couple to alpha channels with different numbers of nodes in the relative wave function, explaining the distribution of clustering strength. The results enable improved calculations of astrophysical reaction rates and identify the need for future theoretical work incorporating configuration mixing between different $\hbar\omega$ excitations and continuum coupling to reproduce the broadest resonances.
