# Study of the radiative decay $J/ψ\rightarrowη_{c}+γ$ in light cone sum rules

> At present, there are many experimental and theoretical results for $J/ψ\rightarrowη_{c}+γ$ process, while the results are inconsistent based on different methods and considerations. In this paper, the light cone sum rules method is used in studying the radiative decay $J/ψ\rightarrowη_{c}+γ$. We give the transition form factor of this process based on the leading twist distribution amplitude of $η_{c}$ meson, with this form factor further obtain the decay width. Our result is consistent with those of other sum rules. A comparison of our result with others' about the decay width is also presented.

# Study of the radiative decay $J/\psi\rightarrow\eta_{c}+\gamma$ in light cone sum rules

## Key claim

The light cone sum rules (LCSR) method yields a decay width of $\Gamma = 3.39 \pm 0.34$ keV for $J/\psi \rightarrow \eta_c + \gamma$, which is consistent with other sum rules results but only when the leading twist distribution amplitude of the $\eta_c$ meson is used; the result is sensitive to the charm quark mass and the quark-hadron duality ansatz, and contributions from higher twist distribution amplitudes are not included.

## Method

The authors construct a two-point correlation function between the vacuum and an on-shell $\eta_c$ state, inserting two electromagnetic currents. They match the hadronic representation (separating the $J/\psi$ ground state from excited states and continuum via a threshold parameter $s_0$) with the operator product expansion (OPE) side computed at leading order using the free charm quark propagator and the leading twist light-cone distribution amplitude of the $\eta_c$ meson. After applying Borel transformations to suppress excited-state contributions, they extract the transition form factor $F_{VP}$ at $Q^2=0$, from which the decay width is obtained.

## Key numbers

* Form factor $F_{VP} = 2.293$ at $Q^2=0$ for the $J/\psi \rightarrow \eta_c + \gamma$ transition.
* Decay width $\Gamma = 3.39 \pm 0.34$ keV, with the error (10-15%) dominated by uncertainties in the charm quark mass and the quark-hadron duality ansatz.
* Borel parameter working region: $15\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \leq M^2 \leq 25\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, with central value $M^2 = 20\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
* Threshold parameter $s_0 = 3.65^2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, varied in the range $(3.60^2 - 3.70^2)\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
* Charm quark mass $m_c = 1.27 \pm 0.02$ GeV (from PDG 2018), with form factor and decay width decreasing as $m_c$ increases.
* Input masses: $m_{\eta_c} = 2.9835$ GeV, $m_{J/\psi} = 3.0969$ GeV; ratio $f_{\eta_c}/f_{J/\psi} = 0.54$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LCSR applications to $e^+e^- \rightarrow J/\psi + \eta_c$ (Sun 2009) and $e^+e^- \rightarrow \psi(2S) + \eta_c$ (Tao 2019) to the radiative decay $J/\psi \rightarrow \eta_c + \gamma$. The result is compared with experimental measurements from Crystal Ball (Gaiser 1985), CLEO (Mitchell 2008), and KEDR (Anashin 2014), as well as lattice QCD results (Dudek 2006, Dudek 2009, Gui 2019, Donald 2012, Chen 2011, Becirevic 2013), non-relativistic effective field theories (Brambilla 2005, Pineda 2013), quark models (Ebert 2002, Deng 2016, Barnes 2005, Guo 2014), and other sum rules approaches (Khodjamirian 1979, Beilin 1984, Aliev 1985). The authors note that the LCSR method incorporates relativistic effects through the light-cone distribution amplitude, which is deficient in non-relativistic effective field theories and earlier sum rules; future work will extend the approach to other charmonium radiative transitions such as $\psi' \rightarrow \eta'_c \gamma$, $\psi' \rightarrow \eta_c \gamma$, and $\eta'_c \rightarrow J/\psi \gamma$.
