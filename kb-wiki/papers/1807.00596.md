# Microscopic predictions of the nuclear matter liquid-gas phase transition

> We present first-principle predictions for the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter employing both two- and three-nucleon chiral interactions. Our discussion focuses on the sources of systematic errors in microscopic quantum many body predictions. On the one hand, we test uncertainties of our results arising from changes in the construction of chiral Hamiltonians. We use five different chiral forces with consistently derived three-nucleon interactions. On the other hand, we compare the ladder resummation in the self-consistent Green's functions approach to finite temperature Brueckner--Hartree--Fock calculations. We find that systematics due to Hamiltonians dominate over many-body uncertainties. Based on this wide pool of calculations, we estimate that the critical temperature is $T_c=16 \pm 2$ MeV, in reasonable agreement with experimental results. We also find that there is a strong correlation between the critical temperature and the saturation energy in microscopic many-body simulations.

## Key claim
The authors present first-principle predictions for the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter using chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, finding that systematic uncertainties from the choice of Hamiltonian dominate over many-body approximation uncertainties. The critical temperature is estimated at \(T_c = 16 \pm 2\) MeV, with the caveat that this range reflects the spread across five different chiral forces rather than a rigorous statistical uncertainty.

## Method
The authors employed two non-perturbative many-body methods: the self-consistent Green's functions (SCGF) approach with ladder resummation, including particle-particle and hole-hole intermediate states, and the finite-temperature Brueckner–Hartree–Fock (BHF) method in the quasi-particle limit. Five different chiral Hamiltonians were used, each combining a two-nucleon force (N3LO or N2LO) with a consistently derived three-nucleon force at N2LO, with low-energy constants fit to few-body properties. The liquid-gas phase transition was analyzed via a Maxwell construction of coexisting phases using free energies, chemical potentials, and pressures computed from the many-body calculations.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature: \(T_c = 16 \pm 2\) MeV, estimated from the spread across five chiral Hamiltonians.
- Free energy differences between Hamiltonians: within about 5 MeV across the density range at all temperatures, except for N3LO\(_{\rm full}\) which is substantially more repulsive.
- Relative discrepancy between microscopic and macroscopic chemical potentials: below \(\sim 2\%\) at saturation density and \(T = 15\) MeV.
- Free energy fits used \(\rho_{\rm sat} = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) as the reference saturation density.

## Context
This work extends previous microscopic studies of the nuclear matter liquid-gas phase transition that used phase-shift-equivalent two-nucleon forces (Rios 2008, Soma 2009) or chiral interactions within many-body perturbation theory (Wellenhofer 2014, 2015). The authors go beyond past work by systematically comparing two different many-body methods (SCGF and BHF) and five different chiral Hamiltonians, including the N2LO\(_{\rm sat}\) potential (Ekstroem 2015). The results enable improved uncertainty quantification for the nuclear equation of state at finite temperature, relevant for protoneutron star evolution and binary neutron star mergers.
