# Shell-model Structure of Exotic 135Sb

> Recent studies have provided new experimental information on the very neutron-rich nucleus 135Sb. We have performed a shell-model calculation for this nucleus using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. This gives a very good description of the observed properties of 135Sb. We show that the anomalously low position of the first excited state, J=5/2+, and the strongly hindered M1 transition to the ground state have their origin in the effective neutron-proton interaction.

# Shell-model Structure of Exotic 135Sb

## Key claim

The anomalously low first excited state (J=5/2+) and the strongly hindered M1 transition to the ground state in 135Sb can be explained by the effective neutron-proton interaction within a realistic shell-model calculation, without requiring an ad-hoc decrease of the proton d5/2 single-particle energy as previous studies had proposed.

## Method

The authors performed a shell-model calculation for 135Sb assuming 132Sn as a closed core, with valence neutrons occupying the six levels of the 82-126 shell and valence protons occupying the five levels of the 50-82 shell. The two-body effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential using the Vlow-k approach with a cutoff momentum of 2.2 fm-1, followed by a Q-box plus folded-diagram method including diagrams up to second order in Vlow-k. Single-particle energies were taken from experiment using the spectra of 133Sb and 133Sn, with the exception of the proton s1/2 and neutron i13/2 energies which were taken from prior work.

## Key numbers

- Calculated ground-state binding energy: 16.411 ± 0.074 MeV, compared to experimental value 16.585 ± 0.104 MeV
- Largest discrepancy between calculated and experimental yrast levels: about 300 keV for the 23/2+ state
- Calculated 5/2+ state overestimated by only 100 keV above the experimental value
- Experimental 5/2+ state at 282 keV; calculated value within 100 keV of this
- Experimental half-life of 5/2+ level: T1/2 = 6.0(7) ns
- Experimental upper limit for B(M1; 5/2+ → 7/2+): 0.29 × 10-3 μN2
- Calculated B(M1) with free operator: 25 × 10-3 μN2
- Calculated B(M1) with effective M1 operator: 4 × 10-3 μN2
- Experimental magnetic moment of 7/2+ ground state: 3.0 μN; calculated with free operator: 1.7 μN; with effective M1 operator: 2.5 μN
- Calculated centroids of proton strengths: g7/2 at -10.06 MeV, d5/2 at -9.35 MeV, giving spacing of 0.71 MeV compared to single-particle splitting of 0.96 MeV
- Oscillator parameter: ℏω = 7.88 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of 135Sb by Bhat (1998), Shergur (2002), and Sarkar (2004), which had difficulty reproducing the low-lying 5/2+ state and required ad-hoc adjustments to single-particle energies. The calculation improves upon the authors' own preliminary work (Mach 2005, Korgul 2005) by using a larger intermediate-state space in the Q-box calculation, including the Coulomb interaction, and using a different cutoff momentum. The results demonstrate that a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential can account for the observed properties of 135Sb without invoking shell-structure modifications, and the neutron-proton interaction is tested against the neighboring nucleus 134Sb. This enables further exploration of nuclear structure in neutron-rich nuclei beyond 132Sn relevant to r-process modeling.
