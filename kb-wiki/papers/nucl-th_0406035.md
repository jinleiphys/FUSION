# Method of multiple internal reflections in description of tunneling evolution through barriers

> A method of a non-stationary description of tunneling of a particle through the one-dimensional and spherically symmetric rectangular barriers on the basis of analisis of multiple internal reflections of wave packets in relation on the barrier boundaries, named as the Method of multiple internal reflections, is presented at the first time. For the one-dimensional problem the applicability of this method is proved, its specific features are analyzed. For the spherically symmetric problem the amplitudes of the transmitted and reflected wave packets, times of tunneling and reflection in relation to the barrier are calculated using this method. The effect of Hartman-Fletcher is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-stationary method for describing tunneling through one-dimensional and spherically symmetric rectangular barriers by analyzing multiple internal reflections of wave packets at the barrier boundaries, claiming that this method is validated for both above-barrier and sub-barrier regions (unlike stationary approaches) and that the phase tunneling times calculated converge with results from prior work by Olkhovsky (1992).

## Method

The method decomposes the tunneling process into sequential steps: at each barrier boundary, an incident wave packet is split into transmitted and reflected components, with only the physically relevant exponential form (decreasing or increasing) retained in the barrier region based on causality and the direction of propagation. Recurrence relations for the coefficients of these wave packets are derived from continuity conditions at each boundary, and the total transmitted and reflected wave functions are obtained as infinite series of these partial packets. For the spherically symmetric problem, the same stepwise procedure is applied to the radial part of the Schrödinger equation with a rectangular barrier.

## Key numbers

- For the one-dimensional rectangular barrier, the phase tunneling time is given by:  
  $\tau_{tun}^{Ph} = \frac{m}{\hbar k\xi} \frac{k_0^4 \sinh(2\xi a) + 2a\xi k^2(\xi^2 - k^2)}{4k^2\xi^2 + k_0^4 \sinh^2(\xi a)}$, where $k = \frac{1}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}$, $\xi = \frac{1}{\hbar}\sqrt{2m(V_1 - E)}$, $k_0^2 = k^2 + \xi^2 = 2mV_1/\hbar^2$, and $a$ is the barrier width.

- For high and wide barriers (limit $\xi a \gg 1$), the phase tunneling time approaches $\tau_{tun}^{Ph} \to \frac{2}{v\xi}$, where $v = \hbar k/m$ is the group velocity.

- The time for the first reflected wave packet from the first boundary is $t_{ref}^1 = t_{inc} + \frac{2m}{\hbar k\xi}$.

- The time for the first transmitted wave packet through the first boundary is $t_{tr}^1 = t_{inc} + \frac{m}{\hbar k\xi}$.

- The time for the transmitted wave packet through the second boundary at the second step is $t_{tr}^2 = t_{inc} + \frac{2m}{\hbar k\xi}$.

- The series sums of coefficients for the one-dimensional rectangular barrier satisfy: $\left|\sum_{n=0}^{+\infty} A_T^n\right|^2 + \left|\sum_{n=0}^{+\infty} A_R^n\right|^2 = 1$.

## Context

This work extends the time analysis of tunneling processes previously developed by Olkhovsky (1997, 1992) by providing a non-stationary solution method that explicitly accounts for multiple internal reflections, unlike stationary treatments by McVoy (1967) and Anderson (1989) which only considered plane waves. The method enables calculation of tunneling and reflection times for spherically symmetric barriers for the first time, and it simplifies finding stationary wave functions for barriers of more complex shape (e.g., double-hump rectangular barriers) compared to standard stationary methods. The analysis of the Hartman-Fletcher effect is also included.
