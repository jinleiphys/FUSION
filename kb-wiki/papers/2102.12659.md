# Microscopic calculations of 6He and 6Li with real-time evolution method

> The low-lying cluster states of 6He (a+n+n) and 6Li (a+n+p) are calculated by the real-time evolution method (REM) which generates basis wave functions for the generator coordinate method (GCM) from the equation of motion of Gaussian wave packets. The 0+ state of 6He as well as the 1+, 0+ and 3+ states of 6Li are calculated as a benchmark. We also calculate the root-mean-square (r.m.s.) radii of the point matter, the point proton, and the point neutron of these states, particularly for the study of the halo characters of these two nuclei. It is shown that REM can be one constructive way for generating effective basis wave functions in GCM calculations.

# Microscopic calculations of 6He and 6Li with real-time evolution method

## Key claim

The real-time evolution method (REM) generates basis wave functions for the generator coordinate method (GCM) that yield energy spectra and root-mean-square radii for 6He and 6Li consistent with or better than prior GCM calculations using the same Hamiltonian, but the authors note that the rebound radius parameter (set to 8 fm) and barrier strength (6 MeV/fm^2) are not physically important as long as they are not too large or too small, and that one ensemble of basis generated for 6He was used for both nuclei.

## Method

The authors describe 6He as an alpha cluster plus two valence neutrons and 6Li as an alpha cluster plus a valence neutron and proton, using Gaussian single-particle wave functions with spin-isospin parts. They generate basis wave functions by solving the equation of motion derived from the time-dependent variational principle (real-time evolution method), then superpose these basis states with parity and angular momentum projection within the generator coordinate method framework. The Hamiltonian uses the Volkov No.2 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and the G3RS spin-orbit potential, with the same parameters as prior work by Itagaki (2003) and Furumoto (2018).

## Key numbers

- 6He 0+ ground state energy: -28.37 MeV relative to the alpha+n+n threshold
- 6Li 1+ ground state energy: -30.92 MeV relative to the alpha+n+n threshold
- 6Li 3+ excited state energy: -29.87 MeV relative to the alpha+n+n threshold
- 6Li 0+ excited state energy: -27.58 MeV relative to the alpha+n+n threshold
- 6He 0+ point matter r.m.s. radius: 2.71 fm; point proton radius: 2.03 fm; point neutron radius: 2.99 fm
- 6Li 1+ point matter r.m.s. radius: 2.65 fm; 3+ point matter radius: 2.42 fm; 0+ point matter radius: 2.79 fm
- Harmonic oscillator parameter b = 1.46 fm
- Rebound radius parameter d = 8 fm; barrier strength k = 6 MeV/fm^2

## Context

This work extends the real-time evolution method previously applied to Be and C isotopes by Imai (2019), Zhou (2020), and Shin (2021) to the Borromean halo nucleus 6He and the mirror system 6Li. The results improve upon the GCM calculations of Itagaki (2003) and Furumoto (2018) for the 1+ and 3+ states of 6Li, finding lower energies and larger r.m.s. radii that better describe the halo character of the 6He ground state and the 0+ state of 6Li. The authors suggest that REM may enable future investigation of isospin mixing states, since one ensemble of basis reproduced both T=0 and T=1 states.
