# The Statistical Multifragmentation Model with Skyrme Effective Interactions

> The Statistical Multifragmentation Model is modified to incorporate the Helmholtz free energies calculated in the finite temperature Thomas-Fermi approximation using Skyrme effective interactions. In this formulation, the density of the fragments at the freeze-out configuration corresponds to the equilibrium value obtained in the Thomas-Fermi approximation at the given temperature. The behavior of the nuclear caloric curve at constant volume is investigated in the micro-canonical ensemble and a plateau is observed for excitation energies between 8 and 10 MeV per nucleon. A kink in the caloric curve is found at the onset of this gas transition, indicating the existence of a small excitation energy region with negative heat capacity. In contrast to previous statistical calculations, this situation takes place even in this case in which the system is constrained to fixed volume. The observed phase transition takes place at approximately constant entropy. The charge distribution and other observables also turn out to be sensitive to the treatment employed in the calculation of the free energies and the fragments' volumes at finite temperature, specially at high excitation energies. The isotopic distribution is also affected by this treatment, which suggests that this prescription may help to obtain information on the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

The Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) modified to incorporate Thomas-Fermi free energies and equilibrium densities from Skyrme effective interactions (SMM-TF) predicts a plateau and a kink in the nuclear caloric curve at constant volume for excitation energies between 8 and 10 MeV per nucleon, with a region of negative heat capacity, contrary to previous statistical calculations where such features were observed only at constant pressure.

## Method

The authors replaced the standard SMM internal free energies and fragment volumes with those calculated from the finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi approximation using Skyrme effective interactions. The equilibrium density of each fragment at freeze-out was taken from the Thomas-Fermi calculation rather than assuming normal nuclear density, and the free volume was correspondingly reduced as fragments expand with temperature. The micro-canonical ensemble was used to enforce mass, charge, and energy conservation for the breakup of a 150Nd source at a fixed freeze-out volume of Vχ/V0 = 3.

## Key numbers

- A plateau in the caloric curve is observed for excitation energies between 8 and 10 MeV per nucleon.
- At E*/A = 8 MeV and Vχ/V0 = 3 for 150Nd, 43% of fragmentation events have two temperature solutions, accounting for 76% of the total statistical weight.
- At E*/A = 6 MeV for 150Nd, only 0.03% of events have two temperature solutions, accounting for 0.09% of the statistical weight.
- The average free volume reaches a minimum at E*/A ≈ 8.0 MeV and then rises.
- The entropy per nucleon saturates and even decreases in the SMM-TF model for 8.0 ≲ E*/A ≲ 11.0 MeV.
- The standard SMM parameters used for comparison are ε0 = 16.0 MeV, Tc = 18.0 MeV, and β0 = 18.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) of Bondorf (1995) and the improved version ISMM by incorporating density and free energy information from the finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi approximation, following earlier Thomas-Fermi studies by Suraud (1987) and Brack (1985). The results contradict previous fixed-volume SMM calculations (e.g., Botvina 1995) which found monotonic temperature increases, and instead produce features (plateau, negative heat capacity) previously seen only in isobaric calculations. The sensitivity of isotopic distributions to the treatment suggests this approach can help constrain the nuclear equation of state, particularly the density dependence of the symmetry energy discussed in recent work.
