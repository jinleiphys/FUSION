# Single-particle shell strengths near the doubly magic nucleus $^{56}$Ni and the $^{56}$Ni(p,$γ$)$^{57}$Cu reaction rate in explosive astrophysical burning

> Angle-integrated cross-section measurements of the $^{56}$Ni(d,n) and (d,p) stripping reactions have been performed to determine the single-particle strengths of low-lying excited states in the mirror nuclei pair $^{57}$Cu-$^{57}$Ni situated adjacent to the doubly magic nucleus $^{56}$Ni. The reactions were studied in inverse kinematics utilizing a beam of radioactive $^{56}$Ni ions in conjunction with the GRETINA $γ$-array. Spectroscopic factors are compared with new shell-model calculations using a full $pf$ model space with the GPFX1A Hamiltonian for the isospin-conserving strong interaction plus Coulomb and charge-dependent Hamiltonians. These results were used to set new constraints on the $^{56}$Ni(p,$γ$)$^{57}$Cu reaction rate for explosive burning conditions in x-ray bursts, where $^{56}$Ni represents a key waiting point in the astrophysical rp-process.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first direct experimental determinations of proton single-particle strengths (spectroscopic factors) for the two lowest-lying excited states in $^{57}$Cu via the $^{56}$Ni(d,n) reaction, and uses these to constrain the $^{56}$Ni(p,$\gamma$)$^{57}$Cu reaction rate; the results show no evidence for significant isospin symmetry breaking between the mirror pair $^{57}$Cu and $^{57}$Ni, but the new rate is significantly lower than previous estimates for temperatures above 1 GK.

## Method

Angle-integrated cross sections for the $^{56}$Ni(d,n)$^{57}$Cu and $^{56}$Ni(d,p)$^{57}$Ni stripping reactions were measured in inverse kinematics using a 33.6 MeV/u radioactive $^{56}$Ni beam incident on a deuterated polyethylene target, with beam-like residues collected by the S800 spectrograph and gamma rays detected by the GRETINA array. Spectroscopic factors were extracted by comparing experimental angle-integrated cross sections with theoretical single-particle cross sections calculated using the finite-range adiabatic approximation with the CH89 optical potential and the reaction code FRESCO. Shell-model calculations were performed in the full $pf$ model space using the GPFX1A Hamiltonian plus Coulomb and charge-dependent Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- $^{56}$Ni beam intensity: $3\times10^{5}$ particles per second, with 47% purity
- Beam energy: 33.6 MeV/u
- GRETINA absolute singles efficiency: 5.5% for $E_{\gamma}=1332$ keV
- $^{57}$Cu first excited state: $E_{\rm ex}=1028(1)$ keV, $J^{\pi}=5/2^{-}$, $\sigma_{\rm exp}=2.00(40)$ mb, $C^{2}S_{(d,n)}=0.76(28)$
- $^{57}$Cu second excited state: $E_{\rm ex}=1109(2)$ keV, $J^{\pi}=1/2^{-}$, $\sigma_{\rm exp}=0.28(6)$ mb, $C^{2}S_{(d,n)}=0.62(22)$
- $^{57}$Ni $5/2^{-}$ state: $E_{\rm ex}=768(1)$ keV, $\sigma_{\rm exp}=2.10(60)$ mb, $C^{2}S_{(d,p)}=0.77(31)$
- $^{57}$Ni $1/2^{-}$ state: $E_{\rm ex}=1122(5)$ keV, $\sigma_{\rm exp}=0.50(15)$ mb, $C^{2}S_{(d,p)}=0.73(31)$
- $^{57}$Ni $7/2^{-}$ state: $E_{\rm ex}=2579(4)$ keV, $\sigma_{\rm exp}=1.24(36)$ mb, $C^{2}S_{(d,p)}=8(3)\times10^{-2}$
- Resonance strength for the $1/2^{-}$ state in $^{57}$Cu: $\omega\gamma=1.9(8)\times10^{-7}$ eV at $E_{\rm r}=418$ keV
- Proton separation energy of $^{57}$Cu: 690.3(4) keV

## Context

This work extends the pioneering study of Rehm et al. (1998) on the $^{56}$Ni(d,p)$^{57}$Ni reaction, which had factor-of-two uncertainties on spectroscopic factors, by providing direct measurements for the proton-transfer channel to $^{57}$Cu and higher precision for $^{57}$Ni. It supersedes the earlier attempt by Jenkins et al. (2009) using the $^{56}$Ni($^{3}$He,d) reaction, which suffered from limited resolution and statistics. The new, significantly lower reaction rate for $T>1$ GK compared to the rates of Zhou et al. (1996) and Rehm et al. (1998) will impact models of explosive hydrogen burning in x-ray bursts, where $^{56}$Ni is a key waiting point in the rp-process.
