# Microscopic origin of shape coexistence in the N=90, Z=64 region

> A microscopic explanation of the nature of shape coexistence in the N=90, Z=64 region is suggested, based on calculations of single particle energies through standard covariant density functional theory. It is suggested that shape coexistence in the N=90 region is caused by the protons, which create neutron particle-hole (p-h) excitations across the N=112 3-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator (3D-HO) magic number, signaling the start of the occupation of the 1i13/2 intruder orbital, which triggers stronger proton-neutron interaction, causing the onset of the deformation and resulting in the shape/phase transition from spherical to deformed nuclei described by the X(5) critical point symmetry. A similar effect is seen in the N=60, Z=40 region, in which p-h excitations across the N=70 3D-HO magic number occur, signaling the start of the occupation of the 1h11/2 intruder orbital.

# Microscopic origin of shape coexistence in the N=90, Z=64 region

## Key claim

Shape coexistence in the N=90 region is caused by protons creating neutron particle-hole excitations across the N=112 3D-HO magic number, which initiates occupation of the 1i13/2 intruder orbital and triggers stronger proton-neutron interaction leading to deformation. The authors propose this mechanism as a microscopic explanation, but note that the neutron p-h excitations are confined to a region near the proton mid-shell (Z=66) and do not appear along the whole isotone chains.

## Method

The authors performed single-particle energy calculations using standard covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the DDME2 functional and the code of Niksic et al. (2011). They determined single-particle energies labeled by Nilsson quantum numbers for multiple isotope and isotone chains, identifying particle-hole excitations by examining which orbitals become vacant or occupied relative to shell closures. The analysis covered Po (Z=84), Te (Z=52), and Zr (Z=40) isotopes for neutron-induced shape coexistence, and N=90, N=92, N=60, and N=38 isotones for proton-induced shape coexistence.

## Key numbers

- 2p-2h neutron excitations observed in N=90 isotones for Z=60-64, with the 5/2[523] orbital vacant and the 1/2[660] orbital occupied
- 2p-2h neutron excitations observed in N=92 isotones for Z=60-64, with the 5/2[523] orbital vacant and the 3/2[651] orbital occupied
- 4p-4h proton excitations observed in N=60 isotones for Z=40-42, near the Z=39 midshell
- 4p-4h proton excitations observed in N=38 isotones for Z=40
- 4p-4h proton excitations observed in Po (Z=84) isotopes for N=98-108
- 2p-2h proton excitations observed in Te (Z=52) isotopes for N=64-68
- 6p-6h proton excitations observed in Zr (Z=40) isotopes for N=38-40
- The neutron mid-shells for the isotope chains are 104 (Po), 66 (Te), and 39 (Zr)
- The proton mid-shell for the isotone chains is 66

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of shape coexistence in terms of particle-hole excitations (Wood, 1992; Heyde, 2011) to the N=90 region, which was previously an open question (Garrett, 2022). The authors connect their findings to the proxy-SU(3) symmetry framework (Bonatsos, 2017; Bonatsos, 2020), which predicted shape coexistence occurs only within specific islands of the nuclear chart (7-8, 17-20, 34-40, 59-70, 96-112, 146-168 nucleons). The results also corroborate the relation between shape coexistence and shape/phase transitions studied in the Z=40, N=60 region using the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (Ramos, 2021). The work enables a more exhaustive search for islands of shape coexistence across the nuclear chart using the same CDFT method.
