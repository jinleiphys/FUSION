# Single-nucleon potential decomposition of the nuclear symmetry energy

> The nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(ρ)$ and its density slope $L(ρ)$ can be decomposed analytically in terms of the single-nucleon potential in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. Using three popular nuclear effective interaction models which have been extensively used in nuclear structure and reaction studies, namely, the isospin and momentum dependent MDI interaction model, the Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach and the Gogny Hartree-Fock approach, we analyze the contribution of different terms in the single-nucleon potential to the $E_{sym}(ρ)$ and $L(ρ)$. Our results show that the observed different density behaviors of $E_{sym}(ρ)$ for different interactions are essentially due to the variation of the symmetry potential $U_{sym,1}(ρ,k)$. Furthermore, we find that the contribution of the second-order symmetry potential $U_{sym,2}(ρ,k)$ to the $L(ρ)$ generally cannot be neglected. Moreover, our results demonstrate that the magnitude of the $U_{sym,2}(ρ,k)$ is generally comparable with that of $U_{sym,1}(ρ,k)$, indicating the second-order symmetry potential $U_{sym,2}(ρ,k)$ may have significant corrections to the widely used Lane approximation to the single-nucleon potential in extremely neutron(proton)-rich nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nuclear symmetry energy \(E_{sym}(\rho)\) and its density slope \(L(\rho)\) can be decomposed exactly in terms of the single-nucleon potential, and that the second-order symmetry potential \(U_{sym,2}(\rho,k)\), neglected in the standard Lane approximation, generally contributes non-negligibly to \(L(\rho)\) and may significantly affect predictions for extremely neutron-rich matter.

## Method

The authors analytically decompose \(E_{sym}(\rho)\) and \(L(\rho)\) using the Hugenholtz–Van Hove theorem, expressing them in terms of the single-nucleon potential in asymmetric nuclear matter expanded to second order in isospin asymmetry \(\delta\). They then evaluate the contributions of the symmetry potential \(U_{sym,1}\), the second-order symmetry potential \(U_{sym,2}\), the nucleon effective mass, and their momentum derivatives using three classes of nuclear effective interaction models: the isospin- and momentum-dependent MDI interaction (with parameter sets \(x=-1,0,1\)), the Skyrme Hartree–Fock approach (including SKM*, SLy4, MSL0, and 112 standard Skyrme interactions), and the Gogny Hartree–Fock approach (D1, D1S, D1N, D1M).

## Key numbers

- For the MDI interaction with \(x=1\), \(L = 14.6\) MeV at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.160\) fm\(^{-3}\), with \(L_5 = -33.9\) MeV (the contribution from \(U_{sym,2}\)).
- For the MDI interaction with \(x=-1\), \(L = 105.7\) MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.160\) fm\(^{-3}\), with \(L_5 = 57.2\) MeV.
- For the Gogny D1 interaction, \(L = 18.4\) MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.166\) fm\(^{-3}\), with \(L_5 = -32.7\) MeV.
- For the Skyrme interaction Z-fit, \(L = -49.7\) MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.159\) fm\(^{-3}\), with \(L_5 = -102.4\) MeV.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density \(E_{sym}(\rho_0)\) is approximately \(30.5\) MeV for all MDI parameter sets, with \(E_1 = 18.2\) MeV and \(E_2 = 12.3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier decomposition based on the Hugenholtz–Van Hove theorem by Xu et al. (2010), which assumed the Lane approximation and neglected the second-order symmetry potential and momentum-dependent effective mass effects. By evaluating \(U_{sym,2}\) in three widely used interaction models (MDI, Skyrme, Gogny), the authors show that the second-order term can be comparable in magnitude to the first-order symmetry potential and must be included for accurate determination of \(L(\rho)\), especially for applications to neutron stars and neutron-rich nuclei. This enables future experimental extraction of \(U_{sym,2}\) from nucleon-nucleus and charge-exchange reactions.
