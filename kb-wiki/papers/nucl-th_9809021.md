# Weak Chiral Lagrangian and $pi^+$ Emission in ${}^4_{Lambda}$He

> A new mechanism of $pi^+$ meson emission from the ${}^4_{Lambda}$He hypernucleus is suggested.

## Key claim
A new mechanism for π⁺ emission from ⁴ΛHe is proposed, involving a weak Λ → n + π⁺ + π⁻ decay followed by π⁻ absorption by the hypernuclear core; its contribution is estimated to be comparable to the standard charge-exchange mechanism, potentially resolving the long-standing puzzle of the observed π⁺/π⁻ ratio, though the main ambiguity lies in the off-shell kinematics of the absorbed π⁻ meson.

## Method
The author constructs the weak ΔS = 1 chiral Lagrangian at lowest order (octet component) and expands it to second order in the pion field to obtain the Λ → n + π⁺ + π⁻ vertex. The contribution of this vertex to π⁺ emission in ⁴ΛHe decay is estimated by comparing the ratio of the absorption mechanism amplitude to the standard charge-exchange mechanism amplitude, which reduces to the square root of the ratio of the π⁻ absorption cross section on the core to the π⁰ → π⁺ charge-exchange cross section on the core. Cross sections at relevant energies are taken from experimental data on π⁺ and π⁻ scattering on ³He and ⁴He, scaled by phase-space factors where necessary.

## Key numbers
- Experimental ratio R(π⁺/π⁻) for ⁴ΛHe decay: ≈ (5 ± 2)% (Bohm, Keyes).
- Standard charge-exchange mechanism (Dalitz, Gal) yields R ≈ (1.22–1.29)%, about two standard deviations below the experimental value.
- For ⁴ΛHe: estimated π⁰ + ³He → π⁺ + X charge-exchange cross section at T_lab ≈ 30 MeV: σ^cex ≈ 17 mb (scaled from σ^cex ≈ 30 mb at T_lab = 98.5 MeV using a √(98.5/30) ≈ 1.8 phase-space factor).
- For ⁴ΛHe: π⁻ absorption cross section on ³He at T_lab = 64 MeV: σ^abs = 14.7 ± 2.6 mb.
- Ratio of amplitudes ρ ≈ √(σ^abs/σ^cex) yields M^ππ/M^π ≲ 1, implying comparable contributions from absorption and charge-exchange mechanisms.
- For ⁵ΛHe: π⁻ absorption cross section on ⁴He at T_lab ≈ 60 MeV: σ^abs ≈ 30 mb (Fowler).
- For ⁵ΛHe: π⁰ + ⁴He → π⁺ + X charge-exchange cross section at T_lab = 120 MeV: σ^cex ≈ 14 mb (Baum); ratio yields M^ππ/M^π ≳ 1.
- For heavier hypernuclei (Li–B region): absorption cross section is 22–35% of total π⁻ cross section, charge exchange is 7–5% (Ing), giving an absorption mechanism dominance factor of 3–7 over charge exchange.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing puzzle of π⁺ emission in ⁴ΛHe decay, first studied by Dalitz (1960s) who proposed a two-step Λ → n + π⁰ decay followed by π⁰ → π⁺ charge exchange on the core, yielding a ratio far below experiment. Later work by Gal (1990s) using updated inputs gave R ≈ 1.22–1.29%, still below the experimental value. Other mechanisms, such as Λp → Σ⁺n conversion followed by Σ⁺ → π⁺n decay (Hippel, 1970s), were shown to give negligible contributions (≈ 0.2–0.4%), though Gibson (1990s) argued that with a Σ admixture P_Σ ≈ 9% in the hypernuclear wave function the experimental R could be reproduced. Oka (1990s) related π⁺ emission to the ΔI = 3/2 component of the weak Lagrangian but did not account for π⁻ yields from free Λ decay. The present work introduces a qualitatively different mechanism, weak two-pion emission followed by π⁻ absorption, which is not suppressed by the same kinematic constraints and can yield contributions comparable to or exceeding the standard charge-exchange mechanism, especially in heavier hypernuclei. This suggests that remeasurement of the π⁺ emission probability is highly desirable.
