# Influence of vector interactions on the favored shape of inhomogeneous chiral condensates

> We update a previous study of the effects of vector interactions in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model on the formation of inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking condensates. In particular, by properly considering a spatially modulated vector mean-field associated with the quark number density of the system we show that, as the value of the vector coupling increases, a chiral density wave modulation can become thermodynamically favored over a real sinusoidal modulation. This behavior is found both via a Ginzburg-Landau analysis close to the Lifshitz point, as well as with a full numerical diagonalization of the mean-field Dirac Hamiltonian at vanishing temperature.

# Influence of vector interactions on the favored shape of inhomogeneous chiral condensates

## Key claim

When vector interactions are treated self-consistently with a spatially modulated vector mean-field (rather than approximated by its spatial average), increasing the vector coupling strength causes a chiral density wave (CDW) modulation to become thermodynamically favored over a real sinusoidal modulation in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, at least in the vicinity of the Lifshitz point and for sufficiently large coupling.

## Method

The authors performed a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion of the thermodynamic potential around the chirally restored phase near the Lifshitz point, including terms up to order M^6 and allowing for a spatially varying shifted chemical potential. They also performed a full numerical diagonalization of the mean-field Dirac Hamiltonian in momentum space at zero temperature, treating the mass function as a cosine modulation and the shifted chemical potential as having a constant term plus a cos(2qz) modulation, then solving the stationarity conditions for the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- For G_V = G_S/5, the GL analysis predicts a region near the Lifshitz point where the CDW is favored over the cosine modulation, as shown by the δΩ/Δ^4 = 0 curve in the phase diagram
- For G_V = G_S/2, the region where the CDW is favored over the cosine modulation increases compared to the G_V = G_S/5 case
- The Pauli-Villars regulator cutoff was set to Λ = 757.048 MeV, with scalar coupling G_S = 6.002/Λ^2, fixed by fitting f_π = 88 MeV and the vacuum constituent quark mass to M_vac = 300 MeV
- At G_V = G_S/2 and zero temperature, the amplitude n_1 of the density oscillations for the cosine modulation is almost as large as the average density ⟨n⟩ at the onset of the inhomogeneous phase
- For G_V = 0, the free energy difference between cosine and CDW is δΩ = (α_4/4)Δ^4, with α_4 < 0 in the inhomogeneous phase, favoring the cosine
- At the Lifshitz point where α_4 = 0, the δΩ is dominated by the -R_β β_42 term which is finite and positive for G_V > 0

## Context

This work extends a previous study by Carignano, Nickel, and Buballa (2010) that investigated vector interactions in the NJL model but approximated the vector condensate by its spatial average (the "average-density approximation" or ADA). That earlier work found that vector interactions dramatically enhance the inhomogeneous region and that the real-kink crystal (RKC) remained favored over the CDW. The present work shows that lifting the ADA and allowing spatially modulated vector condensates reverses this preference for sufficiently large vector coupling, with the CDW becoming favored over sinusoidal modulations. This enables future investigations using the improved GL expansion developed by Carignano (2017) to determine whether the CDW becomes the most favored solution throughout the entire inhomogeneous phase, and whether the RKC could evolve continuously into a CDW at nonzero vector coupling.
