# Effective interactions and the nuclear shell-model

> This review aims at a critical discussion of the interplay between effective interactions derived from various many-body approaches and spectroscopic data extracted from large scale shell-model studies. To achieve this, our many-body scheme starts with the free nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, typically modelled on various meson exchanges. The NN interaction is in turn renormalized in order to derive an effective medium dependent interaction. The latter is in turn used in shell-model calculations of selected nuclei. We also describe how to sum up the parquet class of diagrams and present initial uses of the effective interactions in coupled cluster many-body theory.

## Key claim

This review argues that effective interactions for the nuclear shell model can be derived microscopically starting from the free nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, but that the perturbative \(\hat{Q}\)-box method is limited because it includes diagrams only to a certain order and shows no indication of convergence beyond third order, motivating alternative resummation techniques such as parquet-diagram summation and coupled-cluster theory.

## Method

The authors present three many-body frameworks for deriving effective interactions: (1) the \(\hat{Q}\)-box folded-diagram method in Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory, which starts from the free NN interaction renormalized through the \(G\)-matrix (summing ladder diagrams to infinite order via the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone equation); (2) the self-consistent summation of parquet diagrams, which merges the particle-particle (\([12]\) channel), particle-hole (\([13]\) channel), and exchange (\([14]\) channel) diagram classes; and (3) the coupled-cluster method using an exponential ansatz for the wave operator. The review applies these methods to shell-model calculations in the \(1s0d\) and \(1p0f\) shells and for medium-heavy nuclei in the mass region \(A=100\) to \(A=132\).

## Key numbers

* The typical interparticle distance in the NN interaction is of the order of \(1\sim 2\) fm.
* The review covers shell-model calculations for nuclei in the mass region \(A=100\) to \(A=132\).
* The coupled-cluster energy at the single and double excitation level contains contributions identical to those of second-order and third-order many-body perturbation theory, but lacks triple excitation contributions necessary to complete fourth-order many-body perturbation theory.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on microscopic effective interactions for the nuclear shell model, critically comparing the \(\hat{Q}\)-box method of Kuo and co-workers (1990, 1995) with parquet-diagram summation (Blaizot and Ripka 1986) and coupled-cluster theory (Coester and Kümmel 1958, 1960; Bartlett 1981). It also relates to no-core shell-model calculations for light nuclei (Bruce et al.) and variational Monte Carlo approaches (Vijay et al.). The work enables future applications of coupled-cluster and parquet methods to weakly bound nuclei and to the systematic inclusion of three-body forces.
