# The Fast Flavor Instability in Hypermassive Neutron Star Disk Outflows

> We examine the effect of neutrino flavor transformation by the fast flavor instability (FFI) on long-term mass ejection from accretion disks formed after neutron star mergers. Neutrino emission and absorption in the disk set the composition of the disk ejecta, which subsequently undergoes $r$-process nucleosynthesis upon expansion and cooling. Here we perform 28 time-dependent, axisymmetric, viscous-hydrodynamic simulations of accretion disks around hypermassive neutron stars (HMNSs) of variable lifetime, using a 3-species neutrino leakage scheme for emission and an annular-lightbulb scheme for absorption. We include neutrino flavor transformation due the FFI in a parametric way, by modifying the absorbed neutrino fluxes and temperatures, allowing for flavor mixing at various levels of flavor equilibration, and also in a way that aims to respect the lepton-number preserving symmetry of the neutrino self-interaction Hamiltonian. We find that for a promptly-formed black hole (BH), the FFI lowers the average electron fraction of the disk outflow due to a decrease in neutrino absorption, driven primarily by a drop in electron neutrino/antineutrino flux upon flavor mixing. For a long-lived HMNS, the disk emits more heavy lepton neutrinos and reabsorbs more electron neutrinos than for a BH, with a smaller drop in flux compensated by a higher neutrino temperature upon flavor mixing. The resulting outflow has a broader electron fraction distribution, a more proton-rich peak, and undergoes stronger radiative driving. Disks with intermediate HMNS lifetimes show results that fall in between these two limits. In most cases, the impact of the FFI on the outflow is moderate, with changes in mass ejection, average velocity, and average electron fraction of order $\sim 10\%$, and changes in the lanthanide/actinide mass fraction of up to a factor $\sim 2$.

## Key claim

The fast flavor instability (FFI) has a moderate impact on disk outflows from neutron star merger remnants, with changes in mass ejection, average velocity, and average electron fraction of order ~10% and changes in lanthanide/actinide mass fraction of up to a factor ~2, but the specific effect depends strongly on the lifetime of the hypermassive neutron star (HMNS) remnant, with promptly-formed black holes showing decreased electron fraction and long-lived HMNSs producing broader electron fraction distributions with more proton-rich peaks.

## Method

The authors performed 28 time-dependent, axisymmetric, viscous-hydrodynamic simulations of accretion disks around hypermassive neutron stars of variable lifetime (0 ms, 10 ms, 100 ms, and infinite) using the FLASH code with a pseudo-Newtonian potential. Neutrino emission was treated with a 3-species leakage scheme and absorption with an annular-lightbulb scheme, while flavor transformation due to the FFI was included parametrically by modifying absorbed neutrino fluxes and temperatures using an optical-depth-based activation parameter and various mixing coefficients (complete, flavor equilibration, intermediate, and asymmetric lepton-number-preserving schemes). Outflow trajectories were post-processed with the SkyNet nuclear reaction network for nucleosynthesis calculations.

## Key numbers

- Disk initial mass: 0.1 solar masses, with central object mass 2.65 solar masses and viscosity parameter alpha_v = 0.03
- For prompt black hole (BH-ab00 baseline): average electron fraction 0.29, average velocity 2.8 x 0.01c, ejected mass 2.4 x 10^-2 solar masses, red ejecta mass 0.08 x 10^-2 solar masses
- For prompt black hole with complete flavor mixing (BH-ab10): average electron fraction 0.26, average velocity 3.4 x 0.01c, ejected mass 1.8 x 10^-2 solar masses, red ejecta mass 1.09 x 10^-2 solar masses
- For long-lived HMNS (tinf-ab00 baseline): average electron fraction 0.38, average velocity 7.3 x 0.01c, ejected mass 9.7 x 10^-2 solar masses, red ejecta mass 0.51 x 10^-2 solar masses
- For long-lived HMNS with flavor equilibration (tinf-ab07): average electron fraction 0.38, average velocity 8.2 x 0.01c, ejected mass 9.6 x 10^-2 solar masses, red ejecta mass 1.10 x 10^-2 solar masses
- All simulations evolved for 17.735 seconds (5000 orbits at r=50 km), by which time disks lost at least 95% of initial mass
- HMNS neutrino temperatures set constant at 4 MeV for electron neutrinos, 5 MeV for electron antineutrinos, and 6 MeV for heavy lepton neutrinos

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the FFI in post-merger accretion disks, particularly the general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations by Li and Siegel (2021) over 400 ms and the viscous hydrodynamic and 3D MHD simulations by Just et al. (2022) over ~10 s and 500 ms respectively, both of which found ~10% decreases in mass ejection with shifts toward more neutron-rich values. The parametric approach enables a larger parameter study over long-duration disk simulations than previously possible, and the results provide lower limits on ejecta quantities since viscous hydrodynamics models dissipation of MHD turbulence but lack earlier magnetically-driven ejecta. The findings enable predictions for kilonova observations by connecting FFI effects to the electron fraction distribution and lanthanide/actinide content of disk outflows across different HMNS lifetimes.
