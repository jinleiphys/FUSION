# Microscopic Description of Band Structure at Very Extended Shapes in the A ~ 110 Mass Region

> Recent experiments have confirmed the existence of rotational bands in the A \~ 110 mass region with very extended shapes lying between super- and hyper-deformation. Using the projected shell model, we make a first attempt to describe quantitatively such a band structure in 108Cd. Excellent agreement is achieved in the dynamic moment of inertia J(2) calculation. This allows us to suggest the spin values for the energy levels, which are experimentally unknown. It is found that at this large deformation, the sharply down-sloping orbitals in the proton i_{13/2} subshell are responsible for the irregularity in the experimental J(2), and the wave functions of the observed states have a dominant component of two-quasiparticles from these orbitals. Measurement of transition quadrupole moments and g-factors will test these findings, and thus can provide a deeper understanding of the band structure at very extended shapes.

# Microscopic Description of Band Structure at Very Extended Shapes in the A ~ 110 Mass Region

## Key claim

Using the projected shell model, the authors achieve excellent agreement with the experimental dynamic moment of inertia J(2) for the highly deformed rotational band in 108Cd, but only if the measured first gamma-ray energy is assigned to spin I=36 hbar rather than the experimentally estimated range of 40(2) hbar. The authors attribute the irregularity in J(2) specifically to proton i13/2 orbitals, contradicting the experimental suggestion that neutron i13/2 and proton h11/2 orbitals are responsible.

## Method

The projected shell model (PSM) was applied to 108Cd with a fixed quadrupole deformation of epsilon2 = 0.67, using a basis of about 80 quasiparticle states including the vacuum, 2-quasineutron, 2-quasiproton, and 4-quasiparticle configurations from four major shells (N=3,4,5,6) for both neutrons and protons. The Hamiltonian included spherical single-particle, residual quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing terms, with the monopole pairing strength given by G_M = [17.10 ∓ 11.16 (N-Z)/A] A^{-1} and the quadrupole pairing strength set to 0.16 times G_M. Angular momentum projection was performed to restore rotational symmetry, followed by diagonalization in the projected basis.

## Key numbers

- The dynamic moment of inertia J(2) begins at approximately 80 hbar^2 MeV^{-1} at lower spins and drops to 50 hbar^2 MeV^{-1}
- The lower experimental limit for the transition quadrupole moment Q_t is 9.5 e b
- The calculated Q_t gives a constant value of 9.9 e b up to spin I=20 hbar, reaching a maximum of 10.4 e b
- The calculated Q_t shows an increase of about 12% in the spin range from roughly I=20 hbar to I=50 hbar
- The calculated g-factor shows an increase of approximately 25% around the band crossing region
- The first band crossing occurs at I=38 hbar for the proton 2-quasiparticle band
- The neutron 2-quasiparticle bands cross the ground band at approximately I=46 hbar
- The measured gamma-ray cascade spans the spin range I=36 hbar to 56 hbar based on the authors' assignment
- The monopole pairing strength uses an 8% reduction from values used for the A ~ 190 mass region

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model approach previously applied to superdeformed bands in the A ~ 190 region (Sun, 1997), A ~ 130 region (Sun, 1995), A ~ 80 region (Ted), A ~ 60 region (Sun, 1999), and A ~ 30 region (Sun, 2001) to the newly discovered very extended shapes in the A ~ 110 mass region. The paper directly addresses the experimental findings of Clark et al. (2001), who identified a rotational band in 108Cd with the most deformed structure observed to date, lying between super- and hyper-deformation. The calculations predict that measurement of transition quadrupole moments and g-factors can distinguish between the proton i13/2 and neutron i13/2 configurations, and suggest that neighboring nuclei may exhibit similar rotational bands accessible to future experiments.
