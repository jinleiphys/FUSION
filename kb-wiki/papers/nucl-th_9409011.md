# Pushing and Cranking Corrections to the Meson Fields of the Bosonized Nambu \& Jona-Lasinio Model

> We study the effect of center-of-mass motion and rotational corrections on hedgehog meson fields in the bosonized two-flavor Nambu \& Jona-Lasinio model. To remove the spurious motion and to restore good spin and isospin we consider a boosted and rotating soliton instead a static soliton at rest. Modified meson fields are obtained by minimizing a corrected effective energy functional. The importance of the modification is estimated by evaluating expectation values of several observables. Stabile solitonic configurations are obtained for $M\gapp$ 300\,MeV, while static solitons exists for $M\gapp$ 350\,MeV only. Despite the considerable size of the energy corrections (30-50\% of the soliton energy) the main features of the static soliton are preserved. Modified meson profiles are somewhat narrower than static ones and have a different asymptotic behavior, which depends on the isospin quantum number. The modifications increase with increasing constituent quark mass. The valence-quark picture dominates up to very large constituent quark masses. In the physically relevant mass region, the root-mean square radius of the quark distribution is reduced by less than 10 percent. The $Δ$--nucleon mass-splitting is still weaker affected.

## Key claim

The paper shows that applying self-consistent "pushing" (center-of-mass) and "cranking" (rotational) corrections to the bosonized Nambu & Jona-Lasinio soliton modifies the meson fields and stabilizes solitonic configurations for constituent quark masses down to 300 MeV, whereas static solitons exist only for masses above 350 MeV; however, the main features of the static soliton are preserved despite energy corrections of 30-50% of the soliton energy.

## Method

The authors start from static hedgehog meson fields in the two-flavor bosonized Nambu & Jona-Lasinio model, which minimize an effective energy functional. They then apply semiclassical pushing and cranking approaches: the soliton is boosted with velocity V and rotated with angular frequency Omega in isospace, with V and Omega treated as Lagrange multipliers fixed to yield the correct expectation values of momentum and isospin. Modified meson fields are obtained by minimizing a corrected effective energy functional that includes the kinetic energy of center-of-mass motion and rotational energy, using a 7-knot spline parametrization of the profile function with predetermined asymptotic behavior. The quark hamiltonian is diagonalized in a discrete basis, and sea-quark contributions are regularized using Schwinger's proper-time scheme.

## Key numbers

- Static solitons exist only for constituent quark masses M >= 350 MeV, while corrected solitons are stable for M >= 300 MeV
- Energy corrections (center-of-mass and rotational) amount to 30-50% of the soliton energy
- Center-of-mass energy amounts to 15-30% of the total soliton energy (roughly 1240 MeV) for constituent quark masses between 350 MeV and 500 MeV, reaching 50% for M = 1000 MeV
- Rotational corrections amount to roughly half of the center-of-mass correction for small constituent quark masses
- In the physically relevant mass region, the root-mean square radius of the quark distribution is reduced by less than 10 percent
- The Delta-nucleon mass-splitting is even weaker affected than the root-mean square radius
- For M = 500 MeV, the deviation of the spline interpolation from the original profile did not exceed 0.25 degrees, and the difference between iteratively determined and minimized profiles was less than 0.35 degrees
- Valence- and sea-quark energies vary by not more than 1 MeV when using the 7-knot spline representation; the mesonic energy varies up to 2 MeV; the root-mean square radius varies by at most 0.02 fm

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of static solitons in the bosonized Nambu & Jona-Lasinio model (Reinhardt and Wuensch, 1994; Goeke et al., 1991) by incorporating center-of-mass and rotational corrections self-consistently, going beyond the rigid-rotor approximation used in earlier cranking approaches (Cohen, 1986; Adkins et al., 1983; Reinhardt, 1989). The method is the translational analogue of cranking and is equivalent to a semiclassical approximation of the Peierls-Yoccoz projection method (Ring and Schuck, 1980). The results enable improved calculations of nucleon and Delta isobar properties within the Nambu & Jona-Lasinio model, with the corrected soliton energies showing agreement with the experimental nucleon mass for small constituent quark masses, though the Delta mass is underestimated across the whole mass region studied.
