# Standard and non-standard neutrino-nucleus reactions cross sections and event rates to neutrino detection experiments

> Open neutrino physics issues require precision studies, both theoretical and experimental ones, and towards this aim coherent neutral current neutrino-nucleus scattering events are expected to be observed soon. In this work, we explore $ν$-nucleus processes from a nuclear theory point of view and obtain results with high confidence level based on accurate nuclear structure cross sections calculations. Besides cross sections, the present study includes simulated signals expected to be recorded by nuclear detectors, differential event rates as well as total number of events predicted to be measured. Our original cross sections calculations are focused on measurable rates for the Standard Model process, but we also perform calculations for various channels of the non-standard neutrino-nucleus reactions and come out with promising results within the current upper limits of the corresponding exotic parameters. We concentrate on the possibility of detecting (i) supernova neutrinos by using massive detectors like those of the GERDA and SuperCDMS dark matter experiments and (ii) laboratory neutrinos produced near the spallation neutron source facilities (at Oak Ridge National Lab) by the COHERENT experiment. Our nuclear calculations take advantage of the relevant experimental sensitivity and employ the severe bounds extracted for the exotic parameters entering the Lagrangians of various particle physics models and specifically those resulting from the charged lepton flavour violating $μ^{-} \rightarrow e^{-}$ experiments (Mu2e and COMET experiments).

## Key claim

The paper claims that accurate nuclear structure calculations, specifically using the quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) with realistic pairing interactions, yield high-confidence predictions for coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections and event rates, but the results are conditional on the assumption that the nuclear form factors' momentum dependence is properly included, as neglecting this dependence can lead to differences of even an order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear structure calculations using the QRPA framework, constructing nuclear ground states by solving the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) equations iteratively with realistic pairing interactions from the Bonn C-D potential. They computed coherent neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for both Standard Model and non-standard interactions (including non-universal and flavour-changing terms) for a set of nuclei from 12C to 208Pb, using multiple methods for nuclear form factors: QRPA with BCS, fractional occupation probabilities (FOP) in a shell-model approach, and effective analytic expressions. The cross sections were then folded with neutrino flux distributions from supernova and spallation neutron source (SNS) spectra to predict differential event rates and total number of events for detectors like those used in GERDA and SuperCDMS experiments.

## Key numbers

* For 40Ar, the fitted fractional occupation probabilities that reproduce experimental charge density are alpha1 = 0.85, alpha2 = 1.25, alpha3 = 0.85, alpha4 = 0.75.
* For 48Ti, the fitted fractional occupation probabilities are alpha1 = 1.0, alpha2 = 1.5, alpha3 = 0.35, alpha4 = 0.1.
* The maximum nuclear recoil energy is given by T_N^max = 2 E_nu^2 / (M + 2 E_nu), where M is the nuclear mass.
* The momentum transfer in the coherent elastic channel is q^2 = 2 M T_N, with q = 2 E_nu sin(theta/2).
* The Standard Model cross sections for different nuclei differ by up to two orders of magnitude or more between light and heavy nuclear systems.
* The nuclear form factor suppression becomes significant for heavy nuclei even at low energies, with the form factor momentum dependence causing differences of even an order of magnitude compared to calculations neglecting this dependence.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Papoulias and Kosmas, 2014) by performing comprehensive nuclear structure calculations for a wider set of experimentally interesting nuclei, using the same QRPA methods previously applied to muon-to-electron conversion in nuclei (Chiang and Kosmas; Kosmas, 2001). The results are intended to support upcoming experiments such as COHERENT at the SNS at Oak Ridge National Lab, as well as dark matter detectors like GERDA and SuperCDMS that can also detect supernova neutrinos. The paper enables more accurate predictions for event rates in these experiments by incorporating realistic nuclear form factors, and provides a framework for constraining non-standard neutrino interaction parameters using the severe bounds from charged lepton flavour violating experiments like Mu2e and COMET.
