# Semi-Classical Description of the Average Pairing Properties in Nuclei

> We present a new semi-classical theory for describing pairing in finite Fermi systems. It is based in taking the $\hbar \to 0$, i.e. Thomas-Fermi, limit of the gap equation written in the basis of the mean field (weak coupling). In addition to the position dependence of the Fermi momentum, the size dependence of the matrix elements of the pairing force is also taken into account in this theory. An example typical for the nuclear situation shows the improvement of this new approach over the standard Local Density Approximation. We also show that if in this approach some shell fluctuations are introduced in the level density, the arch structure displayed by the quantal gaps along isotopic chains is almost recovered. We also point out that in heavy drip line nuclei pairing is strongly reduced.

## Key claim

The authors present a new semiclassical (Thomas-Fermi) theory for pairing in finite Fermi systems that improves upon the standard Local Density Approximation (LDA) by keeping the size dependence of the pairing force matrix elements, and they show that this approach works in the weak coupling regime where the LDA condition (coherence length smaller than oscillator length) is violated.

## Method

The authors take the \(\hbar \to 0\) (Thomas-Fermi) limit of the gap equation written in the basis of the normal mean field (weak coupling regime), replacing the quantal density matrix with its semiclassical on-shell counterpart. They compute the semiclassical level density and pairing matrix elements using the classical Hamiltonian (with position-dependent effective mass and mean field) obtained from Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) theory for finite-range Gogny D1S force. For realistic applications, they introduce quantal shell fluctuations into the level density by summing Gaussians centered at single-particle energies, while retaining the semiclassical matrix elements, to recover the arch structure of quantal gaps along isotopic chains.

## Key numbers

- For a harmonic oscillator potential with \(\hbar \omega = 8.65\) MeV and constant effective mass \(m^*/m = 0.8\), the TF approach yields a precise average of the quantal gaps, while LDA overestimates by about 20 percent when the pairing force is increased by a factor of 1.5.
- The fluctuating level density is built using Gaussians with width \(\sigma = 0.5\) MeV centered at each single-particle energy.
- The study along the tin isotopic chain covers from \(^{100}\)Sn to \(^{132}\)Sn.
- In the TF limit, the gap vanishes when the chemical potential \(\mu\) equals the depth of the single-particle potential, i.e., exactly at the drip line.

## Context

This work extends the Thomas-Fermi approach to pairing in finite Fermi systems, improving upon the standard Local Density Approximation (LDA) of Kucharek et al. (1989), which fails when the coherence length exceeds the oscillator length. The authors build on their own preliminary applications (Farine et al., 2000; Barranco et al., 2005) and use the Extended Thomas-Fermi method for finite-range interactions (Centelles et al., 1998; Soubbotin et al., 2000). The theory enables the study of average pairing properties in heavy drip-line nuclei and in systems with large particle numbers, such as those in the outer crust of neutron stars, where quantal fluctuations die out and the semiclassical behavior prevails.
