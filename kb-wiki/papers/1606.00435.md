# Axion field and the quark nugget's formation at the QCD phase transition

> We study a testable dark matter (DM) model outside of the standard WIMP paradigm in which the observed ratio $Ω_{\rm dark} \simeq Ω_{\rm visible}$ for visible and dark matter densities finds its natural explanation as a result of their common QCD origin when both types of matter (DM and visible) are formed at the QCD phase transition and both are proportional to $Λ_{\rm QCD}$. Instead of conventional "baryogenesis" mechanism we advocate a paradigm when the "baryogenesis" is actually a charge separation process which always occur in the presence of the $\cal{CP}$ odd axion field $a(x)$. In this scenario the global baryon number of the Universe remains zero, while the unobserved anti-baryon charge is hidden in form of heavy nuggets, similar to Witten's strangelets and compromise the DM of the Universe. We argue that the nuggets will be inevitably produced during the QCD phase transition as a result of Kibble-Zurek mechanism on formation of the topological defects during a phase transition. Relevant topological defects in our scenario are the closed bubbles made of the $N_{\rm DW}=1$ axion domain walls. These bubbles, in general, accrete the baryon (or anti baryon) charge, which eventually result in formation of the nuggets and anti-nuggets carrying a huge baryon (anti-baryon) charge. The main consequence of the model, $Ω_{\rm dark} \approx Ω_{\rm visible}$ is insensitive to the axion mass which may assume any value within the observationally allowed window $10^{-6} {\rm eV} \lesssim m_a \lesssim 10^{-3}{\rm eV}$. We also estimate the baryon to entropy ratio $η\equiv {n_B}/{n_γ}\sim 10^{-10}$ within this scenario. Finally, we comment on implications of these results to the axion search experiments, including microwave cavity and the Orpheus experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed ratio $\Omega_{\rm dark} \approx \Omega_{\rm visible}$ for dark and visible matter densities arises naturally from a common QCD origin at the phase transition, but this result is insensitive to the axion mass within the observationally allowed window $10^{-6} \,{\rm eV} \lesssim m_a \lesssim 10^{-3} \,{\rm eV}$, and the baryon-to-entropy ratio $\eta \sim 10^{-10}$ is determined primarily by the formation temperature $T_{\rm form} \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ rather than by fine-tuned initial conditions.

## Method

The authors propose that during the QCD phase transition, closed bubbles made of $N_{\rm DW}=1$ axion domain walls form via the Kibble-Zurek mechanism. These bubbles accrete baryon or antibaryon charge, and their collapse is halted by Fermi pressure from the quark matter inside, leading to stable nuggets and anti-nuggets in a colour superconducting phase. The preferential formation of anti-nuggets over nuggets arises from the dynamics of the coherent axion field $\theta(x)$, which is correlated on cosmological scales at the start of the phase transition, producing a net visible baryon asymmetry while the global baryon number remains zero.

## Key numbers

- The axion mass window allowed by observations is $10^{-6} \,{\rm eV} \lesssim m_a \lesssim 10^{-3} \,{\rm eV}$.
- Nugget radius scales as $10^{-6} \,{\rm cm} \lesssim R \lesssim 10^{-3} \,{\rm cm}$ across this axion mass range.
- Nugget baryon charge scales as $10^{23} \lesssim B \lesssim 10^{32}$ across this axion mass range.
- The baryon-to-entropy ratio is estimated as $\eta \equiv n_B / n_\gamma \sim 10^{-10}$.
- The formation temperature is estimated as $T_{\rm form} \approx 41 \,{\rm MeV}$, based on the colour superconducting gap $\Delta \sim 100 \,{\rm MeV}$ and the critical temperature $T_{\rm CS} \simeq 0.6 \Delta \simeq 60 \,{\rm MeV}$.
- The preferred mean baryon charge for consistency with observations is $\langle B \rangle \sim 10^{25}$, corresponding to axion mass $m_a \simeq 10^{-4} \,{\rm eV}$.
- The flux of nuggets with $B \sim 10^{25}$ is approximately $dN/(dA \, dt) \approx (10^{25}/B) \,{\rm km}^{-2} \,{\rm yr}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the quark nugget dark matter model originally proposed by Zhitnitsky (2002) and Oaknin and Zhitnitsky (2003), which itself builds on Witten's (1984) strangelet concept. The key advance is a detailed theoretical study of the nugget formation mechanism during the QCD phase transition, addressing hard dynamical questions in strongly coupled QCD that were not explored in the original proposals. The model enables testable predictions across eleven orders of magnitude in frequency, from radio to gamma rays, and connects to direct axion search experiments including microwave cavity experiments and the Orpheus experiment.
