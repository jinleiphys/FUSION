# The Thermodynamics of a 5D Gravity-Dilaton-Tachyon Solution

> We propose a finite-temperature holographic model with a soft-wall geometry that incorporates two scalar fields, dual to the gluon and chiral operators. A series solution is presented as the dynamical, black-hole solution to Einstein's equations. We use the solution to calculate the thermal properties of the corresponding 4D gauge theory. Gluon and chiral thermal condensates contribute leading-order terms that affect the speed of sound through and entropy of the 4D thermal medium. At a temperature $T_c\sim 900$ MeV, we find a phase transition, which is much higher than lattice QCD calculations. However, the transition only exists with nonzero thermal condensates.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a finite-temperature holographic soft-wall model with two scalar fields (dilaton and tachyon) and finds a deconfinement phase transition at approximately 900 MeV, but only when nonzero thermal condensates are present; this transition temperature is much higher than lattice QCD predictions, and the authors note the solution is an expansion valid only in the limit of small z and z_h.

## Method

The authors construct a 5D gravity-dilaton-tachyon action in the Einstein frame, derive the equations of motion for both a thermal AdS (thAdS) solution and a black-hole AdS (bhAdS) solution, and obtain a series expansion for the bhAdS metric, dilaton, tachyon, and blackening function f(z) up to order z^8. They then compute the on-shell action, entropy from the black-hole horizon area, free energy from the action difference between bhAdS and thAdS after matching at the boundary, and the speed of sound from thermodynamic relations, with all quantities depending on a condensate function G(z_h) determined by solving a stiff differential equation.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition temperature is found at T_c ~ 900 MeV, which is much higher than lattice QCD calculations.
- The entropy density at high temperature scales as T^3, with the explicit expression: s = (4 N_c^2 V)/(45 pi) [1/z_h^3 - (2 mu^2)/z_h - (3 sqrt(3) G)/(2 sqrt(2) mu^2 z_h) + 2 mu^4 z_h + 2 sqrt(6) z_h G + (27 z_h G^2)/(16 mu^4)].
- The speed of sound squared v_s^2 recovers the conformal value of 1/3 in the high-temperature limit, matching the upper bound advocated by Cherman (2009).
- The series expansion coefficients for the condensate function G(z_h) are g_{-2} = 1.43290 and g_0 = -6.09417, with the series valid for z_h < 1.
- The 5D gravitational coupling is fixed as G_5 = (45 pi R^3)/(16 N_c^2).
- The dilaton mass squared from the Batell potential is m_phi^2 R^2 = -4, and from the alternative potential is m_phi^2 R^2 = 0; the tachyon mass squared is m_chi^2 R^2 = -3 in both cases.

## Context

This work extends soft-wall holographic models, such as those by Karch (2006) and Batell (2008), to finite temperature by introducing a black-hole solution with two scalar fields dual to gluon and chiral operators, going beyond earlier single-dilaton finite-temperature models by Gursoy (2008, 2009) and Gubser (2008). The approach attempts to introduce more rigor into soft-wall thermodynamics than previous work by Herzog (2006) and Colangelo (2009), and the results enable future study of how thermal condensates affect the deconfinement phase transition and the equation of state of the quark-gluon plasma, though the authors note the field/operator correspondence remains ambiguous and requires further research.
