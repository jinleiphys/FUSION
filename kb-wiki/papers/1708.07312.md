# Improved modeling of photon observables with FREYA

> The event-by-event fission model FREYA has been improved, in particular to address deficiencies in the calculation of photon observables. We discuss the improvements that have been made and introduce several new variables, some detector dependent, that affect the photon observables. We show the sensitivity of FREYA to these variables. We then compare the results to the available photon data from spontaneous and thermal neutron-induced fission.

## Key claim

The improved FREYA model now includes a giant dipole resonance (GDR) form factor to harden the statistical photon spectrum and evaluated discrete transitions from the RIPL-3 database, but the authors note that the parameter governing fragment spins, \(c_S\), is strongly correlated with the total kinetic energy shift \(d\)TKE and its optimal value for a particular nucleus is best determined on the basis of a global analysis of each isotope, so the results for cases other than \(^{252}\)Cf(sf) are preliminary.

## Method

The event-by-event fission code FREYA was modified to conserve total angular momentum including wriggling and bending modes, to modulate the black-body photon spectrum by a GDR form factor with \(E_{\rm GDR}({\rm MeV})=31.2/A^{1/3}+20.6/A^{1/6}\) and \(\Gamma_{\rm GDR}=5\,{\rm MeV}\), and to include evaluated discrete electromagnetic transitions from the RIPL-3 database for fragment de-excitation. For levels without tabulated decay rates, transition probabilities were assigned based on phase-space considerations with a spin-dependent Gaussian factor. The spin temperature was expressed as \(T_S = c_S T_{\rm sc}\) where \(T_{\rm sc}\) is the scission temperature, and the parameter \(c_S\) was varied to study its effect on photon observables.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{252}\)Cf(sf), the best-fit parameter values from a particle-swarm \(\chi^2\) minimization were: \(d\)TKE = 0.5 MeV, \(x = 1.27\), \(c_T = 1.08\), \(e_0 = 10.37\) MeV, and \(c_S = 0.87\).
* The average photon multiplicity for \(^{252}\)Cf(sf) decreased from 11.61 (no GDR, no RIPL) to 9.79 (with GDR, no RIPL) to 8.36 (with both GDR and RIPL).
* The GDR width was fixed at \(\Gamma_{\rm GDR}=5\,{\rm MeV}\).
* The minimum photon energy threshold was set to \(g_{\rm min} = 0.10\) MeV and the maximum half-life for discrete levels to \(t_{\rm max} = 1.5\) ns.
* Increasing \(c_S\) from 0.2 to 2.0 decreased the average neutron multiplicity by up to 12% when all other parameters were held fixed.
* The average fragment angular momentum \(S_{\rm f}\) remained near \(2\hbar\) for \(c_S = 0.2\) and grew with increasing \(c_S\), showing a nearly linear increase in the light mass region.

## Context

This work extends the earlier FREYA photon treatment of Vogt and Randrup (2013) by adding a GDR form factor and discrete transitions from the RIPL-3 database, similar to approaches used in the FIFRELIN and CGMF codes. The improvements enable more realistic comparisons to photon data from spontaneous fission of \(^{252}\)Cf(sf) and thermal neutron-induced fission of \(^{235}\)U and \(^{239}\)Pu, and the authors note that ongoing work aims to produce a better global \(\chi^2\) fit with well-defined uncertainties for \(^{252}\)Cf(sf) and to apply the same method to other fissioning nuclei.
