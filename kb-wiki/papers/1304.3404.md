# Probing the pattern of holographic thermalization with photons

> We investigate the behavior of the retarded Green's function of a U(1) gauge field in holographic N=4 Super Yang-Mills plasma, taking the leading strong coupling corrections into account. First, we use the thermal limit of this quantity to determine the flow of the photon quasinormal mode spectrum away from the infinite 't Hooft coupling limit, and after this specialize to a specific model of holographic thermalization, in which we evaluate the corresponding spectral density. In the latter case, our primary interest lies in the pattern, with which the spectral density approaches its equilibrium form, and how this process depends on the value of the coupling as well as the photon virtuality. All of the results obtained point consistently towards the weakening of the usual top/down pattern of holographic thermalization, once the coupling is decreased from the λ=infinity limit.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how the pattern of holographic thermalization in strongly coupled N=4 Super Yang-Mills plasma changes when the 't Hooft coupling is decreased from the infinite coupling limit, finding that the leading finite coupling corrections weaken the usual top-down thermalization pattern (where high-energy modes thermalize first) and may shift it toward bottom-up behavior, though the effect depends on photon virtuality.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence with a collapsing thin spherical shell model in AdS$_5$ space to study out-of-equilibrium photon production, working in the quasistatic limit where shell motion is slow compared to other time scales. They compute the retarded Green's function of a U(1) gauge field (the photon) at leading order and including the first strong coupling corrections of order $1/\lambda^{3/2}$, solving the equation of motion for transverse and longitudinal electric field components both inside and outside the shell and matching them at the shell location using Israel junction conditions. The quasinormal mode spectrum is obtained from the thermal equilibrium correlator by finding poles of the retarded Green's function using Frobenius expansions.

## Key numbers

- The quasinormal mode spectrum at infinite coupling and zero momentum is $\omega_n(q=0) = 2\pi T n(\pm 1 - i)$ for $n = 0, 1, 2, ...$
- The shell approaches the horizon as $(r_s - r_h)/r_h = e^{-t/\tau_h}$ where $\tau_h = 1/(4\pi T)$
- The finite coupling expansion parameter is $\gamma = \frac{1}{8}\zeta(3)\lambda^{-3/2}$
- The $\gamma$-corrected relation between horizon radius and temperature is $r_h = \pi T / (1 + 265\gamma/16)$
- Numerical results are presented for 't Hooft coupling values $\lambda = 1000$, $\lambda = 300$, and $\lambda = 100$
- The spectral density deviation from equilibrium is studied for virtuality parameters $c = 0$, $c = 0.8$, and $c = 1$ (where $q = c\omega$)
- The shell location parameter is set to $u_s = 1/1.1^2$ for all plots

## Context

This work extends earlier holographic studies of photon production during thermalization by Baier et al. (2012) and Steineder et al. (2012), which found indications of the top-down thermalization pattern shifting toward bottom-up behavior when finite coupling corrections were included for real photons. The paper complements these studies by examining the virtuality dependence of the effect and by computing the quasinormal mode spectrum at finite coupling, which provides additional evidence for the weakening of the top-down pattern. The results enable future work on identifying the coupling strength at which the transition between top-down and bottom-up thermalization occurs, and suggest that maximally virtual photons (dileptons at rest) thermalize faster than real photons at all coupling strengths studied.
