# Constrained caloric curves and phase transition for hot nuclei

> Simulations based on experimental data obtained from multifragmenting quasi-fused nuclei produced in central $^{129}$Xe + $^{nat}$Sn collisions have been used to deduce event by event freeze-out properties in the thermal excitation energy range 4-12 AMeV [Nucl. Phys. A809 (2008) 111]. From these properties and the temperatures deduced from proton transverse momentum fluctuations, constrained caloric curves have been built. At constant average volumes caloric curves exhibit a monotonic behaviour whereas for constrained pressures a backbending is observed. Such results support the existence of a first order phase transition for hot nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that constrained caloric curves for hot nuclei exhibit a monotonic behaviour at constant average volume and a backbending at constant pressure, which supports the existence of a first-order phase transition for hot nuclei; however, the authors note that the classical kinetic temperature \(T_{kin}\) may not be fully relevant, and they apply quantum corrections to proton transverse momentum fluctuations to obtain more reliable temperatures.

## Method

The authors used simulations based on experimental data from multifragmenting quasi-fused nuclei produced in central \(^{129}\)Xe + \(^{nat}\)Sn collisions to deduce event-by-event freeze-out properties (thermal excitation energy, freeze-out volume, total thermal kinetic energy) in the thermal excitation energy range 4–12 AMeV. From these properties, they built constrained caloric curves by sorting events into bins of thermal excitation energy and selecting events with either constant average freeze-out volumes (3.0–4.0 \(V_0\) and 5.0–6.0 \(V_0\)) or constrained pressure ranges (1.3–4.5 and 4.5–7.9 \(\times 10^{-2}\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\)). Temperatures were derived from proton transverse momentum fluctuations, with quantum corrections applied using a Fermi-Dirac distribution and a Fermi energy estimated from semi-classical calculations (\(\epsilon_f \sim 20\) MeV at \(\rho \sim 0.4\rho_0\)).

## Key numbers

* Thermal excitation energy range studied: 4–12 AMeV for quasi-fused nuclei from central \(^{129}\)Xe + \(^{nat}\)Sn collisions.
* Freeze-out volume ranges used for constrained caloric curves: 3.0–4.0 \(V_0\) and 5.0–6.0 \(V_0\), where \(V_0\) is the volume at normal density.
* Pressure ranges used for constrained caloric curves: (1.3–4.5) and (4.5–7.9) \(\times 10^{-2}\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\).
* Quantum-corrected caloric curve shows a plateau at a temperature around 10–11 MeV on the thermal excitation energy range 5–10 AMeV.
* Critical temperature estimated from vanishing of backbending: around 13 MeV for the selected finite systems (mass range 194–238).
* Upper limits of the spinodal region and coexistence region estimated at around 8 and 10 AMeV, respectively.
* Systematic error on quantum temperatures: \(\pm 0.6\) to \(\pm 0.5\) MeV over the considered temperature range, due to \(\pm 0.1\rho_0\) uncertainty in density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition by using constrained caloric curves, which provide a more definitive signature than earlier unconstrained caloric curves that showed only plateaus (e.g., Pochodzalla et al. (1995), Natowitz et al. (2002)). The results are consistent with theoretical predictions from a microcanonical lattice gas model (Chomaz et al. (2000)) and with earlier experimental signals of negative microcanonical heat capacity and bimodality (Chomaz et al. (2004), Borderie et al. (2008)). The paper enables future work on the dynamics of the transition, specifically distinguishing between spinodal fragmentation and molecular dynamics approaches (e.g., Chomaz et al. (2004), Ono et al. (1999), Rizzo et al. (2007)).
