# Investigation of $־±$ clustering with knockout reactions

> Our goal is to reveal how the $־±$ cluster amplitude is probed through $־±$ knockout reactions depending on reaction conditions, e.g., the incident energy. We consider $^{20}$Ne($p$,$p־±$)$^{16}$O and $^{120}$Sn($p$,$p־±$)$^{116}$Cd at 100-400 MeV within the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework. We introduce a masking function which shows how the reaction amplitude in the nuclear interior is suppressed and defines the probed region of the $־±$ cluster wave function. It is clearly shown by means of the masking function that the $־±$ knockout reaction probes the $־±$ cluster amplitude in the nuclear surface region, which is the direct measure of well-developed $־±$ cluster states. A simplified form of the masking function is introduced and the incident energy dependence of the masking effect is investigated. $־±$ knockout reaction can probe the $־±$ cluster amplitude in the nuclear surface region by choosing proper kinematics owing to the masking effect originated from absorptions of distorting potentials, and is a suitable method to investigate how $־±$ cluster states are spatially developed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\alpha$ knockout reaction probes the $\alpha$ cluster amplitude in the nuclear surface region, not the spectroscopic factor, and that this surface amplitude is the direct measure of well-developed $\alpha$ cluster states; this conclusion is conditional on choosing proper kinematics and relies on the masking effect from absorption in distorting potentials.

## Method

The authors performed distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations for the $^{20}$Ne($p$,$p\alpha$)$^{16}$O and $^{120}$Sn($p$,$p\alpha$)$^{116}$Cd reactions at incident energies of 100-400 MeV. They introduced a masking function $D_{lm}(R)$ that quantifies how the reaction amplitude in the nuclear interior is suppressed by absorption from the distorting potentials of the incoming proton and outgoing proton and alpha particle. The radial reaction amplitude $I(R)$ was then analyzed to determine which radial region of the $\alpha$ cluster wave function contributes to the knockout cross section.

## Key numbers

* For $^{20}$Ne($p$,$p\alpha$)$^{16}$O at 392 MeV, the triple differential cross section (TDX) peaks at recoil momentum $p_R \sim 0$ MeV/$c$ for the $l=0$ cluster orbital.
* The $\alpha$ cluster wave function for $^{20}$Ne was constructed with binding energy $\varepsilon_{\alpha} = -4.73$ MeV, radius parameter $r_0 = 1.25 \times 16^{1/3}$ fm, and diffuseness $a_0 = 0.76$ fm.
* For $^{120}$Sn($p$,$p\alpha$)$^{116}$Cd at 392 MeV, the masking function in the asymptotic region is only $0.2$-$0.3$ due to Coulomb interactions; the $\alpha$ cluster binding energy was $\varepsilon_{\alpha} = -4.81$ MeV with $r_0 = 1.25 \times 116^{1/3}$ fm.
* The eikonal masking function parameter $C_{\mathrm{abs}}$ for $^{20}$Ne($p$,$p\alpha$)$^{16}$O at 392 MeV was $0.69$ fm$^{-1}$; $C_{\mathrm{abs}}$ depends weakly on incident energy above 200 MeV but increases rapidly below 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of $\alpha$ clustering from transfer reactions, specifically the $^{16}$O($^{6}$Li,$d$)$^{20}$Ne reaction studied by Fukuda et al. (2016), to the ($p$,$p\alpha$) knockout reaction. The authors show that the masking effect from absorption makes ($p$,$p\alpha$) reactions peripheral, automatically avoiding the nuclear interior where nucleon-nucleon antisymmetrization complicates the definition of $\alpha$ clusters. This enables future experiments to probe spatially developed $\alpha$ cluster states in both light and heavy nuclei, including cases with strong Coulomb effects like $^{120}$Sn.
