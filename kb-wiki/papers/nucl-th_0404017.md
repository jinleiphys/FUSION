# Recent developments in weak-coupling color superconductivity

> Recent developments in weak-coupling color superconductivity are reviewed. These developments are as follows. The mean field gap equation is solved for most common superconducting phases up to subleading order; BCS relation is found to be violated in color-flavor-locking and color-spin-locking phases due to the two-gaps structure of the order parameter; The Debye and Meissner masses of gluons and photon with their rotated partners are calculated for these phases; We found that there is no electromagnetic Meissner effect in spin-one color superconductor; A proof of gauge parameter independence at subleading order is given in covariant gauge.

# Recent developments in weak-coupling color superconductivity

## Key claim

The weak-coupling approach to color superconductivity is valid only at very high density, but it is from first principles and provides guidelines for more phenomenological models. The BCS relation is violated in color-flavor-locking and color-spin-locking phases due to the two-gap structure of the order parameter, and there is no electromagnetic Meissner effect in spin-one color superconductor.

## Method

The mean field gap equation was solved for six different superconducting phases (2SC, CFL, color-spin-locking, and polar phases) up to subleading order, obtaining the gap function at the Fermi surface for all cases. The polarization tensor for gluons and photons was computed in its zero-energy, low-momentum limit for the 2SC, CFL, polar, and CSL phases to yield Debye and Meissner masses. A generalized Ward identity was derived from QCD to prove gauge parameter independence of the gap function and quasiparticle dispersion relation up to subleading order in covariant gauge.

## Key numbers

- Zero temperature gap at the Fermi surface for 2SC phase: $\phi_0^{\rm 2SC} = 2 \, \tilde{b} \, b_0'\, \mu\, \exp\left(-\frac{\pi}{2\, \bar{g}}\right)$, where $\bar{g} \equiv \frac{g}{3 \sqrt{2}\, \pi}$, $\tilde{b} \equiv 256 \pi^4 \left(\frac{2}{N_f g^2} \right)^{5/2}$, and $b_0' \equiv \exp\left(-\frac{\pi^2+4}{8}\right)$
- In spin-1 phases, subleading contributions suppress the gap by factors $e^{-4.5} \simeq 10^{-2}$ to $e^{-6} \simeq 2.5\times 10^{-3}$ relative to the spin-zero gap
- Transition temperature relation: $\frac{T_c}{\phi_0}=\frac{e^\gamma}{\pi} \, \left( \lambda_1^{a_1} \, \lambda_2^{a_2} \right)^{1/2} \simeq 0.57 \, \left( \lambda_1^{a_1} \, \lambda_2^{a_2} \right)^{1/2}$, where $\gamma\simeq 0.577$ is the Euler-Mascheroni constant
- In 2SC and polar phases with one gapped excitation, $T_c/\phi_0 \simeq 0.57$ recovers the BCS relation
- In CFL and CSL phases, the two gaps are $\sqrt{\lambda_1} \, \phi_0 = 2 \, \phi_0$ and $\sqrt{\lambda_2} \,\phi_0 = \phi_0$

## Context

This review extends the weak-coupling approach to color superconductivity originally developed from QCD perturbation theory, building on the single-gluon exchange mechanism in the color-antitriplet channel established by Bailin and Love (1984) and the asymptotic freedom property of QCD. The work solves the gap equation up to subleading order for multiple phases, confirming and extending prior results for the 2SC phase gap exponent from Son (1999), Schafer and Wilczek, Pisarski and Rischke (2000), Hong, and Miransky. The Debye and Meissner mass calculations for spin-1 phases by Schmitt and collaborators are presented for the first time, and the gauge parameter independence proof addresses the gauge-dependence issue raised by Miransky and Hong (2003). These results enable future work on whether spin-1 gaps might be preferred in charge-neutral systems such as compact stellar objects, where a spin-one color superconductor would behave as an electromagnetic superconductor of type I rather than the type II behavior commonly believed for ordinary neutron stars.
