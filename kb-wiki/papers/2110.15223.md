# Godunov variables and convex entropy for relativistic fluid dynamics with bulk viscosity

> Based on the conservation-dissipation formalism proposed by Zhu and collaborators we formulate a general version of the Israel-Stewart theory for relativistic fluid dynamics with bulk viscosity. Our generalization consists in allowing for a wide range of dependence of the entropy density on the bulk viscosity. We show the existence of Godunov-Boillat variables for this model. By known properties of systems possessing such variables, this provides an alternative proof of the recently established existence of solutions for the Israel-Stewart theory locally in time, and a proof that entropy production is positive across weak Lax shocks.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a generalized version of the Israel-Stewart theory for relativistic fluid dynamics with bulk viscosity, constructed via the conservation-dissipation formalism, possesses Godunov-Boillat variables, provided the entropy density is a strictly concave function of its arguments and two additional technical conditions on the thermodynamic derivatives hold.

## Method

The author constructs a generalized Israel-Stewart model by introducing a single dynamic non-equilibrium variable conjugate to the bulk viscosity, allowing the entropy density to depend on this variable in a general way. The equations of motion are then cast into divergence form, and the existence of Godunov-Boillat variables is proven by verifying that the system satisfies the conditions for a causal covariant Godunov-Boillat system, following the framework of Ruggeri and Strumia (1981). The proof involves computing the Hessian of a potential function and showing its definiteness under the stated concavity and inequality conditions.

## Key numbers

* The field manifold is defined as Omega = {U = (u^T, e, nu, C)^T in R^3 x (0, infinity)^3}, where u is the 3-velocity, e the internal energy per particle, nu the specific volume, and C the non-equilibrium variable.
* The condition for strict concavity of the free enthalpy g(theta, p, pi) is required, where theta is temperature, p equilibrium pressure, and pi bulk viscous pressure.
* Two additional inequalities must hold on Omega: 1 + (nu^2/f) (partial p / partial nu)|_{theta,C} >= 0 and 1 + (nu^2/f) ( - (partial pi / partial C)|_{theta,p} + (partial p / partial nu)|_{theta,pi} + 2 (partial pi / partial nu)|_{theta,p} ) >= 0, where f = e + nu(p + pi).

## Context

This work extends the conservation-dissipation formalism of Zhu, Hong, Yang, and Yong (2015) to the relativistic setting, providing a generalization of the standard Israel-Stewart theory for bulk viscosity. The standard Israel-Stewart model is recovered as the special case where the entropy density takes the quadratic form s(e,nu,C) = s^{eq}(e,nu) - C^2/(2 theta^{eq}(e,nu)). The existence of Godunov-Boillat variables provides an alternative proof of local-in-time existence of solutions, previously established by Bemfica, Disconzi, and Noronha (2019) using symmetric hyperbolicity, and also yields positivity of entropy production across weak Lax shocks. This enables further mathematical analysis of shock solutions in dissipative relativistic fluids.
