# The χ-BS(3) approach

## Key claim

A combined chiral and 1/N_c expansion of the Bethe-Salpeter interaction kernel, truncated to chiral order Q^3 and leading order in 1/N_c, yields a good description of kaon-nucleon, antikaon-nucleon, and pion-nucleon scattering data up to laboratory momenta of p_lab ~ 500 MeV, but quantitative agreement should not be expected above sqrt(s) > m_N + 2 m_pi ~ 1215 MeV where inelastic pion production (not included) begins.

## Method

The authors solve the covariant on-shell reduced coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with an interaction kernel evaluated in a combined chiral and 1/N_c expansion, including terms up to chiral order Q^3 and leading order in 1/N_c. An explicit s-channel baryon nonet term with J^P = 3/2^- is included in the kernel to account for inelastic channels not explicitly treated, such as K Delta_mu or K_mu N. A renormalization program matching subthreshold amplitudes ensures approximate crossing symmetry, and a systematic on-shell reduction avoids unphysical dependence on the choice of chiral coordinates or interpolating fields.

## Key numbers

- The approach describes scattering data up to laboratory momenta of p_lab ~ 500 MeV.
- At subleading order Q^2, chiral SU(3) Lagrangian predicts 12 unknown parameters; large N_c sum rules reduce this to 5 parameters.
- At order Q^3, 23 parameters describe symmetry-breaking effects in 3-point meson-baryon vertices; the 1/N_c expansion reduces this to 10 parameters at leading order.
- Pion-nucleon phase shifts are well reproduced up to sqrt(s) ~ 1300 MeV, with the exception of the S_11 phase.
- The inelastic threshold for pion production is at sqrt(s) > m_N + 2 m_pi ~ 1215 MeV.
- Resonance-like structures appear in S_11 and P_11 channels around sqrt(s) ~ 1500 MeV.
- For K^-p scattering, empirical ratios A_n/A_0 with n >= 3 are compatible with zero for p_lab < 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel SU(3) approach of Kaiser, Waas, and Weise (1997) and Caro Ramon et al. (2000) by incorporating a systematic 1/N_c expansion to reduce the number of free parameters at chiral orders Q^2 and Q^3. The approach differs significantly from Caro Ramon et al. (2000), which applied coupled SU(3) channels to pion-induced eta and kaon production at much higher energies (sqrt(s) ~ 1486 MeV and ~ 1695 MeV), where the authors argue more inelastic channels are needed. The method enables a controlled non-perturbative treatment of meson-baryon scattering that generates baryon resonances (except those belonging to large N_c ground states) from coupled-channel dynamics, and can be extended to include further inelastic channels and higher partial waves.
