# An effective field theory for coupled-channel scattering

> The problem of describing low-energy two-body scattering for systems with two open channels with different thresholds is addressed in the context of an effective field theory. In particular, the problem where the threshold is unnaturally small and the cross section at low energy is unnaturally large is considered. It is shown that the lowest-order point coupling associated with the mixing of the channels scales as $Λ^{-2}$ rather than $Λ^{-1}$ (the scaling of the same-channel coupling and the scaling in a single-channel case) where $Λ$ is the ultraviolet cutoff. The renormalization of the theory at lowest order is given explicitly. The treatment of higher orders is straightforward. The potential implications for systems with deep open channels are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for low-energy two-body scattering with two open channels having an unnaturally small threshold and unnaturally large cross sections, the lowest-order point coupling that mixes the channels scales as Λ⁻² (where Λ is the ultraviolet cutoff), rather than Λ⁻¹ as in the same-channel coupling or the single-channel case.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory (EFT) for nonrelativistic two-body s-wave scattering with short-range forces, where the system has two open channels with different thresholds. They regularize the Lippmann-Schwinger equation using a sharp momentum cutoff Λ, solve analytically for the leading-order T-matrix by iterating momentum-independent contact interactions, and determine the renormalization-group flow of the three bare coupling constants (c₁₁, c₂₂, c₁₂) by requiring cutoff independence of the T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The mixing coupling constant c₁₂ scales as Λ⁻², while the same-channel couplings c₁₁ and c₂₂ scale as Λ⁻¹ (the same as the single-channel case)
- The leading-order coupled-channel s-wave T-matrix is expressed in terms of three renormalized constants: a₁₁, a₂₂, and a₁₂
- When the second channel is closed (E < Δ), the effective range r_eff is of order 1/√(μ₂Δ) ≫ 1/M, where M ∼ 1/R is the typical scale of short-range interactions
- When the second channel is open and below the initial channel (Δ negative), the effective range r_eff is approximately imaginary and small: r_eff ≃ i/(a₁₂² (2μ₂|Δ|)^(3/2))

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT for single-channel scattering with unnatural scattering lengths (van Kolck, 1997; Bedaque and van Kolck, 2002) to the coupled-channel case with two unnaturally small scales. The resulting T-matrix reproduces the standard multi-channel generalization of the effective range expansion. The treatment can be extended to higher orders and more channels, and provides a framework for describing systems such as nucleon-nucleus scattering with low-lying nuclear excitations and potentially p-anti-p scattering with annihilation channels.
