# Spin observables in the $pn \to p Λ$ reaction

> The T matrix of the LambdaN-> NN reaction, which is a strangeness changing weak process, is derived. The explicit formulas of the spin observables are given for s-wave p-Lambda final states which kinematically corresponds to inverse reaction of the weak nonmesonic decay of Lambda hypernuclei. One can study interferences between amplitudes of parity- conserving and violating, spin- singlet and triplet and isospin- singlet and triplet. Most of them are not available in the study of the nonmesonic decay. They clarify structure of the reaction and constrain strongly theoretical models for weak hyperon nucleon interaction.

# Spin observables in the $pn \to p \Lambda$ reaction

## Key claim
The paper derives explicit formulas for spin observables in the $pn \to p \Lambda$ reaction, restricted to s-wave $p \Lambda$ final states (corresponding to proton kinetic energy of approximately 400 MeV), and argues that these observables can constrain theoretical models of the weak hyperon-nucleon interaction more cleanly than nonmesonic decay studies because they are less affected by nuclear structure and final-state interaction effects.

## Method
The authors construct the T-matrix for the $pn \to p \Lambda$ reaction assuming rotational invariance in the center-of-mass system, then restrict to s-wave production for the $p \Lambda$ final state (maximum angular momentum J=1). They express the spin structure using six well-known amplitudes (a, b, c, d, e, f) analogous to those used by Block and Dalitz for nonmesonic decay, and derive formulas for the differential cross section and five spin observables (A_p, A_Lambda, A_{pLambda}^T, A_{pLambda}^L, A_{pLambda}^{T'}) in terms of these amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- The Q value of the reaction is 176 MeV (mass difference between neutron and Lambda), requiring 369 MeV proton kinetic energy for a free neutron target
- The observed nonmesonic decay rate gives a cross section of approximately $10^{-39} \text{ cm}^2$ at about 10 MeV above threshold (E_p ~ 400 MeV)
- Theoretically calculated cross sections vary by an order of magnitude, from $10^{-39}$ to $10^{-40} \text{ cm}^2$, depending on the model used
- The asymmetry parameter of the $\Lambda \to p \pi^-$ decay used for Lambda polarization measurement is $\alpha_- = 0.642 \pm 0.013$
- The reaction can be studied up to proton energy around 680 MeV, where Lambda and kaon pair production becomes possible

## Context
This work extends the study of the weak strangeness-changing baryon-baryon interaction beyond the nonmesonic decay of Lambda hypernuclei, which has been limited by nuclear structure effects and final-state interactions as discussed by Ramos, Oset, and others. The inverse $pn \to p \Lambda$ reaction was previously proposed by Kishimoto, Oka, and collaborators as a cleaner probe. The paper provides the formal framework for extracting interference terms between parity-conserving and parity-violating amplitudes, spin-singlet and triplet states, and isospin-singlet and triplet states, which are mostly inaccessible in nonmesonic decay studies. The results enable future experiments using polarized proton beams and Lambda polarization detection to constrain meson-exchange models (which struggle to simultaneously reproduce transition rates and branching ratios as noted by Bhang) and quark-exchange models (Kisslinger, Oka, Inoue) of the weak hyperon-nucleon interaction.
