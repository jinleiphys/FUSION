# Representations in Density Dependent Hadronic Field Theory and compatibility with QCD sum-rules

> Different representations of an effective, covariant theory of the hadronic interaction are examined. For this purpose we have introduced nucleon-meson vertices parametrized in terms of scalar combinations of hadronic fields, extending the conceptual frame of the Density Dependent Hadronic Field Theory. Nuclear matter properties at zero temperature are examined in the Mean Field Approximation, including the equation of state, the Landau parameters, and collective modes. The treatment of isospin channels in terms of QCD sum rules inputs is outlined.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that different field-parameterizations of the nucleon-meson vertices within Density Dependent Hadronic Field Theory (DDHFT) yield nearly indistinguishable predictions for the binding energy and pressure of symmetric nuclear matter for a given input self-energy, despite the formal differences in the vertex definitions. However, the Landau parameter \(f_1\) and the zero-sound dispersion relation do depend on the chosen representation, meaning the field parameterization matters for collective-mode predictions.

## Method

The author extends DDHFT by introducing nucleon-meson vertices parametrized in terms of scalar combinations of hadronic fields (baryon density, scalar density, meson mean fields) and examines three distinct parameterizations (cases a, b, c) for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. Nuclear matter properties at zero temperature are computed in the Mean Field Approximation (MFA), using as input either Dirac-Brueckner self-energies from Typel (2001) or QCD sum-rule self-energies from Drukarev (1999). The vertices are obtained either by algebraic relations (standard DDHFT) or by solving differential equations that enforce equality between the model self-energies and the input self-energies.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (used as reference density throughout).
- Binding energy minimum requirement: \(E_B \simeq -16\ \text{MeV}\) at \(n_0\) for phenomenological nuclear matter.
- Using a fitted parameterization with \(\alpha_s = -0.278\), \(\alpha_v = -2.93\), and \(\lambda = 0.697\), the model yields a binding energy of \(-14\ \text{MeV}\) at \(n \simeq 1.1\, n_0\).
- The QCD sum-rule input from Drukarev (1999) gives \(\alpha_s = -2.309\), \(\alpha_v = -0.66\), and \(\lambda = -0.261\).
- Nuclear compressibility obtained: \(K \simeq 440\ \text{MeV}\) at normal density (empirical range: 230–270 MeV).
- Monopole mode energy coefficient: \(\alpha_0 \simeq 145\ \text{MeV}\) (empirical: 80 MeV).
- Quadrupole mode energy coefficient: \(\alpha_2 \simeq 77\ \text{MeV}\) (empirical: 63 MeV).
- Zero-sound threshold densities: \(n/n_0 = 1.25\) for approach (b) and \(n/n_0 = 2\) for the standard DDHFT treatment.

## Context

This work extends the DDHFT framework previously developed by Toki (1991), Haddad (1995), Lenske (1998), and Typel (2001), which was originally designed to incorporate Dirac-Brueckner self-energies into a covariant hadronic field model. The author had previously introduced meson-dependent vertices for symmetric matter in Aguirre (2004); here the formalism is generalized to isospin-asymmetric matter and systematically compared with QCD sum-rule inputs from Drukarev (1999). The results enable future studies of neutron-star structure and collective modes in dense matter, while also highlighting that the choice of vertex representation affects predictions for quadrupole excitations and zero-sound propagation, even when the equation of state is unchanged.
