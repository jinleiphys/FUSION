# Antibaryons in massive heavy ion reactions: Importance of potentials

> In the framework of RQMD we investigate antiproton observables in massive heavy ion collisions at AGS energies and compare to preliminary results of the E878 collaboration. We focus here on the considerable influence of the *real* part of an antinucleon--nucleus optical potential on the antiproton momentum spectra.

## Key claim

The real part of the antinucleon-nucleus optical potential, applied only after freeze-out in a cascade calculation, substantially fills the dip at low transverse momentum in antiproton spectra from central Au+Au collisions at 10.7 GeV, bringing the RQMD simulation into better agreement with preliminary E878 data; the authors caution that this simple model interaction is far from unique and that a self-consistent treatment of real and imaginary parts is still needed.

## Method

The authors used the RQMD (Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model in pure cascade mode (nucleon-nucleon potentials switched off) to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 10.7 GeV. Antiprotons were propagated with the usual cascade until their last strong interaction, after which their trajectories were calculated under the influence of a relativistic optical potential derived from Dirac equation optical model parameters for proton-nucleus scattering, transformed via G-parity to the antiproton case. The real part of the Schrödinger equivalent potential was constructed from Lorentz-scalar and Lorentz-vector Yukawa form factors with a cutoff distance of 1.5 fm, corresponding to an averaged annihilation cross section of about 70 mb.

## Key numbers

- The Schrödinger equivalent potential for an antiproton in nuclear matter at groundstate density is about -250 MeV at \(p_{\bar p}=0\) GeV/c, increasing to -170 MeV at \(p_{\bar p}=1\) GeV/c.
- The actual averaged SE-potential of antiprotons at freeze-out in central Au+Au collisions at 10.7 GeV is about -70 MeV.
- The Coulomb potential for antiprotons in the same system is less than -10 MeV (though for pions with \(p_t<100\) MeV the \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio increases by 25 percent due to Coulomb effects).
- The \(p_t\)-integrated antiproton spectrum at midrapidity is affected by less than 25 percent when the potential interaction is included compared to the pure cascade calculation.
- The mass parameters used for the Yukawa form factors are \(\mu_V=3.952\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(\mu_S=2.787\) fm\(^{-1}\); the coupling constants are \(g_V=13.5\) MeV·fm and \(g_S=10.9\) MeV·fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of mean-field effects on particle spectra by V. Koch (1991) and Shu (1991) to antiproton observables in nucleus-nucleus collisions, specifically addressing the influence of the real part of the antinucleon-nucleus optical potential. The approach is similar in spirit to the usual treatment of Coulomb distortion of particle spectra as in Gyulassy (1981). The paper enables future self-consistent transport calculations that treat both the real and imaginary parts of the \(\overline N N\) potential simultaneously, as the authors note that absorption strength differs by orders of magnitude among different transport calculations.
