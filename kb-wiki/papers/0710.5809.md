# Spectral properties of massless and massive quarks coupled with massive boson at finite temperature

> We study the properties of massless and massive quarks coupled with a scalar and pseudoscalar boson at finite temperature in Yukawa models at the one-loop order. The behavior of the spectral function and the pole structure of the propagator are analyzed as functions of temperature $T$ and the quark mass $m_f$. It is shown that the three-peak structure of the spectral function found in a previous work for massless quarks is formed at temperatures comparable to the boson mass even for finite $m_f$, but gradually ceases to exist as $m_f$ becomes larger. We identify the three poles of the quark propagator corresponding to the collective excitations of the quark in the complex energy plane. It is shown that the three trajectories made by the poles along with a variation of $T$ undergo a structural rearrangement at a critical quark mass when $m_f$ is increased. This suggests that the physics content of the collective quark excitations is changed in a drastic way at this point. The results are nicely accounted for with the notion of the level mixing induced by a resonant scattering of the massive boson with quarks and holes of thermally excited anti-quarks.

## Key claim

The three-peak structure in the quark spectral function, previously found for massless quarks, persists at finite quark mass \(m_f\) for temperatures comparable to the boson mass, but gradually disappears as \(m_f\) increases; the trajectories of the three corresponding poles in the complex energy plane undergo a structural rearrangement at a critical quark mass \(m_f/m_b \simeq 0.21\), indicating a qualitative change in the nature of the collective quark excitations.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop retarded quark self-energy at finite temperature in Yukawa models with a massive scalar or pseudoscalar boson, using the imaginary-time formalism and analytic continuation. They analyzed the quark spectral function \(\rho_+(\omega)\) at zero momentum and solved the pole equation \(z - m_f - \Sigma_+(z) = 0\) in the complex energy plane, tracking the three physically relevant poles as functions of temperature \(T\) and quark mass \(m_f\). The ultraviolet divergence in the \(T=0\) part was removed via on-shell renormalization, and the \(T\)-dependent part was obtained using the Kramers-Kronig relation.

## Key numbers

* The three-peak structure in the spectral function is clearly visible for \(m_f/m_b = 0.1\) at \(\tau \equiv T/m_b \simeq 1\), but is hardly seen for \(m_f/m_b = 0.2\) and absent for \(m_f/m_b = 0.3\).
* The critical quark mass at which the pole trajectories undergo a structural rearrangement is \(m_f/m_b \simeq 0.21\).
* For \(m_f = 0\), the residues of the three poles (A), (B), and (C) become equal in magnitude at \(\tau \simeq 1.2\).
* The sum of the real parts of the three residues, \(Z_{\rm Tot}\), remains close to unity (within about 20%) over the entire temperature range studied for \(m_f = 0\).
* For \(m_f = 0\), the poles (B) and (C) reach the real axis at \(\omega = m_b\) when \(\tau \simeq 4.2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kitazawa, Kunihiro, Nemoto, 2006) which found a three-peak structure in the quark spectral function for massless quarks coupled to massive bosons, motivated by the possible existence of hadronic excitations (soft modes) near the QCD critical temperature. The present paper examines how a finite Dirac quark mass modifies this structure, showing that the three-peak phenomenon is robust only for small masses. The results enable future studies of quark quasi-particle properties in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma near \(T_c\), where both current and dynamical quark masses may be non-negligible.
