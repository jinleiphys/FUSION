# On the Lorentz structure of the symmetry energy

> We investigate in detail the density dependence of the symmetry energy in a relativistic description by decomposing the iso-vector mean field into contributions with different Lorentz covariant properties. We find important effects of the iso-vector, scalar channel (i.e. $δ$-meson like) on the high density behavior of the symmetry energy. Applications to static properties of finite nuclei and to dynamic situations of heavy ion collisions are explored and related to each other. The nuclear structure studies show only moderate effects originating from the virtual $δ$ meson. At variance, in heavy ion collisions one finds important contributions on the reaction dynamics arising from the different Lorentz structure of the high density symmetry energy when a scalar iso-vector $δ$ field is introduced. Particularly interesting is the related neutron/proton effective mass splitting for nucleon transport effects and for resonance and particle production around the threshold. We show that the $δ$-like channel turns out to be essential for the production of pions, when comparing with experimental data, in particular for high momentum selections.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the scalar isovector channel (δ-meson-like) has only moderate effects on static properties of finite nuclei, but produces important contributions to the dynamics of heavy ion collisions through the splitting of neutron and proton effective masses, which is essential for reproducing experimental pion production data, particularly at high momentum selections.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using both constant-coupling nonlinear RMF models (NLρ, NLρδ, NL3) and density-dependent hadronic (DDH) models with various parametrizations of the isovector channel. They solved the Dirac equation for nucleons coupled to classical meson fields (σ, ω, ρ, δ) self-consistently for finite nuclei and used a relativistic transport model (RQMD) to simulate heavy ion collisions, comparing charged pion yields with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation: a₄ = 30.5 MeV for NLρ and NLρδ models; a₄ = 37.4 MeV for NL3; a₄ ≈ 33.4 MeV for DDH models
- Saturation density: ρ_sat = 0.148 fm⁻³ for NL models; ρ_sat = 0.153 fm⁻³ for DDH models
- Nucleon effective mass at saturation: m*_N = 0.6 M for NL models; m*_N = 0.55 M for DDH models
- Compressibility: K_NM = 271.7 MeV for NL models; K_NM = 240.0 MeV for DDH models
- Meson masses: m_σ ≈ 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 769 MeV (763 MeV in VDD models), m_δ = 980 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: 16 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean field studies of symmetric nuclear matter (Serot and Walecka, 1986; Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990) into the isovector channel, building on earlier suggestions that the δ-meson contribution could be important (Liu et al., 2002; Greco et al., 2003). The paper contrasts with non-relativistic approaches (Bao-An Li, 2003) that did not include isospin effects in the momentum-dependent part of effective forces. The findings enable future experimental studies with radioactive beams at facilities like GSI to probe the high-density symmetry energy through pion production measurements.
