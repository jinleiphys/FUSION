# Collision energy dependence of elliptic flow splitting between particles and their antiparticles from an extended multiphase transport model

> Based on an extended multiphase transport model, which includes mean-field potentials in both the partonic and hadronic phases, uses the mix-event coalescence, and respects charge conservation during the hadronic evolution, we have studied the collision energy dependence of the elliptic flow splitting between particles and their antiparticles. This extended transport model reproduces reasonably well the experimental data at lower collision energies but only describes qualitatively the elliptic flow splitting at higher beam energies. The present study thus indicates the existence of other mechanisms for the elliptic flow splitting besides the mean-field potentials and the need of further improvements of the multiphase transport model.

## Key claim

The extended multiphase transport model, which includes mean-field potentials in both the partonic and hadronic phases, reproduces the elliptic flow splitting between particles and antiparticles reasonably well at lower collision energies but only qualitatively at higher beam energies, indicating that other mechanisms beyond mean-field potentials are needed.

## Method

The authors extended the string melting version of the AMPT model by incorporating mean-field potentials for partons from a 3-flavor NJL model and for hadrons from relativistic mean-field theory and chiral effective Lagrangians. They used a mixed-event coalescence algorithm for hadronization and corrected charge conservation in the hadronic phase, then simulated Au+Au collisions at RHIC-BES energies from 7.7 to 39 GeV, fitting the parton scattering cross section to reproduce experimental charged particle elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- Parton scattering cross sections used to fit charged particle elliptic flow: 1 mb at 7.7 GeV, 3 mb at 11.5 GeV, 5 mb at 19.6 GeV, 8 mb at 27 GeV, and 10 mb at 39 GeV
- At saturation density (0.16 fm^-3), nucleon potential is about -60 MeV while antinucleon potential is about -260 MeV
- At saturation density, K+ potential is about +20 MeV while K- potential is about -125 MeV
- The vector coupling strength ratio R_V = G_V/G is constrained between 0.5 and 1.1 by comparing to experimental relative v2 difference data at 7.7 GeV
- Hadron formation time of 0.7 fm/c introduced in the AMPT model

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Xu 2014) that reproduced elliptic flow splitting at only 7.7 GeV by adding mixed-event coalescence and charge conservation to the AMPT model. The study builds on prior theoretical work attributing v2 splitting to transported versus produced quarks (Dunlop 2011, Greiner 2012, Ivanov 2013), hydrodynamic evolution at finite baryon chemical potential (Steinheimer 2012, Hatta 2015), and radial flow differences (Sun 2015). The results indicate that at higher beam energies, additional mechanisms beyond mean-field potentials are required, and the model can be improved by modifying the coalescence algorithm, including isovector coupling, and better treating baryon-antibaryon annihilation.
