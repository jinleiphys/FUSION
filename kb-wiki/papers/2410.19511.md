# Recent advances in coupled cluster computations of open-shell atomic nuclei

> In this contribution, we report on recent progress in coupled-cluster simulations of open-shell atomic nuclei using interactions consistently derived from chiral effective field theory. In particular, we compare different coupled-cluster approaches by computing binding energies and electric dipole polarizabilities in medium-mass calcium isotopes.

# Recent advances in coupled cluster computations of open-shell atomic nuclei

## Key claim
The equation-of-motion (EOM) coupled-cluster approach extended to two-particle-removed (2PR) nuclei yields binding energies and electric dipole polarizabilities in medium-mass calcium isotopes that are consistent with other coupled-cluster variants, though the 2PR results are preliminary and lack full uncertainty quantification. The authors note that approximate triples corrections may ultimately be needed to reach excellent agreement with experimental measurements.

## Method
The authors compare four coupled-cluster (CC) formulations for open-shell nuclei: single-reference CCSD on symmetry-unrestricted reference states, Bogoliubov CCSD (BCCSD) which breaks particle-number conservation, and two equations-of-motion (EOM) approaches, two-particle-removed (2PR) and two-particle-attached (2PA), which treat open-shell nuclei as excitations of a closed-shell neighbor. Binding energies are computed using the 1.8/2.0 (EM) chiral interaction in a harmonic oscillator basis with Nmax=12 and ħω=16 MeV, while electric dipole polarizabilities are obtained from the dipole response via the Lorentz integral transform using the ΔNNLOGO(394) potential.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies of even-mass Ca isotopes (A=38, 40, 42, 44, 46, 48, 50) computed with 2PR-CC, 2PA-CC, BCCSD, and CCSD using the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction at Nmax=12, ħω=16 MeV, with three-body matrix elements up to E_max^(3)=16
- Electric dipole polarizabilities α_D in Ca isotopes computed with 2PR-CC (preliminary, Nmax=12, ħω=16 MeV) compared to 2PA-CC and closed-shell CC results from Bonaiti (2024) using the ΔNNLOGO(394) potential
- 2PR and 2PA results are compatible in 38Ca and 50Ca within the assessed 2PA uncertainties

## Context
This work extends the EOM coupled-cluster formalism to two-particle-removed nuclei, building on prior developments for two-particle-attached systems by Jansen (2011) and Bonaiti (2024), and on Bogoliubov CC approaches by Signoracci (2015) and Tichai (2024). The benchmark comparisons between CC variants serve as a consistency check for the theory, and the authors plan to perform full-scale computations in both even and odd nuclei to obtain a more comprehensive description of medium-mass nuclei. The dipole polarizability results connect to nuclear matter properties such as the symmetry energy, relevant for astrophysical phenomena as discussed by Rocamaza (2018).
