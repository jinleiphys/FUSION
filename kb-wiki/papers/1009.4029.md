# Momentum distributions from three-body decaying 9Be and 9B resonances

> The complex-rotated hyperspherical adiabatic method is used to study the decay of low-lying $^9$Be and $^9$B resonances into $α$, $α$ and $n$ or $p$. We consider six low-lying resonances of $^9$Be ($1/2^\pm$, $3/2^\pm$ and $5/2^\pm$) and one resonance of $^9$B ($5/2^-$) to compare with. The properties of the resonances at large distances are decisive for the momentum distributions of the three decaying fragments. Systematic detailed energy correlations of Dalitz plots are presented.

## Key claim

The momentum distributions of fragments from three-body decaying \(^9\)Be and \(^9\)B resonances are determined primarily by the resonance properties at large distances, and the Dalitz plots for the \(5/2^-\) states of the two mirror nuclei are almost indistinguishable despite the additional Coulomb interaction between the \(\alpha\)-particle and the proton in \(^9\)B.

## Method

The complex-rotated hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method was used to solve the Faddeev equations for the three-body system (\(\alpha+\alpha+n\) or \(\alpha+\alpha+p\)). The angular part of the Hamiltonian was solved at fixed hyperradius to obtain adiabatic potentials, and the hyperradial wave functions were then computed from coupled equations. A three-body short-range potential was included to adjust the small-distance part and reproduce the correct resonance energies, while the Ali-Bodmer \(\alpha-\alpha\) potential and the \(\alpha\)-nucleon interaction from Cobis et al. (1997) were used for the two-body interactions.

## Key numbers

* \(^9\)Be \(5/2^-\) resonance at 2.43 MeV excitation energy: Dalitz plot shows no zero-probability points, with density increasing toward higher \(\alpha\) energies due to Coulomb repulsion.
* \(^9\)Be \(1/2^-\) resonance at 2.82 MeV: Dalitz plot exhibits more structure with zero-probability points or curves.
* \(^9\)Be \(5/2^+\) resonance at 3.03 MeV: Dalitz plot shows structure and zero-probability points or curves.
* \(^9\)Be \(3/2^+\) resonance at 4.69 MeV: Dalitz plot shows structure and zero-probability points or curves.
* \(^9\)Be \(3/2^-\) resonance at 5.59 MeV: Dalitz plot is very similar to the \(5/2^-\) case, with no zeroes and density increasing toward higher \(\alpha\) energies.
* \(^9\)B \(5/2^-\) resonance at 2.34 MeV: Dalitz plot is almost indistinguishable from that of the \(^9\)Be \(5/2^-\) resonance at 2.43 MeV.
* Maximum possible energy for \(\alpha\)-particles is \(5/9 E_{res}\); for neutrons it is \(8/9 E_{res}\).

## Context

This work extends the cluster-model description of \(^9\)Be and its mirror \(^9\)B, previously studied by Alvarez-Rodriguez et al. (2008) and others, by providing systematic two-dimensional energy correlations (Dalitz plots) for six low-lying resonances of \(^9\)Be and one of \(^9\)B. The results are directly comparable to experimental distributions and enable tests of the decay mechanisms (sequential vs. direct) for these resonances, particularly the astrophysically important \(5/2^-\) state. The approach builds on the complex-scaled hyperspherical adiabatic method developed by Nielsen et al. (2001) and Fedorov et al. (2004).
