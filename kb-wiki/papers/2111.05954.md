# Fixed-Point Few-Body Hamiltonians in Quantum Mechanics

> We revisited how Weinberg's ideas in Nuclear Physics influenced our own work and lead to a renormalization group invariant framework within the quantum mechanical few-body problem, and we also update the discussion on the relevant scales in the limit of short-range interactions. In this context, it is revised the formulation of the subtracted scattering equations and fixed-point Hamiltonians applied to few-body systems, in which the original interaction contains point-like singularities, such as Dirac-delta and/or its derivatives. The approach is being illustrated by considering two-nucleons described by singular interactions. This revision also includes an extension of the renormalization formalism to three-body systems, which is followed by an updated discussion on the applications to four particles.

## Key claim

The paper revisits how Weinberg's ideas on effective chiral Lagrangians led to a renormalization group invariant framework for few-body quantum mechanics, showing that fixed-point Hamiltonians exist for singular interactions (Dirac-delta and its derivatives) and that the subtraction point can slide without affecting physical results, provided the Callan-Symanzik equation is satisfied.

## Method

The authors formulate subtracted Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the T-matrix, where the number of subtractions n is chosen to regularize divergent integrals from singular potentials. They derive the fixed-point renormalized Hamiltonian H_R = H_0 + V_R from the n-th order subtracted equation, showing that V_R is independent of the subtraction scale mu^2. The method is illustrated by diagonalizing the renormalized Hamiltonian with a momentum cutoff Lambda for a Yukawa-plus-Dirac-delta potential, and by constructing explicit renormalized interactions for a four-term-singular bare potential and for the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP) with contact terms.

## Key numbers

* Three bound-state energies from a reference potential (in inverse-squared length units): epsilon^(0) = -2.3822, epsilon^(1) = -0.20297, epsilon^(2) = -0.020643; these are reproduced exactly in the Lambda -> infinity limit by the renormalized Hamiltonian with a Dirac-delta plus Yukawa interaction, independent of the subtraction point choice (mu^2 = 2.3822, 0.2088, or 0.02217).
* For the OPEP application: g_a = 1.25 (axial coupling constant), f_pi = 93 MeV (pion weak-decay constant), m_pi = 138 MeV (pion mass); the mixing parameter for the ^3S_1-^3D_1 channel converges in the mu -> infinity limit with one subtraction and the triplet scattering length fixed.
* For the four-term-singular potential, three subtractions (n=3) are required to regularize the LS equation, with the renormalized strengths lambda_R00, lambda_R10, lambda_R11 fixed at a reference energy -overline(mu)^2.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach to quantum mechanics pioneered by Wilson (1970, 1971, 1974, 1983) and the subtracted scattering formalism developed by Frederico (2000). It builds directly on Weinberg's chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces (1990, 1991, 1992) and the non-perturbative renormalization scheme for singular interactions proposed by Frederico (2000). The method enables systematic renormalization of few-body systems with contact interactions, including three-body Faddeev equations and four-particle systems where a new scale appears, and has been applied to chiral nuclear forces by Timoteo (2005, 2007, 2011) and others.
