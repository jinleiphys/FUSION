# Pinning down the mechanism of neutrinoless double beta decay with measurements in different nuclei

> A measurement of neutrinoless double beta decay in one isotope does not allow to determine the underlying physics mechanism. We discuss the discrimination of mechanisms for neutrinoless double beta decay by comparing ratios of half life measurements for different isotopes. Six prominent examples for specific new physics contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay are analyzed. We find that the change in corresponding ratios of half lives varies from 60% for supersymmetric models up to a factor of 5-20 for extra-dimensional and left-right-symmetric mechanisms.

## Key claim

A measurement of neutrinoless double beta decay in a single isotope cannot determine the underlying physics mechanism, but comparing ratios of half-life measurements across different isotopes can discriminate between mechanisms because the new physics parameter cancels in the ratio, assuming one mechanism dominates and interference is neglected.

## Method

The authors calculated half-life ratios for six prominent new physics mechanisms (light Majorana neutrino exchange, SUSY-accompanied neutrino exchange, gluino exchange in R-parity violating SUSY, two types of right-handed currents in left-right symmetric models, and Kaluza-Klein neutrino exchange in extra-dimensional models) normalized to the half-life of ⁷⁶Ge. They used nuclear matrix elements from the pn-QRPA approach, with the SUSY-accompanied mechanism computed for the first time in that framework, and adopted existing numerical values for the other mechanisms from the literature.

## Key numbers

- For SUSY mechanisms, the half-life ratio between ⁸²Se and ¹³⁶Xe varies by 60% compared to the mass mechanism.
- For the left-right symmetric λλ contribution, a comparison of measurements in ¹²⁸Te and ¹⁵⁰Nd yields a variation of more than a factor of 20.
- For extra-dimensional models with a large brane shift parameter (a = 10 GeV⁻¹), the half-life ratios for ¹⁵⁰Nd and ¹⁰⁰Mo vary by more than a factor of 5.
- For the left-right symmetric ηη contribution, a comparison of half-life ratios between ¹⁰⁰Mo and ¹³⁶Xe yields a variation of 70%.
- The half-life ratio for the mass mechanism (ℛ^{m_ν}) for ¹²⁸Te relative to ⁷⁶Ge is 3.26; for the λλ contribution it is 18.40.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of mechanism discrimination in neutrinoless double beta decay beyond earlier proposals such as angular correlation analysis (Doi, 1982) and comparative studies of 0νββ and 0νβ⁺/EC decay (Hirsch, 1994). The authors note that their approach can be complemented by neutrino mass searches in Tritium beta decay and cosmology, as well as searches for lepton flavor violating decays (Cirigliano, 2004). The paper enables future experimental programs to design multi-isotope measurement strategies, with next-generation experiments already proposed for ⁷⁶Ge (GERDA, MAJORANA), ⁸²Se (Super-NEMO), ¹⁰⁰Mo (MOON), ¹³⁰Te (CUORE), and ¹³⁶Xe (EXO, XMASS).
