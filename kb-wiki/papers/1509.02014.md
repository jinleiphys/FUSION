# Charge-exchange resonances and restoration of the Wigner SU(4)-symmetry in heavy and superheavy nuclei

> Energies of the giant Gamow-Teller and analog resonances - $E_{\rm G}$ and $E_{\rm A}$, are presented, calculated using the microscopic theory of finite Fermi system. The calculated differences $ΔE_{\rm G-A}=E_{\rm G}-E_{\rm A}$ go to zero in heavier nuclei indicating the restoration of Wigner SU(4)-symmetry. The calculated $ΔE_{\rm G-A}$ values are in good agreement with the experimental data. The average deviation is 0.30 MeV for the 33 considered nuclei for which experimental data is available. The $ΔE_{\rm G-A}$ values were calculated for heavy and superheavy nuclei up to the mass number $A $= 290. Using the experimental data for the analog resonances energies, the isotopic dependence of the difference of the Coulomb energies of neighboring nuclei isobars analyzed within the SU(4)-approach for more than 400 nuclei in the mass number range of $A$ = 3 - 244. The Wigner SU(4)-symmetry restoration for heavy and superheavy nuclei is confirmed. It is shown that the restoration of SU(4)-symmetry does not contradict the possibility of the existence of the "island of stability" in the region of superheavy nuclei.

# Charge-exchange resonances and restoration of the Wigner SU(4)-symmetry in heavy and superheavy nuclei

## Key claim

The energy difference between the giant Gamow-Teller resonance and the analog resonance decreases to zero in heavy and superheavy nuclei, indicating restoration of Wigner SU(4)-symmetry; however, this restoration does not contradict the possible existence of the "island of stability" in superheavy nuclei, because the average spin-orbit splitting energy \(E_{ls}\) tends to a finite value and does not vanish.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) with a local nucleon-nucleon delta-interaction in the Landau-Migdal form, solving a system of secular equations for the effective field to obtain Gamow-Teller and analog resonance energies. For heavy and superheavy nuclei, they employed an approximate analytical model solution (Equation 2) that expresses the dimensionless energy difference \(y = \Delta E_{\rm G-A}/E_{ls}\) as a function of the parameter \(x = \Delta E/E_{ls}\), where \(\Delta E = (4/3)\varepsilon_F (N-Z)/A\) and \(E_{ls}\) is the average spin-orbit splitting energy parameterized as \(E_{ls} = 20N^{-1/3} + 1.25\) MeV for \(N > 80\). They also analyzed the isotopic dependence of Coulomb displacement energies for more than 400 nuclei using a two-parameter formula within the SU(4)-approach.

## Key numbers

* The average deviation between calculated and experimental \(\Delta E_{\rm G-A}\) values is 0.30 MeV for the 33 considered nuclei with available experimental data.
* For \(^{60}\)Ni, the difference between calculated and experimental \(\Delta E_{\rm G-A}\) is 0.38 MeV; for \(^{208}\)Pb, it is less than 0.10 MeV.
* The leftmost and rightmost data points in the dimensionless plot correspond to \(^{60}\)Ni with \(x = 0.52\) and \(^{208}\)Pb with \(x = 2.15\).
* For the Coulomb displacement energy analysis of more than 400 nuclei in the mass range \(A = 3-244\), the fitted parameters are \(a = 1416\) keV and \(b = -698\) keV with a standard deviation \(\delta E < 100\) keV.
* For odd-\(Z\) nuclei, the SU(4)-scheme gives \(\beta \approx 83/T_{Z}\) keV.
* Calculations were performed for heavy and superheavy nuclei up to mass number \(A = 290\), including the specific isotopes \(^{257}\)Fm, \(^{271}\)Sg, \(^{280}\)Ds, and \(^{290}\)Lv.
* The energy differences \(\Delta E_{\rm G-A}\) calculated by the full TFFS equations for four heavy nuclei differ within 0.1 MeV from those calculated by the approximate analytical formula.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier calculations from 1972-1973 (Lutostansky et al., 1972; Lutostansky et al., 1973) that first predicted the decreasing gap between Gamow-Teller and analog resonances in heavy isotopes, and the hypothesis of Wigner SU(4)-symmetry restoration. It builds on the theory of finite Fermi systems developed by Migdal (1967) and the SU(4)-supersymmetry concept of Wigner. The analysis of Coulomb displacement energies follows the approach of Gershtein et al. (2002) but extends it from nuclei with \(A < 60\) to more than 400 nuclei up to \(A = 244\). The results enable more confident application of SU(4) theory to describe heavy nuclei properties, particularly mass relations such as the Franzini-Radicatti relation, and address the question of whether SU(4)-symmetry restoration contradicts the existence of the "island of stability" in superheavy nuclei.
