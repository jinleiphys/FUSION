# Colliding poles with colliding nuclei

> In these proceedings, I will discuss collisions of poles in the complex plane as a signature of phase transitions for theories relevant to the quark gluon plasma. I will begin with an illustrative example, namely the chiral phase transition, which can be characterized by colliding poles as a function of temperature. Then, recognizing the interplay between weak and strong coupling sectors in a typical collision, I will introduce a hybrid model with a weakly broken symmetry, which has a rich quasi-hydrodynamic phenomenological description where hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic poles are unified by a common dispersion relation. I will show that energy is transferred initially from the soft to the hard sector before irreversibly transferring back to the soft sector at late times, and that the model reproduces many features common to dissipative systems with a weakly broken symmetry including the k-gap.

## Key claim

Collisions of poles in the complex plane serve as a signature of phase transitions in theories relevant to the quark-gluon plasma, including both the chiral phase transition and transitions arising from the interplay between weak and strong coupling sectors; the paper argues this pole-collision picture unifies seemingly different phenomena under a common analytic structure.

## Method

The author first analyzes the O(4) chiral phase transition model in the mean-field limit, computing the spectral density for the axial charge density-density correlator from linearized equations of motion with dissipation. Then a semiholographic model is introduced that couples a holographic (strongly coupled) sector to a dynamical boundary scalar field (weakly coupled sector) via a coupling parameter β, and the quasinormal mode spectrum is computed for both homogeneous and inhomogeneous perturbations.

## Key numbers

- For the O(4) model, the mass term changes sign as m² = m₀²(T - T_c) near the critical temperature, with the mean field condensate determined by m₀²(T - T_c)σ + λσ³ - H = 0
- In the semiholographic model, the first pole collision (diffusion pole with quasi-hydro pole) occurs at k ∼ 0.554π for β√T = 0.35
- The second pole collision (transient unstable mode with semiholographic pole) occurs at k ∼ 2.433π for β√T = 0.35
- The k-gap formation is shown for the specific case β√T = 0.15

## Context

This work extends the discussion of analytic structures in QGP descriptions from Kurkela (2019), comparing kinetic theory (with branch cuts), holography (with "Christmas tree" pole structures), and hydrodynamic theories such as Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) from Muller (1967) and Israel (1979). The O(4) model analysis builds on prior work by Grossi (2020, 2021), Florio (2021), and Soloviev (2021). The semiholographic approach follows the framework developed by Faulkner (2010), Iancu (2014), Mukhopadhyay (2015), and others, and the k-gap phenomenon connects to quasi-hydrodynamic frameworks from Grozdanov (2018) and has been observed experimentally in gallium by Khusnutdinoff (2020). The work enables future studies of quasinormal mode interactions with Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics and with branch cuts from kinetic theory near phase transitions.
