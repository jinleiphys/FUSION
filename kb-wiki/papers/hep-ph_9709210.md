# Modification of the $ϕ$-meson spectrum in nuclear matter

> The vacuum spectrum of the $ϕ$-meson is characterized by its decay into $K{\bar K} $. Modifications of the $K {\bar K}$-loops in baryonic matter change this spectrum. We calculate these in-medium modifications taking both s- and p-wave kaon-nucleon interactions into account. We use results of the in-medium $K$ and $\bar K$ spectra determined previously from a coupled channel approach based on a chiral effective Lagrangian. Altogether we find a very small shift of the $ϕ$ meson mass, by less than 10 MeV at normal nuclear matter density $ρ_0$. The in-medium decay width of the $ϕ$ meson increases such that its life time at $ρ=ρ_0$ is reduced to less than 5 fm/c. It should therefore be possible to observe medium effects in reactions such as $π^- p \to ϕn$ in heavy nuclei, where the $ϕ$ meson can be produced with small momentum.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that at normal nuclear matter density, the mass of the φ meson shifts by less than 10 MeV (less than 1% of its free mass), while its decay width increases by about an order of magnitude, reducing its lifetime to less than 5 fm/c; the abstract's hedge is that this mass shift is "very small" and the lifetime reduction is conditional on the φ meson being produced with small momentum in a heavy nucleus.

## Method

The authors calculated the in-medium φ meson self-energy by replacing the vacuum kaon propagators in the φ → K⁺K⁻ and φ → K⁰L K⁰S loop integrals with in-medium kaon propagators, using previously determined kaon spectral functions from a coupled-channel chiral SU(3) effective Lagrangian approach. Both s-wave and p-wave kaon-nucleon interactions were included, with p-wave contributions involving Λ, Σ, and Σ* intermediate states, and the resulting density-dependent φ-nucleon amplitude was used to compute the in-medium spectral function and propagator for a φ meson at rest in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The free φ meson vacuum width is 4.4 MeV, corresponding to a lifetime of about 45 fm/c.
- At normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the in-medium φ meson width reaches Γ_φ ≃ 45 MeV.
- The φ meson lifetime at ρ = ρ₀ is τ_φ ≃ 4.4 fm/c.
- The in-medium mass shift of the φ meson is less than 10 MeV at ρ = ρ₀ (about 1% of its free mass).
- The bare mass of the φ meson is set to 910 MeV.
- The charged kaon mass used is m_K = 493 MeV.
- At ρ = ρ₀, the in-medium K⁺ mass is m_K⁺⋆ ≃ 535 MeV.
- The subtraction constant c₀ = 0.11 is used to fit vacuum data.
- The strong meson coupling constant g = 6.5 and the φ strong coupling constant g_φ ≃ -14.
- The SU(3) axial coupling constants are D = 0.75 and F = 0.51, with g_A = D + F = 1.26.
- The nucleon axial form factor cutoff is Λ_A = 1.05 GeV.
- Baryon masses used: M_N = 938 MeV, M_Λ = 1.116 GeV, M_Σ = 1.191 GeV, M_Σ* = 1.383 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel chiral effective Lagrangian approach to kaon-nucleon interactions (Waas, Klingl, and Weise, 1996) by applying it to the φ meson self-energy in nuclear matter. The results are consistent with the in-medium QCD sum rule analysis of Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and Klingl, Kaiser, and Weise (1996). The predicted broadening of the φ meson at normal nuclear matter density enables experimental searches for in-medium modifications using reactions such as π⁻ p → φ n in heavy nuclei, which could be performed at GSI with the HADES detector.
