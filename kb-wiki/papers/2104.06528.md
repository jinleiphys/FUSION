# Induced Surface and Curvature Tensions Equation of State of Hadrons with Relativistic Excluded Volumes and its Relation to Morphological Thermodynamics

> An analytical formula that accurately accounts for the Lorentz contraction of the excluded volume of two relativistic hadrons with hard-core repulsion is worked out. Using the obtained expression we heuristically derive the equation of state of Boltzmann particles with relativistic excluded volumes in terms of system pressure and its surface and curvature tension coefficients. The behavior of effective excluded volumes of lightest baryons and mesons is studied at very high temperatures (particle number densities) and for very large values of degeneracy factors. Several parameterizations of the obtained equation of state demonstrate a universal asymptotics of the effective excluded volume at high particle number densities. It is peculiar, that the found maximal packing fraction $η\simeq 0.75$ of Lorentz contracted particles is very close to the dense packing limit of classical hard spheres of same radius $η_{exc} \approx 0.74$. We show that the developed equation of state is the grand canonical formulation of the morphological thermodynamics approach applied to Lorentz contracted rigid spheres.

## Key claim
The paper derives an equation of state for Boltzmann particles with relativistic excluded volumes that accounts for Lorentz contraction of hard-core hadrons, and shows that this equation of state is the grand canonical formulation of morphological thermodynamics applied to Lorentz-contracted rigid spheres. The maximal packing fraction of Lorentz-contracted particles is found to be η ≈ 0.75, which is very close to the dense packing limit of classical hard spheres of the same radius (η_exc ≈ 0.74).

## Method
The authors first develop an analytical formula for the excluded volume of two Lorentz-contracted relativistic hard spheres, expressed in terms of the particles' radii, gamma-factors, and the angle between their momenta. Using this expression, they heuristically derive the induced surface and curvature tensions (ISCT) equation of state by extrapolating the low-density virial expansion to high densities, postulating a system of self-consistent equations for pressure p, surface tension coefficient Σ, and curvature tension coefficient K. The resulting system is then generalized with adjustable parameters (α_p, β_p, α_k^S, α_k^C, β_k^S, β_k^C) to reproduce higher virial coefficients, and the effective excluded volume is studied for baryons and mesons at high temperatures and large degeneracy factors.

## Key numbers
- Maximal packing fraction of Lorentz-contracted particles: η ≈ 0.75, compared to the classical hard sphere dense packing limit η_exc ≈ 0.74.
- The coefficient ã = 22/(9π) ≈ 0.778 is chosen to exactly reproduce the excluded volume of two non-relativistic hard spheres in the limit γ_l = γ_k = 1.
- For a non-relativistic sphere and an ultra-relativistic disk of equal radii, the averaged excluded volume from the approximate formula differs from the exact result by only 1.16%.
- The total number of spin-isospin states of all known hadrons and hadron resonances is up to 3–4 × 10^3.

## Context
This work extends the induced surface and curvature tensions (ISCT) equation of state previously developed for classical hard spheres and hard discs (Nazar19, QSTAT2019, LFtrans3) to include Lorentz contraction of relativistic hadrons. It builds upon the relativistic generalization of cluster and virial expansions for rigid spheres by Bugaev (Bugaev_2), which was the only hard-sphere equation of state proven to be causal in the high-pressure limit. The results enable incorporation of a causal, relativistic excluded-volume treatment into the hadron resonance gas model, replacing earlier truncated ISCT versions (IST3, X2, X3, X4) that were only valid at low particle densities.
