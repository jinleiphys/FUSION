# Production of $ΩNN$ and $ΩΩN$ in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Even though lots of $Λ$-hypernuclei have been found and measured, multi-strangeness hypernuclei consisting of $Ω$ are not yet discovered. The studies of multi-strangeness hypernuclei help us further understand the interaction between hyperons and nucleons. Recently the $ΩN$ and $ΩΩ$ interactions as well as binding energies were calculated by the HAL-QCD's lattice Quantum Chromo-Dynamics (LQCD) simulations and production rates of $Ω$-dibaryon in Au + Au collisions at RHIC and Pb + Pb collisions at LHC energies were estimated by a coalescence model. The present work discusses the production of more exotic triple-baryons including $Ω$, namely $ΩNN$ and $ΩΩN$ as well as their decay channels. A variation method is used in calculations of bound states and binding energy of $ΩNN$ and $ΩΩN$ with the potentials from the HAL-QCD's results. The productions of $ΩNN$ and $ΩΩN$ are predicted by using a blast-wave model plus coalescence model in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and $2.76$ TeV. Furthermore, plots for baryon number dependent yields of different baryons ($N$ and $Ω$), their dibaryons and hypernuclei are made and the production rate of a more exotic tetra-baryon ($ΩΩNN$) is extrapolated.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the production yields of the exotic triple-baryon states \(\Omega NN\) and \(\Omega\Omega N\) in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the predictions depend on a coalescence model combined with a blast-wave model and on binding energies calculated via a variation method using HAL-QCD lattice potentials; the results are therefore conditional on the specific potential parametrizations and the simplified Wigner functions used.

## Method

The authors first solved the non-relativistic three-body Schrödinger equation for \(\Omega NN\) and \(\Omega\Omega N\) using a variation method with hyperspherical-harmonics basis functions, employing \(\Omega N\) and \(\Omega\Omega\) potentials from HAL-QCD lattice simulations and a Malfliet-Tjon potential for \(NN\). For systems where the third baryon is weakly bound, a folding model was used to treat the three-body system as a dibaryon-baryon molecule. The bound-state wave functions were then used to construct Wigner density functions, which were combined with a blast-wave model for the phase-space distributions of nucleons and \(\Omega\) hyperons to compute cluster multiplicities via a coalescence model.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of \(\Omega pn\): \(22.0 \pm 2.2\) MeV (this work, variation method), compared to 21.3 MeV from Garcilazo and Valcarce (2019).
* Binding energy of \(\Omega nn\) (folding model): \(2.81 \pm 1.26\) MeV (this work), compared to 2.35 MeV from Garcilazo and Valcarce (2019).
* Binding energy of \(\Omega pp\) (folding model): \(4.02 \pm 1.26\) MeV (this work), compared to 3.04 MeV from Garcilazo and Valcarce (2019).
* Binding energy of \(\Omega\Omega n\) (folding model, \(\Omega n+\Omega\) channel): \(6.77 \pm 3.17\) MeV (this work), compared to 5.1 MeV from Garcilazo and Valcarce (2019).
* Binding energy of \(\Omega\Omega p\) (folding model, \(\Omega p+\Omega\) channel): \(10.2 \pm 3.2\) MeV (this work), compared to 6.5 MeV from Garcilazo and Valcarce (2019).
* The most probable position of the \(pn\Omega\) ground state in six-dimensional phase space is at \((r,q) \sim (2\ \text{fm}, 200\ \text{MeV})\).
* Collision energies considered: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (Au+Au at RHIC) and \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV (Pb+Pb at LHC).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of \(\Omega\)-dibaryon production (Zhang et al., 2020) to the three-body systems \(\Omega NN\) and \(\Omega\Omega N\), using the same HAL-QCD potentials (Iritani et al., 2019; Gongyo et al., 2018) that predicted \(\Omega N\) and \(\Omega\Omega\) binding energies. It complements the Faddeev-equation calculations of Garcilazo and Valcarce (2019) by providing a different method (variation and folding) and by adding production-rate predictions for heavy-ion collisions. The results are intended to guide experimental searches for multi-strangeness hypernuclei at RHIC and LHC, and the authors also extrapolate to a tetra-baryon (\(\Omega\Omega NN\)) as a future possibility.
