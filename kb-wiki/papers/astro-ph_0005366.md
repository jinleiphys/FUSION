# Simulation of the Spherically Symmetric Stellar Core Collapse, Bounce, and Postbounce Evolution of a 13 Solar Mass Star with Boltzmann Neutrino Transport, and Its Implications for the Supernova Mechanism

> With exact three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport, we simulate the stellar core collapse, bounce, and postbounce evolution of a 13 solar mass star in spherical symmetry, the Newtonian limit, without invoking convection. In the absence of convection, prior spherically symmetric models, which implemented approximations to Boltzmann transport, failed to produce explosions. We are motivated to consider exact transport to determine if these failures were due to the transport approximations made and to answer remaining fundamental questions in supernova theory. The model presented here is the first in a sequence of models beginning with different progenitors. In this model, a supernova explosion is not obtained. We discuss the ramifications of our results for the supernova mechanism.

## Key claim
Using exact three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport in spherical symmetry without convection, a 13 solar mass star still does not produce a supernova explosion, indicating that prior failures in spherically symmetric models were not solely due to transport approximations.

## Method
The authors simulated core collapse, bounce, and postbounce evolution of a 13 solar mass star using the AGILE–BOLTZTRAN code, which couples a conservative hydrodynamics solver with a three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport solver in the O(v/c) limit. The neutrino angular distributions were discretized with 6-point Gaussian quadrature, and the neutrino spectra were discretized into 12 energy groups spanning 5 to 300 MeV. The simulation used the Lattimer and Swesty equation of state and the Nomoto and Hashimoto precollapse model, assuming spherical symmetry and Newtonian gravity without convection.

## Key numbers
- Shock stalls at 230 km at 110 ms after bounce, then recedes for the duration of the simulation.
- Electron neutrino burst luminosity: 3.5 × 10^53 erg/s; postbounce accretion phase maximum: 3.6 × 10^52 erg/s.
- Electron antineutrino luminosity maximum: 3.3 × 10^52 erg/s.
- Muon/tau neutrino and antineutrino luminosities reach 2.0 × 10^52 erg/s.
- Muon/tau neutrino and antineutrino rms energies: 20–25 MeV; electron neutrino rms energies: 10–20 MeV.
- Infall velocities below the shock reach -6000 km/s at 600 ms after bounce.
- Maximum variation in total energy during the first 300 ms: ~3 × 10^49 erg (a few percent of total energy).
- Net heating rate convergence: 6- vs. 8-point quadrature rates differ by at most 3 percent; 12- vs. 20-group rates differ by at most 3 percent.

## Context
This work extends the earlier Boltzmann transport simulations by Mezzacappa and Bruenn (1993) and directly tests whether the failure of prior spherically symmetric models using multigroup flux-limited diffusion (Bruenn 1993, Wilson and Mayle 1993) was due to transport approximations. The result that no explosion occurs even with exact transport suggests that multidimensional effects such as convection, rotation, or magnetic fields may be required, or that improvements in precollapse models and input physics (e.g., electron capture rates on nuclear ensembles, nucleon correlations in opacities) are needed. This is the first in a planned sequence of models with different progenitors, with subsequent papers to report on general relativistic cases.
