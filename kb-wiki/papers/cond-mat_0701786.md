# Thermodynamics of a Trapped Unitary Fermi Gas

> We present the first model-independent comparison of recent measurements of the entropy and of the critical temperature of a unitary Fermi gas, performed by Luo et al., with the most complete results currently available from finite temperature Monte Carlo calculations. The measurement of the critical temperature in a cold fermionic atomic cloud is consistent with a value $T_c=0.23(2)epsilon_F$ in the bulk, as predicted by the present authors in their Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key claim

The critical temperature of a unitary Fermi gas in the bulk is \(T_c = 0.23(2)\varepsilon_F\), consistent with the authors' own Monte Carlo calculations, but the comparison with experiment is complicated by the fact that the experiment was performed slightly off resonance (at \(1/k_F a = -0.06\)) rather than exactly at unitarity.

## Method

The authors used their own finite-temperature Monte Carlo results for a homogeneous unitary Fermi gas on an \(8\times 8\times 8\) spatial lattice to generate smooth interpolation formulas for energy, chemical potential, and entropy. They then applied the Local Density Approximation (LDA) to describe the trapped atomic cloud, using only the experimental particle number, trapping potential, and scattering length from the Duke experiment (Luo et al., 2007) as input. The density profile was determined by minimizing the grand canonical thermodynamic potential, and the critical temperature was identified as the point where the superfluid core disappears.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature in the bulk: \(T_c = 0.23(2)\varepsilon_F\) (where \(\varepsilon_F\) is the local Fermi energy at the trap center)
- Critical temperature in harmonic trap units: \(T_c = 0.27(3)\varepsilon_F^{ho}\) (compared to the experimental value \(T_c = 0.29(2)\varepsilon_F^{ho}\) from Luo et al.)
- Critical energy (energy at transition relative to ground state): theoretical \(E_c = 0.32 E_0\) (compared to experimental \(E_c \approx 0.41(5) E_0\) from Luo et al.)
- Critical entropy per particle: theoretical \(S_c = 2.15\) (compared to experimental \(S_c \approx 2.7(2)\) from Luo et al.)
- Ground state energy parameter: \(\xi = 0.44(3)\) from the authors' zero-temperature extrapolation of unrestricted Monte Carlo calculations
- Pairing gap at zero temperature: \(\Delta = 0.504(24)\varepsilon_F\) from Carlson et al. (2003)

## Context

This work provides the first model-independent comparison between finite-temperature Monte Carlo calculations and experimental measurements of entropy and critical temperature in a unitary Fermi gas, specifically the Duke experiment by Luo et al. (2007). The results extend earlier theoretical work by the same authors (Bulgac, Drut, Magierski, 2006) and contradict the lower critical temperature \(T_c = 0.152(7)\varepsilon_F\) reported by Burovski et al. (2006), which would produce energy and entropy values inconsistent with experiment. The analysis enables future precise comparisons at exact unitarity and motivates more accurate Monte Carlo data to resolve remaining discrepancies.
