# Weakly bound states of two- and three-boson systems in the crossover from two to three dimension

> The spectrum and properties of quantum bound states is strongly dependent on the dimensionality of space. How this comes about and how one may theoretically and experimentally study the interpolation between different dimensions is a topic of great interest in different fields of physics. In this paper we study weakly bound states of non-relativistic two and three boson systems when passing continuously from a three (3D) to a two-dimensional (2D) regime within a 'squeezed dimension' model. We use periodic boundary conditions to derive a surprisingly simple form of the three-boson Schr{ö}dinger equation in momentum space that we solve numerically. Our results show a distinct dimensional crossover as three-boson states will either disappear into the continuum or merge with a 2D counterpart, and also a series of sharp transitions in the ratios of three-body and two-body energies from being purely 2D to purely 3D.

# Weakly bound states of two- and three-boson systems in the crossover from two to three dimensions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three-boson bound states undergo a distinct dimensional crossover when a single spatial dimension is continuously squeezed from three dimensions (3D) to two dimensions (2D) using periodic boundary conditions, with higher excited states either disappearing into the continuum or merging with 2D counterparts. The authors hedge that while periodic boundary conditions are not typical in few-body calculations, they expect only quantitative rather than qualitative differences compared to open boundary conditions used in experiments.

## Method

The authors model a "squeezed dimension" by imposing periodic boundary conditions along one spatial axis with variable length L, which interpolates between the 2D limit (R approaching 0) and the 3D limit (R approaching infinity). They derive a generalized Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation for three identical bosons in momentum space, where the momentum along the squeezed dimension is quantized as discrete modes. The two-body T-matrix is regularized analytically, and the three-body bound state equation is solved numerically for fixed two-body energies.

## Key numbers

- For R approaching 0, the dimer energy becomes sqrt(-E2) ~ (pi R)^{-1} * sinh^{-1}(1/2) = 0.153174 * R^{-1}, independent of the scattering length
- For the ground state trimer at epsilon_2 = 10^{-7} and r = 1000, the ratio epsilon_3/epsilon_2 = 93330 in the 3D plateau
- For the first excited state trimer at epsilon_2 = 10^{-7} and r = 1000, the ratio epsilon_3/epsilon_2 = 211.79 in the 3D plateau
- The ground state trimer size matches the squeezed dimension size at r = 217.29 for epsilon_2 = 10^{-7}
- The first excited state trimer size matches the squeezed dimension size at r = 10.35 for epsilon_2 = 10^{-7}
- In the strict 2D limit (r approaching 0), only two bound states remain with ratios epsilon_3/epsilon_2 = 16.52 and epsilon_3/epsilon_2 = 1.27, reproducing the results of Bruch and Tjon

## Context

This work extends the study of Efimov physics in three dimensions (Efimov 1970) and the two-body bound state problem in quasi-2D harmonic traps (Petrov and Shlyapnikov 2001) to a periodic boundary condition geometry that allows continuous interpolation between dimensions. The results complement a recent theoretical study of three-boson states under strong transverse harmonic confinement (Levinsen 2014) and provide a benchmark for numerical methods using periodic boundary conditions in larger systems. The work enables future studies of mass-imbalanced systems in the dimensional crossover, where the 2D limit can contain many more bound states (Bellotti and collaborators, 2014).
