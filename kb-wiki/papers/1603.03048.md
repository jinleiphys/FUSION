# Deuterium target data for precision neutrino-nucleus cross sections

> Amplitudes derived from scattering data on elementary targets are basic inputs to neutrino-nucleus cross section predictions. A prominent example is the isovector axial nucleon form factor, $F_A(q^2)$, which controls charged current signal processes at accelerator-based neutrino oscillation experiments. Previous extractions of $F_A$ from neutrino-deuteron scattering data rely on a dipole shape assumption that introduces an unquantified error. A new analysis of world data for neutrino-deuteron scattering is performed using a model-independent, and systematically improvable, representation of $F_A$. A complete error budget for the nucleon isovector axial radius leads to $r_A^2=0.46(22) \,{\rm fm}^2$, with a much larger uncertainty than determined in the original analyses. The quasielastic neutrino-neutron cross section is determined as $σ(ν_μn \to μ^- p)\big|_{E_ν=1\,{\rm GeV}} = 10.1(0.9) \times 10^{-39}{\rm cm}^2$. The propagation of nucleon-level constraints and uncertainties to nuclear cross sections is illustrated using MINERvA data and the GENIE event generator. These techniques can be readily extended to other amplitudes and processes.

## Key claim

A model-independent z-expansion analysis of neutrino-deuteron scattering data yields a much larger uncertainty on the nucleon isovector axial radius than previous dipole-based extractions, with the axial radius determined as r_A^2 = 0.46(22) fm^2, where the uncertainty is dominated by removal of the dipole shape assumption and inclusion of systematic errors for experimental acceptance corrections and deuteron corrections.

## Method

The authors performed Poisson likelihood fits to binned Q^2 distributions of charged-current neutrino-deuteron scattering events from three bubble chamber experiments (BNL1981, ANL1982, FNAL1983), using a z-expansion parametrization of the axial form factor F_A(q^2) that enforces analyticity and perturbative QCD constraints via sum rules, replacing the traditional dipole ansatz. The neutrino flux for each experiment was reconstructed self-consistently from the published dN/dE_nu distributions, and the fits included a floating normalization parameter for each data set. Systematic uncertainties were evaluated by varying the number of z-expansion coefficients, the choice of t_0 parameter, the deuteron nuclear corrections model, and the vector form factor parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Axial radius: r_A^2 = 0.46(22) fm^2 (combined result from all three data sets with systematic uncertainties)
- Quasielastic neutrino-neutron cross section at E_nu = 1 GeV: sigma(nu_mu n -> mu- p) = 10.1(0.9) x 10^-39 cm^2
- Dipole axial masses from individual experiments (updated constants): BNL1981 m_A = 1.05(5) GeV, ANL1982 m_A = 1.02(5) GeV, FNAL1983 m_A = 1.17(10) GeV
- Normalization factors from z-expansion fits (N_a=4): BNL1981 N_fit = 1.13(+0.13/-0.11), ANL1982 N_fit = 1.14(+0.10/-0.10), FNAL1983 N_fit = 1.21(+0.25/-0.21)
- Q^2 range of data: BNL1981 0.06-3 GeV^2 (49 bins, 1236 events), ANL1982 0.05-2.5 GeV^2 (49 bins, 1792 events), FNAL1983 0-3 GeV^2 (30 bins, 354 events)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of the axial form factor from deuterium data by Bodek (2008), Kuzmin (2007), and Bernard (2001) that relied on the dipole shape assumption, introducing an unquantified error. The z-expansion formalism was previously applied to nucleon form factors by Bhattacharya (2011) and Hill (2010), and the authors note that similar techniques are being applied to vector form factors by Lee (2015). The results enable propagation of rigorous nucleon-level uncertainties to nuclear cross section predictions in event generators like GENIE, as illustrated using MINERvA data from Fiorentini (2013). The larger uncertainty on the axial radius compared to original analyses (which found values near 0.44 fm^2 with smaller errors) demonstrates that previous extractions underestimated the true uncertainty from model dependence.
