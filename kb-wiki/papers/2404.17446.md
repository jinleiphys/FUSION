# Spiral flow of quantum quartic oscillator with energy cutoff

> Theory of the quantum quartic oscillator is developed with close attention to the energy cutoff one needs to impose on the system in order to approximate the smallest eigenvalues and corresponding eigenstates of its Hamiltonian by diagonalizing matrices of limited size. The matrices are obtained by evaluating matrix elements of the Hamiltonian between the associated harmonic-oscillator eigenstates and by correcting the computed matrices to compensate for their limited dimension, using the Wilsonian renormalization-group procedure. The cutoff dependence of the corrected matrices is found to be described by a spiral motion of a three-dimensional vector. This behavior is shown to result from a combination of a limit-cycle and a floating fixed-point behaviors, a distinct feature of the foundational quantum system that warrants further study. A brief discussion of the research directions concerning renormalization of polynomial interactions of degree higher than four, spontaneous symmetry breaking and coupling of more than one oscillator through the near neighbor couplings known in condensed matter and quantum field theory, is included.

## Key claim

The cutoff dependence of renormalized Hamiltonian matrices for the quantum quartic oscillator follows a spiral flow in a three-dimensional parameter space, resulting from a combination of limit-cycle and floating fixed-point behaviors, which explains the previously observed but unexplained numerical drift of matrix elements as the cutoff is lowered.

## Method

The authors start with the quartic oscillator Hamiltonian expressed in dimensionless form using creation and annihilation operators, then compute Hamiltonian matrix elements between harmonic oscillator eigenstates. They apply a Wilsonian renormalization group transformation (RGT) by repeatedly eliminating rows and columns from the eigenvalue problem via Gaussian elimination, reducing the cutoff from a large value N down to a smaller floating cutoff n. The evolution of the Hamiltonian matrices under this procedure is fully parameterized by a three-dimensional vector ξ(n) whose components are ratios of evolving interaction matrix elements to the original ones.

## Key numbers

- For g ~ 10, the renormalized matrix H_R^10 (size 10×10) reproduces the lowest eigenvalue of H^200 (size 200×200) with accuracy ~0.35%, while the plainly cut off matrix H^10 produces a 100 times greater error
- The attractive fixed point for N → ∞ has components (1/6, 5/6, 1/2), which qualitatively explains the numerically observed values ~(0.2, 0.8, 0.5)
- The spiral convergence factor r = (1-p)/(1+p) where p ~ (4gN)^{-1/4}, giving a period t ~ π/p ~ π(4gN)^{1/4}
- The approximate floating fixed-point solution ξ_a^+(n) approximates the actual attractive sequence with relative error smaller than 5% for n ≥ 6 and smaller than 1% for n ≥ 80 when g=1 and E=0

## Context

This work extends the numerical observations of Wójcik, who computed 10×10 matrices whose lowest eigenvalues matched those of 200×200 matrices but found unexplained slow drift of matrix elements as the cutoff changed. The authors demonstrate that this drift results from a spiral RG flow around a floating fixed point, combining limit-cycle behavior (Wilson 1970, Glazek and Wilson) with a floating fixed-point structure. The analysis enables systematic construction of effective low-dimensional Hamiltonian matrices for the quartic oscillator that can be used when the oscillator interacts with other systems, provided the external interaction does not significantly excite states beyond the floating cutoff.
