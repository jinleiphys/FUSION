# Jets in non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma

> Jets are a promising way to probe the non-equilibrium physics of quark-gluon plasma (QGP). We study how an out-of-equilibrium medium induces a jet particle to emit gluons. Evaluation of the emission rate is complicated by Weibel instabilities which lead to an exponential growth of chromomagnetic fields. Deriving a quantum field theoretical description of an unstable QGP medium, we show that the chromomagnetic fields deflect jet particles during the gluon emission.

## Key claim

The authors show that a quantum field theoretical description of an unstable quark-gluon plasma can yield finite, well-defined rates for jet-medium interactions, contrary to earlier naive calculations that gave divergent rates due to Weibel instabilities; this result holds under the condition that the anisotropy of the medium is small (\(\xi \ll g\)) so that the time evolution is slow enough for a controlled calculation.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the retarded and classical \(rr\) correlators in the hard thermal loop (HTL) effective theory extended to non-equilibrium media, isolating the instability pole contribution that leads to exponential growth of chromomagnetic fields. They then evaluate the Feynman diagrams for collinear gluon emission from a jet particle, incorporating both the fluctuating soft gluon cloud (from the non-growing part of the correlator) and the classical instability field (from the growing part), and derive an integral equation for the function \(\mathbf{f}\) that determines the emission rate.

## Key numbers

- The growth rate of chromomagnetic fields due to Weibel instabilities is \(\gamma \sim \xi g \Lambda\), where \(\xi\) is the anisotropy parameter and \(\Lambda\) is the hard scale determined by the momentum distribution of hard particles.
- The time window for the controlled calculation is \((g^2 \Lambda)^{-1} \ll \tau \ll (\xi g \Lambda)^{-1}\), requiring \(\xi \ll g\) for the approximations to hold.
- The strength of the gauge field grows as \(\exp(\gamma \tau)\) and ceases to grow when it reaches \(\sim 1/g^2\), beyond which HTL effective theory breaks down.

## Context

This work extends the HTL effective theory, originally developed for thermal equilibrium by Braaten and Pisarski (1989) and extended to non-equilibrium media by Mrowczynski (2000), to handle the divergent rates that plagued earlier calculations of jet-medium interactions in unstable plasmas (Baier, 2008). The results enable future calculations of jet quenching, photon production, and heavy-quark diffusion in non-equilibrium QGP, which can be used to extract transport coefficients from jet observables in heavy-ion collisions.
