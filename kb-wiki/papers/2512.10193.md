# Impact of the in-medium cross section on cluster spectra in ${}^{40,48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{58,64}\mathrm{Ni}$ collisions at $56$ and $140$ $\mathbf{\mathrm{MeV}}/\mathrm{\mathbf{nucleon}}$

> Although significant efforts have been made to investigate the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, the influence of the in-medium cross section on particle production in transport models is not well constrained. The in-medium cross section reflects the dynamic situation of the medium such as a nontrivial phase space distribution. In this study, we analyze the transverse momentum spectra of $p$, $d$, $t$, ${}^3{\mathrm{He}}$ and $α$ particles emitted near mid-rapidity in central $^{40,48}\mathrm{Ca}$ + $^{58, 64}\mathrm{Ni}$ reactions at $56$ and $140$ $\mathrm{MeV}/\mathrm{nucleon}$. The Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics ($\mathrm{AMD}$) model is chosen as the transport model for data comparison. Central events are selected based on charged-particle multiplicity in both the experimental data and AMD calculations after applying an experimental filter. Our results show that the in-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering cross-sections are more strongly reduced at $56$ $\mathrm{MeV}/\mathrm{nucleon}$ than at $140$ $\mathrm{MeV}/\mathrm{nucleon}$ incident energy.

## Key claim

The in-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering cross-section is more strongly reduced at 56 MeV/nucleon than at 140 MeV/nucleon incident energy, as inferred from comparing Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) calculations with different screening parameters to experimental transverse momentum spectra of light clusters.

## Method

The authors analyzed transverse momentum spectra of protons, deuterons, tritons, helium-3, and alpha particles emitted near mid-rapidity in central collisions of calcium-40 and calcium-48 beams on nickel-58 and nickel-64 targets at 56 and 140 MeV/nucleon. They compared experimental data from the HiRA10 and Microball detector systems at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory to AMD model calculations where the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-section was modified by a screening parameter eta that controls the reduction of the scattering matrix element relative to free-space values.

## Key numbers

- The screening parameter eta = 0.85 reproduces experimental reconstructed impact parameters at 140 MeV/nucleon, while eta = 0.35 is required at 56 MeV/nucleon to match the data.
- At 56 MeV/nucleon, the neutron-rich system Ca-48 + Ni-64 produces approximately 70% more tritons and about 10% more deuterons and alpha particles than the more symmetric Ca-40 + Ni-58 system.
- At 56 MeV/nucleon, the Ca-40 + Ni-58 system produces about 40% more protons and helium-3 than the Ca-48 + Ni-64 system.
- At 140 MeV/nucleon, the Ca-40 + Ni-58 system produces about 15% more protons and helium-3, while the Ca-48 + Ni-64 system produces about 50% more tritons and about 15% more deuterons and alpha particles.
- Central events were selected with charged-particle multiplicity N_C >= 10 at 56 MeV/nucleon and N_C >= 14 at 140 MeV/nucleon, corresponding to impact parameters less than about 3 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy (Tsang 2020, Tsang 2019) by addressing the poorly constrained influence of the in-medium cross-section on particle production in transport models. The AMD model has previously reproduced particle spectra (SpiRIT 2021, SpiRIT 2022, INDRA 2023), spectral ratios (Ono 2016), and charge distributions (Ono 2019, Ono 2020) in various reactions. The finding that the in-medium reduction is stronger at lower incident energy provides new constraints for transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions and will improve the extraction of symmetry energy parameters from cluster observables.
