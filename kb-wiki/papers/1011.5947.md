# Challenges for Modeling Nuclear Structure: Are the Proton and Neutron Masses and A-body Interactions Relevant?

> We discuss some of the challenges that future nuclear modeling may face in order to improve the description of the nuclear structure. One challenge is related to the need for A-body nuclear interactions justified by various contemporary nuclear physics studies. Another challenge is related to the discrepancy in the NNN contact interaction parameters for 3He and 3H that suggests the need for accurate proton and neutron masses in the future precision calculations. MSC2010 Classification: 17B81 Applications to physics, 17B80 Applications to integrable systems, 81R12 Relations with integrable systems, 81V70 Many-body theory, 81V35 Nuclear physics, 81U15 Exactly and quasi-solvable systems, 82B23 Exactly solvable models; Bethe ansatz.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the discrepancy in the NNN contact interaction parameters \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) needed to fit \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He separately can be resolved by using a properly weighted nucleon mass (rather than assuming equal proton and neutron masses), and that A-body interactions are relevant for both light and heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors examine the failure of high-precision NN potentials (AV18, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen, N\(^3\)LO) to reproduce binding energies of A=3 and A=4 systems without three-body forces. They analyze the \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) parameters of the chiral N\(^2\)LO NNN interaction by comparing binding energies of \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He computed with equal nucleon masses versus a mass formula \(m = (Z m_p + (A-Z) m_n)/A\). For heavy nuclei, they apply the exactly solvable Extended Pairing Interaction (EPI) to Sn, Yb, and Pb isotope chains, fitting the interaction strength \(G\) to reproduce experimental binding energies relative to a doubly magic core.

## Key numbers

- The experimental binding energies used for \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He are 8.482 MeV and 7.718 MeV, respectively.
- The experimental binding energy of \(^4\)He is 28.296 MeV, reproduced to within ~0.5 MeV over the entire \(c_D\)-\(c_E\) range considered.
- The kinetic energy of the three-nucleon system is approximately \(K \approx 37\) MeV; the relative nucleon mass deviation \(\delta m/m \approx 0.7 \times 10^{-3}\) yields a kinetic energy shift \(\delta K \approx 26\) keV.
- The binding energy deviation range within the physically relevant region (\(-1 < c_D < 1\)) is \(15 < \delta K < 25\) keV.
- For Sn isotopes, the fitting functions for the EPI strength \(G(A)\) are \(\ln(G(A)) = 365.0584 - 6.4836 A + 0.0284 A^2\) (even \(A\)) and \(\ln(G(A)) = 398.2277 - 7.0349 A + 0.0307 A^2\) (odd \(A\)); for the hole case relative to \(^{132}\)Sn, \(G(A) = \alpha \dim(A)^{-\beta}\) with \(\alpha = 259.436\) and \(\beta = 0.9985\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory studies of three-nucleon forces by Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum (2006), and the high-precision NN potential comparisons by Wiringa (1995). It addresses the non-intersecting \(c_D\)-\(c_E\) curves for \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He found by Navratil (2007) and proposes a resolution via nucleon mass adjustment. The EPI model builds on the Richardson-Gaudin pairing solutions of Dukelsky (2001) and Links (2002), and on earlier applications to Yb and Pb chains by Gueorguiev (2004, 2005). The results suggest that future nuclear modeling codes should incorporate accurate proton and neutron masses and A-body interaction terms, and that the EPI can serve as a verification tool for such codes.
