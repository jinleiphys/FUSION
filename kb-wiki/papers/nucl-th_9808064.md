# Strangeness Modes in Nuclei Tested by Anti-Neutrinos

> Production of negative strangeness in reactions of inelastic anti-neutrino scattering on a nucleus provides information on the modification of strange degrees of freedom in nuclear matter. We calculate cross-sections of the reaction channels $\barν_{e(μ)}\to e^+(μ^+) + K^{-}$ and $\barν_{e(μ)} + p \to \Lb + e^+(μ^+)$ and investigate their sensitivity to the medium effects. In particular, we consider effects induced by the presence of a low-energy excitation mode in the $K^-$ spectrum, associated with the correlated $Λ$-particle and proton-hole states, and the renormalization of the weak interaction in medium. In order to avoid double counting, various contributions to anti-neutrino scattering are classified with the help of the optical theorem, formulated within the non-equilibrium Green's function technique.

## Key claim

The paper argues that anti-neutrino scattering on nuclei can probe the modification of strange degrees of freedom in nuclear matter, specifically through the low-energy excitation mode in the K⁻ spectrum associated with correlated Λ-particle and proton-hole states, but the reaction channel producing a hyperon (Λ) gives a much larger contribution to the lepton cross section than the channel producing a K⁻ meson.

## Method

The authors calculate cross-sections for the reactions $\bar{\nu}_{e(\mu)} \to e^+(\mu^+) + K^{-}$ and $\bar{\nu}_{e(\mu)} + p \to \Lambda + e^+(\mu^+)$ using in-medium kaon spectral densities and short-range Λ-proton correlations. They formulate the in-medium kaon self-energy including s-wave and p-wave (Λ-proton-hole) contributions, renormalize the weak interaction vertices in medium, and classify contributions to anti-neutrino scattering using the optical theorem within the non-equilibrium Green's function technique to avoid double counting.

## Key numbers

- Critical momentum for the upper quasiparticle kaon branch: $k_c \approx 2570$ MeV (with $\lambda = 2d$, constrained by low-energy theorems) or $k_c \approx 1570$ MeV (with $\lambda = 0$, ignoring low-energy theorem constraints)
- Critical momentum estimate from the simpler potential model: $1500\ \text{MeV} < k_c < 1700\ \text{MeV}$ for $\Sigma_{KN}$ between 200 and 400 MeV
- Typical energy of the low-lying Λ-proton-hole branch: $\omega \sim m_\Lambda - m_N \simeq 200$ MeV
- Nuclear matter density: $\rho = \rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}$ with proton concentration $x = 1/2$
- Landau-Migdal parameter estimate: $f'_\Lambda \approx 1.1$
- Total cross section per nucleon for $\bar{\nu}_e \to K^- + e^+$: $A^{-1} \sigma \approx 1 \times 10^{-42}\ \text{cm}^2$
- Total cross section per nucleon for $\bar{\nu}_\mu \to K^- + \mu^+$: $A^{-1} \sigma \approx 4 \times 10^{-42}\ \text{cm}^2$
- Anti-neutrino energy used in calculations: $E_\nu = 1$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the idea of R.F. Sawyer (Sawyer, 1994) who proposed using anti-neutrino decay into a kaon and lepton to probe in-medium kaon modification, but applies it to the low-energy Λ-proton-hole branch of the K⁻ spectrum rather than the high-momentum quasiparticle branch. The formalism parallels the treatment of in-medium pions by Migdal (1978) and Voskresensky (1993), where hyperons play the same role for kaons as Δ isobars do for pions, though crucially the Λ and Σ branches lie below the kaon branch while the Δ branch lies above the pion branch. The results enable experimental tests of kaon spectral density in nuclear matter and could constrain the K⁻-Λ-nucleon interaction, with implications for kaon condensation in neutron stars as discussed by Kolomeitsev and Voskresensky (1995).
