# Measuring differential particle correlations in relativistic nuclear collisions

> This study explores the transverse momentum ($p_T$) dependencies of Symmetric and Asymmetric Correlations (SC and ASC) with one and two particles of interest in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. Leveraging the AMPT model, the investigation delves into the sensitivity of these correlations to the final state effects, providing valuable insights into their potential for constraining the final state effects' $p_T$ dependencies. The HIJING model is employed as a benchmark for non-flow correlations, shedding light on their impact on interpreting SC and ASC data. Moreover, the study points out that differential SC and ASC with one and two particles of interest (POIs) typically incorporate contributions from event-plane angle fluctuations. Consequently, this work highlights the significance of SC and ASC with one and two POIs as valuable tools for investigating the $p_T$ nature of the final state effects and advocates for comprehensive experimental measurements across various beam energies and system sizes to enhance our understanding and provide additional constraints for theoretical models.

## Key claim

This study claims that differential Symmetric and Asymmetric Correlations (SC and ASC) with one and two particles of interest (POIs) are sensitive to final state effects in a transverse momentum dependent way, but the paper explicitly notes that these observables incorporate nonvanishing contributions from event-plane angle fluctuations, which complicates the interpretation of experimental measurements.

## Method

The analysis used the AMPT model (v2.26t9b) with two different parton scattering cross sections (sigma_pp = 3.0 mb and 6.0 mb, corresponding to eta/s values of 0.232 and 0.156) and the HIJING model (v1.411) as a non-flow benchmark, simulating minimum bias Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with approximately 20 million events per setting. Charged particles with transverse momentum 0.0 < p_T < 5.0 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |eta| < 1.0 were selected as POIs, while particles with 0.0 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c served as Particles of Reference (POR). Two-, three-, four-, five-, and six-particle differential correlations were computed using the two-subevents cumulant method with an eta gap of |Delta eta| > 0.7 for two-, three-, and four-particle correlations, and the traditional cumulant method for five- and six-particle correlations.

## Key numbers

*   The AMPT model parameters used were alpha_s = 0.33 with screening masses mu = 1.602 fm^-1 and 2.265 fm^-1, yielding parton scattering cross sections sigma_pp = 6.0 mb and 3.0 mb, corresponding to eta/s = 0.156 and 0.232, respectively.
*   The analysis selected charged particles within transverse momentum range 0.0 < p_T < 5.0 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |eta| < 1.0 as POIs, and 0.0 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c as Particles of Reference.
*   The initial temperature assumed for the AMPT model was T_i = 378 MeV for the system of massless quarks and gluons.
*   The Lund string fragmentation parameters used in AMPT were a = 0.55 and b = 0.15 GeV^-2.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of integrated SC and ASC by Taghavi (2020), Bilandzic (2020, 2021), Mordasini (2019), Li (2021), and Moravcova (2020) to a comprehensive differential analysis in transverse momentum. The study uses the AMPT model to probe final state effects and the HIJING model to benchmark non-flow contributions, addressing concerns raised by Gardim (2012) and Magdy (2022) about event-plane angle fluctuations complicating differential flow measurements. The results advocate for comprehensive experimental measurements across various beam energies and system sizes to provide additional constraints for theoretical models of the quark-gluon plasma transport properties.
