# The Pseudospectral Method for the Dirac Equation with Confining Potential

> We observe that solving the Dirac equation for confined potentials using the generalized pseudospectral (GPS) method leads to deteriorating convergence of energy eigenvalues and highly oscillatory in wave functions as the confinement radius decreases. It is found that this issue stems from the first-order differentiation formulation employed in GPS method. Motivated by this insight, we adopt the kinetically balanced generalized pseudospectral method, which incorporates the kinetically-balanced condition into the GPS method. Numerical results demonstrate that the mono-kinetically-balanced generalized pseu dospectral (MKB-GPS) method yields converged energy eigenvalues and generates smooth, continuous wave functions. This is the first application of the MKB-GPS method to confined potentials, and its effectiveness is validated for small confinement radii.

## Key claim

The standard generalized pseudospectral (GPS) method for solving the Dirac equation with confining potentials produces deteriorating convergence of energy eigenvalues and highly oscillatory wave functions as the confinement radius decreases; this issue is resolved by incorporating the mono-kinetically-balanced condition (MKB-GPS), which introduces a second-order differential operator into the Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate that the standard GPS method, which uses a first-order differentiation formulation, fails for small confinement radii (e.g., R_max = 1 a.u.) by producing oscillatory small-component wave functions and poor energy convergence. They then implement the mono-kinetically-balanced generalized pseudospectral (MKB-GPS) method, which enforces the kinetically-balanced condition by expressing the small-component basis functions as the operator D^+ = (1/2c)(d/dr + κ/r) acting on the large-component basis functions, thereby incorporating second-order derivatives into the discretized Dirac equation. The resulting generalized eigenvalue problem is solved on a mapped grid using Legendre pseudospectral collocation points.

## Key numbers

- For the hydrogen 1s_{1/2} state with confinement radius R_max = 1 a.u., the standard GPS method yields relative errors in energy that become significant (on the order of 10^-2 or worse) compared to the reference solution with N = 500.
- For R_max > 25 a.u., the 1s_{1/2} state relative error reaches approximately 10^-16 using the standard GPS method.
- For R_max > 50 a.u., the 2s_{1/2} state relative error also reaches approximately 10^-16 using the standard GPS method.
- The confinement potential is defined as V(r) = -Z/r for r < R_max and V(r) = ∞ for r > R_max, with R_max set as small as 1 a.u. to model extreme pressure conditions.

## Context

This work extends the GPS method developed by Yao and Chu (1993) and the kinetically-balanced condition introduced by Shabaev (2004) to suppress spurious states in Dirac equation solutions. The MKB-GPS approach was previously applied by Jiao (2021) to unconfined systems, and this paper provides the first application to confined potentials. The results enable accurate relativistic calculations for hydrogen-like ions under extreme confinement, relevant to high-pressure physics and astrophysical environments, and resolve the oscillation problem that arises when the confinement radius becomes small.
