# Hydrodynamic Gradient Expansion Diverges beyond Bjorken Flow

> The gradient expansion is the fundamental organising principle underlying relativistic hydrodynamics, yet understanding its convergence properties for general nonlinear flows has posed a major challenge. We introduce a simple method to address this question in a class of fluids modelled by Israel-Stewart--type relaxation equations. We apply it to (1+1)-dimensional flows and provide numerical evidence for factorially divergent gradient expansions. This generalises results previously only obtained for (0+1)-dimensional comoving flows, notably Bjorken flow. We also demonstrate that the only known nontrivial case of a convergent hydrodynamic gradient expansion at the nonlinear level relies on Bjorken flow symmetries and becomes factorially divergent as soon as these are relaxed. Finally, we show that factorial divergence can be removed using a momentum space cutoff, which generalises a result obtained earlier in the context of linear response.

## Key claim
The hydrodynamic gradient expansion for nonlinear (1+1)-dimensional flows in Israel-Stewart-type theories is factorially divergent, generalizing previous results that were restricted to (0+1)-dimensional comoving flows such as Bjorken flow; the only known nontrivial case of a convergent expansion relies on Bjorken flow symmetries and becomes factorially divergent as soon as these symmetries are relaxed.

## Method
The authors introduce a numerical method to compute the gradient expansion to high orders by first solving the full nonlinear equations of motion (the BRSSS or Denicol-Noronha models) as an initial value problem, then evaluating a recursion relation for the dissipative tensor \(\Pi_\star^{(n)}\) that involves successive derivatives of the background solution \(\mathcal{E}\) and \(U\). The method is applied to (1+1)-dimensional longitudinal flows, where the dynamics depend on one spatial coordinate \(x\) and time \(t\), and the gradient expansion is organized by a formal derivative-counting parameter \(\epsilon\).

## Key numbers
- For the BRSSS model in \(d=4\) with a strong Gaussian overdensity initial condition, the root plot of \(|\Pi_\star^{(n)}|^{1/n}\) shows factorial growth \(\Pi_\star^{(n)} \sim \Gamma(n)\) for \(n\) in the range \([20,130]\) at three sampled spacetime points (Fig. 1).
- In the Denicol-Noronha model for Bjorken flow, the gradient expansion coefficients grow geometrically, with \(\Pi_\star^{(n)} \propto (-\frac{a b \mathrm{Kn}}{4})^{n-1}\) where \(\mathrm{Kn} = 1/(\rho_0 \tau_0 \sigma_T)\) is the Knudsen number; for the example shown, \(\rho(\tau_0)=4\), \(T(\tau_0)=2\), \(\tau_0=0.1\), and \(a=0.5\), \(b=10\), \(\sigma_T=1\).
- When a momentum-space cutoff is imposed via a coarse numerical grid (lattice spacing corresponding to \(N_x \times N_t\) grid points), the factorial growth transitions to geometric growth at large \(n\), with the asymptotic growth rate set by the largest eigenvalue of the operator \(\mathcal{M}\), which scales with the inverse lattice spacing; the expansion was evaluated to order \(n=8000\) on a coarse grid (Fig. 2).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion that were limited to (0+1)-dimensional comoving flows, notably Bjorken flow (Bjorken, 1983) and Gubser flow (Gubser, 2010), where factorial divergence was observed. It also generalizes a linear-response result by Heller et al. (2020) showing that a momentum-space cutoff can remove factorial divergence. The method applies to the class of Israel-Stewart-type models, including the BRSSS model (Baier et al., 2008) and the Denicol-Noronha model (Denicol and Noronha, 2019), and enables future studies of optimal truncation and practical utility of divergent asymptotic series for a wider set of flows.
