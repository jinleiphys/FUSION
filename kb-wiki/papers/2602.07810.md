# Observation of a broad $p$-wave resonant state in $^{9}$He

> We report on the two-body invariant-mass spectroscopy of $^{9}$He, populated via the 1$p$1$n$ knockout reaction from the two-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}$Li at $\sim$250 MeV/nucleon. A broad $p$-wave resonant state of $^{9}$He was observed at 1.28(1) MeV with a width of 0.82(4) MeV.

## Key claim
The paper reports the observation of a broad p-wave resonant state in 9He at 1.28(1) MeV with a width of 0.82(4) MeV, populated via the 1p1n knockout reaction from 11Li. The abstract glosses over the fact that this width is nearly an order of magnitude larger than previously reported values, which were derived from datasets with limited statistical significance.

## Method
The experiment was performed at the RIKEN Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory using a 246 MeV/nucleon 11Li beam incident on a 150-mm thick liquid hydrogen target. The 9He nucleus was populated via the 11Li(p,pd) and 11Li(p,2pn) knockout reactions, and the two-body invariant mass was reconstructed from the momenta of the 8He fragment and a single detected neutron using the SAMURAI spectrometer and NEBULA neutron detector array. The relative-energy spectrum was fitted with a Breit-Wigner p-wave resonance on top of a constant background, convoluted with the experimental resolution of 0.40 MeV (FWHM) obtained from GEANT4 simulations.

## Key numbers
- Resonant energy of the p-wave state in 9He: 1.28(1) MeV above the 8He + n threshold
- Decay width of the p-wave state: 0.82(4) MeV
- Experimental energy resolution (FWHM): 0.40 MeV at 1.3 MeV
- Channel radius used in fitting: 4.35 fm (increasing to 6.0 fm reduces the width by about 4%)
- 11Li beam energy: 246 MeV/nucleon with intensity of 1 x 10^5 particles per second

## Context
This work extends previous studies of 9He that reported a p-wave resonance at 1.2(1) MeV with a width of 130(+170/-130) keV from transfer reactions (Kalanee 2013) and at 2.0(2) MeV with width ~2 MeV (Golovkov 2007), as well as results from double-charge exchange reactions (Seth 1987) and elastic scattering (Rogachev 2003). The much larger decay width observed here (0.82 MeV versus previous values below 0.13 MeV) agrees reasonably well with theoretical predictions from ab initio variational Monte Carlo (Nollett 2012), no-core shell model with continuum (Vorabbi 2018), and shell model in continuum (Volya 2005) calculations, which anticipate a width around 1 MeV for the lowest 1/2- state. The result enables better benchmarking of nuclear models for neutron-rich helium isotopes and clarifies the structure of 9He, which has implications for understanding the 10He system.
