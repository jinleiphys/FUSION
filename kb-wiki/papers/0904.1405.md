# Correlated Gaussian Hyperspherical Method for Few-Body Systems

> We develop an innovative numerical technique to describe few-body systems. Correlated Gaussian basis functions are used to expand the channel functions in the hyperspherical representation. The method is proven to be robust and efficient compared to other numerical techniques. The method is applied to few-body systems with short range interactions, including several examples for three- and four-body systems. Specifically, for the two-component, four-fermion system, we extract the coefficients that characterize its behavior at unitarity.

## Key claim
The Correlated Gaussian Hyperspherical (CGHS) method combines correlated Gaussian basis functions with the hyperspherical representation to efficiently solve the Schrödinger equation for few-body systems with short-range interactions, but its accuracy is limited by linear dependence issues in the basis set rather than by basis set truncation alone.

## Method
The method expands the hyperangular channel functions in a basis of correlated Gaussian functions, which depend on interparticle distances and are symmetrized for identical particles. For each fixed hyperradius \(R\), the Hamiltonian matrix elements are evaluated analytically for three-body systems and reduced to a sum of one-dimensional numerical integrations for four-body systems, using orthogonal transformations to simplify the integration coordinates. The resulting adiabatic hyperspherical potential curves \(U_\nu(R)\) and non-adiabatic couplings \(P_{\nu\nu'}\) and \(Q_{\nu\nu'}\) are then used to construct coupled one-dimensional radial equations.

## Key numbers
- For three identical bosons at unitarity, the coefficient \(s_0\) extracted from the lowest potential curve \(U_0(R) = -(s_0^2 + 1/4)/(2\mu R^2)\) is \(s_0 \approx 1.0059\), compared to the analytical value \(s_0 \approx 1.0062\).
- For two indistinguishable fermions resonantly interacting with a third equal-mass particle at unitarity, the coefficient \(p_0\) from \(U_0(R) = (p_0^2 - 1/4)/(2\mu R^2)\) is \(p_0 \approx 2.166218\), compared to the analytical value \(p_0 \approx 2.166222\).
- The three-boson calculation used 30 basis functions extended up to \(R = 100 r_0\); the three-fermion calculation used 90 basis functions extended up to \(R = 4000 r_0\).
- For the two-component four-fermion system with scattering length \(a_s = 100 r_0\), the CGHS method reproduces the lowest potential curves from D'Incao et al. (2008) and provides 20 lowest potential curves for even parity and vanishing orbital angular momentum at unitarity.

## Context
This work extends the correlated Gaussian (CG) method, previously limited to bound states in trapped systems (Varga 1995, 1997; Blume 2007), by combining it with the hyperspherical representation to treat continuum states and collisions. It improves upon standard hyperspherical methods that use B-spline or finite-element basis sets (Pack 1987, Esry 1996, Suno 2002), which become computationally demanding for \(N>3\). The CGHS method enables the calculation of multiple hyperspherical potential curves for four-fermion systems, allowing future studies of dimer-dimer scattering and recombination processes beyond the single-channel approximation of D'Incao et al. (2008).
