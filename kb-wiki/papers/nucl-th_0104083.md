# Electroweak Probes and Nuclear Effective Field Theory

> There is growing interest in nuclear physics applications of effective field theory. I give a brief account of some of the latest developments in this area. I also describe interplay between this new approach and the traditional nuclear physics approach.

# Electroweak Probes and Nuclear Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) and the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) are complementary rather than competing frameworks; EFT provides a model-independent expansion scheme but currently contains unknown low-energy constants (LECs), while SNPA has predictive power based on empirical tests, and the two approaches can be mutually validating when their results agree.

## Method

The paper reviews nuclear chiral perturbation theory (χPT) in both the Weinberg counting scheme and the polynomial divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise. For two-nucleon systems, the author describes an *ab initio* calculation up to next-to-leading order (NLO) using a potential that includes a uniquely determined one-pion exchange term plus local contact terms whose coefficients (C₀, C₂, D₂) are fixed by low-energy two-nucleon observables; the Lippman-Schwinger equation is then solved with a momentum-cutoff regularization. A hybrid approach is also discussed, where phenomenological nuclear wave functions are used with EFT-derived transition operators.

## Key numbers

- The reasonable cutoff range for the *ab initio* calculation is Λ = 100–300 MeV in the absence of the pion in the theory, and Λ = 200–500 MeV in the presence of the pion.
- The best-fit value of the four-nucleon axial-current counterterm L₁A is L₁A = 5.6 fm³, consistent with naturalness arguments that suggest |L₁A| ≤ 6 fm³.
- The SNPA calculations for μ-d capture, ν-d reactions, and pp-fusion reproduce experimental data within approximately 10% experimental errors.
- The chiral expansion index ν is defined as ν = 2L + 2(N_C − 1) + 2 − (N_A + N_E) + Σᵢ ν̄ᵢ, where ν̄ = d + (n/2) − 2.

## Context

This invited talk from a 2001 workshop reviews the emerging application of effective field theory to nuclear electroweak processes, extending the earlier work of Weinberg (1990) and the PDS scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise. The paper contrasts EFT with the traditional SNPA approach, which has been extensively validated for nuclear electroweak processes. The specific comparison of ν-d reaction cross sections between the EFT calculation of Butler, Cheng, and Kong and the SNPA calculation of Nakamura, Sato, Gudkov, and Kubodera demonstrates the symbiotic relationship between the two frameworks. The results are directly relevant to interpreting experiments at the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO), which measures charged-current and neutral-current reactions on deuterium to study solar neutrino oscillations.
