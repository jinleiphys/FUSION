# On the Geodesic Nature of Wegner's Flow

> Wegner's method of flow equations offers a useful tool for diagonalizing a given Hamiltonian and is widely used in various branches of quantum physics. Here, generalizing this method, a condition is derived, under which the corresponding flow of a quantum state becomes geodesic in a submanifold of the projective Hilbert space, independently of specific initial conditions. This implies the geometric optimality of the present method as an algorithm of generating stationary states. The result is illustrated by analyzing some physical examples.

# On the Geodesic Nature of Wegner's Flow

## Key claim
The authors derive a condition under which the generalized Wegner flow of a quantum state becomes geodesic in a submanifold of the projective Hilbert space, independently of specific initial conditions at flow parameter l=0. This geometric optimality holds for the generalized generator eta^{(a)} when the off-diagonal index i_{a'} satisfies i_{a'} not equal to 2i_a or 3i_a for all a', and the result is proven for initial states that are eigenstates of the diagonal part of the Hamiltonian.

## Method
The authors generalize Wegner's flow equation by replacing the full off-diagonal generator eta^W with a single-term generator eta^{(a)} = [H_d(l), H^{(a)}_{o-d}(l)], where H^{(a)}_{o-d}(l) is one component of the off-diagonal Hamiltonian. They then formulate the flow as a curve in the quantum evolution submanifold of projective Hilbert space, compute the variation of the arc-length functional S = integral of ds/dl, and show that under the condition i_{a'} not equal to 2i_a, 3i_a, the variation vanishes for the initial state |u_n>, proving geodesic flow. The result is illustrated by analyzing the generalized harmonic oscillator, a spin in a magnetic field, and the Jaynes-Cummings model.

## Key numbers
- For the generalized harmonic oscillator with squeezing: the Fubini-Study metric is ds^2 = (1/2)(n^2 + n + 1)[dr^2 + (sinh^2 2r) dphi^2], describing Lobachevsky space
- For the spin-s in a magnetic field: the metric is ds^2 = (1/2)(s^2 + s - m^2)[dtheta^2 + (sin^2 theta) dphi^2], describing a sphere
- For the Jaynes-Cummings model: the metric is ds^2 = d|alpha_n|^2/(1 - |alpha_n|^2) + |alpha_n|^2(1 - |alpha_n|^2)(dtheta_{alpha_n} - dtheta_{gamma_n})^2
- The flow equation for the squeezing parameter lambda(l) in the harmonic oscillator case: d lambda(l)/dl = -4 omega^2(l) lambda(l)
- The flow equation for beta(l) in the spin case: d beta(l)/dl = -beta_z^2(l) beta(l)

## Context
This work extends Wegner's method of flow equations (Wegner 1994), which was originally developed for diagonalizing Hamiltonians in analogy with renormalization group theory and has been applied to the Anderson impurity model, the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model in nuclear physics, and the Tomonaga-Luttinger model. The authors reveal a geometric property hidden in Wegner's flow by connecting it to the Fubini-Study metric on projective Hilbert space, building on prior work by Abe on quantum evolution submanifolds. The result enables the interpretation of Wegner's method as an optimal strategy for quantum state engineering to realize stationary states, and suggests that Wegner's flow may always be geodesic for exactly solvable systems, though this remains an open question.
