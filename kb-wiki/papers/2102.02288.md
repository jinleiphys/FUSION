# $^{229m}$Th isomer from a nuclear model perspective

> The physical conditions for the emergence of the extremely low-lying nuclear isomer $^{229m}$Th at approximately 8 eV are investigated in the framework of our recently proposed nuclear structure model. Our theoretical approach explains the $^{229m}$Th-isomer phenomenon as the result of a very fine interplay between collective quadrupole-octupole and single-particle dynamics in the nucleus. We find that the isomeric state can only appear in a rather limited model space of quadrupole-octupole deformations in the single-particle potential, with the octupole deformation being of a crucial importance for its formation. Within this deformation space the model-described quantities exhibit a rather smooth behaviour close to the line of isomer-ground state quasi-degeneracy determined by the crossing of the corresponding single-particle orbitals. Our comprehensive analysis confirms the previous model predictions for reduced transition probabilities and the isomer magnetic moment, while showing a possibility for limited variation in the ground-state magnetic moment theoretical value. These findings prove the reliability of the model and suggest that the same dynamical mechanism could manifest in other actinide nuclei giving a general prescription for the search and exploration of similar isomer phenomena.

## Key claim

The isomeric state in $^{229m}$Th at approximately 8 eV arises from a very fine interplay between collective quadrupole-octupole and single-particle dynamics, and can only appear in a rather limited model space of quadrupole-octupole deformations in the single-particle potential, with octupole deformation being of crucial importance for its formation.

## Method

The authors apply a nuclear structure model combining a coherent quadrupole-octupole (CQOM) collective core with a deformed shell model (DSM) using a reflection-asymmetric Woods-Saxon potential and BCS pairing correlations with blocking. They perform DSM calculations on a grid in the quadrupole-octupole deformation space ($\beta_2$, $\beta_3$) to identify regions where the isomer can emerge, and compute energies, reduced transition probabilities $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$, and magnetic moments for both the isomeric state and ground state. Coriolis mixing between single-particle orbitals is treated perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- Isomer energy: approximately 8 eV (from abstract; recent experimental values cited include $E_{IS}=8.28(17)$ eV from Seiferle (2019), $E_{IS}=8.30(92)$ eV from Yamaguchi (2019), and $E_{IS}=8.10(17)$ eV from Sikorsky (2020))
- Isomer magnetic moment: $\mu_{\text{IS}}=-0.37(6)\mu_N$ from Thielking (2018) and Mueller (2018); model prediction range $\mu_{\text{IS}}=-0.25\mu_N$ to $-0.35\mu_N$ from Minkov and Pálffy (2019)
- Ground state magnetic moment: experimental value $0.360(7)\mu_N$ from Safronova (2013); model prediction range $\mu_{\text{GS}}=0.53-0.66\mu_N$
- $B(M1)$ reduced transition probability: model prediction $0.006-0.008$ Weisskopf units (W.u.) from Minkov and Pálffy (2017)
- $B(E2)$ reduced transition probability: model prediction $20-30$ W.u. from Minkov and Pálffy (2017)
- Pairing parameter $g_0$ varies between 17.8 MeV and 19.2 MeV; $g_1$ varies around 7.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously developed nuclear structure model (Minkov and Pálffy, 2017, 2019) that successfully predicted reduced transition probabilities and the isomer magnetic moment for $^{229m}$Th. It addresses earlier estimates from the quasiparticle-plus-phonon model (Gulda, 2002; Ruchowska, 2006) and Nilsson model calculations (Dykhne and Tkalya, 1998; Tkalya, 2015) which gave a magnetic moment of $-0.076\mu_N$ that differs significantly from experiment. The analysis enables future searches for similar low-lying isomer phenomena in other actinide nuclei and supports applications such as a nuclear clock based on the $^{229m}$Th transition.
