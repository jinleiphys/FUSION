# Pair production as a probe for the dynamics of nuclear fission and $α$ decay

> Electron-positron pairs can be produced via the Schwinger mechanism in the presence of strong electric fields. In particular, the fields involved in $α$ decay and nuclear fission are strong enough to produce them. The energy of the $e^+e^-$ pair is related to the relative distance and velocity of the daughter nuclei. Thus, the energy distribution of the produced pairs can give information about the dynamics of the fission and $α$ decay processes. A neck model of nuclear fission is used to illustrate how the pairs can be used as a probe of the dynamics.

## Key claim

Electron-positron pairs produced via the Schwinger mechanism during nuclear fission and alpha decay can serve as a probe of the dynamics of these processes, but the extracted energy distribution depends on an ambiguous parameter β that determines whether the ions gain kinetic energy before or after pair creation, and the characteristic low-energy peaks predicted for alpha decay may not be observable in practice because the alpha particle is unlikely to be at rest when it emerges from the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors model pair production by treating the positron as tunneling through an effective potential derived from the Klein-Gordon equation, with the Coulomb potential modified by a screening factor. They compute the positron energy spectrum dN/dE₊ using a neck model for fission dynamics that includes constant nuclear volume, surface tension, and window dissipation from nucleon transfer, and compare it to the spectrum from pure Coulomb repulsion. For alpha decay, they assume the alpha particle emerges from the Coulomb barrier at rest and then accelerates away from the daughter nucleus.

## Key numbers

- For alpha decay of ²⁵²Cf, the Q value is 6.2 MeV and the relative distance between the alpha and daughter nucleus at the moment the alpha exits the Coulomb barrier is determined by this Q value.
- For symmetric fission of ²⁵²Cf, the relative distance between the two fission fragments when they are touching is 12.0 fm.
- The characteristic tunneling time is Δτ = ħ/(2m_T), where m_T is the transverse mass of the positron.
- The change in kinetic energy of the ions at the moment of pair creation, ΔE_k, must be ≥ 2m_e (twice the electron mass).
- For ²⁵²Cf, the positron energy spectrum shows a Coulomb divergence at approximately E₊ ≈ 30 MeV when β = 0 (acceleration after pair creation), which disappears when β = 1 (acceleration before pair creation).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous treatment of pair production in sub-barrier nuclear collisions (Settlemyre, 2021) to the cases of alpha decay and nuclear fission. The neck model for fission dynamics follows the approach of Randrup (window dissipation formula) and prior neck model treatments. The proposed experimental signature, looking for positrons in coincidence with a fission fragment but not with an electron, differs from previous experimental investigations (Tsunoda) that searched for e⁺e⁻ coincidences. The results suggest that measuring the positron energy spectrum could reveal the dynamics of the fission process, properties of vacuum polarization, and tunneling dynamics, while also potentially affecting sub-barrier fusion cross sections relevant to carbon-carbon fusion in stellar cores.
