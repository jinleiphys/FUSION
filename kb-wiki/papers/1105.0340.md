# Longitudinal correlation of the triangular flow event plane in a hybrid approach with hadron and parton cascade initial conditions

> The longitudinal long-range correlations of the triangular flow event plane angles are calculated in a Boltzmann + hydrodynamics hybrid approach. The potential to disentangle different energy deposition scenarios is explored by utilizing two different transport approaches for the early non-equilibrium evolution. In the hadronic transport approach the particle production in high energy heavy ion reactions is mainly governed by string excitation and fragmentation processes which are absent in the parton cascade approach. We find that in both approaches the initial state shows a strong longitudinal correlation of the event plane angles which is diluted but still persists in the final state momentum space distributions of the produced particles. A ridge-like structure can also be caused by near-collinear gluon radiation in a parton cascade approach and does not necessarily prove longitudinal flux tubes in the initial state.

## Key claim

The longitudinal correlation of the triangular flow event plane angle is present in both hadron-string and parton cascade initial conditions, but the correlation in the parton cascade approach arises from near-collinear gluon radiation rather than from longitudinal flux tubes, meaning that a ridge-like structure does not necessarily prove the existence of flux tubes in the initial state.

## Method

The authors used a (3+1)-dimensional Boltzmann + hydrodynamics hybrid approach, with initial conditions generated either by the hadron-string dynamics of UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) or by a parton cascade approach (PCM). The initial state event plane angles for triangular flow were calculated in coordinate space across different rapidity bins, and the same analysis was repeated for the final state momentum space event plane angles after ideal hydrodynamic evolution and hadronic rescattering. The correlation was quantified by comparing the distribution of differences between the event plane angle in the full rapidity range and in individual rapidity slices.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV, the starting time for hydrodynamic evolution was chosen as \(t_{\rm start}=0.5\) fm to fit the final state pion multiplicity at midrapidity.
- The Gaussian smearing width for mapping point particles to densities was \(\sigma=1\) fm for UrQMD initial conditions and \(\sigma=0.5\) fm for PCM initial conditions.
- The transition from hydrodynamic evolution to transport occurred at an energy density of 713 MeV/fm\(^3\) for the hadronic calculation.
- The longitudinal correlation of the triangular flow event plane angle survived over approximately three units in rapidity around \(y=0\) in the final state.

## Context

This work extends the study of triangular flow and initial state fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, following the proposal by Alver (2010) that initial state fluctuations are the major source of structures in two-particle correlations. It directly builds on the authors' previous calculation of the pseudorapidity dependence of triangular flow (Petersen, 2010), which showed flatness over a broad range but did not prove actual correlation. The paper enables experimental measurement of triangular flow event plane angles in different rapidity bins to test whether the event plane angle is constant over rapidity, a common assumption in experimental analyses.
