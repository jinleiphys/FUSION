# The mutable nature of particle-core excitations with spin in the one-valence-proton nucleus 133Sb

> The gamma-ray decay of excited states of the one-valence-proton nucleus 133Sb has been studied using cold-neutron induced fission of 235U and 241Pu targets, during the EXILL campaign at the ILL reactor in Grenoble. By using a highly efficient HPGe array, coincidences between gamma-rays prompt with the fission event and those delayed up to several tens of microseconds were investigated, allowing to observe, for the first time, high-spin excited states above the 16.6 micros isomer. Lifetimes analysis, performed by fast-timing techniques with LaBr3(Ce) scintillators, reveals a difference of almost two orders of magnitude in B(M1) strength for transitions between positive-parity medium-spin yrast states. The data are interpreted by a newly developed microscopic model which takes into account couplings between core excitations (both collective and non-collective) of the doubly magic nucleus 132Sn and the valence proton, using the Skyrme effective interaction in a consistent way. The results point to a fast change in the nature of particle-core excitations with increasing spin.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the nature of particle-core excitations in the one-valence-proton nucleus 133Sb changes rapidly with increasing spin, from collective to non-collective character, based on a newly developed microscopic model that reproduces the observed large difference (almost two orders of magnitude) in B(M1) strength for transitions between positive-parity medium-spin yrast states.

## Method
The experiment used cold-neutron induced fission of 235U and 241Pu targets during the EXILL campaign at the ILL reactor in Grenoble, employing a highly efficient HPGe array (8 EXOGAM clovers, 6 GASP coaxial detectors, 2 ILL-Clover detectors) with a total photopeak efficiency of about 6% at 1.3 MeV. Coincidences between gamma rays prompt with the fission event and those delayed up to several tens of microseconds were investigated using a fully digital, triggerless acquisition system with time stamp intervals of 10 ns, allowing observation of high-spin excited states above the 16.6 microsecond isomer. Lifetimes were measured by fast-timing techniques with LaBr3(Ce) scintillators (FATIMA array), and the data were interpreted using a newly developed Hybrid Configuration Mixing (HCM) model based on Hartree-Fock and Random Phase Approximation calculations with the Skyrme SkX interaction.

## Key numbers
- Half-life of the 13/2+ state: T1/2 = 31(8) ps (average from 235U data giving 32(10) ps and 241Pu data giving 30(12) ps)
- Half-life of the 15/2+ state: T1/2 less than 20 ps (from 235U data giving less than 17 ps and 241Pu data giving less than 21 ps)
- B(M1) for the 15/2+ to 13/2+ transition: greater than 0.24 W.u.
- B(M1) for the 13/2+ to 11/2+ transition: 0.0042(15) W.u.
- Ratio of B(M1) values between the two transitions: approximately 60 experimentally, approximately 20 from theory
- Newly identified gamma-ray transitions above the isomer: 207.9(4) keV, 318.0(4) keV, 561(1) keV, and 243 keV
- New levels located above the isomer: 4734+x, 4844+x, and 5087+x keV (where x is less than 30 keV)
- Core excitations of 132Sn: 2+ at 4041 keV (7 W.u.), 3- at 4352 keV (greater than 7.1 W.u.), 4+ at 4416 keV (4.42 W.u.)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of 133Sb that had identified states up to the 21/2+ isomer from isomer and beta-decay studies (Urban 2000, Urban 2009, Genevey 2000, Sanchez-Vega 1999), but no information existed on positive-parity levels above this isomer. The results contradict the simplified shell-model picture of Urban (2000, 2009) which would predict similar B(M1) values for the transitions, whereas the new HCM model explains the large quenching. The work enables complementary studies with direct reactions to assess spectroscopic factors of single particle states of 133Sb, analogous to the case of the one-valence-neutron nucleus 133Sn (Allmond 2014).
