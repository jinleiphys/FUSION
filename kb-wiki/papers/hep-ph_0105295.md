# First principle derivation of semiclassical force for electroweak baryogenesis

> We perform a systematic gradient expansion on kinetic equations and derive the CP-violating semiclassical force for fermions propagating in presence of a CP-violating wall at a first order electroweak phase transition. The force appears at order h-bar in the flow term of the kinetic equation and agrees with the semiclassical force used for baryogenesis computations. In particular we consider the force for charginos in both the MSSM and NMSSM. We then study the continuity equations for the vector and axial vector currents and stress the role of the latter as the one containing baryogenesis sources. We also show that there is no CP-violating force for bosons to order h-bar in gradient expansion.

## Key claim

The paper provides a rigorous first-principle derivation of the CP-violating semiclassical force for fermions at a first-order electroweak phase transition, showing that the force appears at order \(\hbar\) in the flow term of the kinetic equation and agrees with previous WKB-based results; the derivation is limited to the collisionless limit and to first order in the gradient expansion, and the authors explicitly show that there is no CP-violating force for bosons to this order.

## Method

The authors start from the exact Dirac equation for the Wightman function \(G^<_{\alpha\beta}(u,v)\) with a CP-violating spatially varying pseudoscalar mass term \(m(z) = |m(z)|e^{i\theta(z)}\), reduce the problem to 1+1 dimensions by considering particles moving perpendicular to the phase boundary, and perform a Wigner transform followed by a systematic gradient expansion in powers of \(\hbar\). They then separate the resulting equations into constraint equations (which yield the dispersion relation and spectral decomposition) and kinetic equations (which yield the Liouville equation for the on-shell distribution functions), and derive explicit expressions for the quasiparticle energy, group velocity, and semiclassical force for a single chiral fermion and for \(N\) mixing fermions, including charginos in the MSSM and NMSSM.

## Key numbers

- Quasiparticle energy for a single chiral fermion: \(\omega_{s\pm} = \omega_0 \mp \hbar \frac{s |m|^2\theta'}{2\omega^2_0}\), where \(\omega_0 \equiv \sqrt{k_z^2 + |m|^2}\).
- Semiclassical force: \(F_{s\pm} = - \frac{{|m|^2}^{\,\prime} }{2\omega_{s\pm}} \pm \hbar \frac{s(|m|^2\theta^{\,\prime})^{\,\prime}}{2\omega_0^2}\).
- Typical wall width at the electroweak phase transition: \(\ell_{\rm wall} \sim 10/T\), with de Broglie wavelength \(\ell_{\rm dB} \sim 1/T\), satisfying \(\ell_{\rm dB} \ll \ell_{\rm wall}\).
- Normalization factor: \(Z_{s\pm} = 1 \mp s\frac{|m|^2\theta'}{2\omega^3_0} = \frac{\omega_{s\pm}}{\omega_0}\).

## Context

This work extends and rigorously justifies the WKB-based approach to electroweak baryogenesis transport equations previously developed by Cline, Joyce, and Kainulainen (2000) and by Joyce, Prokopec, and Turok (1995, 1996), resolving a controversy in the literature where different methods (e.g., the relaxation time approximation of Carena, Quiros, Riotto, Vilja, and Wagner (1997) and the WKB method) gave parametrically different results for chargino and squark sources in the MSSM. The derivation enables a controlled extension beyond first order in \(\hbar\) (which cannot be achieved by WKB methods) and provides a foundation for including collision terms in a model-dependent way, as well as for studying the spontaneous baryogenesis source in the relaxation time approximation.
