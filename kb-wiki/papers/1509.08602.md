# Transverse momentum and pseudorapidity distributions of final-state particles and spatial structure pictures of interacting system in p-Pb collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02 TeV

> The transverse momentum and pseudorapidity distributions of final-state particles produced in proton-lead (p-Pb) collisions at center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair \sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02 TeV are studied in the framework of a multisource thermal model. Experimental results measured by the ALICE and CMS Collaborations are described by the Tsallis transverse momentum distribution and the two-cylinder pseudorapidity distribution. Based on the parameter values extracted from the transverse momentum and pseudorapidity distributions, some other quantities are extracted. Then, the structure pictures of interacting system at the stage of kinetic freeze-out in some spaces are obtained.

# Transverse momentum and pseudorapidity distributions of final-state particles and spatial structure pictures of interacting system in p-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV

## Key claim

The Tsallis distribution with two free parameters (T and q) can describe the transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, protons, and lambdas in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV across different centrality intervals, but only in the narrow transverse momentum region where soft yields dominate and hard (jet) contributions can be neglected; the extracted effective temperatures are less than the lower limit of the expected critical temperature for quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The authors apply a multisource thermal model in which multiple emission sources are formed in the collision, classified into groups with local equilibrium states described by Tsallis statistics. They fit the Tsallis transverse momentum distribution (Eq. 2) to ALICE and CMS data for π±, K±, p+anti-p, and Λ+anti-Λ in p-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, extracting the effective temperature T and non-equilibrium parameter q. For pseudorapidity distributions, they use a two-cylinder model (target p-cylinder and projectile Pb-cylinder) with each source described by Tsallis statistics, neglecting flow velocity as a first approximation.

## Key numbers

- For charged particles in NSD p-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the weighted average values extracted from transverse momentum fits are T = 93 ± 4 MeV, q = 1.142 ± 0.002, and m0 = 174 ± 2 MeV/c²
- The pseudorapidity distribution fit yields cylinder rapidity ranges: y_Tmin = -2.65 ± 0.08, y_Tmax = 0.01 ± 0.01, y_Pmin = 0.01 ± 0.01, y_Pmax = 3.79 ± 0.10, with target cylinder contribution ratio K_T = 0.393 ± 0.002 and χ²/dof = 0.046
- The effective temperature T decreases with increasing centrality percentage, with intercept values (b) in T-C correlations indicating maximum temperatures of 122-311 MeV and minimum temperatures of 66-91 MeV
- The non-equilibrium parameter q increases with centrality percentage, with intercept values (b) in q-C correlations indicating minimum q of 1.06-1.13 and maximum q of 1.14-1.15
- From T-q correlations, q approaches 1.12-1.21 as T approaches 0

## Context

This work extends the multisource thermal model previously applied by Liu et al. to Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (2014), now applied to asymmetric p-Pb collisions at LHC energies. The Tsallis statistics approach follows the formalism developed by Tsallis and applied in high-energy collisions by various authors (Tsallis 1988; Alberico et al. 2000; Cleymans et al. 2012). The results are compared with experimental data from the ALICE Collaboration (Abelev et al. 2013, 2014) and CMS Collaboration (Chatrchyan et al. 2013). The extracted structure pictures in rapidity, momentum, velocity, and coordinate spaces provide a visualization of the interacting system at kinetic freeze-out, enabling future comparisons with hydrodynamic models and studies of flow effects that were neglected in this first approximation.
