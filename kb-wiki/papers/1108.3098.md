# Sound Waves from Quenched Jets

> Heavy ion collisions at RHIC/LHC energies are well described by the (nearly ideal) hydrodynamics. Last year this success has been extended to higher angular harmonics, $v_n,n=3..9$ induced by initial-state perturbations, in analogy to cosmic microwave background fluctuations. Here we use hydrodynamics to study sound propagation emitted by quenched jets. We use the so called "geometric acoustics" to follow the sound propagation, on top of the expanding fireball. The conical waves, known as "Mach cones", turn out to be strongly distorted. We show that large radial flow makes the observed particle spectra to be determined mostlly by the vicinity of their intersection with the fireball's space-like and time-like freezeout surfaces. We further show how the waves modify the freezeout surfaces and spectra. We end up comparing our calculations to the two-particle correlation functions at RHIC, while emphasizing that studies of dijet events observed at LHC should provide much better test of our theory.

## Key claim
The authors use geometric acoustics to show that Mach cones from quenched jets are strongly distorted by the radial flow of the expanding fireball, and that the observed particle spectra are determined mostly by the intersection of the sound waves with the fireball's freezeout surfaces, rather than by the Mach cone angle alone.

## Method
The authors solve Hamilton's equations for sound propagation ("phonons") in a moving fluid using the geometric acoustics approximation, both in non-relativistic and relativistic forms, on top of a Hubble-like expanding fireball background. They model the jet energy loss by assuming a stopping distance of 6 fm and place hard scattering events at 1 fm from the nuclear edge for central Au-Au collisions. The resulting Mach cones and sound circles are then intersected with the fireball's time-like and space-like freezeout surfaces to compute particle spectra via the Cooper-Frye formula.

## Key numbers
- The time-averaged sound velocity over QGP, mixed and hadronic phases is <c_s> ≈ 0.4, giving a Mach cone angle θ_M = arccos(<c_s>/v_jet) ≈ 1.159 rad ≈ 66.4 degrees.
- The Hubble constant used is H = 0.075 fm^{-1}, with freezeout time t_f = 12 fm and the Hubble expansion starting at t_0 ≈ 5 fm.
- The jet stopping distance is L_stopping = 6 fm, and the outer fireball radius at freezeout is R_f = exp(H·(t_f - t_0)) ≈ 10.14 fm.
- The intermediate transverse momentum region for hydrodynamically described particles is p_t = 2-3 GeV, while hard trigger hadrons have p_t > 6 GeV and associate particles are in the hydro domain p_t = 1-3 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamical description of heavy ion collisions from harmonic flows (v_n for n=3..9, studied in earlier papers by the same group, Papers I, II, and III) to sound propagation from quenched jets. It builds on the Mach cone idea proposed by Casalderrey-Solana (2004) and Horst, and on direct hydrodynamical simulations by Betz (2010). The paper contrasts with earlier work by Casalderrey-Solana and Shuryak (2005) that used a Hubble-like metric expansion, and it enables predictions for dijet events at LHC, which should provide better tests than existing RHIC two-particle correlation data.
