# Productions of Heavy Flavored Mesons in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions in the Recombination Model

> We get the distributions of shower partons initiated by heavy quarks $c$ and $b$ by studying the fragmentation functions in the framework of the recombination model. The transverse momentum spectra of heavy flavored mesons are predicted with these distributions. We find that the contribution from the recombination of thermal-shower partons is an important part in the total spectrum for the mesons. We predict the heavy flavored meson productions for different centralities with the heavy quark fugacities fitted by the experimental data of $J/ψ$ transverse momentum spectra in Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

The recombination of thermal-shower partons provides an important contribution to the total transverse momentum spectrum of heavy flavored mesons, particularly in the region of $p_T > 1$ GeV/$c$, though at very high $p_T$ the shower-shower (fragmentation) process will become dominant.

## Method

The authors compute shower parton distributions (SPDs) for charm and bottom quarks by inverting fragmentation functions within the recombination model framework. They then calculate the transverse momentum spectra of heavy flavored mesons ($D^0$, $D_s$, $B$, $B_s$, $B_c$) as the sum of three recombination terms: thermal-thermal ($\mathcal{TT}$), thermal-shower ($\mathcal{TS}$), and shower-shower ($\mathcal{SS}$), with the heavy quark fugacities fitted to experimental $J/\psi$ data from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark fugacity $\gamma_c = 0.26$ and radial flow velocity $v_T = 0.3c$ fitted from $J/\psi$ transverse momentum spectra in central Au+Au collisions
- For $D^0$ production, radial flow velocity adjusted to $v_T = 0.42c$ to fit STAR data at $p_T < 2$ GeV/$c$
- Hadronization parameters: temperature $T = 175$ MeV, proper time $\tau = 5$ fm, transverse area radius $\rho_0 = 9$ fm
- Energy loss factor $\beta L = 2.39$ for $0-20\%$ centrality Au+Au collisions, applied equally to light quarks, gluons, and heavy quarks $c$ and $b$
- For $D^0$ meson, the wave function parameter ratio $a/b = m_u/m_c \simeq 1/5$ ($a=1$); for $D_s$, $a/b = m_s/m_c \simeq 3/10$ ($a=3$)
- For $b$-flavored mesons: $a/b = m_d/m_{\overline{b}} \simeq 3/50$ ($a=3$) for $B$; $a/b = m_s/m_{\overline{b}} \simeq 4.5/50$ ($a=4.5$) for $B_s$; $a/b = m_c/m_{\overline{b}} \simeq 3/10$ ($a=3$) for $B_c$
- Peterson fragmentation parameters for $B_s$: $N=0.0201$, $\epsilon=0.0177$; for $B_c$: $N=0.0227$, $\epsilon=0.0098$
- For non-central collisions, centrality-dependent parameters: $\gamma_c = \gamma_b$ ranges from 0.26 (0-20%) to 0.06 (60-92%); $\beta L$ ranges from 2.39 (0-20%) to 0.78 (60-92%)

## Context

This work extends the recombination model previously applied to light hadrons (Peng and Yang, 2010) and $J/\psi$ production (Peng and Yang, 2010) to heavy flavored mesons containing $c$ and $b$ quarks. The approach builds on the shower parton distribution formalism developed in earlier recombination model studies (Hwa and Yang, 2003; Hwa and Yang, 2004) and uses fragmentation functions from the literature (Kniehl et al., 2001; AKK collaboration, 2005). The results enable predictions for $D^0$, $D_s$, $B$, $B_s$, and $B_c$ production across different centralities in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, providing testable spectra for comparison with future experimental measurements.
