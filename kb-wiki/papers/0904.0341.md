# New insight on pseudospin doublets in nuclei

> The relevance of the pseudospin symmetry in nuclei is considered. New insight is obtained from looking at the continuous transition from a model satisfying the spin symmetry to another one satisfying the pseudospin symmetry. This study suggests that there are models allowing no missing single-particle states in this transition, contrary to what is usually advocated. It rather points out to an association of pseudospin partners different from the one usually assumed, together with a strong violation of the corresponding symmetry. A comparison with results obtained from some relativistic approaches is made.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to the widely accepted view that pseudospin symmetry requires missing single-particle states (the "intruder" states), a continuous transition from a spin-symmetry model to a pseudospin-symmetry model shows all states are present but in a different order, implying a different association of pseudospin partners than the standard one.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-relativistic Hamiltonian with a potential that is a linear combination of a local spin-symmetry term and a non-local pseudospin-symmetry term, weighted by a parameter x varying from 0 to 1. They solved for single-particle spectra and wave functions using both harmonic-oscillator and Woods-Saxon potentials, tracking the evolution of states from the spin-symmetry limit (x=0) to the pseudospin-symmetry limit (x=1). They also extended the analysis to a relativistic Dirac framework to compare with prior relativistic mean-field results.

## Key numbers

* For the Woods-Saxon model, the extra node appearing in the tail of the wave function for orbits with j = l + 1/2 contributes at most 3% to the norm, compared to several 10% for a usual node.
* The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used were V0 = 40 MeV, r0 = 5 fm, and a = 0.65 fm, appropriate for a nucleus such as 40Ca.
* The harmonic-oscillator parameter was set to omega0 = 7 MeV so that the spectra of the HO and WS models roughly overlap.
* The transition parameter x was varied from -0.2 to 1.0, with x = -0.2 recovering the usual spin-orbit splitting and the standard pseudospin doublets.

## Context

This work extends the debate on pseudospin symmetry in nuclei, which was introduced by Arima (1969) and Hecht (1969) and given a relativistic explanation by Ginocchio (1997) based on the cancellation of scalar and vector self-energies. The authors directly challenge the conclusion of Leviatan (2001) that pseudospin partners of intruder states are missing, and they compare their non-relativistic spectra to the relativistic results of Chen et al. (2002) and Lisboa et al. (2004). The study enables a re-examination of pseudospin doublet assignments and suggests that the form of the nuclear potential, not just the cancellation of self-energies, is crucial for understanding the phenomenon.
