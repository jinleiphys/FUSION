# The weak magnetic field effect on dilepton polarization in heavy-ion collisions

> The measurement of the magnetic field created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions is challenging, due the the fact that the magnetic field decays so drastically that in a thermalized quark-gluon plasma the field strength becomes rather weak. By incorporating the weak magnetic effect into the medium, and especially into the production formalism of dileptons from the quark-gluon plasma, the effect of dilepton polarization is studied through the dilepton angular distribution. We find that the anisotropic coefficients in the dilepton spectrum are quite sensitive to the orientation and strength of the weak field. Accordingly, these coefficients provide ideal probes for the magnetic field in realistic experiments.

## Key claim

The anisotropic coefficients in the dilepton angular distribution are sensitive to the orientation and strength of a weak magnetic field (satisfying \(|eB| \ll m_\pi^2\)) inside the quark-gluon plasma, making them ideal probes for the magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions, though the field decays so drastically that it becomes rather weak in the thermalized medium.

## Method

The authors incorporate a weak magnetic field into the quark-gluon plasma by adding a dissipative correction to the quark distribution function, derived from the electrical current induced by the field. They then compute the dilepton production rate from the \(q\bar{q} \to \gamma^* \to l\bar{l}\) process using the photon self-energy and lepton tensor, and extract the five anisotropic coefficients (\(\lambda_\theta\), \(\lambda_\phi\), \(\lambda_{\theta\phi}\), \(\lambda_\phi^\perp\), \(\lambda_{\theta\phi}^\perp\)) from the angular distribution of the dilepton in the helicity frame. The calculation is performed for a Bjorken-flow expanding QGP with a time-dependent magnetic field \(B(\tau) = B_0 / [1 + (\tau/\tau_B)^2]\), using parameters for mid-central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength at the instant of collision is taken as \(e B_0 = 3 m_\pi^2\), with \(m_\pi\) the pion mass.
- The magnetic field lifetime \(\tau_B\) is varied from 0.2 fm/c to 0.4 fm/c and 0.6 fm/c.
- The initial QGP temperature is \(T(\tau_0) = 300\) MeV at \(\tau_0 = 1.0\) fm/c, and dilepton production is integrated from \(\tau_0\) to \(\tau_f = 5\) fm/c.
- The electrical conductivity is set to \(\sigma_{\rm el}/T = 2\), extracted from perturbative QCD.
- The electron mass is \(m_e = 0.51\) MeV, and the up and down quark masses are \(m_u = m_d = 5\) MeV.
- The invariant mass of the virtual photon is fixed at \(M = 2\) GeV for the \(\lambda_\theta\) results shown in Fig. 3.

## Context

This work extends the study of weak magnetic field effects on direct photon emission (Sun 2023) and local lambda hyperon polarization (Sun 2024) to the case of dilepton polarization. It contrasts with previous studies that assumed a strong magnetic field (Tuchin 2013, Sadooghi 2016, Das 2019, Wang 2020, Chaudhuri 2021, Wang 2022) by treating the field as weak (\(|eB| \ll m_\pi^2\)) and incorporating it via dissipative corrections to the quark distribution. The results enable experimental extraction of the magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions through measurement of the anisotropic coefficients in the dilepton angular distribution, particularly \(\lambda_\theta\).
