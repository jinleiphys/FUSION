# Impact of nuclear "pasta" on neutrino transport in collapsing stellar cores

> Nuclear ``pasta'', nonspherical nuclei in dense matter, is predicted to occur in collapsing supernova cores. We show how pasta phases affect the neutrino transport cross section via weak neutral current using several nuclear models. This is the first calculation of the neutrino opacity of the phases with rod-like and slab-like nuclei taking account of finite temperature effects, which are well described by the quantum molecular dynamics. We also show that pasta phases can occupy 10--20% of the mass of supernova cores in the later stage of the collapse.

# Impact of nuclear "pasta" on neutrino transport in collapsing stellar cores

## Key claim

Nuclear pasta phases (rod-like and slab-like nuclei) modify the neutrino transport cross section in collapsing supernova cores, with the effect depending on whether the calculation uses liquid drop models or quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations that include finite temperature effects and disorder. The pasta phases can occupy 10–20% of the mass of supernova cores in the later stage of collapse, but the actual impact on neutrino opacity depends on the specific nuclear model and temperature.

## Method

The authors calculated the angle-averaged transport cross section for elastic neutrino scattering via weak neutral current from pasta phases, using the partial static structure factor formalism within the Wigner-Seitz approximation. They compared results from two approaches: a liquid drop model based on Baym, Bethe, and Pethick (BBP) and the Lattimer-Swesty equation of state (LS-EOS), and QMD simulations using two different nuclear force models (model 1 from Maruyama et al. and model 2 from Chikazumi et al. and Kido et al.) at a proton fraction of 0.3. For the QMD simulations, they calculated the angle-averaged radial distribution function from snapshots with 16384 or 2048 nucleons and obtained the static structure factor via Fourier transform with a Hanning window.

## Key numbers

- The amplification factor ⟨S̄(Eν)⟩ for the transport cross section is zero below Eν ≈ 25 MeV, rapidly increases at 25–30 MeV, peaks around 30–40 MeV, and approaches x_n + (c_v^(p)/c_v^(n))^2 x_p above 100 MeV in the liquid drop model at Y_L = 0.3
- At a fixed density of 0.0165 fm⁻³ and T = 1 MeV, the LS-EOS calculation shows the peak of ⟨S̄(Eν)⟩ decreases monotonically with increasing temperature
- At a fixed density of 0.0660 fm⁻³ in QMD model 2, the phase with slab-like nuclei at 1–2 MeV transforms to rod-like bubbles at 3–4 MeV, above which nuclear surface cannot be identified
- The pasta phases occupy 0.13 M⊙ (14% of the nuclear region) for non-rotating cores (T/|W| = 0) and 0.30 M⊙ (23% of the nuclear region) for rotating cores (T/|W| = 0.01) just before bounce in simulations of a 15 M⊙ progenitor
- The radius range for pasta phases is 1.1–1.3 × 10⁶ cm for non-rotating cores and 0.8–1.6 × 10⁶ cm for rotating cores

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrino opacity in dense matter by Itoh (1975) on spatial correlations among nuclei and by Horowitz et al. on QMD simulations, which did not reproduce rod-like and slab-like nuclei due to a short screening length of 10 fm. The authors use nuclear data from their own previous liquid drop model calculations (Watanabe et al., 2003, 2005) and the Lattimer-Swesty equation of state (1991), and perform two-dimensional axisymmetric simulations of rotational core collapse using the Shen et al. equation of state. The results enable future construction of systematic numerical tables or fitting formulas for the elastic neutrino scattering cross section in pasta phases, which can be incorporated into hydrodynamic core collapse simulations to study the effect on lepton fraction and explosion dynamics.
