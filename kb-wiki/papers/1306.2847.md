# Correlations between charge radii, E0 transitions, and M1 strength

> In the framework of the interacting boson model, relations are derived between nuclear charge radii, electric monopole transition rates, and summed magnetic dipole transition in even-even nuclei. The proposed correlations are tested in the rare-earth region.

## Key claim

In the interacting boson model (IBM-1), correlations are derived between nuclear charge radii, electric monopole (E0) transition rates, and summed magnetic dipole (M1) strength in even-even nuclei, but the relations are model-dependent and the tests in the rare-earth region are limited by large experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The author uses the simplest version of the interacting boson model (IBM-1), which describes nuclear collective excitations in terms of s and d bosons without distinguishing neutron and proton bosons. Charge radius, E0 transition, and M1 operators are expressed in terms of the d-boson number operator, and analytic relations are derived connecting isotope shifts, isomer shifts, and summed M1 strength. The proposed correlations are tested against experimental data for even-even isotopes from Ce (Z=58) to W (Z=74), using a constant coefficient η = 0.50 fm² for all isotopes while adjusting the coefficient |α| for each isotope series separately.

## Key numbers

- η = 0.50 fm², the constant coefficient in the charge radius operator kept fixed for all isotopes from Ce to W
- |g_ν − g_π| ≈ 0.83 μ_N, the difference between neutron and proton boson g-factors obtained by adjusting the summed M1 strength expression to observed data in rare-earth nuclei
- The ratio r₀²/η = 3.08, determined from a fit to radii, with effective charges e_n = 0.5e and e_p = e
- The largest peaks in isotope shifts occur for ¹⁵²⁻¹⁵⁰Sm, ¹⁵⁴⁻¹⁵²Gd, and ¹⁵⁶⁻¹⁵⁴Dy (the difference in radii between N=90 and N=88 isotopes)
- The correlation between summed M1 strength and ρ(E0) values is tested for eight nuclei: ¹⁵⁰,¹⁵²,¹⁵⁴Sm, ¹⁵⁴,¹⁵⁶,¹⁵⁸Gd, ¹⁶⁶Er, and ¹⁷²Yb
- The ¹⁶⁶Er point follows from a recent experiment where the fourth J^π = 0⁺ level at 1934 keV was identified as the β-vibrational band head

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Zerguine (2008, 2012) that proposed a simultaneous description of charge radii and electric monopole transitions in the rare-earth region. The new correlations between summed M1 strength and charge radii differ from an earlier relation proposed by Heyde (1993) which correlated summed M1 strength with charge radii themselves rather than their differences. The results enable future tests of the IBM-1 framework, particularly for extending the analysis to transitions between J^π ≠ 0⁺ states and generalizing the relations to transitional nuclei, while highlighting the need for more precise experimental data on isotope shifts through the shape transition region.
