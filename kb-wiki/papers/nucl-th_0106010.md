# Baryon Fluctuations and the QCD Phase Transition

> The dynamic separation into phases of high and low baryon density in a heavy ion collision can enhance fluctuations of the net rapidity density of baryons compared to model expectations. We demonstrate that event-by-event proton and antiproton measurements can be used to observe this phenomenon. We then perform real-time lattice simulations to show how these fluctuations arise and how they can survive through freeze out.

# Baryon Fluctuations and the QCD Phase Transition

## Key claim

Event-by-event proton and antiproton measurements can reveal super-Poissonian baryon number fluctuations that arise from phase separation during a first-order QCD phase transition at high baryon density, provided the expansion time scale is sufficiently large relative to the droplet growth time scale; the effect may also apply to RHIC collisions if the low baryon density systems approximate a first order transition.

## Method

The authors formulate a dissipative-hydrodynamic model (Model B in the condensed matter classification) for baryon density evolution, combining a Ginzburg-Landau free energy with baryon diffusion and Bjorken longitudinal expansion. They perform 2+1 dimensional real-time lattice simulations using a forward Euler method with thermal noise seeding, solving the dimensionless evolution equation for the order parameter on a lattice with next-nearest-neighbor spatial derivatives.

## Key numbers

- The baryon diffusion coefficient is taken as D ~ 8 fm, yielding a spinodal decomposition time scale τ_R ~ 1 fm for a correlation length ξ ~ 1 fm
- The fastest-growing mode wavenumber is k_R = √(m²/2κ), with growth time τ_R = 8ξ²/D
- At RHIC, N_antiproton/N_proton ~ 0.6 at √s = 130 A·GeV; at top SPS energy (√s = 17.5 A·GeV), the ratio is ~6%
- The system reaches the critical density at τ_0 = 5 fm; fluctuations grow appreciably by τ ~ 2τ_0
- For Au+Au collisions at b < 10 fm, the estimated upper bound from wounded nucleon model is Ω_p(Au+Au) < 0.01, using N(b) ≈ 59 for b = 10 fm and 372 for b = 0
- The system is unstable only for τ < 2.3 τ_0 for the chosen free energy parameters

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon number fluctuations as a probe of the QCD phase transition order, following earlier proposals by Mishustin and Gavin, and builds on the theoretical framework of Rajagopal for the high baryon density regime where the transition is first order. The authors generalize condensed matter techniques (Bray) for spinodal decomposition to the nonequilibrium context of heavy ion collisions. The results enable experimental searches using proton and antiproton measurements at RHIC and SPS, and complement the work of Jeon, Koch, and Asakawa on charge fluctuations from hadronization without a phase transition. Future work requires more realistic free energy parameterizations informed by lattice QCD (Gavai, Fodor) and comparison with RQMD and HIJING benchmark simulations.
