# Charge symmetry violation in the parton distributions of the nucleon

> We point out that charge symmetry violation in both the valence and sea quark distributions of the nucleon has a non-perturbative source. We calculate this non-perturbative charge symmetry violation using the meson cloud model, which has earlier been successfully applied to both the study of SU(2) flavour asymmetry in the nucleon sea and quark-antiquark asymmetry in the nucleon. We find that the charge symmetry violation in the valence quark distribution is well below 1%, which is consistent with most low energy tests but significantly smaller than the quark model prediction about 5%-10%. Our prediction for the charge symmetry violation in the sea quark distribution is also much smaller than the quark model calculation.

## Key claim

Charge symmetry violation (CSV) in both the valence and sea quark distributions of the nucleon has a non-perturbative origin, and the meson cloud model predicts CSV in the valence quark distribution well below 1%, significantly smaller than the quark model prediction of 5%-10%, and also much smaller for the sea quark distribution.

## Method

The authors used the meson cloud model, in which the physical nucleon fluctuates into virtual baryon-meson Fock states (e.g., N → Nπ, N → Δπ, N → ΛK, N → ΣK). They calculated the CSV by taking into account the mass differences among the baryons and mesons in the corresponding fluctuations of the proton and neutron, which cause differences in the fluctuation probabilities and thus in the non-perturbative contributions to the parton distributions. The parton distributions in the virtual baryons and mesons were taken from the GRS98 and GRV98 parameterizations and evolved to Q² = 4 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The probability of the fluctuation p → nπ⁺ is 0.202, while n → pπ⁻ is 0.205, giving about a 1% excess of the latter.
- The probability of p → Δ⁺⁺π⁻ is 0.0481, while n → Δ⁻π⁺ is 0.0475, also about a 1% excess.
- The probability of p → ΛK⁺ is 0.0127, while n → ΛK⁰ is 0.0125, about a 2% excess.
- The ratio δd_v/dᵖ_v exhibits a maximum of about 0.2% at x = 0.1.
- The ratio δu_v/uᵖ_v is smaller than 0.3% in the region x > 0.02.
- The mass differences used include m_p - m_n = -1.3 MeV, m_{π±} - m_{π⁰} = 4.6 MeV, and m_{K⁰} - m_{K⁺} = 4.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the meson cloud model, previously applied to SU(2) flavour asymmetry (Thomas, 1983; Holtmann et al.; Speth et al.; Melnitchouk et al.; Kumano) and quark-antiquark asymmetry (Holtmann et al.; Signal and Thomas; Cao and Signal), to the study of charge symmetry violation. It contradicts quark model calculations by Sather (1992), Rodionov et al. (1994), Benesh et al. (1995, 1996), and Ma (1996), which predicted CSV in valence distributions of 5%-10% and positive δd_v for most x values. The results are consistent with low-energy tests of charge symmetry and with the phenomenological analysis of Boros et al. (1999) which found no significant CSV in the strange sea. The work enables more reliable predictions for CSV in parton distributions, particularly in the low-x region where quark-diquark models are unreliable.
