# Nuclear equation of state for core-collapse supernova simulations with realistic nuclear forces

> A new table of the nuclear equation of state (EOS) based on realistic nuclear potentials is constructed for core-collapse supernova numerical simulations. Adopting the EOS of uniform nuclear matter constructed by two of the present authors with the cluster variational method starting from the Argonne v18 and Urbana IX nuclear potentials, the Thomas-Fermi calculation is performed to obtain the minimized free energy of a Wigner-Seitz cell in non-uniform nuclear matter. As a preparation for the Thomas-Fermi calculation, the EOS of uniform nuclear matter is modified so as to remove the effects of deuteron cluster formation in uniform matter at low densities. Mixing of alpha particles is also taken into account following the procedure used by Shen et al. (1998, 2011). The critical densities with respect to the phase transition from non-uniform to uniform phase with the present EOS are slightly higher than those with the Shen EOS at small proton fractions. The critical temperature with respect to the liquid-gas phase transition decreases with the proton fraction in a more gradual manner than in the Shen EOS. Furthermore, the mass and proton numbers of nuclides appearing in non-uniform nuclear matter with small proton fractions are larger than those of the Shen EOS. These results are consequences of the fact that the density derivative coefficient of the symmetry energy of our EOS is smaller than that of the Shen EOS.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new nuclear equation of state (EOS) table for core-collapse supernova simulations using realistic nuclear potentials (Argonne v18 and Urbana IX) rather than phenomenological models, but they must modify the uniform-matter EOS at low densities to remove the effects of deuteron cluster formation that arise naturally in their variational method, because those clusters would be treated separately in the subsequent Thomas-Fermi calculation for non-uniform matter.

## Method

The authors start from the EOS of uniform nuclear matter calculated with the cluster variational method using the Argonne v18 two-body and Urbana IX three-body nuclear potentials, with a two-body cluster approximation and constraints to reproduce results from Fermi Hypernetted Chain calculations. They modify the healing distance condition to suppress deuteron cluster formation at low densities, then include alpha particle mixing following the procedure of Shen et al. (1998, 2011). Finally, they perform Thomas-Fermi calculations for a Wigner-Seitz cell containing a single heavy nucleus, free neutrons, free protons, and alpha particles to obtain the minimized free energy of non-uniform nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\); saturation energy: \(E_0 = -16.09\) MeV; incompressibility: \(K = 245\) MeV; symmetry energy: \(E_{\mathrm{sym}} = 30.0\) MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass: \(2.21 M_{\odot}\); mass at critical density \(n_{\mathrm{c}} = 0.90\) fm\(^{-3}\): \(2.16 M_{\odot}\)
- Healing distance parameters: \(b_{\mathrm{h}} = 6.38\) fm, \(c_{\mathrm{h}} = 10\)
- RMS deviation of calculated nuclear masses from experimental data: 2.99 MeV for 2219 nuclides with \(Z\) and \(N \ge 2\) (modified EOS), compared to 3.09 MeV for the unmodified EOS
- Alpha particle binding energy: \(B_{\alpha} = 28.3\) MeV; effective alpha particle volume: \(v_{\alpha} = 24\) fm\(^3\)
- Gradient coefficient: \(F_0 = 68.00\) MeV fm\(^{-5}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous uniform-matter EOS (Togashi et al., 2014, 2015) to include a self-consistent non-uniform phase, whereas earlier they had connected their uniform EOS to the Shen EOS for non-uniform matter. The resulting EOS is softer than the Shen EOS (based on relativistic mean field theory with parameter set TM1), as indicated by smaller neutron star radii and a smaller density derivative coefficient of the symmetry energy (30.0 MeV versus 36.9 MeV for Shen). The new table enables core-collapse supernova simulations with an EOS derived from realistic nuclear forces rather than phenomenological models, and the authors note that the critical densities for the phase transition from non-uniform to uniform matter are slightly higher than those of the Shen EOS at small proton fractions.
