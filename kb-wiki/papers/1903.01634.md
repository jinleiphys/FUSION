# Resonance width for a particle-core coupling model with a square-well potential

> We derive a simple formula for the width of a multi-channel resonance state. To this end, we use a deformed square-well potential and solve the coupled-channels equations. We obtain the $S$-matrix in the Breit-Wigner form, from which partial widths can be extracted. We apply the resultant formula to a deformed nucleus and discuss the behavior of partial width for an $s$-wave channel.

## Key claim

The paper derives a compact, semi-analytic formula for the partial decay widths of a multi-channel resonance state in a particle-core coupling model using a deformed square-well potential, extending the single-channel formula of Bohr and Mottelson. The key hedge is that the formula is approximate and intended for qualitative estimates, requiring careful examination of several approximations for quantitative discussions.

## Method

The authors solve coupled-channels equations for a neutron-core system using a deformed square-well potential expanded to first order in the quadrupole deformation coordinate. They obtain the S-matrix in Breit-Wigner form by expanding the determinant of the logarithmic derivative matrix around the resonance energy, from which partial widths are extracted as Γ_α = 2γ²_α P_α, where γ²_α is the reduced width and P_α is the penetrability. The formula is applied to a two-channel rotational coupling between |0⁺⊗s⟩ and |2⁺⊗d⟩ channels for the deformed nucleus ³⁰Mg.

## Key numbers

- For the two-channel I=0 case with V₀ = -24.5 MeV: resonance energy E_r = 0.97 MeV and total width Γ_tot = 0.98 MeV
- For the two-channel I=0 case with V₀ = -23 MeV: resonance energy E_r = 2.07 MeV and total width Γ_tot = 0.96 MeV
- For the two-channel I=2 case with V₀ = -25.6 MeV: resonance energy E_r = 0.99 MeV and total width Γ_tot = 0.17 MeV
- For the two-channel I=2 case with V₀ = -23 MeV: resonance energy E_r = 2.46 MeV and total width Γ_tot = 0.98 MeV
- The excitation energy of the first 2⁺ state in ³⁰Mg is taken as ε₂ = 1.48 MeV
- The deformation parameter β₂ = 0.21, estimated from B(E2)↑ = 241(31) e²fm⁴ with radius R = 5.1 fm
- For the spherical d-wave test case (A∼30): square-well radius R = 3.95 fm, depth V₀ varied from -27 MeV to -21 MeV, reduced mass μ = 30m_N/31

## Context

This work extends the single-channel resonance width formula of Bohr and Mottelson (1969) to multi-channel resonances in particle-core systems, where channel coupling effects are known to play an important role in one-nucleon decays of unbound nuclei (Hagino 2001, Karny 2003, Fossez 2016). The formula provides a simple semi-analytic tool for analyzing experimental data such as beta-delayed neutron emissions from neutron-rich nuclei, complementing more numerically intensive coupled-channels calculations. The authors note that the resonance behavior of an s-wave channel arising from coupling to a d-wave channel, even when the d-wave channel is closed, is distinct from a Feshbach resonance since no bound state exists in the excited channel.
