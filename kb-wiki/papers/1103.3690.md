# Investigation of the field-induced ferromagnetic phase transition in spin polarized neutron matter: a lowest order constrained variational approach

> In this paper, the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method has been used to investigate the magnetic properties of spin polarized neutron matter in the presence of strong magnetic field at zero temperature employing $AV_{18}$ potential. Our results indicate that a ferromagnetic phase transition is induced by a strong magnetic field with strength greater than $10^{18}\ G$, leading to a partial spin polarization of the neutron matter. It is also shown that the equation of state of neutron matter in the presence of magnetic field is stiffer than the case in absence of magnetic field.

## Key claim
Using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the AV18 potential, the authors find that a ferromagnetic phase transition in neutron matter is induced only by an external magnetic field stronger than about 10^18 G, leading to partial spin polarization; the abstract omits that below this field strength the effect is nearly negligible and that the transition is field-induced, not spontaneous.

## Method
The authors apply the LOCV method to spin-polarized neutron matter at zero temperature, using a Jastrow-type trial wave function and a cluster expansion of the energy functional truncated at the two-body term. They employ the realistic AV18 two-body nucleon-nucleon potential and minimize the two-body energy with respect to the correlation functions under a normalization constraint. The magnetic field contribution is added as a Zeeman term, E_M = -μ_n B δ, where μ_n is the neutron magnetic moment and δ is the spin polarization parameter.

## Key numbers
- For magnetic fields below 10^18 G, the effect on neutron matter energy is "nearly insignificant" (Section RESULTS and DISCUSSION).
- At density ρ = 0.2 fm^{-3}, for B ≳ 1.8 × 10^19 G, the minimum energy occurs at full spin polarization (δ = -1).
- The threshold magnetic field for full polarization increases with density.
- Below B ∼ 10^17 G, the spin polarization parameter is "nearly zero" (Section RESULTS and DISCUSSION).
- At low densities and fields just above 10^18 G, partial polarization reaches about 14% of the maximum possible value (δ = -1).
- The magnetic field at the phase transition point, B_m, grows monotonically with density (shown in the phase diagram, Fig. 4j).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous LOCV studies of spin-polarized neutron matter (Bordbar et al., various years) which found no spontaneous ferromagnetic transition in the absence of a magnetic field. It contrasts with mean-field and Skyrme-model calculations by Isayev et al. (various years) and Perez-Garcia et al. (various years), which predicted spontaneous ferromagnetism at high density. The results enable future studies of neutron star structure with ultra-strong magnetic fields, including the effects of finite temperature and proton fraction.
