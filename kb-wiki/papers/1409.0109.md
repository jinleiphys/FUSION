# Generalized seniority with realistic interactions in open-shell nuclei

> Generalized seniority provides a truncation scheme for the nuclear shell model, based on pairing correlations, which offers the possibility of dramatically reducing the dimensionality of the nuclear shell-model problem. Systematic comparisons against results obtained in the full shell-model space are required to assess the viability of this scheme. Here, we extend recent generalized seniority calculations for semimagic nuclei, the Ca isotopes, to open-shell nuclei, with both valence protons and valence neutrons. The even-mass Ti and Cr isotopes are treated in a full major shell and with realistic interactions, in the generalized seniority scheme with one broken proton pair and one broken neutron pair. Results for level energies, orbital occupations, and electromagnetic observables are compared with those obtained in the full shell-model space. We demonstrate that, even for the Ti isotopes, significant benefit would be obtained in going beyond the approximation of one broken pair of each type, while the Cr isotopes require further broken pairs to provide even qualitative accuracy.

## Key claim

The generalized seniority truncation with one broken proton pair and one broken neutron pair [(v_p,v_n)=(2,2)] provides a quantitatively successful description of low-lying states for Ti isotopes in the upper pf shell, but fails qualitatively for Cr isotopes, where further broken pairs are required; even for Ti isotopes, significant benefit would be obtained from going beyond this approximation.

## Method

The authors constructed a generalized seniority basis for even-even Ti and Cr isotopes in the full pf shell (f7/2, p3/2, p1/2, f5/2 orbitals) using collective S-pair condensates with independently determined proton and neutron pair amplitudes obtained variationally. They performed calculations in the (v_p,v_n)=(2,2) model space (one broken pair of each type) using the FPD6 and GXPF1 realistic interactions, and compared results for level energies, orbital occupations, and electromagnetic observables against full shell-model calculations carried out with the code NuShellX.

## Key numbers

* Ground state energy difference between generalized seniority and full space for Ti isotopes: maximum deviation of 1.1 MeV for N=24 (Ti-46) with FPD6, 0.9 MeV with GXPF1
* Ground state energy difference for Cr isotopes: maximum deviation of 4.5 MeV for N=24 (Cr-48) with FPD6, 3.2 MeV with GXPF1
* Average residual energy difference for Ti 0+1 states across the shell: 0.65 MeV (FPD6), 0.45 MeV (GXPF1)
* Average residual energy difference for Cr 0+1 states across the shell: 2.48 MeV (FPD6), 1.62 MeV (GXPF1)
* Excitation energy of the 4+1 state in Ti at N=28: reproduced to 0.060 MeV (approximately 2%) for FPD6 and 0.017 MeV (approximately 0.6%) for GXPF1
* Average deviation of neutron orbital occupations for Ti ground state: 0.10 nucleon (FPD6), 0.08 nucleon (GXPF1)
* Average deviation of neutron orbital occupations for Cr ground state: 0.30 nucleon (both interactions)
* Model space dimension reduction: factor of approximately 4x10^2 for Ti-52 (J=0) and approximately 7x10^3 for Cr-54 (J=0)

## Context

This work extends the benchmark generalized seniority calculations for semimagic Ca isotopes by Caprio et al. (2012) to open-shell nuclei with both valence protons and neutrons. The results confirm the hypothesis by Monnoye et al. (2002) that the generalized seniority description improves at subshell closures, as seen in the sharp local minimum in residual energy difference at N=28. The study establishes that the (v_p,v_n)=(2,2) truncation is inadequate for midshell Cr isotopes, motivating future calculations with higher generalized seniority (more broken pairs) to achieve quantitative accuracy in deformed regions of the nuclear chart.
