# Relativistic Quantum Kinetic Theory for Massive Fermions and Spin Effects

> We present the complete first order relativistic quantum kinetic theory with spin for massive fermions derived from the Wigner function formalism in a concise form that shows explicitly how the 32 Wigner equations reduce to 4 independent transport equations. We solve modified on-shell conditions to obtain the general solution and present the corresponding transport equations in three different forms that are suitable for different purposes. We demonstrate how different spin effects arise from the kinetic theory by calculating the chiral separation effect with mass correction, the chiral anomaly from the axial current and the quantum magnetic moment density induced by vorticity and magnetic field. We also show how to generate the global polarization effect due to spin vorticity coupling. The formalism presented may serve as a practical theoretical framework to study different spin effects in relativistic fermion systems encountered in different areas such as heavy ion, astro-particle and condensed matter physics as well.

## Key claim

The paper presents the complete first-order relativistic quantum kinetic theory for massive fermions, showing that the 32 Wigner equations reduce to 4 independent transport equations, but the derivation is restricted to the external or self-consistent mean field approximation and to first order in ℏ.

## Method

The authors start from the covariant Wigner function formalism for massive fermions, expanding the Wigner matrix in terms of 16 independent Γ-matrices. They sort the 32 Wigner equations into groups: eleven provide expressions for other components in terms of the scalar ℱ and axial vector 𝒜μ; five give modified on-shell conditions; five yield transport equations; one provides a subsidiary condition; and the remaining ten are automatically satisfied. They solve the modified on-shell conditions to obtain general solutions and present the transport equations in three forms: Lorentz covariant, integrated over p0, and three-dimensional.

## Key numbers

- The 32 Wigner equations reduce to 4 independent transport equations for the scalar ℱ and axial vector 𝒜μ.
- The chiral separation effect transport coefficient is σ = (ℏ/2π²) ∫₀^∞ dp (n₊ − n₋), where n± = 1/(e^(Ep∓μ)/T + 1).
- At the chiral limit m=0, the CSE coefficient becomes σ|_(m=0) = ℏμ/2π².
- At zero temperature, the CSE coefficient becomes σ|_(T→0) = ℏμ√(1−m²/μ²)/2π².
- The magnetic moment density in the local co-moving frame is M_μ = ℏκB_μ − ℏρω_μ/m, where κ = m∫ dp (n₊ + n₋)/(2π²E_p) is the magnetic susceptibility and ρ is the charge density.

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory for massless fermions (Stephanov 2012, Son 2012, Chen 2012) to massive fermions, and goes beyond earlier relativistic quantum kinetic theory treatments (Heinz 1983, Elze 1986, Vasak 1987, Zhuang 1995, Blaizot 2001) that only considered the zeroth order of the axial vector. The formalism enables self-consistent derivation of spin effects including the chiral separation effect, chiral anomaly, quantum magnetization, and global polarization effect in heavy-ion collisions, motivated by the STAR collaboration's observation of Λ hyperon global polarization (STAR 2017, Adam 2018).
