# Influence of Induced Interactions on the Superfluid Transition in Dilute Fermi Gases

> We calculate the effects of induced interactions on the transition temperature to the BCS state in dilute Fermi gases. For a pure Fermi system with 2 species having equal densities, the transition temperature is suppressed by a factor $(4e)^{1/3}\approx 2.2$, and for $ν$ fermion species, the transition temperature is increased by a factor $(4e)^{ν/3-1} \approx 2.2^{ν-3}$. For mixtures of fermions and bosons the exchange of boson density fluctuations gives rise to an effective interaction, and we estimate the increase of the transition temperature due to this effect.

# Influence of Induced Interactions on the Superfluid Transition in Dilute Fermi Gases

## Key claim
The transition temperature to the BCS state in dilute Fermi gases is modified by induced interactions even in the low-density limit, with suppression for two fermion species but enhancement for four or more species. The result is derived under the assumption that induced interactions are small compared to the bare interaction, and that final-state effects are negligible.

## Method
The authors solve the linearized gap equation for the superfluid transition temperature, including both the bare two-body interaction and the induced interaction from medium polarization. They eliminate the bare interaction in favor of the free-space T-matrix, average the induced interaction over the Fermi surface, and compute the resulting shift in the exponential factor determining T_c. For fermion-boson mixtures, they use the static limit of the boson density-density response function in the Bogoliubov approximation.

## Key numbers
- For a pure Fermi system with 2 species having equal densities, the transition temperature is suppressed by a factor $(4e)^{1/3} \approx 2.2$ relative to the result without induced interactions.
- For $\nu$ fermion species, the transition temperature is multiplied by a factor $(4e)^{\nu/3-1} \approx 2.2^{\nu-3}$.
- For a $^6$Li / $^{87}$Rb mixture with $n_B = 10^{14}$ cm$^{-3}$ and estimated $|a_{BF}| = 100 a_0$, the increase in T_c is a factor of 2.7 for $k_F|a| = 0.2$ and 1.3 for $k_F|a| = 0.5$.
- If $|a_{BF}|$ were twice as large (200 $a_0$), these factors become 17.6 and 2.2 respectively.
- The neutron-neutron scattering length is approximately -18.8 fm, while the neutron-proton one is 5.4 fm.

## Context
This work extends the classic result of Gorkov and Melik-Barkhudarov (1961) on the suppression of the transition temperature in spin-1/2 Fermi gases, providing a unified framework for arbitrary numbers of fermion species and for fermion-boson mixtures. The induced interaction mechanism is analogous to phonon-induced pairing in metallic superconductors and to spin-fluctuation effects in liquid $^3$He studied by Wölfle (1976). The results are relevant for understanding superfluidity in neutron matter (where Clark et al., Wambach et al., and Schulze et al. have found comparable suppression factors) and for predicting transition temperatures in ultracold atomic gas mixtures such as $^6$Li and $^{87}$Rb, where knowledge of boson-fermion scattering lengths is identified as a critical input.
