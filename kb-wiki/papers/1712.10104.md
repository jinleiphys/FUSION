# Prospects for $γγ\to$ Higgs observation in ultraperipheral ion collisions at the Future Circular Collider

> We study the two-photon production of the Higgs boson, $\rm γγ\to H$, at the Future Circular Collider (FCC) in ultraperipheral PbPb and pPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 39$ and 63 TeV. Signal and background events are generated with MADGRAPH 5, including $γ$ fluxes from the proton and lead ions in the equivalent photon approximation, yielding $\rm σ(γγ\to H)$ = 1.75 nb and 1.5 pb in PbPb and pPb collisions respectively. We analyse the H$\,\to b\bar{b}$ decay channel including realistic reconstruction efficiencies for the final-state $b$-jets, showered and hadronized with PYTHIA 8, as well as appropriate selection criteria to reduce the dominant exclusive $γγ\to b\bar{b}$ continuum background. Observation of $\rm PbPb\xrightarrow{γγ}(Pb)\,H\,(Pb)$ is achievable in the first year with the expected PbPb integrated luminosities.

## Key claim
The two-photon production of the Higgs boson in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at the Future Circular Collider can be observed with statistical significance close to 5 sigma in the first year of operation, but only if combining two different experiments or doubling the nominal integrated luminosity; for pPb collisions, reaching 5 sigma would require about 8 months of running instead of the nominal 1-month run per year.

## Method
The authors used MADGRAPH 5 to generate signal and background events, incorporating photon fluxes from protons and lead ions via the equivalent photon approximation. The Higgs boson decaying to b-quark pairs was showered and hadronized with PYTHIA 8, and the final-state b-jets were reconstructed using the Durham kt algorithm in FASTJET. Kinematical cuts on jet transverse momentum (55-62.5 GeV) and angular distribution (|cos theta_j1j2| < 0.5) were applied to suppress the dominant exclusive gamma-gamma to bbbar continuum background, along with b-jet tagging efficiencies of 70% and mistagging probabilities of 5% for charm and 1.5% for light quarks.

## Key numbers
- Higgs boson cross section in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV: sigma(gamma-gamma to H) = 1.75 nb
- Higgs boson cross section in pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 63 TeV: sigma(gamma-gamma to H) = 1.5 pb
- Expected signal events after cuts in PbPb: approximately 21 signal counts over approximately 28 background events in the dijet mass window 117-133 GeV, for integrated luminosity 110 nb^-1
- Expected signal events after cuts in pPb: approximately 5 signal events over 6.7 background events, for integrated luminosity 29 pb^-1
- b-jet tagging efficiency: 70%; b-jet mistagging probability for charm quarks: 5%; for light quarks: 1.5%
- Dijet mass resolution at the Higgs peak: approximately 6 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of exclusive gamma-gamma to Higgs production in ultraperipheral collisions at LHC energies by d'Enterria (2009), where observation was found unfeasible with nominal LHC luminosities. The FCC's higher collision energies (factors of 7 above LHC) and integrated luminosities (factors of 30 above LHC) make observation possible. The measurement provides an independent determination of the Higgs-gamma coupling based on s-channel production rather than Higgs decays, complementing studies of central hadronic Higgs production at FCC by d'Enterria (2017).
