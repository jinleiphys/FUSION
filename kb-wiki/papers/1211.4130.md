# Relating pseudospin and spin symmetries through chiral transformation with tensor interaction

> We address the behavior of the Dirac equation with scalar ($S$), vector ($V$) and tensor ($U$) interactions under the $γ^{5}$ discrete chiral transformation. Using this transformation we can obtain from a simple way solutions for the Dirac equation with spin ($Δ=V-S=0$) and pseudospin ($Σ=V+S=0$) symmetries including a tensor interaction. As an application, the Dirac equation with scalar, vector and tensor Cornell radial potentials is considered and the correct solution to this problem is obtained.

# Relating pseudospin and spin symmetries through chiral transformation with tensor interaction

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the discrete chiral transformation (gamma-5) connects spin symmetry (Delta = V - S = 0) and pseudospin symmetry (Sigma = V + S = 0) in the Dirac equation even when a tensor interaction is present, allowing solutions for one symmetry to be obtained from the other by changing the sign of mass, tensor coupling, and the quantum number k. The connection holds provided the energy conditions E not equal to -m (for spin symmetry) and E not equal to m (for pseudospin symmetry) are satisfied.

## Method
The Dirac Hamiltonian with scalar (S), vector (V), and tensor (U) radial interactions is expressed in terms of the combinations Sigma = V + S and Delta = V - S. The spinor is decomposed into upper and lower radial components f_k(r) and g_k(r) using spinor spherical harmonics. Under the gamma-5 chiral transformation, the spinor components are interchanged (f becomes -ig, g becomes if) and k changes sign, while Sigma and Delta swap roles and the tensor potential changes sign. The coupled radial equations are reduced to Schrodinger-like equations for each symmetry limit, and the specific case of Cornell-type radial potentials (linear plus Coulomb terms) is solved analytically using the biconfluent Heun differential equation.

## Key numbers
- For the spin symmetry case (Delta = 0) with Cornell potentials Sigma = a_1 r + b_1/r and U = a_2 r + b_2/r, the energy spectrum is given by E = -(m_E / a_E) +/- (m_E / a_E) * sqrt(1 - (a_E / m_E^2) * [2 a_2 (k - b_2 - 1/2) - (2n + 2 lambda + 3) |a_2| + (a_E - 2) m^2]), where m_E = m a_1^2 / (4 a_2^2) and a_E = 1 + a_1^2 / (4 a_2^2).
- For the pseudospin symmetry case (Sigma = 0) with Delta = a_1 r + b_1/r and U = a_2 r + b_2/r, the spectrum is E = (m_E / a_E) +/- (m_E / a_E) * sqrt(1 - (a_E / m_E^2) * [2 a_2 (k - b_2 + 1/2) - (2n + 2 lambda + 3) |a_2| + (a_E - 2) m^2]), with lambda = -1/2 + (1/2) |1 - 2k + 2 b_2|.
- For the ground state (n = 0) in the spin symmetry case, the parameter b_1 is constrained to b_1 = -(a_1 / (2 |a_2|)) * (1 + |1 - 2 b_2 + 2k|).

## Context
This work extends the connection between pseudospin and spin symmetries previously established through charge conjugation by Castro and collaborators (2010, 2011) to include tensor interactions via the discrete chiral transformation. It builds on earlier studies showing that pseudospin and spin symmetries correspond to SU(2) symmetries of the Dirac Hamiltonian with vector and scalar potentials, with pseudospin symmetry known to account for degeneracies in nuclear single-particle spectra (Arima, 1969; Hecht, 1969). The tensor interaction treatment is motivated by relativistic mean-field calculations (Furnstahl, 1997) and relativistic Hartree models (Mao, 2003) suggesting tensor contributions to pseudospin splittings in nuclei. The paper corrects erroneous calculations in prior treatments of the Cornell potential by Leau (2004) and Heun (2005), providing the correct solution via the biconfluent Heun differential equation. This enables future applications to nuclear structure problems where tensor interactions are significant.
