# An unconventional deformation of the nonrelativistic spin-1/2 Fermi gas

> We explore a generalization of nonrelativistic fermionic statistics that interpolates between bosons and fermions, in which up to $K$ particles may occupy a single-particle state. We show that it can be mapped exactly to $K$ flavors of fermions with imaginary polarization. In particular, for $K\!=\!2$, we use such a mapping to derive the virial coefficients and relate them to those of conventional spin-1/2 fermions in an exact fashion. We also use the mapping to derive next-to-leading-order perturbative results for the pressure equation of state. Our results indicate that the $K\!=\!2$ particles are more strongly coupled than conventional spin-$1/2$ fermions, as measured by the interaction effects on the virial expansion and on the pressure equation of state. In the regime set by the unitary limit, the proposed $K\!=\!2$ deformation represents a universal many-body system whose properties remain largely unknown. In particular, the system can be expected to become superfluid at a critical temperature $T_c$ higher than that of the unitary limit. We suggest it may be possible to realize this system experimentally by engineering a polarized coupling to an electrostatic potential. Finally, we show that the $K\!=\!2$ system does not display a sign problem for determinantal Monte Carlo calculations, which indicates that $T_c$ can at least in principle be calculated with conventional methods.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a nonrelativistic Fermi gas generalized to allow up to K=2 particles per single-particle state can be mapped exactly to a spin-1/2 Fermi gas with an imaginary polarization (a complex chemical potential with imaginary part ±π/3), and that this K=2 system is more strongly coupled than conventional spin-1/2 fermions, with a predicted higher superfluid critical temperature at unitarity.

## Method

The authors define the K=2 statistics by modifying the path-integral representation of the fermionic partition function, replacing the product of two fermionic determinants with a product of determinants containing complex fugacities w = e^{iπ/3}z and w* = e^{-iπ/3}z. They derive virial coefficients up to fourth order by relating them to known coefficients of the polarized spin-1/2 Fermi gas from Refs. VE1, VE2, VE3. They also perform a second-order perturbation theory calculation (first order in the coupling C) to obtain the pressure equation of state, renormalizing the coupling using the second-order virial expansion result.

- Δb₂^(K=2) = √2 (second virial coefficient at unitarity)
- Δb₃^(K=2) = -0.3551... (third virial coefficient at unitarity)
- Δb₄^(K=2) = -0.435... (fourth virial coefficient at unitarity)
- The noninteracting virial coefficients for arbitrary K are b_{0,K, m(K+1)} = -K × (1/n^(5/2)) for m = 1,2,3..., with the bosonic case recovered as K → ∞ and fermionic as K = 1
- The effective chemical potential for the K=2 system is βμ_eff = βμ ± iπ/3 for spin-up and spin-down respectively
- The mean-field study of Ref. ImaginaryPolarizationPRL found that for an imaginary polarization of π/3, the critical temperature is about 30% higher than in the unpolarized case, suggesting T_c/ε_F ≈ 0.2 for the K=2 system

## Context

This work extends the study of universal many-body systems beyond the well-known spin-1/2 unitary Fermi gas (ZwergerBook, StrinatiReview, UltracoldAtoms4) by introducing a deformation of fermionic statistics related to Gentile statistics (Gentile). The K=2 system maps to a spin-1/2 Fermi gas with imaginary polarization, which was previously studied in Refs. ImaginaryPolarizationPRL and ImaginaryPolarization1DPRA. The authors note that for K > 2, attractive interactions at unitarity lead to Thomas collapse (ThomasCollapse), but the K=2 case avoids this instability and has no sign problem for determinantal Monte Carlo calculations, enabling future numerical determination of the critical temperature. The work also relates to the nonrelativistic conformal algebra for fermions at unitarity studied by Nishida and Son (UFGConformal, NishidaSon1D4B).
