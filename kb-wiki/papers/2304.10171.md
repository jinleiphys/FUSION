# Probing the nucleon effective mass splitting with the light particle emission

> In this work, we first analyze the correlations among different nuclear matter parameters which are obtained by the 119 effective Skyrme interaction sets. The values of the correlation coefficients illustrate that the magnitude of the effective mass splitting is crucial for the tight constraints of the symmetry energy via HICs. Thus, the main object of this work is to investigate the impacts of the effective mass splitting on the heavy ion collisions observables. The $^{86}$Kr+$^{208}$Pb system at the beam energy ranging from 25A MeV to 200A MeV are simulated within the framework of the improved quantum molecular dynamics model (ImQMD-Sky) for this goal. Our calculations show that the slopes of the spectra of $\ln$[Y(n)/Y(p)] and $\ln$[Y(t)/Y($^3$He)], which are the logarithms of the neutron to proton yield ratios and triton to Helium-3 yield ratios, are directly related to the effective mass splitting and can be used to probe the effective mass splitting.

# Probing the nucleon effective mass splitting with the light particle emission

## Key claim
The slopes of the logarithmic neutron-to-proton yield ratio ln[Y(n)/Y(p)] and the logarithmic triton-to-helium-3 yield ratio ln[Y(t)/Y(^3He)] are directly related to the effective mass splitting and can serve as probes of this quantity in heavy ion collisions, but the correlation analysis shows that constraining the effective mass splitting is necessary before the symmetry energy can be tightly constrained via heavy ion collisions.

## Method
The authors used the improved quantum molecular dynamics model (ImQMD-Sky) with Skyrme-type momentum-dependent interactions to simulate the ^86Kr + ^208Pb system at beam energies from 25A MeV to 200A MeV. They first analyzed correlations among nuclear matter parameters (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy coefficient, slope of symmetry energy, isoscalar and isovector effective masses) using 119 Skyrme interaction parameter sets selected according to empirical constraints. They then calculated the logarithmic yield ratios of neutrons to protons and tritons to helium-3 as functions of the effective mass splitting.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy range: 25A MeV to 200A MeV for the ^86Kr + ^208Pb system
- 119 effective Skyrme interaction sets were used for the correlation analysis
- The empirical constraints applied to select Skyrme sets include: incompressibility K0 between 200 MeV and 280 MeV, symmetry energy coefficient S0 between 25 MeV and 35 MeV, slope of symmetry energy L between 30 MeV and 120 MeV, isoscalar effective mass m_s^*/m between 0.6 and 1.0, and the isospin effective mass splitting parameter f_I between -0.5 and 0.4
- The wave packet width sigma_r = 1.32 fm gives a kinetic energy correction C(sigma) = 8.97 MeV, which is about 25% of the Fermi energy at normal density (~35 MeV)

## Context
This work extends previous efforts to constrain the effective mass splitting using heavy ion collisions, which have yielded conflicting results: nucleon-nucleus optical potential analysis favors m_n^* > m_p^* (Li 2004), while heavy ion collision data support m_n^* < m_p^* (Zhang 2014, Coupland, Feng, Su 2017). The paper builds on the ImQMD model that previously described neutron-to-proton yield ratios and isospin diffusion for constraining symmetry energy at subsaturation density (Tsang 2009). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from the CSHINE spectrometer at HIRFL/Lanzhou, which recently measured yields of tritons and helium-3 in the ^86Kr + ^208Pb system.
