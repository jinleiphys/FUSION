# Baryon form factors from Dyson-Schwinger equations

> I briefly summarize the application of the Dyson-Schwinger/Faddeev approach to baryon form factors. Recent results for nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors as well as N-Delta-Gamma electromagnetic transition form factors are discussed. The calculation of the current diagrams from the quark-gluon level enables an analysis of common features, such as the implications of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and quark orbital angular momentum, the timelike structure of the form factors, and their interpretation in terms of missing pion-cloud effects.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger/Faddeev approach, implemented in a rainbow-ladder truncation that omits three-quark irreducible interactions, can account for the overall properties of the nucleon and Delta "quark core" but systematically underestimates experimental form factors at low momentum transfer (Q² ≲ 2 GeV²) and small pion masses due to the absence of pion-cloud contributions.

## Method

The authors solve the covariant Faddeev equation for the nucleon and Delta bound-state wave functions using a rainbow-ladder kernel that models the quark-gluon interaction via a dressed gluon exchange with the Maris-Tandy effective coupling. The current matrix elements for electromagnetic, axial, and transition form factors are then constructed by coupling external currents to all internal quark lines and kernels, with the quark-photon and quark-axial vertices determined from inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations that incorporate meson poles. For the N-Delta-gamma transition, a quark-diquark simplification is employed where scalar and axialvector diquark correlations approximate the two-quark scattering matrix.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar anomalous magnetic moment is calculated as κ^s = -0.12(1), compared to the experimental value κ^s_exp = -0.12.
- The axial charge g_A = G_A(0) underestimates the experimental value by 20-25%.
- The magnetic dipole transition form factor G_M^*(Q^2) at Q² = 0 underestimates experimental data by approximately 25%.
- p-wave contributions account for roughly 30% of the nucleon's canonical normalization, while d-wave contributions are below 1%.
- The rho-meson term contributes approximately 50% to the nucleon's squared charge radii across the current-mass range.
- The calculated isovector Dirac radius (r_1^v)^2 underestimates experimental values but converges with lattice data at larger quark masses.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Faddeev approach previously applied to mesons and nucleon static properties (Eichmann 2009) to a comprehensive set of baryon form factors including axial and N-Delta-gamma transition form factors. The results are compared with experimental data, lattice QCD results, and chiral effective field theory predictions (Procura 2006), showing that the rainbow-ladder truncation reproduces the baryon's "quark core" but misses pion-cloud effects that are essential at low momentum transfer. The approach enables future studies of baryon excitations, virtual Compton scattering, and pion electroproduction, while requiring technical improvements such as residue calculus for large Q² access and truncations beyond rainbow-ladder to incorporate pion-cloud corrections and hadronic decay channels.
