# Baryon flow at SIS energies

> We calculate the baryon flow $< P_x/A(y) >$ in the energy range from .25 to $\le 2.5 AGeV$ in a relativistic transport model for $Ni+Ni$ and $Au+Au$ collisions employing various models for the baryon self energies. We find that to describe the flow data of the FOPI Collaboration the strength of the vector potential has to be reduced at high relative momentum or at high density such that the Schrödinger- equivalent potential at normal nuclear density decreases above 1 GeV relative kinetic energy and approaches zero above 2 GeV.

# Baryon flow at SIS energies

## Key claim

The baryon flow data from the FOPI Collaboration for Ni+Ni and Au+Au collisions at SIS energies (0.25 to 2.5 AGeV) can only be described by a relativistic transport model if the vector potential strength is reduced at high relative momentum or high density, such that the Schrodinger-equivalent potential at normal nuclear density decreases above 1 GeV relative kinetic energy and approaches zero above 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic transport (RBUU) calculations for Ni+Ni and Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 0.25 to 2.5 AGeV, employing various Lagrangian-based models for the scalar and vector baryon self-energies. They introduced momentum-dependent form factors with cutoff parameters Lambda_s (0.95 to 1.05 GeV) and Lambda_v (0.9 to 1.0 GeV) at the interaction vertices to modify the momentum dependence of the mean fields. The calculated baryon sideward flow, defined as the slope d/dy of the average transverse momentum per nucleon at midrapidity, was compared to experimental data from the FOPI Collaboration for impact parameters b=4 fm (Ni+Ni) and b=6 fm (Au+Au).

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: 0.25 to 2.5 AGeV for both Ni+Ni and Au+Au systems
- Nuclear incompressibility for hard EOS (NL3): K = 380 MeV; for soft EOS: K = 194 MeV
- Cutoff parameters for momentum-dependent form factors: Lambda_s = 0.95 to 1.05 GeV, Lambda_v = 0.9 to 1.0 GeV
- Vector potential at 3 times nuclear saturation density: 460 MeV for NL3*, 653 MeV for NL3, and 1020 MeV for the sigma-omega model
- Flow for Ni+Ni rises up to 1 AGeV and decreases above 1 AGeV; flow for Au+Au saturates above 1 AGeV and decreases at or above 2.5 AGeV

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic transport calculations by Li et al. (1997) and Sahu et al. (1997) that overestimated the experimental flow data at 1.5 and 2 AGeV for Ni+Ni, and contrasts with UrQMD calculations predicting increasing flow up to 4 AGeV and hydrodynamical calculations predicting decreasing flow above 4-5 AGeV. The results demonstrate that the observed A^(1/3) scaling of flow below 1 AGeV breaks down at higher energies, and the extracted momentum-dependent potentials provide constraints for future systematic studies of different mass systems at higher bombarding energies to separate density and momentum dependence of the nuclear mean fields.
