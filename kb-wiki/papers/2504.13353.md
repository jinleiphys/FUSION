# The Unitarity-Limit Expansion for Two Nucleons with Perturbative Pions: Digest and Ideas

> In the Unitarity Limit, the NN S-wave binding energies are zero, the scattering lengths infinite, Physics is universal, i.e. insensitive to details of the interactions, and observables display richer symmetries, namely invariance under both scaling and Wigner's combined SU(4) transformation of spin and isospin. This presentation is a digest of the first quantitative exploration of corrections to this picture when pions are included [1] (see there for a more comprehensive list of references). Since the pion mass and decay constant introduce dimensionful scales in the NN system, they explicitly break the symmetries of the Unitarity fixed point. In $χ$EFT, these symmetries must therefore be classified as emergent. This text focuses on the $χ$EFT variant with Perturbative ("KSW") Pions at next-to-next-to leading order (N2LO). This leads to the Hypothesis that both scale invariance and Wigner-SU(4) symmetry in the Unitarity Expansion show persistence, i.e. the footprint of both combined dominates even for $k\gtrsim m_π$ and is more relevant than chiral symmetry, so that the tensor/Wigner-SU(4) symmetry-breaking part of OPE is suppressed and does not enter before N3LO. Included are also ideas about underlying mechanisms and LO results of $χ$EFT with Nonperturbative Pions in the expansion about Unitarity.

## Key claim

The paper hypothesizes that in the Unitarity Expansion for two nucleons, both scale invariance and Wigner-SU(4) symmetry show "persistence" meaning their combined footprint dominates even for momenta k greater than or approximately equal to the pion mass m_pi, and that the tensor/Wigner-SU(4) symmetry-breaking part of one-pion exchange is suppressed and does not enter before N3LO, despite the pion mass and decay constant explicitly breaking the symmetries of the Unitarity fixed point.

## Method

The author employs chiral effective field theory (ChiEFT) with perturbative (KSW) pions at next-to-next-to leading order (N2LO) in the Unitarity Expansion, where the scattering lengths are demoted so that leading order contains no scale but the scattering momentum. The analysis computes S-wave phase shifts in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels up to center-of-mass momenta of approximately 300 MeV, comparing results to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis and to the pionless EFT (EFT without pions) Unitarity Expansion, which reduces to the effective-range expansion about 1/a = 0.

## Key numbers

* The breakdown scale Lambda_NN = 16 pi f_pi^2 / (g_A^2 M) is approximately 300 MeV, the scale at which iterations of one-pion exchange are not suppressed.
* The Unitarity Window applies for momenta 30 MeV less than or approximately equal to k less than or approximately equal to 300 MeV (lab energies 2 MeV less than or approximately equal to E_lab less than or approximately equal to 200 MeV) for the 1S0 and 3S1 channels.
* In the 1S0 channel, the N2LO result agrees well with the Nijmegen partial wave analysis up to cm momenta less than or approximately equal to 300 MeV, with order-by-order convergence demonstrated.
* In the 3S1 channel, the full N2LO amplitude deviates dramatically from the partial wave analysis just above the one-pion exchange branch-point scale m_pi/2, around k approximately 100 MeV, but taking only the Wigner-SU(4) symmetric central part of the pion's N2LO contribution remedies the discrepancy.

## Context

This work extends the Unitarity Expansion previously developed in pionless EFT to chiral EFT with perturbative (KSW) pions, building on the foundational work of Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998) on the KSW power counting, and on the analytic amplitude derivations by Rupak and Shoresh (1999) for the 1S0 channel and by Fleming, Mehen and Stewart (1999) for the 3SD1 channel. The paper proposes that the Unitarity Expansion provides a paradigm change where NN details are less important than NNN interactions to explain the complexity of the nuclear chart, and suggests that imposing Wigner-SU(4) symmetry on the pion at N2LO by eliminating the tensor part enables extending the Unitarity Expansion to the whole Unitarity Window including k greater than or approximately equal to m_pi.
