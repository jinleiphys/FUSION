# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors from the covariant Faddeev equation

> We compute the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in the Poincare-covariant Faddeev framework based on the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD. The general expression for a baryon's electromagnetic current in terms of three interacting dressed quarks is derived. Upon employing a rainbow-ladder gluon-exchange kernel for the quark-quark interaction, the nucleon's Faddeev amplitude and electromagnetic form factors are computed without any further truncations or model assumptions. The form factor results show clear evidence of missing pion-cloud effects below a photon momentum transfer of ~2 GeV^2 and in the chiral region whereas they agree well with experimental data at higher photon momenta. Thus, the approach reflects the properties of the nucleon's quark core.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of the covariant Faddeev equation, which excludes pion-cloud effects, reproduces the nucleon's electromagnetic form factors well at photon momenta above approximately 2 GeV² but shows clear evidence of missing pion-cloud contributions below that scale and in the chiral region, thereby reflecting the properties of the nucleon's quark core rather than the full physical nucleon.

## Method

The author solves the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for the nucleon using a rainbow-ladder gluon-exchange kernel for the quark-quark interaction, with the dressed quark propagator obtained from the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation using the same kernel. The nucleon's electromagnetic current is constructed from the three-quark Faddeev amplitude without introducing explicit diquark degrees of freedom, and the Dirac and Pauli form factors are computed from the current. The single parameter of the interaction, an infrared scale Λ, is fixed to reproduce the experimental pion decay constant.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass at physical u/d-quark mass: M_N = 0.94 GeV, in excellent agreement with the experimental value.
- The infrared scale in the effective coupling is set to Λ = 0.72 GeV, fixed to reproduce the experimental pion decay constant.
- s-wave contributions account for 0.66 of the nucleon's canonical normalization at the physical pion mass (0.14 GeV), p-waves for 0.33, and d-waves for 0.01.
- At a pion mass of 0.34 GeV, s-wave fraction is 0.67 and p-wave fraction is 0.32; at 0.75 GeV, s-wave is 0.69 and p-wave is 0.30.
- Missing pion-cloud effects are evident below a photon momentum transfer of approximately 2 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the covariant Faddeev equation solution for the nucleon mass reported by Eichmann (2009) to the computation of electromagnetic form factors, without introducing explicit diquark degrees of freedom as in earlier quark-diquark model calculations by Oettel (1998, 2000, 2002), Bloch (1999, 2003), and Cloet (2008). The results agree well with experimental data at higher photon momenta and with quark-diquark calculations, but the rainbow-ladder truncation excludes meson-cloud corrections that are significant at low momentum transfer. The approach enables future studies of baryon structure from QCD's fundamental degrees of freedom, with the next step being the inclusion of beyond-rainbow-ladder effects to capture the full pion-cloud physics.
