# Testing pQCD and AdS/CFT Energy Loss at RHIC and LHC

> We compare calculations of jet quenching observables at sqrt s = 2.76 ATeV to preliminary LHC data from weak-coupling pQCD and strong-coupling AdS/CFT drag energy loss models. Rigorously constrained to sqrt s = 200 AGeV RHIC pi^0 suppression data and introducing no free parameters, the pQCD-based WHDG model simultaneously describes qualitatively the suppression of light hadrons and quantitatively the azimuthal anisotropy (over many centrality classes) of light hadrons and the suppression of D mesons at LHC. The drag predictions shown here--qualitatively constrained to RHIC data--compare poorly to the measured suppression of D mesons, but the current experimental uncertainties are large. The double ratio of D to B meson R_AA(p_T) should provide a robust experimental measurement to distinguish between the very different assumptions of the strength of interactions in the QGP produced in heavy ion collisions; i.e., whether, from a jet quenching standpoint, the medium is either weakly- or strongly-coupled.

## Key claim
The pQCD-based WHDG energy loss model, rigorously constrained to RHIC π⁰ suppression data with no free parameters, qualitatively describes light hadron suppression and quantitatively describes azimuthal anisotropy and D meson suppression at LHC; however, the AdS/CFT drag model qualitatively constrained to RHIC data overpredicts D meson suppression, though current experimental uncertainties are large enough that the strong-coupling calculation is not yet falsified.

## Method
The WHDG model computes high-pT parton energy loss through both elastic and inelastic perturbative processes in a 1D Bjorken-expanding medium, using standard Feynman diagram techniques with α_s=0.3 fixed and geometry set by colliding nuclei. The medium gluon density dN_g/dy is the only free parameter, set by rigorous statistical comparison to RHIC π⁰ suppression data at √s = 200 AGeV, then scaled to LHC using measured charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/dη from ALICE. The AdS/CFT drag model replaces the 4D Feynman diagrammatic approach with a classical string derivation in 5D, with parameters linked to N=4 SYM and qualitatively constrained to RHIC non-photonic electron data.

## Key numbers
- WHDG predictions at √s = 2.76 ATeV for 0-5% central collisions show good qualitative agreement with preliminary CMS R_AA^{h→X} data; calculations overpredict suppression by at worst somewhat more than a standard deviation.
- WHDG calculations are in excellent quantitative agreement with preliminary ATLAS v_2 measurements at √s = 2.76 ATeV for 30-40% and 50-60% centrality bins.
- WHDG predictions of D meson suppression at √s = 2.76 ATeV for 0-20% centrality show excellent agreement with preliminary ALICE data within stated uncertainties.
- Elastic energy loss provides a sizable (~25%) fraction of total energy loss at top LHC energies.
- AdS/CFT drag predictions quantitatively overpredict D meson suppression compared to preliminary ALICE data at √s = 2.76 ATeV for 0-20% centrality.

## Context
This work extends the WHDG pQCD energy loss model (Horowitz 2011, Wicks 2005) and AdS/CFT drag model (Horowitz 2010) from RHIC to LHC energies, addressing the long-standing problem that no single energy loss model had provided a consistent picture of R_AA and v_2 as functions of particle mass, p_T, centrality, and √s. The results enable future discrimination between weak-coupling and strong-coupling descriptions of the QGP through measurements of B meson suppression and the double ratio R_AA^D/R_AA^B at LHC, which are predicted to have opposite p_T-dependencies in the two frameworks.
