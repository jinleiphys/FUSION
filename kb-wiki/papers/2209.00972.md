# Jet modification in absence of QGP-medium: the role of multiparton interactions and color reconnection

> Recent studies of high-multiplicity events in small collision systems (proton-proton and proton-lead) have drawn research interest towards the possibility of the formation of partonic medium in such systems. One of the important consequences of the formation of dense partonic medium is quenching of high-momentum final-state particles resulting in several experimental observations such as suppression in nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$, modification of jet shape observable $ρ(r)$ and jet fragmentation ($z^{\rm ch}$) distributions, etc. In this work, we study $ρ(r)$ and $z^{\rm ch}$ for inclusive charged-particle jets in proton-proton (pp) collisions at $\sqrt{s}$~=~13~TeV using PYTHIA~8 Monash~2013 Monte Carlo simulation. We show that the color reconnection (CR) and multiparton interaction (MPI) mechanisms in PYTHIA~8 can lead to an increased rate of jet production. We also find that the mechanisms of MPI and CR and change in the gluonic contribution in high-multiplicity events result in significant modification of $ρ(r)$ and $z^{\rm ch}$ compared to those in minimum bias events for 10~$<p_{\rm T,\,jet}^{\rm ch}<$~20~GeV/$c$. We notice a direct connection of $\langle N_{\rm MPI}\rangle$ and gluonic contribution with the amount of modification in $ρ(r)$ -- the larger the number of MPIs and/or gluonic contribution, the larger the amount of modification of $ρ(r)$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that jet modification (broadening and softening) observed in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV can be reproduced by PYTHIA 8 Monash 2013 without any quark-gluon plasma medium, and that this modification arises specifically from the interplay of multiparton interactions (MPI), color reconnection (CR), and an increased fraction of gluon-initiated jets in high-multiplicity events.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 1000 million minimum bias proton-proton collision events at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV using PYTHIA 8 Monash 2013 in three configurations: (1) MPI off, CR off; (2) MPI on, CR off; and (3) MPI on, CR on. Charged-particle jets were reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm (R = 0.4) using the FastJet package, requiring jet transverse momentum between 10 and 20 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |eta_jet| < 0.5. The differential jet shape rho(r) and jet fragmentation function z^ch were measured for inclusive and gluon-initiated jets in minimum bias and high-multiplicity event classes, with underlying event subtraction performed using the perpendicular cone method.

## Key numbers

- The average number of multiparton interactions <N_MPI> in high-multiplicity events is 14.5, compared to 3.5 in minimum bias events.
- For inclusive jets with 10 < pT_jet < 20 GeV/c in the MPI-on, CR-on configuration, the jet core (r = 0-0.02) is depleted by about 22% in high-multiplicity events relative to minimum bias.
- The production of high z^ch particles is suppressed by about 40% at z^ch approaching 1 in high-multiplicity events compared to minimum bias.
- The gluon-initiated jet fraction for jets with 10 < pT_jet < 20 GeV/c is 86% in high-multiplicity events and 75% in minimum bias events.
- For event class IV (N_MPI > 20, <N_MPI> = 22), the modification at the jet core reaches up to 40%.
- Compared to the MPI-off, CR-off configuration, switching on only MPI produces about 26% modification of the jet core (r = 0-0.02), while adding CR increases the modification to 33%.

## Context

This work extends studies of jet modification in small collision systems, following the ALICE measurement (2021) of jet fragmentation modification in high-multiplicity pp collisions at 13 TeV, which was also reproduced by PYTHIA 8. It builds on earlier work by the same group (2021) showing that MPI and CR modify jet shape distributions, but here the authors quantify the individual contributions of MPI, CR, and gluonic fraction separately. The results enable a clearer interpretation of jet measurements in small systems by demonstrating that jet modification can arise from vacuum-like mechanisms (MPI and CR) rather than requiring QGP formation, and they provide a baseline for future searches for medium effects in high-multiplicity proton-proton and proton-lead collisions.
