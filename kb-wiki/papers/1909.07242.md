# Three-body description of $\boldsymbol{^{12}}$C: From the hyperspherical formulation to the algebraic cluster model and its application to $\boldsymbolα+\boldsymbol{^{12}}$C inelastic scattering

> Form factors for $α+{^{12}}$C inelastic scattering are obtained within two theoretical ($α+α+α$) approaches: The hyperspherical framework for three identical bosons, and the algebraic cluster model assuming the $D_{3h}$ symmetry of an equilateral triangle subject to rotations and vibrations. Results show a good agreement, with form factors involving the Hoyle state having a slightly larger extension within the hyperspherical approach. Coupled-channel calculations using these form factors are ongoing.

## Key claim

Form factors for α+¹²C inelastic scattering computed within the hyperspherical three-body formalism and the algebraic cluster model show good agreement, but form factors involving the Hoyle state (0⁺₂) exhibit a slightly larger spatial extension in the hyperspherical approach, which may affect the corresponding cross sections.

## Method

The three-α system was solved using the hyperspherical formalism with the Ali-Bodmer α-α nuclear potential and a hard-sphere Coulomb term, expanding hyperradial functions in a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis and adjusting a three-body force to reproduce the energies of the 2⁺₁ bound excited state and the 0⁺₂ Hoyle state. Separately, the algebraic cluster model assumed D₃h symmetry of an equilateral triangle with a radial parameter β = 1.82 fm and a Gaussian α density with d = 0.56 fm⁻², constructing transition densities for the Hoyle-state band via a breathing-mode vibration with amplitude χ₁ ≈ 0.247. Form factors for α+¹²C inelastic scattering were then computed in both approaches using a double-folding procedure with the M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian α density parameter d = 0.56(2) fm⁻² was used in the algebraic cluster model to reproduce the α-particle radius.
- The radial parameter β = 1.82 fm in the algebraic cluster model ensures the 0⁺₁ ground-state radius and B(E2) value to the first 2⁺₁ state are reproduced.
- The breathing-mode amplitude χ₁ ≈ 0.247 in the algebraic cluster model recovers the experimental monopole matrix element M(E0) for the Hoyle state.

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical three-body treatment previously applied to Borromean nuclei such as ⁶He, ¹¹Li, and ⁹Be (Casal, 2014) and the algebraic cluster model for ¹²C (della Rocca, 2017). It compares form factors from these two distinct theoretical approaches for α+¹²C inelastic scattering, finding that both models capture essentially the same geometrical properties of ¹²C. The computed form factors set the basis for ongoing full coupled-channel calculations that will be presented in future work.
