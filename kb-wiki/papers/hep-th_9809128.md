# 3D reduction of the three-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We present a 3D approximation of the three-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation. Our 3D equation is covariantly cluster separable and the two-fermion cluster separated limits are exact equivalents of the corresponding two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equations. The potentials include positive free energy projectors in order to avoid continuum dissolution.

# 3D reduction of the three-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation

## Key claim

The paper presents a 3D approximation of the three-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation that is covariantly cluster separable, but the authors note that the requirement of correct heavy mass limits is only approximately satisfied and would demand reintroduction of some neglected three-body terms. The two-fermion cluster separated limits are exact equivalents of the corresponding two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equations, while the full three-body equation involves approximations including neglect of the purely three-body irreducible kernel contributions.

## Method

The authors start from the two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation and perform a 3D reduction by introducing a constraint fixing the relative energy, splitting the free propagator into an approached propagator (with a delta function constraint) and a rest term, then constructing a reduced kernel through a series expansion. For the three-fermion case, they replace the two-body kernels by instantaneous ones with the spectator fermion put on mass shell, neglect the purely three-body irreducible kernel K123, and perform contour integrations in the complex plane to obtain a set of three coupled integral equations that reduce to a single 3D equation for the integrated Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The free propagator is split as G0 = G_delta + G_R, where G_delta(p0) = -2i pi delta(p0 - s) (Lambda^{++}/(P0 - S)) beta1 beta2
- The 3D two-fermion equation becomes: psi = (Lambda^{++}/(P0 - E1 - E2)) V(P0) psi, with V(P0) = -2i pi integral dp'_0 dp_0 delta(p'_0 - s) beta1 beta2 K_T(p'_0, p_0, P0) delta(p_0 - s)
- The final three-fermion 3D equation is: psi = (Lambda^{+++}/(P0 - E1 - E2 - E3)) [V12(P0 - E3) + V23(P0 - E1) + V31(P0 - E2)] psi
- The integrated amplitude is defined as: psi = (-1/2i pi) integral dp_10 dp_20 dp_30 delta(p_10 + p_20 + p_30 - P0) Phi

## Context

This work extends the 3D reduction techniques for the Bethe-Salpeter equation from the two-fermion case to three fermions, building on Salpeter's 3D propagator approach (which appears automatically for instantaneous kernels). The resulting equation can be written as a set of three Faddeev equations, which the authors note can also be obtained as an approximation of Gross' spectator model equations. The paper enables further investigation into which model (the present one or Gross' model) is closer to the exact Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the authors indicate ongoing work to transform the neglected higher-order three-body contributions into correcting terms for the 3D equation.
