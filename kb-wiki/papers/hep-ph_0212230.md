# Determination of m_s and |V_us| from hadronic tau decays

> The mass of the strange quark is determined from SU(3)-breaking effects in the tau hadronic width. Compared to previous analyses, the contributions from scalar and pseudoscalar spectral functions, which suffer from large perturbative corrections, are replaced by phenomenological parametrisations. This leads to a sizeable reduction of the uncertainties in the strange mass from tau decays. Nevertheless, the resulting m_s value is still rather sensitive to the moment of the invariant mass distribution which is used for the determination, as well as the size of the quark-mixing matrix element |V_us|. Imposing the unitarity fit for the CKM matrix, we obtain m_s(2 GeV)=117+-17 MeV, whereas for the present Particle Data Group average for |V_us|, we find m_s(2 GeV)=103+-17 MeV. On the other hand, using an average of m_s from other sources as an input, we are able to calculate the quark-mixing matrix element |V_us|, and we demonstrate that if the present measurement of the hadronic decay of the tau into strange particles is improved by a factor of two, the determination of |V_us| is more precise than the current world average.

## Key claim

The strange quark mass determination from hadronic tau decays is significantly improved by replacing the theoretical QCD expressions for scalar and pseudoscalar spectral functions with phenomenological parametrisations, but the resulting value remains sensitive to the choice of moment of the invariant mass distribution and to the value of the CKM matrix element |V_us|.

## Method

The authors use SU(3)-breaking differences of moments of the tau hadronic invariant mass distribution, replacing the longitudinal (scalar and pseudoscalar) contributions, which suffer from large perturbative QCD corrections, with phenomenological parametrisations based on known resonance properties and chiral perturbation theory. The scalar us spectral function is taken from a coupled-channel dispersion-relation analysis of Kpi scattering, while pseudoscalar spectral functions are parametrised using kaon and pion poles plus higher excited resonances with decay constants from Maltman and Kambor (2002). The strange quark mass is extracted by solving the moment sum rules for different values of the moment index k.

## Key numbers

* m_s(2 GeV) = 117 ± 17 MeV when imposing the CKM unitarity fit for |V_us| = 0.2225 ± 0.0021
* m_s(2 GeV) = 103 ± 17 MeV when using the present Particle Data Group average for |V_us|
* The perturbative QCD correction Delta_{00}^{L+T}(a_tau) = 0.981 for the (0,0) moment, with contour-improved perturbation theory at alpha_s(M_tau) = 0.334
* The longitudinal sum rules yield delta_pi = 0.049 ± 0.030 and delta_K = 0.50 ± 0.23 for the higher-order chiral corrections to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relations
* The ratio of quark condensates extracted from the longitudinal sum rules is <ss>/<uu> = 0.80 ± 0.58

## Context

This work extends the earlier analyses of Pich and Prades (1999) and Chen et al. (2001) by replacing the poorly convergent QCD expressions for scalar and pseudoscalar correlators with phenomenological hadronic parametrisations, following the approach advocated by Maltman and Kambor (2002) and Jamin, Oller, and Pich (2002). The reduction in theoretical uncertainties enables a more precise determination of m_s from tau decays, and the authors demonstrate that with a factor-of-two improvement in the measurement of tau decays into strange particles, the determination of |V_us| from this sum rule would become more precise than the current world average, potentially addressing the question of CKM unitarity violation in the first row.
