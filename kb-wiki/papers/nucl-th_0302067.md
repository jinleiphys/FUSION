# Vector meson production and nucleon resonance analysis in the Giessen coupled-channel model

> We present a nucleon resonance analysis by simultaneously considering all pion- and photon-induced experimental data on the final states $γN$, $πN$, $2πN$, $ηN$, $KΛ$, $KΣ$, and $ωN$ for energies from the nucleon mass up to $\sqrt s = 2$ GeV. In this analysis we find strong evidence for various higher lying resonances as, e.g., the $P_{13}(1900)$. The $ωN$ production mechanism is dominated by large $P_{11}(1710)$ and $P_{13}(1900)$ contributions.

## Key claim

The Giessen coupled-channel model provides strong evidence for higher-lying nucleon resonances such as the P13(1900), with the omega-N production mechanism dominated by large contributions from the P11(1710) and P13(1900) resonances, based on a simultaneous fit to all available pion- and photon-induced data up to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors developed a unitary coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model (the Giessen model) that solves the Bethe-Salpeter equation via a K-matrix approximation with intermediate particles set on their mass shell. They performed a partial-wave decomposition in total spin J, parity P, and isospin I for all final-state combinations (gamma N, pi N, 2pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, and omega N), using the same Lagrangians for both pion- and photon-induced reactions. Resonance properties were extracted through a chi-squared minimization procedure against a total of about 6800 experimental data points, comprising partial-wave analyses and cross-section and polarization data.

## Key numbers

- Analysis covers energies from the nucleon mass up to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV
- Total of about 6800 experimental data points were used in the chi-squared minimization
- Strong evidence found for the P13(1900) resonance in omega-N production
- Omega-N production dominated by P11(1710) and P13(1900) contributions
- In omega-N photoproduction, a large contribution from the pi t-channel exchange was observed
- A clear threshold effect in K-Lambda photoproduction is visible due to the opening of the omega-N final state

## Context

This work extends the Giessen coupled-channel model previously developed by the same authors (Penner and Mosel, 2002-2003) to include omega-N final states alongside gamma N, pi N, 2pi N, eta N, K Lambda, and K Sigma, addressing the problem of "missing" resonances predicted by quark models that couple weakly to pi N final states. The analysis enables future inclusion of spin-5/2 resonance effects and detailed treatment of individual 2pi N final states (rho N, pi Delta), as noted in the outlook referencing work by Vitalyi (2003).
