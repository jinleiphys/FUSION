# Lepton mass effects in the Bethe-Heitler process

> We develop the full finite lepton mass formalism for the production of real photons via the Bethe-Heitler reaction of unpolarized leptons off unpolarized nucleons. Genuine lepton mass effects are described, in particular their dependence upon the lepton mass and the initial beam energy, as well as their sensitivity to the nucleon isospin. In the minimum momentum transfer region, these effects dominate the muon induced proton cross section and become significant for electron scattering at small $x_B$.

## Key claim

The paper develops a full finite lepton mass formalism for the Bethe-Heitler process and shows that, contrary to the common ultra-relativistic approximation, lepton mass effects can dominate the cross section in specific kinematic regions even at high beam energies, particularly at the minimum momentum transfer and near lepton propagator quasi-singularities.

## Method

The authors derived the complete leptonic tensor for the Bethe-Heitler process including finite lepton mass terms, expressed as a polynomial expansion in the squared reduced lepton mass parameter μ² = m²/Δ². They contracted this tensor with the standard hadronic tensor for unpolarized nucleons, obtaining the differential cross section d⁵σ/dk′dΩₑdΩᵧ. The formalism was then evaluated numerically for electron and muon scattering off proton and neutron targets using the Galster parametrization for nucleon electromagnetic form factors, and compared to the massless limit.

## Key numbers

- For muon scattering off protons at minimum momentum transfer, mass effects become sizeable (Rμ > 1) when xB < 0.26; for electron scattering, when xB < 0.02
- At the lepton propagator quasi-singularity, the ratio Rμ reaches a maximum value of about 1/2 at small τ and a minimum value of about -1 at the maximum kinematically allowed Q²
- The ratio Rμ at minimum momentum transfer scales approximately as 6 × (m²/M²) × (1-xB)²/xB⁴ × (GE²/GM²) for the proton
- The calculations were performed at incident beam energies relevant to JLab and COMPASS, including 190 GeV for muon scattering

## Context

This work extends the massless Bethe-Heitler formalism used in previous calculations (Belitsky et al., 2002) and addresses the ultra-relativistic approximation commonly employed in electron scattering experiments at MAMI, JLab, DESY, and muon scattering at CERN/COMPASS. The authors note that a recent work by Kuraev et al. (2010) on bremsstrahlung and pair-creation processes motivated a closer look at finite mass effects. The results are directly relevant to the experimental determination of generalized nucleon polarizabilities and generalized parton distributions via (deeply) virtual Compton scattering, particularly for the Ji sum rule measurement at future electron-ion colliders.
