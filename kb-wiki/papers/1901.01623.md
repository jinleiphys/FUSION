# The QCD Kondo phase in quark stars

> We study light (u, d) quark matter with charm impurities. These impurities are added to the Lagrangian density. We derive the equation of state (EOS) of this kind of quark matter, which contains a Kondo phase. We explore this EOS and study the structure of stars, identifying the effects of the Kondo phase. Solving the TOV equations and computing the mass-radius diagram, we find that the presence of a Kondo phase leads to smaller and lighter stars.

## Key claim

The presence of a QCD Kondo phase in quark matter, characterized by a non-zero gap \(|\Delta|\), softens the equation of state and leads to lighter and smaller quark stars compared to stars without this phase; however, the softening only occurs for specific values of the Lagrange multiplier \(\lambda\) (near \(\lambda = 0\) GeV), while other values of \(\lambda\) produce harder equations of state and heavier stars.

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian density for light (u, d) quark matter with charm impurities, using a four-quark interaction term with color structure \(\lambda^a \lambda^a\) as in one-gluon exchange. They treat the system in a mean-field approach, factorizing the interaction to obtain condensates and a gap function \(\Delta\). From the mean-field Lagrangian they derive the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature, then compute the pressure and energy density as functions of the light quark chemical potential \(\mu\) and the Lagrange multiplier \(\lambda\) (which acts as an effective chemical potential for heavy quarks). Finally, they solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations for quark stars.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff parameter \(\Lambda = 0.65\) GeV and the coupling strength \(G_c \Lambda^2 = (9/2) \times 2.0\) are taken from the NJL model for \(N_f = 2\).
- The MIT bag model comparison uses quark mass \(m_q = 10\) MeV and statistical factor \(\gamma_q = 6\).
- For small heavy quark densities \(n_Q\), the maximal stellar mass approaches \(M_{\text{max}} \to 3.7 M_{\odot}\).
- The smallest maximal masses occur for \(\lambda = 0\) GeV, with larger masses for all other values of \(\lambda\) (both positive and negative).

## Context

This work extends the study of the QCD Kondo effect in quark matter, first proposed by Yasui (2016) using a non-perturbative mean-field approach, to the astrophysical context of quark stars. It contrasts with earlier work by Weber (1980s) and more recent pQCD studies by Fraga (2018), which concluded that charm-containing quark stars would be unstable. The results show that the Kondo phase softens the equation of state relative to the MIT bag model, but still allows stars with masses up to about \(3.7 M_{\odot}\), which is higher than typical neutron star masses. This enables future studies of transport properties and observational signatures of Kondo phase matter in compact stars.
