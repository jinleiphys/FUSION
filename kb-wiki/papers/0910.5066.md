# Low density symmetry energy effects in the neutron star crust properties

> The form of the nuclear symmetry energy $E_s$ around saturation point density leads to a different crust-core transition point in the neutron star and affect the crust properties. We show that the knowledge about $E_s$ close to the saturation point is not sufficient, because the very low density behaviour is relevant. We also claim that crust properties are strongly influenced by the very high density behavior of $E_s$, so in order to conclude about the form of low density part of the symmetry energy one must isolate properly the high density part.

## Key claim

The authors show that the very low density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy \(E_s\) is essential for determining the crust-core transition point in neutron stars, but that the high density behavior of \(E_s\) also strongly influences crust properties and must be isolated before conclusions about the low density part can be drawn.

## Method

The authors constructed a family of symmetry energy parametrizations (labeled \(k0\), \(k5\), \(k10\)) that share the same isoscalar part of the interaction, the same slope \(L=80\) MeV and curvature \(K_{asy} = -450\) MeV at saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), and nearly identical high-density behavior, but differ only in the asymptotic value of \(E_s\) as density approaches zero (0, 5, and 10 MeV). They then computed the crust-core transition density \(n_c\) from the vanishing of the compressibility under constant chemical potential \(K_\mu\), and calculated the resulting crust thickness and crustal fraction of the moment of inertia for neutron stars of varying masses.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density is taken as \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and the symmetry energy at that density is \(E_s(n_0) = 30 \pm 1\) MeV.
- The isoscalar compressibility at saturation is \(K_0 = 240\) MeV.
- For the \(k\) family, the critical density \(n_c\) for the crust-core transition is 0.080 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(E_s(0)=0\) MeV, 0.073 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(E_s(0)=5\) MeV, and 0.065 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(E_s(0)=10\) MeV.
- For the MDI family, the critical densities are \(n_c = 0.092\) fm\(^{-3}\) (model \(b\)), 0.095 fm\(^{-3}\) (model \(c\)), and 0.160 fm\(^{-3}\) (model \(d\)).
- The upper bound on the crustal moment of inertia for the Vela pulsar from Link (1999) is 1.4% of the total moment of inertia; the \(k10\) model (with \(E_s(0)=10\) MeV) gives a crustal fraction below this value for a wide range of stellar masses, implying a Vela pulsar mass no greater than about 1 \(M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kubis (2007) on the connection between symmetry energy and crust-core transition, and responds to experimental results from Kowalski (2007) and Natowitz (2010) suggesting that the symmetry energy remains large at densities well below \(n_0\), contrary to the common theoretical expectation that it goes nearly linearly to zero. The authors construct a controlled family of symmetry energies to isolate low-density effects from high-density effects, enabling future comparisons with observational constraints such as the Vela pulsar moment of inertia bound from Link (1999).
