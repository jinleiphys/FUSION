# Flavor-dependent EMC effect from a nucleon swelling model

> We present our results for a flavor-dependent EMC effect based on the nIMParton nuclear PDFs, in which the $x$-dependence is described with a nucleon swelling model. The nuclear correction from nucleon swelling is considered through a modification of the initial valence quark distributions instead of a dynamical rescaling. To probe the flavor-dependence of the model, the experimental observables are calculated applying nIMParton nuclear modifications for various experiments: parity-violating deep inelastic scattering on nuclear target, pion-induced Drell-Yan, and W-boson production in proton-nucleus collisions. In addition, we present the expected effect for the spectator-tagged deep inelastic scattering process, which will be performed by the CLAS12 collaboration with the ALERT detector.

# Flavor-dependent EMC effect from a nucleon swelling model

## Key claim

The flavor-dependence of the EMC effect in the nIMParton model arises solely from the difference in the widths of the initial up and down valence quark distributions, rather than from isovector mean fields or flavor-dependent nuclear forces. The predicted differences between up and down quark nuclear modifications are small, with the maximum difference between bound proton and bound neutron EMC ratios reaching about 3% at x around 0.5, in contrast to the larger flavor-dependent effects predicted by the CBT model.

## Method

The authors used the nIMParton nuclear PDFs, which implement a nucleon swelling model where the increase in quark confinement radius modifies the widths of initial valence quark distributions according to the Heisenberg uncertainty principle, rather than through dynamical rescaling. The nucleon swelling parameter δ_A was modeled as proportional to the Residual Strong Interaction Energy (RSIE) with a single free parameter α = 0.00563 MeV⁻¹ determined by global fit to nuclear DIS data. The authors then calculated experimental observables for parity-violating deep inelastic scattering (PVDIS), pion-induced Drell-Yan, W-boson production in proton-nucleus collisions, and spectator-tagged DIS, applying the nIMParton nuclear modifications to probe flavor dependence.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon radius swellings from nIMParton global analysis: 0.8% for deuteron, 2.0% for ³He, and 8.1% for ²⁰⁸Pb
- The free parameter α in the swelling model was fixed at 0.00563 MeV⁻¹ from the global fit
- The maximum difference between bound proton and bound neutron EMC ratios in ⁴He is about 3% at x around 0.5
- The ALERT experiment statistical error bars are expected to be 1 to 2%
- The Q² scale for W-boson production calculations was chosen as the W-boson mass scale, with center-of-mass energy √s = 5.520 TeV for proton-lead collisions
- The initial scale for valence quark distributions was Q₀² = 0.067 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the nIMParton global analysis (Wang et al., 2015) which studied nuclear parton distributions with a nonperturbative input of only three valence quarks, using nucleon swelling to produce the EMC effect. It provides predictions that contrast with the CBT model (Bentz et al., 2010), which predicts larger flavor-dependent nuclear effects from isovector-vector mean fields (ρ⁰ meson) arising from neutron or proton excess in nuclei. The results enable future experiments to distinguish between these models: PVDIS measurements on ²⁰⁸Pb, pion-induced Drell-Yan experiments, W-boson production at LHC or RHIC, and the upcoming CLAS12/ALERT spectator-tagged DIS program at JLab, which can probe the isospin-dependence of the EMC effect with statistical precision of 1-2%.
