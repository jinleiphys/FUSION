# Searching for True Muonium in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the production of the as-yet-undetected true muonium within the quark-gluon plasma formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, employing a relativistic Boltzmann transport framework coupled to viscous hydrodynamic simulations. The obtained effective cross sections for central collisions are 1.23~$μb$ in AuAu collisions with $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$~GeV and 14.2~$μb$ in PbPb collisions with $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$~TeV, resulting in a yield of $\mathcal{O}(10^4)$ and $\mathcal{O}(10^5)$ true muonium per billion $AA$ collisions at RHIC and the LHC, respectively. This establishes heavy-ion collisions as a promising process for detecting true muonium.

# Searching for True Muonium in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Heavy-ion collisions may offer the most promising opportunity for the first observation of true muonium, with yields more than 10^3 times larger than previously estimated by Chen (2012), provided the quark-gluon plasma reaches temperatures of several hundred MeV and the bound state is produced thermally via next-to-leading-order parton processes.

## Method
The authors calculated the scattering amplitudes for the two dominant production channels, q + anti-q -> (mu+ mu-) + g and q + g -> (mu+ mu-) + q, using dressed quark and gluon masses from the Dynamical Quasiparticle Model with temperature-dependent strong coupling. These amplitudes were integrated into a relativistic Boltzmann transport equation that was self-consistently coupled to viscous hydrodynamic simulations (MUSIC package) of the quark-gluon plasma evolution at RHIC and LHC energies. The transport equation included only a gain term, ignoring dissociation due to the long true muonium lifetime relative to the QGP lifetime.

## Key numbers
- Effective cross section for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: 1.23 microbarn
- Effective cross section for central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: 14.2 microbarn
- Yield of true muonium per billion central AA collisions at RHIC: O(10^4)
- Yield of true muonium per billion central AA collisions at LHC: O(10^5)
- Maximum initial QGP temperature at RHIC (tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c): 320 MeV
- Maximum initial QGP temperature at LHC (tau_0 = 0.4 fm/c): 450 MeV
- True muonium mass: approximately 211 MeV (2 x muon mass)
- Binding energy: approximately -1.4/n^2 keV
- Bound state formation time: 1.72 x 10^(-21) seconds
- QGP lifetime: O(10^(-23)) seconds
- Effective cross sections in ultra-peripheral collisions: 19 nb at RHIC and 84 nb at LHC (from Azevedo (2019) rescaled with Ginzburg (1998) ratios)
- True muonium yield changes by a factor of two when QGP temperature varies by 30 MeV

## Context
This work extends the earlier calculation by Chen (2012) by using thermal quark masses with three flavors and temperature-dependent strong coupling, and by employing viscous rather than ideal hydrodynamic simulations, yielding production rates more than 10^3 times larger. The predicted effective cross sections are comparable to those of the Upsilon meson in heavy-ion collisions, placing true muonium between J/psi and Upsilon in detectable yield via di-lepton reconstruction. The result establishes true muonium as a novel probe of early-stage QGP temperature, complementing existing probes such as direct photons (which are produced at later stages and affected by collective flow) and thermal dileptons (which suffer from open-charm hadron decay background).
