# Dispersion relations for hadronic light-by-light scattering in triangle kinematics

> We present a new strategy for the dispersive evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $a_μ$. The new approach directly applies in the kinematic limit relevant for $a_μ$: one of the photons is treated as an external electromagnetic field with vanishing momentum, so that the kinematics corresponds to a triangle. We derive expressions for the relevant single-particle intermediate states, as well as the tensor decompositions of the two-pion sub-processes that appear in addition to those needed in the established dispersive approach. The existing approach is based on a set of dispersion relations for the hadronic light-by-light tensor in four-point kinematics. At present it is not known how to consistently include in this framework resonant intermediate states of spin 2 or larger, due to the appearance of kinematic singularities that can be traced back to the redundancy of the tensor decomposition. We show that our new approach circumvents this problem and enables dispersion relations in the limit of triangle kinematics that are manifestly free from kinematic singularities, paving the way towards a data-driven evaluation of all relevant exclusive hadronic intermediate states.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new dispersive framework for hadronic light-by-light scattering that directly works in the triangle kinematics relevant for the muon anomalous magnetic moment, thereby circumventing the problem of kinematic singularities that plague the established four-point approach for spin-2 and higher intermediate states. The new method enables dispersion relations that are manifestly free from kinematic singularities, provided the sub-process tensor decompositions are handled correctly.

## Method

The authors first take the limit of vanishing photon momentum relevant for the muon g-2, then write dispersion relations in the photon virtualities rather than in the Mandelstam variables. They derive Bardeen-Tung-Tarrach (BTT) tensor decompositions for the required sub-processes: the five-particle process γ*γ*γ → 2π and the process ππ → ππγ. For the γ*γ*γ → 2π process, they construct a set of 74 tensor structures with 38 Tarrach redundancies, then reduce to 27 independent structures in the soft-photon limit after imposing crossing symmetries and removing kinematic zeros. For ππ → ππγ, they derive a decomposition with 6 tensor structures and show that in the soft-photon limit only two independent scalar functions remain.

## Key numbers

* The BTT decomposition for γ*γ*γ → 2π starts with 76 naive rank-3 tensor structures, of which 40 map directly to zero under gauge projectors, leaving 74 off-shell structures in 20 photon-crossing classes.
* The γ*γ*γ → 2π amplitude has 38 Tarrach redundancies, yielding 36 independent structures in D dimensions, reduced to 27 independent structures in 4 space-time dimensions (matching the 3³ = 27 helicity amplitudes for three off-shell photons).
* In the soft-photon limit, the regular part of the γ*γ*γ → 2π amplitude is expressed in terms of 28 structures, further reduced to a basis of 27 structures after trading one redundancy for a kinematic constraint.
* For ππ → ππγ, the BTT decomposition yields 6 tensor structures with 3 Tarrach redundancies; in the soft-photon limit at q→0, only 2 independent scalar functions remain, with crossing symmetry implying one relation between them.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach to hadronic light-by-light scattering developed by Colangelo et al. (2015, 2017), which uses four-point kinematics and BTT decompositions but suffers from kinematic singularities for spin-2 and higher intermediate states. The new triangle-kinematics approach avoids the basis dependence and sum-rule issues that affect tensor-meson resonances like the f₂(1270) in the established framework. It enables future data-driven evaluations of two-pion contributions beyond S-waves, including D-wave resonances, and will complement the existing dispersive approach for scalar and axial-vector contributions.
