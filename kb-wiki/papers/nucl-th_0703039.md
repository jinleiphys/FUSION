# Relativistic mean-field model with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings

> Within the relativistic mean-field approach, we extend the Miyazaki model, where the NN$σ$ and NN$ω$ interactions are modified to suppress the couplings between positive- and negative-energy states of a nucleon in matter. Assuming appropriate density-dependence of the meson-nucleon couplings, we study nuclear matter and finite nuclei. The model can reproduce the observed properties of $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca well. We also examine if the model is natural.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Miyazaki model by introducing density-dependent parameters at the NNσ and NNω vertices, which allows the model to simultaneously reproduce the scalar and vector potentials from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations and the observed incompressibility of nuclear matter, while also yielding good agreement with finite-nuclei data for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca. The hedge is that the density-dependence is assumed via a simple power-law form, and the model is only tested for two specific parameter choices (type A and type B).

## Method

The authors modify the NNσ and NNω vertices using energy projection operators, introducing two parameters (λ₁, λ₂) that control the strength of couplings between positive- and negative-energy nucleon states. These parameters are made density-dependent via λ = 1 − a(ρ_v/ρ₀)^b, where ρ₀ is the saturation density. Two parameter families are studied: type A, where λ₁ˢ = λ₂ᵛ and λ₂ˢ = λ₁ᵛ, and type B, where λ₁ˢ = λ₁ᵛ and λ₂ˢ = λ₂ᵛ. The coupling constants g_σ and g_ω are fixed by the nuclear matter saturation condition (W = −15.75 MeV at ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³), and the four parameters a–d are tuned to reproduce the DBHF scalar and vector potentials and the observed incompressibility K = 210 ± 30 MeV. Finite-nuclei calculations for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca are then performed using a standard iteration procedure.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy per nucleon: W = −15.75 MeV at ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ (input condition)
- Nuclear incompressibility: K = 239.3 MeV (type A), K = 202.9 MeV (type B)
- Coupling constants in vacuum: g_σ = 12.24, g_ω = 14.97 (type A); g_σ = 16.97, g_ω = 20.69 (type B)
- σ meson mass: m_σ = 463.4 MeV (type A), m_σ = 513.0 MeV (type B)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ⁴⁰Ca: 5.78 MeV (type A), 7.33 MeV (type B), compared to experimental 8.45 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon for ¹⁶O: 4.22 MeV (type A), 6.06 MeV (type B), compared to experimental 7.98 MeV
- RMS charge radius of ⁴⁰Ca: 3.482 fm (both types, fitted to experiment)
- RMS charge radius of ¹⁶O: 2.83 fm (type A), 2.77 fm (type B), compared to experimental 2.73 fm
- Neutron-proton radius difference for ⁴⁰Ca: −0.080 fm (type A), −0.074 fm (type B), compared to experimental 0.05 ± 0.05 fm
- Neutron-proton radius difference for ¹⁶O: −0.047 fm (type A), −0.041 fm (type B), compared to experimental 0 fm
- Single-particle energies for ⁴⁰Ca (proton 1d₅/₂): 15.6 MeV (type A), 17.9 MeV (type B), compared to experimental 15.5 MeV
- Single-particle energies for ⁴⁰Ca (proton 2s₁/₂): 11.5 MeV (type A), 12.6 MeV (type B), compared to experimental 10.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Miyazaki model (Miyazaki, 1997), which introduced modified NNσ and NNω vertices to suppress couplings between positive- and negative-energy nucleon states, by making the vertex parameters density-dependent. The model is designed to reproduce the scalar and vector optical potentials from DBHF calculations (Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990) and is compared to Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) (Serot and Walecka, 1986). The approach is related to the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model (Guichon, 1988) and the quark-mean field (QMF) model, which also yield density-dependent couplings through the scalar polarizability. The naturalness analysis using naive dimensional analysis (Georgi, 1993) shows all dimensionless coefficients are below 2.0, supporting the model's validity as an effective field theory. The work enables future studies of finite nuclei with density-dependent relativistic mean-field models that incorporate many-body effects and quark substructure.
