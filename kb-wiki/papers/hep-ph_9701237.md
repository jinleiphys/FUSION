# In-Medium Chiral Perturbation Theory and Pion Weak Decay in the Presence of Background Matter

> Two-point functions related to the pion weak decay constant f_πare calculated from the generating functional of chiral perturbation theory in the mean-field approximation and the heavy-baryon limit. The aim is to demonstrate that Lorentz invariance is violated in the presence of background matter. This fact manifests itself in the splitting of both f_πand the pion mass into uncorrelated time- and spacelike parts. We emphasize the different in-medium renormalizations of the correlation functions, show the inequivalence between the in-medium values of f_πdeduced from Walecka-type models, on the one hand, and QCD sum rules, on the other hand, and elaborate on the importance for some nuclear physics observables.

# In-Medium Chiral Perturbation Theory and Pion Weak Decay in the Presence of Background Matter

## Key claim
Lorentz invariance is violated in the presence of background matter, causing the pion weak decay constant and pion mass to split into uncorrelated timelike and spacelike parts; the in-medium value of fπ deduced from Walecka-type models is inequivalent to that from QCD sum rules, and the quantity fπ^(AP) extracted from the axial-current-pseudoscalar correlator is unphysical compared to the model-independent fπ^(t) that enters the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation.

## Method
The generating functional of chiral perturbation theory (χPT) was used in the mean-field approximation and the heavy-baryon limit to calculate in-medium two-point functions (axial-current–pseudoscalar, axial-vector–axial-vector, and pseudoscalar–pseudoscalar) at tree level. Background matter was introduced by replacing the nucleon bilinear N̄N with the matter density ρ in the effective Lagrangian. Functional derivatives with respect to external sources yielded the in-medium correlation functions, from which the timelike and spacelike components of fπ and the pion mass were extracted separately.

## Key numbers
- The combination (c₂+c₃)mπ² extracted from the isospin-even πN scattering length a⁺πN equals −26 MeV.
- The pion–nucleon sigma term σN = 45 MeV (also given as 0.228 fm⁻¹).
- At normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the timelike in-medium pion mass mπ*² almost equals the free pion mass mπ².
- For a purely spacelike pion momentum at ρ₀, the mass pole is approximately −4 mπ².
- The S-wave weak decay constant fπ^S ≈ 0.9 fπ at ρ₀.
- The P-wave weak decay constant fπ^P ≈ (1/4) fπ at ρ₀.
- For radiative muon capture on ⁴⁰Ca with |k₀| = 0.8 mμ, |k| = 0.2 mμ, and ρ = 0.8 ρ₀: g_p^(t)/g_p ≈ 0.73 and g_p^(s)/g_p ≈ 0.37.
- The ratio C_MEC^(ρ=ρ₀)/C_MEC^(ρ=0) ≈ 1.15, corresponding to a 15% enhancement of the two-body axial charge operator amplitude.

## Context
This work extends chiral perturbation theory to finite nuclear density, building on prior studies of in-medium χPT by Leutwyler (1994), Kaiser, Naus, and Weise (1993), and Birse, McGovern, and Walet (1992). It contrasts with Walecka-type hadrodynamical models by showing that the in-medium σ-field expectation value (fπ^(AP)) is unphysical, while the quantity fπ^(t) from the GOR relation is model-independent. The results enable calculations of in-medium induced pseudoscalar couplings relevant to radiative muon capture experiments (as studied by Roesch et al. and Hass et al.) and provide constraints on two-body axial charge operators needed for first-forbidden weak decays in heavy nuclei (as discussed by Warburton and Towner).
