# Universality of sound modes in kinetic theory

> We present a simple approach to extract hydrodynamic sound modes and non-hydrodynamic modes in kinetic theories from response functions of the energy-momentum tensor. By comparing the response functions in four types of kinetic theories, namely the Relaxation-Time Approximation, scalar $ϕ^4$ theory, SU(3) Yang-Mills theory and QCD kinetic theory, we find a remarkable degree of universality for the sound mode, even beyond the hydrodynamic regime.

## Key claim
The paper claims a remarkable degree of universality in the hydrodynamic sound mode across four different kinetic theories (Relaxation-Time Approximation, scalar phi^4 theory, SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, and QCD kinetic theory), extending even beyond the traditional hydrodynamic regime where gradient expansions are expected to break down.

## Method
The authors compute energy-momentum response functions from linearized perturbations on a homogeneous equilibrium background in four distinct kinetic theories. They extract hydrodynamic sound modes and non-hydrodynamic modes by fitting the response functions to a two-component oscillatory-damped form, separating late-time sound mode behavior (after hydrodynamization time t_bar = 4 pi) from early-time non-hydrodynamic contributions (after t_bar = 0.4 pi). The extracted complex frequencies (dispersion and damping) and residues are compared across theories and against first-order and second-order hydrodynamic predictions.

## Key numbers
- Sound mode dispersion and damping show universality among kinetic theories at wave numbers up to at least k_bar = 1.0, well beyond the hydrodynamic regime.
- At small gradient k_bar = 0.1, all kinetic theories converge to the first-order hydrodynamic limit described by cos(c_s k t) exp(-2/3 (eta/(sT)) k^2 t).
- The hydrodynamization time is identified as t_bar = 4 pi, after which the sound mode dominates the response.
- Non-hydrodynamic mode fitting excludes very early times before t_bar = 0.4 pi, where additional non-hydrodynamic excitations play a prominent role.

## Context
This work extends the study of macroscopic universality in kinetic theories beyond the near-equilibrium hydrodynamic limit established by Romatschke (2015), Moore (2018), and Kurkela (2017). It builds on prior kinetic theory frameworks including the Relaxation-Time Approximation, scalar phi^4 theory from Mullins (2022), SU(3) Yang-Mills kinetic theory from Kurkela (2015), and QCD kinetic theory from Kurkela (2018) and Du (2020). The results suggest the possibility of constructing an effective hydrodynamic theory valid beyond the traditional hydrodynamic regime, which could improve descriptions of the Quark-Gluon Plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
