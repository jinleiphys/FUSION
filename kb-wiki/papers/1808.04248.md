# Angle and magnitude decorrelation in the factorization breaking of collective flow

> The collective harmonic flow in heavy-ion collisions correlates particles at all transverse momenta to be emitted preferably some directions. The factorization breaking coefficient measures the small decorrelation of the flow harmonics at two different transverse momenta. Using the hydrodynamic model I study in details the decorrelation of the harmonic flow due to the flow angle and the flow magnitude decorrelation at two transverse momenta. The effect can be seen in experiment measuring factorization breaking coefficients for the square of the harmonic flow vector at two transverse momenta. The hydrodynamic model predicts that the decorrelation of the flow magnitudes is about one half of the decorrelation of the overall flow (combining flow angle and flow magnitude decorrelations). These results are consistent with the principal component analysis of correlators of flow vectors squared.

# Angle and magnitude decorrelation in the factorization breaking of collective flow

## Key claim

Using a hydrodynamic model, the decorrelation of harmonic flow magnitudes at two different transverse momenta accounts for about one half of the overall flow decorrelation (which combines both flow angle and flow magnitude decorrelations), with the specific ratio depending on the centrality and harmonic order.

## Method

The author performed 3+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations with Monte Carlo Glauber model initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, generating 150 or 600 events per centrality class (0-5% and 30-40%). The flow vectors were reconstructed event-by-event in the transverse momentum range 0.3 to 3.0 GeV, allowing separate calculation of flow angle decorrelation and flow magnitude decorrelation using multiparticle correlators.

## Key numbers

- The flow magnitude decorrelation accounts for roughly one half of the total flow decorrelation: 1 - r_n^{v_n^2}(p_a,p_b) is approximately (1/2) * (1 - r_{n|n;2}(p_a,p_b))
- The subleading eigenmode of the flow magnitude correlation matrix is related to the subleading eigenmode of the flow correlation matrix by a factor of approximately 0.7: v_{v_n^2}^{(1)}(p_a) is approximately 0.7 * v_n^{(1)}(p_a)
- The freeze-out temperature used in the model is 150 MeV
- The initial condition parameter alpha=0.1 is used to describe the centrality dependence of charged particle density
- The transverse momentum range analyzed is p_perp in [0.3, 3.0] GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of flow factorization breaking in pseudorapidity (Bozek 2017, Wu 2018) to the transverse momentum domain, where the separate contributions of flow angle and flow magnitude decorrelation had not been systematically studied. The results are consistent with principal component analysis of correlation matrices of flow vectors squared (Bhalerao 2014). The predictions for weighted factorization breaking coefficients (using different powers of v_n) can be tested experimentally using 4-particle correlations, similar to how angle decorrelation in pseudorapidity was measured by Aaboud (2017) and Jia (2017).
