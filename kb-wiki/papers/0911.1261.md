# Zwitters: particles between quantum and classical

> We describe both quantum particles and classical particles in terms of a classical statistical ensemble, characterized by a probability distribution in phase space. By use of a wave function in phase space both can be treated in the same quantum formalism. The different dynamics of quantum and classical particles resides then only from different evolution equations for the probability distribution. Quantum particles are characterized by a specific choice of observables and time evolution of the probability density. All relations for a quantum particle in a potential, including interference and tunneling, can be described in terms of the classical probability distribution. We formulate the concept of zwitters - particles for which the time evolution interpolates between quantum and classical particles. Experiments can test a small parameter which quantifies possible deviations from quantum mechanics.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that both quantum and classical particles can be described by a classical statistical ensemble with a probability distribution in phase space, and introduces "zwitters" as particles whose time evolution interpolates between quantum and classical behavior. The hedge is that this interpolation is not uniquely defined; the specific form given (a linear combination of quantum and classical Hamiltonians) is only one example, and the parameter quantifying deviations from quantum mechanics is not expected to be a universal number but may depend on the physical setting.

## Method
The author constructs a real classical wave function ψ_C(z,p) from a positive-definite classical probability distribution w(z,p) in phase space, then defines non-commuting quantum observables X_Q and P_Q (Bopp operators) that obey the canonical commutation relation. The quantum time evolution is given by a non-linear differential equation for w (or a linear equation for ψ_C) that reproduces the Wigner function evolution for a quantum particle in a potential. Zwitters are defined by a Hamiltonian H_γ = cos²γ H_W + sin²γ H_L, where H_W gives quantum evolution and H_L gives classical Liouville evolution, with γ=0 for quantum particles and γ=π/2 for classical particles.

## Key numbers
- For a Coulomb potential, the energy width of the zwitter ground state is ΔE = f₁ sin²γ |E₀|, with the calculable constant f₁ satisfying 2 < f₁ < 10.
- The mean energy shift of the zwitter ground state is δE^(γ) = f₂ sin²γ ΔE, with 0.5 ≤ f₂ ≤ 2.5 for a Coulomb potential.
- From relaxation time measurements of nuclear polarized ³He, an estimated bound |γ| ≲ 3 × 10⁻¹⁴ is obtained, based on ΔE < 0.6 × 10⁻²⁰ eV extracted from that system.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior program (Wetterich 2009) of embedding quantum statistics in classical statistics, and builds on the Koopman (1931) Hilbert space formulation of classical mechanics. It contrasts with approaches that treat the Schrödinger equation as a classical field equation (e.g., the "classical system" interpretation). The concept of zwitters enables experimental tests of quantum mechanics by bounding a small parameter γ, and may apply to effective one-particle descriptions of macroscopic objects such as liquid droplets (Cov 2009) or collective quantum states like Bose-Einstein condensates.
