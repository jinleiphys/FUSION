# Photon-tagged and B-meson-tagged b-jet production at the LHC

> Tagged jet measurements in high energy hadronic and nuclear reactions provide constraints on the energy and parton flavor origin of the parton shower that recoils against the tagging particle. Such additional insight can be especially beneficial in illuminating the mechanisms of heavy flavor production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC and their modification in the heavy ion environment, which are not fully understood. With this motivation, we present theoretical results for isolated-photon-tagged and B-meson-tagged b-jet production at center-of-mass energy 5.1 TeV for comparison to the upcoming lead-lead data. We find that photon-tagged b-jets exhibit smaller momentum imbalance shift in nuclear matter, and correspondingly smaller energy loss, than photon-tagged light flavor jets. Our results show that B-meson tagging is most effective in ensuring that the dominant fraction of recoiling jets originate from prompt b-quarks. Interestingly, in this channel the large suppression of the cross section is not accompanied by a significant momentum imbalance shift.

## Key claim

Photon-tagged b-jets exhibit a smaller momentum imbalance shift (and correspondingly smaller energy loss) in nuclear matter than photon-tagged light flavor jets; B-meson tagging ensures that 70-80% of recoiling jets originate from prompt b-quarks, but in this channel the large suppression of the cross section is not accompanied by a significant momentum imbalance shift.

## Method

The authors used Pythia 8 simulations with CTEQ6L1 parton distribution functions and the anti-kT jet algorithm to generate baseline cross sections for isolated-photon-tagged and B-meson-tagged b-jet production in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.1 TeV. They then applied a medium-modified cross section formalism that includes both radiative energy loss (via medium-induced gluon distributions) and collisional energy loss, with the medium modeled using an optical Glauber model with Bjorken expansion, to predict the nuclear modification in central Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon-nucleon pair: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.1 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC
- B-meson-tagged b-jet cross section is more than two orders of magnitude larger than photon-tagged b-jet cross section at the LHC
- In B-meson-tagged b-jet production, the b+bbar subprocess dominates, contributing 70-80% of the cross section across the kinematic region studied
- For photon-tagged b-jets, the mean momentum imbalance <z_jγ> shows a 5% down-shift for the symmetric cut (40 < pTγ < 80 GeV) and a 10% down-shift for the asymmetric cut (80 < pTγ < 120 GeV) in Pb+Pb relative to p+p
- For B-meson-tagged b-jets, <z_jB> shows a slight increase (up-shift) from p+p to Pb+Pb collisions, e.g., from 1.04 to 1.07-1.10 for the symmetric cut depending on coupling and mass parameters
- The coupling between jet and medium is set to g_med = 2.0 (with variations of 1.8 and 2.2), and the mass of the propagating system is varied between m_b and 2m_b

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of inclusive b-jet suppression in heavy ion collisions (Huang, 2013) to more differential observables that can better isolate prompt b-quark energy loss. It builds on earlier theoretical studies of photon-tagged heavy flavor (Stavreva, 2009; Hartanto, 2013; Kang, 2011) and heavy meson-tagged heavy flavor (Vitev, 2006; Zhu, 2006; Cao, 2015) in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus reactions. The predictions are designed for direct comparison with upcoming LHC lead-lead data at 5.1 TeV and can help resolve the long-standing discrepancy between theory and experiment regarding heavy quark suppression in the quark-gluon plasma.
