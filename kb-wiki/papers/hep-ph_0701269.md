# The pp -> K^+Sigma^+n cross section from missing mass spectra

> We utilize existing inclusive data on K^+ meson momentum spectra of the reaction pp -> K^+X at T_p = 2.3 - 2.85 GeV to deduce total cross sections for pp -> K^+Σ^+n. The method used to extract those cross sections is explained and discussed in detail. Our result for T_p = 2.85 GeV is consistent with the data point from a direct measurement at the same beam energy. The cross section obtained for T_p = 2.3 GeV is with 13.7\pm2.3 μb considerably smaller than the value found in a recent experiment by the COSY-11 Collaboration at a somewhat lower beam energy, indicating that the pp -> K^+Σ^+n reaction cross section could exhibit a rather unusual energy dependence.

## Key claim

The authors use inclusive \(K^+\) meson momentum spectra from the reaction \(pp \to K^+ X\) at beam kinetic energies \(T_p = 2.3\)–\(2.85\) GeV to extract total cross sections for \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^+ n\), finding that the cross section at \(T_p = 2.3\) GeV is \(13.7 \pm 2.3\) \(\mu\)b, which is considerably smaller than the COSY-11 Collaboration value at a somewhat lower beam energy, suggesting an unusual energy dependence that the abstract notes but does not quantify as a discrepancy.

## Method

The authors transform inclusive \(K^+\) meson momentum spectra from the \(pp \to K^+ X\) reaction into missing mass (\(M_X\)) spectra using Lorentz-invariant kinematic relations. They fit the \(pp \to K^+ \Lambda p\) contribution to the missing mass spectra below the \(K\Sigma N\) threshold, including \(\Lambda p\) final-state interactions via a Jost-function approach, then subtract this contribution to isolate the sum of the \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^0 p\) and \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^+ n\) channels in the region \(m_\Sigma + m_N \le M_X \le m_\Lambda + m_N + m_\pi\). The \(\Sigma\) production amplitude is determined by fitting the remaining missing mass spectrum, and the total cross section for \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^+ n\) is deduced by using known \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^0 p\) cross sections from direct measurements to separate the two \(\Sigma\) channels.

## Key numbers

- Cross section for \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^+ n\) at \(T_p = 2.3\) GeV: \(13.7 \pm 2.3\) \(\mu\)b (extracted from missing mass spectra of Siebert (year) data).
- Cross section for \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^+ n\) at \(T_p = 2.85\) GeV: consistent with a direct measurement data point at the same beam energy (no explicit number given for the direct point, but the extracted value is \(20.0 \pm 8.6\) \(\mu\)b from the fit with \(\Lambda p\) FSI at \(\theta_K = 32^\circ\)).
- Sum of \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^0 p\) and \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^+ n\) cross sections at \(T_p = 2.54\) GeV and \(\theta_K = 30^\circ\): \(45.7 \pm 1.8\) \(\mu\)b (with \(\Lambda p\) FSI).
- Mean cross section for \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^+ n\) from high-energy bubble chamber data (excess energy \(\epsilon = 60\)–\(1000\) MeV): \(49 \pm 5\) \(\mu\)b.
- COSY-11 result at \(\epsilon = 60\) MeV: as large as the high-energy mean, indicating no energy dependence over that range.

## Context

This work extends the sparse data base for the \(pp \to K^+ \Sigma^+ n\) reaction, which had only five high-energy bubble chamber points (Louttit (year), Sondhi (year), Dunwoodie (year), Chinowsky (year)) and a recent near-threshold measurement by the COSY-11 Collaboration (Rozek (year)). The extracted cross sections fill the energy gap between the COSY-11 result and the high-energy data, enabling tests of model calculations (Laget (year), Sibirtsev (year), Tsushima (year), Shyam (year), Zou (year)) that all underestimate the COSY-11 cross section by an order of magnitude. The results also provide a consistency check for the \(pp \to K^+ \Lambda p\) channel, which is compared with direct measurements from COSY-11 (Balewski (year), Sewerin (year), Kowina (year)) and COSY-TOF (Bilger (year)).
