# Spontaneous symmetry breaking and response functions

> We study the quantum phase transition occurring in an infinite homogeneous system of spin 1/2 fermions in a non-relativistic context. As an example we consider neutrons interacting through a simple spin-spin Heisenberg force. The two critical values of the coupling strength -- signaling the onset into the system of a finite magnetization and of the total magnetization, respectively -- are found and their dependence upon the range of the interaction is explored. The spin response function of the system in the region where the spin-rotational symmetry is spontaneously broken is also studied. For a ferromagnetic interaction the spin response along the direction of the spontaneous magnetization occurs in the particle-hole continuum and displays, for not too large momentum transfers, two distinct peaks. The response along the direction orthogonal to the spontaneous magnetization displays instead, beyond a softened and depleted particle-hole continuum, a collective mode to be identified with a Goldstone boson of type II. Notably, the random phase approximation on a Hartree-Fock basis accounts for it, in particular for its quadratic -- close to the origin -- dispersion relation. It is shown that the Goldstone boson contributes to the saturation of the energy-weighted sum rule for ~25% when the system becomes fully magnetized (that is in correspondence of the upper critical value of the interaction strength) and continues to grow as the interaction strength increases.

## Key claim

The paper studies the quantum phase transition in an infinite homogeneous system of spin-1/2 fermions (neutrons) interacting via a schematic spin-spin Heisenberg force, finding that the random phase approximation on a Hartree-Fock basis (RPA-HF) accounts for the Goldstone boson that appears in the broken-symmetry phase, specifically a type II Goldstone mode with a quadratic dispersion relation, but only for the transverse (spin-flip) response; the longitudinal response shows no collective mode when the system is fully magnetized for a zero-range interaction.

## Method

The authors model an infinite homogeneous neutron system with a ferromagnetic spin-spin interaction of the form \(V(k)=V_1 \vec{\sigma}_1 \cdot \vec{\sigma}_2 \frac{\lambda^2}{k^2+\lambda^2}\), treating the coupling strength \(V_1\) as a control parameter. They compute the lower and upper critical values of \(V_1\) (signaling onset of finite magnetization and full magnetization, respectively) using the Stoner equation derived from the equilibrium condition \(\omega^+_{k_F^+} = \omega^-_{k_F^-}\) within the anomalous single-particle propagator formalism, and compare these to results from RPA-HF. The spin response functions in the broken vacuum are then evaluated in the RPA-HF framework for both longitudinal (along the magnetization axis) and transverse (orthogonal) directions, using a zero-range force for simplicity.

## Key numbers

* Lower critical coupling for zero-range interaction: \(V_{1,c}^{\text{lower}} = -\frac{\pi^2}{mk_F} \cdot \frac{2}{3}\).
* Upper critical coupling for zero-range interaction: \(V_{1,c}^{\text{upper}} = -\frac{\pi^2}{mk_F} \frac{1}{2^{1/3}}\).
* Upper critical coupling for infinite-range force (\(x = \lambda/k_F \to 0\)): \(V_{1,c}^{\text{upper}} = -\frac{\pi^2}{mk_F} \frac{1}{2^{1/3}} \frac{3}{2}\).
* Lower critical coupling for infinite-range force: \(V_{1,c}^{\text{lower}} = -\frac{\pi^2}{mk_F}\).
* The Goldstone boson contributes approximately 25% to the saturation of the energy-weighted sum rule when the system becomes fully magnetized (at the upper critical coupling).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Beraudo et al., 2004) which computed the lower critical coupling in RPA-HF and the upper critical coupling only for zero-range force using the anomalous propagator formalism. Here, they compute both critical values as functions of the interaction range \(\lambda/k_F\) and explore the collective modes in the broken vacuum. The results are relevant to understanding ferromagnetic phase transitions in neutron stars and are analogous to the splitting of the giant dipole resonance in deformed nuclei (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975). The work enables future studies of the relativistic generalization of the system, where the counting rule for Goldstone bosons differs (Low and Manohar, 2002).
