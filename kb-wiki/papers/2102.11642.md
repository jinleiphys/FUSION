# High-precision determination of the electric and magnetic radius of the proton

> Using dispersion theory with an improved description of the two-pion continuum based on the precise Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering, we analyze recent data from electron-proton scattering. This allows for a high-precision determination of the electric and magnetic radius of the proton, $r_E = (0.838^{+0.005}_{-0.004}{}^{+0.004}_{-0.003})\,$fm and $r_M = (0.847\pm{0.004}\pm{0.004})\,$fm, where the first error refers to the fitting procedure using bootstrap and the data while the second one refers to the systematic uncertainty related to the underlying spectral functions.

## Key claim

Using dispersion theory with an improved description of the two-pion continuum based on the precise Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering, the authors determine the electric and magnetic radius of the proton from combined electron-proton scattering data, with the result that the extracted radii are consistent with earlier dispersion-theoretical determinations but have much improved and smaller uncertainties.

## Method

The authors analyze elastic electron-proton scattering cross section data from the PRad collaboration at Jefferson Laboratory and the A1 collaboration at the Mainz Microtron using unsubtracted dispersion relations for the Dirac and Pauli form factors. The spectral functions are expressed in terms of effective vector meson poles and continua, with the key improvement being the incorporation of a new representation of the isovector two-pion continuum derived from a Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering. Fits are performed using a chi-squared minimization with bootstrap resampling (1000 or 5000 samples) to determine statistical uncertainties, while systematic uncertainties from the spectral function parameterization are estimated by varying the number of effective vector meson poles from 2+2 up to 7+7.

## Key numbers

- Electric radius of the proton: r_E = (0.838^{+0.005}_{-0.004}{}^{+0.004}_{-0.003}) fm, where the first error is from the fitting procedure (bootstrap) and the second from systematic uncertainty in the spectral functions
- Magnetic radius of the proton: r_M = (0.847 ± 0.004 ± 0.004) fm, with the same error definitions
- For the PRad data alone: r_E = (0.829 ± 0.012 ± 0.001) fm and r_M = (0.843 ± 0.007^{+0.018}_{-0.012}) fm
- Best combined fit to PRad and Mainz data requires 5 isoscalar and 5 isovector poles with chi-squared/dof = 1.25
- The squared isovector charge radius from a sum rule in earlier work by Hoferichter et al. (2016) is (r_E^v)^2 = 0.405(36) fm^2, consistent with a lattice QCD calculation by Djukanovic et al. (2021) giving (r_E^v)^2 = 0.400(13)_sta(11)_sys fm^2

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theoretical framework of Lorenz et al. (2012, 2014) by incorporating an improved two-pion continuum representation from Hoferichter et al. (2016) based on Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering, and by including the new low-Q^2 PRad data from Xiong et al. (2019). The results are consistent with the CODATA value r_E = 0.8414(19) fm and with other recent analyses by Alarcon et al. (2020), Cui et al. (2021), and Atac et al. (2021), though the authors note that those approaches differ in their theoretical foundations. The improved precision enables future work including Bayesian uncertainty estimation, time-like form factor data, and neutron scattering data to determine neutron radii using chiral effective field theory.
