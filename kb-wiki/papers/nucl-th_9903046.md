# Isospin violation in the two-nucleon system

> I discuss isospin violation in an effective field theory appraoch of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The observed leading order charge independence breaking is explained in terms of one-pion exchange together with a four-nucleon contact term. A general classification scheme of corrections to this leading order results is presented and an explanation is offered why charge-dependent pion-nucleon coupling constants can not play a role in the observed isospin violation in the NN scattering lengths. Charge symmetry breaking, the size of $πγ$-exchange graphs and the pionless theory are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that leading-order charge independence breaking (CIB) in nucleon-nucleon scattering can be explained by the charged-to-neutral pion mass difference in one-pion exchange together with a four-nucleon contact term, but the pion contribution alone is not scale-independent and can never be uniquely disentangled from the contact term contribution.

## Method

The authors use the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) power divergence subtraction method, which resums lowest-order four-nucleon contact terms to generate the large S-wave scattering lengths and treats pion exchange perturbatively. They construct the most general effective Lagrangian including virtual photons and extend the power counting to treat isospin-violating effects, fitting the five coupling constants (C0, C2, D2 for the isospin-symmetric case and E0^(1), E0^(2) for CIB and CSB) to reproduce the np, nn, and pp scattering lengths and the np effective range.

## Key numbers

- CIB scattering length difference: Δa_CIB = 5.7 ± 0.3 fm, defined as (a_nn + a_pp)/2 - a_np
- CSB scattering length difference: Δa_CSB = a_pp - a_nn = 1.5 ± 0.5 fm
- np scattering length: a_np = (-23.714 ± 0.013) fm
- CIB effective range difference: Δr_CIB = 0.05 ± 0.08 fm
- CSB effective range difference: Δr_CSB = 0.10 ± 0.12 fm
- Fitted coupling constants at μ = m: C0 = -3.46 fm^2, C2 = 2.75 fm^4, D2 = 0.07 fm^4, E0^(1) = -6.47 fm^2, E0^(2) = 1.10 fm^2
- Pionless theory parameters: C̃0 = -3.42 fm^2, C̃2 = 6.05 fm^4, Ẽ0^(1) = 8.09 fm^2, Ẽ0^(2) = 1.07 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nucleon-nucleon interactions developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) by systematically including isospin violation from both the quark mass difference and electromagnetism. It provides a classification scheme that explains why charge-dependent pion-nucleon coupling constants are suppressed by two orders in the KSW expansion parameter, resolving a puzzle from earlier potential model calculations. The framework enables systematic calculation of higher-order corrections to CIB and CSB, and the authors note that the πγ-exchange contribution to CIB found in a recent calculation by Cheung and Fearing (1998) (Δa_CIB(πγ)/Δa_CIB(OPE) ≈ -1/14.6) agrees with the expected suppression by two powers of the expansion parameter, unlike some earlier meson-exchange model results.
