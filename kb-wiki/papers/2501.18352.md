# 't Hooft model in the temporal gauge

> I consider QCD$_2$ in the $N_c \to \infty$ limit at fixed $g^2N_c$. The derivation starts from equal-time $q\bar q$ bound states in coordinate space and temporal ($A^0=0$) gauge, avoiding the use of quark and gluon propagators. The wave function is given analytically by a $_1F_1$ function with an explicit frame dependence. In the infinite momentum frame the Fourier transformed wave function satisfies the 't~Hooft equation, however with contributions also from quarks with negative kinetic energy. Such contributions are present also in the rest frame, and do not vanish under boosts.

## Key claim

The paper derives the bound state equation for QCD₂ in the 't Hooft limit using equal-time quantization in temporal gauge (A⁰=0), finding that the wave function contains contributions from quarks with negative kinetic energy in all frames, including the infinite momentum frame where the 't Hooft equation is recovered but with additional components at momentum fractions x<0 and x>1.

## Method

The author constructs color-singlet qq̅ bound states at equal time in coordinate space with a gauge link, avoiding the use of quark and gluon propagators entirely. The bound state equation is derived from the Hamiltonian in temporal gauge, yielding an ordinary differential equation for the wave function that is solved analytically in terms of the confluent hypergeometric function ₁F₁. The Fourier transform of this wave function is then compared to the 't Hooft equation in the infinite momentum frame.

## Key numbers

- The linear potential slope is V' = ½ g² C_F = g² (N_c² − 1)/(4 N_c), where C_F is the Casimir operator in the fundamental representation.
- The bound state mass M satisfies the relation E_P² − P² = M², which is required for continuity of the wave function at x=0 in all frames.
- At large |τ_P|, the asymptotic form of the wave function contains a phase factor exp[iτ_P/4 − i(m²/2V') log(|τ_P|/2)] with normalization N = 4 (V')^{3/2} / (√π m) √(exp(π m²/V') − 1) e^{−θ(−τ) π m²/(2V')}.

## Context

This work extends the original 't Hooft solution (1974) for QCD₂ in the large-N_c limit, which used light-front quantization and planar Feynman diagrams. It differs from the approach of Bars and Green (1977), who used Coulomb gauge (A¹=0) and required special regularization of singular quark propagators. The temporal gauge formulation avoids Dirac constraints and yields local Poincaré generators, while the negative kinetic energy components suggest that bound states acquire finite widths even in the N_c → ∞ limit, enabling overlap between different bound states analogous to string breaking and dual dynamics.
