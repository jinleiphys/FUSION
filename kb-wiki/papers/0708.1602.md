# Three-Body Forces Produced by a Similarity Renormalization Group Transformation in a Simple Model

> A simple class of unitary renormalization group transformations that force hamiltonians towards a band-diagonal form produce few-body interactions in which low- and high-energy states are decoupled, which can greatly simplify many-body calculations. One such transformation has been applied to phenomenological and effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interactions with success, but further progress requires consistent treatment of at least the three-nucleon interaction. In this paper we demonstrate in an extremely simple model how these renormalization group transformations consistently evolve two- and three-body interactions towards band-diagonal form, and introduce a diagrammatic approach that generalizes to the realistic nuclear problem.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a unitary Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) transformation consistently evolves two-body and three-body interactions toward band-diagonal form in a simple bosonic model, but only if the three-body interaction is included in the evolution; omitting the induced three-body force leads to errors in three-boson eigenvalues, including a 50% error for the excited state at strong coupling.

## Method

The authors study SRG evolution of a hamiltonian containing two-body and three-body interactions for bosons in a one-dimensional infinite square well, with the Fock space truncated to the two lowest modes. They solve the flow equation dH_s/ds = [[T, H_s], H_s] in the two-boson sector to determine the evolved two-body interaction V_{2s}, then solve in the three-boson sector to determine the evolved three-body interaction V_{3s}, using a zero-range delta-function interaction as the initial two-body force. They also develop a diagrammatic representation of the flow equations that generalizes to the realistic nuclear problem and isolates connected diagrams from disconnected spectator contributions.

## Key numbers

* The two lowest single-particle energies are E_1 = pi^2/(2mL^2) and E_2 = 4E_1, with the energy scale set by choosing E_1 = 1.
* For weak coupling (g = -0.1), the off-diagonal two-boson matrix element converges exponentially with exponent approximately 36s, showing convergence by s ≈ 0.1.
* For strong coupling (g = -3.0), omitting the three-body interaction V_3 leads to a 50% error in the excited three-boson eigenvalue, while the ground state error is smaller.
* Initial two-body matrix elements include: ⟨a|V_2|a⟩ = ⟨b|V_2|b⟩ = 3g, ⟨a|V_2|b⟩ = 2g, ⟨c|V_2|c⟩ = 4g, ⟨α|V_2|α⟩ = 9g, ⟨β|V_2|β⟩ = 11g, and ⟨α|V_2|β⟩ = 2√3 g.
* For the initial three-body interaction V_3 with coupling g_3, the matrix elements are ⟨α|V_3|α⟩ = 10g_3, ⟨β|V_3|β⟩ = 6g_3, and ⟨α|V_3|β⟩ = 5g_3.

## Context

This work extends the SRG approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions by Bogner, Furnstahl, and Perry (2006, 2007) and by Glazek and Wilson (1993), demonstrating that consistent evolution of three-body forces is necessary for unitarity. The simple model illustrates issues that must be confronted in realistic nuclear calculations, where the induced many-body interactions must remain short-ranged to allow truncation of the hierarchy of many-body operators. The diagrammatic formulation developed here enables generalization to the realistic nuclear problem and suggests approximations for treating the evolution of few-body interactions in many-fermion systems.
