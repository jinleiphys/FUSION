# Construction of continuous collective energy landscapes for large amplitude nuclear many-body problems

> Several protocols are proposed to build continuous energy surfaces of many-body quantum systems, regarding both energy and states. The standard variational principle is augmented with constraints on state overlap, ensuring arbitrary precision on continuity. As an illustration, the lowest energy and excited state paths relevant for the $^{240}$Pu asymmetric fission are studied. The scission is clearly signed, with a neutron excess in the neck, the ultimate glue before its rupture. Our approach can potentially connect any couple of Hilbert space states, which opens up new horizons for various applications.

# Construction of continuous collective energy landscapes for large amplitude nuclear many-body problems

## Key claim

The paper proposes several variational protocols that augment the standard variational principle with constraints on state overlap, enabling the construction of continuous energy surfaces (both in energy and in the many-body state vectors themselves) for nuclear fission and other large-amplitude collective motion, with the caveat that the method requires a user-chosen parameter \(d\) (the squared overlap between adjacent states) that controls the trade-off between continuity precision and computational cost.

## Method

The authors introduce a generalized variational principle that adds Lagrange multipliers to constrain the kernel (overlap) between the trial state and pre-defined configurations (attractors or repulsors). Three specific protocols are developed: the *Link method* connects two known Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) states by iteratively generating intermediate states with fixed squared overlap \(d\); the *Drop method* creates a continuous energy-descent path from a single starting state without requiring a known final state; and the *Deflation method* builds continuous potential energy surfaces (PESs) for excited states by imposing orthogonality to previously constructed lower-energy states. All methods are implemented using the D1S Gogny interaction with an axial two-center harmonic oscillator basis that allows parity symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- The squared overlap between adjacent states along the path is fixed to \(d = 0.990\) for the \(^{240}\)Pu calculations.
- The neutron-to-proton density ratio in the neck region just before scission reaches values three times larger than the bulk ratio \(N/Z = 1.55\).
- The mean total kinetic energy estimated from Coulomb re-acceleration at neck rupture is 175.4 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 177.7 MeV (Rei71, Bou91).
- The method has been tested to build up to ten successive excited states without specific difficulties.
- The scission region is accessed at quadrupole moments \(\langle Q_{20}\rangle > 14 \times 10^{3}\) fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to resolve discontinuities in nuclear PESs that arise from spontaneous symmetry breaking and sudden jumps between different many-body states (Dub12, Reg19, Zde21). It provides a practical alternative to increasing the number of collective degrees of freedom, which is computationally prohibitive for methods like the time-dependent generator coordinate method (Berg1, Berg, Hill1, Hill2, Gout). The approach enables continuous connection of the fission valley to the Coulomb valley for the first time with quantitative control of state overlap, and opens the possibility of including non-adiabatic effects and dissipative dynamics in a common framework, as well as addressing clustering phenomena in nuclei (Warda2, Gir13, Zdeb2, War18, Ebr20, Mer21, von06).
