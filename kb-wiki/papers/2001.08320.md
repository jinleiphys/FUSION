# The theory of direct laser excitation of nuclear transitions

> A comprehensive theoretical study of direct laser excitation of a nuclear state based on the density matrix formalism is presented. The nuclear clock isomer $^{229\text{m}}$Th is discussed in detail, as it could allow for direct laser excitation using existing technology and provides the motivation for this work. The optical Bloch equations are derived for the simplest case of a pure nuclear two-level system and for the more complex cases taking into account the presence of magnetic sub-states, hyperfine-structure and Zeeman splitting in external fields. Nuclear level splitting for free atoms and ions as well as for nuclei in a solid-state environment is discussed individually. Based on the obtained equations, nuclear population transfer in the low-saturation limit is reviewed. Further, nuclear Rabi oscillations, power broadening and nuclear two-photon excitation are considered. Finally, the theory is applied to the special cases of $^{229\text{m}}$Th and $^{235\text{m}}$U, being the nuclear excited states of lowest known excitation energies. The paper aims to be a didactic review with many calculations given explicitly.

## Key claim

Direct laser excitation of a nuclear state is theoretically possible using existing technology for the specific case of the 8.3 eV isomer 229mTh, but only if the laser linewidth is significantly broader than the nuclear transition linewidth and the low-saturation limit applies; the paper provides a comprehensive density-matrix formalism that is identical to atomic laser excitation theory except for the nuclear current density operator, arbitrary multipole order, and inclusion of non-radiative decay channels like internal conversion.

## Method

The authors derive the optical Bloch equations from the quantum optical master equation in Lindblad form for a nuclear two-level system, then extend the formalism to include magnetic sub-states, hyperfine structure, and Zeeman splitting in external fields for both free atoms/ions and nuclei in a solid-state environment. They obtain the explicit form of the Rabi frequency for arbitrary multipole order by expressing the interaction Hamiltonian in terms of irreducible tensors and relating the matrix elements to radiative decay rates. The theory is then applied to calculate nuclear population transfer, Rabi oscillations, power broadening, and two-photon excitation for the specific cases of 229mTh and 235mU.

## Key numbers

- 229mTh isomer excitation energy: 8.28 ± 0.17 eV (from 2019 measurement via IC electron spectroscopy)
- 235mU isomer excitation energy: 76.7 eV
- 229mTh isomer lifetime in neutral, surface-bound atoms: approximately 10 μs (from 2016 IC electron observation)
- Expected nuclear optical clock accuracy: 1.5 × 10^{-19}
- 229Th 29 keV nuclear state decays with 90% probability to the isomeric state
- 229mTh energy initially constrained to -1 ± 4 eV in 1990, then measured as 3.5 ± 1 eV in 1994, corrected to 7.6 eV in 2007, shifted to 7.8 eV, and finally constrained to 8.28 ± 0.17 eV in 2019

## Context

This work provides the theoretical foundation for upcoming experimental attempts at direct laser excitation of 229mTh, which have been proposed using internal conversion electron detection for probing successful excitation (von der Wense 2019). It extends prior theoretical treatments of nuclear laser excitation by Tkalya (1992), Peik and Tamm (2003), and Kazakov (2012), and complements the inverse electronic bridge mechanism approach that was proposed for 229mTh excitation by Tkalya (1992). The formalism enables the development of a nuclear optical clock and supports experiments that aim to determine the isomeric energy value to a precision corresponding to the bandwidth of the scanning laser light.
