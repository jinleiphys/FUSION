# Role of 2p-2h MEC excitations in superscaling

> Following recent studies of inclusive electron scattering from nuclei at high energies which focused on two-nucleon emission mediated by meson-exchange currents, in this work the superscaling behavior of such contributions is investigated. Comparisons are made with existing data below the quasielastic peak where at high momentum transfers scaling of the second kind is known to be excellent and scaling of the first kind is good, in the proximity of the peak where both 1p-1h and 2p-2h contributions come into play, and above the peak where inelasticity becomes important and one finds scaling violations of the two kinds.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether 2p-2h meson-exchange current (MEC) excitations can explain the observed scaling violations in the transverse nuclear response, finding that these contributions are significant at large negative scaling variable values (below the quasielastic peak) but typically account for only about 10% or less of the full scaling function near the peak, with the caveat that 1p-1h MEC and correlation contributions (not included here) partially cancel the 2p-2h effects.

## Method

The authors computed the 2p-2h transverse response function for a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model using MEC currents mediated by pion exchange, including contributions from intermediate nucleons and Delta isobars via the Peccei Lagrangian. They compared the resulting dimensionless scaling function f^MEC with experimental scaling functions extracted from SLAC (Day 1993) and Jefferson Lab (Arrington et al. 1999) inclusive electron scattering data on carbon and gold targets, examining scaling of the first kind (q-dependence) and second kind (k_F-dependence) in three kinematic regions: below the quasielastic peak (psi' < 0), near the peak, and above it (psi' > 0).

## Key numbers

- For carbon, the Fermi momentum k_F = 228 MeV/c and energy shift E_shift = 20 MeV; for gold, k_F = 245 MeV/c and E_shift = 25 MeV
- At psi' = -2 and q ≈ 1.6 GeV/c (JLab 23° data), f^MEC is about 16% of the experimental scaling function
- At the quasielastic peak (psi' = 0) for the 23° data, f^MEC is about 13% of the experimental f
- The second-kind scaling violation implied by the difference between carbon and gold curves at psi' = -2 amounts to about 7%
- Near the QEP at high q, the 2p-2h MEC scaling function goes roughly as k_F^3
- The 1p-1h MEC and correlation contributions reduce the cross section by typically 10-15% in the QEP region for momentum transfers in the few GeV/c range
- For carbon at q ≈ 1 GeV/c, the second-kind scale breaking from 1p-1h effects is about 8% when going from carbon to gold

## Context

This work extends previous studies of superscaling in the quasielastic region by Donnelly et al. (1999, 2002) and the RFG model of Alberico et al. (1988), specifically addressing the transverse scaling violations that had been attributed to MEC effects (Alberico et al. 1990). The calculation uses the recently developed Lorentz- and translation-invariant 2p-2h MEC response of De Pace et al. (2003) and complements the 1p-1h MEC and correlation studies of Amaro et al. (2002, 2003). The results enable future work incorporating both 2p-2h MEC and correlation effects to achieve gauge invariance, and provide a foundation for understanding scaling violations in the resonance region where inelastic contributions dominate.
