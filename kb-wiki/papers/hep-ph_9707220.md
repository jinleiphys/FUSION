# Numerical solution of Q^2 evolution equations for polarized structure functions

> We investigate numerical solution of Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) Q^2 evolution equations for longitudinally polarized structure functions. Flavor nonsinglet and singlet equations with next-to-leading-order $α_s$ corrections are studied. A brute-force method is employed. Dividing the variables x and Q^2 into small steps, we simply solve the integrodifferential equations. Numerical results indicate that accuracy is better than 1% in the region 10^{-5}<x<0.8 if more than two-hundred Q^2 steps and more than one-thousand x steps are taken. Our evolution results are compared with polarized experimental data of the spin asymmetry A_1 by the SLAC-E130, SLAC-E143, EMC, and SMC collaborations. The comparison indicates that we cannot assume A_1 is independent of Q^2. We provide a FORTRAN program for the Q^2 evolution and devolution of polarized nonsinglet-quark, singlet-quark, Delta q_i+ Delta q-bar_i, and gluon distributions (and corresponding structure functions).

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a brute-force numerical method for solving the polarized DGLAP evolution equations achieves accuracy better than 1% in the region 10^{-5} < x < 0.8, provided more than two-hundred Q^2 steps and more than one-thousand x steps are taken, but they note that the program is not efficient enough for repeated use and that a much faster program will be needed in the future.

## Method

The authors discretize the DGLAP integrodifferential equations for longitudinally polarized parton distributions by dividing the variables x and Q^2 into small steps, using logarithmic-x steps to maintain accuracy at small x. They implement both leading-order and next-to-leading-order alpha_s corrections for flavor-nonsinglet and singlet evolution equations, including the coupled equations for singlet-quark, gluon, and individual flavor distributions. The FORTRAN 77 program BFP1 solves these equations step by step in the t variable (related to Q^2) using the brute-force method previously applied to unpolarized distributions.

## Key numbers

- Accuracy better than 1% is achieved in the region 10^{-5} < x < 0.8 with N_x = 1000 x steps and N_t = 200 t steps
- Typical running time on an AlphaServer 2100 4/200 is approximately seven minutes for the nonsinglet case and sixty minutes for the singlet-quark evolution
- The program uses 1617 lines of FORTRAN 77 code including test data
- The QCD scale parameter Lambda is taken as 231 MeV for fitting polarized experimental data
- The initial distribution is supplied at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 and evolved to Q^2 = 200 GeV^2 in the example calculation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous brute-force method for unpolarized DGLAP evolution (Kumano and Miyama, 1997) to the spin-dependent case, using the recently calculated next-to-leading-order splitting functions for polarized parton distributions. The program enables comparison with experimental spin asymmetry A_1 data from SLAC-E130, SLAC-E143, EMC, and SMC collaborations, showing that A_1 cannot be assumed independent of Q^2. The code was used in subsequent work on optimizing polarized parton distributions by fitting experimental data (Hirai, Kumano, and Miyama, 1997).
