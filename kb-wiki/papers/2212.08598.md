# Fock space representations of Bogolyubov transformations as spin representations

> The representation on a Fock space of the group of Bogolyubov transformations is recognized as the spin representation of an orthogonal group. Derivations based on this observation of some known formulas for the overlap amplitude of two Bogolyubov quasi-fermion vacuum states that are in some cases more complete than those in the literature are shown. It is pointed out that the name of an "Onishi formula" is assigned in the literature to two different expressions which are related but have different scopes. One of them has what has been described as a sign problem, the other one, due to Onishi and Yoshida, has a more limited scope and no sign problem. I give a short proof of the latter, whose derivation is missing in the paper by Onishi and Yoshida, and a new, combinatoric, proof of an equivalent formula recently derived by Robledo.

## Key claim

The sign ambiguity in the "Onishi formula" for the overlap amplitude of two Bogolyubov quasi-fermion vacuum states is shown to be an unavoidable consequence of the double-valuedness of the spin representation of the orthogonal group, rather than a problem that can be eliminated by a different choice of phase convention; the formula of Onishi and Yoshida has no sign ambiguity only because it is restricted to vacua with non-zero overlap with the reference vacuum.

## Method

The paper identifies the Fock space representation of the Bogolyubov group with the spin representation of the orthogonal group O(2d), where d is the dimension of the single-fermion state space. Using the Bloch-Messiah decomposition of the Bogolyubov transformation matrix G, the author derives an expression for the overlap amplitude ⟨g₁|g₂⟩ in terms of the matrices A and B that appear in the block decomposition of G. A short proof of the Onishi-Yoshida formula is given by repeated application of a commutation relation, and a new combinatoric proof of the equivalent Robledo formula is provided using Pfaffian identities.

## Key numbers

- The single-fermion state space has finite dimension d, and the Fock space carries a spin representation of the orthogonal group O(2d) in 2d complex dimensions.
- For the unitary Bogolyubov group, the coordinate representation matrices G are real orthogonal and belong to O(2d,ℝ), with determinant ±1.
- The overlap amplitude ⟨g₁|g₂⟩ = √|A₂†A₁ + B₂†B₁|, where the square root reflects the sign ambiguity.
- The Onishi-Yoshida formula gives ⟨g̃₁|g̃₂⟩ = exp(½ tr log(1 + F₁†F₂)), where F = (BA⁻¹)*, valid only when ⟨|g⟩ ≠ 0.
- The Robledo formula expresses the overlap as ω = (-1)^(d(d-1)/2) pf( (P, 1; -1, Q) ), where P and Q are skew symmetric matrices of dimension d.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of Bogolyubov transformation overlaps by connecting them to the spin representation theory of Brauer and Weyl (1935) and Weyl (1939). It clarifies the distinction between two formulas both called "Onishi formula" in the literature: the one in Ring and Schuck (1980) which has a sign problem, and the original formula of Onishi and Yoshida (1966) which has no sign problem but limited scope. The paper provides a missing derivation of the Onishi-Yoshida formula and a new proof of the equivalent Pfaffian formula derived by Robledo (2009), enabling numerically stable calculations of overlap amplitudes in methods such as the generator coordinate method and quantum number projection.
