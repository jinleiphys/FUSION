# Double beta decay of $^{100}Mo$: the deformed limit

> The double beta decay of $^{100}Mo$ to the ground state and excited states of $^{100}Ru$ is analysed in the context of the pseudo SU(3) scheme. The results of this deformed limit are compared with the vibrational one based on the QRPA formalism. Consistency between the deformed limit and the experimental information is found for various $ββ$ transitions, although, in this approximation some energies and B(E2) intensities cannot reproduced.

# Double beta decay of $^{100}Mo$: the deformed limit

## Key claim

The pseudo SU(3) deformed limit can reproduce experimental nuclear matrix elements for the double beta decay of $^{100}Mo$ to either the ground state or the first excited $0^+$ state of $^{100}Ru$, but it is not possible to fit both decays within the same model using the present approximations, pointing to a description of $^{100}Ru$ as a triaxial nucleus.

## Method

The authors applied the pseudo SU(3) scheme to construct deformed wave functions for $^{100}Mo$ and $^{100}Ru$, using Nilsson mean field and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, with occupation numbers determined by filling Nilsson levels from below for both a deformed occupation case (deformation $\beta \approx 0.23$) and a spherical occupation case ($\beta \leq 0.09$). They calculated two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements to the ground state, first $2^+$ state, and first excited $0^+$ state of $^{100}Ru$, comparing results with QRPA calculations and experimental half-lives.

## Key numbers

- Q-value for $^{100}Mo$ double beta decay: $Q^{\beta\beta} = 3.034$ MeV
- Experimental half-lives for $\beta\beta_{2\nu}$ decay to ground state of $^{100}Ru$: of order $10^{19}$ years
- Energy denominator for decay to ground state: $7.95$ MeV; for first $2^+$ state: $7.68$ MeV; for first excited $0^+$ state: $7.39$ MeV
- Hamiltonian parameters fitted from excitation energies: $\chi = 18.83$ keV, $a = 61.75$ keV
- Intrinsic quadrupole moments from pseudo SU(3) wave functions: $Q_\pi = 5.33$, $Q_\nu = 22.5$ (in units of $e~b_0^2$ with $b_0^2 = 4.70$ fm$^2$)
- Experimental intrinsic quadrupole moment: $Q_0(exp) = 57.7$ $e~b_0^2$
- Second order Casimir operator values: $C_2 = 190$ for irrep $(8,6)$ (ground state band), $C_2 = 160$ for irrep $(10,2)$ (excited band)

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA studies of double beta decay in $^{100}Mo$ by Vogel (1986), Civitarese (1991), and Suhonen (1994), which found overestimation of decay amplitudes when using the vibrational spherical limit. The pseudo SU(3) approach provides a deformed rotational limit to compare with the QRPA spherical limit, motivated by earlier studies of shape coexistence in Ru isotopes from the General Collective Model by Troltenier (1991). The results suggest that the first excited $0^+$ state in $^{100}Ru$ may be better described as part of a rotational band rather than a two-phonon vibrational state, though the model cannot simultaneously reproduce decays to both ground and excited states.
