# Geometry of the effective Majorana neutrino mass in the neutrinoless double-beta decay

> The neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay is a unique process to identify the Majorana nature of massive neutrinos, and its rate depends on the size of the effective Majorana neutrino mass $\langle m\rangle_{ee}$. We put forward a novel "coupling-rod" diagram to describe $\langle m\rangle_{ee}$ in the complex plane, by which the effects of the neutrino mass ordering and CP-violating phases on $\langle m\rangle_{ee}$ are intuitively understood. We show that this geometric language allows us to easily obtain the maximum and minimum of $|\langle m\rangle_{ee}|$. It remains usable even if there is a kind of new physics contributing to $\langle m\rangle_{ee}$, and it can also be extended to describe the effective Majorana masses $\langle m\rangle_{eμ}$, $\langle m\rangle_{eτ}$, $\langle m\rangle_{μμ}$, $\langle m\rangle_{μτ}$ and $\langle m\rangle_{ττ}$ which may appear in some other lepton-number-violating processes.

# Geometry of the effective Majorana neutrino mass in the neutrinoless double-beta decay

## Key claim

The authors introduce a "coupling-rod" diagram in the complex plane to geometrically represent the effective Majorana neutrino mass ⟨m⟩_ee, claiming this visualization makes the effects of neutrino mass ordering and CP-violating phases intuitively understandable. The geometric approach remains valid even when new physics contributes to ⟨m⟩_ee, but the authors acknowledge that the diagram's utility depends on choosing the m_2|U_e2|^2 term as the base vector (rather than m_1|U_e1|^2) to avoid the m_1→0 limit making the geometric language invalid.

## Method

The authors decompose ⟨m⟩_ee into three complex vectors in the complex plane: OA = m_2|U_e2|^2 (real and positive by phase convention), AB = m_1|U_e1|^2 e^{iρ}, and CO = m_3|U_e3|^2 e^{iσ}, where ρ and σ are the two Majorana CP-violating phases. The vector CB = OA + AB + CO represents ⟨m⟩_ee, and the relative lengths of these vectors determine whether the two circles formed by rotating AB about A and CO about O are external, tangent, intersecting, or one contained within the other. The authors then derive analytical expressions for the maximum and minimum of |⟨m⟩_ee| in both normal and inverted neutrino mass orderings by considering the geometric configurations.

## Key numbers

- For the normal mass ordering with m_1=0: |⟨m⟩_ee| ranges from 0.68 meV to 4.7 meV, using the 3σ ranges of Δm²_21, Δm²_31, θ_12, and θ_13 from Fogli (2012).
- For the normal mass ordering with m_3=0: |⟨m⟩_ee| ranges from 12.4 meV to 50.1 meV, using the same 3σ input ranges.
- The lower bound of m_2 in the normal ordering is √(Δm²_21) ≈ 8.7×10⁻³ eV.
- In the special case where AB + OC = OA (circles touch externally), m_2 is determined to be approximately 8.4 meV to 9.9 meV from the 3σ ranges of input parameters.
- In the special case where AB - OC = OA (circles touch internally), m_2 is approximately 9.5 meV to 13.7 meV from the 3σ ranges.
- Experimental upper bounds: |⟨m⟩_ee| < 0.19–0.45 eV from EXO-200 (90% CL) and 0.2–0.4 eV from GERDA (90% CL); cosmological constraint m_1 + m_2 + m_3 < 0.23 eV from Planck (95% CL) gives m_2 < 0.08 eV.

## Context

This work extends the geometric approach to ⟨m⟩_ee previously explored by Smirnov (who used m_1|U_e1|² as the base vector) and by Xing (2003) on the conditions for ⟨m⟩_ee = 0. The coupling-rod diagram provides a unified visualization that works for both normal and inverted mass orderings, and can be extended to other effective Majorana masses ⟨m⟩_αβ that appear in lepton-number-violating processes such as neutrino-antineutrino oscillations and H⁺⁺ → ℓ⁺_α ℓ⁺_β decays. The geometric language enables straightforward derivation of the maximum and minimum of |⟨m⟩_ee|, and remains applicable when new physics (e.g., sterile neutrinos or R-parity-violating supersymmetry) contributes linearly to the effective mass.
