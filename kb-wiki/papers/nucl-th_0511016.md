# Hadronization of Expanding QGP

> We discuss how the dynamics of an exploding hot fireball of quark--gluon matter impacts the actual phase transition conditions between the deconfined and confined state of matter. We survey the chemical conditions prevailing at hadronization.

## Key claim

The dynamics of an exploding quark-gluon plasma fireball can shift the phase transition conditions between deconfined and confined matter away from equilibrium expectations, potentially turning a cross-over into a first-order phase transition at small baryon density if strangeness becomes over-saturated.

## Method

The authors analyze how the expansion of a QGP fireball affects strangeness abundance using a Boltzmann approximation with entropy conservation (VT³ = constant), showing that continued expansion after chemical freeze-out leads to over-saturation of strange quarks (γ_s > 1). They then derive a covariant Gibbs condition incorporating the energy-momentum tensor of flowing matter, including the bag constant B for vacuum confinement, to compute how collective flow shifts the phase boundary temperature.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, baryo-chemical potential μ_B ≈ 25 MeV; at LHC, expected μ_B ≈ 1–3 MeV
- For a supercooled QGP breakup at T₁ = 140 MeV with m_s(T₁)/m_s(T₀) ≈ 1.5–2 and T₀ ≈ 210 MeV, γ_s^QGP(T₁) > 1.5
- At RHIC, prior calculations gave γ_s^QGP(T₁) < 1.2
- The bag constant B ≈ (0.2 GeV)⁴
- The dynamical flow effect can shift the critical temperature downward by ΔT = 25 MeV, from an equilibrium crossover at T = 165 MeV to T = 140 MeV
- The ratio s/S in equilibrated QGP with m_s/T = 1 is approximately 0.027, with interaction factor f(α_s) → 1

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies of strangeness production in QGP (Rafelski 1999) and the color-wind effect on hadronization (Rafelski 2000). It builds on lattice QCD phase diagrams from Peikert (1998) and Fodor (2004) for 2+1 flavors. The paper enables interpretation of the "horn" structure in K⁺/π⁺ ratios measured across AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies as a signature of chemical non-equilibrium hadronization, and suggests that at LHC, substantial strangeness over-saturation may occur.
