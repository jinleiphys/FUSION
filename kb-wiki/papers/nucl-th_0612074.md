# Pairing interactions and the vanishing pairing correlations in hot nuclei

> Finite temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations are performed in Sn isotopes using Skyrme and zero-range, density-dependent pairing interactions. For both stable and very neutron-rich nuclei the critical temperature at which pairing correlations vanish is independent of the volume/surface nature of the pairing interaction. The value of the critical temperature follows approximatively the empirical rule T$_c$ $\simeq$ 0.5 $Δ_{T=0}$ for all the calculated isotopes, showing that the critical temperature could be deduced from the pairing gap at zero temperature. On the other hand, the pairing gap at temperatures just below T$_c$ is strongly sensitive to the volume/surface nature of the pairing interaction.

## Key claim

The critical temperature at which pairing correlations vanish in hot nuclei is largely independent of whether the pairing interaction is of volume or surface type, following the approximate empirical rule T$_c$ $\simeq$ 0.5 $\Delta_{T=0}$ for both stable and very neutron-rich Sn isotopes; however, the pairing gap at temperatures just below T$_c$ is strongly sensitive to the volume/surface nature of the pairing interaction.

## Method

Finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FT-HFB) calculations were performed for Sn and Ni isotopes using the Skyrme force SLy4 (and SGII for one test case) for the mean field, together with a zero-range, density-dependent pairing interaction of the form $V(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r^\prime}) = V_0 [1 -\eta (\rho(r)/\rho_0)^{\alpha}] \delta(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r^\prime})$. Volume pairing corresponds to $\eta=0$ and surface pairing to $\eta=1$; the strength $V_0$ was adjusted in each nucleus to reproduce the same total binding energy for both interaction types. The FT-HFB equations were solved under spherical symmetry with a quasiparticle energy cutoff of 60 MeV and a box radius of 18 fm.

## Key numbers

- In $^{124}$Sn, the critical temperature for vanishing pairing correlations is T$_c$ = 0.7 MeV for a surface pairing interaction.
- For $^{104}$Sn, the critical temperature is T$_c$ $\simeq$ 1 MeV, with the pairing gap at T=0 ranging from 1.3 MeV to 1.8 MeV across individual quasiparticle states of the neutron valence shell.
- In the very neutron-rich nucleus $^{170}$Sn, the critical temperature is T$_c$ = 0.8 MeV for both volume and surface pairing interactions.
- For $^{84}$Ni, the surface and volume pairing interactions give critical temperatures differing by at most 100 keV; the same 100 keV upper limit on T$_c$ variation is found in $^{104}$Sn.
- At T=0.7 MeV in $^{170}$Sn, the pairing gap is two times higher for a surface pairing interaction than for a volume one.
- At T=0.8 MeV in $^{104}$Sn, the pairing gap is close to zero in the volume case but remains $\Delta$ = 1 MeV in the surface case.

## Context

This work extends earlier finite-temperature BCS and Bogoliubov studies (Goodman, 1981; Egido and Ring, 1993) and prior FT-HFB calculations using Gogny forces (Egido and Robledo, 2000) or zero-range forces (Khan et al., 2004; Sandulescu, 2004) by systematically comparing volume-type and surface-type density-dependent pairing interactions. The finding that T$_c$ is insensitive to the pairing force localization, while the gap just below T$_c$ is strongly sensitive, opens the possibility of using experimental measurements of level densities and specific heat (Guttormsen et al., 2001; Schiller et al., 2001) to discriminate between volume and surface pairing interactions in nuclei.
