# Longitudinal gluons and Nambu-Goldstone bosons in a two-flavor color superconductor

> In a two-flavor color superconductor, the SU(3)_c gauge symmetry is spontaneously broken by diquark condensation. The Nambu-Goldstone excitations of the diquark condensate mix with the gluons associated with the broken generators of the original gauge group. It is shown how one can decouple these modes with a particular choice of 't Hooft gauge. We then explicitly compute the spectral density for transverse and longitudinal gluons of adjoint color 8. The Nambu-Goldstone excitations give rise to a singularity in the real part of the longitudinal gluon self-energy. This leads to a vanishing gluon spectral density for energies and momenta located on the dispersion branch of the Nambu-Goldstone excitations.

## Key claim

In a two-flavor color superconductor, the Nambu-Goldstone excitations of the diquark condensate mix with the gluons associated with the broken generators of SU(3)c, and this mixing can be decoupled by a particular choice of 't Hooft gauge; the Nambu-Goldstone modes then cause a singularity in the real part of the longitudinal gluon self-energy, leading to a vanishing gluon spectral density for energies and momenta on the Nambu-Goldstone dispersion branch.

## Method

The authors start from the QCD partition function at finite chemical potential, introduce diquark source terms, and transform to Nambu-Gor'kov spinors. They expand the fermion determinant to second order in the Maurer-Cartan one-form, which contains both gluon fields and meson fields (phase fluctuations of the diquark condensate), then tensor-decompose the gluon self-energy and apply a shift of the parallel gauge field component to eliminate mixing terms, thereby deriving the gluon propagator in 't Hooft gauge. The spectral density for the gluon of adjoint color 8 is then computed explicitly from the resulting propagator.

## Key numbers

- Gluon mass parameter squared in normal quark matter at zero temperature: \( m_g^2 = \frac{N_f}{6\pi^2} g^2 \mu^2 \), where \( N_f = 2 \) for two flavors, \( g \) is the QCD coupling constant, and \( \mu \) is the quark chemical potential.
- The gluon self-energy tensors for adjoint colors 4 and 6 are given by \( \Pi^{\mu\nu}_{44}(P) \equiv \Pi^{\mu\nu}_{66}(P) = \frac{g^2}{2} \frac{T}{V} \sum_K \mathrm{Tr}_s [ \gamma^\mu G_0^+(K) \gamma^\nu G^+(K-P) + \gamma^\mu G^-(K) \gamma^\nu G_0^-(K-P) ] \).
- The gluon self-energy tensor for adjoint color 8 is \( \Pi^{\mu\nu}_{88}(P) = \frac{2}{3} \Pi_0^{\mu\nu}(P) + \frac{1}{3} \tilde{\Pi}^{\mu\nu}(P) \), where \( \Pi_0^{\mu\nu} \) is the normal-conducting gluon self-energy and \( \tilde{\Pi}^{\mu\nu} \) involves sums over normal and anomalous propagators.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of gluons in two-flavor color superconductors by Rischke (2000) and Carter and Diakonov (1999). It corrects the treatment of longitudinal gluon degrees of freedom in Rischke (2000), where fluctuations of the diquark condensate were neglected, leading to a polarization tensor that was not explicitly transverse and exhibited unphysical behavior for the plasmon mode at low momentum. The present paper includes those fluctuations via Nambu-Goldstone bosons, restoring transversality and satisfying Slavnov-Taylor identities. This enables a correct calculation of gluon spectral densities in the color-superconducting phase at asymptotically large chemical potential.
