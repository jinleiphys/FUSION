# Predictions for Polarized-Beam/Vector-Polarized-Target Observables in Elastic Compton Scattering on the Deuteron

> Motivated by developments at HIGS at TUNL that include increased photon flux and the ability to circularly polarize photons, we calculate several beam-polarization/target-spin dependent observables for elastic Compton scattering on the deuteron. This is done at energies of the order of the pion mass within the framework of Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory. Our calculation is complete to O(Q^3) and at this order there are no free parameters. Consequently, the results reported here are predictions of the theory. We discuss paths that may lead to the extraction of neutron polarizabilities. We find that the photon/beam polarization asymmetry is not a good observable for the purpose of extracting α_n and β_n. However, one of the double polarization asymmetries, Σ_x, shows appreciable sensitivity to γ_{1n} and could be instrumental in pinning down the neutron spin polarizabilities.

## Key claim

The paper presents predictions for beam-polarization and target-spin dependent observables in elastic Compton scattering on the deuteron, calculated to O(Q^3) in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory with no free parameters; however, the authors note that these are exploratory calculations intended as a reference for future work at O(Q^4) and beyond, and that the photon/beam polarization asymmetry is not useful for extracting the neutron electric and magnetic polarizabilities α_n and β_n.

## Method

The irreducible amplitude for Compton scattering on the deuteron was computed in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory to O(Q^3), including one-body amplitudes from single-nucleon diagrams (tree level and one-pion-loop diagrams) and two-body diagrams involving pion exchange between nucleons. This irreducible amplitude was then sewn to incoming and outgoing deuteron wavefunctions to obtain the full scattering amplitude for elastic γd → γd. The six invariant amplitudes A_1 through A_6 for the γN amplitude were used, and the one-body amplitude was boosted from the γN center-of-mass frame to the γd center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- The O(Q^3) HBχPT predictions for the nucleon polarizabilities are: α_p = α_n = 12.2 × 10^{-4} fm^3, β_p = β_n = 1.2 × 10^{-4} fm^3, γ_{1p} = γ_{1n} = 4.4 × 10^{-4} fm^4, γ_{2p} = γ_{2n} = 2.2 × 10^{-4} fm^4, γ_{3p} = γ_{3n} = 1.1 × 10^{-4} fm^4, γ_{4p} = γ_{4n} = -1.1 × 10^{-4} fm^4 (using g_A = 1.26 and f_π = 93 MeV).
- The PDG values for the proton are: α_p = (12.0 ± 0.6) × 10^{-4} fm^3, β_p = (1.9 ± 0.5) × 10^{-4} fm^3.
- Two neutron experiments gave conflicting results: α_n = (12.6 ± 1.5 ± 2.0) × 10^{-4} fm^3 and α_n = (0.6 ± 5.0) × 10^{-4} fm^3.
- The Baldin sum rule for the neutron gives α_n + β_n = (14.4 ± 0.66) × 10^{-4} fm^3.
- The neutron backward spin polarizability was measured as γ_{πn} = (58.6 ± 4.0) × 10^{-4} fm^4 from quasi-free Compton scattering on the deuteron.

## Context

This work extends previous HBχPT calculations of unpolarized elastic Compton scattering on the deuteron by Beane et al. (2002) and Beane et al. (2003) to include polarization observables, motivated by experimental developments at the HIγS facility at TUNL. The calculations are complementary to those of Hildebrandt et al. (2003) on polarization observables for Compton scattering on free nucleons, and to the work of Chen, Ji, and Li (2004) using pionless effective theory, which breaks down at approximately 50 MeV compared to the present theory's range up to the pion-production threshold. The results provide a reference for future O(Q^4) calculations and could guide experiments aimed at extracting neutron spin polarizabilities, particularly γ_{1n}, through the double-polarization asymmetry Σ_x.
