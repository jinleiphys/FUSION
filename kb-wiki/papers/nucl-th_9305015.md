# Temperature induced alignment in hot rotating nuclei

> Employing a cranking Hamiltonian, in the static path approximation to the partition function, we have studied the behaviour of the moment of inertia of $^{64}Zn$ at a few values of temperatures as a function of the rotation frequency. At a low temperature T = 0.5 MeV and J=16 rotational alignment of $0g_{9/2}$ orbitals takes place. But at a high temperature T = 2.0 MeV about 40 - 45$\%$ of all the angular momenta are generated by the alignment of the same orbitals. This should imply a decrease in collectivity for all the spins at high temperatures.

# Temperature induced alignment in hot rotating nuclei

## Key claim
At low temperature (T = 0.5 MeV) rotational alignment of 0g_{9/2} orbitals occurs only at J=16, but at high temperature (T = 2.0 MeV) about 40-45% of all angular momenta are generated by alignment of the same orbitals even at low spins, implying a decrease in collectivity for all spins at high temperatures.

## Method
The authors employed a cranking Hamiltonian within the static path approximation (SPA) to the partition function, integrating over quadrupole deformation parameters β and γ to account for thermal shape fluctuations. The deformed cranked single-particle Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a model space of 30 orbitals (extending up to 0g_{9/2}) for ¹⁰ protons and ¹⁴ neutrons in ⁶⁴Zn, with ⁴⁰Ca as an inert core. The angular momentum constraint was satisfied by adjusting the cranking frequency ω, and the moment of inertia was computed as ℑ_J = √(J(J+1))/ω_J.

## Key numbers
- At T = 0.5 MeV, a sudden rise in moment of inertia occurs at ω ∼ 1.0 MeV and J ≈ 16, indicating rotational alignment of 0g_{9/2} orbitals.
- At T = 2.0 MeV, about 40-45% of all angular momenta are generated by alignment of 0g_{9/2} orbitals.
- At T = 2.0 MeV and J = 2, there are on average about 1.5 particles in 0g_{9/2} orbitals, contributing more than 40% to the total spin.
- The spherical basis single-particle energies used are -14.4, -10.2, -8.8, -8.3, and -4.4 MeV for the orbitals 0f_{7/2}, 1p_{3/2}, 0f_{5/2}, 1p_{1/2}, and 0g_{9/2}, respectively.
- The value of ℏω₀ = 41A^{-1/3} MeV and α = (ℏω₀)²/χb⁴ with χb⁴ = 70A^{-1.4} MeV.

## Context
This work extends the static path approximation approach previously applied by the authors to zinc isotopes (Agrawal and Ansari, 1993) by adding a cranking term to study spin dependence of level density and moment of inertia. It builds on the SPA framework developed by Lauritzen and Bertsch (1988) and others for describing hot nuclei beyond mean-field theory, where thermal fluctuations in shape degrees of freedom become important at high temperatures. The results suggest that at high temperatures, angular momentum is generated largely by temperature-induced alignment of high-j intruder orbitals rather than collective rotation, which has implications for spectroscopic studies of hot compound nuclei.
