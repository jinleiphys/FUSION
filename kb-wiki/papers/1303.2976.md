# In-medium QCD forces at high temperature

> We derive the quantum dynamics of heavy quark systems in quark-gluon plasma as open quantum systems. The scatterings of a heavy quark with the hard medium particles give rise to Debye screened QCD force and drag force and its fluctuation. We present a unified quantum description of these in-medium QCD forces at high temperature in the leading-order perturbation on the basis of the influence functional formalism.

# In-medium QCD forces at high temperature

## Key claim
The paper derives a unified quantum description of both Debye-screened potential forces and dissipative drag forces acting on heavy quarks in quark-gluon plasma, using the open quantum systems framework. The derivation is performed at leading-order perturbation theory and at high temperature, with the caveat that the results are perturbative and may require extension to non-perturbative regimes for full applicability.

## Method
The author applies the influence functional formalism from non-equilibrium quantum field theory to treat heavy quarks as an open quantum system interacting with a thermal environment of light quarks and gluons. The environmental degrees of freedom are integrated out to obtain an effective action for heavy quark color currents, from which a renormalized effective Hamiltonian is derived. The master equation for the reduced density matrix of heavy quark systems is then obtained from a functional Schrödinger equation, and Ehrenfest relations are extracted to connect with classical Langevin dynamics.

## Key numbers
- Debye mass squared: ω²_D = g²T² (N_c/3 + N_f/6), where N_c is the number of colors and N_f the number of flavors
- The complex potential V(r) at leading order: V(r) = (g²/4π) { e^(-ω_D r)/r - 2iT ω²_D ∫₀^∞ dk sin(kr)/[r(k²+ω²_D)²] }

## Context
This work extends the open quantum systems approach to heavy quark dynamics in QGP, building on prior treatments of heavy quark Langevin dynamics (Svetitsky 1987) and Debye-screened quarkonium potentials (Matsui 1986). It provides a unified framework that recovers the Caldeira-Leggett model for quantum Brownian motion in the short-distance limit and reproduces leading-order perturbative drag force calculations (Moore 2004). The approach enables future derivation of next-to-leading order effects including gluo-dissociation processes, and offers a path toward phenomenological applications such as interpreting bottomonium dimuon spectra from LHC experiments (Chatrchyan 2011).
