# Probing a dilute short lived Quark Gluon Plasma medium with jets

> I look at propagation of jets that act as a probe of the medium in the phenomenologically relevant case of a short lived dilute Quark Gluon Plasma(QGP) created during heavy ion collisions. Working in the regime where the lifetime of the medium is of the order or smaller than the formation time of the energetic jet, I derive a factorization formula with a manifest separation of scales for an illustrative jet substructure observable using the Open Quantum system Effective Field Theory(EFT) approach developed in \cite{Vaidya:2020lih}, in terms of a medium structure function and a medium induced jet function . The medium structure function or the "PDF of the medium" that describes the observable independent physics of the QGP remains identical to that in a dilute long lived medium considered in \cite{Vaidya:2021vxu}, while the medium induced jet function is modified and incorporates quantum interference between the hard interaction that creates the jet and subsequent medium interactions. The medium jet function continues to enjoy a BFKL rapidity evolution just as for a long lived medium, albeit with a modified renormalization scale that now depends on the medium lifetime.

## Key claim

The paper derives a factorization formula for jet substructure in a short-lived, dilute Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) where the medium lifetime is of order or smaller than the jet formation time, showing that the medium structure function (the "PDF of the medium") remains identical to the long-lived medium case while the medium-induced jet function is modified to incorporate quantum interference between the hard interaction and subsequent medium interactions.

## Method

The author uses an Open Quantum System Effective Field Theory (EFT) approach combining Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) with Glauber mode operators for forward scattering, treating the jet as an open quantum system interacting with a QGP bath. The factorization formula is derived for an illustrative jet substructure observable (transverse momentum imbalance between groomed dijets with cumulative jet mass measurement) by expanding the reduced density matrix to quadratic order in the Glauber Hamiltonian, appropriate for a dilute medium where the mean free path is much larger than the formation time.

## Key numbers

- Medium lifetime is of order 10 fm/c (stated as typical QGP lifetime)
- Jet formation time for energetic partons ranges from a few fermi to hundreds of fermi, given by \(t_f^c \sim E_{\text{jet}}/q_T^2 \sim E_{\text{jet}}/T^2\)
- Temperature range for current heavy ion experiments is 150-500 MeV
- Soft radiation formation time \(t_f^s \sim 1/T\) is much smaller than medium lifetime
- The expansion parameter of the EFT is \(\lambda = q_T/Q \sim T/Q \sim \sqrt{e_i}\)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous EFT framework for jet substructure in heavy ion collisions (Vaidya 2020) and the specific case of a long-lived dilute medium (Vaidya 2021) to the more phenomenologically relevant regime where the medium lifetime is comparable to or smaller than the jet formation time. The factorization shows that the medium structure function remains universal and unchanged from the long-lived case, continuing to obey BFKL rapidity evolution, while the medium-induced jet function acquires a modified renormalization scale dependent on the medium lifetime. This enables systematic treatment of jet-medium interactions in current heavy ion experiments at RHIC and LHC, with the dense medium case deferred to future work.
