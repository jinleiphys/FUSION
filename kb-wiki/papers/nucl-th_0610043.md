# Intrinsic-Density Functionals

> The Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and Kohn-Sham procedure are extended to functionals of the localized intrinsic density of a self-bound system such as a nucleus. After defining the intrinsic-density functional, we modify the usual Kohn-Sham procedure slightly to evaluate the mean-field approximation to the functional, and carefully describe the construction of the leading corrections for a system of fermions in one dimension with a spin-degeneracy equal to the number of particles N. Despite the fact that the corrections are complicated and nonlocal, we are able to construct a local Skyrme-like intrinsic-density functional that, while different from the exact functional, shares with it a minimum value equal to the exact ground-state energy at the exact ground-state intrinsic density, to next-to-leading order in 1/N. We briefly discuss implications for real Skyrme functionals.

# Intrinsic-Density Functionals

## Key claim

The paper extends the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and Kohn-Sham procedure to functionals of the localized intrinsic density of self-bound systems like nuclei, but only demonstrates the construction explicitly for a one-dimensional model of fermions with spin-degeneracy equal to particle number N, where the resulting local Skyrme-like functional shares the exact ground-state energy and intrinsic density at its minimum only to next-to-leading order in 1/N.

## Method

The author defines an intrinsic-density functional via Legendre transform of the energy in the presence of a source that couples to the intrinsic density operator, then constructs a mean-field approximation to this functional using a one-dimensional model of N fermions with attractive delta-function interactions and spin-degeneracy N. Corrections beyond mean-field theory are incorporated through the ring-diagram (RPA) correlation energy, and the Kohn-Sham equation is derived by equating the source J0 to the functional derivative of the correction term with respect to intrinsic density, with the source determined from the mean-field orbital through an algebraic relation valid near the ground state.

## Key numbers

- Exact ground-state energy: E_gs = (1/24) m g^2 (N^3 - N)
- Mean-field energy: E_mf = -(m g^2 / 24) N^2 (N - 1), which is correct at leading order O(N^3) but has an erroneous O(N^2) term
- Mean-field orbital: phi(x) = sqrt(N m g) / (2 cosh[N m g (x - a) / 2]), with energy epsilon = -(m g^2 / 8) N (N - 1)
- Exact intrinsic density expansion: rho_I^gs(x) = (m g N^2) / (4 cosh^2(N m g x / 2)) * (1 - (1/N)[1 - 3/(2 cosh^2(N m g x / 2))] + O(1/N^2)), valid for m g |x| greater than approximately 3 ln N / N^2

## Context

This work extends the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem (Hohenberg 1964) and Kohn-Sham procedure (Kohn 1965) to intrinsic densities of self-bound systems, addressing the problem that the usual laboratory-density functional is irrelevant for nuclei because the center-of-mass motion spreads the laboratory density uniformly over all space. The paper builds on the effective-action formalism of Fukuda (1994) and the work of Valiev and Fernando (1997) on functionals of arbitrary Hermitian operators, and uses the inversion method of Okajima (1996) and Pugatch (2002) to incorporate Kohn-Sham orbitals. The one-dimensional model with delta-function interactions is exactly solvable (McGuire 1964, Calogero 1975), and the ring-diagram corrections follow from the RPA correlation energy formalism (Rowe 1980, Blaizot 1986). The results suggest that real Skyrme functionals for nuclei could be understood as approximations to an exact intrinsic-density functional, with the prescription for intrinsic density used by Reinhard (2006) and Schmid (1991) corresponding to the leading-order relation between orbitals and intrinsic density.
