# $伪+伪+t$ cluster structures and Hoyle-analogue states in $^{11}$B

> The structure of $3/2^{-}$ and $1/2^{+}$ states in $^{11}$B is investigated with an $伪+伪+t$ orthogonality condition model (OCM) based on the Gaussian expansion method. Full levels up to the $3/2^{-}_{3}$ and $1/2^{+}_2$ states around the $伪+伪+t$ threshold ($E_x$=11.1 MeV) are reproduced consistently with the experimental energy levels. It is shown that the $3/2_{3}^{-}$ state located around the $^{7}$Li+$伪$ threshold has an $伪+伪+t$ cluster structure, whereas the $3/2_{1}^{-}$ and $3/2_{2}^{-}$ states have a shell-model-like compact structure. We found that the $3/2_{3}^{-}$ state does not possess an $伪$-condensate-like nature similar to the $0^{+}_{2}$ state of $^{12}$C (Hoyle state) which has a dilute $3伪$-condensate structure described by a $(0S_伪)^3$ configuration with about $70$\% probability, although the monopole transition strength of the former is as large as that of the latter. We discuss the reasons why the $3/2_{3}^{-}$ state does not have the condensate character. On the other hand, the $1/2^{+}_{1}$ state just below the $^{7}$Li+$伪$ threshold has a cluster structure which can be interpreted as a parity-doublet partner of the $3/2^{-}_3$ state. We indicate that the $12.56$-MeV state ($J^蟺=1/2^{+}_{2}$) just above the $伪+伪+t$ threshold observed in the $^7$Li($^{7}$Li,$^{11}$B$^*$)$t$ reaction etc. is of the dilute-cluster-gas-like, and is a strong candidate for the Hoyle-analogue state which has a configuration of $(0S_伪)^{2}(0S_{t})$ with about $65$\% probability from the analyses of the single-cluster motions in $^{11}$B. The structure property of the $1/2^{+}$ resonant state is analyzed with the complex scaling method.

## Key claim

The $3/2_{3}^{-}$ state in $^{11}$B, despite having a large monopole transition strength comparable to the Hoyle state in $^{12}$C, does **not** possess an $\alpha$-condensate-like nature; instead, the $1/2^{+}_{2}$ state at 12.56 MeV is identified as the strong candidate for the Hoyle-analogue state, with a $(0S_{\alpha})^{2}(0S_{t})$ configuration at about 65% probability.

## Method

The authors performed an $\alpha+\alpha+t$ orthogonality condition model (OCM) calculation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) to study $3/2^{-}$ and $1/2^{+}$ states in $^{11}$B. The model space was large enough to cover $\alpha+\alpha+t$ gas-like, $^{7}$Li+$\alpha$ cluster, and shell-model configurations, with Pauli-blocking effects properly treated. Single-cluster density matrices were solved to obtain occupation probabilities of $\alpha$ and $t$ orbits, and the complex scaling method (CSM) was applied to analyze resonant states.

## Key numbers

- The $3/2_{3}^{-}$ state appears at $E_x = 8.2$ MeV ($E = -2.9$ MeV relative to the $\alpha+\alpha+t$ threshold), with a nuclear radius $R_N = 3.00$ fm, about 30% larger than the ground state radius of 2.22 fm.
- The calculated isoscalar monopole transition rate for the $3/2_{3}^{-}$ state is $B(\text{E0:IS}) = 92$ fm$^4$, in good agreement with the experimental value of $96 \pm 16$ fm$^4$.
- The $1/2^{+}_{2}$ state at $E_x = 12.56$ MeV has a $(0S_{\alpha})^{2}(0S_{t})$ configuration with about 65% probability, identified as the Hoyle-analogue state.
- The Hoyle state in $^{12}$C has a $(0S_{\alpha})^{3}$ configuration with about 70% probability and $B(\text{E0:IS}) = 120 \pm 9$ fm$^4$.
- The $\alpha-\alpha$ rms distance in the $3/2_{3}^{-}$ state is $R_{\alpha-\alpha} = 4.47$ fm, and the $^{8}\text{Be}-t$ rms distance is $R_{^{8}\text{Be}-t} = 3.49$ fm.
- The spectroscopic factor $S^2$ for the $^{7}\text{Li(g.s)}+\alpha$ channel ($\ell_{74}=0$) in the $3/2_{3}^{-}$ state is 0.278.

## Context

This work extends the earlier $\alpha+\alpha+t$ OCM study by Nishioka et al. (1979) using a larger model space with the Gaussian expansion method, enabling simultaneous description of $^{7}$Li+$\alpha$ cluster configurations and $\alpha+\alpha+t$ gas-like configurations. The paper contradicts the claim from AMD calculations by Kawabata et al. (2007) and Enyo et al. (2007) that the $3/2_{3}^{-}$ state has a clustering feature similar to the Hoyle state, by showing through single-cluster density matrix analysis that it lacks $\alpha$-condensate character. The identification of the $1/2^{+}_{2}$ state at 12.56 MeV as a Hoyle-analogue state in a non-self-conjugate nucleus ($A=11$) opens the possibility of searching for similar cluster-gas states composed of $\alpha$ and triton clusters around their disintegration thresholds in $A \neq 4n$ nuclei.
