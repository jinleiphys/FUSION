# New integral formula and its applications to light nucleus reactions

> A new integral formula, which has not been compiled in any integral tables or mathematical softwares, is proposed to obtain the analytical energy-angular spectra of the particles that are sequentially emitted from the discrete energy levels of the residual nuclei in the statistical theory of light nucleus reaction (STLN). In the cases of the neutron induced light nucleus reactions, the demonstration of the kinetic energy conservation in the sequential emission processes becomes straightforward thanks to this new integral formula and it is also helpful to largely reduce the volume of file-6 in nuclear reaction databases. Furthermore, taking p+$^9$Be reaction at 18 MeV as an example, this integral formula is extended to calculate the energy-angular spectra of the sequentially emitted neutrons for proton induced light nucleus reactions in the frame of STLN.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new integral formula (Equation 4) that enables analytical calculation of energy-angular spectra for sequentially emitted particles in light nucleus reactions, but the formula is explicitly restricted to reactions without polarization of incoming nucleons or orientation of target nuclei.

## Method

The authors derive a new integral formula involving Legendre functions and use it within the statistical theory of light nucleus reactions (STLN) to obtain analytical Legendre expansion coefficients for secondary emitted particles. They apply the formalism to calculate double-differential cross sections for neutron and proton induced reactions on 1p-shell light nuclei, demonstrating energy conservation in sequential emission processes. The method is illustrated for the p+$^9$Be reaction at 18 MeV, where partial neutron spectra from multiple reaction channels are summed to obtain total outgoing neutron energy-angular spectra.

## Key numbers

- p+$^9$Be reaction at incident proton energy $E_p$ = 18 MeV, with outgoing neutron spectra calculated at angle 60$^0$ in the laboratory system
- The partial neutron spectra from the (p, pn)$^8$Be$\rightarrow$(p, pn+2$\alpha$) reaction channel include contributions from the 1st through 17th excited energy levels of the first residual nucleus $^9$Be to the lowest five energy levels of the secondary residual nucleus $^8$Be
- The (p, p$\alpha$)$^5$He$\rightarrow$(p, p$\alpha$+n$\alpha$) channel includes contributions from the 4th through 17th excited energy levels of $^9$Be to the lowest two energy levels of $^5$He
- The (p, n)$^9$B channel includes contributions from the ground state through the 9th excited energy levels of $^9$B
- Only partial cross sections with values larger than 0.1 mb are shown in the figures

## Context

This work extends the statistical theory of light nucleus reactions (STLN) previously developed by Zhang (2009, 2011, 2015) for neutron induced reactions on 1p-shell light nuclei including $^6$Li, $^7$Li, $^9$Be, $^{10}$B, $^{11}$B, $^{12}$C, $^{14}$N, and $^{16}$O. The new integral formula enables analytical treatment of sequential particle emissions between discrete energy levels, which is essential because the large level spacings in light nuclei mean all sequentially emitted particles can reach discrete levels with large widths. The approach is being extended to proton induced reactions and is expected to reduce the volume of file-6 in nuclear reaction databases while maintaining full energy balance.
