# Darwin-Foldy term and proton charge radius

> In this contribution we study the Dirac equation for a finite size proton in an external electric field with explicit introduction of Dirac-Pauli form factors. Our aim is twofold. On the one hand, we wish to study whether our conclusions regarding the exact cancellation between Dirac form factor and Foldy term contributions occurring for the neutron still hold for the proton. On the other hand, we wish to clearly illustrate some of the specific features of the description of a composite particle like the proton with the Dirac equation.

# Darwin-Foldy term and proton charge radius

## Key claim

The authors show that when the Dirac equation is extended with Dirac-Pauli form factors to describe a finite-size proton, the Dirac form factor contribution cancels the Darwin-Foldy term from the proton's anomalous magnetic moment, leaving only the standard Darwin term from the normal magnetic moment; this cancellation means the proton charge radius extracted from the Sachs form factor is unambiguous and the Dirac equation does not necessarily fail at the level of the Darwin-Foldy term, contrary to earlier claims in the literature.

## Method

The authors start from a Dirac Hamiltonian for a finite-size proton in an external electric field, explicitly including the proton's Dirac and Pauli form factors \(F_1\) and \(F_2\). They perform a Foldy-Wouthuysen reduction of this Hamiltonian together with a low-momentum expansion of the form factors, and examine the coefficient of the \(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E}\) term (the Darwin-type term) to identify the contributions from the form factors versus the point-particle Darwin term.

## Key numbers

- The standard Darwin term from the normal part of the proton magnetic moment is \(\frac{e}{8m^2}\) (in natural units), which is the same term present for a structureless electron and does not contribute to the charge radius.
- The proton anomalous dimensionless magnetic moment \(\kappa\) appears in the relation \(F'_1(0) = -\frac{1}{6} r^2_{Ep} + \frac{\kappa}{4m^2}\), connecting the slope of the Dirac form factor to the charge radius.
- The proton charge radius \(r^2_{Ep}\) is defined via the Sachs form factor expansion \(G_E(-\mathbf{q}^2) = 1 - \frac{1}{6} r^2_{Ep} \mathbf{q}^2 + \cdots\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis for the neutron (Bawin and Coon, 1999), where they found an exact cancellation between the Dirac form factor and Foldy term contributions, to the proton case. It agrees with results from the relativistic constituent quark model of Cardarelli and Simula (1999) and the valence quark model of Isgur (1999). The paper addresses the ongoing controversy (Byrne, 1998; Alex, 1999; Bun, 1999) about whether the Darwin-Foldy term should be included in the definition of the nucleon charge radius, and contradicts the earlier claim by Wilets that the Dirac equation fails at the level of the Darwin-Foldy term. The resulting charge radius definition matches that used in chiral perturbation theory calculations (U. Meissner, 1999).
