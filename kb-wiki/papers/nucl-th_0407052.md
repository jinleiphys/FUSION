# Soft Pion Emission from the Nucleon Induced by Twist-2 Light-Cone Operators

> We compute the matrix elements for pion emission from the nucleon induced by twist-two light-cone operators in the kinematics where the pion is close to threshold. It is shown that the results of heavy baryon ChPT are in agreement with the results from the soft-pion theorem approach. Therefore the amplitude of soft-pion emission is directly related to the generalized parton distributions in the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) and the soft-pion theorem approach yield identical results for the matrix elements of twist-2 light-cone operators for pion emission from the nucleon near threshold, contrary to a previous claim by Chen and Savage (2004). The agreement holds provided the effective operators in ChPT are constructed with the correct chiral transformation properties, which reduces the number of unknown functions to two.

## Method

The authors construct the leading-order effective chiral Lagrangian for isovector twist-2 light-cone operators in the heavy baryon formalism, using the non-relativistic expansion of the nucleon spinors and the Breit frame. They compute the tree-level matrix elements for soft pion emission from the nucleon for both vector and axial-vector operators, then perform a non-relativistic expansion of the soft-pion theorem formulas and compare term-by-term with the ChPT results.

## Key numbers

- The isovector anomalous magnetic moment difference: $\kappa_p - \kappa_n \approx 3.706$ (from the sum rule for the GPD $E$).
- The axial coupling constant: $G_A(0) = g_A$ (from the sum rule for the GPD $\widetilde H$).
- The pion pole form of the pseudoscalar form factor: $G_P(\Delta^2) \approx \frac{4M^{2}g_A}{\Delta^2-m_\pi^{2}}$.
- The local limit of the vector operator gives $Q(0)=1$ and the axial-vector operator gives $\widetilde Q(0)=g_A$.

## Context

This work resolves a contradiction reported by Chen and Savage (2004), who claimed that ChPT results for soft pion emission from twist-2 operators did not match the soft-pion theorem approach of Guichon, Mosse, and Vanderhaeghen (2003). The present paper shows that the disagreement arose from an incorrect representation of the effective twist-2 operators in Chen and Savage (2004), which omitted the chiral transformation constraints that reduce the number of unknown functions. The results enable reliable estimates of the contamination from near-threshold pion production in exclusive hard scattering experiments measuring generalized parton distributions, such as those performed by HERMES, ZEUS, and CLAS collaborations.
