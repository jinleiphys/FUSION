# Significance and properties of internucleon correlation functions

> We show that a nuclear Hamiltonian and a set of internucleon correlation functions is in a one-to-one correspondence. The correlation functions for $s$-shell nuclei interacting via the two-nucleon interaction of AV8$^\prime$ type are calculated to exhibit the importance of tensor correlations as well as short-range central correlation. The asymptotic behavior of the correlation functions is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors prove that there is a one-to-one correspondence between a nuclear Hamiltonian (without external potentials, as appropriate for self-bound nuclei) and a set of internucleon correlation functions (iCF), but this proof assumes a non-degenerate ground state and ignores three-body forces.

## Method

The authors express the ground-state energy of a nucleus as an explicit functional of scalar iCF: one momentum-space function \(C(k)\) for the kinetic energy and several radial functions \(C^{(p)}(r)\) for each operator term in the nucleon-nucleon potential. They then calculate these iCF for the \(s\)-shell nuclei \(d\), \(^3\)H, \(^3\)He, and \(^4\)He (ground state and first excited \(0^+\) state) using accurate wave functions expanded in correlated Gaussians with global vectors, employing the AV8\(^\prime\) and G3RS two-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

- The first excited \(0^+\) state of \(^4\)He (\(\alpha^*\)) decays into the \(t+p\) channel with a width of 0.50 MeV.
- For \(\alpha\) using the AV8\(^\prime\) potential, all eight iCF follow \(\sim \exp(-2\kappa r)/r^{2.09}\) with \(\kappa = 0.75\) fm\(^{-1}\) for \(r\) larger than 6 fm.
- The asymptotic behavior parameter \(\eta\) for \(\alpha\) is 0.046 for \(\kappa = 0.75\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The iCF for the deuteron satisfy the asymptotic Hankel-function behavior for \(r \ge 5\) fm.
- The kinetic energy density extends beyond \(k = 6\) fm\(^{-1}\) for all nuclei studied.

## Context

This work extends the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem (Hohenberg and Kohn, 1964) to self-bound nuclear systems by replacing the one-body density with a set of internucleon correlation functions, addressing the fact that the original proof does not apply to nuclei lacking an external potential. The iCF approach is expected to inform variational methods such as variational Monte Carlo, coupled-cluster theory (Bishop), Fermi hypernetted chain theory, and cluster expansion methods (Alvioli), as well as unitary-transformation approaches. The calculations for \(s\)-shell nuclei build on the authors' prior work using correlated Gaussians (Suzuki and Horiuchi, 2008) and enable future studies of heavier nuclei where the iCF shapes are expected to be similar but with reduced peak heights and larger spatial extension.
