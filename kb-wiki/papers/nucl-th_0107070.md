# Diomega production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Using a multiphase transport model, we study the production of a new strange dibaryon (ΩΩ)_{0+} in dense hadronic matter formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The (multi-)strange baryons (Ξand Ω) are produced by strangeness-exchange reactions between antikaons and hyperons in the pure hadronic phase. The rescattering between the omegas at midrapidity leads to a production probability of \simeq 3x10^{-7} (ΩΩ)_{0+} per event at the RHIC energy of \sqrt s=130A GeV. The production probability would be enhanced by two orders of magnitude if (ΩΩ)_{0+} and omega reach chemical equilibrium during heavy ion collisions. We further find that the yield of (ΩΩ)_{0+} increases continuously from SPS to the highest RHIC energy.

# Diomega production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
Using the AMPT transport model without quark-gluon plasma formation, the production probability of the strange dibaryon (ΩΩ)₀₊ in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy √s = 130A GeV is approximately 3×10⁻⁷ per event, but this probability would be enhanced by two orders of magnitude if (ΩΩ)₀₊ and Ω reached chemical equilibrium during the collision.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, which combines HIJING for initial parton and string production, ZPC for parton cascade, Lund string fragmentation for hadronization, and the ART model for final-state hadronic scatterings. Multistrange baryons (Ξ and Ω) were produced via strangeness-exchange reactions (K̄Λ ↔ Ξπ, K̄Σ ↔ Ξπ, K̄Ξ ↔ Ωπ), and diomega production was treated perturbatively through the reactions Ω+Ω → (ΩΩ)₀₊+γ and Ω+Ω → (ΩΩ)₀₊+η, with cross sections parametrized as functions of center-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers
- Production probability of (ΩΩ)₀₊ per event at RHIC √s = 130A GeV: approximately 3×10⁻⁷
- If (ΩΩ)₀₊ and Ω reach chemical equilibrium, the production probability would be approximately 3.0×10⁻⁵ per event, two orders of magnitude higher
- Binding energy of (ΩΩ)₀₊: approximately 116 MeV, with root mean square distance between the two Ω's of 0.84 fm
- Maximum cross section for Ω+Ω → (ΩΩ)₀₊+γ: approximately 1.6 μb at p_Ω ≈ 0.5 GeV
- Maximum cross section for Ω+Ω → (ΩΩ)₀₊+η: approximately 5.5 μb at p_Ω ≈ 1.1 GeV
- Saturated diomega yield at midrapidity: 2.8×10⁻⁷ per event in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 130A GeV
- From SPS energy (√s = 17A GeV) to highest RHIC energy (√s = 200A GeV), Ω⁻ number increases by a factor of about three, while (ΩΩ)₀₊ number increases by about a factor of four
- In the coalescence model, the yield of (ΩΩ)₀₊ at midrapidity for central Au+Au at √s = 130A GeV is 2.6×10⁻⁵
- Mean lifetime of (ΩΩ)₀₊: approximately four times the free Ω lifetime (0.822×10⁻¹⁰ sec) for weak decays, and approximately twice the free Ω lifetime for the nonmesonic decay mode

## Context
This work extends prior studies of multistrange baryon production in heavy ion collisions using the AMPT model (Pal, 2001) and theoretical predictions of deeply bound dibaryons with high strangeness from the chiral SU(3) model (Zhang, 2000; Li, 2001). The diomega (ΩΩ)₀₊ was previously predicted to have a large binding energy of approximately 116 MeV in Resonating Group Method calculations. The authors compare their transport model results with predictions from both the thermal (grand canonical) model and the coalescence model (Schaffner-Bielich, 2000), finding that the AMPT model gives a lower bound on diomega production because additional channels such as Ω⁻+Ξ⁻ → (ΩΩ)₀₊+K⁰ were neglected. The study opens the possibility of detecting this exotic dibaryon at RHIC energies through its weak decay products, particularly in the Ξ⁻Ω⁻ invariant mass distribution.
