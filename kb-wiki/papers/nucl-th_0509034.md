# Momentum-space description of three-nucleon breakup reactions including the Coulomb interaction

> The Coulomb interaction between the two protons is included in the calculation of proton-deuteron breakup and of three-body electromagnetic disintegration of ${}^3\mathrm{He}$. The hadron dynamics is based on the purely nucleonic charge-dependent (CD) Bonn potential and its realistic extension CD Bonn + $Δ$ to a coupled-channel two-baryon potential, allowing for single virtual $Δ$-isobar excitation. Calculations are done using integral equations in momentum space. The screening and renormalization approach is employed for including the Coulomb interaction. Convergence of the procedure is found at moderate screening radii. The reliability of the method is demonstrated. The Coulomb effect on breakup observables is seen at all energies in particular kinematic regimes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Coulomb interaction between the two protons can be included in momentum-space calculations of proton-deuteron breakup and three-body electromagnetic disintegration of ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ using a screening and renormalization approach, with convergence achieved at moderate screening radii; however, the method requires larger screening radii (up to 60 fm) and extrapolation near the $pp$ final-state interaction regime where the renormalization factor becomes ill-defined at zero relative momentum.

## Method

The authors solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations in momentum space for three-nucleon reactions, including the screened Coulomb potential between charged baryons with a smooth screening function $w_R(r) = (\alpha_e/r) e^{-(r/R)^n}$ where $n=4$. The hadronic dynamics uses the purely nucleonic CD Bonn potential and its coupled-channel extension CD Bonn + $\Delta$ allowing for single virtual $\Delta$-isobar excitation. The on-shell breakup transition matrices are renormalized by diverging phase factors to recover the unscreened Coulomb limit, with the screened Coulomb interaction treated in two-baryon partial waves up to orbital angular momentum $L \le 15$ (with $9 \le L \le 15$ perturbatively) and three-baryon total angular momentum up to $\mathcal{J} = 61/2$.

## Key numbers

- Screening radius $R = 20$ fm is sufficient for convergence of spin-averaged differential cross section and spin observables in most kinematic situations, with $R = 30$ fm used for final results
- For $pp$ final-state interaction configurations, screening radii up to $R = 60$ fm are needed for convergence
- The screened Coulomb potential uses exponent $n=4$ in the screening function $w_R(r) = (\alpha_e/r) e^{-(r/R)^n}$
- Two-baryon partial waves with orbital angular momentum $L \le 15$ are included, with $9 \le L \le 15$ treated perturbatively
- Hadronic interaction is taken into account in two-baryon partial waves with total angular momentum $I \le 5$
- Maximal three-baryon total angular momentum considered is $\mathcal{J} = 61/2$
- For electromagnetic reactions, two-baryon partial waves with $L \le 6$ are sufficient for convergence
- Results are shown at proton lab energies of 10.5, 13, 19, and 65 MeV for $pd$ breakup, and at photon lab energies of 15 and 55 MeV for photodisintegration

## Context

This work extends the screening and renormalization approach previously applied to elastic proton-deuteron scattering by Deltuva, Fonseca, and Sauer (2005) to the more challenging case of breakup reactions. It improves upon earlier attempts by Alt et al. (1994), Kievsky et al. (1997), and Suslov et al. (2004) by using realistic hadronic potentials (CD Bonn and CD Bonn + $\Delta$) without separable expansion approximations and with a complete treatment of the Coulomb interaction. The method enables benchmark-quality calculations of $pd$ breakup observables and electromagnetic disintegration of ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ that can be compared with experimental data, though configuration-space treatments by Kievsky et al. (1997) and Suslov et al. (2004) could not yet provide benchmark comparisons due to approximations in their Coulomb treatment and hadronic dynamics.
