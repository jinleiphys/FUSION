# Many pion decays of rho(770) and omega(782) mesons in chiral theory

> The decays rho(770) to 4 pi and omega(782) to 5pi are considered in detail in the approach based on the Weinberg Lagrangian obtained upon the nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry, added with the term induced by the anomalous Lagrangian of Wess and Zumino. The partial widths and excitation curves of the decays rho^0 to 2 pi^+ 2 pi^-, pi^+ pi^- 2 pi^0, rho^{+-} to 2 pi^{+-} pi^{-+} pi^0, rho^(+-} to pi^(+-} 3 pi^0 are evaluated for e^+e^- annihilation, photoproduction and tau lepton decays. The results of calculations are compared with the recent CMD-2 data on the decay rho^0 to 2 pi^+ 2 pi^- observed in e^+e^- annihilation. The omega to 5 pi decay widths and excitation curves in e^+e^- annihilation are obtained. The angular distributions for various combinations of the final pions in the decays rho to 4 pi and omega to 5 pi are written. The perspectives of the experimental study of the above decays in e^+e^- annihilation, tau lepton decays and photoproduction are discussed.

## Key claim

The decays ρ(770)→4π and ω(782)→5π provide a clean test ground for chiral models of vector mesons because the final pions are sufficiently soft (typical momentum ~mπ for ρ→4π and ~0.5mπ for ω→5π) that higher derivative and loop corrections can be neglected, but only at the left shoulder of the ρ resonance where the contribution of higher derivatives vanishes rapidly.

## Method

The authors used the Weinberg Lagrangian from the nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry, supplemented by the Wess-Zumino anomalous term, to compute decay amplitudes for ρ→4π and ω→5π. They evaluated partial widths and excitation curves for e+e- annihilation, photoproduction, and τ lepton decays, comparing with CMD-2 data on ρ0→2π+2π- in e+e- annihilation. The nonrelativistic limit of the amplitudes was used for ω→5π calculations due to the low pion momenta.

## Key numbers

- Γ(ρ0→2π+2π-, mρ) = 0.89 keV (narrow width approximation)
- Γ(ρ0→π+π-2π0, mρ) = 0.24 keV (without anomaly term) and 0.44 keV (with anomaly term)
- Γ(ρ+→π+3π0, mρ) = 0.41 keV
- Γ(ρ+→2π+π-π0, mρ) = 0.71 keV (without anomaly term) and 0.90 keV (with anomaly term)
- B(ρ0→2π+2π-, mρ) = 5.9×10-6
- B(ρ0→π+π-2π0, mρ) = 1.6×10-6 (without anomaly) and 2.9×10-6 (with anomaly)
- B(τ-→ντ2π-π+π0, 740 MeV) = 7.6×10-8 (without anomaly) and 8.4×10-8 (with anomaly)
- B(τ-→ντ3π0π-, 740 MeV) = 4.6×10-8
- The ratio R(s) = Γ(ρ→2π+2π-, s)/ΓLIPS(ρ→2π+2π-, s) changes from 0.4 at √s=650 MeV to 1 at √s=mρ
- Difference between Γ(ρ→π+π-2π0, s) with and without anomaly term: 100% at √s=mρ, 8% at √s=700 MeV, 0.3% at √s=650 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Rittenberg (1969), Bramon (1993), Kuraev (1995), and Birse (1996) on ρ→4π decays, correcting for the Adler condition violation in some prior approaches and emphasizing that the width at the ρ mass alone is inadequate due to the rapid energy dependence of the partial width. The authors note that Birse (1996) and Achasov (1999) obtained B(ρ→4π)≈10-5, while earlier works gave ~10-4. The results enable experimental tests at e+e- colliders (φ factories), τ factories, and photoproduction facilities like Jefferson Laboratory, with the left shoulder of the ρ resonance (√s<700 MeV) identified as the cleanest region for studying chiral dynamics.
