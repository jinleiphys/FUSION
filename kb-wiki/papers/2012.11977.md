# Nuclear charge-exchange excitations based on relativistic density-dependent point-coupling model

> Spin-isospin transitions in nuclei away from the valley of stability are essential for the description of astrophysically relevant weak interaction processes. While they remain mainly beyond the reach of experiment, theoretical modeling provides important insight into their properties. In order to describe the spin-isospin response,vcthe proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) is formulated using the relativistic density-dependent point coupling interaction, and separable pairing interaction in both the $T=1$ and $T=0$ pairing channels. By implementing recently established DD-PCX interaction with improved isovector properties relevant for the description of nuclei with neutron-to-proton number asymmetry, the isobaric analog resonances (IAR) and Gamow-Teller resonances (GTR) have been investigated. In contrast to other models that usually underestimate the IAR excitation energies in Sn isotope chain, the present model accurately reproduces the experimental data, while the GTR properties depend on the isoscalar pairing interaction strength. This framework provides not only an improved description of the spin-isospin response in nuclei, but it also allows future large scale calculations of charge-exchange excitations and weak interaction processes in stellar environment.

## Key claim

The newly formulated proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) based on the density-dependent point-coupling interaction DD-PCX accurately reproduces experimental isobaric analog resonance (IAR) excitation energies in the Sn isotope chain, whereas other models usually underestimate them; however, the Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) properties depend on the isoscalar pairing interaction strength, which remains a free parameter.

## Method

The authors formulated the proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) in the canonical single-nucleon basis of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model, using the relativistic density-dependent point-coupling interaction DD-PCX for the particle-hole channel and a separable pairing interaction for both T=1 and T=0 pairing channels. The pseudovector coupling strength was adjusted to reproduce the experimental GTR excitation energy in 208Pb (19.2 MeV), yielding values of 0.734 for DD-PC1 and 0.621 for DD-PCX. The model was applied to calculate IAR and GTR transition strength distributions in closed-shell nuclei (48Ca, 90Zr, 208Pb) and open-shell Sn isotopes (A=104-132).

## Key numbers

- Pseudovector coupling strength α_PV = 0.734 for DD-PC1 interaction and α_PV = 0.621 for DD-PCX interaction, calibrated to reproduce the experimental GTR excitation energy in 208Pb at 19.2 MeV
- IAR excitation energies in Sn isotopes (A=104-132) calculated with DD-PCX show high accuracy against experimental data from Pham (1995), while DD-PC1 and DD-ME1 interactions provide systematically lower energies
- For closed-shell nuclei, the spread of IAR excitation energies across different interactions is about 1 MeV for 208Pb, with smaller differences for 48Ca and 90Zr
- The PN-RQRPA configuration space truncation used a condition u_qp1 * v_qp2 > 0.001 to exclude two-quasiparticle configurations with two almost empty states

## Context

This work extends the previous PN-RQRPA framework of Paar (2004) that used density-dependent meson-exchange interactions and Gogny pairing, by implementing the recently established DD-PCX point-coupling interaction of Yuksel (2019) with improved isovector properties constrained by nuclear collective excitations. The model enables future large-scale calculations of charge-exchange excitations and weak interaction processes in stellar environments, particularly for nuclei away from stability where experimental data are lacking.
