# Extended systematics of alpha decay half lives for exotic superheavy nuclei

> The experimentally available data on the alpha decay half lives and Q? values for 96 superheavy nuclei are used to fix the parameters for a modified version of the Brown empirical formula through two fitting procedures which enables its comparison with similar fits using Viola-Seaborg and Royer formulas. The new expressions provide very good agreement with experimental data having fewer or the same number of parameters. All formulas with the obtained parameters are then extrapolated to generate half lives predictions for 125 unknown superheavy alpha emitters. The nuclei where the employed empirical formulas maximally or minimally diverge are pointed out and a selection of 36 nuclei with exceptional superposition of predictions was made for experimental reference.

# Extended systematics of alpha decay half lives for exotic superheavy nuclei

## Key claim

The modified Brown formula, with the power of the daughter charge number treated as a free fitting parameter rather than fixed at 0.6, provides comparable or better agreement with experimental alpha decay data for 96 superheavy nuclei while using fewer or the same number of parameters as the Viola-Seaborg and Royer formulas; however, the improvement is marginal in the global fit, and the main advantage emerges in parity-specific fits where the power parameter varies between 0.4666 and 0.5966 depending on the parity class.

## Method

The authors collected experimental Q_alpha values and half-lives for 96 superheavy nuclei and performed least-squares fits using four empirical formulas: the Viola-Seaborg formula, the Royer formula, and two versions of a modified Brown formula (mB1 with six parameters including a parity-dependent hindrance term, and mB2 with twelve parameters having separate coefficients for each parity class). The fitted parameters were then used to predict half-lives for 125 unknown superheavy alpha emitters, using Q_alpha values calculated from a separate five-parameter liquid-drop-model formula.

## Key numbers

- The mB1 fit (6 parameters) achieved rms = 0.3972, compared to rms = 0.3951 for the Viola-Seaborg formula (7 parameters), on the same 96 superheavy nuclei
- The mB2 fit for even-even nuclei achieved rms = 0.0646, compared to rms = 0.0697 for the Royer formula, on 13 even-even nuclei
- The fitted power parameter b in the modified Brown formula ranged from 0.4666 (for odd-odd nuclei in mB2) to 0.5966 (for even-even nuclei in mB2), deviating from the original Brown value of 0.6
- Three nuclei (263108, 266109, 281110) were excluded from fits due to discrepancies of several orders of magnitude between all four theoretical predictions and experimental values
- 36 nuclei were identified with relative deviation epsilon < 10% between the four prediction methods, providing averaged log(T_alpha) values for experimental reference

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of empirical alpha decay formulas by Budaca et al. (2014-2016) and updates the Brown formula originally proposed by Brown (1992) for the specific region of superheavy nuclei. The study contrasts with global fits such as those by Sobiczewski et al. (1989) and Royer (2000) by fitting parameters exclusively to superheavy nuclei data, which yields different parameter values than the original sets. The predictions for 125 unknown alpha emitters adjacent to known nuclei provide reference points for experimental searches, particularly near the Z=108 and N=162 shell closures where the four independent formulas show maximal convergence.
