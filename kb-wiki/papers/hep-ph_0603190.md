# Large-Nc QCD and Pade Approximant Theory

> In the large-Nc limit of QCD, the chiral expansion of the vacuum polarization at low energies determines the whole function at any arbitrarily large (but finite) energy. This result is an immediate consequence of the Theory of Pade Approximants to Stieltjes functions.

## Key claim

In the large-\(N_c\) limit of QCD, the chiral expansion of the vacuum polarization at low energies determines the whole function at any arbitrarily large (but finite) energy, provided the function is a Stieltjes function; this result is an immediate consequence of the Theory of Pade Approximants to Stieltjes functions, but the limits \(Q^2 \to \infty\) and \(M \to \infty\) do not commute, so one cannot expand the approximant in powers of \(1/Q^2\) at high energies.

## Method

The paper demonstrates that the once-subtracted vacuum polarization \(\Phi(Q^2) = (\Pi_V(0) - \Pi_V(Q^2))/Q^2\) can be written as a Stieltjes integral with a bounded, nondecreasing measure, making it a Stieltjes function of \(Q^2\). Using a known convergence theorem for Pade Approximants \(R^{M+J}_M(Q^2)\) with \(J \geq -1\), the author shows that as \(M \to \infty\) the Pade sequence converges to \(\Phi(Q^2)\) for any finite \(Q^2\) in the complex plane except on the cut \(-\infty < Q^2 < -M_\rho^2\). The poles of the approximant lie on the negative real axis and correspond to resonance masses, and the low-energy chiral expansion coefficients \(\mathrm{L}_n\) determine the Pade coefficients.

## Key numbers

* The radius of convergence of the chiral expansion of \(\Phi(Q^2)\) is \(R = M_\rho^2\), the mass of the lowest vector state (the rho meson).
* For the \(R^{M-1}_M\) Pade, the convergence bound is \(|\Phi(Q^2) - R^{M-1}_M(Q^2)| \leq K \left| (\sqrt{M_\rho^2+Q^2} - \sqrt{M_\rho^2}) / (\sqrt{M_\rho^2+Q^2} + \sqrt{M_\rho^2}) \right|^{2M}\), where \(K\) is an unspecified constant.
* In a toy model with masses \(M_n^2 = 1, 2, 3, \dots\), the \(R^0_1\) Pade gives a first pole at \(\mu_1^2 = 1.3684\) (true \(M_1^2 = 1\)); the \(R^1_2\) Pade gives poles at \(\mu_1^2 = 1.0228\) and \(\mu_2^2 = 4.3711\) (true \(M_2^2 = 2\)); the \(R^5_6\) Pade gives poles at \(\mu_1^2 = 1\), \(\mu_2^2 = 2.0002\), \(\mu_3^2 = 3.0526\), and \(\mu_4^2 = 4.8688\) (true \(M_4^2 = 4\)).

## Context

This work extends the connection between large-\(N_c\) QCD and Pade Approximant theory, building on the foundational large-\(N_c\) results of 't Hooft and the chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984). It provides a rigorous mathematical basis for phenomenological approximations like vector meson dominance, as discussed by Ecker et al. (1989), and offers a systematic method to extract resonance masses from low-energy constants. The paper enables future work to compute the unknown chiral coefficients \(\mathrm{L}_n\) from lattice QCD or pion/kaon scattering, and to test the convergence of two-point Pades for connecting the Operator Product Expansion and chiral expansions, as explored by Ecker et al. (1989) and potentially linked to extra-dimensional theories by Erlich et al. (2005).
