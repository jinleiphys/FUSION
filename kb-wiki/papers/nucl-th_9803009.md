# Coherent Photoproduction of Pions on Spin-Zero Nuclei in a relativistic, non-local Model

> A relativistic and non-local model for the coherent photoproduction of pions on spin-zero nuclei is presented. The production operator is derived from an effective Lagrangian model that contains all the degrees of freedom known to contribute to the elementary process. Using the framework of DWIA, the matrix elements of this operator are evaluated using relativistic bound state wave functions. Final state interactions are accounted for via a pion-nucleus optical potential. The effects of in-medium modifications of the production operator are discussed. We give results for $^{12}C$ and $^{40}Ca$, and compare our calculations to the data of the A2 collaboration at MAMI.

## Key claim

The relativistic, non-local DWIA model for coherent neutral-pion photoproduction on spin-zero nuclei reproduces the shape of the A2 collaboration data for $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ but underestimates the absolute cross section by more than a factor of two, indicating that in-medium modifications of the production operator beyond the present treatment are required.

## Method

The production operator was derived from an effective Lagrangian containing nucleon Born terms, direct and crossed $\Delta$-resonance graphs, and an $\omega$-meson t-channel exchange, with coupling constants fixed by elementary $\gamma p \to \pi^0 p$ data. The DWIA matrix elements were evaluated non-locally in position space using relativistic bound-state wave functions from a mean-field Woods-Saxon potential for $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ and $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$, and distorted pion waves from an empirical s- and p-wave optical potential fitted to elastic $\pi$-$^{12}\mathrm{C}$ scattering data up to 330 MeV. The $\Delta$-width was treated as energy-dependent with a fixed average Fermi-sphere value for the momentum-dependent part of the propagator.

## Key numbers

- The $\Delta$-resonance width parameters: $\Gamma_o = 120$ MeV, with a damping parameter $c = 0.5$ GeV in the energy-dependent width formula.
- Coupling constants used: $g_{\pi NN} = 0.97$, $g_{\pi N\Delta} = 2.1$, $g_{\gamma N\Delta} = 0.337$, $g_{\omega\pi\gamma} = 0.313$, $g_{\omega NN}^v = 10$, $g_{\omega NN}^t = 1.4$.
- For $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ at $E_\gamma \approx 175$ MeV, the DWIA calculation underestimates the A2 differential cross section maximum by more than a factor of two.
- The A-dependence exponent $\alpha$ in $\sigma \sim A^\alpha$ is 0.7–0.8 for PWIA and 0.5–0.7 for DWIA, depending on photon energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier DWIA calculations (e.g., Giri 1977, Boffi 1981, Chumbalov 1985) by using a fully relativistic, non-local production operator without non-relativistic or local approximations, and by including both resonant and non-resonant contributions. It also contrasts with $\Delta$-hole model calculations (Carrasco 1993, Koch 1984, Oset 1982) that treat $\Delta$ dynamics more elaborately but often neglect non-resonant terms. The comparison to the new A2 data (Schmitz 1997) over the full $\Delta$ region (140–430 MeV) enables testing models beyond the threshold region, and the results motivate further study of in-medium modifications of the production operator, particularly in the $\Delta$ contribution.
