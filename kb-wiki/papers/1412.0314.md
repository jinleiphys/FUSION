# Viscosity of neutron star matter and $r$-modes in rotating pulsars

> We study viscosity of the neutron star matter and $r$-mode instability in rotating neutron stars. Contributions to the shear and bulk viscosities from various processes are calculated with account of in-medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. A softening of the pion mode and a possibility of the pion condensation are included. The nucleon pairing is incorporated. In the shear viscosity we include the lepton contribution calculated with account of the Landau damping in the photon exchange, the nucleon contribution described by the medium-modified one pion exchange, and other terms, such as the novel phonon contribution in the 1S$_0$ superfluid neutron phase, and the neutrino term in the neutrino opacity region. The nucleon shear viscosity depends on the density rather moderately and proves to be much less than the lepton term. Among the terms contributing to the bulk viscosity, the term from the medium modified Urca reactions possesses strongest density dependence because of the pion softening. Contributions to the bulk viscosity arising from other reactions induced by charged weak currents are included. The radiative bulk viscosity induced by charged and neutral weak currents in the region of the neutrino transparency of the star is also calculated with account for in-medium effects. The computed frequency boundary of the $r$-mode stability for the stars with the mass $\ge 1.8 M_{\odot}$ proves to be above the frequencies of all rotating young pulsars. To explain the stability of rapid rotation of old pulsars in X-ray binaries we propose a novel efficient mechanism associated with appearance of condensates of low-lying modes of bosonic excitations with finite momentum and/or with an enhancement of the inhomogeneous pion/kaon condensates in some parts of the star, if the angular velocity exceeds a critical value.

## Key claim
The paper argues that conventional viscosity mechanisms cannot explain the stability of rapidly rotating old recycled pulsars in X-ray binaries, and proposes a novel mechanism involving the appearance of condensates of low-lying bosonic excitations with finite momentum or an enhancement of inhomogeneous pion/kaon condensates when the angular velocity exceeds a critical value.

## Method
The authors calculated shear and bulk viscosities of neutron star matter using a Fermi-liquid approach with in-medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, including pion-mode softening and the possibility of pion condensation. They incorporated nucleon pairing, lepton contributions with Landau damping in photon exchange, and a novel phonon contribution in the 1S0 superfluid neutron phase. The r-mode stability boundary was then computed using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation with a phenomenological HDD equation of state matched to the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) EoS up to 4n0.

## Key numbers
- The maximum neutron star mass produced by the HDD equation of state is M_max ≈ 2.05 M_sun, in agreement with observations.
- The direct Urca processes do not appear up to densities of 5 n0, corresponding to a star mass M ≈ 1.9 M_sun.
- The Kepler frequency is given by ν_K ≈ 1.2 (M/M_sun)^(1/2) (10 km / R)^(3/2) kHz.
- The fastest observed young pulsar PSR J0537-6910 has a rotation frequency of ν_ex^(max, young) ≈ 62 Hz.
- The maximal rotation frequency of known pulsars, PSR J1748-2446ad, is ν_ex^(max, old) ≈ 716 Hz.
- The opacity temperature for modified Urca processes is T_opac ≈ 22 × 10^9 K.
- The computed frequency boundary of the r-mode stability for stars with mass ≥ 1.8 M_sun is above the frequencies of all rotating young pulsars.

## Context
This work extends the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario developed by Voskresensky (1985-2013) and Blaschke (2004-2013) by incorporating pion-softening effects into viscosity calculations for the first time. It contradicts the conclusion of Vidana (2012) that a lower limit on the symmetry energy slope L could be set from r-mode stability, noting that the data lines in that work were set incorrectly low and that nucleon superfluidity was ignored. The paper enables future studies of r-mode stability in old recycled pulsars by introducing a new damping mechanism based on bosonic condensates, which addresses a problem left unsolved by conventional viscosity mechanisms studied by Lindblom (1998-2001) and Haensel (1992-2001).
