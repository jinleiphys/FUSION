# Nuclear multifragmentation time-scale and fluctuations of largest fragment size

> Distributions of the largest fragment charge, Zmax, in multifragmentation reactions around the Fermi energy can be decomposed into a sum of a Gaussian and a Gumbel distribution, whereas at much higher or lower energies one or the other distribution is asymptotically dominant. We demonstrate the same generic behavior for the largest cluster size in critical aggregation models for small systems, in or out of equilibrium, around the critical point. By analogy with the time-dependent irreversible aggregation model, we infer that Zmax distributions are characteristic of the multifragmentation time-scale, which is largely determined by the onset of radial expansion in this energy range.

## Key claim

The distribution of the largest fragment charge in nuclear multifragmentation around the Fermi energy can be decomposed into a mixture of a Gaussian and a Gumbel distribution, with the relative proportions evolving with bombarding energy. This same behavior is observed for the largest cluster size in critical aggregation models for small systems, and by analogy with the time-dependent irreversible aggregation model, the Zmax distributions are interpreted as characteristic of the multifragmentation time-scale, which is largely determined by the onset of radial expansion.

## Method

Distributions of the largest fragment charge (Zmax) were measured for central collisions of 129Xe + natSn at beam energies from 25 to 50 MeV/A using the INDRA 4π charged product array at GANIL. The Zmax distributions were fitted with an admixture of a Gaussian and a Gumbel distribution, with the positions, widths, and relative weights left as free parameters. The same fitting procedure was applied to largest cluster size distributions from bond percolation on a 6×6×6 cubic lattice and from irreversible aggregation (Smoluchowski) equations for a system of total mass N=216.

## Key numbers

- For 129Xe + natSn central collisions, the scaling parameter Δ changes from Δ∼1/2 below 32 MeV/A to Δ∼1 above 32 MeV/A, based on the correlation between the first two cumulant moments of the Zmax distribution.
- The relative strength parameter R=2η-1 reaches R=0 between 29 and 32 MeV/A for the 129Xe + natSn system, in the same energy range as the change of Δ-scaling.
- For bond percolation on a 6×6×6 lattice, the critical bond probability is pc=0.312, and R≈-0.45 at p=pc.
- For the irreversible aggregation model with system size N=216, R=0 does not coincide with the infinite-system critical gelation time tc, occurring between tc and the time of maximum fluctuations.
- For 58Ni+58Ni collisions, the change of Δ-scaling and Zmax distribution form occurs at a higher bombarding energy of 52 MeV/A.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (PhysRevLett.86.3514, Frankland 2005) which established that the order parameter of nuclear multifragmentation is the size of the largest fragment and that Zmax distributions tend toward Gaussian at lowest energies and Gumbel at highest energies. The new data at 27, 29, and 35 MeV/A probe the transition region between these asymptotic regimes. The interpretation links the Zmax distribution form to the time-scale of fragment formation, which is determined by the onset of radial expansion (Bonnet 2009), and connects to systematic studies of nuclear stopping around the Fermi energy (Lehaut 2010). The mass-dependence of the transition energy for different symmetric systems (36Ar+KCl, 58Ni+58Ni, 129Xe+natSn, 197Au+197Au) is related to nuclear stopping and transport properties of hot nuclear matter.
