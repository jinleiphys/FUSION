# Inter-band B(E1) Strengths in Heavy Nuclei based on the Proxy-SU(3) Scheme

> Rotational bands of positive and negative parity are frequently found experimentally in heavy deformed even-even nuclei along with their corresponding $E2$ intra-band and $E1$ inter-band transitions. In $SU(3)$ symmetry-based models, calculating the $B(E2)$ strengths is relatively straightforward due to the relation between the $E2$ operator and the quadrupole generator of the algebra. However, calculating the $B(E1)$ strengths is a more difficult task, requiring computations involving the shell-model expansion of the dipole operator and multi-shell matrix elements. In this work the $E1$ operator is expanded in $SU(3)$ second quantization and applied to calculate the inter-band $B(E1)$ strengths of the isotope $^{224}$Th using the semi-microscopic algebraic quartet model (SAQM) based on the recently proposed proxy-$SU(3)$ scheme.

## Key claim

The paper extends the semi-microscopic algebraic quartet model (SAQM) based on the proxy-SU(3) scheme to compute inter-band B(E1) strengths for the isotope ²²⁴Th, but the results are compared only with experimental ratios R(E1/E2) derived from static dipole-to-quadrupole measurements rather than direct B(E1) measurements, which have not been reported for this nucleus.

## Method

The E1 transition operator was expanded in SU(3) second quantization formalism, and the resulting irreducible tensor components were applied to calculate reduced matrix elements between the ground-state positive-parity band and the excited negative-parity band of ²²⁴Th. The model space used the leading SU(3) irreps (60,8) for the ground state band and (65,6) for the excited band, with proton and neutron excitations treated independently and combined as a normalized superposition with mixing parameter α. The triple-barred SU(3) reduced matrix elements for the two-shell nucleon transitions were computed using specialized libraries for SU(3) Wigner coefficients and 9-(λ,μ) symbols.

## Key numbers

- The ground state band of ²²⁴Th is described by the leading SU(3) irrep (60,8) with KΠ=0⁺, and the excited negative-parity band by the leading irrep (65,6) with KΠ=0⁻
- The B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) value used as input is 96 (7) W.u., yielding α² = 0.1119 (82) W.u. for the E2 parameter
- The computed B(E1) strengths range from 0.009×10⁻² W.u. (3⁻→2⁺) to 1.283×10⁻² W.u. (12⁺→11⁻)
- The theoretical R(E1/E2) ratio for the 6⁺→5⁻ transition is 4.224×10⁻⁵, compared to the experimental value of 6.38 (14)×10⁻⁵
- The theoretical R(E1/E2) ratio for the 8⁺→7⁻ transition is 5.566×10⁻⁵, compared to the experimental value of 5.231 (57)×10⁻⁵
- The theoretical R(E1/E2) ratio for the 10⁺→9⁻ transition is 6.838×10⁻⁵, compared to the experimental value of 6.117 (18)×10⁻⁵
- The theoretical R(E1/E2) ratio for the 12⁺→11⁻ transition is 7.970×10⁻⁵, compared to the experimental value of 8.023 (18)×10⁻⁵

## Context

This work extends the SAQM calculations for ²²⁴Th previously reported by the same group (PhysRevC.101.054306), which provided deformation parameters, intra-band B(E2) strengths, and low-energy excitation spectra, by now computing inter-band B(E1) strengths. The results are compared with experimental D₀/Q₀ ratios from Ackermann (1993), Schuler (1986), and Leander (1986), and the model's predictions for B(E1) values are intended to stimulate future experimental measurements. The study is motivated by the connection between positive-negative parity band structures and static octupole deformation, with ²²⁴Th predicted to have an octupole deformation parameter ε₃ = 0.11 according to Moller (2016).
