# Point symmetries in the Hartree-Fock approach: Densities, shapes and currents

> Three mutually perpendicular symmetry axes of the second order, inversion, and time reversal can be used to construct a double point group denoted by D2h(TD). Properties of this group are analyzed in relation to the symmetry and symmetry-breaking effects within the mean-field (Hartree-Fock) theories, both in even and odd fermion systems. We enumerate space symmetries of local one-body densities, and symmetries of electromagnetic moments, that appear when some or all of the D2h(TD) elements represent self-consistent mean-field symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper systematically classifies the symmetry properties of local one-body densities and electromagnetic moments under the double point group D2h(TD), which combines three mutually perpendicular twofold rotation axes, inversion, and time reversal, but the actual classification applies only when some or all of these operators represent self-consistent mean-field symmetries, and the results depend explicitly on whether the fermion system has an even or odd number of particles.

## Method

The authors construct the single group D2h(T) for even-fermion systems and the double group D2h(TD) for odd-fermion systems by adding the time-reversal operator to the spatial point group D2h, then derive the multiplication tables and irreducible corepresentations for both groups. They enumerate the transformation properties of local one-body densities (scalar, vector, and tensor) under each group element, and use these to determine the allowed electric and magnetic multipole moments that can appear under various symmetry-breaking schemes.

## Key numbers

- The single group D2h(T) for even systems contains 16 operators: 8 linear (including identity, inversion, three signatures, three simplexes) and 8 antilinear (time reversal, T-inversion, three T-signatures, three T-simplexes)
- The double group D2h(TD) for odd systems contains 32 operators, with the linear subset D2h(D) having 10 equivalence classes: 6 classes of 2 elements each (the three signature/antipartner pairs and three simplex/antipartner pairs) plus 4 one-element classes (identity, rotation by 2pi, inversion, and antipartner of inversion)
- The double group D2h(D) possesses 8 one-dimensional irreps and 2 two-dimensional spinor irreps
- In the Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis, states |n_x n_y n_z, s_z> transform under the double group with eigenvalues given by explicit phase factors, e.g., inversion gives factor (-1)^{n_x+n_y+n_z}, time reversal gives (-1)^{1/2 - s_z} and flips spin

## Context

This work extends the classical treatment of point groups in nuclear mean-field theory by incorporating time reversal as an explicit group element, following the approach of Wigner (1959). It provides a systematic framework for classifying nuclear shapes and current distributions that complements earlier studies of self-consistent symmetries in Hartree-Fock calculations (Bonche et al., 1987; Dobaczewski et al., 1997). The results enable the classification of allowed electric and magnetic multipole moments in both even and odd nuclei, and are directly applicable to calculations using Skyrme effective interactions or the local density approximation (Negele and Vautherin, 1975; Dreizler and Gross, 1990).
