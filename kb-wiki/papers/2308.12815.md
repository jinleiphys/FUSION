# Neutron Scattering off One-Neutron Halo Nuclei in Halo Effective Field Theory

> Neutron scattering off neutron halos can provide important information about the internal structure of nuclei close to the neutron drip line. In this work, we use halo effective field theory to study the $s$-wave scattering of a neutron and the spin-parity $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^+$ one-neutron halo nuclei $^{11}\rm Be$, $^{15}\rm C$, and $^{19}\rm C$ at leading order. In the $J=1$ channel, the only inputs to the Faddeev equations are their one-neutron separation energies. In the $J=0$ channel, the neutron-neutron scattering length and the two-neutron separation energies of $\rm ^{12}Be$, $\rm ^{16}C$ and $\rm ^{20}C$ enter as well. The numerical results show that the total $s$-wave cross sections in the $J=1$ channel at threshold are of the order of a few barns. In the $J=0$ channel, these cross sections are of the order of a few barns for $n$-$^{11}\rm Be$ and $n$-$^{19}\rm C$ scattering, and about 60 $\rm mb$ for the $n$-$^{15}\rm C$ scattering. The appearance of a pole in $p\cotδ$ close to zero in all three cases indicates the existence of a virtual Efimov state close to threshold in each of the $^{12}\rm Be$, $^{16}\rm C$, and $^{20}\rm C$ systems. Observation of this pole would confirm the presence of Efimov physics in halo nuclei. The dependence of the results on the neutron-core scattering length is also studied.

## Key claim

Using halo effective field theory at leading order, the authors predict that the total s-wave cross sections for neutron scattering off the one-neutron halo nuclei 11Be, 15C, and 19C are of the order of a few barns in the J=1 channel, while in the J=0 channel the cross sections are also a few barns for n-11Be and n-19C but only about 60 mb for n-15C. Crucially, a pole in p cot δ close to zero appears in all three J=0 cases, indicating a virtual Efimov state near threshold, but this pole disappears when the neutron-core scattering length aσ is decreased by 0.5 fm for the n-15C system.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev integral equations for s-wave neutron scattering off one-neutron halo nuclei at leading order in halo effective field theory, treating the core as a point-like particle and using zero-range interactions for the nn and nc subsystems. In the J=1 channel, the only input was the one-neutron separation energy of the halo nucleus, while in the J=0 channel the nn scattering length and the two-neutron separation energies of 12Be, 16C, and 20C were also required to fix a three-body counterterm for renormalization. The cutoff dependence was removed by tuning the three-body parameter D0 to reproduce the ground-state two-neutron separation energies.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energies Bσ: 0.502 MeV for 11Be, 1.218 MeV for 15C, and 0.58 MeV for 19C (from AME2020)
- Neutron-core scattering lengths aσ: 6.741 fm for 11Be, 4.27 fm for 15C, and 6.142 fm for 19C
- Two-neutron separation energies B2n: 3.672 MeV for 12Be, 5.468 MeV for 16C, and 3.560 MeV for 20C (from AME2020)
- Total s-wave cross sections in the J=1 channel at threshold: of the order of a few barns for n-11Be, n-15C, and n-19C
- Total s-wave cross section in the J=0 channel at threshold: about 60 mb for n-15C scattering
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_s = -18.6 fm
- Expansion parameters Q/Λ: approximately 0.39 for 11Be, 0.45 for 15C, and 0.60 for 19C

## Context

This work extends previous halo EFT studies of two-neutron halo nuclei (Canham 2008, Hagen 2013, Acharya 2013, Ji 2014) and the Efimov effect in 12Be and 20C (Amorim 1997, Canham 2008, Canham 2009) by calculating neutron scattering cross sections off one-neutron halos rather than bound-state properties alone. The results enable experimental searches for Efimov physics in halo nuclei through neutron scattering measurements at facilities like the China Spallation Neutron Source, and the predicted pole in p cot δ provides a clear signature of a virtual Efimov state that could be observed if appropriate beams of halo nuclei become available.
