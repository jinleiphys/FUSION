# Coupled-channels description of multinucleon transfer and fusion reactions at energies near and far below the Coulomb barrier

> We investigate heavy-ion multinucleon transfer reactions using the coupled-channels formalism. We first use the semi-classical approximation and show that a direct coupling between the entrance and the pair transfer channels improves a fit to the experimental one- and two-neutron transfer cross sections for the 40Ca+96Zr and 60Ni+116Sn systems. We then discuss the validity of the perturbative approach and highlight the effect of high order terms. The effect of absorption is also investigated for energies around the Coulomb barrier. Finally, we use a quantal coupled-channels approach to achieve a simultaneous description of the fusion cross sections and the transfer probabilities for the 40Ca+96Zr reaction. We find a significant effect of the couplings to the collective excited states on the transfer probabilities around the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The authors show that a direct coupling between the entrance channel and the two-neutron transfer channel provides a better description of experimental transfer probabilities than a pure sequential two-neutron transfer scheme, but they caution that this conclusion depends on using a simplified coupling scheme with only a single (or at most a few) intermediate channel, and that the coupling scheme may have to be regarded as an effective one that implicitly takes into account the effect of many other intermediate channels.

## Method

The authors employed both semi-classical time-dependent coupled-channels calculations and full quantal coupled-channels calculations (using a modified version of the CCFULL code) to describe one- and two-neutron transfer cross sections for the 40Ca+96Zr and 60Ni+116Sn systems. They used a three-channel model (0n, 1n, 2n) with phenomenological Woods-Saxon-like coupling form factors, fitting the parameters to experimental transfer probabilities at large distances of closest approach (D > 13.5 fm), and compared two schemes: a pure sequential scheme (with V02 = 0) and a direct transfer scheme (with V01 = V12 and V02 ≠ 0).

## Key numbers

- For the 40Ca+96Zr system with optimum Q-values and the direct transfer scheme: a1 = 1.230 fm, β1 = 51.56 MeV·fm, a2 = 0.700 fm, β2 = 278.9 MeV·fm
- For the 60Ni+116Sn system with optimum Q-values and the direct transfer scheme: a1 = 1.13 fm, β1 = 47.1 MeV·fm, a2 = 0.600 fm, β2 = 384.2 MeV·fm
- The ground-state to ground-state Q-value for two-neutron transfer in 40Ca+96Zr is Q2 = 5.5 MeV
- The s-wave survival probability for the 40Ca+96Zr system was parametrized with barrier heights B = 95 MeV for the 0n and 1n channels and B = 92 MeV for the 2n channel, with widths σ = 1.3 MeV and 1.8 MeV respectively
- For the 60Ni+116Sn system, the survival probability barrier heights were B = 166 MeV for the 0n and 1n channels and B = 160 MeV for the 2n channel, with widths σ = 1.3 MeV, 1.5 MeV, and 2.8 MeV respectively
- The quantal and semi-classical calculations agreed within about 10% for distances of closest approach D > 13.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical and quantal coupled-channels approaches previously applied to multinucleon transfer reactions by Broglia and Winther (1991), Esbensen (1998), and Potel et al. (2013), and addresses the ongoing debate about the relative importance of direct versus sequential two-neutron transfer processes. The study was motivated by recent experiments on 40Ca+96Zr and 60Ni+116Sn by Corradi et al. (2011) and Montanari et al. (2014) that showed enhancement of two-neutron transfer cross sections compared to the independent particle picture. The results enable future work to clarify how the elimination of intermediate states changes the nature of two-neutron transfer couplings, and to develop reaction models that treat transfer and fusion simultaneously.
