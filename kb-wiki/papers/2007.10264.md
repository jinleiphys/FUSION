## Context

This work extends previous studies of unbound A=3 hypernuclear systems by Dalitz and Downs (1959), Garcilazo (1987), and more recent Faddeev calculations using Nijmegen YN potentials (Miyagawa et al. 1995) and chiral constituent quark models (Garcilazo et al. 2007), all of which found both systems unbound. The present calculation uses a consistent effective field theory framework constrained by low-energy scattering data and multiple hypernuclear binding energies, unlike earlier phenomenological approaches. The results contradict the HypHI collaboration's claim of a bound Œõnn state (2013) and support the conclusion of Gal and Garcilazo (2019) that such a bound state is highly unlikely. The calculated Œõd scattering lengths and effective ranges in the JœÄ=3/2+ channel were used by Haidenbauer (2020) in studies of Œõd correlation functions, and the nature of the ¬≥ŒõH* state is the subject of JLab proposal P12-19-002.
