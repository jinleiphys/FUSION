# Partial pressure and susceptibilities of charmed sector in the van der Waals hadron resonance gas model

> We investigate the general susceptibilities in the charm sector by using the van der Waals hadron resonance gas model (VDWHRG). We argue that the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG), which assumes no interactions between hadrons, and the excluded volume hadron resonance gas (EVHRG), which includes only repulsive interactions, fail to explain the lQCD data at very high temperatures. In contrast, the VDWHRG model, incorporating both attractive and repulsive interactions, extends the degree of agreement with lQCD up to nearly 180 MeV. We estimate the partial pressure in the charm sector and study charm susceptibility ratios in a baryon-rich environment, which is tricky for lattice quantum chromodynamics (lQCD) due to the fermion sign problem. Our study further solidifies the notion that the hadrons shouldn't be treated as non-interacting particles, especially when studying higher order fluctuations, but rather one should consider both attractive and repulsive interactions between the hadrons.

## Key claim

The van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model, which includes both attractive and repulsive interactions between hadrons, extends agreement with lattice QCD data for charm-sector susceptibilities and partial pressures up to nearly 180 MeV, whereas the ideal HRG and excluded volume HRG (EVHRG) models fail at high temperatures; however, discrepancies above approximately 160 MeV suggest contributions from deconfined charm degrees of freedom not captured by hadron-based models.

## Method

The authors computed generalized charm susceptibilities and partial pressures of charmed mesons and baryons (with and without strangeness) using the VDWHRG model, which incorporates a van der Waals attractive parameter a = 0.926 GeV fm^3 and a repulsive excluded-volume parameter b derived from hardcore radii (r_M = 0.2 fm for mesons, r_B = 0.62 fm for baryons). They compared these results with ideal HRG and EVHRG models, as well as with lattice QCD data from Kaczmarek (2025) and Sharma (2024), and extended the analysis to finite baryochemical potential and collision-energy-dependent freeze-out conditions using a parametrization from Cleymans (2005).

## Key numbers

* VDWHRG model agrees with lattice QCD partial pressure of charmed baryons up to T ~ 180 MeV, while ideal HRG deviates for T > 160 MeV.
* Attractive parameter a = 0.926 GeV fm^3 and repulsive parameter b derived from hardcore radii r_M = 0.2 fm for mesons and r_B = 0.62 fm for baryons.
* Freeze-out parametrization coefficients: q1 = 0.166 GeV, q2 = 0.139 GeV^-1, q3 = 0.053 GeV^-3, q4 = 1.308 GeV, q5 = 0.273 GeV^-1.
* Charm susceptibility ratios kappa_3^C/kappa_1^C and kappa_4^C/kappa_2^C saturate beyond center-of-mass energy of 10–30 GeV.
* kappa_3^C/kappa_2^C shows decreasing trend beyond sqrt(s_NN) = 10 GeV, with saturation at high energies.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Goswami (2023) and Goswami (2024) on VDWHRG model applications to charm fluctuations, and directly compares with lattice QCD results from Bazavov (2023), Kaczmarek (2025), and Sharma (2024). The VDWHRG model improves upon the ideal HRG and EVHRG models (Rischke 1991, Yen 1997) by including both attractive and repulsive interactions, enabling predictions for charm susceptibilities at finite baryochemical potential where lattice QCD faces the fermion sign problem. The results enable future tests of charm-sector interactions through measurements of open-charm fluctuations and correlations in heavy-ion collisions at LHC Run 3, RHIC Beam Energy Scan II, FAIR, and NICA.
