# Fine-tuning in scalar meson decay with bound-state corrections

> The glueball-quarkonia mixture decay amplitude has been evaluated, in the literature, by diagrammatic techniques for drawing quark lines and the quark-gluon vertex. In this paper we use an alternative approach which consists in a mapping technique, the Fock-Tani formalism, in order to obtain an effective Hamiltonian starting from microscopic interactions. An extra effect is present in this formalism associated to the extended nature of mesons: bound-state corrections, which introduces an additional decay amplitude and sets a fine-tuning procedure for general meson decay calculations. The $f_0(1500) \to ππ$ channel shall be considered as numerical example of the procedure.

## Key claim

The Fock-Tani formalism introduces bound-state corrections to the decay amplitude of scalar mesons with glueball-quarkonia mixing, which sets a fine-tuning procedure for general meson decay calculations; the effect produces a difference of 7% to 11% in the decay rates for the specific example of \(f_0(1500) \to \pi\pi\), depending on the mixing model used.

## Method

The authors apply the Fock-Tani formalism, a mapping technique that transforms composite meson and glueball creation operators into ideal-particle operators via a unitary transformation, to obtain an effective Hamiltonian from microscopic interactions. For the quark sector, they use a \(^{3}P_{0}\) pair-production Hamiltonian, and for the glue sector, a second-order quark-gluon vertex interaction; the resulting effective Hamiltonian includes bound-state correction terms (the \(C^{3}P_{0}\) Hamiltonian) that modify the decay amplitude. The decay rate for \(f_0(1500) \to 2\pi\) is then computed by combining the amplitude with relativistic phase space, using wave-function parameters and mixing coefficients from the literature.

## Key numbers

* Decay rate for \(f_0(1500) \to \pi\pi\) in Model A: 37.6 MeV (standard \(^{3}P_{0}\)) vs. 41.2 MeV (with bound-state corrections, \(C^{3}P_{0}\)).
* Decay rate for Model B: 19.8 MeV (standard) vs. 22.3 MeV (with corrections).
* Decay rate for Model C: 30.1 MeV (standard) vs. 32.5 MeV (with corrections).
* Experimental value for the channel: \(\Gamma_{\rm exp} = 38.04 \pm 2.51\) MeV.
* Meson masses used: \(M_{\pi} = 138\) MeV, \(M_{f_0} = 1505\) MeV.
* Coupling constants: \(\gamma = 0.5\) (quark-antiquark), \(\alpha_s = 0.6\) (quark-gluon).
* Wave-function widths: \(\beta_{\pi} = 0.4\) GeV, \(\beta_q = 0.3\) GeV; best-fit glueball width \(\beta_g \approx 1.6\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard \(^{3}P_{0}\) model for meson decays (Barnes et al.) by incorporating bound-state corrections from the Fock-Tani formalism, which were previously developed for composite meson scattering and decay (da Silva et al., 2008). The method is applied to the scalar glueball-quarkonia mixing problem, where prior studies (Close et al., Faessler et al.) have proposed mixing parameters for the \(f_0(1370)\), \(f_0(1500)\), and \(f_0(1710)\) resonances; the present calculation uses three mixing models from Faessler et al. The approach enables future refinements using Hamiltonian formulations such as QCD in the Coulomb gauge (Swanson et al.) to replace phenomenological parameters with fundamental QCD quantities.
