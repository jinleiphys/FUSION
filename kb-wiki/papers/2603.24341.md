# The dipole strength distribution of $^8$He and decay characteristics

> The weak binding and spatially extended neutron densities characteristic of drip-line nuclei give rise to a distinctive low-energy dipole response. The drip-line nucleus $^8$He is the most neutron-rich bound nucleus with a mass-to-charge ratio of $A/Z=4$. We measure the dipole response of $^8$He, including for the first time the four-neutron decay channel. A total dipole strength of $\sum B(E1)(E^*<15$~MeV$)=0.95(16)~e^2$fm$^2$ and a dipole polarizability of $α_D = 0.61(1)$~fm$^3$ are extracted from the differential Coulomb-excitation cross section and compared to state-of-the-art theoretical calculations employing coupled cluster and three-body approaches. We find that the dipole continuum is dominated, even at high excitation energies well above the $4n$ decay threshold, by two-neutron emission, pointing to a $^6$He$+2n$ structure of the excited dipole mode. No indication was found for a $4n$ final-state correlation, while pronounced $nn$ and $^6$He-$n$ final-state correlations are apparent.

## Key claim

The dipole response of $^8$He is dominated by two-neutron emission even at excitation energies well above the $4n$ decay threshold, indicating a $^6$He+2n structure of the excited dipole mode, with no indication of a four-neutron final-state correlation.

## Method

The dipole response of $^8$He was measured via relativistic Coulomb excitation at approximately 185 MeV/nucleon using a lead target at the RIBF facility. The $^6$He+2n and $^4$He+4n breakup channels were reconstructed in complete kinematics using the SAMURAI spectrometer for charged fragments and the NeuLAND demonstrator plus NEBULA arrays for neutron detection, with the nuclear excitation background subtracted using measurements with carbon, titanium, and tin targets. The differential Coulomb excitation cross sections were converted to dipole strength distributions $dB(E1)/dE^*$ using the equivalent-photon method, and final-state correlations were studied by comparing measured two-body relative-energy spectra to phase-space decay simulations.

## Key numbers

- Total dipole strength integrated up to $E^* = 15$ MeV: $\sum B(E1) = 0.95 \pm 0.01$ (stat.) $\pm 0.16$ (sys.) $e^2$fm$^2$
- Dipole polarizability: $\alpha_D = 0.61 \pm 0.01$ (stat.) $\pm 0.01$ (sys.) fm$^3$
- Energy-weighted dipole strength up to $E^* = 15$ MeV: $\sum E^* B(E1) = 7.55 \pm 0.18$ (stat.) $\pm 1.39$ (sys.) $e^2$fm$^2$MeV, corresponding to about 1/3 of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule
- Integrated Coulomb excitation cross section for $^6$He+2n channel up to $E^* = 15$ MeV: $\sigma_{C,2n} = 243 \pm 1$ (stat.) $\pm 33$ (sys.) mb
- Integrated Coulomb excitation cross section for $^4$He+4n channel up to $E^* = 15$ MeV: $\sigma_{C,4n} = 21.14 \pm 0.02$ (stat.) $\pm 5.34$ (sys.) mb
- $^7$He ground-state resonance energy from benchmark measurement: $E_{\rm r} = 385 \pm 1$ (stat.) $\pm 1$ (sys.) keV with width $\Gamma = 193 \pm 1$ (stat.) $\pm 4$ (sys.) keV

## Context

This work extends previous Coulomb excitation measurements of $^8$He at GSI (Meister, 2002) and MSU (Iwata, 2000) by providing high-statistics data up to 15 MeV excitation energy and measuring the $^4$He+4n decay channel for the first time. The results are compared to ab initio coupled-cluster calculations with the Lorentz integral transform (Bonaiti, 2022), a three-body hyperspherical harmonics model (Grigorenko, 2009), and a five-body cluster orbital shell model (Myo, 2022). The finding that the dipole continuum is dominated by two-neutron emission even at high excitation energies, together with the observed $^7$He resonance and nn correlations, points to a di-neutron correlation structure in the dipole mode, while the absence of a 4n correlation contrasts with the recent observation of a near-threshold structure in the 4n system via alpha-knockout from $^8$He (Duer, 2022).
