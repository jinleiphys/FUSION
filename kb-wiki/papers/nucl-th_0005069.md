# Effective Field Theory For Nuclei: Confronting Fundamental Questions in Astrophysics

> Fundamental issues involving nuclei in the celebrated solar neutrino problem are discussed in terms of an effective field theory adapted to nuclear few-body systems, with a focus on the proton fusion process and the hep process. Our strategy in addressing these questions is to combine chiral perturbation theory -- an effective field theory of QCD -- with an accurate nuclear physics approach to arrive at a more effective effective field theory that reveals and exploits a subtle role of the chiral-symmetry scale in short-distance effects encoded in short-range nuclear correlations. Our key argument is drawn from the close analogy of the principal weak matrix element figuring in the hep process to the suppressed matrix elements in the polarized neutron-proton capture at threshold currently being measured in the laboratories.

# Effective Field Theory For Nuclei: Confronting Fundamental Questions in Astrophysics

## Key claim

The authors argue that the suppressed Gamow-Teller matrix element in the hep process (³He(p,e⁺νₑ)⁴He) can be reliably calculated using a hybrid effective field theory approach, but only up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N⁴LO); beyond this order, the one-to-one correspondence between counter terms and a single experimentally-fixed constant is expected to break down, making parameter-free predictions impossible.

## Method

The authors combine chiral perturbation theory for the irreducible current vertex with accurate nuclear wave functions from the standard potential-model approach (e.g., Argonne potentials), a strategy they call "more effective effective field theory" (MEEFT). They compute the weak current vertex to N⁴LO in chiral counting, while the reducible graphs involving the nuclear potential are iterated to all orders via the Schrödinger equation. The four independent counter terms that appear at N³LO and N⁴LO are shown to combine into a single constant Y, which is fixed by the triton beta decay rate, making the hep matrix element completely determined at that order.

## Key numbers

- The maximum neutrino energy for the hep process is E_ν^max(hep) = 18.795 MeV, higher than the ⁸B neutrino maximum of E_ν^max(⁸B) = 17.980 MeV.
- The Standard Solar Model hep S-factor would need enhancement by more than a factor of 20 relative to the SSM value S_0 to explain the Super-Kamiokande data if interpreted solely as hep neutrinos.
- For the proton fusion process p+p→d+e⁺+νₑ, the calculated S-factor is S_pp^th = 4.05 (1 ± 0.08) × 10⁻²⁵ MeV·b, close to the SSM value S_pp^SSM = 4.00 (1 ± 0.007⁺⁰·⁰²⁰₋₀·₀₁₁) × 10⁻²⁵ MeV·b.
- The Gamow-Teller matrix element for the hep process is predicted as M_GT/g_A = −0.20 ± 0.06, obtained from summing LO, N³LO, N⁴LO, and Y-dependent contributions over a momentum cutoff range Λ_min = 0.5 GeV to Λ_max = 1.0 GeV.
- For the polarized np capture, the predicted ratio R_M1S × 10³ = (−0.50, −0.49) over the hard-core cutoff range r_c = 0.01 fm to 0.8 fm, and R_E2S × 10³ = 0.24 + O(10⁻³).

## Context

This work extends the chiral filter conjecture of Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (KDR) to address the Bahcall challenge regarding the hep process, which had been identified as the most important unsolved problem in theoretical nuclear physics related to solar neutrinos. The authors build on their previous PKMR hybrid approach and the standard nuclear physics calculations of Pandharipande and Schiavilla, using Argonne potentials (Av14, with Av18 noted as potentially better). The results for the polarized np capture are consistent with the independent calculation by Chen, Rupak, and Savage using a different EFT approach. The work enables a confrontation with Super-Kamiokande data to determine whether the observed high-energy solar neutrino excess is due to nuclear physics uncertainties or new physics beyond the Standard Model.
