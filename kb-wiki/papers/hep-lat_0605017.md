# The nucleon electromagnetic form factors from Lattice QCD

> We evaluate the isovector nucleon electromagnetic form factors in quenched and full QCD on the lattice using Wilson fermions. In the quenched theory we use a lattice of spatial size 3 fm at beta=6.0 enabling us to reach low momentum transfers and a lowest pion mass of about 400 MeV. In the full theory we use a lattice of spatial size 1.9 fm at beta=5.6 and lowest pion mass of about 380 MeV enabling comparison with the results obtained in the quenched theory. We compare our lattice results to the isovector part of the experimentally measured form factors.

# The nucleon electromagnetic form factors from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The isovector nucleon electromagnetic form factors computed in quenched and full QCD on the lattice show that unquenching effects are small for the pion masses considered (lowest around 380-400 MeV), but the lattice results disagree with the experimentally extracted isovector form factors in both their magnitude and Q² dependence, particularly for the electric form factor.

## Method

The authors computed the isovector nucleon electromagnetic form factors using Wilson fermions on the lattice, employing the fixed sink method with sequential inversions to evaluate three-point functions. They used gauge invariant Wuppertal smearing at source and sink with hypercubic smearing of gauge links, and extracted the Sachs form factors G_E and G_M from an overconstrained analysis of all lattice momentum vectors contributing to a given Q², using the symmetrized lattice conserved electromagnetic current. Quenched calculations used a 32³×64 lattice at β=6.0 (a≈0.09 fm), while unquenched calculations used two degenerate flavors of dynamical Wilson fermions on 24³×40 and 24³×32 lattices at β=5.6 (a≈0.08 fm).

## Key numbers

- Quenched lattice spatial size: 3 fm at β=6.0, enabling momentum transfers as low as about 0.15 GeV²
- Lowest pion mass in quenched theory: about 400 MeV
- Lowest pion mass in unquenched theory: about 380 MeV
- Quenched lattice spacing: a≈0.09 fm (a⁻¹=2.14(6) GeV)
- Unquenched lattice spacing: a≈0.08 fm (a⁻¹=2.56(10) GeV)
- Quenched hopping parameters: κ=0.1554, 0.1558, 0.1562, giving m_π/m_ρ ratios of 0.64, 0.59, and 0.50 respectively
- Unquenched hopping parameters: κ=0.1575, 0.1580, 0.15825, giving m_π/m_ρ ratios of 0.69, 0.56, and 0.45 respectively
- Number of configurations: 200 for each quenched κ value; 185, 157, and 200 for unquenched κ values respectively
- Local current renormalization constant: Z_v=0.67 (from G_E⁻¹(0))
- Highest accessible momentum transfer at β=6.0: 2π/a≈13 GeV (not reached due to statistical noise)

## Context

This work extends previous quenched lattice calculations of nucleon form factors by QCDSF using improved Wilson fermions, achieving higher accuracy at lower momentum transfers and pion masses. The authors compare their lattice results to the isovector part of experimentally measured form factors extracted from proton and neutron data, using the Galster parametrization for the neutron electric form factor and dipole fits for the proton data. The study enables future chiral extrapolations and continuum limit studies to resolve the observed discrepancy between lattice results and experiment, particularly the different Q² dependence of G_E at low momentum transfers.
