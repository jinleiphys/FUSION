# The JETSCAPE framework: p+p results

> The JETSCAPE framework is a modular and versatile Monte Carlo software package for the simulation of high energy nuclear collisions. In this work we present a new tune of JETSCAPE, called PP19, and validate it by comparison to jet-based measurements in $p+p$ collisions, including inclusive single jet cross sections, jet shape observables, fragmentation functions, charged hadron cross sections, and dijet mass cross sections. These observables in $p+p$ collisions provide the baseline for their counterparts in nuclear collisions. Quantifying the level of agreement of JETSCAPE results with $p+p$ data is thus necessary for meaningful applications of JETSCAPE to A+A collisions. The calculations use the JETSCAPE PP19 tune, defined in this paper, based on version 1.0 of the JETSCAPE framework. For the observables discussed in this work calculations using JETSCAPE PP19 agree with data over a wide range of collision energies at a level comparable to standard Monte Carlo codes. These results demonstrate the physics capabilities of the JETSCAPE framework and provide benchmarks for JETSCAPE users.

## Key claim

The JETSCAPE PP19 tune, based on version 1.0 of the JETSCAPE framework, provides an overall acceptable description of p+p jet data at a level comparable to standard leading-order Monte Carlo codes like PYTHIA 8, but the authors note that leading-order simulations are not expected to fit all aspects of complex collision dynamics equally well, and significant improvements may require consistent next-to-leading order formalisms.

## Method

The JETSCAPE framework was configured in p+p mode using PYTHIA 8.230 to generate hard processes and underlying events (with multi-parton interactions and initial state radiation on, but final state radiation and hadronization off), then extracted light partons with pT > 2 GeV/c and passed them to the MATTER parton shower generator with initial virtuality Q_ini = pT/2 and final virtuality cutoff Q_0 = 1 GeV. After parton showering, two alternative string formation procedures (Colored Hadronization and Colorless Hadronization) were used to form color-singlet string systems, which were then fragmented by PYTHIA 8 string fragmentation and resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive jet cross sections calculated at sqrt(s) = 0.2, 2.76, and 7 TeV for jet radii R between 0.2 and 0.7, covering jet transverse momentum pT_jet from 10 GeV/c to 1 TeV/c
- MATTER initial virtuality parameter Q_ini set to 0.5 * pT for each hard parton
- MATTER final shower parton virtuality Q_0 set to 1 GeV
- Transverse momentum cut for initial partons from PYTHIA to MATTER set at pT > 2 GeV/c
- QCD scale Lambda_QCD set to 0.2 GeV
- Hadron decay cutoff c*tau set to 1 cm
- PYTHIA 8.230 used with NNPDF2.3 LO parton distribution functions with alpha_s = 0.13

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework (Putschke 2019) from its A+A collision capabilities to provide validated p+p baselines using the PP19 tune, which are necessary for meaningful applications to nuclear collisions. The results are compared extensively to PYTHIA 8 (Sjostrand 2014) and to experimental data from CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE collaborations at multiple collision energies. The paper enables future JETSCAPE studies of jet quenching in A+A collisions by establishing the level of agreement with p+p data and providing uncertainty estimates from the two hadronization schemes.
