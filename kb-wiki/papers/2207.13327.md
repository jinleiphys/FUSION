# Impact of tensor force on quantum shell effects in quasifission reactions

> Quantum shell effects drive many aspects of many-body quantal systems and their interactions. Among these are the quasifission reactions that impede the formation of a compound nucleus in superheavy element (SHE) searches. Fragment production in quasifission is influenced by shell effects as a nontrivial manifestation of microscopic dynamics hindering the full equilibration of the composite system to form the compound nucleus. In this Letter, we use the microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory to study 48Ca+249Bk collisions to investigate the influence of the tensor component of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The results show that the inclusion of the tensor force causes the spherical shell effect to become more prominent, particularly for the neutron number yield whose peak is exactly at magic number N = 126. This suggests that the tensor force plays a compelling role in the evolution of dynamical shell effects in nuclear reactions, influencing the competition between spherical and deformed shell gaps.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the tensor component of the Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interaction in time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations for the 48Ca+249Bk reaction causes the spherical shell effect to become more prominent, particularly shifting the neutron number yield peak to exactly the magic number N=126, which is the first evidence that the tensor force influences dynamical shell effects in quasifission reactions.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF calculations on a 3D Cartesian grid for 48Ca+249Bk collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 234 MeV, using the SLy5 and SLy5t Skyrme parameter sets which differ only by the inclusion of the tensor force term. Five orientations of the prolate deformed 249Bk target (β=0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°) were considered with impact parameters from 0 fm in steps of 0.5 fm until quasi-elastic reactions occur, and the results were combined using an integral over impact parameter and orientation to produce average fragment yield distributions.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: 234 MeV for the 48Ca+249Bk reaction
- Neutron yield peak with tensor force (SLy5t): centered at N=126; without tensor force (SLy5): centered near N=122
- Proton yield peak with tensor force: very close to Z=82; without tensor force: around Z=79
- Contact times for most reactions: between 5-10 zs (zeptoseconds)
- Numerical box dimensions: 60×28×48 fm³; grid spacing: 1.0 fm; time step: 0.2 fm/c
- Initial separation of nuclei: 28 fm; final fragment separation for analysis: about 28 fm

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of quasifission for the same 48Ca+249Bk system using the SLy4d interaction (Godbey et al., 2019), and is consistent with those results for the no-tensor case. The findings connect to experimental observations of spherical shell gaps in quasifission for 48Ti+238U (Morjean et al., 2017) and demonstrate that the tensor force, previously shown to affect fusion barriers and cross sections (Guo et al., 2018; Godbey et al., 2019c; Sun et al., 2022), also plays a significant role in quasifission dynamics by strengthening spherical shell effects and influencing the competition between spherical and deformed shell gaps.
