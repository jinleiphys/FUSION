# Some New Results on the H Dibaryon in the Quark Cluster Model

> The H dibaryon channel, (I=0,J=0,S=-2), is revisited in the non-relativistic quark cluster model (NRQCM) using a basis extended beyond the usual set of baryon cluster pairs to include an explicit spatially symmetric 6q state, analogous in structure to the MIT bag model H. We find that the binding predicted using the two-baryon basis alone is significantly deepened by the addition of the additional 6q configuration. The NRQCM thus appears, contrary to earlier findings, to be incompatible with the experimental information available for this channel.

## Key claim

The non-relativistic quark cluster model (NRQCM), when extended to include an explicit spatially symmetric 6-quark configuration analogous to the MIT bag model H, predicts a deeply bound H dibaryon with binding energy far exceeding the experimental upper bound of 28 MeV, contrary to earlier NRQCM findings of no binding or weak binding that used only two-baryon basis states.

## Method

The authors performed a bound-state resonating group method (RGM) calculation for the six-quark system in the (I=0, J=0, S=-2) channel, using a basis that included the conventional two-baryon states (Lambda-Lambda, Sigma-Sigma, and N-Xi) plus an explicit spatially symmetric 6-quark state (called h) constructed to be analogous to the MIT bag model H. They studied two Hamiltonians: one with only short-range confinement and one-gluon-exchange (OGE) potentials, and another that added scalar (sigma) and pseudoscalar (pi and K) meson exchange potentials, with parameters taken from Oka (1987) and Zhang (1994) respectively.

## Key numbers

- Using Hamiltonian H1 (confinement + OGE only) without the h state: no bound dibaryon state, consistent with Oka (1987).
- Using Hamiltonian H1 with the h state added: a weakly bound state with binding energy of 6 MeV.
- Using Hamiltonian H2 (confinement + OGE + meson exchange) without the h state: weak binding energy of 24 MeV, consistent with Straub (1988).
- Using Hamiltonian H2 with the h state added: a very deeply bound state with binding energy of 252 MeV.
- The experimental upper bound on H binding from sequential Lambda decay of double-Lambda hypernuclei is less than 28 MeV (if the candidate is 13-Lambda-Lambda-B, less if the alternate assignment 10-Lambda-Lambda-Be is accepted).
- For +/- 10% changes in the sigma meson mass, the binding energy in the presence of h changes by as much as 15 MeV (inversely with m_sigma).

## Context

This work extends the NRQCM studies of the H dibaryon by Oka (1987) and Straub (1988), which used only two-baryon basis states and found no binding or weak binding respectively. The authors demonstrate that adding a localized 6-quark configuration, analogous to the MIT bag model H from Jaffe (1977), dramatically deepens the predicted binding, making the NRQCM incompatible with the experimental constraint from the sequential Lambda decay observation of Aoki (1991). This result parallels an earlier finding by Maltman (1985) in the (I=0, J=3, S=0) Delta-Delta channel, where adding a localized configuration increased binding from a few MeV to 260 MeV, and highlights the need for caution when truncating basis states in multiquark calculations.
