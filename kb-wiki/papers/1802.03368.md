# Moments of inertia of neutron stars in relativistic mean field theory: the role of the isovector scalar channel

> With the inclusion of the isovector scalar channel in the meson-nucleon couplings, taking DD-ME$δ$ as an effective interaction, the moments of inertia of neutron stars possessing various stellar masses are studied within the density dependent relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. The isovector scalar channel contributes to the softening of the neutron-star matter equation of state (EOS) and therefore the reduction of the maximum mass and radius of neutron stars. Smaller values of the total moment of inertia $I$ and the crustal moment of inertia $Δ{I}$ are then obtained in DD-ME$δ$ via numerical procedure in comparison with those in other selected RMF functionals. In addition, the involvement of the isovector scalar channel lowers the thickness of the neutron star crust and its mass fraction as well. The sensitivity to both the crustal mass and stellar radius causes the crustal moment of inertia to be more obviously reduced than the total one, eventually leading to a suppression on the fraction of crustal moment of inertia $Δ{I}/I$ in DD-ME$δ$. The results indicate the crustal moment of inertia as a more sensitive probe of the neutron-star matter EOS than the total one, and demonstrate that the isovector scalar meson-nucleon couplings in the RMF theory could exert influence over the physics of pulsar glitches.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the isovector scalar (delta) meson-nucleon coupling in the density-dependent relativistic mean field (RMF) theory softens the neutron-star matter equation of state (EOS), leading to smaller total and crustal moments of inertia; the crustal moment of inertia is identified as a more sensitive probe of the EOS than the total moment of inertia, with implications for pulsar glitch physics, but the results depend on the use of the thermodynamical method (a simplification of the dynamical method) for determining the core-crust transition.

## Method

The authors used the density-dependent RMF theory with the DD-MEδ functional, which includes the isovector scalar (δ) meson-nucleon coupling, to compute the equation of state for neutron-star matter under β-equilibrium. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations and the slowly-rotating approximation were solved to obtain the stellar structure, total moment of inertia I, and crustal moment of inertia ΔI. The core-crust transition density was determined using the thermodynamical method (V_ther = 0 condition), and the BPS and BBP models were adopted for the crust EOS.

## Key numbers

- The density slope parameter of the symmetry energy L for DD-MEδ is 52.58 MeV; for comparison, DD-ME2 gives 51.21 MeV, TW99 gives 55.31 MeV, and PKDD gives 90.25 MeV.
- The radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star R_1.4 for DD-MEδ is 12.013 km; for DD-ME2 it is 13.27 km, for TW99 it is 12.361 km, and for PKDD it is 13.685 km.
- The core-crust transition density ρ_t for DD-MEδ is 0.086 fm^{-3}; for DD-ME2 it is 0.087 fm^{-3}, for TW99 it is 0.085 fm^{-3}, and for PKDD it is 0.075 fm^{-3}.
- The core-crust transition pressure P_t for DD-MEδ is 0.537 MeV fm^{-3}; for DD-ME2 it is 0.593 MeV fm^{-3}, for TW99 it is 0.524 MeV fm^{-3}, and for PKDD it is 0.614 MeV fm^{-3}.
- The central density of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star ρ_1.4 for DD-MEδ is 0.52 fm^{-3}; for DD-ME2 it is 0.34 fm^{-3}, for TW99 it is 0.47 fm^{-3}, and for PKDD it is 0.35 fm^{-3}.
- For the Vela pulsar, the DD-MEδ functional predicts a maximum stellar mass at around 1.9 solar masses under the constraint ΔI/I ≥ 0.016.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star properties within covariant density functional theory, specifically by incorporating the isovector scalar (δ) meson channel, which had been included in nucleon-nucleon potentials (Kubis, 1997) and relativistic Brueckner theory (Sugahara, 1997; Lenske, 1998; Hofmann, 2001) but was often omitted in RMF functionals such as TW99 (Typel and Wolter, 1999), DD-ME2 (Lalazissis et al., 2005), and PKDD (Long et al., 2004). The results enable future comparisons with observational constraints on the crustal moment of inertia from pulsar glitch data, particularly for the Vela pulsar, and with more sophisticated dynamical treatments of the core-crust transition. The work also relates to studies linking the core-crust transition density to the symmetry energy slope L (Xu et al., 2009; Fattoyev et al., 2010; Ducoin et al., 2011; Moustakidis, 2012; Providencia, 2014).
