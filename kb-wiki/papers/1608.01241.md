# Role of nuclear physics in oscillations of magnetars

> Strong magnetic fields have important effects on the crustal properties of magnetars. Here we study the magneto-elastic oscillations of magnetars taking into consideration the effect of strong magnetic fields on the crustal composition (magnetised crust). We calculate global magneto-elastic (GME) modes as well as modes confined to the crust (CME) only. The composition of the crust directly affects its shear modulus which we calculate using three different nucleon-nucleon interactions: SLy4, SkM and Sk272. The shear modulus of the crust is found to be enhanced in strong magnetic fields $\geq 10^{17}$ G for all those Skyrme interactions. It is noted that the shear modulus of the crust for the SLy4 interaction is much higher than those of the SkM and Sk272 interactions in presence of magnetic fields or not. Though we do not find any appreciable change in frequencies of fundamental GME and CME modes with and without magnetised crusts, frequencies of first overtones of CME modes are significantly affected in strong magnetic fields $\geq 10^{17}$ G. However, this feature is not observed in frequencies of first overtones of GME modes. As in earlier studies, it is also noted that the effects of crusts on frequencies of both types of maneto-elastic modes disappear when the magnetic field reaches the critical field ($B > 4 \times 10^{15}$ G). Frequencies of GME and CME modes calculated with magnetised crusts based on all three nucleon-nucleon interactions, stellar models and magnetic fields, are compared with frequencies of observed quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOS) in SGR 1806-20 and SGR1900+14. As in earlier studies, this comparison indicates that GME modes are essential to explain all the frequencies as CME modes can explain only the higher frequencies.

## Key claim

The paper finds that while the shear modulus of magnetar crusts is enhanced in strong magnetic fields (≥ 10¹⁷ G) due to Landau quantisation of electrons, this enhancement does not appreciably change the frequencies of fundamental global magneto-elastic (GME) or crust-confined (CME) modes; however, frequencies of first overtones of CME modes are significantly suppressed (by 5–7%) in such strong fields, an effect the abstract does not mention.

## Method

The authors solve the general relativistic perturbation equations for torsional oscillations using the Cowling approximation, assuming a dipole magnetic field and neglecting stellar deformation. They compute the shear modulus of the crust from the composition and equation of state obtained via the Thomas-Fermi model, including Landau quantisation of electrons in strong magnetic fields, for three Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interactions (SLy4, SkM, Sk272). They then calculate both crust-confined (CME) modes (with zero-traction boundary conditions at crust-core interface) and global magneto-elastic (GME) modes (with regularity at the centre) for various stellar masses and magnetic field strengths.

## Key numbers

- Shear modulus enhancement occurs for magnetic fields ≥ 10¹⁷ G; at B = 4.414 × 10¹⁷ G (B* = 10⁴), all electrons populate the zeroth Landau level.
- For a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star, fundamental (n=0, ℓ=2) CME mode frequencies increase linearly with magnetic field for B* > 100, but show no difference between magnetised and non-magnetised crusts.
- First overtone (n=1) CME mode frequencies are suppressed by 5–7% for magnetised crusts at B* = 10⁴, corresponding to a 2–4% increase in the crust thickness-to-radius ratio (ΔR/R).
- For SGR 1806-20, CME mode frequencies above 93 Hz (using 1.3–1.7 M⊙ stars at B = 8 × 10¹⁴ G) agree with observed QPOs at 92.5, 150, 626.5, and 1837 Hz, but frequencies below 93 Hz do not match.
- For SGR 1900+14, CME mode frequencies (using 1.2–1.7 M⊙ stars at B = 4 × 10¹⁴ G) agree with observed QPOs at 28, 53.5, 84, and 155 Hz.
- For GME modes, crust effects disappear above a critical field B > 4 × 10¹⁵ G, where oscillations become purely magnetic.
- GME mode frequencies for SGR 1806-20 (using 1.4–1.5 M⊙ stars at B = 3.1 × 10¹⁵ G) agree with both lower and higher observed QPO frequencies.
- GME mode frequencies for SGR 1900+14 (using 1.3–1.4 M⊙ stars at B = 1.34 × 10¹⁵ G) agree with observed QPOs.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of magnetar oscillations (Sotani et al., 2006, 2007, 2008, 2011; Gabler et al., 2012) by incorporating for the first time the effect of strong magnetic fields on the crustal composition and shear modulus, building on the authors' prior work on magnetised crusts (Nandi et al., 2011a, 2011b). The results enable more realistic comparisons with observed quasi-periodic oscillations from SGR 1806-20 and SGR 1900+14, and show that nuclear physics inputs (particularly the symmetry energy and its slope) affect overtone frequencies, which could help constrain the equation of state when neutron star masses are known accurately.
