# Equation of state for QCD with a critical point from the 3D Ising Model

> Current knowledge of the finite-density QCD equation of state from first principles is limited to a Taylor expansion in the baryonic chemical potential around $μ_B=0$. By means of a scaling form for the equation of state of the 3D Ising model and a non-universal, parametrized map to QCD coordinates, we construct a family of equations of state matching state of the art first principle Lattice QCD calculations and including the correct critical behavior, which can be readily employed in hydrodynamical simulations of heavy ion collisions at finite density, covering most of the BES range at RHIC. This contribution reports on work done within the Fluctuations/Equation of State working group of the BEST Collaboration.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a family of QCD equations of state that match lattice QCD results at zero baryon chemical potential while incorporating critical behavior from the 3D Ising model universality class, but only for a specific parametrized choice of the critical point location and mapping parameters (e.g., μ_BC = 350 MeV, T_C ≈ 143.2 MeV) rather than a unique prediction.

## Method
The authors implement the scaling equation of state of the 3D Ising model using a parametric representation for magnetization, magnetic field, and reduced temperature. They define a non-universal linear map with six parameters to transform Ising variables (h, r) into QCD coordinates (T, μ_B), then decompose lattice QCD Taylor coefficients into an "Ising" contribution from the critical point and a "Non-Ising" background. The full pressure is reconstructed by summing the Non-Ising Taylor expansion and the Ising critical contribution.

## Key numbers
- Critical point location chosen as μ_BC = 350 MeV and T_C ≈ 143.2 MeV for the example parameter set
- Mapping angles: α_1 ≈ 3.85°, α_2 ≈ 93.85°
- Global rescaling parameter w = 1, relative rescaling ρ = 2
- 3D Ising model parameters: a = -0.76201, b = 0.00804 for the function h̃(θ)
- θ_0 ≈ 1.154, the first non-trivial zero of h̃(θ)
- Lattice QCD transition temperature at μ_B = 0: T ≃ 155 MeV

## Context
This work extends the approach of Rajagopal (1992) showing the QCD critical point belongs to the 3D Ising universality class, and builds on parametric representations from Guida (1996) and Nonaka (2004). It uses lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2010) and Bellwied (2015) for Taylor coefficients at zero chemical potential, combined with Hadron Resonance Gas model calculations from Alba (2017). The resulting equations of state are designed for use in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies, enabling searches for the critical point in experimental data.
