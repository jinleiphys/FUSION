# Deep-inelastic and quasielastic electron scattering from $A=3$ nuclei

> We perform a combined analysis of inclusive electron scattering data from $A=3$ nuclei in the deep-inelastic and quasielastic scattering regions, using Monte Carlo analysis methods and the nuclear weak binding approximation to establish the range over which the data can be described within the same theoretical framework. Comparison with quasielastic $^3$He cross sections from SLAC and Jefferson Lab suggests that most features of the $x \gtrsim 1$ data can be reasonably well described in the impulse approximation with finite-$Q^2$ nuclear smearing functions for momentum transfers $Q^2 \gtrsim 1$ GeV$^2$. For the DIS region, we analyze the recent $^3$He to deuterium cross section ratio from the Jefferson Lab E03-103 experiment to explore the possible isospin dependence of the nuclear effects. We discuss the implications of this for the MARATHON experiment at Jefferson Lab, and outline how a Bayesian analysis of $^3$He, $^3$H and deuterium data can robustly determine the free neutron structure function.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that inclusive electron scattering data from $A=3$ nuclei in both the deep-inelastic and quasielastic regions can be described within the same theoretical framework (the weak binding approximation) for momentum transfers $Q^2 \gtrsim 1$ GeV$^2$, but only if one allows for a possible isospin dependence of nucleon off-shell corrections, which previous analyses had assumed to be isospin-independent.

## Method

The authors performed a combined Monte Carlo analysis of inclusive electron scattering data from $^3$He and $^3$H nuclei using the nuclear weak binding approximation, where nuclear structure functions are expressed as convolutions of nucleon momentum distributions (smearing functions) with off-shell nucleon structure functions. They computed on-shell and off-shell smearing functions from the KPSV and SS spectral functions for $A=3$ nuclei, and compared the resulting quasielastic cross sections with data from SLAC and Jefferson Lab at $x \gtrsim 1$, while also fitting the Jefferson Lab E03-103 data on the $^3$He to deuterium cross section ratio to extract the isovector component of nucleon off-shell contributions.

## Key numbers

- Average nucleon off-shell energy $\langle \eps \rangle$ in $^3$He: $-64.28$ MeV (KPSV model) and $-53.66$ MeV (SS model); average kinetic energy $\langle \bm{p}^2 \rangle/2M$: $48.85$ MeV (KPSV) and $38.45$ MeV (SS)
- Average nucleon off-shell energy $\langle \eps \rangle$ in $^3$H: $-66.56$ MeV (KPSV) and $-55.94$ MeV (SS); average kinetic energy $\langle \bm{p}^2 \rangle/2M$: $48.84$ MeV (KPSV) and $38.44$ MeV (SS)
- For $\gamma=1$, the off-shell smearing functions $\widetilde{f}_{22}^N$ have magnitude approximately $3\%$ of their on-shell counterparts at the peak $y \sim 1$ for the proton and approximately $5\%$ for the neutron
- The deuteron bound state component amounts to around $2/3$ of the strength of the proton on-shell smearing function, with the continuum contribution accounting for approximately $1/3$
- The SS model on-shell smearing function has an approximately $5\%$ higher peak around $y \approx 1$ compared to KPSV, while the off-shell function is approximately $5\%$ lower

## Context

This work extends the weak binding approximation framework previously applied to deuterium by Kulagin and Petti (2006) and the CJ15 global QCD analysis to $A=3$ nuclei, specifically addressing the isospin dependence of nucleon off-shell effects that had been left open by Sargsian (2002). The analysis uses the Jefferson Lab E03-103 data from Seely (2009) on the $^3$He to deuterium cross section ratio to constrain the isovector off-shell corrections, and the results directly inform the MARATHON experiment at Jefferson Lab, which aims to extract the free neutron structure function from $^3$He and $^3$H data. The work enables a Bayesian analysis combining $^3$He, $^3$H, and deuterium data to robustly determine the $d/u$ parton distribution ratio at large $x$.
