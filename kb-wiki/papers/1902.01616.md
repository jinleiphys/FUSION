# TDHF Theory and Its Extensions for the Multinucleon Transfer Reaction: a Mini Review

> Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory has been a powerful tool in describing a variety of complex nuclear dynamics microscopically without empirical parameters. In this contribution, recent advances in nuclear dynamics studies with TDHF and its extensions are briefly reviewed, along the line with the study of multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions. The latter lies at the core of this Research Topic, whose application for production of extremely neutron-rich nuclei has been extensively discussed in recent years. Having in mind the ongoing theoretical developments, it is envisaged how microscopic theories may contribute to the future MNT study.

# TDHF Theory and Its Extensions for the Multinucleon Transfer Reaction: a Mini Review

## Key claim

Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory, despite its neglect of two-body correlations and its deterministic nature, can describe the main reaction channels in multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions, including peripheral transfer, deep-inelastic, and quasifission processes, but the description is still not perfect, especially for channels where both neutrons and protons are removed, and improvements require inclusion of one-body fluctuations or pairing effects.

## Method

The paper reviews TDHF theory, where the nuclear wave function is a Slater determinant for all times and the Pauli principle is automatically ensured, with the time evolution governed by the single-particle Hamiltonian derived from an energy density functional. For MNT reactions, transfer probabilities are deduced from the TDHF wave function after collision using the particle-number projection technique, and secondary deexcitation effects are included by coupling TDHF with the statistical compound-nucleus deexcitation model GEMINI++ (the TDHF+GEMINI method). Extended approaches discussed include the time-dependent RPA (TDRPA) from the Balian-Venéroni variational principle, stochastic mean-field (SMF) theory, and time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) for pairing effects.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction at $E_{\rm c.m.}\approx 307$ MeV, TDHF calculations were performed and compared with experimental data for various proton transfer channels, with cross sections shown as a function of mass number of reaction products.
- In peripheral collisions of $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U, a specific impact parameter of $b = 5.5$ fm was used to illustrate MNT processes, with elapsed time indicated in zeptoseconds (1 zs = $10^{-21}$ sec).
- For quasifission in the same system, a smaller impact parameter of $b = 2$ fm was used to show damped collisions.
- In the $^{232}$Th+$^{250}$Cf reaction, a "tip-on-side" orientation resulted in inverse quasifission where about 15 nucleons in the tip of $^{232}$Th were transferred to the side of $^{250}$Cf.
- In the $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn reaction at energies well above the Coulomb barrier, tip collisions resulted in transfer of around 27 nucleons from $^{124}$Sn to $^{238}$U.
- The charge product threshold for neck breaking dynamics was identified as $Z_{\rm P}Z_{\rm T} \gtrsim 1600$.

## Context

This mini review extends the line of work initiated by Zagrebaev and Greiner (2005-2015), who emphasized the importance of shell effects in MNT processes and proposed the concept of inverse quasifission. The paper situates TDHF among other theoretical approaches including the semi-classical GRAZING model, the dinuclear system (DNS) model, the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model, and the Langevin model, noting that TDHF's non-empirical nature allows exploration of optimal reaction mechanisms for producing neutron-rich nuclei. The review enables future work combining microscopic approaches to determine phenomenological parameters in other models, and points to ongoing developments in including pairing dynamics via TDSLDA and TDHFB codes that have just begun to reveal effects of the relative phase of colliding superfluid nuclei on reaction outcomes.
