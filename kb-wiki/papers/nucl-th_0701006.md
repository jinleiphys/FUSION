# Spin 1/2 Fermions in the Unitary Limit

> The energy shift due to the interaction of two particles in a large box is proportional to the free particle scattering phase-shift. This provides an approximation to the effective interaction referred to as the phase-shift approximation. For a many-body fermion system this effective interaction has to be corrected for the Pauli-blocking. It is used to calculate the energy of a spin 1/2 fermion system as a function of the two-body scattering length and effective range. In the unitary limit with scattering length going to infinity and effective range going to zero the energy is 0.540 (in the units of the non-interactiong fermi gas energy) with a pp-ladder summation. Including hh-ladders the energy is 0.570. A smooth crossover from the BCS to the BEC region is observed. Pairing is not included.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the energy of a zero-temperature spin-1/2 fermion system in the unitary limit (infinite scattering length, zero effective range) using a pp-ladder approximation with Pauli blocking but neglecting mean-field dispersion corrections (effective mass m* = 1), finding a universal constant ξ = 0.24 that is substantially smaller than most other theoretical results.

## Method

A rank-1 separable two-body interaction was constructed by inverse scattering from the free-space phase shift δ(k) = π/2, with a momentum cut-off Λ. The in-medium effective interaction was calculated in a pp-ladder (Brueckner) approximation using a regularized G-matrix equation that avoids the cut-off dependence by exploiting the fact that the Pauli operator Q → 1 for momenta above 2k_f. The potential energy per particle was obtained by integrating the momentum-dependent G-matrix over the Fermi sea using eq. (PE), with the analytical expressions for the integrals I_GK(a,y) and I_hh(a,y) given in eqs. (Ipp) and (Ihh).

## Key numbers

- ξ = 0.24 for the total energy in units of the non-interacting Fermi gas energy, using pp-ladder summation only (independent of density and cut-off Λ for Λ > ~3k_f)
- ξ = 4/9 when using only the zero relative momentum component I(0,0) = -2k_f/(4π²) of the effective interaction
- ξ = 0.515 when allowing center-of-mass momentum dependence but using I(0,y) only
- ξ = 0.4 ↔ 0.6 when including hh-ladders (with the caveat that this estimate is not reliable due to pairing instability)
- With m* = 0.9, ξ → 1 as Λ → 10³k_f, showing strong cut-off dependence when effective mass deviates from unity

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner G-matrix approach to the unitary limit problem originally posed by Bertsch (1999), using a separable interaction method previously applied to nuclear matter by Kwon (1995) and Kohler (2004, 2006). The result ξ = 0.24 is substantially lower than the Monte Carlo result ξ = 0.44 ± 0.01 from Carlson et al. (2003) and the lattice result ξ = 0.25(3) from Lee (2006). The paper notes that the analytical expression for the effective interaction agrees with that of Steele (2000) using effective field theory, but the full momentum integration yields a correction from the commonly quoted ξ = 4/9. The author states that a reliable treatment including hh-ladders would require a Green's function calculation, to be presented in a forthcoming report.
