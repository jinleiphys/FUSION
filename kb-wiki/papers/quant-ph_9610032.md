# Scattering Wave Functions at Bound State Poles

> The normalisation relation between the bound and scattering S-state wave functions, extrapolated to the bound state pole, is derived from the Schroedinger equation. It is shown that, unlike previous work, the result does not depend on the details of the potential through the corresponding Jost function but is given uniquely in terms of the binding energy. The generalisations to higher partial waves and one-dimensional scattering are given.

# Scattering Wave Functions at Bound State Poles

## Key claim

The normalization relation between bound and scattering S-state wave functions at the bound state pole is uniquely determined by the binding energy alone, contrary to previous work by Goldberger and Watson (1964) and Joachain (1975) which suggested dependence on the potential through the Jost function. The result holds for all bound state poles and does not depend on the form of the potential.

## Method

The authors start from the radial Schrödinger equation for a particle of mass m in a real spherically symmetric potential V(r) satisfying an integrability condition. They derive the relation by manipulating the Schrödinger equations for bound and scattering states, integrating the resulting expression, and taking the limit as the scattering wave function is analytically continued to the bound state pole at k = iα. The derivation uses the S-matrix parametrization near an isolated bound state pole and the asymptotic behavior of Jost solutions.

## Key numbers

- The principal result (eq. 2.25): lim_{k→iα} {√[2α(α²+k²)] v(k,r)} = -u_α(r), where u_α(r) is the normalized bound state wave function and α is related to the binding energy by E_B = -α²/2m
- For the S-wave Paris neutron-proton triplet potential, the approximation is reliable out to radii of 1.7 fm for center-of-mass energies up to at least 20 MeV
- For a one-term separable potential with shape parameter β, correction terms to eq. (2.25) at r=0 are small providing k² and α² are small compared to β²

## Context

This work extends the standard quantum mechanical result (Schiff, 1968) that a scattering wave function extrapolated to negative energy is proportional to the bound state wave function, but provides the correct normalization factor. It corrects the formal expression given by Goldberger and Watson (1964) and Joachain (1975), which depended on the Jost function and its derivative. The result enables practical approximations for scattering wave functions in terms of bound state wave functions, as demonstrated by Watson (1952) for final state interaction effects in pp→π⁺(pn) and subsequently applied to pp→π⁺(pn) near threshold, pn→η(pn), pd→π⁰(pd), and dp→p(pn) at high momentum transfers.
