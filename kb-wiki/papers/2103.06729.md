# Search for a viable nucleus-nucleus potential for heavy-ion nuclear reactions

> We have constructed an empirical formulae for the fusion and interaction barriers using experimental values available till date. The fusion barriers so obtained have been compared with different model predictions based on the proximity, Woods-Saxon and double folding potentials along with several empirical formulas, time dependent Hartree-Fock theories, and the experimental results. The comparison allows us to find the best model, which is nothing but the present empirical formula only. Most remarkably, the fusion barrier and radius show excellent consonance with the experimental findings for the reactions meant for synthesis of the superheavy elements also. Furthermore, it is seen that substitution of the predicted fusion barrier and radius in classic Wong formula [C. Wong, Phys. Rev. Lett. {31}, 766 (1973)] for the total fusion cross sections satisfies very well with the experiments. Similarly, current interaction barrier predictions have also been compared well with a few experimental results available and Bass potential model meant for the interaction barrier predictions. Importantly, the present formulae for the fusion as well as interaction barrier will have practical implications in carrying out the physics research near the Coulomb barrier energies. Furthermore, present fusion barrier and radius provide us a good nucleus-nucleus potential useful for numerous theoretical applications.

# Search for a viable nucleus-nucleus potential for heavy-ion nuclear reactions

## Key claim
The authors construct empirical polynomial formulas for fusion and interaction barriers from experimental data and claim these formulas provide the best agreement with measured barriers compared to existing models (proximity, Woods-Saxon, double folding, time-dependent Hartree-Fock, and other empirical formulas), but the claim is hedged by the fact that the empirical formulas are fitted to the same experimental data they are then tested against, and the comparison is limited to systems with Coulomb parameter z between 8 and 286.

## Method
The authors collected experimental fusion barrier heights from fusion excitation function measurements and interaction barrier heights from quasi-elastic scattering measurements for a range of heavy-ion systems. They plotted these barriers against the Coulomb interaction parameter z = Z_p Z_t / (A_p^{1/3} + A_t^{1/3}) and fitted the data with polynomial functions: a sixth-degree polynomial for fusion barriers (split into two z-ranges) and a non-linear function for interaction barriers. The fusion barrier radius was obtained from the reduced fusion barrier position S_B, defined as the separation between half-density surfaces, also fitted as a polynomial in z. The empirical predictions were then compared with the Bass model, Christensen-Winther (CW) model, Broglia-Winther (BW) model, Aage Winther (AW) model, Siwek-Wilczyńska and Wilczyński (SW) model, Skyrme energy density function (SEDF) model, Sao Paulo optical potential (SPP), and the Moustabchir-Royer (MR) formula.

## Key numbers
- Fusion barrier formula for 8 ≤ z ≤ 128: B_fu^(1) = -1.2725 + 0.9106z + 5.6932×10^{-4}z^2 + 2.335×10^{-5}z^3 - 4.4975×10^{-7}z^4 + 2.7836×10^{-9}z^5 - 5.2482×10^{-12}z^6 (MeV)
- Fusion barrier formula for 128 ≤ z ≤ 286: B_fu^(2) = -34488.7618 + 1100.6666z - 14.4066z^2 + 9.9275×10^{-2}z^3 - 3.7959×10^{-4}z^4 + 7.6357×10^{-7}z^5 - 6.3136×10^{-10}z^6 (MeV)
- Interaction barrier formula for 59 ≤ z ≤ 313: B_int = 0.09295z^{3/2} - 2.1601z + 35.5879z^{1/2} - 132.8943 (MeV)
- Fusion barrier radius obtained from S_B = -46.089 + 2.0478z - 3.0962×10^{-2}z^2 + 1.9278×10^{-4}z^3 - 4.2492×10^{-7}z^4 (fm), with R_B = S_B + C_1 + C_2
- Present empirical formula predicts fusion barriers within ±4% for the tested range
- For superheavy element reactions, mean square errors: present formula 2.26, WJS formula 4.42, W.D. Myers model 4.98, H.C. Manjunatha formula 5.72
- Bass model underestimates fusion barriers for z ≤ 60 and overestimates for z ≥ 60
- SPP data shows underestimation up to z=40 and overestimation up to 10% for z>40
- Fusion data available for 8 ≤ z ≤ 286; QEL data for 59 ≤ z ≤ 313

## Context
This work extends the empirical approach of Swiatecki (2005), who constructed a third-degree polynomial for fusion barriers (B_WJS = 0.85247z + 0.001361z^2 - 0.00000223z^3 MeV), by using a sixth-degree polynomial and separately treating fusion and interaction barriers. The authors distinguish between fusion barriers (for compound nucleus formation) and interaction barriers (for quasi-elastic scattering), a distinction introduced by Bass (1973, 1974) but often overlooked in later work such as Mitsuoka (2007) and Dutt (2010). The empirical formulas are intended to resolve an anomaly in the work of Sharma and Nandi (2017), where resonance-like structures in elastic x-ray measurements appeared near the fusion barrier rather than the expected interaction barrier. The formulas enable practical prediction of barriers for superheavy element synthesis and for physics research near Coulomb barrier energies, with the authors noting that for very light systems (0 < z ≤ 8) relevant to nuclear astrophysics, the Broglia-Winther model remains preferable.
