# Testing the Chiral Magnetic Effect with Central U+U collisions

> A quark interaction with topologically nontrivial gluonic fields, instantons and sphalerons, violates ¶~ and \CP~ symmetry. In the strong magnetic field of a noncentral nuclear collision such interactions lead to the charge separation along the magnetic field, the so-called chiral magnetic effect (CME). Recent results from the STAR collaboration on charge dependent correlations are consistent with theoretical expectations for CME but may have contributions from other effects, which prevents definitive interpretation of the data. Here I propose to use central body-body $U+U$ collisions to disentangle correlations due to CME from possible background correlations due to elliptic flow. Further more quantitative studies can be performed with collision of isobaric beams.

## Key claim

Central body-body U+U collisions can disentangle the chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal from background correlations due to elliptic flow, because such collisions produce strong elliptic flow with almost no magnetic field, whereas in noncentral Au+Au collisions both effects are strongly correlated.

## Method

The author performed Glauber Monte-Carlo simulations to estimate the magnetic field (using the Lienard-Wiechert potential approach of Skokov (2009)) and elliptic flow (assuming v2 scales with participant eccentricity, with κ=0.2 from experimental measurements) for central Au+Au and U+U collisions at √sNN=200 GeV. Events were selected based on spectator number (Nsp<20) and the magnitude of the flow vector q, and the dependence of elliptic flow and magnetic field on charged multiplicity was compared between the two collision systems.

## Key numbers

- The average elliptic flow is almost a factor of 2 larger in U+U collisions compared to Au+Au collisions in event samples with Nsp<20
- The condition Nsp<20 selects about 1.5% of the most central events in U+U collisions and about 2.3% in Au+Au collisions
- The magnetic field at t=0 at √sNN=200 GeV is about a factor of 2 smaller compared to the maximum value reached at approximately t≈0.05 fm/c
- The magnetic field in noncentral collisions is approximately 10^15 T
- For isobaric nuclei 96_44Ru and 96_40Zr, the magnetic field would be proportional to the nuclei charge and can vary by more than 10%, which could result in 20% variation in the CME signal

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Kharzeev (2004, 2007) for observing the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions and addresses the ambiguity in STAR results (2009) where background correlations from elliptic flow could mimic the CME signal. The use of deformed uranium nuclei was first proposed by Braun-Munzinger for achieving higher energy density at RHIC, and later elaborated by Shuryak (1999) and Kuhlman (2005) for studying elliptic flow. The paper enables experimental tests that can distinguish CME from background by comparing central U+U collisions (where geometry can be controlled) and by using isobaric collisions of 96_44Ru and 96_40Zr, which would have similar particle production but different magnetic field strengths.
