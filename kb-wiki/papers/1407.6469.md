# Deconfinement transition in SU(N) theories from perturbation theory

> We consider a simple massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge for SU($N$) Yang-Mills theory. We compute the corresponding one-loop effective potential for a temporal background gluon field at finite temperature. At this order the background field is simply related to the Polyakov loop, the order parameter of the deconfinement transition. Our perturbative calculation correctly describes a quark confining phase at low temperature and a phase transition of second order for $N=2$ and weakly first order for $N=3$. Our estimates for the transition temperatures are in qualitative agreement with values from lattice simulations or from other continuum approaches. Finally, we discuss the effective gluon mass parameter in relation to the Gribov ambiguities of the Landau-DeWitt gauge.

## Key claim

A simple massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge, with a gluon mass parameter treated as an effective infrared regulator, can reproduce the main features of the deconfinement transition in SU(N) Yang-Mills theory at one-loop order, despite the transition being conventionally considered intrinsically nonperturbative.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop effective potential for a constant temporal background gluon field in a massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge for SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature. The background field potential is evaluated by summing Matsubara frequencies for massive gluon and massless ghost loops in the presence of the background field, using projectors to decompose the color structure. The minimum of this potential as a function of temperature determines the Polyakov loop and the nature of the phase transition.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2) in d=4, the critical reduced mass is \(\tilde m_c \simeq 2.97\), corresponding to a second-order transition.
- For SU(2) in d=3, the critical reduced mass is \(\tilde m_c \simeq 1.72\).
- Using a tree-level fit to vacuum lattice data in the Landau gauge gives a gluon mass \(m = 710\) MeV, yielding a transition temperature \(T_c \simeq 238\) MeV for SU(2).
- For comparison, a typical lattice value for the SU(2) transition temperature is \(T_c = 295\) MeV (Lucini 2012), and a recent FRG value is \(T_c = 230\) MeV (Fister 2013).

## Context

This work extends the massive Landau-gauge approach of Tissier and Wschebor (2010, 2011), which successfully described vacuum gluon and ghost propagators, to finite temperature and the deconfinement transition using the background field formalism. It parallels the functional renormalization group studies of Braun (2007) and Fister (2013) in the Landau-DeWitt gauge, reproducing their qualitative results for the order of the transition (second order for N=2, first order for N=3) at one-loop order. The calculation demonstrates that a simple massive extension can serve as a systematically improvable perturbative starting point for studying center-symmetry restoration, in contrast to standard perturbation theory which fails at transition temperatures.
