# Extracting a free neutron structure function from proton and deuteron deep inelastic scattering data

> Due to the lack of a free neutron target the structure function of the neutron cannot be measured directly and is therefore extracted from deuteron and proton DIS data. Because the deuteron is a bound nuclear system, in order to extract the neutron structure function, one needs to apply model dependent theoretical corrections which dominate the uncertainty at the large xB region. We present here a correlation between the magnitude of the EMC effect and the amount of two nucleon Short Range Correlation (2N-SRC) pairs in nuclei. Using this correlation we propose a phenomenological procedure to extract the free neutron structure function in the xB range of 0.3 to 0.7.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a phenomenological procedure to extract the free neutron structure function from deuteron and proton deep inelastic scattering data, using a linear correlation between the magnitude of the EMC effect and the amount of two-nucleon short-range correlation (2N-SRC) pairs in nuclei, without relying on model-dependent theoretical nuclear corrections.

## Method

The authors used the observed linear correlation between the EMC slope (dR_EMC/dxB) and the SRC scaling factor a_2N(A/d) for various nuclei, as reported in prior work. They extrapolated this correlation to the limit a_2N(A/d)=0, which corresponds to a free np pair with no short-range correlations, to obtain a phenomenological correction factor for the deuteron-to-free-np DIS cross section ratio. This correction was then applied to world data on the deuteron and proton structure function ratio F2_d/F2_p to extract the free neutron structure function F2_n in the Bjorken xB range of 0.3 to 0.7.

## Key numbers

- The intercept of the EMC slope versus SRC scale factor correlation at a_2N(A/d)=0 is |dR_EMC/dxB| = 0.079 ± 0.006.
- The average value of xB where the EMC ratio is unity is b = 0.31 ± 0.04.
- The phenomenological correction is applied in the xB range of 0.3 to 0.7.
- The correction parameter a = 0.079 ± 0.006 is used in the equation sigma_d/(sigma_n + sigma_p) = 1 - a(xB - b).

## Context

This work extends the finding of a linear correlation between the EMC effect and SRC scaling factors reported in a recent study (Weinstein et al., 2011). It provides an alternative to traditional extraction methods that rely on model-dependent nuclear corrections such as Fermi motion, binding, relativistic, and off-shell effects, which dominate uncertainties at large xB. The extracted neutron structure function enables improved determination of the d/u parton distribution function ratio at large xB, and the authors plan to examine this effect in collaboration with the CTEQ group.
