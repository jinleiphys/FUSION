# $ρ$-meson properties at finite nuclear density

> We calculate the momentum dependence of the $ρ$-meson selfenergy based on the dispersion relation for the $ρN$ scattering amplitude $f(ω$) at low nuclear density. The imaginary part of $f(ω)$ is determined from the optical theorem, while the total $ρN$ cross section is obtained within the VDM at high energy and within the resonance model at low energy. Our numerical results indicate a sizeable broadening of the $ρ$-meson width in the medium especially for low relative momenta $p$ while the real part of the $ρ$ selfenergy is found to change its sign and becomes repulsive already at momenta above 100 MeV/c. Extrapolating to nuclear saturation density $ρ_0$ we find a dropping of the $ρ$-mass for $p \approx$ 0 in line with the QCD sumrule analysis of Hatsuda while at high energy an increase of the $ρ$-mass in line with the prediction by Eletsky and Joffe is obtained. However, when including a broadening of the baryonic resonances in the medium, the $ρ$-meson mass shift at $p \approx$ 0 becomes slightly repulsive whereas the width increases substantially.

# ρ-meson properties at finite nuclear density

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of the ρ-meson selfenergy is calculated using a dispersive approach that combines a resonance model at low relative momenta with the Vector Dominance Model (VDM) at high momenta; the low-density approximation predicts a sign change in the real part of the selfenergy from attractive to repulsive above about 100 MeV/c, but when in-medium broadening of baryonic resonances is included at saturation density, the mass shift at zero momentum becomes slightly repulsive rather than attractive.

## Method

The imaginary part of the forward ρN scattering amplitude f(ω) was obtained from the total ρN cross section via the optical theorem, with the cross section computed from the resonance model (saturating with baryonic resonances coupling to the ρ channel) at low energies and from the VDM using ρ-photoproduction data at high energies. The real part of f(ω) was then determined through a dispersion relation with a subtraction at ω₀ = 4.46 GeV, where the subtraction constant was fixed from VDM analysis of ρ-photoproduction differential cross sections measured by DESY-MIT and the Daresbury group. The in-medium ρ-meson mass shift and width broadening were evaluated in the low-density approximation using the relations M_R*² = M_R² − 4π Re f(0) ρ_N and M_R* Γ_R* = M_R Γ_R + 4π Im f(0) ρ_N, and then extrapolated to nuclear saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- The real part of the forward ρN scattering amplitude changes sign at p_ρ ≈ 100 MeV/c, with an estimated model uncertainty of δp_ρ = ±30 MeV/c.
- At saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ and zero momentum, the ρ-mass shift from the low-density approximation agrees with the QCD sumrule result of Hatsuda.
- At high momenta, the ρ-mass shift is approximately 70 MeV at p_ρ = 2 GeV/c and approximately 120 MeV at p_ρ = 7 GeV/c (compared to 60 MeV and 90 MeV respectively from Eletsky and Ioffe).
- The ρ-meson vacuum mass is M_ρ = 770 MeV and its vacuum width is Γ_ρ⁰ = 150 MeV.
- The D₁₃(1520) resonance has a vacuum width Γ_R ≈ 120 MeV and an estimated in-medium width Γ_med ≈ 300 MeV; in-medium widths of all resonances coupled to the ρN channel were taken as twice the vacuum value for the saturation-density calculation.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach of Eletsky and Ioffe (1995) by using ρ-photoproduction data rather than Compton scattering amplitudes, and by incorporating a resonance model for low-energy ρN interactions. The results reconcile the QCD sumrule prediction of a dropping ρ-mass at zero momentum from Hatsuda (1993) with the repulsive mass shift at high momenta predicted by Eletsky and Ioffe, showing they arise from the strong momentum dependence of the ρN scattering amplitude. However, when in-medium broadening of baryonic resonances is included, the result shifts toward qualitative agreement with the microscopic spectral function calculations of Rapp, Chanfray and Wambach (1996) and Klingl et al. (1997), where the ρ-meson broadens significantly rather than dropping in mass at saturation density. The explicit momentum dependence of the selfenergy is intended for incorporation into transport theories analyzing dilepton data from heavy-ion collisions.
