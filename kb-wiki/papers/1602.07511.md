# Exact solution of equations for proton localization in neutron star matter

> The rigorous treatment of proton localization phenomenon in asymmetric nuclear matter is presented. The solution of proton wave function and neutron background distribution is found by the use of the extended Thomas-Fermi approach. The minimum of energy is obtained in the Wigner- Seitz approximation of spherically symmetric cell. The analysis of three different nuclear models suggests that the proton localization is likely to take place in the interior of neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact solution of the Lagrange-Euler equations for a single proton impurity in neutron matter, finding that proton localization occurs for all three nuclear models tested, with localization thresholds systematically lower (by about 10%) than those obtained from earlier approximate variational methods using Gaussian trial functions.

## Method

The authors treat a single proton quantum-mechanically via its wave function while describing neutrons with a local density distribution in the extended Thomas-Fermi approach, within a finite-size Wigner-Seitz cell. They solve the coupled Schrodinger equation for the proton and a nonlinear elliptic equation for the neutron density using a relaxation method, iterating until the proton eigenvalue converges to an accuracy of 10^-4. The calculation is performed for three nuclear models (AV14+UVII, SLy4, and UV14+TNI) across a range of densities relevant to neutron star interiors.

## Key numbers

- Localization threshold for AV14+UVII: 0.745 fm^-3 (mean baryon density), compared to 0.789 fm^-3 from the earlier approximate method
- Localization threshold for UV14+TNI: 0.608 fm^-3 (mean baryon density), compared to 0.731 fm^-3 from the earlier approximate method
- For SLy4 at mean density n = 1.214 fm^-3: energy difference ΔE/A = -10.81 MeV, proton eigenvalue E_p = -245.55 MeV, mean proton radius ⟨r_p⟩ = 0.459 fm
- For AV14+UVII at mean density n = 1.239 fm^-3: ΔE/A = -16.98 MeV, E_p = -550.70 MeV, ⟨r_p⟩ = 0.404 fm
- For UV14+TNI at mean density n = 1.069 fm^-3: ΔE/A = -1.08 MeV, E_p = -297.0 MeV, ⟨r_p⟩ = 0.500 fm
- Gradient coefficient B_N = 31.6 MeV fm^5, adopted from Kutschera (1990)

## Context

This work extends the variational approach of Kutschera (1990, 1993) by solving the full Lagrange-Euler equations exactly and abandoning the assumption of an infinitely large Wigner-Seitz cell, allowing treatment of higher proton fractions relevant to beta-equilibrated neutron star matter. The results confirm that proton localization is plausible in neutron star interiors across different nuclear models (AV14+UVII from Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988); SLy4 from Chabanat et al. (1998); UV14+TNI from Lagaris and Pandharipande (1981)), and enable further studies of proton crystallization (Kutschera, 1995), temperature effects on the localization threshold (Kubis and Wojcik, 2014), and implications for neutron star cooling (Baiko et al., 1999, 2000).
