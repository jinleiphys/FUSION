# Chemical equilibrium study at SPS 158A GeV

> A detailed study of chemical freeze-out in nucleus-nucleus collisions at beam energy 158A GeV is presented. By analyzing hadronic multiplicities within the statistical hadronization approach, the chemical equilibration of p-p, C-C, Si-Si and Pb-Pb systems is studied as a function of the number of participating nucleons in the system. Additionally, Two Component statistical hadronization model is applied to the data and is found to be able to explain the observed strangeness hadronic phase space under-saturation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that strangeness is under-saturated relative to full chemical equilibrium in all collision systems studied at SPS 158A GeV, with the strangeness suppression factor γS increasing monotonically with system size but remaining significantly below 1 even in Pb-Pb collisions. The Two Component statistical hadronization model can explain this observed under-saturation by attributing it to a superposition of an equilibrated fireball and single nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Method

The author analyzed hadronic multiplicities from NA49 collaboration data for p-p, C-C, Si-Si and Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV using the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) with a strangeness suppression factor γS as a free parameter. Small systems (net baryon number less than 10) were treated in the BSQ-canonical ensemble, intermediate systems (10-100 participants) with exact strangeness conservation only, and large systems grand-canonically. The Two Component model (SHM(TC)) was also applied, where hadron production is taken as a superposition of an equilibrated fireball with γS=1 and a component from single nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Key numbers

- γS = 0.445 ± 0.020 for p-p at 158A GeV
- γS = 0.575 ± 0.042 for C-C at 158A GeV
- γS = 0.664 ± 0.050 for Si-Si at 158A GeV
- Temperature T = 177.3 ± 5.2 MeV for p-p at 158A GeV
- Temperature T = 165.7 ± 4.1 MeV for C-C at 158A GeV
- Temperature T = 163.0 ± 4.7 MeV for Si-Si at 158A GeV
- Baryon chemical potential μB = 248.1 ± 12.5 MeV for C-C at 158A GeV
- Baryon chemical potential μB = 245.5 ± 11.0 MeV for Si-Si at 158A GeV
- Mean number of single NN collisions ⟨Nc⟩ = 6 ± 0.4 for C-C in SHM(TC)
- Mean number of single NN collisions ⟨Nc⟩ = 11.4 ± 1.8 for Si-Si in SHM(TC)
- χ²/dof = 13.0/7 for p-p in SHM(γS)
- χ²/dof = 3.4/4 for C-C in SHM(γS)
- χ²/dof = 1.0/4 for Si-Si in SHM(TC)

## Context

This work extends previous findings on strangeness under-saturation in heavy ion collisions at SPS energies, confirming results from earlier analyses of Pb-Pb collisions (Manninen et al., 2004) and system size studies (Becattini et al., 2001; Cleymans et al., 1999). The Two Component model, first introduced for Pb-Pb collisions (Manninen et al., 2004), is here applied to smaller systems C-C and Si-Si, showing that the chemical freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential are determined mostly by beam energy rather than system size, while strangeness equilibration depends strongly on the number of participants. This enables future studies of the interplay between initial beam energy and system size in determining chemical freeze-out conditions.
