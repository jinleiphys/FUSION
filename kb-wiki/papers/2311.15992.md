# Neutron stars in accreting systems -- signatures of the QCD phase transition

> Neutron stars (NS) that are born in binary systems with a main-sequence star companion can experience mass transfer, resulting in the accumulation of material at the surface of the NS. This, in turn, leads to the continuous growth of the NS mass and the associated steepening of the gravitational potential. Supposing the central density surpasses the onset for the phase transition from nuclear, generally hadronic matter to deconfined quark-gluon plasma, which is a quantity currently constrained solely from an upper limit by asymptotic freedom in quantum chromodynamics (QCD), the system may experience a dynamic response due to the appearance of additional degrees of freedom in the equation of state (EOS). This dynamical response might give rise to a rapid softening of the EOS during the transition in the hadron-quark matter co-existence region. While this phenomenon has long been studied in the context of hydrostatic configurations, the dynamical implications of this problem are still incompletely understood. It is the purpose of the present paper to simulate the dynamics of NSs with previously accreted envelopes caused by the presence of a first-order QCD phase transition. Therefore, we employed the neutrino radiation hydrodynamics treatment based on the fully general relativistic approach in spherical symmetry, implementing a three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport and a microscopic model EOS that contains a first-order hadron-quark phase transition. The associated neutrino signal shows a sudden rise in the neutrino fluxes and average energies, becoming observable for the present generation of neutrino detectors for a galactic event, and a gravitational wave mode analysis revealed the behaviors of the dominant $f$ mode and the first and the second gravity $g$ modes that are excited during the NS evolution across the QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper simulates the dynamical response of neutron stars with previously accreted envelopes to a first-order QCD phase transition, finding that the transition produces observable neutrino and gravitational wave signals, but only if the central density surpasses the onset density for the hadron-quark phase transition, which is currently constrained only by an upper limit from asymptotic freedom in QCD.

## Method

The authors employed the spherically symmetric general relativistic neutrino radiation hydrodynamics code AGILE-BOLTZTRAN, solving the three-flavor Boltzmann equation for neutrino transport with a comprehensive set of weak reactions. They constructed initial conditions from hydrostatic Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff solutions using the DD2F-RDF-1.1 hybrid equation of state, which implements a first-order hadron-quark phase transition, for a neutron star with total baryon mass of 1.781 solar masses and an accreted envelope of 0.018 solar masses. The gravitational wave mode analysis was performed using the GREAT code, which solves the eigenvalue problem of hydrodynamic and metric perturbations for spherically symmetric equilibrium models in general relativity.

## Key numbers

- Onset density for the hadron-quark phase transition: 8.8 x 10^14 g cm^-3 at entropy per baryon s = 0.5 k_B
- Onset mass for the phase transition: 1.582 solar masses for the DD2F-RDF-1.1 equation of state at s = 0.5 k_B
- Maximum mass of the DD2F-RDF-1.1 hybrid equation of state: 2.150 solar masses for s = 0.5 k_B and 2.155 solar masses for T = 0
- Central density of initial configurations: 8.5 x 10^14 g cm^-3 for both s = 0.1 k_B and s = 0.5 k_B
- Central temperatures of initial configurations: between about 3 and 16 MeV for entropies per baryon of s = 0.1 k_B and s = 0.5 k_B
- Diagnostic explosion energy: about 5 x 10^49 erg at around 30 ms of simulation time
- Total ejected mass: about 1 x 10^-3 solar masses over the simulation time
- Maximum matter velocities in ejecta: 1.5 x 10^5 km s^-1 at peaks, with escape velocities of 10-60% of the speed of light
- Neutrino luminosities after quark matter appearance: a few times 10^52 erg s^-1, with average energies of 15-25 MeV
- Neutrino luminosities before quark matter appearance: on the order of 10^49 erg s^-1 with average energies of a few MeV
- Radius of massive neutron stars around 2.0 solar masses: 11.1 km for the DD2F-RDF-1.1 equation of state
- Radius of intermediate-mass neutron stars around 1.5 solar masses: 12.4 km for the DD2F-RDF-1.1 equation of state

## Context

This work extends the hydrostatic study of Sotani (2023) on QCD phase transitions in neutron stars by performing dynamical neutrino radiation hydrodynamics simulations, and complements earlier hydrodynamical simulations of first-order QCD phase transitions in core-collapse supernovae by Fischer (2011, 2018) and binary neutron star mergers by Bauswein (2019) and Most (2019). The results enable predictions for multi-messenger signals from accreting neutron stars that undergo a QCD phase transition, with the neutrino signal being observable for current-generation neutrino detectors for a galactic event, and provide gravitational wave mode frequencies that could be compared with future detections to probe the high-density equation of state.
