# Virtual photon-photon scattering

> Based on analyticity, unitarity, and Lorentz invariance the contribution from hadronic vacuum polarization to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon is directly related to the cross section of e^+e^- --> hadrons. We review the main difficulties that impede such an approach for light-by-light scattering and identify the required ingredients from experiment. Amongst those, the most critical one is the scattering of two virtual photons into meson pairs. We analyze the analytic structure of the process gamma^* gamma^* --> pi pi and show that the usual Muskhelishvili-Omnes representation can be amended in such a way as to remain valid even in the presence of anomalous thresholds.

# Virtual photon-photon scattering

## Key claim
The usual Muskhelishvili-Omnes representation for the process gamma* gamma* -> pi pi can be amended to remain valid even when anomalous thresholds appear at large photon virtualities, provided an additional anomalous term is included to account for the distortion of the integration contour.

## Method
The authors analyze the analytic structure of the partial waves for gamma* gamma* -> pi pi, focusing on the S-wave pole-subtracted amplitude. They show that the integrand of the Muskhelishvili-Omnes representation coincides with the discontinuity of the scalar triangle loop function C0(s) up to a factor independent of the photon virtualities, allowing the analytic continuation to be performed by adding an anomalous piece that accounts for the migration of the anomalous branch point onto the first sheet.

## Key numbers
- The anomalous branch points are located at s_± = q1^2 + q2^2 - (q1^2 q2^2)/(2 M_pi^2) ± (1/(2 M_pi^2)) sqrt(q1^2(q1^2 - 4 M_pi^2) q2^2(q2^2 - 4 M_pi^2))
- The condition for anomalous thresholds to appear is q1^2 + q2^2 >= 4 M_pi^2
- The anomalous contribution to the partial wave f0(s; q1^2, q2^2) involves an integral over s_x = 4 M_pi^2 x + (1-x) s_+ with x from 0 to 1
- The discontinuity of the anomalous piece is disc_an f0(s; q1^2, q2^2) = -8 pi / sqrt(lambda(s, q1^2, q2^2)) * t0(s) / Omega0(s)

## Context
This work extends the dispersive approach to hadronic light-by-light scattering contributions to the muon g-2, building on prior work by Colangelo, Hoferichter, Procura, and Stoffer for the two-photon case (GM10, HPS) and by Moussallam (2013) for one off-shell photon. The authors show that the analytic continuation to two off-shell photons is possible despite anomalous thresholds, which enables a model-independent treatment of the most critical experimental input for the light-by-light scattering tensor: the process gamma* gamma* -> pi pi. This is a necessary step toward a fully data-driven determination of the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
