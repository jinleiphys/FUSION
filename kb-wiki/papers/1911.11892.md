# Parton Hadron Quantum Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) -- a Novel Microscopic N-Body Transport Approach for Heavy-Ion Dynamics and Hypernuclei Production

> We present the novel microscopic n-body dynamical transport approach PHQMD(Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics) for the description of particle production and cluster formation in heavy-ion reactions at relativistic energies. The PHQMD extends the established PHSD (Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics) transport approach by replacing the mean field by density dependent two body interactions in a similar way as in the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) models. This allows for the calculation of the time evolution of the n-body Wigner density and therefore for a dynamical description of clusters and hypernuclei formation. The clusters are identified with the MST ('Minimum Spanning Tree') or the SACA ('Simulated Annealing Cluster Algorithm') algorithm which - by regrouping the nucleons in single nucleons and noninteracting clusters - finds the most bound configuration of nucleons and clusters. The selected results on clusters and hypernuclei production from Ref. arXiv:1907.03860 are discussed in this contribution.

# Parton Hadron Quantum Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) – a Novel Microscopic N-Body Transport Approach for Heavy-Ion Dynamics and Hypernuclei Production

## Key claim

The PHQMD approach reproduces the experimentally observed "rise and fall" of intermediate mass cluster multiplicity in Au+Au collisions at 600 A MeV when using a hard equation of state, while a soft equation of state overpredicts the data at large Z_bound_2. The authors present this as a demonstration that the fragment pattern in semi-peripheral reactions can serve as an additional observable to determine the hadronic equation of state experimentally.

## Method

The authors extend the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach by replacing the mean field with density-dependent two-body interactions in the style of Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) models, enabling calculation of the time evolution of the n-body Wigner density. Clusters are identified using either the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithm, based on spatial correlations, or the Simulated Annealing Cluster Algorithm (SACA), which finds the most bound configuration of nucleons and clusters by regrouping nucleons into single nucleons and noninteracting clusters. Collisions among particles are treated in the same way as in PHSD.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at 600 A MeV: PHQMD with hard equation of state reproduces the experimentally observed "rise and fall" of cluster multiplicity for clusters with charge Z in [3,30] as a function of total bound charge Z_bound_2.
- Au+Au collisions at 4 A GeV: multiplicity of hyperclusters shown as a function of impact parameter, with light hypernuclei (A ≤ 4) formed mainly in central collisions and heavier hypernuclei (A ≥ 5) produced dominantly in mid-central collisions.
- Clusters identified by SACA are stable for times larger than 50 fm/c in the 600 A MeV Au+Au system.

## Context

This work extends the PHSD approach of Cassing (2009) and the QMD-type models of Aichelin (1991) and Marty (2012) by combining their collision integrals with density-dependent two-body potential interactions for dynamical cluster formation. It addresses limitations of coalescence models (Zhu, 2015; Feckova, 2016) and statistical methods (Botvina) which cannot describe how clusters form dynamically. The approach enables future studies of hypernuclei production mechanisms from low to ultrarelativistic energies, with an extended version called FRIGA (Fragment Recognition In General Application) under development to include symmetry and pairing energy as well as hyperon-nucleon interactions.
