# Puzzling $B(E2;0^+\rightarrow 2^+)$ strength in the proton dripline nucleus $^{36}$Ca

> Recent measurements of the $E2$ transition rate from the ground state to the first 2$^+$ excited state of the proton dripline nucleus $^{36}$Ca show an unusual pattern when compared to its isotopic neighbor $^{38}$Ca: despite having a higher $E_x(2_1^+)$ excitation energy, the $B(E2; 0^+_1\rightarrow 2^+_1)$ rate in $^{36}$Ca is larger. The question that naturally arises is to what extent this observation can be attributed to the unbound character of the $2^+_1$ state. To understand the influence of the continuum space on the low-energy properties of $^{36}$Ca, we carried out Gamow shell model calculations that can account for the continuum coupling effects associated with the occupation of unbound $fp$ shells. We found that in the threshold $2^+$ state, $^{36}$Ca is spatially diffused, which impacts the observed $B(E2)$ trend.

## Key claim

The abnormally large $B(E2; 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ transition strength in the proton-dripline nucleus $^{36}$Ca, which is larger than in its more bound neighbor $^{38}$Ca despite having a higher excitation energy, can be partly explained by continuum coupling effects that enhance proton $sd \rightarrow fp$ cross-shell excitations and produce a spatially diffuse $2^+_1$ state; however, the authors note that other effects, such as excitations out of the employed model space coupling to the giant quadrupole resonance, are essential for fully reproducing the experimental rates.

## Method

The authors performed Gamow shell model (GSM) calculations using a Berggren basis generated via the spherical Gamow-Hartree-Fock (GHF) approximation, starting from a chiral two-nucleon plus three-nucleon force (EM1.8/2.0). They constructed a valence-space effective Hamiltonian and effective electromagnetic transition operators using many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) within the $sd$-$pf$ model space, with $^{28}$Si as the core. Two low-energy constants of the chiral interaction were optimized using the POUNDerS derivative-free method with an Eigenvector Continuation emulator to reproduce the spectra and proton separation energies of $^{36,38}$Ca. For comparison, standard shell model (SM) calculations were performed using the same interaction but solved in a localized real-space Hartree-Fock basis without continuum.

## Key numbers

- The $2^+_1$ state of $^{36}$Ca has an excitation energy of 3.046(3) MeV, lying above the one-proton threshold at $S_p = 2.600(6)$ MeV and the two-proton threshold at $S_{2p} = 2.650(40)$ MeV.
- The optimized chiral low-energy constants were $\tilde{C}_{^1 S_{0}}^{np} = -0.122$ (in units $10^4$ GeV$^{-2}$) and $C_{^3 S_{1}- ^3 D_{1}} = 0.719$ (in units $10^4$ GeV$^{-4}$), corresponding to scaling factors $\alpha_1 = 0.83$ and $\alpha_2 = 0.87$.
- The MBPT-derived effective charges were $e_p \approx 1.05$ and $e_n \approx 0.25$, significantly lower than the phenomenological ZBM2 values of $e_p = 1.36$ and $e_n = 0.45$.
- In the GSM calculation, the $2^+_1$ state of $^{36}$Ca shows total occupation numbers of the $f_{7/2}$ and $p_{3/2}$ partial waves between 1.7 and 2, indicating two particles in the continuum.
- The inclusion of the continuum caused the $E2$ transition amplitudes for $p$ and $f$ waves in $^{36}$Ca to increase by over 50%, while in $^{38}$Ca only the $f$-wave amplitude increased by 37%.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of continuum-driven collectivization of near-threshold states (Ploszajczak 2020, Okolowicz 2020b) to electromagnetic transitions in proton-dripline nuclei, specifically addressing the experimental puzzle from Dronchi (2023) where $^{36}$Ca showed a surprisingly large $B(E2)$ strength. The results are consistent with a similar conclusion obtained in the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC) framework of Okolowicz (2024), though that work allowed only one proton to scatter into the non-resonant continuum. The findings enable future experimental investigations of the $Z=20$ shell closure weakening towards the proton dripline through mass measurements of proton-unbound vanadium isotopes and spectroscopic factor measurements for proton removal from $^{36,38}$Ca to $^{35,37}$K (Beck 2024).
