# Constraints on the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A from GW170817

> Continued observation of PSR J0737-3039, the double pulsar, is expected to yield a precise determination of its primary component's moment of inertia in the next few years. Since the moment of inertia depends sensitively on the neutron star's internal structure, such a measurement will constrain the equation of state of ultra-dense matter, which is believed to be universal. Independent equation-of-state constraints have already been established by the gravitational-wave measurement of the neutron-star tidal deformability in GW170817. Here, using well-known universal relations among neutron star observables, we translate the reported 90%-credible bounds on tidal deformability into a direct constraint, $I_{\star} = 1.15^{+0.38}_{-0.24} \times 10^{45} \text{ g cm}^2$, on the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A. Should a future astrophysical measurement of $I_{\star}$ disagree with this prediction, it could indicate a breakdown in the universality of the neutron-star equation of state.

## Key claim

Using universal relations between neutron-star observables, the 90%-credible tidal deformability bounds from GW170817 imply a moment of inertia for PSR J0737-3039A of \(I_{\star} = 1.15^{+0.38}_{-0.24} \times 10^{45} \text{ g cm}^2\); this prediction explicitly assumes that GW170817 was a binary neutron star merger and that the neutron-star equation of state is universal across systems.

## Method

The authors computed mass sequences of moment of inertia and tidal deformability for 53 candidate equations of state (relativistic mean-field and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models) by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, Hartle's slow-rotation equation, and the tidal perturbation equation. They then performed log-log polynomial fits to obtain the binary Love relation (mapping \(\Lambda_{1.4}\) to \(\Lambda_{\star}\) for a 1.338 \(M_\odot\) star) and the I-Love relation (mapping \(\Lambda\) to dimensionless moment of inertia \(\bar{I}\)). Applying these relations to the 90%-credible interval on \(\Lambda_{1.4}\) from the GW170817 analysis of LVC (2018) yielded the constraint on \(I_{\star}\), with the fit dispersion (maximum 3% for binary Love, 0.6% for I-Love) folded in as an error.

## Key numbers

- Inferred moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A: \(I_{\star} = 1.15^{+0.38}_{-0.24} \times 10^{45} \text{ g cm}^2\) at 90% confidence, derived from the GW170817 tidal deformability bounds of LVC (2018).
- Less restrictive upper bound from the initial GW170817 analysis (LVC 2017): \(I_{\star} \leq 1.67 \times 10^{45} \text{ g cm}^2\).
- Corresponding dimensionless moment of inertia: \(\bar{I} = 11.10^{+3.64}_{-2.28}\) at 90% confidence; upper bound \(\bar{I} \leq 16.08\) from the initial analysis.
- Inferred dimensionless spin of pulsar A: \(\chi = 0.020^{+0.007}_{-0.004}\) at 90% confidence (upper bound \(\chi \leq 0.029\) from the initial analysis); without the low-spin assumption on GW170817, \(\chi \leq 0.034\).
- Pulsar A mass: 1.338 \(M_\odot\); rotational frequency: 276.8 Hz.

## Context

This work extends the tidal deformability constraints from the gravitational-wave event GW170817 (LVC 2017, LVC 2018) to a direct prediction for the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A, the primary component of the only known double pulsar (Burgay 2003, Lyne 2004). It uses the I-Love relation of Yagi and Yunes (2013) and the binary Love relation of Yagi and Yunes (2016) to translate between observables in a model-independent way. The result enables a future test of equation-of-state universality: if a direct astrophysical measurement of \(I_{\star}\) (expected within a few years from pulsar timing, as described by Kramer and Wex 2009) disagrees with this prediction, it could indicate that neutron stars in different systems do not share the same internal structure. The method can also be applied to other systems such as the recently discovered double neutron star PSR J1946+2052 (Stovall 2018).
