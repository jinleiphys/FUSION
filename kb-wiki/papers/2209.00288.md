# Imprints of clustering in multiplicity fluctuations

> In this paper, we investigate the multiplicity fluctuations of charged particles observed in high-energy nuclear collisions and relate them to the size of hadronizing systems which happen during such processes. We use the average multiplicities $\langle N\rangle $ and variances $Var\left(N\right)$ of multiplicity distributions of charged particles produced in centrality selected collisions of relativistic heavy-ion nuclei to evaluate the dynamic variable $Ω$ and study its dependence on the size of colliding nuclei. We connect the observed system-size dependence of multiplicity fluctuations with the clustering phenomena and the finiteness of the hadronizing sources and the thermal bath.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the system-size dependence of charged-particle multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be attributed to nucleonic clustering at the initial stage of the collision, where the cluster size decreases from about 9 nucleons at SPS energies to about 1 nucleon at LHC energies, but the authors note that this interpretation relies on the assumption that the thermodynamic system is not in equilibrium with a heat bath, as indicated by deviations from a simple linear dependence of \(\Omega N_{part}\) on \(N_{part}\).

## Method

The authors analyzed published experimental data on charged-particle multiplicity distributions from NA49 (Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV), PHENIX (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4\) and 200 GeV), and ALICE (Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760\) GeV). They computed the acceptance-independent dynamic variance \(\Omega = Var(N)/\langle N \rangle^2 - 1/\langle N \rangle\) for each centrality bin and fitted its product with the number of participants \(N_{part}\) or number of binary collisions \(N_{coll}\) using power-law or polynomial functions. The cluster size \(N_c\) was extracted from the fit parameter \(a = 2 N_c \Omega'\), where \(\Omega'\) was obtained from the energy-dependent parametrization of the negative binomial distribution shape parameter \(k\) for proton-proton collisions.

## Key numbers

* For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2760\) GeV (ALICE), \(\Omega \sim N_{part}^{-1.5}\) and \(\Omega \sim \langle N_{coll} \rangle^{-1.0}\).
* The cluster size \(N_c\) extracted from the \(N_{part}\)-based analysis follows \(N_c = 0.9 + 70 (\sqrt{s_{NN}})^{-0.75}\), yielding values: \(N_c = 9.6\) at 17.3 GeV (NA49), \(N_c = 3.8\) at 62.4 GeV (PHENIX), \(N_c = 2.0\) at 200 GeV (PHENIX), and \(N_c = 1.25\) at 2760 GeV (ALICE).
* The cluster size \(N_c\) extracted from the \(N_{coll}\)-based analysis follows \(N_c = 1.626 + 60.59 (\sqrt{s_{NN}})^{-0.75}\), yielding values: \(N_c = 8.75\) at 17.3 GeV, \(N_c = 4.51\) at 62.4 GeV, \(N_c = 2.5\) at 200 GeV, and \(N_c = 1.92\) at 2760 GeV.
* The exponent \(\alpha\) in the scaling \(Var(N)/\langle N \rangle^2 \sim N_{part}^{-\alpha}\) is approximately 1.25, as previously observed by PHENIX and ALICE.

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by the PHENIX Collaboration (PHENIX-2) of a power-law scaling of multiplicity fluctuations with system size, and connects it to the concept of self-organized criticality and clustering in strongly-interacting matter, as previously discussed by Castorina (Castorina4) and Rybczynski (Mryb). The analysis uses the dynamic variance \(\Omega\), which was introduced by Gavin (GavinA) as an acceptance-independent fluctuation measure. The results suggest that detailed studies of clustering in multiplicity fluctuations could help determine whether the QCD critical point exists, as proposed by Stephanov (Stephanov1998, Stephanov1999) and others.
