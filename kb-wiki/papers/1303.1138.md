# Contributions to the event-by-event charge asymmetry dependence for the elliptic flow of pi^{+} and pi^{-} in heavy-ion collisions

> We discuss various contributions to the event-by-event charge-asymmetry dependence of $π^{+}$ and $π^{-}$ elliptic flow, recently measured by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC. It is shown that under general assumptions, the difference between $v_2^+$ and $v_2^-$ at a given fluctuating value of an asymmetry parameter, $A$, is a linear function of $A$, as observed in the preliminary data. We discuss two mechanisms that are qualitatively consistent with the experimental data and result in a signal of the correct order of magnitude. Our subsequent hydrodynamic calculations, assuming local charge conservation at freeze-out, yield a qualitative and partial quantitative understanding of the observed signal, so offering a detailed test of the hydrodynamic model in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The observed linear dependence of the difference between π⁺ and π⁻ elliptic flow on the event-by-event charge asymmetry parameter A can be explained by conventional hydrodynamic mechanisms (local charge conservation at freeze-out and the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow) rather than requiring exotic explanations such as the chiral magnetic wave; the hydrodynamic calculations yield a signal within a factor of 2 of the preliminary STAR data, not exact agreement.

## Method

The authors first derive a general relation showing that under the assumption of small asymmetry A, the difference ⟨v₂⁻⟩_A − ⟨v₂⁺⟩_A is necessarily linear in A whenever the elliptic flow of particles responsible for the asymmetry differs from that of particles giving A=0. They then construct a simplified Monte Carlo model where clusters decay into oppositely charged pion pairs, with the rapidity separation between the pair depending on the cluster transverse momentum, and with v₂ depending on pseudorapidity and p_t. Finally, they perform full 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic calculations with statistical emission at freeze-out including local charge conservation and resonance decays, generating 10⁶ events for 30–40% centrality Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The preliminary STAR data give r_exp ≈ 0.03 for the slope parameter in ⟨v₂⁻⟩_A − ⟨v₂⁺⟩_A = c + rA, measured in |η| < 1 for Au+Au mid-peripheral collisions at various energies.
- The simplified Monte Carlo model yields r ≈ 0.02, a factor of 1.5 smaller than the STAR data.
- The full 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic calculation gives r = 0.012 ± 0.004, about one half of the observed magnitude.
- The mechanism based on the pseudorapidity dependence of v₂ (Fig. 1) is responsible for approximately 35% of the signal in the simplified model.
- When v₂ is measured in |η| < 1.5 and A in |η| < 1, the slope drops to r ≈ 0.006; when v₂ is in |η| < 2, the result is lower by approximately a factor of 10.
- The simplified model uses an average cluster number ⟨N_c⟩ = 570, mean p_t of 0.7 GeV, and produces approximately 380 charged particles in |η| < 1, roughly matching 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at 30–40% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic understanding of charge-dependent correlations in heavy-ion collisions, specifically the charge balance function measurements described by Jeon (2001) and successfully modeled by Schlichting (2010) and Bozek (2012). It directly addresses the preliminary STAR data from Ke (2012) and Wang (2012) that had been interpreted as evidence for the chiral magnetic wave proposed by Kharzeev (2010) and Burnier (2011, 2012). The authors show that the same signal arises naturally from local charge conservation at freeze-out combined with the known pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow from PHOBOS (Alver, 2006), and that models without late local charge conservation (such as UrQMD and AMPT) fail to reproduce the effect. The work enables a detailed test of hydrodynamic models and predicts a similar but weaker signal for triangular flow v₃.
