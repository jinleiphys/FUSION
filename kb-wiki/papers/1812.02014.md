# Baryonic or quarkyonic matter?

> During the last years it has become possible to address the cold and dense regime of QCD directly for sufficiently heavy quarks, where combined strong coupling and hopping expansions are convergent and a 3d effective theory can be derived, which allows to control the sign problem either in simulations or by fully analytic calculations. In this contribution we review the effective theory and study the $N_c$-dependence of the nuclear liquid gas transition, as well as the equation of state of baryonic matter in the strong coupling limit. We find the transition to become more strongly first order with growing $N_c$, suggesting that in the large $N_c$ limit its critical endpoint moves to high temperatures to connect with the deconfinement transition. Furthermore, to leading and next-to-leading order in the strong coupling and hopping expansions, respectively, the pressure is found to scale as $p\sim N_c$. This suggests that baryonic and quarkyonic matter might be the same at nuclear densities. Further work is needed to see whether this result is stable under gauge corrections.

## Key claim

The authors find that, to leading and next-to-leading order in the strong coupling and hopping expansions respectively, the pressure scales as \(p\sim N_c\) after the baryon onset transition, suggesting that baryonic and quarkyonic matter might be the same at nuclear densities; however, they caution that further work is needed to see whether this result is stable under gauge corrections.

## Method

The authors derive a 3d effective lattice theory for QCD with heavy quarks by integrating over spatial gauge links and quark fields in a combined character and hopping expansion, then generalise the gauge integrations to arbitrary \(SU(N_c)\) groups using known results for Haar integrals over class functions. They evaluate the static determinant and the first hopping correction analytically for general \(N_c\) with \(N_f=2\) flavours, and extract the leading large-\(N_c\) behaviour of the pressure and baryon density both below and above the baryon onset transition.

## Key numbers

* The static determinant for \(N_f=2\) at general \(N_c\) is given by \(z_0 = 1 + \frac{1}{6}(h_1^{N_c}+h_1^{3 N_c})(N_c+3)(N_c+2)(N_c+1) + \frac{1}{12}h_1^{2 N_c}(N_c+3)(N_c+2)^2(N_c+1) + h_1^{4 N_c}\).
* For \(\mu>m\) (above onset), the leading-order pressure scales as \(a^4 p_0 \sim \frac{4 \log(h_1)}{N_{\tau}} N_c\) and the first correction as \(a^4 p_1 \sim -12 N_c\).
* For \(\mu>m\), the leading-order baryon density scales as \(a^3 n_0 \sim 4 N_c\) and the first correction as \(a^3 n_1 \sim -288 N_{\tau} \frac{N_c^5}{h_1^{N_c}}\).
* The binding energy per baryon at zero temperature is proportional to \(\kappa^2\): \(\epsilon = -\frac{4}{3}\frac{1}{a^3n_B}\left(\frac{6h_1^3+3h_1^6}{z_0}\right)^2\,\kappa^2+\ldots\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous effective lattice theory for heavy quarks (Fromm et al., 2013; Langelage et al., 2014) to general \(N_c\), and connects to large-\(N_c\) conjectures about a quarkyonic phase by McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The results suggest that the nuclear liquid-gas transition becomes more strongly first order with growing \(N_c\), with its critical endpoint moving to higher temperatures to eventually merge with the deconfinement transition, reproducing the rectangular phase diagram predicted by large-\(N_c\) arguments. The next necessary step is the inclusion of gauge corrections while keeping \(g^2 N_c\) fixed, to test whether the \(p\sim N_c\) scaling survives.
