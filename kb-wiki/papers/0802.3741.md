# Three-nucleon interactions: A frontier in nuclear structure

> Three-nucleon interactions are a frontier in understanding and predicting the structure of strongly-interacting matter in laboratory nuclei and in the cosmos. We present results and discuss the status of first calculations with microscopic three-nucleon interactions beyond light nuclei. This coherent effort is possible due to advances based on effective field theory and renormalization group methods in nuclear physics.

# Three-nucleon interactions: A frontier in nuclear structure

## Key claim
Three-nucleon (3N) interactions are required by renormalization and needed to break off the empirical Tjon line to accurately describe experimental binding energies of light nuclei, but their effects depend on the resolution scale and theoretical convention that enters all parts of the Hamiltonian.

## Method
The authors evolve nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials to lower momentum cutoffs using renormalization group (RG) methods, producing low-momentum interactions (V_lowk) that become universal for cutoffs below about 2 fm^-1. They then incorporate corresponding 3N interactions from chiral effective field theory (EFT) at N^2LO, fitting the D- and E-term couplings to the ^3H and ^4He binding energies for a range of cutoffs, and apply these interactions in no-core shell model (NCSM), coupled-cluster (CC), and Hartree-Fock plus second-order many-body calculations.

## Key numbers
- The breakdown scale for chiral EFT is roughly Λ_b ~ m_ρ, with pion mass m_π ≈ 140 MeV
- The low-energy constants from πN scattering are constrained as: c_1 = -0.9^{+0.2}_{-0.5}, c_3 = -4.7^{+1.2}_{-1.0}, c_4 = 3.5^{+0.5}_{-0.2} (all in GeV^-1)
- The extrapolated CCSD(T) binding energy of ^4He is -28.23 MeV, agreeing with the exact result -28.20(5) MeV
- For cutoffs Λ ≲ 2 fm^-1, 3N interactions become perturbative and their expectation values are natural, of order (Q/Λ_b)^3 ⟨V_lowk⟩
- Lower values for c_3 add approximately -2 MeV at saturation density in neutron matter
- Towards denser matter, 4N force contributions of E/A ~ 1 MeV would not be unreasonable

## Context
This work extends the program of low-momentum interactions (V_lowk) developed by Bogner, Kuo, and collaborators, and builds on chiral EFT formulations of nuclear forces by Weinberg, van Kolck, and others. The authors show that cutoff variation of NN-only results explains the empirical Tjon line (correlation of ^3H and ^4He binding energies), and that including 3N interactions is necessary to break off this line. The results enable systematic calculations in medium-mass nuclei via coupled-cluster methods, provide guidance for constructing a universal density functional for nuclei, and support the interpretation that phenomenological monopole shifts in shell model interactions are due to 3N contributions, linking understanding of the shell model and drip lines to 3N forces.
