# Two-proton events in the 17F(p,2p)16O reaction

> In a recent experimental study (Gomez del Campo et al, PRL 86, 43 (2001)) of the reaction 17F(p,2p)16O, two-proton events were measured from excitations near a 1-, E*=6.15 MeV state in 18Ne. We calculate by means of R-matrix theory the resonant two-proton production cross section and branching ratios. We conclude that it is unlikely that two-proton production via population of the 1- state is sufficient to explain the observed two-proton events. Alternative sources of such events are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the resonant two-proton production cross section via the 1⁻, E*=6.15 MeV state in ¹⁸Ne is insufficient to explain the observed two-proton events reported by Gomez del Campo (2001), estimating the resonant contribution at only 0.03–0.2 mb compared to the measured 1.5–4.0 mb.

## Method

The authors use R-matrix theory to calculate resonant two-proton production cross sections and branching ratios for the 1⁻ state in ¹⁸Ne populated via the ¹⁷F(p,2p)¹⁶O reaction. They estimate simultaneous two-proton emission widths using a formula from Grigorenko (2001) and compare these with a diproton model, varying the channel radius from 2.5 to 4.5 fm to assess theoretical uncertainties. They also estimate contributions from a nearby 2⁻ state and from nonresonant breakup of ¹⁷F on protons.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit for resonant two-proton production cross section via the 1⁻ state: 310 mb (from π/k² = 0.31 barn at resonance)
- Estimated two-proton production cross section through the 1⁻ resonance: 0.03–0.2 mb, much smaller than the measured value of 1.5–4.0 mb reported by Gomez del Campo (2001)
- Branching ratio Γ₂p/Γ for the 1⁻ state: (1.5–5)×10⁻⁴ for simultaneous emission (channel radius 2.5–4.5 fm) and (2–10)×10⁻⁴ for diproton model
- Estimated cross section for the 2⁻ state: σ₂p²⁻ ∼ 0.1–0.4 mb
- Total width of the 1⁻ state: Γ = 50 keV
- Ratio of Γ₁p(gs)/Γ₁p(1/2⁺) ∼ 2, giving Γ₁p(gs)/Γ ∼ 2/3

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of Gomez del Campo (2001) on the ¹⁷F(p,2p)¹⁶O reaction, where two-proton events were measured from excitations near the 1⁻ state in ¹⁸Ne. The authors contrast the situation with true two-proton decay as originally described by Goldansky (1960) and with democratic decay studied by Bochkarev (1984, 1992) and Danilin (1987), noting that the ¹⁸Ne case differs because one-proton decay channels to weakly bound states in ¹⁷F are open. The work enables future complete kinematics experiments to discriminate between direct breakup of ¹⁷F on protons and resonant decay of ¹⁸Ne states, and calls for identification of the inelastic ¹⁷F(1/2⁺)+p channel.
