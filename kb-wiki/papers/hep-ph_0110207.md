# Relativistic direct Urca processes in cooling neutron stars

> We derive a relativistic expression for neutrino energy losses caused by the direct Urca processes in degenerate baryon matter of neutron stars. We use two different ways to calculate the emissivity caused by the reactions to our interest. First we perform a standard calculation by Fermi's ''golden'' rule. The second calculation, resulting in the same expression, is performed with the aid of polarization functions of the medium. Our result for neutrino energy losses strongly differs from previous non-relativistic results. We also discuss nonconservation of the baryon vector current in reactions through weak charged currents in the medium, when the asymmetry between protons and neutrons is considered. The above effects, not discussed in the literature before, substantially modify the polarization functions responsible for the induced weak charged currents in baryon matter.

## Key claim

The authors derive a relativistic expression for neutrino energy losses from direct Urca processes in neutron stars, which strongly differs from previous non-relativistic results. The key condition is that in the relativistic regime, energy conservation can only be fulfilled by accounting for the difference in potential energies of protons and neutrons, and the proton recoil cannot be neglected.

## Method

The authors performed two independent calculations of the neutrino emissivity: first using Fermi's golden rule, and second using the polarization functions of the medium. They derived the relativistic polarization tensors for charged weak currents in baryon matter, accounting for the non-conservation of the baryon vector current that arises from the asymmetry between protons and neutrons in the medium. The calculations were performed in the mean field approximation using a Walecka-type relativistic model of nuclear matter with sigma, omega, and rho meson exchange.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino emissivity formula contains a factor of 457π/10080 times G_F^2 C^2 T^6, where G_F is the Fermi weak coupling constant and C = cos θ_C = 0.973 for ΔS = 0 processes
- The threshold density for direct Urca processes in the model used is n_th = 1.79 n_0, where n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is the nuclear matter saturation density
- The energy-momentum conservation requires a difference in effective masses of protons and neutrons of order 10^2 MeV in the free relativistic gas
- The final integration over energy variables yields the factor 457/5040 π^6 T^6

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic treatment of direct Urca processes by Lattimer et al. (1991), who derived a simple formula for neutrino energy losses assuming free particles and neglecting proton recoil. The authors show that their relativistic formula reduces to the known non-relativistic result of Lattimer et al. (1991) in the limit where Fermi momenta are much smaller than effective masses. The work also corrects assumptions made by Kim, Piekarewicz, and Horowitz (1995) and Reddy et al. (1999) about the form of polarization tensors for charged currents, showing that the vector-vector polarization tensor cannot be written in terms of longitudinal and transverse components when baryon vector current is not conserved. The results enable more accurate calculations of neutron star cooling rates at high densities, though the authors note that correlations among baryons and superfluidity effects, which would suppress the rates, are left for future work.
