# Diquark condensates and the magnetic field of pulsars

> We study the consequences of superconducting quark cores in neutron stars for the magnetic fields of pulsars. We find that within recent nonperturbative approaches to the effective quark interaction the diquark condensate forms a superconductor of second kind whereas previously quark matter was considered as a first kind superconductor. In both cases the magnetic field which is generated in the surrounding hadronic shell of superfluid neutrons and superconducting protons can penetrate into the quark matter core since it is concentrated in neutron vortices where the field strength exceeds the critical value. Therefore the magnetic field will not be expelled from the superconducting quark core with the consequence that there is no decay of the magnetic fields of pulsars. Thus we conclude that the occurence of a superconducting quark matter core in pulsars does not contradict the observational data which indicate that magnetic fields of pulsars have life times larger than $10^7$ years.

# Diquark condensates and the magnetic field of pulsars

## Key claim

The presence of a superconducting quark matter core in pulsars does not contradict observed magnetic field lifetimes exceeding 10^7 years, because the magnetic field generated in the surrounding hadronic shell can penetrate into the quark core via quantized vortex structures, regardless of whether the diquark condensate forms a type-I or type-II superconductor.

## Method

The authors employ a Ginzburg-Landau framework for relativistic superconducting quarks to compute the coherence length, penetration depth, and critical fields of the diquark condensate, using nonperturbative effective quark interactions (NJL-type models). They then model the magnetic structure of a rotating hybrid star with a quark core, considering the entrainment of superconducting protons by superfluid neutron vortices in the hadronic "npe"-phase, and calculate the conditions under which the resulting magnetic field penetrates into the quark core as clusters of quark vortex lines.

## Key numbers

- Diquark energy gap Δ ≈ 100 MeV at chemical potential μ ≈ 400 MeV
- Coherence length ξ_q = 0.8 × 10^{-13} cm
- Penetration depth λ_q ≈ 3.6 × 10^{-12} cm
- Thermodynamical critical field H_cm ≈ 8.7 × 10^{17} G
- Ginzburg-Landau parameter κ = 34 (for Δ = 100 MeV, μ ≈ p_F ≈ 400 MeV/c), indicating type-II superconductivity
- Upper critical field H_{c2}^q ≈ 3 × 10^{19} G
- Lower critical field H_{c1}^q = 1.8 × 10^{16} G
- Quark vortex flux Φ_q = 6 Φ_0, where Φ_0 = 2 × 10^{-7} G cm^2 is the proton flux quantum
- For pulsar Vela PSR 0833-45 (Ω = 70 rad s^{-1}): neutron vortex lattice spacing b = 10^{-3} cm, proton vortex cluster radius δ_n = 10^{-5} cm, quark vortex cluster radius δ_q = 4.3 × 10^{-7} cm
- Maximum magnetic field at neutron vortex center H(0) ≈ 4.7 × 10^{16} G (for λ_p = 30 fm, k = 0.7, ξ_n = 30 fm)
- Mean magnetic induction in quark vortex clusters reaches order 10^{18} G

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Bailin and Love (1984), who argued that magnetic fields would be expelled from superconducting quark cores within ≈ 10^4 years due to the Meissner effect, using a perturbative gluon propagator and assuming a homogeneous magnetic field. The present paper shows that with nonperturbative effective interactions (instantons, nonperturbative gluon propagators) as developed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998), Rapp et al. (1998), and others, the diquark condensate becomes a type-II superconductor, and that even for type-I superconductivity the magnetic field can penetrate via vortex structures generated by neutron-proton entrainment in the hadronic shell. The results enable future work on the rotational evolution and magnetic field decay of hybrid stars with quark cores, linking the magnetic field lifetime to the pulsar's spin-down timescale rather than to the Meissner expulsion time.
