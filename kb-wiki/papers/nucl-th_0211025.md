# Determination of Matter Surface Distribution of Neutron-rich Nuclei

> We demonstrate that the matter density distribution in the surface region is determined well by the use of the relatively low-intensity beams that become available at the upcoming radioactive beam facilities. Following the method used in the analyses of electron scattering, we examine how well the density distribution is determined in a model-independent way by generating pseudo data and by carefully applying statistical and systematic error analyses. We also study how the determination becomes deteriorated in the central region of the density, as the quality of data decreases. Determination of the density distributions of neutron-rich nuclei is performed by fixing parameters in the basis functions to the neighboring stable nuclei. The procedure allows that the knowledge of the density distributions of stable nuclei assists to strengthen the determination of their unstable isotopes.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the matter density distribution in the surface region of neutron-rich nuclei can be determined well using relatively low-intensity beams from upcoming radioactive beam facilities, but only when parameters in the basis functions are fixed to those of neighboring stable nuclei; the determination deteriorates in the central region as data quality decreases.

## Method

The authors performed a model calculation using the eikonal approximation with the t-rho form of the optical potential to relate nuclear matter density distributions to proton-nucleus elastic scattering differential cross sections at 1.047 GeV incident proton energy. They generated 25 sets of pseudo data from a three-parameter Fermi density distribution for 58Ni and a relativistic mean field density for 78Ni, then applied a model-independent least-square fitting procedure using Kamimura-Gaussian basis functions to extract the density distributions and analyze both statistical and systematic errors.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy: 1.047 GeV, chosen to match SATURN, Saclay experiments
- Three yield parameter values examined: B0 = 0.1, 1.0, and 10.0 mb/str, corresponding to minimum uncertainties of 2.0%, 3.3%, and 6.7% respectively
- Number of basis functions M = 10 chosen for the analysis, with radial range R = 10 fm
- For 58Ni, the three-parameter Fermi distribution parameters: w = -0.1308, c = 4.3092 fm, a = 0.5169 fm
- Center-of-mass scattering angles taken greater than 4 degrees with 1.0 degree separations, reduced near forward direction
- Maximum scattering angle: 25 degrees, corresponding to q_max = 750 MeV
- Resolution of fitted density distribution: Delta r = pi/q_max = 0.85 fm
- Maximum distance probed: r_max = 30 fm
- For M = 10 with best-fit parameters, the relative completeness error increases from about 0.0001 at the central region to about 0.01 around 7 fm

## Context

This work extends the model-independent method developed for electron scattering charge density determinations (Friar and Negele, 1975; Dreher et al., 1974) to the matter density distributions of neutron-rich nuclei, which had not been previously addressed. The authors note that while Alkhazov et al. (1978) and Chaumeaux et al. (1978) reviewed earlier model-independent analyses of matter densities, and Ray et al. (1978, 1979), Hoffmann et al. (1980), and Alkhazov et al. (1977) studied matter density distributions along similar lines, none addressed the surface density determination for nuclei near the drip lines. The work enables future analyses of data from RIBF at RIKEN and RIA facilities by establishing how experimental accuracy relates to the precision of density determination in the surface region.
