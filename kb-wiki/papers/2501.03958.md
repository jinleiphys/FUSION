# Nuclear cross sections from low-energy interactions

> We present a method to calculate neutron scattering cross sections for deformed nuclei using many--body wavefunctions described with multiple reference states. Nuclear states are calculated with the generator coordinate method using a low energy effective Hamiltonian. Using these states, a non--local and energy dependent optical potential is consistently constructed, allowing to directly investigate the role of nuclear structure properties in nuclear scattering. The case of neutron scattering on $^{24}$Mg is presented. The results are compared to experiment and to phenomenological optical potentials at energies below 13 MeV, demonstrating the importance of low--energy collectivity in elastic and non--elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to calculate neutron scattering cross sections for deformed nuclei using many-body wavefunctions from the generator coordinate method (GCM), and demonstrates that low-energy collectivity captured by GCM resolves the overestimation of elastic scattering cross sections that previously occurred when using self-consistent Green's functions with only particle-hole excitations (Idini 2019).

## Method

The authors construct an optical potential for neutron scattering on 24Mg using the Green's function formalism, where the self-energy is built from spectroscopic amplitudes of GCM states for the A±1 systems (23Mg and 25Mg) relative to the A ground state (24Mg). The GCM basis consists of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov vacua varied over quadrupole deformation parameters β and γ, neutron-proton pairing fields, and cranking constraints, with states projected to good angular momentum and particle number. The spectroscopic amplitudes are completed to satisfy sum rules using a spherical Hartree-Fock solution, and the resulting energy-dependent, non-local optical potential is used to solve the momentum-space Schrödinger equation via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- Total and elastic neutron scattering cross sections for n+24Mg are calculated for neutron energies from 0.5 MeV to 13 MeV
- The effective Hamiltonian used the SLy4 Skyrme parameterization with 17 major shells in the HF calculation, but only the 586 lowest energy HF states were used as the single-particle basis for the GCM
- The βγ plane was sampled with 275 states; keeping states below 25 MeV resulted in 179 HFB basis states
- Angular momentum projection used (6,12,24) points for the (α,β,γ) angles
- The smoothing parameter η was set to η = (a/π)(E-Ef)²/[(E-Ef)²+b²] with a = 12 MeV and b = 22.36 MeV
- The calculated elastic cross section reproduces experiments up to 9 MeV but overestimates them for neutron energies above 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Idini (2019), who used self-consistent Green's functions to calculate neutron elastic scattering on 16O and 40Ca and found that the overestimation of elastic scattering cross sections was due to lacking correlations and collectivity. The present method overcomes this issue by using GCM with a low-energy effective Hamiltonian (Ljungberg 2022) that captures collective degrees of freedom, showing that GCM is suitable to reproduce the low-energy collectivity important for reaction properties. The results enable the systematic construction of microscopic optical potentials for heavy and deformed nuclei, though the method currently underestimates compound nucleus contributions at lower energies, which is under further investigation (Sargsyan 2024).
