# Effects of core polarization on the nuclear Schiff moment

> Schiff moments were calculated for a set of nuclei with full account of core polarization effects. A finite range P and T violating weak nucleon-nucleon interaction has been used in the calculations. While in the absence of core polarization the Schiff moment depends on one combination of the weak interaction constants, in the presence of core polarization the Schiff moment depends on all three constants separately. The dominant contribution comes from isovector, $ΔT=1$, part of the weak interaction. The effects of core polarization were found to have in general a large effect on the Schiff moments.

# Effects of core polarization on the nuclear Schiff moment

## Key claim
Core polarization effects produce large corrections to nuclear Schiff moments, reducing the lowest-order values by factors of two to three, and cause the Schiff moment to depend separately on all three isospin channels (ΔT=0,1,2) of the P,T-violating weak nucleon-nucleon interaction rather than on a single combination of constants.

## Method
The authors calculated Schiff moments for a set of spherical nuclei using a finite-range P,T-violating weak nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from π⁰ exchange, with full inclusion of core polarization effects via the random phase approximation (RPA) formalism. The single-particle basis was obtained from a partially self-consistent Woods-Saxon mean-field potential, and the residual strong interaction was treated within Migdal's theory to renormalize both the Schiff operator and the weak interaction contributions.

## Key numbers
- For ¹³³Cs, the bare single-particle Schiff moment (without core polarization) for the ΔT=1 channel is -0.109 g_s g₁ e fm³ from the direct potential; after core polarization the total Schiff moment becomes -0.02 g_s g₁ e fm³.
- For ¹³³Cs, the total Schiff moment after core polarization is 0.006 g_s g₀ - 0.02 g_s g₁ - 0.04 g_s g₂ e fm³, compared to the bare value of 0.08 g_s g₀ - 0.02 g_s g₁ - 0.21 g_s g₂ e fm³.
- For ¹⁹⁹Hg, the bare Schiff moment is -0.09 g_s g₀ - 0.09 g_s g₁ + 0.18 g_s g₂ e fm³; after core polarization it becomes -0.00004 g_s g₀ - 0.055 g_s g₁ + 0.009 g_s g₂ e fm³.
- For ²¹¹Rn, the bare Schiff moment is -0.12 g_s g₀ - 0.12 g_s g₁ + 0.24 g_s g₂ e fm³; after core polarization it becomes -0.019 g_s g₀ + 0.061 g_s g₁ + 0.053 g_s g₂ e fm³.
- For ²²⁵Ra (assumed spherical), the bare Schiff moment is 0.08 g_s g₀ + 0.08 g_s g₁ - 0.16 g_s g₂ e fm³; after core polarization it becomes 0.033 g_s g₀ - 0.037 g_s g₁ - 0.046 g_s g₂ e fm³.
- The renormalized single-particle contribution for ¹³³Cs is reduced by approximately a factor of 2: -0.003 g_s g₀ - 0.056 g_s g₁ - 0.016 g_s g₂ e fm³.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of Schiff moments that used either single-particle values (for proton-odd nuclei) or simple 2p-1h configurations (for neutron-odd nuclei) without core polarization. The authors note that previous calculations by Spevak et al. (1997) found enhanced Schiff moments in quadrupole+octupole deformed nuclei, and that Engel et al. (2003) used a finite-range interaction for ²²⁵Ra. The present results show that core polarization reduces Schiff moments by factors of two to three, implying that experimental upper limits on atomic electric dipole moments correspond to higher upper limits on the P,T-violating weak interaction constants than previously estimated. The finite-range interaction reduces mixing matrix elements compared to zero-range interactions, but the reduction is similar in spherical and deformed nuclei, so the enhancement factors for deformed nuclei relative to spherical ones are preserved.
