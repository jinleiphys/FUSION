# Rôle of the pion electromagnetic form factor in the $Δ(1232) \to γ^\ast N$ timelike transition

> The $Δ(1232) \to γ^\ast N$ magnetic dipole form factor ($G_M^\ast$) is described here within a new covariant model that combines the valence quark core together with the pion cloud contributions. The pion cloud term is parameterized by two terms: one connected to the pion electromagnetic form factor, the other to the photon interaction with intermediate baryon states. The model can be used in studies of pp and heavy ion collisions. In the timelike region this new model improves the results obtained with a constant form factor model fixed at its value at zero momentum transfer. At the same time, and in contrast to the Iachello model, this new model predicts a peak for the transition form factor at the expected position, i.e. at the $ρ$ mass pole. We calculate the decay of the $Δ\to γN$ transition, the Dalitz decay ($Δ\to e^+ e^- N$), and the $Δ$ mass distribution function. The impact of the model on dilepton spectra in pp collisions is also discussed.

## Key claim

The new covariant model for the $\Delta(1232) \to \gamma^\ast N$ magnetic dipole form factor improves upon the constant form factor model in the timelike region and, unlike the Iachello model, predicts a peak at the expected $\rho$ mass pole position; however, the model may overestimate dilepton spectra at high beam energies, possibly due to the omission of a $W$ dependence in the pion cloud weight parameter $\lambda_\pi$.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid covariant model combining a valence quark core contribution (calibrated to lattice QCD data) with a pion cloud contribution parameterized by two terms: one linked to the pion electromagnetic form factor $F_\pi(q^2)$ (diagram a) and another modeling photon interactions with intermediate octet and decuplet baryon states (diagram b). The pion cloud term was fitted to $F_\pi$ data using a monopole form with a logarithmic counterterm, and the model was applied to calculate $\Delta$ Dalitz decay widths, mass distributions, and dilepton spectra in pp collisions using the GiBUU transport model.

## Key numbers

- The fit to $|F_\pi(q^2)|^2$ data yields parameters $\alpha = 0.696$ GeV$^2$ and $\beta = 0.178$ for the pion form factor parameterization.
- The pion cloud strength parameter is $\lambda_\pi = 0.441$, with cutoff $\Lambda_\pi^2 = 1.53$ GeV$^2$ from previous works.
- Two values for the dipole cutoff $\Lambda_D^2$ are considered: $0.71$ GeV$^2$ and $0.90$ GeV$^2$, with $0.71$ GeV$^2$ giving a slightly better description of spacelike data.
- The $\Delta \to \pi N$ width parameterization uses a cutoff $\kappa = 0.197$ GeV.
- The model is tested for $\Delta$ masses $W = 1.232$ GeV, $1.6$ GeV, $1.8$ GeV, and $2.2$ GeV in the timelike region.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model of Ramalho et al. (previous works) for the $\Delta \to \gamma^\ast N$ transition, which previously used a simpler one-term pion cloud parameterization and a function $F_\rho$ from the Iachello model that produced a displaced peak. The new model corrects this deficiency by directly using pion form factor data and adding a second term for intermediate baryon states. The results enable improved calculations of dilepton spectra in pp and heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that further study of the $W$ dependence of the pion cloud is needed to resolve remaining discrepancies with HADES data at 3.5 GeV beam energy.
