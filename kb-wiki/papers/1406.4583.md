# Verification of Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution with Big-Bang Nucleosyntheis theory

> The current Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) model has been constructed based on a nuclear reaction network operating with thermal reactivities of Maxwell-Boltzmann (MB) distribution plasma. However, does the classical MB distribution still hold for the extremely high-temperature (in order of 10$^9$ K) plasma involved in the Big-Bang environment? In this work, we have investigated the impact of non-extensive Tsallis statistics (in $q$-Guassian distribution) on the thermonuclear reaction rates. We show for the first time that the reverse rates are extremely sensitive to the non-extensive $q$ parameter. Such sensitivity does not allow a large deviation of non-extensive distribution from the usual MB distribution. With a newly developed BBN code, the impact of primordial light-element abundances on $q$ values has been studied by utilizing the most recent BBN cosmological parameters and the available nuclear cross-section data. For the first time, we have accurately verified the microscopic MB distribution with the macroscopic BBN theory and bservation. By comparing the recent observed primordial abundances with our predictions, only a tiny deviation of $\pm$6$\times$10$^{-4}$ at most can be allowed for the MB distribution. However, validity of the classical statistics needs to be studied further for the self-gravitating stars and binaries of high-density environment, with the extreme sensitivity of reverse rate on $q$ found here.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the classical Maxwell-Boltzmann (MB) distribution is verified for the Big-Bang plasma environment, with a maximum allowed deviation of only ±6×10⁻⁴, but this verification is conditional on the extreme sensitivity of reverse reaction rates to the non-extensive parameter q, which prevents large deviations from MB statistics.

## Method

The authors replaced the standard Maxwell-Boltzmann velocity distribution with a q-Gaussian distribution from Tsallis non-extensive statistics in the calculation of thermonuclear reaction rates for 17 key BBN reactions. They numerically computed both forward and reverse rates using the q-Gaussian distribution without approximations, implemented these rates in a newly developed BBN reaction network code, and performed a chi-square fit comparing predicted primordial abundances of D, ³He, and ⁴He against recent observational data to constrain the allowed range of the non-extensive parameter q.

## Key numbers

- Maximum allowed deviation from MB distribution: ±6×10⁻⁴ (from the abstract and text)
- For the ²H(d,p)³H reaction at q=0.999, reverse rate deviates from MB by factors of 4×10¹⁰ at 0.2 GK and 3.3 at 1.0 GK
- For q in range 0.91≤q≤1, reverse rates deviate from MB by about 200 orders of magnitude for ²H(d,p)³H and 30 orders of magnitude for ³He(n,p)³H
- Forward rates for ²H(d,p)³H deviate from MB by at most a factor of 2 in region 0.1≤T₉≤1.0 and 0.91≤q≤1.1
- Forward rates for ³He(n,p)³H deviate from MB by at most 2% in the same region
- Baryon-to-photon ratio η=(6.203±0.137)×10⁻¹⁰ from WMAP 9-year data
- Neutron lifetime τₙ=887.7 s used in the model
- Predicted abundances with MB distribution: ⁴He=0.2485, D/H=2.54×10⁻⁵, ³He/H=1.01×10⁻⁵, ⁷Li/H=5.34×10⁻¹⁰

## Context

This work extends the investigation of non-Maxwellian distributions in BBN previously studied by Bertulani et al. (2013), who found a larger allowed deviation of -12% to 5% from MB distribution. The present paper obtains a much more stringent constraint (0.06%) primarily because it coherently applies the non-extensive distribution to reverse rate calculations, which Bertulani et al. did not do. The results also contrast with earlier work by Degl'Innocenti et al. (1998) who used helioseismology to constrain deviations in the solar interior. The paper enables future studies of non-extensive statistics in higher-density environments such as self-gravitating stars and close binary systems (nova, x-ray bursts, Type I supernova), where the classical MB distribution may not hold.
