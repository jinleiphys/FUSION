# A fully microscopic model of total level density in spherical nuclei

> A fully microscopic model for the description of nuclear level density (NLD) in spherical nuclei is proposed. The model is derived by combining the partition function of the exact pairing solution plus the independent-particle model at finite temperature (EP+IPM) with that obtained by using the collective vibrational states calculated from the self-consistent Hartree-Fock mean field with MSk3 interaction plus the exact pairing and random-phases approximation (SC-HFEPRPA). Two important factors are taken into account in a fully microscopic way, namely the spin cut-off and vibrational enhancement factors are, respectively, calculated using the statistical thermodynamics and partition function of the SC-HFEPRPA without any fitting parameters. The numerical test for two spherical $^{60}$Ni and $^{90}$Zr nuclei shows that the collective vibrational enhancement is mostly dominated by the quadrupole and octupole excitations. This is the first microscopic model confirming such an effect, which was phenomenologically predicted long time ago and widely employed in several NLD models. In addition, the influence of collective vibrational enhancement on nuclear thermodynamic quantities such as excitation energy, specific heat capacity and entropy is also studied by using the proposed model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a fully microscopic model for nuclear level density (NLD) in spherical nuclei that, for the first time, confirms microscopically that collective vibrational enhancement is dominated by quadrupole and octupole excitations, an effect previously only treated phenomenologically. The model contains no fitting parameters to experimental NLD data, though the pairing interaction strength is selected to match experimental odd-even mass differences.

## Method

The model combines the partition function of the exact pairing plus independent-particle model at finite temperature (EP+IPM) with the partition function of collective vibrational states from self-consistent Hartree-Fock mean field with MSk3 interaction plus exact pairing and random-phase approximation (SC-HFEPRPA). The spin cut-off factor is calculated microscopically via statistical thermodynamics using single-particle spin projections and quasiparticle energies, while the vibrational enhancement is included directly in the total partition function without empirical formulas. The total NLD is then obtained from the total partition function using the saddle-point approximation.

## Key numbers

- For $^{60}$Ni: average binding energy BE/A = -8.736 MeV (experimental: -8.780 MeV) and energy of first $2^+$ state $E_{2_1^+}$ = 1.336 MeV (experimental: 1.333 MeV), obtained from SC-HFEPRPA calculation.
- For $^{60}$Ni: experimental NLD data available from $E^* \sim 0$ up to $\sim 23$ MeV, well above neutron binding energy $B_n = 11.358$ MeV.
- For $^{90}$Zr: NLD data available only below the neutron binding energy $B_n$.
- EP+IPM calculation takes less than 5 minutes; SC-HFEPRPA takes about 1 hour per multipolarity; total calculation per nucleus takes less than 6 hours on a PC.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous EP+IPM model (Hung et al., 2019) by replacing the phenomenological Woods-Saxon single-particle potential with a Hartree-Fock mean field using MSk3 interaction, and by replacing empirical formulas for spin cut-off and vibrational enhancement with fully microscopic calculations. It addresses shortcomings of prior microscopic models such as HFBCS and HFBC (from RIPL-2 and RIPL-3 databases), which require normalization to experimental data and treat pairing approximately. The model enables fully parameter-free NLD predictions for spherical nuclei and can be extended to deformed systems in future work.
