# Persistence of cluster structure in the ground state of $^{11}$B

> The persistence of $α+α+t$ cluster configuration in the ground state of $^{11}$B is discussed. The $α$ particles emitted from excited states of $^{12}$C could be utilized for cancer treatment, where the cluster states are created by a clinical proton beam on the $^{11}$B target. Although the cross section has been already available, for the nuclear structure side, whether the ground state of $^{11}$B contains the seeds of the cluster states is a crucial question, since the cluster structure may be washed out by the spin-orbit interaction, which has been known as a driving force to break the $α$ clusters. For this purpose, in addition to the basis states with cluster configurations, we include their breaking effects by employing the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM). The inclusion of the breaking effect of $α+α+t$ cluster structure is found to contribute to the lowering of the ground-state energy by about 2 MeV. Assuming the typical three-$α$ cluster state of $^{12}$C as an equilateral triangular configuration with the relative distances of $\approx 4$ fm, the ground state of $^{11}$B is found to have a certain squared overlap with such state when a proton approaches. The third $3/2^{-}$ state has been suggested as a cluster state both theoretically and experimentally, and we confirmed the well-developed clustering.

## Key claim

The ground state of $^{11}$B retains a significant $\alpha+\alpha+t$ cluster component (about 70%) despite the spin-orbit interaction, which is known to break $\alpha$ clusters; the inclusion of cluster breaking effects via the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM) lowers the ground-state energy by about 2 MeV, but the cluster structure persists.

## Method

The authors employed the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM) to generate basis states that smoothly connect $\alpha$-cluster model wave functions to $jj$-coupling shell-model ones, thereby incorporating spin-orbit effects. They superposed 135 intrinsic basis states (75 $\alpha+\alpha+t$ cluster configurations, 12 AQCM states, and 48 $\alpha+\alpha+3N$ states) using the generator coordinate method (GCM) after angular momentum projection to $J^\pi = 3/2^-$, with the Tohsaki interaction for the central part and the G3RS spin-orbit interaction.

## Key numbers

- The inclusion of cluster breaking effects lowers the ground-state energy of $^{11}$B by about 2 MeV.
- The ground $3/2^-$ state is obtained at $-73.84$ MeV, compared to the experimental value of $-76.20$ MeV.
- The ground-state energy measured from the $\alpha+\alpha+t$ threshold is $-11.40$ MeV, compared to the experimental value of $-11.20$ MeV.
- The largest squared overlap within the $\alpha+\alpha+t$ cluster configurations for the ground state is 0.68 (68%), for a basis state with $\alpha$--$\alpha$ distance of 3 fm and triton at 2 fm from the center at an angle of $30^\circ$.
- The largest squared overlap for the ground state overall is 0.81, for an AQCM basis state with $\Lambda = 0.2$ and $R = 2$ fm.
- The squared overlap between $p+^{11}$B and a three-$\alpha$ cluster state (equilateral triangle with $\alpha$--$\alpha$ distance of 4 fm) is 10--20% at small distances, with angular momentum projected to $2^+$.
- The theoretical $\alpha$ energy is $-27.31$ MeV (experimental: $-28.29$ MeV); the theoretical triton energy is $-7.82$ MeV (experimental: $-8.48$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AQCM analysis of $^{12}$C (Itagaki 2016, 2021) to $^{11}$B, addressing the competition between cluster and shell-model configurations. It builds on prior cluster model studies of $^{11}$B by Nishioka (1979), Descouvemont (1995), and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations by Kanada-En'yo (2007), which focused on excited-state clustering. The results enable a microscopic understanding of the nuclear structure basis for Proton Boron Capture Therapy (PBCT), where clinical proton beams on $^{11}$B create three-$\alpha$ cluster states in $^{12}$C that decay by emitting $\alpha$ particles for cancer treatment.
