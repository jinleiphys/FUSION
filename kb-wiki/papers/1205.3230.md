# Convergence properties of {\itshape ab initio} calculations of light nuclei in a harmonic oscillator basis

> We study recently proposed ultraviolet and infrared momentum regulators of the model spaces formed by construction of a variational trial wavefunction which uses a complete set of many-body basis states based upon three-dimensional harmonic oscillator (HO) functions. These model spaces are defined by a truncation of the expansion characterized by a counting number ($\mathcal{N}$) and by the intrinsic scale ($\hbarω$) of the HO basis; in short by the ordered pair ($\mathcal{N},\hbarω$). In this study we choose for $\mathcal{N}$ the truncation parameter $N_{max}$ related to the maximum number of oscillator quanta, above the minimum configuration, kept in the model space. The ultraviolet (uv) momentum cutoff of the continuum is readily mapped onto a defined uv cutoff in this finite model space, but there are two proposed definitions of the infrared (ir) momentum cutoff inherent in a finite-dimensional HO basis. One definition is based upon the lowest momentum difference given by $\hbarω$ itself and the other upon the infrared momentum which corresponds to the maximal radial extent used to encompass the many-body system in coordinate space. Extending both the uv cutoff to infinity and the ir cutoff to zero is prescribed for a converged calculation. We calculate the ground state energy of light nuclei with "bare" and "soft" $NN$ interactions. By doing so, we investigate the behaviors of the uv and ir regulators of model spaces used to describe $^2$H, $^3$H, $^4$He and $^6$He with $NN$ potentials Idaho N$^3$LO and JISP16. We establish practical procedures which utilize these regulators to obtain the extrapolated result from sequences of calculations with model spaces characterized by ($\mathcal{N},\hbarω$).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that convergence of *ab initio* calculations of light nuclei in a harmonic oscillator basis can be systematically understood and extrapolated using effective field theory concepts of ultraviolet (UV) and infrared (IR) momentum cutoffs, but the choice of IR cutoff definition (either \(\lambda = \sqrt{m_N \hbar\omega}\) or \(\lambda_{sc} = \sqrt{(m_N \hbar\omega)/(N+3/2)}\)) significantly affects the extrapolation strategy and the interpretation of convergence.

## Method

The authors perform variational no-core full configuration (NCFC) calculations of ground-state energies for \(^2\)H, \(^3\)H, \(^4\)He, and \(^6\)He using two soft nucleon-nucleon (\(NN\)) potentials: Idaho N\(^3\)LO (with a 500 MeV/c regulator cutoff) and JISP16. The model spaces are defined by the truncation parameter \(N_{max}\) (maximum oscillator quanta above the minimum configuration) and the harmonic oscillator energy \(\hbar\omega\), denoted as the ordered pair \((N_{max}, \hbar\omega)\). The UV cutoff is defined as \(\Lambda = \sqrt{m_N (N+3/2) \hbar\omega}\), and two competing IR cutoffs are tested: \(\lambda = \sqrt{m_N \hbar\omega}\) and \(\lambda_{sc} = \sqrt{(m_N \hbar\omega)/(N+3/2)}\). The authors analyze the behavior of ground-state energies as functions of these cutoffs to establish extrapolation procedures.

## Key numbers

- For \(^4\)He with the Idaho N\(^3\)LO potential, the ground-state energy converges exponentially with \(N_{max}\), as expected for a smooth potential with the Yukawa singularity regulated away.
- The UV cutoff is defined as \(\Lambda = \sqrt{m_N (N+3/2) \hbar\omega}\) with \(m_N = 938.92\) MeV, giving \(\Lambda\) in units of MeV/c.
- The IR cutoff \(\lambda = \sqrt{m_N \hbar\omega}\) is proportional to \(\sqrt{\hbar\omega}\), while \(\lambda_{sc} = \sqrt{(m_N \hbar\omega)/(N+3/2)}\) decreases both as \(\hbar\omega \to 0\) at fixed \(N\) and as \(N \to \infty\) at fixed \(\hbar\omega\).
- For \(^6\)He, a truncation at \(N_{max}=4\) allows one neutron to occupy the \(N=5\) HO shell while the other valence neutron remains in the \(N=1\) shell, with the remaining four nucleons in the filled \(N=0\) shell.

## Context

This work extends the convergence analysis of variational calculations in a harmonic oscillator basis, building on earlier studies by Delves (1972) and Schneider (1972) who established asymptotic convergence rates for smooth and nonsmooth potentials. It directly applies the UV/IR cutoff framework introduced in the context of effective field theory for the no-core shell model (EFTNCSM) by the same group. The paper enables practical extrapolation procedures for NCFC calculations with soft potentials like Idaho N\(^3\)LO and JISP16, which are used without renormalization for nuclei up to \(A=6\) by Maris (2009) and Navratil (2004). The results contrast with earlier variational approaches that required extensive numerical minimization over \(\hbar\omega\) and provide a physically motivated alternative to the traditional convergence analysis based solely on \(N_{max}\).
