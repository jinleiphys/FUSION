# Photofission and Quasi-Deuteron-Nuclear State as Mixing of Bosons and Fermions

> The empirical-phenomenological quasi-deuteron photofission description is theoretically justified within the semiclassical, intermediate statistics model. The transmutational fermion (nucleon) - boson (quasi-deuteron) potential plays an essential role in the present context and is expressed in terms of thermodynamical and of microscopical quantities, analogous to those commonly used in the superfluid nuclear model.

## Key claim

The paper provides a theoretical justification for the empirical quasi-deuteron (QD) photofission model using a semiclassical intermediate statistics framework, where the transmutational potential (TP) governing the fermion (nucleon) to boson (QD) transition is expressed in terms of thermodynamical and microscopical quantities analogous to those in the superfluid nuclear model. The authors hedge that the relation between photon energy and nuclear temperature (E_gamma = A/8 T^2) is not rigorously exact, as Monte Carlo calculations show the excitation energy is about 15% smaller than the photon energy, though they verify their results are not modified by this reduction.

## Method

The authors develop a semiclassical kinetic model with coupled continuity equations for nucleons (fermions, kappa = -1) and quasi-deuterons (bosons, kappa = +1) in velocity space, incorporating a transmutational current for the 2N <-> D conversion. They calculate the free nucleon fraction (xi_N) and QD fraction (xi_D) from their earlier phenomenological QD photofission model, using the probability F_2(E_gamma) that a QD pair participates in photofission, and then derive the chemical potentials and transmutational potential eta_{2N->D} via Eq. (11). The TP is also interpreted microscopically by analogy with the BCS ground state, writing the nuclear QD state as a product over states k of (u_k + c_k a_k^dag + v_k a_k^dag a_{-k}^dag)|0>, where u_k^2, c_k^2, and v_k^2 are probabilities for empty, single-nucleon, and QD-pair occupancy respectively.

## Key numbers

- The QD energy region studied is between 40 MeV and 100 MeV excitation energy.
- The effective number of QD pairs is C(N,Z) = 7.72 * NZ/A.
- The Pauli blocking factor uses D = 60 MeV, where D/2 is the average energy to excite each nucleon of a pair above the Fermi level.
- The nuclear density is assumed equal to infinite nuclear matter: 0.17 nucleon fm^{-3}.
- The nuclear interaction is described by a square well with V_N = V_D = V_0 = -48 MeV.
- For ^238U, the transmutational potential eta_{2N->D}(90 MeV) = 0, and at this energy F_2(90 MeV) ≈ 0.5.
- For ^209Bi, the TP is nearly constant and negative; for ^232Th and ^238U, the TP is an increasing function of E_gamma.
- The chemical potential mu_D ≈ V_0 = -48 MeV for ^238U and ^232Th at low excitation temperatures T = 1.1-1.9 MeV, and varies between -55 MeV and -51 MeV for ^209Bi.
- The nucleon mass is m_N = 939 MeV; the QD mass is m_D = 2*m_N = 1878 MeV.
- The spin-isospin degeneration factor is f_N = 4 for nucleons and f_D = 3 for QD (S=1, T=0).
- Table values of F_2(E_gamma): for ^209Bi at 40 MeV: 0.0002, at 100 MeV: 0.019; for ^232Th at 40 MeV: 0.109, at 100 MeV: 0.383; for ^238U at 40 MeV: 0.183, at 100 MeV: 0.578.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier phenomenological QD photofission model (Delsanto et al., 1992; Kaniadakis et al., 1993) by providing a theoretical foundation based on semiclassical intermediate statistics (Kaniadakis and Quarati, 1994; Kaniadakis, 1994). The approach enables a connection between statistical kinetic models and microscopic many-body approaches like the superfluid nuclear model (BCS theory), and allows the interpretation of the transmutational potential in terms of a phase transition described by a generalized Clausius-Clapeyron equation. The work is relevant for understanding photofission of heavy nuclei (Bi, Th, U) in the QD energy region and could be extended to higher energies as done by Arruda-Neto et al. (1995).
