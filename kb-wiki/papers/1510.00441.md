# Dilepton emission in high-energy heavy-ion collisions with dissipative hydrodynamics

> In this contribution we study the effects of three transport coefficients of dissipative hydrodynamics on thermal dilepton anisotropic flow observables. The first two transport coefficients investigated influence the overall size and growth rate of shear viscous pressure, while the last transport coefficient governs the magnitude of net baryon number diffusion in relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics. All calculations are done using state-of-the-art 3+1D hydrodynamical simulations. We show that thermal dileptons are sensitive probes of the transport coefficients of dissipative hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

Thermal dileptons are sensitive probes of three specific transport coefficients of dissipative hydrodynamics, shear viscosity, shear relaxation time, and net baryon number conductivity, but the study focuses on qualitative trends rather than precise extraction, because the equation of state at finite baryon chemical potential and the size of transport coefficients at lower beam energies remain poorly constrained.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D second-order Israel-Stewart dissipative hydrodynamical simulations of Au+Au collisions at two beam energies: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV (RHIC top energy) and \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV (BES energy). At 200 GeV, they varied the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) using a linear temperature-dependent parameterization and the shear relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) via a scaling parameter \(b_\pi\); at 7.7 GeV, they introduced a finite net baryon chemical potential equation of state and turned on baryon diffusion with conductivity \(\kappa\). Thermal dilepton spectra and elliptic flow \(v_2\) were computed from the evolving medium using in-medium vector meson decays in the hadronic phase and Born annihilation in the quark-gluon plasma, including dissipative corrections to the distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, three values of the shear relaxation time parameter were used: \(b_\pi = 5, 10, 20\) (where \(\tau_\pi = b_\pi \, \eta/(\varepsilon+P)\)), with \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) (i.e., \(m=0\)).
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, three slopes of the temperature-dependent \(\eta/s\) were used: \(m = 0, 0.2427, 0.5516\) in \(\eta/s = m(T/T_{tr}-1) + 1/(4\pi)\), with \(T_{tr}=0.18\) GeV.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV, the baryon diffusion relaxation time was set to \(\tau_V = 0.2/T\) and the conductivity to \(\kappa = 0.2 \, n_B/\mu_B\).
- The initial proper time was \(\tau_0 = 0.4\) fm/c at 200 GeV and \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c at 7.7 GeV.
- Freeze-out occurred at \(T_{FO}=145\) MeV at 200 GeV and at a constant energy density of 0.1 GeV/fm\(^3\) at 7.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dissipative hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Vujanovic (2014)) by simultaneously examining shear relaxation time and temperature-dependent shear viscosity effects on dilepton flow, and by introducing baryon diffusion at lower beam energies for the first time in this context. The results show that dilepton \(v_2\) is sensitive to these transport coefficients where hadronic \(v_2\) is not, enabling future combined extractions of transport properties using both hadronic and electromagnetic probes.
