# Pseudoscalar and vector toponia in a Dyson--Schwinger--Bethe--Salpeter framework

> We study the pseudoscalar ($J^{PC}=0^{-+}$) and vector ($1^{--}$) top--antitop (toponium) systems within the rainbow--ladder truncation of the Dyson--Schwinger and Bethe--Salpeter equations, employing the Qin--Chang effective interaction. After validating the framework in the charmonium and bottomonium sectors, we extend it consistently to the top sector, incorporating renormalisation-group running of the current quark mass and a careful treatment of the number of active flavours. We compute masses and leptonic decay constants for $N_f=5$ and $6$, then analyse their dependence on the renormalisation scale in the range $μ=400-800\,\text{GeV}$. The resulting toponium masses lie near $344-346\,\text{GeV}$ with hyperfine splittings below $0.14-0.17\,\text{GeV}$, while the decay constants are large, $6-7\,\text{GeV}$, and exhibit the expected heavy-quark scaling behaviour. We find only mild sensitivity to the renormalisation point and a systematic reduction of binding when increasing $N_f$. Although the physical top quark decays weakly before hadronisation, our results demonstrate that, within a Poincaré-covariant nonperturbative framework, quantum chromodynamics (QCD) generates tightly correlated pseudoscalar and vector toponium systems in that extreme heavy-quark limit.

# Pseudoscalar and vector toponia in a Dyson–Schwinger–Bethe–Salpeter framework

## Key claim

Within a Poincaré-covariant nonperturbative Dyson–Schwinger–Bethe–Salpeter framework using the rainbow–ladder truncation and Qin–Chang effective interaction, QCD generates tightly correlated pseudoscalar and vector toponium systems in the extreme heavy-quark limit, even though the physical top quark decays weakly before hadronisation. The results characterise the strength of QCD-induced quark–antiquark correlations in that limit, providing a baseline against which more refined treatments incorporating unstable-particle dynamics can be compared, but do not include explicit finite-width effects associated with the electroweak decay of the top quark.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Dyson–Schwinger equation and the homogeneous Bethe–Salpeter equation in the rainbow–ladder truncation, employing the Qin–Chang effective interaction which combines an infrared-enhanced term with a perturbative ultraviolet tail. After validating the framework against charmonium and bottomonium masses and leptonic decay constants, they extended it to the top sector by incorporating renormalisation-group running of the current quark mass and a careful treatment of the number of active flavours, computing masses and decay constants for Nf = 5 and Nf = 6 over renormalisation scales μ = 400–800 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Toponium masses lie near 344–346 GeV for both pseudoscalar and vector channels across the renormalisation scales studied.
- Hyperfine splittings are below 0.14–0.17 GeV, reflecting heavy-quark spin symmetry in the extreme mass limit.
- Leptonic decay constants are large, 6–7 GeV, consistent with increasingly compact spatial structure of heavy quarkonia.
- For Nf = 5, the pseudoscalar mass ranges from 344.537 GeV (μ = 400 GeV) to 345.992 GeV (μ = 800 GeV); the vector mass ranges from 344.708 GeV to 346.164 GeV over the same scales.
- For Nf = 6, the pseudoscalar mass ranges from 343.432 GeV (μ = 400 GeV) to 344.415 GeV (μ = 800 GeV); the vector mass ranges from 343.567 GeV to 344.551 GeV.
- The input top-quark mass in the MSbar scheme is mtMSbar(mt) = 163.643 GeV, converted to MOM scheme values ranging from approximately 160 GeV to 152 GeV over μ = 400–800 GeV.
- The charmonium and bottomonium masses are reproduced at the few-percent level: m_ηc = 2.982 GeV (theoretical) vs 2.984 GeV (experimental); m_Υ = 9.476 GeV vs 9.460 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson–Schwinger–Bethe–Salpeter approach previously applied to charmonium and bottomonium by Qin (2018) to the extreme heavy-quark regime of the top quark, providing a fully relativistic baseline for hypothetical toponium states. Recent CMS and ATLAS analyses of proton–proton collision data at √s = 13 TeV have revealed significant excesses of ttbar events near kinematic threshold compatible with a pseudoscalar quasi-bound state, as suggested by non-relativistic QCD calculations by Beneke (2013, 2024) and others. The present results enable comparison with NRQCD-based phenomenological descriptions and provide a foundation for future studies incorporating finite-width effects from electroweak top decay and corrections beyond rainbow–ladder truncation.
