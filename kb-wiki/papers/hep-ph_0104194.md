# Masses of the pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons in two flavor color superconducting phase

> The masses of the pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons in the color superconducting phase of dense QCD with two light flavors are estimated by making use of the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis effective action. Parametrically, the masses of the doublet and antidoublet bosons are suppressed by a power of the coupling constant as compared to the value of the superconducting gap. This is qualitatively different from the mass expression for the singlet pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson, resulting from non-perturbative effects. It is argued that the (anti-) doublet pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons form colorless [with respect to the unbroken SU(2)_{c}] charmonium-like bound states. The corresponding binding energy is also estimated.

## Key claim

The masses of the doublet and antidoublet pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons in the two-flavor color superconducting (S2C) phase of dense QCD are parametrically suppressed by a power of the coupling constant relative to the superconducting gap, while the singlet pseudo-NG boson mass is exponentially suppressed by non-perturbative instanton effects; these estimates rely on the assumption of asymptotically large chemical potential \(\mu \gg \Lambda_{QCD}\) and are valid only up to an order of magnitude at realistic compact-star densities.

## Method

The authors used the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) effective action formalism to compute the curvature of the effective potential along the directions in color-flavor space corresponding to the five pseudo-NG bosons associated with the approximate axial color symmetry. The leading-order contribution to the masses came from a two-loop vacuum diagram (Fig. 1e) involving two quark loops and two gluon propagators, evaluated in the Landau gauge to avoid gauge-dependence issues from non-transverse polarization tensors. The calculation employed asymptotic forms of the one-loop gluon polarization tensor in the color-superconducting phase, with the dominant momentum region being \(|\Delta| \lesssim q \lesssim \mu\) and \(0 \lesssim |q_4| \lesssim |\Delta|\).

## Key numbers

- Doublet/antidoublet pseudo-NG boson mass: \(M^2 \simeq C_M \sqrt{\alpha_s} |\Delta|^2\) with \(C_M \approx 7 \times 10^2\), where \(|\Delta|\) is the superconducting gap and \(\alpha_s\) is the strong coupling constant.
- Singlet pseudo-NG boson (\(\eta\)) mass: \(M^2_{\eta} \simeq (C_{\eta} / \alpha_s^7) |\Delta^-|^2 \exp(-2\pi / \alpha_s)\) with \(C_{\eta} \simeq 10^5\).
- Binding energy of colorless charmonium-like bound states from doublet-antidoublet pairs: \(E_n \simeq C_E \alpha_s^{-39/4} |\Delta^-| n^{-2} \exp(-3\sqrt{2}\pi^{3/2} / \sqrt{\alpha_s})\) with \(C_E \approx 1.8 \times 10^{11}\) for principal quantum number \(n=1,2,\ldots\).
- Confinement scale of the unbroken \(SU(2)_c\) subgroup: \(\Lambda_{QCD}^{\prime} \simeq |\Delta^-| \exp[-C_0 \alpha_s^2 \exp(C / \sqrt{\alpha_s})]\) with \(C = 3(\pi/2)^{3/2}\) and \(C_0 \simeq 10^{-3}\).
- Dielectric constant for the Coulomb potential between static charges: \(\epsilon \approx 2 \alpha_s \mu^2 / (9\pi |\Delta^-|^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies (Miransky, Shovkovy, Wijewardhana, 2000) which proposed the existence of five light pseudo-NG bosons in the S2C phase, by providing the first mass estimates for these states. The singlet mass result builds on the non-perturbative instanton calculation of Son (2000). The paper also connects to the analysis of Rischke (2000) on the confinement of the unbroken \(SU(2)_c\) subgroup, showing that the colored doublet and antidoublet pseudo-NG bosons must form colorless bound states analogous to charmonium, with binding dynamics dominated by perturbative Coulomb forces rather than linear confinement. The results enable future studies of the low-energy spectrum and transport properties of dense quark matter relevant to neutron-star cores.
