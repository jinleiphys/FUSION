# Masses of doubly heavy tetraquarks $T_{QQ^\prime}$ in a relativized quark model

> In the present work, the mass spectra of doubly heavy tetraquarks $T_{QQ^\prime}$ are systematically investigated in a relativized quark model. The four-body systems including the Coulomb potential, confining potential, spin-spin interactions, and relativistic corrections are solved within the variational method. Our results suggest that the $IJ^P=01^+$ $bb \bar u \bar d$ state is 54 MeV below the relevant $\bar B \bar B$ and $\bar B \bar B^*$ thresholds, which indicates that both strong and electromagnetic decays are forbidden, and thus this state can be a stable one. Its large hidden color component and small root mean square radius demonstrate that it is a compact tetraquark rather than a loosely bound molecule or point-like diquark-antidiquark structure. Our predictions of the doubly heavy tetraquarks may provide valuable information for future experimental searches.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the \(IJ^P=01^+\) \(bb\bar u\bar d\) state is 54 MeV below the \(\bar B\bar B\) and \(\bar B\bar B^*\) thresholds, making it stable against strong and electromagnetic decays, but this result depends on the specific relativized quark model and variational method used, and the binding energy is smaller than the more than 100 MeV predicted by most other studies.

## Method

The authors solved a four-body Schrödinger-type equation using a relativized quark model Hamiltonian that includes a relativistic kinetic energy term, a one-gluon-exchange potential with smeared Coulomb and spin-spin interactions, and a confining potential, all with momentum-dependent relativistic corrections. The spatial wave function was expanded in a set of Gaussian basis functions in Jacobi coordinates, and the generalized eigenvalue problem was solved with up to \(10^3\) basis functions to ensure numerical stability. Color, flavor, and spin matrix elements were computed for all possible configurations of the \(QQ^\prime\bar q\bar q^\prime\) systems, and mixing between configurations with the same quantum numbers was included.

## Key numbers

- The \(IJ^P=01^+\) \(bb\bar u\bar d\) state has a mass of 10550 MeV, which is 54 MeV below the \(\bar B\bar B\) and \(\bar B\bar B^*\) thresholds.
- The lowest \(cc\bar u\bar d\) state has \(IJ^P=01^+\) with a mass of 4041 MeV, which is 165 MeV above the \(DD^*\) threshold.
- The \(IJ^P=01^+\) \(bb\bar u\bar d\) state has a root mean square radius and large hidden color component consistent with a compact tetraquark, not a loosely bound molecule.
- For the \(bb\bar u\bar d\) system, the pure \(|\{bb\}^{\bar 3}_1 [\bar u \bar d]^3_0\rangle_1\) configuration has a diagonal Hamiltonian expectation of 10551 MeV, and the mixing with the \(|\{bb\}^6_0 [\bar u \bar d]^{\bar 6}_1\rangle_1\) configuration is small (off-diagonal 20 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the relativized quark model of Godfrey, Capstick, and Isgur (1985, 1986) to four-body tetraquark systems for the first time, going beyond previous nonrelativistic quark model calculations and diquark-approximation studies such as those by Ebert et al. (2007). The prediction of a stable \(bb\bar u\bar d\) tetraquark agrees with the consensus of earlier works by Karliner and Rosner (2017) and Eichten and Quigg (2017), but the binding energy of 54 MeV is smaller than the more than 100 MeV found in most other studies. The results provide specific mass predictions for future experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb.
