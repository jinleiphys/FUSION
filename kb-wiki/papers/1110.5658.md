# Fluctuating Initial Conditions and Anisotropic Flows

> In this work we study the connection between anisotropic flows and lumpy initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at 200GeV. We present comparisons between anisotropic flow coefficients and eccentricities up to sixth order, and between initial condition reference angles and azimuthal particle distribution angles. We also present a toy model to justify the lack of connection between flow coefficients and eccentricities for individual events.

## Key claim

For Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV with lumpy initial conditions, the average anisotropic flow coefficient ⟨v_n⟩ is proportional to the average eccentricity ⟨ε_n⟩ only for n=2 (elliptic flow); for higher harmonics, especially n=3 and n≥4, the relation is not linear and the event-by-event connection between v_n and ε_n is weak because the eccentricity definition does not capture internal lumpiness.

## Method

The authors used the NeXSPheRIO code, which combines NeXuS event-by-event initial conditions with 3+1D relativistic ideal hydrodynamics (SPheRIO), to simulate 1000 Au+Au events at 200 GeV divided into six centrality bins. They computed anisotropic flow coefficients v_n and reference angles Ψ_n from the Fourier expansion of the azimuthal distribution of charged particles at mid-rapidity, and compared them to eccentricity coefficients ε_n and spatial angles Φ_n from the initial energy density. They also constructed a toy model with controlled tube-like initial conditions (one tube, three inner tubes, and a configuration with ε_3≈0) using a 2+1D boost-invariant version of SPheRIO to isolate the effect of peripheral versus internal matter on triangular flow.

## Key numbers

- For the One-Tube model, triangular flow v_3^(1t) ≈ 0.0110 with ε_3^(1t) ≈ 0.067.
- For the three inner tubes configuration, v_3^(3t) ≈ 0.00095 with ε_3^(3t) ≈ 0.067.
- For the configuration with ε_3≈0 (fourth tube), v_3^(4t) ≈ 0.0116.
- The average elliptic flow ⟨v_2⟩ is proportional to ⟨ε_2⟩ except for ε_2 ≥ 0.6, where a small deviation appears.
- For triangular flow, ⟨v_3⟩ increases with ⟨ε_3⟩ for ⟨ε_3⟩ < 0.4, but decreases for ⟨ε_3⟩ ≥ 0.4.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Qiu (2011) and Qin (2010) that used smooth initial conditions and found a close relation between v_2 and ε_2 and between v_3 and ε_3; the present results confirm the n=2 relation but show a different, non-constant behavior for n=3 when using lumpy initial conditions. The tube model analysis builds on the One-Tube Model of Hama (2009) to demonstrate that peripheral matter dominates the final momentum anisotropy in central collisions, explaining why eccentricity coefficients alone cannot predict event-by-event flow. The findings enable improved understanding of how initial-state fluctuations map to final-state anisotropic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and motivate the development of more sophisticated measures of initial geometry beyond simple eccentricity.
