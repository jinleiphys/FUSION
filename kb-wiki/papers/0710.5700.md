# Photon polarization as a probe for quark-gluon plasma dynamics

> Prospects of measuring polarized photons emitted from a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) are discussed. In particular, the detection of a possible quark spin polarization in a QGP using circularly polarized photons emitted from the plasma is studied. Photons leave the QGP without further interaction and thus provide a primary probe for quark polarization within the QGP. We find that photon polarization cannot solely arise due to a possible QGP momentum space anisotropy, but may be enhanced due to it. In particular, for oblate momentum distributions and high photon energies, quark polarization is efficiently transfered to photon polarization. The role of competing sources of polarized photons in heavy-ion collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

Photon polarization cannot arise solely from momentum-space anisotropy in the quark-gluon plasma; quark spin polarization is required to produce circularly polarized photons, and the effect is enhanced for oblate momentum distributions and high photon energies.

## Method

The authors calculate the production rate of polarized photons from a quark-gluon plasma using hard 2→2 processes (Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation) and soft contributions from the photon self-energy within the Hard Loop approximation. They model global quark polarization by assuming all quarks have spins aligned along a common direction with an energy-dependent polarization degree, and treat momentum anisotropy by stretching isotropic distribution functions along the beam axis with an anisotropy parameter ξ. The hard and soft contributions are separated by an intermediate cutoff scale k* ∼ √g T, and the resulting photon rates are computed via Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers

- The quark-gluon plasma exists at temperatures T ≳ 170 MeV, with a lifetime τ ∼ 5 fm/c.
- For a typical example with E/T = 5 and ξ = 0, the photon rate is shown as a function of azimuthal angle φ, corresponding to photon energies of 1 GeV or higher for T ≳ 200 MeV.
- For an oblate anisotropy ξ = 10 and a global spin polarization p̄_rest ≈ 0.05, the visibility of circular polarization is V ≈ 0.04.
- The visibility increases with growing oblate anisotropy ξ ≳ 0 and photon energy, but decreases for prolate distributions ξ < 0.
- For energies E/T ≲ 4, the results are sensitive to the polarization model assumption; for larger energies the result is rather insensitive to the choice of intermediate cutoff scale.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of global quark polarization in non-central heavy-ion collisions by Liang and Wang (2004) and studies by Gao and others (2007) on Λ-hyperon polarization, which found an upper limit |P_Λ,Λ̄| ≲ 0.02 at RHIC. It contrasts with earlier work by Goloviznin and others (1988) that studied polarized photon emission as a geometrical surface effect at much lower photon energies. The results enable using circularly polarized photons as a direct probe of quark spin polarization in the QGP, complementary to hyperon polarization measurements which are affected by later hadronization stages. The authors also discuss competing sources including prompt photons, fragmentation photons, background photons from π⁰ decay (which cannot carry polarization due to the pion's spin zero), and photons from jets interacting with strong magnetic fields as proposed by Kharzeev (2007).
