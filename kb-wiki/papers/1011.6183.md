# Heavy quarks at RHIC and LHC within a partonic transport model

> Production and space-time evolution of heavy quarks in central and non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC are studied with the partonic transport model Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS). In addition to the initially created heavy quarks in hard parton scatterings during nucleon-nucleon collisions, secondary heavy quark production in the quark-gluon plasma is investigated and the sensitivity on various parameters is estimated. In BAMPS heavy quarks scatter with particles of the medium via elastic collisions, whose cross section is calculated with the running coupling and a more precise implementation of Debye screening. In this framework, we compute the elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor of heavy quarks and compare it to the experimental data.

## Key claim
The leading-order perturbative QCD cross section for elastic heavy quark scattering, even with running coupling and improved Debye screening, must be multiplied by a K factor of 4 to match experimental elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor data at RHIC; the authors hypothesize this factor accounts for radiative energy loss but note this assumption requires verification.

## Method
The authors used the partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings) to simulate the space-time evolution of heavy quarks in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. Heavy quarks interact with the medium via elastic collisions, with cross sections calculated using a running coupling and a Debye screening mass determined by comparison to hard thermal loop calculations. Initial heavy quark yields were obtained from PYTHIA scaled to heavy-ion collisions, while secondary production in the QGP was simulated within BAMPS using various initial gluon distribution models.

## Key numbers
- In Au+Au collisions at RHIC with sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, between 0.3 and 3.4 charm pairs are produced in the QGP, depending on the model of initial gluon distribution, charm mass, and whether a K=2 factor is employed.
- In Pb+Pb collisions at LHC with sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV, between 11 and 55 charm pairs are produced during the QGP evolution.
- In Pb+Pb collisions at LHC with sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, between 5 and 28 charm pairs are created during the QGP phase.
- The elastic gluon-heavy quark cross section increases by about a factor of 10 when using running coupling and improved Debye screening compared to constant coupling alpha_s=0.3.
- A K factor of 4 must be applied to the leading-order elastic cross section to match experimental data for heavy flavor electrons at RHIC.

## Context
This work extends the BAMPS parton cascade framework (Xu, 2004; Xu, 2007) to heavy quarks, building on prior studies of elastic energy loss by Gossiaux (2008) and Peshier (2008) that used running coupling and improved Debye screening. The results contradict the finding that leading-order elastic scattering with constant coupling is sufficient to explain heavy quark suppression, instead suggesting that radiative processes (not yet implemented for heavy quarks in BAMPS) may be necessary. The work enables future inclusion of next-to-leading order cross sections and 2-to-3 interactions for heavy quarks, as already implemented for gluons in BAMPS (Xu, 2004).
