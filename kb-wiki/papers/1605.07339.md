# Quark confinement potential examined by excitation energy of the Lambda_c and Lambda_b baryons in a quark-diquark model

> The possibility to have diquark configuration in heavy baryons, such as Lambda_c and Lambda_b, is examined by a nonrelativistic potential model with a heavy quark and a light scalar diquark. Assuming that the Lambda_c and Lambda_b baryons are composed of the heavy quark and the point-like scalar-isoscalar ud diquark, we solve the two-body Schrodinger equation with the Coulomb plus linear potential and obtain the energy spectra for the heavy baryons. Contrary to our expectation, it is found that the potential determined by the quarkonium spectra fails to reproduce the excitation spectra of the Lambda_c and Lambda_b in the quark-diquark picture, while the Lambda_c and Lambda_b spectra is reproduced with a half strength of the confinement string tension than for the quarkonium. The finite size effect of the diquark is also examined and it is found that introduction of a finite size diquark would resolve the failure of the spectrum reproduction. The Xi_c excitation energy is also calculated and is found to be smaller than Lambda_c in the quark-diquark model. This is not consistent with the experimental observation.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the confinement potential determined by quarkonium spectra (with string tension k = 0.9 GeV/fm) fails to reproduce the excitation spectra of Lambda_c and Lambda_b baryons in a quark-diquark picture, and that the observed spectra can only be reproduced with a string tension approximately half as strong (k = 0.47 GeV/fm), contradicting the expected universality of the confinement force based on color charge.

## Method

The authors solve the nonrelativistic two-body Schrodinger equation for a system composed of a heavy quark (charm or bottom) and a point-like scalar-isoscalar ud diquark, using a Coulomb plus linear confinement potential. They first confirm that the potential with alpha = 0.4 and k = 0.9 GeV/fm reproduces charmonium, bottonium, D, D_s, B, and B_s meson spectra, then apply the same potential to the quark-diquark system and compare the calculated excitation energies with experimental data for Lambda_c and Lambda_b. They also examine the finite size effect of the diquark and calculate Xi_c excitation energies using a strange scalar diquark.

## Key numbers

- The experimental spin-weighted average excitation energy of the Lambda_c 1p state from the 1s ground state is 0.330 GeV
- With meson-fitted potential parameters (alpha = 0.4, k = 0.9 GeV/fm) and diquark mass in the range 0.3 to 1.0 GeV/c^2, the calculated Lambda_c 1p excitation energy ranges from 0.5 GeV to 0.44 GeV, all above the experimental value
- The string tension that reproduces both Lambda_c and Lambda_b excitation spectra is k = 0.47 GeV/fm, approximately half of the meson value of 0.9 GeV/fm
- The strange diquark mass determined from the Lambda_c and Xi_c mass difference (0.18 GeV/c^2) is m_ds = 0.76 GeV/c^2
- The calculated Xi_c 1p excitation energy is 0.313 GeV, which is smaller than the Lambda_c 1p excitation energy, contrary to experimental observation

## Context

This work extends the quark-diquark model approach previously applied to light baryons by Goldstein (1979) and Liu (1983), and to ground state masses by Kim (2011) using QCD sum rules, by specifically examining excitation energies of heavy baryons Lambda_c and Lambda_b. The finding that the confinement string tension must be halved for quark-diquark systems compared to quark-antiquark systems challenges the expected universality of the color confinement force, and the authors suggest that introducing a finite diquark size could resolve this discrepancy. The result that Xi_c excitation energy is calculated to be smaller than Lambda_c, opposite to experiment, poses an additional puzzle for the quark-diquark picture that may require mixing with the Xi_c' state or other mechanisms beyond the simple model.
