# Pionic Degrees of Freedom in Atomic Nuclei and Quasielastic Knockout of Pions by High-Energy Electrons

> The nonlinear model of pionic condensate in nuclei by G. Preparata can be efficiently verified by investigation of the quasielastic knockout process of pions out of nuclei by high energy electrons. First, a momentum distribution (MD) of the collective pions has a bright maximum at q=0.3 Gev.Second the excitation spectrum of a recoil nucleus is concentrated at low energies E lesser than 1MeV. The results for the pion knockout from mesonic clouds of individual nucleons are absolutely different. The latter results are presented both for pion and rho-meson clouds localized on nucleons.

# Pionic Degrees of Freedom in Atomic Nuclei and Quasielastic Knockout of Pions by High-Energy Electrons

## Key claim

The nonlinear model of pionic condensate in nuclei proposed by Preparata can be verified by the quasielastic knockout process A(e,e'π)A*, provided that the momentum distribution of collective pions shows a sharp maximum at q₀ ≈ 0.3 GeV and the recoil nucleus excitation spectrum is concentrated at energies below 1 MeV; these signatures are qualitatively different from those of pions knocked out from mesonic clouds localized on individual nucleons.

## Method

The authors extend the relativistic pole approximation formalism for quasielastic knockout of mesons from nucleons (previously developed for p(e,e'π)n reactions) to nuclear targets, separating longitudinal and transverse virtual photon contributions via Rosenbluth separation. For the Preparata model, they assume the coherence domain corresponds to the whole nucleus and describe the collective pion by a standing P-wave radial wave function j₁(q₀r) confined within the nuclear radius, normalized to 0.1 pions per nucleon. They compute the momentum distribution of collective pions by Fourier transformation and compare it with the momentum distribution of pions localized on nucleons, the latter obtained by convolving the free-nucleon pion momentum distribution with the shell-model nucleon momentum distribution in the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The collective pion momentum q₀ is approximately 0.3 GeV, derived from the condition √(q₀² + m_π²) = m_Δ − m_N.
- The number of collective pions n_{i,coll} per nucleon in nuclei with N=Z is approximately 0.1 for each pion charge state (π⁺, π⁻, π⁰).
- The recoil energy of the final nucleus for collective pion knockout is E_{rec,A} ≈ q₀²/(2Am_N) ≤ 1 MeV for A ≈ 70–80.
- The recoil energy transferred to a single nucleon in localized pion knockout is E_{rec,N} ≈ q₀²/(2m_N) ≈ 50 MeV.
- The normalization constant c for the collective pion wave function is 0.027.
- The formalism is applicable at Q² values of 2–4 (GeV/c)² for the longitudinal cross section and Q² > 2 (GeV/c)² for the transverse cross section.
- The final knocked-out pion energy should be not lower than 1 GeV to avoid Δ-isobar resonance effects in the final-state interaction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic pole approximation for meson knockout from nucleons (Neudatchin et al., 1998–2000) to nuclear targets, proposing a direct experimental test of the Preparata model of pionic condensate (Preparata, 1990s), which itself extends Dicke's superradiance concept from atomic physics to nucleon-Δ-pion systems. The predicted sharp momentum distribution at q₀ ≈ 0.3 GeV and low recoil excitation energy contrast sharply with the smooth momentum distributions expected from pions localized on nucleons, which would produce recoil nucleon emission at ≈50 MeV excitation. The proposed (e,e'π) reaction complements the (π,2π) quasielastic knockout process (initiated by others around 2000) and offers cleaner extraction of momentum distributions than (γ,π) reactions, where multiple diagram amplitudes interfere. The approach enables future experimental searches for collective pionic degrees of freedom in medium-mass nuclei using electron beams of a few GeV.
