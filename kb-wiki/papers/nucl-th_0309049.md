# Schwinger tunneling and thermal character of hadron spectra

> It is shown that an oscillatory character of the solutions of the collisionless kinetic equations describing production of the quark-gluon plasma in strong color fields leads to the exponential (thermal-like) transverse-momentum spectra of partons produced in the soft region (100 MeV < pt < 1 GeV). In addition, the production of partons in the very soft region (pt < 100 MeV) is clearly enhanced above the thermal-like background.

# Schwinger tunneling and thermal character of hadron spectra

## Key claim

The oscillatory time-dependence of color fields in collisionless kinetic equations for quark-gluon plasma production leads to exponential (thermal-like) transverse-momentum spectra in the soft region (100 MeV < pT < 1 GeV), without any rescattering or thermalization processes. The mechanism works only for string tensions larger than the elementary string tension, making it applicable to heavy-ion collisions but not to elementary systems such as electron-positron annihilations.

## Method

The author solves semi-classical collisionless kinetic equations for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons in the abelian dominance approximation, using one-dimensional boost-invariant geometry with time-dependent color fields obtained from Gauss law. The transverse-momentum spectra are computed by integrating the Schwinger tunneling production rate over proper time, where the force F acting on partons varies in time due to screening and oscillations of the color field.

## Key numbers

- The exponential fit to the computed spectrum for initial color charge k=2 gives an inverse-slope parameter T = 220 MeV, fitted at pT = 350 MeV.
- The exponential fit for k=3 gives T = 270 MeV, fitted at pT = 350 MeV.
- For k=2, the mean transverse momentum is ⟨pT⟩ = 376 MeV and the rapidity density per unit transverse area is dN/dy = 1.2.
- For k=3, the mean transverse momentum is ⟨pT⟩ = 426 MeV and the rapidity density per unit transverse area is dN/dy = 1.9.
- The rough estimate using the time average of F gives T = 258 MeV for k=2 and T = 307 MeV for k=3.
- The initial string tension is set to σg = 3 GeV/fm.
- The exponential behavior holds in the soft region 100 MeV < pT < 1 GeV, with enhancement above the exponential background for pT < 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the idea of Bialas (2000) that fluctuations of the string tension in the Schwinger formula can produce thermal-like spectra, but replaces the static Gaussian distribution of string tension with a time-dependent force arising naturally from oscillatory solutions of kinetic equations. The approach contrasts with thermal models that attribute the exponential spectra observed at RHIC to rescattering processes, and with earlier work by the author and collaborators (BCDFosc, BCapp, DFhq) that studied mean pT but not the full transverse-momentum spectra. The results enable a dynamical explanation of fast thermalization in heavy-ion collisions without invoking collision integrals, while the enhancement at very low pT matches experimental pion spectra usually attributed to resonance decays.
