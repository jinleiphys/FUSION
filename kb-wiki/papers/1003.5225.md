# Analysis of bulk and surface contributions in the neutron skin of nuclei

> The neutron skin thickness of nuclei is a sensitive probe of the nuclear symmetry energy having multiple implications for nuclear and astrophysical studies. However, precision measurements of this observable are difficult. The analysis of the experimental data may imply some assumptions about the bulk or surface nature of the formation of the neutron skin. Here, we study the bulk or surface character of neutron skins of nuclei following from calculations with Gogny, Skyrme, and covariant nuclear mean-field interactions. These interactions are successful in describing nuclear charge radii and binding energies but predict different values for neutron skins. We perform the study by fitting two-parameter Fermi distributions to the calculated self-consistent neutron and proton densities. We note that the equivalent sharp radius is a more suitable reference quantity than the half-density radius parameter of the Fermi distributions to discern between the bulk and surface contributions in neutron skins. We present calculations for nuclei in the stability valley and for the isotopic chains of Sn and Pb.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness in nuclei can arise from either a difference in the bulk radii of neutrons and protons, a difference in their surface diffuseness, or a combination of both; the authors find that the equivalent sharp radius is a more suitable reference quantity than the half-density radius for separating these contributions, and that mean-field models predict an intermediate character for the neutron skin in stable nuclei, with a preference for the surface-diffuseness mechanism.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent mean-field calculations using the Gogny D1S, Skyrme SLy4, NL3, and FSUGold interactions to obtain neutron and proton densities for stable nuclei and for isotopic chains of Sn and Pb. They fitted two-parameter Fermi (2pF) distributions to these densities by matching the quadratic and quartic radial moments, then decomposed the neutron skin thickness into bulk and surface contributions using the equivalent sharp radius and surface width derived from the 2pF parameters.

## Key numbers

* For $^{208}$Pb, the experimental neutron skin thickness of $\Delta R_{np}=0.16$ fm (from antiprotonic x-ray data) can be reproduced by 2pF distributions with $C_n-C_p$ ranging from 0.00 fm (with $a_n-a_p=0.127$ fm) to 0.10 fm (with $a_n-a_p=0.073$ fm).
* The Gogny D1S fit to $^{208}$Pb gives $C_n-C_p=0.041$ fm and $a_n-a_p=0.081$ fm; the Skyrme SLy4 fit gives $C_n-C_p=0.072$ fm and $a_n-a_p=0.085$ fm.
* The proton 2pF parameters for $^{208}$Pb from experimental charge density are $C_p=6.704$ fm and $a_p=0.438$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutron skin formation from antiprotonic atom experiments (Trzcinska et al., 2004; Jastrzebski et al., 2004), which had suggested a preference for the "neutron halo-type" distribution (increased diffuseness) over the "neutron skin-type" (increased bulk radius). The authors provide a theoretical framework using successful mean-field models to quantify the bulk versus surface decomposition, enabling future comparisons with parity-violating electron scattering experiments and with droplet model predictions (Swinarski, 2005).
