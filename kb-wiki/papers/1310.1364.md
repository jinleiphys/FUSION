# The influence of the symmetry energy on the cone-azimuthal emission

> In the framework of the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, effects of the symmetry energy on the evolutions of free n/p ratio and charged pion ratio in the semi-central collision of $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at an incident beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon are studied. At the beginning of the reaction (before 11 fm/c) they are both affected by the low-density behavior of the symmetry energy but soon after are affected by the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy after nuclei are compressed (after 11 fm/c) and the effects of the symmetry energy are generally smaller compared with the central collision case. Interestingly, their dependences on the symmetry energy are shown to arise with increase of cone-azimuthal angle of the emitted particles. In the direction perpendicular to the reaction plane, the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio or free n/p ratio especially at high kinetic energies exhibits significant sensitivity to the symmetry energy.

# The influence of the symmetry energy on the cone-azimuthal emission

## Key claim
In semi-central collisions of 197Au+197Au at 400 MeV/nucleon, the sensitivity of the free neutron/proton ratio and the charged pion ratio to the nuclear symmetry energy increases with the cone-azimuthal angle of emitted particles, and is largest in the direction perpendicular to the reaction plane, especially at high kinetic energies. However, the overall effect of the symmetry energy on these ratios is smaller in semi-central collisions than in central collisions, and the sensitivity only becomes evident when selecting particles by their emission angle.

## Method
The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with a momentum-dependent mean-field potential (MDI) to simulate 400,000 semi-central events of 197Au+197Au at 400 MeV/nucleon with an impact parameter of b=7 fm. They compared two parametrizations of the symmetry energy: a stiff case (x=0) and a soft case (x=1), while keeping symmetric nuclear matter properties fixed. Pions were produced via decay of Delta(1232) and N*(1440) resonances, and the authors analyzed the free n/p ratio and the (pi-/pi+)-like ratio as functions of time, azimuthal angle, and kinetic energy.

## Key numbers
- Incident beam energy: 400 MeV/nucleon for 197Au+197Au collisions
- Impact parameter: b = 7 fm (semi-central)
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is 211 MeV
- The symmetry energy effect on the total charged pion ratio is about 5%, but in the direction perpendicular to the reaction plane at high kinetic energies the effect can be more than 20%
- The crossover time for the symmetry energy dependence of both ratios is approximately 11 fm/c

## Context
This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy effects in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the work of Yong et al. and Msup (referenced as [yongso,msup]) which showed that particles emitted perpendicular to the reaction plane carry information from the high-density squeeze-out region. It builds on earlier proposals of observables such as the free n/p ratio (Li 2004) and the pi-/pi+ ratio (Li 2002, Gai 2004) as probes of the symmetry energy. The study addresses the confusion in the field where different analyses of FOPI data (Reisdorf 2007) have led to conflicting conclusions about whether the symmetry energy is stiff (Feng 2010, Russotto 2011) or soft (Xiao 2009, Xie 2013) at suprasaturation densities. The results suggest that angle-selected measurements, particularly at high kinetic energies perpendicular to the reaction plane, could provide a more discriminating probe to constrain the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy.
