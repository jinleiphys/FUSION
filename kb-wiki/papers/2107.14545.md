# Chiral condensates for neutron stars in hadron-quark crossover: from a parity doublet nucleon model to an NJL quark model

> We study the chiral condensates in neutron star matter from nuclear to quark matter domain. We describe nuclear matter with a parity doublet model (PDM), quark matter with the Nambu--Jona-Lasino (NJL) model, and a matter at the intermediate density by interpolating nuclear and quark matter equations of state. The model parameters are constrained by nuclear physics and neutron star observations. Various condensates in the interpolated domain are estimated from the chemical potential dependence of the condensates at the boundaries of the interpolation. The use of the PDM with substantial chiral invariant mass ($m_0 \gtrsim 500$ MeV, which is favored by the neutron star observations) predicts the mild chiral restoration, and the significant chiral condensate remains to baryon density $n_B \sim 2-3n_0$ ($n_0\simeq 0.16\,{\rm fm}^{-3}$: nuclear saturation density), smoothly approaching the NJL predictions for the color-flavor-locked phase at $n_B \gtrsim 5n_0$. The same method is applied to estimate diquark condensates, number densities of up-, down- and strange-quarks, and the lepton fraction. In our descriptions the chiral restoration in the interpolated domain proceeds with two conceptually distinct chiral restoration effects; the first is associated with the positive scalar density in a nucleon, relevant in dilute regime, and the other primarily arises from the modification of the quark Dirac sea, which is triggered by the growth of the quark Fermi sea. We discuss several qualitative conjectures to interpolate the microphysics in nuclear and quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in neutron star matter, chiral restoration proceeds through two conceptually distinct mechanisms, one driven by the scalar density inside nucleons (dominant at low density) and the other by modifications of the quark Dirac sea triggered by the growth of the quark Fermi sea (dominant at high density), and that using a parity doublet model with a chiral invariant mass \(m_0 \gtrsim 500\) MeV (favored by neutron star observations) predicts only mild chiral restoration, with significant chiral condensate persisting up to baryon densities \(n_B \sim 2-3n_0\).

## Method

The authors describe nuclear matter using a parity doublet model (PDM) for nucleons coupled to meson potentials, and quark matter using a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector repulsion and diquark attraction (to describe the color-flavor-locked phase). For the intermediate density region (\(n_B \simeq 2-5n_0\)), they interpolate the equations of state from the nuclear and quark matter models, and estimate various condensates (chiral, diquark, quark number densities, lepton fraction) by differentiating the unified generating functional for the pressure with respect to external fields coupled to those condensates.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density is \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\,{\rm fm}^{-3}\).
- For the PDM with \(m_0 \gtrsim 500\) MeV, significant chiral condensate remains to baryon density \(n_B \sim 2-3n_0\).
- The NJL model predictions for the color-flavor-locked phase are approached at \(n_B \gtrsim 5n_0\).
- The chiral invariant mass values studied are \(m_0 = 500, 600, 700, 800, 900\) MeV; the corresponding nucleon sigma term \(\Sigma_N\) ranges from 91.9 MeV (for \(m_0=500\) MeV) to 75.7 MeV (for \(m_0=900\) MeV).
- The scalar meson mass in the PDM ranges from \(m_\sigma \simeq 396\) MeV (for \(m_0=500\) MeV) to \(m_\sigma \simeq 271\) MeV (for \(m_0=900\) MeV).
- The effective quark masses at \(n_B \sim 5n_0\) in the NJL model are \(\sim 50\) MeV for up- and down-quarks and \(\sim 300\) MeV for strange quarks.
- The strangeness sigma-term \(\Sigma_{sN}\) from lattice QCD is estimated to range from \(\simeq 17\) MeV to \(\simeq 42\) MeV, giving \(N_s \simeq 0.2-0.4\) (about \(0.01-0.05\) times the light-quark scalar charge \(N_\sigma\)).

## Context

This work extends the three-window interpolation framework developed by Masuda et al. (2012, 2015) and Kojo et al. (2014), which previously focused only on equations of state, to now compute microscopic condensates in the interpolated domain. It contrasts with purely hadronic models (e.g., traditional \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) models by Walecka (1974) and Serot (1984)) by incorporating a parity doublet structure with a chiral invariant mass, which leads to milder chiral restoration and smaller neutron star radii (12–13 km rather than 13–14 km, as shown in Minamikawa et al. (2020)). The results enable future studies of microphysics in the hadron-quark crossover region, including the behavior of diquark condensates and strangeness fractions that differ from purely hadronic predictions.
