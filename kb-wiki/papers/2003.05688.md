# $D^{(\ast)}N$ interaction and the structure of $Σ_c(2800)$ and $Λ_c(2940)$ in chiral effective field theory

> We study the $DN$ and $D^\ast N$ interactions to probe the inner structure of $Σ_c(2800)$ and $Λ_c(2940)$ with the chiral effective field theory to the next-to-leading order. We consider the contact term, one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange contributions to characterize the short-, long- and mid-range interactions of the $D^{(\ast)}N$ systems. The low energy constants of the $D^{(\ast)}N$ systems are related to those of the $N\bar{N}$ interaction with quark level Lagrangian that inspired by the resonance saturation model. The $Δ(1232)$ degree of freedom is also included in the loop diagrams. The attractive potential in the $[DN]_{J=1/2}^{I=1}$ channel is too weak to form bound state, which indicates the explanation of $Σ_c(2800)$ as the compact charmed baryon is more reasonable. Meanwhile, the potentials of the isoscalar channels are deep enough to yield the molecular states. We obtain the masses of the $[DN]_{J=1/2}^{I=0}$, $[D^\ast N]_{J=1/2}^{I=0}$ and $[D^\ast N]_{J=3/2}^{I=0}$ systems to be $2792.0$ MeV, $2943.6$ MeV and $2938.4$ MeV, respectively. The $Λ_c(2940)$ is probably the isoscalar $D^\ast N$ molecule considering its low mass puzzle. Besides, the $Λ_c(2940)$ signal might contain the spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ and spin-$\frac{3}{2}$ two structures, which can qualitatively explain the significant decay ratio to $D^0p$ and $Σ_cπ$. We also study the $\bar{B}^{(\ast)}N$ systems and predict the possible molecular states in the isoscalar channels. We hope experimentalists could hunt for the open charmed molecular pentaquarks in the $Λ_c^+π^+π^-$ final state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\Sigma_c(2800)$ is more likely a compact charmed baryon rather than a $DN$ molecule, while the $\Lambda_c(2940)$ is probably an isoscalar $D^\ast N$ molecule, but this conclusion depends on the inclusion of $\Delta(1232)$ contributions and the specific cutoff choice, with the molecular binding energies being sensitive to these details.

## Method

The authors constructed $DN$ and $D^\ast N$ interactions using chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order, including contact terms, one-pion-exchange, and two-pion-exchange contributions with $\Delta(1232)$ degrees of freedom in loop diagrams. The low energy constants for the $D^{(\ast)}N$ systems were estimated by relating them to those of the $N\bar{N}$ interaction via a quark-level Lagrangian inspired by the resonance saturation model. The momentum-space potentials were Fourier-transformed to coordinate space with a Gaussian regulator, and the Schrodinger equation was solved to search for bound states.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the $[DN]_{J=1/2}^{I=0}$ bound state: 2792.0 MeV, with binding energy approximately -11.1 MeV
- Mass of the $[D^\ast N]_{J=1/2}^{I=0}$ bound state: 2943.6 MeV, with binding energy approximately -1.5 MeV
- Mass of the $[D^\ast N]_{J=3/2}^{I=0}$ bound state: 2938.4 MeV, with binding energy approximately -6.7 MeV
- The $\Lambda_c(2940)$ mass measured by BaBar: 2939.8 ± 1.3 ± 1.0 MeV; by Belle: 2938.0 ± 1.3^{+2.0}_{-4.0} MeV; by LHCb: 2944.8^{+3.5}_{-2.5} ± 0.4^{+0.1}_{-4.6} MeV
- The $\Lambda_c(2940)$ width measured by BaBar: 17.5 ± 5.2 ± 5.9 MeV; by Belle: 13^{+8+27}_{-5-7} MeV; by LHCb: 27.7^{+8.2}_{-6.0} ± 0.9^{+5.2}_{-10.4} MeV
- Pion decay constant used: $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV
- Axial-vector coupling constant: $g_a \simeq 1.29$
- Charmed meson axial coupling: $g \simeq -0.59$
- $\Delta(1232)$-nucleon mass splitting: $\delta_a = m_\Delta - m_N$
- $D^\ast$-$D$ mass splitting: $\delta_b = m_{D^\ast} - m_D$

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach previously applied to $NN$ interactions (Weinberg, 1990; Epelbaum, 2008; Machleidt, 2011) and heavy-flavor systems such as $BB^\ast$ and $B^\ast B^\ast$ bound states (Wang, 2018) and pentaquarks (Wang, 2019) to the $D^{(\ast)}N$ systems. It contradicts the molecular interpretation of $\Sigma_c(2800)$ previously proposed in studies such as Zhang (2012) and Zhao (2016), while supporting the molecular explanation for $\Lambda_c(2940)$ first suggested by He (2006) and studied further by Ortega (2012) and others. The results enable future experimental searches for open-charmed molecular pentaquarks in the $\Lambda_c^+\pi^+\pi^-$ final state and provide predictions for $\bar{B}^{(\ast)}N$ molecular states in isoscalar channels.
