# Long-wavelength phonons in the crystalline and pasta phases of neutron-star crusts

> We study the long-wavelength excitations of the inner crust of neutron stars, considering three phases: cubic crystal at low densities, rods and plates near the core-crust transition. To describe the phonons, we write an effective Lagrangian density in terms of the coarse-grained phase of the neutron superfluid gap and of the average displacement field of the clusters. The kinetic energy, including the entrainment of the neutron gas by the clusters, is obtained within a superfluid hydrodynamics approach. The potential energy is determined from a model where clusters and neutron gas are considered in phase coexistence, augmented by the elasticity of the lattice due to Coulomb and surface effects. All three phases show strong anisotropy, i.e., angle dependence of the phonon velocities. Consequences for the specific heat at low temperature are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that long-wavelength phonons in neutron-star crusts exhibit strong anisotropy in all three phases studied (cubic crystal, rods, and plates), with the angle dependence of phonon velocities being a general feature that the abstract presents as a finding rather than a prediction requiring specific model assumptions about entrainment and elasticity.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian density using coarse-grained variables: the averaged phase of the neutron superfluid gap and the average displacement field of the clusters. Kinetic energy was computed via superfluid hydrodynamics including entrainment effects from Martin (2016), while potential energy combined a phase-coexistence model with Coulomb and surface contributions to determine elastic constants for each pasta phase geometry.

## Key numbers
- For the BCC crystalline phase: elastic constants \(C_{11}-C_{12} = 0.06545\, C_0\) and \(C_{44} = 0.2437\, C_0\), where \(C_0 = \pi e^2 \bar{n}_p^2 R_{\mathrm{WS}}^2\)
- For the lasagne phase: elastic coefficients \(B_{11} = \frac{4}{3} (1-6u+6u^2) C_0\), \(B_{13} = \frac{4}{3} (-1+2u^2) C_0\), and \(B_{33} = \frac{4}{3} u^2 C_0\)
- For the spaghetti phase: shear modulus in the \(xy\) plane \(C_{66} = \frac{1}{4} C_0\)
- For the lasagne phase: fourth-order shear coefficient \(K_1 = \frac{4}{45}\pi e^2\bar{n}_p^2 R_{\mathrm{WS}}^4 (1-u)^2(1+2u-2u^2)\)
- Electron bulk modulus \(K = (\pi/3)^{2/3} \hbar c\, \bar{n}_p^{4/3}\)

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach of Cirigliano (2011) to anisotropic pasta phases, incorporating entrainment effects from the hydrodynamic model of Martin (2016). The elastic constants for the crystalline phase build on calculations by Fuchs (1936) and Ogata (1990), while the pasta-phase elasticity follows the framework of Pethick (1998). The results enable improved calculations of low-temperature specific heat in neutron-star crusts, which is relevant for cooling models and glitch physics.
