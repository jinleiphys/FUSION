# Vector meson mass in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum

> We calculate the mass of the vector meson in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum. This is accomplished by separating the four quark operators appearing in the vector and axial vector meson sum rules into chiral symmetric and symmetry breaking parts depending on the contribution of the fermion zero modes. We then identify each part from the fit to the vector and axial vector meson masses. By taking the chiral symmetry breaking part to be zero while keeping the symmetric operator to the vacuum value, we find that the chiral symmetric part of the vector and axial vector meson mass to be between 550 and 600 MeV. This demonstrates that chiral symmetry breaking, while responsible for the mass difference between chiral partner, accounts only for a small fraction of the symmetric part of the mass.

# Vector meson mass in the chiral symmetry restored vacuum

## Key claim

The chiral symmetric part of the vector and axial vector meson mass lies between 550 and 600 MeV, demonstrating that chiral symmetry breaking accounts only for the mass difference between chiral partners but contributes only a small fraction of the symmetric part of the mass. This result depends on separating four-quark operators into chiral symmetric and symmetry breaking parts based on fermion zero mode contributions, then setting the symmetry breaking part to zero while keeping the symmetric operator at its vacuum value.

## Method

The authors separate the four-quark operators appearing in the vector and axial vector meson QCD sum rules into chiral symmetric and symmetry breaking parts, using the Banks-Casher relation to identify which operators receive contributions from fermion zero modes. They fit the magnitudes of these operators to the known vacuum masses of the rho and a1 mesons using Borel transformed QCD sum rules with spectral functions including both pole and continuum contributions, then compute the common mass by setting the chiral symmetry breaking operator B to zero while keeping the symmetric operator S at its vacuum value.

## Key numbers

- Chiral symmetric part of the vector and axial vector meson mass: between 550 and 600 MeV
- For the delta function pole approximation: symmetric mass = 550 MeV (with threshold s0 = 1.12 GeV^2 for rho and 0.75 GeV^2 for a1 in the symmetric limit)
- For Breit-Wigner pole with rho width 150 MeV and a1 width 200 MeV (B-W^1): symmetric mass = 580 MeV
- For Breit-Wigner pole with rho width 150 MeV and a1 width 400 MeV (B-W^2): symmetric mass = 600 MeV
- Vacuum four-quark condensate values: M_rho = 1.72 x 10^-3 GeV^6 (delta), M_a1 = -1.25 x 10^-3 GeV^6 (delta)
- Chiral symmetry breaking operator B = 7.42 x 10^-4 GeV^6 (delta), symmetric operator S = 5.65 x 10^-4 GeV^6 (delta)
- Input parameters: alpha_s = 0.36, quark condensate = (-0.23 GeV)^3, gluon condensate = (0.35 GeV)^4

## Context

This work extends the in-medium QCD sum rule approach of Hatsuda (1991) for light vector mesons, which assumed vacuum saturation making four-quark condensates proportional to the square of the quark condensate. The authors improve upon this by separating chiral symmetric and breaking contributions using the Banks-Casher formula (Banks and Casher, 1980) and the operator decomposition method of Cohen (1996) and Lee (2019). The result enables future studies of hadron masses in chiral symmetry restored conditions relevant to heavy ion collisions at J-PARC (Ohnishi, 2019) and other facilities, and provides a quantitative benchmark for lattice QCD studies of hadronic correlation functions after cooling, as done by Chu (1994).
