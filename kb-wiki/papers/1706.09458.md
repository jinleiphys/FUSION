# The Efimov effect for heteronuclear three-body systems at positive scattering length and finite temperature

> We study the recombination process of three atoms scattering into an atom and diatomic molecule in heteronuclear mixtures of ultracold atomic gases with large and positive interspecies scattering length at finite temperature. We calculate the temperature dependence of the three-body recombination rates by extracting universal scaling functions that parametrize the energy dependence of the scattering matrix. We compare our results to experimental data for the 40K-87Rb mixture and make a prediction for 6Li-87Rb. We find that contributions from higher partial wave channels significantly impact the total rate and, in systems with particularly large mass imbalance, can even obliterate the recombination minima associated with the Efimov effect.

## Key claim

The paper finds that higher partial wave contributions to three-body recombination rates in heteronuclear mixtures with large positive interspecies scattering length significantly impact the total rate at finite temperature, and in systems with particularly large mass imbalance, these contributions can even obliterate the recombination minima associated with the Efimov effect. This contradicts the suggestion by D'Incao and Esry (2006) that the dominant contribution comes from the J=0 channel at all energies.

## Method

The authors solved the modified Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation to obtain elastic scattering phase shifts for atom-diatom scattering in heteronuclear A1A2A2 systems with large positive interspecies scattering length. They extracted universal scaling functions s_ij(x) that parametrize the energy dependence of the S-matrix, then used these to calculate the three-body recombination rate K3(E) for each partial wave J. Finally, they performed a thermal average over K3(E) to obtain the recombination rate constant alpha_T at finite temperature for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For the 40K-87Rb system, the J=1 partial wave becomes larger than the S-wave contribution around energy E_D, corresponding to temperature T_KRb = 0.3 E_D/k_B approximately 0.1 (a/a_0)^(-2) K, where a_0 is the Bohr radius
- For the 6Li-87Rb system, higher partial waves become significant at very low energy approximately 10^(-3) E_D, corresponding to temperature T_LiRb = 10^(-3) E_D/k_B approximately 0.015 (a/a_0)^(-2) K
- The recombination minimum for 40K-87Rb was determined to be at a_*0 approximately 5000 a_0, using the experimental value a_* = 230 a_0 +/- 30 a_0 from Bloom et al. (2013)
- For 6Li-87Rb, the recombination minimum position was a_*0 approximately 610 a_0, derived from |a_-| = 1870 a_0 +/- 121 a_0 from Maier et al. (2015)
- The inelasticity parameter for 40K-87Rb was eta_* = 0.05 +/- 0.02 from Helfrich et al. (2010), and for 6Li-87Rb was eta_* = 0.2 from Petrov and Werner (2015)
- A temperature of approximately 10 nK would be necessary to clearly observe recombination minima in the 40K-87Rb experiment

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature S-matrix formalism developed by Braaten et al. (2008) for three-boson systems to heteronuclear mixtures, building on the zero-temperature effective-field-theory analysis of Helfrich et al. (2010). The results are compared to experimental data for the 40K-87Rb mixture from Bloom et al. (2013) and provide predictions for 6Li-87Rb. The paper enables future experimental searches for Efimov features in heteronuclear systems by identifying the temperature and scattering length regimes where recombination minima can be observed, and it suggests that systems with larger mass imbalance (such as 6Li-87Rb or 6Li-133Cs) may be more favorable for observing universal behavior despite requiring lower temperatures.
