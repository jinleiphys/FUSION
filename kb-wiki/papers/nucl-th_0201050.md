# Baryon Wave Functions in Covariant Relativistic Quark Models

> We derive covariant baryon wave functions for arbitrary Lorentz boosts. Modeling baryons as quark-diquark systems, we reduce their manifestly covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation to a covariant 3-dimensional form by projecting on the relative quark-diquark energy. Guided by a phenomenological multigluon exchange representation of a covariant confining kernel, we derive for practical applications explicit solutions for harmonic confinement and for the MIT Bag Model. We briefly comment on the interplay of boosts and center-of-mass corrections in relativistic quark models.

# Baryon Wave Functions in Covariant Relativistic Quark Models

## Key claim
Covariant baryon wave functions for arbitrary Lorentz boosts can be derived analytically by modeling baryons as quark-diquark systems and projecting the Bethe-Salpeter equation onto a covariant three-dimensional form, but the approach is limited by the phenomenological nature of the confining kernel, whose full momentum dependence remains unknown.

## Method
The author reduces the manifestly covariant four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation for a quark-diquark system to a covariant three-dimensional form by projecting on the relative quark-diquark energy using a delta function constraint. A phenomenological multigluon exchange kernel is assumed, parametrized as a superposition of weighted gluon exchange contributions with power n, which after Fourier transformation yields confinement potentials proportional to r^(2n). The resulting Dirac equation for the large component is solved analytically for two limiting cases: harmonic confinement (n=2) and the MIT Bag Model (n approaching infinity).

## Key numbers
- For harmonic confinement, the ground state energy is given by epsilon_b(P) approximately equal to (1 + P^2/M^2) * sqrt(V_c)/m, where V_c is proportional to 1/R^4.
- The effective size parameter for the boosted harmonic oscillator wavefunction is a(P)^2 = (M^2/(P^2 + M^2)) * a^2, showing Lorentz quenching along the boost direction.
- Simple rescaling approximations for the wavefunction break down completely for boost momenta of P/M greater than or equal to 1.
- For very small boost momenta, approximate rescaling formulas involve a factor of 1/(sqrt(3) * lambda(P)) where lambda(P) = M/sqrt(M^2 + P^2).

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to incorporate Lorentz boosts into baryon wavefunctions, which previously relied on ad hoc kinematical prescriptions for rescaling coordinates along the momentum transfer direction (references 26-27). The approach contrasts with light-cone formulations (reference 25) which suffer from loss of strict rotational invariance. The paper enables practical calculations of form factors at large momentum transfers (typically 1 GeV/c and beyond) for experiments at MAMI, ELSA, MIT, JLAB, DESY, COSY, and CELSIUS, though the author notes that a deeper analytical understanding of confinement from QCD is required, pointing to recent work on confinement in Coulomb gauge (reference 38) and meron solutions (reference 39).
