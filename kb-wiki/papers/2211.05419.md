# The proton radius and its relatives -- much ado about nothing?

> I summarize the dispersion-theoretical analysis of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors. Special emphasis is given on the extraction of the proton charge radius and its relatives, the proton magnetic radius as well as the neutron magnetic radius. Some recent work on the hyperfine splitting in leptonic hydrogen and on radiative corrections to muon-proton scattering is also discussed. Some views on future studies are given.

# Wiki Page: The proton radius and its relatives – much ado about nothing?

## Key claim
Dispersion-relation (DR) analysis of electron-proton scattering data and timelike form factor data has consistently yielded a "small" proton charge radius near 0.84 fm since 1976, and the so-called proton radius puzzle was anticipated in 2007 when DR analysis could not accommodate the then-accepted larger radius from electronic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements.

## Method
The author performs a dispersion-theoretical analysis of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, reconstructing spectral functions from pion-nucleon scattering data, the pion vector form factor, and vector meson contributions (narrow poles, broad poles, and continua) constrained by perturbative QCD behavior. The spectral functions are fitted to the world data set of about 1800 data points from electron-proton scattering in the spacelike region and e+e- ↔ pp, nn reactions in the timelike region. Statistical uncertainties are obtained via bootstrap, and systematic uncertainties by varying the number of vector meson poles such that total χ² changes by no more than 1%.

## Key numbers
- Proton charge radius: r_E^p = 0.840^{+0.003}_{-0.002}{}^{+0.002}_{-0.002} fm (statistical and systematic errors)
- Proton magnetic radius: r_M^p = 0.849^{+0.003}_{-0.003}{}^{+0.001}_{-0.004} fm
- Neutron magnetic radius: r_M^n = 0.864^{+0.004}_{-0.004}{}^{+0.006}_{-0.001} fm
- Zemach radius of the proton: r_Z^p = 1.054^{+0.003}_{-0.002}{}^{+0.000}_{-0.001} fm
- Recoil contribution to muonic hydrogen hyperfine splitting: Δ_recoil^{μH} = (837.6^{+2.8}_{-1.0}) × 10^{-6} = 837.6^{+2.8}_{-1.0} ppm
- Recoil contribution to electronic hydrogen hyperfine splitting: Δ_recoil^{eH} = (526.9^{+1.7}_{-0.4}) × 10^{-8}
- Neutron charge radius squared (input from ed scattering using chiral nuclear EFT): (r_E^n)^2 = -0.105^{+0.005}_{-0.006} fm²
- Isovector electric radius from DR: r_E^V = 0.900(2)(2) fm
- Isovector magnetic radius from DR: r_M^V = 0.854(1)(3) fm

## Context
This work extends the dispersion-theoretical approach pioneered by Frazer (1959) and Hohler (1976) and most recently updated by Lin (2021). The DR results consistently give a small proton charge radius (≈0.84 fm), in agreement with the CREMA muonic hydrogen measurement by Antognini (2013) and the present CODATA value of 0.8414(19) fm, while contradicting earlier larger values from electronic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements such as Fleurbaey (2018) with 0.877(13) fm. The analysis enables precise predictions for upcoming experiments including PRad-II at Jefferson Lab, the MUSE experiment at PSI, and the AMBER experiment at CERN, and provides high-precision recoil corrections for hyperfine splitting measurements in muonic hydrogen that are being pursued by three experimental collaborations aiming at 1-10 ppm relative accuracy.
