# Solitons and wavelets: Scale analysis and bases

> We use a one-scale similarity analysis which gives specific relations between the velocity, amplitude and width of localized solutions of nonlinear differential equations, whose exact solutions are generally difficult to obtain. We also introduce kink-antikink compact solutions for the nonlinear-nonlinear dispersion K(2,2) equation, and we construct a basis of scaling functions similar with those used in the multiresolution analysis. These approaches are useful in describing nonlinear structures and patterns, as well as in the derivation of the time evolution of initial data for nonlinear equations with finite wavelength soliton solutions.

# Solitons and wavelets: Scale analysis and bases

## Key claim

The paper proposes a one-scale similarity analysis (OSA) that provides algebraic relations between the velocity, amplitude, and width of localized solutions of nonlinear partial differential equations without requiring explicit knowledge of the exact solutions, but the method may fail for strictly monotonic solutions or solutions with singularities.

## Method

The authors substitute traveling wave ansatz into the nonlinear PDE, replacing time derivatives with spatial derivatives via the traveling wave transformation, then replace the function and its spatial derivatives with their characteristic scales (amplitude A and width L) to obtain an algebraic equation relating A, L, and velocity V. They apply this one-scale analysis (OSA) to three classes of nonlinear PDEs, dispersive, diffusive, and dispersive-diffusive equations, and compare the predicted relations with known exact solutions. Additionally, they construct kink-antikink compact solutions for the K(2,2) equation and build a nonlinear basis of scaling functions inspired by multiresolution analysis.

## Key numbers

* For the KdV equation, the OSA predicts that when velocity is proportional to amplitude (V = 2A), the width L becomes a function of amplitude only, matching the exact soliton relation.
* For the K(2,2) equation compacton solution, the amplitude is A = 4V/3 and the half-width is L = 4 (dimensionless units), independent of amplitude.
* For the K(2,2) equation, the compacton solution is given by η_c(x-Vt) = (4V/3) cos²[(x-Vt)/4] for |x-Vt| < 2π and zero otherwise.
* For the generalized KdV equation with quadratic dispersion K(2,2), the kink-antikink solution has a flat region of arbitrary length λ and the compacton width remains L = 4.
* For the Gross-Pitaevski equation, the half-width of the exact nonstationary solution is L = 1/√(v_c² - p²), where v_c = √(1-aV) is the Landau critical velocity.

## Context

This work extends the study of compacton solutions introduced by Rosenau (1993) and the multiresolution analysis framework from wavelet theory, providing a systematic method to extract parameter relations for nonlinear PDEs where inverse scattering or group symmetry methods are not applicable. The approach enables prediction of solution behavior for equations like K(n,m) where exact solutions are unknown, and the construction of nonlinear bases from K(2,2) compactons offers a new tool for analyzing multi-scale nonlinear structures in nuclear physics, nonlinear optics, and fluid dynamics. The method is distinct from traditional similarity (dimensional) analysis because it yields relations with numeric coefficients rather than just power-law dependencies.
