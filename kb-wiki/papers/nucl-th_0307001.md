# Correlation Between the Deuteron Characteristics and the Low-energy Triplet np Scattering Parameters

> The correlation relationship between the deuteron asymptotic normalization constant, $A_{S}$, and the triplet np scattering length, $a_{t}$, is investigated. It is found that 99.7% of the asymptotic constant $A_{S}$ is determined by the scattering length $a_{t}$. It is shown that the linear correlation relationship between the quantities $A_{S}^{-2}$ and $1/a_{t}$ provides a good test of correctness of various models of nucleon-nucleon interaction. It is revealed that, for the normalization constant $A_{S}$ and for the root-mean-square deuteron radius $r_{d}$, the results obtained with the experimental value recommended at present for the triplet scattering length $a_{t}$ are exaggerated with respect to their experimental counterparts. By using the latest experimental phase shifts of Arndt et al., we obtain, for the low-energy scattering parameters ($a_{t}$, $r_{t}$, $P_{t}$) and for the deuteron characteristics ($A_{S}$, $r_{d}$), results that comply well with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that 99.7% of the deuteron asymptotic normalization constant \(A_{S}\) is determined solely by the triplet np scattering length \(a_{t}\), and that the currently recommended experimental value of \(a_{t} = 5.424\ \mathrm{fm}\) leads to exaggerated values for both \(A_{S}\) and the deuteron root-mean-square radius \(r_{d}\) when compared with direct experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors used the effective-range expansion for triplet np scattering, including the shape parameter \(P_{t}\), to derive analytic relations between the deuteron asymptotic normalization constant \(A_{S}\), the deuteron binding energy \(\varepsilon_{d}\), and the low-energy scattering parameters \(a_{t}\), \(r_{t}\), and \(P_{t}\). They compared these relations against precise values from 17 realistic nucleon-nucleon potential models and against partial-wave analyses of Arndt et al. (2003), then computed the low-energy scattering parameters that best reproduce the experimental deuteron properties and phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- 99.7% of the asymptotic normalization constant \(A_{S}\) is determined by the triplet scattering length \(a_{t}\).
- The experimental deuteron binding energy is \(\varepsilon_{d} = 2.22458900\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (Greene et al., 1986).
- The experimental asymptotic normalization constant from elastic pd scattering is \(A_{S} = 0.8781\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}\) (Borbély et al., 1985).
- The experimental root-mean-square deuteron radius from Bérard et al. (1973) is \(r_{d} = 1.9635\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Using the experimental deuteron properties, the authors obtain low-energy scattering parameters: \(a_{t} = 5.4050\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(r_{t} = 1.7505\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(P_{t} = -0.0231\).
- From the latest phase-shift analysis of Arndt et al. (2003), the extracted parameters are: \(a_{t} = 5.4030\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(r_{t} = 1.7495\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(P_{t} = -0.0259\).
- The currently recommended experimental triplet scattering length \(a_{t} = 5.424\ \mathrm{fm}\) (Dumbrajs et al., 1983) yields \(A_{S} \simeq 0.8845\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}\) and \(r_{d} = 1.9711\ \mathrm{fm}\), both exaggerated relative to experiment.
- The B set (using \(r_{d} = 1.9635\ \mathrm{fm}\)) reproduces experimental phase shifts with absolute error about \(0.06^{\circ}\) at \(1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and about \(1^{\circ}\) at \(50\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the empirical linear correlation between \(A_{S}\) and \(a_{t}\) noted by Ericson (1984) and Klarsfeld et al. (1986), and the correlation between \(r_{d}\) and \(a_{t}\) established by Sprung (1984). It resolves contradictions among values of \(A_{S}\) ranging from \(0.7592\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}\) to \(0.9863\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}\) reported in the literature (Blokhintsev et al., 1977; Locher and Misutani, 1978). The results enable consistent determination of both deuteron properties and low-energy scattering parameters from either scattering data or bound-state measurements, and show that the partial-wave analysis of Arndt et al. (2003) is fully compatible with the direct experimental deuteron data.
