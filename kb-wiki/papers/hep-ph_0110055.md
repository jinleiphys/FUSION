# Application of Roy's equations to analysis of pion-pion experimental data

> The scalar-isoscalar, scalar-isotensor and vector-isovector pion-pion partial wave amplitudes are analyzed. Preliminary results indicate that only the scalar-isoscalar amplitude fitted to the "down-flat" data satisfies Roy's equations and consequently crossing symmetry.

# Application of Roy's equations to analysis of pion-pion experimental data

## Key claim
Only the scalar-isoscalar amplitude fitted to the "down-flat" solution of the pion-pion isoscalar S-wave satisfies Roy's equations and consequently crossing symmetry; the "up-flat" solution is incompatible with crossing symmetry for the S-wave in the mass range 840 to 950 MeV.

## Method
The authors constructed a three-channel unitary model (ππ, K̅K, and effective (2π)(2π)) with chiral symmetry constraints on scattering lengths and slope parameters, then fitted the scalar-isoscalar phase shifts to the "down-flat" and "up-flat" experimental solutions. They tested compatibility with crossing symmetry by comparing the real parts of partial waves calculated from phase shifts and inelasticities (input) against the output from Roy's equations, which are twice-subtracted fixed-t dispersion relations valid up to 1.15 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Roy's equations are valid for center-of-mass energies between 4m_π² and 68m_π² (1.15 GeV)
- Two-loop chiral perturbation theory constraints used: a₀⁰ = 0.219 ± 0.005 m_π⁻¹, a₀² = -0.042 ± 0.001 m_π⁻¹
- Slope parameters from chiral perturbation theory: b₀⁰ = 0.279 ± 0.011 m_π⁻³, b₀² = -0.076 ± 0.002 m_π⁻³
- Vector-isovector scattering length a₁¹ = 0.035 m_π⁻¹ used in the Schenk parameterization
- The "up-flat" and "down-flat" solutions differ mainly in the mass range 800 to 980 MeV
- For the S-wave between 840 and 950 MeV, the "up-flat" solution shows no overlap between input and output bands, while the "down-flat" solution shows overlap for m_ππ ≤ 950 MeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Kaminski (1997) which reanalyzed π⁻p → π⁺π⁻n data at 17.2 GeV/c and found the "up-flat" and "down-flat" ambiguity for the isoscalar S-wave between 600 and 980 MeV. The authors use the three-channel model of Kaminski (1999) with chiral symmetry constraints from Donoghue (1990) and two-loop calculations by Amoros (2000). The results are consistent with a joint analysis by Kaminski (2001) of CERN-Munich, CERN-Cracow-Munich, and Brookhaven E852 data that eliminates the "up-flat" solution. This enables more reliable determination of scalar-isoscalar meson parameters and provides a framework to check amplitudes below the K̅K threshold.
