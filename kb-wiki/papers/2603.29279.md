# Comment on "Lattice QCD constraints on the critical point from an improved precision equation of state"

> A recent Letter~\cite{Borsanyi:2025dyp} employs lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state, combined with entropy-density contour analysis, to place a lower bound of $μ_B \gtrsim 450$~MeV on the location of the QCD critical endpoint (CEP). While the underlying lattice calculations represent an important advance in precision and systematic control, the method used to infer constraints on the CEP is not directly sensitive to critical behavior. In particular, the use of entropy contours does not directly probe the singular structure associated with the CEP, does not explicitly incorporate the relevant thermodynamic scaling fields, and relies on assumptions that are not strictly satisfied in finite systems. Consequently, the reported exclusion of a CEP below $μ_B \approx 450$~MeV cannot be regarded as model-independent, as model-independent constraints require observables that are directly sensitive to the singular scaling behavior associated with critical phenomena.

# Comment on "Lattice QCD constraints on the critical point from an improved precision equation of state"

## Key claim

The entropy-contour method used by Borsanyi (2025) to place a lower bound of μB ≳ 450 MeV on the QCD critical endpoint (CEP) does not provide a direct probe of critical behavior, because entropy density is dominated by the regular component of the thermodynamic potential rather than the singular contributions associated with critical phenomena. The reported exclusion of a CEP below μB ≈ 450 MeV cannot be regarded as model-independent, as model-independent constraints require observables directly sensitive to universal scaling behavior.

## Method

The author critiques the entropy-density contour analysis of Borsanyi (2025) by examining whether the method probes the singular structure of the thermodynamic potential near the CEP, which is governed by universal scaling laws of the 3D Ising universality class. The argument evaluates whether entropy contours can detect critical behavior given that the singular contribution to the entropy density remains subleading relative to the regular component, and assesses the impact of finite-volume effects, analytic continuation assumptions, and the model dependence introduced by combining entropy-contour analysis with a phenomenologically determined chemical freeze-out line from Andronic (2018).

## Key numbers

- The lower bound of μB ≳ 450 MeV on the CEP location reported by Borsanyi (2025) is critiqued as not model-independent.
- The author notes that finite-volume effects can substantially attenuate observable imprints even for large but finite volumes (e.g., LT = 4).
- The quoted 2σ bound from Borsanyi (2025) is described as quantifying uncertainties within the entropy-contour construction, but not reflecting sensitivity to critical fluctuations.

## Context

This comment extends the critique of entropy-based methods for locating the QCD critical endpoint, building on the framework of Stephanov (1998, 2008) which established that critical behavior near the CEP is governed by 3D Ising universality class scaling fields r and h, and that higher-order susceptibilities of conserved charges exhibit characteristic non-monotonic behavior and universal scaling. The author argues that robust constraints require scaling-based analyses using multiple independent constraints, as previously demonstrated by Lacey (2014, 2024), and that finite-size scaling approaches from Fisher (1971), Barber (1983), and Fraga (2011) are necessary for interpreting finite-system results from lattice simulations and heavy-ion collisions.
