# Parity violation in deuteron photo-disintegration

> We analyze the energy dependence for two types of parity-non-conserving (PNC) asymmetries in the reaction $γD\to np$ in the near-threshold region. The first one is the asymmetry in reaction with circularly polarized photon beam and unpolarized deuteron target. The second one corresponds to those with an unpolarized photon beam and polarized target. We find that the two asymmetries have quite different energy dependence, and their shapes are sensitive to the PNC-meson exchange coupling constants. The predictions for the future possible experiments to provide definite constraints for the PNC-coupling constants are discussed.

# Parity violation in deuteron photo-disintegration

## Key claim

The two parity-non-conserving asymmetries in the reaction γD → np, the circularly-polarized-beam asymmetry (A_RL) and the polarized-deuteron-target asymmetry (A_D), have quite different energy dependences near threshold, and their shapes are sensitive to the PNC-meson exchange coupling constants; however, the ΔI=1 (π-exchange) contribution to A_RL is strongly suppressed by cancellations among competing terms, making A_D the more promising observable for extracting the weak πNN coupling constant h_π.

## Method

The authors calculate the PNC asymmetries A_RL and A_D for γD → np in the near-threshold region (E_γ up to about 10 MeV above threshold) using a potential-model approach with the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) PNC meson-exchange potential (π, ρ, ω exchanges). The regular M1 and E1 transition amplitudes and the parity-odd admixtures to the deuteron and continuum np wave functions are computed using two realistic NN potentials: the Paris potential (soft repulsive core) and the Hamada-Johnston (HJ) potential (hard core), with the DDH "best values" for the PNC coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The A_RL asymmetry at threshold (ΔE_γ → 0) is 3.35 × 10⁻⁸ for the Paris potential, consistent with previous calculations of circular photon polarization in np → Dγ (P_γ = 1.8–5.6 × 10⁻⁸).
- A_RL changes sign and crosses zero at ΔE_γ ≈ 1.3 MeV for the Paris potential.
- The difference between Paris and HJ potential results for A_RL at small ΔE_γ = 0.01–1 MeV amounts to a factor of 2.5–3.
- The total cross section difference between Paris and HJ potentials does not exceed 5% and disappears at ΔE_γ → 0.
- The deuteron D-state probability used: P_D(Paris) = 0.0577, P_D(HJ) = 0.0697.
- The threshold energy E_thr ≈ ε = 2.23 MeV (deuteron binding energy).
- The ratio V_1^γ / V_2^γ at threshold is estimated as approximately −0.18 from a plane-wave Born approximation.
- The coherent sum of ΔI=1 (π-exchange) contributions to A_RL is smaller by a factor of 27 compared to the result of Oka (previous work).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of PNC effects in the deuteron photodisintegration reaction, which were limited by the use of hard-core potentials (Lee, Oka) or zero-range approximations (KK, Korkin). The authors use the more realistic Paris potential with soft repulsion, designed for proper description of short-range phenomena, and compare with the hard-core HJ potential to illustrate the effect of short-range correlations. The analysis shows that A_RL is dominated by ΔI=0,2 PNC forces and is relatively insensitive to the weak πNN coupling h_π due to cancellations, contrary to the claim of Oka. The deuteron spin asymmetry A_D, however, retains sensitivity to the isovector ΔI=1 interaction and may provide constraints on h_π, complementing measurements from the radiative np capture reaction (where a new measurement is in preparation at LANSCE) and resolving inconsistencies with the ¹³³Cs anapole moment analysis.
