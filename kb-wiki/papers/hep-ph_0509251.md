# Extended Gaussian ensemble or q-statistics in hadronic production processes?

> The extended Gaussian ensemble introduced recently as a generalization of the canonical ensemble, which allows to treat energy fluctuations present in the system, is used to analyze the inelasticity distributions in high energy multiparticle production processes.

## Key claim

The extended Gaussian ensemble (EGE) can successfully describe the distribution of inelasticity (the fraction of available energy transformed into observed secondaries) in high-energy multiparticle production, but it does not describe single-particle rapidity distributions, where the best fit is obtained with the fluctuation parameter \(\gamma = 0\) or slightly negative, meaning the data do not require EGE for single-particle spectra.

## Method

The authors apply the extended Gaussian ensemble, which adds a constraint on the variance of the mean energy per secondary to the standard information-theory maximum-entropy procedure (which normally uses only the mean energy constraint). They compare the resulting rapidity distributions \(p(y)\) from EGE with those from Tsallis \(q\)-statistics, and also apply EGE to model the inelasticity distribution \(\chi(K)\) by treating the total energy as divided between a particle-producing subsystem and an environment, expanding the entropy around the mean energy to obtain a Gaussian form for \(\chi(K)\).

## Key numbers

- For the Gaussian fit to inelasticity distributions at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV: \(\langle K \rangle = 0.52\), \(\sigma = 0.24\).
- For the Gaussian fit to inelasticity distributions at \(\sqrt{s} = 900\) GeV: \(\langle K \rangle = 0.38\), \(\sigma = 0.20\).
- For the Lorentzian fit at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV: \(\langle K \rangle = 0.52\), \(\sigma = 0.25\).
- For the Lorentzian fit at \(\sqrt{s} = 900\) GeV: \(\langle K \rangle = 0.39\), \(\sigma = 0.17\).
- The relation connecting the Gaussian width \(\sigma\), temperature \(T_0\), and nonextensivity parameter \(q\) is \(T_0 = \sqrt{2(q-1)} \sigma_E\) where \(\sigma_E = \sigma \cdot \sqrt{s}\).

## Context

This work extends the information-theory approach to multiparticle production previously developed using Shannon entropy (MaxEnt, 1990s) and Tsallis entropy (MaxEntq, 2000s) by introducing the extended Gaussian ensemble proposed in statistical mechanics (eGe, 2004). The analysis uses the same multiparticle production data as in MaxEntq (2004) for \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) and 900 GeV, and shows that EGE provides a natural explanation for the Gaussian shape of inelasticity distributions that were previously fitted empirically, while also connecting the width of that distribution to the temperature and nonextensivity parameter \(q\) deduced from rapidity distributions.
