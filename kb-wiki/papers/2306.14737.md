# Tidal heating as a direct probe of strangeness inside neutron stars

> It has been discussed whether viscous processes in neutron star matter during a binary inspiral can damp out the tidal energy induced by the companion and heat up the star. Earlier investigations concluded that this tidal heating is negligible for normal neutron star matter. In this work, we suggest a novel effect of tidal heating involving strange matter in the neutron star interior, that can significantly heat up the star, and is potentially observable by current and future gravitational wave detectors. We propose that this could serve as a direct probe of strangeness in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that tidal heating from bulk viscosity due to non-leptonic weak interactions involving hyperons (specifically Λ hyperons) in neutron star cores can heat the star to temperatures two orders of magnitude higher than earlier estimates, and that this effect is potentially detectable as a phase shift in gravitational wave signals, serving as a direct probe of strangeness in neutron star interiors. The authors qualify that this is a proof-of-principle study considering only the dominant Λ hyperon process, which provides a lower limit on reaction rates and an upper limit on hyperon bulk viscosity.

## Method

The authors calculate the hyperon bulk viscosity coefficient analytically using relaxation times of the non-leptonic weak process n + p ↔ p + Λ, considering several Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) equation of state parametrizations consistent with multi-messenger observations. They compute the bulk viscous dissipation of the dominant l=m=2 f-mode oscillations excited by tidal interaction during binary inspiral, using the Cowling approximation for f-mode frequencies and Newtonian tidal heating formalism. The thermal evolution equation is integrated from infinite separation to merger to estimate the temperature reached, and the accumulated phase shift in the gravitational wave signal is calculated from the additional energy loss due to viscous dissipation.

## Key numbers

- Hyperon bulk viscosity reaches resonant maximum values of 10^31 - 10^32 gm cm^-1 s^-1 in the temperature range 10^8 - 10^9 K, which is 10^8 - 10^10 times the canonical shear viscosity from e-e scattering
- Temperatures reached at separation D = 3R (just before merger) range from 8.7×10^8 K to 7.9×10^9 K for different EOSs and masses, compared to earlier estimates of ~3×10^7 K (Lai 1994) and ~10^8 K (Arras et al. 2019)
- Net phase difference accumulated at gravitational wave frequency ~500 Hz ranges from 0.0008 rad (TM1 EOS, 1.6 M⊙) to 0.47 rad (FSU2 EOS, 2.0 M⊙) for equal mass binaries
- For component masses ≥ 1.8 M⊙, the phase shift is ~0.1 - 0.5 rad, which is comparable to or larger than the phase uncertainty of ±0.1 rad for GW170817 and ±0.02 rad for A+ detectors
- The Λ hyperon onset density occurs around twice nuclear saturation density (~2n₀, where n₀ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³) for all EOSs considered

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Lai (1994) and Arras et al. (2019) who concluded that tidal heating from shear and bulk viscous damping in normal neutron star matter is negligible, finding that hyperon bulk viscosity produces heating two orders of magnitude higher. The study builds on extensive work on hyperon bulk viscosity in the context of r-mode damping (Jones 2001, Lindblom 2002, Haskell 2010) and applies it for the first time to tidal heating during binary inspiral. The results suggest that tidal heating could serve as a direct probe of strangeness in neutron stars, and that future gravitational wave detections with third-generation detectors like Cosmic Explorer could measure this effect even for lower mass neutron stars, while non-detection could place upper limits on hyperon fraction in neutron star cores.
