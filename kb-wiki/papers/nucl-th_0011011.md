# Local equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions: Microscopic analysis of a central cell versus infinite matter

> We study the local equilibrium in the central $V = 125$ fm$^3$ cell in heavy-ion collisions at energies from 10.7 AGeV (AGS) to 160 AGeV (SPS) calculated in the microscopic transport model. In the present paper the hadron yields and energy spectra in the cell are compared with those of infinite nuclear matter, as calculated within the same model. The agreement between the spectra in the two systems is established for times $t \geq 10$ fm/$c$ in the central cell. The cell results do not deviate noticeably from the infinite matter calculations with rising incident energy, in contrast to the apparent discrepancy with predictions of the statistical model (SM) of an ideal hadron gas. The entropy of this state is found to be very close to the maximum entropy, while hadron abundances and energy spectra differ significantly from those of the SM.

## Key claim

The hadronic matter in the central cell of heavy-ion collisions at AGS and SPS energies reaches a quasiequilibrium "steady state" that agrees with infinite nuclear matter simulations in the same microscopic model, but this state differs significantly from the ideal thermal and chemical equilibrium predicted by the statistical model of an ideal hadron gas, even though its entropy density is within 6-10% of the maximum entropy.

## Method

The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate both a central cell of volume 125 fm^3 in Pb+Pb collisions at energies from 10.7 AGeV to 160 AGeV and infinite nuclear matter in a cubic box of the same volume with periodic boundary conditions. They extracted the energy density, baryon density, and strangeness density from the cell at each time step, used these values to initialize the box simulations, and compared the hadron yields and energy spectra between the two systems as well as against predictions from the statistical model of an ideal hadron gas.

## Key numbers

- Central cell volume: V = 125 fm^3
- Energy range studied: 10.7 AGeV (AGS) to 160 AGeV (SPS)
- Agreement between cell and box spectra established for times t >= 10 fm/c
- For the box initialized with SPS cell conditions at t=10 fm/c: energy density epsilon = 468 GeV/fm^3, baryon density rho_B = 0.09 fm^{-3}, strangeness density rho_S = -0.01 fm^{-3}
- Apparent temperatures in the box at equilibrium: T_N = 127 MeV for nucleons, T_pi = 106 MeV for pions, with relative difference (T_N - T_pi)/T_N = 0.17
- Saturation times in the box: hadron yields saturate at 50-60 fm/c, kaon yields saturate at 70-90 fm/c
- In the box at equilibrium: 45% of reactions are inelastic, 27% elastic, 28% resonance decays; among inelastic collisions, 78% go via resonance formation and 22% via string formation
- Mean total energy per hadron in the box: 1.0 +/- 0.1 GeV
- Entropy per baryon deviation from equilibrium: about 4% at AGS energy and about 6-10% at SPS energy
- Total entropy density difference at SPS: (s_SM - s_mic)/s_SM = 6%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of local equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions (Bravina et al., 1999) and infinite nuclear matter simulations (Belkacem et al., 1998; Brandstetter et al., unpublished; Bratkovskaya et al., 2000). The paper resolves an apparent contradiction: while the central cell results deviate from statistical model predictions, they agree with infinite matter simulations in the same microscopic model. The steady state concept from Kondepudi and Prigogine (1998) is invoked to describe the quasiequilibrium. The findings challenge the standard hydrodynamic picture of sequential chemical and thermal freeze-out and suggest that microscopic models lack certain inverse reactions (N -> 2 and N -> 1 with N >= 3), leading to cyclic processes that prevent true equilibrium.
