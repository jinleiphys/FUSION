# Nuclear Axial Currents in Chiral Effective Field Theory

> Two-nucleon axial charge and current operators are derived in chiral effective field theory up to one loop. The derivation is based on time-ordered perturbation theory, and accounts for cancellations between the contributions of irreducible diagrams and the contributions due to non-static corrections from energy denominators of reducible diagrams. Ultraviolet divergencies associated with the loop corrections are isolated in dimensional regularization. The resulting axial current is finite and conserved in the chiral limit, while the axial charge requires renormalization. A complete set of contact terms for the axial charge up to the relevant order in the power counting is constructed.

## Key claim

The paper derives two-nucleon axial charge and current operators in chiral effective field theory up to one loop, finding that the resulting axial current is finite and conserved in the chiral limit, while the axial charge requires renormalization. The derivation accounts for cancellations between irreducible diagrams and non-static corrections from energy denominators of reducible diagrams, leading to differences from the earlier heavy-baryon perturbation theory results of Park et al. (1993).

## Method

The derivation uses time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) to separate irreducible and reducible contributions, with the nuclear axial charge and current operators defined from irreducible amplitudes. Ultraviolet divergences from loop corrections are isolated using dimensional regularization, and a complete set of contact terms for the axial charge up to the relevant chiral order is constructed. The method builds on the formalism developed by Pastore et al. (2008, 2009, 2011) and Piarulli et al. (2013) for nuclear potentials and electromagnetic currents.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order one-body axial current operator: \( \mathbf{j}_{5,a}^{(-3)}(\mathbf{q}) = -\frac{g_A}{2} \tau_{1,a} \left[ \boldsymbol{\sigma}_1 - \frac{\mathbf{q}}{q^2 + m_\pi^2} \boldsymbol{\sigma}_1 \cdot \mathbf{q} \right] (2\pi)^3 \delta(\mathbf{p}_1 + \mathbf{q} - \mathbf{p}_1') + (1 \rightleftharpoons 2) \)
- Leading-order one-body axial charge operator: \( \rho_{5,a}^{(-2)}(\mathbf{q}) = -\frac{g_A}{4m} \tau_{1,a} \boldsymbol{\sigma}_1 \cdot (\mathbf{p}_1' + \mathbf{p}_1) (2\pi)^3 \delta(\mathbf{p}_1 + \mathbf{q} - \mathbf{p}_1') + (1 \rightleftharpoons 2) \)
- Two-pion exchange axial charge contributions at order \( Q \) involve loop functions with \( s_j = \sqrt{4 m_\pi^2 + \mathbf{k}_j^2} \), evaluated in dimensional regularization with \( d_\epsilon = -\frac{2}{\epsilon} + \gamma - \ln 4\pi + \ln \frac{m_\pi^2}{\mu^2} - 1 \)
- Contact terms for the axial charge at order \( Q \) involve four operators \( O_1 \) through \( O_4 \) with unknown low-energy constants \( z_i \)

## Context

This work extends the pioneering derivation of nuclear axial currents by Park et al. (1993) using heavy-baryon perturbation theory, but employs a time-ordered perturbation theory formulation that accounts for cancellations between irreducible and reducible diagram contributions. The results are relevant for low-energy tests of physics beyond the Standard Model in beta-decay experiments, neutrino interactions with nuclei (particularly for the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment), and neutrino processes in core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers. The derived operators enable more accurate calculations of nuclear weak interaction processes and complement prior work on chiral effective field theory potentials by Epelbaum et al. (2000) and electromagnetic currents by Pastore et al. (2008, 2009, 2011).
