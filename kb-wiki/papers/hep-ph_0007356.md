# Diagonalization of the strongly coupled lattice QCD Hamiltonian

> We construct a solution to the equation of motion of Hamiltonian lattice QCD in the strong coupling limit using Wilson fermions which exactly diagonalizes the Hamiltonian to second order in the field operators. This solution obeys the free lattice Dirac equation with a dynamical mass which is identified with the gap. The equation determining this gap is derived and it is found that the dynamical quark mass is a constant to lowest order in N_c but becomes momentum dependent once 1/N_c corrections are taken into account. We interpret our solution within the framework of the N-quantum approach to quantum field theory and discuss how our formalism may be systematically extended to study bound states at finite temperature and chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a solution to the equation of motion of Hamiltonian lattice QCD in the strong coupling limit using Wilson fermions that exactly diagonalizes the Hamiltonian to second order in the field operators, but only under the condition that the solution obeys the free lattice Dirac equation with a dynamical mass identified as the gap; the dynamical quark mass is a constant only to lowest order in N_c and becomes momentum dependent once 1/N_c corrections are taken into account.

## Method

The author uses Smit's effective Hamiltonian for strongly coupled lattice QCD with Wilson fermions and applies an ansatz that is the solution of the free lattice Dirac equation with r=0, where the mass term is now the dynamical quark mass rather than the current quark mass. The equation of motion for the effective Hamiltonian is equated with the free lattice Dirac equation, allowing the derivation of a gap equation for the dynamical quark mass. The second-order off-diagonal Hamiltonian is shown to vanish using the equation of motion for the eta spinor and orthogonality conditions, demonstrating exact diagonalization to second order in field operators.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling constant K_C approximately 0.73 for the case M_0 = 0 and r = 0 (lowest order in N_c)
- Critical coupling constant K_C approximately 0.84 for the case M_0 = 0 and r = 1
- The critical coupling constant approaches infinity as the Wilson parameter r approaches 1
- The dynamical quark mass at r=1 is one order of magnitude less than that obtained with only the O(N_c^0) contribution

## Context

This work extends the Hamiltonian formulation of strong coupling QCD using Wilson fermions originally studied by Smit (1980), who derived an effective Hamiltonian using the 1/N_c approximation and applied spin wave theory to determine the vacuum energy density and excitation spectrum. The results for the dynamical quark mass are qualitatively different from those obtained by Le Yaouanc, Oliver, Pene and Raynal (1986), who applied the Bogoliubov-Valatin variational method to the same effective Hamiltonian and found a momentum-independent dynamical mass with chiral symmetry breaking occurring for all values of the coupling constant K > 0. The approach enables systematic extension to study bound states at finite temperature and chemical potential through the N-quantum approach to quantum field theory, using the Haag expansion to go beyond mean field approximations.
