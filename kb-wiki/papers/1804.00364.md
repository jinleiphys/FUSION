# Fluctuations in the $^{235}$U(n,f) cross section

> We examine the autocorrelation function of the $^{235}$U(n,f) reaction with a view to quantify the presence of intermediate structure in the cross section. Fluctuations due to compound nucleus resonances on the eV energy scale are clearly visible up to $\sim100$ keV neutron energies. Structure on the one-keV energy scale is not present as a systematic feature of the correlation function, although it is present in the data covering the region around 20 keV.

# Fluctuations in the $^{235}$U(n,f) cross section

## Key claim

The authors find that while compound-nucleus resonance fluctuations on the eV scale are clearly present in the $^{235}$U(n,f) cross section up to neutron energies of ~100 keV, there is no systematic evidence for intermediate structure on the one-keV energy scale; the previously reported structure around 22 keV appears as an isolated feature rather than a systematic property of the correlation function.

## Method

The authors compute the autocorrelation function $R(\epsilon)$ of experimental $^{235}$U(n,f) cross sections from three high-resolution datasets (Bowman 1971, Perez 1974, Moore 1978) over neutron energies from 2 to 100 keV. They compare the extracted peak heights and widths to predictions from a statistical Breit-Wigner model and from a GOE-based Hamiltonian simulation code called "Mazama," which samples resonance parameters to generate synthetic cross sections. The analysis subdivides the energy range into windows of 3-20 keV width to search for systematic correlation structures.

## Key numbers

- In the resolved resonance region (10-30 eV), the autocorrelation peak height $R(0) = 4.23$ and half-maximum width $\Gamma_{HM} = 0.09$ eV from ENDF data; the Mazama GOE model gives $R(0) = 3.6 \pm 1.4$ and $\Gamma_{HM} = 0.10 \pm 0.02$ eV for the same energy range.
- The compound-nucleus resonance spacing $\bar D = 0.47$ eV (for each spin-parity combination), with total resonance width $\Gamma \approx 0.102$ eV from the sum of fission width (0.064 eV) and capture width (0.038 eV).
- In the 10-25 keV window, the $R(0)$ spike height is approximately 0.02 for the Perez (1974) data, consistent with the compound-nucleus prediction $R_{\Delta E} \approx R(0) \pi \Gamma / \Delta E$ using $\Delta E = 0.05$ keV.
- The broader shoulder structure in the autocorrelation extends out to about 0.5 keV in the 10-25 keV window.
- Above 25 keV, no systematic correlation structure beyond the compound-nucleus spike is observed across seven energy windows from 2 to 100 keV.

## Context

This work extends the fluctuation analysis of neutron-induced fission cross sections beyond the isolated resonance region, addressing claims of intermediate structure from experiments in the 1970s (Bowman 1971, Perez 1974, Moore 1978). The authors find that the 22 keV peak reported by Perez (1974) is an isolated feature, not a systematic intermediate structure, and suggest it might arise from second-well resonances persisting above the fission barrier. The study enables future experimental campaigns to clarify the origin of such isolated structures, particularly through photofission measurements that can access excitation energies below those of neutron-induced fission.
