# Weak Hyperon Production in ep Scattering

> We study the kinematics and cross section of the scattering process $e p \rightarrow e Σ^+$. The cross section is expressed in terms of complex form factors characterizing the hadron vertices. We estimate the cross section for small momentum transfer using known experimental information. To first order in the momentum transfer, we obtain a model independent result for the photon-exchange part of the cross section, which is completely determined by the decay width $Γ(Σ^+ \rightarrow pγ)$. For the kinematics of the parity violation experiment at MAMI, this first order result gives rise to a ratio of $(dσ/dΩ)_{ep\rightarrow eΣ^+}$ $/(dσ/dΩ)_{ep\rightarrow ep} \simeq 4.0\times 10^{-15}$. The $Z^0$-exchange and interference parts give much smaller contributions due to the suppression of the flavor changing weak neutral current in the standard model. Feasibility of the experimental measurement is briefly discussed.

# Weak Hyperon Production in ep Scattering

## Key claim

The cross section for $e p \rightarrow e \Sigma^+$ is severely suppressed relative to elastic $ep$ scattering, with a model-independent first-order (in momentum transfer) result for the photon-exchange part that is completely determined by the weak radiative decay width $\Gamma(\Sigma^+ \rightarrow p\gamma)$; however, this result applies only for small momentum transfer and the $Z^0$-exchange and interference contributions are negligible due to suppression of the flavor-changing weak neutral current.

## Method

The differential cross section for $e p \rightarrow e \Sigma^+$ was expressed in terms of complex form factors parameterizing the hadron vertices $p\gamma\Sigma^+$ and $pZ^0\Sigma^+$, with the cross section decomposed into pure photon-exchange, pure $Z^0$-exchange, and interference parts. The photon-exchange part was estimated to first order in the squared four-momentum transfer $q^2$ using the experimentally measured decay width and asymmetry parameter of $\Sigma^+ \rightarrow p\gamma$, while the $Z^0$-exchange and interference contributions were bounded using the experimental branching ratio of $K^0_L \rightarrow \mu^+\mu^-$ to constrain the flavor-changing weak neutral current coupling.

## Key numbers

- Minimum initial electron energy for the process: $E_{\rm min} \simeq 285$ MeV
- Ratio of differential cross sections at MAMI kinematics ($\theta \simeq 35^0$, $q^2 \simeq -0.16$ GeV$^2$ for the hyperon production): $(d\sigma/d\Omega)_{ep\rightarrow e\Sigma^+} / (d\sigma/d\Omega)_{ep\rightarrow ep} \simeq 4.0 \times 10^{-15}$
- Upper bound on the $Z^0$-exchange contribution relative to elastic $ep$ scattering: $(d\sigma/d\Omega)_Z / (d\sigma/d\Omega)_{ep\rightarrow ep} \leq 7 \times 10^{-22}$
- Estimated axial vector coupling for the flavor-changing weak neutral current: $|g^Z_{\rm A}| \simeq 2 \times 10^{-6}$
- Experimental branching ratio of $\Sigma^+ \rightarrow p\gamma$: $(1.25 \pm 0.07) \times 10^{-3}$
- Asymmetry parameter for $\Sigma^+ \rightarrow p\gamma$: $\alpha_\gamma = -0.76 \pm 0.08$

## Context

This work provides the first theoretical investigation of the scattering process $e p \rightarrow e \Sigma^+$, which was suggested by experimentalists at MAMI (Maas, private communication, 1996) who were considering measuring such processes. The study extends the understanding of the $p\gamma\Sigma^+$ vertex, which also appears in the weak radiative decay $\Sigma^+ \rightarrow p\gamma$, a process that remains poorly understood despite substantial theoretical effort (Bassalleck, 1995). The results enable experimental feasibility assessments at low-energy electron facilities such as MAMI, MIT-Bates, and TJNAF, though the severe suppression factor of $4 \times 10^{-15}$ relative to elastic scattering makes meaningful measurement unlikely with current capabilities.
