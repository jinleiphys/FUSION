# Baryon and meson screening masses

> In a strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma, collective excitations of gluons and quarks should dominate over the excitation of individual quasi-free gluon and quark modes. To explore this possibility, we computed screening masses for ground-state light-quark mesons and baryons at leading-order in a symmetry-preserving truncation scheme for the Dyson-Schwinger equations using a confining formulation of a contact-interaction at nonzero temperature. Meson screening masses are obtained from Bethe-Salpeter equations; and baryon analogues from a novel construction of the Faddeev equation, which employs an improved quark-exchange approximation in the kernel. Our treatment implements a deconfinement transition that is coincident with chiral symmetry restoration in the chiral limit, when both transitions are second order. Despite deconfinement, in all T=0 bound-state channels, strong correlations persist above the critical temperature, T>T_c; and, in the spectrum defined by the associated screening masses, degeneracy between parity-partner correlations is apparent for T >1.3T_c. Notwithstanding these results, there are reasons (including Golberger-Treiman relations) to suppose that the inertial masses of light-quark bound-states, when they may be defined, vanish at the deconfinement temperature; and that this is a signal of bound-state dissolution. Where a sensible comparison is possible, our predictions are consistent with results from contemporary numerical simulations of lattice-regularised QCD.

## Key claim

Despite deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration occurring at a common critical temperature \(T_c\) in the chiral limit, strong correlations persist in all \(T=0\) bound-state channels above \(T_c\), and parity-partner screening masses become degenerate only for \(T \gtrsim 1.3\,T_c\); however, the authors note that inertial masses of light-quark bound-states likely vanish at \(T_c\) due to Goldberger-Treiman relations, signaling bound-state dissolution.

## Method

The authors computed screening masses for ground-state light-quark mesons and baryons at leading order in a symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, using a confining formulation of a vector\(\times\)vector contact interaction extended to nonzero temperature via Matsubara frequency sums. Meson screening masses were obtained from Bethe-Salpeter equations, and baryon screening masses from a novel Faddeev equation construction employing an improved quark-exchange approximation in the kernel that retains both scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations. Confinement was implemented via an infrared regulator \(\tau_{\rm ir}\) in the proper-time regularization, and deconfinement was introduced by making \(\tau_{\rm ir}\) temperature-dependent, with the deconfinement transition coincident with chiral symmetry restoration in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement in the chiral limit: \(T_c\) (value not explicitly given in the truncated text; the paper states the transition is second order).
* Degeneracy between parity-partner screening masses becomes apparent for \(T \gtrsim 1.3\,T_c\).
* Spin-orbit coupling parameter at \(T=0\): \(g_{\rm SO}^{T=0} = 0.24\), which reproduces the experimental \(a_1\)-\(\rho\) splitting.
* Infrared regulator \(\tau_{\rm ir} = 1/\Lambda_{\rm ir} \approx 0.8\) fm at \(T=0\) implements confinement.
* The temperature-dependent spin-orbit coupling parameter is given by \(g^2_{\rm SO}(T) = 1 - \frac{M(T)}{M(0)}(1-[g^{T=0}_{\rm SO}]^2)\).

## Context

This work extends continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of hadron properties at nonzero temperature, building on prior analyses using the Global Colour-symmetry Model (Mo, 2010) and Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model (Mu, 2012) that treated mesons and baryons differently or omitted axial-vector diquark correlations. It contrasts with thermal finite-energy sum rule results (Dominguez, 1992) that found nucleon masses rising near \(T_c\). The authors' retention of axial-vector diquark correlations in the Faddeev equation at nonzero temperature is novel, and their predictions are consistent with lattice-regularised QCD simulations (Pushkina, 2004; Cheng, 2010; Petreczky, 2012) where correlations persist above \(T_c\) and parity-partner screening masses become equal. The work enables future reconstruction of inertial masses from screening masses via analytic continuation of thermal Schwinger functions.
