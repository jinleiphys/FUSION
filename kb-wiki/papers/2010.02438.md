# Daejeon16 interaction with contact-term corrections for heavy nuclear systems

> The Daejeon16 two-nucleon interaction is employed in many-body approaches based on the mean-field approximation. The perturbative character of Daejeon16 is verified by comparing results for 16O from the Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation and from the no-core shell model and by examining the magnitude of perturbative corrections to the HF energy in light and heavy nuclei. In order to approximately describe energies and radii across the nuclear chart, a phenomenological correction in the form of a two-plus-three-nucleon contact interaction is introduced. With fitted parameters we achieve a very good description of medium-mass nuclei in terms of energy and size and also in terms of the centroid energy of the giant monopole resonance and the dipole polarizability calculated within the random-phase approximation (RPA). Our results provide further justification for the use of Daejeon16 augmented with phenomenological corrections as an effective interaction of perturbative character in a variety of applications.

# Daejeon16 interaction with contact-term corrections for heavy nuclear systems

## Key claim

The Daejeon16 two-nucleon interaction, when augmented with a phenomenological two-plus-three-nucleon contact correction of the form \(V_\rho = \frac{1}{6}(1+\hat{P}_\sigma)\{t_0 + t_3\rho\}\delta(\vec{r}_i-\vec{r}_j)\), can approximately describe energies and radii of medium-mass nuclei, but **no single optimal pair of \((t_0, t_3)\) parameters exists that simultaneously satisfies both energy and radius criteria for all nuclei from \(^{16}\)O to \(^{208}\)Pb**; a global fit would require relaxing at least one of the adopted tolerance criteria.

## Method

The Daejeon16 interaction was employed in spherical Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations within a harmonic-oscillator basis of 13 shells (\(e_{\max}=12\)) for closed-shell nuclei from \(^{16}\)O to \(^{208}\)Pb. Second-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT(2)) corrections to the HF ground-state energy were computed, and the random-phase approximation (RPA) was used to calculate the centroid energy of the giant monopole resonance and the electric dipole polarizability. A phenomenological contact correction with parameters \(t_0\) (attractive, two-body) and \(t_3\) (repulsive, three-body) was introduced and scanned over a grid (\(t_0\) from \(-270\) to \(0\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) in steps of 10, \(t_3\) from \(0\) to \(2500\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^6\) in steps of 100) to identify acceptable combinations satisfying energy and radius criteria.

## Key numbers

- **\(^{16}\)O HF energy (Daejeon16):** \(-106.5\) MeV (with both \(b_1=1.44\) fm and \(b_2=2.04\) fm bases); HF+MBPT(2) gives \(-126.6\) MeV (\(b_1\)) and \(-130.4\) MeV (\(b_2\)), compared to experimental \(-127.619\) MeV.
- **\(^{16}\)O HF point-proton radius (Daejeon16):** \(2.24\) fm (both bases); HF+MBPT(2) gives \(2.36\) fm (\(b_1\)) and \(2.43\) fm (\(b_2\)), compared to experimental \(2.581\) fm.
- **\(^{16}\)O NCSM result (Daejeon16):** \(-131.4(7)\) MeV, with radius \(\approx 2.4\) fm and intrinsic kinetic energy \(290\) MeV.
- **Perturbative energy correction per particle (Daejeon16):** roughly \(0.5\)–\(1.5\) MeV for all nuclei studied, smaller than for other interactions.
- **Optimal parameter band:** approximately described by \(t_3 \approx (-20~\mathrm{fm}^3/3)\,t_0\); example sets are \((-180\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\), \(1200\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^6\)) for mid-mass nuclei and \((-240\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\), \(1600\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^6\)) for heavy nuclei.
- **\(^{28}\)O HF:** correctly predicted particle-unbound (neutron Fermi energy positive) with Daejeon16 alone; the neutron Fermi energy changes sign at approximately \(t_0 = -167\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) along the optimal band.
- **HF density inside \(^{208}\)Pb (Daejeon16):** exceeds \(0.6\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of the Daejeon16 interaction (Shirokov 2016), originally fitted to nuclei up to \(^{16}\)O, to heavier closed-shell nuclei within mean-field and RPA frameworks. It follows earlier efforts to correct renormalized interactions with phenomenological three-nucleon contact terms, such as the UCOM+SRG approach with a repulsive three-nucleon term (Gunther 2010, Hergert 2011, Papakonstantinou 2014) and the \(V_{\mathrm{low}-k}\) or NNLO\(_{\mathrm{opt}}\) potentials with variable-strength three-nucleon contact terms (Bianco 2014, Knapp 2014, 2015). The present correction is found to be weaker than that required for the UCOM interaction (Roth 2006), consistent with the observation that Daejeon16 already contains much of the relevant saturation physics. The inability to find a single global parameter set motivates future work with more flexible density dependencies or nucleus-dependent corrections, and enables systematic studies of medium-mass and heavy nuclei using Daejeon16 as a perturbative starting point.
