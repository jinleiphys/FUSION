# Electric quadrupole and magnetic octupole moments of the Delta

> Using a covariant spectator constituent quark model we predict an electric quadrupole moment -0.043 efm^2 and a magnetic octupole moment -0.0035 efm^3 for the Delta+ excited state of the nucleon.

## Key claim

Using a covariant spectator constituent quark model with D-state admixtures constrained by lattice QCD data, the authors predict an electric quadrupole moment and a magnetic octupole moment for the Δ⁺, but note that the octupole prediction is highly sensitive to model details and only becomes reliable when the model is fitted to lattice data.

## Method

The authors describe the Δ baryon as a quark-diquark system with a dominant S-state wave function and an admixture of two D-states (D3 with L=2, S=3/2 and D1 with L=2, S=1/2), with mixing coefficients a and b. They compute the electromagnetic current in the covariant spectator formalism to first order in the mixing coefficients, extracting the electric quadrupole form factor G_E2(0) and magnetic octupole form factor G_M3(0) at Q²=0. Two parameterizations are used: Spectator 1 (Sp 1) fits the γN→Δ transition data, while Spectator 2 (Sp 2) fits the valence part of the wave function directly to lattice QCD data before extrapolating to physical masses.

## Key numbers

- Electric quadrupole moment of Δ⁺: Q_Δ⁺ = -0.043 e·fm² (from model Sp 2)
- Magnetic octupole moment of Δ⁺: O_Δ⁺ = -0.0035 e·fm³ (from model Sp 2)
- For model Sp 1: G_E2(0) for Δ⁺ = -1.93 (dimensionless); for model Sp 2: G_E2(0) for Δ⁺ = -1.68 (dimensionless)
- For model Sp 1: G_M3(0) for Δ⁺ = -0.023 (dimensionless); for model Sp 2: G_M3(0) for Δ⁺ = -1.70 (dimensionless)
- Mixing coefficients in Sp 1: 0.88% D3 state and 4.36% D1 state; in Sp 2: 0.72% for both D3 and D1 states
- Integral values for Sp 1: I'_D3 = -7.00, I'_D1 = 1.59; for Sp 2: I'_D3 = -6.65, I'_D1 = 0.24

## Context

This work extends covariant spectator quark model calculations of Δ form factors (Ramalho, Peña, Gross, previous works) and is stimulated by recent lattice QCD results from Alexandrou et al. (2007, 2009) which revived interest in Δ quadrupole and octupole moments. The predictions are compared with nonrelativistic quark models (Isgur 1982, Krivoruchenko 1991), Buchmann's deformed pion cloud model (2008), chiral quark-soliton model results (Ledwig 2008), and QCD sum rules (Azizi 2008). The results enable future lattice QCD simulations to constrain the octupole moment more precisely and help determine whether the Δ⁺ has an oblate charge and magnetic distribution.
