# Impact of nuclear effects on the extraction of neutrino oscillation parameters

> We study the possible impact of nuclear effects and final state interactions on the determination of the oscillation parameters due to mis-reconstruction of non-quasi-elastic events as quasi-elastic events at low energies. We analyze a $ν_μ$ disappearance experiment using a water Čerenkov detector. We find that, if completely ignored in the fit, nuclear effects can induce a significant bias in the determination of atmospheric oscillation parameters, particularly for the atmospheric mixing angle. Even after inclusion of a near detector a bias in the determination of the atmospheric mixing angle comparable to the statistical error remains.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects and final state interactions, if completely ignored in the fit, can induce a significant bias in the determination of atmospheric oscillation parameters, particularly for the atmospheric mixing angle; even after inclusion of a near detector, a bias in the determination of the atmospheric mixing angle comparable to the statistical error remains.

## Method

The authors simulated a νμ disappearance experiment using a water Čerenkov detector with a muon neutrino beam of mean energy 600 MeV, selecting only single-ring events (one charged particle above Čerenkov threshold). They used the GiBUU transport model to generate migration matrices and cross sections for 16O, accounting for non-quasi-elastic events (Δ production, one-pion production, higher resonances, deep inelastic scattering, and two-particle-two-hole interactions) that can be mis-reconstructed as quasi-elastic events when pions are absorbed in the nucleus. A binned Poissonian χ² analysis was performed with 20% correlated normalization and 20% uncorrelated shape systematic uncertainties, including a simulated ideal near detector with energy-independent near/far ratio.

## Key numbers

- Input oscillation parameters: θ23 = 45°, Δm²31 = 2.45 × 10⁻³ eV²
- Beam mean energy: 600 MeV
- For 5 years of data taking at 750 kW beam power: approximately 850 true quasi-elastic events and approximately 1300 quasi-elastic-like events
- Data divided into 100 MeV bins between 0.2 and 2 GeV
- Systematic uncertainties: 20% normalization error (bin-to-bin correlated) and 20% shape error (bin-to-bin uncorrelated)
- For α = 0.3-0.4 (fraction of migration neglected in the fit), the minimum χ² value would still be low enough that the fit may be accepted, even with a near detector, corresponding to a 1σ bias in the determination of the mixing angle

## Context

This work extends prior qualitative studies by Lalakulich (2012), Martini (2012), and Nieves (2012) on the impact of final state interactions and multinucleon interactions on event distributions, providing the first quantitative estimate of the bias that nuclear effect uncertainties may induce in neutrino oscillation parameter determination. The study follows a similar approach to those earlier works but adds a systematic analysis of how mis-reconstructed non-quasi-elastic events affect the extraction of θ23 and Δm²31. The results indicate that future experiments, particularly those measuring leptonic CP violation, will require independently verified nuclear models, as near detectors alone may not be sufficient to eliminate biases.
