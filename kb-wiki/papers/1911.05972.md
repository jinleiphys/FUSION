# Three-body correlations in mesonic-atom-like systems

> Three-body correlations in three-body exotic atoms are studied with simple models that consist of three bosons interacting through a superposition of long- and short-range potentials. We discuss the correlations among particles by comparing the energy shifts given by precise three-body calculations and by the Deser-Trueman formula, in which the long- and short-range contributions are factorized. By varying the coupling of the short-range potential, we evaluate the ranges of the strength where the two-body correlations dominate and where the three-body correlations cannot be neglected.

# Three-body correlations in mesonic-atom-like systems

## Key claim

For three-body exotic-atom-like systems with pairwise long- and short-range interactions, the factorization of energy shifts into separate long- and short-range contributions (as in the Deser-Trueman formula) holds only below a critical short-range coupling strength; above that threshold, three-body correlations become non-negligible and the factorization breaks down, with the critical coupling depending on whether the short-range potential acts between all pairs or only a subset.

## Method

The authors performed variational three-body calculations using a correlated Gaussian basis with the stochastic variational method, solving a generalized eigenvalue problem for two model systems of three spinless bosons. Model I consists of three identical bosons with attractive long-range (regularized Coulomb) and attractive short-range (Gaussian) potentials between all pairs; Model II has two identical bosons (mass 1, charge +1) and a third particle (mass 1/2, charge -1) with repulsive long-range interactions between the identical bosons and short-range attraction only between the third particle and each of the two identical bosons. The validity of factorization was quantified using a determinant method applied to a matrix of energy shifts computed for different long-range potential ranges and short-range strengths.

## Key numbers

- For Model I (three identical bosons), the critical short-range coupling where factorization breaks down is approximately lambda_c ~ 0.4, while the two-body system with the same potentials shows breakdown at lambda_c ~ 0.6.
- For Model II (two identical bosons plus a lighter opposite-charge particle), the critical short-range coupling is approximately lambda_c ~ 0.8.
- The variational three-body calculations were converged to within 10^-4 in energy.
- The range parameters for both long- and short-range potentials were set to mu_LR = mu_SR = 30 (in units where the inverse Bohr radius is 1).
- The determinant was considered effectively zero when |det S_2| was less than approximately 10^-6, based on the order of magnitude of matrix elements and calculation accuracy.
- The short-range coupling lambda = 1 corresponds to the threshold for a two-body bound state from the short-range potential alone.

## Context

This work extends a preliminary study by Richard and Fayard (2017) on three identical bosons with long- and short-range interactions, providing more quantitative analysis using the determinant method to assess factorization. The study is motivated by kaonic atoms (kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium) studied experimentally by the SIDDHARTA collaboration (2011, 2012) and theoretically by Horiuchi et al. (2017), where the antikaon-nucleon interaction is extracted from energy shifts. The results enable future work on excited states, larger submatrix determinants to test sum-of-products factorization, and extension to complex potentials that incorporate meson-baryon absorption effects relevant to the antikaon-nucleon interaction.
