# CGC for Ultra-Peripheral Pb+Pb Collisions at the Large Hadron Collider: a more realistic calculation

> We provide the first calculation of two-gluon production at mid-rapidity in ultra-peripheral collisions in the Color Glass Condensate framework. To estimate systematic uncertainty associated with poor understanding of the wave function of the nearly real photon, we consider two diametrically different models: the dilute quark-antiquark dipole approximation and a vector meson, in which color charge density is approximated by McLerran-Venugopalan model. In the experimentally relevant range, the target nucleus can be faithfully approximated by a highly saturated state. This simplification enables us to perform efficient numerical simulations and extract the two-gluon correlation functions and the associated azimuthal harmonics.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first calculation of two-gluon production at mid-rapidity in ultra-peripheral collisions within the Color Glass Condensate framework, but the results are unavoidably model-dependent due to poor understanding of the nearly real photon wave function; the authors hedge that their calculation uses two diametrically different models (dipole and vector meson) to estimate systematic uncertainty, and that the target nucleus can only be faithfully approximated by a highly saturated state in the experimentally relevant range.

## Method

The authors calculate inclusive two-gluon production from the projectile wave function using the eikonal approximation for the emission vertex, valid when the two observed gluons have sufficiently different rapidities but not so different that evolution corrections become important. They model the projectile photon wave function using two approaches: a dilute quark-antiquark dipole approximation (with a modified perturbative wave function using a fixed effective quark mass of approximately 200 MeV) and a vector meson model approximated by the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model with Gaussian color charge density correlators. For the dense nuclear target, they employ the factorized dipole approximation (FDA), which treats the target as a near-black-disk object and reduces complicated multi-Wilson-line correlators to products of dipole S-matrices, enabling efficient numerical Fourier transforms to extract two-gluon correlation functions and azimuthal harmonics.

## Key numbers

- The effective quark mass parameter used in the modified perturbative photon wave function is set to Q approximately 200 MeV (see Section on numerical details).
- The condition for applicability of the factorized dipole approximation is Q_s^2 * S_proj_perp >> 1, where Q_s is the saturation momentum of the nucleus and S_proj_perp is the effective transverse area of the projectile, which for a nearly real photon is approximately Lambda_QCD^{-2}.
- For N_c = 3, the symmetric part of the trace of four fundamental generators simplifies to Tr(t^a t^b t^c t^d)_sym = (1/18)(delta^{ab} delta^{cd} + delta^{ac} delta^{bd} + delta^{ad} delta^{bc}).

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based theoretical analysis of ultra-peripheral collisions from forward rapidity (studied by Shi et al. (2020)) to mid-rapidity, which is crucial because experimental data from ATLAS (2021) shows extremely strong rapidity dependence and forward production accounts for only a small fraction of particles. The calculation uses the factorized dipole approximation previously developed by Kovner et al. (2017, 2018) and applied to particle production and correlations by Altinoluk et al. (2018), Kovner et al. (2018), and Li et al. (2021); this enables the first mid-rapidity two-gluon production calculation in UPC within CGC, though the authors note their work is not the final word given the necessary approximations.
