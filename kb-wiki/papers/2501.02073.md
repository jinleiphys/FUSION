# Symmetry and causality constraints on Fermi liquids

> We investigate symmetry and causality constraints on interacting Fermi liquids. Whereas Galilean or Lorentz boost symmetry leads to a well-known constraint on the first Landau parameter $F_1$, we show that scale invariance similarly constrains $F_0$. In the case of conformal Fermi liquids, we show that causality constraints on the particle-hole continuum and on zero sound strongly restrict the available parameter space for interacting Fermi liquids. We also consider nonlinear response, which we show is sensitive to additional ``generalized Landau parameters'' even at lowest orders in the long wavelength limit. We impose Galilean, Lorentz and scale symmetry on these generalized Landau parameters, obtaining further nonlinear constraints. We test our constraints in several microscopic models that enter a Fermi liquid phase.

## Key claim

While Galilean or Lorentz boost symmetry is known to constrain the first Landau parameter \(F_1\), the authors show that scale invariance similarly constrains the zeroth Landau parameter \(F_0\), and that causality constraints on the particle-hole continuum and on zero sound strongly restrict the available parameter space for interacting Fermi liquids, particularly in the conformal case.

## Method

The authors use Fermi surface bosonization to construct an effective field theory for interacting Fermi liquids, parametrized by Landau parameters \(F_\ell\) and generalized Landau parameters for nonlinear response. They impose spacetime symmetries (Galilean, Lorentz, and scale invariance) on static susceptibilities derived from the EFT, and apply microcausality constraints (analyticity of retarded Green's functions in the forward light cone) to the particle-hole continuum and collective modes. They test their constraints in microscopic models including free Dirac fermions, Dirac fermions with four-Fermi interactions, and a large \(N\) Chern-Simons matter theory.

## Key numbers

* For Galilean symmetry: \(1+F_1 = m_*/m\), where \(m_* \equiv p_F/v_F\) is the effective mass and \(m\) is the central charge of the Galilean group; for Lorentz symmetry: \(1+F_1 = m_*/\mu\), where \(\mu = \epsilon_F\) is the chemical potential.
* For scale invariance: \(1+F_0 = 2\mu/(v_F p_F)\) under Schrodinger symmetry and \(1+F_0 = \mu/(v_F p_F)\) under conformal symmetry, both valid in \(d=2\) and \(d=3\) spatial dimensions.
* For a conformal Fermi liquid, the Fermi velocity is fixed as \(v_F^2 = 1/[(1+F_0)(1+F_1)]\), leading to the causality bound \(F_0 F_1 + F_0 + F_1 \geq 0\) from subluminality of the particle-hole continuum.
* Demanding zero sound be subluminal in a conformal Fermi liquid gives the stronger constraint \(2F_0 F_1 + F_0 + F_1 \geq 0\), requiring \(F_0 \geq -1/2\).
* In the strong coupling limit \(F_0, F_1 \to \infty\) with fixed ratio, zero sound speed approaches \(v_0 = 1/\sqrt{2}\), matching the conformal speed of sound of a superfluid.

## Context

This work extends classic constraints on Fermi liquids from boost symmetries (Pines and Nozieres 2018, Pitaevskii and Lifshitz 1980, Baym and Chin 1976) to include scale invariance and causality. It provides new UV/IR constraints on marginal Landau parameters, complementing recent work on UV/IR constraints in effective field theories (Paulos 2017, Bellazzini 2020, Arkani-Hamed 2020, Caron-Huot 2020) and on compressible phases (Luttinger 1960, Oshikawa 2000, Else 2020, Komargodski 2021). The nonlinear response analysis extends the EFT of Delacretaz (2022) to include generalized Landau parameters, and the causality bounds are potentially of phenomenological interest for QCD at high densities.
