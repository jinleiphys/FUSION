# Polarized Photons from the Early Stages of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The polarization of real photons emitted from early-time heavy-ion collisions is calculated, concentrating on the contribution from bremsstrahlung and quark-antiquark annihilation processes at leading order in the strong coupling. The effect of an initial momentum space anisotropy of the parton distribution is evaluated using a model for the non-equilibrium scattering kernel for momentum broadening. The effect on the photon polarization is reported for different degrees of anisotropy. The real photons emitted early during in-medium interactions will be dominantly polarized along the beam axis.

## Key claim

Real photons emitted from early-stage heavy-ion collisions via bremsstrahlung and quark-antiquark annihilation at leading order in the strong coupling will be dominantly polarized along the beam axis, but this result depends on the presence of an initial momentum-space anisotropy in the parton distribution and is evaluated using a model for the non-equilibrium scattering kernel that focuses on anisotropic corrections to chromoelectric screening.

## Method

The authors calculate the polarization-dependent photon emission rates from bremsstrahlung and pair annihilation processes in a longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma, extending the framework of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001-2002) to anisotropic media. They model the non-equilibrium medium using the Romatschke-Strickland (2003) anisotropic distribution with parameter ξ, and construct a toy collision kernel for momentum broadening that includes anisotropic corrections to the Debye mass while expanding to first order in ξ. The integro-differential equation for the momentum-broadening function f is solved numerically in impact-parameter space, with the collision kernel expanded in ξ to obtain the zeroth-order isotropic solution and first-order anisotropic correction.

## Key numbers

- The anisotropic collision kernel is expanded to order O(ξ), with ξ ≪ 1 quantifying the degree of momentum-space anisotropy
- The equilibrium Debye mass m_D0² appears in the isotropic collision kernel C_eq(q_⊥) = g² C_F T (1/q_⊥² − 1/(q_⊥² + m_D0²))
- The anisotropic Debye mass is m_D²(φ_q) = (1 − 2ξ/3) m_D0² + ξ m_D0² cos² φ_q
- The hard scale Λ (akin to temperature) sets the scale for the anisotropic collision kernel C(q_⊥) = g² C_F Λ (1/q_⊥² − 1/(q_⊥² + m_D²(φ_q)))
- The polarized splitting functions are F_in(ζ) = (2−ζ)²/ζ and F_out(ζ) = ζ, where ζ = k/(p_x + k) is the photon momentum fraction

## Context

This work extends previous estimates of photon polarization from early-time heavy-ion collisions by Baym et al. (2014), Schenke et al. (2006), and Bhattacharya et al. (2015), which considered only Compton scattering and qq̅ annihilation at leading order, by including the equally important bremsstrahlung and pair-annihilation channels that involve the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect. The calculation sets foundations for evaluating virtual photon polarization states through dilepton final states, which are more experimentally accessible than real photon polarization measurements. The formalism can be extended to include transverse expansion and finite rapidity effects.
