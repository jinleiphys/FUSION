# Microscopic study of Ca$+$Ca fusion

> We investigate the fusion barriers for reactions involving Ca isotopes $\mathrm{^{40}Ca}+\mathrm{^{40}Ca}$, $\mathrm{^{40}Ca}+\mathrm{^{48}Ca}$, and $\mathrm{^{48}Ca}+\mathrm{^{48}Ca}$ using the microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory coupled with a density constraint. In this formalism the fusion barriers are directly obtained from TDHF dynamics. We also study the excitation of the pre-equilibrium GDR for the $\mathrm{^{40}Ca}+\mathrm{^{48}Ca}$ system and the associated $γ$-ray emission spectrum. Fusion cross-sections are calculated using the incoming-wave boundary condition approach. We examine the dependence of fusion barriers on collision energy as well as on the different parametrizations of the Skyrme interaction.

## Key claim
The DC-TDHF method reproduces experimental fusion cross-sections for Ca isotope reactions without adjustable parameters, but the quality of agreement depends on the collision energy used to generate the potential barrier, with the lowest collision energy giving the best fit to sub-barrier cross-sections.

## Method
The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations in 3D geometry using the full Skyrme interaction (primarily SLy4, also UNEDF0 and UNEDF1) for head-on collisions of Ca isotopes. At selected times during the TDHF evolution, they applied a density constraint to perform static Hartree-Fock minimization, yielding the ion-ion interaction potential V(R) from the difference between the density-constrained energy and the binding energies of the separated nuclei. Fusion cross-sections were then calculated by solving the Schrodinger equation with the incoming-wave boundary condition method, using a coordinate-dependent mass parameter to transform the potential.

## Key numbers
- Barrier heights for 40Ca+40Ca: 53.02 MeV (E_TDHF=55 MeV), 53.43 MeV (E_TDHF=60 MeV), 53.57 MeV (E_TDHF=65 MeV); corresponding barrier positions: 10.41 fm, 10.32 fm, 10.23 fm.
- Barrier heights for 48Ca+48Ca: 50.98 MeV (E_TDHF=55 MeV), 51.37 MeV (E_TDHF=65 MeV), 51.52 MeV (E_TDHF=70 MeV); corresponding barrier positions: 10.80 fm, 10.63 fm, 10.52 fm.
- Barrier heights for 40Ca+48Ca: 51.11 MeV (E_TDHF=55 MeV), 51.45 MeV (E_TDHF=60 MeV), 51.81 MeV (E_TDHF=70 MeV); corresponding barrier positions: 10.75 fm, 10.65 fm, 10.51 fm.
- For 48Ca+48Ca at E_TDHF=55 MeV, the UNEDF1 parametrization gave a barrier peak 187 keV higher than SLy4.
- During the approach phase, the DC-TDHF potential traced the point Coulomb potential to within less than 150 keV.

## Context
This work extends the DC-TDHF method previously applied to systems such as 132Sn+64Ni, 64Ni+64Ni, and 16O+208Pb (Umar and Oberacker, 2006-2010) to the Ca isotope systems that have been the focus of recent experimental attention (Stefanini, 2009; Jiang, 2010; Montagnoli, 2010, 2011). The results address the failure of standard coupled-channel calculations using frozen densities at deep sub-barrier energies, which has been partially remedied by including a repulsive core (Esbensen, 2006, 2010) or neck degrees of freedom (Itoh, 2007). The DC-TDHF approach enables a parameter-free microscopic description that naturally incorporates dynamical effects such as neck formation, mass exchange, and internal excitations, and can be extended to study pre-equilibrium GDR excitation and gamma-ray emission spectra.
