# Light vector and axial mesons effective couplings to constituent quarks

> Low energy effective couplings of baryons' constituent quarks to light vector and axial mesons are derived by considering quark polarization for a dressed one gluon exchange quark interaction. The quark field is splitted into two components, one for background constituent quarks and another one for quark-antiquark states, light mesons and the scalar chiral condensate. By considering a large quark effective mass derivative expansion, several effective coupling constants are resolved as functions of the original model parameters and of components of the quark and gluon propagators. Besides the leading single vector meson-constituent quark gauge-type effective coupling, several two-vector and axial mesons-constituent quark couplings are also obtained in the next leading order. Among these, vector and axial mesons mixings induced by constituent quark currents are found.Approximated and exact ratios between the effective coupling constants in the limit of very large quark effective mass and numerical estimates are exhibited. Numerical results of the corresponding form factors and of the (strong) vector meson quadratic radius are also presented.

## Key claim
Effective couplings of constituent quarks to light vector and axial mesons, including a gauge-type coupling and several two-meson couplings, can be derived from a dressed one-gluon exchange interaction. The derivation relies on a large quark effective mass derivative expansion, meaning the resulting coupling constants and their ratios are strictly valid only in the limit of a very large quark effective mass, which is not fully realized by the numerical estimates.

## Method
The author starts with a dressed one-gluon exchange quark interaction and performs a Fierz transformation to expose the flavor structure. The quark field is split into background constituent quarks and sea quarks using the background field method, and light meson fields are introduced via the auxiliary field method. A large quark effective mass and derivative expansion of the sea quark determinant is then performed to extract the leading and next-to-leading order effective coupling constants for vector and axial mesons to constituent quarks.

## Key numbers
- The leading gauge-type coupling constants for both isovector and isoscalar mesons are set to a typical value of $g_{r1} = g_{v1} = 12$ to reproduce nucleon-nucleon potential values.
- The approximated ratio of the two-meson coupling constant to the leading coupling is $\frac{g_{va-j}}{g_{r1}} \sim \frac{3}{ 2 M^* }$, where $M^*$ is the quark effective mass.
- The exact ratio between two derived coupling constants is $\frac{g_{\epsilon v}}{g_{va-j}} = - \frac{1}{ M^* }$.
- The vector meson mass parameter is given by ${M_v^{(0)}}^2 = - i d_1 8 N_c \; Tr' \; (( {S}_0 (k) S_0 (k) ))$, which is the same for all vector and axial mesons in this formalism.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous derivations of constituent quark-pion effective couplings (Braghin, 2016, 2017) to the vector and axial meson sector. It provides a QCD-based derivation for effective couplings that are often parameterized phenomenologically, such as in the works of Sakurai and the Hidden Gauge Approach. The results enable a more fundamental calculation of vector meson-nucleon interactions and their momentum dependence via form factors, which are crucial for short-range nuclear potentials and in-medium effects.
