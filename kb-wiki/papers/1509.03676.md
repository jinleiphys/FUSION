# Nuclear medium effect on nuclear modification factor of protons and pions in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions

> Nuclear modification factor ($R_{cp}$) of protons and pions are investigated by simulating Au + Au collisions from 0.8 to 1.8$A$ GeV in a framework of an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. $R_{cp}$ of protons rises with the increase of \pt~ at different beam energies owing to radial flow and Cronin effect. The rate of increase of \rcp~ is suppressed at higher beam energies. The significant difference of $R_{cp}$ between protons and pions indicates different medium effects between protons and pions. By changing the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, the $R_{cp}$ of protons changes a lot, while the $R_{cp}$ of pions does not. Taking the pion absorption into account, the $R_{cp}$ of pions becomes close to unity without $p_{T}$ dependence after deactivating the reaction $πN \rightarrow Δ$, while there is nearly no change on proton. This suggests that the pion absorption plays a dominant role on pion dynamics and have slight effect for proton dynamics.

# Nuclear medium effect on nuclear modification factor of protons and pions in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The nuclear modification factor $R_{cp}$ of protons rises with transverse momentum at beam energies from 0.8 to 1.8$A$ GeV due to radial flow and the Cronin effect, but this rise is suppressed at higher beam energies; the significant difference between proton and pion $R_{cp}$ indicates that pion absorption (via the $\pi N \rightarrow \Delta$ channel) dominates pion dynamics while having only a slight effect on proton dynamics, and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section strongly affects proton $R_{cp}$ but not pion $R_{cp}$.

## Method

The authors simulated Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 0.8 to 1.8$A$ GeV using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state including momentum-dependent interaction (SM-EOS). Pions were produced exclusively via $\Delta$ resonance decay, with inelastic channels $NN \rightarrow N\Delta$, $\Delta \rightarrow N\pi$, $\Delta N \rightarrow NN$, and $N\pi \rightarrow \Delta$ included. The nuclear modification factor $R_{cp}$ was computed as the ratio of particle yields in central (0-20% centrality) to peripheral (40-80% centrality) collisions, normalized by the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions $\langle N_{coll}\rangle$, with a rapidity cut $|Y/Y_{proj}|<0.1$. The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section was varied using a density-dependent reduction factor $\eta$ from 0 to 0.9, and the pion absorption channel was deactivated to isolate its effect.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 0.8, 1.0, 1.2, 1.5, and 1.8$A$ GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- In-medium NNCS reduction factor $\eta$ values tested: 0, 0.2, 0.5, 0.7, and 0.9 at 1$A$ GeV.
- Pion absorption probability in Au+Au at 1$A$ GeV: 81% (cited from Bass 1995).
- Coalescence parameters for fragment identification: $\Delta r = 3.5$ fm and $\Delta p = 300$ MeV/c between nucleons.
- Wave packet width parameter $L = 2.16$ fm$^2$ for the Au+Au system.
- Symmetry potential strength $t_6 = 100$ MeV.
- Blast-wave fit parameters: both radial flow velocity $\beta$ and freeze-out temperature $T$ increase with beam energy from 0.8 to 1.8$A$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Lv et al., 2015) on proton $R_{cp}$ in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions by systematically comparing protons and pions, and by investigating the role of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section and pion absorption. The study uses the IQMD model (Hartnack et al., 1998) and compares pion spectra with FOPI experimental data (Pelte et al., 1997). The results connect to the broader literature on nuclear modification factors in relativistic heavy ion collisions (RHIC experiments, STAR collaboration) where $R_{cp}$ suppression at high $p_T$ is attributed to partonic energy loss, in contrast to the enhancement observed here at intermediate energies due to radial flow and the Cronin effect. The work enables future extraction of the in-medium NNCS reduction factor by comparing simulated radial flow and $R_{cp}$ with experimental data.
