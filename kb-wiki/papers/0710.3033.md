# Photoproduction of $ω$ and $ω$ in the Nuclear Medium

> We reanalyze data from ELSA on $ω$ production in nuclei, from where claims of a large shift of the mass were made earlier, which are tied to a certain election of the background in nuclei, very different in shape to the one on the proton. The reanalysis shows that the data demand a very large width of the $ω$ in the medium, with no need for a shift of the mass, for which the experiment is quite insensitive. We study possible $ω$ bound states in the nucleus and find that, even assuming a small width, they could not be observed with the present ELSA resolution. Finally we show that, due to the interplay of background and $ω$ signal, a two bump structure appears with the ELSA set up for the $(γ,p)$ reaction that should not be misidentified with a signal of a possible $ω$ bound state in the nucleus.

## Key claim

The ELSA data on ω photoproduction in nuclei, previously claimed to show a large downward mass shift of about 100 MeV, are actually compatible with a very large in-medium width of the ω (approximately 90 MeV at normal nuclear matter density) and no mass shift; the experiment is too insensitive to the mass shift to determine it precisely.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation of the photonuclear reaction A(γ,ω→π⁰γ)X, including Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and final-state interactions of the ω and pions. The ω mesons were produced according to an in-medium spectral function that depends on the local nuclear density, and the nuclear transparency ratio T_A was calculated for a range of targets from ¹²C to ²³⁸U. The simulated invariant mass spectra from π⁰γ events were compared to the ELSA data for a ⁹²Nb target, using the same background shape as determined from the hydrogen target.

## Key numbers

- The free ω width is Γ_free = 8.49 MeV.
- The best-fit in-medium collisional width is Γ_abs = 90 × (ρ(r)/ρ₀) MeV, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter density.
- The incident photon beam for the transparency ratio analysis was constrained to 1.45 GeV < E_γ < 1.55 GeV.
- The experimental resolution at ELSA is about 50 MeV (used for folding the ω signal) and also 55 MeV (Gaussian resolution used in the mass spectra analysis).
- The higher momentum cut on the π⁰γ pair was |p_π⁰ + p_γ| < 500 MeV (and also < 400 MeV in some analyses).
- The incident photon beam for the mass spectra analysis was constrained to 0.9 GeV < E_γ^in < 2.6 GeV.
- A mass shift of m_ω ± 40 (ρ/ρ₀) MeV produces a band narrower than the statistical fluctuations.

## Context

This work reanalyzes the CBELSA/TAPS data from Trnka et al. (2005), which had claimed evidence for a ~100 MeV downward shift of the ω mass in the nuclear medium. The reanalysis shows that the data instead demand a large in-medium width (about 90 MeV at ρ₀) with no mass shift. The authors also study possible ω bound states in the nucleus using the Green function method of earlier work (NPA 435, NPA 761) and find that even with an ideal attractive potential (scattering length a = 1.6 + 0.3i fm from Klingl et al.), the bound states would not be observable with the present ELSA resolution. The work enables future experiments with better resolution to distinguish between width and mass-shift scenarios.
