# Many body effects in nuclear matter QCD sum rules

> We calculate the single-particle nucleon characteristics in symmetric nuclear matter with inclusion of the 3N and 4N interactions. We calculated the contribution of the 3N interactions earlier, now we add that of the 4N ones. The contribution of the 4N forces to nucleon self energies is expressed in terms of the nonlocal scalar condensate (d=3) and of the configurations of the vector-scalar and the scalar-scalar quark condensates (d=6) in which two diquark operators act on two different nucleons of the matter.These four-quark condensates are obtained in the model-independent way. The density dependence of the nucleon effective mass, of the vector self energy and of the single-particle potential energy are obtained. We traced the dependence of the nucleon characteristics on the actual value of the pion-nucleon sigma term. We obtained also the nucleon characteristics in terms of the quasifree nucleons, with the noninteracting nucleons surrounded by their pion clouds as the starting point. This approach leads to strict hierarchy of the many body forces.

# Many body effects in nuclear matter QCD sum rules

## Key claim

The paper calculates the contribution of four-nucleon (4N) forces to nucleon self-energies in symmetric nuclear matter using QCD sum rules, finding that 4N forces increase the nucleon effective mass by 73 MeV at saturation density while their contribution to the vector self-energy is consistent with zero, a result that depends on the specific model chosen for the continuum threshold in the sum rules.

## Method

The authors extend their previous QCD sum rule calculations for symmetric nuclear matter by including 4N interactions in addition to the previously treated 2N and 3N forces. The 4N force contributions are expressed in terms of the nonlinear scalar condensate (dimension 3) and of vector-scalar and scalar-scalar four-quark condensates (dimension 6) where two diquark operators act on two different nucleons of the matter, with these four-quark condensates obtained in a model-independent way. The nonlinear scalar condensate contributions are computed using results from chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) for the sigma-term contributions σ⁽²⁾ and σ⁽³⁾, while the four-quark condensate contributions are obtained by replacing the free-nucleon matrix element κ_N with κ_N + κ⁽²⁾ in the expressions previously derived for 3N forces.

## Key numbers

- At ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ (saturation density) and σ_N = 45 MeV, the 4N forces increase the nucleon effective mass m* by 73 MeV (from 574 MeV with 3N forces to 647 MeV with 3N+4N forces)
- The 3N forces alone diminish m* by 26 MeV at ρ₀ (from 599 MeV in gas approximation to 574 MeV)
- The 3N forces diminish the vector self-energy Σ_V by 37 MeV at ρ₀ (from 222 MeV to 185 MeV)
- The 4N forces change Σ_V by 7 MeV at ρ₀, but the authors state this is consistent with zero within the model uncertainty
- σ⁽³⁾ = -9.49 MeV at ρ₀, while σ⁽²⁾ = 2.83 MeV at ρ₀
- The total contribution of many-body forces (3N+4N) increases m* by 48 MeV and decreases Σ_V by 30 MeV at ρ₀
- For the quasifree nucleon presentation at ρ₀: σ̂_N = 63 MeV, σ̂⁽²⁾ = -23 MeV, σ̂⁽³⁾ = -5.3 MeV
- The nucleon effective mass m* varies from 746 MeV (σ_N = 35 MeV) to 500 MeV (σ_N = 60 MeV) at ρ₀ with all many-body forces included

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of 3N force contributions in nuclear matter QCD sum rules (Drukarev et al., 2015) by adding 4N forces. The nonlinear scalar condensate contributions are obtained from chiral perturbation theory results (Kaiser, 2002) for the density-dependent sigma term. The approach contrasts with traditional nuclear physics treatments based on nucleon-nucleon potentials, instead expressing nucleon self-energies in terms of QCD condensates. The results enable improved predictions for nucleon properties in nuclear matter and establish a hierarchy where 2N forces dominate, 3N forces provide smaller corrections, and 4N forces give still smaller contributions to the vector self-energy, though the 4N contribution to the effective mass can exceed the 3N contribution due to cancellations in the scalar channel.
