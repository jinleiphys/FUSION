# Effects of hadron-quark phase transition on properties of Neutron Stars

> We investigate the possible scenario of deconfinement of hyperon rich hadronic matter to quark matter at high densities and the resulting hybrid star (HS) properties are analyzed. In the relativistic mean-field framework, we construct the equation of state (EoS) of hadronic matter using the effective chiral model while the pure quark matter is described using the MIT Bag model. We revisit the hyperon puzzle and analyze the possibility of hadron-quark phase transition with proper choice of the bag constant. In static condition the maximum mass of the resultant HSs are in good agreement with the recent observational bounds on the same from high mass pulsars such as PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432. On invoking the phenomenon of phase transition, the radius of canonical mass ($R_{1.4}$) and value of $R_{1.6}$ predicted by the model lie within the range prescribed from binary neutron star (BNS) merger detected by the LIGO-Virgo collaboration in 2017. The surface redshift obtained for the HSs also satisfy the constraints from pulsars RX J0720.4-3125 and 1E 1207.4-5209. It is noteworthy that unlike several other works, we add no modifications to the original form of the Bag model to satisfy these recent observational and empirical constraints on NS properties. We also discuss the rotational aspects of the HSs by calculating the properties like rotational mass, radius, energy density, moment of inertia at different angular velocities. The maximum bound on rotational frequency from the rapidly rotating pulsars like PSR B1937+21 and PSR J1748-2446ad are satisfied with the HS configuration. We also test the universality of our hybrid EoS in terms of normalized moment of inertia.

## Key claim

The authors show that a proper choice of the bag constant (without any modifications to the original MIT Bag model) allows hybrid stars containing hyperons to satisfy recent observational constraints on neutron star mass, radius, and surface redshift, thereby offering a resolution to the "hyperon puzzle" that does not require additional repulsive interactions or modified gravity.

## Method

The hadronic phase is described using an effective chiral model in the relativistic mean-field framework, including the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ−,0,+, Ξ−,0) with hyperon coupling constants fixed by reproducing empirical binding energies (−28 MeV for Λ, +30 MeV for Σ, −18 MeV for Ξ). The pure quark phase (u, d, s quarks) is described using the standard MIT Bag model with bag constants B^(1/4) = 180 and 200 MeV. The hadron-quark phase transition is treated via Maxwell construction (local charge neutrality), assuming high surface tension at the interface. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved for static stars, and the RNS code is used for rotating configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hybrid stars: consistent with PSR J1614-2230 (M = 1.928 ± 0.017 M⊙) and PSR J0348+0432 (M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙)
- Radius of canonical 1.4 M⊙ star (R1.4): satisfies constraint R1.4 < 13.76 km from GW170817 (Fattoyev 2018) and 12.00 ≤ R1.4 ≤ 13.45 km (Most 2018)
- Radius of 1.6 M⊙ star (R1.6): satisfies upper limit R1.6 ≤ 13.3 km and lower limit R1.6 ≥ 10.68^(+0.15)_(−0.04) km from GW170817 (Bauswein 2017)
- Surface gravitational redshift Z_S: satisfies constraints from pulsar 1E 1207.4-5209 (Z_S = 0.12 – 0.23) and RX J0720.4-3125 (Z_S = 0.205^(+0.006)_(−0.003))
- Rotational frequency: satisfies maximum bound from rapidly rotating pulsars PSR B1937+21 and PSR J1748-2446ad
- Nuclear saturation density ρ0 = 0.153 fm^(−3), binding energy B/A = −16.3 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32 MeV, incompressibility K = 303 MeV, slope parameter L0 = 87 MeV
- Hyperon threshold densities: Λ appears at ≈ 1.9ρ0, Ξ− at 2.6ρ0, Σ− at 3.4ρ0, Ξ0 at 7.2ρ0

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the hyperon puzzle using the same effective chiral model (Jha 2006, 2007, 2008) by including all hyperon species with realistic binding energies rather than only the Λ hyperon. It contrasts with approaches that require modified gravity (Doneva 2013, Astashenok 2014), repulsive hyperon-hyperon interactions (Bednarek 2012, Weissenborn 2012), or modified versions of the MIT Bag model such as density-dependent bag constants (Yazdizadeh 2012, Logoteta 2013) or one-gluon exchange corrections (Miyatsu 2013). The results enable future studies of hybrid star tidal deformability and universal relations for moment of inertia, which can be tested against upcoming gravitational wave observations and neutron star radius measurements from missions like NICER.
