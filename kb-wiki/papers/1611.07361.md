# The Peak Structure in the In-Flight ${}^{3}\text{He} ( K^{-} , \, Λp ) n$ Reaction Around the $\bar{K} N N$ Threshold

> We theoretically investigate the origin of the peak structure around the $K^{-} p p$ threshold observed in the in-flight ${}^{3}\text{He} ( K^{-} , \, Λp ) n$ reaction in the recent E15 experiment at J-PARC. For this purpose, we consider two scenarios to produce the peak. One is that the $Λ(1405)$ is generated but it does not correlate with $p$, and the uncorrelated $Λ(1405) p$ system subsequently decays into $Λp$. The other one is that the $\bar{K} N N$ bound state is indeed generated and decays into $Λp$. As a result, the experimental signal is qualitatively well reproduced in the $\bar{K} N N$ bound scenario, definitely discarding the uncorrelated $Λ(1405) p$ one.

## Key claim

The peak structure near the \(K^{-}pp\) threshold observed in the J-PARC E15 experiment is qualitatively well reproduced by the scenario in which a \(\bar{K} N N\) bound state is generated and decays into \(\Lambda p\), while the alternative scenario of an uncorrelated \(\Lambda(1405)p\) system is definitely discarded.

## Method

The authors calculated the \(\Lambda p\) invariant mass spectrum for the in-flight \({}^{3}\text{He}(K^{-},\Lambda p)n\) reaction at a kaon laboratory momentum of \(k_{\text{lab}} = 1\ \text{GeV}/c\) using two distinct theoretical mechanisms. In the uncorrelated \(\Lambda(1405)p\) scenario, the amplitude was constructed from a first collision amplitude taken from differential cross sections and a second collision amplitude from the chiral unitary approach, with the \({}^{3}\text{He}\) wave function described by an antisymmetrized harmonic oscillator. In the \(\bar{K} N N\) bound scenario, the multiple kaon scattering between two nucleons was treated using the fixed center approximation, which generated a resonance pole at \(2354 - 36i\ \text{MeV}\).

## Key numbers

- The experimental peak observed by E15 has mass \(M_X = 2355^{+6}_{-8}\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 12\ (\text{sys.})\ \text{MeV}\) and width \(\Gamma_X = 110^{+19}_{-17}\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 27\ (\text{sys.})\ \text{MeV}\).
- In the \(\bar{K} N N\) bound scenario, the multiple scattering amplitudes generate a resonance pole at \(2354 - 36i\ \text{MeV}\).
- The total cross section in the \(\bar{K} N N\) bound scenario is \(7.6\ \mu\text{b}\) (option A) or \(5.6\ \mu\text{b}\) (option B), consistent with the empirical value \(7 \pm 1\ \mu\text{b}\) from Sada (2016).
- In the uncorrelated \(\Lambda(1405)p\) scenario, the calculated peak appears at more than \(2370\ \text{MeV}\), inconsistent with the experimental peak at \(2355\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work addresses the ongoing debate about the existence and properties of the \(\bar{K} N N\) (or \(K^{-}pp\)) bound state, which has been studied theoretically by Akaishi (2002), Shevchenko (2006), Ikeda (2007), Dote (2008), Wycech (2008), Bayar (2011), and Barnea (2012), and experimentally by Agnello (2005), Yamazaki (2010), Tokiyasu (2013), Ichikawa (2014), and Hashimoto (2014). The results support the interpretation that the peak observed in the J-PARC E15 experiment by Sada (2016) is indeed a signal of the \(\bar{K} N N\) bound state, and the authors anticipate further confirmation from high-statistics data in the second run of E15, including angular dependence and the \(\Lambda p / \Sigma^{0}p\) branching ratio.
