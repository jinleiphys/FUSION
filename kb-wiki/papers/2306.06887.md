# Dynamics of the $O(4)$ critical point in QCD: critical pions and diffusion in Model G

> We present a detailed study of the finite momentum dynamics of the $O(4)$ critical point of QCD, which lies in the dynamic universality class of Model G. The critical scaling of the model is analyzed in multiple dynamical channels. For instance, the finite momentum analysis allows us to precisely extract the pion dispersion curve below the critical point. The pion velocity is in striking agreement with the predictions relation and static universality. The pion damping rate and velocity are both consistent with the dynamical critical exponent $ζ= 3/2$ of Model G. Similarly, although the critical amplitude for the diffusion coefficient of the conserved $O(4)$ charges is small, it is clearly visible both in the restored phase and with finite explicit symmetry breaking, and its dynamical scaling is again consistent with $ζ=3/2$. We determine a new set of universal dynamical critical amplitude ratios relating the diffusion coefficient to a suitably defined order parameter relaxation time. We also show that in a finite volume simulation, the chiral condensate diffuses on the coset manifold in a manner consistent with dynamical scaling, and with a diffusion coefficient that is determined by the transport coefficients of hydrodynamic pions. Finally, the amplitude ratios (together with other non-universal amplitudes also reported here) compile all relevant information for further studies of Model G both in and out of equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the finite-momentum dynamics of the O(4) critical point in QCD, which belongs to the Model G dynamic universality class, is consistent with the dynamical critical exponent ζ = d/2 = 3/2 across multiple observables, but the critical amplitude for the diffusion coefficient of the conserved O(4) charges is small, making its critical scaling weak and requiring careful extraction.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical simulations of Model G by numerically solving stochastic Langevin equations for the O(4) order parameter (σ, π) coupled to conserved vector and axial charges, using a symplectic integrator for Hamiltonian evolution combined with Metropolis updates for dissipation. They analyzed statistical two-point correlation functions of fields and charges as functions of momentum and time in three regimes: the restored phase (H=0, above Tc), the broken phase (H=0, below Tc), and along the critical line (tr=0, H≠0). The simulations were performed on cubic lattices with sizes ranging from L=48 to L=128, with parameters λ=4, χ0=5, Γ0=1, and D0=1/3 in lattice units where g=Tc=1.

## Key numbers

- The dynamical critical exponent extracted from multiple channels is ζ = 3/2 (d/2 with d=3), consistent with the theoretical expectation for Model G
- The critical mass parameter is m_c² = -4.8110(4) for λ=4
- The magnetization amplitude in the broken phase is B⁻ = 0.988(7)
- The magnetic field normalization constant is H₀ = 5.15(5)
- Static critical exponents used: β = 0.380(2), δ = 4.824(9), ω = 0.755(5)
- The bare diffusion coefficient is D₀ = 1/3 in lattice units
- The pion velocity satisfies v² = f²/χ₀, and the total damping coefficient is D_A = Γ + D

## Context

This work is a direct continuation of Florio (2021) and extends the zero-momentum analysis of Model G simulations to finite momenta, enabling precise extraction of the pion dispersion curve and critical diffusion. The study builds on the dynamical universality classification of Hohenberg and Halperin (1977) and the application of Model G to QCD by Rajagopal (1992), and it provides the necessary amplitude ratios and transport coefficients for future studies of non-equilibrium dynamics in expanding critical systems, including the quantitative analysis of how the chiral crossover in real-world QCD is influenced by the proximity to the O(4) critical point.
