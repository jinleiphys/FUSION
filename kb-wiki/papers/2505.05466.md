# Comparison of integral equations used to study $T_{cc}^+$ for a stable $D^*$

> We perform a detailed comparison between three formalisms used in recent studies of $DD^*$ scattering, which aim to understand the properties of the doubly-charmed tetraquark, $T_{cc}^+(3875)$. These methods are the three-particle relativistic field theory (RFT) formalism, the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger (LS) equation with chiral effective field theory potentials, and the two-particle relativistic framework proposed by Baiao Raposo and Hansen (BRH approach). In a simplified single-channel setting, we derive the conditions under which the infinite-volume integral equations from the RFT and BRH approaches reduce to the LS form. We present numerical examples showing that differences between these methods can be largely removed by adjusting short-range couplings. We also address a number of technical issues in the RFT approach.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the infinite-volume integral equations from the three-particle relativistic field theory (RFT) and the Baiao Raposo-Hansen (BRH) approaches can be reduced to the Lippmann-Schwinger (LS) form under a combined nonrelativistic limit and pole-dominance approximation, and that numerical differences between the three formalisms can be largely removed by adjusting short-range couplings.

## Method

The authors compare three formalisms for DD* scattering: the three-particle RFT approach, the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation with chiral effective field theory potentials, and the two-particle relativistic BRH framework. They restrict all formalisms to a single-channel approximation (s-wave Dπ with J=1) and derive the conditions under which the RFT and BRH integral equations reduce to the LS form. Numerical comparisons are performed using parameters close to those describing lattice QCD results from Padmanath (2022), with the RFT ladder equation solved and the LS equation projected onto the J=1, L=0 channel.

## Key numbers

- The D*-D mass splitting satisfies Δ ≈ mπ/2, with mπ/MD ≈ 0.15, based on the lattice calculation of Padmanath (2022)
- The energy range of interest has ENR/mD between -0.01 and 0.04
- The power counting used is: Δ/mD ∼ mπ/mD ∼ ε, and ENR/mD ∼ p²/mD² ∼ ε²
- The D* pole momentum q0 is given by q0 = λ^(1/2)(mD*², mD², mπ²)/(2mD*), which is purely imaginary for a stable D*
- The residue ζ² is given by ζ² = -½ q0² g_DD*π² = -q0² g² (2mD mD*)/fπ², with fπ ≈ 130 MeV

## Context

This work extends the comparison of formalisms used to study the doubly-charmed tetraquark Tcc+(3875), following lattice QCD computations by Du (2023), Collins (2024), and Prelovsek (2025) that reveal it as a subthreshold complex pole in DD* scattering at heavier-than-physical quark masses. The paper connects the three-body RFT approach (originating from the work of Hansen and Sharpe) with the nonrelativistic LS approach used by Meng and Epelbaum (2021-2024) and the relativistic two-particle BRH approach proposed by Baiao Raposo and Hansen (2023). This comparison enables future work to determine which formalism is most suitable for analyzing lattice QCD results and understanding the Tcc+ properties at the physical point where three-body DDπ decay becomes relevant.
