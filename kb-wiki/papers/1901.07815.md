# Transport properties from Charm to Bottom: $p_T$ suppression, anisotropic flow $v_n$ and their correlations to the bulk dynamics

> We study the propagation of heavy quarks (HQs) in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) by means of a relativistic Boltzmann transport (RBT) approach. The non-perturbative interaction between heavy quarks and light quarks is described by means of a quasi-particle approach able to describe simultaneously the experimental data for the nuclear suppression factor $R_{\rm AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ of D mesons from RHIC to LHC energies. In the same framework we predict the B meson nuclear modification factor at LHC. Finally, we discuss the relevance of initial state fluctuations that allows to extend the analysis to high order anisotropic flows $v_n(p_T)$ as well as to investigate the role of QCD interaction in developing correlations between the light and the heavy flavour anisotropic flows.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relativistic Boltzmann transport approach with a quasi-particle model for non-perturbative heavy quark interactions can simultaneously describe D meson nuclear suppression factor \(R_{\rm AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) from RHIC to LHC energies, but this success depends on including a hybrid hadronization model of coalescence plus fragmentation, without which the simultaneous description fails.

## Method

The authors solve relativistic Boltzmann transport equations for charm quarks interacting with a bulk medium of quarks and gluons, where the bulk evolution uses a cross section fixed to maintain \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\). The non-perturbative heavy quark-light quark interaction is described by a quasi-particle model that softens the equation of state to approximate lattice QCD results. Hadronization of charm quarks to D mesons is performed via a hybrid model of coalescence plus fragmentation when the temperature drops below \(T_c = 155\) MeV, and the initial charm quark momentum distribution follows the FONLL calculation.

## Key numbers

* D meson \(R_{\rm AA}\) and \(v_2(p_T)\) are calculated for \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV at RHIC and \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV at LHC.
* Initial temperature at the fireball center is \(T_0 = 365\) MeV with initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c for RHIC, and \(T_0 = 490\) MeV with \(\tau_0 = 0.3\) fm/c for LHC.
* Coalescence increases D meson \(R_{\rm AA}\) for momenta larger than 1 GeV, and increases D meson \(v_2(p_T)\) by about 30% compared to fragmentation alone.
* The linear correlation coefficient between light and heavy flavor flow harmonics is \(C(v_n^{light}, v_n^{heavy}) \sim 0.9\) for \(n = 2, 3\) in the quasi-particle model.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical efforts to describe heavy quark observables using various models (vanHees 2007, Alberico 2011, Cao 2015, Uphoff 2011, Berrehrah 2014, Song 2015, Das 2013, Das 2015, Beraudo 2014, Scardina 2017) by providing a unified framework that simultaneously reproduces both \(R_{\rm AA}\) and \(v_2\) across RHIC and LHC energies. The event-by-event analysis of higher-order anisotropic flows \(v_n(p_T)\) and correlations between light and heavy flavor flows enables future discrimination of the temperature dependence of heavy quark transport coefficients, particularly through comparison of the quasi-particle model with pQCD-based interactions.
