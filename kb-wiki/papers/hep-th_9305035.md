# Soliton Solutions of Relativistic Hartree's Equations

> We study a model based on $N$ scalar complex fields coupled to a scalar real field, where all fields are treated classically as c-numbers. The model describes a composite particle made up of $N$ constituents with bare mass $m_0$ interacting both with each other and with themselves via the exchange of a particle of mass $μ_0$. The stationary states of the composite particle are described by relativistic Hartree's equations. Since the self-interaction is included, the case of an elementary particle is a nontrivial special case of this model. Using an integral transform method we derive the exact ground state solution and prove its local stability. The mass of the composite particle is calculated as the total energy in the rest frame. For the case of a massless exchange particle the mass formula is given in closed form. The mass, as a function of the coupling constant, possesses a well pronounced minimum for each value of $μ_0/m_0$, while the absolute minimum occurs at $μ_0=0$.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact ground-state soliton solutions for relativistic Hartree equations in 3+1 dimensions for a system of N scalar complex fields coupled to a real scalar field, treating all fields classically; the mass of the composite particle as a function of the coupling constant possesses a well-pronounced minimum for each value of the exchange-particle mass to bare-mass ratio μ₀/m₀, with the absolute minimum occurring at μ₀=0, but only under the condition that the coupling constant lies within a specific existence window bounded both above and below.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled system of relativistic Hartree equations for spherically symmetric stationary states using an integral transform method, where the wave function ψ(r) and potential φ(r) are expressed as Laplace-type integrals with unknown spectral functions. Substituting these transforms into the equations of motion yields coupled integral equations for the spectral functions, which are solved exactly via a power-series ansatz in the coupling parameters η and ζ, leading to recurrence relations for the coefficient functions φ_{nm}. The boundary conditions at the origin then determine the allowed values of η and ζ, from which the constituent energies, total mass, and size parameter are computed.

## Key numbers

- The numerical constant α₀ in the mass formula for μ₀=0 is α₀ = 3.52(2), with fit coefficients α₁ = 10.9(2) and α₂ = 3.82(9) for the σ-dependent case.
- For μ₀=0, the absolute minimum of the ground-state mass is M/Nm₀ = 2√2/3 ≈ 0.943.
- The ground state is locally stable for μ₀ < √2 m₀; for μ₀ > 0.36 m₀ the ground state becomes unstable for all possible values of the coupling constant.
- The constituent energy in the ground state is ε = (m₀/√2) √(1 + √(1 − (g²N/4π m₀² α)²)), with the excited-state constituent energy ε* = (m₀/√2) √(1 − √(1 − (g²N/4π m₀² α)²)), both real only when g²N/4π m₀² α ≤ 1.
- The size parameter at minimal mass for μ₀=0 is r₀ = √2 δ₀ / α₀ m₀, where δ₀ is a numerical constant.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic Hartree equations used by Witten (1979) for baryons in the large-Nc limit of QCD to the fully relativistic case, providing exact solutions rather than approximate ones. The model is connected to QCD via the identification g²/4π(4m²) = 4αₛ(m)/3, relating the coupling to the strong running coupling constant. The results enable systematic study of how the physical mass and size of composite particles depend on the coupling constant, the exchange-particle mass, and the number of constituents, and the author indicates that a forthcoming paper will apply a similar self-interaction bound-state approach to quantum electrodynamics.
