# Shell model study of even-even 132-134Te neutron-rich nuclei

> Large-scale shell model calculations were performed for neutron-rich even-even $^{132-136}$Te using a realistic effective interaction derived from CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential for the positive and negative parity states. The calculated results are compared with the recently available experimental data. The transition rates B(E2; 0$^+\to2^+$) are also calculated by taking into consideration core polarization effect by choosing best effective charges for proton and neutron. The result of our theoretical calculations are compared with experimental data and with the previous theoretical work. A very good agreement were obtained for all nuclei.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell model calculations using a CD-Bonn derived effective interaction with a \(^{100}\)Sn core (rather than the more common \(^{132}\)Sn core) reproduce experimental level energies and B(E2) transition rates for \(^{132,134}\)Te up to spin \(J \leq 10\), but the model fails for higher-spin states (\(J > 10\)) due to inadequacy of the model space; for \(^{136}\)Te a different interaction (KH5082) with a \(^{132}\)Sn core is used and gives good agreement only up to \(J^\pi = 10^+\).

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations were performed using the OXBASH code with the SN100PN Hamiltonian (based on a CD-Bonn renormalized \(G\) matrix) for \(^{132}\)Te and \(^{134}\)Te, taking \(^{100}\)Sn as the inert core with valence protons and neutrons in the \(0g_{7/2}\), \(1d_{5/2}\), \(1d_{3/2}\), \(2s_{1/2}\), and \(0h_{11/2}\) orbitals. For \(^{136}\)Te, the core was taken as \(^{132}\)Sn with the KH5082 effective interaction (originally fitted for \(^{208}\)Pb and scaled to the \(^{132}\)Sn region), using modified single-particle energies from Brown et al. (2005). Transition rates B(E2; \(0^+ \rightarrow 2^+\)) were calculated with harmonic oscillator radial wave functions using effective charges \(e_p^{\text{eff}} = 1.47\) and \(e_n^{\text{eff}} = 0.72\).

## Key numbers

- First \(2^+\) state in \(^{132}\)Te predicted at 954 keV; experimental value is 974 keV (absolute difference 20 keV); previous QRPA calculation by Terasaki et al. (2004) predicted 1211 keV (difference 237 keV).
- First \(2^+\) state in \(^{134}\)Te predicted at 1211 keV; absolute difference from experiment is 68 keV; previous theoretical work by Garret et al. (2004) predicted 1375 keV (difference 96 keV).
- First \(2^+\) state in \(^{136}\)Te predicted at 866 keV.
- Effective charges used: \(e_p^{\text{eff}} = 1.47\) and \(e_n^{\text{eff}} = 0.72\).
- Proton single-particle energies (MeV): \(-9.68\) (\(0g_{7/2}\)), \(-8.72\) (\(1d_{5/2}\)), \(-7.24\) (\(1d_{3/2}\)), \(-7.34\) (\(2s_{1/2}\)), \(-6.88\) (\(0h_{11/2}\)).
- Neutron single-particle energies (MeV) for \(^{132,134}\)Te: \(-9.74\) (\(0g_{7/2}\)), \(-8.97\) (\(1d_{5/2}\)), \(-7.31\) (\(1d_{3/2}\)), \(-7.62\) (\(2s_{1/2}\)), \(-7.38\) (\(0h_{11/2}\)).

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of neutron-rich Te isotopes near the doubly magic \(^{132}\)Sn region, previously performed by Brown et al. (2005) using a \(^{132}\)Sn core, by testing a \(^{100}\)Sn core for \(^{132,134}\)Te. The calculations are compared with experimental data from Radford et al. (2002) and Terasaki et al. (2004), and with prior theoretical work by Sarkar and Sarkar (2004) using empirical Hamiltonians. The results show that the choice of core does not significantly affect the calculated spectra, and the approach enables testing of effective interactions for neutron-rich nuclei with \(N \leq 82\) and \(N \geq 82\) using different model spaces.
