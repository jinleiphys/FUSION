# Nucleon form factors, B-meson factories and the radiative return

> The feasibility of a measurement of the electric and magnetic nucleon form factors at $B$-meson factories through the radiative return is studied. Angular distributions allow a separation of the contributions from the two form factors. The distributions are presented for the laboratory and the hadronic rest frame, and the advantages of different coordinate systems are investigated. It is demonstrated that $Q^2$ values up to 8 or even 9 GeV$^2$ are within reach. The Monte Carlo event generator PHOKHARA is extended to nucleon final states, and results are presented which include Next-to-Leading Order radiative corrections from initial-state radiation. The impact of angular cuts on rates and distributions is investigated and the relative importance of radiative corrections is analysed.

# Nucleon form factors, B-meson factories and the radiative return

## Key claim

The radiative return at B-meson factories can measure the electric and magnetic nucleon form factors in the time-like region up to Q² values of 8 or even 9 GeV², with sufficient event rates to separate the two form factors through angular distributions, provided that realistic acceptance cuts and next-to-leading order radiative corrections are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors extended the Monte Carlo event generator PHOKHARA (version 4.0) to simulate the processes e⁺e⁻ → p p̅ γ and e⁺e⁻ → n n̅ γ, including next-to-leading order (NLO) radiative corrections from initial-state radiation (ISR) only. They derived analytical expressions for the fully differential cross section in both the laboratory frame and the hadronic rest frame, and investigated the angular distributions of the nucleons in various coordinate systems to determine the feasibility of separating the electric and magnetic form factors. The form factor parametrization from Iachello, Jackson, and Lande (1973) was implemented, with the analytical continuation to the time-like region taken from Iachello (1984) and Brodsky (1973).

## Key numbers

- Cross sections integrated over Q² < 10 GeV² with BaBar-like acceptance cuts: 59.3 fb for protons and 125 fb for neutrons
- With 100 fb⁻¹ integrated luminosity and 0.5 GeV² bin size, expected event counts around 2500 at 4 GeV², 600 at 5 GeV², and 250 at 6 GeV², yielding statistical precision of 2%, 4%, and 6% respectively
- With 1000 fb⁻¹, 8 GeV² could be reached with approximately 6% statistical precision
- NLO radiative corrections distort distributions by 1–3% compared to leading order, while integrated cross sections differ by less than 0.5%
- The LO test of the PHOKHARA implementation agreed with analytical predictions to better than 0.1% for Q² < 8 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the radiative return method, previously applied to two-pion and two-muon production (Binner 1999, Rodrigo 2001) and four-pion production (Czyz 2002), to nucleon-antinucleon final states. It addresses the long-standing difficulty in separating electric and magnetic form factors in the time-like region, where previous measurements from e⁺e⁻ → p p̅ and p p̅ → e⁺e⁻ (extending to 6 GeV² and 14 GeV² respectively) had large errors and assumed G_M = μ_p G_E. The results complement space-like measurements from Jefferson Lab (JLab1, JLab2) up to 5.6 GeV², which showed disagreement with the Rosenbluth method possibly due to two-photon exchange corrections (Guichon 2003, Chen 2004). The method enables form factor separation over a wide kinematic range at existing B-factories, and the PHOKHARA generator provides a tool for experimental analysis including NLO corrections.
