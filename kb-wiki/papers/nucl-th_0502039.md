# Naive Dimensional Analysis for Three-Body Forces Without Pions

> For systems of three identical particles in which short-range forces produce shallow two-particle bound states, and in particular for the ``pion-less'' Effective Field Theory of Nuclear Physics, I extend and systematise the power-counting of three-body forces to all partial-waves and orders, including external currents. With low-energy observables independent of the details of short-distance dynamics, the typical strength of a three-body force is determined from the superficial degree of divergence of the three-body diagrams which contain only two-body forces. This naïve dimensional analysis must be amended as the asymptotic solution to the leading-order Faddeev equation depends for large off-shell momenta crucially on the partial wave and spin-combination of the system. It is shown by analytic construction to be weaker in most channels with angular momentum smaller than 3 than expected. This demotes many three-nucleon forces to high orders. Observables like the quartet-S-scattering length are less sensitive to three-nucleon forces than guessed. I also comment on the Efimov effect and limit-cycle for non-zero angular momentum.

## Key claim

The paper extends and systematises the power-counting of three-body forces in pion-less Effective Field Theory to all partial waves and orders, showing that naive dimensional analysis must be amended because the asymptotic solution to the leading-order Faddeev equation depends crucially on the partial wave and spin-combination of the system, with the result that three-body forces are weaker in most channels with angular momentum smaller than 3 than previously expected, demoting many three-nucleon forces to higher orders.

## Method

The author determines the superficial degree of divergence of three-body diagrams containing only two-body forces by analysing the asymptotic (large off-shell momentum) behaviour of the half off-shell amplitude in each partial wave, which is obtained from the analytic solution of the leading-order Faddeev equation. The asymptotic form of the amplitude is found to be proportional to \(p^{-s_l(\lambda)-1}\) where \(s_l(\lambda)\) is a complex number depending on the partial wave \(l\) and the spin-parameter \(\lambda\). The order at which a three-body force is needed as a counter-term to absorb cut-off dependence is then determined by the condition \(\Re[n - s_l(\lambda) - s_{l'}(\lambda')] \geq 0\) for a correction at N\(^n\)LO.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy at leading order is \(\gamma^2/M\), much smaller than the breakdown scale \(\Lambda_{\pi/}\) of the EFT.
- The deuteron size is approximately \(4.3\;\mathrm{fm}\).
- The asymptotic form of the half off-shell amplitude at large off-shell momenta \(p\gg k\) is \(t^{(l)}_\lambda(p) \propto k^l\;p^{-s_l(\lambda)-1}\).
- The two-nucleon propagator in the UV limit scales as \(D(E - q^2/(2M), q) \to -2/(\sqrt{3}q)\) for \(q\gg E,\gamma_{s/t}\).
- The superficial degree of divergence of a diagram is \(n - s_l(\lambda) - s_{l'}(\lambda')\).
- The effective range \(r_0\) enters at NLO as one insertion; \(r_0^n\) enters at N\(^n\)LO as \(n\) insertions; the correction \(r_n\) starts contributing with one insertion at N\(^{2n+1}\)LO.

## Context

This work extends the power-counting analysis of three-body forces in pion-less Effective Field Theory (EFT(\(\pi/\))) beyond the doublet S-wave channel, where a three-body force was previously shown to appear at leading order to stabilise the wave-function against collapse (Danilov, 1961; Bedaque et al., 1999). The results apply to any three-particle system with large two-particle scattering lengths, including identical spin-less bosons such as \(^4\)He dimers and trimers bound by van-der-Waals forces, as well as loss rates in Bose-Einstein condensates near Feshbach resonances (Braaten, 2004). The findings enable systematic classification of which three-body forces are needed at each order in the EFT expansion, and show that observables like the quartet S-wave scattering length are less sensitive to three-nucleon forces than previously guessed.
