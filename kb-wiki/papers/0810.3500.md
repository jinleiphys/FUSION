# Non-Strange Meson Production with Hadronic Probes

> Recent progress in the field of meson production in hadron collisions is presented. Special emphasis is put new developments in the application of effective field theory methods to pion production, but also theoretical concepts for two-pion, eta, and vector-meson production will be presented.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) can be applied to single-pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions only after accounting for an additional scale, the threshold momentum \(p_{\rm thr} = \sqrt{M_N m_\pi}\), which modifies the power counting; without this modification, higher-order corrections appeared large and the convergence of the series was questionable.

## Method

The author reviews the application of effective field theory (ChPT) to meson production in hadron collisions, focusing on the reaction \(NN\to NN\pi\). The method combines transition operators calculated perturbatively in ChPT with nuclear wave functions to account for non-perturbative few-nucleon dynamics, using the concept of reducibility to separate wave-function and operator effects. For two-pion production, the leading nucleonic diagrams are enumerated, but no full ChPT calculation yet exists; for heavier mesons like \(\eta\) and vector mesons, final-state interactions are used to extract model-independent information.

## Key numbers

* Threshold momentum for single-pion production: \(p_{\rm thr} = \sqrt{M_N m_\pi}\), giving \(p_{\rm thr}/\Lambda_\chi \simeq 0.4\).
* Threshold momentum for two-pion production: \(p_{\rm thr}/\Lambda_\chi = 0.54\).
* Threshold momentum for \(\eta\) production: \(p_{\rm thr}/\Lambda_\chi \simeq \sqrt{m_\eta/M_N} = 0.8\).
* The leading \(4N\pi\) contact term for \(pp\to d\pi^+\) is enhanced by a factor of 4/3 when going to next-to-leading order, which resolved the apparent discrepancy with data.

## Context

This review extends the work of Weinberg (1992) on applying ChPT to few-nucleon systems and addresses the convergence problems noted by Park, Unserd, and Hanhart (various years) for \(NN\to NN\pi\). It builds on the modified power counting introduced by Hanhart (2003) and implemented by Hanhart and collaborators (2004), which treats the Delta-isobar as a dynamical degree of freedom following Bira and Rocha (early 2000s). The results enable high-accuracy determinations of \(\pi N\) scattering lengths via \(\pi d\) scattering (Baru et al., 2005) and connect to three-nucleon forces, isospin-violating reactions, and weak processes like tritium beta decay. For heavier mesons, the work references the observation of strong \(\eta{}^3\)He interactions in reactions studied by Pfeiffer (2004) and the Saclay, ANKE, and COSY-11 collaborations.
