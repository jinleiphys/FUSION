# Space-time versus particle-hole symmetry in quantum Enskog equations

> The non-local scattering-in and -out integrals of the Enskog equation have reversed displacements of colliding particles reflecting that the -in and -out processes are conjugated by the space and time inversions. Generalisations of the Enskog equation to Fermi liquid systems are hindered by a request of the particle-hole symmetry which contradicts the reversed displacements. We resolve this problem with the help of the optical theorem. It is found that space-time and particle-hole symmetry can only be fulfilled simultaneously for the Bruckner-type of internal Pauli-blocking while the Feynman-Galitskii form allows only for particle-hole symmetry but not for space-time symmetry due to a stimulated emission of Bosons.

## Key claim

The space-time symmetry (Enskog-type reversed displacements in scattering-in and -out) and the particle-hole symmetry (required for Fermi liquid systems) can only be fulfilled simultaneously when the internal Pauli blocking during collisions is of the Bruckner type; the Feynman-Galitskii form of internal Pauli blocking allows only particle-hole symmetry but not space-time symmetry due to stimulated emission of bosons.

## Method

The authors start from the Kadanoff-Baym equation for non-equilibrium Green's functions and apply quasiparticle and quasiclassical approximations to obtain a non-local kinetic equation with gradient corrections expressed as shifts of arguments. They then use the optical theorem (identity \(T^R \mathcal{A} T^A = T^A \mathcal{A} T^R\)) to rearrange the causal scattering-out integral into an anti-causal form, which flips the signs of all non-local corrections. This rearrangement is performed separately for the Bruckner approximation (\(\mathcal{A}_B \approx (1-f_k)(1-f_p)\)) and the Galitskii-Feynman approximation (\(\mathcal{A}_{GF} \approx (1-f_k-f_p)\)) of the two-particle spectral function.

## Key numbers

- Collision duration \(\Delta_t = \partial\phi/\partial\Omega\) where \(\phi\) is the scattering phase shift; energy shift \(\Delta_E = -\frac{1}{2}\partial\phi/\partial t\); momentum shift \(\Delta_K = \frac{1}{2}\partial\phi/\partial r\); spatial displacements \(\Delta_2, \Delta_3, \Delta_4\) are given by derivatives of \(\phi\) with respect to momenta.
- For classical hard spheres: collision delay \(\Delta_t \to 0\); energy/momentum gain \(\Delta_{E,K} \to 0\); displacement of particles at collision \(\Delta_{2,4} = D\) where \(D\) is the hard-sphere diameter; scattering phase shift \(\phi \to \pi - |q|D\).
- The Bruckner two-particle spectral function: \(\mathcal{A}_B(t_1,t_2,k,p) \approx (1-f_k)(1-f_p) e^{-i(\epsilon_k+\epsilon_p)(t_1-t_2)}\).
- The Galitskii-Feynman two-particle spectral function: \(\mathcal{A}_{GF}(t_1,t_2,k,p) \approx (1-f_k-f_p) e^{-i(\epsilon_k+\epsilon_p)(t_1-t_2)}\).

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing paradox in extending the classical Enskog equation (Enskog 1921, Chapman and Cowling 1990) to quantum Fermi liquid systems. Earlier quantum generalizations by Boercker (1969), Laloe (1989), and others produced gradient corrections that did not reproduce the opposite signs of non-local corrections in scattering-in versus scattering-out that characterize the Enskog equation. The resolution uses the optical theorem to connect causal and anti-causal expansions, showing that the Bruckner-type internal Pauli blocking (common in earlier heavy-ion reaction studies) preserves both symmetries, while the more sophisticated Galitskii-Feynman approximation introduces stimulated boson emission terms that break space-time symmetry. This enables Monte-Carlo simulations of non-local kinetic equations for nuclear matter using the Bruckner approximation, as demonstrated in Morawetz et al. (1999).
