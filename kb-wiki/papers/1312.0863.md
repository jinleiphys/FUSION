# Meson-cloud effects in the electromagnetic nucleon structure

> We study how the electromagnetic structure of the nucleon is influenced by a pion cloud. To this aim we make use of a constituent-quark model with instantaneous confinement and a pion that couples directly to the quarks. To derive the invariant 1- photon-exchange electron-nucleon scattering amplitude we employ a Poincaré- invariant coupled-channel formulation which is based on the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics. We argue that the electromagnetic nucleon current extracted from this amplitude can be reexpressed in terms of pure hadronic degrees of freedom with the quark substructure of the pion and the nucleon being encoded in electromagnetic and strong vertex form factors. These are form factors of bare particles, i.e. eigenstates of the pure confinement problem. First numerical results for (bare) photon-nucleon and pion-nucleon form factors, which are the basic ingredients of the further calculation, are given for a simple 3-quark wave function of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the electromagnetic nucleon current can be reexpressed in terms of pure hadronic degrees of freedom, with the quark substructure of the pion and nucleon encoded in electromagnetic and strong vertex form factors of "bare" particles (eigenstates of the pure confinement problem), but only first numerical results for these bare form factors are presented, not the full pion-cloud contribution to the physical nucleon form factors.

## Method

The authors employ a Poincaré-invariant coupled-channel formulation based on the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics with Bakamjian-Thomas construction. They derive the invariant 1-photon-exchange electron-nucleon scattering amplitude by solving a multichannel mass eigenvalue problem that includes 3-quark, 3-quark-pion, and photon channels, then reduce it via Feshbach projection to an optical potential. The calculation is split into two steps: first, evaluating the gamma-bare-nucleon and pion-bare-nucleon vertex form factors at the quark level using a simple SU(6) spin-flavor symmetric 3-quark wave function; second, using these as input for hadronic-level diagrams of the physical nucleon dressed by a pion cloud.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass: m_q = 0.263 GeV; wave function parameters: beta = 0.607 GeV, gamma = 3.5 (used in the 3-quark wave function of the bare nucleon).
* The bare proton magnetic and electric form factors are shown (Fig. 3) with the electric form factor coinciding with the phenomenological Kelly fit; the magnetic form factor deviates at higher Q^2.
* The strong pi-tilde-N-tilde-N form factor (normalized to 1 at Q^2=0) is calculated for both pseudoscalar and pseudovector pion-quark coupling, with the pseudovector result showing better agreement with lattice QCD predictions from Erkol (2009) and Liu (1995).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic constituent-quark model approach of Glozman (1997) and Wagenbrunn (2000) by treating the pion dynamically rather than via instantaneous exchange, following the coupled-channel framework of Kleinhappel (2012). It is motivated by the Bethe-Salpeter-Dyson-Schwinger calculation of Eichmann (2011), which found "clear signals of missing pion-cloud effects" in the low-Q^2 region of nucleon form factors. The results enable a first estimate of the pionic contribution to electromagnetic nucleon form factors, with planned refinements including gamma-pi-tilde-N-tilde-N vertices, nucleonic excitations within the pion loop, and more sophisticated 3-quark wave functions.
