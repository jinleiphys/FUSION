# Neutron spin dynamics in polarized targets

> We present neutron elastic scattering amplitude for arbitrary polarized target in irreducible spherical tensor representation. The general approach for the description of neutron spin dynamics for the propagation trough the medium with an arbitrary polarization is discussed in a relation to the search for time reversal invariance violation in neutron scattering.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic description of neutron spin dynamics in arbitrarily polarized targets using irreducible spherical tensor representation, showing that for targets with spin \(I>1/2\), higher-rank tensor polarizations (beyond vector polarization) can coexist and affect neutron spin dynamics, potentially suppressing time-reversal invariance violating (TRIV) observables even when these higher-order polarizations cannot themselves mimic TRIV effects.

## Method

The authors derive the forward elastic neutron scattering amplitude for arbitrary target polarization by expanding the target density matrix in terms of statistical tensors \(t_{q\kappa}\) (spherical tensor polarizations) and expressing the amplitude as a sum over tensor ranks \(q\) from 0 to \(2I\). They then apply the Breit-Wigner resonance approximation for slow neutrons, considering only \(s\)- and \(p\)-wave resonances, and evaluate the amplitude for the specific case of a target with spin \(I=7/2\) (using \(^{139}\text{La}\) as an example) to obtain explicit expressions for the coefficients of each tensor term in the amplitude expansion.

## Key numbers

- The \(p\)-wave resonance in \(^{139}\text{La}\) is at energy \(E_p = 0.734\ \text{eV}\).
- The two nearest \(s\)-wave resonances in \(^{139}\text{La}\) are at \(E_{s0} = -48.63\ \text{eV}\) (spin \(J=4\)) and \(E_{s1} = 72.3\ \text{eV}\) (spin \(J=3\)).
- For a target with spin \(I=7/2\), the maximum rank of the target spin tensor contributing to low-energy neutron scattering is \(q=3\), due to the restriction to \(s\)- and \(p\)-wave resonances.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron spin dynamics in polarized targets (e.g., Bunakov (1987), Stodolsky (1985), Kabir (1988, 1989), Skoy (1996), Bowman (2014), Gudkov (2017)) which focused primarily on vector-polarized targets. The paper provides a general framework for analyzing how higher-rank tensor polarizations affect neutron spin propagation, which is necessary for designing new TRIV experiments and controlling systematic effects, particularly for targets with spin \(I>1/2\) where tensor polarizations of rank \(q\geq2\) can be significant.
