# Energy Dependent Calculations of Fission Product, Prompt, and Delayed Neutron Yields for Neutron Induced Fission on $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U, and $^{239}$Pu

> We perform energy dependent calculations of independent and cumulative fission product yields for $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U, and $^{239}$Pu in the first chance fission region. Starting with the primary fission fragment distributions taken from available experimental data and analytical functions based on assumptions for the excitation energy and spin-parity distributions, the Hauser-Feshbach statistical decay treatment for fission fragment de-excitation is applied to more than 1,000 fission fragments for the incident neutron energies up to 5 MeV. The calculated independent fission product yields are then used as an input of $β$-decay to produce the cumulative yield, and summation calculations are performed. Model parameters in these procedures are adjusted by applying the Bayesian technique at the thermal energy for $^{235}$U and $^{239}$Pu and in the fast energy range for $^{238}$U. The calculated fission observable quantities, such as the energy-dependent fission yields, and prompt and delayed neutron yields, are compared with available experimental data. We also study a possible impact of the second chance fission opening on the energy dependence of the delayed neutron yield by extrapolating the calculation.

## Key claim

The Hauser-Feshbach Fission Fragment Decay (HF$^3$D) model, extended to include beta-decay calculations, can simultaneously reproduce energy-dependent fission product yields, prompt neutron yields, and delayed neutron yields for neutron-induced fission on $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U, and $^{239}$Pu up to 5 MeV, but only after Bayesian adjustment of model parameters at thermal energies (or 1.2 MeV for $^{238}$U). The flat energy-dependence of delayed neutron yield $\overline{\nu}_d$ for $^{235}$U and $^{239}$Pu is attributed to a coincidental compensation of increasing and decreasing delayed neutron precursors, while the step-function-like drop near 5 MeV is qualitatively explained by the opening of second-chance fission, though the calculated transition energy is about 1.5 MeV higher than experimental data.

## Method

Starting from primary fission fragment distributions characterized by multi-Gaussian mass yields, excitation energy, and spin-parity distributions, the authors applied the Hauser-Feshbach statistical decay treatment to over 1,000 fission fragments for incident neutron energies up to 5 MeV. The independent fission product yields from this calculation were then fed into a beta-decay summation calculation to produce cumulative yields and delayed neutron yields. Model parameters (Gaussian fractions, widths, mass shifts, anisothermal parameter $R_T$, spin factor $f_J$, and odd-even factors $f_{Z0}$ and $f_{N0}$) were adjusted using the Bayesian KALMAN code at thermal energy for $^{235}$U and $^{239}$Pu and at approximately 1.2 MeV for $^{238}$U, fitting to cumulative fission product yields, prompt neutron multiplicity $\overline{\nu}_p$, and delayed neutron multiplicity $\overline{\nu}_d$.

## Key numbers

* For $^{235}$U at thermal: prior $\overline{\nu}_p = 2.38$ adjusted to posterior $\overline{\nu}_p = 2.415$; prior $\overline{\nu}_d = 0.0195$ adjusted to posterior $\overline{\nu}_d = 0.0169$ (evaluated values are 2.41-2.42 and 0.0159 respectively).
* For $^{239}$Pu at thermal: prior $\overline{\nu}_d = 0.00888$ adjusted to posterior $\overline{\nu}_d = 0.00665$ (evaluated value 0.00645).
* Model parameters adjusted for $^{235}$U include 11 parameters fitted to 8 data points; posterior $f_{Z0} = 1.78$ (prior 1.00) with 6.6% uncertainty.
* For $^{239}$Pu: posterior $f_{Z0} = 2.58$ (prior 1.00) with 4.4% uncertainty; TKE posterior = 179.4 MeV (prior 178.2 MeV).
* For $^{238}$U at approximately 1 MeV: posterior $f_{Z0} = 2.386$ (prior 1.00) with 5.3% uncertainty; posterior TKE = 170.5 MeV (prior 171.4 MeV).
* Energy-dependent parameters for $^{235}$U: $R_{T1} = -0.0476$ MeV$^{-1}$, $f_{Z1} = -0.296$ MeV$^{-1}$, $f_{N1} = -0.161$ MeV$^{-1}$ to make odd-even effect vanish at $E_n = 6$ MeV.
* For $^{239}$Pu: $R_{T1} = -0.0507$ MeV$^{-1}$, $f_{Z1} = -0.430$ MeV$^{-1}$, $f_{N1} = -0.156$ MeV$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the HF$^3$D model of Okumura (2018) for prompt neutron emission to include beta-decay calculations, providing a consistent framework for both prompt and delayed neutron yields. It builds on earlier interpretations by Alexander (1977) linking delayed neutron energy-dependence to odd-even effects, and by Minato (2018) who used Katakura's systematics without explicit statistical decay. The model enables simultaneous evaluation of fission product yields, $\overline{\nu}_p$, and $\overline{\nu}_d$ for nuclear data libraries, and the authors note that full multi-chance fission treatment is underway in Lovell (2021). The calculated transition energy for second-chance fission being 1.5 MeV higher than experimental data remains an open question requiring further investigation in both theory and experiment.
