# Electromagnetic Properties of the N=50 Isotones with the p35-i3 Hamiltonian

> The nuclei with 50 neutrons that lie between $^{78}$Ni and $^{100}$Sn have provided benchmark studies of the nuclear shell model for protons in the $\{0f_{5/2}, 1p_{3/2}, 1p_{1/2}, 0g_{9/2}\}$ model space. New Hamiltonians for this model space have recently been obtained based on valence-space in-medium renormalization-group (VS-IMSRG) methods with two- and three-nucleon interactions. The two-body matrix elements (TBME) obtained from these ab-initio methods served as the starting point for singular-value decomposition (SVD) method fits of the TBME to experimental binding energies and excitation energies, resulting in Hamiltonians called p35-i2, p35-i3 and p30-i3. In this paper, magnetic moments, quadrupole moments, $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$ values obtained with these Hamiltonians are presented and compared to experiment. Results obtained from various Hamiltonians are compared to assess the theoretical uncertainties.

## Key claim

The p35-i3 Hamiltonian, derived from VS-IMSRG(3f2) starting points with 35 SVD parameters, provides a good description of electromagnetic observables in N=50 isotones, but the theoretical uncertainties are larger for nuclei with A < 88 (RMS deviation 0.12 MeV for energies) compared to those with A ≥ 88 (RMS deviation 0.05 MeV), and the magnetic moment RMS deviation of 0.50 μ_N between experiment and theory is about a factor of two larger than the theoretical Hamiltonian uncertainty of 0.24 μ_N.

## Method

The authors calculated magnetic moments, quadrupole moments, B(M1) and B(E2) values for all nuclei between ⁷⁹Cu and ⁹⁹In using the NuShellX code with the p35-i3, p35-i2, p30-i3, jj44a, and n50j Hamiltonians in the πj4 model space (0f₅/₂, 1p₃/₂, 1p₁/₂, 0g₉/₂). For E2 operators, they used nucleus-dependent radial wavefunctions from the Skx Skyrme energy-density functional with an effective proton charge e_p = 1.8, while for M1 operators they used orbital-dependent effective g-factors from fits to data in jw88em. They compared results across Hamiltonians to assess theoretical uncertainties and against experimental data from the ENSDF database.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation between experiment and theory for magnetic dipole moments: 0.50 μ_N (p35-i3 Hamiltonian)
- Theoretical Hamiltonian uncertainty for magnetic moments: 0.24 μ_N (from comparison of p30-i3 and p35-i3)
- RMS deviation between experiment and theory for quadrupole moments: 3.0 e·fm² (with e_p = 1.8)
- Theoretical uncertainty for quadrupole moments: 5 e·fm² (from comparison of p30-i3 and p35-i3)
- Effective proton charge used for E2 operator: e_p = 1.8 (compared to free-nucleon value e_p = 1.0 which gave RMS deviation of 20 e·fm²)
- B(E2; 0⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) for ⁸⁰Zn: 730(90) e²fm⁴ (experiment) vs 585 e²fm⁴ (p35-i3)
- B(E2; 0⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) for ⁹⁰Zr: 627(34) e²fm⁴ (experiment) vs 604 e²fm⁴ (p35-i3)
- B(E2; 8⁺₁ → 6⁺₁) for ⁹²Mo: 28.6(3) e²fm⁴ (experiment) vs 32.0 e²fm⁴ (p35-i3)
- B(E2; 8⁺₁ → 6⁺₁) for ⁹⁴Ru: 0.09(1) e²fm⁴ (experiment) vs 1.42 e²fm⁴ (p35-i3)
- Magnetic moment of ⁸⁷Rb (3/2⁻): 2.75131(12) μ_N (experiment) vs 2.86469 μ_N (p35-i3)
- Quadrupole moment of ⁸⁹Y (9/2⁺): -43(6) e·fm² (experiment) vs -40.62 e·fm² (p35-i3)
- B(M1; 2⁺₂ → 2⁺₁) for ⁸⁸Sr: 0.074(5) μ_N² (experiment) vs 0.078 μ_N² (p35-i3)

## Context

This work extends the shell-model studies of N=50 isotones from ⁷⁸Ni to ¹⁰⁰Sn, building on the SVD-fitted Hamiltonians jj44a (Brown 2004) and n50j (jW88), and the VS-IMSRG-derived Hamiltonians from Yuan (2024). The new p35-i3 Hamiltonian generally outperforms the older jj44a and n50j Hamiltonians for B(E2; 0⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) transitions and performs comparably to n50j for 8⁺₁ → 6⁺₁ transitions. The results enable improved predictions for electromagnetic observables in this region and demonstrate that the IMSRG(3f2) starting point provides a better foundation for SVD fits than IMSRG(2), particularly for nuclei with A < 88 where configuration mixing is more significant.
