# On the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter

> We consider the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter in the absence of Pauli blocking. It is evaluated using the partial-wave analysis of $NN$ scattering data. Our results are compared with that of a realistic calculation to estimate the effect of this blocking. It is also possible to use our results as a check on the realistic calculations.

## Key claim

The nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter can be evaluated in the limit where Pauli blocking is omitted, using only experimental NN scattering data via partial-wave analysis, and this limiting result can serve as a check on realistic self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations.

## Method

The authors derived a first-order virial expansion formula for the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter, expressing it as an integral over the spin-averaged, forward NN scattering amplitude in vacuum. They evaluated this integral at zero temperature using the partial-wave phase-shift analysis and Saclay amplitudes from the Nijmegen group, and also performed an approximate evaluation using only s-waves in the effective range approximation.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density (p_F = 1.35 fm^{-1}), the realistic Brueckner calculation gives a real mass shift of -87 MeV; the authors' calculation attains this value at a higher density corresponding to p_F = 1.70 fm^{-1}.
- For the effective range approximation, the spin singlet scattering length a = -23.7 fm and effective range r = 2.7 fm; for the spin triplet state, a = 5.39 fm and r = 1.70 fm.
- The number density of symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature is given by n = 2 p_F^3 / (3 pi^2), where p_F is the Fermi momentum.
- Higher partial waves contribute little up to about p_F = 1 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the virial expansion approach to the nucleon self-energy, previously applied in statistical mechanics by Leutwyler, Jeon, and Mallik, to nuclear matter at zero temperature. The results can be compared with the self-consistent relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation of Brockmann and collaborators to quantify the importance of Pauli blocking in the Fermi medium. The calculation also provides a direct experimental check on those realistic calculations: redoing the Brueckner calculation without the Pauli operator should reproduce the same density dependence of the self-energy found here.
