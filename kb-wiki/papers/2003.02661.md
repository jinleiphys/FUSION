# Comment on "Enhanced deuterium-tritium fusion cross sections in the presence of strong electromagnetic fields"

> In their article [Phys.\ Rev.\ C {\bf 100}, 064610 (2019)], Lv, Duan, and Liu study the enhancement of deuterium-tritium fusion reactions by the electromagnetic field of an x-ray free-electron laser (XFEL). While we support the general idea (which was put forward earlier in our rapid communication [Phys.\ Rev.\ C {\bf 100}, 041601(R) (2019)]), we find that the time-averaged potential approximation used by Lv, Duan, and Liu is not justified in this regime and does not take into account important effects. Due to those effects, the enhancement mechanism may actually be more efficient than predicted by Lv, Duan, and Liu.

## Key claim

The time-averaged potential approximation used by Lv, Duan, and Liu (2019) to model deuterium-tritium fusion enhancement by an XFEL field is not justified for XFEL frequencies in the keV regime; the neglected effects may actually make the enhancement mechanism more efficient than their predictions.

## Method

The authors analyze the validity of the time-averaged potential approximation by comparing relevant time scales for deuterium-tritium tunneling in an oscillating electromagnetic field. They compute the Büttiker-Landauer traversal time for the tunneling process and compare it to the XFEL oscillation period, and they also consider the Franz-Keldysh effect and Floquet side-band physics to argue that the threshold frequency of 1 keV assumed by Lv, Duan, and Liu (2019) is too low for the approximation to hold.

## Key numbers

- The Büttiker-Landauer traversal time τ for deuterium-tritium fusion is given by τ = ħ (π/4) √(2μc²/ℰ³) α_QED, where α_QED ≈ 1/137 is the fine-structure constant.
- For an initial kinetic energy ℰ = 4 keV, the inverse Büttiker-Landauer traversal time 1/τ is around 1 keV, meaning XFEL frequencies in the keV regime probe frequency-dependent effects not captured by the time-averaged potential approximation.
- The nuclear energy barrier height is 0.37 MeV and the well depth is between 30 and 40 MeV, which facilitate a huge number of phase oscillations of the wave function during one XFEL period at 1 keV frequency.

## Context

This comment responds to Lv, Duan, and Liu (2019) and extends the authors' own earlier rapid communication (Queisser and Schützhold, 2019) which first proposed using XFEL fields to assist deuterium-tritium fusion. The comment identifies that the time-averaged potential approximation used by Lv, Duan, and Liu (2019) neglects important effects such as the Franz-Keldysh effect (Franz, 1958; Keldysh, 1958) and Büttiker-Landauer traversal time physics (Büttiker, 1983), and suggests that the actual enhancement may be more efficient than predicted. This work enables more accurate modeling of laser-assisted fusion that accounts for frequency-dependent tunneling dynamics.
