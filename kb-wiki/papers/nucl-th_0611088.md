# Final State Distortions in Two-Particle Correlations

> Final state interactions in two-particle interferometry is considered for two-particle potentials which are the Coulomb plus "strong" one. For a simple model of the source we provide a full numerical calculation of the correlation function with taking into account two-particle Coulomb interaction plus final state strong interaction. It is found that for small sources (R=1-4 fm) the contribution from the strong interaction is appreciable and depresses the correlations while for large fireballs (R=7-10 fm) this contribution is small in comparison to the Coulomb one.

## Key claim

For small source radii (1–4 fm), the inclusion of a strong repulsive potential between pions appreciably depresses the correlation function beyond the effect of the Coulomb interaction alone; for large fireball radii (7–10 fm), the strong-interaction contribution becomes small compared to the Coulomb one, and the Gamov factor alone overestimates the final-state distortion for sources larger than about 4 fm.

## Method

The authors solved the stationary Schrödinger equation for the relative motion of two pions, using a potential that is the sum of the Coulomb term \(V_{\rm Coul}(r)=\alpha/r\) and a strong repulsive Yukawa potential \(V_{\rm str}(r)=V_0 \exp(-m_\rho r)/(m_\rho r)\) with \(V_0=2.6\) GeV and \(m_\rho=770\) MeV, chosen to match pion-pion scattering phase shifts. They inserted the resulting distorted wave function \(\phi_{{\bf q}/2}({\bf r})\) into the two-particle probability expression in the pair center-of-mass system, using a Gaussian source function with radius \(R_0\) and a Boltzmann factor at freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm f}\), and computed the correlation function \(C({\bf q})\) numerically for several source sizes.

## Key numbers

- Source radii studied: \(R_0 = 2.0,\ 4.5,\ 7.1,\ 10\) fm.
- Strong potential parameters: \(V_0 = 2.6\) GeV, \(m_\rho = 770\) MeV.
- Pion mean radius (hard-core scale): \(\langle r_\pi \rangle \approx 0.66\) fm.
- For \(R_0 = 2\) fm and \(R_0 = 4.5\) fm, the strong interaction appreciably depresses the correlation function at small relative momentum.
- For \(R_0 = 7.1\) fm, the strong-interaction contribution is comparatively small.
- For \(R_0 = 10\) fm, the influence of the strong FSI is negligible.
- The Gamov factor \(G(|{\bf q}|) = 2\pi\eta/(e^{2\pi\eta}-1)\) with \(\eta = \alpha m_\pi/|{\bf q}|\) overestimates FSI effects for sources with \(R_0 \ge 4\) fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of final-state interactions in two-particle interferometry, particularly the treatment of strong repulsion between pions (Pratt 1990, Suzuki 1987, Bowler 1988) and the use of the Gamov factor for Coulomb corrections. It provides a full numerical calculation that includes both Coulomb and strong potentials, showing that for small sources (R ≤ 4 fm) the strong repulsion cannot be neglected, while for large sources (R ≥ 7 fm) it is negligible. The results clarify the relationship between the extracted correlation radius and the true size of the homogeneity region, suggesting that when strong FSI are present, the measured radius may exceed the homogeneity radius and approach the overall fireball size.
