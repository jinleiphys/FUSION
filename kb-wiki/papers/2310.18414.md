# A loophole in the proofs of asymptotic freedom and quantum triviality

> In 1973, Coleman and Gross proved that in four dimensions, only non-abelian gauge theories can have asymptotic freedom. More recently, Aizenman and Duminil-Copin proved that four dimensional scalar field theories are quantum trivial in the continuum. Both of these proofs have a loophole, and it is the same loophole in both proofs: The proofs assume that the scalar self-coupling in the UV is positive definite. While this is a perfectly reasonable and classically very intuitive assumption, it is an assumption nevertheless. In this work, I show that the assumption of coupling positivity is violated in a concrete quantum field theory, the O(N) model, in the large N limit. Surprisingly, despite the classically nonsensical unbounded potential, the negative coupling has no pathological consequence for propagators, the free energy or cross sections. This suggests that interacting scalar field theories with asymptotic freedom in four dimensions are possible, despite long-held opinions to the contrary.

# A loophole in the proofs of asymptotic freedom and quantum triviality

## Key claim

The proofs by Coleman and Gross (1973) and Aizenman and Duminil-Copin (2019) that four-dimensional scalar field theories cannot be asymptotically free or nontrivial both assume the scalar self-coupling in the UV is positive definite; this assumption is violated in the O(N) model in the large N limit, where the running coupling becomes negative in the UV while all physical observables remain well-behaved.

## Method

The author analyzes the O(N) model in four dimensions using the large N expansion, introducing a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation with an auxiliary field to obtain an exact effective potential. The theory is renormalized in dimensional regularization with the MS-bar scheme, yielding an exact beta function and running coupling. Physical observables including the free energy, pole mass, and cross section are computed non-perturbatively at leading and next-to-leading order in the large N expansion.

## Key numbers

- The running coupling in the large N limit is given by λ_R(μ̄) = 4π² / ln(Λ_MS̄² / μ̄²), which becomes negative for μ̄ > Λ_MS̄ and approaches zero from below as μ̄ → ∞.
- The free energy density for the thermodynamically preferred saddle is F = -N e² Λ_MS̄⁴ / (128π²), with the saddle mass m = √e Λ_MS̄.
- The cross section at NLO in large N is given by σ(E) = (4π)³ / (N² E² |1 - 2√(1 - 4m²/(E²+i0⁺)) atanh(1/√(1 - 4m²/(E²+i0⁺)))|²).
- A stable scalar bound state exists with mass m₂ ≈ 1.84 m.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Symanzik (1973) that classically unstable potentials may yield well-defined quantum field theories, and builds on the demonstration by Bender and Bottcher (1998) that non-Hermitian Hamiltonians with classically unstable potentials can have real positive eigenenergies. The results contradict the long-held belief from Coleman and Gross (1973) and Aizenman and Duminil-Copin (2019) that interacting scalar field theories in four dimensions cannot exist in the continuum. The findings reopen the possibility that scalar field theories with a finite number of components, such as the N=4 Higgs field in the Standard Model, might be non-trivial and asymptotically free.
