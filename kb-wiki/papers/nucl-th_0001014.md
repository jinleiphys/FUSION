# Vector Mesons in Medium and Dileptons in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Theoretical approaches to assess modifications of vector mesons in the medium, as well as their experimental identification via electromagnetic probes, are discussed. Implications for the nature of chiral symmetry restoration in hot/dense matter are outlined and put into context with the axialvector channel.

## Key claim

Medium modifications of vector mesons, characterized by strong broadening and the appearance of low-lying excitations rather than simple mass shifts, encode an approach towards chiral symmetry restoration in hot/dense matter, as signaled by a decrease of the pion decay constant when evaluating the first Weinberg sum rule.

## Method

The authors review hadronic many-body calculations of vector-meson selfenergies in nuclear matter, computed from gauge-invariant and chirally symmetric Lagrangians, including medium modifications in the pion cloud (through nucleon-hole and delta-hole bubbles) and direct 'rhosobar' excitations of baryon resonances. They also discuss model-independent approaches such as low-density expansions using soft pion theorems and current algebra, as well as QCD sum rules and generalized Weinberg sum rules. The in-medium spectral functions are then applied to calculate thermal dilepton and photon production rates in heavy-ion collisions, which are compared to experimental data from the CERN-SpS.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass in vacuum: M_q ≃ 0.4 GeV
- Mass splitting between chiral partners in the hadron spectrum: ΔM ≃ 0.5 GeV (e.g., ρ(770)-a1(1260))
- Duality threshold in vacuum: M_dual ≃ 1.5 GeV
- Long-range part of the πN sigma term: σ̅_πN ≃ 20 MeV
- Naive extrapolation of mixing parameter to chiral restoration point (ξ = 1/2) gives critical density: ρ_c ≃ 2.5 ρ_0
- In-medium width of N(1520) resonance: Γ_N(1520)^med ≃ 200-300 MeV
- Decrease of pion decay constant at nuclear saturation density (ρ_N = ρ_0): ∼ 30%
- Hadronic dilepton production rate shows melting of ρ resonance down to invariant masses of ∼ 0.5 GeV near the expected phase boundary

## Context

This review extends earlier finite-temperature analyses by Dey, Eletsky, and Ioffe (1990) and finite-density work by Krippa (1998) using soft pion theorems and current algebra, and contrasts with the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (1991) which predicted universal dropping of all light hadron masses. The hadronic many-body approach builds on calculations by Herrmann, Friman, and Nörenberg (1992), Chanfray and Schuck (1992), and others, and is applied to describe dilepton data from the CERES and HELIOS-3 experiments at CERN-SpS. The work enables further experimental tests with the PHENIX experiment at RHIC and the HADES experiment at GSI.
