# Omega Production in pp Collisions

> A model-independent irreducible tensor formalism which has been developed earlier to analyze measurements of $\vec{p}\vec{p}\to pp π^\circ$, is extended to present a theoretical discussion of $\vec{p}\vec{p}\to pp ω$ and the polarization of $ω$ in $pp\to pp \vecω$. The recent measurement of unpolarized differential cross section for $pp\to pp ω$ is analyzed using this theoretical formalism.

# Omega Production in pp Collisions

## Key claim
The paper extends a model-independent irreducible tensor formalism, previously developed for analyzing polarized proton-proton collisions producing neutral pions, to the production of omega mesons in pp collisions. The formalism enables extraction of initial singlet and triplet state contributions to the differential cross section and characterization of omega meson polarization, but the analysis of existing unpolarized data only provides evidence for the presence of an initial spin singlet amplitude in addition to the threshold triplet amplitude, pending future polarized beam experiments.

## Method
The authors generalize their irreducible tensor operator formalism from the spinless pion case to the spin-1 omega meson, expressing the transition matrix in terms of partial wave amplitudes and irreducible tensor amplitudes of rank Lambda. They derive expressions for the double differential cross section in terms of beam and target analyzing powers and spin correlation tensors, and relate the Fano statistical tensors of the omega meson to observables in its radiative decay omega -> pi0 gamma. The formalism is applied to the six partial wave amplitudes that contribute near threshold, and the unpolarized differential cross section is integrated to yield a polynomial form in cos^2 theta.

## Key numbers
- The branching ratio for the decay omega -> pi0 gamma is 8.92 percent, which enables measurement of omega polarization via angular distribution of circularly polarized radiation.
- The theoretical OZI rule estimate for the ratio of phi to omega production is 4.2 x 10^-3 after phase space correction.
- The short-range part of the NN interaction relevant for omega production corresponds to a distance of about 0.2 fm.
- The unpolarized differential cross section takes the form d sigma_0 = a_0 + a_2 cos^2 theta, where a_0 involves [|T_1|^2 + 3|T_2 + (1/sqrt(10)) T_3|^2] and a_2 involves [|T_3|^2 - 2 sqrt(10) Re(T_2 T_3*)] after integration over the final NN invariant mass.

## Context
This work extends the model-independent irreducible tensor formalism of Ramachandran and collaborators (2004) from pp -> pp pi0 to pp -> pp omega, building on the earlier relation derived by Bilenky and Ryndin for total cross sections. The formalism is designed to analyze data from polarized beam and polarized target experiments at facilities like COSY, where proposals exist for heavy meson production studies (Moskal et al., Hanhart). The analysis of existing unpolarized differential cross section data (published by other experimental groups) confirms the polynomial form predicted by the formalism, and the paper shows how future measurements of spin observables can determine individual partial wave amplitudes and the spin structure of the reaction.
