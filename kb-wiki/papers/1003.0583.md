# Hyperfine Effects in Ionic Orbital Electron Capture

> The K-orbital electron capture in ions with one or two electrons is analized for a general allowed nuclear transition. For ionic hyperfine states the angular neutrino distribution and the electron capture rate are given in terms of nuclear matrix elements. A possible application towards the determination of neutrino parameters is outlined.

## Key claim

For allowed nuclear transitions in ionic orbital electron capture, the hyperfine structure of the ion fundamentally modifies both the angular neutrino distribution and the total capture rate, and these effects could in principle be exploited to determine neutrino mixing parameters such as θ₁₃ and δ_CP, though the experimental requirements (very high Lorentz factors, large numbers of decays, and polarized beams) are extremely demanding.

## Method

The author derives the transition amplitudes for K-orbital electron capture in hydrogen-like (one K-electron) and helium-like (two K-electrons) ions using the standard ΔS=0 weak interaction Hamiltonian with coupling constant G_F V_ud/√2 and renormalized axial coupling g_A, in the allowed approximation. The amplitudes are expressed in terms of reduced Wigner-Eckart matrix elements for hyperfine states F = I ± 1/2, and the angular neutrino distributions and total capture rates are computed for the cases I' = I-1, I' = I, and I' = I+1. For helium-like ions, the total rate is related to the hydrogen-like rates via a spin-dependent factor first obtained by Pat (2009).

## Key numbers

- For the 1⁺ → 0⁺ decay of ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺, the hyperfine amplitude for F=1/2 is proportional to -√3 χ_L⁺ χ_λ, while the amplitude for F=3/2 is zero.
- For the proposed 0⁺ → 1⁺ EC transition e⁻ + ¹¹⁰Sn → ¹¹⁰In* ν_e, the Q-value is 267 keV and the experimental lifetime τ = 4.11 h.
- For the proposed ¹⁵⁶Yb hybrid setup, the EC branch has a neutrino energy of 3.46 MeV and the β⁺ branch has an end neutrino energy of 2.44 MeV; the halflife is t₁/₂ = 26.1 seconds.
- For the Rolinec-Sato Setup II with baseline L = 250 km and detector radius R = 100 m, the required boost to detect neutrinos emitted at right angle in the rest frame is γ = 2500; the authors take γ = 2000, giving cos(θ_rf)_max = 0.22.

## Context

This work extends the seminal analysis of hyperfine effects in EC decays by Folan and Tsifrinovich (2006), who showed for a spin-1/2 mirror transition (³¹S → ³¹P) that the rate for F=0 states is 340 times larger than for F=1 states. It also generalizes the theoretical analysis of Pat (2009), who calculated the ratio of decay rates for helium-like and hydrogen-like ions as a function of nuclear spin I. The results are directly applicable to experiments at GSI studying ¹⁴⁰Pr, ¹⁴²Pm, and ¹²²I ions, where an A-dependent modulation in the capture rate has been interpreted as due to neutrino oscillations (though this interpretation remains controversial). The paper outlines how polarized H-like ions could be used in future neutrino factory proposals by Sato (2005) and Bernabeu et al. (2006) to help determine the neutrino mixing angle θ₁₃ and the CP violation parameter δ_CP.
