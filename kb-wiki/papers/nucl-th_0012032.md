# Point Form Electrodynamics and the Gupta-Bleuler Formalism

> The Gupta-Bleuler formalism for photons is derived from induced representation theory. The representation for the little group for massless particles, the two dimensional Euclidian group, is chosen to be the four dimensional nonunitary representation obtained by restricting elements of the Lorentz group to the Euclidian group. Though the little group representation is nonunitary, it is shown that the representation of the Poincaré group is unitary. As a consequence of the four dimensional representation, the polarization vector, which connects the four-vector potential with creation and annihilation operators, is given in terms of boosts, coset representatives of the Lorentz group with respect to the Euclidian group. Several polarization vectors (boost choices) are worked out, including a front form polariation vector. The different boost choices are shown to be related by the analogue of Melosh rotations, namely Euclidian group transformations.

# Point Form Electrodynamics and the Gupta-Bleuler Formalism

## Key claim

The paper shows that by choosing a four-dimensional nonunitary representation of the little group E(2) for massless particles, rather than the usual one-dimensional unitary representation, the four-vector potential operator can be linked to photon creation and annihilation operators via a boost (coset representative of the Lorentz group with respect to E(2)), while still maintaining a unitary representation of the full Poincaré group. This generalizes the Gupta-Bleuler formalism so that photon creation and annihilation operators transform as irreducible representations of massless particles under Lorentz transformations, not as four-vectors.

## Method

The author constructs photon states and wave functions using induced representation theory, where the little group E(2) is represented by the four-dimensional nonunitary representation obtained by restricting Lorentz group elements to E(2). A modified inner product using the Lorentz metric g = diag(1,-1,-1,-1) is defined to make the Poincaré group representation unitary, and the photon Hilbert space is restricted to wave functions satisfying φ(k,0) = φ(k,3) to ensure positive definiteness. Photon creation and annihilation operators are then introduced with transformation properties inherited from the one-particle states, and the four-vector potential operator at the space-time point zero is defined using the boost matrix B_{μα}(k) as the polarization vector linking these operators to the field.

## Key numbers

- The standard four-vector for massless particles is chosen as k^{st} := (1,0,0,1), defining the little group E(2) = {Λ ∈ SO(1,3) | Λk^{st} = k^{st}}.
- The helicity boost B_H(k) is given explicitly as a 2×4 matrix with components involving chχ, shχ, and the unit vector components, where |k| = e^χ.
- The front form boost B_F(k) is given as a 4×4 matrix with entries such as k_0/2 + 1/k_+, k_x/k_+, and k_z/2 - 1/k_+, where k_± = k_0 ± k_z.
- The commutation relation for creation and annihilation operators is [c(k,α), c†(k',α')] = -g_{α,α'} k_0 δ³(k - k').

## Context

This work extends the standard Wigner (1939) and Weinberg treatment of massless particles, which uses the degenerate one-dimensional unitary representation of E(2) leading to two helicity states but problems with gauge invariance and the four-vector potential transformation. It generalizes the Gupta-Bleuler formalism (1950) by making photon creation and annihilation operators transform as single-particle states under Lorentz transformations rather than as four-vectors. The approach enables the construction of electromagnetic interactions as vertex interactions in the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics, where different boost choices (helicity, front form) are related by the massless analogue of Melosh rotations, namely E(2) transformations. The method is stated to be applicable to massless spin-two particles (gravitons) as well.
