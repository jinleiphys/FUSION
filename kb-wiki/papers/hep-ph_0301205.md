# Numerical evaluation of a two loop diagram in the cutoff regularization

> The sunset diagram of $λϕ^4$ theory is evaluated numerically in cutoff scheme and a nonzero finite term (in accordance with dimensional regularization (DR) result) is found in contrast to published calculations. This finding dramatically reduces the critical couplings for symmetry breaking in the two loop effective potential discussed in our previous work.

# Numerical evaluation of a two loop diagram in the cutoff regularization

## Key claim
The sunset diagram in cutoff regularization contains a nonzero finite constant term proportional to $\Omega^2$ (denoted $C$), contrary to earlier published calculations that implicitly set this term to zero. This nonzero constant dramatically lowers the critical couplings required for dynamical symmetry breaking in the two-loop effective potential of massless $\lambda\phi^4$ theory.

## Method
The authors numerically evaluated the sunset diagram $I_2(\Omega)$ in cutoff regularization by first removing the power-law divergent term $2\Lambda^2$ through differentiation with respect to $M=\Omega^2$, leaving integrals with at most logarithmic divergences. They performed one integration analytically and the remaining one-dimensional integrals numerically using adaptive step sizes matched to the steepness of the integrand, for cutoff scales $\beta/M = 10^4$ and $10^5$. A second approach directly subtracted the $2\beta$ term from the integrand before numerical integration, using homogeneous step sizes, to cross-check the result.

## Key numbers
- The finite constant $C$ in the sunset diagram expansion $I_2(M) = \frac{1}{(4\pi)^4}\{2\beta - \frac{3M}{2}\ln^2\frac{M}{\beta} + 3M\ln\frac{M}{\beta} + CM\}$ was found to lie in the interval $[4, 4.3]$.
- For $\beta/M = 10^4$, the differentiation method gave $C = 4.13048$; for $\beta/M = 10^5$, $C = 4.15412$.
- The direct subtraction method gave $C = 4.14128$ (step $10^{-4}$), $4.14274$ (step $10^{-3}$), and $4.156178$ (step $10^{-2}$) for $\beta/M = 10^4$; and $C = 4.160739$ (step $10^{-2}$), $4.1633$ (step $0.005$) for $\beta/M = 10^5$; and $C = 4.162993$ (step $10^{-2}$) for $\beta/M = 10^6$.
- With $C=4$, the critical coupling $\lambda_{cr}$ in the $\mu^2_\Lambda$ scheme dropped from 5.1152 to 2.5684, and in the Jackiw scheme from 6.5797 to 2.7181.
- The stable critical coupling $\hat{\lambda}_{cr}$ in the $\mu^2_\Lambda$ scheme dropped from 6.5797 to 2.7181, and in the Jackiw scheme from 10.698 to 2.8967.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of dynamical symmetry breaking in massless $\lambda\phi^4$ theory (Yang and Ruan, 2002), which had found that symmetry breaking solutions depend sensitively on the finite constant term in the sunset diagram. The calculation corrects the earlier result of Jackiw (1974), which did not explicitly give the $\Omega^2$ term in the cutoff regularization of $I_2(\Omega)$. The finding that $C$ is nonzero and approximately 4 makes the cutoff scheme more favorable than dimensional regularization for this problem, analogous to the situation in effective field theory applications to nucleon interactions (Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner, 1998-2000; Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck, 1996). The numerical techniques demonstrated here enable efficient evaluation of divergent multiloop integrals in cutoff regularization without requiring extremely large cutoff scales.
