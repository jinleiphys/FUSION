# Can nuclear physics explain the anomaly observed in the internal pair production in the Beryllium-8 nucleus?

> Recently the experimentalists in [PRL 116, 042501 (2016)] announced observing an unexpected enhancement of the electron-positron pair production signal in one of the Beryllium-8 nuclear transitions. The following studies have been focused on possible explanations based on introducing new types of particle. In this work, we improve the nuclear physics modeling of the reaction by studying the pair emission anisotropy and the interferences between different multipoles in an effective field theory inspired framework, and examine their possible relevance to the anomaly. The connection between the previously measured on-shell photon production and the pair production in the same nuclear transitions is established. These improvements, absent in the original experimental analysis, should be included in extracting new particle's properties from the experiment of this type. We then study the possibility of using the nuclear transition form factor to explain the anomaly. The reduction of the anomaly's significance by simply rescaling our predicted event count is also investigated.

## Key claim

The authors improve the nuclear physics modeling of the electron-positron pair production in Beryllium-8 by including pair emission anisotropy and multipole interference effects absent in the original experimental analysis, but find that these improvements cannot explain the observed anomaly; they further show that invoking a hypothetical M1 transition form factor to explain the anomaly would require an unrealistic length scale on the order of 10s of fm for the Beryllium-8 nucleus.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory (EFT) inspired model for the proton-Lithium-7 scattering and the subsequent electromagnetic transitions to the Beryllium-8 ground state, incorporating E1, M1, and E2 multipoles. They calibrate the model parameters against existing on-shell photon production data (total S-factor, angular anisotropy coefficients a1 and a2) from the literature, then use the calibrated model to predict the electron-positron pair production cross section and angular distributions. The pair production calculation includes the full angular dependence from the beam-target scattering state and interference between multipoles, which were omitted in the original experimental analysis.

## Key numbers

- The two Beryllium-8 1+ resonances have excitation energies of 0.895 MeV (MIS) and 0.385 MeV (MIV) above the proton-Lithium-7 threshold, with radiative decay widths of 1.9 +/- 0.4 eV and 15.0 +/- 1.8 eV, and strong decay widths of 138 +/- 6 keV and 10.7 +/- 0.6 keV, respectively
- The Beryllium-8 ground state lies 17.2551 MeV below the proton-Lithium-7 threshold
- The E2 contribution to the total cross section is on the order of a few percent at the MIS resonance peak
- The form factor needed to explain the anomaly would require a length scale on the order of 10s of fm for the Beryllium-8 nucleus

## Context

This work extends the nuclear physics modeling of the internal pair production anomaly reported by Krasznahorkay et al. (2016) in Physical Review Letters 116, 042501, which had previously been attributed to new particles by Feng et al. (2016) and others. The authors improve upon the original analysis by Krasznahorkay et al. (2016) and Gulyas et al. (2015), which used the framework of Rose (1949) that neglected beam-target anisotropy and multipole interference. The calibrated model enables future experimental analyses to properly account for nuclear physics effects when extracting possible new particle properties from this reaction.
