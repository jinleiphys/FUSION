# Effective operators in two-nucleon systems

> Effective Hamiltonians and effective electroweak operators are calculated with the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki formalism for two-nucleon systems. Working within a harmonic oscillator basis, first without and then with a confining harmonic oscillator trap, we demonstrate the effects of renormalization on observables calculated for truncated basis spaces. We illustrate the renormalization effects for the root-mean-square point-proton radius, electric quadrupole moment, magnetic dipole moment, Gamow-Teller transition and neutrinoless double-beta decay operator using nucleon-nucleon interactions from chiral Effective Field Theory. Renormalization effects tend to be larger in the weaker traps and smaller basis spaces suggesting applications to heavier nuclei with transitions dominated by weakly-bound nucleons would be subject to more significant renormalization effects within achievable basis spaces.

# Effective operators in two-nucleon systems

## Key claim
The Okubo-Lee-Suzuki (OLS) renormalization method reproduces exact results for electroweak observables in two-nucleon systems across all truncated basis spaces, but the effects of basis truncation on the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element can exceed a factor of two for weakly-bound systems, indicating that consistent operator renormalization is essential for reliable predictions in limited basis spaces.

## Method
The authors solved two-nucleon systems (neutron-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-proton channels) using chiral Effective Field Theory nucleon-nucleon interactions (LENPIC at various chiral orders and Idaho-N³LO) in a harmonic oscillator basis, both in free space and with an additional confining harmonic oscillator trap. They applied the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki unitary transformation method to derive effective Hamiltonians and effective electroweak operators for truncated basis spaces defined by the maximum total oscillator quanta (Nmax), and compared results from the truncated basis approach and the OLS-renormalized approach against exact results obtained from very large basis diagonalizations (Nmax=400 or Nmax=200).

## Key numbers
- Deuteron ground state energy from LENPIC-N²LO interaction: exact result obtained at Nmax=400 with three values of ℏΩ (5, 10, 20 MeV), verified to at least 5 significant figures against numerical solution of the Schrödinger equation
- For ⁶Li ground state energy using chiral N²LO NN interaction: extrapolated complete-basis result is −31.0(2) MeV, with uncertainty of 200 keV; at Nmax=18 and ℏΩ=28 MeV the variational upper bound is −29.928 MeV, about 1.1 MeV above the extrapolated result; experimental ground state energy is −31.995 MeV
- OLS eigenvalues agree with the corresponding subset of exact eigenvalues to at least 6 significant figures for all basis parameters tested
- Gamow-Teller matrix element quenching factor in truncated calculations deviates from unity by at most about 6%, with largest deviations at ℏΩ=5 MeV
- Neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element from truncation approach does not approach the exact result until approximately Nmax=40 for ℏΩ=5 MeV, Nmax=20 for ℏΩ=10 MeV, and Nmax=10 for ℏΩ=20 MeV

## Context
This work extends the OLS renormalization framework, previously applied to effective Hamiltonians in the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) as developed by Barrett et al. (2013), to consistently renormalize electroweak operators for two-nucleon systems. It builds on pioneering work by Anderson et al. (2010) who investigated deuteron electromagnetic form factors with renormalization group methods, but distinguishes itself by using a different renormalization method, different interactions, a broader set of observables, and the inclusion of a confining trap. The results enable systematic treatment of operator renormalization in NCSM calculations for heavier nuclei, particularly for neutrinoless double-beta decay where truncation effects can be severe, and suggest that phenomenological quenching factors for Gamow-Teller transitions may have a theoretical foundation in basis truncation effects.
