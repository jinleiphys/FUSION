# Structure of $^{14}$C and $^{14}$O nuclei calculated in the variational approach

> The structure of mirror $^{14}$C and $^{14}$O nuclei has been studied in the framework of the five-particle model (three $α$-particles and two nucleons). Interaction potentials are proposed, which allowed the energy and radius of $^{14}$C nucleus, as well as the energy of $^{14}$O one, to agree with experimental data. On the basis of the variational approach with the use of Gaussian bases, the energies and wave functions for five-particle systems under consideration are calculated. The charge radius of $^{14}$O nucleus, as well as the charge density distributions and the form factors for both nuclei, are predicted.

## Key claim

The authors propose a five-particle model (three alpha-particles plus two nucleons) with specifically tuned interaction potentials that simultaneously reproduces the experimental energy and charge radius of \(^{14}\)C and the experimental energy of \(^{14}\)O, and then predicts the charge radius of \(^{14}\)O and the charge density distributions and form factors for both nuclei, with the hedge that these predictions await future experimental confirmation.

## Method

The nuclei \(^{14}\)C and \(^{14}\)O are treated as systems of three alpha-particles plus two extra neutrons (for \(^{14}\)C) or two extra protons (for \(^{14}\)O). The five-particle Schrodinger equation is solved variationally using a Gaussian basis expansion, with the wave function expressed as a superposition of symmetrized gaussoids. Pair interaction potentials between alpha-particles, between nucleons and alpha-particles, and between the two extra nucleons are chosen with local and separable nonlocal components; the parameters are adjusted iteratively until the calculated ground-state energies and the charge radius of \(^{14}\)C match experimental values. Charge density distributions are then obtained by convolving the calculated point-particle densities with experimental charge densities of the alpha-particle and the proton (Helm approximation).

## Key numbers

*   Ground-state energy of \(^{14}\)C: \(-20.398\) MeV (experimental value \(-20.398\) MeV), reckoned from the threshold of decay into three alpha-particles and two nucleons.
*   Ground-state energy of \(^{14}\)O: \(-13.845\) MeV (experimental value \(-13.845\) MeV), on the same energy scale.
*   Charge radius of \(^{14}\)C: \(2.500\) fm (experimental values \(2.496\) fm and \(2.503\) fm from two references).
*   Predicted charge radius of \(^{14}\)O: \(2.415\) fm, with an estimated calculation error of \(\pm 0.005\) fm.
*   Average kinetic energy per extra neutron in \(^{14}\)C: \(32.66\) MeV; per alpha-particle in \(^{14}\)C: \(6.83\) MeV.
*   Average kinetic energy per extra proton in \(^{14}\)O: \(31.77\) MeV; per alpha-particle in \(^{14}\)O: \(6.62\) MeV.
*   Root-mean-square relative distance between extra nucleons (\(r_{NN}\)) in \(^{14}\)C: \(2.621\) fm; in \(^{14}\)O: \(2.732\) fm.
*   Root-mean-square charge radius of the point-like alpha-particle distribution (\(R_{\alpha}\)) in \(^{14}\)C: \(1.852\) fm; in \(^{14}\)O: \(1.882\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous five-particle alpha-cluster model calculations for lighter nuclei such as \(^{6}\)He, \(^{6}\)Li, \(^{10}\)Be, and \(^{10}\)C (Grinyuk and Piatnytskyi, earlier works) to the mirror pair \(^{14}\)C and \(^{14}\)O. The interaction potentials are slightly modified from those used for \(^{10}\)Be (Grinyuk and Piatnytskyi, earlier work) to achieve agreement with experimental data for the heavier system. The predicted charge radius of \(^{14}\)O and the charge form factors for both nuclei provide testable quantities for future electron scattering experiments, and the authors suggest that further insight could be gained by calculating pair correlation functions and momentum distributions.
