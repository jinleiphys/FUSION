# Probing nuclear matter with jets

> Jet physics in relativistic heavy ion collisions, which combines perturbative QCD jet production with quark and gluon energy loss and in-medium parton shower modification, has emerged as a powerful tool to probe the properties of strongly-interacting matter formed in high-energy nuclear reactions. We present selected results for the modification of jet cross sections and related observables in the ambiance of hot and/or dense nuclear medium. We focus on the inclusive jet spectrum and dijets [${\cal O}(α_s^3) $], and $Z^0/γ^*$ tagged jets [${\cal O}(G_Fα_s^2) $] in the framework of perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

Jet observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, calculated at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, can probe the properties of the quark-gluon plasma through medium-induced modifications of jet cross sections, but the magnitude and pattern of these modifications depend sensitively on the jet cone size, the coupling strength of the jet to the medium, and whether collisional energy loss is included alongside radiative energy loss.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive jet spectra at ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^3)$ and $Z^0/\gamma^*$-tagged jet cross sections at ${\cal O}(G_F\alpha_s^2)$ using the collinear factorization approach of perturbative QCD, incorporating both initial-state cold nuclear matter effects (via nuclear parton distribution functions) and final-state medium-induced parton energy loss. The in-medium parton splitting formalism in the soft gluon approximation is used to model the fully differential distribution of radiative energy loss, and collisional energy loss is included in selected calculations. Results are presented for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^{\rm 1-jet}$ for inclusive jets in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV shows a clear dependence on jet size $R$ when only radiative energy loss is included, but this dependence "will almost disappear" when collisional energy loss is added.
- $Z^0/\gamma^*$-tagged jet transverse momentum spectra are calculated for p+p and Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s}=4.0$ TeV, showing a shift to smaller $p_{T_{\rm jet}}$ values due to parton energy loss.
- Dijet asymmetry distributions in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV and momentum imbalance distributions in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV are presented; for radiative energy loss there is a distinct dependence on jet cone radius $R$, but when collisional energy loss is included the dependence on jet size becomes very weak while sensitivity to the coupling strength is amplified.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD framework for jet production in heavy-ion collisions by combining next-to-leading order calculations with medium-induced parton energy loss, building on earlier studies of jet quenching and parton energy loss by Vitev (2009), He (2011), and Neufeld (2010, 2012). The results provide theoretical predictions that can be compared to experimental measurements of dijet momentum asymmetry by ATLAS and CMS at the LHC, and enable future extraction of the jet-medium coupling strength and the relative contributions of radiative versus collisional energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.
