# Resonant Atom-Dimer Relaxation in Ultracold Atoms

> Three-body systems with large scattering length display universal phenomena associated with a discrete scaling symmetry. These phenomena include resonant enhancement of three-body loss rates when an Efimov three-body resonance is at the scattering threshold. In particular, there can be resonant peaks in the atom-dimer relaxation rate for large positive scattering length. We improve upon earlier studies and calculate the atom-dimer relaxation rate as a function of temperature using a Bose-Einstein distribution for the thermal average. As input, we use calculations of the atom-dimer scattering phase shifts from effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the atom-dimer relaxation rate for ultracold Cs atoms using a Bose-Einstein distribution for the thermal average and full effective field theory phase shifts, but finds that the extracted resonance position (a* = 397 a0) is incompatible with the value from three-body recombination data (a* ≈ 1200 a0), and the theory overpredicts the 40 nK data by a factor of two.

## Method

The authors compute the atom-dimer relaxation coefficient β by thermally averaging the inelastic cross section using Bose-Einstein distribution functions for atoms and dimers in a harmonic trap. The S-wave atom-dimer scattering phase shifts are taken from effective field theory calculations parametrized as a function of ka and a/a*, with the effects of deep dimers incorporated by making the replacement ln a* → ln a* − iη*/s0 in the amplitude. The resulting rate equation for dimer loss is evaluated numerically and compared to experimental data from Knoop et al. (2008) at temperatures of 170 nK and 40 nK.

## Key numbers

- Resonance position a* = 397 a0 extracted from fitting the 170 nK data (with χ²/dof = 1.2)
- Resonance width parameter η* = 0.034 from the 170 nK fit; η* = 0.016 from fitting the 40 nK data (excluding two data points)
- Critical temperatures: Tc,A ≈ 94 nK for atoms and Tc,D ≈ 32 nK at 170 nK; Tc,A ≈ 35 nK and Tc,D ≈ 18 nK at 40 nK
- Chemical potentials at 170 nK: μA = −2.74 × 10⁻⁷ kB K, μD = −8.17 × 10⁻⁷ kB K
- Chemical potentials at 40 nK: μA = −1.21 × 10⁻⁸ kB K, μD = −8.87 × 10⁻⁸ kB K
- Atom number NA = 10⁵, dimer number ND = 4 × 10³, trap frequency ω̄ = 45 Hz at 170 nK
- Atom number NA = 3 × 10⁴, dimer number ND = 4 × 10³, trap frequency ω̄ = 25.2 Hz at 40 nK
- Error from neglecting k > kD contributions is at most 0.2% for the largest scattering length at T = 170 nK
- Using effective range expansion instead of full phase shifts shifts a* by about 3% to a higher value (407 a0) while η* remains unchanged

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Braaten (2003) and Braaten (2006) by using full effective field theory phase shifts instead of the effective range expansion and by employing Bose-Einstein rather than Boltzmann distributions for the thermal average. The paper also corrects an error in Braaten (2006) where a factor of k² was omitted in the thermal average. The results are applied to experimental data from Knoop et al. (2008) on atom-dimer relaxation in ultracold Cs, and the discrepancy between the extracted resonance position and that from three-body recombination data in Kraemer (2006) suggests non-universal physics or four-body losses may be important, particularly at lower temperatures.
