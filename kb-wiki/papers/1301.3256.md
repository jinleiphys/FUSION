# Extracting the nuclear symmetry potential and energy from neutron-nucleus scattering data

> According to the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, the symmetry energy and its density slope can be decomposed uniquely in terms of the single-nucleon potential in asymmetric nuclear matter which, at normal density, can be constrained by the nucleon optical model potential extracted from analyzing the nucleon-nucleus scattering data. To more accurately extract information on the symmetry energy and its density slope at normal density from neutron-nucleus scattering data, going beyond the well-known Lane potential, we include consistently the second order terms in isospin asymmetry in both the optical model potential and the symmetry energy decomposition. We find that the strength of the second-order symmetry potential $U_{sym,2}$ in asymmetric nuclear matter is significant compared to the first-order one U_{sym,1}$, especially at high nucleon momentum. While the $U_{sym,1}$ at normal density decreases with nucleon momentum, the $U_{sym,2}$ is found to have the opposite behavior. Moreover, we discuss effects of the $U_{sym,1}$ and $U_{sym,2}$ on determining the density dependence of the symmetry energy, and we find that the available neutron-nucleus scattering data favor a softer density dependence of the symmetry energy at normal density with a negligible contribution from the $U_{sym,2}$.

# Extracting the nuclear symmetry potential and energy from neutron-nucleus scattering data

## Key claim

The available neutron-nucleus scattering data favor a softer density dependence of the symmetry energy at normal density with a negligible contribution from the second-order symmetry potential $U_{sym,2}$, although the magnitude of $U_{sym,2}$ is significant compared to the first-order potential $U_{sym,1}$ at high nucleon momentum. The extracted slope parameter $L(\rho_0)=44.98\pm22.31$ MeV is closer to the lower end of previous constraints, while the symmetry energy $E_{sym}(\rho_0)=37.24\pm2.26$ MeV is closer to the upper end.

## Method

The authors extended the traditional phenomenological optical model potential (OMP) for neutron-nucleus scattering to include isospin-dependent terms up to second order in $(N-Z)/A$ for the real central potential depth $V_v$, and used the Monte Carlo method with 1000 random samples of 10% of the experimental database to evaluate parameter uncertainties. Using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove (HVH) theorem and the analytical decomposition of the symmetry energy and its density slope in terms of single-nucleon potentials, they extracted the first-order symmetry potential $U_{sym,1}$ and second-order symmetry potential $U_{sym,2}$ at saturation density from the OMP parameters, then evaluated the contributions of each term to $E_{sym}(\rho_0)$ and $L(\rho_0)$.

## Key numbers

- $E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 37.24 \pm 2.26$ MeV, obtained from neutron-nucleus scattering data using the HVH decomposition with empirical $\rho_0=0.16\pm0.02$ fm$^{-3}$ and $\mathcal{E}_0=-16\pm0.5$ MeV
- $L(\rho_0) = 44.98 \pm 22.31$ MeV, the density slope parameter at saturation density
- $E^1_{sym}(\rho_0) = 18.36 \pm 1.49$ MeV (kinetic contribution) and $E^2_{sym}(\rho_0) = 18.88 \pm 1.62$ MeV (first-order symmetry potential contribution)
- $L_5(\rho_0) = 0.81 \pm 14.48$ MeV, the contribution from the second-order symmetry potential $U_{sym,2}$, which is small but carries the largest uncertainty among the five terms
- $L_4(\rho_0) = -46.11 \pm 11.87$ MeV, the contribution from the momentum dependence of $U_{sym,1}$
- $U_{sym,1}$ becomes negative when nucleon momentum exceeds approximately $p=470$ MeV/c (corresponding to $\mathcal{E}=90$ MeV)
- Using $\rho_0=0.16\pm0.02$ fm$^{-3}$ and the extracted $U_0(\mathcal{E})$ gives $\mathcal{E}_0=-28.52\pm4.85$ MeV, $E_{sym}(\rho_0)=40.12\pm2.84$ MeV, and $L(\rho_0)=44.61\pm24.40$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Xu, Li, and Chen (2010), who first applied the HVH theorem to decompose the symmetry energy in terms of single-nucleon potentials and obtained $E_{sym}(\rho_0)=31.3\pm4.5$ MeV and $L(\rho_0)=52.7\pm22.5$ MeV using only the first-order Lane potential. The present study goes beyond the Lane potential by including second-order isospin asymmetry terms, following the theoretical demonstration by Chen et al. (2012) that the second-order symmetry potential contribution to $L(\rho_0)$ depends on the interactions used and cannot be simply neglected. The results verify the assumption made in Xu et al. (2010) that the $L_5$ term is small, but reveal that the second-order potential contributes the dominant uncertainty to $L(\rho_0)$, motivating future use of the dispersive optical model to obtain more reliable OMP parameters at negative energies.
