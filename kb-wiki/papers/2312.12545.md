# One and Two-Body Current Contributions to Lepton-Nucleus Scattering

> Modeling lepton-nucleus scattering with the accuracy required to extract neutrino-oscillation parameters from long- and short-baseline experiments necessitates retaining most quantum-mechanical effects. One such effect is the interference between one- and two-body current operators in the transition currents, which has been known to enhance the cross-sections, especially in transverse kinematics. In this work, we incorporate such interference in the spectral function formalism, which combines relativistic currents and kinematics with an accurate description of the initial target state. Our analysis of lepton-scattering off $^{12}$C demonstrates that interference effects appreciably enhance the transverse electromagnetic response functions and the flux-folded neutrino-nucleus cross section, in both cases, improving the agreement with experimental data. We discuss the impact on the neutrino-oscillation program and the determination of nucleon axial form factors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including the interference between one- and two-body current operators within the spectral function formalism appreciably enhances the transverse electromagnetic response functions and flux-folded neutrino-nucleus cross sections for lepton scattering off $^{12}$C, improving agreement with experimental data. The authors note that this interference effect is particularly significant in the quasielastic region, where it contributes approximately 20% of the peak height in the transverse response, contrary to earlier expectations that such interference would peak in the dip region.

## Method

The authors extended the quantum Monte Carlo-based spectral function (QMC-SF) formalism to include interference between one- and two-body current operators leading to single-nucleon knockout final states. They computed the electromagnetic and electroweak response tensors for $^{12}$C using relativistic currents and kinematics, incorporating meson exchange currents (pion-in-flight, seagull, pion-pole, and delta excitation diagrams) with medium modifications to the delta propagator. The spectral functions were constructed from variational Monte Carlo calculations of spectroscopic overlaps and two-nucleon momentum distributions, with final state interactions included via a folding function.

## Key numbers

- At momentum transfer $|\mathbf{q}| = 570$ MeV, the interference and pure two-body components each contribute about 20% of the peak height to the transverse response function of $^{12}$C
- The interference contribution to inclusive electron-$^{12}$C cross sections increases the strength in the quasielastic region by approximately 10%
- For the transverse response at $|\mathbf{q}| = 500$ MeV, the contribution of proton-neutron pairs is about 10 times as large as that of proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairs
- The Fermi momentum for $^{12}$C is taken as $k_F = 225$ MeV
- The pion-nucleon coupling cutoff is $\Lambda_\pi = 1300$ MeV, and the delta-nucleon cutoff is $\Lambda_{\pi N\Delta} = 1150$ MeV
- The delta resonance mass is $M_\Delta = 1232$ MeV, with vector form factor parameters $M_V = 0.84$ GeV and axial form factor parameters $M_{A\Delta} = 1.05$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the QMC-SF formalism previously benchmarked against Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations by Nikolakopoulos (2023), which had demonstrated the accuracy of GFMC at intermediate momentum transfer but could not include interference effects due to factorization limitations. The results are consistent with GFMC calculations by Lovato (2016, 2017, 2020) showing interference enhancement of the transverse response in the quasielastic region, and with similar findings from the short-time approximation by Pastore (2019) and relativistic mean-field approaches by Franco-Munoz (2022, 2023). The improved treatment enables more accurate predictions for neutrino-oscillation experiments like DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande, where percent-level cross section accuracy is required, and provides isospin-resolved predictions relevant for MicroBooNE and SBND experiments measuring exclusive final states.
