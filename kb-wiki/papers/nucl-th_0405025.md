# Di-neutron elastic transfer in the 4He(6He,6He)4He reaction

> Elastic $^{6}$He+$^4$He data measured at $E_{\rm c.m.}=11.6,$ 15.9, and 60.3 MeV have been analyzed within the coupled reaction channels (CRC) formalism, with the elastic-scattering and two-neutron ($2n$) transfer amplitudes coherently included. Contributions from the direct (one-step) and sequential (two-step) $2n$-transfers were treated explicitly based on a realistic assumption for the $2n$-transfer form factor. The oscillatory pattern observed in $^4$He($^6$He,$^6$He)$^4$He angular distribution at low energies was found to be due to an interference between the elastic scattering and $2n$-transfer amplitudes. Our CRC analysis shows consistently that the direct $2n$-transfer strongly dominates over the sequential transfer and thus confirms the dominance of 2$n-^4$He configuration over the $n-^5$He one in the $^6$He wave function. This result suggests a strong clusterization of the two valence neutrons and allows, therefore, a reliable estimate for the \emph{di-neutron} spectroscopic amplitude.

# Di-neutron elastic transfer in the 4He(6He,6He)4He reaction

## Key claim

The oscillatory pattern observed in the angular distribution of elastic 4He(6He,6He)4He at low energies arises from interference between elastic scattering and two-neutron (2n) transfer amplitudes, with the direct one-step 2n-transfer strongly dominating over the sequential two-step transfer; this dominance confirms that the 2n-4He configuration prevails over the n-5He configuration in the 6He wave function, but the most reliable di-neutron spectroscopic amplitudes require accounting for interference between direct and indirect 2n-transfer via the 2+ excitation of 6He.

## Method

The authors performed coupled reaction channels (CRC) calculations using the code FRESCO, coherently including elastic scattering and 2n-transfer amplitudes for 6He+4He data at center-of-mass energies of 11.6, 15.9, and 60.3 MeV. The direct (one-step) and sequential (two-step) 2n-transfer contributions were treated explicitly using realistic transfer form factors based on core-plus-valence-neutron wave functions for 5He and 6He, with the 6He ground state wave function modeled as a 4He core plus two p3/2 neutrons in S-wave (L=J=0) and P-wave (L=J=1) configurations. Semi-microscopic optical potentials were constructed from double-folding model real potentials with CDM3Y6 interaction and Woods-Saxon imaginary potentials.

## Key numbers

- 6He two-neutron separation energy: 0.975 MeV
- Center-of-mass energies analyzed: 11.6, 15.9, and 60.3 MeV
- Best-fit di-neutron spectroscopic amplitude for 6He ground state (SA_g.s.) without 2+ coupling: 1.15 ± 0.15 at 11.6 and 15.9 MeV; 1.00 ± 0.15 at 60.3 MeV
- Best-fit SA_g.s. with indirect transfer via 2+ excitation included: 0.95 at 11.6 MeV, 0.85 at 60.3 MeV
- Di-neutron spectroscopic amplitude for 6He 2+ state (SA_2+): 1.3 ± 0.1
- Total reaction cross section at 11.6 MeV without 2+ coupling: 859 mb; with 2+ coupling: 801 mb
- Total reaction cross section at 60.3 MeV without 2+ coupling: 829 mb; with 2+ coupling: 797 mb
- Integrated 2+ inelastic cross section at 11.6 MeV: 53.8 mb; at 60.3 MeV: 36.1 mb
- Renormalization factor for real folded potential (N_V): 1.15
- P-wave probability in 6He wave function: 15% (S-wave: 85%)
- Proton deformation length for 2+ excitation: 1.75 fm; neutron deformation length: 2.55 fm; isoscalar deformation length: 2.35 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of 4He(6He,6He)4He data by Ter-Akopian (1998) at 60.3 MeV and Raabe (1999, 2003) at 11.6 and 15.9 MeV, which had been treated with simpler DWBA or optical model approaches by Oganessian (1999) and Raabe (2003). It directly addresses the need for a consistent CRC treatment across all three energies, as called for by Krouglov (2001) who analyzed only the 60.3 MeV data. The results contradict the CDCC analysis by Rusek and Kemper (2000), which attributed the backward-angle rise to breakup channel coupling alone; the present work shows that 2n-transfer is the dominant mechanism. The extracted spectroscopic amplitudes enable future structure calculations of 6He that properly account for both di-neutron and cigar configurations, and motivate measurement of inelastic 4He(6He,6He*_2+)4He scattering for independent determination of SA_2+.
