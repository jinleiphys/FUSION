# Dark matter effects on the properties of quark stars and the implications for the peculiar objects

> We systematically investigate the observable properties of dark matter-admixed quark stars (DQSs) using the confined-isospin-density-dependent-mass model in combination with the generic bosonic self-interacting dark matter model. Our results show that the dark matter (DM) can significantly influence the properties of quark stars including the mass, radius, and the central pressure at the maximum mass configurations. Moreover, we observe that the mass of DMparticles and the DMfraction significantly affect the types of stellar configurations, and we study these configurations in detail under various scenarios and predict the possibility that two recently observed peculiar objects HESS J1731-347 and PSR J014-4002E are DQSs.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates how dark matter (DM) affects the observable properties of quark stars, predicting that two recently observed peculiar objects, HESS J1731-347 and PSR J0514-4002E, could be dark matter-admixed quark stars (DQSs), but only under specific combinations of DM particle mass and DM fraction, with the actual feasibility depending on which quark matter equation of state is used.

## Method

The authors model quark matter using the confined-isospin-density-dependent-mass (CIDDM) model with two parameter sets (DI-85 and DI-3500), and model dark matter as a generic bosonic self-interacting fluid with a single scale parameter. They solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations assuming quark matter and dark matter couple only through gravity, computing mass-radius relations and maximum masses for DQSs with DM particle masses of 100, 200, 500, and 1000 MeV and DM fractions up to 30%.

## Key numbers

- Pure quark star maximum masses: 2.34 solar masses (DI-3500 EOS) and 2.02 solar masses (DI-85 EOS)
- Pure dark matter star maximum masses: 17.762, 4.441, 0.711, and 0.177 solar masses for DM particle masses 100, 200, 500, and 1000 MeV respectively
- For DI-3500 EOS with DM particle mass 500 MeV and 5% DM fraction, maximum mass decreases from 2.34 to 2.14 solar masses; for 100 MeV DM particles at same fraction, maximum mass decreases to 2.30 solar masses
- At 20% DM fraction with 100 MeV DM particles, maximum masses reach 2.59 solar masses (DI-3500, 10.7% increase) and 2.29 solar masses (DI-85, 13.4% increase)
- For DI-3500 EOS with DM particle mass 500 MeV, allowed DM fraction to maintain maximum mass above 2 solar masses is 8.7%; for DI-85 EOS with same DM mass, allowed fraction is only 0.5%
- HESS J1731-347 mass: 0.77 solar masses (range 0.60 to 0.97 solar masses); radius: 10.4 km (range 9.62 to 11.26 km)
- PSR J0514-4002E mass range: 2.09 to 2.71 solar masses at 95% confidence

## Context

This work extends prior studies of DM effects on neutron stars (e.g., Das 2021b, Biswal 2021) to the less-explored case of strange quark stars, using the CIDDM model from Chu (2014) and the bosonic self-interacting DM model from Maselli (2017) and Leung (2022). The results enable future astronomical observations to constrain DM particle mass and DM fraction parameters if DQSs are identified, and provide a framework for interpreting the nature of the compact objects HESS J1731-347 (Doroshenko 2022) and the companion of PSR J0514-4002E (Barr 2024).
