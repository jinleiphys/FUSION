# Excess of pions with chiral symmetry restoration

> We study the effect of the chiral phase transition on pion production in hot hadronic matter. The phase of restored chiral symmetry is characterized by the appearance of the scalar $σ$-meson as a chiral partner of the pion as well as by the degeneracy of the vector and axial-vector mesons. We find rapid thermal and chemical equilibration of these degrees of freedom in the symmetric phase. Provided that the chiral transition temperature is not considerably high, the presence of a chirally symmetric phase will result in $\sim 1.6$ times more thermal pions in the final state.

## Key claim

Provided that the chiral transition temperature is not considerably high (Tχ ≤ 160 MeV), the presence of a chirally symmetric phase in hot hadronic matter will result in approximately 1.6 times more thermal pions in the final state compared to a scenario without chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors used the SU(2)L × SU(2)R linear sigma model including vector (ρ) and axial-vector (a1) mesons as gauge fields to describe hadronic matter near the chiral phase transition. They calculated chemical relaxation times for inelastic reactions such as ππ ↔ ππππ and ππ ↔ σσ in both the broken and chirally symmetric phases, then used the resulting chemical equilibrium conditions (chemical potentials) to estimate the final pion abundance after resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Chemical relaxation time for ππ ↔ ππππ in the symmetric phase at T = 180 MeV: τch = 2.8 fm (for λ = 21.4, corresponding to mσ = 1 GeV).
- Chemical relaxation time for ππ ↔ σσ in the symmetric phase at T = 180 MeV: 0.3 to 0.6 fm (for mπ = mσ = 140 MeV, mρ = ma1 = 770 MeV).
- Chemical relaxation time for ππ ↔ σσ in the symmetric phase with thermal masses (mπ = mσ = 220 MeV, mρ = ma1 = 980 MeV): considerably slower but still allowing equilibrium near Tχ ≈ 220 MeV.
- Chemical relaxation time for ππ ↔ ππππ in the broken phase at T ∼ 160 MeV: τch ≃ 200 fm.
- Induced pion chemical potential from instant chiral condensate buildup at Tχ = 160 to 200 MeV: μπ = 55 to 66 MeV.
- Decay width of σ → ππ in the broken phase: Γσππ ≃ 800 MeV (for mσ ∼ 1 GeV) or 600 MeV (for mσ ∼ 0.6 GeV).
- Final state pion enhancement factor from chiral symmetry restoration: ∼ 1.6 times more thermal pions.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of chemical equilibration in hot hadronic matter by Song and Koch (1996) and builds on the linear sigma model framework used by Pisarski (1993) and others. The prediction of a ∼1.6-fold pion excess connects to experimental analyses of SPS heavy-ion collisions: one analysis by Gadiz (1996) found such an excess, while another by Stachel (1996) did not. The paper enables future experimental searches for chiral symmetry restoration signatures through precise measurements of pion multiplicities in heavy-ion collisions.
