# Heavy quark-antiquark free energy and thermodynamics of string-hadron avoided crossings

> The correlation function between two Polyakov loops encodes the free-energy shift due to a pair of separated colour conjugated sources in the hot QCD medium. This is analyzed in terms of a novel Källén-Lehmann spectral representation for the separating distance, implying an increasing and concave free-energy at all temperatures. We express the heavy $\bar{Q} Q$ free-energy shift below the phase transition in QCD in terms of colour neutral purely hadronic states with no explicit reference to quarks and gluons. Good agreement with lattice data is achieved when considering the avoided crossing mechanism underlying string breaking and with standard quenched values of the string tension known from charmonium and bottomonium phenomenology. We also address the role of the corresponding entropy shift and its renormalization group properties.

## Key claim

The heavy quark-antiquark free-energy shift below the QCD phase transition can be expressed entirely in terms of colour-neutral hadronic states and a single bosonic string potential, with no explicit reference to quarks and gluons, provided the avoided crossing mechanism underlying string breaking is incorporated; this representation achieves good agreement with lattice data.

## Method

The authors derive a Källén-Lehmann spectral representation for the Polyakov loop correlator as a function of the separation distance \(r\), which implies the free-energy is increasing and concave at all temperatures. They model the confined phase using a hadron resonance gas combined with a string potential, incorporating an avoided crossing structure between the string state and heavy-light meson-antimeson states. The free-energy shift is then computed from the spectral representation and compared to lattice data for the Polyakov loop correlator.

## Key numbers

* The hadronic limiting temperature from Hagedorn is \(T_H = 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The crossover between hadron gas and quark-gluon liquid occurs at a temperature of about \(150\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (Aoki 2006).
* The string tension used is taken from standard quenched values known from charmonium and bottomonium phenomenology (no explicit numerical value given in the text).
* The ground state degeneracy for a fundamental representation source screened by a single light antiquark is \(D_R = 2N_f\) for \(N_f\) mass-degenerate light quark flavors.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration of quark-hadron duality at finite temperature for the quark self-energy via the renormalized Polyakov loop (Megias 2012), and for higher representations (Megias 2013). It provides a hadronic description of the heavy quark-antiquark free-energy that complements lattice calculations (Borsanyi 2015, Bazavov 2016) and enables further study of the entropy shift and its renormalization group properties, as well as the thermodynamics of string breaking in QCD.
