# Spectroscopy of heavy nuclei by configuration mixing of symmetry restored mean-field states: Shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes

> We study shape coexistence and low-energy excitation spectra in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes using configuration mixing of angular-momentum and particle-number projected self-consistent mean-field states. The same Skyrme interaction SLy6 is used everywhere in connection with a density-dependent zero-range pairing force.

## Key claim

Configuration mixing of angular-momentum and particle-number projected self-consistent mean-field states, using the Skyrme interaction SLy6 and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force, can reproduce the low-energy excitation spectra and shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes, but the model significantly overestimates the excitation energy of the oblate band in some nuclei.

## Method

The authors generated a set of HF+BCS wave functions with a constraint on the mass quadrupole deformation, then restored rotational and particle-number symmetry via projection operators. They performed a variational configuration mixing on the collective deformation coordinate using the Generator-Coordinate Method (GCM) to obtain states with good angular momentum, from which quadrupole moments and transition probabilities were computed directly in the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- In $^{186}$Pb, the deformation energy of the prolate minimum from particle-number projection alone fortuitously reproduces the experimental value of 0.650 MeV for the prolate $0^+$ state.
- In $^{186}$Pb, the deformation energy of the oblate minimum from particle-number projection alone is 1.1 MeV, overestimating the experimental value of 0.532 MeV for the oblate $0^+$ state.
- In $^{186}$Pb, two excited $0^+$ states are observed below 700 keV.
- The oblate minimum disappears below $A=184$, while a prolate minimum develops below $A=192$.
- Calculated oblate and prolate bands cross for $^{188}$Pb, at slightly larger neutron number than suggested by experiment.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent mean-field methods (Skyrme interaction SLy6) to low-energy spectroscopy of heavy nuclei by including projection and configuration mixing, building on prior studies of shape coexistence in Pb isotopes (And00, Jul01). The results support the interpretation of the Pb spectra as shape coexistence, but the overestimation of the oblate band's excitation energy points to needed improvements in the effective interaction, pairing interaction, or inclusion of additional collective modes, as suggested by Dug02.
