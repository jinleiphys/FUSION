# Microscopic description of pair transfer between two superfluid systems (II): a quantum mixing of Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Bogolyubov trajectories

> While superfluidity is accurately grasped with a state that explicitly breaks the particle number symmetry, a precise description of phenomena like the particle transfer during heavy-ion reactions can only be achieved by considering systems with good particle numbers. We investigate the possibility to restore particle number in many-body dynamical problems by mixing-up several Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Bogolyubov (TDHFB) trajectories. In our approach, each trajectory is independent from the others and the quantum mixing between trajectories is deduced from a variational principle. The associated theory can be seen as a simplified version of the Multi-Configuration TDHFB (MC-TDHFB) theory. Its accuracy to tackle the problem of symmetry restoration in dynamical problems is illustrated for the case of two superfluid systems that exchange particles during a short time. In Ref. [Phys. Rev. C 97, 034627 (2018)], using a schematic model where two systems initially described by a pairing Hamiltonians are coupled during a short contact time, it was demonstrated that statistical mixing of TDHFB trajectories can only qualitatively describe the transfer process and that a fully quantum treatment is mandatory. We show here that the present MC-TDHFB approach gives an excellent agreement with the exact solution when the two superfluids are the same (symmetric case) or different (asymmetric case) and from weak to strong interaction strength. Finally, we discuss the benefits and bottleneck of this method in view of its application to realistic systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Multi-Configuration Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Bogolyubov (MC-TDHFB) approach, where independent TDHFB trajectories are mixed quantum mechanically via a variational principle, can accurately reproduce the exact quantum solution for particle transfer between two superfluid systems, provided the mixing coefficients are properly constrained to the image of the overlap matrix.

## Method

The authors use a schematic model of two small superfluid systems (A and B), each described by a pairing Hamiltonian with doubly degenerate single-particle levels, coupled by a time-dependent Gaussian interaction that simulates a short contact. They first generate a set of independent TDHFB trajectories starting from initial HFB states rotated by different gauge angles (to break and restore particle number symmetry). The quantum mixing coefficients for these trajectories are then determined by solving a time-dependent Hill-Wheeler-like equation derived from the Dirac-Frenkel variational principle, which includes a projection operator to keep the mixing function in the image of the time-dependent overlap matrix.

## Key numbers

- The coupling interaction has a Gaussian shape with characteristic time τ_c = 0.28 ħ/g, and the evolution is studied in the time range [-1.2, +1.2] ħ/g.
- The contact interaction is active approximately between t ≈ -0.6 ħ/g and t ≈ +0.6 ħ/g.
- The Fomenko scheme is used to discretize the gauge angles, taking values lπ/L for integer l in [1, L].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Regnier, 2018) which showed that a statistical (phase-space) mixing of TDHFB trajectories could only qualitatively describe particle transfer and required artificial rescaling of the coupling. It also addresses difficulties encountered by Scamps et al. (2017) in mixing TDHFB trajectories due to uncontrolled relative phases. The present MC-TDHFB approach provides a fully quantum treatment that agrees with exact solutions for both symmetric and asymmetric superfluid systems, and it enables future application to realistic heavy-ion collisions where particle number restoration is essential.
