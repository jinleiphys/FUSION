# Di-jet asymmetric momentum transported by QGP fluid

> We study the collective flow of the {QGP}-fluid which transports the energy and momentum deposited from jets. Simulations of the propagation of jets together with expansion of the {QGP}-fluid are performed by solving relativistic hydrodynamic equations numerically in the fully (3+1)-dimensional space. Mach cones are induced by the energy-momentum deposition from jets and extended by the expansion of the {QGP}. As a result, a large fraction of the transverse momentum deposited from jets is compensated by low-$p_{T}$ particles at large angles from the jet axis. This result is consistent with the data from the {CMS} Collaboration and gives a novel interpretation of them.

## Key claim

The paper claims that low-\(p_T\) particles at large angles from the jet axis, originating from collective flow of the QGP fluid, compensate a large fraction of the transverse momentum imbalance in asymmetric dijet events, providing a novel interpretation of CMS data. The hedge is that this result depends on the assumption that deposited energy and momentum are immediately equilibrated into the fluid and that the freeze-out occurs at a fixed proper time of \(\tau_f = 9.6\ \mathrm{fm}/c\).

## Method

The authors performed fully (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with source terms representing energy and momentum deposited by traversing jet partons, using an ideal gas equation of state for massless partons. A back-to-back pair of massless partons with initial energy \(p^0_{\mathrm{jet}} = 200\ \mathrm{GeV}\) was propagated through an expanding QGP medium with a smooth initial energy density profile for central Pb-Pb collisions, and the asymmetry ratio \(A_J\) was controlled by varying the pair creation position \(x_0\). Particle momentum distributions were calculated from the hydrodynamic output via the Cooper-Frye formula at a fixed freeze-out proper time.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at origin: \(e(\tau_0, \boldsymbol{x}=0) = 100\ \mathrm{GeV/fm^3}\) at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm}/c\).
- Initial jet parton energy: \(p^0_{\mathrm{jet}} (\tau=0) = 200\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for each parton in the back-to-back pair.
- Freeze-out proper time: \(\tau_f = 9.6\ \mathrm{fm}/c\).
- Jet cone size used for analysis: \(\Delta R = \sqrt{\Delta \phi^2 + \Delta \eta^2} = 0.8\).
- In the out-of-cone region, only particles with \(p_T < 2\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\) contribute positively to the total-\(p_T\) along the jet axis.
- The initial energy density profile in the \(\eta_s\)-direction is flat for \(|\eta_s| < 10\) with a half-Gaussian width \(\sigma_\eta = 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic simulations of jet-medium interactions to explain the transverse momentum balance in asymmetric dijet events observed by the CMS Collaboration (2011). It builds on prior studies of Mach cone formation in QGP by Stoecker (2004) and Casalderrey-Solana (2004), and uses the MC-Glauber model initial conditions from Hirano (2010). The results enable a direct link between experimental dijet asymmetry data and theoretical pictures of medium excitation by jet energy deposition, suggesting that low-\(p_T\) particles at large angles are a signature of collective flow rather than purely jet fragmentation.
