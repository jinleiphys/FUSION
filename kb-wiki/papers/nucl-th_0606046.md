# Locating strongly coupled color superconductivity using universality and experiments with trapped ultracold atoms

> Cold fermionic atoms are known to enter the universal strongly coupled regime as their scattering length $a$ gets large compared to the inter-particle distances. Recent experimental data provide important critical parameters of such system. We argue that quarks may enter the same regime due to marginal binding of diquarks, and if so one can use its universality in order to deduce such properties as the slope of the critical line of color superconductivity, $dT_c/dμ$. We further discuss limitations on the critical temperature itself and conclude that it is limited by $T_c<70\, MeV$.

# Locating strongly coupled color superconductivity using universality and experiments with trapped ultracold atoms

## Key claim

If quarks enter the same universal strongly coupled regime as cold fermionic atoms due to marginal binding of diquarks, then the slope of the critical line of color superconductivity can be deduced as dT_c/dμ ≈ 0.1, and the critical temperature is limited by T_c < 70 MeV, though this cannot be proven at this time.

## Method

The author uses the universality of strongly coupled fermionic systems with large scattering length to relate atomic experimental data to quark matter, assuming that diquarks have a marginal binding line trailing the phase boundary. Experimental critical parameters from trapped ultracold atom experiments (specifically the Duke group's measurements of collective mode damping and specific heat) are used to extract the universal constant α_Tc = T_c/E_F, which is then applied to quark matter by accounting for the different Fermi surface structure and using a quadratic dispersion approximation for quark quasiparticles.

## Key numbers

- α_Tc = T_c/E_F = 0.35 from the lowest break in damping of collective vibrational modes in trapped fermionic atoms (Kinast et al.)
- α_Tc = 0.27 from a change in specific heat in trapped fermionic atoms (Kinast et al.)
- α_2 = T_2/T_F ≈ 0.7–0.8 for the temperature at which pairs themselves melt out
- dT_c/dμ = α_Tc/3 ≈ 0.1 for the slope of the critical line of color superconductivity
- T_c < 70 MeV as the upper limit on the critical temperature of strongly coupled color superconductivity
- T_2 = 150 MeV for the disappearance of pairing (dashed lines)

## Context

This work extends the idea of marginal binding lines for hadronic states in QCD, previously discussed for charmonium (Shuryak and Zahed, around 2004), glueballs, and light quark mesons, to diquark channels and color superconductivity. It builds on three known mechanisms for color superconductivity: electric Coulomb interaction (Bailin and Love, 1984), instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction (Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, Velkovsky; Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek), and magnetic interaction (Son, 1999). The paper enables using atomic experimental data to constrain the QCD phase diagram and suggests that while the color superconducting domain may be unreachable in heavy ion collisions, the region where non-condensed bound diquarks exist (T = 100–150 MeV, μ = 500–600 MeV) might be accessible at low-energy RHIC runs and the future GSI facility FAIR.
