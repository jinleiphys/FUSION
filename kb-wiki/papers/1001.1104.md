# Weak nonmesonic decay spectra of hypernuclei

> We compute one- and two-nucleon kinetic-energy spectra and opening-angle distributions for the nonmesonic weak decay of several hypernuclei, and compare our results with some recent data. The decaymics is described by transition potentials of the one-meson-exchange type, and the nuclear structure aspects by two versions of the independent-particle shell model (IPSM). In version IPSM-a, the hole states are treated as stationary, while in version IPSM-b the deep hole states are considered to be quasi-stationary and are described by Breit-Wigner distributions.

## Key claim
The independent-particle shell model (IPSM) reproduces well the BNL experiment for $^4_\Lambda$He but does not reproduce well the FINUDA experiment for the one-nucleon kinetic-energy spectra in $^5_\Lambda$He, $^7_\Lambda$Li, and $^{12}_\Lambda$C, indicating that final state interactions and the two-nucleon-induced decay mode must be appraised relative to this lowest-order picture.

## Method
The authors compute one- and two-nucleon kinetic-energy spectra and opening-angle distributions for the nonmesonic weak decay of hypernuclei using Fermi's golden rule, with transition potentials of the one-meson-exchange type (including $\pi$, $\eta$, $K$, $\rho$, $\omega$, $K^*$ mesons) and two versions of the independent-particle shell model. In IPSM-a, hole states are treated as stationary, while in IPSM-b deep-hole states are described as quasi-stationary with Breit-Wigner distributions. The initial hypernuclear state is taken as a $\Lambda$ in the $1s_{1/2}$ single-particle state weakly coupled to an $(A-1)$ nuclear core.

## Key numbers
- The $Q$-value for the mesonic mode $\Lambda \to N\pi$ is approximately 37 MeV.
- The $Q$-value for the nonmesonic weak decay $\Lambda N \to nN$ is approximately 120 to 135 MeV.
- The mass difference $\Delta M = M_\Lambda - M_N = 176$ MeV.
- For $^{12}_\Lambda$C, the theoretical proton kinetic-energy spectrum is peaked around 85 MeV and reproduces data well for energies larger than 50 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of nonmesonic weak decay spectra from earlier studies by the same group (Bar08, Bau09) and compares with recent experimental data from KEK, FINUDA, and BNL. The IPSM is presented as the appropriate lowest-order approximation, against which future work should appraise the effects of final state interactions and the two-nucleon-induced decay mode. The paper specifically compares with the BNL experiment for $^4_\Lambda$He (Par07) and the FINUDA experiment for $^5_\Lambda$He, $^7_\Lambda$Li, and $^{12}_\Lambda$C (Agn08).
