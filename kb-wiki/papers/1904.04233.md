# Constraining the neutron-matter equation of state with gravitational waves

> We show how observations of gravitational waves from binary neutron star (BNS) mergers over the next few years can be combined with insights from nuclear physics to obtain useful constraints on the equation of state (EoS) of dense matter, in particular, constraining the neutron-matter EoS to within 20% between one and two times the nuclear saturation density $n_0\approx 0.16\ {\text{fm}^{-3}}$. Using Fisher information methods, we combine observational constraints from simulated BNS merger events drawn from various population models with independent measurements of the neutron star radii expected from x-ray astronomy (the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) observations in particular) to directly constrain nuclear physics parameters. To parameterize the nuclear EoS, we use a different approach, expanding from pure nuclear matter rather than from symmetric nuclear matter to make use of recent quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations. This method eschews the need to invoke the so-called parabolic approximation to extrapolate from symmetric nuclear matter, allowing us to directly constrain the neutron-matter EoS. Using a principal component analysis, we identify the combination of parameters most tightly constrained by observational data. We discuss sensitivity to various effects such as different component masses through population-model sensitivity, phase transitions in the core EoS, and large deviations from the central parameter values.

## Key claim

The authors show that combining gravitational wave observations from binary neutron star mergers with nuclear theory and x-ray astronomy (specifically NICER) can constrain the neutron-matter equation of state to within 20% between one and two times nuclear saturation density, but this result depends on Gaussian error assumptions and linear error propagation, with the authors noting that a proper analysis requires a more expensive Bayesian approach.

## Method

The authors use Fisher information methods to combine simulated gravitational wave observations from binary neutron star mergers drawn from various population models with independent neutron star radius measurements expected from NICER. They parameterize the nuclear equation of state by expanding from pure neutron matter rather than symmetric nuclear matter, using recent quantum Monte Carlo calculations, and employ a compressible liquid drop model for the inner crust with a speed-of-sound parameterization for the inner core. A principal component analysis identifies the combination of parameters most tightly constrained by observational data.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} (corresponding to mass density rho0 = 2.7e14 g/cm^3)
- Neutron-matter equation of state constrained to within 20% between one and two times nuclear saturation density
- NICER projected measurement of J0437: M = 1.44 +/- 0.07 solar masses with 10% measurement of R
- After about Nobs = 15 gravitational wave observations, limited improvement from additional Nobs = 100 observations
- Gravitational wave observations alone could constrain the slope L of the symmetry energy at approximately 15% level corresponding to Delta L approximately 10 MeV
- Inner crust extends from n = n_drip approximately 2e-3 n0 to n = n_core approximately 2 n0 with radial thickness approximately 2 km
- Outer core extends from n approximately 0.5 n0 to n = n_c approximately 2 n0
- Pure neutron matter equation of state calculations feasible up to nucleon number density n_c approximately 2 n0

## Context

This work extends earlier constraints from GW170817 (De 2018, Tews 2018, Abbott 2018) by incorporating insights from nuclear physics through a new parameterization that avoids the parabolic approximation. The approach uses recent quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Gandolfi 2009, 2010, 2012, 2014) and chiral effective field theory results (Wlazlowski 2014, Gandolfi 2014, Lynn 2015) to directly constrain neutron-matter properties. The analysis enables future gravitational wave observations to provide constraints on the neutron-matter equation of state comparable to or better than upcoming neutron skin experiments (Horowitz 2012, 2014) and combined constraints from ab initio calculations and astrophysical observations (Hebeler 2010, 2013).
