# Jet quenching in the hadron gas: an exploratory study

> The suppression of high momentum particles in heavy-ion collisions in comparison to elementary reactions is one of the main indications for the formation of a quark-gluon plasma. In recent studies, full jets are being reconstructed and substructure observables are gaining importance in assessing the medium modifications of hard probes. In this work, the effect of the late stage hadronic interactions are explored within the hadronic transport approach SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons). High momentum particles are incorporated in a radially expanding hadron gas to analyse the corresponding angular distributions, also refered to as `jet shape' observables. We find that the full hadron gas can be approximated with a pion gas with constant elastic cross-sections of 100 mb. In addition, the temperature and probe energy dependence of diffusion coefficients $\tilde{q}$ and $\tilde{e}$ quantifying the transverse and parallel momentum transfers are extracted. The species dependence and the importance of different interaction types are investigated. Parametrizations are presented that can be employed in future jet quenching calculations to include the effect of the hadronic phase.

## Key claim

The hadronic phase after the quark-gluon plasma can produce significant jet quenching effects that should not be neglected, with the transverse momentum transfer coefficient for a 10 GeV pion in a 150 MeV hadron gas reaching about 0.38 GeV^2/fm, roughly 3 to 4 times smaller than typical QGP values but still sizeable enough to affect jet substructure observables.

## Method

The authors used the hadronic transport approach SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) to simulate high-momentum particles traversing a thermally initialized hadron gas. They studied angular energy distributions ("jet shapes") by placing a high-pT particle in an expanding sphere of hadronic matter at T=150 MeV and measuring the momentum distribution after freeze-out. They also extracted diffusion coefficients by analyzing the first collision of high-pT particles with an infinite hadronic medium in a box with periodic boundary conditions, computing the average transverse and longitudinal momentum transfer per mean free path.

## Key numbers

- For a 10 GeV pion in a hadron gas at T=150 MeV, the transverse diffusion coefficient is approximately 0.38 GeV^2/fm, compared to QGP values of 1.2 ± 0.3 GeV^2/fm at T=370 MeV and 1.9 ± 0.7 GeV^2/fm at T=470 MeV.
- The parametrization for the transverse diffusion coefficient of a pion is: q̃_π(pT, T) = 5.14 × 10^{-18} GeV^2/fm × (pT/GeV)^{0.87} × (T/MeV)^{7.35}, valid for temperatures between 100 and 175 MeV and momenta between 1 and 120 GeV.
- The parametrization for the longitudinal diffusion coefficient of a pion is: ẽ_π(pT, T) = 9.31 × 10^{-19} GeV^2/fm × (pT/GeV)^{0.17} × (T/MeV)^{7.59}, valid for the same ranges.
- A full hadron gas at T=150 MeV can be approximated by a pion gas with constant elastic cross-sections of 100 mb for jet shape calculations.
- At T=150 MeV, a hard-sphere model with 100 mb cross-sections fills approximately 33% of the volume, while 30 mb cross-sections fill about 5% of the volume.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadronic effects on jet quenching, such as the purely hadronic approach by Cassing (2003) that achieved qualitative description of the nuclear modification factor at RHIC, and the Angantyr+UrQMD studies by Bierlich (2018) showing significant suppression effects at LHC energies. The paper provides the first systematic quantification of hadronic phase effects on jet shapes and diffusion coefficients, enabling their inclusion in full jet quenching frameworks like JETSCAPE. The results suggest that future jet quenching phenomenology should incorporate hadronic afterburner effects, particularly for jet substructure observables, to avoid drawing premature conclusions about QGP properties.
