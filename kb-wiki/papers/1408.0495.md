# Baseline for the cumulants of net-proton distributions at STAR

> We present a systematic comparison between the recently measured cumulants of the net-proton distributions by STAR for 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=7.7-200 GeV and two kinds of possible baseline measures, the Poisson and Binomial baselines. These baseline measures are assuming that the proton and anti-proton distributions independently follow Poisson statistics or Binomial statistics. The higher order cumulant net-proton data are observed to deviate from all the baseline measures studied at 19.6 and 27 GeV. We also compare the net-proton with net-baryon fluctuations in UrQMD and AMPT model, and convert the net-proton fluctuations to net-baryon fluctuations in AMPT model by using a set of formula.

## Key claim

The measured higher-order cumulants of net-proton distributions in 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at STAR deviate from both Poisson and Binomial baselines at 19.6 and 27 GeV, but the paper does not claim that this deviation is a definitive signature of the QCD critical point; rather, it presents these baselines as a necessary step to isolate non-critical physics effects before any such claim can be made.

## Method

The authors compared the published STAR data for cumulants (C1 through C4) of net-proton distributions in 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV against two analytic baselines: a Poisson baseline (Skellam distribution) using only the measured mean numbers of protons and anti-protons, and a Binomial/Negative Binomial baseline using both the measured mean and variance of the proton and anti-proton distributions. They also compared net-proton fluctuations to net-baryon fluctuations in the UrQMD and AMPT transport models, and applied formulas from Asakawa and Kitazawa to convert net-proton cumulants to net-baryon cumulants in the AMPT model.

## Key numbers

* The mean values of net-proton, proton, and anti-proton distributions increase almost linearly with the average number of participant nucleons (<N_part>) for each beam energy from sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV.
* The Poisson baseline predicts kappa*sigma^2 = 1 for net-proton distributions; the data deviate most strongly from this at 19.6 and 27 GeV for the fourth-order cumulant C4.
* The Binomial baseline, which uses the measured variance as an additional input, describes the data up to third order but fails for the fourth-order net-proton cumulant C4 at 19.6 and 27 GeV.
* In AMPT and UrQMD models, the kappa*sigma^2 values of net-baryon distributions are systematically lower than those of net-proton distributions, with larger differences at lower energies than at higher energies.

## Context

This work extends the STAR Collaboration's published measurements of net-proton cumulants (STAR, 2014) by constructing explicit Poisson and Binomial baselines to quantify deviations that could be related to the QCD critical point. It builds on theoretical expectations that higher moments of conserved quantities are sensitive to the correlation length near a critical point (Stephanov, 2009; Asakawa and Kitazawa, 2012) and on Lattice QCD calculations of susceptibilities. The conversion formulas from net-proton to net-baryon fluctuations follow the work of Asakawa and Kitazawa (2012). The results enable future comparisons to QCD-based calculations to determine whether the observed deviations at 19.6 and 27 GeV arise from critical phenomena or from non-critical physics such as resonance decays and hadronic scattering.
