# Ground states and excited states of hypernuclei in Relativistic Mean Field approach

> Hypernuclei have been studied within the framework of Relativistic Mean Field theory. The force FSU Gold has been extended to include hyperons. The effective hyperon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions have been obtained by fitting experimental energies in a number of hypernuclei over a wide range of mass. Calculations successfully describe various features including hyperon separation energy and single particle spectra of single-Λhypernuclei throughout the periodic table. We also extend this formalism to double-Λhypernuclei.

## Key claim

The authors extend the FSU Gold Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) Lagrangian to include hyperons, and by fitting only the scalar meson-hyperon coupling constant (while fixing the vector coupling from the naive quark model) they achieve a root-mean-square deviation of 0.64 MeV for single-Λ separation energies across hypernuclei from A=16 to A=208, contradicting earlier claims that RMF systematically under-predicts binding in heavy hypernuclei.

## Method

The FSU Gold RMF Lagrangian density was augmented with hyperon terms including couplings to σ, ω, ρ, σ*, and φ mesons. For single-Λ hypernuclei, the ω-Λ coupling was fixed at 2/3 of the ω-nucleon coupling from the naive quark model, while the σ-Λ coupling was varied to minimize the root-mean-square deviation of experimental Λ separation energies for 12 hypernuclei with A ≥ 16. For double-Λ hypernuclei, the φ-Λ coupling was taken from the quark model and the σ*-Λ coupling was adjusted to reproduce the ΔB_ΛΛ value for ⁶_ΛΛHe. Spherical symmetry and BCS pairing (strength 300 MeV·fm) were assumed.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation for single-Λ separation energies: 0.64 MeV for FSU Gold, compared to 0.72 MeV for NLSH
- Λ potential depth in nuclear matter from the fitted parameters: -28.7 MeV (compared to the canonical -30.0 MeV)
- FSU Gold binding energy per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter: 16.30 MeV
- For ²⁰⁸_ΛPb, the calculated Λ separation energy is 25.12 MeV (experimental: 26.30 ± 0.86 MeV)
- For ⁶_ΛΛHe, calculated B_ΛΛ = 3.98 MeV and ΔB_ΛΛ = 0.62 MeV (experimental ΔB_ΛΛ = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV from the NAGARA event reanalysis)
- For ¹⁰_ΛΛBe, calculated B_ΛΛ = 14.03 MeV (experimental: 17.7 ± 0.7 MeV) and ΔB_ΛΛ = 0.27 MeV (experimental: 4.3 ± 0.4 MeV)
- For ¹³_ΛΛB, calculated B_ΛΛ = 21.17 MeV (experimental: 27.5 ± 0.7 MeV) and ΔB_ΛΛ = 0.19 MeV (experimental: 4.8 ± 0.7 MeV)
- Excitation energy of the p₃/₂ Λ state in ⁸⁹_ΛY: calculated 6.10 MeV (experimental 6.01 MeV)
- Excitation energy of the d₅/₂ Λ state in ⁸⁹_ΛY: calculated 12.81 MeV (experimental 12.79 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the FSU Gold RMF force, previously successful for normal nuclei and collective modes (Todd-Rutel, 2005), into the hypernuclear sector. It improves upon earlier RMF calculations using NLSH (Shen, 2006) and contradicts the conclusion of Guleria (2011) that RMF under-predicts binding in hypernuclei above A=87 compared to Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations. The fitted parameters enable predictions for single-Λ excited states and double-Λ hypernuclei, and the results for ΔB_ΛΛ in light double-Λ systems support the weaker ΛΛ interaction indicated by the NAGARA event (KEK, 2001) over older emulsion data suggesting ΔB_ΛΛ ≈ 4-5 MeV.
