# Equation of State at High Baryon Densities from a Thermodynamically Informed Neural Network

> We present a four-dimensional equation of state for strongly interacting matter at finite temperature and conserved charge densities, constructed using a deep neural network. It is designed for direct use in hybrid models of relativistic heavy-ion collisions: it reproduces hadron resonance gas thermodynamics at typical particlization scales, is consistent with lattice QCD at low baryon chemical potential, and extrapolates into the high-density region inaccessible to either approach, which is precisely the regime targeted by RHIC BES, FAIR, HADES, and CBM. Thermodynamic consistency throughout the full phase space is enforced via a physics-informed loss function. We demonstrate the developed equation of state by implementing it at zero net strangeness and fixed electric-to-baryon charge ratio within the integrated hydrokinetic model.

## Key claim

The paper presents a four-dimensional equation of state for strongly interacting matter constructed using a deep neural network that reproduces hadron resonance gas thermodynamics at particlization scales, is consistent with lattice QCD at low baryon chemical potential, and extrapolates into the high-density region up to mu_B approximately 1 GeV, but the construction explicitly does not include a phase transition or critical endpoint and is intended as a proof-of-concept for hybrid-model applications.

## Method

The neural network takes temperature and the three conserved-charge chemical potentials (mu_B, mu_Q, mu_S) as inputs and outputs the pressure, with all other thermodynamic quantities obtained through automatic differentiation. Training data combines lattice QCD results via a Taylor expansion truncated at fourth order in chemical potentials over the temperature range 135 to 220 MeV with mu_B/T less than or equal to 3, and hadron resonance gas model calculations sampled over the domain T from 0.01 to 0.55 GeV and mu_B from -0.1 to 1.1 GeV. Thermodynamic consistency across the full domain is enforced through a physics-informed loss function that penalizes violations of stability conditions at collocation points, using a residual network architecture with 16 residual blocks of 64 neurons each and tanh activations.

## Key numbers

* Training dataset contains approximately 2.0 x 10^5 points total, with 1.2 x 10^5 from HRG and 0.8 x 10^5 from lattice QCD
* Lattice QCD input covers temperature range 135 MeV less than T less than 220 MeV on 48^3 x 12 lattices, with Taylor expansion reliable for mu_B/T less than or equal to 2 to 2.5
* The EoS domain extends to mu_B up to 1.0 GeV, T from 0.02 to 0.5 GeV, mu_Q from -0.04 to 0.04 GeV, and mu_S from -0.1 to 0.4 GeV
* Maximum relative error between neural-network EoS and HRG model in the energy density is 3.0 percent, with mean absolute error of 0.5 percent over the region 0.1 to 0.5 GeV/fm^3 in energy density and 0 to 0.4 fm^{-3} in baryon density
* The squared speed of sound reaches values as high as c_s^2 approximately 0.5 in the high-density extrapolation region, exceeding the conformal limit of 1/3

## Context

This work extends non-parametric approaches to the QCD equation of state, building on interpolation schemes using hyperbolic tangent functions and cubic splines by Huovinen (2009) and Monnai (2019), as well as Gaussian process regression by Gong (2024). The construction is designed for direct use in hybrid models of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at beam energies relevant to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, HADES, and FAIR/CBM, where baryon chemical potentials at chemical freeze-out can reach approximately 800 MeV as discussed by Cleymans (2005) and Andronic (2017). The approach enables future work incorporating a phase transition or critical endpoint, following the methodology of Kapusta (2021) and Parotto (2018), and can serve as a smooth background EoS for such studies.
