# Production of secondaries in soft p+pb collisions at LHC

> We calculate the inclusive spectra of secondaries produced in soft (minimum bias) p+Pb collisions in the framework of Quark-Gluon String Model at LHC energy, and by taking into account the inelastic screening corrections (percolation effects). The role of these effects is expected to be very large at very high energies, and they should decrease the spectra about 3 times in the midrapidity region and increase them about 2 times in the fragmentation region at the energy of LHC.

## Key claim

The Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) with inelastic screening (percolation) corrections predicts that at LHC energies the inclusive spectra of secondaries in soft p+Pb collisions are decreased by about 1.7 times in the midrapidity region and increased by about 1.5 times in the fragmentation region, relative to calculations without these corrections, with the model normalized to a single ALICE data point.

## Method

The authors used the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM), where high-energy hadron-nucleus interactions proceed via Pomeron exchange, with each cut Pomeron producing two showers of secondaries. The inclusive spectra are calculated from the convolution of diquark, valence quark, and sea quark distributions with fragmentation functions, using the Gribov-Glauber Multiple Scattering Theory to treat interactions with multiple target nucleons. Inelastic screening (percolation) effects are incorporated by introducing a maximum number of Pomerons \(n_{max}\) that can be emitted by one nucleon, above which additional Pomerons fuse and do not contribute to the final state; the value \(n_{max} = 13\) is used at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) TeV, normalized to ALICE data.

## Key numbers

*   The average number of inelastic interactions of a proton with lead nucleons is \(\langle \nu \rangle_{pPb} \approx 7.9\), using \(\sigma^{pp}_{inel} = 72\) mb and \(\sigma^{pPb}_{prod} = 1900\) mb at \(\sqrt{s} = 5\) TeV.
*   The ALICE experimental point used for normalization is \(dn_{ch}/d\eta = 16.81 \pm 0.71\) measured in the window \(|\eta_{lab}| \le 2\).
*   The inclusive density \(dn_{ch}/dy\) in the midrapidity region differs by about 1.7 times between calculations with and without percolation effects at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) TeV.
*   The nuclear modification factor \(R_{ch}(x_F)\) in the fragmentation region differs by about 1.5 times at \(x_F \geq 1/2\) between calculations with and without percolation effects.
*   The average number of Pomerons in a minimum bias p+Pb collision at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) TeV is about 24, which is of the same order as \(n_{max} = 25\).

## Context

This work extends the application of the Quark-Gluon String Model with inelastic screening corrections, previously used to describe the saturation effects seen in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Phobos, Phenix collaborations), to p+Pb collisions at LHC energies. The calculations are normalized to a single ALICE data point and provide predictions for the rapidity and \(x_F\) distributions of charged hadrons, protons, \(\Lambda\), and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) secondaries. The results enable a test of the percolation approach and its connection to the Colour Glass Condensate and other models (e.g., EPOS), and are in reasonable numerical agreement with the work of DKLN.
