# Electric Dipole Moments of Neutron-Odd Nuclei

> The electric dipole moments (EDMs) of neutron-odd nuclei with even protons are systematically evaluated. We first derive the relation between the EDM and the magnetic moment operators by making use of the core polarization scheme. This relation enables us to calculate the EDM of neutron-odd nuclei without any free parameters. From this calculation, one may find the best atomic system suitable for future EDM experiments.

# Electric Dipole Moments of Neutron-Odd Nuclei

## Key claim
The nuclear EDM of neutron-odd nuclei with even protons can be calculated parameter-free from the relation between EDM and magnetic moment operators via the core polarization scheme, but the extraction of the neutron EDM from neutral atomic systems is impossible due to electron shielding; only in ionic systems (with two electrons stripped off) can the nuclear EDM be directly related to the neutron EDM with a reduction factor of 2/Z.

## Method
The authors derive a relation between the nuclear EDM operator and the magnetic moment operator using the core polarization scheme, where the effective g-factor δg_s is treated as a free parameter α fitted to reproduce observed nuclear magnetic moments. For each neutron-odd nucleus with even protons, they compute the nuclear EDM d_A in the A^{++} ionic state (two electrons stripped) using equation (13), which expresses d_A as a linear combination of the neutron EDM d_n and proton EDM d_p, with coefficients determined by the spin expectation value ⟨s_z⟩ and the core polarization parameters α_p and α_n.

## Key numbers
- Neutron EDM experimental upper limits: d_n ≈ (0.3 ± 0.5) × 10⁻²⁵ e·cm (Smith et al.) and d_n ≈ (1.9 ± 5.4) × 10⁻²⁶ e·cm (Harris et al.)
- ¹²⁹Xe EDM upper limit: d_Xe ≈ (0.7 ± 3.3 ± 0.1) × 10⁻²⁷ e·cm
- Hg atomic EDM upper limit: d_Hg ≈ (0.49 ± 1.29 ± 0.76) × 10⁻²⁹ e·cm
- For ⁹Be (Z=4, J^P=3/2⁻): d_A = 0.23 d_n + 0.063 d_p
- For ¹⁷O (Z=8, J^P=5/2⁺): d_A = 0.125 d_n (no proton EDM contribution)
- For ¹²⁹Xe (Z=54, J^P=1/2⁺): d_A = 0.015 d_n + 0.0065 d_p
- For ²⁰¹Hg (Z=80, J^P=3/2⁻): d_A = 0.0097 d_n + 0.0051 d_p
- Suppression factor for nuclear EDM in neutral atomic systems: approximately 2.4 × 10⁻⁶ d_n

## Context
This work extends the core polarization approach of Arima and Noya for magnetic moments to EDM calculations, and addresses the Schiff theorem problem identified by earlier work (Oshima, Itoi) showing that neutral atomic EDMs are shielded by electrons. The authors argue that ionic systems avoid this shielding, enabling direct access to nucleon EDMs, and they provide a systematic table of candidate nuclei for future storage-ring EDM experiments as proposed by Orlov et al. The calculation is parameter-free once α is fixed from magnetic moments, with estimated reliability at the 20% level.
