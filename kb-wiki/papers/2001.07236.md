# Skyrme RPA for nuclear resonances: trouble with magnetic modes

> We discuss major differences between electric and magnetic excitations in nuclei appearing in self-consistent calculation based on Skyrme energy-density functionals. Tools of analysis are Landau-Migdal parameters for bulk properties and RPA for resonance modes of $^{208}$Pb as representative of finite nuclei. We show that the relation between the effective mass and the effective particle-hole interaction, well known in the Landau-Migdal theory, explains the success of self-consistent calculations of electric transitions in such approaches. This effect, however, does not automatically exist in the magnetic case. This calls for further developments of the Skyrme functional in the spin channel.

# Skyrme RPA for nuclear resonances: trouble with magnetic modes

## Key claim

Self-consistent Skyrme energy-density functional calculations successfully describe electric (natural parity) giant resonances because the effective mass and the effective particle-hole interaction are correlated through a "backflow" effect analogous to that in Landau-Migdal theory; however, this beneficial correlation does not automatically hold for magnetic (spin) modes, and the spin-dependent Landau-Migdal parameters derived from the Skyrme force assumption show a trend with effective mass that is counter-productive for compensating changes in single-particle level density.

## Method

The authors analyze the spin-independent and spin-dependent Landau-Migdal (LM) parameters for a collection of Skyrme parametrizations with systematically varied nuclear matter properties (effective mass, incompressibility, symmetry energy, Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule enhancement). They compute the LM parameters from the Skyrme energy-density functional and compare the trends with effective mass to the analytical relations derived from nuclear matter properties. They then perform random phase approximation (RPA) and time blocking approximation (TBA) calculations for electric giant resonances (isoscalar monopole, isoscalar quadrupole, isovector dipole) and magnetic 1+ states in 208Pb, comparing results across parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- The normalization factor C0 for bare-mass normalization is approximately 150 MeV fm^3, while the effective-mass normalization factor C0* is approximately 150 MeV fm^3 times m/m*.
- For the SV-bas parametrization (m*/m = 0.90): f0 = -0.05, f'0 = 1.34, f1 = -0.33; for SV-mas07 (m*/m = 0.70): f0 = -0.37, f'0 = 1.01, f1 = -1.29; for SV-m56k6 (m*/m = 0.56): f0 = -0.63, f'0 = 0.43, f1 = -2.39.
- The spin LM parameters from the force-based option (no tensor terms) give g0 = -(3+3kappa_TRK) - (3a_sym - 2B/A)/T_F + (26/5)(m/m*) and g'0 = (B/A)(1/T_F) + (3/5)(m/m*), where the mass dependence enters with a plus sign (opposite to the f-parameter trend).
- Adjusted spin parameters from Tselyaev (2019) that reproduce M1 modes in 208Pb yield g0 close to zero for parametrizations with m*/m approximately 1, with a slight tendency to decrease with decreasing m*/m.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Skyrme energy-density functionals for nuclear excitations, building on the Landau-Migdal theory of Finite Fermi Systems (Migdal, 1967) and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach (Skyrme, 1959; Bender, 2003). The authors use the framework of nuclear matter properties (incompressibility, effective mass, symmetry energy) established in earlier work (Kluepfel, 2009; Lyutorovich, 2012). The paper directly addresses the insufficiency of conventional Skyrme functionals for magnetic excitations noted in recent studies (Vesely, 2009; Nesterenko, 2010; Tselyaev, 2019), where spin-relevant parameters had to be substantially readjusted to reproduce experimental data. The analysis enables future development of Skyrme functionals with improved spin-channel behavior, recommending the "free" energy-density functional approach (option iii) where spin terms are adjusted independently from natural-parity terms, rather than the force-based assumption that ties spin properties to kinetic properties.
