# Two pion photoproduction on nucleons and nuclei in the rho and sigma regions

> In this talk we report on two physical processes, the photoproduction of $ρ$ mesons in nuclei and the $π^0 π^0$ photoproduction in nuclei at low energies. In the first case the aim is to observe experimentally the theoretically predicted changes on the $ρ$ properties in the nuclear medium. In the second case one wishes to investigate the modifications of the $ππ$ interaction in the nuclear medium in the region of the $σ$ meson. In the first case we observe that it is quite difficult to see large effects due to the $ρ$ modification in the medium because the detection of the $ρ$ is done through the two pions which are produced in the $Δ$ region and are largely distorted in the medium. In the second case, when the I=0 part of the $ππ$ interaction is substituted by the in medium $ππ$ amplitude, we observe a very large shift of strength in the invariant mass distribution to small values of the mass in $^{12}C$ and $^{208}Pb$ with respect to the distribution of the elementary reaction. This spectacular shift appears to be corroborated by recent experiments at Mainz reported in this same Workshop.

# Two pion photoproduction on nucleons and nuclei in the rho and sigma regions

## Key claim

The paper argues that observing medium modifications of the rho meson via rho photoproduction in nuclei is quite difficult because the pions from rho decay are produced in the Delta resonance region and are largely distorted in the medium, while for the sigma region the substitution of the I=0 part of the pi pi interaction by the in-medium pi pi amplitude produces a very large shift of strength in the invariant mass distribution to small values of the mass in 12C and 208Pb, which appears to be corroborated by recent experiments at Mainz.

## Method

The authors used an updated model for gamma N to pi pi N containing tree level diagrams with couplings to nucleons and resonances (Delta(1232), N*(1440), N*(1520), Delta(1700)) plus rho meson production from N*(1520) and Delta(1700) decays and a gamma N N rho contact term. For nuclear calculations, they evaluated the photon selfenergy in nuclear matter using the local density approximation and incorporated pion distortion via eikonal approximation with quasielastic cross sections from experiment and absorption coefficients from theoretical studies of the Delta selfenergy. For the sigma region, they implemented final state interaction in the I=0 channel by summing a diagrammatic series using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the lowest order chiral Lagrangian as kernel.

## Key numbers

- The rho width in the medium was modeled as Gamma_rho = Gamma_rho_free * (1 + rho/rho_0), corresponding to doubling the rho width at normal nuclear matter density
- Even with this doubled width, the changes in the peak of the cross section were only about 20 percent decrease with a corresponding increase of the width by about the same amount
- For the sigma meson generated dynamically via the Bethe-Salpeter approach, the pole was found at mass m_sigma = 470 MeV with width Gamma_sigma = 400 MeV, consistent with experimental analysis giving m_sigma = 478 (+24/-23) +/- 17 MeV and Gamma_sigma = 324 (+42/-40) +/- 21
- The photon energy used for rho photoproduction studies was E_gamma = 1250 MeV
- The nuclear targets studied were 40Ca for rho photoproduction and 12C and 208Pb for the sigma region
- Even hypothetical mass changes of the rho as large as 150 MeV at normal nuclear matter density produced only very moderate effects

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UChiPT) developed by Oller, Oset, and collaborators (1997-2001) to study meson modification in nuclear media. It builds on earlier pioneering work by Schuck (1988) on pi pi scattering amplitude modification in the medium and subsequent calculations by Chanfray (1991), Mull (1992), and Aouissat (1993). The paper contrasts with the findings of Rapp (1999) regarding pion-induced two pion production, noting that strong pion absorption leads to smaller effective densities than assumed in that work. The results enable future experimental tests at Mainz and other facilities to observe medium modifications of the sigma meson through photon-induced two pion production, while cautioning that rho photoproduction is not a promising channel for observing rho medium effects due to the strong distortion of pions in the Delta resonance region.
