# Observable effects of symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions at RIA energies

> Within an isospin-dependent transport model for nuclear reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei, we perform a comparative study of $^{100}Sn+^{124}Sn$ and $^{100}Zn+^{124}Sn$ reactions at beam energies of 30 MeV/A and 400 MeV/A to identify optimal experimental conditions and observables for investigating the equation of state (EOS) of neutron-rich matter at RIA. Several observables known to be sensitive to the density-dependence of the symmetry energy are examined as a function of impact parameter for both reaction systems. In particular, the strength of isospin transport/diffusion is studied by using rapidity distributions of free nucleons and their isospin asymmetries. An approximate isospin equilibrium is established even for peripheral collisions with a very soft symmetry energy at 30 MeV/A. At 400 MeV/A, however, a strong isospin translucency is observed even in most central collisions with either soft or stiff symmetry energies. Origins of these observations and their implications to determining the EOS of neutron-rich matter at the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) are discussed.

# Observable effects of symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions at RIA energies

## Key claim

Within an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, the study shows that the density-dependence of the symmetry energy can be probed through different observables depending on beam energy, but the sensitivity reverses between subnormal and supranormal densities depending on whether the symmetry energy is soft (gamma=0.5) or stiff (gamma=2). The abstract's claim of "approximate isospin equilibrium" at 30 MeV/A is qualified: it occurs only with the soft symmetry energy (gamma=0.5), not with the stiff one (gamma=2).

## Method

The study uses an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate reactions of 100Sn (N/Z=1) and 100Zn (N/Z=2.3) projectiles on a 124Sn (N/Z=1.48) target at beam energies of 30 MeV/A and 400 MeV/A across impact parameters from 1 to 7 fm. The symmetry energy is parameterized as E_sym(rho) = E_sym(rho_0) * u^gamma with gamma varied between 0.5 (soft) and 2 (stiff), using a fixed E_sym(rho_0)=32 MeV and a stiff isoscalar EOS with K_0=380 MeV. Free nucleons are defined as those with local densities below rho_0/8 at freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 30 MeV/A and 400 MeV/A
- Symmetry energy at normal density: E_sym(rho_0)=32 MeV, with gamma parameter varied between 0.5 and 2
- Isoscalar incompressibility used: K_0=380 MeV
- Impact parameters examined: 1, 5, and 7 fm
- At 30 MeV/A with gamma=0.5, the n/p ratio at mid-rapidity differs by more than a factor of 2 compared to gamma=2 at high transverse momenta
- At 400 MeV/A, the pi-/pi+ ratio is about 25% higher with gamma=0.5 than with gamma=2 in central collisions
- The density range probed at 400 MeV/A extends up to about 3 times normal nuclear matter density (3 rho_0), while at 30 MeV/A only densities up to about 1.2 rho_0 contribute

## Context

This work extends prior transport model studies of symmetry energy effects by Li (1997, 2000, 2002) and others (Frauendorf 1998, Xu 2000, Tan 2001, Baran 2002, Tsang 2003) to the specific beam energies and reaction systems planned for the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA). The parameterization of the symmetry energy follows Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen (2000) based on variational many-body calculations by Akmal (1997). The study enables experimental planning at RIA by identifying that the n/p ratio at mid-rapidity with high transverse momenta is most sensitive to the symmetry energy at subnormal densities at Fermi energies, while neutron-proton differential flow and the pi-/pi+ ratio are more sensitive to supranormal densities at higher beam energies.
