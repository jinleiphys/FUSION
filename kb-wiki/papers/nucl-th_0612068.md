# How does transverse (hydrodynamic) flow affect jet-broadening and jet-quenching ?

> We give the modification of formulas for $p_{\perp}$-broadening and energy loss which are necessary to calculate parton interactions in a medium with flow. Arguments are presented leading to the conclusion that for large $p_{\perp}$-spectra observed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, the influence of transverse flow on the determination of the "quenching power" of the produced medium is small. This leaves open the question of the interpretation of data in a consistent perturbative framework.

## Key claim

The authors argue that, for large transverse momentum spectra observed at RHIC, the influence of transverse (radial) hydrodynamic flow on the determination of the medium's "quenching power" (the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$) is small, and therefore transverse flow does not resolve the discrepancy between perturbative QCD estimates and the large values of $\hat{q}$ extracted from data.

## Method

The authors derive a Lorentz-boost transformation for the local transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ in a moving medium, obtaining $\hat{q} = \hat{q}_0 \, \gamma(v) (1 + v \cos\theta)$, where $\hat{q}_0$ is the value in the medium rest frame, $v$ is the transverse flow velocity, and $\theta$ is the angle between the parton direction and the flow. They then compute the ratio $R_{flow}$ of the average transverse momentum broadening $\langle \Delta p_\perp^2 \rangle$ with and without transverse flow, using an ideal hydrodynamic model with Bjorken longitudinal expansion and a radial flow that starts from zero initial velocity, integrated over a cylindrical medium of radius $R_A = 6.4$ fm with initial time $\tau_0 = 0.5$ fm.

## Key numbers

*   The ratio $R_{flow} = (\Delta p_\perp^2)_{Bj+flow} / (\Delta p_\perp^2)_{Bj}$ is smaller than 1, with the effect of transverse flow being of order $\le 10\%$.
*   For the characteristic gluon energy $\omega_c$, the ratio $R_{\omega_c} = (\omega_c)_{Bj+flow} / (\omega_c)_{Bj}$ is approximately $0.85$ for $\tau_0 = 0.5$ fm and initial temperature $T_0 = 400$ MeV.
*   The QGP lifetime is taken as $\tau_{QGP} \simeq 4.5$ fm, based on a final temperature $T \simeq 200$ MeV.
*   The initial time is fixed at $\tau_0 = 0.5$ fm, and the nucleus radius is $R_A = 6.4$ fm (for Au).

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Zakharov-Wiedemann formalism for radiative energy loss and $p_\perp$-broadening, previously developed for static or longitudinally expanding media (Bjorken 1983), to include transverse flow. It directly addresses the finding by Eskola et al. (2004) that the extracted transport coefficient $c$ (where $\hat{q} \simeq c \, \varepsilon^{3/4}$) is much larger than the perturbative estimate of $c \simeq 2$, leading to speculation about a strongly coupled QGP. The authors show that the reduction of $c$ from 10 to 2 claimed by Renk et al. (2005, 2006) using a strong radial flow is not supported when the correct Lorentz transformation of $\hat{q}$ is applied, leaving the perturbative/non-perturbative dilemma unresolved.
