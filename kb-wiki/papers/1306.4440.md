# Asymptotic Approximation for Bethe-Salpeter Equation and its Applications to Deuteron

> Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved for bound state composed of two fermions mediated by pion exchange force of the pseudovector coupling. Expanding the amplitude by gamma matrices the one-dimensional integral equation is derived. It reproduces the binding energy of deuteron. The relation with the quadrupole moment is also discussed in the framework of the asymptotic approximation.

# Asymptotic Approximation for Bethe-Salpeter Equation and its Applications to Deuteron

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the deuteron bound state is solved in Minkowski space by expanding the amplitude in gamma matrices and performing a Fourier transform to coordinate representation, but the resulting quadrupole moment is about 17 percent lower than the experimental value because the asymptotic approximation does not fully account for the short-range core region of the wave function.

## Method
The homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation with pseudovector pion exchange is expanded into scalar, vector, tensor, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar sectors using gamma matrices. After Fourier transformation and taking the limit as relative time goes to zero, the equation reduces to a one-dimensional differential equation equivalent to a nonrelativistic eigenvalue problem with an inverse-square potential plus residual interactions. The tensor sector is expanded in vector spherical harmonics to obtain coupled radial equations for the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) system, and the binding energy and quadrupole moment are computed from the asymptotic form of the wave functions.

## Key numbers
- The core radius achieved to reproduce the deuteron binding energy is 0.3–0.4 fm.
- The four-dimensional cutoff mass \(\Lambda\) is chosen as \(\Lambda \sim 0.4\)–0.5 GeV, about half as large as that of traditional meson-exchange interactions.
- The D-state probability of deuteron extracted from the experimental magnetic dipole moment is \(P_D = 0.039\).
- The calculated quadrupole moment is about 17 percent lower than the experimental value of 0.286 fm\(^2\).
- The strength of the inverse square potential has the upper bound \(g \leq 1/4\) to obtain binding energy.
- The isospin factor for deuteron is \(\langle \vec{\tau}_p \cdot \vec{\tau}_n \rangle = -3\) for the isospin state \(I=0\).

## Context
This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach to the deuteron problem, following the formal developments of Gell-Mann and Low, Nakanishi, and Goldstein on the relativistic two-body bound state. It contrasts with traditional meson-exchange calculations that use cutoff procedures on three-momentum, here generalized to four dimensions. The result that the quadrupole moment is underestimated by 17 percent indicates that the asymptotic approximation neglects short-range core contributions, and the author suggests that including the inner parts of the wave function and solving the pseudo-scalar and axial-vector components simultaneously could improve agreement with experiment (Pavanello, 2010).
