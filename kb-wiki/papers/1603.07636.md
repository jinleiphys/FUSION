# Three-cluster breakup in deuteron-deuteron collisions: single-scattering approximation

> We present results for the three-cluster breakup in deuteron-deuteron collisions at 130 and 270 MeV deuteron beam energy. The breakup amplitude is calculated using the first term in the Neumann series expansion of the corresponding exact four-nucleon equations. In analogy with nucleon-deuteron breakup where an equivalent approximation is compared with exact calculations, we expect this single-scattering approximation to provide a rough estimation of three-body breakup observables in quasifree configurations. We predict the nucleon-deuteron and deuteron-deuteron three-cluster breakup cross sections to be of a comparable size and thereby question the reliability of the recent experimental data [A. Ramazani-Moghaddam-Arani, Ph.D. thesis, University of Groningen, 2009; A. Ramazani-Moghaddam-Arani et al., EPJ Web of Conferences 3, 04012 (2010)] that is smaller by about three orders of magnitude. We also show that an equivalent single-scattering approximation provides a reasonable description of deuteron-deuteron elastic scattering at forward scattering angles.

## Key claim

The authors use a single-scattering approximation (SSA) to estimate deuteron-deuteron three-cluster breakup cross sections at 130 and 270 MeV, and argue that the resulting cross sections are comparable in size to nucleon-deuteron breakup cross sections, thereby questioning the reliability of existing experimental data that are smaller by about three orders of magnitude. The hedge is that the SSA is expected to provide only a rough estimation of three-body breakup observables in quasifree configurations, and its reliability decreases with increasing energy of the final neutron.

## Method

The breakup amplitude is calculated using the first term in the Neumann series expansion of the exact four-nucleon Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas (AGS) equations, retaining only terms first order in the subsystem transition operators. This single-scattering approximation requires solving the three-nucleon AGS equations for the transition operator U1, which is then used to compute both elastic and three-cluster breakup amplitudes for deuteron-deuteron collisions. The method is validated by comparing SSA results with exact three-nucleon calculations for proton-deuteron breakup at comparable energies, and by comparing SSA predictions with experimental data for deuteron-deuteron elastic scattering at 232 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Proton-deuteron total breakup cross sections: exact 69.1 mb and SSA 109 mb at 95 MeV proton beam energy; exact 49.7 mb and SSA 63.5 mb at 200 MeV proton beam energy.
- Deuteron-deuteron three-cluster total breakup cross section from SSA-4 with CD Bonn + Delta potential: 189 mb at 130 MeV and 57 mb at 270 MeV deuteron beam energy.
- The experimental deuteron-deuteron three-cluster breakup cross section at 130 MeV is smaller than the SSA prediction by a factor of approximately 1000.
- Deuteron-deuteron elastic scattering at 232 MeV: SSA results are reasonably described at forward scattering angles up to 40 degrees in the center-of-mass frame.

## Context

This work extends the single-scattering approximation previously applied to deuteron-deuteron elastic scattering (Deltuva and Fonseca, earlier work) to three-cluster breakup calculations. The authors compare their SSA results with exact three-nucleon calculations for proton-deuteron breakup to validate the method, and with experimental data from Ramazani-Moghaddam-Arami (2009) and Ramazani-Moghaddam-Arami et al. (2010) at KVI. The findings contradict those experimental data by three orders of magnitude but are supported by preliminary data at 160 MeV from Khatri (PhD thesis). This work enables future exact four-nucleon calculations and provides a benchmark for experimental normalization.
