# Second Order Transport Coefficient from Chiral Anomaly at Weak Coupling: Diagrammatic Resummation

> We compute one of the second order transport coefficients arising from the chiral anomaly in a high temperature weakly coupled regime of quark-gluon plasma. This transport coefficient is responsible for the CP-odd current that is proportional to the time derivative of the magnetic field, and can be considered as a first correction to the chiral magnetic conductivity at finite, small frequency. We observe that this transport coefficient has a non-analytic dependence on the coupling as $\sim 1/(g^4 \log(1/g))$ at weak coupling regime, which necessitates a re-summation of infinite ladder diagrams with leading pinch singularities to get a correct leading log result: a feature quite similar to that one finds in the computation of electric conductivity. We formulate and solve the relevant CP-odd Schwinger-Dyson equation in real-time perturbation theory that reduces to a coupled set of second order differential equations at leading log order. Our result for this second order transport coefficient indicates that chiral magnetic current has some resistance to the time change of magnetic field, which may be called "chiral induction effect". We also discuss the case of color current induced by color magnetic field.

## Key claim

The authors compute the second-order transport coefficient $\xi_5$, which gives the first correction to the chiral magnetic conductivity at finite frequency, and find it has a non-analytic dependence on the coupling constant $\sim 1/(g^4 \log(1/g))$ at weak coupling, requiring resummation of infinite ladder diagrams with leading pinch singularities to obtain the correct leading-log result.

## Method

The authors use real-time Schwinger-Keldysh perturbation theory in the "ra"-basis to compute the retarded current-current correlation function for a single Weyl fermion species in QED at high temperature. They identify pinch singularities in the P-odd part of the 1-loop diagram at first order in frequency, then formulate and solve a CP-odd Schwinger-Dyson equation that sums all multi-loop ladder diagrams with leading pinch singularities, reducing it to a coupled set of second-order differential equations at leading-log order. The analysis keeps finite external momentum $\vec k$ up to first order to extract the P-odd part, but proves that $\vec k$ dependence in the denominators of pinching propagators does not contribute.

## Key numbers

* For QED with a single right-handed Weyl fermion: $\xi_5 = {-3.006 \over e^4\log(1/e)}{\mu\over T}$
* For 2-flavor massless QCD ($N_c=3$) with $Q_u=3/2$ and $Q_d=-1/3$: $\xi_5^{\rm QCD} = {-2.003\over g^4\log(1/g)}{\mu_A\over T}$, where $\mu_A$ is an axial chemical potential
* The 1-loop result (before resummation) gives $\xi_5^{\rm 1-loop} = -{\mu\over 6\pi^2\zeta}$, with damping rate $\zeta \sim g^2\log(1/g)T$
* The static chiral magnetic conductivity is $\sigma_\chi = {\mu\over 4\pi^2}$ for QED

## Context

This work extends the computation of anomalous transport coefficients beyond the first-order (static) chiral magnetic effect, whose magnitude is fixed by anomaly coefficients and has been confirmed in both weak coupling computations by Kharzeev (2009), Landsteiner (2011), Golkar (2012), and Satow (2014), and strong coupling computations by Yee (2009), Rebhan (2009), and others. The non-analytic coupling dependence and need for ladder resummation parallels the situation for shear viscosity and electric conductivity as studied by Baym (1990) and Arnold (2000), as well as the chiral electric separation conductivity by Huang (2013). The result enables computation of second-order anomalous transport coefficients in weakly coupled gauge theories like QCD and electro-weak theory, and the negative sign of $\xi_5$ relative to $\sigma_\chi$ indicates a "chiral induction effect" where the chiral magnetic current resists time changes of the magnetic field.
