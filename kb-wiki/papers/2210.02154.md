# Models of $J/Ψ$ photo-production reactions on the nucleon

> The $J/Ψ$ photo-production reactions on the nucleon can provide information on the roles of gluons in determining the $J/Ψ$-nucleon ($J/Ψ$-N) interactions and the structure of the nucleon. The information on the $J/Ψ$-N interactions is needed to test lattice QCD (LQCD) calculations and to understand the nucleon resonances such as $N^*(P_c)$ recently reported by the LHCb Collaboration. In addition, it is also needed to investigate the production of nuclei with hidden charms and to extract the gluon distributions in nuclei. The main purpose of this article is to review six reaction models of $γ+ p \rightarrow J/Ψ+p$ reactions which have been and can be applied to analyze the data from Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab). The formulae for each model are given and used to obtain the results to show the extent to which the available data can be described. The models presented include the Pomeron-exchange model of Donnachie and Landshoff (Pom-DL) and its extensions to include $J/Ψ$-N potentials extracted from LQCD (Pom-pot) and to also use the constituent quark model (CQM) to account for the quark substructure of $J/Ψ$ (Pom-CQM). The other three models are developed from applying the perturbative QCD approach to calculate the two-gluon exchange using the generalized parton distribution (GPD) of the nucleon (GPD-based), two- and three-gluon exchanges using the parton distribution of the nucleon ($2g+3g$), and the exchanges of scalar ($0^{++}$) and tensor ($2^{++}$) glueballs within the holographic formulation (holog). The results of investigating the excitation of the nucleon resonances $N^*(P_c)$ in the $γ+ p \rightarrow J/Ψ+p$ reactions are also given.

## Key claim

This review demonstrates that six distinct reaction models for J/ψ photo-production on the nucleon can be unambiguously distinguished using forthcoming JLab data at large |t| and at energies very near the J/ψ production threshold, but the Pom-pot model is the only one that fits data from threshold to very high energy W=300 GeV.

## Method

The authors review six reaction models for γ + p → J/Ψ + p: the Pomeron-exchange model of Donnachie and Landshoff (Pom-DL) and its extensions to include J/Ψ-N potentials from lattice QCD (Pom-pot) and constituent quark model (Pom-CQM); a generalized parton distribution-based model (GPD-based); a two- and three-gluon exchange model using parton distributions (2g+3g); and a holographic glueball-exchange model (holog). For each model, the authors present the explicit formulae and use them to compute cross sections, comparing with available JLab data. The Argonne-Osaka dynamical formulation is used to investigate N*(Pc) resonance excitation within these models.

## Key numbers

- The Pom-DL model parameters include: α_P(t) = 1.08 + 0.25 t for ρ and ω, α_0 = 1.12 for φ, α_0 = 1.25 for J/Ψ and Υ; β_u/d = 2.07 GeV^{-1}, β_s = 1.38 GeV^{-1}, β_c = 0.32 GeV^{-1}, β_b = 0.45 GeV^{-1}; μ_0^2 = 1.1 GeV^2
- The LQCD-derived J/Ψ-N potential from Kawanai and Sasaki has Yukawa form with α = 0.1 and μ = 0.6 GeV, giving a scattering length of -0.09 fm
- The Brodsky-Miller J/Ψ-N potential gives a scattering length of -0.24 fm; the Kaidalov-Volkovitsky result gives -0.05 fm
- The 2g+3g model uses form factors F²_{2g}(t) = F²_{3g}(t) = exp(1.13 t) with t in GeV²
- The Pom-DL model cannot describe JLab data at W < 7 GeV, while the Pom-pot model fits from threshold to W = 300 GeV
- The VMD coupling constant 1/f_{J/Ψ} in the Pom-pot model requires reduction by a factor of about 0.4–0.7 to fit JLab data

## Context

This review extends the Pomeron-exchange framework of Donnachie and Landshoff (1984, 1992, 1995, 1998) for vector meson photo-production, which successfully described high-energy data using the Pomeron-photon analogy and factorization approximation. The paper contrasts this with perturbative QCD approaches: the GPD-based model following Guo et al. (2021), the 2g+3g model from Brodsky et al. (2001) applied to JLab data by GlueX (2019), and the holographic QCD model of Mamo and Zahed (2019, 2021). The work enables testing lattice QCD calculations of J/Ψ-N potentials from Kawanai and Sasaki (2010, 2011), investigating N*(Pc) pentaquark states reported by LHCb (2015–2021), and studying gluon distributions in nuclei and hidden-charm nuclear states.
