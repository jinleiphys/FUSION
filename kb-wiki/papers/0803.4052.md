# Critical Zeeman Splitting of Fermi Superfluidity at Infinite Scattering Length

> We determine the critical Zeeman energy splitting for Fermi superfluidity at infinite s-wave scattering length according to the Monte Carlo and experimental results of the equations of state. Based on the universality hypothesis, we show that there exist two critical fields $H_{c1}$ and $H_{c2}$, between which a superfluid-normal mixed phase is energetically favored, and model-independent formulae for $H_{c1}$, $H_{c2}$ and the critical population imbalance $P_c$ are derived. Using recent Monte Carlo and experimental results of $P_c$, $H_{c1}$ and $H_{c2}$ are determined. It is found $H_{c1}=0.41ε_{\text F}$ and $H_{c2}=0.50ε_{\text F}$, with $ε_{\text F}$ being the Fermi energy of non-interacting gas.

## Key claim

The paper determines the lower and upper critical Zeeman fields for a homogeneous unitary Fermi gas at infinite scattering length, showing that a superfluid-normal mixed phase is energetically favored between these two fields, and that the normal phase at the phase transition is partially polarized rather than fully polarized as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors use the universality hypothesis to construct exact equations of state for the superfluid and normal phases in the grand canonical ensemble, then transform to the canonical ensemble with fixed total particle number density to determine the critical fields. By combining Monte Carlo data for the universal constants ξ (the Bertsch parameter), γ (the critical ratio H/μ at the first-order transition), and the critical population imbalance Pc, they derive model-independent formulae for Hc1 and Hc2 without requiring full knowledge of the scaling function G(x).

## Key numbers

- Hc1 = 0.407 εF (lower critical Zeeman field for homogeneous unitary Fermi gas, using Monte Carlo data)
- Hc2 = 0.503 εF (upper critical Zeeman field for homogeneous unitary Fermi gas, using Monte Carlo data)
- ξMC = 0.42(1) (Bertsch parameter from Monte Carlo)
- γMC = 0.967 (critical ratio H/μ from Monte Carlo)
- PcMC = 0.389 (critical population imbalance from Monte Carlo)
- γEXP = 0.95 and PcEXP = 0.36 (experimental values from reference [exp])
- G'(γMC) = 2.587 (derivative of scaling function at γ, extracted from Monte Carlo data)
- H0 ≃ 1.26 εF (transition field from partially to fully polarized normal state from Monte Carlo)
- Mean field results for comparison: Hc1MF = 0.477 εF, Hc2MF = 0.693 εF, PcMF = 0.933

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit in unitary Fermi gases, which had only considered the first-order superfluid-normal transition point in the grand canonical ensemble (Carlson, 2005) or assumed the normal phase was fully polarized. The authors build on the phase separation picture proposed by Sheehy (2006) and He (2007) for the BCS-BEC crossover, and use Monte Carlo data from Carlson (2003) and experimental data from the MIT group (2006) to determine the two critical fields for a homogeneous system. The results enable future calculations of the scaling function G(x) from beyond-mean-field theories and can be generalized to finite temperature to locate the tricritical point.
