# $X$ and $Z_{cs}$ in $B^+\to J/ŽłŽĢK^+$ as $s$-wave threshold cusps and alternative spin-parity assignments to $X(4274)$ and $X(4500)$

> Recent LHCb's amplitude analysis on $B^+\to J/ŽłŽĢK^+$ suggests the existence of exotic $X$ and $Z_{cs}$ hadrons, based on an assumption that Breit-Wigner resonances describe all the peak structures. However, all the peaks and also dips in the spectra are located at relevant meson-meson thresholds where threshold kinematical cusps might cause such structures. This points to the importance of an independent amplitude analysis with due consideration of the kinematical effects, and this is what we do in this work. Our model fits well $J/ŽłŽĢ$, $J/ŽłK^+$, and $K^+ŽĢ$ invariant mass distributions simultaneously, demonstrating that all the $X$, $Z_{cs}$, and dip structures can be well described with the ordinary $s$-wave threshold cusps. Spin-parity of the $X(4274)$ and $X(4500)$ structures are respectively $0^-$ and $1^-$ from our model, as opposed to $1^+$ and $0^+$ from the LHCb's. With all relevant threshold cusps considered, the number of fitting parameters seems to be significantly reduced. The LHCb data requires $D_s^{(*)}\bar{D}^{*}$ scattering lengths in our model to be consistent with zero, disfavoring $D_s^{(*)}\bar{D}^{*}$ molecule interpretations of $Z_{cs}(4000)$ and $Z_{cs}(4220)$ and, via the SU(3) relation, being consistent with previous lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

All peak and dip structures in the LHCb data for $B^+\to J/\psi\phi K^+$, including the claimed exotic $X$ and $Z_{cs}$ hadrons, can be described by ordinary $s$-wave threshold cusps from one-loop diagrams without requiring Breit-Wigner resonances or virtual poles near thresholds.

## Method

The authors constructed a model of one-loop mechanisms for $B^+\to J/\psi\phi K^+$ with $s$-wave meson-meson pairs at thresholds, including $D_{sJ}^{(*)}\bar{D}_s^{(*)}$, $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^{(*)}$, $\psi'\phi$, $D_s^+\bar{D}^{*0}$, and $D_s^{*+}\bar{D}^{*0}$ channels, plus $K^*_J$ excitation mechanisms in Breit-Wigner form. They simultaneously fitted the $J/\psi\phi$, $J/\psi K^+$, and $K^+\phi$ invariant mass distributions from LHCb data using 16 mechanisms with 29 fitting parameters, and examined the effect of $D_s^{(*)}\bar{D}^{*0}$ rescattering by varying the interaction strengths.

## Key numbers

- $\chi^2/{\rm ndf}=1.77$ for the default model fit to all three invariant mass distributions ($\chi^2=102.3+94.2+113.7$ with $3\times 68-29$ degrees of freedom)
- For $D_s^{+}\bar{D}^{*0}$ scattering: allowed range $-0.33 < h_\alpha < 0.93$, corresponding to scattering length $-0.12 < a {\rm (fm)} < 0.06$ and a virtual pole at 93~MeV below threshold or deeper
- For $D_s^{*+}\bar{D}^{*0}$ scattering: allowed range $-0.17 < h_\alpha < 2.02$, corresponding to scattering length $-0.21 < a {\rm (fm)} < 0.03$ and a virtual pole at 103~MeV below threshold or deeper
- With momentum-independent production vertices for $D_s^{+}\bar{D}^{*0}$: allowed range $-1.09 < h_\alpha < -0.25$, scattering length $0.04 < a {\rm (fm)} < 0.22$, virtual pole at $48-99$~MeV below threshold
- Cutoff value $\Lambda=1000$~MeV used in all dipole form factors, with stability tested at $\Lambda=750$, 1250, and 1500~MeV

## Context

This work extends the threshold cusp interpretation previously applied to $X(4140)$ and $X(4700)$ by Swanson, Liu, Dong, Ortega, and Nakamura (various years) to all structures in the $B^+\to J/\psi\phi K^+$ decay, including the $Z_{cs}$ peaks. The authors provide alternative spin-parity assignments of $0^-$ for $X(4274)$ and $1^-$ for $X(4500)$, contradicting the LHCb's $1^+$ and $0^+$ assignments from their 2021 amplitude analysis. The scattering length results disfavor $D_s^{(*)}\bar{D}^{*}$ molecule interpretations of $Z_{cs}(4000)$ and $Z_{cs}(4220)$, and are consistent via SU(3) with lattice QCD results from Prelovsek (2013, 2015), Chen (2014), Ikeda (2016), and Cheung (2017) that found no narrow $Z_c(3900)$ pole. The work enables future six-dimensional amplitude analyses that incorporate threshold cusp mechanisms rather than assuming Breit-Wigner resonances for all peaks.
