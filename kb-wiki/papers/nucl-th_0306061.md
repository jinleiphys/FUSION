# Neutron and proton drip lines using the modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula

> Proton and neutron separation energies have been calculated using the extended Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula. This modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula describes minutely the positions of all the old and the new magic numbers. It accounts for the disappearance of some traditional magic numbers for neutrons and provides extra stability for some new neutron numbers. The neutron and proton drip lines have been predicted using this extended Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula. The implications of the proton drip line on the astrophysical rp-process and of the neutron drip line on the astrophysical r-process have been discussed.

## Key claim

The modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula, which includes an asymmetry term with a fitted constant k=17 and a modified pairing term with c=30, predicts neutron and proton drip lines that improve upon the old Bethe-Weizsacker formula, but the author acknowledges that the formula "wrongly predicts" several nuclei as stable (such as ^5Li, ^6Be, ^15F, ^16Ne) and misplaces some neutron drip line nuclei (e.g., predicting ^26O as bound when it is not), indicating the model cannot capture all fine details.

## Method

The author used the modified Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula from Samanta and Adhikari (2002), which incorporates a modified asymmetry term with a fitted constant k=17 and a modified pairing term with c=30, while keeping the volume, surface, Coulomb, and symmetry coefficients unchanged from the old formula. One-proton and one-neutron separation energies were calculated from binding energies using standard definitions, and drip line nuclei were identified as those where the respective separation energy becomes positive for the first time when scanning neutron number N for fixed atomic number Z. The results were tabulated for all elements from Z=2 to Z=118.

## Key numbers

- Fitted asymmetry term modifying constant: k = 17
- Modified pairing term constant: c = 30
- Volume coefficient: a_v = 15.79 MeV
- Surface coefficient: a_s = 18.34 MeV
- Coulomb coefficient: a_c = 0.71 MeV
- Symmetry coefficient: a_sym = 23.21 MeV
- Pairing coefficient: a_p = 12 MeV
- The formula predicts ^32Ne, ^35Na, ^38Mg, and ^41Al as the last bound isotopes of neon, sodium, magnesium, and aluminium, respectively
- The formula wrongly predicts ^5Li, ^6Be, ^15F, and ^16Ne as stable
- The formula puts the neutron drip line at ^17B, ^20C, and ^26O, while ^19B and ^22C are also stable but ^25,26O are not
- For nuclei beyond Z=47, the proton drip line using the old and modified BW formulas are identical; for nuclei beyond Z=30, the neutron drip line using the old and modified BW formulas are identical
- The r-process path follows neutron separation energies close to 1.2-2.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula approach by incorporating modifications from Samanta and Adhikari (2002) that account for the disappearance of traditional magic numbers (e.g., N=8 for Z=4, N=20 for Z=12-17) and extra stability for new neutron numbers (e.g., N=6 for Z=3-8, N=14 for Z=7-10, N=16 for Z=7-8). The predicted drip lines are relevant for planning mass measurement experiments and for understanding the astrophysical r-process and rp-process paths, as changes in magic numbers in neutron-rich regions could modify the r-process path and potentially resolve inconsistencies between calculations and observations for r-process abundances. The paper notes that the even-odd staggering is prominent for the proton drip line (proton drip line for even Z nuclei is further from beta-stability than for odd Z nuclei) but not strong for the neutron drip line.
