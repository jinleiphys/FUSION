# Spectral and Transport Properties of Quark-Gluon Plasma in a Nonperturbative Approach

> Nonperturbative methods play an important role in quantum many-body systems, especially in situations with an interplay of continuum and bound states and/or large coupling strengths between the constituents. Employing the Luttinger-Ward functional (LWF) we have computed the equation of state (EoS) of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) using fully dressed selfconsistent 1- and 2-body propagators. We first give an alternative derivation of our previously reported results for resumming the ladder diagram series of the LWF using a "matrix log" technique which accounts for dynamically formed bound and resonant states. Two types of solutions were found in selfconsistent fits to lattice-QCD data for the EoS, heavy-quark free energy and quarkonium correlators: a strongly coupled scenario (SCS) with broad parton spectral functions and strong meson resonances near the transition temperature vs. a weakly coupled scenario (WCS) with well-defined parton quasiparticles and weak meson resonances. Here, we discuss how these solutions can be distinguished by analyzing the pertinent transport properties. We focus on the specific shear viscosity, $(4π)η/s$, and the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient, $(2πT) {\cal D}_s$, including its mass dependence. At low temperatures, in the SCS, they turn out to be a factor of 2 within their conjectured quantum lower bound, while they are a factor of 2-5 larger in the WCS. At higher temperatures, the transport parameters of the two scenarios approach each other. We propose the ratio $ (4πη/s)/(2πT {\cal D}_s )$ as a measure to distinguish the perturbative and strong-coupling limits of 5/2 and 1, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two distinct nonperturbative solutions (a strongly coupled scenario and a weakly coupled scenario) which both fit lattice-QCD data for the equation of state, heavy-quark free energy, and quarkonium correlators can be distinguished by their transport properties, specifically the specific shear viscosity and heavy-quark diffusion coefficient, rather than by equilibrium properties alone.

## Method

The authors employ a selfconsistent thermodynamic T-matrix approach within the Luttinger-Ward functional formalism, using a QCD-inspired Hamiltonian with an in-medium Cornell-type potential. They solve coupled Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations for fully dressed one- and two-body propagators, resumming ladder diagrams via a matrix-logarithm technique. The input potential parameters and bare parton masses are constrained by fitting to lattice-QCD data for the equation of state, heavy-quark free energy, and quarkonium correlator ratios, yielding two distinct scenarios (SCS and WCS). Transport coefficients are then computed from the resulting selfconsistent spectral functions and scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Specific shear viscosity (4π)η/s in the SCS at low temperatures: a factor of 2 within the conjectured quantum lower bound
- Specific shear viscosity (4π)η/s in the WCS at low temperatures: a factor of 2-5 larger than the quantum lower bound
- Heavy-quark diffusion coefficient (2πT) D_s in the SCS at low temperatures: a factor of 2 within the conjectured quantum lower bound
- Heavy-quark diffusion coefficient (2πT) D_s in the WCS at low temperatures: a factor of 2-5 larger than the quantum lower bound
- Proposed ratio (4πη/s)/(2πT D_s) equals 5/2 in the perturbative limit and 1 in the strong-coupling limit
- Input potential parameters for SCS: α_s=0.270, σ=0.225 GeV², m_d=-0.238 GeV + 2.915 T, c_s=0.01
- Input potential parameters for WCS: α_s=0.270, σ=0.210 GeV², m_d=0.975 GeV - 0.135 GeV²/T, c_s=0.1
- At low temperatures, quark-to-gluon mass ratio M_q/M_g approaches C_F/C_A=4/9, the Casimir ratio for SU_C(3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous T-matrix approach (Liu 2017) which established the two scenarios from fits to lattice-QCD data, by now computing transport properties that can distinguish them. The approach is complementary to first-principles lattice-QCD computations (Ratti 2018) which are performed in Euclidean space-time and cannot easily extract real-time transport coefficients, and to other nonperturbative methods such as Dyson-Schwinger, functional-renormalization group, and Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio approaches (Qin 2013, Fischer 2017, Khan 2015, Herbst 2013, Fukushima 2017, Bastian 2018). The results enable phenomenological discrimination between strongly and weakly coupled scenarios using heavy-ion collision observables, and the proposed ratio of transport coefficients provides a new measure to assess the coupling strength of the quark-gluon plasma.
