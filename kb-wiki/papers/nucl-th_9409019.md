# Three-Body Halos. II. from Two- to Three-Body Asymptotics

> The large distance behavior of weakly bound three-body systems is investigated. The Schrödinger equation and the Faddeev equations are reformulated by an expansion in eigenfunctions of the angular part of a corresponding operator. The resulting coupled set of effective radial equations are then derived. Both two- and three-body asymptotic behavior are possible and their relative importance is studied for systems where subsystems may be bound. The system of two nucleons outside a core is studied numerically in detail and the character of possible halo structure is pointed out and investigated.

## Key claim

The paper shows that weakly bound three-body systems can exhibit either two-body or three-body asymptotic behavior depending on whether subsystems are bound, and that the transition between these regimes determines the character of possible halo structures. The authors hedge that the relative importance of two- versus three-body asymptotics depends on the specific binding energies of the subsystems.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Schrödinger equation and Faddeev equations by expanding in eigenfunctions of the angular part of the corresponding operator, deriving a coupled set of effective radial equations. They then study numerically the system of two nucleons outside a core, analyzing the asymptotic behavior and halo structure.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of three-body halos (Fedorov, Jensen, and Riisager, 1994) by focusing on the asymptotic behavior at large distances. It connects to the broader study of weakly bound nuclear systems where halo structures appear, such as Borromean nuclei where no two-body subsystems are bound. The reformulation of the Faddeev equations enables systematic treatment of both two-body and three-body asymptotics, which is necessary for understanding halo nuclei with one or two loosely bound valence nucleons.
