# Rare hyperon decays with missing energy

> We explore the strangeness-changing decays of the lightest hyperons into another baryon plus missing energy within and beyond the standard model (SM). In the SM these processes arise from the loop-induced quark transition $s\to dν\barν$ and their branching fractions are estimated to be less than $10^{-11}$. In the presence of new physics (NP) the rates of these hyperon decays with missing energy could increase significantly with respect to the SM expectations because of modifications to the SM process or contributions from additional modes with new invisible particles. Adopting a model-independent approach and taking into account constraints from the kaon sector, we find that the current data on $K\toπν\barν$ do not permit sizable NP impact on the hyperon decays via underlying operators having mainly parity-even quark parts. In contrast, NP operators with primarily parity-odd quark parts are much less restricted by the existing bounds on $K\to\rm invisible$ and $K\toππν\barν$ and consequently could produce substantially amplifying effects on the hyperon modes. Their NP-enhanced branching fractions could reach levels potentially observable in the ongoing BESIII experiment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that new physics (NP) operators with primarily parity-odd quark parts are much less restricted by existing kaon bounds than parity-even operators, and consequently could produce substantially amplifying effects on hyperon decay rates with missing energy, potentially reaching levels observable in the ongoing BESIII experiment.

## Method

The author adopts a model-independent effective Lagrangian approach for the quark-level transition \(s\to d\texttt{f}\bar{\texttt{f}}\), where \(\texttt{f}\) is an invisible Dirac fermion (either a Standard Model neutrino or a nonstandard particle). The baryonic matrix elements for hyperon decays \(\mathfrak B\to\mathfrak B'\texttt{f}\bar{\texttt{f}}\) and \(\Omega^-\to\Xi^-\texttt{f}\bar{\texttt{f}}\) are estimated using leading-order flavor-SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, and the differential decay rates are derived. Constraints from kaon decays \(K\to\pi\nu\bar\nu\), \(K\to\text{invisible}\), and \(K\to\pi\pi\nu\bar\nu\) are applied to restrict the NP coupling parameters.

## Key numbers

- Standard Model branching fractions for hyperon decays: \(\Lambda\to n\nu\bar\nu\) at \(7.1\times10^{-13}\), \(\Sigma^+\to p\nu\bar\nu\) at \(4.3\times10^{-13}\), \(\Xi^0\to\Lambda\nu\bar\nu\) at \(6.3\times10^{-13}\), \(\Xi^0\to\Sigma^0\nu\bar\nu\) at \(1.0\times10^{-13}\), \(\Xi^-\to\Sigma^-\nu\bar\nu\) at \(1.3\times10^{-13}\), \(\Omega^-\to\Xi^-\nu\bar\nu\) at \(4.9\times10^{-12}\)
- Expected BESIII sensitivity (90% CL): \(\Lambda\to n\nu\bar\nu\) at \(3\times10^{-7}\), \(\Sigma^+\to p\nu\bar\nu\) at \(4\times10^{-7}\), \(\Xi^0\to\Lambda\nu\bar\nu\) at \(8\times10^{-7}\), \(\Xi^0\to\Sigma^0\nu\bar\nu\) at \(9\times10^{-7}\), \(\Omega^-\to\Xi^-\nu\bar\nu\) at \(2.6\times10^{-5}\)
- Form-factor modifications increase hyperon decay rates by up to approximately 16 percent
- Kaon decay constant \(f_K = 155.6(4)\) MeV used in kaon matrix elements

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Li (2016) and Hu (2018) that considered hyperon decays due to new \(s\to d\nu\bar\nu\) interactions with the same chiral structure as the Standard Model. The paper generalizes to NP operators with other Lorentz structures and nonstandard invisible fermions, finding that parity-odd operators are much less constrained by existing kaon data from NA62 and KOTO on \(K\to\pi\nu\bar\nu\) and by bounds on \(K\to\text{invisible}\) and \(K\to\pi\pi\nu\bar\nu\). The results enable future searches at BESIII, which could produce \(10^6\)-\(10^8\) hyperons per year in \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\) decays, to probe NP-enhanced rates that may be orders of magnitude above Standard Model predictions.
