# Out of equilibrium Chiral Vortical Effect in Holography

> We study the chiral vortical effect far from equilibrium in a strongly coupled holographic field theory. Rotation is represented as a perturbation via a gravito-magnetic field on top of a five-dimensional charged AdS Vaidya metric. We also introduce a momentum relaxation mechanism by linear scalar field backgrounds and study the CVE dynamics as function of the charges, temperature and momentum relaxation. The far from equilibrium behavior shows that the CVE builds up with a significant delay in time compared to the quasi instantaneous equilibration of the background metric. We also pay special attention to the effects of the gravitational contribution to the axial anomaly in the CVE of the axial current. We develop an analytic estimate of this delay and also compute the quasi-normal modes near equilibrium which determine the late time ring down.

## Key claim

The chiral vortical effect (CVE) builds up with a significant delay compared to the quasi-instantaneous equilibration of the background metric when the system is driven far from equilibrium, and this delay can be estimated analytically; the effect depends on the gravitational contribution to the axial anomaly and on momentum relaxation.

## Method

The authors model a strongly coupled holographic field theory using a five-dimensional charged AdS Vaidya metric with rotation introduced as a gravito-magnetic perturbation. They include three massless scalar fields with linear profiles to break translational symmetry (momentum relaxation) and solve the linearized equations of motion for the gauge field and metric perturbations. The time evolution of the vector, axial, and energy currents is computed from the asymptotic expansion of the bulk fields near the boundary, following a quench in the black brane mass parameter.

## Key numbers

- The quench is performed from an initial horizon position \(u_h^{in}=1\) to a final horizon position \(u_h^{fin}=0.8\).
- The quench parameter is set to \(\tau=0.05\) for the momentum relaxation dependence studies.
- The Chern-Simons couplings are chosen as \(\alpha = N_f (16\pi^2)^{-1}\) and \(\lambda = N_f (384\pi^2)^{-1}\), with \(N_f\) the number of Dirac fermions; for numerical purposes \(\alpha = B_g = 1\) and \(\lambda = 32/768\).
- The transition between oscillating and monotonically decreasing behavior in the vector and axial currents occurs around \(k \simeq 1.5\).
- The equilibrium response coefficients are \(\sigma = 16 \alpha \mu \mu_5\), \(\sigma_5 = 8 \alpha (\mu^2 + \mu_5^2) + 64 \pi^2 \lambda T^2\), and \(\xi = (16/3) \alpha \mu_5 (3\mu^2 + \mu_5^2) + 128 \pi^2 \lambda \mu_5 T^2\).

## Context

This work extends the out-of-equilibrium holographic study of the chiral magnetic effect by Fernandez-Pendas and Landsteiner (2019) to the chiral vortical effect, and builds on equilibrium holographic calculations of the CVE by Amado et al. (2011), Erdmenger et al. (2008), and Banerjee et al. (2008). The momentum relaxation mechanism follows Andrade and Withers (2013). The results enable future studies of anomaly-induced transport in heavy ion collisions, where large vorticity has been observed by the STAR collaboration (2017), and clarify the role of the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly in out-of-equilibrium settings.
