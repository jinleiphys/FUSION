# Meson-Exchange Currents in pp-Bremsstrahlung

> The long-standing discrepancy between pp-bremsstrahlung data and calculations based on the relativistic impulse approximation current is substantially reduced by the inclusion of the PV$γ$ and intermediate-state $Δ$-resonance iso-scalar meson-exchange currents. The success of the standard procedures adopted here shows that pp-bremsstrahlung provides a powerful tool for the further study of $T$=1 isoscalar exchange currents. The need for addition high-precision data is stressed.

## Key claim

The long-standing discrepancy between pp-bremsstrahlung data and relativistic impulse approximation calculations is substantially reduced by including PVγ and intermediate-state Δ-resonance isoscalar meson-exchange currents, but the authors stress that additional high-precision data are needed for further study of resonance width and relativistic effects.

## Method

The authors extended their earlier relativistic meson-baryon model calculations of pp-bremsstrahlung observables to include the dominant meson-exchange currents: intermediate-state Δ-resonance excitation via π- and ρ-meson exchange, and vector-pseudoscalar (ρπγ, ωπγ, ρηγ, ωηγ) interactions. They adopted the RuhrPot NN-interaction to ensure consistency between the meson-exchange currents and the wave functions, with all coupling constants fixed to experimental values or derived from SU(6) relations, introducing no free parameters. The exchange currents were calculated perturbatively, automatically including meson-recoil contributions and avoiding the need for wave function re-orthonormalization terms.

## Key numbers

- The NΔπ coupling constant was taken as g_{NΔπ}=28.85, consistent with the observed Δ-resonance width Γ_Δ=115 MeV.
- The vector-pseudoscalar coupling constants were fixed to experimental values: g_{ρπγ}=0.55, g_{ωπγ}=2.03, g_{ρηγ}=1.39, and g_{ωηγ}=0.31.
- The impulse approximation rescatter amplitudes represent a correction of less than 15% to the dominant nucleon-pole contributions.
- Calculations were performed at E_lab=280 MeV with coplanar kinematics at θ_1=16.0° and θ_2=27.8°.
- Partial waves were summed to J_max=8 for the NN-interaction calculations.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Eden et al. (1993) that used only relativistic impulse approximation and shared the discrepancy with experiment reported by Michaelian et al. (1990) and others. It follows the indicative calculations of Taitor et al. (1978), who showed large contributions from Δ-resonance exchange currents at photon energies as low as 80 MeV, but improves upon them by including all interferences without soft-photon approximation. The results demonstrate that pp-bremsstrahlung cannot be used to test off-shell NN t-matrices in impulse approximation, but instead requires a microscopic theory with consistent treatment of strong form factors, NN-interaction, and exchange currents, enabling future study of resonance width effects and relativistic corrections.
