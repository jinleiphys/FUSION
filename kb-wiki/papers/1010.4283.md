# The Path to Improved Reaction Rates for Astrophysics

> This review focuses on nuclear reactions in astrophysics and, more specifically, on reactions with light ions (nucleons and alpha particles) proceeding via the strong interaction. It is intended to present the basic definitions essential for studies in nuclear astrophysics, to point out the differences between nuclear reactions taking place in stars and in a terrestrial laboratory, and to illustrate some of the challenges to be faced in theoretical and experimental studies of those reactions. The discussion revolves around the relevant quantities for astrophysics, which are the astrophysical reaction rates. The sensitivity of the reaction rates to the uncertainties in the prediction of various nuclear properties is explored and some guidelines for experimentalists are also provided.

## Key claim

This review argues that the standard Gaussian approximation for the Gamow window (the astrophysically relevant energy range for charged-particle reactions) is inadequate for most cases, and that the actual energy dependence of the integrand in the reaction rate must be numerically examined to derive reliable energy windows, which can be shifted by several MeV relative to the standard formula.

## Method

The paper reviews the basic definitions of astrophysical reaction rates, the derivation of stellar cross sections that account for reactions on thermally excited nuclear states, and the reciprocity relations between forward and reverse stellar rates. It then examines the energy dependence of the reaction rate integrand for compound and resonant reactions, showing that the cross section is governed by the smallest width among the entrance and exit channels, and that the standard Gamow window approximation (Eqs. 13-14) fails when the exit channel width dominates the energy dependence. The author presents numerical results from Ref. [energywindows] that provide revised effective energy ranges for astrophysics.

## Key numbers

- The standard approximation for the Gamow window location is \(E_{0} = 0.12204\left(m_{Aa} \tilde{Z}_a^{2}\tilde{Z}_A^{2}T_{9}^{2}\right)^{\frac{1}{3}}\) MeV, and for the width \(\Delta = 0.23682\left(m_{Aa} \tilde{Z}_a^{2}\tilde{Z}_A^{2}T_{9}^{5}\right)^{\frac{1}{6}}\) MeV (Eqs. 13-14).
- For the reaction \(^{112}\)Sn(p,\(\alpha\))\(^{109}\)In at \(T=5\) GK, the maximum of the actual integrand is shifted by several MeV to energies higher than the maximum \(E_0\) of the Gaussian approximation.
- For the reaction pair \(^{104}\)Pd(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{105}\)Ag and \(^{104}\)Pd(p,n)\(^{104}\)Ag at 2 GK, the energy windows are 1.5-2.8 MeV and 5.07-5.7 MeV, respectively, with the (p,n) threshold at 5.06 MeV.
- For neutron-induced reactions in the s process, the encountered maximum energies are 8-60 keV.
- For charged-particle reactions, relevant energy windows reach a few MeV for protons and several MeV up to about 10 MeV for \(\alpha\) particles.
- The paper considers plasma temperatures up to 5 GK for individual rates, and notes that above 10 GK, continuum effects require correction factors to the nuclear partition function with integration to \(E_\mathrm{max} \gg 35\) MeV.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on astrophysical reaction rates found in textbooks such as those by Rolfs and Rodney ("Cauldrons in the Cosmos") and Iliadis ("Nuclear Physics of Stars"), and addresses the oversimplification of the standard Gamow window approximation that has been widely used by experimentalists. The paper provides guidelines for experimentalists and theorists by showing that the effective energy ranges for reactions must be determined numerically (as done in Ref. [energywindows]) rather than from the standard formulae, and that different exit channels for the same entrance channel can have completely different energy windows. This enables more targeted experimental measurements and more reliable theoretical predictions for nucleosynthesis modeling.
