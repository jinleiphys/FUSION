# Level density for deformations of the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble

> Formulas are derived for the average level density of deformed, or transition, Gaussian orthogonal random matrix ensembles. After some general considerations about Gaussian ensembles we derive formulas for the average level density for (i) the transition from the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) to the Poisson ensemble and (ii) the transition from the GOE to $m$ GOEs.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit, numerically simple formulas for the average level density of two deformed Gaussian orthogonal ensembles, but the derivation relies on the approximation that unlinked binary associations dominate the averages of traces of powers of matrices, and for the GOE-to-Poisson transition the resulting formula shows small discrepancies in the transition region that can be corrected by enforcing exact second and fourth moments.

## Method

The authors extend Wigner's method of approximating averages of traces of powers of matrices by keeping only terms with unlinked binary associations of matrix indices. For the GOE-to-Poisson transition, they write the Hamiltonian as the sum of a diagonal Gaussian matrix (Poisson) and an off-diagonal GOE matrix, then compute the average level density as the Fourier transform of the product of the Fourier transforms of the two component densities. For the GOE-to-\(m\) GOEs transition, they treat the Hamiltonian as a block-diagonal matrix plus a coupling matrix and derive the level density as a weighted sum of semicircle laws for each block.

## Key numbers

- For the GOE-to-Poisson transition, the level density is given by Eq. (poigoe): \(\rho_\lambda(E) = \frac{2}{\pi}\frac{\sqrt{A}}{\Lambda}\int_0^\infty \frac{dx}{x} e^{-x^2/4\Lambda^2} J_1(x) \cos\frac{\sqrt{A}xE}{\Lambda}\), where \(\Lambda = \lambda\sqrt{N}\) and \(A = N/4\) for the calculations shown.
- The semicircle radius for the GOE is \(a = \sqrt{N/A}\); for the calculations with \(A = N/4\), the radius is \(a = 2\).
- For the GOE-to-Poisson transition, the largest deviation between the approximate formula and numerical diagonalization occurs around \(\Lambda \approx 1.5\) (corresponding to \(\lambda \approx 0.05\) for \(N=1000\)).
- For the GOE-to-\(m\) GOEs transition, the level density is \(\rho(E) = \sum_{i=1}^m \frac{M_i}{N} \rho_1(a_i, E)\) with \(a_i^2 = a^2\left[\frac{M_i}{N} + \lambda^2(1 - \frac{M_i}{N})\right]\), demonstrated for three blocks of sizes \(M_1=50\), \(M_2=400\), and \(M_3=550\) with total \(N=1000\).

## Context

This work extends the original deformed ensemble approach of Rosenzweig and Porter (1960) and the Stieltjes transform methods of Pastur (1972) and Pandey (1981) by providing explicit formulas that are simpler to evaluate numerically. The GOE-to-Poisson transition formula enables more accurate unfolding of fluctuation measures than the polynomial unfolding used in Sargeant (1999), while the GOE-to-\(m\) GOEs formula applies to symmetry-breaking problems in nuclear physics (e.g., isospin mixing as studied by Aberg (2004)) and in quartz block experiments (Ellegard (1996), Schaadt (2003)). The results also connect to the work of Kunz (1998) on ensembles of the form \(H = H_0 + (\Lambda/N^c)H_1\), where the asymptotic behavior depends critically on the exponent \(c\).
