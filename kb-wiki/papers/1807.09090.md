# Meson-baryon coupling constants of the SU(3) baryons with flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking

> We investigate the strong coupling constants for the baryon octet-octet, decuplet-octet, and decuplet-decuplet vertices with pseudoscalar mesons within a general framework of the chiral quark-soliton model, taking into account the effects of flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking to linear order in the expansion of the strange current quark mass. All relevant dynamical parameters are fixed by using the experimental data on hyperon semileptonic decays and the singlet axial-vector constant of the nucleon. The results of the strong coupling constants for the baryon octet and the pseudoscalar meson octet are compared with those determined from the Jülich-Bonn potential and the Nijmegen extended soft-core potential for hyperon-nucleon scattering. The results of the strong decay widths of the baryon decuplet are in good agreement with the experimental data. The effects of $SU_f(3)$ symmetry breaking are sizable on the $η'$ coupling constants. We predict also the strong coupling constants for the $Ω$ baryons.

## Key claim

The paper computes meson-baryon strong coupling constants for octet-octet, decuplet-octet, and decuplet-decuplet vertices within the chiral quark-soliton model, including flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking to linear order in the strange current quark mass, and finds that while the computed decay widths of the baryon decuplet agree well with experiment, the effects of SU(3) symmetry breaking are sizable on the eta-prime coupling constants.

## Method

The authors use the chiral quark-soliton model (chiQSM) to compute axial-vector transition constants between baryon multiplets, fixing all six dynamical parameters (a1 through a6) by fitting to five experimental hyperon semileptonic decay constants and the flavor-singlet axial-vector constant of the nucleon. They then relate the axial-vector Adler form factors C5^A(0) and h1(0) to pseudovector coupling constants via the partially conserved axial-vector current hypothesis, using experimental pion and kaon decay constants f_pi = 92.4 MeV and f_K = 113.0 MeV. For eta and eta-prime coupling constants, they apply octet-singlet mixing with mixing angle theta_p = -23.00 degrees.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar coupling constant g_piNN / sqrt(4pi) = 3.638 +/- 0.018 (with SU(3) breaking), compared to 3.639 from the Nijmegen ESC08a potential and 3.795 from the Julich-Bonn potential
- Pseudovector coupling constant f_piNDelta = 1.777 +/- 0.008 (with SU(3) breaking), compared to 1.68 from the Julich-Bonn potential
- Pseudovector coupling constant f_KXiOmega = 8.130 +/- 0.080 (with SU(3) breaking), predicted for the Omega baryon
- Decay width of Delta -> N pi: 88.58 +/- 1.31 MeV (total), compared to experimental 116-120 MeV
- Decay width of Sigma* -> Lambda pi: total 36.25 +/- 0.42 MeV for Sigma*+, compared to experimental 36.0 +/- 0.7 MeV
- Decay width of Xi*0 -> Xi pi: total 11.26 +/- 0.17 MeV, compared to experimental 9.1 +/- 0.5 MeV
- Ratio Gamma[Sigma* -> Sigma] / Gamma[Sigma* -> Lambda] = 0.180 +/- 0.002, compared to experimental 0.135 +/- 0.011
- Pseudoscalar coupling g_eta'NN / sqrt(4pi) = 0.611 +/- 0.088 (total with SU(3) breaking), compared to 2.443 from Nijmegen ESC08a
- Pseudoscalar coupling g_eta'LambdaLambda / sqrt(4pi) = 5.486 +/- 0.329 (total), compared to 4.634 from Nijmegen ESC08a

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of baryon mass splittings (Yang 2010, Yang 2011) and hyperon semileptonic decay constants (Yang 2015) within the chiral quark-soliton model, now computing all meson-baryon coupling constants without additional free parameters. The results are compared with those from the Nijmegen extended soft-core potential (ESC08a, Rijken 2010) and the Julich-Bonn hyperon-nucleon potential (Holzenkamp 1989, Haidenbauer 2005), which assumed exact SU(3) symmetry; the present work shows that SU(3) breaking effects are particularly important for eta-prime couplings. The predicted coupling constants for Omega baryons enable future studies of NOmega and OmegaOmega interactions relevant to new experimental programs at J-PARC and recent lattice QCD predictions of the dibaryon (OmegaOmega) by Gongyo (2017).
