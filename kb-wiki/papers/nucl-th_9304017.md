# Quasielastic neutrino scattering from oxygen and the atmospheric neutrino problem

> We examine several phenomena beyond the scope of Fermi-gas models that affect the quasielastic scattering (from oxygen) of neutrinos in the 0.1 -- 3.0 GeV range. These include Coulomb interactions of outgoing protons and leptons, a realistic finite-volume mean field, and the residual nucleon-nucleon interaction. None of these effects are accurately represented in the Monte Carlo simulations used to predict event rates due to $μ$ and $e$ neutrinos from cosmic-ray collisions in the atmosphere. We nevertheless conclude that the neglected physics cannot account for the anomalous $μ$ to $e$ ratio observed at Kamiokande and IMB, and is unlikely to change absolute event rates by more than 10--15\%. We briefly mention other phenomena, still to be investigated in detail, that may produce larger changes.

## Key claim

The authors examine several nuclear-physics effects beyond the Fermi-gas model that affect quasielastic neutrino scattering from oxygen in the 0.1–3.0 GeV range, and conclude that while these effects modify absolute event rates by up to 10–15%, they cannot account for the anomalous muon-to-electron ratio observed at Kamiokande and IMB.

## Method

The authors compare nonrelativistic Fermi-gas (NRFG) and relativistic Fermi-gas (RFG) models with a finite-volume Woods-Saxon potential model that includes bound states, resonances, and Coulomb interactions of outgoing protons and leptons. They then incorporate residual nucleon-nucleon interactions via Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations, both in nuclear matter (using a pi+rho+delta-function parameterization and a Landau-Migdal contact force) and in a finite-volume continuum RPA (using the Landau-Migdal force and the Bonn meson-exchange G-matrix). All calculations are folded with the atmospheric neutrino flux of Gaisser (1988) to obtain lepton yields as a function of outgoing lepton momentum.

## Key numbers

- The RFG and NRFG models agree to within about 10% for lepton momenta up to 950 MeV/c; for example, at 550 MeV/c the RFG/NRFG yield ratio for muons is 1.10 and for electrons is 1.10 (Table I).
- Coulomb repulsion of outgoing protons reduces the combined neutrino+antineutrino yield by about 3% across lepton momenta from 150 to 950 MeV/c (Table II).
- In nuclear matter, the Landau-Migdal force reduces yields by about 12% relative to the NRFG with binding (e.g., ratio 0.88 for muons at 550 MeV/c), while the pi+rho+delta-function force gives ratios close to 1.0 (e.g., 0.98 for muons at 550 MeV/c) (Table III).
- In the finite-volume continuum RPA, the Bonn potential gives yield ratios within a few percent of the free Woods-Saxon response (e.g., 0.99 for muons at 500 MeV/c), while the Landau-Migdal force gives ratios around 0.84–0.87 for lepton momenta 200–500 MeV/c (Table IV).
- The absolute RFG yields for muons at 550 MeV/c are 3.97 × 10⁻⁴¹ (s·MeV)⁻¹ and for electrons 2.07 × 10⁻⁴¹ (s·MeV)⁻¹ (Table I).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrino scattering from oxygen, particularly the RFG model of Smith and Moniz (1972) and the lower-energy study by Kuramoto (1990), by examining finite-volume effects and residual interactions that are not included in the Monte Carlo simulations used by the Kamiokande and IMB experiments. The results support the robustness of the muon-to-electron ratio as a measure of the atmospheric neutrino anomaly, and they identify two-nucleon knockout and excess pion production as phenomena still to be investigated that may produce larger changes in absolute rates.
