# Exploring Quark Matter

> These proceedings contain research results presented during the Workshops "Quark Matter in Astro- and Particle Physics" held at the University of Rostock, November 27-29, 2000 and "Dynamical Aspects of the QCD Phase Transition" held at the ECT* in Trento, March 12-15, 2001.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that plasma oscillations arise naturally from the back-reaction between a strong electric field and the particle-antiparticle pairs it creates via the Schwinger mechanism, and that the oscillation frequency depends on the initial field strength, the collision frequency, and the momentum-dependence of dressed-parton masses, but the quantitative results are presented in arbitrary units, so the claims are qualitative rather than numerically predictive.

## Method

The authors solve a quantum Vlasov equation with a relaxation-time approximation for the collision term, coupled self-consistently to Maxwell's equation for the electric field. They model a relativistic heavy-ion collision using an impulse profile for the external field, compute the single-particle distribution function for fermions, and extract the time evolution of the electric field. They also incorporate momentum-dependent dressed-quark masses from a simple Dyson-Schwinger equation model to study the effect on plasma oscillations.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density expected at RHIC: ε ∼ 10–100 GeV/fm³.
- String tension from lattice QCD: σ ∼ 4 Λ_QCD² ∼ 1 GeV/fm.
- Critical temperature for deconfinement in the DSE model: T_c = 0.17 GeV.
- Current-quark mass used in the DSE model: m_0 = 5 MeV.
- Mass-scale parameter in the DSE model: η = 1.33 GeV.
- Constituent-quark mass at T_c from the DSE model: m(0,T_c) ≈ 0.35 GeV.
- Impulse profile parameters used in the collisionless case: A_0 = 10, b = 0.5 (arbitrary units).
- Relaxation times used in the collisional case: τ_r = 5.0 (arbitrary units) and others compared.

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube picture of particle production (Nussinov, 1979) and the Schwinger mechanism (Schwinger, 1951) by incorporating back-reaction and plasma oscillations, building on earlier studies by Kluger et al. (1991) and Kajantie and Matsui (1985). It uses a quantum kinetic equation approach developed by Rau and Müller (1996) and Schmidt et al. (1999), and introduces a relaxation-time approximation for collisions following Bloch et al. (1999). The results enable future studies of non-equilibrium QGP evolution and the role of momentum-dependent masses in heavy-ion collisions.
