# Natural orbitals for the ab initio no-core configuration interaction approach

> Ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for the nuclear many-body problem have traditionally relied upon an antisymmetrized product (Slater determinant) basis built from harmonic oscillator orbitals. The accuracy of such calculations is limited by the finite dimensions which are computationally feasible for the truncated many-body space. We therefore seek to improve the accuracy obtained for a given basis size by optimizing the choice of single-particle orbitals. Natural orbitals, which diagonalize the one-body density matrix, provide a basis which maximizes the occupation of low-lying orbitals, thus accelerating convergence in a configuration-interaction basis, while also possibly providing physical insight into the single-particle structure of the many-body wave function. We describe the implementation of natural orbitals in the NCCI framework, and examine the nature of the natural orbitals thus obtained, the properties of the resulting many-body wave functions, and the convergence of observables. After taking $^3\mathrm{He}$ as an illustrative testbed, we explore aspects of NCCI calculations with natural orbitals for the ground state of the $p$-shell neutron halo nucleus $^6\mathrm{He}$.

## Key claim
Natural orbitals, obtained by diagonalizing the one-body density matrix from an approximate initial NCCI calculation in a harmonic oscillator basis, can accelerate convergence of subsequent NCCI calculations for a given basis size, but the improvement is conditional on the initial approximate solution yielding useful approximate densities, and the resulting basis sacrifices the exact center-of-mass factorization guaranteed by the traditional oscillator basis.

## Method
The authors implement symmetry-adapted natural orbitals within the NCCI framework by first performing a traditional Nmax-truncated harmonic oscillator basis calculation for a reference many-body state, then constructing the rotational scalar one-body density matrix and diagonalizing it separately within each lj subspace to obtain natural orbitals as unitary transformations on the original oscillator radial wave functions. These natural orbitals are then used as the single-particle basis for a subsequent NCCI calculation, with the Daejeon16 internucleon interaction applied to 3He and 6He.

## Context
This work extends the use of natural orbitals from atomic and molecular electron-structure theory (Lowdin 1955, Davidson 1972) into ab initio nuclear NCCI calculations, building on preliminary results reported by Constantinou (2017) which used the JISP16 interaction rather than the Daejeon16 interaction used here. The approach is complementary to alternative methods where natural orbitals are obtained from a deuteron problem (Puddu 2018) or from many-body perturbation theory for closed-shell nuclei (Tichai 2019, Hoppe 2021), and it enables improved convergence for neutron halo nuclei such as 6He while raising open questions about center-of-mass factorization in non-oscillator bases.
