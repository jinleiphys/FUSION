# $K_1/K^*$ enhancement as a signature of chiral symmetry restoration in heavy ion collisions

> Based on the fact that the mass difference between the chiral partners is an order parameter of chiral phase transition and that the chiral order parameter reduces substantially at the chemical freeze-out point in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, we argue that the production ratio of $K_1$ over $K^*$ in such collisions should be substantially larger than that predicted in the statistical hadronization model. We further show that while the enhancement effect might be contaminated by the relatively larger decrease of $K_1$ meson than $K^*$ meson during the hadronic phase, the signal will be visible through a systematic study on centrality as the kinetic freeze-out temperature is higher and the hadronic life time shorter in peripheral collisions than in central collisions.

## Key claim

The production ratio of $K_1$ over $K^*$ in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions should be substantially larger than the statistical hadronization model prediction because the mass difference between these chiral partners is an order parameter of chiral phase transition and the chiral order parameter is substantially reduced at chemical freeze-out; however, the enhancement signal may be contaminated by the larger hadronic absorption of $K_1$ relative to $K^*$ during the hadronic phase, but should remain visible through systematic centrality studies since peripheral collisions have higher kinetic freeze-out temperatures and shorter hadronic lifetimes.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian using the massive Yang-Mills approach to describe interactions of vector and axial-vector mesons, including $K_1$, $K^*$, $K$, $\pi$, and $\rho$ mesons. They determined coupling constants and mixing angles for the $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$ states (and corresponding $K^*$ states) by fitting to six experimental decay widths. They then calculated cross sections for four $K_1$ absorption processes ($K_1\pi\rightarrow K\pi$, $K_1\pi\rightarrow K^*\rho$, $K_1\rho\rightarrow K^*\pi$, $K_1\rho\rightarrow K\rho$) using these amplitudes with form factors and a cutoff parameter $\Lambda = 1.8$ GeV. Finally, they constructed a rate equation to model the time evolution of $K_1$ abundance during the hadronic phase, incorporating both dissociation and regeneration via detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature for QCD chiral crossovers at zero baryon chemical potential is $156.5 \pm 1.5$ MeV from lattice calculations.
- The vacuum decay widths used for fitting: $\Gamma_{K_1(1270)\rightarrow K\rho} = 37.8$ MeV (experimental), $\Gamma_{K_1(1270)\rightarrow K^*\pi} = 14.4$ MeV (experimental), $\Gamma_{K_1(1400)\rightarrow K\rho} = 5.2$ MeV (experimental), $\Gamma_{K_1(1400)\rightarrow K^*\pi} = 163$ MeV (experimental).
- Best-fit coupling parameters: $g_A = 15.5$, $g_B = -10.4$, $\cos\theta_V = 0.6469$, $\sin\theta_V = 0.7626$, $\cos\theta_A = 0.724$, $\sin\theta_A = -0.6898$.
- The cutoff parameter for form factors is set to $\Lambda = 1.8$ GeV.
- The coupling constant $g = 8.49$ is determined from fitting the experimental decay width of $\rho \rightarrow \pi\pi$, yielding $g_{\rho\pi\pi} = g_{\rho\rho\rho} = 6.006$ and $g_{KK\rho} = 3.003$.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Song (2018) that $K_1$ and $K^*$ are ideal chiral partners for studying partial chiral symmetry restoration because both have vacuum widths smaller than 100 MeV. It builds on lattice QCD results for the pseudo-critical temperature (Bazavov 2018, Borsanyi 2010) and statistical hadronization model analyses of chemical freeze-out (Andronic 2012, Stachel 2013). The study enables experimental searches for chiral symmetry restoration signals in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC through centrality-dependent measurements of the $K_1/K^*$ ratio, complementing earlier work on $K^+/\pi^+$ and $(\Lambda+\Sigma^0)/\pi^-$ ratios (Cassing 2015).
