# Microscopic calculations of transport properties of neutron matter

> We discuss the results of calculations of the shear viscosity and thermal conductivity of pure neutron matter, carried out within the Landau-Abrikosov-Khalatnikov formalism. The probability of neutron-neutron collisions in the nuclear medium has been obtained from a realistic potential, using both the correlated basis function and the $G$-matrix approach. The results of our work indicate that medium modifications of nucleon-nucleon scattering are large, their inclusion leading to a dramatic enhancement of the transport coefficients. On the other hand, the results obtained from the two theoretical schemes appear to be in fairly good agreement.

## Key claim
Medium modifications of nucleon-nucleon scattering in pure neutron matter are large, and their inclusion leads to a dramatic enhancement of the shear viscosity and thermal conductivity; however, the results obtained from the correlated basis function (CBF) and G-matrix approaches are in fairly good agreement with each other, suggesting that the model dependence from the many-body approach is not critical as long as a realistic NN potential is used.

## Method
The shear viscosity and thermal conductivity of pure neutron matter were calculated within the Landau-Abrikosov-Khalatnikov formalism, using in-medium neutron-neutron collision probabilities derived from the truncated Argonne v6' potential. The effective interactions were obtained via two distinct many-body approaches: the correlated basis function (CBF) method and the G-matrix approach (solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation with the continuous prescription). The transport coefficients were then computed from the angle-averaged scattering probabilities, including the exact Brooker-Sykes corrections to the leading-order Abrikosov-Khalatnikov results.

## Key numbers
- At nuclear matter equilibrium density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3), the shear viscosity times T^2 (eta T^2) obtained from the CBF and G-matrix effective interactions is approximately 1.4 x 10^15 g cm^-1 s^-1 MeV^2, compared to about 6 x 10^13 g cm^-1 s^-1 MeV^2 from the Skyrme SLy4 interaction.
- At nuclear matter equilibrium density, the thermal conductivity times T (kappa T) obtained from the CBF and G-matrix formalisms is approximately 4 x 10^31 erg cm^-1 s^-1, compared to about 4 x 10^30 erg cm^-1 s^-1 from the Skyrme SLy4 interaction.
- At center-of-mass energies above 100 MeV, the in-medium total neutron-neutron cross section at nuclear matter equilibrium density is quenched by a factor of about 3 relative to the free-space cross section.
- The difference between the total cross sections obtained from the CBF and G-matrix approaches does not exceed about 20% at center-of-mass energies above 10 MeV.
- The partial wave expansion converges such that including only l=0 and l=1 partial waves yields a total cross section within less than 5% of the fully converged result.
- The effective masses from the CBF and G-matrix approaches differ by no more than a few percent in the density range studied.

## Context
This work extends the Landau-Abrikosov-Khalatnikov formalism (Abrikosov and Khalatnikov, 1959) to pure neutron matter using realistic effective interactions, contrasting with earlier studies that neglected medium modifications and used free-space NN scattering phase shifts (e.g., Flowers and Itoh, 1976; Haensel, 1991). The results enable more reliable predictions of neutron star properties, such as the damping of gravitational-wave-driven r-mode instabilities and neutron star cooling, by providing transport coefficients from a consistent many-body framework. The paper also highlights that Skyrme-type effective interactions (e.g., SLy4), while useful for bulk properties, yield transport coefficients that are significantly lower than those from realistic potential-based approaches.
