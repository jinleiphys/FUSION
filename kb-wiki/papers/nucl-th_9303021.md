# The Quasi-Elastic Nuclear Response

> We explore the nuclear responses at intermediate energies, particularly in the spin longitudinal and spin transverse isovector channels, within the continuum random phase approximation framework. We also employ an extension of the standard random phase approximation to account for the spreading width of the single particle states through the inclusion of a complex and energy-dependent nucleon self-energy. The nuclear responses are then used as the basic ingredient to calculate hadronic reactions in the Glauber theory framework. Here both one and two-step contributions to the multiple scattering series in the quasi-elastic peak region are taken into account. We find evidence for shell effects in the one-step response and a strong dependence on the momentum regime of the two-step contribution.

# The Quasi-Elastic Nuclear Response

## Key claim
The spin-longitudinal and spin-transverse isovector nuclear responses in the quasi-elastic peak region show evidence for shell effects in the one-step contribution and a strong dependence on the momentum regime of the two-step contribution, but the RPA correlations alone miss the correct q-dependence of the strength when compared to electron scattering data, suggesting that contributions beyond the particle-hole Hilbert space (such as 2p-2h and meson exchange current terms) are needed.

## Method
The authors calculated the spin-isospin nuclear response functions within the continuum random phase approximation (RPA) framework, using a particle-hole interaction that includes the Landau-Migdal parameter g', pion exchange, and rho meson exchange. They extended the standard RPA by incorporating a complex, energy-dependent nucleon self-energy to account for the spreading width of single-particle states, using phenomenological formulas fitted to particle widths in medium-heavy nuclei. The nuclear responses were then used as input to calculate hadronic reactions within the Glauber theory framework, including both one-step and two-step contributions to the multiple scattering series in the quasi-elastic peak region.

## Key numbers
- The Landau-Migdal parameter g' was set to 0.7 throughout the calculations.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used were: W0 = -54.8 MeV, Wso = -10 MeV, R = 1.27 A^(1/2) fm, a = 0.67 fm.
- The phenomenological spreading width parameters were: alpha = 10.75 MeV, epsilon_0 = 18 MeV, epsilon_1 = 110 MeV.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant was f_pi^2/(4 pi hbar c) = 0.08, and the rho coupling was f_rho^2/mu_rho^2 = 2.18 (f_pi^2/mu_pi^2).
- Cut-off parameters for the monopole form factors were Lambda_pi = 1300 MeV and Lambda_rho = 1700 MeV.
- The proton probe energy was 795 MeV for the (p,n) and (p,p') reactions analyzed.
- The maxima of (p,n) cross-sections appear hardened by about 25 MeV with respect to those observed in (p,p') reactions.
- Surface responses were calculated for effective total cross-sections of 30 mb and 40 mb, at momentum transfers q = 1.54 fm^(-1) and q = 2.31 fm^(-1).

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of spin-isospin responses in the bound state RPA framework (Alb87, Toki87, Alb88, De Pace 1990, De Pace 1991) and continuum RPA (Arima 1988, Ichimura 1989) by incorporating the spreading width of single-particle states through a complex self-energy, following the approach of Smith and Wambach (1988). The authors test their model against deep inelastic electron scattering data on 40Ca from Saclay (Meziani 1985) and charge-exchange (p,n) data at Los Alamos (Taddeucci 1991), as well as (p,p') data (Chrien 1980). The work enables future inclusion of 2p-2h and meson exchange current contributions to improve agreement with experimental data, and provides a framework for analyzing reactions with composite probes such as (d,2p) and (3He,t).
