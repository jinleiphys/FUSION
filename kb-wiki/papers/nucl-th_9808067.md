# Saturation Properties of Nuclear Matter with a Nonlocal Confining Solitons

> We examine saturation properties of a quark-based picture of nuclear matter. Soliton matter consisting of nonlocal confining solitons is used to model nuclear matter. Each composite nucleon is described by a non-topological soliton as given by the Global Color Model. We apply techniques and concepts from the discription of crystal lattices. In particular, the Wigner-Seitz approximation is used to calculate the properties of the soliton lattice at the mean-field level. We focus on infinite nuclear matter at around standard nuclear matter density with the simplest one-parameter assumption for the gluon propagator. the saturation density and incompressibility are calculated as functions of the single parameter of the model

## Key claim
Using a one-parameter nonlocal confining soliton model (the Global Color Model) with the Wigner-Seitz approximation, the authors find that nuclear matter saturation density and incompressibility fall in a reasonable range, but caution that the kinetic energy of the soliton as a whole is neglected and the agreement does not signal a deep physical understanding of nuclear matter at the quark level.

## Method
The authors model infinite nuclear matter as a lattice of non-topological solitons from the Global Color Model (GCM), using a delta-function gluon propagator with a single strength parameter α. They apply the Wigner-Seitz approximation to replace the cubic primitive cell with a spherical cell of radius R, solve the Dirac equation for quarks and the Klein-Gordon equation for the meson field self-consistently in momentum and coordinate space, and compute the total soliton energy as a function of density for three values of α (1.25, 1.35, and 1.45 GeV).

## Key numbers
- Saturation density ρ_min/ρ_0 = 0.66 ± 0.10 for α = 1.25 GeV, 0.73 ± 0.11 for α = 1.35 GeV, and 0.98 ± 0.15 for α = 1.45 GeV, where ρ_0 = 0.17 fm⁻³ is standard nuclear matter density.
- Incompressibility K = 137 ± 30 MeV for α = 1.25 GeV, 220 ± 40 MeV for α = 1.35 GeV, and 309 ± 50 MeV for α = 1.45 GeV.
- The total energy at the minimum for α = 1.25 GeV is 1844 MeV; subtracting center-of-mass motion yields an estimated soliton mass of approximately 1560 MeV.
- The root mean square proton radius from the single soliton is 0.71 fm (α = 1.25 GeV), 0.67 fm (α = 1.35 GeV), and 0.64 fm (α = 1.45 GeV); the experimental value is approximately 0.83 fm.
- The pion decay constant f_π = 111 MeV (α = 1.25 GeV), 120 MeV (α = 1.35 GeV), and 129 MeV (α = 1.45 GeV); the experimental value is 93 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the GCM soliton lattice that focused on the quark deconfinement transition at high density (Johnson and Fai, 1997; Johnson, Fai, and Frank, 1996) by examining saturation properties near standard nuclear density. The approach applies condensed-matter techniques (Wigner-Seitz approximation, originally from Wigner and Seitz, 1933) to a quark-based model, following earlier soliton-matter studies by Banerjee (1985), Reinhardt (1985), and Achtzehnter (1985). The results enable future calculations of in-medium properties such as the axial-vector coupling constant and correlation functions, and motivate the use of more realistic gluon propagators (Tandy, 1997) to simultaneously fit the pion decay constant and proton radius.
