# Multiple-quantized vortices in rotating LOFF state of ultracold Fermi superfluid gas

> A rotating ultracold S-wave superfluid Fermi gas is considered, when the population imbalance (or equivalently the mismatch in chemical potentials) corresponds to the Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) state in the vicinity of the Lifshitz critical point. It is shown that under these conditions the critical angular velocity in two-dimensional systems is an oscillating function of temperature and population imbalance giving rise to reentrant superfluid phases. This leads to vortex lattices with multiple-quantized circulation quanta. The reason for this behavior is the population by Cooper pairs of the Landau levels above the lowest one.

# Multiple-quantized vortices in rotating LOFF state of ultracold Fermi superfluid gas

## Key claim

The critical angular velocity Ωc2 for the transition from normal to superfluid state in a two-dimensional rotating ultracold Fermi gas near the Lifshitz critical point is an oscillatory function of temperature and population imbalance, leading to reentrant superfluid phases and vortex lattices with multiple-quantized circulation quanta. This behavior arises specifically under the quasiclassical condition ħΩc2 < π(kBTc)²/μ, where the effect is due to population of higher Landau levels by Cooper pairs rather than normal-state Landau quantization.

## Method

The authors consider a two-component ultracold S-wave Fermi gas in a quasi-two-dimensional magnetic trap under rotation, with population imbalance corresponding to the LOFF phase near the Lifshitz critical point in the T-δμ plane. They linearize the gap equation near the second-order transition line and perform a small-q expansion of the kernel K0(q) up to fourth order in momentum, using the di-gamma function and its derivatives to express the expansion coefficients k2 and k4. The eigenvalue problem for the Hamiltonian Ĥ = (p̂ - 2MVΩ)²/2M is solved, yielding the critical angular velocity Ωc2,±(n) for each Landau level index n.

## Key numbers

- The Lifshitz critical point occurs at t* = 0.56 and δμ̄* = 1.04, where t = T/Tc0 and δμ̄ = δμ/Tc0
- The magnitude of Ωc2(n) near the Lifshitz point is estimated as Ωc2(n) ~ (10⁻¹ - 10⁻²)(Tc²/EF)
- For superfluid ⁶Li with EF ~ 2 μK and Tc ~ EF/10, the predicted Ωc2(n) is less than 30-300 s⁻¹
- The quasiclassical condition for the oscillatory effect is ħΩc2 < π(kBTc)²/μ

## Context

This work extends studies of the LOFF phase in ultracold Fermi gases, which was previously investigated in the context of phase separation in magnetic traps by Machida and Torma (2006) and in optical lattices by Koponen et al. (2007). The rotating LOFF state provides an analog to orbital effects in metallic superconductors, where similar oscillatory behavior of the upper critical field was studied by Gruenberg and Gunther (1966) and Bulaevskii (1973). The results enable experimental investigation of multiply-quantized vortex lattices in ultracold Fermi gases and may be relevant to rotating two-dimensional metallic superconductors in parallel magnetic fields and color-superconducting quark matter.
