# Relativistic Effects in Exclusive pd Breakup Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> The relativistic Faddeev equation for three-nucleon scattering is formulated in momentum space and directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. Relativistic invariance is achieved by constructing a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group on the three-nucleon Hilbert space. The exclusive breakup reaction at 508 MeV is calculated based on a Malfliet-Tjon type of two-body interaction and the cross sections are compared to measured cross sections at this energy. We find that the magnitude of the relativistic effects can be quite large and depends on the configurations considered. In spite of the simple nature of the model interaction, the experimental cross sections are in surprisingly good agreement with the predictions of the relativistic calculations. We also find that although for specific configurations the multiple scattering series converges rapidly, this is in general not the case.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in exclusive pd breakup at 508 MeV can be quite large and depend strongly on the specific angular configuration, but despite using only a simple Malfliet-Tjon type two-body interaction, the relativistic calculations show surprisingly good agreement with experimental cross sections.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Faddeev equation for three-nucleon scattering directly in momentum space using vector variables without partial wave decomposition, constructing a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group on the three-nucleon Hilbert space. A Malfliet-Tjon type two-body interaction (superposition of attractive and repulsive Yukawa potentials) was used, and the nonrelativistic and relativistic interactions were made phase-shift equivalent by adding the interaction to the square of the mass operator. The five-fold laboratory differential cross section for exclusive breakup was calculated and compared to experimental data from the Saturne-2 facility at a projectile kinetic energy of 508 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Projectile kinetic energy: 508 MeV for the exclusive pd breakup reaction
- For symmetric angle pairs at 38.1 degrees and 41.5 degrees, relativistic cross sections are considerably larger than nonrelativistic ones; for angle pairs around 44 degrees and larger, relativistic cross sections fall below nonrelativistic ones
- For the coplanar star configuration at x_q = 1 (one particle ejected forward), the first-order multiple scattering result is about two orders of magnitude higher than the fully converged result
- The relativistic Faddeev equation calculations converge well up to 2 GeV, indicating applicability of the vector-variable formulation for intermediate energies

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic momentum-space Faddeev equation without partial waves developed by Hang and collaborators to the relativistic domain, and builds on the formulation of Poincaré invariant Faddeev equations detailed in Lin (2007). It contrasts with the partial-wave-based relativistic Faddeev calculations of Witala and collaborators below pion-production threshold, and with the nonrelativistic calculations using high-precision NN forces such as AV18, CD-Bonn, and Nijmegen potentials. The results enable future calculations with realistic spin-dependent interactions at intermediate energies and demonstrate that the multiple scattering series does not converge reliably at these energies, requiring full solution of the Faddeev equation.
