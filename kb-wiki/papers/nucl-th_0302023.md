# Relativistic Quantum Mechanics - Particle Production and Cluster Properties

> This paper constructs relativistic quantum mechanical models of particles satisfying cluster properties and the spectral condition which do not conserve particle number. The treatment of particle production is limited to systems with a bounded number of bare-particle degrees of freedom. The focus of this paper is about the realization of cluster properties in these theories.

## Key claim

This paper constructs relativistic quantum mechanical models that satisfy cluster properties and the spectral condition while allowing particle production, but only for systems with a bounded number of bare-particle degrees of freedom. The construction deliberately avoids the infinite-degree-of-freedom problems of quantum field theory by using conserved additive quantum numbers to limit the Hilbert space.

## Method

The author generalizes the fixed-N construction of Poincaré-invariant quantum theories by replacing the fixed number of constituent particles with a set of conserved additive quantum numbers (called charges) that can only take non-negative integer values. The Hilbert space for a given set of charges is a direct sum of tensor products of irreducible representation spaces of the covering group of the Poincaré group, where each term in the direct sum has a different bare-particle content but the same total charge. Cluster properties are formulated using partitions of the conserved charges, with the key technical modification that the Hilbert space factorizes as a tensor product of subsystem spaces plus a residual orthogonal complement that accounts for bare particles with charges in different clusters.

## Key numbers

- The Hilbert space for a given charge value {N} = (n_1, ..., n_K) is a direct sum: H_{N} = ⊕_{i=1}^n (⊗_{k=1}^{n_i} H_{m_{ik} j_{ik}}), where H_{mj} is the mass m, spin j irreducible representation space of ISL(2,C)
- In the Lee model example, the Hilbert space for charges (1,1) is H_{1,1} = (H_N ⊗ H_π) ⊕ H_Δ, a direct sum of the two-particle N-π space and the one-particle Δ space
- The Möbius function for the partial ordering of partitions is given by μ(a ⊇ b) = (-)^{n_a} ∏_{i=1}^{n_a} (-)^{n_{b_i}}(n_{b_i}-1)! for a ⊇ b, and 0 otherwise

## Context

This work extends the fixed-N construction of Poincaré-invariant quantum theories with cluster properties developed by Sokolov, and by Coester, Polyzou, and collaborators in the 1980s-1990s. The paper specifically generalizes the construction in Polyzou (1997) to treat variable numbers of particles, using conserved charges to limit the number of bare-particle degrees of freedom. This enables mathematically well-defined models that complement formal quantum field theory approaches, with applications to few-body reactions at accelerators such as TJNAF, and provides a framework for extending the relativistic few-body program to reactions involving particle production.
