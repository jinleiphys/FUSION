# Probing the Phase Boundary between Hadronic Matter and the Quark-Gluon-Plasma in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We discuss recent data on particle production with emphasis on the degree of thermal and chemical equilibration achieved. The data are interpreted in terms of a resonance gas model. The phase boundary constructed between the resonance gas and the quark-gluon plasma is shown to be very close to the deduced parameters characterizing the hadronic fireball at freeze-out.

# Probing the Phase Boundary between Hadronic Matter and the Quark-Gluon-Plasma in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The freeze-out parameters extracted from hadron abundance data at AGS and SPS energies lie very close to the calculated phase boundary between hadronic matter and the quark-gluon plasma, suggesting that the phase boundary was reached or crossed during the fireball evolution, even though the system is purely hadronic at freeze-out. This proximity is presented as indirect evidence, contingent on confirmation of chemical equilibration for heavy systems such as Au+Au and Pb+Pb.

## Method

The authors constructed the phase boundary by equating the chemical potential and pressure of a hadron resonance gas (containing all known baryons and mesons up to 2 GeV and 1.5 GeV, respectively, with excluded volume corrections for baryon interactions) with those of a non-interacting quark-gluon plasma (massless gluons, u and d quarks, and strange quarks with m_s = 150 MeV, using a bag constant B = 262 MeV/fm^3). They then fitted hadron abundance ratios from AGS and SPS experiments with Si and S beams using a thermal model with two thermodynamic variables (temperature T and baryon chemical potential mu_B) to extract freeze-out parameters.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at zero baryon density from lattice QCD: T_c = 145 +/- 10 MeV
- Freeze-out parameters at AGS energy: mu_B = 0.54 GeV, T between 120 and 140 MeV
- Freeze-out parameters at SPS energy: mu_B = 0.17 (0.18) GeV, T = 160 (170) MeV
- Baryon density at freeze-out for both AGS and SPS: rho_B approximately 0.06/fm^3
- Pion density at freeze-out: rho_pi approximately 0.08/fm^3 (AGS, T = 120 MeV) and rho_pi approximately 0.30/fm^3 (SPS, T = 160 MeV)
- Transition temperature at mu_B = 0.8 GeV: T_c approximately 120 MeV
- Softest point in equation of state at mu_B = 0.54 GeV: energy density epsilon approximately 1.2 GeV/fm^3, pressure P approximately 50 MeV/fm^3
- Predicted K^+/pi^+ ratio for RHIC: about 19%
- Predicted nucleon plus anti-nucleon count for RHIC fireball volume V = 9524 fm^3: about 170

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analysis of Braun-Munzinger, Stachel, Wessels, and Xu (1994, 1996) to systematically compare freeze-out parameters with the phase boundary constructed from lattice QCD results (DeTar, 1995) and a resonance gas model. The finding that freeze-out parameters lie close to the phase boundary builds on the equation-of-state approach of Hung and Shuryak (1994). The paper enables future experiments at RHIC and LHC to test whether chemical equilibration of strangeness persists for heavy systems, which would provide indirect evidence for either crossing the phase boundary or achieving partial restoration of chiral symmetry with reduced hadron masses, as proposed by Ko, Wu, Xia, and Brown (1991). The alternative interpretation of in-medium mass changes is contested by Lutz, Steiner, and Weise (1994), who find that the K^+ mass may increase with baryon density.
