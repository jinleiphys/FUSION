# The Onset of Nuclear Structure Effects in Near-Barrier Elastic Scattering of Weakly-Bound Nuclei: $^6$He and $^6$Li Compared

> The elastic scattering of the halo nucleus $^6$He from heavy targets at incident energies near the Coulomb barrier displays a marked deviation from the standard Fresnel-type diffraction behavior. This deviation is due to the strong Coulomb dipole breakup coupling produced by the Coulomb field of the heavy target, a specific feature of the nuclear structure of $^6$He. We have performed Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels calculations for the elastic scattering of $^{6}$He and $^6$Li from $^{58}$Ni, $^{120}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm, $^{181}$Ta and $^{208}$Pb targets in order to determine the range of $Z_{\mathrm T}$ where this nuclear-structure specific coupling effect becomes manifest. We find that the strong Coulomb dipole breakup coupling effect is only clearly experimentally distinguishable for targets of $Z_{\mathrm T} \approx 80$.

## Key claim

The strong Coulomb dipole breakup coupling effect that causes the elastic scattering of the halo nucleus $^6$He to deviate from standard Fresnel-type diffraction is only clearly experimentally distinguishable from the analogous $^6$Li scattering for targets with atomic number $Z_{\mathrm T} \approx 80$, and only when the incident energy is a few MeV above the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations were performed for the elastic scattering of $^6$He and $^6$Li from five targets ($^{58}$Ni, $^{120}$Sn, $^{144}$Sm, $^{181}$Ta, $^{208}$Pb) at two energies corresponding to $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B}$ values of 1.005 and 5.534 MeV. For $^6$He, a modified two-body di-neutron ($\alpha + ^2n$) cluster model was used with the binding energy of the di-neutron increased to 1.6 MeV to match more sophisticated three-body models; the continuum was discretized into bins up to a maximum excitation energy of 7.7 MeV including $L=0,1,2,3$ partial waves and the 1.8 MeV $2^+$ resonant state. For $^6$Li, similar CDCC calculations were performed using an $\alpha + d$ cluster model. All calculations used the code Fresco with single-folded interaction potentials and global optical potentials for the $\alpha$+target and $^2n$/deuteron+target systems.

## Key numbers

- The critical target atomic number for clearly distinguishable structure-specific coupling effects is $Z_{\mathrm T} \approx 80$.
- For $^{58}$Ni ($Z_{\mathrm T} = 28$), the calculated $^6$Li and $^6$He angular distributions are "absolutely identical" when plotted as a function of $\theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - \theta_{\mathrm g}$.
- For $^{144}$Sm ($Z_{\mathrm T} = 62$) at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B} = 1.005$ MeV, a complete lack of Coulomb rainbow is observed for $^6$He, but the difference from $^6$Li is not experimentally detectable.
- For $^{181}$Ta ($Z_{\mathrm T} = 73$) at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B} = 1.005$ MeV, the difference would be "barely detectable" with measurements at $\pm 1\%$ precision for $^6$Li and $\pm 2\%$ for $^6$He.
- For $^{208}$Pb ($Z_{\mathrm T} = 82$) at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} - V_{\mathrm B} = 1.005$ MeV, a "clearly measurable difference" between $^6$He and $^6$Li is seen; these energies correspond to incident $^6$Li energy of 33.06 MeV and $^6$He energy of 22.38 MeV.
- The CDCC calculations used a maximum excitation energy of $\epsilon = 7.7$ MeV for the $^6$He continuum, with bin widths $\triangle k = 0.1$ fm$^{-1}$, and integrated coupled equations up to $R = 80$ fm with 200 partial waves.

## Context

This work extends previous observations of anomalous elastic scattering for $^6$He from heavy targets (Rus03, Mor07) and for $^{18}$O + $^{184}$W (Tho77), where strong Coulomb coupling effects were identified. The study systematically maps the target charge dependence of the structure-specific Coulomb dipole breakup coupling effect, showing that it only becomes clearly distinguishable for targets with $Z_{\mathrm T} \approx 80$, and that $^6$Li provides a benchmark for comparison because its similar effect (from $E2$ breakup, since $E1$ is forbidden for $^6$Li $\rightarrow \alpha + d$) is much weaker. The results enable future experiments on other weakly-bound radioactive nuclei (e.g., $^{11}$Li, $^{11}$Be) to focus on heavy targets like $^{208}$Pb at energies a few MeV above the Coulomb barrier to maximize the visibility of nuclear-structure-dependent coupling effects.
