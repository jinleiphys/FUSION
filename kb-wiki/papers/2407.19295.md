# On the determination of the $D$ meson width in the nuclear medium with the transparency ratio

> We have studied the feasibility of the experimental determination of the width of a $D$ meson in a nuclear medium by using the method of the nuclear transparency. The cross section for inclusive production of a $D^+$ in different nuclei is evaluated, taking care of the $D^+$ absorption in the nucleus, or equivalently, the survival probability of the $D^+$ in its way out of the nucleus from the point of production. We use present values of the in medium width of $D$ mesons and calculate ratios of the cross sections for different nuclei to the $^{12} \text{C} $ nucleus as reference. We find ratios of the order of $0.6$ for heavy nuclei, a large deviation from unity, which indicates that the method proposed is adequate to measure this relevant magnitude, so far only known theoretically.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the nuclear transparency ratio method can determine the in-medium width of the D meson, which is currently only known theoretically. The authors find that the transparency ratio deviates substantially from unity (reaching about 0.6 for heavy nuclei), but this result depends on using present theoretical values for the D meson width and assumes that the imaginary part of the D meson selfenergy is dominated by genuine absorption rather than quasielastic scattering.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for the inclusive reaction gamma A -> D+ D- A' in different nuclei (12C, 40Ca, 93Nb, 208Pb) using a Monte Carlo integration of the phase space for the elementary process gamma N -> D+ D- N. They evaluate the survival probability of the D+ meson as it travels through the nucleus using the imaginary part of the D+ selfenergy in nuclear matter from Tolos (2009), and compute the transparency ratio normalized to 12C. The calculations are performed for photon energies around sqrt(s) = 10 GeV to maximize the fraction of D+ mesons with lab momentum below 2 GeV, and the authors also test the sensitivity by using realistic nuclear density profiles and a momentum-dependent production amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The minimum sqrt(s) for gamma N -> D+ D- N is 4.678 GeV, corresponding to a photon lab momentum of about 11.17 GeV
- The imaginary part of the D+ selfenergy at nuclear saturation density and p_D,lab = 1 GeV is taken as -Im Pi = 0.1 GeV^2, which gives a D width in the medium of about 47 MeV
- The optimal photon lab momentum for producing D+ mesons with p_D,lab < 2 GeV is about 50 GeV, corresponding to sqrt(s) = 10 GeV
- The transparency ratio for 208Pb relative to 12C reaches values of about 0.6 for the nominal selfenergy value
- Using realistic nuclear densities and a momentum-dependent amplitude, the transparency ratio for 208Pb changes by about 5% compared to the simplified treatment
- The minimum photon lab momentum needed to produce low-momentum D+ mesons with p_D+,lab < 2 GeV is about 21 GeV

## Context

This work extends the transparency ratio method previously applied to eta' mesons by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (2012) and originally proposed for antiprotons by Hernandez (1992). The theoretical in-medium D meson properties are taken from Tolos (2009). The method is proposed as the first experimental reaction to measure the D meson width in nuclear matter, and could be implemented at future facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) in the US, the EicC in China, or a possible upgrade of the GlueX experiment at Jefferson Lab to 22 GeV electron energy as proposed by Accardi (2023).
