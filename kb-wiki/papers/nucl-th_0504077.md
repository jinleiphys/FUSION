# New exactly solvable reflectionless potentials of Gamov's type

> In paper SUSY-hierarchies of one-dimensional potentials with continuous energy spectra are studied. Use of such hierarchies for analysis of reflectionless potentials is substantiated from the physical point of view. An interdependence (based on Darboux transformations) between spectral characteristics of potentials-partners is determined, an uniqueness of its solution in result of use of boundary conditions is shown. A rule of construction of new reflectionless potentials on the basis of known one is corrected, its proof is proposed. At first time a general solution for a superpotential $W_{n+m}(x)$ with an arbitrary number n+m in the studied hierarchy on the basis of only one known partial solution for the superpotential $W_{n}(x)$ with the selected number n is found. A general solution of a hierarchy of inverse power (reflectionless) potentials is obtained. Such a hierarchy can be interesting as an example for solution of a known problem of search of general solutions of the hierarchies of different types (both in standard and parasupersymmetric quantum mechanics). A consequent statement and analysis of exactly solvable reflectionless potentials of Gamov's type, which at their shapes look qualitatively like scattering potentials in two-particle description of collisions between particles and nuclei or decay potentials in two-particle description of decay of compound spherical nuclear systems, are presented.

## Key claim
The paper claims that by using a specific SUSY-hierarchy defined with the constant $C_n = 0$ (rather than the standard $C_n = E_0^{(n)}$), one can construct new exactly solvable reflectionless potentials of "Gamov's type." The key hedge is that this construction relies on the assumption that the reflectionless property of any potential stems from its SUSY-interdependence with the constant potential, a proof of sufficiency for which the author states is not yet known.

## Method
The author uses Darboux transformations within supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSY QM) to establish interdependences between the spectral characteristics (wave functions, transmission/reflection amplitudes) of potential-partners with continuous energy spectra. Starting from a constant potential (which is trivially reflectionless), the author solves the Riccati equation to find five partial solutions for the superpotential $W_1(x)$ and its partner potential. By applying a corrected "Rule" that a potential is reflectionless if it is a SUSY-partner to a reflectionless potential and its wave function has a specific asymptotic form, the author selects the reflectionless solutions from these five. A general solution for a superpotential $W_{n+m}(x)$ with an arbitrary number in the hierarchy is then found using a known partial solution $W_n(x)$ and the property $\tilde{W}_{n+m}(x) = (-1)^m \tilde{W}_n(x)$.

## Key numbers
- The paper presents five partial solutions for the potential-partner $V_2(x)$ to a constant potential $V_1(x) = A^2$. These include a constant potential $A^2$, a potential $\frac{2\alpha^2}{(x+x_0)^2}$ (for $A=0$), and potentials proportional to $|A|^2 \left(1 \pm \frac{2}{\sinh^2(|A|(x+x_0)/\alpha)}\right)$ and $|A|^2 \left(\frac{2}{\cos^2(|A|(x+x_0)/\alpha)} - 1\right)$ for different conditions on $A^2$ and $W_1(x)$.
- The general solution for the superpotential $W_{n+m}(x)$ is given by the transformation $y(x) = 1/(W_{n+m}(x) - \tilde{W}_{n+m}(x))$, leading to a solution involving an integral of an exponential function of $\tilde{W}_{n+m}(x)$.

## Context
This work extends the study of reflectionless potentials in SUSY QM, which is typically focused on discrete energy spectra (as in the standard hierarchy described in Cooper (1995)), to the case of continuous energy spectra. The author corrects a previously proposed rule for constructing new reflectionless potentials from Maydanyuk (2005) and provides a proof for it. This enables the construction of a new class of exactly solvable potentials ("Gamov's type") that qualitatively resemble scattering potentials in nuclear physics, potentially allowing for more realistic analytical models of particle-nucleus collisions and nuclear decay.
