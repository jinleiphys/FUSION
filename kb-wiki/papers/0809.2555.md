# Electromagnetic Processes in $χ$EFT

> Nuclear electromagnetic currents derived in a chiral-effective-field-theory framework including explicit nucleons, $Δ$ isobars, and pions up to N$^2$LO, {\it i.e.} ignoring loop corrections, are used in a study of neutron radiative captures on protons and deuterons at thermal energies, and of $A$=2 and 3 nuclei magnetic moments. With the strengths of the $Δ$-excitation currents determined to reproduce the $n$-$p$ cross section and isovector combination of the trinucleon magnetic moments, we find that the cross section and photon circular polarization parameter, measured respectively in $n$-$d$ and $\vec{n}$-$d$ processes, are significantly underpredicted by theory.

## Key claim

The chiral-effective-field-theory (χEFT) electromagnetic currents up to N²LO (ignoring loop corrections) significantly underpredict the measured cross section and photon circular polarization parameter for neutron-deuteron radiative capture at thermal energies, with the discrepancy growing as the momentum-space cutoff increases from 500 to 800 MeV.

## Method

The authors used nuclear electromagnetic currents derived in χEFT including nucleons, Δ isobars, and pions up to N²LO (tree level only, no loop corrections). They calculated magnetic moments of A=2 and 3 nuclei and thermal neutron radiative captures on protons and deuterons, using realistic two- and three-nucleon potentials (Argonne v18 or CD-Bonn with Urbana IX or Urbana IX* three-nucleon forces) and hyperspherical harmonics wave functions. The strengths of the Δ-excitation currents were fixed by reproducing the n-p capture cross section and the isovector combination of the trinucleon magnetic moments, and the cutoff dependence was studied by varying the Gaussian regulator parameter Λ between 500 and 800 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron magnetic moment: LO contribution 0.8469 n.m. (AV18) and 0.8521 n.m. (CDB); N²LO relativistic correction −0.0082 n.m. (AV18) and −0.0080 n.m. (CDB); experimental value 0.8574 n.m.
- Trinucleon isoscalar magnetic moment combination: LO contribution 0.4104 n.m. (AV18/UIX) and 0.4183 n.m. (CDB/UIX*); N²LO relativistic correction −0.0045 n.m. (AV18/UIX) and −0.0052 n.m. (CDB/UIX*); experimental value 0.4257 n.m.
- n-p radiative capture cross section at thermal energies: used to fix C_Δ parameter (value not given in text).
- Trinucleon isovector magnetic moment combination μ_V: experimental value −2.553 n.m.; LO contribution −2.159 n.m. (AV18/UIX) and −2.180 n.m. (CDB/UIX*); N²LO-Δ contribution ranges from −0.258 n.m. (Λ=500 MeV) to −0.250 n.m. (Λ=800 MeV) for AV18/UIX.
- n-d capture cross section σ_T at thermal energies: experimental value 0.508 ± 0.015 mb; N²LO prediction ranges from 0.450 mb (Λ=500 MeV) to 0.315 mb (Λ=800 MeV) with AV18/UIX.
- Photon circular polarization parameter R_c: experimental value −0.42 ± 0.03; N²LO prediction ranges from −0.437 (Λ=500 MeV) to −0.331 (Λ=800 MeV) with AV18/UIX.
- At N²LO, the cross section is underpredicted by 11% (Λ=500 MeV) to 38% (Λ=800 MeV); |R_c| is overestimated by 4% at Λ=500 MeV and underestimated by 21% at Λ=800 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the χEFT current derivations of Pastore (2008) and is similar to the approach of Park (1996) but uses a different formalism. The calculations use the Argonne v18 potential (Wiringa 1995) or CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt 2001) with Urbana IX (Pudliner 1997) or Urbana IX* (Viviani 2007) three-nucleon forces, and hyperspherical harmonics wave functions (Kievsky 2008). The results show that N²LO currents are insufficient to describe n-d capture data, motivating the inclusion of N³LO loop corrections and extension to processes such as p-d capture and ³He(n,γ)⁴He at thermal energies.
