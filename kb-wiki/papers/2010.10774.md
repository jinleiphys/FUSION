# Anisotropy in Dijet Production in Exclusive and Inclusive Processes

> We investigate the effect of soft gluon radiations on the azimuthal angle correlation between the total and relative momenta of two jets in inclusive and exclusive dijet processes. We show that the final state effect induces a sizable $\cos(2ϕ)$ anisotropy due to gluon emissions near the jet cones. The phenomenological consequences of this observation are discussed for various collider experiments, including diffractive processes in ultraperipheral $pA$ and $AA$ collisions, inclusive and diffractive dijet production at the EIC, and inclusive dijet in $pp$ and $AA$ collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

Soft gluon radiation from final-state jets induces a sizable $\cos(2\phi)$ anisotropy in dijet production that is not power-suppressed, and this radiative contribution must be subtracted to extract genuine initial-state structure such as the elliptic gluon Wigner distribution or linearly polarized gluon distribution.

## Method

The authors calculate the soft factor $S_J(q_\perp)$ for dijet production at leading order in $\alpha_s$ using eikonal gluon emission amplitudes, imposing theta-function constraints to restrict emissions to angles outside the jet cones of radius $R$ (narrow jet approximation $R\ll 1$). They then perform all-order resummation of the Sudakov logarithms by Fourier-transforming to impact parameter $b_\perp$-space and exponentiating the one-loop result, using the $b_*$-prescription for the running coupling and a non-perturbative parameter $g_\Lambda$ at large $b_\perp$. The formalism is applied separately to diffractive photoproduction ($\gamma A \to q\bar{q} + A$), inclusive photoproduction ($\gamma p \to jjX$), and inclusive $pp$ collisions ($gg\to gg$ channel), with the TMD framework incorporating both initial-state and final-state radiation where appropriate.

## Key numbers

- For diffractive dijet production at the LHC with $P_\perp = 35$ GeV, $R=0.4$, $\Delta y_{12}=0$: the leading-order ratio $\alpha_2/\alpha_0 \approx 0.14$; the resummed $\langle \cos(2\phi)\rangle$ is shown as a function of $q_\perp$ for non-perturbative parameters $g_\Lambda = 0.05$, $0.1$, $0.2$ GeV$^2$.
- For inclusive dijet in $\gamma p$ collisions at the EIC with $\sqrt{S_{\gamma p}} = 100$ GeV, $P_\perp = 15$ GeV, same rapidity: the resummed $\langle \cos(2\phi)\rangle$ shows stronger $q_\perp$-dependence than the diffractive case, with the leading-order curve going to zero as $q_\perp \to 0$.
- For inclusive dijet in $pp$ collisions at the LHC with $\sqrt{S_{pp}} = 7$ TeV, $P_\perp = 100$ GeV, both jets at mid-rapidity: the $\langle \cos(2\phi)\rangle$ is shown as a function of $q_\perp$ for the $gg\to gg$ channel.
- The coefficients for the $\cos(2\phi)$ anisotropy at leading order are: $\alpha_2 = (\alpha_s C_F / 2\pi) \cdot 2 \ln(a_2/R^2)$ for diffractive dijets, where $a_2$ ranges from $1/4$ (for $\Delta y_{12}=0$) to $1/e$ (for $\Delta y_{12}\to\infty$); for inclusive $\gamma p$: $\alpha_2^{\gamma g} = (\alpha_s/2\pi)[C_A \ln(a_1/R^2) - (1/N_c)\ln(a_2/R^2)]$ with $a_1=1/e$; for $gg\to gg$: $\alpha_2^{gg} = (\alpha_s C_A/2\pi)[2\ln(a_1/R^2) + ((\hat t^2+\hat u^2)/(2(\hat s^2-\hat t\hat u)))\ln(a_2/a_1)]$.

## Context

This work extends the resummation formalism for jet azimuthal anisotropies developed by Catani et al. (2014, 2017) to dijet production in exclusive and inclusive processes, and is directly motivated by a preliminary CMS measurement (2020) of $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry in ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC. The results show that the radiative $\cos(2\phi)$ contribution from soft gluons is comparable to or larger than the signal from the elliptic gluon Wigner distribution proposed by Hatta et al. (2016) and Hagiwara et al. (2016), and also contaminates the extraction of the linearly polarized gluon distribution proposed by Boer et al. (2010, 2016) and Dumitru et al. (2015). The paper enables a systematic subtraction of final-state radiation effects in tomographic studies of nucleons and nuclei at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
