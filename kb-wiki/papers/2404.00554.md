# Order of the SU(N_f) x SU(N_f) chiral transition via the functional renormalization group

> Renormalization group flows of the $SU(N_f)\times SU(N_f)$ symmetric Ginzburg-Landau potential are calculated for a general number of flavors, $N_f$. Our approach does not rely on the $ε$ expansion, but uses the functional renormalization group, formulated directly in $d=3$ spatial dimensions, with the inclusion of all possible (perturbatively) relevant and marginal operators, whose number is considerably larger than those in $d=4$. We find new, potentially infrared stable fixed points spanned throughout the entire $N_f$ range. By conjecturing that the thermal chiral transition is governed by these ``flavor continuous" fixed points, stability analyses show that for $N_f\geq 5$ the chiral transition is of second-order, while for $N_f=2,3,4$, it is of first-order. We argue that the $U_{\rm A}(1)$ anomaly controls the strength of the first-order chiral transition for $N_f=2,3,4$, and makes it almost indistinguishable from a second-order one, if it is sufficiently weak at the critical point. This could open up a new strategy to investigate the strength of the $U_{\rm A}(1)$ symmetry breaking around the critical temperature.

## Key claim

The authors claim that for \(N_f \geq 5\) the thermal chiral transition is of second order, while for \(N_f = 2,3,4\) it is of first order, but they hedge that the first-order transition for \(N_f = 2,3,4\) can be made almost indistinguishable from a second-order one if the \(U_{\rm A}(1)\) anomaly is sufficiently weak at the critical point.

## Method

The authors used the functional renormalization group (FRG) in the local potential approximation (LPA) directly in \(d=3\) spatial dimensions, without relying on the \(\epsilon\) expansion. They expanded the Ginzburg-Landau potential up to \({\cal O}(\phi^6)\) to include all perturbatively relevant and marginal operators, which are considerably more numerous than in \(d=4\). The flow equations were projected onto chirally invariant combinations of the field \(\Phi\), and the \(\beta\) functions were obtained by matching coefficients of a two-component background field expansion.

## Key numbers

* For \(N_f \geq 5\), the chiral transition is predicted to be of second order; for \(N_f = 2,3,4\), it is predicted to be of first order.
* In the \(N_f \rightarrow \infty\) limit, a fixed point \(C^\infty_1\) with one relevant direction was found at \(\bar{m}^2 = -0.37351\), \(\bar{g}_1 = 4.89039\), \(\bar{g}_2 = -0.54978\).
* For \(N_f = 3\), a fixed point \({A}_{1*}^3\) with one relevant direction was found at \(\bar{m}^2 = -0.41126\), \(\bar{g}_1 = 0.73099\), \(\bar{g}_2 = -0.88199\), \(\bar{a} = -0.46585\), \(\bar{b} = -0.91131\).
* For \(N_f = 4\), the fixed point \(\tilde{C}^4_2\) (anomaly-free) has two relevant directions (one under the constraint that anomalous couplings vanish) at \(\bar{m}^2 = -0.38129\), \(\bar{g}_1 = 0.31042\), \(\bar{g}_2 = -0.25480\).
* For \(N_f = 2\), the anomaly-free fixed point \(\tilde{C}^2_2\) has two relevant directions (one under the constraint that anomalous couplings vanish) at \(\bar{m}^2 = -0.20599\), \(\bar{g}_1 = 1.33367\), \(\bar{g}_2 = -1.88211\).

## Context

This work extends the previous FRG study for \(N_f=3\) by Fejos (2022) to arbitrary flavor numbers, and contradicts the long-standing prediction from the \(\epsilon\) expansion by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) that the chiral transition is of first order for \(N_f > 2\). The results are relevant to ongoing lattice QCD simulations (e.g., Cuteri et al. 2021, Zhang et al. 2024) and Dyson-Schwinger studies (Bernhardt et al. 2023) that have found evidence for a possible second-order transition for \(N_f=3\). The work enables a new strategy to probe the strength of \(U_{\rm A}(1)\) symmetry breaking near the critical temperature by comparing the observed transition order with the predictions for different anomaly strengths.
