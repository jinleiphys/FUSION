# Parton distribution functions probed in ultraperipheral collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Vector meson production in ultra-peripheral pA and AA collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are very sensitive to Parton Distribution Functions (PDF) as well as to their leading-order, next-to-leading-order, and medium corrections. This process is a complimentary tool to explore the effects of different PDFs in particle production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus central collisions. Existing and forthcoming data available, e.g., from ALICE and CMS, may be used in conjunction with our theoretical predictions to constrain the PDFs. We make predictions for rapidity distributions and for cross sections of J/$ψ$ , $ψ(2S)$ and $Υ$ production at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5$ TeV. We use the second energy as representative for the Run 2 of PbPb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

Ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC provide a highly sensitive probe of gluon parton distribution functions (PDFs) and their nuclear medium modifications, but the predictive power is limited by large uncertainties in the low-x region of the PDFs, as demonstrated by the wide spread of theoretical predictions across different PDF sets.

## Method

The authors calculate vector meson photoproduction cross sections in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions using the equivalent photon approximation, where the photon flux from one nucleus interacts with gluons in the other nucleus via photon-gluon fusion. The cross section depends quadratically on the gluon distribution, with nuclear medium effects incorporated through the modification factor R_A(x,Q^2) applied to free proton PDFs. Rapidity distributions are computed for J/psi, psi(2S), and Upsilon(1S) production at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 5 TeV using multiple PDF sets including MSTW08, CT14, CJ12, and nCTEQ15, with medium corrections from EPS09, nDS, and HKN07 parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- J/psi production cross section at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV ranges from 2.2 mb (CJ12 + EPS09) to 83 mb (MSTW08 without medium corrections)
- J/psi production cross section at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV ranges from 2.8 mb (CJ12 + EPS09) to 220 mb (MSTW08 without medium corrections)
- Coherent J/psi rapidity distribution at mid-rapidity (y=0) for sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV: experimental value 2.38^{+0.34}_{-0.24} mb from ALICE, compared to theoretical prediction of 2.42 mb using MSTW08 with EPS09 medium corrections
- Upsilon(1S) production cross section at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV ranges from 3.5 mb (MSTW08 + EPS08) to 17 mb (MSTW08 without medium corrections)
- Upsilon(1S) production cross section at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV ranges from 6.7 mb (MSTW08 + EPS08) to 40 mb (MSTW08 without medium corrections)
- The resolved photon component contributes at the level of about 10% for c-cbar pair production and 20% for b-bbar pair production, but is negligible for bound state production

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Bertulani et al. (2011, 2012) and Goncalves and Bertulani (2002) on using ultraperipheral collisions to probe gluon distributions, incorporating newer PDF releases (CT14, CJ12, nCTEQ15) and improved treatment of the equivalent photon spectrum with nuclear diffuseness. The results show that ALICE data from Abbasi et al. (2013) on coherent J/psi production favor PDF sets with moderate gluon shadowing (EPS09), while sets without shadowing or with very strong shadowing (EPS08) are incompatible with measurements. This enables future LHC data from Run 2 at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV to further constrain the small-x behavior of nuclear gluon distributions, which remain poorly determined by existing deep inelastic scattering data alone.
