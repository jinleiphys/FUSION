# Nuclear applications of ANL-Osaka amplitudes: pion photo-productions on deuteron

> The Argonne National Laboratory-Osaka University (ANL-Osaka) amplitudes (http://www.phy.anl.gov/theory/research/anl-osaka-pwa/) are applied to study pion photo-production reactions on the deuteron target. Within the multiple scattering formulation, we predict the cross sections of $d(γ,π)NN$ in the nucleon resonance region. The calculations include the impulse term and the final-state interaction (FSI) terms due to pion-exchange and nucleon-exchange. We show that the off-shell effects, calculated from the meson-exchange mechanisms, on the propagations of the exchanged nucleon and pion are significant in determining the reaction amplitudes. The FSI effects on the predicted cross sections are found to be important at energies near the $Δ$(1232) resonance, and are still significant at higher energies. The results are in good agreement with most of the available data of $d(γ,π^0)np$ and $d(γ,π^-)pp$ reactions.

## Key claim

The ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channel model, when applied to pion photo-production on the deuteron, predicts cross sections that agree well with most available data for \(d(\gamma,\pi^0)np\) and \(d(\gamma,\pi^-)pp\) reactions, but only after including off-shell effects from meson-exchange mechanisms and final-state interactions; the agreement is improved but not fully resolved by refitting the \(\gamma n \to \pi N\) amplitudes to include recent Mainz and JLab data.

## Method

The authors used the ANL-Osaka model, which is an energy-independent Hamiltonian with meson-exchange potentials and bare nucleon-resonance states fitted to about 26,000 data points of \(\pi N\) and \(\gamma N\) reactions up to invariant mass \(W \approx 2\) GeV, to generate \(\gamma N \to \pi N\) and \(\pi N \to \pi N\) amplitudes. These were combined with the CD-Bonn \(NN\) potential for the deuteron wave function and \(NN \to NN\) amplitudes within a multiple-scattering formulation that includes impulse, nucleon-exchange, and pion-exchange terms, with loop integrations over off-energy-shell two-body matrix elements boosted from the two-body center-of-mass frame to the \(\gamma d\) laboratory frame using relativistic quantum mechanics.

## Key numbers

- The ANL-Osaka model was fitted to about **26,000 data points** of \(\pi N\) and \(\gamma N\) reactions up to invariant mass \(W \simeq 2\) GeV.
- The partial-wave amplitudes for \(\gamma N, \pi N \to \pi N\) included up to and including **\(L=5\)** (where \(L\) is the \(\pi N\) orbital angular momentum).
- The \(NN \to NN\) partial wave amplitudes included up to and including **\(J=3\)** (where \(J\) is the \(NN\) total angular momentum).
- The new fit included recent Mainz data for \(\gamma n \to \pi^0 n\) and JLab data for \(\gamma n \to \pi^- p\) at photon energies in the laboratory frame from **\(E_\gamma = 500\)–750 MeV** region, where discrepancies with the original ANL-Osaka predictions were largest for \(d(\gamma,\pi^0)np\).
- Off-shell effects were shown to be significant at **\(E_\gamma = 305\) MeV** for \(d(\gamma,\pi^0)pn\) and at **\(E_\gamma = 700\) MeV** for \(d(\gamma,\pi^-)pp\), particularly in forward angles (\(\cos\theta_{\pi,c} \to 1\)).

## Context

This work extends the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channel model, previously developed for \(\pi N\) and \(\gamma N\) reactions (Kamano et al., 2013; Kamano et al., 2016), to the simplest nuclear target, the deuteron, following earlier investigations of \(d(\gamma,\pi)NN\) reactions by Arenhoevel, Fix, Levchuk, Schumacher, and others. The approach differs from that of Tarasov and Tarasov (who used on-shell approximations with monopole form factors) by including exact off-energy-shell two-body matrix elements from the ANL-Osaka model and CD-Bonn potential. The results enable future applications of the ANL-Osaka model to pion and eta production on heavier nuclei in the nucleon resonance region, and provide a framework for testing the isospin structure of nucleon resonances extracted from deuteron data.
