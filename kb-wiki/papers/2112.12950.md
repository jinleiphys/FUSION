# Chirality dependence in charge and heat transport in thermal QCD

> In the presence of weak magnetic field ($B$), novel phenomena, similar to Hall effect in condensed matter physics, emerge both in charge and heat transport in a thermal QCD medium. Here, we have employed the kinetic theory approach within a quasiparticle framework, wherein the effective masses for left (L) and right-handed (R) chiral modes of quarks are seen different, lifting the prevalent degeneracy. Another implication of weak $B$ is that the coefficients assume a tensorial structure: The diagonal elements represent the usual conductivities: $σ_{\text{Ohmic}}$ and $κ_0$ as the coefficients of charge and heat transport, respectively and the off-diagonal elements denote their Hall counterparts: $σ_{\text{Hall}}$ and $κ_1$, respectively. Finally, we have derived some coefficients, namely, the Knudsen number and Lorenz number in Wiedemann-Franz law. Lorenz number and Hall-Lorenz number for L-mode increases and for R-mode decreases with a magnetic field. it does not remain constant with $T$ hence violating the Wiedemann-Franz law.

## Key claim
In a thermal QCD medium with a weak magnetic field (|eB| << T²) and finite quark chemical potential, the effective masses of left-handed and right-handed chiral modes of quarks become different, lifting the degeneracy present in the strong-field limit; this chirality dependence causes the Ohmic electrical conductivity to decrease for the L-mode and increase for the R-mode with magnetic field, while the Hall conductivity increases for both modes.

## Method
The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation within the relaxation-time approximation, using a quasiparticle framework where quark effective masses were computed from the static limit of the one-loop quark self-energy in perturbative thermal QCD with a weak background magnetic field. The medium-generated masses for left- and right-handed chiral modes were obtained separately from the pole of the dressed quark propagator, and the transport coefficients were calculated for each chiral mode using the resulting chirality-dependent quasiparticle masses.

## Key numbers
- The weak magnetic field condition is defined as |eB| << T², with the strong field limit being |eB| >> T².
- The thermal mass squared for quarks at finite chemical potential is given by m_th² = (1/8)g²C_F(T² + μ²/π²), where C_F = 4/3 for N_C = 3.
- The left-handed mode mass squared is m_L² = m_th² + 4g²C_F M², and the right-handed mode mass squared is m_R² = m_th² - 4g²C_F M², where M²(T,μ,m_f0,q_fB) = (|q_fB|/(16π²))[(πT/(2m_f0)) - ln2 + (7μ²ζ(3))/(8π²T²)].
- The Knudsen number Ω is always less than unity for the entire temperature range studied.
- The Lorenz number (κ₀/σ_Ohmic T) and Hall-Lorenz number (κ₁/σ_Hall T) for the L-mode increase with magnetic field and for the R-mode decrease with magnetic field, and do not remain constant with temperature.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of transport in strongly magnetized QGP (where only the lowest Landau level is occupied) to the weak-field regime where higher Landau levels are accessible, building on the quasiparticle approach of A. Ayala (2018) for the running coupling in a magnetic field and the dispersion relation analysis of A. Bandyopadhyay (2016). The chirality-dependent masses contrast with the degenerate left- and right-handed modes found in the strong magnetic field limit by S. Rath (2017). The results enable further study of the chiral magnetic effect and the violation of the Wiedemann-Franz law in weakly magnetized QGP, and complement lattice simulations by S. Gupta (2004), G. Aarts (2015), and H.-T. Ding (2011).
