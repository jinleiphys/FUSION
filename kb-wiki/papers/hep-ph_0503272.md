# Pion Superfluidity and Meson Properties at Finite Isospin Density

> We investigate pion superfluidity and its effect on meson properties and equation of state at finite temperature and isospin and baryon densities in the frame of standard flavor SU(2) NJL model. In mean field approximation to quarks and random phase approximation to mesons, the critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity is exactly the pion mass in the vacuum, and corresponding to the isospin symmetry spontaneous breaking, there is in the pion superfluidity phase a Goldstone mode which is the linear combination of the normal sigma and charged pion modes. We calculate numerically the gap equations for the chiral and pion condensates, the phase diagrams, the meson spectra, and the equation of state, and compare them with that obtained in other effective models. The competitions between pion superfluidity and color superconductivity at finite baryon density and between pion and kaon superfluidity at finite strangeness density in flavor SU(3) NJL model are briefly discussed.

## Key claim
The critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity is exactly the pion mass in the vacuum, a relation that is independent of model parameters, regularization scheme, and color degrees of freedom, but the paper notes this holds specifically within the mean field approximation to quarks and random phase approximation to mesons in the NJL model.

## Method
The authors employed the standard flavor SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with mean field approximation for quarks and random phase approximation (RPA) for mesons. They solved the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensate and pion condensate numerically, calculated the thermodynamic potential, and derived meson spectra and the equation of state at finite temperature and isospin chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity is exactly the vacuum pion mass, μ_I^c = m_π = 0.134 GeV (in the real world with nonzero current quark mass).
- The model parameters used: in the real world, m_0 = 0.005 GeV, Λ = 0.653 GeV, G = 4.93 GeV^{-2}; in the chiral limit, m_0 = 0, Λ = 0.65 GeV, G = 5.01 GeV^{-2}.
- The chiral condensate in the vacuum is σ_u = σ_d = (-0.25 GeV)^3.
- The pion condensate tends to zero at about μ_I = 1.75 GeV.
- The ratio of pressure to energy density saturates at p/ε ∼ 0.7 at μ_I ∼ 0.5 GeV.
- The isospin density corresponding to the saturated equation of state is about 5 times the normal nuclear density n_0 = 0.17/fm^3.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of QCD phase structure at finite isospin density using the NJL model, following the lattice QCD findings of Kogut et al. (2000-2002) that the critical isospin chemical potential is about the pion mass. It builds on the analytical proof by He et al. (2005) at quark level that μ_I^c = m_π, and compares results with chiral perturbation theory calculations by Son and Stephanov (2001). The paper enables further investigation of competition between pion superfluidity and color superconductivity at finite baryon density, and between pion and kaon superfluidity in the flavor SU(3) NJL model.
