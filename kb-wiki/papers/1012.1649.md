# Particle Production in s_NN = 2.76 TeV Heavy Ion Collisions

> We obtain within the statistical hadronization model the hadron yields $dh/dy$ in heavy ion reactions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV. We discuss the dependence both on hadronization temperature $T$, and on critical hadronization pressure $P$. We consider observables distinguishing the hadronization models and conditions.

## Key claim

Within the statistical hadronization model (SHM), the authors predict hadron yields at LHC \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV by fixing the total charged hadron yield \(dh/dy \simeq 1800\), but the results depend strongly on whether chemical equilibrium or chemical non-equilibrium conditions are assumed, with the non-equilibrium model requiring additional constraints from strangeness yield and hadronization pressure.

## Method

The authors used the SHARE suite of programs to compute hadron yields per unit rapidity at mid-rapidity for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV. For the chemical equilibrium SHM, they fixed \(\gamma_q = \gamma_s = 1\) and varied the hadronization temperature \(T\) from 159 to 189 MeV, adjusting the volume to maintain \(dh/dy = 1800\). For the chemical non-equilibrium SHM, they fixed the specific strangeness yield \(s/S \simeq 0.037\) and varied the hadronization pressure \(P\) from 60.3 to 90.1 MeV/fm\(^3\), allowing \(\gamma_q\), \(\gamma_s\), and \(T\) to vary while maintaining \(dh/dy = 1800\) and the constraint \(E/TS > 1\).

## Key numbers

- Total charged hadron yield at mid-rapidity: \(dh/dy \simeq 1800\), derived from measured pseudo-rapidity density \(dh/d\eta = 1584 \pm 4 \pm 76\) (syst) for the 5% most central Pb-Pb collisions.
- In the chemical equilibrium model, hadronization temperature \(T\) ranges from 159 to 189 MeV, with corresponding volumes \(dV/dy\) from 5285 to 1538 fm\(^3\) and entropy content \(dS/dy\) from 15155 to 14420.
- In the chemical non-equilibrium model, hadronization pressure \(P\) ranges from 60.3 to 90.1 MeV/fm\(^3\), with temperatures \(T\) from 131.2 to 139.6 MeV, light quark occupancy \(\gamma_q \simeq 1.60\), and strange quark occupancy \(\gamma_s\) from 2.913 to 2.721.
- The bulk thermal energy content per unit rapidity is \(dE/dy = 2.00 \pm 0.05\) TeV in both models.
- The specific strangeness yield in the equilibrium model ranges from \(s/S = 0.0245\) to 0.0270, while in the non-equilibrium model it is fixed at \(s/S \simeq 0.037\).
- The \(\phi\) meson yield is enhanced by a factor of about 1.5 in the non-equilibrium model compared to the equilibrium model, with \(\phi\) yields of 19.0 to 21.9 (equilibrium) versus 30.0 to 31.4 (non-equilibrium) per unit rapidity.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model approach previously applied at RHIC energies (Letessier and Rafelski, 2005) to the LHC energy of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, using the first LHC charged particle multiplicity measurement from Aamodt (2010) as a constraint. The paper contrasts the chemical equilibrium SHM (as used by Andronic et al., 2005, 2008 and Becattini, 2010) with the chemical non-equilibrium SHM, which introduces quark phase-space occupancy parameters \(\gamma_q\) and \(\gamma_s\) to account for the rapid hadronization of an overpopulated quark-gluon plasma. The results enable future experimental tests to distinguish between these hadronization scenarios using observables such as \(\phi/h\), K\(^*\)/K, and K/\(\pi\) ratios, and provide constraints for hydrodynamic models of the QGP fireball.
