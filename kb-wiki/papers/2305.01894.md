# Sea contribution to the charge radii and quadrupole moment of $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^+, \frac{3}{2}^+$ baryons

> An operator formalism is used on the wavefunction of baryons to compute their charge radii and quadrupole moments. Total anti-symmetric wavefunction in spin, color and flavor space is framed for $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^+$ nucleons and $J^P=\frac{3}{2}^+$ hyperons. To understand the importance of sea, statistical model is used in conjugation with the detailed balance principle. Within the statistical approach, the importance of sea with quarks and gluons are studied using the relevant probabilities that are associated with spin, flavor, and color space. The present work also focuses on individual contributions of valence and sea which contains terms of scalar, vector and tensor sea. The obtained results are in agreement with available theories and few experimental outcomes. Our computed results may provide important information for upcoming experimental findings.

# Sea contribution to the charge radii and quadrupole moment of \(J^P=\frac{1}{2}^+, \frac{3}{2}^+\) baryons

## Key claim
The statistical model combined with the detailed balance principle can reproduce charge radii and quadrupole moments of octet and decuplet baryons when sea contributions (scalar, vector, and tensor) are included, but the results depend on the specific choice of statistical model parameters (Model D) and on fitting the operator parameters A, B, and C to available experimental data via chi-squared minimization.

## Method
The authors construct total antisymmetric wavefunctions in spin, color, and flavor space for \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+\) nucleons and \(J^P = \frac{3}{2}^+\) hyperons, including sea components (gluons and quark-antiquark pairs) with spin 0 (scalar), 1 (vector), and 2 (tensor). They apply operator expressions for the charge radii and quadrupole moment operators to these wavefunctions, obtaining expressions in terms of statistical coefficients (\(a_0, a_8, a_{10}, b_1, b_8, b_{10}, c_8, d_8\)) and operator parameters (A, B, C). The statistical coefficients are determined using the statistical model with the detailed balance principle, where the probability of each Fock state is computed from transition probabilities between states via processes such as \(q \leftrightarrow qg\), \(g \leftrightarrow q\bar{q}\), and \(g \leftrightarrow gg\). The operator parameters A, B, and C are fitted to experimental data on charge radii and quadrupole moments.

## Key numbers
- Proton charge radius: \(r_p = 0.8409 \pm 0.0004\) fm (experimental value from PDG, cited in text)
- Neutron charge radius squared: \(r_n^2 = -0.115 \pm 0.0017\) fm\(^2\) (experimental value from PDG, cited in text)
- \(\Sigma^{-}\) charge radius: \(0.78 \pm 0.10\) fm (experimental value from PDG, cited in text)
- PRad experiment proton charge radius: \(0.831 \pm 0.007_{\text{stat.}} \pm 0.012_{\text{sys.}}\) fm (Jefferson Lab, cited in text)
- H-spectroscopy proton radius: \(r_p = 0.833 \pm 0.010\) fm (two experiments, cited in text)
- Alternative H-spectroscopy proton radius: \(r_p = 0.877\) fm (one experiment, cited in text)
- Ratio of electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole amplitude for \(\Delta^+\): \(E2/M1 = -0.025 \pm 0.005\) (JLab/Mainz collaborations, cited in text)
- Quadrupole transition moment \(\Delta^+ \rightarrow N\): \(-0.018 \pm 0.009 \pm 0.034\) fm\(^2\) (LEGS collaboration, cited in text)
- Quadrupole transition moment \(\Delta^+ \rightarrow N\): \(-0.0846 \pm 0.0033\) fm\(^2\) (Mainz collaboration, cited in text)

## Context
This work extends the statistical model previously applied by Singh and Upadhyay (various years) to baryon spin distributions, masses, and magnetic moments, now computing charge radii and quadrupole moments. It builds on the detailed balance principle introduced by Zhang et al. (various years) for flavor asymmetry. The results are compared with earlier theoretical approaches including the quark model (various authors), light cone QCD sum rules (various authors), the Skyrme model with bound-state approach (various authors), the 1/N\(_c\) expansion method (various authors), lattice QCD (various authors), and the chiral constituent quark model (various authors). The computed values may guide upcoming experimental measurements of baryon electromagnetic properties.
