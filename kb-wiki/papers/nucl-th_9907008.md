# Scalar Isoscalar pion pairs in nuclei and the A(pi,pipi)X reaction

> The reaction A(pi,pi pi)X has been studied at low energies, paying particular attention to the interaction of the two final pions in the scalar isoscalar (I=J=0) channel. We have developed a microscopic model for the pion production, and then implemented the two pion final state interaction by using the results of a non-perturbative unitary coupled-channels method based in the standard chiral Lagrangians. The resulting model, describes well the reaction on the nucleon for all different isospin channels. Finally, we have considered the reaction in nuclei. Our calculation takes into account Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, pion absorption, and also the strong modification of the pi-pi interaction in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed enhancement of the low-invariant-mass dipion cross section in the $A(\pi^-,\pi^+\pi^-)X$ reaction on nuclei is not fully reproduced by a realistic model that includes medium-dependent $\pi\pi$ interactions in the scalar isoscalar channel, contrary to earlier simplified calculations. The authors hedge that the discrepancy could be due to either a stronger density dependence of the $\pi\pi$ interaction than existing models predict, or to the modification of destructive interference between large amplitude pieces in the nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors developed a microscopic model for the elementary $\pi N \to \pi\pi N$ reaction using effective chiral Lagrangians including nucleons, $\Delta$ resonances, and Roper resonances, with coupling constants taken from independent experiments. They implemented the final state interaction of the two pions in the scalar isoscalar channel using a non-perturbative unitary coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter approach based on chiral Lagrangians, both in vacuum and in nuclear matter. For the nuclear reaction, they incorporated Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, pion absorption, quasielastic scattering, and the medium-modified $\pi\pi$ interaction, evaluating the cross section with an eikonal approximation for flux losses.

## Key numbers

- The $\pi N \to \pi\pi N$ model reproduces total cross sections for three charge channels without free parameters; the $\pi^+\pi^-$ channel total cross section is slightly overestimated by about 20% on deuterium.
- The $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitude in vacuum is reproduced up to 1.2 GeV using a cutoff of $q_{max} = 1$ GeV in the Bethe-Salpeter equation.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter $g' = 0.7$ and $NN\pi$ coupling constant $f = 1$ are used in the pion selfenergy calculation.
- The pion kinetic energy threshold for experimental acceptance is $T_\pi > 11$ MeV.
- The Roper resonance coupling constants used are $\tilde{f} = 0.477$, $f_{N^* \Delta \pi} = 2.07$, and $C = 2.3 m_\pi^{-1}$.
- The $\Delta N\pi$ coupling constant is $f^* = 2.13$, and the $\Delta\Delta\pi$ coupling is $f_\Delta = 4/5 f_{NN\pi}$.
- The $\pi N$ s-wave scattering constants are $\lambda_2 = 0.045$ from chiral perturbation theory and $\lambda_2 = 0.052$, $\lambda_1 = 0.048$ from fitting scattering lengths.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of the $A(\pi,2\pi)X$ reaction beyond the simplified model of Rapp et al. (1999), which used a less complete elementary production amplitude and simpler nuclear effects. The authors use the $\pi\pi$ interaction model of Oller and Oset (1997) for vacuum and its extension to nuclear matter by Chiang, Oset, and Vicente-Vacas (1997). The paper enables future experimental investigations using electromagnetically induced reactions, which would avoid the strong initial pion absorption that limits sensitivity to density-dependent effects in the present work.
