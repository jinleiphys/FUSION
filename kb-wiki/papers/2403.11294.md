# Probing multi-particle bunching from intermittency analysis in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> It has been demonstrated that factorial moments analysis in dependence from the size of phase-space cells (when the latter is decreased but is still considerably large), exhibits sensitivity to particle bunching within a system situated in the two-dimensional cell. Based on recent findings concerning fluctuations in charge particle density observed in central Au + Au collisions at various energies ranging from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 to 200 GeV at RHIC/STAR, it is proposed that analyzing factorial moments in relativistic heavy-ion collisions could serve as a method to investigate density fluctuations linked to correlation phenomena. Our understanding of particle production mechanisms may be enriched by using factorial moments analysis to gain new information contained in them that was previously unavailable.

## Key claim

Factorial moment analysis in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can probe multi-particle bunching (density fluctuations) even when phase-space cells are large, not only in the zero-size limit where intermittency effects typically saturate; the observed power-law behavior of scaled factorial moments may arise from a single correlated bunch of particles in one cell rather than from critical phenomena.

## Method

The authors analyze STAR Collaboration data on scaled factorial moments \(\Delta F_q(M)\) in two-dimensional transverse momentum space \((p_{Tx}, p_{Ty})\) from central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) to 200 GeV. They model the data by assuming that a single cell contains a bunch of particles with a geometric (Bose-Einstein) multiplicity distribution, while all other cells follow a Poisson distribution, and extract the average multiplicity \(m\) in the bunch from the measured \(\Delta F_q\) values. They also consider a scenario with multiple bunches described by a negative binomial distribution with partial coherence parameter \(\lambda\), and examine the scale-invariant behavior of the bunch multiplicity as a function of cell size.

## Key numbers

* For central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, the average multiplicity in a bunch follows \(m = a (M^2/1000)^b\) with \(a \simeq 3.32 \cdot q^{-0.732} - 5.33 \cdot q^{-2}\) and \(b = 1.46 \cdot q^{-1} - 0.81\).
* For the multi-bunch scenario at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, the coherence parameter is estimated as \(\lambda = 0.02\) and the negative binomial parameter as \(k = 2.56 (M^2)^{-0.42}\).
* The power-law index for \(\Delta F_q\) in the multi-bunch model is \(\Phi_q = 0.42(q-1)\).
* For large \(M^2 > 1000\), the ratio \(m(\sqrt{s_{NN}})/\langle N(\sqrt{s_{NN}})\rangle\) relative to the 7.7 GeV value follows \((0.985 - 0.0236 \ln \sqrt{s_{NN}})\).
* For a bunch of transverse size \(\delta = 0.04\) GeV or 0.06 GeV, the multiplicity in the bunch scales as \(m(M^2) \propto (M^2)^{-\kappa}\) with \(\kappa = 0.46\) and 0.49 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the intermittency analysis framework developed by Antoniou et al. (2006, 2017) and the NA49 Collaboration (2009, 2012) by showing that the power-law behavior of factorial moments can be explained by simple multi-particle bunching rather than requiring critical point physics. It directly uses the STAR Collaboration (2023) data on \(\Delta F_q(M)\) from Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The results suggest that future dynamical modeling of heavy-ion collisions is needed to determine whether this bunching scenario is consistent with the full experimental spectrum.
