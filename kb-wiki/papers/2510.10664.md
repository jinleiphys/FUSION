# Investigation of entanglement in pure final polarization states from neutron-deuteron elastic ccattering and exclusive deuteron break

> We investigate the pure polarization states of the outgoing neutron deuteron pair in elastic polarized neutron polarized deuteron scattering, as well as the pure polarization states of the three free nucleons produced in the corresponding deuteron breakup reaction. Our aim is to provide clear evidence of entanglement in their spin degrees of freedom. These final states can be generated from pure spin states of the incoming nd system, where both the neutron and the deuteron are strongly polarized by maximizing the occupation of a specific magnetic substate. To fully characterize the final configurations, we compute the corresponding spin density matrices using the high precision CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. Among these pure final spin states, we identify strongly entangled, Bell-like states that include at most small admixtures of components diminishing entanglement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pure final spin states in neutron-deuteron elastic scattering and exclusive deuteron breakup can exhibit strong entanglement resembling Bell-like states, but only when the incoming neutron and deuteron are both maximally polarized to produce a pure initial state, and even then the entangled states contain small admixtures of entanglement-spoiling components that can reach up to approximately 30% of the norm.

## Method

The author solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equation using the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential at four incoming neutron laboratory energies (8.5, 37.5, 65, and 135 MeV), neglecting three-nucleon forces. Six pure initial spin states were constructed from combinations of maximally polarized neutron and deuteron states with the quantization axis along the y-direction, and the resulting pure final spin density matrices were computed from the transition amplitudes for both elastic scattering and breakup reactions. Entanglement was quantified using the entanglement power and concurrence measures, and Bell-like states were identified by requiring that a single pair of opposite-spin-projection amplitudes contribute at least 70% of the norm.

## Key numbers

- Four incoming neutron laboratory energies were studied: 8.5, 37.5, 65, and 135 MeV
- Twelve entangled Bell-like states were identified across all energies when requiring the dominant pair of amplitudes to exceed approximately 70% of the norm (entanglement-spoiling component up to 30%)
- Reducing the entanglement-spoiling component threshold from 30% to 20% reduced the number of entangled states from twelve to six
- Further reducing to 10% left only a single entangled state (state No. 8 at E = 135 MeV)
- State No. 8 at E = 135 MeV had the smallest entanglement-spoiling contribution at about 8.8% of the norm
- The entanglement power and concurrence reach maximum values of 0.5 for maximally entangled states
- At E = 37.5 MeV, both entanglement measures remained small for scattering angles up to approximately 80 degrees for all polarization combinations

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis of entanglement in neutron-proton scattering (Witala, 2024) to the three-nucleon system, investigating both elastic neutron-deuteron scattering and exclusive deuteron breakup. The study builds on recent interest in nuclear entanglement and its experimental signatures in nucleon pairs, following work by Beane (2019, 2021), Bai (2022, 2023, 2024), Liu (2023), and Kirchbach on entanglement power in nucleon-nucleon scattering. The results enable future experimental searches for entangled Bell-like states in few-nucleon systems using polarized beams and targets, though the presence of entanglement-spoiling components in all identified states suggests that perfectly pure entangled states may be extremely difficult to achieve in practice.
