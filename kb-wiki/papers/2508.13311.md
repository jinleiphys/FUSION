# Seeing Through the Nucleus: A Review of Color Transparency Phenomena

> We review the current status of the phenomenon of Color Transparency (CT), a fundamental consequence of the description of hadrons from Quantum Chromo Dynamics. CT refers to the vanishing of final (and/or initial) state interactions with the nuclear medium for exclusive process at sufficiently high enough momentum transfers. We discuss the current experimental observations relating to CT and their theoretical implications for other high energy processes. Future CT experiments and facilities are also described.

## Key claim

Color Transparency (CT) is a prediction of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) that hadrons produced in high-momentum-transfer coherent processes can fluctuate to a reduced transverse size, color-neutral configuration that only barely interacts with the nuclear medium; however, the phenomenon requires three specific conditions to be satisfied, and recent experiments have shown that CT does not occur in quasielastic electron-induced proton knockout up to momentum transfers of 14.2 GeV\(^2\), suggesting that a point-like configuration was not formed in those kinematics.

## Method

The authors review experimental measurements of nuclear transparency in quasielastic \((e,e'p)\) reactions, meson electroproduction, photoproduction, and exclusive hadron scattering, comparing results to theoretical models including the quantum diffusion model and the hadronic basis expansion approach. They analyze the Fermilab E791 experiment on coherent production of two jets by high-energy pions (\(\pi A \to A + 2\) jets), the Jefferson Lab Hall C \((e,e'p)\) experiment on carbon targets up to \(Q^2 < 14\) GeV\(^2\), and the Hall B CLAS experiment on \(\rho^0\)-meson electroproduction. The theoretical framework examines the minimal Fock component mechanism versus the Feynman mechanism for hard scattering, and uses the quantum diffusion model parameter \(\Delta M^2\) to characterize the expansion of point-like configurations.

## Key numbers

- The Jefferson Lab Hall C experiment ruled out CT effects in quasielastic proton knockout on carbon up to \(Q^2 < 14\) GeV\(^2\) (PhysRevLett.126.082301)
- The BNL \((p,pp)\) experiments observed an initial rise in transparency for effective proton momentum \(p_p = 6 - 9.5\) GeV/\(c\), followed by a decrease at higher momentum (Leksanov:2001ui, Aclander:2004zm)
- The quantum diffusion model parameter \(\Delta M^2\) was initially estimated as 0.7–1 GeV\(^2\) based on the additive quark model, but recent \((e,e'p)\) experiments indicate a significantly larger value, with \(\Delta M^2 \gtrsim 3\) GeV\(^2\) from the positive parity baryon spectrum
- Superconformal baryon-meson symmetry and light-front holographic QCD calculations yield \(\Delta M^2 \approx 3.5\) GeV\(^2\) (Caplow-Munro:2021xwi)
- The pion electroproduction experiment showed the energy scale for onset of CT in mesons is \(\sim 1\) GeV (Clasie:2007aa)
- The photoproduction experiment on \(^4\)He and \(^2\)H targets studied transparency as a function of \(|t|\) up to \(|t| < 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) at center-of-mass scattering angles of 70\(^\circ\) and 90\(^\circ\) (Dutta:2003mk)

## Context

This review extends the earlier comprehensive review by Dutta et al. (2013) on color transparency experiments, incorporating the recent null result from Jefferson Lab Hall C (PhysRevLett.126.082301) that contradicted earlier expectations from the BNL \((p,pp)\) experiments (Mardor:1998zf, Leksanov:2001ui, Aclander:2004zm). The work connects CT studies to the Feynman mechanism versus minimal Fock component debate in hard exclusive processes, and explores implications for the Hagedorn statistical bootstrap model of the quark-gluon plasma phase transition. Future experiments at Jefferson Lab with higher beam energies will extend the \(Q^2\) range for \(\rho^0\) and \(\pi^+\) transparency studies, and photoproduction experiments using the \(\sim 8.5\) GeV photon beam in Hall D will explore different processes in \(^2\)H, \(^4\)He, and \(^{12}\)C.
