# Implications of the Dirac CP phase upon parametric resonance for sub-GeV neutrinos

> We perform an analytic and numerical study of parametric resonance in a three-neutrino framework for sub-GeV neutrinos which travel through a periodic density profile. Commensurate with the initial level of approximation, we develop a parametric resonance condition similar to the exact condition for two-neutrino systems. For a castle wall density profile, the ν_e to ν_μoscillation probability is enhanced significantly and bounded by cos^2 θ_{23}. The CP phase δenters into the oscillation probability as a phase shift. For several cases, we examine the interplay between the characteristics of the castle wall profile and the CP phase and determine which profiles maximize the separation between oscillations with δ= 0, π/2, π. We also consider neutrinos which travel along a chord through the earth, passing from the mantle to core and back to mantle again. Significant enhancement of the oscillation probability is seen even in the case in which the neutrino energy is far from the MSW resonant energies. At 500 GeV, the difference between oscillation probabilities with δ=0 and δ=π/2 is maximized.

# Implications of the Dirac CP phase upon parametric resonance for sub-GeV neutrinos

## Key claim

For sub-GeV neutrinos traveling through a periodic (castle wall) density profile, the three-neutrino system can be reduced to an effective two-neutrino system in the propagation basis, and the parametric resonance condition from two-neutrino theory applies approximately; the resulting ν_e → ν_μ oscillation probability is bounded by cos² θ₂₃ rather than unity, and the CP phase δ enters only as a phase shift in the oscillation probability at leading order in θ₁₃.

## Method

The authors develop an analytic approximation for three-neutrino oscillations through a periodic "castle wall" density profile (two alternating constant-density regions) by rotating to the density-dependent propagation basis of Peres (2004), which block-diagonalizes the Hamiltonian for sub-GeV energies. They derive a parametric resonance condition (X̂₃ = 0) analogous to the exact two-neutrino condition, and compute the ν_e → ν_μ oscillation probability after an integer number of periods to leading order in θ₁₃. Numerical full three-neutrino simulations (with Δ₃₁ oscillations averaged) are compared to the analytic results for realistic Earth mantle/core densities.

## Key numbers

- The maximum effective potential in Earth's interior is V ∼ 5 × 10⁻¹³ eV, corresponding to density ∼13 g/cm³.
- For Δ₃₁ ∼ 2.4 × 10⁻³ eV² and E ∼ 1 GeV, the small parameter ε := 2EV/Δ₃₁ < 0.4.
- The MSW resonance energy in the mantle (density 4.5 g/cm³) is E_R ∼ 100 MeV; in the core (density 11.5 g/cm³) it is E_R ∼ 40 MeV, using θ₁₂ = 0.58.
- In the example with θ₁₃ = 0.15, ε = 2EV_B/Δ₃₁ = 0.07 for the core density region.
- The oscillation probability after integer periods is bounded by cos² θ₂₃; for θ₂₃ = 0.785 (maximal mixing), this bound is 0.5.
- For the half-wavelength condition example with θ₁₃ = 0.3, the phase offset ψ = −0.3 cos δ, and the oscillation phase per period is Φ = 2.49.

## Context

This work extends the two-neutrino parametric resonance formalism of Akhmedov (1999) and Akhmedov (2005) to three-neutrino oscillations including the Dirac CP phase, specifically for sub-GeV neutrinos traversing Earth's mantle and core. It builds upon the propagation basis approach of Peres (2004) and the semi-analytic Earth neutrino studies of Akhmedov (2012) and Akhmedov (2012) which considered energies above 1 GeV. The results enable assessment of how CP-violating effects (δ = 0, ±π/2, π) can be distinguished via parametric resonance in future long-baseline or atmospheric neutrino experiments, particularly for sub-GeV energies where the solar mass-squared difference drives the oscillations.
