# Hadron Interactions from lattice QCD

> We review our strategy to study hadron interactions from lattice QCD using newly proposed potential method. We first explain our strategy in the case of nuclear potentials and its application to nuclear physics. We then discuss the origin of the repulsive core, by adding strange quarks to the system. We also explore a possibility for H-dibaryon to exist in flavor SU(3) limit of lattice QCD. We conclude the paper with an application of our strategy to investigate the maximum mass of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The HAL QCD potential method can extract hadron interactions from lattice QCD and reproduce qualitative features of the nuclear force, but quantitative agreement with experiment requires simulations at the physical pion mass (140 MeV) rather than the heavier masses (700 MeV or 470 MeV) used in the presented results.

## Method

The HAL QCD method proceeds in three steps: first, two-body potentials are calculated from lattice QCD numerical simulations; second, physical observables such as binding energy and scattering phase shift are obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with these potentials; third, the same two-body potentials are used to investigate few-hadron systems or many-body systems like nuclei and nuclear matter. The nuclear potential for the spin-singlet channel was calculated at lattice spacing a ≈ 0.1 fm with pion mass mπ ≈ 700 MeV, and the flavor-singlet potential was studied in the flavor SU(3) limit (mu = md = ms) at MPS ≈ 1015 MeV and MPS ≈ 470 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass in the nuclear potential calculation: mπ ≈ 700 MeV, compared to mπ ≈ 140 MeV in Nature
- Scattering length in the spin-singlet channel at mπ ≈ 700 MeV: 1.6 ± 1.1 fm, compared to about 20 fm at the physical pion mass
- Binding energy of the 4-nucleon ground state (4He) in the flavor SU(3) limit at MPS ≈ 470 MeV: about 5.1 MeV
- Flavor-singlet potential studied at MPS = 1015 MeV on volumes L ≈ 2, 3, and 4 fm at a ≈ 0.12 fm
- H-dibaryon binding energy in the flavor SU(3) limit: about 20 to 50 MeV at the range of MPS studied
- Root-mean-square distance of the H-dibaryon compared to the deuteron: 3.8 fm for the deuteron in Nature
- Empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter: (kF, E/A) ≈ (1.36 fm⁻¹, -15.7 MeV)
- Maximum mass of neutron star at MPS ≈ 470 MeV: 0.53 times the solar mass M⊙
- Extrapolated maximum mass to MPS ≈ 140 MeV: 2.2 M⊙ with radius 12 km

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method first proposed by Ishii (2006) and Aoki (2009) for extracting nuclear potentials from lattice QCD. The finding that the flavor-singlet potential shows attraction rather than repulsion at short distances supports the explanation by Oka (1983, 1986) that the repulsive core originates from partial restriction of color combinations due to the Pauli principle among quarks. The results enable future lattice QCD simulations at the physical pion mass using the K computer in Kobe, Japan (peak speed 10 PetaFlops) to predict nuclear matter properties and neutron star masses from first principles.
