# Nucleon form factors and parton distributions in nonlocal chiral effective theory

> We present a review of recent applications of nonlocal chiral effective theory to hadron structure studies. Starting from a nonlocal meson--baryon effective chiral Lagrangian, we show how the introduction of a correlation function representing the finite extent of hadrons regularizes the meson loop integrals and introduces momentum dependence in vertex form factors in a gauge invariant manner. We apply the framework to the calculation of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, unpolarized and polarized parton distributions, as well as transverse momentum dependent distributions and generalized parton distributions. Assuming that the nonlocal behavior is a general property of all interactions, we also discuss the application to the lepton anomalous magnetic moment in nonlocal QED.

## Key claim

Nonlocal chiral effective theory, which introduces a correlation function representing the finite extent of hadrons, provides a gauge-invariant regularization of meson loop integrals and generates momentum-dependent vertex form factors automatically, extending the range of applicability beyond conventional chiral perturbation theory (which is valid only for \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.1\) GeV\(^2\)).

## Method

The authors construct a nonlocal meson–baryon effective chiral Lagrangian where the interaction vertices contain a correlation function that smears the fields over a finite region, representing the hadron size. This nonlocal Lagrangian is made gauge invariant by including gauge links, which generate both the regulator for loop integrals and the \(Q^2\) dependence of form factors at tree level. The framework is then applied to compute nucleon electromagnetic form factors, unpolarized and polarized parton distributions, transverse momentum dependent distributions, and generalized parton distributions via meson loop diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The dipole mass parameter for nucleon form factors is \(Q_0^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\) (from the dipole form factor \(G_D = 1/(1+Q^2/Q_0^2)^2\)).
- The axial form factor dipole mass parameter from MiniBooNE is \(M_A^2 = 1.10^{+0.13}_{-0.15}\) GeV\(^2\).
- The axial radius of the nucleon is \(\langle r_A \rangle \approx 0.64\) fm.
- The flavor singlet axial charge (quark spin fraction) from JAM Collaboration analysis is \(\Delta\Sigma = 0.36(9)\) at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- The strange quark polarization from inclusive and semi-inclusive DIS data analyses is \(\Delta s^+ = -0.03(10)\).
- The lattice QCD axial charge is \(g_A = 1.271(13)\) (from Chang et al.).
- The proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen is \(\langle r_E^p \rangle \approx 0.84\) fm, while recent electron-proton scattering gives 0.831 fm and electronic hydrogen Lamb shift gives 0.833 fm.

## Context

This review extends the finite range regularization (FRR) approach used by Young, Leinweber, and others for lattice QCD extrapolations, but makes it more systematic by generating the regulator from a nonlocal Lagrangian rather than introducing it ad hoc. The nonlocal chiral EFT has been previously applied by the authors to electromagnetic and strange form factors (He et al., 2015), PDFs (He et al., 2018), and Sivers functions (Salamu et al., 2015). The framework enables calculations of sea quark contributions to nucleon structure that can be compared with lattice QCD results and experimental data from Jefferson Lab, COMPASS, and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
