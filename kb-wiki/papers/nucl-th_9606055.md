# Physics of Coulomb Corrections in Hanbury-Brown Twiss Interferometry in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We discuss the elementary physics of the final state Coulomb interactions in Hanbury-Brown Twiss interferometry, showing -- with explicit comparison to E877 data for $π^+π^-$ and $π^\pm p$ -- that the Coulomb corrections in the pair correlation function can be well understood in terms of simple classical physics. We connect the classical picture with descriptions in terms of Coulomb wave functions, and investigate the influence of the ``central'' Coulomb potential on the pair correlation function.

# Physics of Coulomb Corrections in Hanbury-Brown Twiss Interferometry in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The Coulomb corrections in pair correlation functions for Hanbury-Brown Twiss interferometry can be well understood through simple classical physics rather than the standard Gamow factor, provided that the pair separation at creation is larger than the classical turning point, which is the case for most pairs observed in heavy ion collisions, where the characteristic source radius (5–15 fm) exceeds the turning point (≈2 fm at q ≈ 10 MeV/c).

## Method

The authors construct a toy model that neglects the Coulomb interaction between the pair for separations less than an initial radius r₀, and includes only the relative Coulomb interaction for separations greater than r₀, using energy conservation to relate the final observed relative momentum q to the initial momentum q₀ at r₀. They compare this model to E877 data for π⁺π⁻, π⁻p, and π⁺p systems produced in Au+Au collisions at the AGS, and also connect the classical picture to a quantum-mechanical description using s-wave Coulomb wave functions in the WKB approximation. The influence of the central Coulomb potential from the remaining charged particles is treated by assuming an effective central charge Z_eff.

## Key numbers

- For pions, the classical turning point r_t ≈ (200 fm)/q², where q is measured in MeV/c; at q ≈ 10 MeV/c, r_t ≈ 2 fm.
- The two-particle Bohr radius a₀ = 387 fm for ππ and 222 fm for πp.
- For ππ pairs, a₀q = 1/|η| = 1.96 × q/(MeV/c) ≫ 1; for πp, a₀q = 1.13 × q/(MeV/c) ≫ 1.
- For pions with r₀ = 10 fm, the relation between final and initial relative momentum is q² = q₀² ± 20 (MeV/c)².
- The toy model gives a good account of E877 data for r₀ in the range of 9–15 fm.
- Using the Gamow factor instead of the proper Coulomb correction leads to a correlation function that is ~30% wider, implying a correspondingly reduced radius parameter.
- For Z_eff ≈ 150, r_a ≈ 7 fm, and p_a ≈ 300 MeV/c, the central Coulomb potential causes a change in the extracted radius of ten percent.

## Context

This work extends earlier treatments of Coulomb corrections in HBT interferometry that used the Gamow factor (Gyulassy and collaborators, 1979, 1981) or considered finite source size effects (Pratt, 1986; Bowler, 1985). The authors show that the standard Gamow correction, which assumes pair creation at separations small compared to the classical turning point, is not physically correct for heavy ion collisions where the source radius is much larger than the turning point. The toy model provides a simple method to extract the mean separation of pairs when screening effects become negligible from unlike-sign data, and then apply the correct Coulomb correction to like-sign pairs. This enables more accurate extraction of source radii from correlation functions, and the procedure can be extended to multi-dimensional analyses in terms of q_out, q_side, and q_long.
