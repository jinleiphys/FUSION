# The Phase Structure of Dense QCD from Chiral Models

> A new phase of dense QCD proposed in the limit of large number of colors, Quarkyonic Phase, is discussed in chiral approaches. The interplay between chiral symmetry breaking and confinement together with the $N_c$ dependence of the phase diagram are dealt with in the PNJL model. We also discuss a possible phase at finite density where chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken while its center remains unbroken. The quark number susceptibility exhibits a strong enhancement at the restoration point of the center symmetry rather than that of the chiral symmetry. This is reminiscent of the quarkyonic transition.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a non-standard pattern of chiral symmetry breaking (to $SU(N_f)_V \times (Z_{N_f})_A$) can produce an intermediate phase at finite density where chiral symmetry is broken but the center symmetry is restored, and that the resulting enhancement of baryon number susceptibility is reminiscent of the quarkyonic transition in the $N_c=3$ world, though the analysis does not exclude the possibility that both transitions occur simultaneously.

## Method

The author uses the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model to study the interplay between chiral symmetry breaking and confinement, including the $N_c$ dependence of the phase diagram. A general Ginzburg-Landau free energy is constructed with 2-quark ($M$) and 4-quark ($\Sigma$) condensates to explore phases under the alternative symmetry breaking pattern $SU(N_f)_L \times SU(N_f)_R \to SU(N_f)_V \times (Z_{N_f})_A$. The mean-field potential is analyzed to map the phase diagram onto the $(T,\mu)$ plane.

## Key numbers

- Two tricritical points are located in the Ginzburg-Landau phase diagram: TCP$_1$ at $(A,B)=(0,1/4)$ and TCP$_2$ at $(A,B)=(1/4,-1/8)$, with a triple point $T$ at $(A,B)=(1/8,0)$.
- The pion decay constant is expressed as $F_\pi = \sqrt{\sigma_0^2 + \frac{8}{3}\chi_0^2}$, where $\sigma_0$ and $\chi_0$ are the expectation values of the scalar fields from the 2-quark and 4-quark condensates, respectively.
- For $N_c=3$, the deconfinement and chiral crossover lines are on top of each other in a wide range of $\mu$, with a critical point associated with chiral symmetry appearing around the junction of those crossovers.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the Quarkyonic Phase, originally proposed for large $N_c$ by McLerran and collaborators (2007), to the $N_c=3$ world using chiral models. It builds on the PNJL model developed by Meisinger, Rischke, and others, and considers an alternative symmetry breaking pattern previously discussed by Stern (1998) and others, which was ruled out at zero density by Shifman (2000) but may be viable at finite density. The paper enables future microscopic calculations using Schwinger-Dyson equations or NJL-type models with quartic operators, and raises open questions about baryon chirality assignment and the anomaly matching conditions at finite temperature and density.
