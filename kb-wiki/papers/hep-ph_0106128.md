# On the Normalization of the Neutrino-Deuteron Cross Section

> As is well-known, comparison of the solar neutrino fluxes measured in SuperKamiokande (SK) by $ν+ e^- \to ν+ e^-$ and in the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) by $ν_e + d \to e^- + p + p$ can provide a ``smoking gun'' signature for neutrino oscillations as the solution to the solar neutrino puzzle. This occurs because SK has some sensitivity to all active neutrino flavors whereas SNO can isolate electron neutrinos. This comparison depends crucially on the normalization and uncertainty of the theoretical charged-current neutrino-deuteron cross section. We address a number of effects which are significant enough to change the interpretation of the SK--SNO comparison.

## Key claim

The theoretical charged-current neutrino-deuteron cross section used by the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) to isolate the electron neutrino flux is underestimated by about 6% due to three overlooked normalization effects, which could qualitatively change the outcome of the comparison between SuperKamiokande (SK) and SNO that is intended as a model-independent test for neutrino oscillations.

## Method

The authors identify three corrections to the total cross section for the reaction ν_e + d → e⁻ + p + p that were omitted in the most refined recent calculations by Butler, Chen, and Kong (BCK) and by Nakamura, Sato, Gudkov, and Kubodera (NSGK): (1) the use of an outdated value of the weak axial coupling g_A, which should be -1.267 rather than -1.26 or -1.254; (2) the inner radiative correction that renormalizes the Fermi constant G_F differently for semileptonic processes than for muon decay; and (3) the outer radiative correction from bremsstrahlung and low-momentum internal photon exchange. They also examine the consistency of Towner's radiative corrections to the differential cross section by integrating his tabulated results for two different treatments of bremsstrahlung energy.

## Key numbers

- The measured solar neutrino flux in SK, in units of the expected electron neutrino flux from the Standard Solar Model, is 0.45 ± 0.02, with systematic uncertainty dominating.
- For ν_e → ν_μ, ν_τ oscillations, the ν_e flux is 0.34 and the ν_μ, ν_τ flux is 0.66, so that the measured flux in SK is 0.34 + 0.66/6 = 0.45.
- The present value of g_A is -1.267 ± 0.004; BCK use -1.26 (making their cross section about 1% too small) and NSGK use -1.254 (making their cross section about 2% too small).
- The inner radiative correction increases the ν_e + d → e⁻ + p + p cross section by 2.4%.
- Towner's total radiative corrections (including bremsstrahlung energy in the deposited energy) are about 4.4%, giving an outer radiative correction of about 2% after subtracting the inner 2.4%.
- BCK and NSGK each underestimate the total cross section by about 6%.
- BCK and NSGK claim an uncertainty of about 3% in the range of energies relevant for solar neutrinos, and they agree to about 1%.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the theoretical charged-current neutrino-deuteron cross section that is critical for the comparison between SuperKamiokande (which detects all active neutrino flavors via ν + e⁻ scattering) and the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (which isolates electron neutrinos via ν_e + d → e⁻ + p + p). The authors follow the analogous percent-level corrections to the theoretical ν̅_e + p → e⁺ + n cross section studied by Vogel (1984) and Vogel and Beacom (1999) that were necessary to achieve agreement with experiment. The paper identifies three effects overlooked by the most refined recent calculations of Butler, Chen, and Kong (2001) and Nakamura, Sato, Gudkov, and Kubodera (2001), and questions the consistency of Towner's (1998) radiative corrections to the differential cross section, calling for additional work on the bremsstrahlung spectrum before the 3% theoretical uncertainty claimed by BCK and NSGK can be considered attainable.
