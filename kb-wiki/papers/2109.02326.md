# Collapse of N$=$28 magicity in exotic $^{40}$Mg -- Probe of deformed halo and 2n-radioactivity at Mg neutron drip-line

> The exotic phenomenon of two-neutron halos and 2n-radioactivity are explored in the neutron-rich $^{40,42,44}$Mg by employing various variants of the relativistic mean-field approach. The extended tail of spatial density distributions including the enhanced neutron radii and skin thickness, pairing correlations, single-particle spectrum and wave functions predict $^{40,42,44}$Mg to be strong candidates for deformed neutron halos. Weakening of magicity at N$=$28 plays a significant role in the existence of a weakly bound halo in $^{40}$Mg which is currently the heaviest isotope of Mg accessible experimentally. Large deformation, mixing of f-p shell Nilsson orbitals and the valence neutron occupancy of p-states leads to a reduced centrifugal barrier and broader spatial density distributions that favour 2n-radioactivity in $^{42,44}$Mg.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the N=28 shell closure completely collapses in the exotic nucleus \(^{40}\)Mg, enabling a deformed two-neutron halo structure, while the heavier isotopes \(^{42,44}\)Mg are predicted to be two-neutron unbound and thus candidates for 2n-radioactivity. The key qualifier is that the existence of a halo does not necessarily lead to 2n-radioactivity, as \(^{40}\)Mg is weakly bound but not 2n-unbound, whereas \(^{42,44}\)Mg are unbound and may exhibit 2n-decay.

## Method

The authors employed several variants of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach, including the NL3*, TMA, PK1, FSU-Gold, FSU-Garnet, DD-ME2, and DD-PC1 parameterizations, to calculate neutron separation energies, single-particle spectra, pairing correlations, and radial density distributions for \(^{40,42,44}\)Mg. They also used shell-model calculations and the Nilsson-Strutinsky method (NSM) for comparison, and analyzed the shell gap, deformation, and neutron skin thickness to assess the weakening of N=28 magicity and the formation of halos.

## Key numbers

- \(^{40}\)Mg: two-neutron separation energy S\(_{2n}\) = 0.67 ± 0.71 MeV from AME2020 mass surface extrapolation; RMF(NL3*) gives S\(_{2n}\) = 2.45 MeV and S\(_n\) = 1.69 MeV.
- \(^{42}\)Mg: RMF(NL3*) gives S\(_{2n}\) = -0.06 MeV and S\(_n\) = 0.24 MeV, indicating 2n-unbound but one-neutron bound.
- \(^{44}\)Mg: RMF(NL3*) gives S\(_{2n}\) = -0.27 MeV and S\(_n\) = 0.38 MeV, also 2n-unbound.
- Shell gap (\(\Delta\)E) between \(\nu\)1f\(_{7/2}\) and \(\nu\)2p\(_{3/2}\) in \(^{40}\)Mg is about 1 MeV or less, the smallest among N=28 isotones, confirming collapse of magicity.
- Deformation of \(^{40}\)Mg is prolate with \(\beta\) = 0.453 (from Watanabe 2014, cited in text).
- Decay energies for 2n-emission estimated with RMF(NL3*): 60 keV for \(^{42}\)Mg and 270 keV for \(^{44}\)Mg.
- Neutron pairing energy at N=28 in Mg isotopes is non-zero and maximal among isotopes from N=8 to N=32, indicating broken magicity.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of neutron halos and 2n-radioactivity in the Mg isotopic chain, building on prior experimental observations of \(\gamma\)-ray transitions in \(^{40}\)Mg by Crawford (2019) and mass measurements from AME2020. It contradicts the expectation of a spherical magic N=28 nucleus for \(^{40}\)Mg, consistent with earlier reports of weakening N=28 magicity in neutron-rich isotones by Bastin (2007) and Watanabe (2014). The results enable future experimental searches for 2n-radioactivity in \(^{42,44}\)Mg and provide a theoretical framework linking deformed halos, pairing correlations, and the collapse of shell closures near the neutron drip-line.
