# Polyakov loop in 2+1 flavor QCD

> We study the temperature dependence of the renormalized Polyakov loop in 2+1 flavor QCD for temperatures T<210 MeV. We extend previous calculations by the HotQCD collaboration using the highly improved staggered quark action and perform a continuum extrapolation of the renormalized Polyakov loop. We compare the lattice results with the prediction of non-interacting static-light hadron resonance gas, which describes the temperature dependence of the renormalized Polyakov loop up to T<140 MeV but fails above that temperature. Furthermore, we discuss the temperature dependence of the light and strange quark condensates.

## Key claim

The renormalized Polyakov loop in 2+1 flavor QCD can be described by a non-interacting static-light hadron resonance gas model only up to T < 140 MeV, and the model fails above that temperature, indicating that conventional static-light hadron states are insufficient to explain the rapid decrease of the static quark free energy at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with physical strange quark mass and light quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of 160 MeV, on 40^3 × 10 and 32^3 × 8 lattices. They carried out continuum extrapolations of the renormalized Polyakov loop using 1/Nτ^2 scaling for temperatures T ≥ 135 MeV, and averaged Nτ = 8 and Nτ = 10 data for lower temperatures. The lattice results were compared with a hadron resonance gas model constructed from lattice QCD calculations of static-light and static-strange meson spectra from the ETMC collaboration and static baryon spectra, supplemented by quark model predictions for higher excited states.

## Key numbers

- The continuum extrapolated renormalized Polyakov loop was obtained for temperatures from T = 117 MeV to T = 210 MeV in 5 MeV steps
- The hadron resonance gas model describes lattice results up to T < 140 MeV but fails above that temperature
- The parameter Δ (subtracted mass of the lowest static-light meson) was fixed at Δ = 593 ± 18 MeV by matching to the lattice result at T = 117 MeV
- The lightest static-strange meson energy relative to the lightest static-light meson was estimated as E0^s = 84 MeV for infinite heavy quark mass
- The lowest lying positive and negative parity baryons correspond to 79 states, while all baryon states included from the diquark model correspond to 984 states
- The contribution of higher excited baryon states is uncertain by a factor of 2.5 for T > 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2011) using the HISQ action and performs an independent continuum extrapolation, complementing earlier continuum results obtained using the stout action (Borsanyi 2010). The hadron resonance gas approach follows the suggestion of Megias (2012) but uses lattice QCD calculations of the static-light hadron spectrum from Michael (2010) and Wagner (2011) rather than experimental heavy-light meson data. The results enable future investigation of whether exotic static-light hadron states or partial chiral symmetry restoration can explain the discrepancy between lattice data and the hadron resonance gas model for T > 140 MeV.
