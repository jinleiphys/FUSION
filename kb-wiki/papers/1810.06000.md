# Simple description of the temperature dependencies of the width of fission-fragment mass yield in 197Au and 209Bi at intermediate energies

> Simple approach is used to a description of the experimental data for the widths of fission fragment mass yields in 197Au and 209Bi at low and intermediate energies. This approach based on both the expression for the temperature dependence of the width of fission fragment mass yield and the mass of the most probable fragment. The expression for the width of fission fragment mass yield depends on the mass of the most probable fragment, the surface terms of the energy level density parameter, the temperature and the stiffness parameter of the potential related to mass-asymmetric degree of freedom. It is shown that the contribution of the surface term of the energy level density parameter into the width is important for description of the experimental data in wide range of energies.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a simple expression for the width of fission-fragment mass yield, which includes both volume and surface terms of the energy level density parameter, describes experimental data for photo-fission of 197Au and 209Bi across a wide range of temperatures, whereas the earlier Strutinsky formula (using only the volume term) fails at high temperatures (T ≳ 1 MeV). The key qualifier is that the approach requires substituting the fissioning system's mass by twice the most probable fragment mass (2Aprob) and using an effective temperature Teff to account for pre-fission neutron evaporation.

## Method

The authors apply an expression for the width σ² = 2A²T / (C + 2κA²/³T²), derived from a statistical model including both volume and surface contributions to the level density parameter, to experimental data for the photo-fission of 197Au and 209Bi induced by bremsstrahlung. They account for neutron evaporation by replacing the original fissioning nucleus mass A with 2Aprob (twice the most probable fragment mass) and using an effective temperature Teff calculated from the reduced excitation energy after neutron emission. The stiffness parameter C is extracted by fitting Eq. (1) to the experimental width data, and the quality of fit is compared to that obtained using the Strutinsky formula (Eq. 7, with β=0).

## Key numbers

- Stiffness parameter C = 460 ± 10 MeV for 197Au, obtained by fitting Eq. (1) to experimental data.
- Stiffness parameter C = 719 ± 20 MeV for 209Bi, obtained by fitting Eq. (1) to experimental data.
- For 197Au, the deviation S (chi-squared per degree of freedom) is 0.194 using Eq. (1) versus 18.4 using the Strutinsky formula (Eq. 7).
- For 209Bi, the deviation S is 1.99 using Eq. (1) versus 26.4 using the Strutinsky formula (Eq. 7).
- The most probable fragment mass Aprob for 197Au at bremsstrahlung end-point energies 300, 350, and 400 MeV is 97, 95, and 93, respectively, with corresponding effective temperatures Teff of 3.561, 3.572, and 3.570 MeV.
- The coefficients for the level density parameter are α = 0.0722396 MeV⁻¹ and β = 0.195267 MeV⁻¹, taken from the back-shifted Fermi gas model.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Denisov et al. (2018, Ref. dbkss) by applying it to photo-fission data for 197Au and 209Bi, which had not been analyzed with these expressions before. It contrasts with the earlier Strutinsky (1964) formula, which uses only the volume term of the level density parameter and predicts a linear temperature dependence of the width at all temperatures, whereas the new expression accounts for surface effects that cause the width to saturate and eventually decrease at high temperatures. The results enable more accurate modeling of fission-fragment mass distributions at intermediate energies and provide stiffness parameters C for these nuclei that can be compared with values from particle-induced fission of neighboring nuclei such as 201Tl (C = 382.5 MeV) and 209Bi/210Po.
