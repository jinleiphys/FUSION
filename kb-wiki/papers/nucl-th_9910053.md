# Infinite Nuclear Matter on the Light Front: A Modern Approach to Brueckner Theory

> Understanding an important class of experiments requires that light-front dynamics and related light cone variables k^+ and k_perp be used. If one uses k^+ as a momentum variable, the corresponding canonical spatial variable is x^-=x^0-x^3 and the time variable is x^0+x^3. This is the light front (LF) approach of Dirac. A relativistic light front formulation of nuclear dynamics is developed and applied to treating infinite nuclear matter in a method which includes the corelations of pairs of nculeons. This is light front Brueckner theory.

## Key claim
Light-front Brueckner theory, when applied to infinite nuclear matter, yields nucleons carrying 84% of the nuclear plus-momentum (and potentially over 90% when 2p-2h correlations are included), which is a significant improvement over the mean-field result of 65% and approaches the 90% needed to explain the EMC effect, but the author notes this is only "a good start" and "an excellent start" rather than a definitive resolution.

## Method
The author develops a light-front formulation of nuclear dynamics starting from a Lagrangian (the Walecka model, QHD1) and constructs the energy-momentum tensor to obtain the plus-momentum and minus-momentum operators. For the correlated case, the Lagrangian is decomposed into nucleon kinetic terms, meson terms, and a two-nucleon one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) including pions, rho, omega, sigma, eta, and delta mesons; the nucleon-nucleon correlations are treated via the Brueckner G-matrix in the independent pair approximation, with retardation effects included in the OBEP. The light-front version of the Lippman-Schwinger equation is solved, and the resulting wave function contains both nucleon-nucleon correlations and explicit mesons.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per nucleon: 14.7 MeV (with NN correlations included)
- Fermi momentum: \(k_F = 1.37\) fm (with NN correlations included)
- Compressibility: 180 MeV (with NN correlations included)
- Effective nucleon mass: \(M + g_s \phi = 0.79 M\) (with NN correlations), compared to the mean-field value of \(0.56 M\)
- Nucleons carry 84% of the nuclear plus-momentum from the uncorrelated Fermi gas part of the wave function; including 2p-2h correlations is estimated to raise this above 90%
- Ratio of excess pions per nucleon: \(N_\pi / A = 5\%\), compared to a previously computed result of 15% (Birse and Frankfurt)
- In the mean-field approximation, nucleons carry only 65% of the nuclear plus-momentum, with vector mesons carrying 35%

## Context
This work extends the light-front dynamics approach of Dirac (1949) to infinite nuclear matter, addressing the EMC effect discovered by the European Muon Collaboration and the subsequent "crisis in nuclear physics" identified by Bertsch et al. The author argues that previous conventional explanations of nuclear binding and Fermi motion effects had not been properly evaluated using light-front dynamics. The paper builds on prior work by Miller and collaborators (1997) and Machleidt and Miller, and contrasts with the mean-field results of the Walecka model (QHD1) which gave nucleons only 65% of the plus-momentum. The computed 5% excess pions is much smaller than the 15% found by Birse and Frankfurt, and the author suggests this may be consistent with Drell-Yan data based on phenomenological calculations by Jung and Miller. The approach enables future calculations of nuclear structure functions and high-energy nuclear reactions using light-front wave functions.
