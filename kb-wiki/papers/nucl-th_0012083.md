# Constraint Molecular Dynamics approach to Fermionic systems

> We propose a Constraint Molecular Dynamics model for Fermionic system. In this approach the equations of motion of wave packets for the nuclear many-body problem are solved by imposing that the one-body occupation probability $\bar{f}(r,p,t)$ can assume only values less or equal to 1. This condition reflects the Fermionic nature of the studied systems and it is implemented with a fast algorithm which allows also the study of the heaviest colliding system. The parameters of the model have been chosen to reproduce the average binding energy and radii of nuclei in the mass region $A=30\sim 208$. Some comparison to data is given.

## Key claim
The Constraint Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model enforces the fermionic condition that the one-body occupation probability \(\overline{f}(r,p,t)\) must remain less than or equal to 1 at each time step, thereby maintaining the Pauli principle in a semiclassical molecular dynamics framework without requiring a fully antisymmetrized wave function. The authors claim this constraint allows the model to reproduce ground-state properties and collision outcomes that standard Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) cannot, but the agreement with experimental data is still imperfect for light-charged-particle yields.

## Method
The authors represent each nucleon as a Gaussian wave packet with independent dispersions in coordinate (\(\sigma_r\)) and momentum (\(\sigma_p\)) space, allowing \(\sigma_p\sigma_r \ge \hbar/2\). The equations of motion for the centroids are solved via a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method, coupled with two algorithms: (1) standard two-body elastic collisions with Pauli blocking computed from the occupation probability, and (2) a constraint that at each time step, for each particle \(i\), the occupation \(\overline{f}_i\) (integrated over a phase-space hypercube of volume \(h^3\)) is forced to be \(\le 1\) by performing many-body elastic scatterings among the nearest identical particles within \(3\sigma_r\) and \(3\sigma_p\). The effective interaction includes Skyrme-type volume and density-dependent terms, an asymmetry term, a surface term, and the Coulomb interaction.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies of \(-8.2\) MeV for \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) and \(-8.4\) MeV for \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) are obtained from ground-state configurations.
- The model parameters are \(\sigma_r = 1.3\) fm, \(\sigma_p/\hbar = 0.47\) fm\(^{-1}\), and \(C_s = -2\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^2\).
- The volume term parameters are \(t_0 = -356\) MeV and \(t_3 = 303\) MeV, reproducing symmetric nuclear matter saturation with compressibility 200 MeV; the asymmetry term uses \(a_{\text{sym}} = 32\) MeV.
- For \(^{40}\text{Ca}+^{40}\text{Ca}\) at 35 MeV/nucleon, the CoMD collision rate reaches a maximum about 3 times greater than in QMD.
- The typical CPU time for a system of mass number around 80 for 300 fm/\(c\) is about 10 seconds on a 600 MHz Unix machine.

## Context
This work extends the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) approach (Aichelin, 1991) by adding a constraint that enforces the Pauli principle, overcoming the tendency of QMD to evolve toward a classical Boltzmann distribution. It contrasts with fully antisymmetrized methods such as Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (Feldmeier, 1990) and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (Ono, 1992), which are computationally expensive for systems with total mass above 200. The CoMD model enables practical simulations of heavy-ion collisions for the heaviest systems, and the authors demonstrate its ability to reproduce experimental isotope distributions for \(^{40}\text{Ca}+^{40}\text{Ca}\) and Au+Au at 35 MeV/nucleon, where QMD fails to produce intermediate-mass fragments.
