# Photons from the Color Glass Condensate in p+A collisions

> We report on a first NLO computation of photon production in p+A collisions at collider energies within the Color Glass Condensate framework, significantly extending previous LO results. At central rapidites, our result is the dominant contribution and probes multi-gluon correlators in nuclei. At high photon momenta, the result is directly sensitive to the nuclear gluon distribution. The NLO result contains two processes, the annihilation process and the process with $q\bar{q}$ pair and a photon in the final state. We provide a numerical evaluation of the photon spectrum from the annihilation process.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first next-to-leading order (NLO) computation of photon production in proton-nucleus (p+A) collisions at collider energies within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, extending previous leading-order (LO) results. The NLO result is the dominant contribution at central rapidities, but the numerical evaluation is provided only for the annihilation (class II) process, not for the full NLO result including the quark-antiquark-photon final state (class III) process.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in the dilute-dense approximation, where the proton color density is treated as small compared to the nucleus density. They compute the amplitudes for two NLO processes: the annihilation process (class II) where a gluon from the proton emits a quark-antiquark pair that annihilates into a photon, and the process with a quark-antiquark pair and a photon in the final state (class III). The inclusive photon rate is obtained by squaring the amplitudes, performing color averaging over the sources, and integrating over final-state phase space, with the class II result expressed in terms of an inelastic quadrupole Wilson line correlator.

## Key numbers

* Photon transverse momentum spectrum from the class II process is computed for two values of the saturation scale: Q_s = 5 Λ_QCD and Q_s = 10 Λ_QCD.
* The soft part of the spectrum (up to k_{γ⊥} ~ 2 Q_s) is parametrized as exp(-√(k_{γ⊥}² + (0.5 Q_s)²) / (0.5 Q_s)).
* The semi-hard part (k_{γ⊥} ≳ 2 Q_s) is fitted by a power law tail (log(k_{γ⊥}/Q_s))^{1.5} / k_{γ⊥}^{5.6}.
* Number of photons per unit rapidity as a function of quark mass m is parametrized as (log(m/Λ_QCD))^{1.8} / m^{2.6} for m ≳ 2 Λ_QCD.

## Context

This work extends the LO CGC calculation of photon production from valence quark bremsstrahlung by Gelis and Jalilian-Marian (2002), which was sensitive only to the two-point gluon correlator of the nucleus. The NLO result probes multi-gluon correlations in nuclei, substantiating related investigations using hadron production by Blaizot et al. (2004), Fujii et al. (2006, 2013), Kang et al. (2013), and Ma et al. (2014, 2015). The results enable a direct systematic study of the nuclear gluon distribution function and higher-twist corrections, and may help resolve an experimental puzzle concerning the excess of soft photons compared to predictions by the Low-Burnett-Kroll soft photon theorem.
