# Nuclear vector polarizability correction to hyperfine splitting

> The interaction of orbital electrons with the charge and magnetic moment of the nucleus polarizes it, and the detailed description requires a careful treatment of the nuclear vector polarizability. We present a complete and closed form expression for the resulting contribution to hyperfine splitting in light atomic systems.

# Nuclear vector polarizability correction to hyperfine splitting

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete closed-form expression for the nuclear vector polarizability correction to hyperfine splitting in light atomic systems, showing that this correction is of order O(Z α m/m_p) and that the leading logarithmic contribution vanishes, contrary to earlier work by Khriplovich et al. (2001) which could not be rewritten in such a form.

## Method

The author splits the nuclear structure correction into three parts: the Low correction (δE_Low), the Zemach correction (δE_Zemach), and the polarizability correction (δE_pol). The derivation uses the two-photon exchange forward scattering amplitude in temporal gauge, separates the integral into low-energy and high-energy parts, and expands the effective nuclear Hamiltonian in the small parameter Q/m_p (the ratio of characteristic nucleon momentum to nucleon mass, about 0.10–0.15). Contributions from electric dipole, magnetic dipole, electric quadrupole, and relativistic couplings are calculated systematically, with the cutoff dependence Λ cancelling between all terms.

## Key numbers

- The experimental hyperfine splitting in deuterium: ν_exp = 327,384.3525222(17) kHz
- The difference between experimental and QED-predicted hyperfine splitting in deuterium: (ν_exp − ν_QED)/ν_F = 138 ppm
- Proton Zemach radius: 1.086(12) fm
- Neutron Zemach radius: −0.042 fm
- For the proton, the sum of recoil and polarizability corrections to the g-factor: δg_p/g_p = 7.14 ppm (5.84 ppm recoil + 1.30 ppm polarizability)
- The characteristic momentum ratio Q/m_p in typical nuclei: 0.10–0.15
- For the ¹¹Be nucleus, the electric dipole contribution δ₁E_hfs can be as large as B(E1) m m_p E_F ≈ 10⁻⁴ E_F, with B(E1) ≈ 0.1 e² fm²

## Context

This work extends the treatment of nuclear structure corrections to hyperfine splitting beyond the Low correction (Low, 1950) and the Zemach correction (Zemach, 1956), which were recently reanalyzed for deuterium, tritium, and ³He by Friar and Payne (2005). The derivation is similar to that of Khriplovich et al. (2001) for deuterium but differs in details and in the resulting formula, in particular, the present result has no logarithmic cutoff dependence. The paper enables improved theoretical predictions for hyperfine splitting in light atoms and ions, and identifies that the polarizability correction sets the ultimate accuracy limit (expected to be a few ppm) for such systems. The author notes that Friar and Payne (2005) claimed δE_pol is negligibly small, in disagreement with the present findings.
