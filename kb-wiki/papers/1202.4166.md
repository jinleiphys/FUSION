# Triton photodisintegration in three-dimensional approach

> Two- and three- particles photodisintegration of the triton is investigated in a three-dimensional (3D) Faddeev approach. For this purpose the Jacobi momentum vectors for three particles system and spin-isospin quantum numbers of the individual nucleons are considered. Based on this picture the three-nucleon Faddeev integral equations with the two-nucleon interaction are formulated without employing the partial wave decomposition. The single nucleon current as well as $π-$ and $ρ-$ like exchange currents are used in an appropriate form to be employed in 3D approach. The exchange currents are derived from AV18 NN force. The two-body t-matrix, Deuteron and Triton wave functions are calculated in the 3D approach by using AV18 potential. Benchmarks are presented to compare the total cross section for the two- and three- particles photodisintegration in the range of $E_γ<30 MeV$. The 3D Faddeev approach shows promising results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-dimensional (3D) Faddeev approach for calculating triton photodisintegration cross sections that avoids the truncation problems and complicated recoupling algebra of partial wave decomposition, but the results show less agreement with experimental data at low photon energies (below 20 MeV) and the authors note that the overestimation indicates the need for three-nucleon force effects which were not included in this calculation.

## Method

The authors formulated three-nucleon Faddeev integral equations using Jacobi momentum vectors and individual nucleon spin-isospin quantum numbers as basis states, without employing partial wave decomposition. They calculated the two-body t-matrix, deuteron wave function, and triton wave function using the AV18 potential in this 3D approach, and included single-nucleon currents as well as pi- and rho-like meson exchange currents derived from AV18. The integral equations were solved by generating a Neumann series summed with Pade approximation, using a six-dimensional grid with Gaussian quadrature and Cubic-Hermit spline interpolation.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections calculated for photon energies E_gamma < 30 MeV for both two-body (gamma + H-3 -> n + d) and three-body (gamma + H-3 -> 3N) photodisintegration channels
- The Neumann series iteration required n = 10 iterations to reach convergence with epsilon = 10^-2
- A momentum cutoff at 150 fm^-1 was used in the numerical Fourier-Bessel transformation of the potential
- The fine-structure constant alpha approximately 1/137 appears in the cross section formulas

## Context

This work extends the 3D Faddeev approach previously developed by the same group (Rice, Fachruddin (2000), Hadizadeh (2011), Bayegan (2008-2011)) to photodisintegration processes, and provides benchmarks against partial wave calculations by Golak (2005) and experimental data from Faul (1981), Skopik (1979), and Kosiek (1966). The results show that the Siegert theorem and explicit meson exchange current predictions are close at lower energies but diverge at higher energies, and the authors identify the inclusion of three-nucleon forces as the next necessary step to improve agreement with experiment.
