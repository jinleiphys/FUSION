# Heavy flavours in high-energy nuclear collisions: quenching, flow and correlations

> We present results for the quenching, elliptic flow and azimuthal correlations of heavy flavour particles in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions obtained through the POWLANG transport setup, developed in the past to study the propagation of heavy quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma and here extended to include a modeling of their hadronization in the presence of a medium. Hadronization is described as occurring via the fragmentation of strings with endpoints given by the heavy (anti-)quark Q(Qbar) and a thermal parton $qbar(q)$ from the medium. The flow of the light quarks is shown to affect significantly the R_AA} and v_2 of the final D mesons, leading to a better agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper claims that modeling heavy-quark hadronization as string fragmentation with a thermal light parton from the medium (rather than vacuum fragmentation) significantly improves agreement with experimental data for D-meson nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow, but this improvement is conditional on the light parton inheriting the collective flow of the fluid.

## Method
The authors extended their POWLANG transport setup, which uses the relativistic Langevin equation to simulate heavy-quark propagation in the Quark-Gluon Plasma, by adding an in-medium hadronization model. When a heavy quark reaches a fluid cell below the decoupling temperature T_dec, a light antiquark is extracted from a thermal momentum distribution in the local rest frame of the fluid, boosted to the laboratory frame using the fluid four-velocity from hydrodynamics, and then a string is formed between the heavy quark and light antiquark and fragmented via PYTHIA 6.4.

## Key numbers
- D^0 meson R_AA in central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV shows a bump around p_T ≈ 1.5 GeV/c due to radial flow from light thermal partons.
- Two decoupling temperatures were explored: T_dec = 0.155 GeV and T_dec = 0.17 GeV.
- Light quarks were treated as dressed particles with effective masses m_u/d = 0.33 GeV and m_s = 0.5 GeV.
- D-meson R_AA and v_2 results are presented for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV at the LHC, compared to ALICE data.
- In-plane and out-of-plane R_AA of D mesons are shown for the 30-50% centrality class in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous POWLANG transport setup (Beraudo et al., 2012-2014) by adding in-medium hadronization, which was previously treated only with vacuum fragmentation functions. The results are compared to experimental data from the STAR Collaboration (2014) at RHIC and the ALICE Collaboration (2012-2014) at the LHC. The new hadronization mechanism enables predictions for more differential observables like D-h and e-h azimuthal correlations, though the authors note these are exploratory and leave extraction of medium information for future work.
