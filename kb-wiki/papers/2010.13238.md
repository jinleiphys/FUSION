# Vector mesons spectrum in a medium with a chiral imbalance induced by the vacuum of fermions

> The properties of the light vector mesons in the presence of local parity breaking medium with a chiral imbalance are considered in the vector-meson dominance model. Applying the finite lowest-order radiatively induced local effective Lagrangian, initially developed for the QED, for the vector $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons, we obtained the mass spectrum as a function of momentum and chiral chemical potential $μ_5$. We showed that in addition to the Chern-Simons term, splitting the transverse polarisations of the mesons, there is another radiatively induced contribution that becomes important at momentum and $μ_5$ around a few hundred MeV.

## Key claim

The authors show that, in addition to the known Chern-Simons term that splits the transverse polarizations of vector mesons, a radiatively induced contribution proportional to \(\xi b_0^2 |\vec{k}|^2/m^2\) becomes important when the chiral chemical potential \(\mu_5\) and momentum \(|\vec{k}|\) are both around a few hundred MeV, a condition the abstract glosses over by not specifying the threshold.

## Method

The authors start from a finite lowest-order radiatively induced local effective Lagrangian originally developed for QED and apply it to the vector-meson dominance (VDM) model for the \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) mesons in the SU(2) flavor sector. They substitute the vector-meson field combination into the Lagrangian, diagonalize the mass matrix via an orthogonal transformation to decouple the photon, \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) fields, and then derive the modified Maxwell equations and dispersion relations for the transverse polarizations in the presence of a constant axial-vector background \(b_\mu\) (related to the chiral chemical potential \(\mu_5\)). The mass spectrum is obtained as a function of momentum \(|\vec{k}|\) and \(b_0\) (or \(\mu_5\)).

## Key numbers

- The \(\rho\) meson mass at zero chiral imbalance is \(m_V = m_\rho = 0.7755\ \mathrm{GeV}\); the \(\omega\) meson mass at zero chiral imbalance is \(0.7766\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- The dimensionless parameters for the \(\rho\) meson are \(\xi_\rho = 0.9119\) and \(\zeta_\rho = 2.7357\); for the \(\omega\) meson, \(\xi_\omega = 0.9145\) and \(\zeta_\omega = 2.7435\).
- The effective squared mass for the \(\rho\) meson is \(\bar{m}_\rho^2 = (0.7755\ \mathrm{GeV})^2 + 0.9119\ b^2\); for the \(\omega\) meson, \(\bar{m}_\omega^2 = (0.7766\ \mathrm{GeV})^2 + 0.9145\ b^2\).
- The dispersion relation for the transverse modes gives \(m^{*2} = \bar{m}^2 \pm \zeta b_0 |\vec{k}| + \xi b_0^2 |\vec{k}|^2/m^2\), with the numerical coefficients for the \(\rho\) meson: \(m_\rho^{*2} = (0.7755\ \mathrm{GeV})^2 + 0.9119\ b^2 \pm 2.7357\ b |\vec{k}| + 10.13\ (\mathrm{GeV}^{-2})\ b^2 |\vec{k}|^2\); for the \(\omega\) meson: \(m_\omega^{*2} = (0.7766\ \mathrm{GeV})^2 + 0.9145\ b^2 \pm 2.7435\ b |\vec{k}| + 10.16\ (\mathrm{GeV}^{-2})\ b^2 |\vec{k}|^2\).
- The relation between the axial-vector background and the chiral chemical potential is \(b_0 \simeq 0.5\ \mu_5\).
- The consistency condition for real vector meson energy requires \(b_0 < m_V\).

## Context

This work extends the treatment of local parity breaking (LPB) in a quark-hadron medium, previously studied by Andrianov (2012) using a Chern-Simons term, by including a radiatively induced contribution from the fermion vacuum that becomes significant at momenta and chiral chemical potentials around a few hundred MeV. The approach adapts the finite lowest-order radiatively induced local effective Lagrangian for Lorentz invariance violation (LIV) developed by Alfaro (2006, 2009) for QED to the vector-meson dominance model. The results enable more precise predictions for the mass splitting of \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) mesons in heavy-ion collision fireballs where a chiral imbalance may occur.
