# Lüscher's finite volume test for two-baryon systems with attractive interactions

> For the attractive interaction, the Lüscher's finite volume formula gives the phase shift at negative squared moment $k^2<0$ for the ground state in the finite volume, which corresponds to the analytic continuation of the phase shift at $k^2<0$ in the infinite volume. Using this fact, we reexamine behaviors of phase shifts at $k^2 <0$ obtained directly from plateaux of effective energy shifts in previous lattice studies for two nucleon systems on various volumes. We have found that data, based on which existences of the bound states are claimed, show singular behaviors of the phase shift at $k^2<0$, which seem incompatible with smooth behaviors predicted by the effective range expansion. This, together with the fake plateau problem for the determination of the energy shift, brings a serious doubt on existences of the $NN$ bound states claimed in previous lattice studies at pion masses heavier than 300 MeV.

# Lüscher's finite volume test for two-baryon systems with attractive interactions

## Key claim
The authors argue that previous lattice QCD claims of nucleon-nucleon (NN) bound states at pion masses heavier than 300 MeV are invalid because the extracted energy shifts show singular behaviors in the Lüscher finite-volume test that are incompatible with smooth effective range expansion (ERE) predictions, and because these data suffer from a "fake plateau" problem due to excited-state contamination.

## Method
The authors apply Lüscher's finite-volume formula to reexamine previously published lattice data for two-nucleon systems in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels. For each dataset, they convert the reported energy shifts ΔE into k cot δ₀(k) at negative squared momentum k²<0 (corresponding to attractive interactions) and plot these as functions of (k/m_π)² across multiple lattice volumes, comparing the behavior to expectations from the effective range expansion and the bound-state condition.

## Key numbers
- For YKU2011 data at m_π = 0.8 GeV, YIKU2012 at m_π = 0.51 GeV, and YIKU2015 at m_π = 0.30 GeV, k cot δ₀(k)/m_π shows singular behavior in all six cases (both ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels), with (k/m_π)² values much smaller than 0.25 (the expected ERE convergence radius).
- For NPL2012 data at m_π = 0.39 GeV, NPL2013 at m_π = 0.81 GeV, and NPL2015 at m_π = 0.45 GeV, k cot δ₀(k)/m_π at k²<0 decreases as volume increases, and for NPL2015 data the negative k² points suggest a negative effective range r₀ inconsistent with the ERE fit to positive k² data.
- For NPL2013 data at m_π = 0.81 GeV, data from boosted systems with total momentum P = n × 2π/L (n=0,1,2) disagree between n=0 and n=2 on each volume, despite the finite-volume formula predicting nearly overlapping results.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous critique of the "direct method" for extracting hadron interactions in lattice QCD (Iritani 2015, 2016), which identified the fake plateau problem where excited-state contamination at the 10% level can produce misleading plateau structures in effective energy shifts. The paper directly challenges claims of NN bound states from the YKU/NPL collaborations (Yamazaki 2011, 2012, 2015; Beane 2011, 2012; Orginos 2015) and contrasts with the HAL QCD potential method, which predicts no NN bound states at heavier pion masses. The proposed finite-volume test provides a simple diagnostic tool to detect unreliable energy shifts before more sophisticated analyses are performed.
