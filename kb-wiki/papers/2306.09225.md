# Theory of heavy-quarks contribution to the quark-gluon plasma viscosity

> The shear viscosity of quark gluon plasma is customarily estimated in the literature using kinetic theory, which, however, is well known to break down for dense interacting systems. Here we propose an alternative theoretical approach based on recent advances in the physics of dense interacting liquid-like systems, which is valid for strongly-interacting and arbitrarily dense relativistic systems. With this approach, the viscosity of strongly interacting dense heavy-quarks plasma is evaluated analytically, at the level of special relativity. For QGP well above the confinement temperature, the theory predicts that the viscosity increases with the cube of temperature, in agreement with evidence.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the shear viscosity of a strongly interacting dense heavy-quark plasma increases with the cube of temperature for QGP well above the confinement temperature (T > 2Tc), but this result depends on several approximations: treating heavy quarks non-relativistically, assuming a Debye-type density of states, and taking the zero-shear rate limit where the Lorentz factor and speed of sound are approximately temperature-independent.

## Method

The author derives an analytical expression for the shear viscosity of heavy quarks in QGP by combining nonaffine response theory (originally developed for dense disordered systems) with a relativistic Langevin transport model for heavy quarks. The complex shear modulus is obtained from a generalized Langevin equation via Fourier transformation and normal mode decomposition of the Hessian matrix, then the zero-frequency limit of the loss modulus yields the viscosity. The final temperature dependence is extracted by substituting a Debye-type density of states, a quadratic frequency dependence for the affine-force correlator, and scaling relations for the spring constant, Debye frequency, and Lorentz factor with temperature.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless parameter r_c^3 n for heavy quarks in QGP is in the range 10^3 to 10^4, where r_c is the interaction range (1 fm) and n is the number density, confirming kinetic theory is inapplicable.
- The heavy quark mass is approximately 5 GeV for bottom and 1.5 GeV for charm, while the QGP temperature is estimated at ≤ 0.3 GeV.
- The viscosity scales as η ∼ T^3 for QGP well above the confinement temperature (T > 2Tc).
- The degeneracy factor of 12 is included for 3 colors, 2 spin states, and particle-antiparticle in the final viscosity expression.

## Context

This work extends the nonaffine response theory of Zaccone (2023) and Scossa-Romano to relativistic systems, providing an alternative to kinetic theory (Chapman-Enskog) which breaks down for dense interacting systems with r_c^3 n ∼ 10^3-10^4. The approach contrasts with AdS/CFT methods (Noronha, 2022) that cannot provide microscopic mechanistic understanding, and with Green-Kubo lattice QCD calculations (Altenkort, 2022) that require fitting long-time tails. The result η ∼ T^3 agrees with earlier calculations by Redlich, Shuryak, and Kapusta and Gale (2023), and the framework enables future extensions incorporating microscopic details along the lines of Toneev.
