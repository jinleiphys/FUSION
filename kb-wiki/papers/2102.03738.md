# Electronic width of the $ψ(3770)$ resonance interfering with the background

> Methods for extracting the $ψ(3770)\to e^+e^-$ decay width from the data on the reaction cross section $e^+e^-\to D\bar D$ are discussed. Attention is drawn to the absence of the generally accepted method for determining $Γ_{ψ(3770)e^+e^-}$ in the presence of interference between the contributions of the $ψ(3770)$ resonance and background. It is shown that the model for the experimentally measured $D$ meson form factor, which satisfies the requirement of the Watson theorem and takes into account the contribution of the complex of the mixed $ψ(3770)$ and $ψ(2S)$ resonances, allows uniquely determine the value of $Γ_{ψ(3770)e^+e^-}$ by fitting. The $Γ_{ψ(3770)e^+ e^-}$ values found from the data processing are compared with the estimates in the potential models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the commonly used parametrizations for extracting the electronic width \(\Gamma_{\psi(3770)e^+e^-}\) from the \(e^+e^-\to D\bar D\) cross section are ambiguous due to interference between the \(\psi(3770)\) resonance and background, and that a model satisfying the Watson theorem (unitarity condition) and accounting for the mixing of \(\psi(3770)\) and \(\psi(2S)\) resonances via \(D\bar D\) intermediate states allows a unique determination of this width.

## Method

The authors construct a model for the isoscalar electromagnetic form factor \(F^0_D(s)\) of the \(D\) meson that includes the contributions of the \(\psi(3770)\) and \(\psi(2S)\) resonances mixed through their common \(D\bar D\) decay channels, using a vector dominance approach with finite width corrections in the resonance propagators to ensure proper analytic properties. They fit this model to the Born cross section data for \(e^+e^-\to D\bar D\) from the BES, CLEO, BABAR, and Belle Collaborations in the energy region up to about 3.9 GeV, treating the \(\psi(3770)\) mass, its coupling to \(D\bar D\), its coupling to the virtual photon, and the relative coupling strength \(z\) of \(\psi(2S)\) to \(D\bar D\) as free parameters, while fixing the \(\psi(2S)\) mass and electronic width from PDG values.

## Key numbers

- \(\Gamma_{\psi(3770)e^+e^-} = 0.189\) keV from the fit of the mixed-resonance model (with \(\chi^2 = 127.6\) for 84 degrees of freedom), compared to \(\Gamma_{\psi(3770)e^+e^-} \approx 0.25\) keV from the solitary \(\psi(3770)\) resonance fit (with \(\chi^2 = 459\) for 84 degrees of freedom).
- The fit yields \(\Gamma_{\psi(3770)D\bar D} = 51.88\) MeV and \(m_{\psi(3770)} = 3.7884\) GeV, with the zero in the form factor \(F^0_D(s)\) at \(\sqrt{s} \approx 3.818\) GeV.
- The ratio \(\Gamma_{\psi(3770)e^+e^-} / \Gamma_{\psi(2S)e^+e^-} \approx 0.081\), using \(\Gamma_{\psi(2S)e^+e^-} = 2.33\) keV from PDG.
- The PDG average value for \(\Gamma_{\psi(3770)e^+e^-}\) is \((0.262 \pm 0.018)\) keV, while the KEDR Collaboration found two ambiguous solutions: \(\Gamma_{\psi(3770)e^+e^-} = 160^{+78}_{-58}\) eV (solution I) and \(420^{+72}_{-80}\) eV (solution II).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Achasov and Shestakov (2012, 2013) by incorporating finite width corrections in the resonance propagators to eliminate spurious singularities present in earlier parametrizations. It directly addresses the ambiguity in determining \(\Gamma_{\psi(3770)e^+e^-}\) highlighted by the KEDR Collaboration (Anashin et al., 2018), where two equally good fits gave electronic widths differing by a factor of about 2.6. The model enables a unique extraction of the electronic width and provides a framework for analyzing future high-precision data on the \(e^+e^-\to D\bar D\) cross section, which could help resolve the long-standing discrepancy between experimental values and theoretical estimates from potential models (e.g., Eichten et al., 1980; Rosner, 2001; Soni et al., 2020).
