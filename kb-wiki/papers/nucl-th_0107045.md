# Quark effects and isospin-violation in ${}1S_0$ NN scattering

> We present the results of the effects of various mechanisms on the quark level, including QED-QCD interference effect, on isospin violation in ${}1S_0$ NN scatterings with the new sets of strength parameters obtained by fitting to mass splittings in the baryon isomultiplets listed in the most recent PDG compilation. We also give the matrix elements of the various potential operators which would be useful to study of these charge dependent effects in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quark-level mechanisms, including a newly refitted QED-QCD interference effect, can explain the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in $^1S_0$ NN scattering without invoking $\rho-\omega$ mixing, but the charge independence breaking (CIB) from these same quark mechanisms carries the opposite sign relative to experiment, leaving the CIB problem unresolved in quark models.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with a six-quark Hamiltonian including kinetic energy, confinement, one-gluon-exchange, one-photon-exchange, quark-pion coupling, and QED-QCD mixing (penguin-like and box-like diagrams). The strength parameters of the QED-QCD mixing potential are refitted to baryon isomultiplet mass splittings from the most recent PDG compilation, using the new value of $\Sigma_c^{++} - \Sigma_c^0 = 0.66 \pm 0.28$ MeV. The resonanting group method (RGM) is employed to solve the scattering equation and extract scattering length differences for $^1S_0$ NN scattering.

## Key numbers

- The new refit using $\Sigma_c^{++} - \Sigma_c^0 = 0.66 \pm 0.28$ MeV gives $\delta a_{CSB}$ (quark contributions) ranging from $1.35$ fm to $1.56$ fm, close to the experimental value of $1.5$ fm.
- Compared to previous results using $\Sigma_c^{++} - \Sigma_c^0 = 0.2 \pm 0.5$ MeV, $\delta a_{CSB}$ decreases by about $0.27$ fm and $\delta a_{CIB}$ decreases by about $0.15$ fm.
- The quark-exchange Coulomb effect contributes $0.38$ fm to $\delta a_{CIB}$, and the charge-dependent quark-exchange OPE effect contributes only $0.08$ fm.
- The quark contribution to $a(pp-np)$ ranges from $-1.68$ to $-0.57$ fm, opposite in sign to the experimental value of about $6.78$ fm.
- The $\Delta N$ splitting $m_{\Delta} - m_N \simeq 260$ MeV gives $\alpha_s = 1.624$ with $m_0 = 330$ MeV.
- The oscillator length $\alpha = 320$ MeV $= 0.617$ fm$^{-1}$, and the confining constant $e_0 = -385$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark-level isospin violation by Maltman, Stephenson, and Goldman (1990) on QED-QCD mixing effects in baryon mass splittings and the $^3$He-$^3$H binding energy difference, and by Chemtob (1983), Brauer et al. (1985), and Wang et al. (1988) on quark mass difference and Coulomb effects in NN scattering. The paper provides matrix elements of the various potential operators for use in finite nuclei, and shows that while the quark-level CSB agrees well with experiment, the CIB results worsen the agreement with meson-exchange calculations by Yang (1983), Coon (1982), and Friar (1996), making the charge independence breaking in NN scattering an open issue in quark models.
