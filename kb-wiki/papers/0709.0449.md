# Comment on "Ab Initio study of 40-Ca with an importance-truncated no-core shell model"

> In a recent Letter [Phys. Rev. Lett. 99, 092501 (2007)], Roth and Navratil present an importance-truncation scheme for the no-core shell model. The authors claim that their truncation scheme leads to converged results for the ground state of 40-Ca. We believe that this conclusion cannot be drawn from the results presented in the Letter. Furthermore, the claimed convergence is at variance with expectations of many-body theory. In particular, coupled-cluster calculations indicate that a significant fraction of the correlation energy is missing.

# Comment on "Ab Initio Study of 40-Ca with an Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model"

## Key claim

The authors argue that Roth and Navratil (2007) cannot legitimately claim converged results for the ground state of 40-Ca using their importance-truncation scheme, because the convergence with respect to the number of particle-hole excitations (step iii) is not demonstrated, and the claimed convergence contradicts expectations from many-body theory.

## Method

The comment examines the convergence criteria presented in Roth and Navratil (2007) for the no-core shell model with importance truncation, specifically testing convergence against three axes: the number of states retained in importance sampling, the size of the model space (Nmax), and the number of particle-hole excitations. The authors compare the reported results to their own coupled-cluster CCSD(T) calculations for the same nucleus and interaction (Vlowk), and cite quantum chemistry benchmarks for small molecules and nuclear structure calculations for 56-Ni to illustrate the known inadequacy of 3p3h truncations.

## Key numbers

- Coupled-cluster CCSD(T) results from Hagen et al. (2007) indicate the ground state energy for 40-Ca with the Vlowk potential is about 40 MeV lower than the value reported in Roth and Navratil (2007).
- The Roth and Navratil (2007) results for 40-Ca with the VUCOM potential show considerable slopes at Nmax=16, indicating convergence with respect to model space size is not yet established.
- For the Vlowk potential, convergence with respect to model space size is shown only for fixed 3p3h excitations, and only for the small model space with Nmax=4.

## Context

This comment extends the critique of importance-truncated no-core shell model calculations by pointing out that the truncation produces unlinked diagrams and lacks size extensivity, as discussed in Bartlett (2007). The authors contrast their coupled-cluster approach (Hagen et al., 2007), which is size extensive and includes a significant fraction of 4p4h excitations, with the 3p3h truncation used by Roth and Navratil (2007). They cite quantum chemical calculations for small molecules (Bartlett, 2007) and nuclear structure calculations for 56-Ni (Horoi et al.) to demonstrate that 3p3h truncations are systematically less accurate than coupled-cluster theory. The comment enables future work by establishing that proper convergence demonstrations for 40-Ca must include explicit verification of convergence with respect to the number of particle-hole excitations beyond 3p3h.
