# Coupled-cluster computations of optical potential for medium-mass nuclei

> Recent progress in the numerical solution of the nuclear many-body problem and in the development of nuclear Hamiltonians rooted in Quantum Chromodynamics, has opened the door to first-principle computations of nuclear reactions. In this article, we discuss the current status of ab initio calculations of nucleon-nucleus optical potentials for medium-mass systems, with a focus on results obtained with the coupled-cluster method.

# Coupled-cluster computations of optical potential for medium-mass nuclei

## Key claim

The coupled-cluster method combined with Green's function theory can produce ab initio nucleon-nucleus optical potentials for medium-mass nuclei without free parameters, but at the CCSD truncation level the computed absorption is practically negligible at scattering energies below approximately 20 MeV, indicating that the wavefunctions are not sufficiently correlated to reproduce the imaginary part of the potential.

## Method

The optical potential is obtained by solving the Dyson equation after computing the Green's function directly with the coupled-cluster method, using the Lanczos continued fraction technique for numerical stability. To handle the continuum, the Green's function is analytically continued into the complex-energy plane by working in a Berggren basis that includes bound, resonant, and complex-continuum states generated by the Hartree-Fock potential. The ground state of the target nucleus is computed at the CCSD level, while the Lanczos vectors in the particle-attached and particle-removed equation-of-motion are truncated at the 2p-1h and 1p-2h excitation levels, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The CCSD binding energy of $^{40}$Ca is 299.28 MeV for ($N_{max},N_3$) = (14, 16), compared to the experimental value of 342.05 MeV
- Neutron separation energies for $^{40,48}$Ca are consistently within 600 keV of experimental values
- The difference between $^{41}$Ca energies at ($N_{max},N_3$)=(14,14) and (14,16) is approximately 220 keV for the ground state and approximately 350 keV for the $J^{\pi} = 1/2^-$ second excited state
- The computed $3^-$ excited state in $^{40}$Ca has energy $E_{EOM-CCSD} (3^{-})$ = 3.94 MeV (experimental value is 3.74 MeV)
- The computed $2^+$ excited state in $^{48}$Ca has energy $E_{EOM-CCSD}(2^+)$ = 4.65 MeV (experimental value is 3.83 MeV)
- The first excited $0^+$ state in $^{40}$Ca is computed at approximately 16 MeV above the ground state (experimental value is 3.35 MeV)
- Charge radii used for non-locality analysis: 3.48 fm for $^{40}$Ca and 3.46 fm for $^{48}$Ca
- Inclusion of partial waves with angular momentum $L\leq 5$ for $^{40}$Ca and $L\leq 6$ for $^{48}$Ca is sufficient for elastic scattering at the energies considered

## Context

This work extends the ab initio computation of nucleon-nucleus optical potentials to medium-mass nuclei ($^{40,48}$Ca) using the coupled-cluster method, building on prior Green's function approaches such as the Self Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) method (Waldecker, Jenkins, Idini) and earlier coupled-cluster work (Hagen 2012). The results are compared to the phenomenological Koning-Delaroche potential (2003) and experimental data. The approach enables first-principle predictions for regions of the nuclear chart unexplored experimentally, particularly for rare-isotope-beam facilities, and can guide new parametrizations of phenomenological potentials by providing insights on form factors, energy-dependence, and dependence on isospin-asymmetry of the target. The main open challenge is the lack of absorption at low scattering energies, which requires going beyond the CCSD truncation level to include more correlations in the wavefunctions.
