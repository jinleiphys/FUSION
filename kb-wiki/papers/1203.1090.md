# Hadronic molecules with both open charm and bottom

> With the one-boson-exchange model, we study the interaction between the S-wave $D^{(*)}/D^{(*)}_s$ meson and S-wave $B^{(*)}/B^{(*)}_s$ meson considering the S-D mixing effect. Our calculation indicates that there may exist the $B_c$-like molecular states. We estimate their masses and list the possible decay modes of these $B_c$-like molecular states, which may be useful to the future experimental search.

## Key claim

Using the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model with S-D mixing effects, the authors predict that there may exist loosely bound hadronic molecular states composed of a charmed meson and a bottom meson, but only for 17 out of 24 possible configurations, and only when the phenomenological cutoff parameter Lambda falls within specific ranges (1 to 5 GeV) and the binding energy is between 0 and -20 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed effective potentials for the interaction between S-wave D(*)/Ds(*) mesons and S-wave B(*)/Bs(*) mesons using the OBE model with pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson exchange, incorporating heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry Lagrangians. They solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation numerically using the FESSDE Fortran program and cross-checked with a MATLAB package MATSCE, requiring binding energies in the range of 0 to -20 MeV and cutoffs between 1 and 5 GeV to identify loosely bound molecular states.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy for X_{s1} (DB system, I(J^P)=0(0+)): -1.28 MeV at Lambda=1.3 GeV, with r_RMS=2.58 fm
- Binding energy for Z_{s1} (D*B system, I(J^P)=0(1+)): -2.10 MeV at Lambda=1.3 GeV, with r_RMS=2.07 fm
- Binding energy for Y_{s1}^{J=0} (D*B* system, I(J^P)=0(0+)): -2.70 MeV at Lambda=1.25 GeV, with r_RMS=2.00 fm
- Binding energy for Y_{s1}^{J=1} (D*B* system, I(J^P)=0(1+)): -3.59 MeV at Lambda=1.25 GeV, with r_RMS=1.76 fm
- Binding energy for Y_{s1}^{J=2} (D*B* system, I(J^P)=0(2+)): -1.28 MeV at Lambda=0.96 GeV, with r_RMS=2.59 fm
- The coupling constants used: g=0.59 (from D*+ width), beta=0.9 (vector meson dominance), lambda=0.56 GeV^{-1} (light cone sum rule), g_s=3.73/(2*sqrt(6)) (from Falk 1992)
- The B_c(1^1S_0) mass is 6277 MeV (from Nakamura 2010)

## Context

This work extends the OBE model approach previously applied to charmonium-like and bottomonium-like molecules by Tornqvist (1993), Swanson (2003), Liu (2008), and Sun (2011) to the unexplored sector of systems containing both open charm and open bottom quarks. The predicted B_c-like molecular states with masses above 7 GeV could be searched for at LHCb and future Z0 factories, with decay modes into B_c mesons plus light mesons providing experimental signatures analogous to the discovery of X(3872) in J/psi pi+ pi- and Y(3940) in J/psi omega.
