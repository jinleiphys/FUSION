# Dark/Visible Parallel Universes and Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We develop a model for visible matter-dark matter interaction based on the exchange of a massive gray boson called herein the Mulato. Our model hinges on the assumption that all known particles in the visible matter have their counterparts in the dark matter. We postulate six families of particles five of which are dark. This leads to the unavoidable postulation of six parallel worlds, the visible one and five invisible worlds. A close study of big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN), baryon asymmetries, cosmic microwave background (CMB) bounds, galaxy dynamics, together with the Standard Model assumptions, help us to set a limit on the mass and width of the new gauge boson. Modification of the statistics underlying the kinetic energy distribution of particles during the BBN is also discussed. The changes in reaction rates during the BBN due to a departure from the Debye-Hueckel electron screening model is also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper develops a mirror dark matter model based on an SU(3) gauge symmetry that introduces a massive "Mulato" boson coupling ordinary and dark sectors, and finds that five dark sectors are required to explain the observed dark matter to baryonic matter density ratio, but only if the dark sectors are colder than the visible universe (T'/T < 0.52 for five dark sectors) to remain consistent with Big Bang Nucleosynthesis bounds.

## Method

The authors construct a gauge model with an SU(3) symmetry where Standard Model fermions are grouped by electric charge into four multiplets, and each of N_Q sectors (one visible plus N_DM dark sectors) has its own copy of the Standard Model. They introduce a massive gauge boson (the Mulato) that couples sectors, with mass generated by a scalar field condensate. They then apply constraints from BBN on the effective number of relativistic degrees of freedom, from CMB acoustic oscillations, and from galaxy dynamics to bound the dark-to-visible temperature ratio and the number of dark sectors. They also examine modifications to BBN reaction rates from electron screening using the Debye-Hueckel model and from non-extensive (Tsallis) statistics.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter to baryonic matter density ratio: Omega_DM / Omega_b = 4.94 ± 0.66, derived from WMAP values Omega_DM = 0.222 ± 0.026 and Omega_b = 0.0449 ± 0.0028
- BBN bound on extra degrees of freedom: Delta g* = 1.75 Delta N_nu < 3.85, corresponding to 3.46 < N_nu < 5.2
- Temperature ratio bound for five dark sectors: T'/T < 0.52, derived from T'/T < 0.78 / N_DM^(1/4) with N_DM = 5
- Baryon asymmetry ratio for five dark sectors: eta' ~ 7 eta, where eta = 6.19 × 10^(-10) from WMAP
- Lower bound on number of dark sectors from CMB: N_DM > 0.35, from T'/T >= 0.6 requirement
- Debye radius approximation during BBN: R_D = R_D^(0) T_9^(-1), with R_D^(0) ~ 6.1 × 10^(-5) cm and R_D^(0) ~ 3.7 × 10^(-5) cm for two baryon density regimes
- Baryon density parameter h changes from h ~ 2.1 × 10^(-5) to h ~ 5.7 × 10^(-5) around T_9 ~ 2

## Context

This work extends the mirror dark matter framework of Foot (various years), Berezhiani (1996, 2001), and others by introducing a specific SU(3) gauge symmetry to mediate interactions between visible and dark sectors, rather than assuming generic weak couplings. The model is constrained by BBN bounds on relativistic degrees of freedom (Hoyle, various years) and by CMB acoustic oscillation constraints from Berezhiani (2001). The analysis of electron screening during BBN builds on Salpeter's Debye-Hueckel model (1954) and subsequent corrections by Mitler (1977), while the non-extensive statistics discussion follows Tsallis (1988) and the authors' own prior work (BFH12). The paper enables future searches for the Mulato boson at colliders and further refinement of dark sector temperature constraints from precision cosmology.
