# A Goldstone Boson Primer

> These lectures are an extremely condensed version of the theory of Goldstone bosons, with general features illustrated using a simple model. A more comprehensive version of these lectures, which includes a general discussion of effective theories of Goldstone bosons, including applications to the low-energy behaviour of pions, spin waves (in antiferromagnets and ferromagnets), and to the SO(5) proposal for high-T_c superconductors may be found in hep-th/9808176.

# A Goldstone Boson Primer

## Key claim

Goldstone bosons are massless particles that arise whenever a continuous global symmetry does not preserve the ground state, and they completely decouple at zero energy, becoming weakly interacting at low energies. This decoupling is a general consequence of the inhomogeneous transformation law of the Goldstone field under the broken symmetry, and it holds regardless of the details of the underlying model at leading order in the low-energy derivative expansion.

## Method

The paper uses a simple complex scalar field model with a U(1) symmetry and a Mexican-hat potential, analyzed semiclassically in the limit of small coupling constant lambda. The spectrum is obtained by expanding about the vacuum expectation value v = mu/sqrt(lambda), revealing a massive radial mode (Scr) with mass m_scr = sqrt(lambda)*v and a massless angular mode (Sci). The low-energy effective theory is constructed by integrating out the heavy Scr field, and the Goldstone boson properties are derived from the symmetry transformation law expressed in polar field coordinates (chi, theta).

## Key numbers

- The vacuum expectation value is v = mu / sqrt(lambda), where mu and lambda are parameters of the scalar potential.
- The mass of the heavy radial mode is m_scr^2 = lambda * v^2.
- The tree-level scattering amplitude for Scr-Sci scattering vanishes in the limit of zero momentum for the massless Sci particle, with the cancellation involving terms -1/2 + 3/2 - 1 = 0 in units of lambda.
- The Goldstone boson kinetic term yields a conserved current j^mu = sqrt(2) * v * partial^mu phi + ... , where v is the symmetry-breaking scale.

## Context

This paper is a condensed lecture version of a more comprehensive treatment (Burgess, 1998, hep-th/9808176) that applies Goldstone boson effective field theories to pions, spin waves in antiferromagnets and ferromagnets, and the SO(5) proposal for high-Tc superconductors. The work extends the general framework of spontaneously broken symmetries and Goldstone's theorem (Guralnik, 1968; Leutwyler; Hofmann) by emphasizing the utility of nonlinear field redefinitions and derivative expansions in constructing low-energy effective Lagrangians. It enables practitioners to analyze any system with spontaneous symmetry breaking using a universal low-energy description parameterized by the symmetry-breaking scale, without needing the full ultraviolet theory.
