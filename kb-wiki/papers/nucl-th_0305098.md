# Do we understand the $ηN$ interaction from the near threshold $η$ photoproduction on the deuteron?

> The effects of final state interaction in incoherent $η$ photoproduction on deuteron are studied within a three-body approach including a realistic $NN$ potential. The results are compared with available data, and differences with other theoretical predictions are analyzed. The role of the $ηN$ interaction and the possibility of extracting the $ηN$ scattering parameters from this reaction are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to claims in prior work by Sibirtsev et al. (2001, 2002), the near-threshold incoherent eta photoproduction on the deuteron is **not** well understood within existing theoretical approaches, and that extracting the eta-nucleon scattering parameters from this reaction is practically impossible due to weak sensitivity and strong model dependence.

## Method

The authors solved three-body integral equations for the final eta-NN system, using a separable representation of the Bonn potential (BEST3/BEST4) for the NN interaction and a realistic deuteron wave function. The eta-N interaction was modeled via an isobar ansatz coupling to the S11(1535) resonance, with parameters fitted to reproduce an eta-N scattering length of a_etaN = (0.5 + i0.32) fm. The lowest S-wave three-body configuration was treated exactly, while higher partial waves were handled perturbatively (first-order approximation, FOA). The coherent channel gamma d -> eta d was also calculated and added to obtain the inclusive cross section for comparison with data from Hejny et al. (2002).

## Key numbers

*   The eta-N scattering length used as the central value is a_etaN = (0.5 + i0.32) fm.
*   At photon energy E_gamma = 635 MeV, the full three-body treatment enhances the first-order result by about a factor of two.
*   The NN final-state interaction enhancement is reduced asymptotically to about 1.5% at E_gamma = 780 MeV.
*   The eta-N final-state interaction gives an enhancement of about 20% at E_gamma = 635 MeV, which is visibly smaller than the 45% reported by Sibirtsev et al. (2002) for the same energy.
*   The impulse approximation (IA) cross section of Sibirtsev et al. (2002) at E_gamma = 635 MeV is larger than the authors' IA by about a factor of 3.6.
*   The coherent cross section uses the ratio alpha = |g_(s)| / |g_(gamma p -> S11)| = 0.25, close to the value 0.22 of Bennhold and Tanabe (1991).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body calculations (Fix and Arenhoevel, 1997, 2002) by replacing the simple Yamaguchi potential with a realistic Bonn potential for the NN interaction. It directly contradicts the conclusions of Sibirtsev et al. (2001, 2002), who claimed that a first-order approximation (FOA) could describe the data and that the reaction is sensitive to the eta-N scattering length. The paper argues that the three-body dynamics are essential and that the method of extracting eta-N parameters from this reaction, as proposed by Sibirtsev et al., is unreliable. The results point to the need for further theoretical work, particularly on the short-range part of the eta-NN wave function and the role of intermediate pion-nucleon-nucleon states.
