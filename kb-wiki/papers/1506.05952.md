# The Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

> The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model is presented and its application to the description of the electromagnetic properties of baryons is reviewed. The results concerning the elastic nucleon form factors and the electromagnetic excitation of baryon resonances are compared with the recent experimental data

# The Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM), with only three free parameters fitted to the baryon spectrum, provides realistic three-quark wave functions that reproduce the overall trend of helicity amplitudes and elastic nucleon form factors, but discrepancies at low momentum transfer suggest that explicit quark-antiquark configurations are missing from the model.

## Method

The authors model baryons as three constituent quarks interacting via a hypercentral potential consisting of a Coulomb-like term (-τ/x) plus a linear confining term (αx), with a hyperfine interaction treated as a perturbation. The three-quark dynamics is described using hyperspherical coordinates, where the hyperradius x depends on all three quark coordinates, making the potential a three-body interaction. The quark mass is fixed to 1/3 of the nucleon mass, and the three free parameters (τ, α, and the hyperfine strength) are fitted to the baryon spectrum; the resulting wave functions are then used to predict helicity amplitudes for electromagnetic excitation of baryon resonances and elastic nucleon form factors.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass fixed to 1/3 of the nucleon mass
- Potential parameters: τ = 4.59 and α = 1.61 fm⁻²
- The calculated proton radius is about 0.5 fm
- Relativistic boosting of nucleon states produces a deviation of about 15% in the ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p at Q² = 2 GeV²
- The ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p from the relativistic hCQM with constituent quark form factors is compared with experimental data up to Q² = 5 GeV² and extended to 12 GeV²
- The ratio F_p = Q² F₂^p/F₁^p is used to study the high Q² behavior, with the zero of R_p occurring when F_p = 4 M_p² (M_p being the proton mass)

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model originally proposed by the authors (Giannini and Santopinto, earlier work) and compares its predictions with recent experimental data from Jefferson Lab (JLab) on elastic nucleon form factors and baryon resonance excitations. The model builds on lattice QCD calculations (Bali, 2001) that suggest a long-range spin-independent confinement plus short-range spin-dependent terms, and it contrasts with models using only two-body forces or harmonic oscillator bases, which cannot reproduce the observed degeneracy between the Roper resonance and negative parity states. The results point toward the need for "unquenched" constituent quark models that include quark-antiquark creation effects, as pursued in recent work by the authors and collaborators (UCQ, 2014; Santopinto and others, 2015).
