# Role of the total isospin 3/2 component in three-nucleon reactions

> We discuss the role of the three-nucleon isospin T=3/2 amplitude in elastic neutron-deuteron scattering and in the deuteron breakup reaction. The contribution of this amplitude originates from charge-independence breaking of the nucleon-nucleon potential and is driven by the difference between neutron-neutron (proton-proton) and neutron-proton forces. We study the magnitude of that contribution to the elastic scattering and breakup observables, taking the locally regularized chiral N4LO nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented by the chiral N2LO three-nucleon force. For comparison we employ also the Av18 nucleon-nucleon potential combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. We find that the isospin T=3/2 component is important for the breakup reaction and the proper treatment of charge-independence breaking in this case requires the inclusion of the 1S0 state with isospin T=3/2. For neutron-deuteron elastic scattering the T=3/2 contributions are insignificant and charge-independence breaking can be accounted for by using the effective t-matrix generated with the so-called "2/3-1/3" rule.

## Key claim

The isospin T=3/2 component in three-nucleon reactions is important for the deuteron breakup reaction, where proper treatment of charge-independence breaking requires including the 1S0 state with isospin T=3/2; however, for neutron-deuteron elastic scattering the T=3/2 contributions are insignificant and charge-independence breaking can be accounted for using the effective t-matrix generated with the "2/3-1/3" rule.

## Method

The authors solved three-nucleon Faddeev integral equations in momentum-space partial wave basis for neutron-deuteron elastic scattering and breakup, including both total isospin T=1/2 and T=3/2 basis states. They employed the locally regularized chiral N4LO nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented by the chiral N2LO three-nucleon force, and for comparison the Av18 nucleon-nucleon potential combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. Calculations were performed at four incoming neutron laboratory energies (13, 65, 135, and 250 MeV) with partial wave states up to total two-nucleon angular momentum jmax=5 and three-nucleon total angular momentum Jmax=25/2.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton, proton-proton (Coulomb subtracted), and neutron-neutron 1S0 scattering lengths: -23.75 +/- 0.01 fm, -17.3 +/- 0.8 fm, and -18.5 +/- 0.3 fm, respectively
- Difference between np and nn t-matrices ranges up to approximately 10%
- Changing the 1S0 t-matrix from tnp to tnn changes total cross section values by up to approximately 2%
- Restricting to tnp or tnn t-matrices in the 1S0 channel changes elastic scattering cross sections and spin observables by up to approximately 1%
- Calculations performed at four neutron laboratory energies: 13, 65, 135, and 250 MeV
- Regulator cutoff Lambda = 1000 MeV used for additional nonlocal regularization of three-nucleon force matrix elements
- Three-nucleon force parameters: cD = 6.0, cE = -1.0943 (with Lambda = 700 MeV notation)

## Context

This work extends the investigation of charge-independence breaking in three-nucleon systems by Witala (1991) by including three-nucleon forces. The results show that for elastic neutron-deuteron scattering, the simpler "2/3-1/3" rule treatment is sufficient, while for breakup reactions the full T=3/2 component in the 1S0 state must be included. This enables significant reduction of partial waves in time-consuming three-nucleon calculations, which is particularly important for fixing parameters of higher-order chiral three-nucleon force components by fitting to three-nucleon scattering observables. The authors suggest future investigation of reactions where only T=3/2 components contribute in the final state, such as 3H + pi- -> n + n + n.
