# Structural Properties of Rotating Hybrid Compact Stars with Color-Flavour-Locked Quarks Matter core and its Tidal Deformability

> We investigate the possible sequels, for the hybrid compact stars consisting of nucleons, hyperons and three flavour color-flavor-locked quarks phase under global neutrality and chemical equilibrium conditions. The hadron equations of state are computed within the framework of energy density functionals based on the relativistic mean field theory by employing two different model parameterizations. The quarks matter phase of equation of state is computed by using Quarks Quasiparticle model derived from a non-relativistic energy density-functional approach. A plausible set of hybrid equations of state for superdense hadron-quarks matter is obtained and, which satisfies the constraints provided by bulk nuclear matter, the observational data of frequencies and maximum Gravitational mass 2$M_\odot$ with their extracted radii, Keplerian limits, secular axisymmetric instability and limits of tidal deformabilities from GW170817 binary neutron stars (BNS) merger. We obtained structural properties of nonrotating and rotating compact stars, the evolutionary sequences with constant baryonic mass star spinning down by electromagnetic and gravitational radiations and, discuss the issues relating to phase transition from hadrons to quarks matter phase. The internal structure of rotating star with observed spin down frequencies, exhibiting shrinkage of soft quarks core of compact stars are discussed for constant baryonic mass. We present the theoretically computed limits of radii for the spin down configurations of hybrid stars corresponding to the recently observed millisecond pulsars. We also present and discuss the calculated results for tidal deformability, $Λ$ and tidal parameters $Λ_1$, $Λ_2$ of non spining hybrid star which are consistent with the waveform model analysis of GW170817 BNS.

# Structural Properties of Rotating Hybrid Compact Stars with Color-Flavour-Locked Quarks Matter Core and its Tidal Deformability

## Key claim

The authors construct hybrid equations of state for compact stars containing nucleons, hyperons, and color-flavor-locked quark matter that satisfy constraints from the 2 solar mass pulsar observations and the GW170817 tidal deformability limits, but only for a specific set of model parameterizations (BSR3 and IOPB-I for hadronic matter, with a quark quasiparticle model using a CFL gap of 50 MeV and specific hyperon-meson couplings).

## Method

The hadronic phase was computed using extended field-theoretical relativistic mean field (EFTRMF) models with BSR3 and IOPB-I parameterizations, including nucleons and hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi) under chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality. The quark matter phase was computed using the Quark Quasiparticle Model (QQPM) with color-flavor-locked (CFL) pairing, employing a running strong coupling constant and effective quark masses. The mixed phase was constructed using the Glendenning construction based on Gibbs conditions for mechanical and chemical equilibrium, and rotating stellar structures were computed using the KEH method and RNS code.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of non-rotating hybrid stars ranges from 1.83 to 2.38 solar masses, with radii between 11.76 km and 12.08 km
- For the NHymQ4 EOS (nucleons, hyperons, and quarks), the pressure at 6 times nuclear saturation density is 4.2 x 10^35 dyne/cm^2
- The CFL color superconducting gap parameter Delta is taken as 50 MeV
- Quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 4 MeV/c^2, m_s = 95 MeV/c^2
- The mixed phase for nucleon-quark EOSs occurs at baryon densities ranging from approximately 0.30-0.40 fm^-3 to 0.39-0.52 fm^-3 depending on the model
- For the NHymQ4 EOS, Lambda hyperons appear at threshold density approximately 0.35 fm^-3 and quark phase transition occurs at 0.63 fm^-3
- The coupling strength parameter ranges from 3 to 5 for chemical potentials in the range of approximately 320-510 MeV
- The empirical formula for maximum Keplerian frequency uses f_0 = 1.22 kHz

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hybrid compact stars by Dhiman (2007) using EFTRMF models and by Xia (2014) using the QQPM approach, combining them with CFL pairing following Agrawal (2009). The results are compared with observational constraints from the GW170817 binary neutron star merger event analyzed by Abbott (2018, 2019), the 2 solar mass pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, and millisecond pulsar observations including PSR J0437-4715 targeted by the NICER mission. The work enables future comparisons with high-precision radius measurements from NICER and eXTP missions, and provides evolutionary sequences for spinning-down hybrid stars that can be tested against observed pulsar spin frequencies.
