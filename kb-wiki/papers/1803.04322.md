# Charmonium states in strong magnetic fields

> The medium modifications of the masses of the charmonium states (J/$ψ$, $ψ$(3686) and $ψ$(3770)) in asymmetric nuclear matter in the presence of strong magnetic fields are studied using an effective chiral model. The mass modifications arise due to medium modifications of the scalar dilaton field, which simulates the gluon condensates of QCD within the effective hadronic model. The effects due to the magnetic field as well as isospin asymmetry are observed to be appreciable at high densities for the charmonium states, which can have consequences, e,g, in the production of the open charm mesons and the charmonium states, in the asymmetric heavy ion collisions at the compressed baryonic matter (CBM) experiments at the future facility at GSI. The presence of magnetic field leads to Landau quantization of the energy levels of the proton in the asymmetric nuclear matter. The effects of the anomalous magnetic moments of the proton and neutron on the masses of the charmonium states are studied and are observed to lead to larger masses of the charmonium states, as compared to when these effects are not taken into account.

## Key claim

The masses of charmonium states (J/ψ, ψ(3686), ψ(3770)) in asymmetric nuclear matter are modified by strong magnetic fields through changes in the scalar dilaton field that simulates QCD gluon condensates, with the effects being appreciable only at high densities and depending on whether the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons are included.

## Method

The authors used a chiral SU(3) effective model in the mean field approximation to compute the in-medium masses of charmonium states in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter under external magnetic fields. The mass modifications arise from the medium change of the scalar dilaton field χ, which is related to the gluon condensate via the trace anomaly of QCD. The coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields (σ, ζ, δ, χ) were solved for given baryon density, isospin asymmetry parameter η = (ρn − ρp)/(2ρB), and magnetic field strengths, including Landau quantization for protons and the anomalous magnetic moments of both protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum value of the dilaton field is χ₀ = 409.77 MeV.
- For symmetric nuclear matter (η=0) at ρB = ρ₀ (nuclear matter saturation density) with |eB| = 4mπ², the dilaton field χ is 406.57 MeV with AMM and 406.49 MeV without AMM.
- At ρB = 5ρ₀ in symmetric matter with |eB| = 4mπ², χ = 396.38 MeV with AMM and 395.6 MeV without AMM.
- For η=0.5 (pure neutron matter) at ρB = 5ρ₀ with |eB| = 4mπ², χ = 397.64 MeV with AMM and 397.34 MeV without AMM.
- The mass shift of J/ψ at ρB = ρ₀ (η=0) with AMM is −8.16 MeV for |eB| = 4mπ², 8mπ², and 10mπ², and −8.22 MeV for |eB| = 12mπ².
- At ρB = 5ρ₀ (η=0) with AMM, the J/ψ mass shift is −32.93 MeV (|eB|=4mπ²), −31 MeV (8mπ²), −29.8 MeV (10mπ²), and −28.44 MeV (12mπ²).
- Without AMM at ρB = 5ρ₀ (η=0), the J/ψ mass shift is −34.745 MeV (4mπ²), −37.14 MeV (8mπ²), −38.05 MeV (10mπ²), and −38.73 MeV (12mπ²).
- The mass shift of ψ(3686) at ρB = ρ₀ in zero magnetic field is −117 MeV, and for ψ(3770) it is −155 MeV, within the chiral effective model.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter β is 0.51 GeV for J/ψ (1S), 0.38 GeV for ψ(3686) (2S), and 0.37 GeV for ψ(3770) (1D).
- The charm quark mass is taken as m_c = 1.95 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonium mass modifications in nuclear matter using the chiral effective model (AmarvDmesonTprc, Amarvepja) and the linear density approximation (LeeKo) to include the effects of strong magnetic fields and isospin asymmetry. The results are relevant for understanding charmonium production in asymmetric heavy-ion collisions at the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at GSI, where large magnetic fields are created. The approach enables future studies of open charm meson production and charmonium dissociation in magnetized nuclear matter.
