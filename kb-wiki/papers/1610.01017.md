# Empirical parametrizations of the resonance amplitudes based on the Siegert's theorem

> We present parametrizations of the $γ^\ast N \to N(1535)1/2^-$, $γ^\ast N \to N(1520)3/2^-$ and $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)3/2^+$ transition amplitudes that are compatible with the analytic constraints at the pseudothreshold (Siegert's theorem). The presented parametrizations also provide a fair description of the experimental data. For the case of the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)3/2^+$ transition, we discuss how the pion cloud parametrizations of the electric and the Coulomb quadrupole form factors can be adjusted according to the Siegert's theorem.

## Key claim
The paper presents parametrizations of the γ*N → N(1535)1/2⁻, γ*N → N(1520)3/2⁻, and γ*N → Δ(1232)3/2⁺ transition amplitudes that satisfy the analytic constraints at the pseudothreshold (Siegert's theorem), while also providing a fair description of experimental data. For the Δ(1232)3/2⁺ transition specifically, the standard form of the Siegert's theorem (E₁₊ = λ S₁/₂/|q|) must be corrected by an additional factor of 1/|q| to obtain the correct relation between form factors at the pseudothreshold.

## Method
The author derives analytic constraints from the requirement that elementary form factors (Dirac, Pauli, and multipole form factors) are free of kinematic singularities at the pseudothreshold, where the photon three-momentum vanishes (Q² = -(M_R - M)²). For each resonance transition, these constraints yield specific relations between transverse and scalar amplitudes (Siegert's theorem), which are then used to construct empirical parametrizations (MAID-SG, MAID-SG2) based on rational functions of Q². For the Δ(1232)3/2⁺ transition, the author modifies existing large-N_c parametrizations of the pion cloud contributions to the electric (G_E^π) and Coulomb (G_C^π) quadrupole form factors by introducing a factor 1/(1 + Q²/(M_Δ² - M²)) to improve compatibility with the Siegert's theorem.

## Key numbers
- For γ*N → N(1535)1/2⁻: at the pseudothreshold, the scalar amplitude satisfies S₁/₂ ∝ |q|, with the relation A₁/₂ = λ S₁/₂/|q| where λ = √2 (M_R - M).
- For γ*N → N(1520)3/2⁻: at the pseudothreshold, the transverse amplitudes satisfy A₁/₂ = A₃/₂/√3, and the form factors satisfy G_E = ((M_R - M)/M_R) G_C.
- For γ*N → Δ(1232)3/2⁺: at the pseudothreshold, the form factors satisfy G_E = ((M_R - M)/(2 M_R)) G_C, with the corrected relation E₁₊/|q| = λ S₁/₂/|q|² where λ = √2 (M_R - M).
- For the Δ(1232)3/2⁺ transition, the MAID-SG2 parametrization induces falloffs G_E ∝ 1/Q⁴ and G_C ∝ 1/Q⁶ for very large Q², as expected from pQCD.
- The modified large-N_c parametrization for G_E^π improves the Siegert's theorem violation from O(1/N_c²) to O(1/N_c⁴) at the pseudothreshold.

## Context
This work extends previous parametrizations such as MAID2007 (Drechsel 2007) which failed to satisfy the Siegert's theorem for the N(1535)1/2⁻ transition, and provides corrected versions (MAID-SG, MAID-SG2) that respect the analytic constraints. The discussion of pion cloud contributions for the Δ(1232)3/2⁺ transition builds on large-N_c parametrizations by Buchmann (1998) and Pascalutsa (2007) relating G_E^π and G_C^π to the neutron electric form factor G_En, and shows how those parametrizations can be adjusted to satisfy the Siegert's theorem. The results enable more consistent extraction of resonance transition form factors from experimental data and provide constraints for theoretical models incorporating meson cloud effects beyond the impulse approximation.
