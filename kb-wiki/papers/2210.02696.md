# Nuclear symmetry energy and the PREX-CREX neutron skin puzzle within the KIDS framework

> I briefly review the KIDS theoretical framework for the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and energy density functional (EDF), I discuss recent results for the curvature parameter of the symmetry energy, and I address the PREX-CREX puzzle. I show that it is possible to obtain EDF models which can reproduce both PREX-II and CREX results each within its respective error bars. Such EDFs correspond to EoSs which soften towards low densities, as could be attributed to clusterization. Before such a scenario is considered viable, the dipole polarizability should also be examined.

# Nuclear symmetry energy and the PREX-CREX neutron skin puzzle within the KIDS framework

## Key claim
It is possible to obtain energy density functional (EDF) models within the KIDS framework that reproduce both the PREX-II and CREX neutron skin measurements each within their respective error bars, but only if the equation of state (EoS) softens strongly towards low densities, a behavior that could be attributed to clusterization. The author notes that before such a scenario is considered viable, the dipole polarizability must also be examined.

## Method
The author explores the EoS parameter space within the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) theoretical framework, which expands the nuclear matter energy in powers of the Fermi momentum and allows independent variation of symmetry-energy parameters. For a first search, basic parameters (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy J and slope L, and curvature parameter K_tau) are varied within fiducial ranges, while a second search extends to five EoS parameters including skewness and kyrtosis. The resulting EDF models are used in Hartree-Fock calculations of ground-state nuclear properties for closed-shell nuclei, and the four models satisfying both CREX and PREX-II constraints within error bars are identified.

## Key numbers
- CREX measurement: neutron skin thickness Δr_np = 0.121 ± 0.5 fm for ⁴⁸Ca
- PREX-II measurement: neutron skin thickness Δr_np = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm for ²⁰⁸Pb
- Fiducial ranges explored: ρ₀ = 0.155–0.161 fm⁻³, −E₀ = 15.5–16.1 MeV, K₀ = 190–250 MeV, J = 30–34 MeV, L = 40–70 MeV, −K_τ = 250–500 MeV
- For the successful models: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, E₀ = −16 MeV, K₀ = 220 or 240 MeV, J = 32 or 33 MeV, L = 40–70 MeV
- The successful EoSs require very large skewness (order of GeV) and kyrtosis (tens of GeV)
- Average deviation per datum for 19 basic nuclear data (binding energies and charge radii) does not exceed 1% for the successful models

## Context
This work extends the KIDS framework studies of symmetry-energy parameters (Papakonstantinou, 2022) and addresses the tension between the PREX-II measurement for ²⁰⁸Pb and the CREX measurement for ⁴⁸Ca, which standard EDF models cannot reconcile (Roca 2018, Yu 2020). Previous KIDS-based studies (Papakonstantinou 2021, Xu 2022) had suggested that K_sym is negative and no lower than −200 MeV, and that K_τ likely lies between −400 and −300 MeV. The results here suggest a decoupling between the density dependence at low and normal densities, similar to previous results suggesting decoupling of normal and dense regimes, and open the question of whether such highly skewed EoSs give reasonable results for the electric dipole polarizability and other isovector observables.
