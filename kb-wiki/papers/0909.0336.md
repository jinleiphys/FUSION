# Toward an Ab-initio Description of Quasiparticle Properties

> Preliminary ab-initio applications of many-body Green's functions theory to the ground state of He-4 suggest that high accuracy can be achieved in the so-called Faddeev-random-phase-approximation method. We stress the potentialities of this approach for microscopic studies of medium-large nuclei and report applications to 1s0d and 1p0f-shell nuclei. In particular, we discuss the role of long-range correlations on spectroscopic factors and their dependence on asymmetry.

## Key claim

Preliminary ab-initio applications of many-body Green's functions theory to the ground state of He-4 suggest that high accuracy can be achieved in the Faddeev-random-phase-approximation (FRPA) method, but the converged result for full self-consistency is not yet known and more work is in progress.

## Method

The author employed the Faddeev-random-phase-approximation (FRPA) to expand the nuclear self-energy within self-consistent Green's functions (SCGF) theory, using a harmonic oscillator basis and the Vlowk interaction derived from the Argonne v18 potential. For calculations of quasiparticle states in O-16, both the VUCOM and Argonne v18 interactions were used, with a G-matrix resummation of ladder diagrams from outside the model space for the latter. For Ni-56, the chiral N3LO interaction was modified by a monopole correction to reproduce the experimental gap between the 1p3/2 and 0f7/2 orbits.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of He-4 from FRPA: -29.00 MeV (converged to within 10 keV), compared to exact Faddeev-Yakubowsky result of -29.19(5) keV
- Spin-orbit splitting for O-16 with VUCOM: Delta E(p1/2-p3/2) = 4.5 MeV, Delta E(d3/2-d5/2) = 3.9 MeV
- Spin-orbit splitting for O-16 with Argonne v18 (G-matrix): Delta E(p1/2-p3/2) = 3.1 MeV, Delta E(d3/2-d5/2) = 3.6 MeV
- Experimental spin-orbit splitting for O-16: Delta E(p1/2-p3/2) = 6.18 MeV, Delta E(d3/2-d5/2) = 5.08 MeV
- Particle-hole gap for O-16 with Argonne v18: Delta E(s1/2-p1/2) = 12.2 MeV (experimental: 12.4 MeV)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 1p3/2 ground state transition between Ni-56 and Ni-57: 0.62 (FRPA plus shell model correction), compared to experimental value 0.58(11)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 0f7/2 orbit in Ni-55: 0.69 (FRPA plus shell model correction)

## Context

This work extends ab-initio Green's functions calculations from light nuclei to medium-mass systems in the 1s0d and 1p0f shells, building on prior coupled-cluster (CC) studies by Hag. (2007, 2008, 2009) and earlier SCGF work by Dickhoff and Van Neck, and Barbieri (2006, 2009). The FRPA approach is particularly suited for medium-heavy isotopes where collective states are important, and the results open a new way to derive the properties of shell model Hamiltonians from realistic nuclear forces. The calculations show that spectroscopic factors for nuclei around Ni-56 receive very small corrections from missing many-body correlations, but three-nucleon forces or phenomenological corrections will be needed to achieve reliable predictions for the dependence on proton-neutron asymmetry.
