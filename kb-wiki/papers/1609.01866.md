# Relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory for finite nuclei

> Starting with a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, for the first time the full relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations are solved for finite nuclei in a Dirac-Woods-Saxon basis. No free parameters are introduced to calculate the ground-state properties of finite nuclei. The nucleus $^{16}$O is investigated as an example. The resulting ground-state properties, such as binding energy and charge radius, are considerably improved as compared with the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results and much closer to the experimental data. This opens the door for \emph{ab initio} covariant investigations of heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

For the first time, the full relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) equations are solved self-consistently for a finite nucleus (¹⁶O) using only a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction (Bonn A) with no free parameters, but the calculation still deviates from experimental data by 6% in binding energy and 7% in charge radius, and the authors note that rearrangement terms and higher-order diagrams are not yet included.

## Method

The Bethe-Goldstone equation and the relativistic Hartree-Fock equations are solved simultaneously by iteration in a Dirac-Woods-Saxon (DWS) basis of sufficient size, with the G-matrix describing two-nucleon scattering in the finite nuclear medium. The Pauli operator is treated fully without angle averaging, and the continuous choice is used for the starting energy dependence of the G-matrix. The calculation is performed for ¹⁶O using the Bonn A interaction, with a cut-off energy of 1.1 GeV for the single-particle basis.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of ¹⁶O: E = -120.7 MeV (experimental value: -127.6 MeV, deviation 6%)
- Charge radius of ¹⁶O: r_c = 2.52 fm (experimental value: 2.70 fm, deviation 7%)
- Point matter radius of ¹⁶O: r_m = 2.38 fm
- Proton 1p spin-orbit splitting in ¹⁶O: ΔE_ls = 6.0 MeV (experimental value: 6.3 MeV, deviation 5%)
- Convergence achieved for single-particle energy cut-off ε_cut ≈ 1.1 GeV
- Non-relativistic BHF results for comparison: E = -105.0 MeV, r_c = 2.29 fm (deviations 18% and 16% respectively)

## Context

This work extends the success of relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory in nuclear matter (Anastasio 1983, Brockmann 1984, ter Haar 1987) to finite nuclei, avoiding the effective density approximation (EDA) used by Müther, Machleidt, and Brockmann (1988, 1990) and the local density approximation (LDA) used by Brockmann (1992), Fritz (1993), and Van Dalen (2010, 2011). The results are comparable to state-of-the-art ab initio calculations such as the No Core Shell Model with N³LO (Roth 2011, E = -119.7 MeV) and the Coupled Cluster method (Hagen 2009, E = -121.0 MeV). This method opens the door for parameter-free covariant investigations of heavier nuclei, where exact solutions are impossible, and may provide microscopic understanding of open questions in phenomenological density functional theories.
