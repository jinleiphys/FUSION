# Structure of particle-hole nuclei around 100Sn

> We have performed shell-model calculations for the three odd-odd nuclei 100In, 102In, and 98Ag, with neutron particles and proton holes around 100Sn. We have used a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, the neutron-proton channel being explicitly treated in the particle-hole formalism. Particular attention has been focused on the particle-hole multiplets, which are a direct source of information on the neutron-proton effective interaction. We present our predictions for the two lowest lying multiplets in 100In, for which no spectroscopic data are yet available. For 98Ag and 102In comparison shows that our results are in very good agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential reproduce the available experimental data for the odd-odd nuclei 98Ag and 102In, and predict the two lowest-lying particle-hole multiplets in 100In for which no spectroscopic data yet exist; the ground state of 100In is predicted to be J^pi = 6^+ from the pi g_{9/2}^{-1} nu d_{5/2} multiplet, but this result depends on the assumption that the nu g_{7/2} level lies only 10 keV below the nu d_{5/2} level, a spacing subject to an uncertainty of about 100 keV.

## Method

The authors assumed 100Sn as a closed core and performed shell-model calculations for the odd-odd nuclei 100In, 102In, and 98Ag, with neutron valence particles in the 50-82 shell and proton valence holes in the 28-50 shell. They derived a realistic effective interaction from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential by first constructing a low-momentum potential V_low-k with a cutoff momentum Lambda = 2.1 fm^{-1}, then applying the Q-box plus folded diagram method including diagrams up to second order in V_low-k. The neutron single-particle and proton single-hole energies were determined from analysis of light Sn isotopes and N=50 isotones, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The neutron d_{5/2} and g_{7/2} single-particle energies are nearly degenerate, with the g_{7/2} level lying 10 keV below the d_{5/2} level (epsilon_{d_{5/2}} - epsilon_{g_{7/2}} = 0.010 MeV).
- The splitting between the centroid energies of the pi g^{-1}_{9/2} nu d_{5/2} and pi g^{-1}_{9/2} nu g_{7/2} multiplets in 100In is about 400 keV.
- The dispersion (energy spread) of the pi g^{-1}_{9/2} nu g_{7/2} multiplet in 100In is 630 keV, compared to 360 keV for the pi g^{-1}_{9/2} nu d_{5/2} multiplet.
- In 100In, the J^pi = 7^+ state from the pi g^{-1}_{9/2} nu g_{7/2} configuration lies at 110 keV excitation energy above the predicted 6^+ ground state.
- The largest discrepancy between calculated and experimental energies for 98Ag and 102In is 200 keV, for the second 7^+ state in 102In.
- The experimental 8^+ state in 98Ag at 1.115 MeV is identified with the calculated first 8^+ state at 1.315 MeV; the experimental 8^+ state in 102In at 0.980 MeV is identified with the calculated first 8^+ at 1.210 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of proton particle-neutron hole nuclei around 132Sn (Coraggio et al., 2002) to the 100Sn region, using the same methodology based on the CD-Bonn potential. The particle-hole multiplets in 100In, 98Ag, and 102In exhibit the same pattern as those in 132Sb and 208Bi, with the highest- and lowest-spin members having the highest excitation energy and the state with next-to-highest J being the lowest, consistent with the Brennan-Bernstein coupling rule (Brennan and Bernstein, 1960). The results enable predictions for 100In that can guide future experiments, and the diagonal matrix elements differ somewhat from empirical matrix elements derived by fitting levels in nuclei with Z=46-49 and N=51-54 (Sohler et al., 2002).
