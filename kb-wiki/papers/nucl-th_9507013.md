# Incompressibility of nuclear matter, and Coulomb and volume-symmetry coefficients of nucleus incompressibility in the relativistic mean field theory

> The volume coefficient $K$(=incompressibility of the nuclear matter), the Coulomb coefficient $K_c$, and the volume-symmetry coefficient $K_{vs}$ of the nucleus incompressibility are studied in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory, with aid of the scaling model. It is found that $K= 300\pm 50$MeV is necessary to account for the empirical values of $K_v$, $K_c$, and $K_{vs}$, simultaneously. The result is independent on the detail descriptions of the potential of the $σ$-meson self-interaction and is almost independent of the strength of the $ω$-meson self-interaction.

## Key claim

The authors find that a nuclear matter incompressibility of \(K = 300 \pm 50\) MeV is necessary to simultaneously reproduce the empirical values of the volume coefficient \(K\), the Coulomb coefficient \(K_c\), and the volume-symmetry coefficient \(K_{vs}\) of nucleus incompressibility, and this conclusion holds regardless of the detailed form of the \(\sigma\)-meson self-interaction potential and is almost independent of the strength of the \(\omega\)-meson self-interaction.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic mean field theory based on the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) model with nonlinear \(\sigma\) terms, and also consider an additional vector self-interaction term for the \(\omega\) meson. They apply the scaling model to express the coefficients \(K\), \(K_c\), and \(K_{vs}\) in terms of nuclear matter properties, and then calculate \(K_{vs}\) as a function of the effective nucleon mass \(M^*\) for various sets of \(K\) and \(K_c\) taken from the literature, without specifying the explicit form of the \(\sigma\) self-interaction potential \(U(\phi)\).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility \(K = 300 \pm 50\) MeV is required to simultaneously account for empirical values of \(K\), \(K_c\), and \(K_{vs}\).
- For \(K = 300\) MeV and \(K_c = -3.990\) MeV, the calculated \(K_{vs}\) ranges from \(-289\) to \(-98\) MeV (without vector self-interaction), in good agreement with empirical values.
- For \(K = 250\) MeV and \(K_c = -0.7065\) MeV, the calculated \(K_{vs}\) ranges from \(50\) to \(410\) MeV (without vector self-interaction), which is somewhat larger than empirical values.
- With vector self-interaction strength \(y=5.0\), for \(K = 300\) MeV and \(K_c = -3.990\) MeV, \(K_{vs}\) ranges from \(-303\) to \(-289\) MeV.
- Input parameters used: normal baryon density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), nucleon mass \(M = 939\) MeV, binding energy \(a_1 = 15.75\) MeV, symmetry energy \(a_4 = 30.0\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Kouno et al. (1995) and others on the relation between the incompressibility \(K\) and the Coulomb coefficient \(K_c\), using the relativistic mean field theory with nonlinear \(\sigma\) terms and with nonlinear \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) terms. The authors show that their conclusion is more general than earlier parameter-dependent calculations, such as those by Rudaz et al. (1995) and Bodmer and Price (1995), and is consistent with the generator coordinate calculations for breathing-mode giant monopole resonance by Stoitsov, Ring and Sharma (1995) which suggest \(K \approx 300\) MeV. The result enables a model-independent constraint on nuclear matter incompressibility from empirical data on nucleus incompressibility coefficients.
