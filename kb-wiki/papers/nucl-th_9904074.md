# Enhanced Signatures for Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> The probability distribution in $R$, the proportion of neutral pions to all pions emitted in heavy ion collisions in certain kinematic regions, has been suggested as a signal of a disoriented chiral condensate (D$χ$C). Here we note that the signature can be greatly enhanced by making suitable cuts in the data. In particular, we consider reducing the data set such that the $k$ pions with lowest $p_T$ are all neutral. We find that, given such cuts, $<R >$ can be substantially different from 1/3. For example, for a single D$χ$C domain without contamination due to incoherently emitted pions, $<R>$ is 3/5 given the pion with lowest $p_T$ is neutral, and 5/7 given the two pions with lowest $p_T$ are both neutral, etc.. The effects of multi-domain D$χ$C formation and noise due to incoherent pion emission can be systematically incorporated. Potential applications to experiments and their limitations are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the signature of a disoriented chiral condensate (DχC) in heavy ion collisions can be greatly enhanced by applying successive cuts to retain only events where the \(k\) pions with the lowest transverse momentum are all neutral; under this condition, the expectation value of the neutral pion fraction \(\langle R \rangle\) shifts away from 1/3, with the shift increasing with \(k\), provided the pions originate from a DχC domain.

## Method

The authors derive the conditional probability distribution \(f_k(R)\) for the neutral pion fraction \(R\) given that the \(k\) pions with the lowest \(p_T\) are all neutral, starting from an isosinglet density matrix for a single DχC domain. They then generalize to a multi-domain scenario with \(N\) domains and a coherent fraction \(\chi\) (fraction of pions from DχC domains), incorporating incoherently emitted pions as noise, and compute the shift \(\Delta\) in \(\langle R \rangle\) and \(\langle Q \rangle\) (charged pion fraction) as a function of \(k\), \(\chi\), and \(N\).

## Key numbers

- For a single DχC domain with no incoherent contamination, \(\langle R \rangle = 3/5\) when the pion with the lowest \(p_T\) is neutral (\(k=1\)), and \(\langle R \rangle = 5/7\) when the two pions with the lowest \(p_T\) are both neutral (\(k=2\)).
- The general formula for a single domain is \(\langle R \rangle_k = (2k+1)/(2k+3)\); for \(k=0\) this gives \(\langle R \rangle_0 = 1/3\).
- In the weak signal limit (small \(\chi/N\)), the shift \(\Delta\) is given by \(\Delta = 2 \chi^2 k / (15 N) + \mathcal{O}(\chi^3/N^2)\).
- As \(k \to \infty\) with fixed \(\chi\) and \(N\), \(\langle R \rangle \to 1/3 + (2/3)\chi\) and \(\langle Q \rangle \to (1/3)(1-\chi)\).

## Context

This work extends the standard DχC signature based on the probability distribution \(f_0(R) = 1/(2\sqrt{R})\) (And, 1990; Bjorken, 1992; Rajagopal, 1993) which is washed out by multi-domain formation and incoherent backgrounds. The proposed cuts enhance the signal-to-noise ratio by selecting events where the lowest-\(p_T\) pions are neutral, exploiting isospin alignment in the condensate. The results enable experimental searches for DχC at heavy ion colliders by providing a systematic procedure to extract a shifted \(\langle R \rangle\) that grows with successive cuts, with the limitation that the maximum shift is bounded by the coherent fraction \(\chi\).
