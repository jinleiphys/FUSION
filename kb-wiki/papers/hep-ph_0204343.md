# Photon and Gluon Emission in Relativistic Plasmas

> We recently derived, using diagrammatic methods, the leading-order hard photon emission rate in ultra-relativistic plasmas. This requires a correct treatment of multiple scattering effects which limit the coherence length of emitted radiation (the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect). In this paper, we provide a more physical derivation of this result, and extend the treatment to the case of gluon radiation.

## Key claim

The paper provides a more physically intuitive derivation of the leading-order hard photon emission rate in ultra-relativistic plasmas (previously derived using diagrammatic methods) and extends the treatment to gluon radiation, showing that the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect must be included because the formation time of the emitted radiation is comparable to the mean free time for soft scattering.

## Method

The authors reformulate the problem of bremsstrahlung and pair annihilation as propagation of emitting particles through a classical random background color gauge field, treating the soft gluons (with momentum transfers of order gT) as a Gaussian random field. They sum the infinite class of ladder diagrams representing multiple scattering during the emission process by converting them into a Boltzmann-like integral equation, using a relativistic Schrodinger equation formalism for nearly on-shell particles in the soft gauge background.

## Key numbers

- The typical momentum transfer q of an exchanged gluon is of order gT, which is also the order of the inverse Debye screening length for color
- The angle of deflection in a collision is of order theta ~ g (where g is the strong coupling)
- The formation time of the photon is of order (g^2 T)^{-1}, which is also the order of the mean free time between collisions with momentum transfers of order gT
- The typical angle between the directions of the photon and the emitter(s) is theta ~ g
- The effective thermal mass of hard quarks is of order gT

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous diagrammatic derivation (Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe, 2002) of the leading-order photon emission rate by providing a more physical derivation, and generalizes the treatment to gluon emission. Prior discussions of the LPM effect (Migdal, 1955; Zakharov; Baier et al.; Gyulassy and Wang) focused on systems with static scatterers, whereas this work addresses the dynamically screened color electric and magnetic fields of an ultra-relativistic plasma where scatterers move at nearly the speed of light. The results are needed for computing thermalization and transport processes in hot QCD plasmas and for interpreting hard photon emission as a diagnostic probe of heavy ion collisions.
