# Exclusive Processes: Theory Introduction

> We review the recent developments on the theoretical description of exclusive processes at medium and asymptotical energies. These are illustrated based on a few examples.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys theoretical developments in exclusive processes at medium and asymptotical energies, but the abstract's claim of "recent developments" is qualified by the fact that the presentation is limited to a few illustrative examples rather than a comprehensive survey, and many of the formalisms described (such as collinear factorization for GPDs) have known limitations and competing mechanisms that may be significant even at large scales.

## Method

The paper reviews collinear factorization frameworks for exclusive processes, including the description of generalized parton distributions (GPDs), generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), and transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) in the context of deep virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), meson electroproduction, and related processes. It then discusses the perturbative Regge limit of QCD at large center-of-mass energies, where loop corrections involving powers of ln s require resummation approaches such as the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) formalism.

## Key numbers

* Proton form factor scaling: F_n(q^2) ~ C/(Q^2)^(n-1), where n is the minimal number of constituents (n=2 for mesons, n=3 for baryons), based on counting rules from Brodsky (1973, 1974)
* Large-angle elastic scattering scaling: dσ/dt ~ (α_S(p_⊥^2)/s)^(n-2), where n is the number of external fermionic lines (n=8 for ππ → ππ), from Brodsky (1981)
* Brodsky-Lepage mechanism for ππ → ππ scales as dσ_BL/dt ~ s^(-6), while the competing Landshoff mechanism scales as dσ_L/dt ~ s^(-5)
* Total cross-section in the Regge limit: σ_tot^(h1 h2) ~ s^(α_P(0)-1) with α_P(0) > 1, from Fadin (1975), Kuraev (1976, 1977), and Balitsky (1978)

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on exclusive processes in QCD from the 1970s and 1980s, including the counting rules of Brodsky and Farrar (1973, 1974) and the competing Landshoff mechanism (1974) for large-angle scattering. It connects the early perturbative QCD descriptions of form factors to modern frameworks involving GPDs (Mueller 1998, Radyushkin 1996, Ji 1996), GDAs (Diehl 1998), and TDAs (Pire 2004), and discusses how these formalisms enable future experimental programs at JLab@12 GeV, COMPASS-II, LHeC, EIC, and ILC. The paper also addresses open questions regarding transversity GPDs, factorization limitations for transversely polarized mesons, and the need for resummation of threshold singularities in DVCS coefficient functions.
