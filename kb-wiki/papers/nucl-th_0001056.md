# Adiabatic Selfconsistent Collective Coordinate Method for Large Amplitude Collective Motion in Superconducting Nuclei

> An adiabatic approximation to the selfconsistent collective coordinate method is formulated in order to describe large amplitude collective motions in superconducting nuclei on the basis of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations of motion. The basic equations are presented in a local harmonic form which can be solved in a similar way as the quasiparticle RPA equations. The formalism guarantees the conservation of nucleon number expectation values. An extension to the multi-dimensional case is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper formulates an adiabatic approximation to the selfconsistent collective coordinate method (SCCM) that provides a consistent and practical scheme for describing large amplitude collective motions in superconducting nuclei, with the caveat that the formalism relies on an expansion in powers of the collective momentum and particle number deviation, making it suitable only for slow (adiabatic) motions where these quantities remain small.

## Method

The authors start from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations and introduce an adiabatic approximation by expanding the TDHFB state vector and collective Hamiltonian in powers of the collective momentum \(p\) and the particle number deviation \(n = N - N_0\). They derive zeroth, first, and second order equations for the collective subspace, which are then transformed into a set of local harmonic equations that have the same structure as quasiparticle RPA equations. The formalism incorporates the gauge angle \(\varphi\) and its conjugate momentum \(N\) to handle particle number conservation in superconducting nuclei, and the basic equations are solved by determining the infinitesimal generators \(\hat{Q}(q)\) and \(\hat{P}(q)\) at each point along the collective path.

## Key numbers

- The collective Hamiltonian for a system with \(N_0\) particles is given as \(\mathcal{H}(q,p,N_0) = V(q) + \frac{1}{2} B(q) p^2\), where \(V(q)\) is the collective potential and \(B(q)\) is the mass parameter defined as \(B(q) = -\langle \phi_q | [[\hat{H}, \hat{Q}(q)], \hat{Q}(q)] | \phi_q \rangle\).
- The local stiffness parameter is defined as \(C(q) = \partial^2 V / \partial q^2 - \Gamma(q) \partial V / \partial q\), with \(\Gamma(q) = -(1/2B(q)) \partial B / \partial q\).
- The chemical potential term \(\lambda(q)\) is defined as \(\lambda(q) = \langle \phi_q | [\hat{H}, i\hat{\Theta}(q)] | \phi_q \rangle\), where \(\hat{\Theta}(q)\) is the time-odd generator associated with the particle number variable.

## Context

This work extends the selfconsistent collective coordinate method (SCCM) developed by Marumori and collaborators to include an adiabatic approximation, making it applicable to large amplitude motions like fission and shape transitions where the power series expansion of the SCCM may not converge. It also builds on the adiabatic TDHF (ATDHF) theory of Villars, Baranger, and Veneroni, addressing the non-uniqueness problem identified by Goeke, Reinhard, and others by incorporating a second-order equation similar to that of Mukhopadhyay and Pal. The formalism enables future realistic calculations of collective paths and collective Hamiltonians for superconducting nuclei, including spontaneous fission and shape coexistence phenomena, by providing equations that can be solved numerically in a manner analogous to standard RPA calculations.
