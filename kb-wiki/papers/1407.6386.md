# Colored condensates deep inside neutron stars

> It is demonstrated how in the absence of solutions for QCD under conditions deep inside compact stars an equation of state can be obtained within a model that is built on the basic symmetries of the QCD Lagrangian, in particular chiral symmetry and color symmetry. While in the vacuum the chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken, it gets restored at high densities. Color symmetry, however, gets broken simultaneously by the formation of colorful diquark condensates. It is shown that a strong diquark condensate in cold dense quark matter is essential for supporting the possibility that such states could exist in the recently observed pulsars with masses of 2 $M_\odot$.

# Colored condensates deep inside neutron stars

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that within a Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model respecting QCD symmetries, a strong diquark condensate in cold dense quark matter is essential for supporting the possibility that recently observed pulsars with masses of 2 solar masses could exist as hybrid stars with a color superconducting quark matter core. The claim is conditional on the specific choice of vector meson and scalar diquark coupling strengths, which must be tuned simultaneously to satisfy the mass constraint.

## Method
The author employs a 3-flavor PNJL model in the meanfield approximation, augmented with isoscalar vector and scalar diquark interaction channels, to compute the thermodynamic potential under constraints of electric neutrality and beta-equilibrium. The resulting zero-temperature equation of state is then combined with a DBHF hadronic equation of state via a Maxwell construction, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius and mass-central density sequences for compact star configurations.

## Key numbers
- Two recently observed pulsars with masses of 2 solar masses: PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230, used as constraints for hybrid star models.
- The critical density for the deconfinement transition in heavy-ion collisions (symmetric nuclear matter) should exceed 3.5 saturation densities unless the diquark coupling satisfies eta_V > 1.0.
- The maximum allowable mass of a sequence is determined by the onset of strange quark matter formation (CFL phase), which softens the equation of state so that all configurations with strange quark matter cores lie on an unstable branch.

## Context
This work extends the application of effective QCD models, particularly the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model as reviewed by Klevansky (1992), Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994), and Buballa (2003), to the interior of compact stars. It addresses the hyperon puzzle and the direct Urca cooling problem that arise in purely hadronic scenarios, as discussed by Lastowiecki and Blaschke (2011) and Klahn et al. (2006). The results enable future searches for "mass twins", two high-mass pulsars with very different radii that would signal a strong deconfinement phase transition, as suggested by Blaschke et al. (2013), and may contribute to understanding supernova explosion mechanisms.
