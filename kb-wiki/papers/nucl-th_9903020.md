# Three-Body Scattering Below Breakup Threshold: An Approach without using Partial Waves

> The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering below the three-body breakup threshold is directly solved without employing a partial wave decomposition. In the simplest form it is a three-dimensional integral equation in four variables. From its solution the scattering amplitude is obtained as function of vector Jacobi momenta. Based on Malfliet-Tjon type potentials differential and total cross sections are calculated. The numerical stability of the algorithm is demonstrated and the properties of the scattering amplitude discussed.

# Three-Body Scattering Below Breakup Threshold: An Approach without using Partial Waves

## Key claim

The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering below the three-body breakup threshold can be solved directly in momentum space using vector Jacobi momenta without any partial wave decomposition, yielding stable numerical results; however, the method is demonstrated only for spinless identical bosons with Malfliet-Tjon type potentials, and the authors explicitly note that above the breakup threshold the logarithmic singularities inherent to breakup would require separate treatment.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equation for three identical bosons in momentum space by working directly with vector Jacobi momenta (magnitudes and angles between momentum vectors) rather than expanding in partial waves. The integral equation for the transition operator T is reduced to a three-dimensional integral equation in four variables (p, x_p, q, x_q) by choosing the z-axis parallel to q, which simplifies the free propagator. The Neumann series is generated iteratively and summed using the Padé method, with the two-body t-matrix and its pole at the deuteron bound state energy handled by extracting the residue explicitly.

## Key numbers

- The three-body scattering length for the MT-IIIa potential is 2.034 fm, and for the MT-IVa potential is 0.887 fm.
- The three-body binding energy for the MT-IIIa potential is -19.8625 MeV, and for the MT-IVa potential is -25.1632 MeV.
- Total cross section calculations at 3 MeV show variation of about 0.3% when grid points are increased successively in one variable at a time.
- The two different methods of calculating the total cross section (angular integration versus optical theorem) differ consistently by about 0.2%.
- The numerical error is estimated to be 0.5% or lower.
- Typical grid parameters: NP1 = 40 and NP2 = 16 points for momentum grids; 32 Gauss points for angular integration; NQ1 = 24 and NQ2 = 16 points for the q-integration; 18 integration points for x''; 16 points for the phi'' integration.
- The momentum cutoff for the q-integration is q_max = 20 fm^{-1}, with q_1 = 5 fm^{-1}.
- The p variable grid uses p_1 = 7 fm^{-1} and p_max = 30 fm^{-1}.
- 15-18 terms of the Neumann series are needed to obtain a converged result.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous direct vector-variable approach to the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation (Elster 1998) and the bound state Faddeev equation (Elster 1998) to the three-body scattering regime below breakup. It contrasts with the traditional partial wave decomposition method, which becomes increasingly cumbersome and less physically transparent at higher projectile energies (a few hundred MeV) due to the proliferation of partial waves. The approach enables future extension to scattering above the breakup threshold and provides a foundation for calculations with more realistic nucleon-nucleon forces that have simple momentum-space structure (e.g., one-boson-exchange forces), as opposed to the complicated partial wave representations of such forces (Machleidt 1987).
