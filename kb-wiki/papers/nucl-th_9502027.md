# Theories for multiple resonances

> Two microscopic theories for multiple resonances in nuclei are compared, n-particle-hole RPA and quantized Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF). The Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model is used as test case. We find that quantized TDHF is superior in many respects, except for very small systems.

## Key claim

Quantized Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) is superior to n-particle-hole RPA (RPA-N) for describing multiple resonances in nuclei, except for very small systems where RPA-N performs better; the comparison is made using the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model as a test case.

## Method

The authors compared two microscopic theories for multiple resonance excitations: RPA-N, an extension of RPA to higher n-particle-hole states derived from equations-of-motion techniques, and quantized TDHF, which selects quantum states from classically allowed TDHF orbits by requiring strictly periodic orbits with integer action. Both methods were applied to the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model, a simplified shell model with two degenerate bands of single Fermion states, using the SU(2) algebra of collective operators K_+, K_-, and K_0. The comparison was performed for three system sizes (Omega = 5, 14, and 50) representing light, medium, and heavy nuclei, across a range of coupling strengths chi.

## Key numbers

- The LMG model has two parameters: coupling strength chi and system size Omega (particle number or size of active phase space); the critical coupling is at chi = 1, almost independent of Omega.
- The RPA frequency for the collective mode in the domain chi < 1 is omega = epsilon * sqrt(1 - chi^2).
- For the small system size Omega = 5, RPA-5 performed very well for multiple resonances, while quantized TDHF looked inferior.
- For medium and large systems (Omega = 14 and 50), quantized TDHF results were "astonishingly close to the exact solution" for multiple resonances.
- RPA-2 could only describe the system up to the second excited state but performed "surprisingly well" at its upper limit omega_2.
- Quantized TDHF provided robust results across all ranges of chi, including the critical point chi = 1, where RPA-5 ran into difficulties.
- For the first excited state at chi < 1, the excitation energy goes to zero as chi approaches 1, merging into one of the two ground states for large Omega.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical investigations of multiple resonances using second RPA (Yan, Chomaz, Lauritsch) and Boson expansions (reviewed in BosExp), and contrasts with the plain RPA which is known to break down near the critical coupling (Holzwarth). The LMG model was originally introduced to understand collective modes in RPA (Lipkin, Meshkov). The results show that quantized TDHF is more robust at the critical point and for larger systems, while RPA-N remains the method of choice for small systems at not too high excitation energies. The findings apply also to Boson expansions, which are close relatives of RPA-N.
