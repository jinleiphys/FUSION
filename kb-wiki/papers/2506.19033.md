# Probing jet-medium interactions via jet substructure observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present a comprehensive study of jet substructure observables in $pp$ and PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$~TeV using a multi-phase transport model. To suppress background contamination, the constituent subtraction method is employed for both PbPb and smeared $pp$ events. The jet splitting momentum fraction ($z_g$) and the groomed jet mass to the ungroomed jet transverse momentum ($M_g / p_{T,\text{jet}}$) are reconstructed using the Soft Drop algorithm with two grooming parameter settings. With $z_\text{cut} = 0.1$ and $β= 0.0$, a slight modification in the $z_g$ distribution is observed in central PbPb collisions, whereas a pronounced enhancement in the high $M_g / p_{T,\text{jet}}$ region is found, particularly at low $p_{T,\text{jet}}$ and in more central events. A detailed analysis of the dynamical evolution stages reveals that this enhancement primarily originates from jet-medium interactions, whereas the contributions from hadronization and hadronic rescatterings are largely mitigated by the grooming procedure. In contrast, under a stronger grooming condition ($z_\text{cut} = 0.5$, $β= 1.5$), no significant changes in $M_g / p_{T,\text{jet}}$ are observed, indicating that the medium-induced modifications are predominantly associated with large-angle scattering.

# Probing jet-medium interactions via jet substructure observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model with constituent subtraction and Soft Drop grooming, the authors find that a pronounced enhancement in the high $M_g / p_{T,\text{jet}}$ region in central PbPb collisions originates primarily from jet-medium interactions during the parton cascade stage, while contributions from hadronization and hadronic rescatterings are largely mitigated by the grooming procedure; this enhancement is observed only with the weaker grooming condition ($z_\text{cut} = 0.1$, $\beta = 0.0$) and not with the stronger grooming condition ($z_\text{cut} = 0.5$, $\beta = 1.5$), indicating that medium-induced modifications are predominantly associated with large-angle scattering.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting mechanism to simulate $pp$ and PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, with parton cross sections set to 3 mb (with jet-medium interactions) and 0 mb (baseline without interactions). Jets were reconstructed using the anti-$k_t$ algorithm with $R = 0.4$, and background contamination was suppressed via the constituent subtraction method applied to both PbPb and smeared $pp$ events. The Soft Drop algorithm with two grooming parameter settings ($z_\text{cut} = 0.1$, $\beta = 0.0$ and $z_\text{cut} = 0.5$, $\beta = 1.5$) was used to reconstruct the jet splitting momentum fraction ($z_g$) and the groomed jet mass to ungroomed jet transverse momentum ($M_g / p_{T,\text{jet}}$), with an angular requirement of $\Delta R_{12} > 0.1$.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV for both $pp$ and PbPb collisions
- Jet radius parameter: $R = 0.4$
- Pseudorapidity requirement: $|\eta_\text{jet}| < 1.3$
- Parton cross section settings: 3 mb (with interactions) and 0 mb (without interactions)
- Soft Drop parameter set 1: $z_\text{cut} = 0.1$, $\beta = 0.0$
- Soft Drop parameter set 2: $z_\text{cut} = 0.5$, $\beta = 1.5$
- Angular cut: $\Delta R_{12} > 0.1$
- Jet transverse momentum ranges studied: $140 < p_{T,\text{jet}} < 160$ GeV, $160 < p_{T,\text{jet}} < 180$ GeV, $200 < p_{T,\text{jet}} < 250$ GeV
- Centrality classes: 0-10% most central PbPb collisions, with centrality dependence shown across multiple intervals
- With $z_\text{cut} = 0.1$ and $\beta = 0.0$: a slight modification in the $z_g$ distribution is observed in central PbPb collisions, while a pronounced enhancement in the high $M_g / p_{T,\text{jet}}$ region is found, particularly at low $p_{T,\text{jet}}$ and in more central events
- With $z_\text{cut} = 0.5$ and $\beta = 1.5$: no significant changes in $M_g / p_{T,\text{jet}}$ are observed across all centrality intervals

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT model studies of jet observables including dijet asymmetry (Ma 2013), $\gamma$-jet imbalance (Ma 2013), jet fragmentation functions (Ma 2013, Duan 2022), jet shape (Ma 2013), and ungroomed jet mass (Duan 2023). The results are compared with CMS experimental measurements of $z_g$ (CMS 2017) and $M_g / p_{T,\text{jet}}$ (CMS 2018) at 5.02 TeV, and with theoretical predictions from JEWEL (Milhano 2017, 2022), SCET (Chien 2016), JETSCAPE (2023), higher twist formalism (Chang 2017), LBT (Luo 2021), and Hybrid model (Casalderrey-Solana 2014, D'Eramo 2018). The findings enable future incorporation of inelastic radiative energy loss processes into the AMPT framework to study additional observables such as the jet splitting radius ($r_g$).
