# Wave packet scattering from an attractive well

> Wave packet scattering off an attractive well is investigated in two spatial dimensions numerically. The results confirm what was found previously for the one dimensional case. The wave scattered at large angles is a polychotomous (multiple peak) coherent train. Large angle scattering is extremely important for low impinging velocities and at all impact parameters. The effect disappears for packets more extended than the well. Experiments to detect the polychotomous behavior are suggested.

## Key claim

Wave packet scattering off an attractive well in two spatial dimensions produces a polychotomous (multiple peak) coherent wave train at large angles, but only when the initial packet is narrower than the well width; the effect disappears for packets more extended than the well.

## Method

The author solved the time-dependent nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation in two spatial dimensions using the method of Goldberg et al., expanding the wave function in partial waves up to l_max = 50 (or 70 for large impact parameters) and applying a substitution Phi_l = Psi_l / sqrt(r) to simplify the numerical treatment. A minimal-uncertainty Gaussian wave packet was launched from x0 = -10 toward a Gaussian attractive well, and the scattered wave function was computed as a function of angle and radial distance at various times.

## Key numbers

- Packet width Delta = 0.5 (narrow) versus Delta = 2 (wide), with well width w = 1 or w = 2, showing polychotomous behavior only for narrow packets.
- Mass m = 20 used to keep relativistic corrections small; packet width inverse Delta^{-1} approx 2, with maximum mode speed less than v = 1.
- For the one-dimensional case, the amplitude at the origin decays as |psi(0)| = C / t^{1.55} (polynomial, not exponential).
- For two-dimensional scattering at t = 300, backward (180 deg) scattering shows clear multiple peaks for q = 0.5, 1.0, 1.5 with zero impact parameter; the effect persists at 90 deg and 0 deg but is cleaner in the backward direction.
- For a shallow well depth V0 = 0.03, the polychotomous behavior disappears (figure 19).
- Partial wave expansion accuracy better than 1% with l_max = 50; normalization error less than 0.01% over hundreds of thousands of time steps.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous one-dimensional study (Kalbermann, 1999) to two spatial dimensions, confirming that the polychotomous reflected wave train persists in higher dimensions. The effect is related to the formation of a metastable quasi-bound state inside the well that decays polynomially rather than exponentially, differing from transient diffraction-in-time phenomena (Moshinsky). The author suggests connections to anomalous large-angle scattering (ALAS) in nuclear alpha-particle scattering (Michel, year not given) and proposes experiments using cold Bose traps, optics, or sound propagation to detect the multiple-peak structure.
