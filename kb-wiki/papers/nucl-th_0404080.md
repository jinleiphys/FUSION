# Nuclear Forces from Chiral Dynamics

> We show that from analysis of time-ordered diagrams for the two-nucleon $T$-matrix in chiral perturbation theory it follows that low energy nucleon dynamics is governed by the generalized dynamical equation, which was derived in [J.Phys.A., v.32, p.5657 (1999)], with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator. An operator is presented that parametrizes the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the 1S0 channel at leading order of the Weinberg power counting.

## Key claim

The paper argues that low-energy nucleon dynamics derived from time-ordered diagrams in chiral perturbation theory is not governed by the Schrödinger equation but by a generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator; the authors show this specifically for the 1S0 channel at leading order of Weinberg power counting, where the resulting T-matrix is well-defined without regularization or renormalization.

## Method

The authors analyze time-ordered diagrams for the two-nucleon T-matrix in chiral perturbation theory and show that the T-matrix structure (Equation 13) cannot be generated by a Hamiltonian potential but instead satisfies the generalized dynamical equation (Equation 8) derived in Gainutdinov (1999). They determine the asymptotic behavior of the T-matrix for large |z| by solving the differential equation (Equation 8) with boundary conditions from the chiral Lagrangian, obtaining the nonlocal-in-time interaction operator for the 1S0 channel. The resulting T-matrix is then constructed by solving the generalized dynamical equation with this operator, yielding a scattering amplitude expressed in terms of the pure Yukawa theory solution plus a contact term.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order asymptotic expansion of the T-matrix for the 1S0 channel is: ⟨p₂|T(z)|p₁⟩ = b₁/√(-z) - b₂/z - b₃(ln z)/z + B₀₁(z,p₁) + B₁₀(z,p₂) + V_π(p₁,p₂) + o(|z|⁻¹), where b₁ = -4π/(M√M) and b₃ = -2b₁² 4πα_π ∫ d³q₁ d³q₂/(2π)⁶ (q₁-q₂)⁻²(1-q₁²/M)⁻²(1-q₂²/M)⁻¹
- The one-pion exchange potential is V_π(p₂,p₁) = -4πα_π/(q²+m_π²) with α_π = g_A² m_π²/(8π f_π²)
- The contact parameter C = -(4π)² M⁻³ b₂⁻¹, where b₂ is a free parameter
- The scattering amplitude is A(p) = A_π - {χ(E_p,p)}² [C⁻¹ - G^{(R)}_{E_p}(0,0)]⁻¹, where A_π is the pure Yukawa amplitude

## Context

This work extends the generalized quantum dynamics (GQD) framework developed by Gainutdinov (1999) to chiral perturbation theory, following earlier application to pionless effective field theory in Gainutdinov (2002). The paper directly addresses the UV divergence problem in the Weinberg program for deriving nuclear forces from QCD via chiral perturbation theory, as reviewed in the EFT nuclear forces literature. The resulting scattering amplitude matches the form obtained by Kaplan, Savage and Wise (Kaplan2) through diagram summation and renormalization, but the GQD approach avoids regularization and renormalization entirely. This enables a new path to constructing a consistent theory of nuclear forces free from ultraviolet divergences.
