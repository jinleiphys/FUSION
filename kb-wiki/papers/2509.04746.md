# The Three-Body Limit Cycle: Universal Form for General Regulators

> The Efimov effect, a remarkable realization of discrete scale invariance, emerges in the three-body problem with short-range interactions and is understood as a renormalization group (RG) limit cycle within Short-Range Effective Field Theory (SREFT). While the analytic form of the three-body renormalization relation has been established for a sharp cutoff regulator, its universality for other regulators remains underexplored. In this work, we derive the universal functional form of the three-body renormalization relation for general separable regulators through a detailed analysis of the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian and Faddeev equations. We find that the relation follows from a real Möbius transformation characterized by three parameters. This universality is verified numerically for various regulators. Although the functional form remains the same, the parameters characterizing the limit cycle exhibit regulator dependence. These findings broaden the class of RG limit cycles in SREFT and offer a more complete understanding of three-body renormalization.

## Key claim

The three-body renormalization relation for general separable regulators follows a universal functional form given by a real Möbius transformation characterized by three parameters, but the specific values of those parameters (the phase, amplitude, and reference scale) depend on the choice of regulator, contrary to the common assumption that the phase is fixed at arctan(1/s₀).

## Method

The authors analyze the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) and Faddeev equations for three identical bosons at the two-body unitarity limit, using separable (nonlocal) regulators. They derive the functional form of the three-body low-energy constant H₀(Λ/Λ₊) by showing that the asymptotic solution of the STM equation depends linearly on H₀, leading to a real Möbius transformation between H₀ and the phase of the wave function. The universality is verified numerically by solving both the STM and Faddeev equations with sharp cutoff, Gaussian (n=1), quartic super-Gaussian (n=2), and sextic super-Gaussian (n=3) regulators, fitting the resulting H₀(Λ) curves to the predicted form.

## Key numbers

- s₀ ≈ 1.00624, the universal constant characteristic of the Efimov limit cycle
- For the sharp cutoff regulator in the STM equation: δ₀ = 0.7823, h₀ = 0.879, b₀ = 2.61 (from literature)
- For the Gaussian regulator (n=1) in the STM equation: δ₀ = 1.0463, h₀ = 1.8024, b₀ = 4.4436
- For the quartic super-Gaussian (n=2) in the STM equation: δ₀ = 0.8869, h₀ = 1.4744, b₀ = 3.4930
- For the sextic super-Gaussian (n=3) in the STM equation: δ₀ = 0.8361, h₀ = 1.2804, b₀ = 3.2042
- For the Gaussian regulator (n=1) in the Faddeev equation: δ₀ = 0.7094, h₀ = 0.7976, b₀ = 2.3965
- For the quartic super-Gaussian (n=2) in the Faddeev equation: δ₀ = 0.4455, h₀ = 1.0189, b₀ = 2.6236
- For the sextic super-Gaussian (n=3) in the Faddeev equation: δ₀ = 0.3766, h₀ = 1.0037, b₀ = 2.5985

## Context

This work extends the analytic form of the three-body renormalization relation, previously established only for a sharp cutoff regulator by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998, 1999) and further analyzed by Chen et al. (2025), to general separable regulators. It shows that the commonly used fitting formula with fixed phase δ₀ = arctan(1/s₀) for Gaussian regulators (Platter, 2004, 2005) is a special case of a broader class of RG limit cycles. The results enable more accurate few- and many-body calculations using smoother regulators, which are widely employed in nuclear and atomic physics, and clarify that the regulator dependence of the limit-cycle parameters (δ₀, h₀, b₀) is physical and must be accounted for when extracting three-body parameters from numerical calculations.
