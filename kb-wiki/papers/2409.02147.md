# Anisotropic pressure effect on central EOS of PSR J0740+6620 in the light of dimensionless TOV equation

> It is generally agreed upon that the pressure inside a neutron star is isotropic. However, a strong magnetic field or superfluidity suggests that the pressure anisotropy may be a more realistic model. We derived the dimensionless TOV equation for anisotropic neutron stars based on two popular models, namely the BL model and the H model, to investigate the effect of anisotropy. Similar to the isotropic case, the maximum mass $M_{max}$ and its corresponding radius $R_{Mmax}$ can also be expressed linearly by a combination of radial central pressure $p_{rc}$ and central energy density $\varepsilon_{c}$, which is insensitive to the equation of state (EOS). We also found that the obtained central EOS would change with different values of $λ_{BL}$ ($λ_{H}$), which controls the magnitude of the difference between the transverse pressure and the radial pressure. Combining with observational data of PSR J0740+6620 and comparing to the extracted EOS based on isotropic neutron star, it is shown that in the BL model, for $λ_{BL}$ = 0.4, the extracted central energy density $\varepsilon_{c}$ changed from 546 -- 1056 MeV/fm$^{3}$ to 510 -- 1005 MeV/fm$^{3}$, and the extracted radial central pressure $p_{rc}$ changed from 87 -- 310 MeV/fm$^{3}$ to 76 -- 271 MeV/fm$^{3}$. For $λ_{BL}$ = 2, the extracted $\varepsilon_{c}$ and $p_{rc}$ changed to 412 -- 822 MeV/fm$^{3}$ and 50 -- 165 MeV/fm$^{3}$, respectively. In the H model, for $λ_{H}$ = 0.4, the extracted $\varepsilon_{c}$ changed to 626 -- 1164 MeV/fm$^{3}$, and the extracted $p_{rc}$ changed to 104 -- 409 MeV/fm$^{3}$. For $λ_{H}$ = 2, the extracted $\varepsilon_{c}$ decreased to 894 -- 995 MeV/fm$^{3}$, and the extracted $p_{rc}$ changed to 220 -- 301 MeV/fm$^{3}$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when extracting the central equation of state (EOS) of PSR J0740+6620 from observational data, the assumption of isotropic pressure leads to systematically different central energy density and radial central pressure ranges compared to anisotropic models, with the magnitude and direction of the shift depending on the specific anisotropy model (BL or H) and the anisotropy parameter value.

## Method

The authors derived a dimensionless form of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for anisotropic neutron stars using two popular anisotropy models: the BL model and the H model. They expanded the dimensionless radial pressure, tangential pressure, energy density, and enclosed mass as polynomials in the dimensionless radial coordinate near the stellar center, matched coefficients, and obtained expressions relating the maximum mass and corresponding radius to the central radial pressure and central energy density. Using 36 different nuclear EOSs, they established linear universal relations between maximum mass and a combination of central variables for each anisotropy parameter value, then applied these relations to observational mass and radius constraints of PSR J0740+6620 to extract the allowed central EOS region.

## Key numbers

- For the BL model with lambda_BL = 0.4: extracted central energy density changed from 546-1056 MeV/fm^3 (isotropic) to 510-1005 MeV/fm^3; extracted radial central pressure changed from 87-310 MeV/fm^3 to 76-271 MeV/fm^3
- For the BL model with lambda_BL = 2: extracted central energy density changed to 412-822 MeV/fm^3; extracted radial central pressure changed to 50-165 MeV/fm^3
- For the H model with lambda_H = 0.4: extracted central energy density changed to 626-1164 MeV/fm^3; extracted radial central pressure changed to 104-409 MeV/fm^3
- For the H model with lambda_H = 2: extracted central energy density changed to 894-995 MeV/fm^3; extracted radial central pressure changed to 220-301 MeV/fm^3
- Causality constraint for the BL model gives maximum reduced radial central pressure p_hat_rc = 0.374 (same as isotropic); for the H model, p_hat_rc = 0.354 at lambda_H = 0.4 and p_hat_rc = 0.303 at lambda_H = 2
- The linear fits for M_max versus alpha (BL model) have slopes ranging from 0.172 x 10^4 (lambda_BL = 0) to 0.096 x 10^4 (lambda_BL = 2), with Pearson coefficients 0.975-0.997

## Context

This work extends the dimensionless TOV equation approach developed by previous authors (DTOV1, DTOV2) for isotropic neutron stars to the case of anisotropic pressure, motivated by observations of exotic compact objects such as the secondary component of GW190814 (mass 2.50-2.67 solar masses) and the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 (mass 2.35 +/- 0.17 solar masses). The paper shows that anisotropy can significantly affect the extraction of central EOS from observational data, and that different anisotropy models (BL model versus H model) produce opposite trends in how the extracted central EOS shifts relative to the isotropic case. This enables future work to incorporate anisotropy as a systematic uncertainty when constraining the high-density nuclear EOS from neutron star observations.
