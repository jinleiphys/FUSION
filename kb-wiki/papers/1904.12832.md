# Viscosity spectral functions of resonating fermions in the quantum virial expansion

> We consider two-component fermions with a zero-range interaction both in two and three dimensions and study their spectral functions of bulk and shear viscosities for an arbitrary scattering length. Here the Kubo formulas are systematically evaluated up to the second order in the quantum virial expansion applicable to the high-temperature regime. In particular, our computation of the bulk viscosity spectral function is facilitated by expressing it with the contact-contact response function, which can be measured experimentally under the periodic modulation of the scattering length. The obtained formulas are fully consistent with the known constraints on high-frequency tail and sum rule. Although our static shear viscosity agrees with that derived from the kinetic theory, our static bulk viscosity disagrees. Furthermore, the latter for three dimensions exhibits an unexpected non-analyticity of $ζ\sim(\ln a^2)/a^2$ in the unitarity limit $a\to\infty$, which thus challenges the "crossover" hypothesis.

## Key claim

The paper derives viscosity spectral functions for two-component fermions with zero-range interactions using the quantum virial expansion up to second order in fugacity, valid in the high-temperature regime; however, the static bulk viscosity obtained from the Kubo formalism disagrees with kinetic theory predictions and exhibits an unexpected non-analyticity of ζ ∼ (ln a²)/a² in the three-dimensional unitarity limit a → ∞, challenging the "crossover" hypothesis.

## Method

The Kubo formulas for bulk and shear viscosity spectral functions are systematically evaluated up to O(z²) in the quantum virial expansion, where z = e^{βμ} is the fugacity. The bulk viscosity computation is facilitated by expressing it via the contact–contact response function, while the shear viscosity requires resummation of the static limit to obtain a Drude form. The two-body scattering T-matrix is used to evaluate the imaginary parts, and the results are checked against known high-frequency tail and sum rule constraints.

## Key numbers

- For d=3, the static bulk viscosity in the unitarity limit a→∞ scales as λ_T³ζ = (2√2 z²)/(9π) × (ln(e^{-1-γ}ã²))/ã² + O(z²ã⁻⁴, z³), where ã = √(mT)a.
- For d=3, the static shear viscosity in the unitarity limit exhibits a weaker non-analyticity starting with η ∼ (ln a²)/a⁶.
- For d=2, the static bulk viscosity has asymptotic form lim_{a→0,∞} λ_T²ζ = (2π z²)/(ln⁴(e^{-γ}ã²)) + O(z² ln⁻⁶ ã², z³).
- The high-frequency tail of the bulk viscosity for d=3 is lim_{ω→∞} ζ(ω) = (C/(9a²)) × (4π/|mω|³/²), where C is the contact density.
- The high-frequency tail of the shear viscosity for d=3 is lim_{ω→∞} η(ω) = (π C)/(4π|mω|¹/²).

## Context

This work extends the quantum virial expansion approach to viscosity spectral functions for two-component fermions in two and three dimensions, building on prior studies of transport in ultracold Fermi gases such as those by Taylor (2010), Enss (2011), and Hofmann (2011). The static shear viscosity agrees with kinetic theory results from Massignan (2005), Bruun (2005), and Schäfer (2012), but the static bulk viscosity disagrees with kinetic theory predictions by Dusling (2013) and Chafin (2013). The results enable experimental extraction of the bulk viscosity spectral function via periodic modulation of the scattering length, as described by Fujii (2018), and call for future higher-order virial expansion studies to resolve the discrepancy with kinetic theory.
