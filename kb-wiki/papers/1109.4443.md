# Charged-Current and Neutral-Current Coherent Pion Productions --- Theoretical Status

> 4 pages, 2 figures, Contribution to NUFACT 11, XIIIth International Workshop on Neutrino Factories, Super beams and Beta beams, 1-6 August 2011, CERN and University of Geneva

# Charged-Current and Neutral-Current Coherent Pion Productions, Theoretical Status

## Key claim
The theoretical status of charged-current (CC) and neutral-current (NC) coherent pion production is reviewed, with the central finding that recent microscopic dynamical models and nuclear PCAC-based approaches give fairly similar cross sections, but these disagree significantly with Monte Carlo codes based on the Rein-Sehgal model and with experimental data. The abstract glosses over the fact that the validity of the widely-used Rein-Sehgal model is questionable for neutrino energies below about 2 GeV and for light nuclei such as carbon-12, and that a major discrepancy remains between all recent theoretical calculations and the latest experimental ratio of CC to NC coherent pion cross sections.

## Method
The paper reviews two main theoretical approaches: PCAC-based models, which relate the coherent pion amplitude to elastic pion-nucleus scattering via the partially conserved axial current, and dynamical microscopic models, which combine elementary neutrino-induced pion production amplitudes off a single nucleon, nuclear medium effects on delta propagation, and pion-nucleus final state interactions. The author compares predictions from these approaches with each other and with experimental data from recent neutrino experiments, including SciBooNE, and examines the eta-distribution used in NC data analyses.

## Key numbers
- The Rein-Sehgal model corrected for finite muon mass gives a 25% reduction of the CC coherent pion cross section for neutrino energy of 1.3 GeV.
- The nuclear PCAC approach yields a reduction of coherent pion cross sections by approximately a factor of 1/2 compared to the original Rein-Sehgal model.
- The Goldberger-Treiman value for the axial form factor C5^A(0) is 1.2; some models require this value to be 20% smaller to fit neutrino data.
- The latest SciBooNE data give the ratio of CC coherent pion to NC coherent pion cross sections as sigma_CC/sigma_NC = 0.14^{+0.30}_{-0.28}, while all recent theoretical calculations give sigma_CC/sigma_NC = 1.5 to 2.

## Context
This review extends the comparison of coherent pion production models initiated at the NUINT09 workshop (Boyd, 2009), where cross sections from many theoretical models and Monte Carlo codes were compared. The paper contrasts the Rein-Sehgal model (Rein and Sehgal, 1983) and its corrections (Rein and Sehgal, 1999; Hernandez et al., 2007) with nuclear PCAC approaches (Kartavtsev, 2006; Paschos, 2009) and microscopic dynamical models (Kelkar, 1997; Singh, 1998; Amaro, 2009; Martini, 2009; Nakamura, 2010; Hernandez, 2010). The work enables re-analysis of existing neutrino oscillation data, particularly for nu_e appearance events where NC coherent pi0 production can fake the signal, using more realistic coherent pion models rather than the Rein-Sehgal model.
