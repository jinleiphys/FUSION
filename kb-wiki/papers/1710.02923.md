# Green's function method for the single-particle resonances in a deformed Dirac equation

> Single-particle resonances are crucial for exotic nuclei near and beyond the drip lines. Since the majority of nuclei are deformed, the interplay between deformation and orbital structure near threshold becomes very important and can lead to an improved description of exotic nuclei. In this work, the Green's function (GF) method is applied to solve the coupled-channel Dirac equation with quadrupole-deformed Woods-Saxon potentials for the first time. The detailed formalism for the partial-wave expansion of the Green's function is presented. A new approach getting exact values for energies and widths of resonant states by the GF method is proposed. Numerical checks are carried out by comparing with our previous implementation of the spherical GF method and the results from the deformed complex momentum representation~(CMR), the analytical continuation of the coupling constant (ACCC), and the scattering phase shift (SPS) methods, and it is proved that the GF method is very effective and reliably for describing resonance states, no matter they are narrow or broad, spherical or deformed. Finally, Nilsson levels for bound and resonant orbitals in the halo candidate nucleus $^{37}$Mg are calculated from the deformed GF method over a wide range of deformations and some decisive hints of $p$-wave halo formation are shown in this nucleus, namely, the crossing between the configurations $1/2[321]$ and $5/2[312]$ at deformation parameter $β>0.5$ may enhance the probability to occupy the $1/2[321]$ orbital that is originated from the $2p_{3/2}$ shell.

## Key claim

The Green's function (GF) method is applied to solve the coupled-channel Dirac equation with quadrupole-deformed Woods-Saxon potentials for the first time, and a new approach to extract exact values for energies and widths of resonant states by scanning the complex energy plane is proposed; the method is claimed to be effective for both narrow and broad resonances, whether spherical or deformed, but the numerical validation is limited to a specific parameter set and a single nucleus (³⁷Mg).

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Dirac equation for axially quadrupole-deformed Woods-Saxon potentials by expanding the Dirac spinor in spherical spinor harmonics and constructing the radial Green's function from two linearly independent solutions integrated inward from infinity and outward from the origin. The density of states is computed from the imaginary part of the Green's function, and resonant energies and widths are extracted by scanning the complex energy plane to locate poles where the density of states exhibits an extreme. The method is benchmarked against spherical GF results, the complex scaling method (CSM), the complex momentum representation (CMR), the analytical continuation of the coupling constant (ACCC), and the scattering phase shift (SPS) method.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical resonant state \(1f_{5/2}\) at deformation \(\beta=0\) with \(N=1\) partial wave, the GF method gives energy \(E=3.8512\) MeV and width \(\Gamma/2=0.4819\) MeV, exactly matching the CSM result.
- For the spherical resonant state \(2p_{1/2}\) at \(\beta=0\) with \(N=1\), the GF method gives \(E=0.3427\) MeV and \(\Gamma/2=0.2537\) MeV, exactly matching the CSM result.
- For the spherical resonant state \(1g_{9/2}\) at \(\beta=0\) with \(N=1\), the GF method gives \(E=9.4863\) MeV and \(\Gamma/2=1.6715\) MeV, compared to the CSM result of \(E=9.4864\) MeV and \(\Gamma/2=1.6715\) MeV.
- The energy step used in the density-of-states calculation is \(d\varepsilon = 1\times10^{-4}\) MeV, yielding an accuracy for energies and widths up to \(0.1\) keV.
- The infinitesimal imaginary part \(\epsilon\) in the density-of-states formula is taken as \(1\times10^{-6}\) MeV.
- The radial mesh step is \(0.1\) fm with a box cutoff at \(R_{\rm box}=20\) fm.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters are \(S_{\rm WS}=-420.3\) MeV, \(V_{\rm WS}=349.7\) MeV, radius \(R=3.705\) fm, and diffuseness \(a=0.67\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the Green's function method for the Dirac equation from spherical systems (Sun et al., 2014) to deformed systems, enabling the treatment of single-particle resonances in axially deformed nuclei. It provides a foundation for developing a fully self-consistent deformed continuum covariant density functional theory that treats pairing, deformation, and the continuum on the same footing. The method is validated against the complex scaling method (CSM) of Guo et al., the complex momentum representation (CMR) of Li et al. (2016), the analytical continuation of the coupling constant (ACCC) method, and the scattering phase shift (SPS) method of Li et al. (2010), and is applied to the halo candidate nucleus ³⁷Mg, where a crossing between configurations \(1/2[321]\) and \(5/2[312]\) at deformation \(\beta>0.5\) is identified as a possible signature of \(p\)-wave halo formation.
