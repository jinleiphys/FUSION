# Relaxation Time Approximation for a multi-species relativistic gas

> We generalize a recent prescription for the relaxation time approximation for the relativistic Boltzmann equation for systems with multiple particle species at finite temperature. This is performed by adding counter-terms to the traditional Anderson-Witting ansatz for each particle species. Our approach allows for the use of momentum-dependent relaxation times and the obedience of local conservation laws regardless of the definition of the local equilibrium state. As an application, we derive the first order Chapman-Enskog corrections to the equilibrium distribution and display results for the hadron-resonance gas. We also demonstrate that our collision term ansatz obeys the second law of thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors generalize a recent relaxation time approximation (RTA) prescription for the relativistic Boltzmann equation to systems with multiple particle species, adding counter-terms to the traditional Anderson-Witting ansatz so that local conservation laws are obeyed regardless of the definition of the local equilibrium state and even when momentum-dependent relaxation times are used.

## Method

The authors construct a novel RTA collision term for a chargeless multi-species relativistic gas by adding projection operators onto the conserved four-momentum basis to the standard Anderson-Witting ansatz, ensuring the collision term respects the zero-eigenvalue property of the linearized collision operator for energy-momentum. They then perform a first-order Chapman-Enskog expansion to derive the non-equilibrium correction to the single-particle distribution function, expanding the deviation function in a complete basis of irreducible tensors and orthogonal polynomials. The resulting solution is applied to the hadron-resonance gas (HRG) to compute the first-order corrections for a power-law parametrization of the relaxation time with particle energy.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound squared is given by \(c_s^2 = \partial P_0 / \partial \varepsilon_0 = \mathcal{J}_{3,1} / \mathcal{J}_{3,0}\), where \(\mathcal{J}_{n,q}\) are thermodynamic integrals defined in Eqs. (17)-(20) of the paper.
- The entropy density satisfies \(\varepsilon_0 + P_0 = T s_0 = T \partial P_0 / \partial T = \mathcal{J}_{3,1} / T\).
- The first-order Chapman-Enskog solution for the shear sector reduces to a simple extension of traditional RTA results, while the bulk sector contains a novel contribution proportional to the particle energy (Eqs. (52), (53), and (54) in the paper).

## Context

This work extends the single-species RTA prescription of Rocha and Denicol (2021) to multi-species systems, fixing the fundamental flaws of the traditional Anderson-Witting RTA (Anderson and Witting, 1974) which violates energy-momentum conservation when momentum-dependent relaxation times are used or when the local equilibrium state is not defined via Landau matching. The results enable improved particlization models for heavy-ion collision simulations, where the non-equilibrium correction to the hadron-resonance gas distribution function is needed for the Cooper-Frye method (Cooper and Frye, 1974), and the approach is consistent with the second law of thermodynamics as demonstrated by the authors.
