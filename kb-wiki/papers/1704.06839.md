# Charge redistribution from novel magneto-vorticity coupling in anomalous hydrodynamics

> We discuss new transport phenomena in the presence of both a strong magnetic field and a vortex field. Their interplay induces a charge distribution and a current along the magnetic field. We show that the associated transport coefficients can be obtained from a simple analysis of the single-particle distribution functions and also from the Kubo formula calculation. The consistent results from these analyses suggest that the transport coefficients are tied to the chiral anomaly in the (1+1) dimension because of the dimensional reduction in the lowest Landau levels.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the interplay between a strong magnetic field and a vortex field in a chiral fluid induces a vector-charge redistribution and an axial current along the magnetic field, with the associated transport coefficients being tied to the chiral anomaly in (1+1) dimensions due to dimensional reduction in the lowest Landau levels, and these coefficients are independent of temperature and chemical potential.

## Method

The authors computed the density and current of chiral fermions in the lowest Landau level (LLL) by expanding the equilibrium distribution function with respect to the energy shift from spin-vorticity coupling, using the linear dispersion relation of LLL fermions. They also performed a Kubo formula calculation of the linear response, relating the retarded correlator between the energy-momentum tensor (perturbed by the vortex field) and the induced current to the anomaly diagram in (1+1) dimensions.

## Key numbers

* The induced vector-charge density is \(\Delta n_{V} = q_f \frac{C_A}{2} (\mathbf{B} \cdot \boldsymbol{\omega})\), with \(C_A = 1/(2\pi^2)\).
* The induced axial current is \(\Delta j^{3}_{A} = |q_f| \frac{C_A}{2} (\mathbf{B} \cdot \boldsymbol{\omega}) \hat{B}\), with the same anomaly coefficient \(C_A = 1/(2\pi^2)\).
* The density shift is independent of temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\) because \(f_0(0) + \bar{f}_0(0) = 1\) identically.
* The higher Landau level states are gapped with a large interval \(\sim \sqrt{2 q_f B}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of anomalous transport in chiral fluids beyond the separate effects of magnetic fields (chiral magnetic effect) and vorticity (chiral vortical effects), which were previously treated as first-order in gradient expansion by Son and Surowka (2009). The authors note that in magnetohydrodynamics, the magnetic field is not screened, requiring treatment beyond conventional gradient expansion. This enables the prediction of new charge redistribution and current phenomena in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, where both strong magnetic fields and fluid vorticity are present.
