# $D$-dimensions Dirac fermions BEC-BCS cross-over thermodynamics

> An effective Proca Lagrangian action is used to address the vector condensation Lorentz violation effects on the equation of state of the strongly interacting fermions system. The interior quantum fluctuation effects are incorporated as an external field approximation indirectly through a fictive generalized Thomson Problem counterterm background. The general analytical formulas for the $d$-dimensions thermodynamics are given near the unitary limit region. In the non-relativistic limit for $d=3$, the universal dimensionless coefficient $ξ={4}/{9}$ and energy gap $Δ/ε_f ={5}/{18}$ are reasonably consistent with the existed theoretical and experimental results. In the unitary limit for $d=2$ and T=0, the universal coefficient can even approach the extreme occasion $ξ=0$ corresponding to the infinite effective fermion mass $m^*=\infty$ which can be mapped to the strongly coupled two-dimensions electrons and is quite similar to the three-dimensions Bose-Einstein Condensation of ideal boson gas. Instead, for $d=1$, the universal coefficient $ξ$ is negative, implying the non-existence of phase transition from superfluidity to normal state. The solutions manifest the quantum Ising universal class characteristic of the strongly coupled unitary fermions gas.

# D-dimensions Dirac fermions BEC-BCS cross-over thermodynamics

## Key claim

The paper claims that the universal dimensionless coefficient ξ and energy gap Δ/ε_f for the BEC-BCS crossover of strongly interacting fermions can be derived analytically using an effective Proca Lagrangian with a fictive Thomson Problem counterterm background, yielding results consistent with Monte Carlo simulations and experiments, but only in the non-relativistic limit for d=3, and with the caveat that the method relies on an external field approximation that incorporates quantum fluctuations indirectly rather than through a fully self-consistent many-body treatment.

## Method

The author constructs an effective relativistic quantum field theory with Dirac fermions coupled to a massive Proca vector field, where the vector boson mass squared contains both a bare term and a fluctuating term representing many-body renormalization effects. The interior quantum fluctuation/correlation effects are incorporated as an external field approximation through a fictive generalized Thomson Problem counterterm background, which introduces a negative Debye mass squared determined by the gauge invariant static infrared limit of the vector boson polarization tensor. General analytical formulas for the energy density functional, pressure, and entropy density are derived for d-dimensions near the unitary limit using the relativistic Hartree approximation and density functional theory formalism, with the universal coefficients obtained by Taylor series expansion in the non-relativistic limit.

## Key numbers

- For d=3 in the non-relativistic limit: universal coefficient ξ = 4/9 (approximately 0.44), energy gap Δ/ε_f = 5/18 (approximately 0.278)
- For d=3: universal expansion coefficients ζ = 5π/18 (approximately 0.87) and v = π²/12 (approximately 0.82) for the energy density expansion in 1/(k_f a)
- For d=3 unitary limit: critical temperature T_c ≈ 0.157 T_f from the BCS relation T_c = e^γ Δ/π
- For d=3 unitary limit: pressure ratio P = (1/6) P_FG, sound speed v = (√2/6) v_f, ratio of pressure to energy density = 1/4
- For d=2 at T=0: universal coefficient ξ = 0, corresponding to infinite effective fermion mass m* = ∞
- For d=1: universal coefficient ξ is negative, implying no phase transition from superfluidity to normal state
- General formula for d-dimensions non-relativistic: ξ = (d-2)(d+1)/d², Δ/ε_f = (d-2)(d+2)/(2d²)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous relativistic continuum field theory approach (Chen 2006) to arbitrary spatial dimensions, building on the observation that the unitary limit problem shares infrared singularities with hot and dense gauge field theory. The results for d=3 (ξ=4/9, Δ/ε_f=5/18) are consistent with Monte Carlo calculations by Astrakharchik (2004) and the effective theory power counting of Steele, as well as recent quantum Monte Carlo results (2006). The d=2 solution (ξ=0, m*→∞) maps to strongly coupled two-dimensional electrons discussed by Zhang (2005) and resembles three-dimensional BEC of an ideal boson gas, while the d=1 negative ξ aligns with the general theorem that one-dimensional systems cannot sustain phase transitions. The approach enables analytical treatment of the unitary limit beyond mean-field theory and perturbative expansions, with the d-dimensional results manifesting quantum Ising universal class characteristics of strongly coupled unitary fermion gases.
