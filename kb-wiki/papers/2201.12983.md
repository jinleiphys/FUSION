# Global calculation of two-neutrino double-$β$ decay within the finite amplitude method in nuclear density functional theory

> Two-neutrino double-beta ($2νββ$) decay has been used to constrain the neutron-proton part of effective interactions, which in turn is used to compute the nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay, the observation of which would have important consequences for fundamental physics. We carefully examine $2νββ$ matrix elements within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation with nuclear energy density functionals. We work with functionals that are fit globally to single-beta-decay half-lives and charge-exchange giant-resonance energies, but not to $2νββ$ half-lives themselves, to evaluate the $2νββ$ nuclear matrix elements for all important nuclei, including those whose half-lives have not yet been measured. Such a comprehensive evaluation in large model spaces without configuration truncation requires an efficient computational scheme; we employ a double contour integration within the finite amplitude method. The results generally reproduce the nuclear matrix element extracted from half-lives well, without the use of any of those half-lives in the fitting procedure. We present predictions of the matrix elements in a total of 27 nuclei with half-lives that are still unmeasured.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that the finite amplitude method (FAM) within nuclear density functional theory can efficiently compute two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements for all relevant nuclei without configuration truncation, and that globally fitted energy density functionals (not adjusted to reproduce measured half-lives) generally reproduce the matrix elements extracted from experimental half-lives well.

## Method
The authors use the proton-neutron finite amplitude method (pnFAM) with a double contour integration technique to compute the sum over intermediate states in the two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix element, avoiding explicit construction of the large QRPA matrix. They employ the SkM* Skyrme functional with volume-type pairing, fitting pairing strengths to experimental odd-even staggering, and use the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov solver hfbtho with 20 harmonic-oscillator major shells in a cylindrical basis. The overlap between intermediate states from initial and final nuclei is handled using the quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff approximation (QTDA) with α=0.

## Key numbers
- 27 nuclei with unmeasured half-lives have predicted matrix elements
- 11 nuclei with measured half-lives are used for comparison
- 20 harmonic-oscillator major shells included, corresponding to 1771 single-particle states for neutrons and protons
- 257,686 K=0 two-quasiparticle states and 256,025 K=±1 two-quasiparticle states in the pnFAM
- Integration up to 120 MeV reproduces more than 99.9% of the Ikeda sum rule in all nuclei studied
- 202 discretization points (n_r=202) used on each contour with convergence parameter γ=5
- Q values computed: 4.05 MeV for ⁷⁶Ge→⁷⁶Se, 4.98 MeV for ¹³⁰Te→¹³⁰Xe, 5.55 MeV for ¹³⁶Xe→¹³⁶Ba, 2.68 MeV for ¹⁵⁰Nd→¹⁵⁰Sm
- HFB overlap values: 0.803 for ⁷⁶Ge, 0.133 for ¹³⁰Te, 0.378 for ¹³⁶Xe, 0.400 for ¹⁵⁰Nd

## Context
This work extends the pnFAM approach previously developed for beta-decay rates and Gamow-Teller strength distributions (Hinohara and Engel, 2014) to compute two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements. It builds on the deformed-nucleus pnQRPA calculations with Skyrme EDFs by Mustonen and Engel (2013) that computed matrix elements in several experimentally important isotopes, and on the global optimization of time-odd terms in EDFs using single-beta-decay rates and giant-resonance energies (Mustonen et al., 2016). The method enables comprehensive evaluation without configuration truncation, allowing predictions for 27 nuclei with unmeasured half-lives and providing a pathway to more reliable neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element calculations.
