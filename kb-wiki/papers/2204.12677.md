# Simulating fusion reactions from Coulomb explosions within a transport approach

> We have studied nuclear fusion reactions from the Coulomb explosion of deuterium clusters induced by high-intensity laser beams within a transport approach. By incorporating the D+D $\rightarrow$ n + He$^3$ channel as inelastic collisions based on the stochastic method, we have calibrated the neutron yield from the simulation in a box system with that from the reaction rate equation. After justifying the Coulomb explosion of a single cluster by comparing results with available theoretical limits, we have then investigated the dynamics from Coulomb explosions of systems with different cluster numbers and different deuteron numbers in clusters. We find that the kinetic energy spectrum of deuterons at the final stage is different from that when neutrons are abundantly produced, corresponding to significantly different reaction rates. We also extrapolate the neutron yield result from small systems to large systems based on an intuitive parameterized form and compare with the available experimental result. The present framework can be extended by incorporating more channels, and useful for further studies of nuclear fusion reactions in plasma systems at higher energies reached in more recent experiments.

## Key claim

The authors show that the kinetic energy spectrum of deuterons from Coulomb explosions of deuterium clusters is different from a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, and that the neutron yield from D+D fusion depends nonlinearly on cluster size and number, with the reaction rate being significantly lower than that predicted by assuming thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors used the EPOCH particle-in-cell (PIC) transport code to simulate the Coulomb explosion of deuterium clusters after complete electron stripping by a high-intensity laser. They incorporated the D+D → n+³He fusion channel as inelastic collisions using a stochastic method, calibrating the neutron yield against the reaction rate equation in a uniform box system. They then simulated Coulomb explosions of 8, 27, and 64 deuterium clusters with varying deuteron numbers per cluster, including a log-normal distribution of cluster sizes, and extrapolated neutron yields to larger systems using a parameterized fit.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron number density inside clusters: 4.9 × 10¹⁰ μm⁻³
- Average deuteron number per cluster in default case: 11392
- Cluster radius for default case: 0.0038 μm
- Distance between neighboring clusters: 0.056 μm
- Time step for reaction probability evaluation: 0.002 fs
- Cell volume for reaction probability evaluation: 0.01 × 0.01 × 0.01 μm³
- Gamov constant for D+D reactions: 31.3970 √(keV)
- Reaction rate for thermal equilibrium at 50 keV: about 10⁻²⁰ μm³ fs⁻¹
- Reaction rate from kinetic energy distribution for N=64 clusters: 1.359 × 10⁻²⁴ μm³ fs⁻¹, compared to 3.023 × 10⁻²⁴ μm³ fs⁻¹ from a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution at k_BT = 2 keV
- Reaction rate at t=75 fs for N=64: about 4.404 × 10⁻²⁵ μm³ fs⁻¹, about 1/3 of that at t=125 fs
- Fit exponent B in N_n/N = A N^B: about 0.42 for both fixed N_c and log-normal distribution
- Fit coefficient A: 9.2 × 10⁻¹⁴ for fixed N_c = 11392; 13.4 × 10⁻¹⁴ for log-normal distribution of N_c

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb explosion model of Ditmire et al. (1999) and Zweiback et al. (2000, 2002) by using a transport approach rather than assuming thermal equilibrium as in Bang et al. (2013) and Baraffe et al. (2013). The authors show that the non-equilibrium dynamics leads to different reaction rates than Maxwell-Boltzmann-based calculations, and provide a framework that can be extended to incorporate more reaction channels for studying fusion reactions in plasma systems at higher energies from more recent experiments.
