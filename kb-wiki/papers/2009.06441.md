# Limiting masses and radii of neutron stars and their implications

> We combine equation of state of dense matter up to twice nuclear saturation density ($n_{\rm sat}=0.16\, \text{fm}^{-3}$) obtained using chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT), and recent observations of neutron stars to gain insights about the high-density matter encountered in their cores. A key element in our study is the recent Bayesian analysis of correlated EFT truncation errors based on order-by-order calculations up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in the $χ$EFT expansion. We refine the bounds on the maximum mass imposed by causality at high densities, and provide stringent limits on the maximum and minimum radii of $\sim1.4\,{\rm M}_{\odot}$ and $\sim2.0\,{\rm M}_{\odot}$ stars. Including $χ$EFT predictions from $n_{\rm sat}$ to $2\,n_{\rm sat}$ reduces the permitted ranges of the radius of a $1.4\,{\rm M}_{\odot}$ star, $R_{1.4}$, by $\sim3.5\, \text{km}$. If observations indicate $R_{1.4}<11.2\, \text{km}$, our study implies that either the squared speed of sound $c^2_{s}>1/2$ for densities above $2\,n_{\rm sat}$, or that $χ$EFT breaks down below $2\,n_{\rm sat}$. We also comment on the nature of the secondary compact object in GW190814 with mass $\simeq 2.6\,{\rm M}_{\odot}$, and discuss the implications of massive neutron stars $>2.1 \,{\rm M}_{\odot}\,(2.6\,{\rm M}_{\odot})$ in future radio and gravitational-wave searches. Some form of strongly interacting matter with $c^2_{s}>0.35\, (0.55)$ must be realized in the cores of such massive neutron stars. In the absence of phase transitions below $2\,n_{\rm sat}$, the small tidal deformability inferred from GW170817 lends support for the relatively small pressure predicted by $χ$EFT for the baryon density $n_{\rm B}$ in the range $1-2\,n_{\rm sat}$. Together they imply that the rapid stiffening required to support a high maximum mass should occur only when $n_{\rm B} \gtrsim 1.5-1.8\,n_{\rm sat}$.

## Key claim

The paper combines chiral effective field theory (χEFT) predictions of the equation of state up to twice nuclear saturation density with observational constraints to refine bounds on neutron star masses and radii, but the results depend on the assumption that χEFT remains valid up to 2 n_sat and that no phase transitions occur below that density.

## Method

The authors constructed the equation of state for charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated neutron-star matter using a quadratic expansion in isospin asymmetry based on Bayesian uncertainty-quantified χEFT calculations at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter. They matched this low-density EOS to a constant sound-speed EOS above a matching density n_mat (typically 1.5-2.0 n_sat) and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations, exploring both causal (c_s^2=1) and subluminal sound speeds.

## Key numbers

- The χEFT predictions from n_sat to 2 n_sat reduce the permitted ranges of the radius of a 1.4 M_sun star by approximately 3.5 km
- Upper bound on R_1.4 is about 12.9 km when matching at n_mat=2.0 n_sat, and about 13.6 km when matching at n_mat=1.5 n_sat
- For a maximum mass of 2.0 M_sun, the minimum radius R_min(1.4 M_sun)=8.2 km and R_max=8.4 km
- To 2-sigma confidence, the minimum radius of a 1.6 M_sun star ranges from 9.2-12.2 km as M_max varies from 2.00 M_sun to 2.93 M_sun
- If observations indicate R_1.4 < 11.2 km, then either the squared speed of sound c_s^2 > 1/2 for densities above 2 n_sat, or χEFT breaks down below 2 n_sat
- For massive neutron stars >2.1 M_sun (2.6 M_sun), strongly interacting matter with c_s^2 > 0.35 (0.55) must be realized in their cores
- The rapid stiffening required to support a high maximum mass should occur only when n_B is greater than approximately 1.5-1.8 n_sat

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Lattimer (2000) that showed radii of 1.4 M_sun neutron stars correlate with pressure in the density range 1-3 n_sat, and by the BUQEYE collaboration (Drischler 2020) that introduced Bayesian uncertainty quantification for χEFT truncation errors. The results complement studies of the secondary compact object in GW190814 with mass 2.6 M_sun by Tan (2020), Lim (2020), Tews (2020), and others, and provide a framework for interpreting future NICER observations of massive pulsars like PSR J0740+6620 and radio observations with the Square Kilometre Array.
