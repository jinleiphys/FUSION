# Does the light and broad sigma(500) exist?

> The lightest scalar and pseudoscalar nonets are discussed within the framework of the broken U3$\times$U3 linear sigma model, and it is shown that already at the tree level this model works remarkably well predicting scalar masses and couplings not far from present experimental values, when all parameters are fixed from the pseudoscalar masses and decay constants. It is argued that this strongly suggests that the light and very broad $σ$ resonance exists near 500 MeV.

# Does the light and broad sigma(500) exist?

## Key claim
The linear U3×U3 sigma model at tree level predicts scalar masses and couplings close to experimental values when all parameters are fixed from pseudoscalar masses and decay constants, which strongly suggests that the light and very broad σ resonance exists near 500 MeV. However, the author notes that no single analysis of the ππ S-wave alone could decide on the nature of the σ, and the decision about its existence cannot be made using ππ S-wave data alone due to model-dependent ambiguities in analytic continuation to a pole far from the physical region.

## Method
The author fixes five of six parameters of the broken U3×U3 linear sigma model Lagrangian using the experimental values of mπ, mK, m²η+m²η', fπ=92.42 MeV, and fK=113 MeV, leaving the λ' parameter free. Tree-level scalar masses and trilinear couplings are then computed from the Lagrangian and compared with experimental values for the σ(500), a0(980), f0(980), and K*0(1430) mesons.

## Key numbers
- λ = 11.57, μ̄² = 0.1424 GeV², c = -1701 MeV, u = d = 65.35 MeV, s = 94.45 MeV (tree-level parameters fixed from pseudoscalar data)
- Predicted mσ = 651 MeV (λ'=1) or 619 MeV (λ'=3.75), compared to experimental range 400-1200 MeV
- Predicted ma0 = 1028 MeV, compared to experimental 983 MeV
- Predicted mκ = 1123 MeV, compared to experimental 1430 MeV
- Predicted mf0 = 1229 MeV (λ'=1) or 1188 MeV (λ'=3.75), compared to experimental 980 MeV
- σ → ππ coupling sum ∑g²/4π = 2.17 GeV² (model) versus 1.95 GeV² (experiment from Achasov)
- σ pole positions from multiple analyses range from approximately 370-i356 MeV to 870-i370 MeV, with the author's own previous result at 470-i250 MeV

## Context
This work extends the linear sigma model approach of Scadron and collaborators, which had few phenomenological applications despite being over 30 years old. The author argues that the naive quark model (NQM) fails for the scalars because it lacks chiral symmetry, which is crucial for understanding the σ and f0 since they have the same quantum numbers as the vacuum. The results suggest that the σ(500), a0(980), f0(980), and K*0(1430) belong to the same nonet as chiral partners of the π, η, K, and η', and that unitarization along the lines of the author's earlier unitarized quark model (UQM) would enable more detailed data comparison.
