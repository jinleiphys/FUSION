# Distinct ground state features and the decay chains of Z = 121 Superheavy Nuclei

> A fully systematic study of even and odd isotopes (281 $\leq$ A $\leq$ 380) of Z = 121 superheavy nuclei is presented in theoretical frameworks of Relativistic mean-field plus state dependent BCS approach and Macroscopic-Microscopic approach with triaxially deformed Nilson Strutinsky prescription. The ground state properties namely shell correction, binding energy, two- and one- proton and neutron separation energy, shape, deformation, density profile and the radius are estimated that show strong evidence for magicity in N = 164, 228. Central depletion in the charge density due to large repulsive Coulomb field indicating bubble-like structure is reported. A comprehensive analysis of the possible decay modes specifically $α$-decay and spontaneous fission (SF) is presented and the probable $α$-decay chains are evaluated. Results are compared with FRDM calculations and the available experimental data which show excellent agreement.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutron numbers N = 164 and N = 228 are new magic numbers in Z = 121 superheavy nuclei, based on shell correction minima, zero deformation, and separation energy discontinuities, but notes that N = 164 lies beyond the proton drip line with negative separation energy, qualifying its magicity as occurring in an unbound region.

## Method

The authors employed two theoretical frameworks: (i) Relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with a state-dependent BCS pairing approach using a delta-force interaction, solved via expansion on an axially deformed harmonic-oscillator basis with quadrupole constrained calculations for potential energy surfaces; and (ii) a macroscopic-microscopic approach using the triaxially deformed Nilsson-Strutinsky method (NSM) with shell corrections added to a liquid-drop macroscopic energy. Ground state properties (binding energy, separation energy, shell correction, deformation, shape, charge density) were computed for even and odd isotopes of Z = 121 with mass numbers A = 281 to 380, and alpha-decay half-lives were calculated using multiple empirical formulas (Viola-Seaborg, Brown, Sobiczewski, universal decay law, unified formula, and Royer formula) alongside spontaneous fission half-lives using the formula of Xu et al.

## Key numbers

- Shell correction shows a deep minimum of approximately 12 MeV at N = 228 (A = 349) and a shallower minimum of approximately 5.28 MeV at N = 164 (A = 285), indicating magicity.
- The first one-proton unbound nuclei in Z = 121 are predicted at A = 304 (N = 183) from RMF and at A = 292 (N = 171) from NSM; the first two-proton unbound nuclei are at A = 301 (N = 180) from RMF and A = 297 (N = 176) from NSM.
- For the decay chain of 293121, the Q_alpha value is 12.63 MeV and the alpha-decay half-life from the Viola-Seaborg formula is 3.74 x 10^-04 seconds, with spontaneous fission half-life of 1.22 x 10^+22 seconds, indicating alpha decay as the dominant mode.
- For 294121, the Q_alpha value is 12.73 MeV and the alpha-decay half-life from the Viola-Seaborg formula is 5.01 x 10^-04 seconds, with spontaneous fission half-life of 6.14 x 10^+22 seconds.
- The average binding energy per nucleon (B.E./A) for the isotopes between A = 293 and 312 is approximately 7.2 MeV, close to the average for experimentally known nuclei in this region.
- Nuclei with A = 293 to 302 (approximately 10 potential candidates) are found with long alpha-decay chains, while nuclei with A = 303 to 312 are terminated by spontaneous fission after 3, 2, or 1 alpha decays.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of superheavy nuclei beyond Z = 118, building on prior experimental efforts at GSI, RIKEN, and JINR (Hofmann, 2000; Morita, 2007; Oganessian, 2010) and theoretical predictions of shell closures at N = 172, 184, 198, 228, 238, 258 by Moller (1994), Rutz (1996), Cwiok (1996), and Zhang (2004). The results are compared with Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) calculations by Moller (2012) and experimental data from Oganessian (2015), showing good agreement. The identification of alpha-decay chains for Z = 121 isotopes with A = 293-302 provides specific candidates for future experimental synthesis attempts, while the predicted bubble-like structures in charge density extend recent work on central depletion in superheavy nuclei by Berger (2001), Decharge (2003), Afanasjev (2005), and Saxena (2018).
