# Quantum Chaos in A=46--50 Atomic Nuclei

> The spectral statistics of low--lying states of several $fp$ shell nuclei are studied with realistic shell--model calculations. For Ca isotopes, we find significant deviations from the predictions of the random--matrix theory which suggest that some spherical nuclei are not as chaotic in nature as the conventional view assumes.

## Key claim

For low-lying states (up to 6 MeV above the yrast line) of Ca isotopes in the A=46–50 region, realistic shell-model calculations show significant deviations from the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) predictions of random-matrix theory, suggesting that some spherical nuclei are not as chaotic as the conventional view assumes; the degree of chaoticity increases with excitation energy but remains below GOE even at higher energies.

## Method

Exact shell-model calculations were performed in the (f7/2, p3/2, f5/2, p1/2) space assuming a ⁴⁰Ca inert core, using the ANTOINE code with a Lanczos algorithm. Energy spectra for total angular momentum J=0 to J=9 and total isospin T=Tz were computed for ⁴⁶V, ⁴⁶Ti, ⁴⁶Sc, ⁴⁶Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, and ⁵⁰Ca using a minimally modified Kuo-Brown realistic force with monopole improvements. The nearest-neighbor spacing distribution P(s) of unfolded levels was analyzed and fitted to the Brody distribution to extract the chaoticity parameter ω, which interpolates between Poisson (ω=0, regular) and GOE (ω=1, chaotic).

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁶Ca, the Brody parameter ω = 0.72, 0.83, and 0.88 for levels below 12, 16, and 20 MeV, respectively, showing increasing chaoticity with excitation energy.
- For the combined set ⁴⁶V+⁴⁶Ti+⁴⁶Sc up to 6 MeV above the yrast line, the Brody parameter indicates chaotic behavior (near GOE), while for the combined set ⁴⁶Ca+⁴⁸Ca+⁵⁰Ca up to 6 MeV, strong deviations toward regularity are observed.
- The number of J=0–9 spacings below 4, 5, and 6 MeV ranges from 42, 66, and 105 in ⁴⁶Ca to 86, 149, and 231 in ⁴⁶Ti, respectively.
- For the semi-magic nucleus ¹¹⁶Sn, experimental analysis yields ω = 0.51 ± 0.19 for levels below 4.3 MeV excitation energy.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of quantum chaos in nuclei beyond the sd shell region, where previous shell-model calculations (e.g., Ormand and Broglia, 1992) found GOE-like behavior, into the fp shell (A=46–50). The results contradict the prevailing view that spherical nuclei generally show GOE-level chaoticity, and instead support findings by Bae et al. (1995) for heavy single-closed-shell nuclei ²¹²Rn and ²¹²Pb, where regular motion was observed. The work enables systematic investigation of the borderlines in mass number and excitation energy between order and chaos in nuclei, and suggests that the weakness of like-nucleon residual interactions in semi-magic nuclei is responsible for the reduced chaoticity.
