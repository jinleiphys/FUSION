# Low-$Q^{2}$ nucleon structure from an infrared-safe evolution scheme

> The low-$Q^2$ nucleon structure is given with a simple nonperturbative input of three valence quarks and an all-order infrared-safe evolution scheme, showing some consistences with the experimental data. The resonance peaks in the experimental data of $F_2$ structure function are found to be modulated by the valence quark distributions. With little sea quark distribution at low $Q^2$ the valence-quark bump is clearly shown in the structure function. The three valence quark distributions at the hadronic scale is found to be the dominant origin of the PDFs at the hard scales. The high-twist corrections are needed to explain the sizeable discrepancy between the theory and the experimental measurements at low $Q^2$. The infrared-safe evolution scheme is a powerful tool for connecting the nucleon structures in the nonperturbative and perturbative regions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the three valence quarks of the quark model are the dominant origin of parton distribution functions (PDFs) at hard scales, but this conclusion is conditional: the theoretical predictions show sizeable discrepancies with experimental data at low \(Q^2\), and high-twist corrections are needed to explain these discrepancies.

## Method

The author applies an infrared-safe evolution scheme to a nonperturbative input of three valence quark distributions obtained from the maximum entropy method (MEM) at a hadronic scale \(Q_0^2 = 0.0001\) GeV\(^2\). The evolution scheme modifies standard DGLAP equations by including dressed parton mass functions from Continuum Schwinger Methods (CSMs), a saturating running strong coupling \(\alpha_{\rm s}\) that is finite in the infrared, and gluon-gluon recombination corrections. The evolved PDFs are used to compute the proton structure function \(F_2\) at low \(Q^2\), which is compared to JLab experimental data from Armstrong (2001).

## Key numbers

* The saturating strong coupling \(\alpha_{\rm s}\) reaches \(\alpha_{\rm s}/\pi \sim 0.97\) in the infrared limit.
* The QCD scale parameter used in the saturating \(\alpha_{\rm s}\) parametrization is \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.234\) GeV.
* The hadronic scale for the input valence quark distributions is set to \(Q_0^2 = 0.0001\) GeV\(^2\).
* The evolution is found to be insensitive to the choice of input scale as long as \(Q_0^2 \lesssim 0.1\) GeV\(^2\).
* The \(F_2\) structure function is computed and compared to JLab data at low \(Q^2\) scales (0.001, 0.01, 0.1, 1, 10, and 100 GeV\(^2\) are shown in one figure).

## Context

This work extends the infrared-safe evolution scheme proposed by Wang (2024) and Yin (2023) to study the origin of PDFs, using nonperturbative input from the maximum entropy method (Wang, 2014). It tests the scheme against JLab data from Armstrong (2001) and finds that while resonance peaks in the data are modulated by valence quark distributions, the factorization theorem is violated at low \(Q^2\), requiring high-twist corrections. The results enable future studies of the transition from nonperturbative to perturbative QCD, particularly at facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), the Electron-ion collider in China (EicC), and the JLab 24 GeV upgrade.
