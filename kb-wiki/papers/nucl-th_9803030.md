# Analysis of chiral and thermal susceptibilities

> We calculate the chiral and thermal susceptibilities for two confining Dyson-Schwinger equation models of QCD with two light flavours, a quantitative analysis of which yields the critical exponents, beta and delta, that characterise the second-order chiral symmetry restoration transition. The method itself is of interest, minimising the influence of numerical noise in the calculation of the order parameter for chiral symmetry breaking near the critical temperature. For the more realistic of the two models we find: T_c approx. 153 MeV, and the non-mean-field values: beta = 0.46 +/- 0.04, delta = 4.3 +/- 0.3 and 1/(beta delta)= 0.54 +/- 0.05, which we discuss in comparison with the results of other models.

## Key claim

The chiral symmetry restoration transition in two confining Dyson-Schwinger equation models of QCD with two light flavours is second-order, but the critical exponents obtained from the more realistic ultraviolet-improved model (beta = 0.46 ± 0.04, delta = 4.3 ± 0.3) are non-mean-field and differ significantly from those of the N=4 Heisenberg magnet, contradicting the expectation that QCD with two light flavours belongs to that universality class.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation at finite temperature for two model gluon propagators: an infrared-dominant model with a contact interaction and an ultraviolet-improved model with a Debye-mass term and a mass-scale parameter m_t = 0.69 GeV fixed by pi- and rho-meson properties. They computed the chiral and thermal susceptibilities (derivatives of the order parameter B(p=0,omega_0) with respect to explicit chiral symmetry breaking mass and temperature, respectively) for a range of small explicit symmetry breaking parameters h = m/T, located the pseudocritical temperatures from the susceptibility peaks, and extracted the critical exponents beta and delta from the scaling of peak heights with h.

## Key numbers

* For the ultraviolet-improved model: critical temperature T_c ≈ 153 MeV
* For the ultraviolet-improved model: critical exponent beta = 0.46 ± 0.04
* For the ultraviolet-improved model: critical exponent delta = 4.3 ± 0.3
* For the ultraviolet-improved model: 1/(beta delta) = 0.54 ± 0.05
* For the infrared-dominant model: mean-field exponents beta = 0.5, delta = 3.0, T_c = 0.159155 × eta with eta ≈ 1.06 GeV, giving T_c ≈ 169 MeV
* For comparison: N=4 Heisenberg magnet exponents from the literature: beta^H = 0.38 ± 0.01, delta^H = 4.82 ± 0.05
* For comparison: lattice QCD results from Karsch and Laermann (1994) on an 8^3 × 4 lattice: beta^lat = 0.30 ± 0.08, delta^lat = 4.3 ± 0.5

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to finite temperature QCD previously developed by the authors (Roberts and Williams, 1994; Pichowsky, 1996; Ivanov, 1997) and applies the susceptibility method to determine critical exponents, which is more accurate near the critical temperature than direct analysis of the order parameter. The results contradict the conjecture by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) that two-flavour QCD belongs to the same universality class as the N=4 Heisenberg magnet, and differ from lattice results by Karsch and Laermann (1994) for beta while agreeing for delta. The method enables future study of more realistic Dyson-Schwinger equation models with proper renormalisation group behaviour, such as that of Maris and Roberts (1997).
