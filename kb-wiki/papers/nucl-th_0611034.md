# Study of nucleon resonances with electromagnetic interactions

> Recent developments in using electromagnetic meson production reactions to study the structure of nucleon resonances are reviewed. Possible future works are discussed.

## Key claim
Electromagnetic meson production reactions provide the primary experimental tool for extracting nucleon resonance (N*) parameters, but the extraction of resonance photocouplings from data is model-dependent due to ambiguities in non-resonant background treatments and coupled-channel effects, as demonstrated by systematic differences between single-channel and coupled-channel analyses.

## Method
The authors review experimental developments in electromagnetic meson production reactions (π, η, 2π, ω, KΛ, KΣ channels) using data from CW electron accelerators at BATES, JLAB, and MAMI, with polarized beams, targets, and polarimeters. Partial-wave analyses using Unitary Isobar Models (UIM), dynamical coupled-channel models (Sato-Lee, Giessen group), and isobar models (MAID, DMT, JLAB-MSU) are applied to extract resonance transition form factors and photocoupling amplitudes from exclusive electroproduction data.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic dipole transition form factor G*_M for γ*N → Δ(1232) measured over Q² = 0.06–6.0 GeV², confirming rapid Q² falloff relative to elastic dipole G_d
- Quadrupole ratios R_EM = E_{1+}/M_{1+} and R_SM = S_{1+}/M_{1+} for Δ(1232) measured at Q² = 0.2 GeV² from CLAS data at E = 1.046 GeV
- Roper resonance (P₁₁(1440)) photocoupling A^p_{1/2} shows zero-crossing confirmed by CLAS06 data at higher Q² (1.72–4.16 GeV²)
- Helicity amplitudes for S₁₁(1535): A^p_{1/2} = 92 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (Giessen coupled-channel) vs 30 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (SAID single-channel)
- Helicity amplitudes for D₁₃(1520): A^p_{3/2} = 145 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (Giessen) vs 135 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (SAID)
- Helicity amplitudes for F₁₅(1680): A^p_{3/2} = 116 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (Giessen) vs 129 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (SAID)
- Helicity amplitudes for P₃₃(1232): A^p_{1/2} = -128 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2}, A^p_{3/2} = -253 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} (Giessen)
- 16 neutral pion electroproduction response functions measured at Q² = 1.0 GeV² by JLAB/Hall A, with 12 observed for the first time
- S₁₁(1535) branching fractions assumed: β_{πN} = 0.4, β_{ηN} = 0.55, Γ_{tot} = 150 MeV

## Context
This review extends the earlier survey by Burkert and Lee (2004) and summarizes results from the CLAS, Hall A, MAMI, and BATES experimental programs. The work contrasts single-channel analyses (SAID by Arndt et al., 1996) with coupled-channel approaches (Giessen group by Shklyar et al., 2005; Sato-Lee dynamical model, 1996, 2001) to highlight model dependence in resonance parameter extraction. The results enable future studies of "missing" resonances in the 2π channel above W = 1.8 GeV and motivate improved lattice QCD calculations (Alexandrou et al., 2005) with proper chiral extrapolation as demonstrated by Pascalutsa and Vanderhaeghen (2006).
