# Projected Linear Response Theory for Charge-Exchange Excitations and Double Beta Decay

> The projected random phase approximation (PRPA) for charge-exchange excitations is derived from the time-dependent variational principle. Explicit results for the unperturbed energies (including the self-energy corrections), the PRPA matrices, and the transition matrix elements are presented. The effect of the projection procedure on the two-neutrino $ββ$ decay in $^{76}Ge$ is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The projected random phase approximation (PRPA) and the standard quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) yield qualitatively similar results for two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements, despite the PRPA incorporating particle-number projection; the abrupt variation of the decay amplitude as a function of the particle-particle interaction strength persists in both approaches.

## Method

The authors derive the PRPA for charge-exchange excitations from the time-dependent variational principle, using a canonical transformation that introduces complex variables \(z_p\) and \(z_n\) to generate particle-number-projected states. The PRPA matrices and transition amplitudes are expressed in terms of contour integrals over these variables, and the formalism is applied to the \(0^+\rightarrow 0^+\) two-neutrino double beta decay transition in \(^{76}\text{Ge}\) using single-particle energies from neighboring odd-odd nuclei, neglecting self-energy corrections.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutrino double beta decay amplitude \(\mathcal{M}_{2\nu}\) for \(^{76}\text{Ge} \rightarrow {^{76}\text{Se}}\) is fitted by a \((1,1)\)-Padé approximant \(\mathcal{M}_{2\nu} \cong \mathcal{M}_{2\nu}(t=0) \frac{1-t/t_0}{1-t/t_1}\), where \(t = v^{pp}_t / v^{pair}_s\) is the ratio of spin-triplet particle-particle strength to spin-singlet pairing strength.
- The parameters \(t_0\) and \(t_1\) denote the zero and pole of \(\mathcal{M}_{2\nu}\), respectively, and are treated as free parameters; the specific numerical values for \(^{76}\text{Ge}\) are listed in a table in the paper (table not reproduced in the truncated text).
- The PRPA excitation energies \(E_\mu(\alpha J)\) for \(\mu = \pm 1\) describe transitions to odd-odd nuclei \((Z\pm 1, N\mp 1)\), with both positive and negative energy solutions being physically meaningful.
- For \(J^\pi = 0^+\) and \(1^+\), the transition amplitudes satisfy the sum rule \(\sum_{E_{\mu=1}(\alpha J)>0} |\Lambda_\mu(\alpha J)|^2 - \sum_{E_{\mu=-1}(\alpha J)>0} |\Lambda_{-\mu}(\alpha J)|^2 = (2J+1)(N-Z)\).

## Context

This work extends the QRPA treatment of double beta decay by incorporating particle-number projection, building on prior efforts by Faessler and collaborators (Tubingen group, 1990s) who developed a different PRPA formalism. The authors contrast their approach with that of the Tubingen group, noting differences in the treatment of the RPA equations (single vs. two separate equations), the definition of the \(A\) and \(B\) matrices, and the handling of spurious components. The results enable more rigorous tests of nuclear structure models for double beta decay, particularly for assessing the sensitivity of decay amplitudes to the particle-particle interaction strength.
