# Radiative energy loss of neighboring subjets

> We compute the in-medium energy loss probability distribution of two neighboring subjets at leading order, in the large-$N_c$ approximation. Our result exhibits a gradual onset of color decoherence of the system and accounts for two expected limiting cases. When the angular separation is smaller than the characteristic angle for medium-induced radiation, the two-pronged substructure lose energy coherently as a single color charge, namely that of the parent parton. At large angular separation the two subjets lose energy independently. Our result is a first step towards quantifying effects of energy loss as a result of the fluctuation of the multi-parton jet substructure and therefore goes beyond the standard approach to jet quenching based on single parton energy loss. We briefly discuss applications to jet observables in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper computes the in-medium radiative energy loss probability distribution for two neighboring subjets at leading order in the large-Nc approximation, showing a gradual onset of color decoherence that interpolates between coherent energy loss (when the angular separation is smaller than the characteristic medium-induced radiation angle) and independent energy loss (at large angular separation). The result is a first step beyond the standard single-parton energy loss approach to jet quenching, but is restricted to the regime where the dipole forms instantaneously, the radiated gluons are very soft, and overlapping formation times are neglected.

## Method

The authors derive the two-prong energy loss probability by resumming multiple soft primary medium-induced gluon emissions off a color dipole (quark-antiquark pair) with proper treatment of interferences, working in the large-Nc limit and using the quasi-instantaneous emission approximation where formation times are much smaller than the medium length. They compute the direct and interference spectra for medium-induced radiation, incorporate color decoherence via a decoherence parameter that depends on the jet quenching parameter, the opening angle, and the medium path length, and then generalize the result from a color singlet antenna to arbitrary color representations.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic color decoherence time is t_decoh ~ (q_hat * theta_12^2)^{-1/3}, where q_hat is the jet transport coefficient and theta_12 is the angular separation between subjets.
- The characteristic medium-induced formation time is t_form ~ sqrt(omega/q_hat), and the corresponding radiation angle is theta_f ~ (q_hat/omega^3)^{1/4}.
- The characteristic frequency of medium-induced radiation is omega_c = q_hat * L^2, where L is the medium length.
- The soft energy scale governing the single-parton energy loss probability is omega_s = alpha_bar^2 * omega_c, where alpha_bar = alpha_s * C_F/pi.
- The decoherence parameter for a homogeneous medium is Delta_med(t) = 1 - exp[-(1/12) * q_hat * theta_12^2 * t^3].
- The single-parton energy loss probability is ProbOne(epsilon) = sqrt(2*omega_s/epsilon^3) * exp(-2*pi*omega_s/epsilon).

## Context

This work extends the single-parton quenching weight formalism of Baier (2001) to two-parton systems, addressing the problem of how energy is collectively removed from a color dipole formed early in the medium. It confirms earlier expectations from Casalderrey-Solana (2011) and Mehtar-Tani (2011, 2012) about the role of finite medium resolution power, and provides a novel tool for studying jet observables in heavy-ion collisions that goes beyond the standard approach based on single parton energy loss. The result enables future calculations of medium modifications to jet substructure observables, particularly for two-pronged configurations that can be identified using jet substructure techniques.
