# Determining the $Θ^+$ quantum numbers through the $K^+p\to π^+K^+n$ reaction

> We study the $K^+p\to π^+K^+n$ reaction with some kinematics suited to the production of the $Θ^+$ resonance recently observed. We show that, independently of the quantum numbers of the $Θ^+$, a resonance signal is always observed in the $K^+$ forward direction. In addition, we also show how a combined consideration of the strength at the peak, and the angular dependence of polarization observables can help determine the $Θ^+$ quantum numbers using the present reaction.

## Key claim

The authors show that in the $K^+p\to \pi^+K^+n$ reaction, a resonance signal from the $\Theta^+$ is always observed in the $K^+$ forward direction regardless of the $\Theta^+$ quantum numbers, but that a combined analysis of the peak strength and the angular dependence of polarization observables can determine the spin, parity, and isospin of the $\Theta^+$, with the caveat that the unpolarized angular dependence alone is too weak to discriminate among cases.

## Method

The authors model the $K^+p\to \pi^+K^+n$ reaction using chiral Lagrangians, including pion-pole, contact, and $\rho$-exchange diagrams, and then incorporate the $\Theta^+$ resonance through final-state interaction in the $K^+n$ system. They evaluate the double differential cross section for an initial kaon momentum of 850 MeV/c in the laboratory frame, choosing kinematics where the final pion momentum is small to suppress spin-nonflip contributions. They compute the cross section for three possible $\Theta^+$ quantum number assignments ($J^P = 1/2^-$, $1/2^+$, $3/2^+$ with isospin $I=0$ or $1$) and also analyze a polarized cross section where the initial proton spin is aligned with the beam and the final neutron spin is opposite.

## Key numbers

- The initial kaon three-momentum in the laboratory frame is $k_{in}(Lab)=850$ MeV/c, corresponding to $\sqrt{s}=1722$ MeV, allowing coverage of $K^+n$ invariant masses up to $M_I=1580$ MeV.
- The $\Theta^+$ resonance mass and width used in the calculations are $M_R=1540$ MeV and $\Gamma=20$ MeV.
- The three-momentum cutoff used to regularize the loop function $G(M_I)$ is $q_{max}=630$ MeV/c, taken from prior $\bar{K}N$ scattering work.
- The strong form factor at the $MMB$ vertex uses a monopole form with $\Lambda \sim 900$ MeV.
- For a typical polarization rate of 80%, the polarized cross section for the $I,J^P=0,1/2^+$ case decreases by about 10% relative to the 100% polarized case.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of hadron-induced and photon-induced reactions for the $\Theta^+$ resonance, which was recently observed by the LEPS collaboration (Nakano, 2003) and confirmed by DIANA (Barmin, 2003), CLAS (Stepanyan, 2003), and SAPHIR (Barth, 2003). The $\Theta^+$ was predicted by Diakonov et al. (1997) using the chiral quark soliton model with quantum numbers $(I,J^P)=(0,1/2^+)$, but alternative theoretical studies (Capstick, 2003; Stancu, 2003; Karliner, 2003; Jaffe, 2003) and lattice simulations (Csikor, 2003) have considered other possibilities. The reaction $K^+p\to \pi^+K^+n$ is proposed as a clean alternative to photo-induced reactions, building on a model by Oset (1996) for the same reaction without the $\Theta^+$ resonance. The results enable a concrete experimental program to determine the $\Theta^+$ quantum numbers, particularly through the polarized cross section measurement at 90 degrees.
