# Algebraic treatment of the hyper-Coulomb problem

## Key claim

The paper presents a completely algebraic solution of the six-dimensional hyper-Coulomb problem using the dynamical group SO(7,2), claiming that the hyper-Coulomb potential provides a good lowest-order approximation for baryon phenomenology, but only when the linear confining term is not too strong and its effects can be treated perturbatively.

## Method

The authors construct an oscillator realization of the SO(7,2) dynamical algebra in terms of seven boson creation and annihilation operators, using the complementarity between SO(2,1) and SO(6) within a single irreducible representation of SO(7,2). The energy spectrum is obtained by expressing the Schrodinger equation in terms of SO(2,1) generators and applying a tilting rotation to diagonalize the compact generator T3 for bound states. Form factors are derived algebraically by expressing coordinate operators as linear combinations of SO(7,2) generators and evaluating representation matrix elements of the discrete series D+ of SO(2,1).

## Key numbers

* Energy spectrum: E = -mu tau^2 / (2 n^2), where n = omega + 5/2, with omega = 0, 1, 2, ...
* Ground state principal quantum number: n_0 = 5/2
* Elastic form factor for the six-dimensional hyper-Coulomb potential: F_0(k) = 1 / (1 + (25/(16 mu^2 tau^2)) k^2)^(7/2)
* General elastic form factor for m-dimensional hyper-Coulomb potential: F_0(k) = 1 / (1 + ((m-1)^2/(16 mu^2 tau^2)) k^2)^((m+1)/2)
* Ground state expectation value of exp(-i beta L_78): (cosh(beta/2))^(-2n_0) = (cosh(beta/2))^(-5)

## Context

This work extends the algebraic treatment of the hydrogen atom using the SO(4,2) dynamical group (Barut) to the six-dimensional hyper-Coulomb problem relevant for three-body systems, particularly constituent quark models of baryons. The hyper-Coulomb potential provides an alternative to the six-dimensional harmonic oscillator (the other exactly solvable case), with the key difference that hyper-Coulomb form factors decrease as a power law in momentum transfer rather than as a Gaussian (CKO). The authors apply their results to nucleon form factors, comparing with experimental data and with complete calculations from FGPS that include a linear confining term, finding comparable quality for the N(1520)D13 and N(1535)S11 resonances.
