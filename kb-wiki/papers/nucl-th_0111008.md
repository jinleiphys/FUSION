# Statistical Fluctuations as Probes of Dense Matter

> The use of statistical fluctuations as probes of the microscopic dynamics of hot and dense hadronic matter is reviewed. Critical fluctuations near the critical point of QCD matter are predicted to enhance fluctuations in pionic observables. Chemical fluctuations, especially those of locally conserved quantum numbers, such as electric charge and baryon number, can probe the nature of the carriers of these quantum numbers in the dense medium.

# Statistical Fluctuations as Probes of Dense Matter

## Key claim
Statistical fluctuations in hadronic observables from relativistic heavy-ion collisions can preserve information about the microscopic dynamics of hot and dense matter from earlier stages of the collision, even though their averages only reflect freeze-out conditions, provided the system has finite volume and finite lifetime. The survival of these fluctuations depends critically on the rapid expansion of the fireball and the diffusion rates of conserved quantum numbers.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical relations connecting statistical fluctuations in a subvolume to thermodynamic susceptibilities and heat capacity, then applies these to two classes of observables: thermal fluctuations (mean transverse momentum fluctuations) and chemical fluctuations (net charge and baryon number fluctuations). For chemical fluctuations, the analysis compares predictions for a weakly coupled hadron gas versus a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma, and examines the survival of fluctuations through the hadronic phase using transport theory and Langevin dynamics for rapidity diffusion.

## Key numbers
- For fluctuations from the sigma field near the critical point: $F_\sigma - 1 \approx 0.14 (\xi_\sigma / 6\ \text{fm})^2$, where $\xi_\sigma$ is the correlation length.
- NA49 reported $F = 1.004 \pm 0.004$ or $\Phi_p = 0.6 \pm 1\ \text{MeV}$ for Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN-SPS after corrections for two-particle correlations.
- STAR reported $\Phi_p \approx 35\ \text{MeV}$ for Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
- For a weakly interacting QGP, the ratio of baryon number to charge fluctuations is $\langle \Delta B^2 \rangle_{\rm QGP} / \langle \Delta Q^2 \rangle_{\rm QGP} = 2/5$.
- The typical longitudinal velocity of a baryon in the hadronic phase is $\bar{v}_z = (8T/\pi M)^{1/2} \approx 0.32c$.
- Shuryak and Stephanov estimated minimal rapidity intervals for survival of fluctuations: $\Delta y_{\rm diff}^{(\pi)} \approx 2.2$ for charge fluctuations and $\Delta y_{\rm diff}^{(N)} \approx 0.9$ for baryon number fluctuations.
- For net charge fluctuations, an uncorrelated thermal pion gas gives $\tilde{D} = 4$; a weakly interacting QGP gives $\tilde{D} \approx 1$.
- STAR reported $\tilde{D} \approx 3$ for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130\ \text{GeV}$ in pseudorapidity window $|\Delta\eta| < 0.7$.
- NA49 found $\tilde{D}$ slightly in excess of 4 for Pb+Pb collisions at 40, 80, and 158 GeV/u beam energy, independent of pseudorapidity interval $0.3 < \Delta\eta < 3.5$.

## Context
This review extends the thermal model approach to hadron production in nuclear collisions (Rafelski, Braun-Munzinger, Heiselberg, Cleymans, Floratos) by focusing on fluctuations rather than averages. It builds on earlier work by Heiselberg on statistical fluctuations and by Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak on critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point. The paper connects to lattice QCD results for quark number susceptibilities (Gottlieb, 1987) and provides a framework for interpreting experimental data from NA49 and STAR, while identifying net baryon number fluctuation measurements as a remaining experimental challenge. The work enables future studies of balance functions (Bass, Danielewicz, Pratt, 2000) and other differential fluctuation observables at RHIC and LHC energies.
