# Magnetohydrodynamics and charged currents in heavy ion collisions

> The hot QCD matter produced in any heavy ion collision with a nonzero impact parameter is produced within a strong magnetic field. We study the imprint the magnetic fields produced in non-central heavy ion collisions leave on the azimuthal distributions and correlations of the produced charged hadrons. The magnetic field is time-dependent and the medium is expanding, which leads to the induction of charged currents due to the combination of Faraday and Hall effects. We find that these currents result in a charge-dependent directed flow $v_1$ that is odd in rapidity and odd under charge exchange. It can be detected by measuring correlations between the directed flow of charged hadrons at different rapidities, $\langle v_1^\pm (y_1) v_1^\pm (y_2) \rangle$.

## Key claim

The magnetic fields produced in non-central heavy ion collisions induce electric currents in the quark-gluon plasma via Faraday and Hall effects, resulting in a charge-dependent directed flow \(v_1\) that is odd in rapidity and odd under charge exchange; however, the relative strength of the Faraday versus Hall effects depends on transverse momentum and rapidity, with the Hall effect sometimes dominating at low \(p_T\) and small rapidity at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors solved Maxwell's equations for the electromagnetic fields produced by the charged spectators and participants in non-central heavy ion collisions, assuming a constant electrical conductivity \(\sigma = 0.023~\text{fm}^{-1}\) and a uniform proton distribution within a sphere of radius \(R\). They modeled the expanding quark-gluon plasma using Gubser's analytic solution to relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for a conformal fluid with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\), then computed the total velocity of charged fluid elements by solving the Lorentz force equation with a drag term in the local fluid rest frame. Finally, they applied the Cooper-Frye freezeout procedure at \(T_f = 130~\text{MeV}\) to obtain the hadron spectra and extract the directed flow \(v_1\) for pions.

## Key numbers

- The initial magnetic field strength at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=200~\text{AGeV}\)) with impact parameter \(b=4~\text{fm}\) is \(e|\vec B|/m_\pi^2 \approx 1\text{--}3\) at about 0.1-0.2 fm/\(c\) after the collision.
- The initial magnetic field strength at LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=2.76~\text{ATeV}\)) with impact parameter \(b=4~\text{fm}\) is \(e|\vec B|/m_\pi^2 \approx 10\text{--}15\) at some earlier time.
- The drag coefficient used is \(\mu m = 6.8 T^2\) for a 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda=6\pi\) and temperature \(T=255~\text{MeV}\).
- The freezeout temperature is set to \(T_f = 130~\text{MeV}\) for both RHIC and LHC collisions.
- The electric conductivity is \(\sigma = 0.023~\text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The classical treatment is validated by comparing magnetic energy to single photon energy: the ratio varies from \(\sim 1000\) to \(\sim 50\) as proper time \(\tau\) increases from 0.3 fm to 0.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of magnetic field effects in heavy ion collisions by Kharzeev (2007), Skokov (2009), Tuchin (2010-2013), Voronyuk (2011), Deng (2012), and McLerran (2013), who estimated the magnetic field strengths but did not compute the resulting charge-dependent directed flow. The paper provides a framework for extracting the electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma and the initial magnetic field strength from experimental measurements of charge-dependent flow correlations, building on the hydrodynamic description of Gubser (2010) and the Cooper-Frye freezeout formalism (1974). The proposed correlation observables are analogous to those measured with high precision by the STAR collaboration (Abelev 2009).
