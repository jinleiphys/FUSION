# Systematic study of unfavored $\mathcalα$ decay half-lives of closed shell nuclei related to ground and isomeric states

> In present work, the unfavored $\mathcalα$ decay half-lives and $\mathcalα$ preformation probabilities of closed shell nuclei related to ground and isomeric states around $Z=82$, $N=82$ and 126 shell closures are investigated by adopting two-potential approach from the perspective of valence nucleon (hole) and isospin asymmetry of the parent nucleus. The results indicate that $\mathcalα$ preformation probability is linear dependence on $N_p N_n$ or $N_p N_n I$, the same as the case of favored $\mathcalα$ decay in our previous work [X.-D. Sun \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. C 94, 024338 (2016)]. $N_p$, $N_n$, and $I$ represent the number of valence protons (holes), the number of valence neutrons (holes), and the isospin of the parent nucleus, respectively. Fitting the $\mathrmα$ preformation probabilities data extracted from the differences between experimental data and calculated half-lives without a shell correction, we give two linear formulas of the $\mathcalα$ preformation probabilities and the values of corresponding parameters. Based on the formulas and corresponding parameters, we calculate the $\mathrmα$ decay half-lives for those nuclei. The calculated results can well reproduce the experimental data.

# Systematic study of unfavored α decay half-lives of closed shell nuclei related to ground and isomeric states

## Key claim

For unfavored α decay of closed shell nuclei around Z=82, N=82 and 126, the α preformation probability Pα is linearly dependent on the product of valence protons and valence neutrons (NpNn) or on NpNn times the isospin asymmetry I, extending the same linear relationship previously found only for favored α decay in Sun et al. (2016). The linear formulas reproduce experimental half-lives well, but the linear fit is notably better for nuclei around the Z=82, N=126 doubly magic core than for those around the Z=82, N=82 core, which the authors attribute to the N=82 core being unbound.

## Method

The authors used the two-potential approach (TPA) to calculate α decay half-lives, with a cosh-parametrized nuclear potential whose depth and diffuseness parameters (a0=0.5958 fm, V0=192.42+31.059(Nd-Zd)/Ad MeV) were previously fitted to 164 even-even nuclei. For each nucleus, they first extracted the α preformation probability Pα as P0 times the ratio of the calculated half-life (assuming a constant P0) to the experimental half-life, using P0=0.35 for odd-A nuclei and P0=0.18 for doubly-odd nuclei. They then fitted linear formulas Pα = a(NpNn)/(Z0+N0) + b and Pα = c NpNn I + d separately for odd-A and doubly-odd nuclei in three regions around the shell closures, and used those formulas to compute predicted half-lives.

## Context

This work extends the linear NpNn relationship for α preformation probabilities, previously established for even-even nuclei by Seif et al. (2011) and for favored α decay of odd-A and doubly-odd nuclei by Sun et al. (2016), to the case of unfavored α decay (where the α particle carries nonzero orbital angular momentum l). The study covers ground and isomeric states around the Z=82, N=82 and N=126 shell closures, treating both states in a unified way as in the authors' prior work. The results enable systematic predictions of unfavored α decay half-lives for closed-shell nuclei and suggest that the valence proton-neutron interaction remains the dominant factor determining α preformation even when the decay is hindered by angular momentum mismatch.
