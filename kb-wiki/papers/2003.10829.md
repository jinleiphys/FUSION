# Sensitivity of the Mean Field Dynamics to the Spatial Distribution in Nuclear One-body Dissipations

> Started from the static excited finite atomic nucleus, we have simulated the dynamical propagation of the nucleons using Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (QMD) without the collision term and calculated the Largest Lyapunov Exponent (LLE). The sensitivity of the nonlinear mean field dynamics to the initial conditions, in particular to the initial spatial distribution of the nucleons in certain thermodynamical stable condition, has been confirmed numerically by calculating the LLE defined in the event space. Comparatively, the Lyapunov-like exponent defined in phase space represents more clearly the sensitivity of the nonlinear dynamics to the mechanical stability of the initial conditions within the nuclear reaction time at intermediate energies. These conclusions are not only helpful for us to understand more deeply the mechanism of the nuclear reaction dynamics at intermediate energies, but it is also beneficial for clarifying further the concrete performances of deterministic chaos in the heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper numerically confirms that the nonlinear mean-field dynamics of an excited finite nucleus is sensitive to the initial spatial distribution of nucleons under certain thermodynamically stable conditions, but the Lyapunov-like exponent defined in phase space more clearly reflects this sensitivity to mechanical stability within the nuclear reaction time at intermediate energies than the Largest Lyapunov Exponent defined in event space.

## Method

The authors initialized a finite nucleus (²⁰⁸Pb) with nucleon positions sampled uniformly in a Fermi sphere and momenta sampled from a Fermi-Dirac distribution at a given temperature and density. They then simulated the dynamical propagation of nucleons using the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model without the collision term, employing a Skyrme-type mean field. The Largest Lyapunov Exponent (LLE) was calculated from the divergence of trajectories in event space, and a Lyapunov-like exponent was defined in phase space to measure the system's expansion.

## Key numbers

- The initial densities used for the system at temperature T = 6 MeV ranged from ρ(0) = 0.02 fm⁻³ to ρ(0) = 0.14 fm⁻³, with the saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The initial distance between two events for LLE calculation was set to ‖d(0)‖ ≤ 10⁻⁷.
- For the initial density ρ(0) = 0.04 fm⁻³ at T = 6 MeV (mechanical unstable region), the Lyapunov-like exponent s(t) was positive within the nuclear reaction time scale (t ≤ 300 fm/c).
- For the initial density ρ(0) = 0.14 fm⁻³ at the same temperature, s(t) was negative in the same time range.
- The averaged density profile was plotted at time t = 800 fm/c for initial densities ρ(0) = 0.11 fm⁻³ and ρ(0) = 0.06 fm⁻³ at T = 6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of deterministic chaos in nuclear one-body dissipation, building on prior calculations of the Largest Lyapunov Exponent in hot nuclear matter by the same group (Refs. 35, 36) and earlier work on chaos in nuclear reactions (Refs. 38-41). The paper introduces a Lyapunov-like exponent defined in phase space to better capture the sensitivity of the mean-field dynamics to mechanical stability, which may help clarify the chaotic mechanism of multifragmentation and the role of one-body dissipation in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies.
