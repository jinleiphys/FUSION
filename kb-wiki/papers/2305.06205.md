# Constraining the chirally motivated $πΣ$-$\bar{K}N$ models with the $πΣ$ photoproduction mass spectra

> The paper presents a first time attempt on a combined fit of the $K^{-}p$ low-energy data and the $πΣ$ photoproduction mass spectra, performed without fixing the meson-baryon rescattering amplitudes to a specific $πΣ- \bar{K}N$ coupled channels model obtained from fitting exclusively the $K^{-}p$ data. The formalism adopted to describe the photoproduction process is based on chiral perturbation theory and employs a limited number of free parameters. The achieved description of the photoproduction mass distributions is not quite satisfactory, leaving a room for improving the photo-kernel construction, but still provides additional constraints on the positions of the $Λ(1405)$ poles. In particular, the presented models tend to limit the mass of the lower pole and yield a larger width of the $\bar{K}N$ related pole at a higher mass.

# Constraining the chirally motivated πΣ-¯KN models with the πΣ photoproduction mass spectra

## Key claim
The combined fit of K⁻p low-energy data and CLAS πΣ photoproduction mass spectra provides additional constraints on the Λ(1405) pole positions, but the description of the photoproduction mass distributions is not quite satisfactory, leaving room for improving the photo-kernel construction. The models tend to limit the mass of the lower pole and yield a larger width of the ¯KN-related pole at a higher mass.

## Method
The authors performed a combined fit of the Prague meson-baryon coupled-channel model parameters to both K⁻p reaction data (kaonic hydrogen characteristics, threshold branching ratios, and low-energy total cross sections to eight MB channels) and CLAS πΣ photoproduction mass spectra at √s = 2.1 GeV. The photoproduction formalism is based on chiral perturbation theory with tree-level amplitudes constructed from 16 M-structures representing Weinberg-Tomozawa, Born, and anomalous graphs, and includes additional form factors with adjustable regularization scales βc and βK⁺ to modify the photoproduction kernel and regulate the intermediate meson-baryon loop function.

## Key numbers
- The total number of experimental data points fitted is 252, comprising 34 (π⁰Σ⁰), 30 (π⁻Σ⁺), and 32 (π⁺Σ⁻) CLAS mass distribution points, plus K⁻p threshold and cross-section data
- The P0 model (with fixed meson-baryon amplitudes from Bruns (2021)) achieved χ²/dof = 5.40, while the P1 global minimum achieved χ²/dof = 3.34, P2 achieved 4.41, and P3 achieved 4.72
- The Λ(1405) lower pole z₁ positions: P0 at (1353, -43) MeV, P2 at (1347, -71) MeV, P3 at (1345, -58) MeV; the P1 model does not generate this pole
- The Λ(1405) higher pole z₂ positions: P0 at (1428, -24) MeV, P1 at (1421, -43) MeV, P2 at (1425, -46) MeV, P3 at (1425, -45) MeV
- The Λ(1670) pole z₃ positions: P0 at (1677, -14) MeV, P2 at (1725, -57) MeV, P3 at (1665, -7.1) MeV; the P1 model does not generate this pole
- The βπΣ regularization scale was found close to 200 MeV across models, while β¯KN values reached up to 1500 MeV (the preset maximum)
- The βK⁺ scale was approximately 700-800 MeV in models with χ²/dof < 5

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Bruns (2022) which tested several fixed meson-baryon scattering amplitudes against CLAS data, by now allowing the parameters of the Prague model (Bruns 2021) to be refitted to the combined data set. It also incorporates new AMADEUS data (Piscicchia 2022) on π⁰Λ and π⁰Σ⁰ cross sections at very low kaon momenta, which help separate isospin contributions. The results show that including CLAS photoproduction data constrains the Λ(1405) pole positions more tightly than fits to K⁻p data alone, though the description of the π⁻Σ⁺ channel remains problematic. The work enables future improvements to the photoproduction kernel construction and suggests that low-energy K⁻p data alone cannot clearly distinguish between one-pole and two-pole models of the Λ(1405).
