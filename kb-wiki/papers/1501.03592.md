# Superfluid dynamics of 258Fm fission

> Theoretical description of nuclear fission remains one of the major challenges of quantum many-body dynamics. The slow, mostly adiabatic motion through the fission barrier is followed by a fast, non-adiabatic descent of the potential between the fragments. The latter stage is essentially unexplored. However, it is crucial as it generates most of the excitation energy in the fragments. The superfluid dynamics in the latter stage of fission is obtained with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory including BCS dynamical pairing correlations. The fission modes of the 258Fm nucleus are studied. The resulting fission fragment characteristics show a good agreement with experimental data. Quantum shell effects are shown to play a crucial role in the dynamics and formation of the fragments. The importance of quantum fluctuations beyond the independent particle/quasi-particle picture is underlined and qualitatively studied.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic method that incorporates both superfluid dynamics and non-adiabatic effects in the latter stage of fission, showing good agreement with experimental data for 258Fm, but the authors note that beyond-mean-field fluctuations are important and a quantitative description of fragment distributions requires further developments.

## Method

The fission process is divided into two steps: first, a slow adiabatic evolution across the fission barrier is treated with constrained Hartree-Fock plus BCS pairing (CHF+BCS) to find valleys in the potential energy surface; second, the non-adiabatic descent to scission is simulated using time-dependent Hartree-Fock with dynamical BCS pairing correlations (TDHF+BCS). The calculations use the SLy4d Skyrme energy density functional with a constant-G pairing interaction, performed on a Cartesian grid of 88×19.2×19.2 fm³ with 0.8 fm mesh spacing and a time step of 1.5×10⁻²⁴ s.

## Key numbers

- Three fission valleys identified for 258Fm: symmetric compact fragment (scf), symmetric elongated fragment (sef), and asymmetric elongated fragment (aef)
- Times to reach scission: ~2 zs for scf mode, ~5.4 zs for aef mode, and ~3.2 zs for sef mode
- Total kinetic energies (TKE) of fragments: 238 MeV for scf mode, 185 MeV for aef mode, and 163 MeV for sef mode
- Pairing strengths: G_n = 24/N MeV and G_p = 19/Z MeV
- Standard deviation for nucleon number distribution in scf mode: σ_TDRPA = 2.35 compared to σ_TDHF = 1.35

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent mean-field approach of Simenel (2014) for 264Fm fission, which neglected pairing correlations, to include superfluid dynamics applicable across the nuclear chart. The three adiabatic valleys found are similar to those obtained by Bonneau (2006), Staszczak (2009), and Ichikawa (2009) for the same nucleus. The method enables future studies of shell effects at finite temperature and motivates development of beyond-mean-field approaches such as the time-dependent random-phase approximation with pairing correlations.
