# High energy collisions of strongly deformed nuclei: An old idea with a new twist

> UU collisions can provide about 30% larger densities compared to central PbPb ones. New aspect is generation of rather deformed initial states. We show that those can be effectively used to resolve a number of outstanding issues, from corrections to hard processes, elliptic flow (the QGP push issue), and the mechanism of $J/ψ$ suppression. UU collisions are studied by a simple Monte-Carlo model, and it is shown how selecting two control parameters - the number of participant nuclei and deformation - one can select particular geometry of the collision.

# High energy collisions of strongly deformed nuclei: An old idea with a new twist

## Key claim

Uranium-uranium collisions can provide about 30% larger energy densities than central lead-lead collisions, but the more important new aspect is that the deformed initial states allow experimental selection of particular collision geometries by controlling two parameters: the number of participant nucleons and the deformation. The actual gain in total initial density for UU over PbPb is estimated at 1.34, smaller than the factor of 1.8 previously suggested by Braun-Munzinger.

## Method

A simple Monte-Carlo program initializes nucleons inside nuclei modeled as homogeneous ellipsoids with long semi-axis R_l = 8.4 fm and short semi-axis R_s = 6.5 fm (deformation parameter δ ≈ 0.27), then follows their paths through another nucleus. For each event, the participant nucleons are identified, the tensor T_ij = ⟨x_i x_j⟩ in the transverse plane is diagonalized to find eigenvalues R_+^2 and R_-^2, and the participant density n_p = N_part/(π R_+ R_-) and deformation R_+/R_- are computed.

## Key numbers

- Deformation ratio R_l/R_s for uranium: 1.29, derived from δ_U ≈ 0.27
- Participant density gain from deformation alone: factor 1.16 for U relative to spherical nucleus of same A
- Participant density gain for UU over PbPb: factor 1.24
- Total initial density gain for UU over PbPb (including non-linear multiplicity scaling): factor 1.34
- Accessible deformation ratios R_+/R_- in central UU collisions: up to 1.35, while central PbPb collisions remain very spherical
- Ratio R_z/R_t can vary between about 1.3 and 1/1.3, giving a span of 1.7 for disentangling hard process corrections

## Context

This work extends the "old idea" of selecting head-on long-long collisions by triggering on maximal number of participants, previously documented in a memo by Braun-Munzinger (unpublished) who estimated a factor 1.8 gain in energy density for UU over AuAu. The paper proposes new applications beyond density enhancement: using deformed initial states to separate initial-state from final-state QCD corrections to hard processes, to study elliptic flow and the equation of state (the "QGP push" issue) at SPS energies, and to investigate the mechanism of J/ψ suppression via azimuthal dependence. The work enables experimental decoupling of finite-size effects from deformation effects in heavy-ion collisions, particularly relevant for interpreting NA49 elliptic flow data and NA50 J/ψ suppression results presented at Quark Matter 1999.
