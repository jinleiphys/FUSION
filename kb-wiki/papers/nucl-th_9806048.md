# Equation of state of hadronic matter with dibaryons in an effective quark model

> The equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter with the inclusion of non-strange dibaryons is studied. We pay special attention to the existence of a dibaryon condensate at zero temperature. These calculations have been performed in an extended quark-meson coupling model with density-dependent parameters, which takes into account the finite size of nucleons and dibaryons. A first-order phase-transition to pure dibaryon matter has been found. The corresponding critical density is strongly dependent on the value of the dibaryon mass. The density behavior of the nucleon and dibaryon effective masses and confining volumes have also been discussed.

## Key claim

A first-order phase transition from symmetric nuclear matter to pure dibaryon matter is predicted at zero temperature, but the critical density is strongly dependent on the assumed value of the in-vacuum dibaryon mass, which is not experimentally confirmed and is treated as a free parameter of the model.

## Method

The authors extended the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model by including non-strange dibaryons represented as spherical MIT bags confining six quarks, with density-dependent bag parameters. The model was solved in the Mean Field Approximation, using the Zimanyi-Moszkowski hadronic model to relate the bag constant to baryon density via equal effective nucleon mass and pressure conditions. The Gibbs criterion (2μ_N = μ_D and P_nuc = P_dib) was applied to locate the phase transition between pure nuclear matter and pure dibaryon matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, with binding energy per particle ε₀ = 16 MeV (reproduced at Q=0).
- For an assumed dibaryon mass M_D = 1970 MeV, the phase transition occurs at critical pressure P₀ = 8.8 × 10⁻² fm⁻⁴.
- At this pressure, the transition density in nuclear matter is ρ_I = 2.55 ρ₀, and in the dibaryon condensate is ρ_II = 3.18 ρ₀, giving a density discontinuity of 0.63 ρ₀.
- The ratio of thermodynamic compressibility in dibaryon condensate to nuclear matter at ρ_B = 3.5 ρ₀ is 0.706.
- The dibaryon mass is constrained to 1940 MeV < M_D < 2000 MeV to keep the transition density in the range 1.5 ρ₀ to 4 ρ₀.
- Free nucleon bag radius: R₁⁰ = 0.8 fm; dibaryon bag radius at zero density: R₂⁰ = 0.954 fm⁻¹ (note: units as in text).
- Bag parameter at zero density: B⁰ = 0.5546 fm⁻⁴.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988) and the density-dependent parameterization of Aguirre (1997) by including dibaryons as finite-size six-quark bags. The predicted transition density of ρ_D/ρ₀ = 2.55 is consistent with values from hadronic field theory models: 2.87 for nuclear matter in the work of Glendenning (1998) and 2.69 in the work of Kälbermann (1992). The reduced compressibility at high densities could be relevant for the collapse of massive stars.
