# Intermittency and fractal behaviour of charged particles generated using EPOS4 and PYTHIA8 at LHC energies

> Large density fluctuations of charged particles are the promising signatures for exploring the QCD phase transition and critical point in heavy ion collisions. These fluctuations are expected to exhibit fractal and scale invariant behaviour, which is probed using intermittency methodology. Intermittency is the phenomenon of power law growth of the normalized factorial moments ($F_{\rm{q}}$) of the number density distributions over decreasing bin size. This is studied for the charged particles simulated using PYTHIAN and EPOS4 (UrQMD ON/OFF) for Pb Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$ = 5.02 TeV. Scaling behaviour of $F_{\rm{q}}$ are studied as a function of phase space partitioning and second order moments to quantify the particle production nature within the default constraints of the two models. The scaling exponent related to the phase transition and parameters connected to fractal nature obtained for these models are also reported.

## Key claim

The study finds that normalized factorial moments of charged-particle multiplicity distributions in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV simulated with EPOS4 and PYTHIA8 do **not** exhibit the power-law M-scaling expected for intermittent, scale-invariant fluctuations, indicating that the particle production mechanisms in these models are dominated by statistical fluctuations rather than critical or self-similar dynamics. The scaling exponent nu extracted from F-scaling lies between 1.6 and 1.9 across all models and centrality classes, far above the value of approximately 1.3 predicted by Ginzburg-Landau theory for a system at a second-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors analyzed 200,000 PYTHIA8 events and 300,000 EPOS4 events (with UrQMD hadronic afterburner both ON and OFF) for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. Charged particles (pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons) within |eta| <= 0.8 and full azimuth (0 to 2pi) were binned onto a two-dimensional (eta, phi) grid with M bins per dimension (M from 4 to 82), and normalized factorial moments F_q(M) were computed for orders q = 2, 3, 4, 5. The scaling behavior was characterized through M-scaling (F_q vs. M), F-scaling (F_q vs. F_2), and extraction of intermittency indices phi_q, generalized (Renyi) dimensions D_q, and the scaling exponent nu from the relation beta_q proportional to (q-1)^nu.

## Key numbers

*   Scaling exponent nu for all models (PYTHIA8, EPOS4 UrQMD ON, EPOS4 UrQMD OFF) across centralities 0-5%, 5-10%, and 10-20% and transverse momentum ranges 0.4-2.0, 0.4-1.5, and 0.4-1.0 GeV/c lies in the range 1.6 to 1.9.
*   The analysis used 200,000 PYTHIA8 events and 300,000 EPOS4 events for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
*   Normalized factorial moments were calculated for moment orders q = 2, 3, 4, and 5, with the number of bins M ranging from a minimum of 4 to a maximum of 82.
*   The generalized (Renyi) dimensions D_q showed negligible q-dependence within uncertainties, indicating monofractal character rather than multifractal behavior.

## Context

This work extends the methodology of Bialas and Peschanski (1986) for intermittency analysis in high-energy collisions to the latest versions of the EPOS4 and PYTHIA8 event generators, providing a baseline for interpreting experimental data from the LHC. The absence of intermittent scaling contrasts with predictions from Ginzburg-Landau theory (Hwa, 1992) and the SCR model (Hwa, 2011), which predict specific scaling exponents for systems at a second-order QCD phase transition. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from ALICE at the LHC to search for genuine critical fluctuations, and they quantify the extent to which current Monte Carlo models, which lack explicit phase transition physics, reproduce or fail to reproduce the scaling signatures expected near the QCD critical point.
