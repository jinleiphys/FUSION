# Core structure of two-dimensional Fermi gas vortices in the BEC-BCS crossover region

> We report $T=0$ diffusion Monte Carlo results for the ground-state and vortex excitation of unpolarized spin-1/2 fermions in a two-dimensional disk. We investigate how vortex core structure properties behave over the BEC-BCS crossover. We calculate the vortex excitation energy, density profiles, and vortex core properties related to the current. We find a density suppression at the vortex core on the BCS side of the crossover, and a depleted core on the BEC limit. Size-effect dependencies in the disk geometry were carefully studied.

## Key claim

The paper reports diffusion Monte Carlo results showing that the vortex core structure in a two-dimensional Fermi gas evolves from a density suppression on the BCS side to a completely depleted core on the BEC limit, but these findings are conditional on the disk geometry with hard walls and the specific finite-size systems studied (58 to 70 particles), with size-effect dependencies carefully accounted for.

## Method

The authors used fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) at zero temperature to simulate unpolarized spin-1/2 fermions confined in a two-dimensional disk with hard-wall boundary conditions. They employed BCS-type trial wave functions with pairing orbitals constructed from single-particle Bessel-function states, including a Jastrow factor from two-body solutions of the modified Poschl-Teller potential, and optimized variational parameters using stochastic reconfiguration. The vortex excitation was created by coupling single-particle states with angular quantum numbers differing by one, and extrapolated estimators combined VMC and DMC results for density and current operators.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy per particle for the bulk system at interaction strength η=0.00: -2.3740(3) E_FG
- Ground-state energy per particle for the bulk system at η=1.50: 0.3523(2) E_FG
- Vortex excitation energy per particle increases from BCS to BEC side, with values ranging across η from 0.00 to 1.50
- Chemical potential changes sign at η ≈ 0.90 for the ground-state disk system and at η ≈ 0.85 for the vortex state
- Density suppression at the vortex core of approximately 30% at η=1.5, with completely depleted core at η ≤ 0.25
- Surface tension parameter λ_s ranges from 6(2) E_FG k_F^{-1} at η=0.00 to 13(1) E_FG k_F^{-1} at η=1.50
- The effective range parameter k_F r_eff was held constant at 0.006 throughout the simulations

## Context

This work extends previous DMC studies of 2D Fermi gases in bulk periodic systems (Gandolfi et al., 2016) to the disk geometry with hard walls, and is analogous to earlier 3D vortex calculations in a cylinder (Madeira et al., 2016) and in helium-4 (Ortiz, 1995). The results enable future experimental studies of vortex properties in 2D Fermi gases and provide a benchmark for theoretical descriptions of neutron matter vortices in neutron stars, where nuclear pasta phases may confine neutrons to quasi-2D configurations.
