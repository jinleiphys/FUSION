# Cavitation and thermal dilepton production in QGP

> We study the \textit{non-ideal} effects arising due to viscosity (both bulk and shear), equation of state ($ε\neq 3P$) and cavitation on thermal dilepton production from QGP at RHIC energies. We calculate the first order corrections to the dilepton production rates due to shear and bulk viscosities. Ignoring the cavitation can lead to a wrong estimation of dilepton spectra. We show that the shear viscosity can enhance the thermal dilepton spectra whereas the bulk viscosity can suppress it. We present the combined effect of bulk and shear viscosities on the dilepton spectra.

# Cavitation and thermal dilepton production in QGP

## Key claim
The authors show that ignoring cavitation, the point at which longitudinal pressure becomes zero due to viscous effects, can lead to erroneous estimation of thermal dilepton spectra from QGP at RHIC energies. The shear viscosity enhances thermal dilepton spectra while bulk viscosity suppresses it, and these competing effects must be evaluated together with the cavitation time rather than the full cooling time to critical temperature.

## Method
The authors used second-order causal Israel-Stewart relativistic hydrodynamics with one-dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow to model the QGP evolution. They calculated first-order viscous corrections to the dilepton production rate from quark-antiquark annihilation using the 14-moment Grad's method for both shear and bulk viscosity modifications to the distribution functions, and used a lattice QCD-based non-ideal equation of state with parametrized temperature-dependent bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio.

## Key numbers
- At M = 1 GeV, dilepton flux from non-ideal equation of state is about 125% larger than from ideal equation of state
- Initial conditions: initial time τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c, initial temperature T₀ = 310 MeV, nuclear rapidity y_nuc = 5.3
- Critical temperature T_c = 190 MeV
- System reaches T_c at τ_f = 5.5 fm/c, but cavitation occurs earlier at τ_c = 2.5 fm/c at temperature 210 MeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 1/4π (KSS bound)
- Bulk viscosity parametrization: a = 0.901 (height), ΔT = T_c/14.5 (width), b = 0.061
- Equation of state parameters: d₂ = 0.24 GeV², d₄ = 0.0054 GeV⁴, c₁ = 0.2073 GeV, c₂ = 0.0172 GeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of non-ideal effects on thermal photon production (Bhatt 2010) to thermal dileptons, and builds on shear viscosity studies of dilepton production by Dusling (2008) which used ideal equation of state without bulk viscosity. The results enable more accurate interpretation of dilepton measurements at RHIC by showing that cavitation must be accounted for when viscous hydrodynamics is used, and that the competing effects of shear and bulk viscosity on the spectra must be disentangled.
