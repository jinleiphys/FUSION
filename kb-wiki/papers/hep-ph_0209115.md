# Understanding the proton spin "puzzle" with a new "minimal" quark model

> We investigate the spin structure of the nucleon in an extended Jaffe-Lipkin quark model. In addition to the conventional $3q$ structure, different $(3q)(Q\bar{Q})$ admixtures in the nucleon wavefunction are also taken into account. The contributions to the nucleon spin from various components of the nucleon wavefunction are discussed. The effect due to the Melosh-Wigner rotation is also studied. It is shown that the Jaffe-Lipkin term is only important when antiquarks are negatively polarized. We arrive at a new "minimal" quark model, which is close to the naive quark model, in order to understand the proton spin "puzzle".

## Key claim

The authors show that the proton spin "puzzle" can be understood within a quark model framework that is close to the naive SU(6) quark model, provided one includes both the Melosh-Wigner rotation effect (a relativistic correction) and baryon-meson fluctuations in the nucleon wavefunction; the Jaffe-Lipkin term with vector mesons is only necessary when antiquarks are negatively polarized, which is not required by current data.

## Method

The authors extended the Jaffe-Lipkin quark model by including higher Fock states as baryon-meson (BM) fluctuations, where the meson is a pseudoscalar (e.g., pion) rather than a vector meson. They calculated the contributions to the nucleon spin from valence quarks, sea quarks, and orbital angular momentum using mixing angles between the bare three-quark state, the BM state, and the Jaffe-Lipkin state, and then applied the Melosh-Wigner rotation factor to connect the quark spin in the rest frame to the quark helicity measured in polarized deep inelastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- The integrated spin structure function for the proton, Gamma^p = 0.136, and for the neutron, Gamma^n = -0.03, from the SMC, E142, and E143 experiments.
- The Bjorken sum rule gives Delta Q^3 = G_A/G_V = 1.261 from neutron beta decay.
- The flavor SU(3) axial charge Delta Q^8 = 0.675 from hyperon decays.
- In the naive SU(6) model with only three valence quarks and Melosh-Wigner rotation, the required averaged rotation factors are <M_u> = 0.517 and <M_d> = 0.921, giving a ratio r_{d/u} = 1.78.
- Including the baryon-meson fluctuation p = n pi^+ (with sin^2 alpha cos^2 beta = 0.148 from the Gottfried sum rule violation) and no Jaffe-Lipkin term (beta=0) gives <M_u> = 0.598, <M_d> = 0.878, and r_{d/u} = 1.47.
- Including additional BM fluctuations (p = n pi^+ at ~15%, p = Lambda K^+ at ~3%, p = Delta^{++} pi^- at ~1%) gives <M_u> = 0.624, <M_d> = 0.912, and r_{d/u} = 1.46.
- The sum of quark helicities in the naive SU(6) model with Melosh-Wigner rotation is Sigma approx 0.38, far from the naive quark model value of 1.

## Context

This work extends the Jaffe-Lipkin quark model (Jaffe and Lipkin, 1991) by including baryon-meson fluctuations with pseudoscalar mesons, as suggested by Keppler et al. (1995), and by incorporating the Melosh-Wigner rotation effect previously studied by Ma (1991, 1998) and Brodsky (1994). The analysis shows that the proton spin puzzle can be resolved without requiring a large negative sea quark polarization, contrary to the original Jaffe-Lipkin conclusion. This enables a consistent description of polarized deep inelastic scattering data, the Gottfried sum rule violation observed by the NMC collaboration, and hyperon beta decay within a "minimal" quark model that is close to the naive SU(6) picture.
