# Hybrid Stars in the Framework of different NJL Models

> We compute models for the equation of state (EoS) of the matter in the cores of hybrid stars. Hadronic matter is treated in the non-linear relativistic mean-field approximation, and quark matter is modeled by three-flavor local and non-local Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models with repulsive vector interactions. The transition from hadronic to quark matter is constructed by considering either a soft phase transition (Gibbs construction) or a sharp phase transition (Maxwell construction). We find that high-mass neutron stars with masses up to $2.1-2.4 M_\odot$ may contain a mixed phase with hadrons and quarks in their cores, if global charge conservation is imposed via the Gibbs conditions. However, if the Maxwell conditions is considered, the appearance of a pure quark matter core either destabilizes the star immediately (commonly for non-local NJL models) or leads to a very short hybrid star branch in the mass-radius relation (generally for local NJL models).

## Key claim

High-mass neutron stars with masses up to 2.1–2.4 solar masses may contain a mixed phase of hadrons and quarks in their cores, but only if the phase transition is modeled via the Gibbs construction with global charge conservation; under the Maxwell construction, pure quark matter cores either destabilize the star immediately (for non-local NJL models) or produce only a very short hybrid star branch (for local NJL models).

## Method

The authors computed equations of state for hybrid star cores by combining a non-linear relativistic mean-field description of hadronic matter with three-flavor local and non-local Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models including repulsive vector interactions for quark matter. The hadron-to-quark phase transition was constructed using either the Gibbs construction (soft transition, global charge neutrality) or the Maxwell construction (sharp transition, local charge neutrality), and the resulting mass-radius relations were solved from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- High-mass neutron stars with masses up to 2.1–2.4 solar masses may contain a mixed quark-hadron phase under the Gibbs construction.
- For the Gibbs construction, the mixed phase consists of approximately 30–60% quark matter for local NJL models and approximately 30–35% for non-local NJL models, depending on the hadronic and quark matter parameter choices.
- The observed neutron stars J1614-2230 (1.928 ± 0.017 solar masses), J0348+0432 (2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses), and B1957+20 (2.39 +0.36 -0.29 solar masses) are cited as examples that may contain significant quark-hadron matter under the Gibbs construction.
- The surface tension threshold for favoring the Gibbs construction is cited as approximately 5–40 MeV/fm², while values around 50–300 MeV/fm² would favor the Maxwell construction.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Orsaria (2012, 2013), Ranea-Sandoval (2015), and Spinella (2015) that used local and non-local NJL models with vector interactions to describe quark matter in hybrid stars. The paper directly addresses the open question of whether a mixed quark-hadron phase can exist in massive neutron stars, showing that the answer depends critically on the surface tension between hadronic and quark matter and on whether the Gibbs or Maxwell construction is adopted. The results enable future studies of color superconductivity in the quark phase and the use of different hadronic lagrangians, such as density-dependent nuclear field theory, to further constrain hybrid star properties.
