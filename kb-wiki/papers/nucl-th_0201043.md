# Elastic e-d Scattering Data and the Deuteron Wave Function

> What range of momentum components in the deuteron wave function are available e d elastic scattering data sensitive to ? This question is addressed within the context of a model calculation of the deuteron form factors, based on realistic interactions and currents. It is shown that the data on the $A(q)$, $B(q)$, and $T_{20}(q)$ observables at $q \leq 6$ fm$^{-1}$ essentially probe momentum components up to $\simeq 4 m_π$.

## Key claim

The available elastic electron-deuteron scattering data for momentum transfers up to q = 6 fm^-1 are sensitive to deuteron momentum components only up to approximately 4 m_pi, based on a model calculation using realistic interactions and currents treated consistently to order (v/c)^2.

## Method

The authors constructed a model of the deuteron using a relativistic Hamiltonian with a one-pion-exchange potential and short-range Argonne v18 potential, solving the momentum-space Schrodinger equation. They truncated the deuteron S- and D-wave momentum-space components using a smooth cutoff at momenta p_n = n m_pi (n = 1, 2, 4) and calculated the A(q), B(q), and T20(q) observables including both one-body and two-body (pi-exchange, rho-exchange, and rho-pi-gamma) currents. The calculations were performed in momentum space using Gaussian integration for one-body matrix elements and Monte Carlo techniques for two-body matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The data on A(q), B(q), and T20(q) at q <= 6 fm^-1 probe momentum components up to approximately 4 m_pi (where m_pi is the pion mass)
- The Lorentz factor gamma_B for q = 6 fm^-1 is approximately 1.05, corresponding to a 5% Lorentz contraction
- The rho-pi-gamma coupling constant g_{rho-pi-gamma} = 0.56, obtained from the measured width of rho -> pi gamma decay
- The rho NN tensor coupling constant kappa_{rho NN} = 6.1 from the Bonn 2000 potential
- The monopole form factor cutoff Lambda_pi = 1.2 GeV/c for both pi NN and rho NN form factors
- The isoscalar Pauli form factor F2^S(0) = -0.12 (in units of nuclear magnetons)

## Context

This work extends and improves upon earlier calculations by Schiavilla and Riska (1991) and Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla (1995), which selectively retained relativistic corrections of order (v/c)^2, by treating all facets consistently to that order. The results are relevant for chiral effective field theories of nuclear forces and currents, where the effective Lagrangian is obtained after integrating out states with momenta greater than a specified cutoff; the present work suggests that deuteron structure from theories with cutoff momentum up to 4 m_pi should reproduce existing elastic e-d data. The authors note that data at larger q values (above 6 fm^-1) are needed to test the deuteron wave function at momenta above 4 m_pi, and that improved data on B(q > 6 fm^-1) and new data on T20(q > 6 fm^-1) are required.
