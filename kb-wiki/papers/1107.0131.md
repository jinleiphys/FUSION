# Isobaric yield ratios in heavy-ion reactions, and symmetry energy of neutron-rich nuclei at intermediate energies

> The isobaric yield ratios of the fragments produced in the neutron-rich 48Ca and 64Ni projectile fragmentation are analyzed in the framework of a modified Fisher model. The correlations between the isobaric yield ratios (R) and the energy coefficients in the Weiszäcker-Beth semiclassical mass formula (the symmetry-energy term asym, the Coulomb-energy term ac, and the pairing-energy term ap) and the difference between the chemical potentials of the neutron and proton (μn-μp) are investigated. Simple correlations between (μn-μp)/T, ac/T, asym/T, and ap/T (where T is the temperature), and lnR are obtained. It is suggested that (μn-μp)/T, ac/T, asym/T, and ap/T of neutron-rich nuclei can be extracted using isobaric yield ratios for heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that the symmetry-energy coefficient (a_sym/T), Coulomb-energy coefficient (a_c/T), pairing-energy coefficient (a_p/T), and the difference between neutron and proton chemical potentials (μ_n-μ_p)/T can be extracted from isobaric yield ratios using a modified Fisher model, but only for a single projectile fragmentation reaction without requiring calibration across multiple reaction systems as in prior work (Huang 2010). The method works well for isobars with small I values, but for very neutron-rich isobars with large I, insufficient data prevent extraction of a_sym/T and a_c/T.

## Method
The authors analyze isobaric yield ratios R(I+2,I,A) and R(I+4,I,A) for fragments produced in 140 A MeV 48Ca + 9Be and 64Ni + 9Be projectile fragmentation reactions, using experimental data from Mocko (2006). They derive linear correlations between the logarithm of these yield ratios and combinations of (μ_n-μ_p)/T, a_sym/T, a_c/T, and a_p/T within a modified Fisher model framework, then extract these parameters by fitting the correlations as functions of fragment mass number A and charge number Z.

## Key numbers
- Projectile energies: 140 A MeV for both 48Ca + 9Be and 64Ni + 9Be reactions
- The correlation (8a_sym/A + 2a_c/A^{1/3})/T is fitted using Eq. (sym2) for fragments with I ≥ 3, where values for ln[R(I,I-2,A)] - ln[R(I+2,I,A)] overlap
- For mirror nuclei with I = -1 and I = 1, ln[R(1,-1,A)] = [(μ_n-μ_p) + 2a_c(Z-1)/A^{1/3}]/T (Eq. Rmirror1)
- The pairing term a_p/T is extracted for isobars with I = 0 and I = 2 using Eqs. (ap02_1) through (ap24_3), with sgn = +1 for odd-odd nuclei and sgn = -1 for even-even nuclei
- For the 64Ni reaction, mirror nuclei data were insufficient to produce figures analogous to those for 48Ca

## Context
This work extends the modified Fisher model analysis of isobaric yield ratios previously applied by Huang (2010), who required calibration of Δμ(Z/A) across multiple reaction systems. The present method enables extraction of symmetry energy coefficients from a single projectile fragmentation reaction, avoiding the need for multi-system calibration. The results support the use of isobaric yield ratios as a probe for the symmetry energy of neutron-rich nuclei at intermediate energies, and the authors suggest that future work should investigate projectile fragmentation at different energies to study the temperature dependence of the extracted parameters.
