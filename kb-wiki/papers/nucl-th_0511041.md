# Self-Consistent Description of Collective Excitations in the Unitary Correlation Operator Method

> The fully self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) is constructed within the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), which describes the dominant interaction-induced short-range central and tensor correlations by a unitary transformation. Based on the correlated Argonne V18 interaction, the RPA is employed in studies of multipole response in closed-shell nuclei across the nuclide chart. The UCOM-RPA results in a collective character of giant resonances, and it describes rather well the properties of isoscalar giant monopole resonances. However, the excitation energies of isovector giant dipole resonances and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances are overestimated due to the missing long-range correlations and three-body contributions.

## Key claim

The fully self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) constructed within the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) using the correlated Argonne V18 interaction successfully describes isoscalar giant monopole resonances, but systematically overestimates excitation energies of isovector giant dipole resonances and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances by several MeV due to missing long-range correlations and three-body contributions.

## Method

The authors first applied the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) to the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon interaction, generating a correlated interaction \(V_{\text{UCOM}}\) through unitary transformations that encode short-range central and tensor correlations. They solved the Hartree-Fock (HF) equations using this correlated interaction to obtain single-particle wave functions and energies for closed-shell nuclei, then constructed the fully self-consistent RPA equations using the same correlated interaction for both the HF mean field and the residual particle-hole interaction, with consistently transformed multipole transition operators.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{40}\)Ca, the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) centroid energies were 25.1, 26.2, and 27.1 MeV for tensor correlator range constraints \(I_{\vartheta}^{(S=1,T=0)}\) = 0.07, 0.08, and 0.09 fm\(^3\), respectively.
- For \(^{208}\)Pb, the ISGQR centroid energy lowered by 1.2 MeV when changing the tensor correlator constraint from \(I_{\vartheta}^{(S=1,T=0)}\) = 0.09 fm\(^3\) to 0.07 fm\(^3\).
- The UCOM-RPA overestimated isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) centroid energies by approximately 3-7 MeV across the nuclei studied.
- The UCOM-RPA overestimated isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) experimental values by approximately 8 MeV for the short-range tensor correlator (\(I_{\vartheta}^{(S=1,T=0)}\) = 0.07 fm\(^3\)).
- In heavy nuclei, isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) energies were overestimated by approximately 1-3 MeV.
- Decreasing the tensor correlator range constraint from 0.09 fm\(^3\) to 0.07 fm\(^3\) lowered IVGDR peak energies by approximately 2-3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the UCOM-HF ground-state calculations of Roth et al. (2005) to collective excitations, providing the first description of giant resonances in closed-shell nuclei based on correlated realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. The results are compared with experimental data from Youngblood et al. (1999), Shlomo et al. (1993), Sharma et al. (1988), Adrich et al. (2005), Berman et al. (1975), Poelhekken et al. (1989), Ritt et al. (1993), and Bertrand et al. (1979), as well as with theoretical results from relativistic RPA (Paar et al., 2003; Ma et al., 2001; Niksic et al., 2002; Lalazissis et al., 2005) and nonrelativistic RPA (Drozdz et al., 1990). The discrepancies indicate that future work must include long-range correlations beyond simple RPA (e.g., through Second-RPA or RPA on correlated ground states) and three-body interactions to achieve quantitative agreement with experiment.
