# Charmed-baryon production in antiproton-proton collisions within an effective Lagrangian model

> We study the productions of charmed baryons ${\bar Λ}_c^- Λ_c^+$, ${\bar Λ}_c^- Σ_c^+$, and ${\bar Σ}^- Σ_c^+$ in the antiproton-proton collisions within an effective Lagrangian model that has only the baryon-meson degrees of freedom and involves the physical hadron masses. The baryon production proceeds via the $t$-channel exchanges of $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$ mesons in the initial collision of the antiproton with the target proton. The distortion effects in the initial and final states are accounted for by using an eikonal approximation-based procedure. We find that the reaction amplitudes of all the production channels are dominated by the $D^{*0}$ meson-exchange diagrams. We discuss the relative roles of tensor and vector components of the $D^{*0}$ coupling in the $D^{*0}$ meson-exchange component of the total production cross sections. The magnitudes of the cross sections are predicted for each final state for the range of beam momenta of relevance to the ${\bar P}ANDA$ experiment.

## Key claim

The paper predicts total cross sections for charmed-baryon production in antiproton-proton collisions using an effective Lagrangian model with only baryon-meson degrees of freedom, finding that the D*0 meson-exchange diagrams dominate all channels. The cross sections are largest for the anti-Lambda_c- Lambda_c+ channel, with the anti-Lambda_c- Sigma_c+ and anti-Sigma_c- Sigma_c+ channels smaller by factors of about 10 and 100 respectively, though these predictions carry uncertainty due to the lack of experimental data to constrain form factors and distortion parameters.

## Method

The reactions are described as a sum of t-channel D0 and D*0 meson-exchange diagrams between the antiproton and proton, using effective Lagrangians at the charmed-baryon-meson-nucleon vertices with coupling constants determined from SU(4) symmetry arguments in DN and anti-DN scattering studies. Distortion effects in the initial and final states are accounted for by an eikonal approximation-based procedure using an imaginary Gaussian potential with fixed parameters (V0 = 0.8965 GeV, mu = 0.3369 GeV, impact parameter 0.327 GeV^-1), while final-state interactions are neglected based on their small effect (10-15%) shown for the anti-Lambda_c- Lambda_c+ channel. Off-shell behavior is regulated by dipole form factors with cutoff parameter 3.0 GeV for D0 vertices and a different functional form with cutoff 2.7 GeV for D*0 vertices.

## Key numbers

* Threshold beam momenta: 10.162 GeV/c for anti-p p -> anti-Lambda_c- Lambda_c+, 10.99 GeV/c for anti-p p -> anti-Lambda_c- Sigma_c+, and 11.85 GeV/c for anti-p p -> anti-Sigma_c- Sigma_c+
* At beam momentum around 15 GeV/c, the total cross section for anti-Lambda_c- Lambda_c+ production is about one order of magnitude larger than that for anti-Lambda_c- Sigma_c+ production
* The D0 exchange contributions are nearly 2 orders of magnitude smaller than D*0 exchange contributions for the anti-Lambda_c- Lambda_c+ final state, and nearly an order of magnitude smaller for the anti-Lambda_c- Sigma_c+ final state at higher beam momenta
* Ratio of tensor to vector coupling constants at the ND*0Y_c+ vertices is approximately 3 (from Table I: g_DBN/sqrt(4pi) = 1.590 and f_DBN/sqrt(4pi) = 5.183 for ND*0Lambda_c+; g_DBN/sqrt(4pi) = 0.918 and f_DBN/sqrt(4pi) = -2.222 for ND*0Sigma_c+)
* The D0 meson-exchange terms are about two orders of magnitude smaller than D*0 exchange terms for the anti-Sigma_c- Sigma_c+ channel at beam momenta around 15 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the author's previous single-channel effective Lagrangian model for anti-p p -> anti-Lambda_c- Lambda_c+ (Shyam 2014) to the anti-Lambda_c- Sigma_c+ and anti-Sigma_c- Sigma_c+ channels, providing cross section predictions for beam momenta from threshold to 18 GeV/c relevant to the PANDA experiment at FAIR. The results are compared to the Julich meson-exchange model (Haidenbauer 2017), where the anti-Lambda_c- Lambda_c+ cross sections approximately agree but the anti-Lambda_c- Sigma_c+ cross sections are smaller by factors of about 10, and to quark-gluon string model calculations (Khodjamirian 2012; Kaidalov 1994; Titov 2008; Gorenstein 2009) where the present cross sections are at least an order of magnitude larger. The predictions enable planning for charmed-baryon spectroscopy, charm hypernuclei production, D-mesonic nuclei studies, and medium effects on charmed-hadron properties at PANDA.
