# Leading Infrared Logarithms from Unitarity, Analyticity and Crossing

> We derive non-linear recursion equations for the leading infrared logarithms in massless non-renormalizable effective field theories. The derivation is based solely on the requirements of the unitarity, analyticity and crossing symmetry of the amplitudes. That emphasizes the general nature of the corresponding equations. The derived equations allow one to compute leading infrared logarithms to essentially unlimited loop order without performing a loop calculation. For the implementation of the recursion equation one needs to calculate tree diagrams only. The application of the equation is demonstrated on several examples of effective field theories in four and higher space-time dimensions.

## Key claim

The authors derive non-linear recursion equations for leading infrared logarithms (LLs) in massless non-renormalizable effective field theories using only the general principles of unitarity, analyticity, and crossing symmetry, rather than relying on detailed loop calculations or renormalization group methods. The derivation is valid for theories where the anomalous dimension of fields vanishes at leading order, and the recursion requires only tree-level input for initial conditions.

## Method

The authors start from the partial wave decomposition of the 4-point scattering amplitude in a massless scalar effective field theory with a generic action containing derivative interactions. They impose elastic unitarity on the right cut and use a dispersion relation (Roy-type equation) combined with crossing symmetry to relate the left-cut discontinuity to the right-cut discontinuity. By substituting the leading-log expansion of the partial waves into these constraints and collecting coefficients of equal logarithmic order, they obtain a closed non-linear recursion relation (Eq. w=ww) for the LL coefficients ω_{nl}^I, which depend on loop order n, angular momentum l, and internal symmetry representation I. The method is generalized to arbitrary even space-time dimension D and to theories with both renormalizable and non-renormalizable couplings.

## Key numbers

* For the 4D O(N+1)/O(N) sigma model (k=1), the tree-level initial conditions are ω_{10}^{I=0}=N-1, ω_{10}^{I=2}=-1, and ω_{11}^{I=1}=1; all other ω_{1l}^I are zero.
* For the 4D O(N) symmetric phi^4 theory (k=0), the tree-level initial conditions are ω_{10}^{I=0}=-(N+2) and ω_{10}^{I=2}=-2; all other ω_{1l}^I are zero.
* The summed LL series for the O(N) phi^4 theory yields t_0^{I=0}(s) = -(π/2) (N+2)/(4πF)^2 * 1/[1 + b_1/(4πF)^2 ln(μ^2/s)], where b_1 = (N+8)/2 is the one-loop beta function coefficient.
* For the 6D O(N) symmetric phi^4 theory (k=0, D=6), the large-N limit gives ω_{n0}^{I=0} = -N * (-N/[2(D-3)])^{n-1}.
* In the mixed theory (ChPT plus phi^4 term) for N=3, the one-loop beta function coefficient of the O(3) phi^4 theory is b_1 = 11/2, and the anomalous dimensions for the dressed one-loop chiral logs are γ_1=9/11, γ_2=40/33, γ_3=10/33.

## Context

This work extends the earlier non-linear recursion approach of MKV (2009) for LLs in massless effective field theories, which required detailed analysis of counterterm structures and loop calculations. The present derivation shows that the same recursion follows purely from S-matrix principles, making it more general and simpler to apply. The method reproduces the standard renormalization group equations for renormalizable theories (e.g., phi^4 theory) as a special case, and it enables computation of LL coefficients to essentially unlimited loop order (e.g., 99-loop coefficients for massless ChPT) using only tree-level input and the recursion. The approach can be generalized to theories with fields of various spins and in arbitrary even dimensions, including applications to low-energy quantum gravity and critical phenomena.
