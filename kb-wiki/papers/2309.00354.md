# Calculation of microscopic nuclear level densities based on covariant density functional theory

> A microscopic method for calculating nuclear level density (NLD) based on the covariant density functional theory (CDFT) is developed. The particle-hole state density is calculated by combinatorial method using the single-particle levels schemes obtained from the CDFT. Then the level densities are obtained by taking into account collective effects such as vibration and rotation. Our results are compared with those from other NLD models, including phenomenological, microstatistical, and non-relativistic HFB combinatorial models. The comparison suggests that the general trends among these models are basically the same, except for some deviations from different NLD models. In addition, the NLDs of the CDFT combinatorial method with normalization are compared with experimental data, including the observed cumulative number of levels at low excitation energy and the measured NLDs. Compared with the existing experimental data, the CDFT combinatorial method can give reasonable results.

## Key claim

The covariant density functional theory (CDFT) combinatorial method can produce nuclear level densities (NLDs) that are as reliable as other microscopic and phenomenological models, but the method currently yields a root-mean-square deviation factor of \(f_{\rm{rms}}=3.62\) for s-wave neutron resonance spacings, which is larger than the \(f_{\rm{rms}}=1.8\) of the phenomenological back-shifted Fermi gas model and the \(f_{\rm{rms}}=2.1\) of the microstatistical method, indicating that the CDFT combinatorial method still requires improvements in pairing correlations and collective effects.

## Method

The single-particle energies, spin projections, and parities are obtained by solving the Dirac equation within covariant density functional theory using the PK1 effective interaction. A combinatorial generating function is then expanded to count particle-hole configurations, yielding the particle-hole state density as a function of excitation energy, spin projection, and parity. Collective rotational and vibrational enhancements are applied to obtain the final level density, with rotational effects treated using the rigid-body moment of inertia and vibrational enhancement via an entropy-based exponential factor; a phenomenological damping function smooths the transition between spherical and deformed level densities.

## Key numbers

*   The root-mean-square deviation factor \(f_{\rm{rms}}\) between theoretical and experimental s-wave neutron resonance spacings is 3.62 for the CDFT combinatorial method, based on 66 nuclei.
*   Under the same calculation conditions, the Gogny (D1S) interaction based on HFB plus combinatorial method gives \(f_{\rm{rms}}=7.25\).
*   The phenomenological back-shifted Fermi gas model achieves \(f_{\rm{rms}}=1.8\), and the microstatistical method achieves \(f_{\rm{rms}}=2.1\).
*   The HFB combinatorial method based on Skyrme effective interaction with improved pairing and collective effects yields \(f_{\rm{rms}}=2.3\).
*   For the damping function, the parameters 0.18 and 0.038 are used in the expression \(f_{\rm{dam}}=1-1/(1+\exp{(\beta-0.18)/0.038})\), where \(\beta\) is the quadrupole deformation parameter.

## Context

This work extends the combinatorial method for NLDs, previously developed with non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approaches using Skyrme and Gogny effective interactions (Hilaire 2006, Goriely 2008), to the covariant density functional theory framework. It also builds on a prior microstatistical method based on CDFT (PhysRevC.102.054606) that could only calculate energy-dependent NLDs, whereas the combinatorial method provides energy-, spin-, and parity-dependent NLDs. The results enable future improvements by incorporating energy-dependent pairing correlations and a partition function approach for vibrational enhancement, which will support studies of neutron capture processes such as the r-process.
