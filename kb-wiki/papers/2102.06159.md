# First analysis of world polarized DIS data with small-$x$ helicity evolution

> We present a Monte Carlo based analysis of the combined world data on polarized lepton-nucleon deep-inelastic scattering at small Bjorken $x$ within the polarized quark dipole formalism. We show for the first time that double-spin asymmetries at $x<0.1$ can be successfully described using only small-$x$ evolution derived from first-principles QCD, allowing predictions to be made for the $g_1$ structure function at much smaller $x$. Anticipating future data from the Electron-Ion Collider, we assess the impact of electromagnetic and parity-violating polarization asymmetries on $g_1$ and demonstrate an extraction of the individual flavor helicity PDFs at small $x$.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate for the first time that double-spin asymmetries in polarized deep-inelastic scattering at Bjorken x < 0.1 can be successfully described using only small-x helicity evolution (KPS evolution) derived from first-principles QCD, but the analysis is limited to leading-order double-logarithmic approximation and requires an ad hoc matching parameter x₀ whose value is not uniquely determined by current data.

## Method

The authors perform a Monte Carlo based Bayesian analysis within the JAM framework, fitting the world polarized DIS data on longitudinal double-spin asymmetries for proton, deuteron, and ³He targets at x < 0.1 and Q² > 1.69 GeV². They solve the KPS evolution equations for the polarized dipole amplitude in the large-Nc limit at double-logarithmic approximation, using a linear ansatz for the initial conditions with flavor-dependent parameters, and implement a matching procedure at x₀ = 0.1 to connect to large-x physics. For the EIC projections, they generate pseudodata for A|| and APV asymmetries and perform a separate fit extracting individual flavor helicity PDFs.

## Key numbers

- Fit to 122 data points from SLAC, EMC, SMC, COMPASS, and HERMES experiments yields χ²/Npts = 1.01
- Extracted parameters for proton initial conditions: aₚ = -1.33 ± 0.30, bₚ = 0.49 ± 0.44, cₚ = 2.24 ± 0.16
- Extracted parameters for neutron initial conditions: aₙ = -2.47 ± 0.65, bₙ = 3.03 ± 1.01, cₙ = 0.30 ± 0.36
- For fits to existing data, relative uncertainty δg₁ᵖ/g₁ᵖ at small x is approximately 25% for JAMsmallx versus approximately 100% for the DSSV fit
- EIC pseudodata include 487 points each for A|| and APV, with center-of-mass energies √S = {29, 45, 63, 141} GeV for proton and √S = {29, 66, 89} GeV for deuteron and ³He, with integrated luminosities of 100 fb⁻¹ and 10 fb⁻¹ respectively
- With EIC pseudodata included, relative uncertainty of g₁ᵖ reduces to sub-percent level

## Context

This work extends the KPS small-x helicity evolution formalism developed by Kovchegov, Pitonyak, and Sievert (2015-2018) to actual data analysis for the first time, building on earlier theoretical work by Kirschner (1983), Bartels (1995-1996), and others. It differs from earlier phenomenological approaches by Blumlein (1996) which mixed small-x resummation into polarized DGLAP splitting functions, and from the DSSV global analysis by de Florian (2014, 2019) which uses standard DGLAP evolution with ad hoc parametrizations at an input scale. The analysis enables genuine predictions for the g₁ structure function and helicity PDFs at small x beyond what DGLAP-based extrapolations can provide, and demonstrates the potential impact of future EIC data for flavor separation and determination of the onset of small-x evolution.
