# Recovering Relativistic Nuclear Phenomenology from the Quark-Meson Coupling Model

> The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for nuclear matter, which describes nuclear matter as non-overlapping MIT bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons is modified by the introduction of a density dependent bag constant. The density dependence of the bag constant is related to that of the in-medium effective nucleon mass through a scaling ansatz suggested by partial chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter. This modification overcomes drawbacks of the QMC model and leads to the recovery of the essential features of relativistic nuclear phenomenology. This suggests that the modification of the bag constant in the nuclear medium may play an important role in low- and medium-energy nuclear physics.

# Recovering Relativistic Nuclear Phenomenology from the Quark-Meson Coupling Model

## Key claim

The standard quark-meson coupling (QMC) model predicts scalar and vector potentials for the nucleon that are much smaller than those required by relativistic nuclear phenomenology; by introducing a density-dependent bag constant that decreases with density according to a scaling ansatz motivated by partial chiral symmetry restoration, the large canceling scalar and vector potentials characteristic of Dirac phenomenology can be recovered, provided the scaling parameter κ is in the range 7–10.

## Method

The authors modify the QMC model for symmetric nuclear matter by replacing the fixed bag constant with a density-dependent bag constant B that scales as (M_N*/M_N)^(κ/3), where M_N* is the in-medium effective nucleon mass. The nucleon is treated as a static MIT bag containing three quarks coupled to scalar (σ) and vector (ω) mean fields; the bag radius is determined by the equilibrium condition ∂M_N*/∂R = 0. The coupling constants g_σ and g_ω are fitted to reproduce the nuclear matter binding energy of −16 MeV at saturation density ρ_N^0 = 0.17 fm⁻³ for various values of κ and free-space bag radii R_0 = 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ_N^0 = 0.17 fm⁻³; binding energy: −16 MeV
- For R_0 = 0.8 fm and κ = 0 (standard QMC): g_σ²/4π = 21.87, g_ω²/4π = 1.14, nuclear incompressibility K = 200 MeV
- For R_0 = 0.8 fm and κ = 9: g_σ²/4π = 2.04, g_ω²/4π = 6.33, K = 471 MeV
- For R_0 = 0.8 fm and κ = 10: g_σ²/4π = 1.20, g_ω²/4π = 11.17, K = 1058 MeV
- QHD-I reference values (R_0 = 0.8 fm): g_σ²/4π = 8.45, g_ω²/4π = 12.84, K = 540 MeV
- Free-space bag parameters for R_0 = 0.8 fm: B_0^(1/4) = 157.5 MeV, Z_0 = 1.628
- At saturation density, using m_ρ*/m_ρ ≈ 0.8 and B/B_0 ≈ Φ⁴ gives B/B_0 ≈ 0.4, requiring κ ≈ 9.1 for R_0 = 0.8 fm
- For κ ≈ 9 at saturation density: R/R_0 ≈ 1.25; for R_0 = 0.6–0.7 fm, R ≈ 0.75–0.875 fm, giving bag volume fraction 4πR³ρ_N⁰/3 ≈ 0.3–0.48

## Context

This work extends the QMC model originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and refined by Fleck et al. (1990) and Saito and Thomas (1994), which described nuclear matter as non-overlapping MIT bags exchanging scalar and vector mesons. The standard QMC model predicted scalar and vector potentials too weak to explain spin-orbit splittings in finite nuclei or spin-observables in nucleon-nucleus scattering, in contrast to relativistic nuclear phenomenology (Hama et al., 1990; Wallace, 1987) and finite-density QCD sum rules (Cohen et al., 1995). The modification enables the model to recover the large canceling scalar and vector potentials comparable to those from Dirac phenomenology and QHD-I (Serot and Walecka, 1986), while suggesting that the in-medium modification of the bag constant may play an important role in low- and medium-energy nuclear physics. The approach can be applied to finite nuclei and other nuclear physics problems.
