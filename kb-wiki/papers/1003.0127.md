# Excited nucleon electromagnetic form factors from broken spin-flavor symmetry

> A group theoretical derivation of a relation between the N --> Delta charge quadrupole transition and neutron charge form factors is presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a group-theoretical derivation showing that the relation between the neutron charge radius and the N→Δ quadrupole moment follows from broken SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry, specifically from second-order symmetry-breaking operators transforming as the **405** irreducible representation. The derivation relies on the assumption that the charge density operator contains spin-dependent two-quark operators with fixed relative strengths determined by the SU(6) group algebra.

## Method

The author performs an SU(6) group-theoretical analysis of the baryon ground state **56**-plet, decomposing the charge density operator into Coulomb multipoles and identifying the J=0 (charge monopole) and J=2 (charge quadrupole) components as different subtensors of a common **405**-dimensional irreducible tensor operator. Using the generalized Wigner-Eckart theorem, the matrix elements for the neutron charge radius and the N→Δ quadrupole moment are expressed in terms of a common reduced matrix element multiplied by SU(6) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, which provide the numerical relation between the two observables.

## Key numbers

- Neutron charge radius: \( r_n^2 = 4 B a \) (from two-quark operator evaluation, where B parametrizes color and orbital matrix elements and a is the strength of the spin-scalar term)
- N→Δ quadrupole moment: \( Q_{N \to \Delta} = 2\sqrt{2} \, B a \) (from the same two-quark operator evaluation)
- The ratio between the two quantities gives \( Q_{N \to \Delta} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \, r_n^2 \) (Eq. 3 in the paper)
- SU(6) scalar factors: \( f^{\bf 405}_{(8,1)} = -2/\sqrt{10} \) for the rank-0 tensor and \( f^{\bf 405}_{(8,5)} = 1/\sqrt{10} \) for the rank-2 tensor, giving a factor of -2 between them

## Context

This work extends the author's previous quark-model derivation (Buchmann 2004) of the relation between the N→Δ charge quadrupole form factor and the neutron charge form factor, showing that the relation follows purely from SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry without specific quark-model assumptions. The analysis builds on the foundational SU(6) work of Gürsey and Radicati (1964), Sakita (1964), and Beg, Lee, and Pais (1964), and connects to the large-Nc expansion of QCD (Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar 1995; Luty and March-Russell 1998). The result enables a symmetry-based understanding of baryon electromagnetic structure and suggests a path toward generalizing the relation to finite momentum transfers using the same group-theoretical framework.
