# The Nuclear Level Density and the Determination of Thermonuclear Rates for Astrophysics

> The prediction of cross sections for nuclei far off stability is crucial in the field of nuclear astrophysics. In recent calculations the nuclear level density -- as an important ingredient to the statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) -- has shown the highest uncertainties. We present a global parametrization of nuclear level densities based on the back-shifted Fermi-Gas formalism. Employment of an energy-dependent level density parameter $a$ and microscopic corrections from a recent FRDM mass formula by Möller et al.\ leads to a highly improved fit of level densities at the neutron-separation energy in the mass range $20\le A \le 245$. The importance of using proper microscopic corrections from mass formulae is emphasized. The resulting level description is well suited for astrophysical applications. The level density can also provide clues to the applicability of the statistical model which is only correct for a high density of excited states. Using the above description one can derive a ``map'' for the applicability of the model for reactions of stable and unstable nuclei with neutral and charged particles.

## Key claim

The paper presents a global parametrization of nuclear level densities based on the back-shifted Fermi-Gas formalism that achieves a significantly improved fit to experimental data, but the reliability for nuclei far from stability depends on the robustness of the underlying Möller et al. (1995) mass model, for which no final conclusion is yet available.

## Method

The authors employed an energy-dependent level density parameter a(U,Z,N) following the Ignatyuk formalism, with microscopic corrections taken from the FRDM mass formula of Möller et al. (1995). They performed a fit of three free parameters (alpha, beta, gamma) to experimental s-wave neutron resonance spacing data for 278 nuclei at the neutron separation energy, using pairing gaps derived from experimental mass differences of neighboring nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Averaged ratio of calculated to experimental level densities g = 1.5 for the best fit across 278 nuclei in the mass range 20 ≤ A ≤ 245
- Best-fit parameter values: alpha = 0.1336, beta = -0.06712, gamma = 0.04862
- For the majority of nuclei, absolute deviation between predicted and experimental level densities is less than a factor of 2
- Previous calculations showed uncertainties of a factor of 5 (Thielemann, Arnould and Truran 1987), a factor of 8 (Woosley et al. 1978), and beyond a factor of 10 (Gilbert and Cameron 1965) at the neutron separation energy
- A minimum of 3.5 levels within the Gamow peak width (FWHM) was assumed as the necessary condition for statistical model applicability for charged-particle reactions

## Context

This work extends the back-shifted Fermi-Gas approach of Gilbert and Cameron (1965) by incorporating an energy-dependent level density parameter and microscopic corrections from the Möller et al. (1995) mass formula, improving upon earlier global descriptions that showed uncertainties of a factor of 5 or more. The resulting level density description enables recalculation of thermonuclear rates for many astrophysically important reactions and provides applicability maps for the statistical model across the nuclear chart, identifying which nuclei require special attention or experimental investigation.
