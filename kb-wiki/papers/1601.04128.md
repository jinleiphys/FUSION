# Analytic approaches to relativistic hydrodynamics

> I summarize our recent work towards finding and utilizing analytic solutions of relativistic hydrodynamic. In the first part I discuss various exact solutions of the second-order conformal hydrodynamics. In the second part I compute flow harmonics $v_n$ analytically using the anisotropically deformed Gubser flow and discuss its dependence on $n$, $p_T$, viscosity, the chemical potential and the charge.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that analytic solutions of second-order conformal relativistic hydrodynamics can be found using Weyl transform techniques, and that flow harmonics \(v_n\) can be computed analytically from the anisotropically deformed Gubser flow, yielding explicit functional forms for their dependence on harmonic order \(n\), transverse momentum \(p_T\), shear viscosity, chemical potential, and charge, though the quantitative agreement with experimental data should not be taken too seriously given the many assumptions involved.

## Method

The author constructs exact solutions of the second-order conformal hydrodynamic equation by applying Weyl transformations to map Minkowski-space hydrodynamics onto de Sitter or anti-de Sitter spaces, where the flow equations simplify. For the Gubser flow, small \(\cos n\phi\) perturbations are introduced to the energy density and flow velocity, and the linearized equations are solved analytically in the early-time regime \(\tau \ll L\). The resulting solutions are then inserted into the Cooper-Frye freezeout formula to extract \(v_n(p_T)\) via integration over a constant-energy-density hypersurface, with explicit results obtained in the low- and high-\(p_T\) limits and for \(p_T\)-integrated harmonics.

## Key numbers

* Energy density for the unorthodox Bjorken flow scales as \(\varepsilon = C / \tau^4\), where the normalization \(C\) is fixed by transport coefficients via \(C^{1/4}= \frac{3\eta-16\tau_\pi +2\tau_{\pi\pi} \pm \sqrt{ (3\eta-16\tau_\pi +2\tau_{\pi\pi})^2+4(4\lambda_1-3)(2\tau_\sigma+\tilde{\eta}_3)}}{4(4\lambda_1-3)}\) (dimensionless transport coefficients).
* At low \(p_T\) (\(p_T \ll B^3 T\)), the flow harmonic ratio is \(\frac{v_n(p_T)}{\epsilon_n} \approx \frac{27}{32}\frac{n(n-1)\Gamma(3n)}{\Gamma(4n)}\frac{TK_0(p_T/T)}{p_T K_1(p_T/T)}\left(\frac{64p_T}{B^3T}\right)^n\).
* At high \(p_T\) (\(p_T \gg B^3 T\)), \(\frac{v_n(p_T)}{\epsilon_n} \approx \frac{500p_T}{27TB^3}\left(\frac{\sqrt{5}}{3}\right)^{n-1}\left(n-1-\frac{27K}{200}n\right)\).
* The \(p_T\)-integrated \(v_n\) decays exponentially with \(n\) as \(v_n \sim e^{-n\ln (4B^3/27)}\).
* The viscous correction to \(v_n\) grows linearly with \(n\): \(v_n/v_n^{ideal} = 1-{\mathcal O}\left(n \frac{\eta}{s}\right)\).
* The charge-dependent difference \(\Delta v^X_2 / v_2^{X,ideal} \approx \frac{27 K}{80} \left(\mu_B B+\mu_S S+\mu_I I \right)\).

## Context

This work extends the Gubser flow solutions (Gubser, 2010) to second-order conformal hydrodynamics and to anisotropic perturbations, enabling analytic computation of flow harmonics. It provides a framework for understanding the functional dependence of \(v_n\) on \(n\), viscosity, and chemical potential, and offers analytic formulas that can be tested against experimental data from RHIC and the LHC. The results enable future numerical studies to benchmark their codes against exact second-order solutions, as demonstrated with the CLVisc code by Pang et al. (2014), and provide a baseline for interpreting charge-dependent flow measurements such as the charged pion \(v_2\) difference reported by STAR (Adamczyk, 2015).
