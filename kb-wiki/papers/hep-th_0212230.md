# Critical Temperature of the Deconfining Phase Transition in (2+1)d Georgi-Glashow Model

> We find the temperature of the phase transition in the (2+1)d Georgi-Glashow model. The critical temperature is shown to depend on the gauge coupling and on the ratio of Higgs and gauge boson masses. In the BPS limit of light Higgs the previous result by Dunne, Kogan, Kovner, and Tekin is reproduced.

## Key claim

The critical temperature of the deconfining phase transition in the (2+1)d Georgi-Glashow model depends on both the gauge coupling and the ratio of Higgs to gauge boson masses, not solely on the gauge coupling as previously claimed. In the BPS limit where the Higgs is light, the result reproduces the earlier value found by Dunne, Kogan, Kovner, and Tekin (2001).

## Method

The authors reformulated the finite-temperature partition function of the (2+1)d Georgi-Glashow model as a two-dimensional Coulomb gas of monopoles and W bosons, valid in the regime where the temperature is much larger than the photon mass but much smaller than the W and Higgs masses. They then mapped this Coulomb gas onto an effective bosonic field theory with two scalar fields and cosine potentials, and performed a renormalization group analysis of the running fugacities to determine the temperature at which both perturbations become relevant at the same scale.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature formula: \( T_c = \frac{g^2}{4\pi} \frac{\epsilon+2}{2\epsilon+1} \), where \(\epsilon = \epsilon(m_H/m_W)\) is the function defining the monopole action \( S_0 = \frac{4\pi v}{g} \epsilon(m_H/m_W) \)
- In the BPS limit \( m_H \ll m_W \): \(\epsilon(0) = 1\), giving \( T_c = g^2/(4\pi) \), matching the result of Dunne, Kogan, Kovner, and Tekin (2001)
- The function \(\epsilon\) ranges from \(\epsilon(0)=1\) to \(\epsilon(\infty) \approx 1.787\) in the Georgi-Glashow model
- The non-perturbative scale at the critical temperature: \(\mu \sim T \exp\left(-\frac{2\epsilon+1}{3}\frac{4\pi v}{g}\right)\)
- The critical region width: \(\frac{|T - T_c|}{T_c} \lesssim \frac{g}{v}\)
- Correlation length outside the critical region: \(\xi \sim \frac{1}{T} \exp\left( \frac{2\pi\epsilon gv}{g^2-2\pi T}\right)\) for \(T < T_c\), and \(\xi \sim \frac{1}{T} \exp\left( \frac{4\pi gv}{8\pi T-g^2}\right)\) for \(T > T_c\)

## Context

This work extends and corrects the earlier analysis by Agasyan and Zarembo (1998), who found \(T_c = g^2/(2\pi)\) by considering only monopoles in the Coulomb gas, and the subsequent treatment by Dunne, Kogan, Kovner, and Tekin (2001), who included W bosons and found \(T_c = g^2/(4\pi)\) in the BPS limit. The present result shows that the critical temperature depends continuously on the Higgs-to-W mass ratio, interpolating between these previous values. The analysis enables a quantitative understanding of the deconfining phase transition in a weakly-coupled setting that shares features with QCD, and predicts that the Polyakov loop correlator exhibits Ising-type critical behavior only at distances larger than an exponentially large scale \(\mu^{-1}\), with a continuously varying exponent at intermediate distances.
