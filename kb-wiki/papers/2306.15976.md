# A method for probing the formation of quark matter

> Based on a multi-phase transport model for relativistic heavy-ion collisions, effects of the parton scatterings on the production of strangeness in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are studied. It is found that the distributions of strange quark and strange baryon, especially for the double strangeness $Ξ^{-}$, are significantly affected by the parton scatterings in heavy-ion collisions below $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim$ 10 GeV. Given parton scatterings as a signal of the formation of quark matter, the transverse momentum distribution of the ratio of single and double strangeness $(Λ+Σ^{0})/Ξ^{-}$ produced in heavy-ion collisions may serve as a potential probe of the emergence of quark matter, or equivalently, the occurrence of hadron-quark phase transition in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum distribution of the ratio of single to double strangeness, \((\Lambda+\Sigma^{0})/\Xi^{-}\), may serve as a potential probe of the emergence of quark matter (or equivalently, the hadron-quark phase transition) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but only for collisions below \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim 10\) GeV and with the caveat that a definite conclusion cannot be drawn before more detailed studies on model dependence are carried out.

## Method

The study uses the string-melting version of the AMPT (AMPT-SM) multi-phase transport model, which includes parton cascade via Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC), to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam energies below \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 10\) GeV. For comparison, a pure hadron cascade mode (AMPT-HC) with hadronic mean-field potentials and no parton degrees of freedom is also used. The yields and transverse momentum distributions of \(\Lambda+\Sigma^{0}\) and \(\Xi^{-}\) are computed, and their ratio is compared between the two modes to isolate the effect of parton scatterings.

## Key numbers

- The parton cascade significantly affects the strange quark density distribution in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7\) GeV, making it sparser compared to the case without parton cascade.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7\) GeV, both \(\Lambda+\Sigma^{0}\) and \(\Xi^{-}\) yields are reduced when parton scatterings are included; the ratio \((\Lambda+\Sigma^{0})/\Xi^{-}\) is very sensitive to the parton cascade.
- The AMPT-SM model's results for the ratio \((\Lambda+\Sigma^{0})/\Xi^{-}\) roughly fit experimental data above \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \simeq 4\) GeV, suggesting the occurrence of quark matter above that energy.
- The effect of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) on the \((\Lambda+\Sigma^{0})/\Xi^{-}\) ratio is less than one fifth that of the parton cascade.

## Context

This work extends studies of strangeness as a probe of dense nuclear matter and the QCD phase diagram, building on prior measurements of \(\Lambda\) and \(\Xi^{-}\) by E895, NA49, HADES, and STAR collaborations (Chung 2001, Adams 2020, and others). It also connects to the beam energy scan (BES) program at RHIC and the goals of the CBM and NICA facilities. The paper suggests that the \((\Lambda+\Sigma^{0})/\Xi^{-}\) ratio is a more suitable observable than proton flow (sideward or elliptic flow) for detecting a localized hadron-quark phase transition, because strangeness production shows less cancellation in its sensitivity to the EoS over time.
