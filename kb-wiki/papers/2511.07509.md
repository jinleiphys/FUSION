# Rotating the Color Glass Condensate

> High-energy QCD evolution beyond leading order suffers from instabilities driven by large collinear logarithms. We present a framework, consistent with the standard high-energy operator product expansion (OPE), that restores perturbative stability order by order. The method involves a change of basis in the space of high-energy operators, which modifies both the evolution kernel and the coefficient functions while leaving physical observables invariant. Within this factorization scheme, we derive a next-to-leading-order renormalization-group equation whose numerical solution exhibits stable evolution up to large rapidities, thereby establishing a solid foundation for precision studies of gluon saturation at current and future colliders.

## Key claim

The paper presents a framework that restores perturbative stability to the next-to-leading-order (NLO) Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation by implementing a change of basis in the space of high-energy operators, which modifies both the evolution kernel and coefficient functions while leaving physical observables invariant. The method is consistent with the standard high-energy operator product expansion (OPE), unlike previous ad-hoc modifications that altered the evolution kernel without preserving the OPE structure.

## Method

The authors introduce a similarity transformation via an operator exp(-L) that rotates the dipole-chain basis in the space of high-energy operators, analogous to scheme transformations in collinear factorization. This transformation introduces a collinear factorization scale mu^2 that couples to the rapidity scale zeta through the composite variable Y = ln(zeta/mu^2), effectively reorganizing the perturbative expansion to separate transverse and longitudinal degrees of freedom. The rotated NLO BK equation is derived by computing the commutator [K_LO, L_LO] and applying the Campbell identity to transform the evolution kernel, then solving the resulting equation numerically using a McLerran-Venugopalan-inspired initial condition.

## Key numbers

* Fixed coupling constant bar_alpha_s = 0.3 used in numerical simulations
* Initial saturation scale Q_0 = 1.0 GeV and infrared scale Lambda = 0.2 GeV in the initial condition
* Evolution remains stable up to rapidity Y = 10, extending beyond the kinematic reach of HERA DIS measurements
* At Y = 5 and Y = 10, the evolved quantity bar_N_12 = 1 - bar_S_12 shows stable, positive behavior, in contrast to the standard NLO BK equation which drives bar_N_12 to unphysical negative values

## Context

This work extends the line of research on high-energy QCD evolution beyond leading order, which previously suffered from instabilities driven by large collinear logarithms in the NLO BK equation (Lappi 2015) and the NLO BFKL equation (Fadin 1998, Ciafaloni 1998). Prior attempts to stabilize the evolution included ad-hoc modifications of the LO BK kernel (Beuf 2014, Iancu 2015, Motyka 2009) and reformulation in terms of target rapidity (Ducloue 2019, Altinoluk 2025), but these approaches were unsatisfactory because they altered the evolution kernel while ostensibly evolving the same operators as those in the high-energy OPE. The present framework enables precision studies of gluon saturation at current and future colliders, notably the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), and can be extended to N^2LO accuracy using the recently computed BK kernel at that order (Caron-Huot 2016, Brunello 2025).
