# Nuclear constraints on non-Newtonian gravity at femtometer scale

> Effects of the non-Newtonian gravity on properties of finite nuclei are studied by consistently incorporating both the direct and exchange contribution of the Yukawa potential in the Hartree-Fock approach using a well-tested Skyrme force for the strong interaction. It is shown for the first time that the strength of the Yukawa term in the non-Newtonian gravity is limited to $\log(|α|)<1.75/[λ(\rm fm)]^{0.54} + 33.6$ within the length scale of $λ=1-10$ fm in order for the calculated properties of finite nuclei not to be in conflict with accurate experimental data available.

## Key claim

The paper derives the first quantitative nuclear constraint on the strength of a non-Newtonian Yukawa-type gravitational potential at femtometer scales, showing that the allowed strength parameter \(\alpha\) must satisfy \(\log(|\alpha|) < 1.75/[\lambda(\text{fm})]^{0.54} + 33.6\) for \(\lambda = 1-10\) fm, based on the requirement that gravity's contribution to nuclear binding energies and charge radii must not exceed the approximately \(2\%\) uncertainty with which existing nuclear models reproduce experimental data.

## Method

The authors incorporated both the direct and exchange contributions of the Yukawa potential into a Hartree-Fock (HF) calculation using the MSL0 Skyrme force for the strong interaction. They solved the Schrödinger equation with modified single-particle potentials, effective masses, and spin-orbit form factors arising from the Yukawa term, using a density-matrix expansion to evaluate the exchange contribution. The charge radii and binding energies of \(^{208}\text{Pb}\), \(^{120}\text{Sn}\), and \(^{48}\text{Ca}\) were computed for varying Yukawa strength \(\alpha\) at fixed length scales \(\lambda = 1\) fm and \(10\) fm, and the results were compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear upper limit on the Yukawa strength from binding energies is \(\log(|\alpha|) < 1.75/[\lambda(\text{fm})]^{0.54} + 33.6\) for \(\lambda = 1-10\) fm.
- The constraint from charge radii is \(\log(|\alpha|) < 1.18/[\lambda(\text{fm})]^{0.79} + 35.0\) for the same length range.
- The binding-energy constraint translates to a limit on the boson-nucleon coupling constant: \(\log(g^2) < 0.10 [\mu (\text{MeV})]^{0.54} - 3.53\) for boson masses between \(20\) MeV and \(200\) MeV.
- The Yukawa potential with \(\alpha = -1.24 \times 10^{36}\) and \(\lambda = 10\) fm gives a coupling \(g^2/4\pi = 1/137\), matching the Coulomb coupling between two protons at short distances.

## Context

This work extends experimental tests of the inverse-square law of gravity, which previously reached down to only about 10 fm via neutron scattering experiments (Barber 1975, Nesvizhevsky 2004, Pokotilovski 2006, Kamiya 2008, Nesvizhevsky 2008), into the previously unexplored 1-10 fm region. The constraint provides a reference for testing non-Newtonian gravity theories such as those with extra dimensions (Fujii 1971), scalar-tensor-vector gravity (Wolf 2006), and \(f(R)\) gravity (Capozziello 2009), and it limits the properties of weakly-coupled gauge bosons (axions or U-bosons) that could mediate such a Yukawa potential.
