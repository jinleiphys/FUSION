# Hadron production in pp and p-Pb collisions: A mass dependent phenomenon

> The mass dependence plays a significant role in the yield enhancement or suppression of hadrons in pp and p-Pb collisions at the LHC energies. This has been observed by parameterizing the variation of yield ratios between any two hadrons with event charged-particle multiplicity using a single empirical function. We notice that this variation is independent of all quantum numbers and solely depends on masses of hadrons and masses of their valence quarks. The function shows that the amount of quark deconfinement increases with event multiplicity, and the quark coalescence favours more the production of heavier hadrons compared to lighter ones.

# Hadron production in pp and p-Pb collisions: A mass dependent phenomenon

## Key claim
The variation of hadron yield ratios with event charged-particle multiplicity in pp and p-Pb collisions can be described by a single empirical function that depends only on hadron masses and valence quark masses, independent of any quantum numbers. The function suggests that quark coalescence favors heavier hadron production at high multiplicity, while fragmentation suppresses heavier hadron production at low multiplicity, but the authors caution that the function may not apply to events with non-zero net baryon density.

## Method
The authors parameterized published ALICE data on primary yield ratios of hadrons to pions (p/π, Kₛ⁰/π, Λ/π, Ξ/π, Ω/π) as a function of charged-particle multiplicity density ⟨dNch/dη⟩ in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV and p-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV. They constructed an empirical function with two terms: one depending on valence quark masses (dominant at high multiplicity) and one depending on hadron masses (dominant at low multiplicity), and performed a simultaneous fit to five data sets with three free parameters.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit parameter values: a = 1.35 ± 0.13, b = 1.84 ± 0.11, c = 0.18 ± 0.034, with χ²/ndf = 0.38 for the simultaneous fit
- Valence quark masses used in the function: u = 0.0022 GeV/c², d = 0.0047 GeV/c², s = 0.096 GeV/c² (from PDG)
- For individual ratio predictions, χ²/ndf values are below 1 for all ratios except Ω/π, which is 1.07
- Data used span pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV and p-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, with measurements at mid-rapidity |y| < 0.5

## Context
This work extends the observation of strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity pp collisions reported by ALICE (2017, 2019) and p-Pb collisions, where yields of strange and multi-strange particles relative to pions increase with multiplicity. The authors provide a unified parameterization that goes beyond the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) explanation of strangeness canonical suppression, which was shown insufficient to account for φ meson enhancement in heavy-ion collisions. The function predicts that the mass-dependent pattern should also appear in charm hadron production, suggesting future measurements of heavy flavor in pp collisions could test the model's predictive capability.
