# Chiral Relaxation Time at the Crossover of Quantum Chromodynamics

> We study microscopic processes responsible for chirality flips in the thermal bath of Quantum Chromodynamics at finite temperature and zero baryon chemical potential. We focus on the temperature range where the crossover from chirally broken phase to quark-gluon plasma takes place, namely $T \simeq (150, 200)$ MeV. The processes we consider are quark-quark scatterings mediated by collective excitations with the quantum number of pions and $σ$-meson, hence we refer to these processes simply as \sugg{to} one-pion (one-$σ$) exchange\sugg{s}. We use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to compute equilibrium properties of the thermal bath, as well as the relevant scattering kernel to be used in the collision integral to estimate the chiral relaxation time $τ$. We find $τ\simeq 0.1 ÷1$ fm/c around the chiral crossover.

# Chiral Relaxation Time at the Crossover of Quantum Chromodynamics

## Key claim

The chiral relaxation time τ is estimated to be 0.1–1 fm/c around the QCD chiral crossover (T ≈ 150–200 MeV) using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, but this result depends on the assumption that one-pion and one-σ exchange are the dominant chirality-flipping processes and that the chiral chemical potential μ₅ is negligibly small compared to temperature.

## Method

The authors compute the chiral relaxation time τ by evaluating the Boltzmann collision integral for quark-quark scattering mediated by collective excitations with pion and σ-meson quantum numbers, using a two-flavor NJL model to determine the thermal bath properties (constituent quark mass, chiral condensate) and the in-medium meson propagators via the random phase approximation. The scattering kernel is expanded to first order in μ₅/T, and the collision rate Γ = 1/τ is obtained from the linear response of the chiral density to a small chiral chemical potential. A separate calculation merges the NJL model below the critical temperature with a quasiparticle model above it, where the thermal mass is fitted to lattice data on pressure, entropy, and energy density.

## Key numbers

- τ ≈ 0.1–1 fm/c in the temperature range T ≈ 150–200 MeV (the chiral crossover region).
- The NJL model parameters used are: Λ = 653 MeV (hard cutoff), m₀ = 5.39 MeV (bare quark mass), and G = 2.14/Λ² (NJL coupling constant).
- The chiral density at vanishing quark mass is given by n₅ = N_c N_f μ₅ T²/3, with N_c = 3 and N_f = 2.
- The relaxation time decreases with increasing temperature, despite partial restoration of chiral symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral relaxation in the quark-gluon plasma by Manuel (2015), who considered gluon- and photon-mediated Compton scattering, by showing that pion-mediated processes are one to two orders of magnitude faster near the chiral crossover. The results contradict the simple ansatz τ ∝ 1/M_q used in Ruggieri (2016), which neglected the opening of phase space at finite temperature. The computed relaxation time is essential for determining the equilibrium chiral density n₅^(eq) in systems with parallel electric and magnetic fields, where the chiral anomaly generates a chiral imbalance.
