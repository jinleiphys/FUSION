# Implication the observed $ψ(3770)\to p\bar{p}π^0$ for studying the $p\bar{p}\to ψ(3770)π^0$ process

> We study the charmonium $p \bar{p} \to ψ(3770) π^0$ reaction using effective lagrangian approach where the contributions from well established $N^*$ states are considered, and all parameters are fixed in the process of $e^+e^- \to p \bar{p}π^0$ at center of mass energy $\sqrt{s} = 3.773$ GeV. The experimental data on the line shape of the mass distribution of the $e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}π^0$ can be well reproduced. Based on the studying of $e^+e^- \to p \bar{p}π^0$, the total and differential cross sections of the $p \bar{p} \to ψ(3770) π^0$ reaction are predicted. At the same time we evaluated also the cross sections of the $p \bar{p} \to ψ(3686) π^0$ reaction. It is shown that the contribution of nucleon pole to this reaction is largest close to the reaction threshold. However, the interference between nucleon pole and the other nucleon resonance can still change the angle distributions significantly. Those theoretical results may be test by the future experiments at $\overline{\mbox{P}}$ANDA.

## Key claim

The observed mass distribution of \(e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}\pi^0\) at \(\sqrt{s}=3.773\) GeV can be well reproduced by including contributions from the nucleon pole and five well-established \(N^*\) resonances, and the resulting fitted parameters allow predictions for the \(p\bar{p} \to \psi(3770)\pi^0\) reaction; however, the predictions depend on the assumption that the same hadronic form factors apply to both the \(e^+e^-\) and \(p\bar{p}\) processes, and the form factors themselves cannot be determined without ambiguities due to the lack of available experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with vector meson dominance for the \(\gamma\psi(3770)\) coupling and included contributions from the nucleon pole (\(N(938)\)) and five \(N^*\) states: \(N(1440)\), \(N(1520)\), \(N(1535)\), \(N(1650)\), and \(N(1720)\). They performed a 15-parameter \(\chi^2\) fit to the BESIII data on the \(p\pi^0\) and \(\bar{p}\pi^0\) invariant mass distributions of \(e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}\pi^0\) at \(\sqrt{s}=3.773\) GeV, extracting the products of coupling constants \(g_{N^*} = g_{\psi(3770)NN^*}g_{\pi NN^*}\) and relative phases. Using cross symmetry, they then applied these same parameters to predict the total and differential cross sections for \(p\bar{p} \to \psi(3770)\pi^0\) and, after a separate fit to \(\psi(3686)\to p\bar{p}\pi^0\) data, for \(p\bar{p} \to \psi(3686)\pi^0\).

## Key numbers

- Total cross section of \(e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}\pi^0\) at \(\sqrt{s}=3.773\) GeV: \(\sigma_{\text{total}} = 7.71\) pb (experimental value from BESIII, used to convert event counts to differential cross sections).
- Fitted cutoff parameter for form factors: \(\beta = 6.2 \pm 3.5\) (from the \(\psi(3770)\) fit, with \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.03\)).
- Fitted coupling product for the nucleon pole in the \(\psi(3770)\) process: \(g_{N(938)} = (8.00 \pm 0.46) \times 10^{-3}\).
- Predicted total cross section for \(p\bar{p} \to \psi(3770)\pi^0\) at \(E_{\text{cm}} = 5.26\) GeV: \(0.056\) nb.
- Coupling constant for \(\psi(3686)NN\): \(g_{\psi(3686)NN} = 9.4 \times 10^{-4}\), determined from \(B(\psi(3686)\to p\bar{p}) = 2.8 \times 10^{-4}\).
- Branching fraction for \(\psi(3686) \to p\bar{p}\pi^0\) via the nucleon pole only: \(7.5 \times 10^{-5}\) (our result); experimental value from BESIII: \(6.42^{+0.20+1.78}_{-0.20-1.28} \times 10^{-5}\).

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-pole exchange model previously applied to \(p\bar{p} \to \pi^0 J/\psi\) by Wiele et al. (2013) to the higher charmonium states \(\psi(3770)\) and \(\psi(3686)\), and it uses the BESIII data on \(e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}\pi^0\) (Ablikim et al., 2014) and \(\psi(3686) \to p\bar{p}\pi^0\) (Ablikim et al., 2012) to constrain the \(N^*\) couplings. The predictions for \(p\bar{p} \to \psi(3770)\pi^0\) and \(p\bar{p} \to \psi(3686)\pi^0\) are intended to guide future experimental searches at the PANDA facility, where the interference between the nucleon pole and \(N^*\) resonances is shown to significantly alter the angular distributions compared to a pure nucleon-pole calculation.
