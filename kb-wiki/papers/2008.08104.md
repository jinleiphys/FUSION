# The role of the chiral anomaly in polarized deeply inelastic scattering I: Finding the triangle graph inside the box diagram in Bjorken and Regge asymptotics

> We revisit the role of the chiral "triangle" anomaly in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) of electrons off polarized protons employing a powerful worldline formalism. We demonstrate how the triangle anomaly appears at high energies in the DIS box diagram for the polarized proton structure function $g_1(x_B,Q^2)$ in both the Bjorken limit of large $Q^2$ and in the Regge limit of small $x_B$. We show that the operator product expansion is not required to extract the anomaly in either asymptotics though it is sufficient in the Bjorken limit. Likewise, the infrared pole in the anomaly arises in both limits. The leading contribution to $g_1$, in both Bjorken and Regge asymptotics, is therefore given by the expectation value of the topological charge density, generalizing a result previously argued by Jaffe and Manohar to hold for the first moment of $g_1$. In follow-up work, we will show how our results motivate the derivation of a helicity-dependent effective action incorporating the physics of the anomaly at small $x_B$ and shall discuss the QCD evolution of $g_1(x_B,Q^2)$ in this framework.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the chiral triangle anomaly provides the leading contribution to the polarized proton structure function \(g_1(x_B,Q^2)\) in both the Bjorken limit of large \(Q^2\) and the Regge limit of small \(x_B\), without requiring the operator product expansion (OPE) to extract the anomaly in either asymptotic regime, though the OPE is sufficient in the Bjorken limit.

## Method

The authors employ a worldline formalism to compute the antisymmetric part of the hadron tensor for polarized deeply inelastic scattering, representing the box diagram as a functional integral over bosonic and Grassmann worldline variables coupled to background electromagnetic and gluon fields. They expand the worldline action to second order in the background gluon field and evaluate the resulting four-point function \(\Gamma^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}_A[k_1,k_3,k_2,k_4]\) corresponding to the box diagram, then take the Bjorken limit (\(Q^2 \to \infty\) with fixed \(x_B\)) and the Regge limit (\(x_B \to 0\) with fixed \(Q^2\)) by collapsing pairs of worldline interaction points in each limit.

## Key numbers

- The first moment of \(g_1\) is expressed as \(\int_0^1 dx_B\, g_1(x_B,Q^2) = \frac{1}{18} (3F + D + 2\Sigma(Q^2))\), where \(\Sigma(Q^2)\) is the net light quark helicity.
- Experimental extraction from COMPASS gives \(2\Sigma(Q^2) = 0.32 \pm 0.03(\text{stat.}) \pm 0.03(\text{syst.})\) at \(Q^2 = 3\) GeV\(^2\).
- Experimental extraction from HERMES gives \(2\Sigma(Q^2) = 0.330 \pm 0.011(\text{th.}) \pm 0.025(\text{exp.}) \pm 0.028(\text{evol.})\) at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The naive quark model expectation is \(2\Sigma(Q^2) = 0.6 \pm 0.12\), significantly above the experimental values.
- The intrinsic non-perturbative scale of QCD is \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} \approx 200\) MeV, used to characterize the Bjorken limit condition \(u_1 - u_3 \sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2/Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the chiral anomaly in polarized DIS beyond the first moment of \(g_1\) (previously addressed by Jaffe and Manohar (1989)) to the structure function \(g_1(x_B,Q^2)\) itself in both Bjorken and Regge asymptotics. The authors build on their own prior worldline formalism for unpolarized DIS (Tarasov and Venugopalan, 2019) and provide a quantitative treatment of the triangle anomaly at small \(x_B\) that had been lacking despite qualitative discussions by Bass (1997, 2004) and Wakamatsu (2019). The results motivate a helicity-dependent effective action for small \(x_B\) and will inform the QCD evolution of \(g_1\) in follow-up work, with direct relevance to future polarized DIS experiments at the Electron Ion Collider.
