# Lindblad-driven quarkonium production in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the production of the conventional quarkonium states in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions using an open quantum system framework based on the Lindblad equation. Starting from the complex-valued in-medium potential, we derive the dissociation temperature and thermal decay width for each state, and compute their survival probabilities for a system undergoing Bjorken expansion. We then extend the framework to include recombination from thermalized charm and bottom quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, deriving a coalescence model for quarkonia from the Lindblad equation under the adiabatic approximation. The methodology provides a unified, first-principles-inspired description of suppression and recombination for both charmonium and bottomonium.

# Lindblad-driven quarkonium production in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper derives a unified open-quantum-system framework for both suppression and recombination of conventional quarkonium states in heavy-ion collisions, but the recombination contribution is significant only for charmonium (J/ψ) and negligible for bottomonium (Υ(1S)) because the bottom-quark multiplicity at LHC energies is roughly two orders of magnitude smaller than the charm-quark multiplicity.

## Method

The authors start from the complex-valued in-medium heavy-quark potential (Gauss law model of Lafferty and Rothkopf) and solve the frequency-space Schrödinger equation to obtain in-medium spectral functions, from which binding energies, thermal decay widths, and dissociation temperatures are extracted for each state. They then embed these results in a Lindblad equation framework, deriving a coalescence model for recombination under the adiabatic approximation, and compute the nuclear modification factor R_AA for Pb–Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV using Bjorken expansion with a Glauber-model transverse temperature profile.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass m_c^fit = 1.4692 GeV, bottom quark mass m_b^RS' = 4.882 GeV
- Vacuum Cornell potential parameters: α̃_s = 0.513 ± 0.002, √σ = 0.412 ± 0.004 GeV, c = −0.161 ± 0.003 GeV
- J/ψ dissociation temperature T_d ≈ 372 MeV; ψ(2S) dissociation temperature T_d ≲ 155 MeV
- Υ(1S) dissociation temperature T_d ≈ 564 MeV; Υ(2S) ≈ 223 MeV; Υ(3S) ≈ 164 MeV; Υ(4S) < 155 MeV
- Initial conditions for Pb–Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: T_0 = 500 MeV, t_0 = 0.6 fm
- Charm quark thermal relaxation time τ_c^therm ≈ 4 fm; bottom quark τ_b^therm ≈ 12 fm
- pp cross sections at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: dσ_{pp→J/ψ}/dy|_y=0 = 5.64 μb (prompt); dσ_{pp→Υ(1S)}/dy|_y=0 = 60.2 nb
- Open heavy flavor cross sections: dσ_{pp→c\bar{c}}/dy|_|y|<0.5 = 1.165 mb; dσ_{pp→b\bar{b}}/dy|_|y|<0.5 = 34.5 μb

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Lindblad-equation treatment of the exotic state X(3872) (Armesto 2024, Armesto 2025) to conventional quarkonium ground states, using the Gauss law complex potential parametrization of Lafferty and Rothkopf (2019). The framework unifies suppression via the imaginary part of the potential (Landau damping) with a coalescence model derived from the Lindblad equation under the adiabatic approximation, providing a first-principles-inspired alternative to phenomenological transport approaches such as those of Grandchamp (2003). The results enable quantitative predictions for R_AA of J/ψ and Υ(1S) that can be compared with ALICE and CMS data at LHC energies.
