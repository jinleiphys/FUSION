# The effect of single-particle space-momentum angle distribution on two-pion HBT correlation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Using several source models, we analyze the transverse momentum dependence of HBT radii in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The results indicate that the single-particle space-momentum angle distribution plays an important role in the transverse momentum dependence of HBT radii. In a cylinder source, we use several formulas to describe the transverse momentum dependence of HBT radii and the single-particle space-momentum angle distribution. We also make a numerical connection between them in the transverse plane.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the transverse momentum dependence of HBT radii in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is primarily caused by the narrowing of the single-particle space-momentum angle distribution with increasing transverse momentum, rather than solely by collective flow or source lifetime effects, and that this connection can be numerically parameterized in the transverse plane.

## Method

The authors used the CRAB (Correlation After Burner) code to generate two-pion correlation functions from several source models: a Gaussian source, a space-momentum angle correlation source with a cut-off function w(Δφ), a homogeneous expansion source, and a cylinder expansion source. They extracted HBT radii (R_o, R_s, R_l) by fitting the correlation functions with a Gaussian form in different transverse momentum bins, and simultaneously analyzed the single-particle space-momentum angle distributions (Δφ in 3D, Δθ in the transverse plane) by fitting them with an exponential function f(Δφ) = c₁ exp(c₂ cos(Δφ)). They then established numerical relations between the power-law exponent b of the HBT radii pT-dependence and the fit parameters c₁, c₂ of the angle distributions.

## Key numbers

- Gaussian source radius R = 6.0 fm, temperature T = 100 MeV, pion mass m = 139.58 MeV/c², with 9 pT bins in the range 125 MeV/c < pT < 625 MeV/c.
- For the Gaussian source with Δt = 0 fm/c, HBT radii are almost equal to the source radii (≈6 fm) and show no pT dependence; with Δt = 6 fm/c, R_o increases gradually with pT while R_s and R_l change little.
- For the space-momentum angle correlation source with α = π/2, R_o values are lower than R_s and R_l and are mostly flat as a function of pT; with α = π/3, R_s and R_l become smaller while R_o changes slightly.
- For the cylinder expansion source, the fit parameter c₂ for the normalized cos(Δθ) distribution ranges from 0.0339 ± 0.0003 (η_Tmax = 0.1003, pT 150-250 MeV) to 0.3480 ± 0.0006 (η_Tmax = 0.6931, pT 450-600 MeV).
- The power-law exponent b for R_out and R_side is related to the c₁ and c₂ fit parameters via equations such as b(k₁) = μ₁₁ k₁^μ₁₂ with μ₁₁ = -0.026 ± 0.001 and μ₁₂ = -24.3 ± 0.5 for R_out.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of HBT interferometry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on the blast-wave model (Siemens and Rasmussen, 1979) and prior HBT analyses (e.g., Zhang et al., 2016; Bialas, 2015). It directly addresses the observation that HBT radii shrink with increasing transverse momentum, which is typically associated with collective flow (Lisa et al., 2008; Heinz and Kolb, 2002). The paper provides a new numerical framework connecting the single-particle space-momentum angle distribution to the pT-dependence of HBT radii in the transverse plane, which could enable extraction of the angle distribution from experimental HBT data or prediction of HBT radii from theoretical angle distributions, particularly relevant for the Beam Energy Scan Phase II (BES-II) at RHIC where flow is weaker.
