# A New Correlator to Detect and Characterize the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> A charge-sensitive in-event correlator is proposed and tested for its efficacy to detect and characterize charge separation associated with the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in heavy ion collisions. Tests, performed with the aid of two reaction models, indicate discernible responses for background- and CME-driven charge separation, relative to the second- ($Ψ_{2}$) and third-order ($Ψ_{3}$) event planes, which could serve to identify the CME. The tests also indicate a degree of sensitivity which would enable robust characterization of the CME via Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model comparisons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new charge-sensitive correlator, \(R_{\Psi_m}(\Delta S)\), designed to give distinct, discernible responses for background-driven versus CME-driven charge separation relative to the second-order (\(\Psi_2\)) and third-order (\(\Psi_3\)) event planes, which could allow unambiguous identification of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors construct the correlator \(R_{\Psi_m}(\Delta S)\) as the ratio of two correlation functions: \(C_{\Psi_m}(\Delta S)\), which quantifies charge separation parallel to the magnetic field (perpendicular to \(\Psi_2\)), and \(C_{\Psi_m}^{\perp}(\Delta S)\), which quantifies charge separation perpendicular to the magnetic field (parallel to \(\Psi_2\)). The correlation functions are built from the distribution of a charge-separation observable \(\Delta S\) in real events divided by the same distribution after random charge shuffling. The method is tested using the AMPT model (background only) and the AVFD model (which includes anomalous transport from the CME) for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- For 30-40% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV, the AVFD model shows a change from a convex-shaped \(R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S)\) distribution for background-driven charge separation (\(a_1 = 0\)) to a concave-shaped distribution when a modest input CME-driven charge separation of \(a_1 = 1.0\%\) is introduced.
- For 40-50% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV, a rudimentary comparison of the \(R(\Delta S)\) correlators from preliminary STAR data and AVFD calculations suggests the presence of a CME-driven charge separation with \(a_1 = 1\%\).
- The widths \(\sigma_{R_{\Psi_2}}\) of the \(R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S'')\) distributions for AVFD events at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV increase with collision centrality (10-20%, 30-40%, 50-60%), reflecting the trend expected for the magnitude of the magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing search for the Chiral Magnetic Effect, which has been inconclusive using the standard \(\gamma_{\alpha\beta}\) correlator because it gives the same qualitative response to both background and CME-driven charge separation, as noted in measurements at RHIC (Abelev 2009, Adamczyk 2013) and the LHC (Abelev 2012, Khachatryan 2016). The new \(R_{\Psi_m}(\Delta S)\) correlator is designed to overcome this ambiguity by providing distinct responses for signal and background, and its sensitivity enables robust characterization of the CME via comparisons with the AVFD model (Jiang 2016). This work enables future differential measurements across different collision systems and energies at RHIC and the LHC, including upcoming isobaric collision experiments.
