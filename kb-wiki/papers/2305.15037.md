# Ab initio study of nuclear clustering in hot dilute nuclear matter

> We present a systematic ab initio study of clustering in hot dilute nuclear matter using nuclear lattice effective field theory with an SU(4)-symmetric interaction. We introduce a method called light-cluster distillation to determine the abundances of dimers, trimers, and alpha clusters as a function of density and temperature. Our lattice results are compared with an ideal gas model composed of free nucleons and clusters. Excellent agreement is found at very low density, while deviations from ideal gas abundances appear at increasing density due to cluster-nucleon and cluster-cluster interactions. In addition to determining the composition of hot dilute nuclear matter as a function of density and temperature, the lattice calculations also serve as benchmarks for virial expansion calculations, statistical models, and transport models of fragmentation and clustering in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first ab initio calculations of the cluster composition of hot dilute nuclear matter, using nuclear lattice effective field theory with an SU(4)-symmetric interaction rather than high-fidelity chiral forces; the authors note that extension to realistic chiral forces is straightforward but requires new algorithms currently under development.

## Method

The authors perform nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) simulations using a Wigner SU(4)-symmetric Hamiltonian with lattice spacing 1.32 fm and averaged twisted boundary conditions, employing the pinhole trace algorithm to compute canonical ensemble expectation values at fixed nucleon number and temperature. They introduce a method called light-cluster distillation, which fits computed correlation functions (G11, G21, G31, G22) as weighted sums of correlation functions for individual dimers, trimers, and alpha clusters in their ground states, extracting the cluster abundances w2, w3, and w4 via least-squares fitting.

## Key numbers

* At density ρ = 0.007 fm⁻³ and temperature T = 10 MeV, the correlation functions show a strong peak at short distances consistent with a gas of small clusters (A=16 nucleons in box L=10).
* Lattice simulations are performed at temperatures from 10 to 20 MeV for densities ρ = 0.007 and 0.014 fm⁻³.
* The heavier-cluster mass fraction with A > 4 is estimated to reach about 10% at density 0.01 fm⁻³ and temperature 10 MeV.
* At low densities around ρ ≃ 0.001 fm⁻³, the agreement between lattice results and ideal gas predictions is excellent for all cluster types.
* Notable deviations from ideal gas abundances appear around ρ ≃ 0.007 fm⁻³ for all types of clusters.
* At T = 5 MeV with box length L=10, for A ≤ 4 and Z ≤ 1, deviations between lattice and ideal gas results for dimers are negligible; for A=4 and Z=2, lattice results give slightly lower dimer abundances than the ideal gas.

## Context

This work extends previous NLEFT calculations that determined the equation of state and phase diagram of nuclear matter (Lu 2019) to now include the first ab initio determination of cluster composition. The results serve as benchmarks for virial expansion calculations (Horowitz 2005), statistical models, and transport models of fragmentation and clustering in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and enable future studies using high-fidelity chiral nuclear forces combined with perturbative quantum Monte Carlo and wave function matching techniques.
