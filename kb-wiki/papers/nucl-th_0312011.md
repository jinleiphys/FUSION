# Structure studies of conventional and novel excitations to the continuum in reactions with unstable nuclei

> The themes covered in the present thesis are: a detailed study of double phonon giant resonances in normal nuclei, the enhancement of the excitation of Giant Pairing Vibrations using weakly-bound nuclei, an extension of the Steinwedel-Jensen model for Giant Dipole Resonances to treat surface effects and presence of neutron skin and, finally, a model on breakup reactions in weakly-bound dicluster nuclei.

## Key claim

This thesis investigates how the continuum spectrum of nuclei, both stable and unstable, can be exploited to study conventional giant resonances and novel excitation modes, with the central finding that using weakly-bound unstable projectiles enhances the excitation of the Giant Pairing Vibration due to optimal Q-value matching, and that including nuclear surface diffuseness in the Steinwedel-Jensen model strongly modifies predicted giant dipole resonance energies even for stable nuclei.

## Method

The author develops a semiclassical model based on the Alder-Winther formalism for heavy-ion collisions, solving coupled-channel equations in perturbation theory along classical trajectories determined by Coulomb plus nuclear potentials. For the giant pairing vibration study, RPA and BCS+RPA structure calculations of monopole states in the continuum are combined with coupled-channel calculations for two-neutron transfer reactions using both a conventional 14C beam and an unstable 6He beam. For the giant dipole resonance, the Steinwedel-Jensen model is extended to include nuclear surface diffuseness and neutron skin effects. For dicluster nuclei, a simple cluster model with an effective Coulomb plus Woods-Saxon nuclear potential is used to compute properties of 6Li and 7Li.

## Key numbers

* Double Giant Quadrupole Resonance in 40Ca+40Ca at 44 MeV/n: observed as a bump at 34 MeV excitation energy
* Double Giant Dipole Resonance in 136Xe on 208Pb at 700 MeV/n: found at 28 ± 1 MeV with width 6 ± 2 MeV and cross section 175 ± 50 mb
* Double Giant Dipole Resonance in 208Pb on 208Pb at 640 MeV/n: found at 23.8 MeV with width about 6 MeV and cross section about 350 mb
* For the reaction 40Ar + 208Pb at 40 MeV/n exciting the GQR in lead: deformation parameters βC = βN = 0.07, with maximum destructive interference between Coulomb and nuclear fields at impact parameter b ≈ 12.5 fm
* The single GQR energy in lead is taken as 11 MeV for the bombarding energy dependence study

## Context

This work extends the study of double phonon giant resonances first observed experimentally by Bothe and Gentner (1937) for the GDR and by the LAND collaboration at GSI for the double GDR, where standard harmonic models systematically underestimate cross sections by a factor of two. The thesis addresses this discrepancy by examining nuclear coupling effects and strength distribution spreading. The giant pairing vibration study builds on prior (t,p) transfer reaction searches that yielded unpublished results, proposing that weakly-bound projectiles like 6He can enhance the excitation. The modified Steinwedel-Jensen model for GDR with surface diffuseness extends the original model of Steinwedel and Jensen (1950) and the Goldhaber-Teller model (1948). The dicluster breakup study connects to experimental work at Laboratori Nazionali di Legnaro on breakup reactions of light weakly-bound nuclei, confirming older results obtained in halo nuclei.
