# Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes of Upsilons

> Based on lattice non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) studies we present results for Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for $Υ(1S)$, $Υ(2S)$ and $Υ(3S)$ in vacuum as well as in quark-gluon plasma. Our study is based on 2+1 flavor $48^3 \times 12$ lattices generated using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action and with a pion mass of $161$ MeV. At zero temperature the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes follow the expectations based on non-relativistic potential models. At non-zero temperatures, the interpretation of Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes turns out to be more nuanced, but consistent with our previous lattice QCD study of excited Upsilons in quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes of bottomonium states at zero temperature closely resemble potential-model wave functions, but at non-zero temperature the correlator used to define the amplitude cannot be simply interpreted as an in-medium wave function; the Euclidean time dependence of the correlator must be studied to avoid misleading conclusions about the survival of excited Upsilons in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors used lattice non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) on 2+1 flavor HISQ ensembles with a pion mass of 161 MeV and lattice spacing 0.1088 fm. They computed mixed correlators between optimized bottomonium operators and point-split meson operators with quark-antiquark separation r, then extracted Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes at zero temperature and studied the temperature and Euclidean time dependence of these correlators at six temperatures between 151 and 334 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 161 MeV (continuum limit value)
- Lattice spacing: 0.1088 fm (zero temperature, beta=6.74)
- Effective bottom quark mass extracted from BS amplitudes: 5.52 +/- 0.33 GeV (using Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(2S))
- Effective bottom quark mass from Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(3S): 5.82 +/- 0.51 GeV
- Temperatures studied: 151, 172, 199, 251, and 334 MeV (all on 48^3 x 12 lattices)
- Zero temperature lattice: 48^4, 192 gauge configurations with 8 sources each
- Finite temperature ensembles: 384 configurations each

## Context

This work extends previous lattice NRQCD studies of bottomonium spectral functions in the quark-gluon plasma by Larsen et al. (2019) and earlier studies of charmonium Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes at non-zero temperature by Umeda (2000), Ohno (2008), and others. The results support the potential model description for bottomonium in vacuum, consistent with the static potential from Wilson loops by Bazavov (2014). The findings enable future work on reconstructing in-medium potentials from lattice data and clarify the limitations of interpreting Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes as wave functions at high temperature.
