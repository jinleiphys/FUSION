# Low-energy structure and $β$ decay properties of neutron-rich nuclei in the region of a shape phase transition

> The low-energy structure and $β$ decay properties of the neutron-rich even-mass nuclei near the neutron number $N=60$ that are experimentally of much interest are investigated within the framework of the nuclear density functional theory and the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model. By using the results of the constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations based on the relativistic energy density functional, the interacting-boson Hamiltonian describing the even-even core nuclei, the boson-fermion, and the fermion-fermion interactions are determined. The Gamow-Teller transition strengths, with the corresponding operator being constructed without introducing further phenomenological adjustment, are computed by using the wave functions of the initial and final nuclei of the $β$ decay. The triaxial quadrupole potential energy surfaces computed for the $N=60$ even-even isotones suggest a pronounced $γ$ softness. The calculated energy spectra for the even-even and odd-odd nuclei in the Rb to Cd isotopic chains exhibit an abrupt change in nuclear structure around $N=60$, as suggested experimentally. The predicted $β$-decay $\log{}_{10}ft$ values underestimate the measured values for the nuclei with low $Z$ and with $N \leqslant 60$, exhibit a rapid increase for $N>60$, reflecting the nuclear structure evolution, and agree rather well with the measured values for those nuclei with $Z$ being not far from the proton major shell closure $Z=50$. Sensitivity of the predicted $β$-decay properties to the model assumptions and parameters employed in the nuclear structure calculations is discussed, specifically, by comparing results obtained based on the different choices of the underlying energy density functional.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a consistent description of low-energy structure and β-decay properties for neutron-rich nuclei near N=60 can be achieved by combining relativistic density functional theory with the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model, but the predicted log₁₀ft values systematically underestimate measured values for nuclei with low Z and N ≤ 60, while agreeing well only for nuclei with Z near the Z=50 shell closure.

## Method

The author performed constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force to obtain triaxial quadrupole potential energy surfaces for even-even Kr to Cd isotopes with neutron numbers 54 ≤ N ≤ 64. These mean-field results were mapped onto an interacting boson model (IBM-2) Hamiltonian, which was then extended to the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM-2) by including single-particle degrees of freedom for odd nucleons and a residual neutron-proton interaction. The Gamow-Teller β-decay operator was constructed using the same occupation probabilities from the mean-field calculations without introducing additional phenomenological parameters.

## Key numbers

- The triaxial quadrupole potential energy surfaces for N=60 isotones from ⁹⁶Kr to ¹⁰⁸Cd show pronounced γ softness, with ¹⁰⁴Ru exhibiting a triaxial minimum around γ ≈ 30°
- The Inglis-Belyaev moment of inertia from RHB calculations was increased by 30% to match observed moments of inertia
- Boson effective charges were set to eᵇ_ν = eᵇ_π = 0.0577 e·b, and fermion effective charges to eᶠ_ν = 0.5 e·b and eᶠ_π = 1.5 e·b, taken from previous IBFFM-2 calculations for Ge and As nuclei
- The separable pairing force strength was set to 728 MeV·fm³ to reproduce the pairing gap from the Gogny D1S interaction
- The predicted β-decay log₁₀ft values underestimate measured values for nuclei with low Z and N ≤ 60, show a rapid increase for N > 60, and agree well with measurements for nuclei with Z near the proton major shell closure Z=50

## Context

This work extends the author's previous mapped IBM framework applied to γ-soft nuclei near Ba and Xe regions (Nomura 2020), neutron-deficient Ge and As nuclei (Nomura 2022), and neutron-rich Pd and Rh nuclei (Nomura 2022), as well as two-neutrino double-β decay studies (Nomura 2022). The study covers even-even and odd-odd nuclei from Z=36 (Kr) to Z=48 (Cd) with 54 ≤ N ≤ 64, a region where rapid shape phase transitions around N=60 are expected. The results enable systematic predictions of β-decay properties for r-process nucleosynthesis modeling and provide a framework for calculating nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-β decay candidates such as ⁹⁶Zr and ¹⁰⁰Mo.
