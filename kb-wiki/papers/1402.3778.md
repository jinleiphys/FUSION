# In-medium antikaon and eta-meson interactions and bound states

> The role played by subthreshold meson-baryon dynamics is demonstrated in kaonic-atom, Kbar-nuclear and eta-nuclear bound-state calculations within in-medium models of Kbar-N and eta-N interactions. New analyses of kaonic atom data reveal appreciable multi-nucleon contributions. Calculations of eta-nuclear bound states show, in particular, that the eta-N scattering length is not a useful indicator of whether or not eta mesons bind in nuclei nor of the widths anticipated for such states.

# In-medium antikaon and eta-meson interactions and bound states

## Key claim
Subthreshold meson-baryon dynamics, particularly the energy dependence of in-medium scattering amplitudes, is essential for correctly predicting kaonic-atom observables and nuclear bound states of antikaons and eta mesons; however, the calculated binding and widths are highly model-dependent, and the eta-nucleon scattering length is not a reliable predictor of whether eta mesons bind in nuclei or of the widths of such states.

## Method
The authors constructed in-medium meson-baryon scattering amplitudes using separable interactions matched to SU(3) chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, solving coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations with Pauli blocking and hadron self-energies. They then used these amplitudes as input to Klein-Gordon equations for kaonic atoms and meson-nuclear bound states, imposing self-consistency between the subthreshold energy shift (which depends on binding energies, kinetic energies, and the amplitude itself) and the density-dependent scattering amplitude. For kaonic atoms, they supplemented the one-nucleon in-medium amplitude with phenomenological many-nucleon contributions fitted to global atomic data.

## Key numbers
- The downward energy shift δ√s in kaonic atoms and η-nuclear bound states reaches approximately 40 MeV at 0.5ρ₀ (nuclear matter density ρ₀).
- For the K⁻-nuclear three-body system K⁻pp (I=1/2), the calculated ground-state binding energy is 4.3 MeV below the 11.4 MeV centroid of the I=0 KbarN quasibound state.
- Widths of single-Kbar few-body clusters are of order 40 MeV from KbarN → πY, with additional KbarNN → YN contributions up to about 10 MeV in K⁻pp and about 20 MeV in four-body systems.
- For many-body K⁻ quasibound states in Ca, the overall width is estimated in the range Γ_K ∼ 50–70 MeV including many-nucleon contributions.
- The ηN scattering length at threshold has a common imaginary part of approximately 0.2–0.3 fm across several models, but subthreshold imaginary parts differ substantially, leading to calculated 1s_η-nuclear widths ranging from below 5 MeV (GW model) to over 20 MeV (M1 model).

## Context
This work extends the program of the Prague-Jerusalem Collaboration (Cieplý, Mareš, Gazda, Friedman, Gal, Barnea) to incorporate medium modifications and self-consistent subthreshold dynamics into meson-nuclear bound-state calculations, building on earlier chiral-model fits by Ikeda, Hyodo and Weise (2011) and by Cieplý and Smejkal (2012). It contradicts the assumption that threshold scattering lengths suffice to predict binding, as argued by Wycech (1971) and others for kaonic atoms. The results enable more reliable predictions for experimental searches for Kbar- and η-nuclear bound states, such as those by the COSY-GEM collaboration (2009), and highlight the need to distinguish between competing ηN interaction models (GW, CS, M1, M2, IOV/GR) in future measurements.
