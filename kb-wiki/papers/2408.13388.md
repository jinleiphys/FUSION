# Neutral pion polarizabilities from four-point functions in lattice QCD

> We report a proof-of-principle lattice QCD simulation of the electric and magnetic polarizabilities for a neutral pion in the four-point function method. The results are based on the same quenched Wilson ensembles on a $24^3\times 48$ lattice at $β=6.0$ with pion mass from 1100 to 370 MeV previously used for a charged pion. For electric polarizability, the results are largely consistent with those from the background field method and ChPT. In contrast, there are significant differences for magnetic polarizability among the four-point function method, the background field method, and ChPT. The situation points to the potentially important role of disconnected diagrams for a neutral pion. We elucidate a transparent quark decomposition in the four-point function method that can be used to shed light on the issue.

## Key claim
This proof-of-principle lattice QCD simulation of neutral pion electric and magnetic polarizabilities using the four-point function method finds that electric polarizability results are largely consistent with the background field method and chiral perturbation theory, but magnetic polarizability results show significant discrepancies among the three approaches, potentially due to the important role of disconnected diagrams that are omitted in this study.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on quenched Wilson ensembles with a 24^3 x 48 lattice at beta=6.0, using pion masses from 1100 to 370 MeV. They derived new momentum-space formulas for neutral pion electric and magnetic polarizabilities from four-point correlation functions, applied them using conserved vector currents, and computed only the connected quark-line diagrams (diagrams A, B, and C) while leaving disconnected contributions (diagrams D through H) for future work. The polarizabilities were obtained by integrating the four-point functions over current separation time and extrapolating to zero momentum transfer using the two lowest momentum points.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses used: 1104.7(1.2) MeV, 795.0(1.1) MeV, 596.8(1.4) MeV, and 367.7(2.2) MeV
- Lattice size: 24^3 x 48 with inverse lattice spacing 1/a = 2.312 GeV
- Electric polarizability at the heaviest pion mass (1104.7 MeV): alpha_E = 0.716(3) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Electric polarizability at the lightest pion mass (367.7 MeV): alpha_E = 0.030(4) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Magnetic polarizability at the heaviest pion mass: beta_M = 1.048(2) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Magnetic polarizability at the lightest pion mass: beta_M = 0.61(1) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Chiral extrapolation to physical point gives alpha_E = -0.31(1) to -0.77(2) x 10^{-4} fm^3 and beta_M = 0.19(2) to 0.17(4) x 10^{-4} fm^3
- ChPT prediction at two-loop order for neutral pion: alpha_E = -0.40(18) x 10^{-4} fm^3, beta_M = 1.50(27) x 10^{-4} fm^3

## Context
This work extends the four-point function method previously applied to charged pions by Lee et al. (2023) to neutral pions, where the vanishing electromagnetic form factor eliminates Born contributions and elastic terms. The results for electric polarizability agree with background field calculations by Lujan et al. (2016), Alexandru et al. (2010), and Detmold et al. (2009), as well as with chiral perturbation theory. For magnetic polarizability, the results disagree with background field calculations by Bali et al. (2017) and Luschevskaya et al. (2016) but agree with Bignell et al. (2019), while differing from the improved eigenmode projection analysis by Bignell et al. (2020). The quark decomposition in the four-point function formalism enables direct separation of connected and disconnected contributions, which can help resolve the magnetic polarizability puzzle.
