# Solitons in relativistic mean field models

> Assuming that the nucleus can be treated as a perfect fluid we study the conditions for the formation and propagation of Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) solitons in nuclear matter. The KdV equation is obtained from the Euler and continuity equations in nonrelativistic hydrodynamics. The existence of these solitons depends on the nuclear equation of state, which, in our approach, comes from well known relativistic mean field models. We reexamine early works on nuclear solitons, replacing the old equations of state by new ones, based on QHD and on its variants. Our analysis suggests that KdV solitons may indeed be formed in the nucleus with a width which, in some cases, can be smaller than one fermi.

## Key claim

KdV solitons may indeed be formed in the nucleus with a width which, in some cases, can be smaller than one fermi, but the quantitative results are not entirely conclusive and the subject deserves further investigation.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian based on QHD-I, then introduce a new derivative-coupling term between the baryon and vector meson fields to generate a Laplacian of baryon density in the energy density. They apply the mean field approximation to derive the equation of state, combine it with nonrelativistic Euler and continuity equations, and perform a standard reductive perturbation expansion in stretched coordinates to obtain the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) equation. The soliton solution is then expressed in terms of the microscopic parameters (masses, couplings) and the sound velocity derived from the EOS.

## Key numbers

- The derivative coupling term is estimated to be of order \(p^2/m_V^2 \sim k_F^2/m_V^2 \sim 0.12\), using Fermi momentum \(k_F \simeq 0.28\) GeV and vector meson mass \(m_V \simeq 0.8\) GeV.
- The diffusion constant in nuclear matter at densities comparable to equilibrium and temperatures of order 80 MeV is \(D \simeq 0.35\) fm; extrapolation to \(T=0\) points to values even smaller.
- For the five models considered (QHD, NL1, NL3, NL3-II, NL-SH), the sound velocity \(c_s\) ranges from 0.16 (NL1) to 0.25 (QHD), and the equilibrium density \(\rho_0\) is 0.19 fm\(^{-3}\) for QHD and 0.15 fm\(^{-3}\) for the NL models.
- The soliton width \(\lambda\) is given by \(\lambda = (g_V / m_V^2) \sqrt{2\rho_0 / [(u - c_s) c_s M]}\), and the maximal projectile momentum is \(p_{\text{max}} = 2 c_s M / \sqrt{1 - 4 c_s^2}\).

## Context

This work reexamines early studies of nuclear solitons by FRSW (1980s) and by Abu (1990s), replacing their phenomenological or Skyrme-force-based equations of state with modern relativistic mean field models from Lalazissis (1997). It extends the formalism by introducing a derivative coupling term into the QHD Lagrangian to produce the higher-order spatial derivatives required for KdV solitons. The results suggest that soliton widths can be smaller than one fermi for some parameter choices, which could enable reinterpretation of nuclear transparency data in proton-nucleus collisions at low energies, and may also be relevant to supersonic phenomena observed at RHIC.
