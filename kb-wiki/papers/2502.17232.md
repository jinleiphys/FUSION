# Studying Effects of Evolution on Charge Separation in Small Collision System with A MultiPhase Transport model

> In relativistic high energy heavy-ion collisions, Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) could produce a charge separation in QGP. The charge separation could survive into final hadron system during evolution, observed as correlator $Δγ$. This physics procedure could also occurs in small collision system if CME emerge in the QGP droplet. In this paper, we study the effects of QGP droplet evolution on charge separation in small collision system, based on AMPT model. Our calculation indicates that, with given initial charge separation, the effects of parton level evolution and hadron level evolution weaken the charge separation indeed, but there is still enough signals could remained. Furthermore, we found that, the contribution of background to $Δγ$ is negligible in small collision system. So, we propose the small collision system being a good place to confirm contribution of CME.

## Key claim

The authors argue that, contrary to previous assumptions, the charge separation signal from the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) can survive the full parton and hadron evolution in small collision systems like p+Au, and that background contributions to the Δγ correlator are negligible in such systems, making them a clean environment to isolate CME signals.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (A MultiPhase Transport) model to simulate p+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV. An initial charge separation was artificially introduced by interchanging the y-component of momentum for 7% of selected quarks (upward-moving anti-up with downward-moving up, and upward-moving down with downward-moving anti-down), mimicking the CME-induced current. The evolution was then tracked through the Zhang parton cascade (ZPC) for parton-level evolution, followed by hadronization via a quark recombination model, and finally hadron-level evolution using the A Relativistic Transport (ART) model.

## Key numbers

*   Initial charge separation values after the quark interchange stage were γ_OS ≈ 1.58 × 10⁻³ and γ_SS ≈ -1.38 × 10⁻³.
*   After parton-level (ZPC) evolution, the γ_αβ values decreased to about ±0.001, which is 75% of the initial values for both γ_OS and γ_SS.
*   After hadron-level evolution, about 40% of the initial charge separation survived into the final hadron system.
*   For the 0% interchange percentage (no CME), the γ_αβ results were zero within statistical error after both parton and hadron evolution, indicating negligible background in p+Au collisions.
*   After hadron-level evolution, only 30% of γ_OS values survived, while 50% to 90% of γ_SS values survived in the range P_+ > 0.6 GeV.
*   For the Δη dependence after hadron-level evolution, only 15% of γ_OS survived and about 30% of γ_SS survived.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Zhang 2021, Wu 2024) which demonstrated that the electromagnetic field orientation in p+A collisions has a non-vanishing correlation with the reaction plane, enabling CME signals. It directly addresses the common assumption that the small spacetime scale of the QGP droplet in small collision systems would erase any initial charge separation. The finding that background contributions to Δγ are negligible in p+Au contrasts with results from Au+Au collisions (Ma 2011), where significant background was observed. This enables future work where the initial charge separation can be set event-by-event proportional to the magnetic field strength and direction, allowing a more realistic test of whether small collision systems can confirm the CME.
