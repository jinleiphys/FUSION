# Pseudoscalar Meson and Charmed Baryon Scattering Lengths

> We have calculated the scattering lengths between the pseudoscalar meson and charmed triplet, sextet, and excited sextet baryon to the third order with the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The chiral expansion of the pion channels converges well.

## Key claim

The paper calculates pseudoscalar meson and charmed baryon scattering lengths to third order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the chiral expansion converges well only for some pion and eta channels, not for all channels considered.

## Method

The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) to compute the threshold T-matrices for scattering between pseudoscalar mesons (π, K, η) and charmed baryons in the triplet (B₃̅), sextet (B₆), and excited sextet (B₆*) representations, expanding to O(ε³). They included explicit contributions from B₆ and B₆* intermediate states in the φB₃̅ scattering rather than absorbing their effects into higher-order low energy constants, and estimated the unknown LECs using SU(4) flavor symmetry and quark model relations.

## Key numbers

- Mass differences between charmed baryon multiplets: δ₁ = M(B₆*) - M(B₆) = 67 MeV, δ₂ = M(B₆) - M(B₃̅) = 127 MeV, δ₃ = M(B₆*) - M(B₃̅) = 194 MeV
- Coupling constants used: |g₁| = 0.98, |g₂| = 0.60, |g₃| = 0.85, |g₄| = 1.0, |g₅| = 1.5, |g₆| = 0
- Scattering length for πΞ_c (I=1/2): 0.3 ± 0.0052 fm (total)
- Scattering length for KΞ_c (I=0): 0.85 ± 0.076 fm (total)
- Scattering length for K̄Ξ_c (I=0): 1.6 ∓ 0.038 fm (total)
- Scattering length for πΣ_c (I=0): 0.7 fm (total)
- Scattering length for KΩ_c (I=1/2): 1.4 + 0.56 i fm (total)
- Scattering length for K̄Σ_c (I=1/2): 2. + 0.092 i fm (total)
- Low energy constants estimated via SU(4) symmetry: c̄₀ = -0.32 GeV⁻¹, c̄₁ = -0.52 GeV⁻¹, c₀ = -0.61 GeV⁻¹, c₁ = -0.98 GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy hadron interactions using chiral perturbation theory, such as the lattice studies by Flynn (2007), Ohki (2008), and Detmold (2011), and the molecular state calculations by Voloshin (2004) and Lee (2011). The calculated scattering lengths provide input for determining whether attractive interactions exist between pseudoscalar mesons and charmed baryons, which is essential for assessing the possibility of forming molecular states such as those composed of D mesons and charmed baryons, and for interpreting coupled-channel effects that shift masses of states like the D_{s0}(2317).
