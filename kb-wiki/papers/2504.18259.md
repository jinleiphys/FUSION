# Equation of state and Fermi liquid properties of dense matter based on chiral effective field theory interactions

> We present results for the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter obtained in many-body-perturbation theory (MBPT) up to third order, based on various chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions used in ab initio calculations of nuclei. We extract equation of state properties, such as the incompressibility and the symmetry energy, and discuss estimates of the theoretical uncertainties due to neglected higher-order contributions in the MBPT expansion as well as the chiral effective field theory expansion. In addition, we discuss the Fermi liquid approach to nuclear matter. We calculate all two- and three-nucleon contributions to the quasiparticle interaction up to second order in MBPT and present results for the Landau parameters, effective mass, and speed of sound for pure neutron matter.

## Key claim

The paper presents equation-of-state results for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using many-body perturbation theory up to third order, based on multiple chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, but the authors note that the Hamiltonian uncertainties from the chiral effective field theory expansion dominate over the many-body uncertainties, and a joint analysis framework is needed for comprehensive theoretical uncertainty quantification.

## Method

The authors employed many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) up to third order around a Hartree-Fock reference state, evaluating individual diagrams using Monte Carlo integration to a precision of 5 keV per particle. Three-nucleon interactions were included in a single-particle operator basis for efficient evaluation, and the calculations were performed at zero temperature using a grand-canonical ensemble with T = 10^{-3} MeV. For the Fermi liquid properties, all two- and three-nucleon contributions to the quasiparticle interaction were calculated up to second order in MBPT.

## Key numbers

- Energy per particle of pure neutron matter for all studied interactions agrees within about 3 MeV at saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- For the N2LO EMN 500 interaction at saturation density, the MBPT(2) correction is -4.1 MeV and the MBPT(3) correction is 0.5 MeV for pure neutron matter
- For the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction at saturation density, the MBPT(2) correction is -0.6 MeV and the MBPT(3) correction is -0.06 MeV for pure neutron matter
- For symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, the MBPT(2) correction for 1.8/2.0 (EM) is -2.6 MeV and for N2LO EMN 500 is -15.1 MeV
- EFT truncation uncertainties at N2LO at 2n0 are Delta E/A = +/- (6-8) MeV in symmetric nuclear matter and +/- (9-10) MeV in pure neutron matter at 68% confidence level
- For the DeltaNNLO_GO (450) interaction at n0, EFT uncertainties are +/- 1.9 MeV for pure neutron matter and +/- 1.4 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter
- Incompressibility K ranges from 251 MeV to 352 MeV at MBPT(3) across all interactions
- Symmetry energy Esym is constrained to the range 32.0 MeV to 33.9 MeV at MBPT(3)

## Context

This work extends previous MBPT calculations by Drischler (2019) and Keller (2021, 2023) to include new chiral interactions, particularly Delta-full interactions from Ekstrom (2017) and Jian (2020) and new low-resolution 1.8/2.0 interactions from Arthuis (2024) that were fitted to improve nuclear radii. The results provide equation-of-state parameters and Fermi liquid properties (Landau parameters, effective mass, speed of sound) for pure neutron matter that can serve as inputs for astrophysical studies of neutron star structure, core-collapse supernovae, and mergers.
