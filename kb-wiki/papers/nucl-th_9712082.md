# Relativistic Charge Form Factor of the Deuteron

> Relativistic integral representation in terms of experimental neutron-proton scattering phase shifts alone is used to compute the charge form factor of the deuteron $G_{Cd}(Q^2)$. The results of numerical calculations of $|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ are presented in the interval of the four-momentum transfers squared $0 \leq Q^2 \leq 35 fm^{-2}$. Zero and the prominent secondary maximum in $ |G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ are the direct consequences of the change of sign in the experimental $^3S_1$- phase shifts. Till the point $Q^2 \simeq 20 fm^{-2}$ the total relativistic correction to $|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|$ is positive and reaches the maximal value of 25% at $Q^2 \simeq 14 fm^{-2}$.

## Key claim

The zero and secondary maximum in the deuteron charge form factor arise directly from the change of sign (positive to negative) in the experimental \(^3S_1\) neutron-proton phase shifts at intermediate energies, and the total relativistic correction to the form factor is positive up to \(Q^2 \simeq 20\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\), reaching a maximum of 25% at \(Q^2 \simeq 14\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\).

## Method

The authors compute the deuteron charge form factor \(G_{Cd}(Q^2)\) using a relativistic integral representation that expresses the form factor in terms of experimental neutron-proton scattering phase shifts in the triplet channel (specifically \(^3S_1\), \(^3D_1\), and the mixing parameter \(\varepsilon_1\)) and experimental nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The Jost matrix formalism for two-channel scattering is employed, with the phase shifts taken from the Virginia Tech analysis (VPI group) covering laboratory energies \(0 < E_{\mathrm{lab}} \leq 1100\) MeV. The nucleon form factors are taken in a simple dipole form: \(G_{Ep} = (1+Q^2/18.23\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2})^{-2}\), \(G_{Mp}/\mu_p = G_{Mn}/\mu_n = G_{Ep}\), and \(G_{En} \equiv 0\) as a first step.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron charge form factor \(|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|\) is calculated over the range \(0 \leq Q^2 \leq 35\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\).
- The zero in \(|G_{Cd}(Q^2)|\) occurs at approximately \(Q^2 \simeq 21\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\) in the central calculation.
- The total relativistic correction reaches a maximum of 25% at \(Q^2 \simeq 14\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\).
- The asymptotic D/S ratio of the radial deuteron wave functions is taken as \(\rho = 0.0277\).
- Variation of the experimental phase shifts can shift the zero position from \(Q^2 = 16\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\) to \(Q^2 = 23\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\).
- The secondary maximum lies in the interval \(26 \leq Q^2 \leq 32\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\), with its height varying by up to a factor of seven depending on the phase shift set.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic impulse approximation approach previously outlined by the authors (Afanasev et al., 1994) and reviewed in related references, applying it specifically to the deuteron charge form factor. The results are consistent with the first experimental evidence for a zero in \(G_{Cd}\) from MIT-Bates (1990s) at about \(Q^2 = 20\;\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\). The approach enables comparison with other relativistic treatments, such as that of Arnold, Carlson, and Gross (1980), and is intended to be tested against forthcoming Jefferson Lab data from experiment E-94-018 at higher momentum transfers where relativistic corrections become significant.
