# Fluctuations and isentropes near the chiral critical endpoint

> Isentropic trajectories crossing the chiral phase transition near the critical endpoint (CEP) are studied for two light quark flavors. The calculations are performed within an effective chiral model with quark-meson interactions, belonging to the same universality class as QCD. We confront mean-field thermodynamics with the functional renormalization group approach, where fluctuations are properly taken into account. We establish a connection between modifications of the isentropic trajectories found in mean-field calculations at the crossover transition near the CEP and the order of the phase transition in the chiral limit. Furthermore, the isentropes obtained with the renormalization group are completely smooth at the crossover transition and do not in any way reflect the proximity of the CEP. In particular, our results do not show the recently conjectured focussing of isentropes from the crossover region towards the critical endpoint.

## Key claim

The authors find that, contrary to earlier conjectures, isentropic trajectories computed with the functional renormalization group (FRG) are completely smooth at the crossover transition and do not show any focusing towards the chiral critical endpoint (CEP), even though the quark-meson model belongs to the same universality class as QCD.

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic potential of the two-flavor quark-meson model using both mean-field (MF) approximation and the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach, which includes fluctuations non-perturbatively. The FRG flow equation is solved using two distinct methods: a grid method discretizing the potential on a one-dimensional field grid, and a Taylor expansion method around the scale-dependent running minimum truncated at third order. Isentropic trajectories (contours of constant entropy per baryon s/n) are then obtained from the pressure as functions of temperature T and quark chemical potential μ.

## Key numbers

- The CEP position in the FRG grid method is approximately (T_c, μ_c) = (14, 328) MeV
- The CEP position in the FRG Taylor expansion method is approximately (T_c, μ_c) = (64, 336) MeV
- In the mean-field approximation without vacuum term, the CEP is at (T_c, μ_c) = (130, 147) MeV
- In the mean-field approximation with vacuum term included, the CEP shifts to (T_c, μ_c) = (51, 316) MeV
- The crossover temperature at μ=0 increases from approximately 150 MeV to almost 210 MeV when the vacuum term is included in the mean-field calculation
- Model parameters are fixed to vacuum values: pion mass M_π = 138 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV, sigma mass M_σ = 670 MeV, constituent quark mass M_q = 335 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of isentropic trajectories in effective chiral models, particularly the quark-meson and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models studied by Scavenius (2000), and directly tests the conjecture by Nonaka (2004) and Asakawa (2008) that the CEP acts as an attractor for isentropes on the crossover side. The results contradict this focusing conjecture and show that the kink structure seen in some mean-field calculations is an artifact of neglecting the fermion vacuum term. The smooth isentropes obtained with FRG agree qualitatively with lattice QCD results and with mean-field calculations that include the vacuum term, enabling more reliable predictions for experimental searches of the CEP in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and FAIR.
