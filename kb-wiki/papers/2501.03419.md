# Rapidity asymmetry of jet-hadron correlation as a robust signal of diffusion wake induced by di-jets in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Diffusion wake accompanying a Mach cone is a unique feature of the medium response to projectiles traveling at a speed faster than the velocity of sound. This is also the case for jet-medium interaction inside the quark-gluon plasma in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. It leads to a depletion of soft hadrons in the opposite direction of the propagating jet and has been recently observed in $Z$-jet events of Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. In di-jet events, however, the diffusion wake of one jet usually overlaps with the medium-induced hadron enhancement of other jet without a clear signal except a reduction of the hadron enhancement, unless there is a large rapidity gap between the two jets. We propose to use the rapidity asymmetry of jet-hadron correlations in di-jets with a finite rapidity gap relative to that without, as a robust and background-free signal of the diffusion wake. The asymmetry emerges because the diffusion wake of one jet is shifted to a finite rapidity relative to the other jet. Consequently, a depletion of soft hadrons appears in the shifted rapidity region of the diffusion wake and an enhancement in the rapidity region of the other jet whose soft hadron enhancement is no longer or less reduced by the diffusion wake as in di-jets without a rapidity gap. We predict the rapidity asymmetry using both theoretical and mixed-event background subtraction for different values of the rapidity gap within the CoLBT-hydro model. Future measurements of this rapidity asymmetry with high statistics data on di-jets should provide more precise insights into the jet-induced diffusion wake and properties of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the rapidity asymmetry of jet-hadron correlations in di-jet events with a finite rapidity gap, relative to di-jets without a rapidity gap, is a robust and background-free signal of the diffusion wake. The hedge is that this signal works because the diffusion wake of one jet is shifted to a finite rapidity relative to the other jet, producing a depletion of soft hadrons in the shifted rapidity region and an enhancement in the rapidity region of the other jet whose soft hadron enhancement is no longer reduced by the diffusion wake.

## Method
The authors used the CoLBT-hydro model, which integrates the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model with the event-by-event (3+1)D CCNU-LBNL viscous hydrodynamic (CLVisc) model, to simulate di-jet transport in the quark-gluon plasma. Initial di-jet configurations were generated by PYTHIA8, and full jets were reconstructed using the FASTJET anti-kT algorithm from hadron spectra after theoretical background subtraction (subtracting the same hydro event without the di-jet). They calculated correlations between leading full jets and charged hadrons in di-jet events for central 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, for different values of the rapidity gap between the two jets, and also applied mixed-event background subtraction to mimic experimental analysis.

## Key numbers
- Leading jet transverse momentum threshold: p_T^jet1 > 120 GeV/c; sub-leading jet threshold: p_T^jet2 > 90 GeV/c.
- Soft charged hadron range: 1 <= p_T^h <= 2 GeV/c.
- Jet cone size: R = 0.4; jet pseudorapidity range: |eta_jet1,jet2| < 1.6; azimuthal angle difference between jets: |Delta_phi_jet1,jet2| > pi/2.
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions.
- Rapidity gap ranges studied: |Delta_eta_jet1,jet2| < 0.5, 0.5 < |Delta_eta_jet1,jet2| < 1, and |Delta_eta_jet1,jet2| > 1.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of jet-induced diffusion wake observed in Z-jet events by CMS (2024) and ATLAS (2023, 2024) in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, where a diffusion wake valley in rapidity was directly identified. The authors build on the suggestion by Pablos (2019) to use di-jets with a finite rapidity gap to avoid overlap between the diffusion wake of one jet and the medium-induced hadron enhancement of the other jet. The proposed rapidity asymmetry method enables experimental study of the diffusion wake using the far more abundant di-jet events rather than the statistically limited Z/gamma-jet events, and is designed to be background-free by canceling contributions from bulk hadron production and multiple parton interactions.
