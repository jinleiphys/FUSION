# Goldstone's Theorem on a Light-Like Plane

> I review various aspects of chiral symmetry and its spontaneous breaking on null planes, including the interesting manner in which Goldstone's theorem is realized and the constraints that chiral symmetry imposes on the null-plane Hamiltonians. Specializing to QCD with N massless flavors, I show that there is an interesting limit in which the chiral constraints on the null-plane Hamiltonians can be solved to give the spin-flavor algebra SU(2N), recovering a result originally found by Weinberg using different methods.

# Goldstone's Theorem on a Light-Like Plane

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry can be spontaneously broken on a null-plane even though the null-plane vacuum is invariant under the full chiral symmetry group, meaning that all symmetry breaking must reside in the Hamiltonians rather than in vacuum condensates. The proof of Goldstone's theorem on a null-plane relies entirely on physical matrix elements and does not require the existence of symmetry-breaking condensates, unlike the standard textbook proof.

## Method

The author reviews the null-plane formulation of QCD with N massless flavors, where the three reduced Hamiltonians (M² and M𝒥_r) encode all dynamical information and satisfy a U(2) algebra with the kinematical generator 𝒥₃. The chiral charges are shown to annihilate the null-plane vacuum, and the commutators of the axial charges with the reduced Hamiltonians are evaluated to derive constraints on how the Hamiltonians transform under SU(N)_R ⊗ SU(N)_L. Using the reduction formula and treating the explicit symmetry-breaking operator as an interpolating field, the author shows that the matrix element of the commutator [Q̃⁵_α, M²] between momentum eigenstates forces the existence of N²-1 massless Goldstone bosons whose masses vanish proportionally to the explicit symmetry-breaking parameter.

## Key numbers

- N²-1 massless Goldstone bosons emerge in the symmetry limit for QCD with N massless flavors
- The Goldstone boson mass satisfies M_π² = c_p ε_χ, where ε_χ is the explicit symmetry-breaking parameter
- The Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation takes the form -M ⟨Ω|ψ̄ψ|Ω⟩ = (1/2) N M_π² F_π² + ... in instant-form QCD
- On the null-plane, the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation becomes M ⟨0| i ψ̄₊ γ⁺ (1/∂⁺_M) ψ₊ |0⟩ = (1/2) N M_π² F_π² + ...
- For baryons in large-N_c QCD, ε^α ∼ 1/N_c, while matrix elements of chiral charges between baryon states scale as N_c
- The ground-state baryon assignment yields the 56-dimensional representation of SU(6)

## Context

This work extends the null-plane formulation of chiral symmetry breaking developed by Beane (2013) and connects to Weinberg's (1969, 1994) algebraic approach to chiral constraints, which used a completely different viewpoint that does not rely on null-plane quantization. The author shows that in the limit where chiral symmetry breaking in M² is small, the null-plane QCD operator algebra reduces to the spin-flavor algebra SU(2N), recovering a result originally found by Weinberg using different methods. This enables a derivation of the non-relativistic quark model's SU(6) symmetry directly from QCD symmetries and their pattern of breaking, without assuming constituent quark degrees of freedom, and opens the door to investigating how O(ε) corrections modify this simple picture.
