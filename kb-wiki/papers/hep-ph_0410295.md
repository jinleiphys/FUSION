# Heavy-quarkonium interaction in QCD at finite temperature

> We explore the temperature dependence of the heavy-quarkonium interaction based on the Bhanot-Peskin leading order perturbative QCD analysis. The Wilson coefficients are computed solving the Schrodinger equation in a screened Coulomb heavy-quark potential. The inverse Mellin transform of the Wilson coefficients then allows for the computation of the 1S and 2S heavy-quarkonium gluon and pion total cross section at finite screening/temperature. As a phenomenological illustration, the temperature dependence of the 1S charmonium thermal width is determined and compared to recent lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The paper explores how the heavy-quarkonium interaction with gluons and pions is modified at finite temperature by modeling medium effects through a screened Coulomb (Yukawa) heavy-quark potential, finding that the threshold for inelastic dissociation shifts to lower energies and the cross-section magnitude increases, but the authors caution that the approach is only valid when the Debye mass remains smaller than the Rydberg energy (temperatures below about 350 MeV) and when the screening parameter is small.

## Method

The authors start from the Bhanot-Peskin leading-order perturbative QCD framework for heavy-quarkonium-hadron scattering, which expresses the forward scattering amplitude as an operator product expansion of Wilson coefficients. They compute the Wilson coefficients numerically by solving the Schrodinger equation for 1S and 2S heavy-quarkonium states in a screened Coulomb potential (Yukawa type) with screening parameter mu, then perform an inverse Mellin transform to obtain the gluon dissociation cross sections. The heavy-quarkonium-pion cross sections are obtained by convoluting the gluon cross section with the gluon distribution in the pion (GRV LO parameterization). For finite temperature, the screening parameter is identified with the Debye mass m_D(T) using a running coupling constant with two-loop running and interpolation between short and long distance regimes following Kaczmarek (2004).

## Key numbers

* Charmonium Rydberg energy: epsilon_0c = 0.78 GeV; inverse Bohr radius: a_0c^{-1} = 1.23 GeV
* Bottomonium Rydberg energy: epsilon_0b = 0.75 GeV; inverse Bohr radius: a_0b^{-1} = 1.96 GeV
* Critical temperature for deconfinement in SU(3) pure gauge theory: T_c = 270 MeV (Karsch 2001)
* The charmonium binding energy drops by a factor of two already at T/T_c = 0.5
* The approach is valid for temperatures below about 350 MeV, where the Debye mass is smaller than the Rydberg energy
* The in-medium J/psi thermal width is larger by a factor of two or more up to T = T_c compared to the vacuum result

## Context

This work extends the Bhanot-Peskin (1979) perturbative QCD analysis of heavy-quarkonium dissociation, which was derived for pure Coulomb bound states and did not include medium effects. The authors note that previous phenomenological applications (Xu 1996) used the vacuum perturbative cross sections to estimate heavy-quarkonium dissociation in heavy-ion collisions. The paper enables more realistic estimates of J/psi and Upsilon suppression in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating Debye screening effects, and the computed thermal width is compared to lattice QCD results from Umeda (2002). The authors suggest the framework could be extended to realistic confining potentials from charmonium and bottomonium spectroscopy (Kwong 1987).
