# High-precision nuclear forces from chiral EFT: State-of-the-art, challenges and outlook

> We review a new generation of nuclear forces derived in chiral effective field theory using the recently proposed semilocal regularization method. We outline the conceptual foundations of nuclear chiral effective field theory, discuss all steps needed to compute nuclear observables starting from the effective chiral Lagrangian and consider selected applications in the two- and few-nucleon sectors. We highlight key challenges in developing high-precision tree-body forces, such as the need to maintain consistency between two- and many-body interactions and constraints placed by the chiral and gauge symmetries after regularization.

# High-precision nuclear forces from chiral EFT: State-of-the-art, challenges and outlook

## Key claim

The authors argue that the finite-cutoff version of nuclear chiral effective field theory, despite lacking full renormalizability when truncated at finite order, is so far the only scheme that has been advanced to high chiral orders and successfully applied to a broad range of few- and many-nucleon systems, provided the momentum cutoff is kept of the order of the breakdown scale rather than taken arbitrarily large.

## Method

The authors employ the unitary transformation method to decouple the purely nucleonic subspace of Fock space from the rest, deriving nuclear potentials from the effective chiral Lagrangian order-by-order in the chiral expansion. They use the semilocal regularization approach of Epelbaum (2014) and Reinert (2017) to introduce a finite momentum-space cutoff while preserving the long-range part of the interaction, and then solve the A-body Schrodinger equation nonperturbatively to obtain nuclear observables. The short-range low-energy constants appearing in the potentials are determined by fitting to low-energy experimental data, with truncation uncertainties estimated through order-by-order convergence checks and naturalness tests of the extracted constants.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter Q is defined as Q in {p/Lambda_b, M_pi/Lambda_b} where Lambda_b is the breakdown scale expected to be of the order of the rho-meson mass, with an upper bound set by 4 pi F_pi
- The chiral order nu of a connected N-nucleon irreducible diagram with L loops is given by nu = -4 + 2N + 2L + sum_i V_i Delta_i, where Delta_i = d_i + n_i/2 - 2
- The most accurate currently available nuclear potentials are at fifth order in the chiral expansion, i.e. at N^4LO
- At N^4LO, the NN potential receives contributions from 12 new short-range interactions proportional to D_i, and the 3NF involves a number of new short-range operators still being derived
- The NN potentials of Reinert (2017) provide a nearly perfect description of the mutually consistent neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data below pion production threshold from the Granada-2013 database

## Context

This work reviews the latest generation of chiral nuclear forces based on the semilocal regularization method of Epelbaum (2014) and Reinert (2017), which extends the foundational approach of Weinberg (1990, 1991) for applying chiral perturbation theory to nuclear interactions. The paper discusses ongoing efforts by the Low-Energy Nuclear Physics International Collaboration (LENPIC) to develop consistent many-body forces and solve the structure and reactions of light nuclei. The key challenge highlighted is maintaining consistency between two- and many-body interactions after regularization, particularly for three-nucleon forces beyond tree level, which are much less understood quantitatively than the two-nucleon sector.
