# Dileptons in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Due to their penetrating nature, dileptons are a valuable probe for the properties of the hot and/or dense medium created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Dilepton invariant-mass spectra provide direct access to the properties of the electromagnetic current-correlation function in strongly interacting matter. In this paper an overview is given of our current theoretical understanding of the dilepton phenomenology in comparison to recent data in heavy-ion collisions at the CERN SPS.

## Key claim

This review argues that high-precision dilepton data from the CERN SPS favor hadronic many-body theory models predicting a strong broadening of vector-meson spectral functions with little mass shift over the dropping-mass scenario for chiral symmetry restoration, but notes that a definite conclusion on whether dileptons in the intermediate-mass region originate from partonic or hadronic sources requires a more precise value of the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors review theoretical models for the electromagnetic current correlator in hot and/or dense hadronic matter and in the quark-gluon plasma, comparing them to dilepton invariant-mass and transverse-momentum spectra from heavy-ion collisions at the CERN SPS. They employ a thermal fireball parameterization that evolves from a quark-gluon plasma through a mixed phase to a hadron-resonance gas, using three equations of state with different critical and chemical freeze-out temperatures, and implement spectral functions from hadronic many-body theory, the T-rho approximation, and the chiral-reduction formalism.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement: T_c approximately 160-190 MeV, as found in finite-temperature lattice QCD calculations.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions: T_ch approximately 160-175 MeV, from thermal model evaluations of particle abundances.
- Thermal freeze-out temperature: T_fo approximately 90-130 MeV.
- Three equations of state used: EoS-A with T_c = T_ch = 175 MeV, EoS-B with T_c = T_ch = 160 MeV, and EoS-C with T_c = 190 MeV and T_ch = 160 MeV.
- Radial acceleration parameter in the fireball model: a_perp = 0.085 c^2/fm.
- Semi-central collision calculation defined by dN_ch/dy = 140.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical understanding of dilepton phenomenology in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on prior work by Rapp (1999) on electromagnetic probes and by van Hees (2006, 2007) on thermal fireball parameterizations. It directly compares hadronic many-body theory models (Rapp 1999), the T-rho approach (Eletsky 2001), and the chiral-reduction formalism (Steele 1996) to precision dimuon data from the NA60 collaboration in 158 A GeV In-In collisions and dielectron data from the CERES/NA45 collaboration in Pb-Au collisions. The findings enable future work connecting Weinberg sum rules and lattice QCD calculations of chiral order parameters to extract more direct evidence for chiral symmetry restoration from dilepton signals.
