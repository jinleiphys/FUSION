# High-energy dipole scattering amplitude from evolution of low-energy proton light-cone wave functions

> The forward scattering amplitude of a small dipole at high energies is given in the mean field approximation by the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation. It requires an initial condition $N(r; x_0)$ describing the scattering of a dipole with size $r$ off the target that is probed at momentum fraction $x_0$. Rather than using ad hoc parameterizations tuned to high-energy data at $x\ll x_0$, here we attempt to construct an initial scattering amplitude that is consistent with low-energy, large-$x$ properties of the proton. We start from a non-perturbative three quark light-cone model wave function from the literature. We add ${\cal O}(g)$ corrections due to the emission of a gluon, and ${\cal O}(g^2)$ virtual corrections due to the exchange of a gluon, computed in light-cone perturbation theory with exact kinematics. We provide numerical data as well as analytic parameterizations of the resulting $N(r; x_0)$ for $x_0=0.01 - 0.05$. Solving the BK equation in the leading logarithmic (LL) approximation towards lower $x$, we obtain a fair description of the charm cross section in deeply inelastic scattering measured at HERA by fitting one parameter, the coupling constant $α_s\simeq 0.2$. However, without the option to tune the initial amplitude at $x_0$, the fit of the high precision data results in $χ^2/N_\text{dof} = 2.3$ at $N_\text{dof} =38$, providing clear statistical evidence for the need of systematic improvement e.g. of the photon wave function, evolution equation, and initial condition.

## Key claim

The authors construct an initial condition for the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation from a non-perturbative three-quark light-cone wave function plus perturbative gluon corrections, rather than using ad hoc parameterizations tuned to high-energy data. However, the resulting fit to HERA charm production data yields a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 2.3 with 38 degrees of freedom, providing "clear statistical evidence for the need of systematic improvement" of the photon wave function, evolution equation, and initial condition.

## Method

The authors start from a non-perturbative three-quark light-cone wave function for the proton from Schlumpf (1992) and Brodsky (1994), fixed to the proton radius, anomalous magnetic moments, and axial vector coupling. They add O(g) corrections from gluon emission and O(g^2) virtual corrections from gluon exchange computed in light-cone perturbation theory with exact kinematics, then compute the dipole-proton scattering amplitude from the color charge density correlator. The impact-parameter-averaged dipole amplitude is used as an initial condition for leading-logarithmic BK evolution at x0 = 0.01, and the strong coupling constant is fitted to HERA charm production data.

## Key numbers

- The optimal strong coupling constant extracted from fitting HERA charm data is alpha_s = 0.200 for a Gaussian proton profile and alpha_s = 0.181 for a hard sphere profile.
- The fit yields chi^2/N_dof = 2.3 at N_dof = 38, indicating statistically significant need for systematic improvement.
- The saturation scale Q_s^2 at x0 = 0.01 is 0.100 GeV^2 (Gaussian profile), increasing approximately as 1/x^0.47.
- After 6 units of rapidity evolution (to x ~ 2.5 x 10^-5), the saturation momentum reaches Q_s ~ 1 GeV with saturation radius r_s ~ 0.3 fm.
- The anomalous dimension gamma of the dipole amplitude at x0 = 0.01 is 1.001 +/- 0.001, consistent with unity.
- The proton transverse area from the Gaussian profile is 9.8 mb; from the hard sphere profile it is 19.6 mb.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Dumitru (2020, 2021) by using a non-perturbative three-quark wave function from Schlumpf (1992) and Brodsky (1994) to compute the dipole-proton scattering amplitude, rather than fitting ad hoc initial conditions to HERA data as done in earlier BK analyses by Albacete (2009, 2010) and Lappi (2013). The method is similar in spirit to earlier light-cone Hamiltonian calculations by Hautmann (1999, 2000, 2006, 2007) that set initial conditions for QCD scale evolution to high virtuality Q^2, but here the goal is initial conditions for evolution to small x. The results enable future systematic improvements including running coupling corrections, NLO evolution, and improved photon wave functions.
