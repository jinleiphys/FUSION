# Impact of different extended components of mean field models on transport coefficients of quark matter and their causal aspects

> Role of different extensions of Nambu\textendash Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model like addition of vector interaction, Polyakov loop extended version (PNJL) and the entangled PNJL (EPNJL) models on transport coefficients like shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, electrical conductivity and thermal conductivity are critically analyzed. We have considered the standard expressions of transport coefficients, obtained in relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory. Influence of temperature dependent order parameters on temperature profile of transport coefficients are analyzed. Causal aspect of massless case to these different extended components of mean field models are also picturized, where an approximated lower and upper bound are drawn for shear relaxation time.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares how different extensions of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, specifically the addition of vector interaction, the Polyakov loop extended version (PNJL), and the entangled PNJL (EPNJL) model, affect the temperature dependence of transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, electrical conductivity, and thermal conductivity) of quark matter, while also examining causal constraints on the shear relaxation time for the massless case.

## Method

The authors compute transport coefficients using standard expressions derived from the relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory, employing both a constant relaxation time and temperature-dependent relaxation times. They solve the gap equations for each model version (NJL, NJL with vector interaction, PNJL, and EPNJL) at zero chemical potential to obtain temperature-dependent constituent quark masses and modified distribution functions, then evaluate the phase space integrals for shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, electrical conductivity, and thermal conductivity. For the causal analysis, they derive approximate lower and upper bounds for the shear relaxation time in the massless limit.

## Key numbers

* Chiral transition temperature for NJL model at μ=0: T_Σ ≈ 0.177 GeV
* Chiral transition temperature for PNJL model at μ=0: T_Σ = 0.233 GeV; deconfinement transition temperature: T_Φ = 0.228 GeV
* Chiral transition temperature for EPNJL model with (α₁, α₂) = (0.1, 0.1) at μ=0: T_Σ = 185 MeV; deconfinement transition temperature: T_Φ = 183 MeV
* Constituent quark mass at low temperature: M ≈ 0.320 GeV; current quark mass at high temperature: m₀ ≈ 0.010 GeV
* Vector coupling strength range considered: 0 ≤ G_V/G_S ≤ 1

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic calculations of shear viscosity in effective QCD models, particularly those using the NJL model (G_IFT, 2012; G_CAPSS, 2013; Weise, 2012; Marty, 2013; Deb, 2016) and the PNJL model (Kinkar_PNJL, 2017), by systematically comparing multiple model extensions including vector interaction and entangled Polyakov loop dynamics. The results provide input for viscous hydrodynamic models used to analyze experimental data from RHIC and LHC, where a very small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is inferred, and for modeling transport in dense quark matter relevant to compact stars and future facilities like FAIR at GSI and NICA at JINR.
