# Chiral NN model and Ay puzzle

> We analyze the results by chiral NN models for the two-nucleon system and calculate the predictions for the nucleon vector analyzing power of elastic nucleon-deuteron (Nd) scattering, Ay, by these models. Our conclusion is that a QUANTITATIVE chiral two-nucleon potential does not resolve the Nd Ay puzzle (when only two-body forces are included).

# Chiral NN model and Ay puzzle

## Key claim

A quantitative chiral two-nucleon potential that accurately reproduces the world neutron-proton data does not resolve the nucleon-deuteron (Nd) vector analyzing power Ay puzzle when only two-body forces are included; the apparent resolution reported by Epelbaum et al. (2001) using a next-to-leading-order (NLO) chiral potential is invalid because that potential fails to reproduce the NN data except below 8 MeV laboratory energy.

## Method

The authors computed the nucleon vector analyzing power Ay for elastic Nd scattering at 3, 10, and 65 MeV using three-body continuum Faddeev calculations with several chiral NN potentials: the NLO potential of Epelbaum et al. (1998), the higher-order "Idaho" chiral potential of Entem and Machleidt (2001), and a modified version of the Idaho potential with enhanced triplet-P phase shifts at low energy. They compared the phase shifts and chi-squared per datum for the reproduction of the 1999 world np database up to 210 MeV against the Nijmegen multi-energy np analysis (PWA93) and high-precision potentials such as CD-Bonn.

## Key numbers

- The NLO chiral potential produces chi-squared/datum = 37 for the 1999 np database up to 210 MeV, and is acceptable only in the 0-8 MeV interval where chi-squared/datum = 1.05.
- The Idaho chiral potential achieves chi-squared/datum = 0.98 for the 0-210 MeV range, comparable to PWA93 (0.97) and CD-Bonn (0.97).
- The spin-orbit phase shift combination Delta_LS at 25 MeV is 0.93 degrees for PWA93, 1.09 degrees for NLO (17% larger), and 0.92 degrees for the Idaho potential.
- The modified chiral potential with enhanced spin-orbit force produces chi-squared/datum = 1.27 for 0-210 MeV, exceeding the authors' threshold of 1.2 for a "quantitative" fit.
- For energies below 20 MeV, the Ay is predicted about 30% too small in the angular region around 120 degrees center-of-mass angle where the maximum occurs, when using realistic NN forces.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing Ay puzzle first identified by Stolk and Tjon (1978) using the Reid soft-core potential and later confirmed by the Graz-Osaka group (1987) with the Paris potential. The paper directly contradicts the claim by Epelbaum et al. (2001) that a chiral NLO potential resolves the puzzle, showing that the NLO potential is not quantitative. The authors demonstrate that a quantitative chiral potential (Idaho) produces the same underprediction of Ay as conventional high-precision potentials (CD-Bonn, Argonne V18, Nijmegen potentials), and that attempts to modify the triplet-P waves to enhance Ay lead to unacceptable fits to NN data. The work enables future focus on chiral three-nucleon forces at higher orders in chiral perturbation theory as the expected resolution, following suggestions by Huber et al. (1999), Huber et al. (2001), Friar (2001), and Canton and Schadow (2001).
