# Can the strong interactions between hadrons be determined using femtoscopy?

> In the last decades, femtoscopic measurements from heavy-ion collisions have become a popular tool to investigate the strong interactions between hadrons. The key observables measured in such experiments are the two-hadron momentum correlations, which depend on the production mechanism of hadron pairs and the final-state interactions. Given the complexity of ultra-relativistic collision experiments, the source term describing the production mechanism can only be modeled phenomenologically based on numerous assumptions. The commonly employed approach for analyzing femtoscopic data relies on the Koonin-Pratt formula, which relates the measured correlation functions with the relative wave function of an outgoing hadron pair and a source term that is assumed to be universal. Here, we critically examine this universality assumption and show that for strongly interacting particles such as nucleons, the interpretation of femtoscopic measurements suffers from a potentially large intrinsic uncertainty. We also comment on the ongoing efforts to explore three-body interactions using this experimental technique.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the commonly used femtoscopic approach for extracting hadronic interactions from heavy-ion collisions suffers from a fundamental flaw: the assumption that the source function describing particle production is universal across different interaction models violates basic principles of quantum mechanics, because the source term must be off-shell consistent with the interaction model to yield unambiguous results.

## Method

The authors perform a Gedankenexperiment in which two analysts, Alice and Bob, analyze two-particle correlation functions using different but unitarily equivalent representations of the same underlying strong interaction. Alice uses a chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon potential at fifth order (N4LO+) with a Gaussian source of radius 1.5 fm, while Bob uses the same physics but with the interaction transformed by a unitary transformation that changes the off-shell behavior while preserving all on-shell observables. The correlation functions are computed using the Koonin-Pratt formula in momentum-space partial-wave representation, and the results are compared when Bob assumes the source is universal versus when he applies the corresponding unitary transformation to the source.

## Key numbers

- Source radius set to r0 = 1.5 fm for the Gaussian source function
- Two unitary transformations are considered: set-I with parameters α = 1.0 fm⁻¹, β = 0.25 fm⁻¹ and set-II with α = 0.7 fm⁻¹, β = 2.0 fm⁻¹
- The chiral EFT potential used is at fifth order (N4LO+) with cutoff Λ = 450 MeV
- Phase shifts from all three interactions (Alice, Bob-I, Bob-II) agree to machine precision, confirming they are physically equivalent

## Context

This work critically examines the standard femtoscopy methodology used by experiments at RHIC, LHC, GSI/FAIR, and J-PARC-HI, which relies on the Koonin-Pratt formula (Koonin 1977, Pratt 1998) and the assumption of a universal source function. The paper extends earlier discussions of off-shell ambiguities in nuclear potentials (Birse 2012) and unitary transformations (Haftel 1971, Sauer 1974) to the femtoscopy context. The findings raise concerns about the feasibility of extracting three-body forces from heavy-ion collisions as attempted by ALICE (2022, 2023) and Kievsky (2023), and suggest that future uncertainty analyses must account for the intrinsic scheme dependence of hadronic interactions.
