# Real-Time Evolution of Soft Gluon Field Dynamics in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The dynamics of gluons and quarks in a relativistic nuclear collision are described, within the framework of a classical mean-field transport theory, by the coupled equations for the Yang-Mills field and a collection of colored point particles. The particles are used to represent color source effects of the valence quarks in the colliding nuclei. The possibilities of this approach are studied to describe the real time evolution of small $x$ modes in the classical effective theory in a non-perturbative coherent manner. The time evolution of the color fields is explored in a numerical simulation of the collision of two Lorentz-boosted clouds of color charged particles on a long three-dimensional gauge lattice. We report results on soft gluon scattering and coherent gluon radiation obtained in SU(2) gauge symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a classical mean-field transport theory, coupling Yang-Mills fields to colored point particles on a three-dimensional gauge lattice, can describe the real-time evolution of soft gluon modes in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, but only under the condition that the particles' kinetic energy is held constant when crossing cell boundaries (i.e., the Coulomb force on the particles is set to zero), which turns the particles into passive color sources rather than fully dynamical degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations coupled to Wong's equations for colored point particles on a spatial SU(2) gauge lattice in temporal gauge, using a Kogut-Susskind formulation. The valence quarks of two colliding nuclei are represented by ensembles of test particles carrying fractional color charge, which are Lorentz-boosted onto a large contracted lattice; the coupling constant is adiabatically increased from zero to g=2 to generate quasi-stable propagating color field configurations. The collision is simulated by evolving the coupled field-particle system for two boosted clouds of color-charged particles on lattices of up to 4^2 × 32768 points with lattice spacings of 0.3 fm (transverse) and 0.1 fm (longitudinal).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon radius used: r_n = 1.32 fm (rounded to 1.2 fm in the simulation); proton radius R_P = 5.47 fm, neutron radius R_N = 5.80 fm for Pb-208.
- Lattice spacing: a_l = 0.3 fm in each direction for the baseline calculation; longitudinal spacing reduced to a_3 = 0.1 fm for the refined lattice.
- Kinetic energy per nucleon: 100 GeV/u, corresponding to Lorentz factor γ = 106.5.
- Initial distance between nuclei: d_0 = 16000 lattice spacings (45.07 fm in the center-of-velocity frame) for the baseline; d_0 = 32000 lattice spacings for the refined lattice.
- Final coupling constant: g = 2.0, increased adiabatically at a rate Δg/Δt ≤ (3000a)^{-1}.
- Number of test particles per dual lattice cell: n_b = 6.
- Charge fluctuation parameter μ extracted from the simulations: μ = 0.06 fm^{-1} for the baseline lattice (a_3 = 0.3 fm) and μ = 0.04 fm^{-1} for the refined lattice (a_3 = 0.1 fm).
- Time step width: Δt = 0.03/γ fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier one-dimensional studies of colliding Yang-Mills wave packets by Hu and Mueller (1995) and three-dimensional wave-packet collisions by Poeschl and Mueller (1998) by introducing explicit color-charged particle sources to represent valence quarks. The approach is positioned as a bridge between the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) of random light-cone color sources for small-x gluons and parton cascade models (Geiger, 1995) for semi-hard dynamics at larger x; it enables future inclusion of hard parton collisions while maintaining a coherent description of soft gluon fields. The authors note that their extracted μ values (0.04-0.06 fm^{-1}) fall below the estimates of Gyulassy and McLerran (1997) for RHIC and LHC energies, which they attribute to the small transverse lattice size and the neglect of semi-hard gluon sources.
