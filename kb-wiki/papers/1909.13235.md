# A new description of transverse momentum spectra of identified particles produced in proton-proton collisions at high energies

> The transverse momentum spectra of identified particles produced in high energy proton-proton ($p+p$) collisions are empirically described by a new method with the framework of participant quark model or the multisource model at the quark level, in which the source itself is exactly the participant quark. Each participant (constituent) quark contributes to the transverse momentum spectrum, which is described by the TP-like function, a revised Tsallis--Pareto-type function. The transverse momentum spectrum of the hadron is the convolution of two or more TP-like functions. For a lepton, the transverse momentum spectrum is the convolution of two TP-like functions due to two participant quarks, e.g. projectile and target quarks, taking part in the collisions. A discussed theoretical approach seems to describe the $p+p$ collisions data at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, 2.76 TeV, and 13 TeV very well.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the transverse momentum spectra of identified particles in high-energy proton-proton collisions can be described by a revised Tsallis-Pareto-type (TP-like) function, where the spectrum of each hadron is the convolution of two or three TP-like functions representing the contributions of participant quarks, and this approach describes data at center-of-mass energies of 200 GeV, 2.76 TeV, and 13 TeV "very well", though the authors note that the single TP-like function (without convolution) is often equally good for fitting, and the convolution approach is motivated more by providing a physical picture at the quark level rather than by superior fit quality.

## Method

The authors revise the standard Tsallis-Pareto-type function by adding a power index \(a_0\) on \(p_{\rm T}\), creating a "TP-like function" \(f(p_{\rm T}) = C \times p_{\rm T}^{a_0} \times (1 + (\sqrt{p_{\rm T}^2 + m_0^2} - m_0)/(nT))^{-n}\). For mesons, the transverse momentum spectrum is taken as the convolution of two TP-like functions (one per participant quark); for baryons, it is the convolution of three TP-like functions. The model is fitted to published experimental data from the PHENIX, ALICE, CMS, LHCb, and ATLAS collaborations at three collision energies, using the relation \(p_{\rm T} = p_{t1} + p_{t2}\) (or with three quarks for baryons) where \(p_{ti}\) is the contribution from the \(i\)-th participant quark.

- At \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, for \((\pi^++\pi^-)/2\) fitted with the single TP-like function (Eq. 8): \(T = 0.129 \pm 0.001\) GeV, \(n = 9.449 \pm 0.020\), \(a_0 = 0.890 \pm 0.004\), \(\chi^2/\text{ndof} = 5/39\).
- At \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, for \((\pi^++\pi^-)/2\) fitted with the convolution of two TP-like functions (Eq. 10): \(T = 0.209 \pm 0.002\) GeV, \(n = 7.774 \pm 0.025\), \(a_0 = -0.540 \pm 0.020\), \(\chi^2/\text{ndof} = 6/39\).
- At \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, for \(\pi^++\pi^-\) with the single TP-like function: \(T = 0.130 \pm 0.001\) GeV, \(n = 6.882 \pm 0.021\), \(a_0 = 0.937 \pm 0.002\), \(\chi^2/\text{ndof} = 46/59\).
- At \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, for \(\pi^++\pi^-\) with the convolution: \(T = 0.209 \pm 0.001\) GeV, \(n = 4.970 \pm 0.022\), \(a_0 = -0.490 \pm 0.003\), \(\chi^2/\text{ndof} = 67/59\).
- At \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, for \((\pi^++\pi^-)/2\) with the single TP-like function: \(T = 0.129 \pm 0.001\) GeV, \(n = 4.862 \pm 0.021\), \(a_0 = 0.806 \pm 0.003\), \(\chi^2/\text{ndof} = 57/18\).
- At \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, for \((\pi^++\pi^-)/2\) with the convolution: \(T = 0.182 \pm 0.001\) GeV, \(n = 4.401 \pm 0.024\), \(a_0 = -0.390 \pm 0.002\), \(\chi^2/\text{ndof} = 54/18\).
- Constituent quark mass used for light quarks (\(u\), \(d\)): \(m_{0i} = 0.31\) GeV/\(c^2\); proton rest mass: \(m_0 = 0.938\) GeV/\(c^2\).

## Context

This work extends the use of Tsallis-Pareto-type functions (previously applied by the STAR and ALICE collaborations, and by Thakur and Zhangbu in related contexts) by introducing an additional power index \(a_0\) and a convolution framework based on the participant quark model or multisource model at the quark level. The approach aims to provide a unified description of transverse momentum spectra across the full \(p_{\rm T}\) range for various identified particles, avoiding the correlations and non-smooth interlinkages of two-component superposition methods used in earlier work (e.g., by the PHENIX and ALICE collaborations). The results enable predictions of \(p_{\rm T}\) distributions at other energies or rapidities where data are not yet available, once parameter dependencies on energy and rapidity are established.
