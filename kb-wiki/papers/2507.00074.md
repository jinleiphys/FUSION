# Studying few cluster resonances with quantum neural network driven iterative Harrow-Hassidim-Lloyd algorithm

> By using the quantum computing the properties of hypernuclei ${}^5_Λ$He, ${}^{\ 6}_{ΛΛ}$He and ${}^9_Λ$Be can be investigated within microscopic cluster model. Our approach combines quantum neural network (QNN) with iterative Harrow-Hassidim-Lloyd (IHHL) algorithm (abbreviated as QNN-IHHL) to solve the quantum many-body problem. To efficiently describe resonance phenomena, we employ complex scaling and eigenvector continuation techniques, providing a robust framework for identifying few-cluster resonance parameters within quantum computing. To validate our quantum algorithm, the resonant $4^{+}$ state of ${}^9_Λ$Be is chosen as a core example. With QNN-IHHL algorithm we realize a fully quantum workflow, which provides a novel framework and some ground work for exploring resonance properties in complex nuclear many-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully quantum computational framework (QNN-IHHL) for solving three-cluster resonance problems in hypernuclei, but the validation is limited to a single resonant state (the 4+ state of ⁹_ΛBe) using quantum simulations rather than actual quantum hardware, and the approach relies on combining complex scaling and eigenvector continuation to handle the non-Hermitian nature of resonances.

## Method

The authors combine a quantum neural network (QNN) with an iterative Harrow-Hassidim-Lloyd (IHHL) algorithm to solve the quantum many-body problem for few-cluster hypernuclei within a microscopic cluster model. Complex scaling is applied to transform the Hamiltonian into a non-Hermitian form that exposes resonance poles, and eigenvector continuation is used to reduce the computational dimension and qubit requirements. The QNN is used to obtain bound-state solutions via variational minimization, while the IHHL algorithm iteratively solves the non-Hermitian eigenvalue problem for resonant states, with the full workflow implemented on quantum simulators.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of ⁵_ΛHe is reproduced as 3.10 MeV, in agreement with the experimental value 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV (RevModPhys.88.035004).
- The binding energy of ⁶_ΛΛHe is reproduced as 6.6 MeV, in agreement with the experimental value 6.91 ± 0.16 MeV (PhysRevC.88.014003).
- The binding energy of ⁹_ΛBe is reproduced as 6.99 MeV, in agreement with the experimental value 6.63 MeV (BERTINI1981365, PhysRevLett.51.2085, BRUCKNER1976481).
- The size parameter b_N is taken as 1.36 fm for the alpha particle.
- The Fermi momentum k_F for the YNG Lambda-N interaction is 0.9 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on non-Hermitian nuclear resonant states for two-body systems (Zhang 2024) and their iterative non-Hermitian algorithm based on the Harrow-Hassidim-Lloyd algorithm (Zhang 2025). It builds upon the complex scaling method (Aguilar 1971, Balslev 1971) and eigenvector continuation techniques (PhysRevLett.121.032501) that have been applied in low-energy nuclear physics and nuclear scattering emulators. The QNN-IHHL framework enables future application of bound-state techniques such as the trap method (Zhang 2019, Zhang 2020) to handle scattering phase shifts in complex nuclear many-body systems.
