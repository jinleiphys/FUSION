# Flavor symmetry breaking in the $Δ$ sea

> The discovery of a sizeable asymmetry in the $\bar u$ and $\bar d$ distributions in the proton was one of the more consequential experimental findings in hadron physics last century. Although widely believed to be related to the fundamental role of chiral symmetry in QCD, a definitive verification of this hypothesis has remained elusive. We propose a novel test of the role of chiral symmetry in generating the sea flavor asymmetry by comparing the $\bar d-\bar u$ content in the proton with that in the $Δ^+$ baryon, where a significant enhancement is expected around the opening of the $N π$ decay channel. Recent developments in lattice QCD suggest a promising way to test this prediction in the near future.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that comparing the $\bar d-\bar u$ asymmetry in the $\Delta^+$ baryon with that in the proton can provide a definitive test of whether chiral symmetry breaking generates the sea flavor asymmetry, because the $\Delta^+$ asymmetry is expected to be significantly enhanced near the $N\pi$ decay threshold at $m_\pi \approx \Delta$.

## Method
The authors compute the $\bar d-\bar u$ asymmetry in the proton and $\Delta^+$ using chiral effective theory, where the asymmetry arises from convolutions of valence antiquark distributions in the pion with chiral splitting functions for the processes $N \to N\pi$, $N \to \Delta\pi$, $\Delta \to N\pi$, and $\Delta \to \Delta\pi$. The splitting functions are regularized using several forms (exponential, Regge, monopole, cutoff) with parameters from the JAM global PDF analysis, and the pion PDF is taken from lattice QCD reconstructions over a range of pion masses.

## Key numbers
- The $\Delta \to N\pi$ splitting function shows a large cusp at $y \approx 0.2$ when $m_\pi = 0.3$ GeV, corresponding to the opening of the $N\pi$ decay channel at $m_\pi = \Delta \approx 0.3$ GeV.
- At $m_\pi = 0.5$ GeV, the ratio of $\bar d-\bar u$ asymmetry in $\Delta^+$ to that in the proton is $\approx 80\%$ at $x \approx 0.1$.
- At $m_\pi = 0.3$ GeV, the same ratio becomes $\gtrsim 200\%$ at $x \approx 0.1$, a factor of 2.5 enhancement.
- The chiral limit values used are $M^{(0)} = 0.8$ GeV and $M_\Delta^{(0)} = 1.1$ GeV, with the exponential regulator cutoff $\Lambda = 0.87$ GeV from the JAM analysis.
- The ETM Collaboration plans simulations at $m_\pi$ values where $(m_\pi - \Delta)/m_\pi \approx 3\%$.

## Context
This work extends the chiral symmetry explanation of the $\bar d-\bar u$ asymmetry in the proton, first predicted by Thomas (1983) and confirmed experimentally by the New Muon Collaboration (1991), by proposing a test in the $\Delta^+$ baryon. It builds on lattice QCD quasi-PDF methods developed by Ji (2013) and recent simulations at physical pion mass by Alexandrou (2018) and Chen (2018). The predicted enhancement near the $N\pi$ threshold could provide independent confirmation of the pion cloud mechanism, complementing the SeaQuest experiment at Fermilab that is exploring the asymmetry at larger $x$.
