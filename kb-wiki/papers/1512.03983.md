# Description of resonant states in the shell model

> A technique for describing scattering states within the nuclear shell model is proposed. This technique is applied to scattering of nucleons by $α$ particles based on ab initio No-Core Shell Model calculations of $^5$He and $^5$Li nuclei with JISP16 NN interaction.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Single-State (SS) HORSE formalism that extracts low-energy phase shifts and resonance parameters directly from a single shell-model eigenstate, avoiding the need to sum over many eigenstates or introduce additional Berggren basis states as in the No-Core Gamow Shell Model or additional RGM calculations as in the NCSM/RGM approach.

## Method

The authors use the HORSE (Harmonic Oscillator Representation of Scattering Equations) formalism, splitting the infinite oscillator basis into an internal subspace P (N ≤ 𝒩) where the full Hamiltonian acts, and an external subspace Q (N > 𝒩) where only kinetic energy is retained. They derive a simplified Single-State (SS) HORSE expression for the phase shift at a shell-model eigenenergy E_ν that depends only on the regular and irregular free solutions S_{𝒩+2,ℓ}(E_ν) and C_{𝒩+2,ℓ}(E_ν), eliminating the need to sum over many eigenstates. The method is applied to nα scattering in ^5He and ^5Li using ab initio No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations with the JISP16 NN interaction, with NCSM model spaces up to N_max=16 and oscillator frequencies ℏΩ from 10 to 40 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For ^5He(3/2^-): resonance energy E_r = 0.93 MeV, width Γ = 1.01 MeV (N_max=4÷16 fit)
- For ^5He(1/2^-): resonance energy E_r = 1.84 MeV, width Γ = 5.49 MeV (N_max=4÷16 fit)
- For ^5Li(3/2^-): resonance energy E_r = 2.05 MeV, width Γ = 1.35 MeV (N_max=4÷16 fit)
- For ^5Li(1/2^-): resonance energy E_r = 3.29 MeV, width Γ = 4.70 MeV (N_max=4÷16 fit)
- NCSM calculations performed with N_max ranging from 2 to 16 for natural parity states (1/2^- and 3/2^-) and from 1 to 15 for the unnatural parity state (1/2^+)
- Oscillator frequencies ℏΩ ranged from 10 to 40 MeV in steps of 2.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the HORSE formalism (also called Algebraic Version of RGM, as in Fill (year) or Bang (year)) to a single-state approximation that can be directly applied to NCSM calculations, avoiding the computational complexity of the full Continuum Shell Model (Volya, year) or the No-Core Gamow Shell Model (Jimmy, year). The method enables extraction of resonance parameters from NCSM calculations with relatively small model spaces (N_max ≤ 6), which the authors note is encouraging for future applications to heavier nuclear systems. The results are compared with R-matrix analysis of experimental data from Csoto (1997).
