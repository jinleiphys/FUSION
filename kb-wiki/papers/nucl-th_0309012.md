# Compressibility, effective mass and density dependence in Skyrme forces

> Generalized density dependence in Skyrme effective interactions is investigated to get forces valid beyond the mean field approximation. Preliminary results are presented for infinite symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter up to pure neutron matter.

## Key claim

Generalized Skyrme forces with two independent density-dependent terms (rather than the standard single term) allow the incompressibility and the isoscalar effective mass to be chosen independently, whereas in standard Skyrme forces these two quantities are correlated once the density-dependence exponent is fixed. The paper presents preliminary results for infinite symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, with the caveat that the connection to the resummation of Brueckner correlations and renormalization of three-body forces is the aim of ongoing work.

## Method

The authors generalized the Skyrme interaction by allowing each term (central, velocity-dependent, spin-orbit) to carry its own density-dependent factor \(\rho^{i/3}\), then restricted to the case with three non-zero central terms \(t_{0i}\) (with exponents \(i_1, i_2, i_3\) from the set {0,1,2,3}) and standard velocity-dependent terms only at \(j=0\). They fixed the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (\(\rho_0=0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(E/A=-16\) MeV, \(K_\infty=230\) MeV, \(m^*/m=0.8\), and isovector enhancement factor \(\kappa=0.5\)) to determine the parameters \(t_{0i}\), \(\Theta_s\), and \(\Theta_v\), then fitted the \(x_{0i}\) parameters to the neutron matter equation of state of Akmal et al. (1998).

## Key numbers

* Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and energy per particle \(E/A = -16\) MeV were imposed for all parameterizations.
* Incompressibility \(K_\infty = 230\) MeV and isoscalar effective mass \(m^*/m = 0.8\) were chosen as target values.
* Symmetry energy at saturation density: \(a_I \simeq 32.5\) MeV for all three forces [0,1,2], [0,1,3], and [0,2,3].
* For the [0,1,2] force: \(t_{00} = -1855.38\), \(t_{01} = 2343.12\), \(t_{02} = -488.28\) (units not specified, presumably MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{3+2i/3}\)).
* For the [0,1,3] force: \(t_{00} = -1807.41\), \(t_{01} = 2078.04\), \(t_{03} = -299.81\).
* For the [0,2,3] force: \(t_{00} = -1431.36\), \(t_{02} = 3827.79\), \(t_{03} = -2650.09\).

## Context

This work extends the standard Skyrme force framework (e.g., SIII, SkM\(^*\), SLy4) by introducing two density-dependent terms, motivated by the need for forces valid beyond the mean-field approximation in Generator Coordinate Method and Projected Mean Field Method calculations (Duguet 2001, 2002). The results enable independent control of incompressibility and effective mass, which is not possible with a single density-dependent term as shown by Chabanat et al. (1997) for the SLy4 parameterization. The new parameterizations reproduce the variational equation of state of Akmal et al. (1998) for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter up to about \(3\rho_0\), and the symmetry energy agrees with the variational results of Lagaris and Pandharipande (1981).
