# The pasta phase within density dependent hadronic models

> In the present paper we investigate the onset of the pasta phase with different parametrisations of the density dependent hadronic model and compare the results with one of the usual parametrisation of the non-linear Walecka model. The influence of the scalar-isovector virtual delta meson is shown. At zero temperature two different methods are used, one based on coexistent phases and the other on the Thomas-Fermi approximation. At finite temperature only the coexistence phases method is used. npe matter with fixed proton fractions and in beta-equilibrium are studied. We compare our results with restrictions imposed on the the values of the density and pressure at the inner edge of the crust, obtained from observations of the Vela pulsar and recent isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion reactions, and with predictions from spinodal calculations.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the onset of the pasta phase using density dependent hadronic models and finds that the existence and extent of the pasta phase depend strongly on the model parametrization, the proton fraction, and whether matter is in beta-equilibrium; notably, for beta-equilibrium matter at zero temperature, the pasta phase does not appear in the coexisting phases method for models with constant couplings (NL3 and NL3delta) due to high surface tension, but does appear for density dependent models.

## Method

The authors used two methods at zero temperature: the coexisting phases (CP) method based on Gibbs conditions and the Thomas-Fermi (TF) approximation, both applied to npe matter with fixed proton fractions (yp=0.5 and yp=0.3) and in beta-equilibrium. At finite temperature only the CP method was used. They employed three density dependent hadronic parametrizations (TW, DDHdelta, GDFM) and two constant coupling models (NL3, NL3delta), solving the mean field equations for the meson fields and including surface and Coulomb energy contributions for the pasta structures.

## Key numbers

- Transition density for NL3 at yp=0.5: 0.096 fm^-3 (CP) and 0.112 fm^-3 (TF), with corresponding pressures 2.65 MeV/fm^3 (CP) and 2.64 MeV/fm^3 (TF)
- Transition density for NL3 in beta-equilibrium: no pasta phase in CP, 0.054 fm^-3 (TF) with pressure 0.24 MeV/fm^3
- Transition density for DDHdelta in beta-equilibrium: 0.089 fm^-3 (CP) with pressure 0.17 MeV/fm^3, and 0.079 fm^-3 (TF) with pressure 0.10 MeV/fm^3
- Transition density for GDFM in beta-equilibrium: 0.027 fm^-3 (CP) with pressure 0.04 MeV/fm^3, and 0.052 fm^-3 (TF) with pressure 0.13 MeV/fm^3
- Saturation density for NL3/NL3delta: 0.148 fm^-3; for TW and DDHdelta: 0.153 fm^-3; for GDFM: 0.178 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 37.4 MeV (NL3/NL3delta), 32.0 MeV (TW), 25.1 MeV (DDHdelta), 32.11 MeV (GDFM)
- Slope parameter L at saturation: 117/148 MeV (NL3/NL3delta), 55 MeV (TW), 44 MeV (DDHdelta), 57 MeV (GDFM)

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Avancini et al. (2008) on the pasta phase within the non-linear Walecka model (NL3, TM1, GM3 parametrizations) to density dependent hadronic models, and investigates the influence of the scalar-isovector delta meson. The results are compared with constraints from Vela pulsar observations and isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion reactions as reported by Bao et al. (2008), and with predictions from spinodal calculations. The findings enable better understanding of the crust-core interface in neutron stars and help constrain the equation of state for neutron-rich matter at subsaturation densities.
