# Elastic pion-proton and pion-pion scattering via the holographic Pomeron and Reggeon exchange

> The elastic pion-proton and pion-pion scattering are studied in a holographic QCD model, focusing on the Regge regime. Taking into account the Pomeron and Reggeon exchange, which are described by the Reggeized $2^{++}$ glueball and vector meson propagator respectively, the total and differential cross sections are calculated. The adjustable parameters involved in the model are determined with the experimental data of the pion-proton total cross sections. The differential cross sections can be predicted without any additional parameters, and it is shown that our predictions are consistent with the data. The energy dependence of the Pomeron and Reggeon contribution is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that elastic pion-proton and pion-pion scattering cross sections in the Regge regime can be described by a holographic QCD model that includes both Pomeron and Reggeon exchange, with the adjustable parameters determined from pion-proton total cross section data. The hedge is that the differential cross section predictions are consistent with available data, but the authors note that deviations appear at larger |t|/s values and that more precise data in the TeV region are needed to test their results.

## Method

The authors model Pomeron exchange as a Reggeized 2++ glueball propagator and Reggeon exchange as a Reggeized vector meson propagator, with hadron couplings determined by gravitational form factors from bottom-up AdS/QCD models. They derive analytic expressions for total and differential cross sections of elastic πp and ππ scattering, then determine three adjustable parameters (λgππ, λvπ+π+, λvπ-π-) by fitting to experimental πp total cross section data in the range 5 ≤ √s ≤ 100 GeV using the MINUIT package and PDG 2022 data. The remaining six parameters are taken from prior fits to pp and p p̅ scattering data.

## Key numbers

- αP(0) = 1.086 (Pomeron intercept, from fit to pp/p p̅ data at high energies)
- α'P = 0.377 GeV⁻² (Pomeron slope, from fit to pp/p p̅ data at high energies)
- λgpp = 9.699 GeV⁻¹ (Pomeron-proton coupling, from fit to pp/p p̅ data at high energies)
- αR(0) = 0.444 (Reggeon intercept, from fit to pp/p p̅ data at medium energies)
- α'R = 0.925 GeV⁻² (Reggeon slope, from fit to pp/p p̅ data at medium energies)
- λvpp = 7.742 (Reggeon-proton coupling, from fit to pp/p p̅ data at medium energies)
- λgππ = 3.361 ± 0.002 GeV⁻¹ (Pomeron-pion coupling, determined in this work)
- λvπ+π+ = 4.528 ± 0.023 (Reggeon-π⁺ coupling, determined in this work)
- λvπ-π- = 6.049 ± 0.022 (Reggeon-π⁻ coupling, determined in this work)
- σtotπp / σtotpp = 0.62 (total cross section ratio in high energy region)
- σtotππ / σtotpp = 0.28 (total cross section ratio in high energy region)

## Context

This work extends the previous holographic QCD study by Liu and Watanabe (2022) that considered only Pomeron exchange for πp and ππ scattering, by adding Reggeon exchange to correctly describe cross sections in the medium energy region. The Pomeron and Reggeon parameters are taken from prior fits to pp and p p̅ scattering by Xie and Watanabe (2019) and Liu and Watanabe (2022), respectively, exploiting the universality of these exchanges. The results enable predictions for ππ total and differential cross sections that can be tested with future experimental data, and the analysis shows that Reggeon contributions become negligible around √s ∼ 100 GeV.
