# Collapse of Flux Tubes

> The dynamics of an idealized, infinite, MIT-type flux tube is followed in time as the interior evolves from a pure gluon field to a $\overline q \ q$ plasma. We work in color U(1). $\overline q\ q$ pair formation is evaluated according to the Schwinger mechanism using the results of Brink and Pavel. The motion of the quarks toward the tube endcaps is calculated by a Boltzmann equation including collisions. The tube undergoes damped radial oscillations until the electric field settles down to zero. The electric field stabilizes the tube against pinch instabilities; when the field vanishes, the tube disintegrates into mesons. There is only one free parameter in the problem, namely the initial flux tube radius, to which the results are very sensitive. Among various quantities calculated is the mean energy of the emitted pions.

# Collapse of Flux Tubes

## Key claim

The dynamics of an idealized, infinite MIT-type flux tube can be followed from a pure gluon field to a quark-antiquark plasma, with the tube undergoing damped radial oscillations until the electric field vanishes and the tube disintegrates into mesons; however, the results depend sensitively on the single free parameter of the initial flux tube radius, and the calculations are performed in color U(1) rather than full SU(3).

## Method

The authors model an infinite flux tube using the MIT bag model, with pair production evaluated via the Schwinger mechanism using the results of Brink and Pavel, incorporating transverse confinement that gives quarks an effective transverse mass proportional to 1/a. The motion of quarks toward the tube endcaps is calculated using a Boltzmann equation that includes collisions, with the quark-antiquark interaction treated via a one-dimensional Debye-screened potential approximated by a delta-function. The tube radius is determined at each time by minimizing the transverse energy, and the electric field is given by the shielded charge at the endcaps divided by the tube cross-sectional area.

## Key numbers

- String tension: θ = 913 MeV/fm = 0.18 GeV² = 4.63/fm², extracted from heavy quarkonia spectroscopy and Regge trajectories.
- For the MIT model with κ = θ and n_f = 2, the characteristic collapse time τ(0.5 fm) = 7.0 fm/c and τ(1.0 fm) = 1.4 fm/c from the simple estimate.
- For κ = 2θ and n_f = 2, τ(0.5 fm) = 1.7 fm/c and τ(1.0 fm) = 0.68 fm/c from the simple estimate.
- The two lowest transverse modes have |x_±1| = 1.4347, which dominate the pair production rate.
- The pair production rate in the infinite parallel plate limit (no confinement) is w(∞) ≈ 0.25 c/fm⁴ for m_0 → 0.
- Lattice gauge calculations by Sommer (1992) give an equivalent flux tube radius r_eq ≈ 0.3 fm, while the dual superconductivity model by Baker (1990, 1993) gives r_eq ≈ 0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of flux tube breaking via light quark-antiquark pair creation by Brink and Pavel (1991), Greiner and collaborators (1983, 1990, 1992), Warke and Bhalerao (1992), and Flintoft and Birse (1993), who employed variations of the Schwinger parallel plate capacitor model with MIT boundary conditions. The present calculation adds a Boltzmann equation with collisions to follow the dynamics of the quarks and the tube radius, enabling estimates of the collapse time and the mean energy of emitted pions. The results are sensitive to the initial flux tube radius, providing a way to distinguish among different flux tube models (MIT model, lattice gauge calculations, dual superconductivity model, and the chromodielectric model), and the authors note that further corrections from finite length effects and confinement dynamics remain to be included.
