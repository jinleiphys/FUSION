# Similarity Renormalization Group for Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

> The similarity renormalization group (SRG) is based on unitary transformations that suppress off-diagonal matrix elements, forcing the hamiltonian towards a band-diagonal form. A simple SRG transformation applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions leads to greatly improved convergence properties while preserving observables, and provides a method to consistently evolve many-body potentials and other operators.

## Key claim

The similarity renormalization group (SRG), applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions with a simple choice of unitary transformation, drives the Hamiltonian toward a band-diagonal form and improves convergence properties while preserving all observables at all energies, not just low energies as in the Vlowk approach. The key qualifier is that this initial exploration uses a particularly simple choice of SRG transformation, and other choices may be more effective.

## Method

The authors apply Wegner's flow equation formulation of the SRG, using the generator η(s) = [Trel, Hs] where Trel is the relative kinetic energy and Hs is the evolving Hamiltonian. They discretize the relative momentum space on a grid of Gaussian integration points and solve the resulting system of first-order coupled differential equations for the momentum-space potential V_s(k,k') in each partial wave, with the flow parameter λ ≡ s^{-1/4} measuring the spread of off-diagonal strength. The evolution is applied to chiral N³LO potentials with different cutoffs, and the resulting potentials are tested in variational calculations of the deuteron and triton using harmonic oscillator bases.

## Key numbers

- The width of the diagonal in momentum space scales as λ², where λ is measured in fm⁻¹
- At λ = 2 fm⁻¹, the evolved SRG potentials show similar low-momentum matrix elements to Vlowk potentials with momentum cutoff Λ = 2 fm⁻¹
- The largest repulsive Weinberg eigenvalues at zero energy evolve to small values as λ decreases to 2 fm⁻¹ and below, in both the ¹S₀ channel and the ³S₁-³D₁ coupled channel
- For the triton with two-nucleon interactions only, the binding energy reaches a minimum for λ between 1.5 fm⁻¹ and 2 fm⁻¹, then increases rapidly
- Phase shifts are preserved with no visible variation with λ at all energies shown, including above the data-constrained region (Elab > 300 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach to nuclear interactions pioneered by the Vlowk method (Bogner 2003, Bogner 2006), which imposes a cutoff on relative momentum and evolves potentials to lower values. The SRG approach offers a key advantage over Vlowk: the same unitary transformations renormalize all operators, including many-body operators, without requiring the solution of full three-nucleon bound-state or scattering problems. The paper derives the flow equation for three-body interactions explicitly, showing that the evolution of Vthree can be carried out without disconnected pieces, which enables the consistent construction and RG evolution of many-body potentials and other operators when combined with chiral effective field theory advances (Entem and Machleidt 2003, Epelbaum 2005).
