# Sensitivity analysis of the chiral magnetic effect observables using a multiphase transport model

> Because the traditional observable of charge-dependent azimuthal correlator $γ$ contains both contributions from the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and its background, a new observable of $R_{Ψ_{m}}$ has been recently proposed which is expected to be able to distinguish the CME from the background. In this study, we apply two methods to calculate $R_{Ψ_{m}}$ using a multiphase transport model without or with introducing a percentage of CME-induced charge separation. We demonstrate that the shape of final $R_{Ψ_{2}}$ distribution is flat for the case without the CME, but concave for that with an amount of the CME, because the initial CME signal survives from strong final state interactions. By comparing the responses of $R_{Ψ_{2}}$ and $γ$ to the strength of the initial CME, we observe that two observables show different nonlinear sensitivities to the CME. We find that the shape of $R_{Ψ_{2}}$ has an advantage in measuring a small amount of the CME, although it requires large event statistics.

# Sensitivity analysis of the chiral magnetic effect observables using a multiphase transport model

## Key claim

The shape of the $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ distribution is flat for background-only events but concave when a CME-induced charge separation is present, and this observable shows greater sensitivity than the traditional $\gamma$ correlator for detecting a small initial CME signal (below approximately 5% initial charge separation), though it requires large event statistics.

## Method

The authors simulated Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV (30-50% centrality) using the AMPT model with string melting and strict charge conservation. They introduced a CME-induced charge separation by swapping the $p_y$ values of a percentage of downward-moving $u$ ($\bar{d}$) quarks with upward-moving $\bar{u}$ ($d$) quarks in the initial condition. They calculated $R_{\Psi_{m}}$ using both the mixing-particle method and the shuffling-particle method, and compared the responses of $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ and the traditional $\gamma$ correlator to varying initial charge separation percentages.

## Key numbers

- Initial charge separation percentage $f$ defined as $f = (N_{\uparrow(\downarrow)}^{+(-)} - N_{\downarrow(\uparrow)}^{+(-)}) / (N_{\uparrow(\downarrow)}^{+(-)} + N_{\downarrow(\uparrow)}^{+(-)})$, with relation $f = (4/\pi)a_1$ where $a_1$ is the coefficient of the $\sin\phi$ term in the Fourier expansion.
- Particles selected with transverse momenta $0.35 < p_T < 2.0$ GeV/c and pseudorapidity $-1.0 < \eta < 1.0$.
- With 2 million events per case, the shape of $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ becomes sensitive to the CME when the initial charge separation percentage is larger than 5%.
- The $\gamma$ and $\Delta\gamma$ observables show nonlinear responses to the initial charge separation percentage, with $\Delta\gamma$ fitted as $\Delta\gamma = Af^2 + B$.
- The width $\sigma$ of $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ distributions is fitted as $\sigma = A/f$, and the sensitivity of $R_{\Psi_{2}}$ (defined as $\sigma^2 d(\sigma^{-2})/df$) decreases with increasing $f$, while the sensitivity of $\Delta\gamma$ (defined as $(1/\Delta\gamma)(d\Delta\gamma/df)$) increases with $f$.

## Context

This work extends the study of the CME observable $R_{\Psi_{m}}$ proposed by Ajitanand (2010) and Magdy (2017) by implementing it within the AMPT model framework. It builds on the earlier finding by Ma (2011) that a 10% initial charge separation due to the CME can describe experimental data of the $\gamma$ correlator in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, with only 1-2% of charge separation surviving final state interactions. The results confirm that $R_{\Psi_{3}}$ is not sensitive to the CME, consistent with the expectation that the magnetic field direction is uncorrelated with the third event plane. This work enables experimental searches for a small CME signal using the shape of $R_{\Psi_{2}}$, provided sufficient event statistics are available.
