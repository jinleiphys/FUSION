# The role of Coulomb interaction in fragmentation

> We examine the impact of adding a Coulomb term to a constrained system of 147 particles interacting via a Lennard-Jones (LJ) potential, finding that the inclusion of the coulombic interaction produces a shift, but no qualitative changes in the thermodynamical properties of the system. We also performed the systems characterization from a morphological point of view.

## Key claim

Adding a Coulomb term to a Lennard-Jones (LJ) system of 147 particles confined in a spherical box produces a shift in the caloric curve and a flattening of the loop that signals negative specific heat, but does not qualitatively change the thermodynamic properties or the presence of the phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed molecular dynamics simulations of 147 particles confined in a spherical container, interacting via a Lennard-Jones 6-12 potential with a cutoff radius of 3.0 sigma. They added a Coulomb interaction in two ways: a homogeneous case where the Coulomb force acts between all particles (with charge squared q^2 = 0.055), and an inhomogeneous case where only a subset of particles (Z=62, proton fraction 0.4) carry charge (with q^2 = 0.238). They computed caloric curves, kinetic energy fluctuations, and morphological properties including density profiles and fragment mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- System size: 147 particles (A=147) confined in a spherical box; also tested with A=294, Z=124 for the inhomogeneous case.
- Cutoff radius for LJ potential: r_c = 3.0 sigma.
- For the homogeneous Coulomb case: q^2 = 0.055, obtained by comparing binding energy of argon balls with the nuclear drop mass formula assuming proton fraction 0.4.
- For the inhomogeneous Coulomb case: q^2 = 0.238, with proton fraction Z/A = 0.4.
- The shift in the caloric curve loop minimum is approximately 0.3 epsilon, corresponding to the mean Coulombic energy of the system.
- For the homogeneous case at density rho = 0.01 sigma^{-3}, the peak in relative kinetic energy fluctuations A_K exceeds the canonical value of 1.5, indicating negative specific heat.
- The transition from a caloric curve with a loop to a monotonically increasing function occurs between rho = 0.3 sigma^{-3} and rho = 0.4 sigma^{-3}, identified as the critical density for the homogeneous LJ+Coulomb system.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of phase transitions in finite Lennard-Jones systems (Dorso 2002) and addresses a debate about whether Coulomb interaction suppresses the loop in the caloric curve for systems relevant to nuclear multifragmentation. The authors contrast their results with claims by Chomaz (2001) and Gulminelli (2002) that loops exist for systems up to 200 particles, and with Moretto (2002) who argued for an upper mass limit of A=60, and with Raduta (2003) who stated Coulomb forbids the phase transition for A=200, Z=82. The results are consistent with lattice model calculations including Coulomb interaction (Richert 2002). The work enables further study of negative specific heat in metallic clusters, where such effects have been measured experimentally (Schmidt 2001).
