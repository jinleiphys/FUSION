# New magic number for neutron rich Sn isotopes

> The variation of E(2+_1) of (134-140)Sn calculated with empirical SMPN interaction has striking similarity with that of experimental E(2+_1) of even-even (18-22)O and (42-48)Ca, showing clearly that N=84-88 spectra exhibit the effect of gradual filling up of ν(2f_{7/2}) orbital which finally culminates in a new shell closure at N=90. Realistic two-body interaction CWG does not show this feature. Spin-tensor decomposition of SMPN and CWG interactions and variation of their components with valence neutron number reveals that the origin of the shell closure at 140Sn lies in the three body effects. Calculations with CWG3, which is obtained by including a simple three-body monopole term in the CWG interaction, predict decreasing E(2+_1) for (134-138)Sn and a shell closure at 140Sn.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the empirical SMPN interaction predicts a new shell closure at N=90 in neutron-rich Sn isotopes, specifically at \(^{140}\)Sn, based on the calculated increase in the \(2^+_1\) energy. This claim is conditional on the use of the empirical SMPN interaction; the realistic two-body CWG interaction alone does not reproduce this feature, and the shell closure only emerges when a three-body monopole term is added to CWG to produce the CWG3 interaction.

## Method

The authors performed large-basis untruncated shell model calculations for \(^{134-140}\)Sn using both the empirical SMPN and realistic CWG two-body Hamiltonians, with the same set of single-particle energies for valence orbitals above the \(^{132}\)Sn core. They compared the calculated \(E(2^+_1)\) energies with experimental systematics for oxygen and calcium isotopes, and analyzed effective single-particle energies (ESPEs) and spin-tensor decompositions of the interactions. A simple three-body monopole term was then incorporated into the CWG interaction to create CWG3, and the calculations were repeated.

## Key numbers

- The \(E(2^+_1)\) of \(^{134}\)Sn is 726 keV (experimental value).
- With the SMPN interaction, the calculated \(E(2^+_1)\) for \(^{140}\)Sn is 1949 keV, indicating a shell closure at N=90.
- The energy gap between the \(\nu(2f_{7/2})\) and \(\nu(3p_{3/2})\) single-particle orbitals is 854 keV for the \(^{132}\)Sn core; with SMPN, this gap increases to 2.246 MeV at N=90.
- With the CWG interaction, the gap decreases slightly to 826 keV at N=90.
- With the CWG3 interaction (including the three-body monopole term), the calculated \(E(2^+_1)\) values for \(^{134-140}\)Sn are: 0.627 MeV, 0.562 MeV, 0.521 MeV, and 2.532 MeV, respectively.
- The binding energy of \(^{134}\)Sn with CWG3 is -6.307 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of shell evolution in neutron-rich nuclei, specifically above the doubly magic \(^{132}\)Sn core, and draws parallels to the behavior observed in lighter systems such as oxygen and calcium isotopes. It contradicts the predictions of the realistic CWG interaction (Kartamyshev et al., 2008), which yields nearly constant \(2^+_1\) energies, and instead supports the empirical SMPN results (Sarkar and Saha Sarkar, 2009) showing a new shell closure at N=90. The finding that three-body effects are essential to reproduce this shell closure mirrors similar conclusions for lighter nuclei by Otsuka et al. (2010) and Zuker (2003), and enables further study of the r-process nucleosynthesis implications for neutron-rich Sn isotopes.
