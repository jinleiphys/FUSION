# Sign Reversal of Boer-Mulders Functions from Semi-inclusive Deep-Inelastic Scattering to the Drell-Yan Process

> A striking prediction of QCD on the properties of the novel Transverse Momentum Dependent (TMD) distribution functions is that the time-reversal odd Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions extracted from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) will undergo a sign reversal in the Drell-Yan (DY) process. This prediction has been tested by experiments that have focused on the Sivers functions so far. We examine the current status on the theoretical prediction and experimental extraction of the signs of the Boer-Mulders functions from SIDIS and DY. We show that the existing SIDIS and DY data are consistent with the predicted sign reversal of the Boer-Mulders functions for proton's valence quark distribution. Prospects for future experiments at EIC capable of testing the sign reversal of the pion Boer-Mulders functions are also discussed.

# Sign Reversal of Boer-Mulders Functions from Semi-inclusive Deep-Inelastic Scattering to the Drell-Yan Process

## Key claim
The existing semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan (DY) data are consistent with the predicted sign reversal of the Boer-Mulders functions for proton's valence quark distribution, but the unpolarized DY data alone cannot distinguish between the two possible sign solutions; the recent COMPASS polarized DY measurement resolves this ambiguity and favors the sign-reversal scenario.

## Method
The authors review theoretical predictions for the signs of Boer-Mulders functions for nucleons and pions from multiple models (bag model, quark-spectator-diquark model, large-Nc model, relativistic constituent quark model, lattice QCD) and compare these predictions with experimental extractions from SIDIS azimuthal cos2phi moments measured by HERMES and COMPASS, and from unpolarized DY cos2phi coefficients measured by NA10, E615, and the FNAL E866/NuSea collaboration. They also analyze the COMPASS measurement of the A^{sin(2phi-phi_S)}_T asymmetry in pi-minus-induced DY on a transversely polarized proton target, which directly probes the sign of the pion's valence-quark Boer-Mulders function.

## Key numbers
- The best-fit values from SIDIS analysis yield lambda_u = 2.0 and lambda_d = -1.1, implying both u and d valence Boer-Mulders functions are negative in SIDIS.
- The x-dependence of x|h1^{perp u,d}(x)| from SIDIS peaks at x ~ 0.2 with values of ~0.08 for u and ~0.06 for d.
- The mean value of the cos2phi coefficient nu in pi-minus-induced DY is 0.091 +/- 0.009 at 194 GeV/c and 0.169 +/- 0.019 at 252 GeV/c over the 0 < pT < 3 GeV/c range.
- The peak values of x|h1^{perp anti-u, anti-d}(x)| from DY analysis are ~0.0023 for anti-u and ~0.0014 for anti-d.
- The COMPASS A^{sin(2phi-phi_S)}_T asymmetry has an average value of -0.131 +/- 0.046 (stat) +/- 0.047 (syst), which corresponds to a positive sign in the usual convention.
- The average Q^2 for HERMES SIDIS is 2.4 GeV^2 and for COMPASS SIDIS is 3.8 GeV^2.
- The FNAL E866/NuSea p+p and p+d DY experiment used an 800 GeV proton beam with mean Q^2 of 54 GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends the experimental tests of the QCD sign-reversal prediction from the Sivers functions (tested by STAR and COMPASS) to the Boer-Mulders functions, which have received less attention. The analysis builds on prior extractions of Boer-Mulders functions from SIDIS by Barone et al. (2010) and from DY by Barone et al. (2010) and Lu et al. (2010). The paper shows that the recent COMPASS polarized DY measurement resolves the two-fold sign ambiguity that existed in unpolarized DY data, favoring the sign-reversal prediction. The authors propose future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) using the Sullivan process to test the sign reversal of pion Boer-Mulders functions, which would provide a test in the meson sector where the Sivers function vanishes.
