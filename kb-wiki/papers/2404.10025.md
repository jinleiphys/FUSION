# Multi-photon stimulated grasers assisted by laser-plasma interactions

> We investigate theoretically the possibility of achieving the stimulated amplification of $γ$-rays. Herein, our approach circumvents the so-called ``graser dilemma" through a non-linear, multi-photon mechanism. Our work foresees the combination of a high-intensity $γ-$flash generated by the interaction of a high-intensity laser pulse with plasma and intensive photons supplied by an additional laser. We show that multi-photon stimulated emission processes can have a larger effective cross-section compared to a one-photon process. The bandwidth of the supplied photons can also be tuned to curtail linewidth broadening. Naturally, Mossbauer transitions can be chosen to apply the scheme in the first instance. Furthermore, we derive that even multi-photon stimulated emission in the form of an anti-Stokes type could allow our scheme to be applied to non-Mossbauer nuclei, provided that the supplied photon energy can be tuned to compensate for the recoil and other broadening induced losses. The graser development can be spearheaded using multi-PW class high-power laser systems such as the 10 PW installation at Extreme Light Infrastructure - Nuclear Physics (ELI-NP) in Romania.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a multi-photon stimulated emission process, combining a high-intensity gamma-flash from laser-plasma interactions with additional intense laser photons, can circumvent the "graser dilemma" (the incompatibility between available pumping power against spontaneous decay and the minimum required stimulated emission cross-section for gamma-ray lasing). The key hedge is that this approach works provided the supplied photon energy can be tuned to compensate for recoil and other broadening-induced losses, and that the effective cross-section enhancement relies on the combinatorial nature of multi-photon processes rather than violating fundamental limits.

## Method

The authors theoretically derive the effective cross-section for n-photon processes (nPP) in nuclear transitions, generalizing previous n-photon absorption formulas to include both absorption and emission (with positive and negative photon energies). They combine this with estimates from particle-in-cell (PIC) simulations of gamma-flash intensities generated by multi-petawatt high-power laser systems (HPLS) interacting with plasma, and analyze the requirements for achieving population inversion and stimulated emission in nuclear isomers. The scheme involves a temporal coincidence of a gamma-flash from laser-matter interactions with a secondary beam of dense photons from an additional laser, where the multi-photon process goes through virtual states that resonate with intermediate nuclear states.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic stimulated emission cross-section for single-photon processes is approximately sigma_asy ≈ 10^{-22} / (2 pi E_gamma^2) cm^2, with E_gamma in MeV.
- For n-photon processes, the effective cross-section experienced by the last gamma-photon is approximately sigma_eff^{nPP} ≈ 10^{-25} (E_gamma / w_>) product_{i=1}^{n-1} [3·10^{-24} P_i X_i / (Delta E_i)^2] cm^2, where P_i is power density in W/cm^2, Delta E_i in eV, and X_i = A^{2/3} for E1 transitions.
- To achieve sigma_eff^{nPP} ≈ 10^{-19} cm^2 (comparable to single-photon E1 absorption), the gamma-flash intensity must be P_gamma ≳ 10^{17} W/cm^2 per eV interval over 1 keV to 5 MeV, or supplied photons must have P_omega ≈ 10^{18-a+x} W/cm^2 with E_omega ∼ 10^{-a} eV.
- PIC simulations indicate gamma-photon intensity within 1 eV intervals from 1 keV to 5 MeV is P_gamma ≈ 10^9 to 10^{12} W/cm^2.
- For grasing to occur, the minimum effective cross-section required is sigma_eff^{nPP} ≳ 10^{-25} cm^2, assuming N_i ≈ N_tot.
- With sigma_eff^{nPP} ≈ 10^{-20} cm^2 for n ≤ 4, the minimum required concentration of the initial state is N_i/N_tot ≥ 10^{-5}.
- A 10-second half-life isomer corresponds to a natural line width of 3.7×10^{-17} eV.
- For non-Mossbauer nuclei at room temperature with recoil energy loss R_loss = 1 eV, the Doppler breadth is Gamma_D ∼ 0.3 eV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Yang et al., 2023) on isomer pumping and depletion using gamma-flash from HPLS, and builds on earlier graser concepts by Baldwin et al. (1981, 1997) and proposals for non-linear effects with intense optical laser pumping by Collins et al. (1979, 1979, 1979, 1982). The approach is new because previous graser concepts could not foresee the existence of PW-regime HPLS systems that can provide flashes of intensive gamma-rays coincident with intensive optical beams to initiate multi-photon effects. The work enables the possibility of building the first gamma-ray laser using existing multi-PW class laser systems such as the 10 PW installation at ELI-NP in Romania, and suggests that multi-photon stimulated emission in anti-Stokes form could allow application to non-Mossbauer nuclei.
