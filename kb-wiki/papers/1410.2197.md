# Disordered nuclear pasta, magnetic field decay, and crust cooling in neutron stars

> Nuclear pasta, with non-spherical shapes, is expected near the base of the crust in neutron stars. Large scale molecular dynamics simulations of pasta show long lived topological defects that could increase electron scattering and reduce both the thermal and electrical conductivities. We model a possible low conductivity pasta layer by increasing an impurity parameter Q_{imp}. Predictions of light curves for the low mass X-ray binary MXB 1659-29, assuming a large Q_{imp}, find continued late time cooling that is consistent with Chandra observations. The electrical and thermal conductivities are likely related. Therefore observations of late time crust cooling can provide insight on the electrical conductivity and the possible decay of neutron star magnetic fields (assuming these are supported by currents in the crust).

## Key claim

The paper argues that topological defects in nuclear pasta, observed in large-scale molecular dynamics simulations, could reduce both thermal and electrical conductivities, and that a low-conductivity pasta layer can explain the late-time crust cooling observed in the neutron star MXB 1659-29, but the authors note that another interpretation of the data is possible and that the estimate of the effective impurity parameter is uncertain.

## Method

The authors performed classical molecular dynamics simulations of nuclear pasta using a simple two-body nucleon-nucleon potential, with a fixed screening length of 10 fm, at a temperature of 1 MeV, a proton fraction of 0.4, and a baryon density of 0.05 fm⁻³ (1×10¹⁴ g/cm³). They then modeled the effect of a low-conductivity pasta layer on crust cooling by increasing the impurity parameter Q_imp at densities above a threshold ρ_pasta, and compared the resulting cooling curves to Chandra X-ray observations of the low-mass X-ray binary MXB 1659-29.

## Key numbers

- The molecular dynamics simulations were performed at a baryon density of **0.05 fm⁻³** (or **1×10¹⁴ g/cm³**), about 1/3 of nuclear saturation density.
- The rough estimate for the effective impurity parameter from topological defects is **Q_imp ≈ 40**, based on a defect volume fraction f_D ≈ 0.1 and a mean effective charge per cluster **⟨Z⟩ ≈ 20**.
- The model that fits the Chandra observations of MXB 1659-29 uses **Q_imp = 30** for densities above **ρ_pasta = 8×10¹³ g cm⁻³**, with a core temperature **T_c = 2×10⁷ K**.
- The standard cooling model without a pasta layer uses **Q_imp = 3.5** in the inner crust and a core temperature **T_c = 3.05×10⁷ K**.
- The last temperature measurement for MXB 1659-29 is **49 ± 2 eV**.
- The low-conductivity layer must have **ρ_pasta ≳ 5×10¹³ g cm⁻³** to match the observed cooling curve.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Pons et al. (2013) that nuclear pasta may have a high electrical resistivity leading to magnetic field decay in neutron stars, and it builds on crust cooling models by Brown et al. (2009) and Brown and Cumming (2009). The paper enables future work to calculate in more detail the effect of topological defects on electron-pasta scattering, to determine the likely density and distribution of such defects, and to use additional X-ray observations of crust cooling (e.g., from magnetars like SGR 1627-41) to further constrain the pasta thermal conductivity.
