# The Yukawa potential under weak magnetic field

> Weak magnetic field induced corrections for the Yukawa potential due to one pion exchange between two constituent quarks (nucleons) are presented. For that, the constant magnetic field effect on the pion propagator and on the pion form factor are taken into account. An effective gluon propagator parameterized with an effective gluon mass ($M_g\sim 0.5$\,GeV) is considered. In the limit of magnetic field weak with respect to the constituent quark mass and pion mass, analytical and semi-analytical expressions can be obtained. Different types of contributions are found, isotropic or anisotropic, dependent on the pion mass and also on the constituent quark and effective gluon masses. Overall the corrections are of the order of $2\%$ to $5\%$ of the Yukawa potential at distances close to $2$fm, and they decrease slower than the Yukawa potential. The anistropic corrections are considerably smaller than the isotropic components. A sizable splitting between results due to magnetic field dependent neutral or charged pion mass is found.

# The Yukawa potential under weak magnetic field

## Key claim

Weak magnetic field corrections to the Yukawa potential from one-pion exchange between constituent quarks (nucleons) are of order 2% to 5% at distances near 2 fm, but only in the limit where the magnetic field is weak relative to both the constituent quark mass and the pion mass (eB much less than M_q^2 and eB less than m_pi^2). The abstract does not emphasize that the calculation assumes an effective gluon propagator parameterized with a fixed gluon mass of 0.5 GeV and that the results depend sensitively on this choice.

## Method

The authors compute the weak constant magnetic field correction to the Yukawa potential by modifying both the pion propagator and the pion-constituent quark pseudoscalar form factor, using an effective gluon propagator with a gluon mass M_g = 0.5 GeV. The leading magnetic field contributions are of order (eB)^2 and are separated into isotropic and anisotropic components. The Fourier transform of the momentum-space potential is performed analytically where possible, with remaining integrals over Feynman parameters evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- The total magnetic field correction to the Yukawa potential is 2% to 5% of the Yukawa potential at distances close to 2 fm
- The constituent quark mass used is M_q = 0.35 GeV
- The effective gluon mass is M_g = 0.5 GeV
- The pion mass (degenerate) is m_pi = 0.137 GeV
- The pseudoscalar coupling constant is g_ps = 13
- The magnetic field strength is fixed at eB ~ 0.1 M_q^2, corresponding to eB ~ 0.01 GeV^2
- Under weak magnetic field, the constituent quark mass increases by approximately 0.020 GeV
- The neutral pion mass decreases by approximately 0.020 GeV (from 0.135 GeV)
- The charged pion mass increases by approximately 0.020 GeV (from 0.139 GeV)
- For a smaller gluon mass M_g ~ 0.35 GeV, the form factor contributions increase by a factor of approximately (0.5/0.35)^4 ~ 4

## Context

This work extends calculations of magnetic field effects on hadron properties, which have been investigated using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL1, NJL2, NJL3, NJL4) and Lattice QCD (LQCD1, LQCD2). The authors previously derived the pion-constituent quark form factor under weak magnetic fields (JPG2020a) and here apply it to the Yukawa potential. The results enable future studies of nucleon-nucleon interactions in magnetar crusts and peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions, where magnetic fields are present but weaker than hadronic mass scales. The calculation does not include magnetic field effects on the gluon propagator or quark running coupling constant.
