# Mean field exponents and small quark masses

> We demonstrate that the restoration of chiral symmetry at finite-T in a class of confining Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) models of QCD is a mean field transition, and that an accurate determination of the critical exponents using the chiral and thermal susceptibilities requires very small values of the current-quark mass: log_{10}(m/m_u) < -5. Other classes of DSE models characterised by qualitatively different interactions also exhibit a mean field transition. Incipient in this observation is the suggestion that mean field exponents are a result of the gap equation's fermion substructure and not of the interaction.

## Key claim
The restoration of chiral symmetry at finite temperature in a class of confining Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) models of QCD is a mean field transition, but an accurate determination of the critical exponents using chiral and thermal susceptibilities requires extremely small current-quark masses: log10(m/m_u) < -5. The paper suggests that mean field exponents are a result of the gap equation's fermion substructure and not of the interaction itself.

## Method
The authors analyzed the chiral and thermal susceptibilities (χ_h and χ_t) as functions of reduced temperature t and reduced mass h in several classes of DSE models of QCD. They located pseudocritical points where these susceptibilities peak and extracted critical exponents β and δ from the scaling of peak heights with h, using the relations χ_h^pc ∝ h^{-(1-1/δ)} and χ_t^pc ∝ h^{-(1-β)/(βδ)}. The models studied included an infrared-dominant confining model (D_A), a model with a Coulomb-like short-range interaction (D_B), and a model with improved ultraviolet behavior including one-loop logarithmic suppression (D_C), all in the rainbow truncation.

## Key numbers
- For the D_A model: critical temperature T_c = 0.15915 η (with η = 1.06 GeV) in the simplest case, reduced to 0.15646 η upon including 1/N_c corrections to the vertex.
- For the D_B model: the scaling relations (Eqs. pchpct-betaslope) are not valid until log10(h/h_u) < -7, where h_u is the reduced mass giving m_π = 140 MeV.
- For the D_C model with two parameter sets: scaling relations valid only for log10(h/h_u) < -5 (with _1ω = 0.6 m_t) and log10(h/h_u) < -6 (with _2ω = 1.2 m_t), where m_t = 0.5 GeV.
- For the D_C model: T_c(ω) ≈ 88 + 107 ω (in MeV), with ω in GeV.
- A linear fit on log10 h ∈ [-4.5, -3] for the D_B model gave z_h = 0.79 and z_t = 0.40, compared to the mean field values z_h = 2/3 and z_t = 1/3.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of chiral symmetry restoration in DSE models (Arnea, 1998; Karsch and Laermann, 1994) and addresses the conjecture that 2-flavor QCD belongs to the O(4) universality class (Pisarski, 1984). The authors show that mean field exponents appear in multiple DSE model classes, including confining models with infrared singular interactions (Munczek and Nemirovsky, 1983) and separable models, suggesting that the fermion substructure of the gap equation determines the critical behavior. The results imply that lattice QCD simulations may require extremely small quark masses (log10(m/m_u) < -5) to accurately determine critical exponents, potentially explaining discrepancies in recent lattice results (El-Khadra, 1998).
