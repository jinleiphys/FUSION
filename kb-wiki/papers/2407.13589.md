# Functional Renormalization Group analysis of the quark-condensation pattern on the Fermi surface: A simple effective-model approach

> A simple effective model for the intermediate-density regime is constructed from the high-density effective theory of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). In the effective model, under a renormalization-group (RG) scaling towards low momenta, the original QCD interactions lead to four-quark contact interactions for the relevant quark and hole modes around the Fermi surface. The contact interaction in the scalar channel can be traced back to zero-sound-type collinear quark scattering near the Fermi surface in an instanton background. The quark and hole states in opposite directions of a given Fermi velocity form the collective scalar bosonic mode $σ$. The magnitude of $σ$ is investigated via the non-perturbative Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) evolution of the effective average action from the ultraviolet (UV) to the infrared (IR). In the mean background-field approximation for $σ$, nontrivial minima ($\barσ \neq 0$) are found in the IR limit of the effective average action. A nonvanishing $\barσ$ corresponds to condensation of quark and hole states in opposite directions of a given Fermi velocity, in a thin shell-like structure in momentum space around the Fermi surface. This looks similar to the shell-like baryon distribution in momentum space assumed in the quarkyonic-matter concept. However, when including a dynamic bosonic $σ$-mode in the RG flow, we find that its diffusive nature destroys the quark-hole condensate, i.e., the IR potential does not show any minima beyond the trivial one.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while a mean-field (background-field) approximation for the scalar bosonic mode \(\sigma\) yields nontrivial condensation of quark and hole states around the Fermi surface (resembling quarkyonic matter), including a fully dynamical bosonic \(\sigma\)-mode in the Functional Renormalization Group flow destroys this condensate, leaving only the trivial minimum in the infrared potential.

## Method

The authors construct a simple effective model from high-density effective theory (HDET) of QCD, where four-quark contact interactions for quark and hole modes around the Fermi surface arise from zero-sound-type collinear scattering in an instanton background. They bosonize the scalar channel via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and study the effective average action using the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) flow from the ultraviolet to the infrared. The analysis is performed first in a mean background-field approximation for the \(\sigma\) field, and then with a dynamical \(\sigma\)-mode, where the flow equation becomes an advection-diffusion type partial differential equation solved using methods from fluid dynamics.

## Key numbers

* The quark chemical potential is taken in the intermediate-density regime \(\mu \simeq \mathcal{O}(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}})\), with \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}\) denoting the QCD scale.
* The fast-mode condensate in the inhomogeneous quark condensate is suppressed by a factor \(\sim \mathcal{O}(1/\mu^2)\) compared to the slow-mode condensate.
* The condensation amplitude \(\sigma\) of the longitudinal modes has dimension MeV, while \(\Delta_\perp\) denotes the mean contribution from a small transverse patch on the Fermi surface with dimension MeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of inhomogeneous quark condensation, first discussed in the large-\(N_c\) limit by Deryagin (1992), and subsequent investigations by Shuster (1999), Park (1999), and Rapp (2000) on whether such condensation can compete with color superconductivity. It also connects to the quarkyonic-matter concept introduced by McLerran (2007) and later applied to dense nuclear matter by McLerran (2018), Jeong (2019), and others. The paper enables future work on including the BCS-type channel leading to color superconductivity and on understanding why dynamical fluctuations destroy the mean-field condensate pattern.
