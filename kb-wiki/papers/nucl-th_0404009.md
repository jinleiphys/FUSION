# Heavy ion collisions and lattice QCD at finite baryon density

> We discuss a relation between the QCD thermodynamics obtained from a statistical analysis of particle production in heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies and recent LGT results at finite chemical potential. We show that basic thermodynamic properties obtained from the phenomenological statistical operator of a hadron resonance gas that describes particle yields in heavy ion collisions are consistent with recent LGT results. We argue that for $T\leq T_c$ the equation of state derived from Monte--Carlo simulations of two quark--flavor QCD at finite chemical potential can be well described by a hadron resonance gas when using the same set of approximations as used in LGT calculations. We examine the influence of a finite quark mass on the position of the deconfinement transition in temperature and chemical potential plane.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the hadron resonance gas model, which successfully describes particle yields in heavy ion collisions, provides a consistent description of lattice QCD thermodynamics in the confined phase at finite chemical potential, but only when the same approximations (such as large quark mass corresponding to m_pi ≈ 770 MeV) are used in both approaches.

## Method

The authors compared thermodynamic observables from lattice QCD simulations of two-flavor QCD at finite chemical potential with predictions from a non-interacting hadron resonance gas partition function. They examined the factorization of temperature and chemical potential dependence in ratios of baryonic pressure, baryon number density, and baryon number susceptibility, and compared the Taylor expansion coefficients of the lattice pressure (c2, c4, c6) with those from the cosh(μ_B/T) expansion of the hadron resonance gas. To match lattice conditions, they used a parametrization of baryon mass dependence on pion mass and excluded strange baryons.

## Key numbers

- The ratio c2/c4 from the hadron resonance gas expansion of cosh(μ_B/T) is 3/4, and c6/c4 is 0.3; lattice results agree with these values within statistical error for T < T0.
- The lattice results for the phase boundary in 2-flavor QCD with m_pi ≈ 770 MeV are reproduced by the hadron resonance gas under the condition of fixed energy density ε ≈ 0.6 GeV/fm^3.
- The parametrization of baryon mass dependence on pion mass uses A = 0.9 ± 0.1 in the relation m*(m_pi)/m ≈ 1 + A * m_pi / m^2.

## Context

This work extends the connection between statistical model analyses of heavy ion collisions (reviewed in the authors' prior work) and lattice QCD results at finite chemical potential from the Bielefeld-Swansea collaboration (lat). It builds on the observation that freeze-out parameters from SPS and RHIC agree with lattice critical conditions for deconfinement, as noted by Stachel (1999). The results enable using the hadron resonance gas as a quantitative tool to extrapolate lattice QCD thermodynamics to physical quark masses and to map the phase boundary toward phenomenological freeze-out conditions.
