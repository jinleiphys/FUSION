# A Poincaré-covariant study of strange quark stars

> We investigate the properties of dense quark matter and strange quark stars within a nonperturbative, Poincaré-covariant framework. Employing a symmetry-preserving vector$\,\otimes\,$vector contact interaction model, we extend the quark gap equation to the regime of zero temperature and finite quark chemical potential. From the resulting momentum-independent quark propagator, we construct the equation of state (EOS) and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to evaluate the mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities of strange quark stars. We systematically analyze the sensitivity of the EOS and the macroscopic stellar properties to the model parameters, specifically the effective interaction strength and the ultraviolet cutoff. We demonstrate that reducing the coupling constant stiffens the EOS, whereas increasing the ultraviolet cutoff softens it. By confronting our predictions with multi-messenger astrophysical constraints-including pulsar mass measurements and gravitational-wave data-we identify parameter regimes that successfully describe current observations. Specifically, we find that parameter sets with $α_{ir}=0.735π$, $Λ_{uv}=0.905\,\mathrm{GeV}$ and $α_{ir}=0.588π$, $Λ_{uv}=0.9955\,\mathrm{GeV}$, alongside a vacuum bag pressure of $B \approx (0.106\,\mathrm{GeV})^4$, yield stellar properties in excellent agreement with empirical constraints.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Poincaré-covariant contact interaction model within the Dyson-Schwinger equation framework can reproduce observed strange quark star properties, but only when the effective coupling constant is reduced from its vacuum value to account for in-medium effects at high density, and when the ultraviolet cutoff and coupling are adjusted simultaneously to reflect the running of the QCD coupling.

## Method

The authors extended a symmetry-preserving vector⊗vector contact interaction model to zero temperature and finite quark chemical potential, solving the quark gap equation to obtain momentum-independent dressed-quark propagators. From these propagators they constructed the equation of state for strange quark matter under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and tidal deformability equations to compute mass-radius relations and tidal Love numbers for strange quark stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum stellar mass of approximately 1.2 M⊙ for the vacuum parameter set (α_ir = 0.9300π), which is too soft to match observations
- For α_ir = 0.7350π with B = (0.106 GeV)^4, the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star has compactness M/R = 0.1757, Love number k_2 = 0.1753, and tidal deformability Λ_{1.4 M⊙} = 699.1
- For α_ir = 0.8325π with B = (0.106 GeV)^4, the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star has compactness M/R = 0.2146, Love number k_2 = 0.1007, and tidal deformability Λ_{1.4 M⊙} = 147.3
- The simultaneously adjusted set (α_ir = 0.5880π, Λ_uv = 0.9955 GeV) with B = (0.106 GeV)^4 yields Λ_{1.4 M⊙} ≈ 598
- Increasing Λ_uv alone from 0.9050 GeV to 0.9955 GeV while keeping α_ir = 0.7350π reduces maximum mass to 1.12 M⊙
- Reducing only α_ir to 0.5880π while keeping Λ_uv = 0.9050 GeV gives Λ_{1.4 M⊙} ≈ 6600, violating gravitational-wave constraints

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to finite chemical potential for strange quark stars, building on prior contact interaction studies of hadron properties by Gutierrez-Guerrero (2010) and others. It contrasts with phenomenological bag models (Alcock, 1986) and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models (Buballa, 2003) by providing a Poincaré-covariant framework that preserves essential symmetries. The results enable future incorporation of momentum-dependent interactions and more realistic QCD-inspired kernels to refine understanding of the QCD phase diagram at extreme densities.
