# Is Anomalous Production of Omega and anti-Omega Evidence for Disoriented Chiral Condensates?

> No conventional picture of nucleus-nucleus collisions has yet been able to explain the abundance of Omega and anti-Omega hyperons in central collisions between Pb nuclei at 158 A GeV at the CERN SPS. We argue that this is evidence that they are produced as topological defects arising from the formation of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) with an average domain size of about 2 fm.

# Is Anomalous Production of Omega and anti-Omega Evidence for Disoriented Chiral Condensates?

## Key claim

The anomalously high abundance of $\Omega$ and $\overline{\Omega}$ hyperons in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV at the CERN SPS, which cannot be explained by conventional hadronic or transport models, is argued to be evidence that these particles are produced as topological defects (Skyrmions) arising from the formation of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) with an average domain size of about 2 fm. The claim is hedged by the recognition that the inferred domain size is too small to affect any other proposed DCC observables, and that the mechanism does not require a quark-gluon plasma to have formed.

## Method

The authors compare experimental yields of $\Omega$ and $\overline{\Omega}$ from the WA97 and NA49 collaborations to predictions from statistical hadronization models and the UrQMD transport model, showing a factor of 2 to 3 discrepancy. They then calculate chemical equilibration times for $\Omega$ and $\overline{\Omega}$ using thermally averaged cross sections from Koch, Muller and Rafelski (1986) and solve master rate equations, finding equilibration times of hundreds of fm/c. Finally, they estimate the DCC domain size by equating the topological defect production probability per unit volume (0.08 $\xi^{-3}$) to the observed number of $\Omega$ and $\overline{\Omega}$ in a central collision, assuming a particle density of 1.7 hadrons/fm$^3$ at the time of defect formation.

## Key numbers

- $\overline{\Omega}/\Omega = 0.383 \pm 0.081$ and $\Omega + \overline{\Omega} = 0.41 \pm 0.08$ per central Pb+Pb collision at 158 A GeV (WA97 collaboration)
- $\overline{p} = 10 \pm 1.7$, $\Xi^- = 7.5 \pm 1.0$, and $\Xi^- + \overline{\Xi}^+ = 8.2 \pm 1.1$ per central collision, 4$\pi$ integrated (NA49 collaboration, extrapolated)
- On average one $\overline{\Omega}$ is produced for every two central Pb+Pb collisions, corresponding to 0.50 $\overline{\Omega}$ per collision
- Chemical freezeout temperature from fits excluding $\Omega$ and $\overline{\Omega}$ is 145 $\pm$ 5 MeV, compared to 175 $\pm$ 10 MeV when including them
- Chemical equilibration time for $\pi + \Omega \rightarrow K + \Xi$ is 370 fm/c at T = 170 MeV; for $K + \Omega \rightarrow \pi + \Xi$ it is 290 fm/c at T = 170 MeV
- Inverse slope of transverse mass distribution for $\Omega$ and $\overline{\Omega}$ is 251 $\pm$ 19 MeV, more than 5 standard deviations from the systematic trend $T_{eff} = 180 + 105m$ (MeV, with m in GeV)
- Total net baryon number in a central collision is 414
- Estimated DCC domain size $\xi$ = 2 fm, consistent with dynamical calculations ranging from 1.4 to 3 fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of topological defect production in disoriented chiral condensates, previously developed by DeGrand (1984), Heinz (1992), and others, by applying it quantitatively to the specific anomaly in $\Omega$ and $\overline{\Omega}$ yields observed by the WA97 and NA49 collaborations at the CERN SPS. The paper contradicts the assumption that statistical hadronization models or the UrQMD transport model (which falls short by a factor of 2 to 3 for hyperon yields) can explain all observed hadron abundances. It enables future experimental searches at RHIC and theoretical development of microscopic transport models that incorporate DCC formation and topological defect production, while noting that the inferred domain size is too small to affect other proposed DCC observables such as neutral-to-charged pion ratio fluctuations or Bose-Einstein interferometry.
