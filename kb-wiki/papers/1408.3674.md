# Phenomenology of photon and di-lepton production in relativistic nuclear collisions

> We discuss the latest theoretical results on direct photon and dilepton production from relativistic heavy-ion collisions. While the dilepton spectra at low invariant mass show in-medium effects like a collisional broadening of the vector meson spectral functions, the dilepton yield at high invariant masses (above 1.1 GeV) is dominated by QGP contributions for central heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies. The present status of the photon $v_2$ "puzzle" - a large elliptic flow $v_2$ of the direct photons experimentally observed at RHIC and LHC energies - is also addressed. The role of hadronic and partonic sources for the photon spectra and $v_2$ is considered as well as the possibility to subtract the QGP signal from the experimental observables.

# Phenomenology of photon and di-lepton production in relativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim
While the abstract states that dilepton yields at high invariant masses (above 1.1 GeV) are dominated by QGP contributions for central heavy-ion collisions, the full text reveals that the photon elliptic flow "puzzle" remains unresolved: no theoretical model simultaneously describes both the photon spectra and the measured large $v_2$ values, and the role of hadronic bremsstrahlung sources may have been underestimated in standard hydrodynamical calculations.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical results from multiple dynamical approaches: (2+1)D and (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamical models (MUSIC, VISH2+1) with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions, and the non-equilibrium Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach based on off-shell Kadanoff-Baym equations. Photon and dilepton emission rates are computed from thermal field theory (equilibrium) and relativistic kinetic theory (non-equilibrium), with in-medium modifications of hadronic properties incorporated via the Vector Dominance Model and many-body approaches for the $\rho$-meson spectral function.

## Key numbers
- Direct photon yield scales with number of participants as $dN/dy \sim N_{part}^\alpha$ with $\alpha = 1.48 \pm 0.08$ for midrapidity thermal photons at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV (PHENIX data, $p_T < 1.4$ GeV/$c$)
- PHSD predictions for minimal bias Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV give $\alpha (total) = 1.5$ for direct photons, dominated by hadronic contributions, while QGP channels scale with $\alpha (QGP) \sim 1.7$
- Viscous hydro models (VISH2+1, MUSIC) give $\alpha(HG) \sim 1.46$, $\alpha(QGP) \sim 2$, $\alpha(total) \sim 1.7$
- Only about $1/3$ at LHC and $1/4$ at RHIC of total photons come from the "hot" QCD phase with $T > 250$ MeV
- QGP contribution gives up to $50\%$ of direct photon yield below 2 GeV/$c$ in PHSD calculations for Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV
- HSD results (without QGP) underestimate the $v_2$ of inclusive photons by a factor of 2 compared to PHENIX data

## Context
This paper reviews the status of electromagnetic probe phenomenology presented at Quark Matter 2014, extending earlier work by Kapusta (1991) on hadronic photon rates, Rapp and Wambach (1997) on in-medium $\rho$ spectral functions, and the AMY (2001) pQCD rates for thermal QGP radiation. It addresses the photon $v_2$ puzzle first observed by PHENIX (2010, 2012) and confirmed by ALICE, where measured direct photon elliptic flow is comparable to pion $v_2$ contrary to theoretical expectations from Chatterjee (2005, 2013), Liu (2009), and Dion (2011). The dilepton discussion builds on CERES and NA60 measurements at SPS energies showing collisional broadening of the $\rho$ meson, and the PHENIX dilepton excess at RHIC that remains unexplained by standard in-medium scenarios. The work enables future experimental tests with ALICE LHC data and the RHIC Beam Energy Scan II program to disentangle QGP radiation from hadronic sources.
