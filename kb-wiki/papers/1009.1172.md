# The quark-hadron phase transition in weakly isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

> We consider the transition from quark to hadronic matter which may result during the cooling/expansion of the quark-gluon plasma formed in energetic collisions of weakly isospin-asymmetric ions. This transition involves the energy density of u and d quark matter and the one of nearly isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. Within bag models, the former entails knowledge of the bag pressure, a poorly constrained quantity. The bag pressure at high density can be fixed imposing equality of quark and nucleonic energy densities at the (assumed known) transition point. We find this value to be very model dependent.

## Key claim

The bag pressure at the quark-hadron phase transition in weakly isospin-asymmetric matter can be fixed by imposing equality of quark and nucleonic energy densities at an assumed transition point, but the resulting value is very model dependent, particularly sensitive to the stiffness and density dependence of the underlying nucleonic equation of state.

## Method

The author calculates the energy density of u and d quark matter within the MIT bag model, using either a constant bag pressure B or a sharp step-function density dependence for B(rho). The hadronic phase energy density is obtained from the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) nucleonic equation of state for nearly symmetric nuclear matter with isospin asymmetry alpha = 0.2, corresponding to lead nuclei. The transition baryon density is determined by matching the hadronic energy density to an assumed transition energy density, and the bag pressure is then fitted to enforce equality of the quark and hadron energy densities at that point. Results are compared with those from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock plus three-body forces (BHF+TBF) and relativistic mean field (RMF) models from Burgio et al. (2002).

## Key numbers

- For a transition energy density of 0.8 GeV fm^-3, the DBHF model gives a transition baryon density of 0.74 fm^-3 and a fitted asymptotic bag pressure B_asy of 35.7 MeV fm^-3.
- For a transition energy density of 1.1 GeV fm^-3, the DBHF model gives a transition baryon density of 0.93 fm^-3 and B_asy of 58.9 MeV fm^-3.
- With constant B = 55 MeV fm^-3 (original MIT bag model value), the crossing density is about normal nuclear matter density, considered too low to be realistic.
- With constant B = 100 MeV fm^-3, the transition density is 0.93 fm^-3; with B = 200 MeV fm^-3, it is 1.2 fm^-3.
- For the BHF+TBF model from Burgio et al. (2002), B_asy is 36.4 MeV fm^-3 at 0.8 GeV fm^-3 transition energy and 51.1 MeV fm^-3 at 1.1 GeV fm^-3.
- For the RMF model from Burgio et al. (2002), B_asy is 37.9 MeV fm^-3 at 0.8 GeV fm^-3 and 37.8 MeV fm^-3 at 1.1 GeV fm^-3.
- For the more repulsive BHF+TBF model labeled "BOB" from Li and Schulze (2008), B_asy is 49.4 MeV fm^-3 at 0.8 GeV fm^-3 and 122.4 MeV fm^-3 at 1.1 GeV fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Burgio et al. (2002), who studied the quark-hadron phase transition using BHF+TBF and RMF equations of state, by applying the DBHF nucleonic model to the same problem. The author finds that the fitted asymptotic bag pressure varies significantly across different hadronic models, with the "BOB" EoS from Li and Schulze (2008) showing particularly strong sensitivity. The results highlight that independent constraints on the density dependence of the bag constant, for example from EMC effect studies by Jin and Jennings (1997), could help narrow the uncertainty in both the bag pressure and the nucleonic equation of state at the phase transition.
