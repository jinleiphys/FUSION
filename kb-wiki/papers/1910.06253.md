# Do electromagnetic effects survive in the production of lepton pairs in nucleus-nucleus collisions ?

> We discuss production of $J/ψ$ and $e^+ e^-$ pairs in semicentral and peripheral collisions of heavy ions at high energies. We focus on photoproduction mechanism. We present explanation of results of the ALICE collaboration for low transverse momentum production of $J/ψ$ as well as results obtained by the STAR collaboration for $e^+ e^-$ production, also at low transverse momenta. We conclude that such effects are important also in this new corner (semi-central processes) of the phase space.

# Do electromagnetic effects survive in the production of lepton pairs in nucleus-nucleus collisions?

## Key claim
Electromagnetic photoproduction mechanisms for J/ψ and e+e− pairs, previously established only in ultraperipheral collisions, also survive and explain low-transverse-momentum data in semicentral and peripheral heavy-ion collisions, provided the region of overlapping nuclear densities is excluded from the photon flux for J/ψ production.

## Method
The author calculates J/ψ production via photon-nucleus interactions and e+e− pair production via γγ fusion using impact-parameter-dependent photon fluxes derived from nuclear charge form factors. For J/ψ, the overlap region where quark-gluon plasma is created is removed from the b-space integration. For e+e− pairs, the b-space approach is supplemented by a kt-factorization method to compute transverse momentum distributions of the pair.

## Key numbers
- For J/ψ production in ALICE data: theoretical cross sections (Δσ/Δy) are compared for centrality bins above 30%, with the overlap region removed to achieve agreement with data.
- For e+e− production in STAR data at √sNN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions: dielectron invariant-mass spectra are shown for pair-PT < 0.15 GeV in three centrality classes: peripheral (60-80%), semi-peripheral (40-60%), and semi-central (10-40%).
- Experimental acceptance cuts for e+e− analysis: electron pT > 0.2 GeV, |ηe| < 1, and |ye+e−| < 1.
- The γγ fusion contribution dominates in peripheral (60-80%) collisions, while in semi-central (10-40%) collisions all three contributions (γγ fusion, thermal radiation, hadronic cocktail) are of similar magnitude.

## Context
This work extends the photoproduction interpretation first applied to ALICE J/ψ data by Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek (2016) to also cover STAR dielectron data from Adam et al. (2018). The author and collaborators (Klusek-Gawenda, Rapp, Schafer, 2019) previously showed that γγ → e+e− processes with coherent photons explain the STAR low-pT dielectron enhancement. The results enable future studies of transverse momentum distributions of lepton pairs as a function of impact parameter, and provide new constraints on the space-time picture of vector meson creation in the presence of quark-gluon plasma.
