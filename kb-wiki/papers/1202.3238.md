# Hadron production in hot and dense nuclear matter

> We study the hadron production at finite values of temperature and baryon density by means of an effective relativistic mean-field model with the inclusion of the full octet of baryons, the Delta-isobar degrees of freedom and the lightest pseudoscalar and vector mesons. These last particles are considered in the so-called one-body contribution, taking into account of an effective chemical potential and mass depending on the self-consistent interaction between baryons. The analysis is performed by requiring the Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of baryon number, electric charge fraction and zero net strangeness. In this context, we study the behavior of different particle-antiparticle ratios and strangeness production.

# Hadron production in hot and dense nuclear matter

## Key claim
The effective relativistic mean-field model with the full baryon octet, Delta-isobars, and light mesons (treated as a quasi-particle Bose gas with effective chemical potentials) can reproduce particle-antiparticle ratios and strangeness production in agreement with statistical-thermal models and SPS/RHIC data, but only under the condition that the effective meson masses and chemical potentials are self-consistently coupled to the baryon mean-field interactions.

## Method
The study uses an effective relativistic mean-field (RMF) model requiring global conservation of baryon number, electric charge fraction, and zero net strangeness at finite temperature and baryon density. The lightest pseudoscalar and vector mesons are incorporated as a one-body ideal Bose gas contribution with effective chemical potentials and masses derived from the self-consistent baryon interactions, respecting strong interaction processes of meson production and absorption. The coupling constants for meson-nucleon, meson-hyperon, and meson-Delta interactions are fixed to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties and hypernuclear data, using the GM3 parameter set.

## Key numbers
- The GM3 coupling set (from Glen, 1991) is used to determine meson-nucleon coupling constants, reproducing saturation density, binding energy, symmetry energy coefficient, compression modulus, and effective Dirac mass at saturation.
- Particle-antiparticle ratios (including $\overline{p}/p$, $\overline{\Lambda}/\Lambda$, $\overline{\Xi}^+/\Xi^-$, and $K^+/\pi^+$) are computed as functions of $\overline{p}/p$ ratio for different temperatures (e.g., T = 80, 120, 160 MeV shown in figures).
- The $\Delta$ coupling ratios are fixed to $r_s = r_v = 1$ for the reported results.
- The $\Xi^+/\Xi^-$ ratio at T = 80 and 120 MeV is reported to be very close to the $\overline{\Lambda}/\Lambda$ ratio.

## Context
This work extends the effective relativistic mean-field approach of Serot and Walecka (1986) and Greiner et al. (1997) by incorporating meson degrees of freedom as a quasi-particle Bose gas with effective chemical potentials, following the method of Lavagno (2010). It contrasts with the excluded-volume hadron resonance gas approach by using self-consistent mean-field interactions instead of a phenomenological volume parameter. The results are compared to statistical-thermal models and experimental data from SPS and RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2009), and the model is intended for application to future compressed baryonic matter experiments at intermediate baryon densities and temperatures.
