# Neutron Stars and the High Density Equation of State

> One of the key ingredients to understand the properties of neutrons stars is the equation of state at finite densities far beyond nuclear saturation. Investigating the phase structure of quark matter that might be realized in the core of NS inspires theory and observation. We discuss recent results of our work to point out our view on challenges and possibilities in this evolving field by means of a few examples.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the equation of state (EoS) of ultra-dense matter at densities far beyond nuclear saturation remains poorly constrained, and that combining "extremal" neutron star observables (largest mass, smallest radius, highest spin) with heavy-ion collision data can narrow the allowed parameter space, but no single observation yet decisively rules out any EoS model.

## Method

The authors review constraints on the high-density EoS from neutron star mass measurements (via pulsar timing), mass-radius relations from kilohertz quasi-periodic brightness oscillations (QPOs) in low-mass X-ray binaries, and elliptic flow data from heavy-ion collisions. They then discuss separate modeling of nuclear matter EoS and quark matter EoS, with phase transitions constructed thermodynamically, including a Dyson-Schwinger formalism for QCD's gap equation and NJL-type effective models for the quark matter phase structure.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1903+0327 mass: \(1.74 \pm 0.04 \, \mathrm{M_\odot}\) (largest precisely measured pulsar mass)
- PSR B1516+02B mass: \((2.08 \pm 0.19) \, \mathrm{M_\odot}\) at 68% probability, with 95% probability above \(1.72 \, \mathrm{M_\odot}\)
- Average binary radio pulsar mass: \(1.35 \pm 0.04 \, \mathrm{M_\odot}\)
- Maximum QPO frequency observed: 1330 Hz for 4U 0614+091, giving mass constraint \(M < 2.2 \, \mathrm{M_\odot} (1000\,\mathrm{Hz}/\nu_{\mathrm{max}})(1+0.75j)\) and radius constraint \(R < 19.5\,\mathrm{km} (1000\,\mathrm{Hz}/\nu_{\mathrm{max}})(1+0.2j)\)
- For 4U 1636-536: estimated mass \(2.0 \pm 0.1 \, \mathrm{M_\odot}\) from ISCO analysis
- PSR J0751+1807 mass corrected from \(2.1 \pm 0.2 \, \mathrm{M_\odot}\) to \(1.26 \pm 0.2 \, \mathrm{M_\odot}\)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of neutron star EoS constraints from Klahn et al. (2006), Lattimer and Prakash (2006), and Blaschke et al. (2008). It reviews the Dyson-Schwinger approach of Chen et al. (2008) and NJL-type models from Klahn et al. (2006) and Blaschke et al. (2008). The paper enables future work by identifying which specific observational thresholds (e.g., QPO frequencies of 1500-1600 Hz) would rule out the stiffest current EoS models, and by highlighting the need for more reliable "extreme" neutron star data to narrow the allowed parameter space.
