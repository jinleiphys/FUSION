# Formation of Deeply Bound 1s Pionic States in the $^{206}$Pb(d,$^3$He) Reaction

> Recently, deeply bound pionic states were found experimentally in (d,$^3$He) reactions on $^{208}$Pb. The observed spectrum showed an excellent agreement with the DWIA calculation and the dominant peak was attributed to the pionic 2p state contribution. We studied theoretically $^{206}$Pb(d,$^3$He) reactions within the same model, and found that it is very likely to observe the pionic 1s state as an isolated peak in the $^{206}$Pb(d,$^3$He) reaction with feasible energy resolution and statistics.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the pionic 1s state in lead can be observed as an isolated peak in the \({}^{206}\)Pb(d,\({}^{3}\)He) reaction with feasible experimental energy resolution, because the ground state of \({}^{206}\)Pb is expected to have the \(p_{1/2}\) neutron orbital largely empty, which removes a problematic intermediate peak that obscures the 1s signal in the \({}^{208}\)Pb case.

## Method

The authors used the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with an effective number approach, previously validated against the \({}^{208}\)Pb(d,\({}^{3}\)He) data of Yamazaki et al. (1996). They calculated the excitation spectrum for the \({}^{206}\)Pb(d,\({}^{3}\)He) reaction at a deuteron kinetic energy of \(T_d = 600\) MeV, incorporating the realistic ground-state configuration of \({}^{206}\)Pb (with mixing coefficients from Tickle (1969)) and experimental excitation energies and spectroscopic factors for \({}^{205}\)Pb from (d,t) pickup reactions. The effective numbers for each \([l_\pi \otimes j_n^{-1}]\) configuration were normalized by the occupation probability of each neutron orbital in the \({}^{206}\)Pb ground state.

## Key numbers

- The calculated spectrum for \({}^{208}\)Pb(d,\({}^{3}\)He) shows the pionic 1s state contribution as a shoulder only with an energy resolution of FWHM \(\approx 200\) keV.
- For the \({}^{206}\)Pb target, the pionic 1s state appears as an isolated peak at a Q-value of approximately \(-134\) MeV in the simplified calculation without \(p_{1/2}\) contributions.
- The ground state of \({}^{206}\)Pb has the configuration: \(a^2 = 0.54\) for \((2p_{1/2})^{-2}\), \(b^2 = 0.20\) for \((1f_{5/2})^{-2}\), \(c^2 = 0.12\) for \((2p_{3/2})^{-2}\), and \(d^2 = 0.12\) for \((0i_{13/2})^{-2}\).
- The effective numbers for the \([2p \otimes p_{1/2}^{-1}]\) and \([2p \otimes p_{3/2}^{-1}]\) configurations at \(T_d = 600\) MeV are \(7.37 \times 10^{-3}\) and \(1.47 \times 10^{-2}\), respectively; after normalization for \({}^{206}\)Pb they combine to \(1.72 \times 10^{-2}\).
- The negative pion production threshold with the ground state of \({}^{205}\)Pb corresponds to \(Q = -140.87\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical model of Toki and Yamazaki (1988, 1989) for forming deeply bound pionic atoms via direct reactions, which was successfully applied to the \({}^{208}\)Pb(d,\({}^{3}\)He) experiment by Yamazaki et al. (1996). The authors note that in the \({}^{208}\)Pb case, the 1s contribution could only be seen as a skewed shape or shoulder, whereas the \({}^{206}\)Pb target is predicted to yield an isolated 1s peak. This prediction is intended to motivate further experimental efforts in pionic atom spectroscopy, enabling precise study of the 1s pionic state properties.
