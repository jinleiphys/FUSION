# The Transverse Structure of the Baryon Source in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> A direct method to reconstruct the transverse structure of the baryon source formed in a relativistic heavy ion collision is presented. The procedure makes use of experimentally measured proton and deuteron spectra and assumes that deuterons are formed via two-nucleon coalescence. The transverse density shape and flow profile are reconstructed for Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN-SPS. The ambiguity with respect to the source temperature is demonstrated and possible ways to resolve it are discussed.

# The Transverse Structure of the Baryon Source in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The transverse density shape and collective flow profile of the baryon source at freeze-out can be reconstructed from measured proton and deuteron transverse momentum spectra via the coalescence model, but the source temperature remains ambiguous and cannot be determined from these spectra alone.

## Method
The authors assume deuterons form via two-nucleon coalescence at freeze-out, with a position-dependent collective transverse velocity and a position-independent local temperature T₀. They parametrize the dimensionless density function ñ(v) for both protons and deuterons, fit these to the NA44 Pb+Pb 158 A GeV data using a Monte Carlo χ² minimization, then integrate a differential equation linking the proton and deuteron ñ(v) profiles to obtain the velocity profile v(r) and the local proton density n_p(r).

## Key numbers
- Proton rapidity density dN_p/dy = 22 and deuteron rapidity density dN_d/dy = 0.3 for Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV
- Neutron-to-proton ratio R_np = 1.2 assumed in the coalescence calculation
- Freeze-out proper time fixed at τ₀ = 10 fm/c
- For the preferred temperature T₀ ≈ 100 MeV, the mean transverse source radius ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} ≈ 9.2 fm
- Estimated proton density at freeze-out n_p ≈ 0.0038 fm⁻³, derived from nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and a hadron-to-proton ratio of ~15
- The mean transverse velocity ⟨v²⟩^{1/2} grows with decreasing T₀ (values listed in Table 1 for different temperatures)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous coalescence-based approach (Polleri et al., 1998) to reconstruct the full transverse phase space distribution of protons at freeze-out. It directly addresses the inversion problem that single-particle spectra alone cannot uniquely determine the source structure, a limitation noted by Sollfrank et al. (1993) and others. The method enables future analyses incorporating proton-proton HBT correlations to resolve the temperature ambiguity, and could be extended to triton spectra to independently reconstruct the neutron distribution.
