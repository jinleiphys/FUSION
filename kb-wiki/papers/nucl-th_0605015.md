# Semiclassical expansion of quantum characteristics for many-body potential scattering problem

> In quantum mechanics, systems can be described in phase space in terms of the Wigner function and the star-product operation. Quantum characteristics, which appear in the Heisenberg picture as the Weyl's symbols of operators of canonical coordinates and momenta, can be used to solve the evolution equations for symbols of other operators acting in the Hilbert space. To any fixed order in the Planck's constant, many-body potential scattering problem simplifies to a statistical-mechanical problem of computing an ensemble of quantum characteristics and their derivatives with respect to the initial canonical coordinates and momenta. The reduction to a system of ordinary differential equations pertains rigorously at any fixed order in $\hbar$. We present semiclassical expansion of quantum characteristics for many-body scattering problem and provide tools for calculation of average values of time-dependent physical observables and cross sections. The method of quantum characteristics admits the consistent incorporation of specific quantum effects, such as non-locality and coherence in propagation of particles, into the semiclassical transport models. We formulate the principle of stationary action for quantum Hamilton's equations and give quantum-mechanical extensions of the Liouville theorem on the conservation of phase-space volume and the Poincaré theorem on the conservation of $2p$ forms. The lowest order quantum corrections to the Kepler periodic orbits are constructed. These corrections show the resonance behavior.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the many-body potential scattering problem can be reduced to a statistical-mechanical problem of computing an ensemble of quantum characteristics and their derivatives, but this reduction pertains rigorously only to any fixed order in the Planck constant, meaning the method is semiclassical and not an exact quantum solution.

## Method

The authors develop a semiclassical expansion of quantum characteristics, which are the Weyl symbols of Heisenberg operators of canonical coordinates and momenta, in a power series in the Planck constant. They derive a system of coupled first-order ordinary differential equations for the components of quantum characteristics and associated Jacobi fields, and construct the Green function for the lowest order quantum correction to classical phase-space trajectories. The method is applied to construct the lowest order quantum corrections to Kepler periodic orbits.

## Key numbers

- The lowest order quantum corrections to Kepler periodic orbits are constructed and "show the resonance behavior" (no explicit numerical values given for the corrections).
- The Wigner function for a pure state is restricted by the inequality $-2^n \leq W(\xi) \leq 2^n$ where $n$ is the number of degrees of freedom.
- The phase-space volume variance under quantum evolution appears at order $O(\hbar^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the deformation quantization framework developed by Weyl (1927), Wigner (1932), Groenewold (1946), and Moyal (1949) to many-body scattering problems. It provides a systematic semiclassical expansion method that can incorporate quantum effects such as non-locality and coherence into transport models like Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU), Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD), and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD), which are used in heavy-ion collision physics. The method enables calculation of time-dependent physical observables and cross sections while keeping phase-space trajectories as a computational attribute.
