# Enhancing the creation of elements in laser-assisted heavy-ion fusion reactions

> Low-energy fusion of heavy ions is a fascinating coupling-assisted quantum tunnelling problem, whose understanding is crucial for advancing the synthesis of new elements and isotopes. Quantum dynamical coupled-channels calculations of laser-assisted $^{16}$O + $^{238}$U fusion are presented for both a central collision and the total fusion cross-sections, suggesting that laser-nucleus interaction can enhance the average $^{16}$O + $^{238}$U fusion probability by $6-60 \%$ at subbarrier energies using quasi-static laser fields of intensity $10^{27}-10^{29}$ Wcm$^{-2}$ and photon's energy of $1$ eV. Femtosecond laser pulses are shown to reduce this enhancement by many orders of magnitude.

## Key claim
Quantum dynamical coupled-channels calculations suggest that laser-nucleus interaction can enhance the average 16O + 238U fusion probability by 6-60% at subbarrier energies, but only when using quasi-static laser fields of intensity 10^27-10^29 Wcm^-2 and photon energy of 1 eV; femtosecond laser pulses reduce this enhancement by many orders of magnitude.

## Method
The authors solved the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for a 16O projectile incident on a 238U target, treating the target as a rigid rotor with rotational excitations (0+, 2+, and 4+ states of the ground-state rotational band). The laser-nucleus interaction was included via a dipole-approximation potential on the Hamiltonian diagonal, with off-diagonal laser couplings calculated from reduced transition probabilities. Transmission coefficients were obtained by propagating a Gaussian wavepacket using the Crank-Nicolson method, and total fusion cross-sections were summed over partial waves up to l=60.

## Key numbers
- Laser-assisted fusion enhancement of 6-60% for the average 16O + 238U fusion probability at subbarrier energies, using quasi-static laser fields with intensity 10^27-10^29 Wcm^-2 and photon energy 1 eV
- First excited state of 238U target at 44.92 keV, with reduced electric quadrupole transition probability B(E2, I_g to I_e) = 12.09 x 10^4 e^2 fm^4
- Coulomb barrier height for 16O + 238U is 83 MeV
- For the Vulcan laser parameters (I=10^23 Wcm^-2, hbar omega = 1 eV, 500 fs pulses), the largest fusion enhancement is 0.05%
- Off-diagonal laser-nucleus couplings produce a maximum additional enhancement of order 10^-9 %

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous quantum dynamical model for laser-assisted deuterium-tritium fusion (Thomson, 2024) to heavy-ion reactions, specifically incorporating coupled-channels effects from target excitations. The calculations agree with the average trend of experimental fusion cross-sections from Zhang (1994) and Hinde (1995), and use the coupled-channels formalism of CCFULL (Hagino, 1999). The results suggest that while current laser technology (e.g., Vulcan laser) cannot produce significant enhancement, rapid advancements in laser power may eventually make such fusion enhancement achievable.
