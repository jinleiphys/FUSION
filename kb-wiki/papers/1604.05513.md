# Model independent analysis of nearly Lévy correlations

> A model-independent method for the analysis of the two-particle short-range correlations is presented, that can be utilized to describe e.g. Bose-Einstein (HBT), dynamical (ridge) or other correlation functions, that have a nearly Lévy or streched exponential shape. For the special case of Lévy exponent alpha = 1, the earlier Laguerre expansions are recovered, for the alpha = 2 special case, a new expansion method is obtained for nearly Gaussian correlation functions. Multi-dimensional Lévy expansions are also introduced and their potential application to analyze rigde correlation data is discussed.

# Model independent analysis of nearly Lévy correlations

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent expansion method for analyzing two-particle short-range correlation functions that are approximately Lévy (stretched exponential) in shape, without assuming the standard Fourier-transform relationship between the source distribution and the correlation function. The method is valid for Lévy exponents α between 0 and 2, recovering earlier Laguerre expansions for α = 1 and providing a new expansion for nearly Gaussian correlations at α = 2.

## Method

The authors apply a general orthogonal polynomial expansion method to the special case of a Lévy weight function w(t|α) = exp(-t^α), where t = QR is a scaled relative momentum variable. They construct Lévy polynomials L_n(t|α) via Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization with respect to this weight function, using moments μ_{n,α} = (1/α) Γ((n+1)/α). The correlation function is then expressed as C_2(t) = N {1 + λ exp(-t^α) Σ c_n L_n(t|α)}, where the expansion coefficients c_n characterize deviations from the pure Lévy shape. For multivariate correlations, the authors reduce the problem to one dimension by introducing a dimensionless scaling variable that combines multiple momentum or angular components.

## Key numbers

- The Lévy exponent α is defined in the range 0 ≤ α ≤ 2, with α = 1 recovering Laguerre expansions and α = 2 yielding a new Gaussian expansion.
- The moments of the Lévy weight function are given by μ_{n,α} = (1/α) Γ((n+1)/α), where Γ is Euler's gamma function.
- For α = 1, the first three Lévy polynomials are: L_0 = 1, L_1 = t - 1, L_2 = t^2 - 4t + 2.
- For α = 2, the first three Lévy polynomials are: L_0 = 1, L_1 = (1/2)(√π t - 1), L_2 = (1/16)[2(π - 2)t^2 - 2√π t + (4 - π)].

## Context

This work extends the model-independent expansion method of Csörgő (2000) from nearly Gaussian and nearly exponential correlations to the general class of nearly Lévy (stretched exponential) shapes. It builds on the Lévy polynomials introduced by De Kock (2012) and provides a framework applicable to Bose-Einstein (HBT) correlations, dynamical ridge correlations, and other short-range correlation measurements. The method enables systematic analysis of deviations from pure Lévy shapes in high-energy particle and heavy-ion collisions, with potential applications to multivariate ridge correlation data as studied by Janik (2014).
