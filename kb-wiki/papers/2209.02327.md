# "Pseudo-Conformal" Sound Speed in the Core of Compact Stars

> We present the argument that "pseudo-conformal" symmetry permeates from low density near nuclear matter to high density in the core of massive neutron stars. As a support of this argument, we describe how the quenched $g_A\approx 1$ in nuclei and the sound speed $v_s^2/c^2\approx 1/3$ in compact stars are controlled by emerging scale invariance in nuclear interactions. In our description, quasi-baryons could "masquerade" de-confined quarks in the interior of compact stars.

# Wiki Page: "Pseudo-Conformal" Sound Speed in the Core of Compact Stars

## Key claim

The paper argues that "pseudo-conformal" symmetry, rather than true conformal symmetry, governs nuclear matter from low density near nuclear matter up to the core of massive neutron stars, with the sound speed approaching but not exactly equaling the conformal value of v_s²/c² = 1/3. The hedge is that the trace of the energy-momentum tensor remains nonzero even in the chiral limit at relevant densities, so the speed cannot be truly conformal and is therefore termed "pseudo-conformal."

## Method

The author employs a chiral-scale symmetric effective field theory called G$n$EFT, which incorporates hidden local symmetry (represented by light-quark vector mesons ρ and ω) and scale symmetry (via a "genuine dilaton" field) into a Lagrangian that is then mapped to a density functional theory using Landau-Migdal Fermi-liquid fixed-point theory. The hadron-quark continuity is simulated by a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density n_{1/2} ≳ 2 n₀, which is incorporated into the Lagrangian parameters, and the resulting equation of state is computed at mean-field level with renormalization-group treatment.

## Key numbers

- The sound speed approaches v_s²/c² ≈ 1/3 for densities n ∼ (3–5) n₀, where n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter density.
- The topology change density n_{1/2} is placed at ≳ 2 n₀, with the range 2 ≲ n_{1/2}/n₀ < 4 considered phenomenologically viable.
- The vector manifestation density n_{VM} is estimated at ≳ 25 n₀, far above densities relevant to compact stars.
- The quenched axial-vector coupling constant in nuclei is g_A^{obs} ≈ 1, compared to the free-space value g_A = 1.276.
- The Landau-Migdal quenching factor q^{LM} ≈ 0.79–0.80 yields g_A^{LM} ≈ 1.0, consistent with the measured Gamow-Teller strength B_{GT}^{nature} ≈ 10 in ¹⁰⁰Sn decay, giving q^{nature} ≈ 0.75 and g_A^{nature} ≈ 0.96.
- The polytropic index γ drops from ∼3 below n_{1/2} to near 1 at core density ∼6 n₀.

## Context

This work extends the G$n$EFT framework developed by the author and collaborators (PKLMR, 2017) and contrasts with the "sound velocity interpolation method" of Annala et al. (2020) and the conformal dilaton phase of DeTar, Dunne, and Zong (2021). The pseudo-conformal prediction for the sound speed and equation of state shows qualitative consistency with perturbative QCD calculations (pQCD, 2021) that propagate asymptotic high-density constraints down to compact-star densities. The approach enables a unified description of both the quenched g_A in nuclei and the sound speed in neutron stars as manifestations of emergent scale invariance, and it suggests that quasi-baryons may masquerade as deconfined quarks in the stellar core.
