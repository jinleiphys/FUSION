# Non-perturbative Propagators, Running Coupling and Dynamical Quark Mass of Landau gauge QCD

> The coupled system of renormalized Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark, gluon and ghost propagators of Landau gauge QCD is solved within truncation schemes. These employ bare as well as non-perturbative ansaetze for the vertices such that the running coupling as well as the quark mass function are independent of the renormalization point. The one-loop anomalous dimensions of all propagators are reproduced. Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is found, the dynamically generated quark mass agrees well with phenomenological values and corresponding results from lattice calculations. The effects of unquenching the system are small. In particular the infrared behavior of the ghost and gluon dressing functions found in previous studies is almost unchanged as long as the number of light flavors is smaller than four.

# Non-perturbative Propagators, Running Coupling and Dynamical Quark Mass of Landau gauge QCD

## Key claim
The coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for quark, gluon, and ghost propagators in Landau gauge QCD can be solved within truncation schemes that employ bare and non-perturbative vertex ansätze such that the running coupling and quark mass function are independent of the renormalization point, provided the number of light flavors is smaller than four; the effects of unquenching the system are small.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled system of renormalized Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark, gluon, and ghost propagators in Landau gauge QCD using truncation schemes that incorporate both bare and non-perturbative ansätze for the vertices. The non-Abelian part of the quark-gluon vertex was constructed as a product of a ghost dressing function factor and an Abelian part taken from the Curtis-Pennington vertex or a Ball-Chiu construction, with a free parameter d controlling the ghost and gluon dressing dependence. The Yang-Mills sector was treated using a truncation scheme previously employed in Fischer (2002), and the quark DSE was solved self-consistently with the gluon and ghost propagators as input.

## Key numbers
- The scale Λ_QCD was determined to be 0.71 GeV in the MOM scheme for N_f=0, with fit B leading to a change in Λ_QCD of less than one percent when different fitting regions were chosen.
- The infrared fixed point of the running coupling is α(0)=8.915/N_c, known from infrared analysis.
- The anomalous dimension of the ghost propagator is δ=-9/44 for N_f=0, and the gluon anomalous dimension γ is related by γ=-1-2δ.
- The anomalous dimension of the quark mass function is γ_m=12/(11N_c-2N_f).
- The pion decay constant f_π was calculated using an approximation that incorporates only the leading pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, which is expected to underestimate f_π by 10-20 percent.
- The chiral condensate was extracted from the ultraviolet behavior of the quark mass function in the chiral limit using the relation M(x) → (2π²γ_m/3)(-⟨Ψ̄Ψ⟩)/(x(½ ln(x/Λ²_QCD))^(1-γ_m)).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the Dyson-Schwinger equations in pure Yang-Mills theory (reviewed by Alkofer (2000)) and quark DSE model building (reviewed by Roberts (2000)) by presenting for the first time numerical results for the coupled set of quark, gluon, and ghost propagators including the backreaction of quarks on ghosts and gluons. The truncation scheme improves on older approaches by von Smekal (1997) and Atkinson (1998), and provides an explicit numerical solution for the infrared analysis given by Lerche (2002) and Zwanziger (2001). The quenched results are compared with lattice calculations of the quark propagator by Bonnet (2002) and Bowman (2002), finding very good agreement when the constructed vertex functions are employed. The unquenched results show only moderate differences for N_f ≤ 3 compared to the quenched case.
