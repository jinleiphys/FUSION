# Resonantly Interacting Fermions In a Box

> We use two fundamental theoretical frameworks to study the finite-size (shell) properties of the unitary gas in a periodic box: 1) an ab initio Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculation for boxes containing 4 to 130 particles provides a precise and complete characterization of the finite-size behavior, and 2) a new Density Functional Theory (DFT) fully encapsulates these effects. The DFT predicts vanishing shell structure for systems comprising more than 50 particles, and allows us to extrapolate the QMC results to the thermodynamic limit, providing the tightest bound to date on the ground-state energy of the unitary gas: ξ_S <= 0.383(1). We also apply the new functional to few-particle harmonically trapped systems, comparing with previous calculations.

## Key claim

The paper provides the tightest bound to date on the ground-state energy of the unitary gas, ξ_S ≤ 0.383(1), but this is a variational upper bound obtained by extrapolating Quantum Monte Carlo results to zero effective range, not an exact determination.

## Method

The authors performed fixed-node Diffusion Monte Carlo simulations of a symmetric unitary Fermi gas in a periodic cubic box containing 4 to 130 particles, using a trial wave function with a BCS-type pairing function and Jastrow correlations. They tuned the interspecies interaction to infinite scattering length using a modified Poschl-Teller potential and extrapolated to the zero-range limit by simulating at five different effective ranges. They then calibrated a three-parameter Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) density functional against the QMC results and used it to extrapolate to the thermodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy ratio ξ_S ≤ 0.383(1) for the unitary gas in the thermodynamic limit, representing the tightest variational bound to date
- Best-fit SLDA parameters: α = 1.26(2) (inverse effective mass), ξ_S = 0.3826(5), and η = 0.87(2) (pairing gap parameter)
- Reduced chi-squared χ²_r = 0.7 for the SLDA fit to 23 QMC points from N_+ = 4 to N_+ = 130
- For N_+ = 4 particles, ξ(4) = 0.280 ± 0.002; for N_+ = 130 particles, ξ(130) = 0.380 ± 0.002
- The DFT predicts vanishing shell structure for systems with more than 50 particles

## Context

This work extends previous Quantum Monte Carlo studies of the unitary gas by Carlson and collaborators (2005, 2008) and Morris (2010), providing an order-of-magnitude improvement in precision over earlier continuum QMC bounds of ξ_S ≈ 0.40–0.44. The results are consistent with experimental measurements from Duke (Luo, 2009) quoting 0.39(2) and Paris (Navon, 2010) quoting 0.41(1). The new DFT approach builds on the Superfluid Local Density Approximation of Bulgac (2007) and enables extrapolation to systems beyond direct QMC simulation, with applications to cold atomic gases and dilute neutron matter in neutron stars.
