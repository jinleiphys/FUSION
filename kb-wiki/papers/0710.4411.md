# Cluster radioactivity of Th isotopes in the mean-field HFB theory

> Cluster radioactivity is described as a very mass asymmetric fission process. The reflection symmetry breaking octupole moment has been used in a mean field HFB theory as leading coordinate instead of the quadrupole moment usually used in standard fission calculations. The procedure has been applied to the study of the ``very mass asymmetric fission barrier'' of several even-even Thorium isotopes. The masses of the emitted clusters as well as the corresponding half-lives have been evaluated on those cases where experimental data exist.

# Cluster radioactivity of Th isotopes in the mean-field HFB theory

## Key claim
Cluster radioactivity in Thorium isotopes can be described as a very mass asymmetric fission process using the octupole moment as the leading collective coordinate in mean-field HFB theory, rather than the quadrupole moment used in standard fission calculations. The authors acknowledge that for some isotopes the predicted fragment masses differ by two protons from experimental data, and that a path based on minimum action rather than minimum energy might better reproduce the observed decay modes.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the D1S Gogny force to construct two-dimensional potential energy surfaces (PES) as functions of both quadrupole and octupole moments for even-even Thorium isotopes. Fission paths were identified along the bottoms of valleys in these surfaces, with the very mass asymmetric path (cluster emission) followed by constraining the octupole moment Q3. Half-lives were computed using the WKB approximation with collective inertia evaluated along the Q3-constrained path.

## Key numbers
- Cluster emission barriers for Th isotopes are around 25 MeV high, which is huge compared to spontaneous fission barriers.
- For 226Th emitting 18O: log(t1/2 [s]) = 17.80 (HFB) versus experimental lower limit >16.76.
- For 228Th emitting 20O: log(t1/2 [s]) = 20.53 (HFB) versus experimental 20.73.
- For 230Th emitting 24Ne: log(t1/2 [s]) = 26.22 (HFB) versus experimental 24.63.
- For 232Th emitting 24Ne: log(t1/2 [s]) = 31.74 (HFB) versus experimental lower limit >29.20.
- For 232Th emitting 26Ne: log(t1/2 [s]) = 31.36 (HFB) versus experimental lower limit >29.20.
- For 230Th emitting 22O: log(t1/2 [s]) = 28.83 (HFB), no experimental data available.
- For 232Th emitting 22O: log(t1/2 [s]) = 33.39 (HFB), no experimental data available.
- The differences between theoretical and experimental half-lives do not exceed two orders of magnitude.

## Context
This work extends the application of HFB theory with the Gogny D1S force, previously successful for spontaneous fission properties of heavy nuclei (Robledo and Warda cite prior work from 2003-2005), to the study of very mass asymmetric fission leading to cluster emission. The approach treats cluster radioactivity as a fission process rather than as pre-formed cluster emission, filling the gap between alpha decay and spontaneous fission in the fragment mass spectrum. The results enable further studies using action-minimization methods rather than energy-minimization paths to better predict fragment masses and half-lives for cluster radioactivity.
