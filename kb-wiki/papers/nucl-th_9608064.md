# Coherent eta photoproduction on the deuteron in the $S_{11}$ resonance region

> Coherent eta photoproduction on the deuteron is studied in the $S_{11}$ resonance region neglecting eta rescattering and two-body processes. For the elementary reaction on the nucleon, we have considered the dominant $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance and as background the nucleon pole terms as well as $ρ$ and $ω$ meson exchange. We have studied the influence of different choices for the neutron resonance amplitude, different prescriptions for fixing the invariant mass of the resonance amplitude and different methods for deriving the elementary production amplitude for an arbitrary reference frame.

# Coherent eta photoproduction on the deuteron in the S11 resonance region

## Key claim

The plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) for coherent eta photoproduction on the deuteron, neglecting eta rescattering and two-body processes, can reproduce the experimental upper limits near threshold only if the neutron helicity amplitude is chosen within a specific range; the earlier data of Anderson and Prepost (1969) that suggested a dominant isoscalar amplitude are likely overestimates, as they are at variance with more recent upper limits from Beulertz (1994) and the quasifree data of Krusche et al. (1995).

## Method

The elementary gamma N to eta N amplitude was constructed using an effective Lagrangian approach including the dominant S11(1535) resonance in the s- and u-channels, nucleon pole terms, and rho and omega meson exchange in the t-channel. The coherent reaction on the deuteron was evaluated in the impulse approximation using Bonn r-space deuteron wave functions, with the elementary t-matrix implemented in the gamma-deuteron center-of-mass frame. The study systematically compared different prescriptions for the invariant mass of the gamma-nucleon subsystem (Blankenbecler-Sugar, on-shell active nucleon, on-shell spectator) and two methods for deriving the elementary amplitude in an arbitrary reference frame (direct calculation versus Lorentz boost from the gamma-nucleon c.m. frame).

## Key numbers

- The eta production threshold on the deuteron is at photon lab energy E_gamma^Lab = 629 MeV, corresponding to an invariant mass W_gamma_d^thr = 2424 MeV.
- The best-fit eta-nucleon coupling constant for the elementary process is g_eta^2/(4 pi) = 0.4.
- The proton helicity amplitude used is A^p_1/2 = 130 x 10^(-3) GeV^(-1/2).
- The ratio of neutron to proton total cross sections from quasifree data is sigma_n/sigma_p approximately 2/3, leading to A^n_1/2 = -106 x 10^(-3) GeV^(-1/2) for parameter set (A).
- The experimental upper limits for the coherent total cross section are sigma_tot < 0.040 microbarn at E_gamma^Lab = 632.2 MeV and < 0.064 microbarn at E_gamma^Lab = 637 MeV (Beulertz 1994).
- Reproducing the Anderson and Prepost (1969) data requires A^n_1/2 = 114 x 10^(-3) GeV^(-1/2) and e kappa^s_S11 = 340 x 10^(-3) GeV^(-1/2), giving an isoscalar-to-proton amplitude ratio of 0.88.
- The quark model prediction gives A^s_1/2 / A^p_1/2 approximately (-0.02 plus or minus 0.16).

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach of Bennhold and Mart (1994) and Knochelin (1994) for elementary eta photoproduction to the deuteron target, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between the Anderson and Prepost (1969) data and quark model predictions. The study is motivated by new high-quality data from ELSA and MAMI, particularly the quasifree measurements of Krusche et al. (1995) and the upper limits of Beulertz (1994), which contradict the older results. The calculations enable systematic comparison with future coherent deuteron data and provide a baseline for evaluating the importance of eta rescattering effects, which were treated approximately by Hoshi et al. (1979) and Halderson and Rosenthal (1989) and more recently by Kamalov et al. (1995) near threshold.
