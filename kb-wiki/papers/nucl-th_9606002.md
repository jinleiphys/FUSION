# Nucleon-Antinucleon Interaction from the Skyrme Model

> We calculate the nucleon-antinucleon static potential in the Skyrme model using the product ansatz and including some finite $N_C$ (number of colors) corrections. The mid and long range part of the spin-spin and tensor force are correctly given in both iso-spin channels while the central interaction has insufficient mid-range attraction. This is a well known problem of the product ansatz that should be repaired with better Skyrme dynamics.

## Key claim

The Skyrme model with the product ansatz, including finite \(N_C\) corrections and \(\Delta/\overline{\Delta}\) mixing, reproduces the mid- and long-range spin-spin and tensor parts of the nucleon-antinucleon static potential in both isospin channels, but the central interaction lacks sufficient mid-range attraction, a known limitation of the product ansatz that the authors attribute to the need for better Skyrme dynamics.

## Method

The authors calculate the static \(N\overline{N}\) potential by placing a Skyrmion and an antiSkyrmion on a 3D lattice at separation \(R\) along the \(x\)-axis, using the product ansatz with relative grooming (rotation) matrices. They expand the \(S\overline{S}\) interaction energy in terms of the operators \(W\) and \(Z\) from the \(u(4)\) algebraic method of Obba et al. (1992), then project onto nucleon and antinucleon states including finite \(N_C\) corrections. To account for deformation at intermediate distances, they include \(\Delta\) and \(\overline{\Delta}\) intermediate states via both perturbation theory and full Born-Oppenheimer diagonalization in the space of \(N\overline{N}\), \(N\overline{\Delta}\), \(\Delta\overline{N}\), and \(\Delta\overline{\Delta}\).

## Key numbers

* Pion mass \(m_\pi = 139\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, Skyrme parameter \(e = 4.76\) (these values set the long-distance tail via the Goldberger-Treiman relation).
* For zero separation and zero grooming, the \(S\overline{S}\) system has zero total energy; for relative grooming angle \(\beta = \pi\), the total energy is \(4M\) (four times the single Skyrmion mass), indicating strong repulsion.
* The finite \(N_C\) correction factor for nucleon projection is \(P_N = 1 + 2/N_C\); the factor for \(\Delta\) mixing is \(Q_N = \sqrt{(1 - 1/N_C)(1 + 5/N_C)}\).
* Nucleon mass \(M_{1/2} = 932\) MeV, \(\Delta\) mass \(M_{3/2} = 1232\) MeV, with mass difference \(\delta M = 300\) MeV.
* For large separations, the total energy of the \(S\overline{S}\) system on the lattice is within one percent of twice the single Skyrmion mass.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach, previously applied to nucleon static properties (Adkins, 1983) and nucleon-nucleon interactions (Jackson, 1985; Vinh Mau, 1985; Walhout and Wambach, 1990), to the nucleon-antinucleon system. It builds on studies of Skyrmion-antiSkyrmion collisions (Sommermann et al., 1992; Shao, Walet, and Amado, 1993) and uses the algebraic projection method of Obba et al. (1992) for baryon-baryon interactions. The results are compared with phenomenological \(N\overline{N}\) potentials from Bryan-Phillips (1980) and the Nijmegen group (1990), which are based on meson exchange with \(G\)-parity transformation from \(NN\) potentials. The next step is to perform full dynamical Skyrme calculations beyond the product ansatz to repair the central attraction, and then combine this with previous work on annihilation channels to obtain a unified picture of \(N\overline{N}\) annihilation.
