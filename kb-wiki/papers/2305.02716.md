# Bound state solutions of the Schrödinger equation for dibaryons via asymptotic iteration method

> Conventionally, hexaquarks are claimed to be exotic particles, most of which have not yet been experimentally detected. In this work, we study the mass spectra of exotic hadrons known as hexaquarks in the form of dibaryons. We investigate the hexaquark states with the twobody configuration in more detail. Starting from the analytical solution of the radial Schrödinger equation for the Hulthén potential in the framework of the asymptotic iteration method (AIM), we obtain the binding energy and mass spectrum of charm and bottom hexaquarks for different spin states. We strongly recommend searching experimentally for double charm and bottom dibaryons in the future.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the mass spectra of charm and bottom hexaquarks (dibaryons) can be obtained by solving the radial Schrödinger equation with the Hulthén potential using the asymptotic iteration method (AIM), but this result depends on the approximation that the centrifugal term is replaced by a form valid only for small screening parameter values, and the spin-dependent contributions are treated as perturbations.

## Method

The authors model hexaquarks as two-body dibaryon systems and solve the radial Schrödinger equation with the Hulthén potential using the asymptotic iteration method (AIM), applying an approximation for the centrifugal term (1/r² ≈ δ² e^{-δr}/(1-e^{-δr})²) that is valid only for small δr. They then add spin-dependent corrections (spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms) derived from the Breit-Fermi Hamiltonian as first-order perturbations, using expectation values of spin operators for total spin S=1, 2, and 3 states with orbital angular momenta L=0, 1, and 2.

- For Λ_cΛ_c (qqqqcc) dibaryon with J^PC=2^{++}, binding energy E_nl = -64 MeV, total mass M = 4510 MeV
- For Λ_cΛ_c (qqqqcc) dibaryon with J^PC=3^{--}, binding energy E_nl = -36 MeV, total mass M = 4537 MeV
- For Λ_bΛ_b (qqqqbb) dibaryon with J^PC=1^{--}, binding energy E_nl = -351 MeV, total mass M = 10888 MeV
- For Λ_bΛ_b (qqqqbb) dibaryon with J^PC=2^{++}, binding energy E_nl = -156 MeV, total mass M = 11083 MeV
- For Λ_bΛ_b (qqqqbb) dibaryon with J^PC=3^{--}, binding energy E_nl = -88 MeV, total mass M = 11152 MeV
- For Ω_cΩ_c (sssscc) dibaryon with J^PC=1^{--}, binding energy E_nl = -168 MeV, total mass M = 5222 MeV
- For Ω_bΩ_b (ssssbb) dibaryon with J^PC=1^{--}, binding energy E_nl = -378 MeV, total mass M = 11713 MeV
- All calculations use screening parameter δ = 0.25, radial parameter r = 0.25, principal quantum number n = 1, and total spin S = 1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of heavy tetraquark mass spectra (Shiri et al., 2023) to the hexaquark sector, treating them as dibaryon two-body systems. The approach builds on the theoretical framework of Dyson and Xuong (1964) who first proposed dibaryon states based on SU(6) symmetry, and on lattice QCD results by Lyu et al. who found the Ω_cccΩ_ccc system bound by about 5.68 MeV, and Mathur et al. who found a deeply bound Ω_bbbΩ_bbb dibaryon with binding energy about 89 MeV. The results provide predicted masses for double charm and bottom dibaryons that could guide experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb, WASA-at-COSY, and BELLE.
