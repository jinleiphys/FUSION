# Feynman Amplitude Approach to study the Passage of a Jet in a Medium

> In the Feynman amplitude approach for coherent collisions of a jet with medium partons, the Bose-Einstein symmetry with respect to the interchange of the exchanged bosons leads to a destructive interference of the amplitudes in most regions of the phase space but a constructive interference in some other regions. As a consequence, there is a collective longitudinal momentum transfer to the scatterers along the jet direction, each scatterer carrying a substantial fraction of the incident jet longitudinal momentum. The manifestation of the Bose-Einstein interference may have been observed in angular correlations of hadrons associated with a high-p_T trigger in high-energy collisions at RHIC and LHC.

# Feynman Amplitude Approach to study the Passage of a Jet in a Medium

## Key claim
Bose-Einstein interference from symmetrizing Feynman amplitudes for coherent jet-medium collisions leads to a collective longitudinal momentum transfer to medium scatterers, each carrying a substantial fraction of the incident jet momentum. The effect may have been observed in angular correlations of hadrons associated with a high-p_T trigger at RHIC and LHC, but the experimental evidence for a threshold at n=2 scatterers remains suggestive rather than definitive due to large error bars.

## Method
The author develops a Feynman amplitude approach for coherent collisions of a fast fermion (jet) with n medium fermion scatterers in an Abelian gauge theory, then extends to non-Abelian theory for parton scatterers. The total amplitude is obtained by summing over all n! permutations of virtual boson exchange vertices, enforcing Bose-Einstein symmetry. Using results from Cheng and Wu (1987) for the sum of symmetrized amplitudes, the author derives delta-function constraints 2p·q_i ≈ 0 and evaluates the differential cross section including phase-space factors and medium effects via a spectral function D_i(a_i') for the final scatterer states.

## Key numbers
- Longitudinal coherence time Δt_coh ≈ 25 fm/c for a mini-jet with p_jet ∼ 10 GeV and typical transverse momentum transfer q_T ∼ 0.4 GeV at RHIC and LHC, much greater than the mean-free time between collisions.
- For quark scatterers, the average longitudinal momentum fraction ⟨x_i⟩ = ⟨q_iz⟩/p_jet ∼ 1/(2n), where n is the number of scatterers.
- For gluon scatterers, ⟨x_i⟩ ∼ (1 - m_gT/p_jet) / [n cosh^{-1}(p_jet / n m_gT)].
- Momentum kick model analysis of AuAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV at RHIC gives q_z ∼ 0.8-1.0 GeV and ⟨n⟩ ∼ 6 for most central collisions (with attenuation factor f_J ∼ 0.63).
- Momentum kick model analysis of highest multiplicity pp collisions at √s_NN = 7 TeV at LHC gives q_z ∼ 2 GeV and ⟨n⟩ ∼ 2.4.
- PHOBOS data for AuAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV shows a threshold in ridge yield at N_part ∼ 70, consistent with the predicted n ≥ 2 threshold.

## Context
This work extends the potential model approach of Glauber (1959) and others, which treats scatterers as static potentials and cannot treat longitudinal recoil as an independent dynamical variable. The author instead uses the Feynman amplitude approach, building on results from Cheng and Wu (1969, 1987) for symmetrized amplitudes in high-energy scattering. The theoretical framework provides a foundation for the phenomenological momentum kick model previously developed by Wong (2007-2011), which successfully described extensive sets of triggered associated particle data from STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and CMS Collaborations. The results enable interpretation of the "ridge" structure in Δφ-Δη angular correlations as arising from Bose-Einstein interference in coherent jet-medium collisions, and suggest that the effect may already have been observed at RHIC and LHC.
