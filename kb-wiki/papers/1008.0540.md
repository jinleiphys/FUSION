# Inclusive Hadron Production in the CERN-LHC Era

> We present a detailed phenomenological analysis of single-inclusive hadron production at the CERN-LHC in both proton-proton and proton-lead collisions. First data from the LHC experiments on charged hadron spectra are compared to next-to-leading order QCD expectations, and predictions are made for identified pion, kaon, and proton distributions differential in transverse momentum and rapidity for LHC energies from 900 GeV to 14 TeV. The results are obtained with the latest sets of vacuum fragmentation functions based on global QCD analyses, and recently proposed medium modified fragmentation functions are used to model hadronization in proton-lead collisions assuming standard QCD factorization. Besides estimating theoretical ambiguities due to the choice of factorization and renormalization scales and parton densities, we carefully assess uncertainties due to our present knowledge of parton-to-hadron fragmentation functions with the Lagrange multiplier technique. It is outlined to what extent future LHC data will contribute to further our quantitative understanding of hadronization processes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that next-to-leading order (NLO) pQCD calculations using the DSS fragmentation functions and CTEQ6.6 parton densities describe the first LHC charged hadron spectra well, but the normalization of the theoretical curves must be determined by a fit to the data because the total non-single-diffractive cross section is not specified by the experiments.

## Method

The authors performed NLO pQCD calculations of single-inclusive hadron production cross sections using collinear factorization, convoluting parton distribution functions (CTEQ6.6) with partonic hard scattering cross sections and fragmentation functions (DSS sets for pions, kaons, protons, and unidentified charged hadrons). Theoretical uncertainties were estimated by varying the factorization and renormalization scales by a factor of two around the default choice pT, and fragmentation function uncertainties were assessed using the Lagrange multiplier technique. For proton-lead collisions, medium-modified fragmentation functions were implemented via a convolution of vacuum DSS fragmentation functions with a weight function parameterizing nuclear effects.

## Key numbers

- Scale uncertainty for π+ production at √S = 14 TeV amounts to about 25% at pT ≈ 20 GeV and 14% at pT ≈ 100 GeV; at √S = 0.9 TeV the corresponding uncertainties reach up to 30%.
- PDF uncertainty for hadron production at the LHC is about 5% at pT = 20 GeV, computed with the 44 Hessian eigenvector sets from CTEQ6.6.
- At pT ≈ 20 GeV and √S = 7 TeV, pion yields are roughly a factor of 3 larger than those for K+, with proton production suppressed even further.
- The mean momentum fraction ⟨z⟩ for hadron production at the LHC is ≥ 0.4 at η = 0 and ≥ 0.6 at large forward rapidities, ensuring applicability of fragmentation functions which require z ≳ 0.05.
- Proton-lead collisions at the LHC are conceivable up to √S ≈ 8.8 TeV.

## Context

This work extends previous global QCD analyses of fragmentation functions by the DSS group (2007) and applies them to the new energy regime of the LHC, comparing with first data from ATLAS (2010) and CMS (2010). The analysis also builds on the concept of medium-modified fragmentation functions recently introduced for nuclear collisions, providing predictions for proton-lead collisions that will test whether factorization holds in nuclear environments and help distinguish between cold nuclear matter effects and hot QCD matter properties at RHIC and LHC energies.
