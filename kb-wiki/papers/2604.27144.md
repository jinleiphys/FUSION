# Unmasking Hidden Wigner's Symmetry from First Principles

> We present quantitative evidence that high-quality internucleon forces derived from $χ$EFT exhibit a striking dominance of Wigner's supermultiplet symmetry, without invoking the large-$N_c$ limit of QCD or assumptions about specific nuclei. We trace the manifestation of this symmetry in nuclear structure using the \textit{ab initio} Symmetry Adapted Model (SAM) and identify suppressed spin-isospin polarizability. Our calculations show that a majority of $\rm ^4He$, $\rm ^6Li$, and $\rm ^6He$ wave functions is concentrated in a few $\rm U(4)$ irreducible representations, without imposing any \textit{a priori} constraints on the model space. This emergent feature points to a strategy for reducing explosive many-body bases of the NCSM while retaining physically important configurations needed to compute observables.

## Key claim

The paper presents quantitative evidence that realistic internucleon forces derived from chiral effective field theory exhibit a striking dominance of Wigner's supermultiplet symmetry, without invoking the large-\(N_c\) limit of QCD or making assumptions about specific nuclei, though the dominance is demonstrated only for the two-body parts of four specific \(\chi\)EFT interactions and for light nuclei up to \(A=6\).

## Method

The authors expand four chiral effective field theory interactions (N2LO\(_{\text{opt}}\), N2LO\(_{\text{sat}}\), N3LO\(_{\text{EMN500}}\), and N4LO\(_{\text{EMN500}}\)) in terms of U(3) \(\otimes\) U(4) tensors using the Symmetry Adapted Model (SAM), computing the strength distributions of the four U(4) tensor ranks across varying model spaces (\(N_{\max}\)) and oscillator energies (\(\hbar\Omega\)). They then perform ab initio SAM calculations of ground and excited states of \(^4\)He, \(^6\)Li, and \(^6\)He to analyze the probability distributions of U(4) irreducible representations in the wave functions, and test a U(4)-guided truncation strategy by computing binding energies, electromagnetic moments, radii, and Gamow-Teller matrix elements in restricted model spaces.

## Key numbers

* The mean strengths of U(4)-scalar tensors at \(N_{\max}=4\) are about 6-9, 12-14, and 28-53 times larger than those of \([2,2,0,0]\), \([2,1,1,0]\), and \([4,2,2,0]\) tensors, respectively, across the four interactions at \(\hbar\Omega=20\) MeV.
* The standard deviations of the strength distributions of the scalar tensors are roughly 2-5 times larger than those of the other U(4) tensors across the four interactions.
* For \(^6\)Li with N2LO\(_{\text{opt}}\), the \(\langle 0 \rangle 8\) model space delivers 99%, 99%, 97%, and 96% of binding energies for the states \(1^+_1\), \(3^+_1\), \(2^+_1\), and \(1^+_2\), respectively, relative to full-space calculations at \(\hbar\Omega=20\) MeV.
* For \(^6\)He with N2LO\(_{\text{opt}}\), the \(\langle 4 \rangle 8\) space achieves the same level of prediction (99%) for the ground state and first excited state.
* The restricted model spaces for \(^6\)Li and \(^6\)He have dimensions roughly 68-73% and 48-57% of their complete \(N_{\max}=8\) spaces, respectively.
* The complete \(N_{\max}=8\) reduced Gamow-Teller matrix element for \(^6\)Li agrees with the experimental value within 5.3%.
* The irrep \([1,1,0,0]\) represents roughly 90% of the total Gamow-Teller strength for \(^6\)Li.

## Context

This work extends prior investigations of Wigner's SU(4) symmetry in nuclear systems, which had been studied in the large-\(N_c\) limit of QCD (Kaplan, 1996), in nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts (Lee, 2021), and in No-Core Shell Model wave functions (Muli, 2025). The paper enables a new strategy for reducing the explosive many-body basis of the No-Core Shell Model by selecting configurations based on U(4) irreps, while retaining physically important configurations needed to compute observables including binding energies, radii, electromagnetic moments, and weak decay processes.
