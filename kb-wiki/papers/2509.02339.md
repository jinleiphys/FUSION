# Towards a topological data analysis for heavy-ion collisions

> The collective expansion of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in heavy-ion collisions suggests that geometry-inspired approaches can be useful in extracting information about the QGP. In this work, a systematic study of observables based on topological data analysis is provided for simulations of heavy-ion collisions. Specifically, we implement persistent homology observables for metric-based complexes in the heavy-ion model Trajectum and provide predictions for Pb-Pb and O-O collisions, where the tunable model parameters are taken from a Bayesian analysis performed in Pb-Pb collisions. This, in particular, allows us to compute systematic uncertainties on our observables from the uncertainties in the model parameters. To bridge between new and already established observables, we build a dictionary linking the topological observables to traditional ones, such as particle multiplicities, momentum distributions, and the elliptic flow coefficient. While the persistent homology observables largely reflect known phenomenology and do not show enhanced sensitivity to the model's tunable parameters compared to conventional observables, this study demonstrates the viability and robustness of topological techniques in the context of heavy-ion physics. They may offer alternative perspectives and potential applications in heavy-ion physics.

## Key claim

Persistent homology observables computed from simulated heavy-ion collisions largely reflect known phenomenology and do not show enhanced sensitivity to the model's tunable parameters compared to conventional observables, but the study demonstrates the viability and robustness of topological techniques in heavy-ion physics.

## Method

The authors implemented persistent homology observables (Betti curves and persistence distributions) for alpha complexes built from 2-dimensional point clouds of final-state charged hadron transverse momenta in the Trajectum relativistic hydrodynamics code. They computed these observables for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and O-O collisions at 7 TeV, using 20 parameter sets sampled from a Bayesian posterior previously fitted to Pb-Pb data to propagate systematic uncertainties, and built a dictionary linking topological observables to traditional ones such as multiplicities, momentum distributions, and elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, Betti curves were computed for five centrality classes ranging over 0-40%
- For O-O collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV, particle multiplicities are on average predicted to be a factor of 10 smaller than for Pb-Pb
- The kinematic selection excludes particles with p_T < 0.2 GeV/c from the analysis
- The kink in beta_1(r) for radii r approximately 0.2 GeV/c is due to the kinematic selection on particle p_T
- The ratio beta_0^Delta(r)/beta_0(r) shows nontrivial effects for radii of the order of 1-5 times the QCD scale Lambda_QCD approximately 250 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the application of persistent homology to heavy-ion collisions, building on prior studies such as Hamilton (2022) which used more complicated complex constructions, and complements topological machine learning approaches applied to critical fluctuations by Wang (2024). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from LHC Run 3, particularly for O-O and Ne-Ne collisions, and provide a methodological framework for using topological data analysis as an alternative perspective in heavy-ion physics.
