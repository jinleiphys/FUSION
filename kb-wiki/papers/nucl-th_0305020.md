# Electromagnetic response of confined Dirac particles

> The eigenstates of a single massless Dirac particle confined in a linear potential are calculated exactly by direct solution of the Dirac equation. The electromagnetic structure functions are calculated from the Dirac wave functions of the ground and excited states of the particle by coupling to its conserved vector current. We obtain the longitudinal and transverse structure functions as a function of y=nu-q, where nu and q are the energy and momentum transferred to the target in its rest frame. At values of q>~2.5 GeV, much larger than the characteristic energy scale ~440 MeV of the confining potential, the response exhibits y scaling, a generalization of Bjorken scaling. We compare the exact structure functions with those obtained from the ground state wave functions in the plane wave impulse approximation. The deviation from the Callan-Gross relation is compared with the parton model prediction.

## Key claim

The exact electromagnetic response of a single massless Dirac particle confined by a linear potential exhibits y-scaling (a generalization of Bjorken scaling) for momentum transfers q >~ 2.5 GeV, but this scaling does not imply that final-state interactions can be neglected; in fact, FSI persist in the scaling limit and must be included to reproduce the exact response, contrary to the parton model assumption that struck constituents behave as free particles.

## Method

The Dirac equation was solved exactly for a massless fermion in a linear scalar potential V(r) = sqrt(sigma) r by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a basis of static bag model states, using up to 600 radial states per kappa value and kappa ranging from -100 to 100. The longitudinal and transverse structure functions W_L and W_T were computed from the ground and excited state wave functions by coupling a spin-1/2 electromagnetic probe to the conserved vector current, with the response folded using a Breit-Wigner function of width Gamma(nu) to account for hadronization effects.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy E_0 = 840 MeV for string tension sqrt(sigma) = 1 GeV/fm (characteristic energy scale sigma^{1/4} = 440 MeV)
- Ground state rms radius = 0.66 fm
- At q = 12.5 GeV, strength in the timelike region (y > 0) is 7.3% for Gamma_0 = 0 and 11.5% for Gamma_0 = 100 MeV for W_L; for W_T the corresponding values are 1.4% and 4.5%
- Longitudinal static structure function S_L(q) = 1 to within 0.3% for all q considered
- In the limit q -> infinity, S_L^+(q) = 0.5; at q = 12.5 GeV, S_L^+(q) = 0.52
- First excited state energy E_{J=1} = 1100 MeV (260 MeV above ground state)
- Upper component ground state normalization: integral r^2 f_0^2(r) dr = 3/4; lower component: integral r^2 g_0^2(r) dr = 1/4

## Context

This work extends the author's previous semi-relativistic model (Paris 2001) by using the full Dirac equation with a conserved vector current, addressing the ill-defined current operator of the earlier approach. The model is motivated by the observation of Benhar, Pandharipande, and Sick (2000) that the proton's transverse structure function exhibits y-scaling over 5-100 GeV. The results contradict findings by Pace (1998) and Jeschonnek (2002) that FSI have no effect on structure functions, and instead support Brodsky's (2002, 2003) demonstration that FSI affect parton distribution functions and can produce large single-spin asymmetries. The model enables future study of spin-dependent observables such as helicity and transversity distributions, and comparison with experimental data requires inclusion of radiative gluon corrections for Q^2 evolution.
