# Nuclear fission: The "onset of dissipation" from a microscopic point of view

> Semi-analytical expressions are suggested for the temperature dependence of those combinations of transport coefficients which govern the fission process. This is based on experience with numerical calculations within the linear response approach and the locally harmonic approximation. A reduced version of the latter is seen to comply with Kramers' simplified picture of fission. It is argued that for variable inertia his formula has to be generalized, as already required by the need that for overdamped motion the inertia must not appear at all. This situation may already occur above T=2 MeV, where the rate is determined by the Smoluchowski equation. Consequently, comparison with experimental results do not give information on the effective damping rate, as often claimed, but on a special combination of local stiffnesses and the friction coefficient calculated at the barrier.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the temperature dependence of fission decay rates cannot be characterized by a single effective damping parameter η, as often claimed; instead, the rate depends on a specific combination of local stiffnesses and the friction coefficient at the barrier, and above T ≈ 2 MeV the overdamped Smoluchowski limit applies where inertia drops out entirely.

## Method

The authors derive semi-analytical expressions for the temperature dependence of transport coefficients (inertia M, friction γ, stiffness C) based on numerical calculations within the linear response approach and the locally harmonic approximation. They compute these coefficients for ²²⁴Th as a function of deformation and temperature, including shell effects, pairing correlations, and collisional damping, then use them to evaluate Kramers' fission rate formula generalized for variable inertia.

## Key numbers

- The average shell spacing is ℏΩ₀ = 41 MeV / A^(1/3), giving T_shell ≈ 1–2 MeV for the temperature at which shell corrections vanish.
- The vibrational frequency ℏϖ_a ≈ ℏϖ_b ≈ 1 MeV, with deviations within a factor of 2 or less.
- The inverse relaxation time Γ_kin/ℏ = γ/M is approximated by 0.6 T² / (1 + T²/40) MeV for T in MeV.
- The threshold temperature for the "onset of dissipation" in ²²⁴Th is T ≈ 0.6–0.7 MeV, consistent with T_thresh ≈ 1.09–1.22 MeV from Thoebert (2000).
- The ratio T/(2E_b) at threshold is approximately 0.13 from Thoebert (2000).
- Pairing reduces friction with parameters: at the barrier, a = 10 MeV⁻¹ and T₀ = 0.55 MeV; at the minimum, a = 12 MeV⁻¹ and T₀ = 0.48 MeV.
- The overdamped limit applies above T ≈ 2 MeV, where η_b ≳ 1.

## Context

This work extends microscopic calculations of transport coefficients within linear response theory (Hofmann 1997, Hofmann and Ivanyuk 1999) to provide semi-analytical parameterizations usable for broader nuclear systems. It contradicts the common interpretation that experimental fission rates directly measure the effective damping rate η, showing instead that they probe a combination of stiffness and friction. The results enable improved analysis of experimental data from Paul and Thoennessen (1992) and others, and connect to the threshold temperature phenomenon reported by Thoebert (2000).
