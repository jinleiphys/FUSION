# Relativistic Cooper pairing in the microscopic limit of chiral random matrix theory

> Random matrix theory (RMT) provides a powerful framework for analyzing universal features of strongly coupled physical systems. In quantum chromodynamics (QCD), cold quark matter at asymptotically high density is expected to exhibit color superconductivity (CSC), the analogue of superconductivity in condensed-matter systems. Although CSC phases have been studied within RMT primarily in the macroscopic large-$N$ limit, where $N$ denotes the matrix size, it has remained unclear whether an RMT exists that realizes CSC in the microscopic large-$N$ limit. Here we answer this question in the affirmative by introducing a novel non-Hermitian chiral random matrix model. For three quark flavors, we show that the model exhibits spontaneous breaking of color $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ and flavor $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ symmetries down to the diagonal $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ subgroup, thereby reproducing color-flavor locking in dense QCD. For two flavors, we find that color $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ is spontaneously broken to $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ while the chiral symmetry $\mathrm{SU}(2)_{\mathrm{L}}\times\mathrm{SU}(2)_{\mathrm{R}}$ remains unbroken, consistent with the two-flavor color-superconducting phase.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a novel non-Hermitian chiral random matrix model that, for the first time, realizes color superconductivity (CSC) in the microscopic large-\(N\) limit, reproducing the color-flavor locking pattern for three flavors and the two-flavor color-superconducting phase for two flavors, but with a quantitative difference from the exact chiral Lagrangian of the CFL phase in QCD.

## Method

The author constructs a \(12N \times 12N\) Dirac operator from statistically independent left- and right-handed random matrices, with couplings to Gell-Mann matrices and a skew-symmetric matrix \(C = i\sigma_2 \otimes \mathbb{1}_N\). For three flavors, the partition function is rewritten using Hubbard–Stratonovich fields, the large-\(N\) saddle point is evaluated, and the soft-mode sigma-model representation is derived by integrating out quarks and expanding around the saddle point. For two flavors, a similar bilinearization is performed using complex three-component vectors, and the determinants are evaluated in the microscopic limit.

## Key numbers

* For three flavors, the saddle point for the singular values of the diquark fields \(\Delta_{\mathrm{L,R}}\) is located at \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{L}} = \Lambda_{\mathrm{R}} = \sqrt{3/2} \, \mathbb{1}_3\).
* The determinant identity \(\det(\epsilon_{abc}\epsilon_{fgh}\Delta_{ch}) = -2(\det \Delta)^3\) is used in the three-flavor analysis.
* The resulting sigma-model partition function for three flavors is \(Z \sim \int_{\mathrm{U}(3)} \mathrm{d}\hat{U} \, \exp\big(-\frac{1}{6}[\frac{5}{4}(\Tr(\mathcal{M}\hat{U}))^2 - \Tr((\mathcal{M}\hat{U})^2)] + \text{c.c.}\big)\), where \(\mathcal{M} \equiv \sqrt{N} M\) is held fixed as \(N \to \infty\).
* For two flavors, the determinant factor in the partition function is proportional to \(\big[\frac{m_u m_d}{4}(\Omega_{\mathrm{R}}^\dagger \Omega_{\mathrm{L}} - \frac{m_u m_d}{4})^2\big]^{2N}\).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic random matrix theory approach from QCD-like theories without color superconductivity (Kanazawa, 2009; Akemann, 2009, 2010) to three-color QCD at high density, where diquark condensates break color symmetry. It provides the first RMT realization of color-flavor locking in the microscopic limit, complementing the macroscopic RMT studies of CSC phases, and enables future analytical computation of universal spectral correlations of the Dirac operator in dense QCD. The resulting sigma-model is compared to the chiral effective theory of the CFL phase (Son, 1999; Schafer, 2001), showing the same symmetry-breaking pattern but with a quantitative difference in the mass term coefficients.
