# Selected Topics in Three- and Four-Nucleon Systems

> Two different aspects of the description of three- and four-nucleon systems are addressed. The use of bound state like wave functions to describe scattering states in $N-d$ collisions at low energies and the effects of some of the widely used three-nucleon force models in selected polarization observables in the three- and four-nucleon systems are discussed.

# Selected Topics in Three- and Four-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim
Bound-state-like wave functions (square-integrable eigenvectors from a generalized eigenvalue problem) can be used as inputs to integral relations derived from the Kohn Variational Principle to produce second-order estimates of N-d scattering phase shifts below the deuteron breakup threshold, yielding results in complete agreement with exact calculations. The N2LOL three-nucleon force model, when its strengths are adjusted to reproduce the A=3 and A=4 binding energies and the doublet scattering length, appreciably reduces the longstanding discrepancy in vector analyzing powers for p-d and p-3He scattering compared to older three-nucleon force models.

## Method
The author uses integral relations derived from the Kohn Variational Principle to obtain a second-order estimate of the scattering R-matrix: R^(2nd) = A^(-1) B^(2nd), where the matrix elements involve the Hamiltonian and channel wave functions containing regular and regularized irregular solutions. Bound-state-like wave functions are obtained by solving a generalized eigenvalue problem (H - E_n) Psi^(n) = 0 in a complete square-integrable basis; these eigenvectors, which asymptotically go to zero, are then inserted into the integral relations to compute phase shifts at energies E_n^0 = E_n - E_d. For the three-nucleon force analysis, the author compares the Tucson-Melbourne (TM'), Brazil, Urbana IX (URIX), and N2LOL chiral effective field theory models by varying their operator strengths to reproduce the triton and 4He binding energies and the n-d doublet scattering length, then computing polarization observables.

## Key numbers
- AV18 NN potential alone gives B(3H) = 7.624 MeV, B(4He) = 24.22 MeV, and 2a_nd = 1.258 fm
- N3LO-Idaho NN potential alone gives B(3H) = 7.854 MeV, B(4He) = 25.38 MeV, and 2a_nd = 1.100 fm
- AV18+TM' gives B(3H) = 8.440 MeV, B(4He) = 28.31 MeV, and 2a_nd = 0.623 fm
- AV18+URIX gives B(3H) = 8.479 MeV, B(4He) = 28.48 MeV, and 2a_nd = 0.578 fm
- N3LO-Idaho+N2LOL gives B(3H) = 8.474 MeV, B(4He) = 28.37 MeV, and 2a_nd = 0.675 fm
- Experimental values: B(3H) = 8.482 MeV, B(4He) = 28.30 MeV, and 2a_nd = 0.645 +/- 0.003 +/- 0.007 fm
- The n-d effective range function for J=1/2+ and J=3/2+ states computed using bound-state-like wave functions agrees with exact results across the energy interval [0, |E_d|]
- Polarization observables A_y and iT_11 for p-d scattering are shown at E_p = 3 MeV for AV18+N2LOL and AV18+UR models

## Context
This work extends the integral-relation approach developed by Kievsky (2010) and Barletta (2009) for using bound-state techniques to describe scattering states, complementing the stochastic variational method applied to alpha+n scattering by Suzuki (2008). The three-nucleon force analysis builds on a critical comparison of TNF models by Kievsky (2010) and addresses the long-standing discrepancy in polarization observables documented by Kievsky (2001) and Fisher (2006). The results enable further studies of continuum states in few-nucleon systems using bound-state methods and motivate continued refinement of chiral three-nucleon forces to resolve remaining discrepancies in vector analyzing powers.
