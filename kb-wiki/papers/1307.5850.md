# A lattice study of the jet quenching parameter

> We present a first-principle computation of the jet quenching parameter, which describes the momentum broadening of a high-energy parton moving through the deconfined state of QCD matter at high temperature. Following an idea originally proposed by Caron-Huot, we explain how one can evaluate the soft contribution to the collision kernel characterizing this real-time phenomenon, analyzing certain gauge-invariant operators in a dimensionally reduced effective theory (electrostatic QCD), which can be studied non-perturbatively via simulations on a Euclidean lattice. Our high-precision numerical computations at two different temperatures indicate that soft contributions to the jet quenching parameter are large. After discussing the systematic uncertainties involved, we present a quantitative estimate for the jet quenching parameter in the temperature range accessible at heavy-ion colliders, and compare it to results from phenomenological models as well as to strong-coupling computations based on the holographic correspondence.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first-principle lattice computation of the soft contribution to the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) in high-temperature QCD, showing that these soft contributions are large and that a non-perturbative treatment is necessary because perturbative analysis beyond next-to-leading order becomes intrinsically non-perturbative.

## Method

The authors use dimensional reduction to construct electrostatic QCD (EQCD), a three-dimensional effective theory that captures the dynamics of long-wavelength (soft) modes at the scale \(gT\). They perform lattice simulations of EQCD with Wilson discretization, measuring decorated Wilson loops that correspond to light-cone Wilson loops in the original four-dimensional theory, from which they extract the coordinate-space transverse collision kernel \(V(r)\) and then the soft contribution to \(\hat{q}\). The simulations are carried out at two different temperatures, corresponding to \(x_L = 0.1\) and \(x_L = 0.06\), with lattice spacings ranging from \(\beta = 12\) to \(\beta = 80\) and volumes up to \(120^3 \times 168\).

## Key numbers

- The soft contribution to the jet quenching parameter is computed at two temperatures: one corresponding to \(x_L = 0.1\) (lower temperature) and one to \(x_L = 0.06\) (higher temperature), with the temperature related to the EQCD parameters via \(T/\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}} \simeq \exp(7.2 y - 2.1)\) for pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, \(\exp(6.1 y - 2.3)\) for QCD with \(n_f=2\) light flavors, and \(\exp(5.9 y - 2.4)\) for QCD with \(n_f=3\) light flavors.
- The Debye mass parameter is given by \(\mE^2 = (1 + n_f/6) g^2(\mu) T^2\), and the quartic coupling by \(\lambda_3 = (9 - n_f)/(24\pi^2) g^4(\mu) T\).
- The lattice simulations use \(\beta = 6/(\gE^2 a)\) values from 12 to 80, with lattice sizes up to \(120 \times 120 \times 168\) and total statistics ranging from \(7 \times 10^3 \times 10\) to \(1.4 \times 10^6 \times 10\) configurations.
- The value of \(\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}\) for \(n_f=2\) is \(\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}} = 255^{+40}_{-15}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Caron-Huot (2008) to compute the jet quenching parameter via Euclidean lattice methods, providing the first non-perturbative determination of the soft contributions. The results enable model-independent estimates of \(\hat{q}\) at temperatures accessible at heavy-ion colliders, complementing perturbative weak-coupling expansions (Arnold, 2008; Caron-Huot, 2008) and strong-coupling computations based on holographic correspondence. The paper also compares with related studies by Laine (2012), Benzke (2012), and Laine (2013), and opens the possibility for future work combining these lattice results with perturbative hard-mode contributions.
