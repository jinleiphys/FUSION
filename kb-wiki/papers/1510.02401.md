# Effective field theory for nuclear vibrations with quantified uncertainties

> We develop an effective field theory (EFT) for nuclear vibrations. The key ingredients - quadrupole degrees of freedom, rotational invariance, and a breakdown scale around the three-phonon level - are taken from data. The EFT is developed for spectra and electromagnetic moments and transitions. We employ tools from Bayesian statistics for the quantification of theoretical uncertainties. The EFT consistently describes spectra and electromagnetic transitions for $^{62}$Ni, $^{98,100}$Ru, $^{106,108}$Pd, $^{110,112,114}$Cd, and $^{118,120,122}$Te within the theoretical uncertainties. This suggests that these nuclei can be viewed as anharmonic vibrators.

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory (EFT) for nuclear vibrations that describes spectra and electromagnetic transitions in a set of medium-mass nuclei within quantified theoretical uncertainties, suggesting these nuclei can be viewed as anharmonic vibrators. The key condition is that the EFT's breakdown scale is around the three-phonon level (approximately 3 times the phonon energy), meaning the approach is only valid for states below this energy and loses predictive power beyond next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors construct an EFT using quadrupole degrees of freedom (boson creation and annihilation operators \(d^\dagger_\mu\) and \(d_\mu\)) with rotational invariance, taking the breakdown scale \(\Lambda \approx 3\omega\) from the observed proliferation of states at the three-phonon level. The leading-order (LO) Hamiltonian is the harmonic quadrupole oscillator \(\hat{H}_{\rm LO} = \omega \hat{N}\), and next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections include three phonon-number-conserving terms: \(\hat{N}^2\), \(\hat{\Lambda}^2\) (SO(5) Casimir), and \(\hat{I}^2\) (angular momentum squared). Electromagnetic operators are expanded in powers of the small parameter \(\varepsilon = N\omega/\Lambda\), with the quadrupole operator including both minimal coupling and non-minimal couplings parameterized by LECs \(Q_0\), \(Q_1\), and \(Q_{2L}\). Bayesian statistics with log-normal priors is employed to quantify theoretical uncertainties based on the assumption of natural-sized coefficients in the EFT expansion.

## Key numbers

* Phonon energy \(\omega \approx 0.6\) MeV for nuclei with mass number about 100
* Breakdown scale \(\Lambda = 3\omega\) (approximately 1.8 MeV for mass-100 nuclei)
* Expansion parameter \(\varepsilon = N\omega/\Lambda\) for the \(N\)-phonon level
* LO energies: \(E_{\rm LO} = \omega N\) (harmonic oscillator spectrum)
* NLO energy correction: \(e_{\rm NLO} = g_N N^2 + g_v v(v+3) + g_I I(I+1)\), where \(v\) is SO(5) seniority and \(I\) is spin
* LO reduced matrix elements for \(E2\) transitions: \(\langle 0_1^+||\hat{Q}||2_1^+\rangle_{\rm LO} = \sqrt{5}Q_0\), \(\langle 2_1^+||\hat{Q}||0_2^+\rangle_{\rm LO} = \sqrt{2}Q_0\), \(\langle 2_1^+||\hat{Q}||2_2^+\rangle_{\rm LO} = \sqrt{10}Q_0\), \(\langle 2_1^+||\hat{Q}||4_1^+\rangle_{\rm LO} = \sqrt{18}Q_0\)
* LO diagonal quadrupole matrix elements: \(\langle 2_1^+||\hat{Q}||2_1^+\rangle_{\rm LO} = 2\sqrt{5}Q_1\), \(\langle 2_2^+||\hat{Q}||2_2^+\rangle_{\rm LO} = -6\sqrt{5}Q_1/7\), \(\langle 4_1^+||\hat{Q}||4_1^+\rangle_{\rm LO} = 6\sqrt{110}Q_1/7\)
* LO magnetic moments: \(\langle I||\hat{\mu}||I\rangle = g\sqrt{I(I+1)(2I+1)}\), with \(I=4\) states having moments \(\sqrt{6}\) times larger than \(I=2\) states
* Uncertainty estimates: LO uncertainties of size \(\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^2\omega)\), NLO uncertainties of size \(\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^3\omega)\)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to understand nuclear vibrations, which began with Bohr and Mottelson (1952, 1953, 1975) and the harmonic quadrupole oscillator model. It directly addresses the "vibrational problem" documented by numerous experimental studies (Lehmann 1996, Corminboeuf 2000, Kadi 2003, Yates 2005, Williams 2006, Garrett 2007, Bandyopadhyay 2007, Garrett 2008, Batchelder 2009, Chakraborty 2011, Radeck 2012, Batchelder 2012) where \(B(E2)\) transition strengths showed considerable deviations from harmonic predictions. The EFT approach contrasts with earlier anharmonic models by Bes (1969) and Gneuss and Greiner (1969, 1971) that used cubic terms as dominant subleading corrections; the EFT's power counting instead identifies quartic phonon-number-conserving terms as the dominant NLO corrections. The work enables systematic uncertainty quantification for vibrational nuclei and provides testable predictions for diagonal quadrupole moments and magnetic moments that can be compared with future experiments.
