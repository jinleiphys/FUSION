# Conformal anomaly and the vector coupling in dense matter

> We construct an effective chiral Lagrangian for hadrons implemented by the conformal invariance and discuss the properties of nuclear matter at high density. The model is formulated based on two alternative assignment, "naive" and mirror, of chirality to the nucleons. It is shown that taking the dilaton limit, in which the mended symmetry of Weinberg is manifest, the vector-meson Yukawa coupling becomes suppressed and the symmetry energy becomes softer as one approaches the chiral phase transition. This leads to softer equations of state (EoS) and could accommodate the EoS without any exotica consistent with the recent measurement of a $1.97 \pm 0.04\,M_\odot$ neutron star.

## Key claim

The authors construct an effective chiral Lagrangian with conformal invariance and show that taking the "dilaton limit", a procedure that makes Weinberg's mended symmetry manifest, causes the vector-meson Yukawa coupling to become suppressed and the symmetry energy to soften as one approaches chiral phase transition density, potentially accommodating a 1.97 ± 0.04 solar mass neutron star without exotic matter.

## Method

The authors built a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian for pions, vector mesons, and nucleons, implemented with a dilaton field representing broken conformal symmetry. They formulated the model using two alternative chirality assignments for nucleons, "naive" (where nucleon mass comes entirely from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking) and mirror (which allows a chiral-invariant mass term), and then performed a linearization transformation to take the dilaton limit, deriving the resulting effective Lagrangian near chiral restoration.

## Key numbers

- The observed neutron star mass is 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses, used as a constraint on the equation of state
- The vector meson mass is given by m_V = g F_sigma, where g is the HLS gauge coupling and F_sigma is the decay constant associated with the hidden local symmetry breaking
- In the dilaton limit, the parameters become kappa = g_A = g_V = 1, where kappa = (F_pi/F_chi)^2 and g_A, g_V are nucleon coupling constants
- The vector-meson–nucleon coupling g_VN = g (1 - g_V) goes to zero in the dilaton limit
- For the mirror model, the chiral-invariant nucleon mass m_0 is estimated thermodynamically as 210 MeV, using the bag constant B = (0.4 GeV)^4 and glueball mass m_G = 1.7 GeV
- The gluon condensate at the pseudo-critical temperature T_ch ~ 170 MeV is about half its zero-temperature value: <G G>_{T_ch} ~ (1/2) <G G>_{T=0} = 0.0006 GeV^4

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry approach of Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and the dilaton-limit concept proposed by Beane and van Kolck (2005) to include baryonic matter at high density. It builds on the Brown-Rho scaling idea and the two-component dilaton framework of Miransky (2004), where a "soft" dilaton melts with chiral symmetry restoration while a "hard" dilaton remains. The results enable future studies of neutron star equations of state that incorporate the suppression of short-range repulsion near chiral restoration, and the authors note that the mirror model's non-zero m_0 at chiral restoration will produce a different equation of state from the naive model, to be reported subsequently.
