# Comparison of results for the electromagnetic form factors of the proton at low $Q^2$

> The goal in this work is the comparison of five parameterisations of the Sachs form factors of the proton $G^p_E$ (electric) and $G^p_M$ (magnetic) at low $4$-momentum transfer $Q^2$. It will be shown that a simple model, based on two dipoles which admit as parameters the rms electric charge radius $r_{E;p}$ and the rms magnetic radius $r_{M;p}$ of the proton, suffices for the purposes of the phase-shift analyses (PSAs) of the low-energy pion-nucleon ($πN$) data. The replacement of the electromagnetic form factors, currently used in the ETH model of the $πN$ interaction, by the parameterisation of this work will enable the removal from the PSAs of this research programme of the largest part of the dependence on extraneous sources.

# Comparison of results for the electromagnetic form factors of the proton at low $Q^2$

## Key claim
A simple two-dipole model parameterising the proton Sachs form factors $G^p_E$ and $G^p_M$ using only the rms electric charge radius $r_{E;p}$ and rms magnetic radius $r_{M;p}$ suffices for phase-shift analyses (PSAs) of low-energy pion-nucleon ($\pi N$) data with the ETH model, provided reliable radius values are supplied. The differences between this parameterisation and four established schemes (standard dipole, A1, VAMZ, YAHL) are comparable to the differences among those schemes themselves in the $Q^2$ region of interest ($Q^2 \lesssim 0.1$ GeV$^2$).

## Method
The author compared five parameterisations of the proton Sachs form factors $G^p_E$ and $G^p_M$ at low $Q^2$: the standard dipole (Hand 1963), a spline fit from the A1 Collaboration (Bernauer 2010, 2014), the VAMZ Padé approximant (Venkat 2011), the YAHL z-expansion (Ye 2018), and a new two-dipole model proposed in this work. For the new model, the parameters $r_{E;p}$ and $r_{M;p}$ were fixed to the values corresponding to each of the four established parameterisations, and the maximal symmetric mean absolute differences in $G^p_E$ and $G^p_M$ were computed over the $Q^2$ domain up to about 0.1 GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers
- Maximal symmetric mean absolute differences in $G^p_E$ among the four established parameterisations range from 0.23% (A1 vs YAHL) to 2.76% (standard dipole vs VAMZ).
- Maximal symmetric mean absolute differences in $G^p_M$ among the four established parameterisations range from 0.51% (standard dipole vs A1) to 2.05% (A1 vs VAMZ).
- For the new two-dipole model compared with the A1 parameterisation: $d_{\rm max}$ = 2.06% for $G^p_E$ and 2.58% for $G^p_M$, using $r_{E;p}=0.8780$ fm and $r_{M;p}=0.7681$ fm.
- For the new model compared with VAMZ: $d_{\rm max}$ = 1.36% for $G^p_E$ and 0.95% for $G^p_M$, using $r_{E;p}=0.8776$ fm and $r_{M;p}=0.8598$ fm.
- For the new model compared with YAHL: $d_{\rm max}$ = 2.30% for $G^p_E$ and 1.13% for $G^p_M$, using $r_{E;p}=0.8790$ fm and $r_{M;p}=0.8510$ fm.
- The standard dipole uses $r_{E;p}=r_{M;p}=0.8112$ fm, yielding perfect agreement with the new model (0% difference) as expected.
- The $Q^2_{\rm max}$ relevant to the ETH model is approximately 98941.3 MeV$^2$ $\approx$ 0.1 GeV$^2$, attained at pion laboratory kinetic energy $T=100$ MeV and backward scattering.
- In representative PSAs with the ETH model, median relative uncertainty in fitted model parameters is about 5%; maximal relative uncertainties in $\pi^+ p$ differential cross-section predictions between 20 and 45 MeV range from 5.4% to 6.1%.

## Context
This work extends the ETH model of the $\pi N$ interaction (Goudsmit 1994, Matsinos 2006, 2014, 2017) by proposing a self-contained parameterisation of proton form factors that removes dependence on extraneous sources. The comparison includes the standard dipole (Hand 1963), the A1 Collaboration spline fits (Bernauer 2010, 2014), the VAMZ Padé parameterisation (Venkat 2011), and the YAHL z-expansion (Ye 2018). The author notes criticism by Sick (2018) and Sick and Trautmann (2014) regarding extraction of rms radii from form-factor parameterisations. The work also discusses the ongoing proton-radius puzzle, referencing results from muonic hydrogen (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013), electronic hydrogen (Beyer 2017, Bezginov 2019), the PRad experiment (Xiong 2019), and the lower bound from Hagelstein and Pascalutsa (2019). The proposed parameterisation enables future PSAs of low-energy $\pi N$ data to be conducted without importing external form-factor results.
