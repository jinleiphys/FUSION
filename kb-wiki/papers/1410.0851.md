# Effects of the symmetry energy on properties of neutron star crusts near the neutron drip density

> We study the effects of the symmetry energy on the neutron drip density and properties of nuclei in neutron star crusts. The nonuniform matter around the neutron drip point is calculated by using the Thomas--Fermi approximation with the relativistic mean-field model. The neutron drip density and the composition of the crust are found to be correlated with the symmetry energy and its slope. We compare the self-consistent Thomas--Fermi approximation with other treatments of surface and Coulomb energies and find that these finite-size effects play an essential role in determining the equilibrium state at low density.

## Key claim

The neutron drip density increases with the symmetry energy slope \(L\) when using self-consistent Thomas-Fermi calculations, but this correlation holds only for model sets where the symmetry energy is fixed at a reference density of 0.11 fm\(^{-3}\) rather than at saturation density.

## Method

The authors used the Thomas-Fermi approximation within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model to describe nonuniform matter in Wigner-Seitz cells at zero temperature, assuming spherical nuclei in a body-centered-cubic lattice. They compared this self-consistent approach with the coexisting phases (CP) method and the compressible liquid-drop (CLD) model, using four RMF parametrizations (NL3, TM1, FSU, IUFSU) and two additional sets of models generated from TM1 and IUFSU by varying \(g_\rho\) and \(\Lambda_{\rm v}\) to achieve different symmetry energy slopes \(L\) while keeping the symmetry energy fixed at \(n_{\rm fix}=0.11\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

- Neutron drip density \(n_{\rm drip}\) increases with \(L\) for both TM1-based and IUFSU-based model sets; for example, in the TM1 set, \(n_{\rm drip}\) rises from approximately \(2.5 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(L \approx 40\) MeV to about \(6 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) at \(L \approx 110\) MeV (see Fig. 7).
- The symmetry energy at saturation density \(E_{\rm sym}(n_0)\) for the TM1-based models ranges from about 30.5 MeV to 36.5 MeV as \(L\) increases from 40 MeV to 110 MeV (Table II).
- The neutron-skin thickness \(\Delta r_{np}\) of \(^{208}\)Pb for the TM1-based models increases from about 0.17 fm to 0.28 fm as \(L\) increases from 40 MeV to 110 MeV (Table II).
- The proton number \(Z\) of the droplet at the neutron drip point is around 40–50 for most parametrizations, with IUFSU giving a larger \(Z\) (about 60) due to its relatively low \(L\) value (Fig. 6).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron star crusts using the Thomas-Fermi approach by Oyamatsu (2007) and Grill et al. (2012), who found sensitivity of crust properties to the symmetry energy slope. It also directly compares the self-consistent TF method with the CP method and CLD model, showing that the CP method fails at low densities near the neutron drip point because its perturbative treatment of surface and Coulomb energies leads to total energies larger than homogeneous matter. The results enable more reliable predictions of crust composition and neutron drip density for use in neutron star modeling, and they highlight the need for self-consistent finite-size treatments when constraining the symmetry energy slope from crust observations.
