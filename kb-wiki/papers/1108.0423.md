# Model independent determination of the axial mass parameter in quasielastic neutrino-nucleon scattering

> Quasielastic neutrino-nucleon scattering is a basic signal process for neutrino oscillation studies. At accelerator energies, the corresponding cross section is subject to significant uncertainty due to the poorly constrained axial-vector form factor of the nucleon. A model-independent description of the axial-vector form factor is presented. Data from the MiniBooNE experiment for quasielastic neutrino scattering on carbon are analyzed under the assumption of a definite nuclear model. The value of the axial mass parameter, m_A=0.85^{+0.22}_{-0.07} +/- 0.09 GeV, is found to differ significantly from extractions based on traditional form factor models. Implications for future neutrino scattering and pion electroproduction measurements are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper defines the axial mass parameter \(m_A\) in a model-independent way via the slope of the axial-vector form factor at \(q^2=0\), rather than through the traditional dipole ansatz. Under the assumption of a definite nuclear model (the Relativistic Fermi Gas model), the extracted value \(m_A = 0.85^{+0.22}_{-0.07} \pm 0.09\) GeV differs significantly from extractions based on the dipole form factor, suggesting that the dipole ansatz introduces a strong bias in measurements.

## Method

The authors applied a model-independent \(z\)-expansion parameterization of the axial-vector form factor \(F_A(q^2)\), based on analyticity and dispersion relations, to analyze MiniBooNE quasielastic neutrino scattering data on carbon-12. They used the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) nuclear model of Smith and Moniz (1972) to describe nuclear effects, with vector form factors given by the BBA2003 parameterization. The chi-squared function was minimized to extract \(m_A\) from the double differential cross section data, with error intervals defined by \(\Delta \chi^2 = 1\), and the analysis was repeated for different coefficient bounds (\(|a_k|\le 5\) and \(|a_k|\le 10\)) and different \(Q^2_{\rm max}\) cuts.

## Key numbers

- Extracted axial mass parameter from MiniBooNE data: \(m_A = 0.85^{+0.22}_{-0.07} \pm 0.09\) GeV (for \(Q^2_{\rm max}=1.0\) GeV\(^2\), with \(|a_k|\le 5\) bound, first error experimental, second error form factor shape uncertainty)
- Dipole fit to same MiniBooNE data with \(Q^2_{\rm max}=1.0\) GeV\(^2\): \(m_A^{\rm dipole} = 1.29 \pm 0.05\) GeV
- Extracted binding energy parameter from RFG model fit: \(\epsilon_b = 28 \pm 3\) MeV (for \(Q^2_{\rm max}=1.0\) GeV\(^2\), \(k_{\rm max}=7\))
- Extracted axial mass from pion electroproduction data (combined 17-point dataset): \(m_A = 0.92^{+0.12}_{-0.13} \pm 0.08\) GeV
- Dipole fit to same electroproduction data: \(m_A^{\rm dipole} = 1.00 \pm 0.02\) GeV
- Corresponding axial radius from neutrino scattering: \(r_A = 0.80^{+0.07}_{-0.17} \pm 0.12\) fm
- Corresponding axial radius from electroproduction: \(r_A = 0.74^{+0.12}_{-0.09} \pm 0.05\) fm

## Context

This work extends the model-independent \(z\)-expansion formalism previously applied to nucleon vector form factors by Hill (2010) to the axial-vector form factor, addressing tensions between neutrino scattering experiments at different energies and between neutrino scattering and pion electroproduction measurements. The analysis shows that the commonly used dipole ansatz (which yielded world averages of \(m_A^{\rm dipole}=1.026\pm0.021\) GeV from pre-1990 neutrino data and \(1.069\pm0.016\) GeV from pion electroproduction as reported by Bernard et al. (2001), versus MiniBooNE's \(m_A^{\rm dipole}=1.35\pm0.17\) GeV) introduces a strong bias that can explain apparent discrepancies. The results enable robust isolation of nuclear effects from nucleon-level amplitudes and provide a framework for consistent comparison across different experiments, including future measurements from MiniBooNE, NOMAD, K2K, MINOS, and planned neutrino oscillation experiments.
