# Dilepton production in a schematic causal viscous hydrodynamics

> Assuming that in the hot dense matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the energy density, entropy density, and pressure as well as the azimuthal and space-time rapidity components of the shear tensor are uniform in the direction transversal to the reaction plane, we derive a set of schematic equations from the Isreal-Stewart causal viscous hydrodynamics. These equations are then used to describe the evolution dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions by taking the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of $1/4π$ for the initial quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase and of ten times this value for the later hadron-gas (HG) phase. Using the production rate evaluated with particle distributions that take into account the viscous effect, we study dilepton production in central heavy-ion collisions. Compared with results from the ideal hydrodynamics, we find that although the dilepton invariant mass spectra from the two approaches are similar, the transverse momentum spectra are significantly enhanced at high transverse momenta by the viscous effect. We also study the transverse momentum dependence of dileptons produced from QGP for a fixed transverse mass which is essentially absent in the ideal hydrodynamics, and find that this so-called transverse mass scaling is violated in the viscous hydrodynamics, particularly at high transverse momenta.

# Dilepton production in a schematic causal viscous hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The authors claim that while the dilepton invariant mass spectra from viscous and ideal hydrodynamics are nearly identical, the transverse momentum spectra are significantly enhanced at high transverse momenta due to viscous effects; however, this enhancement is most reliable for dileptons from quark-gluon plasma, as the large viscosity in the hadron-gas phase can produce unphysical negative yields for high-invariant-mass dileptons from pion-pion annihilation, requiring those contributions to be set to zero.

## Method

The authors derived a set of schematic equations from Israel-Stewart causal viscous hydrodynamics by assuming uniformity of energy density, entropy density, pressure, and the azimuthal and space-time rapidity components of the shear tensor in the transverse direction. They solved these equations using a quasi-particle model for the QGP equation of state and a resonance gas model for the hadron-gas phase, taking the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio as 1/4π for QGP and ten times that value for HG. Dilepton production rates were computed using particle distributions that include both first-order and second-order viscous corrections, considering quark-antiquark annihilation in QGP and pion-pion annihilation in HG.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: 1/4π for QGP, and ten times this value (10/4π) for the hadron-gas phase
- Initial conditions for RHIC: thermalization time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c, initial temperature T₀ = 338 MeV, initial radial flow velocity V₀ = 0.01 c
- QGP phase duration: approximately 5.6 fm/c; mixed phase: 1.5 fm/c; HG phase: 10.2 fm/c
- Inverse slope parameters of quark and pion transverse momentum spectra in viscous hydrodynamics: 330 MeV and 160 MeV, respectively, at temperatures of 300 MeV and 150 MeV
- Dilepton invariant mass spectra from viscous and ideal hydrodynamics are "almost identical" at all invariant masses
- Transverse mass scaling of dileptons from QGP is violated in viscous hydrodynamics at high transverse momenta

## Context

This work extends the study of Dusling (2008) on viscous effects in dilepton production, which used non-causal Navier-Stokes viscous hydrodynamics and only first-order viscous corrections, by employing the causal Israel-Stewart formalism and including second-order corrections. The study also builds on the schematic ideal hydrodynamics approach of Biro (1981) and Ko (1988). The results enable future comparisons between causal and non-causal viscous treatments of dilepton observables in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, and demonstrate that the transverse mass scaling of dileptons from QGP, previously predicted in ideal hydrodynamics by Asakawa (1993), is broken by viscous effects.
