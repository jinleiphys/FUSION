# Phase diagram determination at fivefold nuclear compression

> In the standard model of particle physics, the strong force is characterized by the theory of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). It is commonly understood from QCD properties that hadrons, at sufficiently high temperatures or densities, melt into their constituent quarks, thereby undergoing a deconfinement transition to a new phase of quarks and gluons, often referred to as quark matter or quark-gluon plasma (QGP) \cite{qcd00,qcd01}. Although QGP has been observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions \cite{qgp1,qgp2}, uncertainties remain about when the onset of deconfinement occurs. After comparing simulations from a reliable hadron and quark relativistic transport model with recent data from the STAR experiment, we determined that the onset of the hadron-quark phase transition occurs at about five times nuclear compression, corresponding to temperature $T\sim$ 112 MeV and baryon chemical potential $μ_{B}\sim$ 586 MeV, in the nuclear matter phase diagram. This discovery has significant implications for the studies of both the early and present universe \cite{ann2006}, including the fraction of dark matter formed in the early universe \cite{bhd2016,bhf1997,pbh20} and the structure and dynamics of neutron stars and their mergers \cite{nature2020}.

## Key claim
By comparing simulations from a hadron and quark relativistic transport model with STAR data, the onset of the hadron-quark phase transition occurs at about five times nuclear compression, corresponding to temperature T ~ 112 MeV and baryon chemical potential μ_B ~ 586 MeV, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the transition is inferred from mixing hadron and quark transport events in varying proportions and is described as occurring "partially" at this point.

## Method
The study used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model in two modes: a pure hadron cascade mode (AMPT-HC) with isospin- and momentum-dependent mean-field potentials, and a string melting mode (AMPT-SM) that includes partonic degrees of freedom. The author compared simulated directed flow (v1) and elliptic flow (v2) for various hadrons against STAR data from Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 3 and 4.5 GeV, then determined the fraction of quark matter by mixing events from the two transport modes in different proportions to match the proton elliptic flow data at √s_NN = 4.5 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The hadron-quark phase transition occurs at baryon density ρ ~ (5 ± 0.7)ρ_0, where ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is the nuclear saturation density.
- The corresponding temperature is T ~ 112 MeV and baryon chemical potential μ_B ~ 586 MeV.
- Approximately one quarter of the matter generated in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 4.5 GeV is quark matter.
- The AMPT-HC model parameters include: saturation density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, binding energy E_0 = -16 MeV, incompressibility κ_0 = 230 MeV, isoscalar effective mass m_s* = 0.7 m, and symmetry energy E_sym(ρ_0) = 34.57 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the search for the QCD phase transition boundary in the baryon-rich region of the nuclear matter phase diagram, which is the primary goal of the Beam Energy Scan II (BES-II) program at RHIC-STAR and the CBM experiment at FAIR. The results are consistent with the author's earlier study of double strangeness production (Yong 2023) and address the ambiguity between STAR findings at √s_NN = 3 GeV (which found no evidence of quark matter) and at √s_NN = 4.5 GeV (which showed trends suggestive of number-of-constituent-quark scaling). The determined phase transition point has implications for studies of primordial black hole formation in the early universe and for neutron star structure and mergers.
