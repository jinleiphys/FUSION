# A Small Parameter Method for Few-Body Problems

> A procedure to solve few-body problems which is based on an expansion over a small parameter is developed. The parameter is the ratio of potential energy to kinetic energy in the subspace of states having not small hyperspherical quantum numbers, K>K_0. Dynamic equations are reduced perturbatively to those in the finite subspace with K \le K_0. The contribution from the subspace with K>K_0 is taken into account in a closed form, i.e. without an expansion over basis functions.

## Key claim

The paper develops a perturbative method for solving few-body problems by expanding over a small parameter defined as the ratio of potential energy to kinetic energy in the subspace of states with hyperspherical quantum numbers \(K > K_0\), where the parameter is roughly \(K_0^{-2}\); the method reduces dynamic equations to those in a finite subspace with \(K \le K_0\) and accounts for the \(K > K_0\) subspace in closed form without basis expansion, with the hedge that the approach is practical only when low-order corrections are sufficient.

## Method

The total Hilbert space is split into subspaces with \(K \le K_0\) and \(K > K_0\) using projectors \(P_{K_0}\) and \(Q_{K_0}\). The high-\(K\) component \(\Psi_\lambda^h\) is expressed perturbatively in terms of the low-\(K\) component \(\Psi_\lambda^l\) via the Green function \(\Gamma_{K_0}(E) = [Q_{K_0}(H-E)Q_{K_0}]^{-1}\), leading to an effective equation for \(\Psi_\lambda^l\) alone: \(P_{K_0}(H-E_\lambda)\Psi_\lambda^l = P_{K_0}V\Gamma_{K_0}(E_\lambda)V\Psi_\lambda^l\). This equation is solved perturbatively by expanding \(\Gamma_{K_0}\) as a series \(G_{K_0} - G_{K_0}U G_{K_0} + \ldots\), where \(G_{K_0}\) is chosen in coordinate or momentum representation to facilitate Monte-Carlo calculations of matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* For the A=4 bound state problem with a realistic NN interaction including a strong core, choosing \(K_0 = 14\) ensures the correct binding energy at an accuracy level better than 0.1 MeV.
* The net number of hyperspherical harmonics with \(K \le 14\) entering the A=4 problem does not exceed several hundreds.
* In a rough estimate for reactions, a typical \(\sigma_I\) value is 10 MeV, and the condition \(\frac{\hbar^2}{2mR^2}(K_0 + \frac{n-2}{2})^2 \gg |V + \langle T_\rho\rangle - \sigma_R|\) is used to determine required \(K_0\).
* For the numerical estimate of convergence in reactions, a Gaussian potential with \(V_0 = 67\) MeV and \(b = 1.5\) fm (triplet potential reproducing scattering length and effective range) is employed, with \(\sigma_0 = 50\) MeV and \(\rho_0 = 2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the method of Efros (earlier work, Ref. [2]) that used an expansion over \(K_0^{-2}\) for solving large systems of linear equations in bound-state problems within the hyperspherical-hyperradial expansion, but which suffered from a rapid increase in the number of hyperspherical states with \(K\) for \(A > 3\). The present method removes the need for basis expansion for \(K > K_0\), making it suitable for reaction calculations of a general type, unlike the Green Function Monte Carlo method which is limited to bound states and does not provide wave functions for subsequent use. It also contrasts with effective interaction approaches (e.g., in oscillator expansion by Ref. [9] and hyperspherical expansion by Ref. [10]) which disregard higher excitations and cannot reproduce correlation effects from those excitations.
