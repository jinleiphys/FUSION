# Photoproduction of vector mesons off nucleons near threshold

> We propose a simple meson-exchange model of the photoproduction of $ρ$- and $ω$-mesons off protons near threshold ($E_γ$ less than 2 GeV). This model provides a good description of the available data and implies a large $ρ$-nucleon interaction in the scalar channel ($σ$-exchange). We use this phenomenological interaction to estimate the leading contribution to the self-energy of $ρ$-mesons in matter. The implications of our calculation for experimental studies of the $ρ$-meson mass in nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a simple meson-exchange model for photoproduction of ρ- and ω-mesons off protons near threshold (Eγ less than 2 GeV) and claims that the model provides a good description of available data, but the authors explicitly note that the (π+σ)-exchange model for ρ⁰ photoproduction is "much less constrained" and "does not provide a detailed understanding," with the fit subject to "large uncertainty" due to spread in the data.

## Method

The authors construct a t-channel meson-exchange model where ω photoproduction is dominated by π⁰-exchange and ρ⁰ photoproduction by σ-exchange, using Vector Meson Dominance to relate electromagnetic vertices to hadronic couplings. They fit the πNN cut-off Λπ = 0.7 GeV to the γp→ωp total cross section data, then determine the σNN cut-off Λσ = 1 GeV, the σρρ cut-off Λσρρ = 0.9 GeV, and the σρρ coupling g²σρρ/4π = 14.8 from the γp→ρ⁰p data. The resulting ρ-nucleon interaction from the σ-exchange is then used to compute the leading (tadpole) contribution to the ρ-meson self-energy in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The πNN coupling constant is taken as g²πNN/4π = 14, with a dipole form factor cut-off Λπ = 0.7 GeV determined from a fit to the γp→ωp total cross section near threshold (Eγthr = 1.108 GeV).
- The ω→π⁰γ coupling constant is g²ωπγ = 3.315, derived from the experimental partial decay width; the derived ωπρ coupling is g²ωπρ ≃ 84.
- The σ-meson mass is taken as mσ = 500 MeV, with σNN coupling g²σNN/4π = 8; the fitted parameters for the σ-exchange are Λσ = 1 GeV, Λσρρ = 0.9 GeV, and g²σρρ/4π = 14.8.
- The resulting ρ-meson self-energy in matter is Σρ(q²) ≃ -2.81 q² ρ(fm⁻³) GeV², leading to a pole shift at nuclear saturation density ρ₀ from Mρ to 0.82 Mρ and a strength reduction of 32%.

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Brown and Rho (1991) that vector meson masses scale with the quark condensate and decrease in nuclear matter, and by Berman and Drell (1964) and Joos and Kramer (1964) on meson-exchange descriptions of photoproduction. The model provides a phenomenological ρ-nucleon interaction in the scalar channel that can be used to compute in-medium ρ-meson properties, enabling experimental studies of ρ-meson mass shifts in nuclei through dilepton decay measurements planned at CEBAF and GSI. The authors note that better data on ρ photoproduction between 1.5 and 2 GeV and on the ω→π⁰μ⁺μ⁻ form factor are needed to constrain the model further.
