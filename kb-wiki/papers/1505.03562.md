# Dominant contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction at sixth order of chiral perturbation theory

> We present the dominant two- and three-pion-exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction at sixth order (next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, N5LO) of chiral perturbation theory. Phase shifts with orbital angular momentum L>=4 are given parameter free at this order and allow for a systematic investigation of the convergence of the chiral expansion. The N5LO contribution is prevailingly repulsive and considerably smaller than the N4LO one, thus, establishing the desired trend towards convergence. Using low-energy constants that were extracted from an analysis of pi-N scattering at fourth order, the predictions at N5LO are in excellent agreement with the empirical phase shifts of peripheral partial waves.

# Dominant contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction at sixth order of chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim
The sixth-order (N5LO) contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction are prevailingly repulsive and considerably smaller than the N4LO ones, establishing a trend towards convergence of the chiral expansion, but only for peripheral partial waves with orbital angular momentum L >= 4, where the predictions are parameter-free and agree with empirical phase shifts when low-energy constants from pi-N scattering at fourth order are used.

## Method
The authors computed the dominant two-pion-exchange and three-pion-exchange contributions to the NN interaction at sixth order (N5LO) of chiral perturbation theory, including relativistic 1/M_N^2 corrections to leading 2pi-exchange. They derived spectral functions for each diagram class and used spectral-function regularization with a cutoff tilde-Lambda varied between 700 and 900 MeV. Phase shifts for peripheral partial waves (L >= 4) were calculated perturbatively via the K-matrix, using low-energy constants extracted from analyses of elastic pi-N scattering at fourth order by the GW and KH groups.

## Key numbers
- The N5LO contribution is "considerably smaller" than the N4LO one (no specific numerical ratio given in text).
- Spectral-function regularization cutoff tilde-Lambda varied over the range 700 to 900 MeV.
- Pion masses used: m_pi0 = 134.9766 MeV, m_pi+- = 139.5702 MeV, average m_pi = 138.039 MeV.
- Average nucleon mass M_N = 938.9183 MeV (twice the reduced mass of the pn-system).
- Axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.29 (effective value absorbing Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy).
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
- Low-energy constants c_i in units of GeV^{-1}: c_1 = -1.13 (GW set), c_2 = 3.69, c_3 = -5.51, c_4 = 3.71.
- Low-energy constants bar-e_i in units of GeV^{-3}: bar-e_14 = 1.75, bar-e_15 = -5.80, bar-e_16 = 1.76, bar-e_17 = -0.58, bar-e_18 = 0.96 (GW set).

## Context
This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of nuclear forces from N4LO (Entem 2015) to N5LO, addressing the convergence issue where N2LO, N3LO, and N4LO contributions were all roughly the same magnitude. Previous peripheral partial wave investigations were conducted at N2LO by Kaiser et al. (1997, 1998), at N3LO by Entem and Machleidt (2002), and at N4LO by Entem (2015). The results enable systematic testing of chiral symmetry predictions for the NN interaction up to sixth order and provide parameter-free predictions for peripheral waves that can be compared with empirical phase shift analyses from the Nijmegen group (Stoks 1993) and the GWU analysis SP07.
