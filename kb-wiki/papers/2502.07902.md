# Building Neutron Stars with the MUSES Calculation Engine

> Exploring the equation of state of dense matter is an essential part of interpreting the observable properties of neutron stars. We present here the first results for dense matter in the zero-temperature limit generated by the MUSES Calculation Engine, a composable workflow management system that orchestrates calculation and data processing stages comprising a collection of software modules designed within the MUSES framework. The modules presented in this work calculate equations of state using algorithms spanning three different theories/models: (1) Crust Density Functional Theory, valid starting at low densities, (2) Chiral Effective Field Theory, valid around saturation density, and (3) the Chiral Mean Field model, valid beyond saturation density. Lepton contributions are added through the Lepton module to each equation of state, ensuring charge neutrality and the possibility of $β$-equilibrium. Using the Synthesis module, we match the three equations of state using different thermodynamic variables and different methods. We then couple the complete equation of state to a novel full-general-relativity solver (QLIMR) module that calculates neutron star properties. We find that the matching performed using different thermodynamic variables affects differently the range obtained for neutron star masses and radii (although never beyond a few percent difference). We also investigate the universality of equation of state-independent relations for our matched stars. Finally, for the first time, we use the Flavor Equilibration module to estimate bulk viscosity and flavor relaxation charge fraction and rates (at low temperature) for Chiral Effective Field Theory and the Chiral Mean Field model.

## Key claim

The MUSES Calculation Engine can produce neutron star equations of state by smoothly matching three different theoretical models (Crust-DFT, Chiral Effective Field Theory, and Chiral Mean Field) across their regimes of validity, but the matching procedure using different thermodynamic variables affects the resulting neutron star masses and radii by up to a few percent.

## Method

The authors constructed a modular workflow system that calculates equations of state from three separate modules: Crust Density Functional Theory (valid at low densities), Chiral Effective Field Theory (valid around saturation density), and the Chiral Mean Field model (valid beyond saturation density). Lepton contributions were added to each equation of state to enforce charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium. The Synthesis module matched the three equations of state using different thermodynamic variables and methods, then coupled the complete equation of state to a full-general-relativity solver (QLIMR) to calculate neutron star properties. Separately, the Flavor Equilibration module estimated bulk viscosity and flavor relaxation rates for Chiral Effective Field Theory and the Chiral Mean Field model.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density is approximately n_sat ~ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The Chiral Effective Field Theory module is most applicable for n_B between approximately 0.5 n_sat and 1.5 n_sat
- The neutron drip density is approximately n_drip ~ 10^{-3} n_sat
- The deconfinement number density used for boundary delimitation is n_dec ~ 4 n_sat (noted as approximate)
- Matching performed using different thermodynamic variables affects neutron star masses and radii by up to a few percent difference

## Context

This work extends the CompOSE database approach (Oertel 2016, Typel 2013) by providing not just equation of state tables but the actual calculation modules with adjustable parameters, addressing the limitation that CompOSE does not allow users to vary free parameters within equations of state. The MUSES framework enables systematic studies of how matching procedures between different theoretical descriptions affect neutron star observables, and enables future connections between neutron star physics and heavy-ion collision physics through a unified software ecosystem. The paper also presents the first calculations of bulk viscosity and flavor relaxation times using the Flavor Equilibration module for Chiral Effective Field Theory and the Chiral Mean Field model.
