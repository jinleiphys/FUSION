# Cold neutrons trapped in external fields

> The properties of inhomogeneous neutron matter are crucial to the physics of neutron-rich nuclei and the crust of neutron stars. Advances in computational techniques now allow us to accurately determine the binding energies and densities of many neutrons interacting via realistic microscopic interactions and confined in external fields. We perform calculations for different external fields and across several shells to place important constraints on inhomogeneous neutron matter, and hence the large isospin limit of the nuclear energy density functionals that are used to predict properties of heavy nuclei and neutron star crusts. We find important differences between microscopic calculations and current density functionals; in particular the isovector gradient terms are significantly more repulsive than in traditional models, and the spin-orbit and pairing forces are comparatively weaker.

## Key claim

Microscopic quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron drops confined in external fields show that standard nuclear energy density functionals (Skyrme models) overbind these systems, indicating that the isovector gradient terms in the functionals must be significantly more repulsive than in traditional models, while the spin-orbit and pairing forces are comparatively weaker.

## Method

The authors performed Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations for systems of up to 40 neutrons confined in harmonic oscillator (HO) potentials with frequencies ℏω = 5 and 10 MeV, and in a Woods-Saxon (WS) well with depth V₀ = -35.5 MeV, radius r₀ = 3 fm, and diffuseness a = 1.1 fm. The neutrons interact via the AV8′ two-nucleon potential plus the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction. The resulting ground-state energies, radii, and density distributions were compared to several standard Skyrme density functionals (SLY4, SLY7, BSK17, SkM*, SkP) and to a modified version (SLY4-adj) where the isovector gradient, spin-orbit, and pairing parameters were adjusted to match the microscopic results.

## Key numbers

- For N=8 neutrons in a 5-MeV HO well, GFMC gives ground-state energy 67.01(1) MeV; AFDMC gives 67.0(2) MeV.
- For N=8 neutrons in a 10-MeV HO well, GFMC gives 135.76(4) MeV; AFDMC gives 134.7(1) MeV.
- For N=40 neutrons in a 5-MeV HO well, AFDMC gives 545.8(1.3) MeV.
- For N=40 neutrons in a 10-MeV HO well, AFDMC gives 1114.3(9) MeV.
- The maximum difference between GFMC and AFDMC results is 3% for the 5-MeV HO drops; typical agreement is within 1%.
- The average density of drops in the 5-MeV HO well is approximately 0.02 fm⁻³ (about 1/8 nuclear matter saturation density).
- The average density of drops in the 10-MeV HO and WS wells is approximately 0.045 fm⁻³ (about 1/3 nuclear matter saturation density).
- The adjusted gradient coefficient G_d for SLY4-adj is 26.5 MeV-fm⁵; for comparison, the original SLY4 value is -16 MeV-fm⁵.
- The adjusted spin-orbit coupling strength is 123 MeV-fm³; the adjusted pairing strength is 500 MeV (reduced from a typical 1 GeV-fm³).

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations of very small neutron drops (N=6,7,8) by Pudliner (1996), Smerzi (1997), and Pederiva (2004), which already indicated substantial differences with traditional Skyrme models. The results provide direct constraints on the isovector gradient, spin-orbit, and pairing terms of nuclear energy density functionals in the pure neutron matter regime, which is not well constrained by fits to nuclear masses (Furnstahl 2000, Brown 1998). The adjusted parameters enable more reliable predictions for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis and for the neutron skin thickness of lead (Reinhard 2010), and can improve density functionals used to model neutron star crusts.
