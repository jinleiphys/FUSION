# Two Particle-Hole Excitations in Charged Current Quasielastic Antineutrino--Nucleus Scattering

> We evaluate the quasielastic and multinucleon contributions to the antineutrino nucleus scattering cross section and compare our results with the recent MiniBooNE data. We use a local Fermi gas model that includes RPA correlations and gets the multinucleon part from a systematic many body expansion of the $W$ boson selfenergy in the nuclear medium. The same model had been quite successful for the neutrino cross section and contains no new parameters. We have also analysed the relevance of 2p2h events for the antineutrino energy reconstruction.

# Two Particle-Hole Excitations in Charged Current Quasielastic Antineutrino–Nucleus Scattering

## Key claim

The same local Fermi gas model that successfully described MiniBooNE neutrino charged-current quasielastic-like data, with no new parameters, also describes the antineutrino data when the sign of the parity-violating pieces of the leptonic tensor is changed and the role of protons and neutrons is exchanged; however, the 2p2h contribution is relatively larger for antineutrinos than for neutrinos, and the unfolding procedure based on quasielastic kinematics produces a strong distortion of the reconstructed cross section at low energies.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic local Fermi gas model with RPA correlations and a systematic many-body expansion of the W boson selfenergy in the nuclear medium to compute the quasielastic and 2p2h contributions to antineutrino–nucleus scattering. The calculation for antineutrinos is obtained from the neutrino case by changing the sign of the antisymmetric piece of the leptonic tensor (affecting the W3 structure function) and exchanging protons and neutrons in all formulae, with proper treatment of Q-values for energy balance. The axial mass is fixed at 1.049 GeV, and results are compared to MiniBooNE data on 12C.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass used: M_A = 1.049 GeV (fixed from previous work, not fitted to antineutrino data)
- The ratio sigma_nu / sigma_antinu is reduced by less than ten percent at the peak when 2p2h mechanisms are included, compared to the pure QE ratio
- The unfolding procedure respects the sigma_nu / sigma_antinu ratio for antineutrino energies above 0.6 GeV but produces strong distortion at lower energies
- MiniBooNE data have an additional 17.2% normalization uncertainty not shown in the cross section figures

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Nieves 2011, Nieves 2012) from neutrino to antineutrino scattering on nuclei, applying the same theoretical framework that successfully described MiniBooNE neutrino CCQE-like data. The results are compared to the recent MiniBooNE antineutrino data (MB2013). The finding that 2p2h contributions are relatively larger for antineutrinos than neutrinos is consistent with the trend found by Amaro (2011) in the superscaling approximation and by Meucci (2012) in the relativistic Green's function model, but differs from the predictions of Bodek (2011) and Martini (2010). The work enables improved energy reconstruction procedures for antineutrino experiments and highlights the need to account for multinucleon mechanisms to avoid misinterpreting nu/antinu asymmetries as CP violation.
