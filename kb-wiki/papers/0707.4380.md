# Nuclear matter with three-body forces from self-consistent spectral calculations

> We calculate the equation of state of nuclear matter in the self-consistent T-matrix scheme including three-body nuclear interactions. We study the effect of the three-body force on the self-energies and spectral functions of nucleons in medium.

# Nuclear matter with three-body forces from self-consistent spectral calculations

## Key claim
The self-consistent T-matrix approximation including a phenomenological density-dependent three-body term can reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter, but the resulting equation of state is slightly stiffer at densities above twice saturation density than those obtained from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) and variational approaches.

## Method
The authors perform iterative self-consistent calculations of dressed nucleon propagators and the in-medium T-matrix using the CD-Bonn two-body potential plus a phenomenological three-body force expressed as an additional density-dependent two-body interaction. The three-body term is motivated by two-pion exchange and includes a short-range repulsive part and an attractive mean-field energy term. Calculations are carried out at several densities between 0.6 and 3 times the saturation density, with the Fermi energy adjusted at each density to satisfy the density constraint via the spectral function.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Three-body interaction parameters: η = 0.7 fm⁻¹, c = 2 fm⁻², I^c = -5.7 MeV, γ₁ = 0.15 fm³
- Attractive mean-field energy parameters: γ₂ = -260 MeV fm⁶, γ₃ = 11 fm³
- Density range studied: ρ = 0.6ρ₀ to ρ = 3ρ₀

## Context
This work extends previous self-consistent T-matrix calculations of nuclear matter (Bozek 2002, Dewulf 2002, Frick 2003) that included only two-body interactions, by adding phenomenological three-body forces. It builds on variational calculations (vcs1, vcs2) and BHF calculations (Baldo 2001, Baldo 2007) that already demonstrated the necessity of three-body forces for realistic saturation properties. The results enable future investigation of the sensitivity of proton spectral functions to the density and isospin dependence of three-body forces, with comparison to experimental data from Rohe (2004).
