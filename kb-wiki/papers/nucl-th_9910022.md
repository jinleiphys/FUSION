# Semiclassical and quantum production rates

> We discuss the effects of the off-shell nucleon propagation for the particle production. Results from nonequilibrium Kadanoff-Baym evolution modeling a heavy ion collision are presented. The production rates are compared to equilibrium emission rates obtained in the same approximation for the self-energy. The comparison of semiclassical and quantum in-medium production rates is performed within the T-matrix approximation. Self-consistent T-matrix resummation allows us to discuss also the effect of the off-shell propagation on the effective in-medium cross section.

# Semiclassical and quantum production rates

## Key claim
Off-shell nucleon propagation significantly enhances quantum particle production rates compared to semiclassical (on-shell) calculations, especially for energetic particles, but this enhancement cannot be defined during the initial nonequilibrium stage of a heavy-ion collision, a meaningful production rate only emerges after the system equilibrates (around 20 fm/c).

## Method
The author solves the nonequilibrium Kadanoff-Baym equations for a spatially homogeneous system of nucleons in the direct second Born approximation, then computes meson production rates from the one-loop meson self-energy. Equilibrium rates are obtained by iteratively solving the coupled set of equations for the nucleon spectral function, self-energy, and Green's functions at finite temperature and density. The T-matrix approximation is used to include ladder resummation, both in a quasiparticle (on-shell) and self-consistent (off-shell) scheme, and the resulting quantum production rates are compared to semiclassical rates derived from a Boltzmann-like collision integral with in-medium matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- The quantum production rate for particles of mass 140 MeV is approximately 2 times larger than the semiclassical rate at equilibrium.
- The meson production rate can only be defined at large times t ≈ 20 fm/c, where the nucleon sector is basically equilibrated.
- Equilibrium spectral functions are calculated at temperature T = 10 MeV and normal nuclear density.
- The single-particle width obtained from the Boltzmann collision integral is systematically larger than that from the self-consistent T-matrix calculation.

## Context
This work extends earlier nonequilibrium Kadanoff-Baym calculations by Danielewicz (1990s), who first found slower equilibration in quantum evolution compared to the quasiparticle limit. The author's previous work (Bozek, 1990s) studied production in nonequilibrium dynamics using the second Born approximation. The self-consistent T-matrix approach follows recent developments by Haus (1990s) and Dickhoff (1990s) for finite-temperature nuclear matter. The results enable improved modeling of subthreshold particle production in heavy-ion collisions, where off-shell effects may explain high-momentum tails of meson and photon spectra that cannot be accounted for by Fermi motion or multiple collisions in standard transport models.
