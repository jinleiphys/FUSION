# Coherent Pions From Neutrino Scattering Off Nuclei

> We describe a model for pion production off nucleons and coherent pions from nuclei induced by neutrinos in the 1 GeV energy regime. Besides the dominant Delta pole contribution, it takes into account the effect of background terms required by chiral symmetry. Moreover, the model uses a reduced nucleon-to-Delta resonance axial coupling, which leads to coherent pion production cross sections around a factor two smaller than most of the previous theoretical estimates. Nuclear effects like medium corrections on the Delta propagator and final pion distortion are included.

## Key claim
The model predicts coherent pion production cross sections that are roughly a factor of two smaller than most previous theoretical estimates, primarily due to the use of a reduced nucleon-to-Delta resonance axial coupling \(C_5^A(0)\) that violates the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation at the \(2\sigma\) level.

## Method
The authors model single-nucleon pion production using a chiral-symmetry-based framework that includes both the dominant Delta(1232) pole mechanism and non-resonant background terms. They fit the axial form factor \(C_5^A(0)\) and axial mass \(M_A\) to ANL and BNL bubble-chamber data on \(\nu_\mu p \to \mu^- p \pi^+\), including deuterium wave-function effects and correlated flux uncertainties. For coherent pion production off nuclei, they sum over individual nucleon wave functions in a local-density Fermi gas, incorporate in-medium modifications of the Delta propagator, and treat final-state pion distortion via a microscopic optical potential in the Klein-Gordon equation.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit axial form factor: \(C_5^A(0) = 1.00 \pm 0.11\) (reduced from the Goldberger-Treiman prediction of 1.2), with axial mass \(M_A = 0.93 \pm 0.07\ \text{GeV}\) and \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 0.42\).
- Total cross section for CC \(\nu_\mu + ^{12}\text{C}\) coherent production: \(4.46 \times 10^{-40}\ \text{cm}^2\) (MiniBoone prediction) and \(3.54 \times 10^{-40}\ \text{cm}^2\) (T2K prediction).
- Total cross section for NC \(\nu_\mu + ^{12}\text{C}\) coherent production: \(3.33 \times 10^{-40}\ \text{cm}^2\) (MiniBoone prediction).
- Total cross section for CC \(\nu_\mu + ^{16}\text{O}\) coherent production: \(4.19 \times 10^{-40}\ \text{cm}^2\) (T2K prediction).
- K2K experimental upper bound for CC \(\nu_\mu + ^{12}\text{C}\): \(< 7.7 \times 10^{-40}\ \text{cm}^2\).
- Nuclear medium effects reduce the coherent cross section by about 60% relative to the plane-wave impulse approximation.

## Context
This work extends the single-nucleon pion production model of Hernandez et al. (2007) to coherent processes on nuclei, incorporating nuclear effects previously studied by Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2007) but improving on their neglect of nucleon momenta in Dirac spinors. The reduced axial coupling contradicts the Goldberger-Treiman relation assumed in earlier models by Fogli and Nardulli (1979) and by Sato et al. (2003), and is consistent with recent lattice QCD results from Alexandrou et al. (2006) and quark-model calculations by Barquilla-Cano et al. (2007). The results enable more reliable background estimates for neutrino oscillation experiments such as MiniBoone, T2K, and K2K, and suggest that the MiniBoone analysis may overestimate the coherent NC cross section.
