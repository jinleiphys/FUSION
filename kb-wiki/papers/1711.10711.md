# Hadrons in Nuclei

> A review on the state-of-the-art of heavy hadrons in nuclei is presented. In particular, the properties of mesons with strangeness and charm in matter are discussed, paying a special attention to the formation of exotic bound states in nuclei.

# Hadrons in Nuclei

## Key claim
This review argues that the properties of strange and charmed mesons in nuclear matter can be understood through unitarized coupled-channel approaches, but the existence of exotic bound states such as $\bar K NN$ and D-mesic nuclei remains experimentally contested, with some observed signals potentially explained by conventional processes or alternative interpretations.

## Method
The paper surveys theoretical models for hadron-nucleus interactions, including unitarized coupled-channel approaches based on chiral effective theories and meson-exchange potentials, relativistic mean-field models, Nambu-Jona-Lasinio schemes, quark-meson coupling models, and QCD sum-rule studies. Experimental results from kaonic atom measurements (SIDDHARTA), heavy-ion collision experiments (KaoS, FOPI, HADES), and dedicated searches for exotic bound states (FINUDA, DISTO, OBELIX, J-PARC E15, AMADEUS) are compared with theoretical predictions using transport model calculations and collision models based on nuclear spectral functions.

## Key numbers
- Energy shift of the $1s$ state in kaonic hydrogen measured by SIDDHARTA: $\Delta E = 283 \pm 36 \pm 6$ eV with width $\Gamma = 541 \pm 89 \pm 22$ eV
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance lies 27 MeV below the $\bar K N$ threshold
- Antikaon-nucleus potential extracted from kaonic atom data: approximately -200 MeV at normal saturation density $\rho_0$
- A $\bar K NN$ bound state candidate observed by J-PARC E15 with binding energy of approximately 20-40 MeV
- A $DNN$ state predicted with $I=1/2$, $J=0^-$, mass approximately 3500 MeV, and width approximately 20-40 MeV
- A $DNN$ state predicted with $I=1/2$, $J=0^-$ and 5.2 MeV binding, plus a $I=1/2$, $J=1^-$ state at 111.2 MeV above threshold
- Best prospects for $D^0$-nucleus bound states identified in $^{24}\mbox{Mg}$, where one $s$-bound state has half-width about a factor of two smaller than the binding energy

## Context
This review extends the long-standing line of work on hadron-nucleus interactions dating back to Dalitz and Tuan (1959) on the $\Lambda(1405)$, and builds on unitarized chiral approaches by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), Oller (2000), and Lutz (2001). It connects to experimental programs at GSI, CERN, RHIC, FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC, and addresses open questions about whether observed states like the $\bar K NN$ candidate from J-PARC E15 are genuine bound states or conventional processes. The review enables future searches for D-mesic nuclei at PANDA (FAIR) using antiproton beams, and informs the interpretation of charmonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions as a probe of quark-gluon plasma formation.
