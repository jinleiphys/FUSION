# Channel coupling effects in $ρ$-meson photoproduction

> We investigate $ρ$-meson photoproduction in a coupled-channels formulation. It is shown that channel coupling effects are large and account for discrepancies observed in several single-channel treatments.

## Key claim

Channel coupling effects, particularly re-scattering via an intermediate pion-nucleon state, are shown to be large and account for major discrepancies observed in single-channel (tree-level) treatments of rho-meson photoproduction, especially in the high momentum transfer region.

## Method

The authors use a K-matrix formalism based on an effective Lagrangian to implement channel coupling non-perturbatively, including s-, u-, and t-channel Born diagrams with form factors and gauge-invariance-restoring contact terms. The model space includes K-Lambda, K-Sigma, phi-N, eta-N, gamma-N, pi-N, and rho-N states, with a number of non-strange resonances included in s- and u-channel contributions. The gamma+N to rho+N and pi+N to rho+N reaction channels are added to a previously developed coupled-channels framework, with parameters mostly unchanged except for the NNomega coupling constant and cut-offs in the pion photoproduction channel.

## Key numbers

* Cut-off value for form factors in the gamma+N to rho+N channel: Lambda = 0.8 GeV^2 (relatively hard)
* Cut-off for sigma-meson t-channel form factor: 1 GeV^2
* Sigma-meson Regge trajectory slope: alpha'_sigma = 0.7 GeV^{-2}; intercept: alpha^0_sigma = -0.4
* Reference scale for Regge propagator: s_0 = 1 GeV^2
* The coupled-channels calculation is extended to energies up to sqrt(s) = 3.5 GeV
* The NNomega coupling constant was reduced by a substantial factor compared to the previous calculation (Usov 2005)
* A prominent peak in eta photoproduction appears at about W = 1.74 GeV, identified as a channel coupling effect rather than a resonant contribution

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels framework previously applied to photo-induced kaon production (Usov 2005) to rho-meson photoproduction, addressing discrepancies seen in tree-level models such as those by Oh (2004) and Zhao (1998, 2000, 2001). The analysis shows that channel coupling via the pion-nucleon intermediate state is essential, consistent with findings by Oh (2002) for omega-meson production. The results enable future global fits and suggest that the peak observed at W = 1.74 GeV in eta photoproduction, which coincides with a possible additional S11 resonance claimed by Saghai (2001) and Chen (2003), may instead arise from channel coupling effects.
