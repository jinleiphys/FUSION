# Multi-strange hadrons and the precision extraction of QGP properties in the RHIC-BES domain

> We systematically compare an event-by-event transport+viscous hydrodynamics hybrid model to data from the RHIC beam energy scan using a general Bayesian method. We demonstrate how the inclusion of multistrange hadron observables affects the outcome of the Bayesian analysis and conduct an in depth analysis of the viability of $ϕ$ and $Ω$ as probes of the transition region between a deconfined quark-gluon plasma and hadronic phase in heavy ion collisions at higher-end RHIC collision energies. Utilizing UrQMD to model the final hadronic interactions, we examine the collision rates of $ϕ$ and $Ω$ and the modification to their transverse momentum spectra due to these interactions.

## Key claim

Including multistrange hadron observables (specifically Ω yields) in a Bayesian model-to-data comparison can constrain the switching energy density between hydrodynamics and hadron transport, whereas analyses without these observables fail to provide such constraints; the authors find that φ and Ω are justified as probes of the phase transition region based on their low hadronic interaction rates and small mean transverse momentum changes during the hadron gas phase.

## Method

The authors used an event-by-event hybrid model combining UrQMD for the initial pre-equilibrium phase and hadronic afterburner with 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics (using a chiral model equation of state for nonzero net-baryon density) and a constant η/s. They performed a Bayesian analysis with Gaussian process emulation trained on approximately 100 Latin hypercube samples across 5 input parameters, comparing model output to RHIC beam energy scan data for φ and Ω at collision energies of 19.6, 39, and 62.4 GeV, both with and without Ω yield data.

## Key numbers

- At √sNN = 39 GeV, the detectable φ mean transverse momentum is about 20% larger than the value at the hypersurface; including undetectable φs gives a slightly smaller increase of approximately 15%, which equals the change in ⟨pT⟩ seen for Ω.
- At √sNN = 39 GeV, detectable φ mesons have hardly any interactions and Ω baryons are likely to have only a few interactions, compared to 5-10 interactions of a typical nucleon.
- The Bayesian analysis was performed with and without Ω yield data for √sNN = 19.6, 39, and 62.4 GeV; including Ω yields produced clear peak regions in probability distributions for the switching energy density, revealing a visible dependence on collision energy.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid model approach of Karpenko (2015) and the Bayesian analysis methods of Novak (2013) and Bernhard (2015) to multistrange hadrons in the RHIC beam energy scan domain. The results demonstrate that φ and Ω can serve as probes of the transition region between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic phase, as originally proposed by Shor (1984), and that their inclusion in Bayesian analyses can significantly constrain model parameters that otherwise remain unconstrained. This enables more precise extraction of QGP properties from heavy ion collision data at RHIC energies.
