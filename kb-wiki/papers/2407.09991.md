# Threshold photo-production of $J/Ψ$ off light nuclei

> We analyze threshold photoproduction of heavy mesons off a deuteron and Helium-4, using the QCD factorization method. Assuming large skewness, the production amplitude is dominated by the leading twist-2 gluonic energy-momentum tensor (EMT). We use our recent results for the gluonic gravitational form factors of light nuclei in the impulse approximation, to estimate the differential cross sections for $J/Ψ$ production off a deuteron and Helium-4 at current electron facilities.

## Key claim

The authors extend the QCD factorization method for threshold photo-production of heavy mesons from nucleons to light nuclei (deuteron and Helium-4), assuming large skewness so that the production amplitude is dominated by the leading twist-2 gluonic energy-momentum tensor; the differential cross sections are estimated using the impulse approximation for the gluonic gravitational form factors, with the caveat that the center-of-mass energy W=10 GeV used implies a low skewness value where higher-order corrections may be needed.

## Method

The threshold photo-production amplitude for J/Ψ on light nuclei is factorized using QCD factorization, where the hard kernel is convolved with the gluonic generalized parton distribution (GPD) of the target. For large skewness, the gluonic GPD is dominated by the leading twist-2 gluonic energy-momentum tensor, reducing the amplitude to the off-forward matrix element of T_g^{++}. The gluonic gravitational form factors (GFFs) for the deuteron and Helium-4, previously derived by the authors in the impulse approximation using the K-Harmonic method and the Argonne v14 potential, are inserted into the factorized amplitude to compute the differential cross sections.

## Key numbers

- J/Ψ mass: M_{J/Ψ} = 3.097 GeV
- Strong coupling at scale 2m_c: α_s(2m_c) ≈ 0.31
- Heavy meson wavefunction at origin squared: |ψ_{J/ψ}(0)|^2 = 0.094 GeV^3 (from the empirical J/Ψ → e⁺e⁻ decay width of 5.55 keV)
- Center-of-mass energy used for cross section plots: W = 10 GeV
- The differential cross sections are computed for the deuteron and Helium-4 targets at W = 10 GeV, showing a diffractive dip for Helium-4 in the impulse approximation for both the K-harmonic and Argonne v14 potential results

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization method for threshold J/Ψ photo-production, previously applied to the nucleon by Guo (2021) and Guo (2023), to light nuclear targets using the gluonic gravitational form factors recently derived by the authors for the deuteron and Helium-4 in the impulse approximation (He 2023, He 2024). The results are motivated by recent JLAB measurements of J/Ψ off nucleon targets by the GlueX collaboration (2019, 2023) and Duran (2022), which showed fair agreement with QCD factorization and dual gravity predictions; the authors anticipate that future measurements at JLAB and the EIC could enable extraction of gluonic GFFs and radii of light nuclei.
