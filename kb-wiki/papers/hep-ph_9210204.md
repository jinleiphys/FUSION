# Natural Hadronic Degrees of Freedom for an Effective QCD Action in the Front Form

> On the one hand any effective action of the QCD, $S[π,\,ρ,\,ω,\ldots,N,\,\overline N,\,Δ^{3/2},\ldots]$, would involve, at least in principle, a tower of hadronic fields of all spins; on the other hand the front form of the field theory has emerged as an appropriate framework to investigate the high energy behaviour of many systems. Motivated by these simple considerations we present a systematic formalism which explicitly provides the front form fields appropriate for constructing a $S[π,\,ρ,\,ω,\ldots,N,\,\overline N,\,Δ^{3/2},\ldots]$. Further, a transformation matrix $Ω(j)$ is constructed which determines how the front form hadronic fields superimpose to yield the more familiar instant form fields. Explicit results for the front form hadronic fields up to spin two are catalogued in appendixes. For spin one half, the $Ω(1/2)$ coincides with the celebrated ``Melosh transformation.'' The formalism, without explicitly invoking any wave equations, reproduces spin one half front-form results of Lepage and Brodsky, and Dziembowski.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic formalism for constructing front-form hadronic fields of arbitrary spin for use in an effective QCD action, without explicitly invoking any wave equations. The key hedge is that while the formalism is general for any spin, the explicit results are catalogued only up to spin two, and the actual connecting link with QCD "does not yet exist in detail."

## Method

The authors construct front-form covariant spinors for the $(j,0)\oplus(0,j)$ representation by using a boost operator $M(L)$ derived from the front-form transformation $L$, which is built from the stability group generators $G_\perp$ and $K_3$ of the $x^+=0$ plane. They then define a transformation matrix $\Omega(j)$ that relates these front-form hadronic wave functions to the instant-form wave functions previously constructed following Weinberg and Ryder. The explicit construction uses expansions of $\exp(\eta \hat b \cdot \vec J)$ and $\exp(-\eta \hat b^\ast \cdot \vec J)$ for spins $j = 1/2, 1, 3/2, 2$, with the front-form helicity operator ${\cal J}_3$ of Soper and Leutwyler-Stern providing the basis for the spinors.

## Key numbers

* For spin one half, the transformation matrix $\Omega(1/2)$ coincides with the celebrated "Melosh transformation" from Melosh (1974) and Dziembowski (1984).
* The normalization constant for the hadronic wave functions is chosen as ${\cal N}(j) = m^{\,j}$, which ensures that massless particles at rest vanish and only helicity $h = \pm j$ states survive in the high energy limit.
* The orthonormality relations yield $\overline{\cal U}_h(p^\mu) {\cal U}_{h'}(p^\mu) = m^{2j} \delta_{hh'}$ and $\overline{\cal V}_h(p^\mu) {\cal V}_{h'}(p^\mu) = -m^{2j} \delta_{hh'}$.
* In the high energy limit, the hadronic wave functions fall off as $\sim (m/p^+)^{j - |h|}$.

## Context

This work extends the instant-form high-spin formalism of Weinberg (1964) and the front-form results of Lepage and Brodsky (1980) and Dziembowski (1984) to arbitrary spins in the front form, providing the unifying link between Weinberg's work on high-spin fields and his work on the infinite momentum frame. The formalism is motivated by the program of Cahill and collaborators to relate the QCD action $S[\overline q, q, A_\mu^a]$ to an effective hadronic action $S[\pi, \rho, \omega, \ldots, N, \overline N, \Delta^{3/2}, \ldots]$ via functional integral techniques, and it enables the construction of front-form Feynman-Dyson propagators and wave equations for hadronic resonances of arbitrary spin.
