# From Heisenberg matrix mechanics to EBK quantization: theory and first applications

> Despite the seminal connection between classical multiply-periodic motion and Heisenberg matrix mechanics and the massive amount of work done on the associated problem of semiclassical (EBK) quantization of bound states, we show that there are, nevertheless, a number of previously unexploited aspects of this relationship that bear on the quantum-classical correspondence. In particular, we emphasize a quantum variational principle that implies the classical variational principle for invariant tori. We also expose the more indirect connection between commutation relations and quantization of action variables. With the help of several standard models with one or two degrees of freedom, we then illustrate how the methods of Heisenberg matrix mechanics described in this paper may be used to obtain quantum solutions with a modest increase in effort compared to semiclassical calculations. We also describe and apply a method for obtaining leading quantum corrections to EBK results. Finally, we suggest several new or modified applications of EBK quantization.

# From Heisenberg matrix mechanics to EBK quantization: theory and first applications

## Key claim
The authors show that the classical limit of Heisenberg matrix mechanics, derived from a trace variational principle, yields the variational principle for invariant tori and the EBK quantization conditions, but the connection between commutation relations and quantization of action variables is more indirect than previously appreciated, with the former corresponding to Poisson brackets and the latter to Lagrange brackets.

## Method
The authors develop a trace variational principle for Heisenberg matrix mechanics, from which Hamilton's equations and Newton's equation are derived for systems with one or more degrees of freedom. They then take the semiclassical limit of this quantum variational principle, using a formal Fourier series representation of matrix elements, to obtain the classical variational principle for invariant tori and Lagrange's equations in Fourier component form. The EBK quantization condition is applied by adjoining action quantization to the classical equations of motion, and numerical studies are performed on standard models with one or two degrees of freedom to compare semiclassical, matrix quantum, and quantum-corrected results.

- The relative error in the semiclassical limit of diagonal matrix elements of products is of order O(1/n²), as shown in Lemma 1 (Eq. 3.7).
- The EBK quantization condition is given as I_i = (n_i + ¼α_i)ℏ (Eq. 4.7), where the Maslov index α_i equals two for an uncoupled vibrational degree of freedom.
- The classical frequencies ω_i(n) are defined as the quantum energy differences: ω_i(n) ≅ E(n₁,...,n_i+½,...) − E(n₁,...,n_i−½,...) (Eq. 4.1).

## Context
This work extends the foundational connection between classical multiply-periodic motion and Heisenberg matrix mechanics, building on earlier developments of the trace variational principle by the authors and their associates (Greenberg, Klein, and collaborators, 1970s–1990s). It contrasts with the more common semiclassical approaches based on trajectory calculations (methods M1a–M1d) and algebraic methods (M2a–M2c) reviewed in the introduction, particularly the work of Percival (1970s) on the relationship between invariant tori and quantum states. The paper enables new applications of EBK quantization by providing a direct algebraic route from matrix mechanics to semiclassical results, and suggests methods for computing leading quantum corrections to EBK results.
