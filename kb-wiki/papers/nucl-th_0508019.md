# Energy Centroids in the presence of random interactions

> In this paper we study energy centroids such as those with fixed spin and isospin, those with fixed irreducible representations for bosons, in the presence of random two-body and/or three-body interactions. Our results show that regularities of energy centroids of fixed spin states reported in earlier works are more robust than expected.

# Energy Centroids in the presence of random interactions

## Key claim

The regularities of energy centroids of fixed spin states reported in earlier works are very robust when extended to include isospin degrees of freedom, random three-body interactions, and fixed irreducible representations of group symmetries, suggesting these behaviors might be intrinsic features of quantum many-body systems interacting by random forces rather than artifacts of specific interaction types.

## Method

The authors computed energy centroids (average energies of states with given quantum numbers) for fermion systems in the sd shell using the Two-body Random Ensemble (TBRE) for two-body matrix elements, and for sd boson systems using random three-body interactions with matrix elements following Gaussian distributions. They applied propagation equations based on group theory (U(N) and SU(3) algebras) to derive analytic expressions for the centroids, and performed numerical experiments with 1000 samples to compute probabilities for centroids to be lowest in energy.

## Key numbers

- For eight nucleons in the sd shell, the summed probability $\sum_I {\cal P}(I,T)$ for energy centroids with fixed isospin T to be lowest is 47.1% for T=0, 11.0% for T=1, 5.5% for T=2, 2.2% for T=3, and 34.2% for T=4
- For twelve nucleons in the sd shell, $\sum_I {\cal P}(I,T)$ is 57.9% for T=0, 0.8% for T=1, 0.5% for T=2, 4.9% for T=3, 0.5% for T=4, 13.6% for T=5, and 21.8% for T=6
- For sd bosons with random three-body interactions, the coefficient C in the relation $\langle \overline{E_I} \rangle_{\rm min} \simeq CI(I+1)$ increases linearly with boson number n, with numerical experiments giving C = 0.03499 n and propagation equation prediction giving C = 0.03331 n
- For sdpf IBM with boson numbers n=8, 9, and 10, the irreps with lowest and highest n_sd carry about 84% of the probability for centroids to be lowest
- For (2s1d) shell nuclei with n=12, the probability splits into the lowest U(4) irrep {3^4} with ~25% and highest irrep {6^2} with ~75%

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Johnson, Bertsch and Dean (1998) on the preponderance of 0+ ground states in even-even nuclei with random interactions, and the spin I energy centroid regularities reported by Zhao et al. (2000-2005). The authors generalize these results to include isospin (mixing results from Zhao (2005) that did not separate different T values), random three-body interactions (extending Bijker and Frank's work on sd bosons), and fixed irreducible representations of group symmetries (extending Kota's work on interacting boson models). The propagation equation approach, based on French's (1969-1970) group theory methods, enables analytic understanding of the observed regularities and enables future studies of energy centroids in more complex systems such as (2p1f)-shell nuclei.
