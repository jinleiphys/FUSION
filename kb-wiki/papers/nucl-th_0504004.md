# Strange form factors and Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We review the contributions of Chiral Perturbation Theory to the theoretical understanding or not-quite-yet-understanding of the nucleon matrix elements of the strange vector current.

# Strange form factors and Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim

Chiral Perturbation Theory cannot predict the strange magnetic moment μs or the strange electric radius ⟨r²E,s⟩ from first principles because new independent low-energy constants appear for the strangeness current that are not constrained by electromagnetic data; however, a parameter-free low-energy theorem exists for the strange magnetic radius ⟨r²M,s⟩, though higher-order corrections make this theorem numerically unreliable.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) to nucleon matrix elements of the strange vector current, analyzing power counting in both SU(2) and SU(3) chiral expansions. It examines the leading non-analytic loop contributions to the strange magnetic radius at order p³, calculates next-to-leading order corrections at order p⁴, and explores alternative regularization schemes including infrared regularization and cutoff regularization to assess the stability of the low-energy theorem.

## Key numbers

- Chiral expansion parameters: Mπ²/Λχ² ≈ 0.02 for SU(2) and MK²/Λχ² ≈ 0.2 for SU(3), with chiral symmetry breaking scale Λχ = 4πFπ ≈ 1160 MeV
- Baryon sector expansion parameters: Mπ/mN ≈ 0.15 for SU(2) and MK/mN ≈ 0.5 for SU(3)
- Leading-order prediction for the strange magnetic radius: ⟨r²M,s⟩ = -0.115 fm²
- SAMPLE experimental result: GM,s(0.1 GeV²) = 0.37 ± 0.20 ± 0.26 ± 0.07, extrapolated to μs = 0.32 ± 0.20 ± 0.26 ± 0.07 using the leading-order theorem
- Order p⁴ correction: ⟨r²M,s⟩⁴ = -(0.04 + 0.3 b_s^r) fm², where b_s^r is expected to be of order 1
- Partial order p⁵ corrections in infrared regularization: Δ⟨r²M,s⟩⁵ = +0.053 fm², about half the size of the leading p³ value

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Ito (several years prior) who identified that ChPT cannot predict μs and ⟨r²E,s⟩ due to independent low-energy constants for the strangeness current. The low-energy theorem for ⟨r²M,s⟩ was originally derived by Hemmert, Meißner, and Steininger (HMS). The paper discusses how ChPT can interrelate experimental data from SAMPLE and HAPPEX measurements, and compares the dimensional regularization results with alternative schemes from Becher (infrared regularization) and cutoff approaches from the UMass group, concluding that the chiral series for strange form factors shows poor convergence in SU(3).
