# Effect of the neutron quadrupole distribution in the TaO$^+$ cation

> We estimate the effect of the tensor parity nonconserving (PNC) interaction in the $^{181}$TaO$^+$ molecular cation. It can be used to probe the unknown quadrupole distribution of the neutrons inside the Ta nucleus. To this end, we evaluate the constant which characterizes this interaction using the relativistic Fock space coupled cluster theory for electronic structure modelling. The $^3Δ_1$ state of the TaO$^+$ cation which can be used to measure the PNC effect is found to be the ground one in agreement with the previous theoretical study.

## Key claim

The tensor parity nonconserving (PNC) interaction in the \(^{181}\)TaO\(^+\) molecular cation can be used to probe the unknown quadrupole distribution of neutrons inside the Ta nucleus, but the expected effect is only about several mHz, making experimental measurement very challenging.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Fock space coupled cluster method with single and double cluster amplitudes (FS-CCSD) to model the electronic structure of TaO\(^+\). They employed a two-step approach: first, a generalized relativistic core potential (GRECP) calculation to smooth valence wavefunctions in the core region, followed by a non-variational restoration technique to obtain correct wavefunctions inside the heavy-atom nucleus. The tensor PNC matrix element \(W_q\) between the \(^3\Delta_1\) and \(^3\Delta_{-1}\) states was computed using a finite field method, with denominator shifting and Pade extrapolation to handle intruder state instabilities.

## Key numbers

* The calculated tensor PNC matrix element is \(W_q = i \times 4.4 \times 10^{-12}\) a.u. for the \(^3\Delta_1\) state of \(^{181}\)TaO\(^+\).
* The estimated PNC effect, using the nuclear quadrupole moment \(Q_p = 3.17\) b for Ta and assuming \(Q_n \approx (N/Z) Q_p\), is \(W_q Q^{TW} \approx 5\) mHz.
* The \(^3\Delta_1\) state is the ground state of TaO\(^+\) with an equilibrium distance \(R_e = 3.11\) a.u. and vibrational frequency \(\omega_e = 1151\) cm\(^{-1}\).
* Transition energies to low-lying states are: \(^3\Delta_2\) at 1408 cm\(^{-1}\), \(^3\Delta_3\) at 3561 cm\(^{-1}\), and \(^1\Sigma_0^+\) at 3568 cm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Flambaum (2017) to study neutron quadrupole distribution via tensor PNC interactions, and builds on prior theoretical studies of TaO\(^+\) by Fleig (2017) as a candidate for \(\mathcal{T},\mathcal{P}\)-violating searches. The calculated effect is similar in magnitude to that found for HfF\(^+\) by Skripnikov (2019), but the ground-state nature of the working \(^3\Delta_1\) term in TaO\(^+\) offers experimental advantages over the metastable state used in HfF\(^+\). The result enables future experiments to extract the neutron quadrupole distribution for the first time, though the effect is orders of magnitude smaller than current experimental sensitivities achieved in BaF experiments by DeMille (2018).
