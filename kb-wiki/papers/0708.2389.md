# Vector and axialvector mesons at nonzero temperature within a gauged linear sigma model

> We consider vector and axialvector mesons in the framework of a gauged linear sigma model with chiral $U(N_f)_R \times U(N_f)_L$ symmetry. For $N_f=2$, we investigate the behavior of the chiral condensate and the meson masses as a function of temperature by solving a system of coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations derived via the 2PI formalism in double-bubble approximation. We find that the inclusion of vector and axialvector mesons tends to sharpen the chiral transition. Within our approximation scheme, the mass of the $ρ$ meson increases by about 100 MeV towards the chiral transition.

## Key claim

The inclusion of vector and axialvector mesons in a gauged linear sigma model with chiral symmetry tends to sharpen the chiral transition, and within the double-bubble approximation of the 2PI formalism, the mass of the ρ meson increases by about 100 MeV as the temperature approaches the chiral transition.

## Method

The authors considered a gauged linear sigma model with chiral \(U(N_f)_R \times U(N_f)_L\) symmetry for \(N_f=2\) and computed the chiral condensate and meson masses as functions of temperature. They derived a system of coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations using the two-particle irreducible (2PI) formalism in the double-bubble approximation, which yields Hartree-Fock-type self-energies. The model includes scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axialvector mesons, with the σ-field shifted by its vacuum expectation value and axial vector fields redefined to eliminate bilinear mixing with pseudoscalar fields.

## Key numbers

- The mass of the ρ meson increases by about 100 MeV towards the chiral transition.
- The chiral phase transition temperature from lattice QCD is cited as \(T_c \sim 150 - 190\) MeV for zero quark chemical potential.
- The QCD scale parameter is \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \sim 200\) MeV, corresponding to a hadronic length scale \(\sim 1\) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry restoration within the 2PI formalism that only included scalar and pseudoscalar particles, such as those by Petropoulos (1998), Lenaghan (1999), and Roder (2003). It also builds on the qualitative analysis by Pisarski (1994), who varied tree-level mass parameters to study meson masses as a function of temperature. The ultimate goal is to enable calculations of the dilepton production rate from hot and dense strongly interacting matter, relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments at CERN-SPS, BNL-RHIC, and future facilities.
