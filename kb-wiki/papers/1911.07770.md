# Elastic transfer and parity dependence of the nucleus-nucleus optical potential

> Background: A recent coupled reaction channel (CRC) study shows that the enhanced oscillation of the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C cross section at backward angles is due mainly to the elastic $α$ transfer or the core exchange. Such a process gives rise to a parity-dependent term in the total elastic $S$-matrix, an indication of the parity dependence of the $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C optical potential (OP). Purpose: To explicitly determine the core exchange potential (CEP) induced by the symmetric exchange of the two $^{12}$C cores in the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering at $E_{\rm lab}= 132$ and 300 MeV, and explore its parity dependence. Method: $S$-matrix generated by CRC description of the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering is used as the input for the inversion calculation to obtain the effective local OP that contains both the Wigner and Majorana terms. Results: The high-precision inversion results show a strong contribution by the complex Majorana term in the total OP of the $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C system, and thus provide for the first time a direct estimation of the parity-dependent CEP. Conclusions: The elastic $α$ transfer or exchange of the two $^{12}$C cores in the $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C system gives rise to a complex parity dependence of the total OP. This should be a general feature of the OP for the light heavy-ion systems that contain two identical cores.

# Elastic transfer and parity dependence of the nucleus-nucleus optical potential

## Key claim
The enhanced oscillation of the elastic 16O+12C cross section at backward angles arises from elastic alpha transfer (core exchange), which generates a complex parity-dependent Majorana term in the optical potential. This parity dependence is a general feature for light heavy-ion systems containing two identical cores, and the core exchange potential can be explicitly determined for the first time through high-precision inversion of the S-matrix from coupled reaction channel calculations.

## Method
The authors performed iterative-perturbative (IP) inversion of the elastic S-matrix obtained from two-channel and ten-channel coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations of 16O+12C scattering at Elab = 132 and 300 MeV, using the code IMAGO. The inversion extracted a local optical potential containing both a parity-independent Wigner term and a parity-dependent Majorana term, with the Majorana component representing the core exchange potential (CEP). The CRC calculations used the code FRESCO with alpha spectroscopic factors from large-scale shell model calculations by Volya and Tchuvilsky, and included direct and indirect alpha transfer channels as well as inelastic excitations of 12C and 16O.

## Key numbers
- The two-channel CRC inversion at 300 MeV achieved precision σ = 3.3 × 10⁻⁴ when including both Wigner and Majorana terms, compared to σ = 2.3 × 10⁻³ when only the Wigner term was allowed
- The two-channel CRC inversion at 132 MeV achieved precision σ = 1.7 × 10⁻³ with both Wigner and Majorana terms
- The ten-channel CRC inversion at 300 MeV achieved precision σ = 1.5 × 10⁻⁵ with both Wigner and Majorana terms
- The ten-channel CRC inversion at 132 MeV achieved precision σ = 1.4 × 10⁻⁴ with both Wigner and Majorana terms
- The grazing angular momenta were Lg ≈ 26 at 132 MeV and Lg ≈ 37 at 300 MeV, where the odd-even staggering in the S-matrix was most pronounced
- The coupling to inelastic channels increased the imaginary Wigner potential at the origin by more than 50 percent relative to the original optical potential

## Context
This work extends the recent CRC study by Phuc et al. (2018) that demonstrated the strong impact of elastic alpha transfer on 16O+12C scattering. It provides the first direct derivation of the core exchange potential from CRC calculations, going beyond earlier approaches that used the linear combination of nuclear orbitals (LCNO) method by Barrette et al. (1986), Ferro et al. (1990), and Gao et al. (1992), or phenomenological parity-dependent potentials by Konnerth et al. (1985) and Vinas et al. (1986). The results confirm suggestions from microscopic Resonating Group Method (RGM) and Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) studies by LeMere et al. (1979), Baye et al. (1977, 1986), and Aoki et al. (1983) that core exchange leads to parity-dependent potentials. The work enables future studies of the dynamic polarization potential from collective excitations in the presence of core exchange, and provides a foundation for understanding parity dependence in other core-identical systems such as 12C+12C and 16O+16O.
