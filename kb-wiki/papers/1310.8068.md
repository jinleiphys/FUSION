# Halo formation in neutron rich $\rm{Ca}$ nuclei

> We have investigated the halo formation in the neutron rich $\rm{Ca}$ isotopes within the framework of recently proposed relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) approach wherein the single particle continuum corresponding to the RMF is replaced by a set of discrete positive energy states for the calculation of pairing energy. For the neutron rich $\rm{Ca}$ isotopes in the vicinity of neutron drip-line, it is found that further addition of neutrons causes a rapid increase in the neutron rms radius with a very small increase in the binding energy, indicating thereby the occurrence of halos. This is essentially caused by the gradual filling in of the loosely bound $3s_{1/2}$ state. Interesting phenomenon of accommodating several additional neutrons with almost negligible increase in binding energy is shown to be due to the pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that halo formation occurs in neutron-rich Ca isotopes near the neutron drip line, specifically caused by the gradual filling of the loosely bound $3s_{1/2}$ state, with the condition that this phenomenon is observed only when the neutron number exceeds N=40 and the Fermi level approaches zero energy.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) approach, where the single-particle continuum from the RMF calculation is replaced by a set of discrete positive energy states generated by enclosing the nucleus in a spherical box. They used a delta-function pairing interaction with strength V0 = 350 MeV fm^3, and performed calculations with two force parameter sets, TMA and NL-SH, for a chain of Ca isotopes from A=34 to A=72.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron drip line occurs at A=70 (N=50) for the TMA force and at A=72 (N=52) for the NL-SH force
- For $^{64}$Ca, the neutron Fermi energy is $\lambda_n = -0.066$ MeV, and the resonant $1g_{9/2}$ state lies at 0.16 MeV with a pairing gap of about 1 MeV
- The neutron rms radius increases from 3.337 fm in $^{40}$Ca to 4.926 fm in $^{70}$Ca (TMA force)
- The $3s_{1/2}$ state becomes bound at $\epsilon = -0.05$ MeV in $^{68}$Ca
- The two-neutron separation energy drops to 0.017 MeV for $^{70}$Ca and becomes negative (-0.103 MeV) for $^{72}$Ca (TMA force)
- The neutron Fermi energy moves from -19.90 MeV in $^{34}$Ca to 0.08 MeV in $^{70}$Ca

## Context

This work extends the RMF+BCS approach previously applied to Ni and Sn isotopes by Yadav et al. (2005) to the Ca isotopic chain, and compares results with continuum relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations by Meng et al. (2002). The finding that halo formation occurs in Ca but not in Ni, Sn, O, or Pb isotopes is consistent with non-relativistic HFB studies of neutron-rich weakly bound nuclei by Bennaceur et al. (2000). The paper enables systematic studies of halo formation in other proton sub-magic neutron-rich nuclei such as Zr isotopes.
