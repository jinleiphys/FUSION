# Collective Modes of Chiral Kinetic Theory in Magnetic Field

> We study collective excitations in systems described by chiral kinetic theory in external magnetic field. We consider high-temperature weak-coupling plasma, as well as high-density Landau Fermi liquid with interaction not restricted to be weak. We show that chiral magnetic wave (CMW) emerges in hydrodynamic regime (at frequencies smaller than collision relaxation rate) and the CMW velocity is determined by thermodynamic properties only. We find that in a plasma of opposite chiralities, at frequencies smaller than the chirality-flipping rate, the CMW excitation turns into a vector-like diffusion mode. In the interacting Fermi liquid, the CMW turns into the Landau zero sound mode in the high-frequency collisionless regime.

## Key claim

The authors show that the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) emerges in the hydrodynamic regime of chiral kinetic theory in an external magnetic field, with its group velocity determined solely by thermodynamic properties (charge susceptibility) and independent of collision details. However, this result is valid only up to linear order in the magnetic field, and the chiral kinetic theory itself is justified only when the temperature satisfies \(T \gg \sqrt{B}\) and \(T \gg \mu\).

## Method

The authors linearize the Boltzmann equation for chiral fermions around equilibrium, incorporating Berry-phase-modified equations of motion in a homogeneous magnetic field. They analyze collective modes in two regimes: a high-temperature weak-coupling plasma of a single Weyl fermion species, and a high-density Landau Fermi liquid with interactions not restricted to be weak. For the plasma case, they use a relaxation time approximation (RTA) for the collision term to obtain analytic solutions beyond the hydrodynamic regime, and for the Fermi liquid case they extend prior work by including a collision term in RTA to study the transition between hydrodynamic and collisionless regimes.

## Key numbers

- Charge susceptibility at zero chemical potential: \(\chi = T^2/6 + \mathcal{O}(B^2/T^2)\)
- Group velocity of the chiral magnetic wave: \(v_B = B / (4\pi^2 \chi)\)
- Normal diffusion constant under RTA at zero magnetic field: \(D_{\text{norm}} = \tau/3\)
- Anomalous diffusion constant for the vector charge mode in the presence of inter-chiral transitions: \(D_{\text{anom}} = v_B^2 \tau_s\), where \(\tau_s\) is the inter-chiral relaxation time
- Vector charge susceptibility for two chiralities: \(\chi_V = T^2/3\)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) first proposed by Kharzeev and Yee (2010) and Newman (2005) by providing a microscopic derivation from chiral kinetic theory. It confirms the hydrodynamic result that the CMW velocity depends only on thermodynamic properties, and goes beyond prior work by studying the dispersion relation outside the hydrodynamic regime using the relaxation time approximation. The analysis of inter-chiral transitions (sphaleron effects) shows a transition from a diffusive mode at low frequencies to the CMW at higher frequencies, consistent with a holographic model study by Jimenez-Alba et al. (2014). For the cold dense Fermi liquid case, the work extends the collisionless analysis of Gorsky et al. (2012) by including a collision term, revealing the transition from the CMW in the hydrodynamic regime to Landau zero sound in the collisionless regime.
