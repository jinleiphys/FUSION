# Anomalous Chiral Superfluidity

> We discuss both the anomalous Cartan currents and the energy-momentum tensor in a left chiral theory with flavour anomalies as an effective theory for flavored chiral phonons in a chiral superfluid with the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten term. In the mean-field (leading tadpole) approximation the anomalous Cartan currents and the energy momentum tensor take the form of constitutive currents in the chiral superfluid state. The pertinence of higher order corrections and the Adler-Bardeen theorem is briefly noted.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the anomalous corrections to constitutive currents in a chiral superfluid, previously derived using hydrodynamical and thermodynamic arguments, arise naturally from the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten effective action in the mean-field (leading tadpole) approximation; however, the mean-field corrections appear to conflict with the Adler-Bardeen non-renormalization theorem, and the authors note that full quantum vertex corrections would restore the theorem.

## Method

The authors start from a left-handed chiral theory with flavor anomalies, described by the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten effective action for the chiral field U. They compute the anomalous Cartan currents and the energy-momentum tensor via the Noether construction and canonical methods. They then expand the chiral field in phonon fluctuations and evaluate expectation values in the superfluid ground state using a mean-field (tadpole) approximation, expanding the vierbeins to leading order in the phonon field squared. Finally, they identify the renormalized phonon field with the local superfluid 4-velocity through Cartan chemical potentials to obtain constitutive forms.

## Key numbers

* The anomaly coefficient is C = N_c / (48 pi^2), where N_c is the number of colors.
* The mean-field expansion of the vierbein expectation value yields <e^{ab}(pi_L)> approx delta^{ab} (1 - N pi_L^2 / 6) and <e^{bB} e^{cC}> approx delta^{bB} delta^{cC} (1 - N pi_L^2 / 12).
* The phonon field expectation value is pi_L^2 = (1/F^2) sum_k (1/(2 omega_k)), with omega_k = k for relativistic phonons in the tadpole approximation.
* The ratio N pi_L^2 / 2 = (n^a mu^a) / (epsilon + p), where n^a is the charge density, mu^a the chemical potential, epsilon the energy density, and p the pressure.
* The renormalized anomalous current contains a term with factor (1 - 3/4 * n^e mu^e / (epsilon + p)) for the dv contribution, compared to (1 - 2/3) in the result of Son and Surowka (2009).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamical analysis of anomalies in dense QCD by Son and Surowka (2009), who used triangle anomalies and thermodynamics to derive amendments to constitutive currents. The authors trace the origin of these anomalous contributions to the Wess-Zumino-Witten term, building on the treatment of anomalies in the color-flavor locked superfluid phase by Son (2000). The results enable a fully quantum mechanical understanding of anomalous transport in chiral superfluids, with relevance to neutron star physics, dense quark matter, and holographic QCD models at finite density.
