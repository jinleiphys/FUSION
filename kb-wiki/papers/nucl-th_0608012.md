# Rearrangement in folding potentials with density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction

> We discuss optical potentials for the nuclear elastic scattering from a variational viewpoint. Density-dependence in the effective $N$-$N$ interaction leads to density rearrangement terms, in addition to the conventional folding term. Effects of the rearrangement on the $N$-$A$ optical potential are illustrated in the nuclear-matter limit. Closely relating to consistency with the saturation, the rearrangement appreciably improves the isoscalar optical potential depth over the previous folding model calculations. The rearrangement gives stronger effects as the density grows. We also present rearrangement terms in the $A$-$A$ double-folding potential. Since the rearrangement terms are relevant to the nuclear structure but should be handled within the reaction model, $N$-$N$ effective interactions applicable both to structure models and to the folding model will be desired for unambiguous description of the nuclear elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The density-dependence in effective nucleon-nucleon interactions produces density rearrangement terms (DRTs) in the folding potential that have been neglected in conventional folding-model calculations; when these DRTs are included, the resulting "renormalized folding potential" (RFP) yields optical potential depths consistent with the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem and the saturation properties of nuclear matter, provided the effective interaction itself respects saturation.

## Method

The authors derive the optical potential for nucleon-nucleus (N-A) scattering from a variational equation, decomposing the total (A+1)-nucleon wavefunction into a target ground state and a projectile wavefunction. They obtain the renormalized folding potential containing both the conventional folding term and a density rearrangement term arising from the density-dependence of the coupling constants in the effective interaction. The formalism is then applied to the nuclear-matter limit to compute the single-particle energy and the optical potential depth as functions of incident energy and density, using several phenomenological effective interactions (Skyrme SLy4, Gogny D1 and D1S, M3Y-P2, and BDM3Y1) and comparing with experimental isoscalar optical potential depths.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density (rho = 0.16 fm^-3), the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem constrains the optical potential at the Fermi momentum to U(k_F0; k_F0) = E_min - k_F0^2/(2M), where E_min is approximately -16 MeV
- The effective mass at the saturation point is M_0^* approximately 0.7 M, giving a slope dU/dE_N = 1 - M_0^*/M at the Fermi momentum
- For BDM3Y1 with the DRT included, the folding potential depth becomes much closer to empirical values and is almost indistinguishable from M3Y-P2 results at incident energies up to approximately 200 MeV
- At twice the saturation density, BDM3Y1 without the DRT gives attractive U(k; k_F) while the RFP becomes repulsive at relatively high incident energies (60, 120, and 180 MeV shown in the density-dependence figure)

## Context

This work extends the folding model approach of Satchler and Love (1979) and the density-dependent M3Y interactions (DDM3Y, BDM3Y, KOB94) by identifying and incorporating the density rearrangement terms that were previously omitted. The DRTs connect to the renormalized Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach of Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux (1977) and to the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem discussed by Haftel and Tabakin (1972). The results enable a unified description of nuclear elastic scattering and nuclear structure using consistent effective interactions, and suggest that the additional energy-dependent factor g(E_N) in BDM3Y1 may be unnecessary for incident energies below 200 MeV when the DRT is included.
