# Analysis of Dilepton Invariant Mass Spectrum in C+C at 2 and 1 AGeV

> Recently the HADES collaboration has published the invariant mass spectrum of $e^+e^-$ pairs, dN/dM$_{e^+e^-}$, produced in C+C collisions at 2 AGeV. Using electromagnetic probes, one hopes to get in this experiment information on hadron properties at high density and temperature. Simulations show that firm conclusions on possible in-medium modifications of meson properties will only be possible when the elementary meson production cross sections, especially in the pn channel, as well as production cross sections of baryonic resonances are better known. Presently one can conclude that a) simulations overpredict by far the cross section at $M_{e^+e^-} \approx M_ω{^0}$ if free production cross sections are used and that b) the upper limit of the $η$ decay into $e^+e^-$ is smaller than the present upper limit of the Particle Data Group. This is the result of simulations using the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) approach.

## Key claim

Simulations using the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) approach show that firm conclusions on possible in-medium modifications of meson properties from the HADES C+C dilepton data will only be possible when the elementary meson production cross sections, especially in the pn channel, as well as production cross sections of baryonic resonances are better known. The abstract's claim that simulations "overpredict by far" the cross section at the omega mass and that the eta decay upper limit is smaller than the PDG value holds only under the specific assumptions of the "standard" simulation setup.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) semi-classical transport model to simulate C+C collisions at 2 and 1 AGeV on an event-by-event basis, adding production cross sections for all particles contributing to the dilepton invariant mass spectrum: np bremsstrahlung, eta Dalitz and direct decay, omega Dalitz and direct decay, rho decay, Delta Dalitz decay, and pi0 Dalitz decay. They compared the simulated dilepton invariant mass spectra, corrected for experimental mass resolution and acceptance using a program provided by the HADES collaboration, to the published HADES data, varying unknown input parameters such as the ratio of pn to pp production cross sections for eta and omega mesons and the in-medium mass of the omega meson.

## Key numbers

*   The average density at omega production points in C+C at 2 AGeV is <rho> = 1.394 rho_0, twice as large as the average density of 0.6 rho_0 in the CBELSA/TAPS gamma A experiment.
*   Using the Brown-Rho scaling formula with the CBELSA/TAPS result, the authors extract alpha = 0.13, yielding an average omega pole mass of M = 641 MeV/c^2 at the average density of the C+C reaction.
*   The branching ratio for eta decay into e+e- gamma is 6 x 10^{-3}, and the Particle Data Group upper limit for the direct eta decay into e+e- is 7.7 x 10^{-5}.
*   The branching ratio for omega decay into e+e- pi is 5.9 x 10^{-4} and into e+e- is 7.14 x 10^{-5}.
*   The branching ratio for rho decay into dileptons is 4.5 x 10^{-5}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions to low energies (2 AGeV) and small systems (C+C), following the earlier exploratory experiments by the DLS collaboration and the higher-energy results from CERES/NA45 and NA60. The authors use the IQMD model to test whether the HADES data can distinguish between different theoretical scenarios for in-medium modifications, such as the mass shift of the omega meson observed by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration and the broadening of the rho meson spectral function calculated by Rapp et al. The study concludes that without better knowledge of the pn production cross sections for eta and omega mesons, heavy-ion reactions at these energies cannot provide robust information on in-medium modifications.
