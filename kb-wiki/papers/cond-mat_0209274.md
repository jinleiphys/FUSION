# Quantum Corrections to Dilute Bose Liquids

> It was recently shown (A. Bulgac. Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 89}, 050402 (2002)) that an entirely new class of quantum liquids with widely tunable properties could be manufactured from bosons (boselets), fermions (fermilets) and their mixtures (ferbolets) by controlling their interaction properties by the means of a Feshbach resonance. We extend the previous mean--field analysis of these quantum liquids by computing the lowest order quantum corrections to the ground state energy and the depletion of the Bose--Einstein condensate and by estimating higher order corrections as well. We show that the quantum corrections are relatively small and controlled by the diluteness parameter $\sqrt{n|a|^3} \ll 1$, even though strictly speaking in this case there is no low density expansion.

## Key claim

The paper shows that quantum corrections to the ground state energy and condensate depletion of self-bound Bose droplets (boselets) are small and controlled by the diluteness parameter \(\sqrt{n|a|^3} \ll 1\), even though strictly speaking there is no low-density expansion in this regime because the system is unstable at densities below the spinodal point.

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian density with contact two-body and three-body interactions, where the three-body coupling constant is derived from the Efimov effect and is large. They split the boson field into a classical condensate and fluctuations, then compute the one-loop quantum corrections to the energy density and particle density using dimensional regularization. A power-counting analysis of generic loop diagrams is performed to show that all higher-order corrections are suppressed by powers of \(\sqrt{n|a|^3}\).

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium particle number density at tree level: \(n_0 = \frac{1}{|a|^3} \frac{1}{\Upsilon}\), where \(\Upsilon \gg 1\) is a dimensionless measure of the three-body coupling strength.
- Tree-level energy per particle at equilibrium: \(\mu_0 = -\frac{\hbar^2}{ma^2} \frac{\pi}{\Upsilon}\).
- Spinodal density below which homogeneous matter is unstable: \(n_s = \frac{2}{3} n_0\).
- One-loop corrected density: \(n = \frac{1}{|a|^3} \frac{1}{\Upsilon} \left( 1 - \frac{72}{5} \alpha \right)\), with \(\alpha = \frac{2^{3/2}}{3\pi^{1/2} \Upsilon^{1/2}}\).
- One-loop corrected energy per particle: \(\frac{\mathcal{E}}{n} = \mu_0 \left( 1 - \frac{16}{5} \alpha \right)\).
- Diluteness parameter: \(\sqrt{n|a|^3} = \Upsilon^{-1/2} \ll 1\).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field analysis of boselets, fermilets, and ferbolets introduced by Bulgac (2002) by computing the leading quantum corrections. It shows that, unlike in liquid \(^4\)He where the non-condensed fraction is near 90%, these dilute self-bound systems remain stable against quantum fluctuations. The results enable future studies of the transition to a coherent mixture of atoms and trimers at higher densities, and suggest that spin-polarized tritium atoms are promising experimental candidates for realizing boselets.
