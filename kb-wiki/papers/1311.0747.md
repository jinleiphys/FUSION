# Hexagonal flow v_6 as a superposition of elliptic v_2 and triangular v_3 flows

> Partial contributions of elliptic v_2 and triangular v_3 flows to the hexagonal v_6 flow are studied within the HYDJET++ model for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s}=2.76 TeV. Scaling of the ratio v_6^{1/6}{Psi_2} / v_2^{1/2}{Psi_2} in the elliptic flow plane, Psi_2, is predicted in the range 1 < p_T < 4 GeV/c for semicentral and semiperipheral collisions. Jets increase this ratio by about 10% and also cause its rise at p_T > 3.5 GeV/c. The part of v_6 coming from v_2 is instantly increasing as the reaction becomes more peripheral, whereas the contribution of v_3 to v_6 drops. This behavior explains the experimentally observed increase of correlations between second and sixth harmonics and decrease of correlations between third and sixth harmonics with rising impact parameter $b$. Our study favors the idea that basic features of the hexagonal flow can be understood in terms of the interplay of elliptic and triangular flows.

## Key claim

The hexagonal flow \(v_6\) in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV can be understood as a nonlinear hydrodynamic superposition of elliptic flow \(v_2\) and triangular flow \(v_3\), with the approximate relation \(v_6 \cong \frac{1}{6} v_2^3 + \frac{1}{2} v_3^2\), rather than arising from genuine hexagonal initial-state fluctuations. The study is performed within the HYDJET++ model, which lacks event-by-event fluctuations and generates only \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) at the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Method

The authors used the HYDJET++ Monte Carlo event generator, which combines a parametrized hydrodynamic description of the soft (thermal) hadronic component with the PYQUEN module for hard parton scattering and energy loss in the medium. For each of four centrality classes (10-20%, 20-30%, 30-40%, 40-50%), approximately \(2 \times 10^6\) Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV were generated. The hexagonal flow \(v_6\) was computed in both the elliptic flow plane \(\Psi_2\) and the triangular flow plane \(\Psi_3\), and two-plane event-plane correlators \(\langle \cos 6(\Psi_2 - \Psi_6) \rangle\) and \(\langle \cos 6(\Psi_3 - \Psi_6) \rangle\) were extracted using the subevent method with a pseudorapidity gap.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(v_6^{1/6}\{\Psi_2\} / v_2^{1/2}\{\Psi_2\}\) in the \(\Psi_2\) plane shows scaling at \(p_t \geq 1\) GeV/\(c\) for centralities 10-50%, approaching the ideal hydrodynamic limit \((1/6)^{1/6} \approx 0.74\).
- Jets increase this ratio by about 10% in the interval \(1 \leq p_t \leq 3\) GeV/\(c\), and cause a further rise at \(p_t \geq 3.5\) GeV/\(c\).
- The ratio \(v_6^{1/6}\{\Psi_3\} / v_3^{1/3}\{\Psi_3\}\) in the \(\Psi_3\) plane is below the ideal hydrodynamic limit \((1/2)^{1/6} \approx 0.89\) and decreases for more peripheral collisions.
- The two-plane correlator \(\langle \cos 6(\Psi_2 - \Psi_6) \rangle\) increases for more peripheral collisions, while \(\langle \cos 6(\Psi_3 - \Psi_6) \rangle\) drops, consistent with ATLAS observations.

## Context

This work extends the study of higher-harmonic flow beyond the quadrangular flow \(v_4\) (previously studied by the same group in 2013) to the hexagonal flow \(v_6\), using the same HYDJET++ framework. It contradicts the interpretation of event-plane correlators from the AMPT model (BOP13), which attributed the drop in \(\langle \cos 6(\Psi_3 - \Psi_6) \rangle\) to a decrease in triangular flow itself; the authors argue instead that the drop is driven by the dominance of \(v_2\) over \(v_3\) in peripheral collisions combined with the random orientation of \(\Psi_2\) and \(\Psi_3\). The results enable a simpler interpretation of experimental \(v_6\) data from the LHC without invoking genuine hexagonal initial-state fluctuations.
