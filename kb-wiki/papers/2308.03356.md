# Relativistic model-free prediction for neutrinoless double beta decay at leading order

> Starting from a manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral Lagrangian, we present a model-free prediction for the transition amplitude of the process $nn\rightarrow pp e^-e^-$ induced by light Majorana neutrinos, which is a key process of the neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) in heavy nuclei employed in large-scale searches. Contrary to the nonrelativistic case, we show that the transition amplitude can be renormalized at leading order without any uncertain contact operators. The predicted amplitude defines a stringent benchmark for the previous estimation with model-dependent inputs, and greatly reduces the uncertainty of $0νββ$ transition operator in the calculations of nuclear matrix elements. Generalizations of the present framework could also help to address the uncertainties in $0νββ$ decay induced by other mechanisms. In addition, the present work motivates a relativistic {\it ab initio} calculation of $0νββ$ decay in light and medium-mass nuclei.

# Relativistic model-free prediction for neutrinoless double beta decay at leading order

## Key claim
The transition amplitude for the $nn\rightarrow pp e^-e^-$ process induced by light Majorana neutrinos can be renormalized at leading order without any uncertain contact operators when using a manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral Lagrangian, contrary to the nonrelativistic case where a contact term is required. The predicted amplitude is model-free within chiral effective field theory, meaning it requires no model-dependent inputs beyond the framework itself.

## Method
The authors start from a manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral Lagrangian and solve the relativistic scattering equation (a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) for the $^1S_0$ channel of $nn\rightarrow pp e^-e^-$. The leading-order strong and neutrino potentials are derived, and the amplitude is computed in momentum space with a separable gaussian regulator, with the low-energy constant in the $^1S_0$ channel fixed by reproducing the scattering length $a_{np}=-23.74$ fm. The ultraviolet behavior of the relativistic propagator (scaling as $\mathcal{O}(\Lambda^{-3})$) is compared to the nonrelativistic propagator (scaling as $\mathcal{O}(\Lambda^{-2})$) to demonstrate renormalizability.

## Key numbers
- The relativistic chiral EFT amplitude at kinematics $|\bm p_i|=25$ MeV and $|\bm p_f|=30$ MeV is $\mathcal{A}^{\rm LO}_\nu = -0.0209~\rm{MeV}^{-2}$, about 10% larger than the previous model-dependent estimation of $-0.0195(5)~\rm{MeV}^{-2}$ from Cirigliano et al. (2021).
- The scattering length used to fix the $^1S_0$ contact term is $a_{np}=-23.74$ fm.
- The pion decay constant is $f_\pi=92.2$ MeV and the nucleon axial coupling is $g_A=1.27$.
- The estimated theoretical uncertainty from truncation of the chiral expansion is roughly $m_\pi/\Lambda_b \approx 23\%$, with $\Lambda_b=600$ MeV.
- For off-shell amplitudes at high final momentum ($|\boldsymbol p_f|=150$ MeV), the relativistic enhancement over nonrelativistic results reaches about 40%.

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of neutrinoless double beta decay previously developed by Cirigliano et al. (2018, 2019, 2021), who showed that a contact operator is required at leading order in the nonrelativistic (heavy baryon) approach. The earlier approach by Cirigliano et al. (2021) introduced model-dependent inputs from elastic intermediate states in analogy to the Cottingham formula to constrain this contact term. The present relativistic framework, similar to the modified Weinberg approach applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering by Epelbaum et al. (2012), eliminates the need for such model-dependent inputs at leading order. This enables a direct benchmark for lattice QCD calculations of $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay (Shanahan 2017, Tiburzi 2017, Davoudi 2021) and motivates relativistic ab initio calculations of $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay in light and medium-mass nuclei.
