# Nuclear effects on generalized parton distributions of 3He

> The relevance of measuring Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) of nuclei is stressed and the unique possibilities offered by nuclear few body systems is emphasized. A realistic microscopic calculation of the unpolarized quark GPD Hq3 of the 3He nucleus is reviewed. Nuclear effects are found to be larger than in inclusive deep inelastic scattering, flavor dependent, increasing with the momentum transfer and the asymmetry of the process. They also depend on the choice of the realistic potential. Besides, it is found that nuclear GPDs cannot be factorized into a Delta^2-dependent and a Delta^2-independent term, as suggested in prescription proposed for finite nuclei.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects on the unpolarized quark GPD H_q^3 of 3He are found to be larger than in inclusive deep inelastic scattering, flavor dependent, increasing with momentum transfer and skewness, and dependent on the choice of realistic nuclear potential; importantly, nuclear GPDs cannot be factorized into a Delta^2-dependent and a Delta^2-independent term, contrary to prescriptions proposed for finite nuclei.

## Method

The author performs an Impulse Approximation (IA) calculation of the quark unpolarized GPD H_q^3 for 3He, using a convolution formula in terms of the nucleon GPD H_q^N and a non-diagonal spectral function P_N^3. The spectral function is evaluated numerically using wave function overlaps from the AV18 NN interaction, with the calculation valid for Delta^2 much less than Q^2 and M^2, covering the kinematical range relevant to the coherent, no-breakup channel of deep exclusive processes off 3He.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear effects increase with momentum transfer; at Delta^2 = -0.25 GeV^2 and xi_3 = 3 xi = 0.2, the ratio R^(0) for the d flavor shows clear dependence on the choice of NN potential (AV18 vs AV14), unlike the forward limit where results are indistinguishable.
- In the forward limit (Delta^2 = 0, xi = 0), the ratio R^(0) for the d flavor calculated with AV18 and AV14 interactions yields results that are not distinguishable as a function of x_3 = 3 x.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear GPDs, beginning with the deuteron by Cano and Pire (2004), and addresses prescriptions for finite nuclei proposed by Polyakov and others. The calculation provides a realistic microscopic benchmark for 3He, a well-known nucleus used as an effective neutron target in both unpolarized and polarized experiments (Friar, 1993). The results enable future planning of coherent DVCS experiments off 3He and tests of the accuracy of factorization prescriptions for nuclear GPDs.
