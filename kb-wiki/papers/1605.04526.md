# From Inverse to Delayed Magnetic Catalysis in Strong Magnetic Field

> We study magnetic field effect on chiral phase transition in a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. In comparison with mean field approximation containing quarks only, including mesons as quantum fluctuations in the model leads to a transition from inverse to delayed magnetic catalysis at finite temperature and delays the transition at finite baryon chemical potential. The location of the critical end point depends on the the magnetic field non-monotonously.

## Key claim

Including mesons as quantum fluctuations in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, beyond the mean-field approximation that contains only quarks, changes the magnetic field effect on the chiral phase transition from inverse magnetic catalysis to delayed magnetic catalysis at finite temperature, and delays the transition at finite baryon chemical potential; the location of the critical end point depends non-monotonously on the magnetic field.

## Method

The author studies the SU(2) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit with a covariant Pauli-Villars regularization scheme, including quarks at mean-field level and mesons (sigma and neutral pion) as quantum fluctuations via the random phase approximation. The meson contribution is incorporated into an effective coupling constant that depends on temperature, baryon chemical potential, and magnetic field, and the quark mass is determined by minimizing the thermodynamic potential. The critical temperature and critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration are computed as functions of the magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration at zero baryon chemical potential decreases for magnetic fields below about \(eB = 50 m_\pi^2\) and then turns around and increases for \(eB > 50 m_\pi^2\), showing a transition from inverse to delayed magnetic catalysis.
* At zero temperature, the critical quark chemical potential \(\mu_q^c = \mu_B^c/3\) drops first and then rises continuously with increasing magnetic field, with the turning point from inverse to delayed magnetic catalysis located around \(eB \simeq 30 m_\pi^2\), larger than the mean-field value of about \(eB \simeq 10 m_\pi^2\).
* The model parameters are set by fitting the chiral condensate \(\langle \bar q q \rangle = -(250\ \text{MeV})^3\) and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\ \text{MeV}\) in vacuum, yielding coupling constant \(G = 9.94\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and cutoff \(\Lambda = 1127\ \text{MeV}\).
* The reasonable magnetic field region in the NJL model is constrained by \(eB < \Lambda^2 \simeq 70 m_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends studies of magnetic catalysis (MC) and inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) in QCD phase transitions, which have been observed in lattice simulations at magnetic fields up to \(eB = 3.25\ \text{GeV}^2\) (Lattice QCD Collaboration, 2016). It builds on the theoretical framework developed by Mao (2015) and is consistent with predictions of delayed magnetic catalysis (DMC) from other model calculations (Dyson-Schwinger equation, functional renormalization group, and two-color lattice QCD). The results enable further investigation of the QCD phase diagram at extremely strong magnetic fields relevant to compact stars and early-universe cosmology, and they highlight the role of mesonic fluctuations in modifying the magnetic field dependence of the chiral transition.
