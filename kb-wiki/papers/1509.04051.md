# Pentaquarks and Tetraquarks at LHCb

> Exotic resonant structures found in $Λ^0_b$ and $\overline{B}^0$ decays into charmonium in the LHCb experiment are discussed. Examination of the $J/ψp$ system in $Λ^0_b\to J/ψK^- p$ decays shows two states each of which must be composed of $uudc\overline{c}$ quarks, and thus are called charmonium pentaquarks. Their masses are $4380\pm 8\pm 29$~MeV and $4449.8\pm 1.7\pm 2.5$~MeV, and their corresponding widths ($Γ$) are $205\pm 18\pm 86$ MeV, and $39\pm 5\pm 19$ MeV. The preferred $J^P$ assignments are of opposite parity, with one state having spin 3/2 and the other 5/2. Models of internal binding of the pentaquark states are discussed. Finally, another mesonic state is discussed, the $Z(4430)^-$ that decays into $ψ' π^-$ and was first observed by the Belle collaboration in $B^0\to ψ' K^+π^-$ decays. Using a sample of approximately 25,000 signal events, LHCb determines the $J^{P}$ to be $1^{+}$.

# Pentaquarks and Tetraquarks at LHCb

## Key claim
The LHCb experiment has observed two charmonium pentaquark states in Λb0 → J/ψ K− p decays, but the interpretation depends on a full amplitude analysis that rules out explanations based solely on known Λ* resonances or non-resonant effects; the preferred J^P assignments are of opposite parity, with one state having spin 3/2 and the other 5/2.

## Method
A full amplitude analysis of Λb0 → J/ψ K− p decays was performed using 3 fb−1 of integrated luminosity from 7 and 8 TeV pp collisions at LHCb. The analysis modeled two decay sequences, Λb0 → J/ψ Λ* with Λ* → K− p, and Λb0 → Pc+ K− with Pc+ → J/ψ p, allowing for their mutual interference, using six independent variables including the invariant K− p mass and decay angles. Fits were performed with both a reduced model (64 free parameters for Λ* states) and an extended model (146 free helicity couplings) to estimate systematic uncertainties, with statistical significances evaluated using toy simulations.

## Key numbers
- Mass of lower-mass pentaquark: 4380 ± 8 ± 29 MeV
- Mass of higher-mass pentaquark: 4449.8 ± 1.7 ± 2.5 MeV
- Width of lower-mass pentaquark: 205 ± 18 ± 86 MeV
- Width of higher-mass pentaquark: 39 ± 5 ± 19 MeV
- Statistical significance: 9 standard deviations for the lower-mass state and 12 standard deviations for the higher-mass state (using the extended model)
- Signal yield: 26,007 ± 166 Λb0 → J/ψ K− p candidates with 5.4% background within ±15 MeV of the mass peak
- Branching fraction B(Λb0 → J/ψ K− p) = (3.04 ± 0.04 +0.55 −0.43) × 10−4
- Product branching fraction for Pc(4380)+: B(Λb0 → Pc(4380)+ K−) × B(Pc(4380)+ → J/ψ p) = (2.56 +1.38 −1.34) × 10−5
- Product branching fraction for Pc(4450)+: B(Λb0 → Pc(4450)+ K−) × B(Pc(4450)+ → J/ψ p) = (1.25 +0.42 −0.40) × 10−5
- For the Z(4430)− tetraquark: mass 4475 ± 7 +15 −25 MeV, width 172 ± 13 +37 −34 MeV, using approximately 25,000 signal events; J^P determined to be 1+

## Context
This work extends the search for exotic hadrons beyond the tetraquark states observed earlier, such as the Z(4430)− first reported by Belle (2007) and subsequently confirmed by LHCb with a full amplitude analysis. The pentaquark observation follows earlier fallacious claims of pentaquark states about ten years ago, as reviewed by Hicks (2012) and Stone (2000). The results enable further theoretical work on the internal binding mechanisms of multiquark states, including tightly bound diquark models following Jaffe (1976) and molecular models, and may inform lattice gauge calculations of exotic hadron stability and neutron star cooling rates.
