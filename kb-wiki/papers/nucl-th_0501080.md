# Exactly Soluble Models for Surface Partition

> The surface partition of large clusters is studied analytically within a frame-work of the ``Hills and Dales Model''. Three formulations are solved exactly by using the Laplace-Fourier transformation method. In the limit of small amplitude deformations, the ``Hills and Dales Model'' gives upper and lower bounds for the surface entropy coefficient of large clusters. A comparison with the 2- and 3-dimensional Ising model surface entropy coefficients is made.

## Key claim

The "Hills and Dales Model" (HDM) provides both upper and lower bounds for the surface entropy coefficient of large clusters in the limit of vanishing amplitude deformations, with the grand canonical and canonical formulations giving the supremum and infimum respectively for the surface entropy coefficients of 2- and 3-dimensional Ising models.

## Method

The authors formulated three statistical ensembles for surface deformations of clusters within the HDM framework: grand canonical (volume conserved on average), canonical (volume conserved exactly), and semi-grand canonical (intermediate). Using the Laplace-Fourier transformation method, they solved the surface partition functions exactly for arbitrary cluster sizes by transforming the constrained sums into exponential forms and evaluating the singularities of the resulting isochoric partition functions. The upper and lower estimates for the surface entropy coefficient were obtained by considering the limits of infinite base size (all deformations allowed) and minimal base size (only smallest base deformations), respectively.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound for grand canonical surface partition (GCSP) surface entropy coefficient: ω^gc_U ≈ 1.060090 (dimensionless)
- Upper bound for canonical surface partition (CSP) surface entropy coefficient: ω^c_U ≈ 0.403233 (dimensionless)
- Upper bound for semi-grand canonical surface partition (SGCSP) surface entropy coefficient: ω^sg_U ≈ 0.806466 (dimensionless)
- Lower bound for GCSP surface entropy coefficient: ω^gc_L ≈ 0.852606 (dimensionless)
- Lower bound for CSP surface entropy coefficient: ω^c_L ≈ 0.283572 (dimensionless)
- Lower bound for SGCSP surface entropy coefficient: ω^sg_L ≈ 0.567143 (dimensionless)
- Geometrical degeneracy prefactor for GCSP leading term: g_gc ≈ 0.38139
- Geometrical degeneracy prefactor for CSP and SGCSP leading term: g_c = g_sg ≈ 0.407025
- For the GCSP, the first complex root real part R^gc_1 ≈ 0.0582, about 18 times smaller than R^gc_0
- For the CSP and SGCSP, the first complex root real part is about 63.6 times smaller than the corresponding leading root

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the Hills and Dales Model (Bugaev, 2004) by introducing canonical and semi-grand canonical formulations that enforce exact volume conservation, complementing the earlier grand canonical treatment. The results are compared with Fisher's droplet model (Fisher, 1967), which postulated that surface entropy is proportional to surface area with coefficient ω, and with surface entropy coefficients for 2- and 3-dimensional Ising models on various lattices (Fisher, 1969). The HDM upper and lower bounds encompass all Ising model values, with the GCSP upper bound (1.060090) being about 6% larger than Fisher's postulate, and the CSP upper bound (0.403233) serving as the infimum for the considered lattices. The formalism enables estimation of upper limits for critical temperatures of higher-dimensional Ising lattices.
