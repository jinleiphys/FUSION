# Inclusive $ω$ photoproduction from nuclei and $ω$ in the nuclear medium

> With the aim of extracting information on the shift of the $ω$ mass in the nuclear medium we analyze data obtained at ELSA from where claims for evidence of a mass shift of the $ω$ have been made. We develop a Monte Carlo simulation code which takes into account the possible reactions in the experimental set up of $(γ~ A \to π^0 γ~X)$ in the vicinity of the $ω$ production region with subsequent $ω\to π^0 γ$ decay. We compare our results with experiment for the distribution of $π^0 γ$ invariant masses and conclude that the distribution is compatible with an enlarged $ω$ width of about 90 MeV at nuclear matter density and no shift in the mass. This change in the width would be compatible with the preliminary results obtained from the transparency ratio in the A dependence of $ω$ production. The discrepancy of the present conclusions with former claims of an evidence for a shift of the $ω$ mass stem from a different choice of background which is discussed in the paper.

## Key claim

The data from ELSA on inclusive ω photoproduction from nuclei are compatible with an enlarged ω width of about 90 MeV at nuclear matter density and no shift in the ω mass, contradicting earlier claims of a mass shift of about 100 MeV; the discrepancy arises from a different choice of background subtraction in the analysis.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte Carlo simulation code that models the reaction γ A → π⁰ γ X in the vicinity of the ω production region, including ω → π⁰ γ decay, and accounts for final-state interactions of the ω meson and its decay products in finite nuclei. The simulation incorporates the elementary γ N → ω N cross section parameterized from data on hydrogen targets, the in-medium ω spectral function with a density-dependent collisional width, quasielastic scattering and absorption of ω mesons, and propagation of pions through the nucleus using a microscopic Δh model and phenomenological cross sections. The simulated π⁰ γ invariant mass distributions were compared with experimental data from the Crystal Barrel/TAPS setup at ELSA.

## Key numbers

- The ω free width is Γ_free = 8.49 MeV, with partial decay widths Γ_{ω→πππ} ≈ 7.56 MeV and Γ_{ω→πγ} ≈ 0.76 MeV.
- The data are compatible with an ω in-medium width Γ_0 = 90 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, with no mass shift.
- The incident photon beam energy range used in the transparency ratio analysis was 1.45 GeV < E_γ < 1.55 GeV.
- A cut on pion kinetic energy T_π > 150 MeV was applied to suppress distorted events from pion final-state interactions; typical π⁰ kinetic energy in the process is T_π ≈ 380 MeV.
- The ω nominal mass is m_ω = 782 MeV; the elementary reaction threshold is E_γ^{th} ≈ 1.1 GeV.

## Context

This work reanalyzes data from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (Trnka et al., 2005) that claimed evidence for a decrease of the ω mass in the medium by about 100 MeV. The present analysis shows that the same data can be explained by an increased ω width of about 90 MeV at nuclear matter density with no mass shift, a conclusion compatible with preliminary transparency ratio results on the A dependence of ω production. The findings are relevant to the broader debate on vector meson properties in nuclei, where theoretical predictions range from attraction of 100-200 MeV (Tsushima et al., Klingl et al.) to no mass change (Mosel et al.) to repulsion of about 50 MeV (Lutz et al.), and the method using transparency ratios follows the approach previously applied to vector mesons by Magas et al. (2003) and Mühlich et al. (2005).
