# Stability windows for proto-quark stars

> We investigate the existence of possible stable strange matter and related stability windows at finite temperature for different models that are generally applied to describe quark stars, namely, the quark-mass density dependent model, the MIT bag model and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We emphasize that, although the limits for stable strange matter depend on a comparison with the ground state of 56Fe, which is a zero temperature state, the quantity that has to be used in the search for strange matter in proto-quark stars is the free energy and we analyze stability windows up to temperatures of the order of 40 MeV. The effects of strong magnetic fields on stability windows are computed and the resulting mass-radius relations for different stages of the proto-quark star are analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for proto-quark stars at finite temperature, the correct quantity to determine the upper limit of the stability window for strange matter is the free energy per baryon rather than the binding energy per baryon, and that using the free energy shifts the stability window to the right (allowing a wider range of parameters) contrary to previous results in the literature.

## Method

The authors computed stability windows for strange quark matter at finite temperatures up to 40 MeV using three different quark matter models: the quark-mass density dependent (QMDD) model in two versions, the MIT bag model, and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. For each model, they imposed the conditions that two-flavor quark matter must be unstable (energy per baryon above 930 MeV at zero temperature) and three-flavor strange matter must be stable (free energy per baryon below 930 MeV), and they analyzed the effects of temperature, magnetic fields up to 7.2 x 10^18 G, and trapped neutrinos with fixed lepton fraction. The resulting equations of state were then used as input to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations for different stages of proto-quark star evolution.

## Key numbers

- For the MIT bag model at T=20 MeV, the stability window using binding energy ranges from 144.1 < B^{1/4} < 147.1 MeV, while using free energy gives 147.1 < B^{1/4} < 150.1 MeV
- For the QMDDv1 model at T=20 MeV, the stability window using binding energy ranges from 65.5 < C < 74.5 MeV fm^{-3}, while using free energy gives 74.5 < C < 79.9 MeV fm^{-3}
- The ratio of the term Ts to energy density reaches almost 20 percent at T=40 MeV
- For the MIT model with fixed entropy per baryon S/A=2, B^{1/4} can vary from 147 to 168.7 MeV for unmagnetized stars and from 147 to 172.2 MeV for magnetized stars
- The MIT model with parametrization A at zero temperature and no magnetic field yields a maximum mass of 1.62 solar masses with radius 9.01 km
- The MIT model with magnetic field at zero temperature yields a maximum mass of 2.02 solar masses with radius 9.04 km
- The QMDDv1 model with parametrization B at zero temperature yields a maximum mass of 2.28 solar masses with radius 12.05 km
- The QMDDv2 model with parametrization D at zero temperature yields a maximum mass of 1.60 solar masses with radius 8.42 km

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange matter stability windows by Chmaj (1989), Chakrabarty (1993), Lugones (1995), and Su (2002) by correctly using the free energy per baryon instead of binding energy per baryon for finite temperature systems, and by including the effects of strong magnetic fields. The paper also builds on the Bodmer-Witten conjecture about strange matter being the true ground state of matter, and on earlier work by Dexheimer (2008) and others on proto-neutron star evolution. The results enable more accurate modeling of proto-quark star evolution during the Kelvin-Helmholtz epoch, particularly for understanding how the stability window changes as the star cools from temperatures around 40 MeV to below 2-4 MeV.
