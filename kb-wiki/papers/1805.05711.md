# Forward di-hadron back-to-back correlations in $\boldsymbol{pA}$ collisions from rcBK evolution

> We study the disappearance of the away-side peak of the di-hadron correlation function in p+A vs p+p collisions at forward rapidities, when the scaterring process presents a manifest dilute-dense asymmetry. We improve the state-of-the-art description of this phenomenon in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC), for hadrons produced nearly back-to-back. In that case, the gluon content of the saturated nuclear target can be described with transverse-momentum-dependent gluon distributions, whose small-$x$ evolution we calculate numerically by solving the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling corrections. We first show that our formalism provides a good description of the disappearance of the away-side azimuthal correlations in d+Au collisions observed at BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energies. Then, we predict the away-side peak of upcoming p+Au data at $~\sqrt[]{s}=200$ GeV to be suppressed by about a factor 2 with respect to p+p collisions, and we propose to study the rapidity dependence of that suppression as a complementary strong evidence of gluon saturation in experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the suppression of the away-side peak in di-hadron correlations in p+A collisions relative to p+p collisions can be described using gluon transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) distributions obtained from numerical solutions of the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling corrections (rcBK), but only for hadrons produced nearly back-to-back (Δφ close to π), and the resulting cross section is free from tunable parameters.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for forward di-hadron production in p+A collisions using TMD factorization in the back-to-back limit, where the total transverse momentum of the pair is much smaller than the individual hadron transverse momenta. The gluon TMDs describing the dense nuclear target are obtained from numerical solutions of the rcBK evolution equation for the fundamental dipole scattering amplitude, using the McLerran-Venugopalan model as initial condition with Q_s0^2 = 0.2 GeV^2 for protons and Q_s0^2 = 0.6 GeV^2 for nuclei. The cross section is then convoluted with NLO MSTW2008 parton distribution functions and DSS14 NLO fragmentation functions to obtain the coincidence probability CP(Δφ) for neutral pions.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale for a proton target at x_0 = 0.01 is Q_s0^2 = 0.2 GeV^2, and for a nuclear target it is Q_s0^2 = 0.6 GeV^2 (a factor 3 larger)
- The away-side peak in p+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV is predicted to be suppressed by about a factor 2 with respect to p+p collisions, independent of the p_t window chosen
- The initial value for the small-x evolution is x_0 = 0.01, and the running coupling uses Lambda = 197 MeV with N_f = 4
- The factorization scale is set to mu^2 = p_t1^2 (the transverse momentum of the leading hadron), with an uncertainty band obtained by taking a scale larger by 50%

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based description of forward di-hadron correlations from earlier studies using Golec-Biernat Wusthoff (GBW) type parameterizations (Stasto, 2011) and Gaussian approximations (Albacete, 2010; Lappi, 2012), by using rcBK evolution to obtain the gluon TMDs. The formalism is applicable only in the back-to-back limit (Dominguez, 2011; Marquet, 2016) and improves upon previous work by using well-constrained rcBK solutions from deep inelastic scattering data (Albacete, 2010). The predictions for the rapidity dependence of the suppression provide a test to distinguish CGC effects from competing descriptions such as nuclear transverse-momentum broadening (Kang, 2011) or energy-momentum conservation corrections (Kopeliovich, 2005; Frankfurt, 2007).
