# Dipole model at Next-to-Leading Order meets HERA data

> Deep inelastic scattering (DIS) total cross section data at small-x as measured by the HERA experiments is well described by Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution in the leading order dipole picture. Recently the full Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) dipole picture total cross sections have become available for DIS, and a working factorization scheme has been devised which subtracts the soft gluon divergence present at NLO. We report our recently published work in which we make the first comparisons of the NLO DIS total cross sections to HERA data. The non-perturbative initial condition to BK evolution is fixed by fitting the HERA reduced cross section data. As the NLO results for the DIS total cross section are currently available only in the massless quark limit, we also fit a light-quark-only cross section constructed with a parametrization of published total and heavy quark data. We find an excellent description of the HERA data. Since the full NLO BK equation is computationally expensive, we use a number of beyond LO prescriptions for the evolution that include most important higher order corrections enhanced by large transverse logarithms, including the recent version of the equation formulated in terms of the target momentum fraction.

## Key claim

The NLO dipole picture, using approximate BK evolution equations that resum higher-order corrections enhanced by large transverse logarithms, provides an excellent description of HERA reduced cross section data, but the precise HERA data cannot differentiate between the different BK evolution schemes or running coupling schemes used.

## Method

The authors performed fits of the non-perturbative initial condition for BK evolution to HERA reduced cross section data using three beyond-leading-order prescriptions of the BK equation: Kinematically Constrained BK (KCBK), Collinearly Resummed BK (ResumBK), and a target-momentum-fraction formulation (TBK). Since the NLO DIS total cross sections are only available in the massless quark limit, the authors also constructed and fitted a light-quark-only cross section using a parametrization of published total and heavy quark data.

## Key numbers

- Fits used initial condition rapidity scales of Y_0,BK = eta_0,BK = ln(1/0.01)
- The NLO cross sections include a soft gluon factorization with lower integration limit z_min = exp(Y_0,if) * Q_0^2 / W^2
- Structure functions were extrapolated to LHeC kinematics, showing moderate differences between schemes even at those energies
- Comparison of computed F_L to H1 collaboration measurements showed good agreement across all evolution schemes

## Context

This work extends the leading-order dipole picture description of HERA small-x DIS data (Aaron 2009) to full NLO accuracy, building on recent derivations of NLO DIS structure functions (Beuf 2016, 2017; Hanninen 2017) and a working soft gluon factorization scheme (Ducloue 2017). The use of resummed BK equations follows earlier work on kinematically constrained BK (Beuf 2014), collinearly resummed BK (Iancu 2015), and target-momentum-fraction evolution (Ducloue 2019). The results enable future comparisons with LHeC kinematics and indicate that a substantial non-perturbative contribution exists in light quark production, implying sizeable theoretical uncertainty in those fits.
