# The astrophysical rate of 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne via recoil-decay tagging and its implications for nova nucleosynthesis

> The 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne reaction is one of two known routes for breakout from the hot CNO cycles into the rp process. Its astrophysical rate depends on the decay properties of excited states in 19Ne lying just above the 15O + alpha threshold. We have measured the alpha-decay branching ratios for these states using the p(21Ne,t)19Ne reaction at 43 MeV/u. Combining our branching ratio measurements with previous determinations of the radiative widths of these states, we calculate the astrophysical rate of 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne. Using this reaction rate, we perform hydrodynamic calculations of nova outbursts and conclude that no significant breakout from the hot CNO cycles into the rp process occurs in classical novae via 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne.

## Key claim

The authors measure upper limits on the alpha-decay branching ratios of excited states in 19Ne and conclude that, under the conditions of classical novae, the 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne reaction rate is less than 0.02% of the 15O beta+ decay rate, meaning no significant breakout from the hot CNO cycles into the rp process occurs via this reaction.

## Method

The authors populated excited states in 19Ne via the p(21Ne,t)19Ne reaction in inverse kinematics using a 21Ne beam at 43 MeV/u. They detected either 19Ne recoils (for gamma decays) or 15O decay products (for alpha decays) in coincidence with tritons in the Big-Bite Spectrometer, using phoswich detectors for particle identification. From the excitation energy spectra, they determined alpha-decay branching ratios for six states in 19Ne and combined these with previously measured radiative widths to compute the astrophysical reaction rate, then performed hydrodynamic nova simulations.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on the alpha-decay branching ratio B_alpha for the 4.033 MeV, 3/2+ state in 19Ne: 4.3 x 10^-4 at 90% confidence level (Bayesian analysis)
- The 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne reaction rate is less than 0.02% of the 15O beta+ decay rate throughout the entire nova envelope, including the hottest innermost shell
- Nova model: 1.35 solar mass ONe white dwarf accreting at 2 x 10^-10 solar masses per year
- Experimental resolution: 90 keV FWHM
- The 15O + alpha threshold in 19Ne: 3.529 MeV
- Peak temperature at the burning shell: 3.21 x 10^8 K

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to measure alpha-decay branching ratios for 19Ne states (Magnus 1990, Laird 2002) which lacked sensitivity for the critical 4.033 MeV state. The authors confirm the conclusions of Davids (2003) and Iliadis (2002) that 15O(alpha,gamma)19Ne does not enable significant breakout from hot CNO cycles in classical novae. The results enable more accurate modeling of nova nucleosynthesis and rule out this reaction as a major pathway for producing heavy elements in novae, directing attention to the other breakout candidate, 18Ne(alpha,p)21Na.
