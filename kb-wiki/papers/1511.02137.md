# Jet-induced modifications of the characteristic of the bulk nuclear matter

> We present our studies on jet-induced modifications of the characteristic of the bulk nuclear matter. To describe such a matter, we use efficient relativistic hydrodynamic simulations in (3+1) dimensions employing the Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) in the parallel programming framework. We use Cartesian coordinates in the calculations to ensure a high spatial resolution that is constant throughout the evolution of the system. We show our results on how jets modify the hydrodynamics fields and discuss the implications.

# Jet-induced modifications of the characteristic of the bulk nuclear matter

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that jet energy loss significantly modifies the hydrodynamic fields of bulk nuclear matter, producing observable Mach cone structures in energy density and velocity distributions, but the authors hedge that the simulations assume the jet energy loss is small compared to the jet energy and that the jet itself is not modified by the medium.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with a source term representing energy deposited by jets, using a Cartesian coordinate lattice with constant spatial resolution. The numerical scheme employed 7th order WENO reconstruction with third order Runge-Kutta time integration, implemented on GPUs using NVIDIA CUDA for parallel computation. Jet energy loss was modeled using two mechanisms (gluon radiation and parton collisions) with parameters kappa_rad=4, kappa_coll=2.5, and C_R/C_F=1, assuming the energy loss per unit length is small compared to the jet energy.

## Key numbers

- Simulation grid: 256 x 256 x 256 cells with spatial resolution dx = 0.1 fm and time step dt = 0.02 fm/c
- Initial simulation time: t0 = 1 fm/c
- Equation of state: p = e/3 (ideal gas)
- GPU implementation achieves speedup of more than 2 orders of magnitude compared to equivalent CPU execution
- Energy density and velocity profiles shown at t = 2.4 fm/c in the xy plane at z = 0
- Energy density 1D sections shown at times from 1.2 fm/c to 2.0 fm/c after simulation start

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma evolution, specifically building on the approach of Hirano (2002) for relativistic hydrodynamics with source terms and the WENO scheme of Shu (1998). The study addresses the little-understood effect of jet energy dissipation on bulk nuclear matter properties such as elliptic flow, in contrast to the extensive prior work on jet energy loss as a probe of plasma properties. The authors plan to add more realistic jet energy loss algorithms and freeze-out to study effects on elliptic flow and higher flow harmonics, enabling connection to experimental observables.
