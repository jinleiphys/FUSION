# The nucleon-sigma coupling constant in QCD Sum Rules

> The external-field QCD Sum Rules method is used to evaluate the coupling constant of the light isoscalar-scalar meson (``$σ$'' or ε) to the nucleon. The contributions that come from the excited nucleon states and the response of the continuum threshold to the external field are calculated. # The nucleon-sigma coupling constant in QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim
The external-field QCD Sum Rules method yields a value for the nucleon-sigma coupling constant that is compatible with the large value required in one-boson exchange potential models of the two-nucleon interaction, but the final numerical result depends on an assumed value for the quark-sigma coupling constant taken from the sigma model.

## Method
The authors used the external-field QCD Sum Rules method, evaluating the vacuum-to-vacuum transition matrix element of two-nucleon interpolating fields in an external constant isoscalar-scalar field. They constructed two sum rules from the invariant functions at the structures 1 and q-hat, and selected the more stable sum rule for numerical analysis. The contributions from excited nucleon states (single-pole terms) and the response of the continuum threshold to the external field were computed and included in the sum rule.

## Key numbers
- Ratio of coupling constants from QCD Sum Rules: g_NNsigma / g_q^sigma = 3.9 ± 1.0
- Quark condensate used: a_q = 0.51 ± 0.03 GeV^3
- Scalar susceptibility: chi = -10 ± 1 GeV^{-1}
- Borel window for analysis: 0.8 GeV^2 ≤ M^2 ≤ 1.4 GeV^2
- Continuum threshold s_0^q varied between 2.0, 2.3, and 2.5 GeV^2, giving approximately 6% uncertainty
- Additional 15% uncertainty from allowing chi_G to vary in the range 6 GeV^{-1} ≤ -chi_G ≤ 14 GeV^{-1}
- Final nucleon-sigma coupling constant (assuming g_q^sigma = 3.7 from the sigma model): g_NNsigma = 14.4 ± 3.7
- Comparison value from Nijmegen soft-core NN potential model: g_NNsigma = 16.9

## Context
This work extends the external-field QCD Sum Rules method, previously applied to pion-nucleon couplings (Hwang 1997, Henley 1996) and vector meson couplings to the nucleon (Weng 1997), to the isoscalar-scalar meson coupling. The result is compared to the Nijmegen soft-core potential (Nagels 1978, Stoks 1994), which was originally derived from Regge-pole theory. The authors plan to extend the method to hyperons and the complete scalar-meson nonet to address the SU(3)-flavor structure of scalar-meson coupling constants to the baryon octet.
