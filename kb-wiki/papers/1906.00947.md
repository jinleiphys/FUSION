# Limiting fragmentation as an initial state probe in heavy ion collisions

> We discuss limiting fragmentation within a few currently popular phenomenological models. We show that popular Glauber-inspired models of particle production in heavy ion collisions, such as the two-component model, generally fail to reproduce limiting fragmentation when all energies and system sizes experimentally available are considered. This is due to the energy-dependence of number of participants and number of collisions. We quantify this violation in terms of the model parameters. We also make the same calculation within a Color Glass Condensate scenario and show that the dependence of the saturation scale on the number of participants generally leads to violation of limiting fragmentation. We further argue that wounded parton models, provided the nucleon size and parton density vary predominantly with Bjorken $x$, could in principle reproduce both multiplicity dependence with energy and limiting fragmentation. We suggest, therefore, that an experimental measurement of deviation from limiting fragmentation in heavy ion collisions, for different system sizes and including the experimentally available range of energies, is a powerful test of initial state models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that popular Glauber-inspired models of particle production in heavy ion collisions, such as the two-component model, generally fail to reproduce limiting fragmentation when all experimentally available energies and system sizes are considered, and that this failure is due to the energy dependence of the number of participants and number of collisions. The authors further claim that a wounded parton model, where nucleon size and parton density vary predominantly with Bjorken \(x\), could in principle reproduce both multiplicity dependence with energy and limiting fragmentation.

## Method

The authors calculated the energy dependence of the ratio \(N_{coll}/N_{part}\) within a Glauber model using a parametrization of the inelastic cross-section \(\sigma = A_1 \ln(\sqrt{s}/m_p) + A_2 [\ln(\sqrt{s}/m_p)]^2\) with \(A_1=25\) and \(A_2=0.146\). They performed a \(\chi^2\) fit to mid-rapidity multiplicity data from the LHC to determine the correlation between the two-component model parameters \(f\) (controlling the admixture of \(N_{part}\) vs \(N_{coll}\)) and \(\alpha\) (controlling the energy scaling of multiplicity per ancestor). Using the best-fit relation \(f_{fit}(\alpha)\), they then computed the second derivative of the rapidity distribution \(d^2N/dy^2\) at fixed \(y-y_0=3\) for Gaussian, triangular, and trapezoidal rapidity shapes, and also computed the logarithmic derivative \(\sqrt{s} C^{-1} dC/d\sqrt{s}\) of the limiting fragmentation constant \(C\). The same analysis was extended to a Color Glass Condensate scenario by examining the dependence of the saturation scale on the number of participants.

## Key numbers

* The inelastic cross-section parametrization uses \(A_1=25\) and \(A_2=0.146\), fitted from data with \(\chi^2=0.97\).
* For the top centrality bin of Au-Au, the fit to \(N_{cp}(\sqrt{s}) = T [\ln(\sqrt{s}/m_p)]^2 + G \ln(\sqrt{s}/m_p) + J\) yields \(T=0.043\), \(G=-0.045\), and \(J=2.1\) with \(\chi^2 \sim 10^{-5}\).
* The fit to the \(f-\alpha\) correlation \(f_{fit}(\alpha) = F_1\alpha^4 + F_2\alpha^3 + F_3\alpha^2 + F_4\alpha + F_5\) gives \(F_{1...5} = -250.1, 175.8, -53.6, 9.9, 0.14\) with \(\chi^2 \sim 10^{-2}\).
* The normalization parameter \(\mathcal{N}_0\) is parametrized as \(\mathcal{N}_0 \simeq N_1 \alpha^2 + N_2 \alpha + N_3\) with \(N_{1..3} = -3.35, 1.32, 0.27\) and \(\chi^2 \sim 10^{-3}\).
* The limiting fragmentation constant \(C\) is defined as approximately 0.65, an energy-independent constant.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of limiting fragmentation from RHIC energies (5-200 GeV), where it was observed to hold for all system sizes (Busza, 2004; PHOBOS Collaboration, 2002; Jeon, 2005), to LHC energies (several TeV), where it has not yet been confirmed or falsified experimentally (ALICE, CMS, ATLAS). The paper contradicts the assumption that Glauber-inspired two-component models (Fernando, 2008; core-corona models) can simultaneously fit LHC mid-rapidity multiplicity data and preserve limiting fragmentation. It enables an experimental test: measurement of deviation from limiting fragmentation in heavy ion collisions for different system sizes across the available energy range can discriminate between initial state models, particularly favoring wounded parton models (Bialas, 1976; Bialas, 1977; Bialas, 1978; Bialas, 1979; Bialas, 1980) over Glauber or Color Glass Condensate scenarios.
