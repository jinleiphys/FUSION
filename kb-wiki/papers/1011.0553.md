# Spin and spin-isospin instabilities in asymmetric nuclear matter at zero and finite temperatures using Skyrme functionals

> Self-consistent mean field methods based on phenomenological Skyrme effective interactions are known to exhibit spurious spin and spin-isospin instabilities both at zero and finite temperatures when applied to homogeneous nuclear matter at the densities encountered in neutron stars and in supernova cores. The origin of these instabilities is revisited in the framework of the nuclear energy density functional theory and a simple prescription is proposed to remove them. The stability of several Skyrme parametrizations is reexamined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that spurious spin and spin-isospin instabilities in Skyrme functionals arise primarily from the time-odd terms proportional to the coupling constants \(C_t^T\), and proposes that setting these constants to zero (\(C_t^T = 0\)) removes all such instabilities at both zero and finite temperatures, while also improving consistency with realistic calculations and sum rules.

## Method

The authors analyze the stability of homogeneous asymmetric nuclear matter within the nuclear energy density functional (EDF) framework using Skyrme functionals. They derive general stability criteria from the Landau Fermi-liquid theory and from a direct comparison of the energy densities of polarized and unpolarized states, and then apply these criteria to reexamine several existing Skyrme parametrizations (SGII, SLy4, SkI1–SkI5, SkO, LNS) by computing Landau parameters and critical densities with and without the \(C_t^T\) terms.

## Key numbers

- The constraint of Kutschera and Wójcik (1994) to prevent ferromagnetic collapse of pure neutron matter is \(t_2(1+x_2) \geq 0\), equivalently \(C_0^\tau + C_1^\tau + C_0^T + C_1^T \geq 0\).
- For the Saclay-Lyon force SLy4, the coupling constant \(C_0^T = -17.21\) MeV fm\(^5\); for SkO, \(C_0^T = -220.54\) MeV fm\(^5\).
- The difference in HFB energies with and without the \(J^2\) and \(J_q^2\) terms reaches about 20 MeV for the heaviest nuclei for BSk17 and up to 30 MeV for SkO.
- The empirical range for the Landau parameter \(G_0^\prime\) from Gamow-Teller resonances is \(1.0 \pm 0.1\).
- The numerical bounds for the parameter \(\Xi = (C_0^T + C_1^T) \rho_q (2 M_q^* / \hbar^2)\) that ensure correct entropy behavior are \(\Xi_1 \simeq -0.21\) and \(\Xi_2 \simeq 0.54\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin instabilities in Skyrme forces by Vidaurre et al. (1984), Kutschera and Wójcik (1994), and Margueron et al. (2009), and addresses the anomalous temperature dependence of the critical density found by Rios et al. (2005) and Isayev (2010). The proposed prescription (\(C_t^T = 0\)) is simpler than earlier extensions by Margueron et al. (2009) and Chamel et al. (2009), and it enables a consistent treatment of both homogeneous matter and finite nuclei without spurious phase transitions, though it fails to reproduce the correct splitting of effective masses in polarized matter found in realistic calculations by Bombaci et al. (2006) and Bordbar et al. (2008).
