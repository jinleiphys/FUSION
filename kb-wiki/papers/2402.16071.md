# Four-gluon vertex in collinear kinematics

> To date, the four-gluon vertex is the least explored component of the QCD Lagrangian, mainly due to the vast proliferation of Lorentz and color structures required for its description. In this work we present a nonperturbative study of this vertex, based on the one-loop dressed Schwinger-Dyson equation obtained from the 4PI effective action. A vast simplification is brought about by resorting to ``collinear'' kinematics, where all momenta are parallel to each other, and by appealing to the charge conjugation symmetry in order to eliminate certain color structures. Out of the fifteen form factors that comprise the transversely-projected version of this vertex, two are singled out and studied in detail; the one associated with the classical tensorial structure is moderately suppressed in the infrared regime, while the other diverges logarithmically at the origin. Quite interestingly, both form factors display the property known as ``planar degeneracy'' at a rather high level of accuracy. With these results we construct an effective charge that quantifies the strength of the four-gluon interaction, and compare it with other vertex-derived charges from the gauge sector of QCD.

## Key claim

The four-gluon vertex in collinear kinematics is studied nonperturbatively using the one-loop dressed Schwinger-Dyson equation from the 4PI effective action, but the analysis treats the four-gluon SDE in isolation by using lattice results as inputs for all other dynamical components rather than solving the full coupled system of equations.

## Method

The authors compute the transversely-projected four-gluon vertex in collinear kinematics (all momenta parallel to a single momentum p) by solving its Schwinger-Dyson equation derived from the four-loop 4PI effective action, using lattice inputs for the gluon and ghost propagators and the three-gluon vertex. Charge conjugation symmetry is employed to eliminate color structures of the type f^{abx}d^{cdx}, reducing the independent color tensors to five. The SDE is projected onto the first three form factors G1, G2, and G3 of a 15-element basis, exploiting the orthogonality of these three tensors to the remaining basis elements.

## Key numbers

- The form factor G1 is moderately suppressed in the infrared, reaching a finite value at the origin (tree-level value is G1^(0) = 1)
- G3 displays a logarithmic divergence in the deep infrared, originating from ghost loops
- For momenta below 1 GeV, results for G1 are compatible with planar degeneracy to within 1.7%
- For momenta below 1.5 GeV, the effective charges satisfy the hierarchy alpha_3g(p) < alpha_4g(p) < alpha_cg(p)
- The projector for G1 is P1 = (7 t1 - 3 t2)/19440, for G2 is P2 = (3 t2 - 2 t1)/12960, and for G3 is P3 = t3/10800, all valid for SU(3)

## Context

This work extends nonperturbative studies of QCD correlation functions, particularly following recent demonstrations of planar degeneracy in the three-gluon vertex by Pinto-Gomez (2022) and Aguilar (2023), where form factors are the same for all momentum configurations on a given plane with less than 10% error. The results enable comparison with ongoing lattice simulations of the four-gluon vertex by Catumba (2021) and Colaco (2023, 2024), and the effective charge hierarchy agrees with earlier continuum results from Cyrol (2014) and Huber (2018). The study is restricted to quenched QCD (no dynamical quarks) and to collinear kinematics, which simplifies the tensorial structure from the general case to only 15 form factors.
