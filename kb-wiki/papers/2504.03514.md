# Quenching through the QCD chiral phase transition

> We present a detailed numerical and analytical study of the out-of-equilibrium dynamics of Model G, the dynamical universality class relevant to the chiral phase transition. We perform numerical 3D stochastic (Langevin) simulations of the $O(4)$ critical point for large lattices in the chiral limit. We quench the system from the high-temperature unbroken phase to the broken phase and study the non-equilibrium dynamics of pion fields. Strikingly, the non-equilibrium evolution of the two-point functions exhibits a regime of growth, a parametrically large enhancement, and a subsequent slow relaxation to equilibrium. We analyze our numerical results using dynamic critical scaling and mean-field theory. The growth of the two point functions is determined by the non-linear dynamics of an ideal non-abelian superfluid, which is a limit of Model G that reflects the broken chiral symmetry. We also relate the non-equilibrium two-point functions to a long-lived parametric enhancement of soft pion yields relative to thermal equilibrium following a quench.

## Key claim

The out-of-equilibrium evolution of the two-point functions following a quench through the chiral phase transition exhibits a regime of growth, a parametrically large enhancement, and a subsequent slow relaxation to equilibrium, which the authors relate to a long-lived parametric enhancement of soft pion yields relative to thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors performed numerical 3D stochastic (Langevin) simulations of Model G, the dynamical universality class relevant to the chiral phase transition, using an O(4) symmetric scalar field coupled to conserved charges on large lattices in the chiral limit. They instantaneously quenched the system from the high-temperature unbroken phase to the broken phase and studied the non-equilibrium dynamics of pion fields, analyzing the results using dynamic critical scaling and mean-field theory.

## Key numbers

- The O(4) model with λ=4 and H=0 has a second order phase transition at m_c^2 = -4.8110(4)
- The static critical exponents used are ν = 0.7377(41), η = 0.0302(16), and β = 0.380(2)
- The dynamical critical exponent ζ = d/2 = 1.5 for three spatial dimensions
- The isospin susceptibility χ_0 = 5, kinetic coefficients Γ_0 = 1 and D_0 = 1/3 in simulation units
- The time step used is Δt = 0.24 a^2/Γ_0 with six dissipative substeps of 0.04 a^2/Γ_0 each

## Context

This work extends previous equilibrium studies of Model G by Florio et al. (2021, 2023) and Schlichting et al. (2019) into the out-of-equilibrium regime, providing the first numerical investigation of quench dynamics in this universality class relevant to the QCD chiral phase transition. The results connect to earlier proposals of disoriented chiral condensate formation by Rajagopal (1993), Blaizot (1992), and Bjorken (1993), and may help explain the ~50% enhancement of pions below p_T < 0.5 GeV observed by the ALICE experiment in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV, which current hydrodynamic models cannot account for.
