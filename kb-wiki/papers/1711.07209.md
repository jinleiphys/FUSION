# Borromean Feshbach resonance in 11Li studied via 11Li(p,p')

> A dipole resonance of 11Li is newly found by a 9Li + n + n three-body model analysis with the complex-scaling method. The resonance can be interpreted as a bound state in the 10Li + n system, that is, a Feshbach resonance in the 9Li + n + n system. As a characteristic feature of the Feshbach resonance of 11Li, the 10Li + n threshold is open above the 9Li + n + n one, which reflects a distinctive property of the Borromean system. A microscopic four-body reaction calculation for the 11Li(p,p') reaction at 6 MeV/nucleon is performed by taking into account the resonance and nonresonant continuum states of the three-body system. The angular distribution of the elastic and inelastic scattering as well as the breakup energy spectrum recently observed are reproduced well.

## Key claim

The authors claim to have found a dipole resonance in 11Li at 0.42 MeV with a width of 0.28 MeV, which they interpret as a "Borromean Feshbach resonance", a bound state in the 10Li + n system that exists because the 10Li + n threshold lies above the 9Li + n + n three-body threshold, a condition unique to Borromean nuclei. The hedge is that the resonance's existence is inferred from a three-body model and validated by reproducing experimental data, but the correspondence with the E1 strength distribution from Coulomb dissociation is "less clear" and "not so conclusive."

## Method

The authors used a 9Li + n + n three-body model with complex-scaling method (CSM) to identify resonant and nonresonant continuum states of 11Li. They then performed microscopic four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations for the 11Li(p,p') reaction at 6 MeV/nucleon, using the Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux (JLM) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with a single adjustable normalization factor N_I = 0.55 for the imaginary part. The three-body wave functions were obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian with a phenomenological three-body force (3BF) whose strength V_3 = -11 MeV was tuned to reproduce the ground state energy of 11Li.

## Key numbers

*   Resonant energy of the dipole resonance in 11Li: ε_R = 0.42 MeV, with half-width Γ/2 = 0.14 MeV (total width Γ = 0.28 MeV), determined from CSM analysis.
*   Ground state energy of 11Li: ε_0 = -0.369 MeV, used to optimize the three-body force strength V_3.
*   Resonant energy (decay width) of the 10Li resonance in the 0p_{1/2} state: 0.46 MeV (0.36 MeV), from the adopted 9Li-n interaction.
*   Breakup cross section integration range: ε from 0 MeV to 1.13 MeV for the angular distribution, and θ_c.m. from 115° to 124° for the energy spectrum.
*   Scaling angle in CSM: θ_c = 20°.
*   Model space for CDCC: states with ε < 5 MeV, yielding 93, 111, and 131 states for I^π = 0^+, 1^-, and 2^+, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 11Li resonances that had yielded contradictory results between hadronic scattering and Coulomb dissociation experiments (Nakamura 2006). The authors specifically address the longstanding open question of an excited state of 11Li (Iek93, Shi95, Zin97, Kor97a, Kor97b, Kan15, Cre02, Gar03, Ers04, Pin12, Hag05, Myo08, Kik13). The finding of a Borromean Feshbach resonance provides a non-contradictory understanding of both the hadronic scattering data (Tanaka 2017) and the E1 strength distribution from Coulomb dissociation (Nakamura 2006). The work enables future studies of continuum structure in Borromean nuclei and calls for more information on the n-9Li interaction and inclusion of 9Li core excitation.
