# Off-shell Chiral Dynamics in the $Λ(1405)$ Resonance and $K^-p$ Femtoscopic Correlations

> We present the first systematic investigation of the $S=-1$ meson--baryon interaction within a fully off-shell covariant unitarized chiral effective field theory framework up to next-to-leading order. In particular, we perform a detailed comparison with the widely used on-shell approximation. We find that the resulting scattering observables are very similar, thereby confirming the validity of key results obtained within the on-shell scheme. A notable advantage of the off-shell treatment, however, is the absence of unphysical left-hand cuts induced by the on-shell approximation. Employing the off-shell amplitudes, we compute the femtoscopic correlation functions for $K^-p$ and $π^\pmΣ^\mp$ pairs. The $K^-p$ correlation functions are found to be consistent with previously published results based on the on-shell approximation, with marginal differences attributed to slight variations in the descriptions of the scattering data. The $π^\pmΣ^\mp$ correlation functions are predicted for the first time, and are expected to provide valuable constraints on the nature of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance and the coupled-channel chiral dynamics of the $K^-p$ system.

# Off-shell Chiral Dynamics in the Λ(1405) Resonance and K⁻p Femtoscopic Correlations

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully off-shell covariant unitarized chiral effective field theory calculation of the S=-1 meson-baryon interaction up to next-to-leading order, finding that scattering observables are very similar to the widely used on-shell approximation, thereby confirming the validity of key results obtained within the on-shell scheme, though the off-shell treatment avoids unphysical left-hand cuts induced by the on-shell approximation.

## Method

The authors derived perturbative Feynman amplitudes within chiral perturbation theory as the interaction kernel and numerically solved the three-dimensionally reduced Bethe-Salpeter integral equation in the off-shell scheme to obtain non-perturbative scattering amplitudes. Low-energy constants and physical cutoffs were determined by a global fit to experimental data including total cross sections for seven final states, threshold branch ratios, and the K⁻p scattering length. Using the so-obtained interactions, they computed two-particle K⁻p and π±Σ∓ femtoscopic correlation functions using both on-shell and off-shell formulations.

## Key numbers

- NLO fit χ²/d.o.f. = 1.98 (off-shell), compared to WT-only χ²/d.o.f. = 4.11
- K⁻p scattering length at NLO: a = (-0.61 ± 0.08) + i(0.99 ± 0.10) fm, consistent with experimental value (-0.65 ± 0.10) + i(0.81 ± 0.15) fm
- Threshold ratio γ = 2.36 ± 0.24 at NLO, matching experimental value 2.36 ± 0.04
- Threshold ratio Rc = 0.658 ± 0.063 at NLO, consistent with experimental 0.664 ± 0.011
- Threshold ratio Rn = 0.184 ± 0.023 at NLO, consistent with experimental 0.189 ± 0.015
- Λ(1380) pole position at NLO: (1375 ± 3) - i(50 ± 1) MeV
- Λ(1405) pole position at NLO: (1433 ± 1) - i(28 ± 2) MeV
- Λ(1380) coupling to πΣ: |g_πΣ| = 7.52 ± 0.66 GeV at NLO
- Λ(1405) coupling to K̄N: |g_K̄N| = 8.15 ± 0.45 GeV at NLO

## Context

This work extends the unitarized chiral effective field theory framework for S=-1 meson-baryon interactions by moving beyond the on-shell approximation used in prior studies by Oset (1997), Jido (2003), Borasoy (2005), Ikeda (2012), and Guo (2012). The off-shell treatment addresses the known deficiency of unphysical left-hand cuts in on-shell schemes, as previously noted by Pittler (2025) and Borasoy (2005). The computation of π±Σ∓ femtoscopic correlation functions for the first time provides new predictions that can be tested against LHC data from ALICE, complementing the K⁻p correlation measurements reported by ALICE (2019, 2022) and the theoretical work by Kamiya (2019) and Encarnacion (2024).
