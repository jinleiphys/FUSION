# Non-perturbative chiral approach to Kp -->gamma Y reactions

> A recently developed nonperturbative chiral approach to describe the S=-1 meson-baryon reactions has been extended to investigate the near threshold $K^- p \to γΛ, γΣ^0$ reactions. With the parameters governed by chiral SU(3) symmetry, we show that the predicted branching ratios $Γ_{K^- p \to γΛ}/Γ_{K^- p \to all}$ and $Γ_{K^-p\to γΣ^0}/Γ_{K^- p \to all}$ are close to the experimental values. The coupling with the $η$ channels, which was shown to be important in the $S=-1$ meson-baryon reactions, is also found to be significant here. Our results are consistent with the interpretation of the $Λ(1405)$ as a quasi-bound meson-baryon state as found in other similar chiral approaches.

## Key claim

The authors extend a nonperturbative chiral unitary coupled-channel approach, previously developed for S=-1 meson-baryon strong interactions, to predict branching ratios for K- p → γΛ and γΣ0 near threshold, finding that the results are close to experimental values without any adjustable parameters beyond those already fixed by the strong-interaction data. The key hedge is that the predicted branching ratio for γΛ production is about 50% larger than the experimental central value, though within experimental uncertainty for γΣ0, and that moderate breakings of SU(3) beyond those implemented by different particle masses may still be needed.

## Method

The authors start from a Bethe-Salpeter equation for the meson-baryon scattering amplitude, using the lowest-order SU(3) chiral Lagrangian as the driving term. They apply a simplification that keeps only the positive-energy component of the baryon propagator and factorize the strong amplitude on-shell, regularizing loop integrals with a cutoff qmax = 630 MeV. For the photoproduction amplitude, they include contact terms, meson-exchange terms, and baryon-pole terms from the chiral Lagrangian with minimal electromagnetic coupling, then use the same on-shell factorization and heavy-baryon approximation to compute the K- p → γY transition amplitude, with the strong final-state interactions included via the coupled-channel T-matrix from the earlier work of Oset et al. (1997).

## Key numbers

*   Cutoff parameter qmax = 630 MeV used to regularize loop integrals, fixed from the strong-interaction study of Oset et al. (1997).
*   Overall SU(3) coupling strength f = 1.15 Fπ, where Fπ = 93 MeV, an approximate average between fπ and fK.
*   Predicted branching ratio BK- p → γΛ = 1.56 (with π channels included) compared to experimental value 0.86 ± 0.16.
*   Predicted branching ratio BK- p → γΣ0 = 1.28 (with π channels included) compared to experimental value 1.48 ± 0.30.
*   Without initial meson-baryon interactions, the γΣ0 production branching ratio is 0.14 and the ratio between the two production rates is an order of magnitude larger than the data.
*   The second-pole term from the meson-exchange diagram changes the γΣ0 branching ratio by about 40%.
*   Omitting the η channels in the strong amplitudes increases the predicted γΛ branching ratio by about 60%.

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative chiral unitary approach of Oset et al. (1997) for S=-1 meson-baryon reactions to photoproduction processes, following earlier studies of K- p → γY reactions by Siegel and Saghai (1995) who used phenomenological separable potentials and needed 30-50% deviations from SU(3) coupling constants. The results support the interpretation of the Λ(1405) resonance as a dynamically generated quasi-bound meson-baryon state, consistent with the findings of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and Oset et al. (1997). The approach enables further tests of chiral SU(3) symmetry in electromagnetic reactions and can be extended to study other photoproduction processes in the strangeness sector.
