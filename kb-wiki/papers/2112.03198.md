# Octet and decuplet baryon $σ$-terms and mass decompositions

> We present a comprehensive analysis of the SU(3) octet and decuplet baryon masses and $σ$-terms using high-precision lattice QCD data and chiral SU(3) effective theory with finite range regularization. The effects of various systematic uncertainties, including from the scale setting of the lattice data and the regularization prescriptions, are quantified. We find the pion-nucleon and strange nucleon $σ$-terms to be $σ_{πN} = 44(3)(3)$ MeV and $σ_{N s} = 50(6)(1)$ MeV, respectively. The results provide constraints on the energy-momentum tensor mass decompositions of the SU(3) octet and decuplet baryons, where we find the trace anomaly and quark/gluon energies decrease for strange baryons due to their larger strange $σ$-terms.

## Key claim

The paper extracts octet and decuplet baryon sigma-terms from lattice QCD data using chiral SU(3) effective theory with finite range regularization, finding that the results are stable across different scale-setting schemes when both PACS-CS and QCDSF-UKQCD data are fitted together with appropriate cuts; the authors cannot reconcile their pion-nucleon sigma-term with the larger values (around 60 MeV) from pionic atom experiments.

## Method

The authors performed global fits of octet and decuplet baryon masses from PACS-CS (Aoki 2008) and QCDSF-UKQCD (Bietenholz 2011) lattice data using a relativistic chiral SU(3) effective theory with finite range regularization, applying the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to extract sigma-terms from the quark-mass dependence of the fitted masses. They compared mass-independent and mass-dependent lattice scale-setting schemes, applied finite-volume corrections, and excluded data at pion masses above 0.25 GeV² and from smaller lattice volumes. The fits used shared chiral SU(3) parameters for octet and decuplet baryons separately, with distinct cutoff parameters for each baryon.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon sigma-term: σ_πN = 44(3)(3)(4) MeV (first uncertainty statistical, second systematic from scale-setting, third theoretical from higher-order chiral corrections)
- Strange nucleon sigma-term: σ_Ns = 50(6)(1)(10) MeV
- For the MILS scheme alone: σ_πN = 46(3) MeV, σ_Ns = 49(8) MeV
- For the MDLS scheme alone: σ_πN = 41(4) MeV, σ_Ns = 50(8) MeV
- Octet fit quality: χ²/dof = 0.78 (MILS) and 0.89 (MDLS)
- Decuplet fit quality: χ²/dof = 2.7 (MILS) and 3.3 (MDLS)
- Average octet mass used in self-energies: M_B = 1142 MeV; average decuplet mass: M_T = 1455 MeV; octet-decuplet mass difference: 313 MeV
- Coupling constants: D = 0.85, F = 0.41, g_A = 1.26, pseudoscalar decay constant f_φ = 93 MeV
- Lattice spacings: a = 0.0907 fm (PACS-CS), a = 0.075 fm (QCDSF-UKQCD); Sommer scale r_0 = 0.4921 fm (PACS-CS)

## Context

This work extends chiral extrapolation analyses of baryon sigma-terms by Shanahan et al. (2012, 2013) and Young and Thomas (2010), who used similar FRR methods but found larger scale-setting discrepancies for σ_Ns (59(6) MeV MILS vs 21(6) MeV MDLS). The results differ from covariant SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory with dimensional regularization by Camalich et al. (2010) (σ_πN = 59(2)(17) MeV, σ_Ns = -4(23)(25) MeV) and Ren et al. (2015) (σ_πN = 55(1)(4) MeV, σ_Ns = 27(27)(4) MeV), and from the QCDSF-UKQCD collaboration's own extrapolation (σ_Nl = 31(3)(4) MeV). The results enable improved baryon mass decompositions via the energy-momentum tensor, showing that trace anomaly and quark/gluon energy contributions decrease for strange baryons, and provide inputs for dark matter models coupling to scalar quark bilinears.
