# Nuclear response theory with multiphonon coupling in a covariant framework

> Background: Nuclear excited states within a wide range of excitation energies are formally described by the linear response theory. Besides its conventional formulation within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) representing excited states as two correlated quasiparticles (2q), there exist extensions for 4q configurations. Such extended approaches are quite successful in the description of gross properties of nuclear spectra, however, accounting for many of their fine features requires further extension of the configuration space. Purpose: This work aims at the development of an approach which is capable of such an extension as well as of reproducing and predicting fine spectral properties, which are of special interest at low energies. Method: The method is based on the covariant density functional theory and time blocking approximation, which is extended for couplings between quasiparticles and multiphonon excitations. Results: The covariant multiphonon response theory is developed and adopted for nuclear structure calculations in medium-mass and heavy nuclei. The equations are formulated in both general and coupled forms in the spherical basis. Conclusions: The developed covariant multiphonon response theory represents a new generation of the approaches to nuclear response, which aims at a unified description of both high-frequency collective states and low-energy spectroscopy in medium-mass and heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper develops a covariant multiphonon response theory that extends the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) to include higher-order quasiparticle-vibration coupling effects beyond the two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) configuration level, aiming for a unified description of both high-frequency collective states and low-energy spectroscopy in medium-mass and heavy nuclei.

## Method

The method is based on covariant density functional theory and the time blocking approximation, extended to include couplings between quasiparticles and multiphonon excitations. The approach formulates the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the nuclear response function in the Dirac-Hartree-BCS basis, where the effective interaction contains both a static part from the covariant energy density functional and a dynamical part from quasiparticle-vibration coupling. Higher-order correlations are introduced iteratively by replacing the uncorrelated two-quasiparticle propagator in the phonon coupling amplitude with a correlated one, generating a hierarchy of configuration spaces from 2q⊗phonon (2p2h) up to 3p3h and beyond.

## Key numbers

- The phonon coupling vertices satisfy the condition γ^{η1η2}_{μ;k1k2}/Ωμ ≪ 1 in most physical cases, justifying the perturbative treatment of the first-order correction to the effective interaction.
- The uncorrelated two-quasiparticle propagator is given by R̃^{(0)η}_{k1k2}(ω) = 1/(ηω - Ek1 - Ek2), where Ek are Bogoliubov energies from the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Hamiltonian.
- The phonon energies Ωμ and quantum numbers μ = {Ωμ, Jμ, Mμ, πμ} characterize the collective vibrational states coupled to quasiparticles.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) developed by Litvinova and collaborators (2008) and its two-phonon version RQTBA-2 (2010, 2013), which were limited to 2p2h configurations. The approach builds on the quasiparticle-vibration coupling concept from Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and the time blocking approximation for superfluid Fermi systems by Tselyaev (2007). The new formalism enables systematic inclusion of multiphonon coupling effects to resolve fine spectral features that previous approaches could not capture, particularly in the low-energy region below the neutron threshold of medium-mass even-even nuclei.
