# Hyperfine Structure Constants for Eu Isotopes: Is The Empirical Formula of HFS Anomaly Universal ?

> We calculate the hyperfine structure constant for the Eu isotopes with shell model wave functions. The calculated results are compared with those predicted by the Moskowitz-Lombardi (M-L) empirical formula. It turns out that the two approaches give the very different behaviors of the hfs constants in the isotope dependence. This should be easily measured by experiment, which may lead to the universality check of the M-L formula.

# Hyperfine Structure Constants for Eu Isotopes: Is The Empirical Formula of HFS Anomaly Universal?

## Key claim

The Moskowitz-Lombardi (M-L) empirical formula predicts a sharp transition in the isotope shift of the hyperfine structure anomaly at A=153 for Eu isotopes, while shell model calculations including both Delta l=0 and Delta l=2 core polarizations show a smooth behavior with no sudden change, provided one introduces a "magnetic absorbing state" (MAS) with vanishing magnetic moment to account for the observed drop in magnetic moment at A=153.

## Method

The authors calculated the magnetic hyperfine structure (hfs) anomaly for Eu isotopes (A=145 to 159) using shell model wave functions with a proton hole in the 2d5/2 orbit and valence neutrons outside the N=82 magic core. They included Delta l=0 core polarization without free parameters and Delta l=2 core polarization by diagonalizing a delta-function residual interaction in a three-state basis (2f7/2, 1h9/2, 2f5/2 neutron orbits), and introduced a collective MAS state for A>=153 to reproduce the observed magnetic moments. The results were compared with predictions from the Moskowitz-Lombardi empirical formula epsilon = alpha/mu, with alpha = -0.005 n.m. for proton orbits after an atomic correction factor C = b(Eu)/b(Hg) approximately 0.5.

## Key numbers

- The Delta l=0 core polarization calculation gives hfs anomaly -epsilon = 1.067% for Eu-145, decreasing to 0.893% for Eu-159, with isotope shifts Delta_{145,A} ranging from 0% (A=145) to -0.174% (A=159)
- The full calculation including both Delta l=0 and Delta l=2 core polarizations gives -epsilon = 1.067% for Eu-145 and 1.019% for Eu-159, with isotope shifts Delta_{145,A} from 0% to -0.048%
- The M-L formula predicts -epsilon = 0.125% for Eu-145 and 0.36% for Eu-159, with isotope shifts Delta_{145,A} from 0% to 0.24%
- The Delta l=2 core polarization contribution to the hfs anomaly for Eu-147 is approximately epsilon^{Delta l=2} approximately 0.01%
- The magnetic moment drops from mu = 3.472 n.m. for Eu-151 to mu = 1.533 n.m. for Eu-153
- The atomic correction factor for applying the M-L formula from Hg to Eu is C = b(Eu)/b(Hg) approximately 0.5, giving alpha = -0.005 n.m. for proton orbits

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic hyperfine structure anomalies from Hg isotopes, where the Moskowitz-Lombardi formula (Moskowitz and Lombardi, 1973) successfully describes isotope shifts, to Eu isotopes where the magnetic moment shows a dramatic change at A=153. The shell model approach follows the formalism of Fujita and Arima (1975) for core polarization effects. The authors note that ion trap methods (Werth et al., 1996) can measure hfs separations with accuracy of 10^-8 or better, making experimental tests feasible. The results challenge the universality of the M-L empirical formula and suggest that measurements of Eu isotope shifts could distinguish between the shell model and empirical predictions.
