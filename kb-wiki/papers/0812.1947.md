# Relativistic QRPA calculation of muon capture rates

> The relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) is applied in the calculation of total muon capture rates on a large set of nuclei from $^{12}$C to $^{244}$Pu, for which experimental values are available. The microscopic theoretical framework is based on the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model for the nuclear ground state, and transitions to excited states are calculated using the PN-RQRPA. The calculation is fully consistent, i.e., the same interactions are used both in the RHB equations that determine the quasiparticle basis, and in the matrix equations of the PN-RQRPA. The calculated capture rates are sensitive to the in-medium quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant. By reducing this constant from its free-nucleon value $g_A = 1.262$ by 10% for all multipole transitions, the calculation reproduces the experimental muon capture rates to better than 10% accuracy.

## Key claim
The relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) reproduces experimental muon capture rates to better than 10% accuracy, but only when the free-nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A = 1.262\) is reduced by 10% to \(g_A = 1.135\) for all multipole transitions, not just the Gamow-Teller channel.

## Method
The nuclear ground state was described using the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the DD-ME2 effective interaction in the particle-hole channel and the Gogny D1S interaction in the pairing channel. Excited states in the final odd-odd nucleus were calculated using the proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA), with the same interactions used consistently in both the ground-state and excitation calculations. The muon 1s wave function was obtained by solving the Dirac equation in coordinate space using B-spline finite elements with the self-consistent ground-state charge density as the Coulomb source.

## Key numbers
- The free-nucleon axial-vector coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.262\); the effective value used to reproduce data is \(g_A = 1.135\), a reduction of 10%.
- With the 10% quenching applied to all multipole operators, the mean deviation between theoretical and experimental total muon capture rates is 6%.
- The ratio of calculated to experimental capture rates with free-nucleon form factors is approximately 1.2 to 1.4 across the nuclide set.
- For point-charge Coulomb potentials (rather than self-consistent finite charge densities), the ratio \(\omega_{\rm calc.} / \omega_{\rm exp.}\) reaches \(\geq 4\) for the heaviest nuclei.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter \(g' = 0.52\) was adjusted to the Gamow-Teller resonance excitation energy in \(^{208}\)Pb and kept constant for all nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous fully consistent RHB+RQRPA framework, previously applied to beta-decay half-lives (Niksic 2005, Marketin 2006) and neutrino-nucleus reactions (Paar 2008), to the calculation of muon capture rates. It directly parallels the nonrelativistic RPA study by Zinner, Langanke and Vogel (2006), which used a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential and A-dependent residual interactions to reproduce experimental rates to better than 15% without quenching. The present fully consistent approach achieves slightly better average accuracy (6% mean deviation) but requires a uniform 10% quenching of the axial-vector coupling in all multipole channels, whereas the earlier work quenched only the true Gamow-Teller \(0\hbar\omega\) strength. The model enables extension to other weak interaction processes (electron capture, neutrino-nucleus reactions) and to nuclei far from stability.
