# Three-body calculations of beta decay applied to $^{11}$Li

> A novel practical few-body method is formulated to include isospin symmetry for nuclear halo structures. The method is designed to describe beta decay, where the basic concept of isospin symmetry facilitates a proper understanding. Both isobaric analogue and anti-analogue states are treated. We derive general and explicit formulas for three-body systems using hyperspherical coordinates. The example of the beta decaying $^{11}$Li ($^{9}$Li+$n$+$n$) is chosen as a challenging application for numerical calculations of practical interest. The detailed results are compared to existing experimental data and good agreement is found at high excitation energies, where the isobaric analogue and anti-analogue states are situated in the daughter nucleus. An interpretation of the decay pattern at lower excitation energies is suggested. Decays of the $^{9}$Li-core and the two halo-neutrons are individually treated and combined to the daughter system with almost unique isospin, which we predict to be broken by about $0.4\%$ probability. Properties of decay products are predicted as possible future tests of this model.

## Key claim

The authors formulate a novel few-body method incorporating isospin symmetry to describe beta decay of halo nuclei, applied to $^{11}$Li, and find good agreement with experimental data at high excitation energies where the isobaric analogue and anti-analogue states reside, while only suggesting an interpretation for the decay pattern at lower excitation energies rather than reproducing it quantitatively.

## Method

The authors use the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method to solve the three-body problem for $^{11}$Li described as a $^{9}$Li core plus two halo neutrons, extending the formalism to include isospin degrees of freedom explicitly. They compute the initial $^{11}$Li ground state wave function and final $^{11}$Be states (ground-state-like, isobaric analogue state, and anti-analogue state) using the same three-body framework with adjusted interactions, then act on the initial wave function with Fermi and Gamow-Teller beta decay operators split into core and halo components. Continuum states are treated by imposing box boundary conditions and discretizing the continuum, with the differential strength obtained by replacing delta functions with Gaussians of width 0.1 to 0.3 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Isospin breaking in the daughter system is predicted to be about $0.4\%$ probability.
- The Gaussian width $\sigma$ used to smooth the differential strength function ranges within $0.1$ MeV and $0.3$ MeV.
- The distance between discrete continuum states in the calculations ranges from $0.05$ MeV to $0.1$ MeV.
- For the halo decay, the Gamow-Teller sum rule gives a total strength of $6$.
- The IAS and AAS isospin wave functions for $^{11}$Be contain mixing coefficients of $\sqrt{2/5}$ and $\sqrt{3/5}$ for the two configurations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier three-body treatment of the $T=5/2$ isospin multiplet members $^{11}$Li and $^{11}$O (Garrido 2020) to intermediate members such as the IAS and AAS in $^{11}$Be. The method builds on the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion approach (Nielsen 2001) and the core-halo factorization approximation from Nilsson (2000) and Jonson (2004). The calculations are compared to experimental data on $^{11}$Li beta decay reviewed by Riisager (2022), and the results enable future predictions of decay product properties that can test the model experimentally.
