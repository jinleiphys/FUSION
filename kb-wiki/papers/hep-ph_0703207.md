# Jets from Massive Unstable Particles: Top-Mass Determination

> We construct jet observables for energetic top quarks that can be used to determine a short distance top quark mass from reconstruction in e+ e- collisions with accuracy better than Lambda_{QCD}. Using a sequence of effective field theories we connect the production energy, mass, and top width scales, Q>> m>> Gamma, for the top jet cross section, and derive a QCD factorization theorem for the top invariant mass spectrum. Our analysis accounts for: alpha_s corrections from the production and mass scales, corrections due to constraints in defining invariant masses, non-perturbative corrections from the cross-talk between the jets, and alpha_s corrections to the Breit-Wigner line-shape. This paper mainly focuses on deriving the factorization theorem for hemisphere invariant mass distributions and other event shapes in e+e- collisions applicable at a future Linear Collider. We show that the invariant mass distribution is not a simple Breit-Wigner involving the top width. Even at leading order it is shifted and broadened by non-perturbative soft QCD effects. We predict that the invariant mass peak position increases linearly with Q/m due to these non-perturbative effects. They are encoded in terms of a universal soft function that also describes soft effects for massless dijet events. In a future paper we compute alpha_s corrections to the jet invariant mass spectrum, including a summation of large logarithms between the scales Q, m and Gamma.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the top invariant mass distribution from reconstruction in $e^+e^-$ collisions is not a simple Breit-Wigner function involving the top width; even at leading order it is shifted and broadened by non-perturbative soft QCD effects, and the peak position increases linearly with $Q/m$ due to these effects, which are encoded in a universal soft function that also describes soft effects for massless dijet events.

## Method

The authors use a sequence of effective field theories (EFTs) to connect the three disparate scales $Q \gg m \gg \Gamma$ for top jet production in $e^+e^-$ collisions: starting from QCD, they match onto Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) with a massive top quark, and then onto boosted Heavy-Quark Effective Theory (bHQET) for the unstable top quarks near mass shell. They derive a QCD factorization theorem for the double differential hemisphere invariant mass distribution $d^2\sigma/dM_t^2 dM_{\bar t}^2$ in the peak region where $M_t^2 - m^2 \sim m\Gamma$, separating the cross section into hard matching coefficients $H_Q$ and $H_m$, jet functions $B_\pm$ describing collinear radiation and top decay, and a universal soft function $S_{\rm hemi}$ describing non-perturbative cross-talk between the jets.

## Key numbers

- Top quark mass from Tevatron: $m = 171.4 \pm 2.1$ GeV (combined measurement)
- Top width: $\Gamma \approx 1.5$ GeV (from $\tau = 1/\Gamma$)
- Ratio $\Gamma/m \approx 1/120$, giving $\ln^2(\Gamma/m) \approx 25$ for large logarithms
- Center-of-mass energy range considered: $Q \sim 0.5$ to $1$ TeV for a future Linear Collider
- SCET expansion parameter: $\lambda \sim m/Q \sim 0.2$ to $0.3$
- Expected precision at LHC: uncertainties at the level of $1$ GeV for top mass measurements
- Expected precision at ILC from threshold scan: systematic uncertainties at the level of $100$ MeV

## Context

This work provides the first analytic framework for systematically describing perturbative and non-perturbative aspects of top quark invariant mass distributions from reconstruction, extending prior work on massless dijet event shapes by Korchemsky (1998, 1999), Bauer (2002), and Lee (2006). It contrasts with the threshold scan method using NRQCD by Hoang (2000, 2001, 2004) and Pineda (2006), which achieves high precision at $Q \approx 2m$ but cannot be used at the LHC due to insufficient invariant mass resolution. The factorization theorem enables definition of a short-distance "jet-mass" scheme that can be determined with precision better than $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$, and opens the possibility to systematically construct top mass observables where non-perturbative effects are suppressed, with future applications to LHC physics and processes involving other unstable colored particles.
