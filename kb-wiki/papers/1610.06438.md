# A quarksonic matter at high isospin density

> Analogous to the quarkyonic matter at high baryon density in which the quark Fermi seas and the baryonic excitations coexist, it is argued that a "quarksonic matter" phase appears at high isospin density where the quark (antiquark) Fermi seas and the mesonic excitations coexist. We explore this phase in detail in both large $N_c$ and asymptotically free limits: In large $N_c$ limit, we sketch a phase diagram for the quarksonic matter. In the asymptotically free limit, we study the pion superfluidity and thermodynamics of the quarksonic matter by using both perturbative calculations and effective model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a "quarksonic matter" phase exists at high isospin density, where quark (antiquark) Fermi seas coexist with confined mesonic excitations, analogous to quarkyonic matter at high baryon density. The authors qualify that this phase appears specifically in the large \(N_c\) limit and the asymptotically free limit, and that in real QCD with \(N_c=3\) the region of quarksonic matter shrinks because the deconfinement temperature decreases with increasing isospin chemical potential.

## Method

The authors analyze the quarksonic matter phase using two complementary approaches. In the large \(N_c\) limit, they sketch a phase diagram in the temperature-isospin chemical potential plane, arguing that the deconfinement temperature is independent of isospin chemical potential and that the quarksonic matter occupies the large \(|\mu_I|\), low-temperature region below \(T_d\). In the asymptotically free limit, they perform perturbative QCD calculations to derive the coefficient \(b_\pi\) governing the pion condensate gap up to sub-leading order in the coupling constant, and then use an effective pure gluodynamics formalism combined with a Polyakov-loop potential to compute the isospin density and heat capacity.

## Key numbers

- The pion condensate gap is given by \(\Delta = b_\pi |g|^{-5} |\mu_I| e^{-\pi/(2\sqrt{2}\bar{g})}\) with \(b_\pi = 256\pi^4 e^{-(4+\pi^2)/16}\), where \(\bar{g} = g/(3\sqrt{2}\pi)\) and \(g\) is the QCD coupling constant.
- The coefficient \(b_\pi\) is less suppressed by a factor \(e^{(4+\pi^2)/16} \approx 2.38\) compared to the analogous coefficient in color superconductivity.
- For \(|\mu_I| > 5\) GeV, the ratio of isospin density to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit \(R_n \approx 1\), indicating the system is well described by a free quark (antiquark) gas.
- The deconfinement temperatures for the numerical calculations are \(T_d = 46.19\) MeV at \(|\mu_I| = 8\) GeV and \(T_d = 54.64\) MeV at \(|\mu_I| = 7\) GeV.
- The heat capacity from the Goldstone mode at low temperature is \(c_\upsilon/T^3 = 2\pi^2/(15\upsilon^3)\) with \(\upsilon \approx 1/\sqrt{3}\) the speed of sound, independent of \(|\mu_I|\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of quarkyonic matter, originally proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) for high baryon density, to the isospin sector, introducing the term "quarksonic matter." It builds on prior studies of pion superfluidity at large isospin chemical potential by Son and Stephanov (2000, 2001) and by He, Jin, and Zhuang (2005), as well as lattice QCD simulations by Kogut, Sinclair, and collaborators (2002, 2004) and Bali et al. (2016). The perturbative calculation of the pion condensate coefficient follows the methodology of Pisarski and Rischke (1999) and Wang and Rischke (2001) for color superconductivity. The effective pure gluodynamics formalism is adapted from Cohen and Sen (2015). The results enable further exploration of exotic phases in QCD phase diagrams, including the effects of magnetic fields, chiral chemical potentials, and curved space.
