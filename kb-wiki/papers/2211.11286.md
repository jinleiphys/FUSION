# Anomalous magnetohydrodynamics with temperature-dependent electric conductivity and application to the global polarization

> We have derived the solutions of the relativistic anomalous magnetohydrodynamics with longitudinal Bjorken boost invariance and transverse electromagnetic fields in the presence of temperature or energy density dependent electric conductivity. We consider the equations of states in a high temperature limit or in a high chiral chemical potential limit. We obtain both perturbative analytic solutions up to the order of \hbar and numerical solutions in our configurations of initial electromagnetic fields and Bjorken flow velocity. Our results show that the temperature or energy density dependent electric conductivity plays an important role to the decaying of the energy density and electromagnetic fields. We also implement our results to the splitting of global polarization for Λand \barΛ hyperons induced by the magnetic fields. Our results for the splitting of global polarization disagree with the experimental data in low energy collisions, which implies that the contribution from gradient of chemical potential may dominate in the low energy collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic and numerical solutions for relativistic anomalous magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) with temperature- or energy-density-dependent electric conductivity in a Bjorken flow, and finds that the resulting splitting of global polarization for Λ and Λ̅ hyperons disagrees with experimental data in low energy collisions, implying that the gradient of chemical potential may dominate in that regime.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic anomalous MHD equations with longitudinal Bjorken boost invariance and transverse electromagnetic fields, assuming charge neutrality and neglecting dissipative terms and vortical effects. They consider two equations of state: a high-temperature limit (EoS-HT) with temperature-dependent conductivity σ ∝ T, and a high chiral chemical potential limit (EoS-HC) with conductivity σ ∝ n₅^(2/3) ε^(-cₛ²/(1+cₛ²)). Perturbative analytic solutions are obtained to order ħ, and numerical solutions are computed for various initial parameters.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c is used for numerical calculations
- Speed of sound cₛ² = 1/3 is assumed for an ideal fluid
- Initial electric conductivity σ₀ is chosen in the range 5–30 MeV (≈ 0.025–0.15 fm⁻¹), based on lattice QCD (Aarts 2007, Ding 2010, Tuchin 2013) and holographic QCD (Pu 2014)
- The ratio E₀²/ε₀ = 0.1 is fixed for calculations of scaled electric field and chiral density
- In the high-temperature limit, the leading-order solution gives E(τ) ∝ (1/τ) exp(-σ(τ)τ) and ε(τ) ∝ (τ₀/τ)^(1+cₛ²)
- The magnetic field decays as B(τ) = B₀ (τ₀/τ)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Siddique (2019) and Wang (2020), which assumed constant electric conductivity, by incorporating temperature- or energy-density-dependent conductivity motivated by lattice QCD (Aarts 2007, Ding 2010) and holographic models (Pu 2014). The results are applied to the splitting of global polarization for Λ and Λ̅ hyperons, which has been studied by Muller (2018), Guo (2019), Buzzegoli (2022), and Xu (2022) in the context of electromagnetic field effects, and by Ryu (2021), Fu (2022), and Wu (2022) regarding baryon chemical potential gradients. The disagreement with experimental data at low energies suggests that chemical potential gradient contributions may be more important than magnetic field effects in that regime.
