# Removing Distortions from Charge Balance Functions

> Charge balance functions provide insight into critical issues concerning hadronization and transport in heavy-ion collisions by statistically isolating charge/anti-charge pairs which are correlated by charge conservation. However, distortions from residual interactions and unbalanced charges cloud the observable. Within the context of simple models, the significance of these effects is studied by constructing balance functions in both relative rapidity and invariant relative momentum. Methods are presented for eliminating or accounting for these distortions.

## Key claim

Charge balance functions can provide insight into hadronization and transport in heavy-ion collisions, but distortions from residual interactions and unbalanced charges significantly cloud the observable; within the context of simple blast-wave models, these distortions can be accounted for or eliminated using proposed methods, though the correction for inter-pair correlations is not fully model-independent.

## Method

The authors constructed charge balance functions in both relative rapidity and invariant relative momentum (\(Q_{\rm inv}\)) using simple blast-wave models with thermal emission from sources characterized by a temperature \(T\) and a distribution of transverse rapidities. They derived analytic expressions for the distortion caused by surplus (unbalanced) charge and proposed a mixed-event subtraction method to eliminate it. For inter-pair correlations, they simulated the distortion by weighting four-particle combinations with correlation weights derived from two-particle correlation functions that include Coulomb interactions and identical-particle interference, assuming Gaussian sources of radius \(R_{\rm inv}=6.0\) fm.

## Key numbers

- The balance function normalization is reduced by approximately 15% due to surplus charge in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, with the surplus component contributing only one or two percent of the balancing charge contribution.
- For the \(p\bar{p}\) balance function model, the number of protons per unit rapidity was taken as 28 and antiprotons as 21, with a total surplus charge \(Q=158\).
- The blast-wave model calculations used temperatures of 90 MeV, 120 MeV, and 150 MeV to illustrate sensitivity to breakup temperature.
- The Gaussian source radius for correlation functions was chosen as \(R_{\rm inv}=6.0\) fm, consistent with RHIC measurements.
- The charged pion multiplicity was set to 300 for both positive and negative pions, roughly consistent with RHIC measurements.
- The classical asymptotic form for the Coulomb correlation function is \(C_{\rm class}(Q_{\rm inv}) \approx 1 - \frac{4\mu\alpha Z_a Z_b}{Q_{\rm inv}^2 R \sqrt{\pi}}\), which agrees with quantum results for \(Q_{\rm inv} > 25\) MeV/c when \(qR > 1\).
- In central collisions, the distortion from inter-pair interactions can be a 20% effect; if correlation functions are understood to the 90% level, the residual systematic uncertainty is of order one or two percent.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Jeon and Pratt (2002) that addressed Hanbury-Brown Twiss correlations, detector acceptance, and charge fluctuations in balance functions. The paper provides methods to correct for two specific distortions: surplus charge from the initial nuclei (important for analyses at SPS or AGS where the fraction of extra protons is much higher) and inter-pair correlations dominated by Coulomb interactions, which become more acute for central collisions at RHIC. The analysis in \(Q_{\rm inv}\) is advocated over relative rapidity because it eliminates sensitivity to collective flow and allows identification of specific resonance contributions; the anisotropy of the balance function in the three projections \(Q_{\rm long}\), \(Q_{\rm out}\), and \(Q_{\rm side}\) is proposed as a crucial test for distinguishing early charge creation (with diffusion along the beam axis) from thermal broadening.
