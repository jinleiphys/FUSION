# Constraints on the isovector properties of finite nuclei from neutron stars observations

> The nuclear symmetry energy plays important role on the structure of finite nuclei as well as on the bulk properties of neutron stars. However, its values at high densities are completely uncertain and the corresponding experimental data have a large error. One possibility to determine or at least estimate the values at high densities is with the help of neutron star observations. Recently, observations of gravitational waves from merging processes of binary neutron star systems provide useful information on both their radius and tidal deformability, quantities directly related to the symmetry energy. In this work, an attempt is made in this direction, namely to see how recent observations can help to constrain the structure of finite nuclei. In particular, in the present study we parameterize the equation of state which describes the asymmetric and symmetric nuclear mater with the help of the parameter $η=(K_0 L^2)^{1/3}$, where $K_0$ is the incompressibility and $L$ the slope parameter. The parameter $η$ is a regulator of the stiffness of the equation of state. We expect that the values of $η$ affect both the properties of finite nuclei as well as of the neutron star properties (where the role of the isovector interaction plays important role). It is natural to expect that constraints, via the parameter $η$ on finite nuclei will imply constraints on the neutron star properties and vice versa. In view of the above statements we propose a simple but self-consistent method to examine simultaneously the effects of the parameter $η$ on the properties of finite nuclei and neutron stars. We found constraints on the latter systems via combination by the recent experiments (PREX-2) and observational data found by the detectors Ligo and Virgo.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the parameter \(\eta = (K_0 L^2)^{1/3}\), which combines the incompressibility \(K_0\) and the slope parameter \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy, can serve as a bridge connecting the isovector properties of finite nuclei and the bulk properties of neutron stars, and that constraints from the PREX-2 experiment (which favors a stiff equation of state) and the GW170817 event (which favors a soft equation of state) together limit \(\eta\) to a specific interval.

## Method

The authors constructed a self-consistent energy density functional based on a parametrization of asymmetric nuclear matter using the parameter \(\eta\). They applied this functional to calculate the neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy coefficients of \(^{208}\)Pb via a variational method with a Fermi-type trial density function. The same functional was then used to construct the equation of state for neutron star matter, including beta-equilibrium conditions, and to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the tidal deformability equations to obtain masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities for neutron stars.

## Key numbers

* The neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb from PREX-2 is \(\Delta R_{\rm skin} = (0.283 \pm 0.071)\) fm, which constrains \(\eta\) to the interval \(\eta \sim [110, 120]\) MeV.
* For \(\eta = 115.38\) MeV (corresponding to \(K_0 = 240\) MeV and \(L = 80\) MeV), the calculated neutron skin thickness is 0.2420 fm, the symmetry energy coefficient \(a_A\) is 21.090 MeV, and the volume coefficient \(a_A^V\) is 32.257 MeV.
* For \(\eta = 115.38\) MeV, the neutron star maximum mass is \(M_{\rm max} = 2.403 M_\odot\), the radius at 1.4 \(M_\odot\) is \(R_{1.4} = 13.331\) km, and the tidal deformability at 1.4 \(M_\odot\) is \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 664.153\).
* For \(\eta = 70.61\) MeV (the lowest value studied), the neutron star maximum mass is \(M_{\rm max} = 2.342 M_\odot\), \(R_{1.4} = 12.151\) km, and \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 333.362\).
* For \(\eta = 147.53\) MeV (the highest value studied), the neutron star maximum mass is \(M_{\rm max} = 2.442 M_\odot\), \(R_{1.4} = 14.535\) km, and \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 1252.559\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Sotani (2014) and Sotani (2022) by using the parameter \(\eta\) to simultaneously study finite nuclei and neutron stars with the same energy density functional. It directly confronts the tension between the stiff equation of state favored by the PREX-2 experiment (Adhikari 2021, Reed 2021) and the softer equation of state implied by the GW170817 gravitational wave event (Abbott 2019). The results show that \(\eta \approx 110\) MeV is a critical value for consistency with GW170817, and that the combined constraints from PREX-2 and LIGO/Virgo observations can narrow the allowed range of \(\eta\), thereby linking the isovector properties of finite nuclei to neutron star observables.
