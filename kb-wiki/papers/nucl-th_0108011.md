# Squeezing lepton pairs out of broken symmetries

> We discuss two possible signatures of symmetry breaking that can appear in dilepton spectra, as measured in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The first involves scalar-vector meson mixing and is related to the breaking of Lorentz symmetry by a hot medium. The second is related to the breaking of Furry's theorem by a charged quark-gluon plasma. Those signals will be accessible to upcoming measurements to be performed at the GSI, RHIC, and the LHC.

# Squeezing lepton pairs out of broken symmetries

## Key claim

Dilepton spectra from relativistic heavy ion collisions can reveal two distinct signatures of symmetry breaking that vanish identically in vacuum: scalar-vector meson mixing (breaking Lorentz symmetry in hot dense matter) and violation of Furry's theorem in a baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma (breaking charge conjugation invariance). These signals are density-dependent and require a finite baryon chemical potential to appear, making them accessible to experiments at GSI, RHIC, and the LHC.

## Method

The authors calculate thermal dilepton production rates from two mechanisms. First, they evaluate $\sigma$-$\omega$ and $\rho$-$a_0$ mixing in dense hadronic matter using an interaction Lagrangian with nucleon-meson couplings, computing the polarization tensor through nucleon-nucleon loops at finite temperature and density. Second, they compute the two-gluon-photon effective vertex from quark loop diagrams at finite chemical potential, extracting the $gg \to e^+e^-$ contribution from the imaginary part of the photon self-energy. Both calculations use relativistic kinetic theory to obtain thermal production rates.

## Key numbers

- For meson mixing: rates are shown at T = 75 MeV with mixing contributions in the invariant mass region between the $\rho$ and $\phi$ peaks, compared against $\pi$-$\pi$ and $K$-$\bar{K}$ annihilation channels
- For the quark-gluon plasma: differential production rates calculated at T = 400 MeV for invariant masses from 0.5 GeV to 2.5 GeV
- Two chemical potential values used: $\mu = 0.1T$ and $\mu = 0.5T$ (with T = 400 MeV)
- Calculation performed for three massless quark flavours
- The crossing point where $gg \to e^+e^-$ and $q\bar{q} \to e^+e^-$ rates become comparable lies near the continuum between the $\phi$ and $J/\psi$ invariant mass region

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of $\sigma$-$\omega$ mixing in nuclear matter by Chin (1977) and subsequent applications to heavy-ion collisions by Wolf et al. (1998) and Saito et al. (1998). The $\rho$-$a_0$ mixing channel was shown recently by Teodorescu (2000). The authors build on the established framework of using dileptons as probes of in-medium vector meson properties via Vector Meson Dominance, as highlighted in theoretical interpretations of CERES collaboration data. The results enable experimental searches by the HADES collaboration at GSI and the PHENIX collaboration at RHIC, with the authors noting that theoretical refinements including Hard Thermal Loop resummation and consistent vector meson spectral densities are needed before quantitative predictions can be made.
