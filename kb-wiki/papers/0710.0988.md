# Confront Holographic QCD with Regge Trajectories of vectors and axial-vectors

> We derive the general 5-dimension metric structure of the $Dp-Dq$ system in type II superstring theory, and demonstrate the physical meaning of the parameters characterizing the 5-dimension metric structure of the \textit{holographic} QCD model by relating them to the parameters describing Regge trajectories. By matching the spectra of vector mesons $ρ_1$ with deformed $Dp-Dq$ soft-wall model, we find that the spectra of vector mesons $ρ_1$ can be described very well in the soft-wall $D3-Dq$ model, i.e, $AdS_5$ soft-wall model. We then investigate how well the $AdS_5$ soft-wall model can describe the Regge trajectory of axial-vector mesons $a_1$. We find that the constant component of the 5-dimension mass square of axial-vector mesons plays an efficient role to realize the chiral symmetry breaking in the vacuum, and a small negative $z^4$ correction in the 5-dimension mass square is helpful to realize the chiral symmetry restoration in high excitation states.

## Key claim

The AdS₅ soft-wall model can describe the Regge trajectories of both vector mesons ρ₁ and axial-vector mesons a₁, provided that chiral symmetry breaking is implemented via a constant 5D bulk mass for the axial-vector meson and a small negative z⁴ correction to that mass is introduced to realize chiral symmetry restoration in high excitation states.

## Method

The authors derive the general 5D metric structure of the Dp-Dq brane system in type II superstring theory and relate the metric parameters (warp factor exponent a₀, dilaton logarithmic coefficient d₀, and quadratic dilaton coefficient c₂) to the Regge trajectory parameters aₙ (radial slope), aₛ (spin slope), and b (intercept). They fit the vector meson ρ₁ spectra using the exact solution of the equation of motion for the soft-wall model, then investigate axial-vector meson a₁ spectra in the same background by introducing a constant 5D mass m₅² for chiral symmetry breaking and a z-dependent mass correction for chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key numbers

- For vector mesons (ρ₁, ρ₃): aₙ^ρ = +0.91 ± 0.23 GeV², aₛ^ρ = +1.08 ± 0.39 GeV², b^ρ = -1.09 ± 1.18 GeV² (from χ² fit to PDG2007 data)
- For axial-vector mesons (a₁, a₃): aₙ^a = +0.81 ± 0.22 GeV², aₛ^a = +0.88 ± 0.39 GeV², b^a = +0.13 ± 1.17 GeV² (from χ² fit to PDG2007 data)
- Mass square difference of chiral partners at n=1: M_a₁² - M_ρ₁² = 0.9947 GeV²; at n=6: 0.3227 GeV²
- Mass square difference of chiral partners for ρ₃ and a₃ at n=1: 0.6408 GeV²; at n=3: 0.2736 GeV²
- Best-fit metric parameters for ρ₁: c₀^ρ = 1, c₂^ρ = 0.2 GeV², d₀^ρ = 0
- Best-fit constant 5D mass for a₁: m₅,a² = 0.5 GeV²
- Experimental bound on c₂ from vector-vector current correlator: |c₂| ≤ 0.21 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the soft-wall holographic QCD model of Karch, Katz, Son, and Stephanov (2006), which introduced a z² dilaton field to produce linear Regge behavior Mₙ² ∝ n, by systematically relating the metric parameters to experimental Regge trajectories. It contrasts with the Sakai-Sugimoto model (2005), which describes chiral symmetry breaking naturally but fails to generate linear Regge behavior. The results enable future studies of meson interactions, branching ratios, and decay widths within a holographic framework that reproduces both vector and axial-vector meson spectra.
