# Ab-initio No-Core Shell Model With Many-Body Forces

> Working in the framework of the ab-initio no-core shell model, we derive two-body effective interactions microscopically for specific harmonic-oscillator basis spaces from the realistic Argonne V8' nucleon-nucleon potential. However, our two-body effective interaction is only the dominant part of the A-body (A >= 3) effective interaction, which also contains three-, four- and, in general, up to A-body components. We model these multi-body components as a simple two-parameter, basis-space-dependent interaction. We then investigate the coupling needed to provide accurate descriptions of selected nuclear observables. We successfully model the multi-body interaction as a zero-range two-body operator times the number operator Nsps that measures the harmonic-oscillator quanta of the spectator nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the missing many-body components of the effective interaction in the ab-initio no-core shell model can be modeled as a simple two-parameter, basis-space-dependent zero-range two-body operator times the number operator measuring harmonic-oscillator quanta of spectator nucleons, but the model is phenomenological and the parameters must be fitted to experimental data in each basis space.

## Method

The authors start from the realistic Argonne V8' nucleon-nucleon potential and derive a two-body effective interaction using the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method for a two-nucleon subsystem in a finite harmonic-oscillator basis space defined by N_max. They then add a phenomenological residual many-body interaction of the form (a + b N_sps) × G δ(r1 - r2), where N_sps measures the oscillator quanta of the spectator nucleons and G = 1 MeV fm^3, and fit the dimensionless parameters a and b to reproduce the ground-state binding energy and first excited 0^+ state of ^4He in each basis space. The resulting effective Hamiltonian is applied to calculate spectra of ^4He, ^4H, and ^4Li.

## Key numbers

- For ^4He with N_max=8 and ℏΩ=19 MeV, the pure two-body Argonne V8' interaction gives a ground-state binding energy error of less than 4% (from 12% at N_max=2) but the 0^+_2 excitation energy error remains at 23% (from 51% at N_max=2)
- The fitted parameters for N_max=8 are a=0.63 (dimensionless) and b=-2.43 (dimensionless), with a positive and b negative
- The experimental root-mean-square point-charge radius of the ^4He ground state is 1.46 fm
- The phenomenological expression ℏΩ = 45/A^(1/3) - 25/A^(2/3) gives ℏΩ=19 MeV for A=4

## Context

This work extends the ab-initio no-core shell model approach of Navratil, Vary, and Barrett (1996, 2000) by addressing the known deficiency that two-body effective interactions alone are insufficient for accurate nuclear observables. The authors model the missing many-body forces phenomenologically, motivated by the multi-G method of Vary (1995) and by effective field theory expansions with zero-range terms as discussed by Lepage. The results enable future application to A > 4 systems, particularly A=5 nuclei, and suggest that introducing spin-isospin dependence in the couplings could further improve agreement with experiment.
