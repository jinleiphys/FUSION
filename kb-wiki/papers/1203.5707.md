# Strangeness nuclear physics: a critical review on selected topics

> Selected topics in strangeness nuclear physics are critically reviewed. This includes production, structure and weak decay of $Λ$--Hypernuclei, the $\bar K$ nuclear interaction and the possible existence of $\bar K$ bound states in nuclei. Perspectives for future studies on these issues are also outlined.

## Key claim

This critical review argues that while significant progress has been made in understanding Λ-hypernuclei through γ-ray spectroscopy and missing-mass measurements, the experimental data on hyperon-nucleon (YN) and hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions remain severely limited, consisting of no more than 850 spin-averaged scattering events for ΛN and ΣN interactions and no data for YY scattering, and that the low-energy data fail to adequately define even the dominant s-wave scattering lengths and effective ranges.

## Method

The authors critically review selected topics in strangeness nuclear physics by synthesizing experimental results from magnetic spectrometer measurements at KEK (SKS), JLab (Hall A and Hall C), and LNF-INFN (FINUDA), as well as γ-ray spectroscopy data on p-shell hypernuclei. They analyze production reactions including strangeness exchange (K⁻ + n → Λ + π⁻), associated production (π⁺ + n → Λ + K⁺), and electroproduction (e + p → e' + Λ + K⁺), and evaluate theoretical models based on chiral effective field theory and Nijmegen potentials (NSC89, NSC97, ESC04, ESC08) for YN and YY interactions.

## Key numbers

- The experimental data on ΛN and ΣN interactions consist of not more than 850 spin-averaged scattering events in the momentum region from 200 to 1500 MeV/c
- The Λ binding energy BΛ saturates at about 27 MeV for heavy hypernuclei, varying linearly with A with a slope of around 1 MeV per unit of A
- The bond energy ΔBΛΛ(⁶ΛΛHe) = (0.67 ± 0.16) MeV for the NAGARA event
- The Σ-nucleus potential is repulsive: Re VΣ(ρ₀) ∼ +(10-50) MeV at normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- The Ξ-nucleus potential is shallow: Re VΞ(ρ₀) ∼ -14 MeV
- The ΣN → ΛN conversion width is theoretically evaluated to be about 30 MeV for a Σ in nuclear matter at normal density
- The Ξ⁻p → ΛΛ conversion width is about 3-8 MeV for the NSC08 interaction model but can be as large as 15 MeV for the NSC04 model
- Mesonless absorption of K⁻ at rest by 2, 3, 4 nucleons in light nuclei occurs with rates of 10⁻³ per stopped K⁻
- The "magic momentum" for the K⁻ + n → Λ + π⁻ reaction is 505 MeV/c, where the momentum transfer qΛ vanishes

## Context

This review extends the work of earlier hypernuclear studies from the emulsion era (Danysz and Pniewski, 1953) and subsequent magnetic spectrometer experiments at BNL, KEK, JLab, and FINUDA, while critically assessing the current understanding of Λ-hypernuclei, Σ-nucleus potentials, and the controversial issue of antikaon nuclear clusters. The paper enables future experimental programs at J-PARC, FAIR (PANDA Collaboration), and GSI (HypHI Collaboration) by identifying key measurements needed, particularly for YN and YY scattering, Ξ-hypernuclei spectroscopy, and non-mesonic weak decay, and by highlighting unresolved discrepancies between theory and experiment for proton emission spectra and the role of the Δ-baryon resonance in non-mesonic weak decay.
