# A novel analysis of the effects of short range correlations in inclusive lepton scattering off nuclei

> It is shown that, if inclusive lepton scattering off nuclei at high momentum transfer is analyzed in terms of proper scaling variables, useful information on Nucleon-Nucleon short range correlations in nuclei can be obtained. The traditional approach to Y-scaling is critically analyzed and a novel approach to Y-scaling, which incorporates the effects from two- and three-nucleon correlations in nuclei, is illustrated.

## Key claim

If inclusive lepton scattering off nuclei at high momentum transfer ($Q^2 \gtrsim 1$ GeV$^2$) is analyzed using scaling variables that incorporate the excitation energy of the residual system generated by short range correlations, rather than the traditional mean-field scaling variable, the binding correction vanishes and the scaling function becomes proportional to the longitudinal momentum distribution, enabling direct access to high-momentum components from two- and three-nucleon correlations.

## Method

The authors critically analyze the traditional Y-scaling approach, which uses a scaling variable $y$ obtained by setting the excitation energy of the $(A-1)$-nucleon system to zero, and show that this leads to large binding corrections that obscure short range correlation effects. They introduce novel scaling variables $y_2$ and $y_3$ that incorporate the average excitation energy of the residual system generated by two-nucleon correlations (2NC) and three-nucleon correlations (3NC), respectively, by inserting these energies into the energy conservation law. The inclusive cross section is then decomposed into contributions from mean field, 2NC, and 3NC momentum distributions, each integrated from the corresponding scaling variable to infinity.

## Key numbers

- The experimental data analyzed cover momentum transfers $2.5 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 7.4$ GeV$^2$ from JLab.
- For $^3He$, the 2NC model reproduces the exact spectral function in the removal energy range $50 \lesssim E \lesssim 200$ MeV, but fails at very low and very high $E$ where 3NC contribute.
- The scaling constants obtained are $C^4 = 2.7$, $C^{12} = 4.0$, and $C^{56} = 4.6$ for $^4He$, $^{12}C$, and $^{56}Fe$, respectively, which bring the scaling functions of these nuclei onto the Deuteron scaling function.
- The binding correction ratio $B^A(q,y_2)/F^A(q,y_2)$ vanishes in the whole region of $y_2$ considered, in contrast to the traditional variable $y$ where it is very large at high negative $y$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1996) on nucleon momentum distributions and the scaling analysis of Frankfurt and Strikman (1981) on cross section ratios. It critically analyzes the traditional Y-scaling method and introduces a novel framework that incorporates two- and three-nucleon correlations through new scaling variables, building on the work of Ciofi degli Atti and West (1995). The approach enables direct extraction of short range correlation effects from inclusive electron scattering data and provides a framework for interpreting the plateaux observed in cross section ratios at $1.5 \lesssim x_{Bj} \lesssim 2$ (2NC region) and $2 \lesssim x_{Bj} \lesssim 3$ (3NC region) from JLab CLAS data.
