# The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron from low-energy QCD

> The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD) is reformulated from the first principles of QCD. The deuteron appears as a neutron-proton collective excitation, i.e. a Cooper np-pair, induced by a phenomenological local four-nucleon interaction in the nuclear phase of QCD. The RFMD describes the deuteron coupled to hadrons through one-nucleon loop exchanges providing a minimal transfer of nucleon flavours from initial to final nuclear states and accounting for contributions of nucleon-loop anomalies which are completely determined by one-nucleon loop diagrams. The dominance of contributions of nucleon-loop anomalies to effective Lagrangians of low-energy nuclear interactions is justified in the large N expansion, where N is the number of quark colours.

## Key claim

The deuteron is reformulated as a neutron-proton collective excitation (a Cooper np-pair) induced by a phenomenological local four-nucleon interaction in the nuclear phase of QCD, with the dominance of one-nucleon loop anomaly contributions justified in the large \(N_C\) expansion, where \(N_C\) is the number of quark colours.

## Method

The authors derive the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD) from QCD by integrating over heavy hadron degrees of freedom to obtain a local four-nucleon interaction, then linearizing to introduce an interpolating deuteron field. They integrate over nucleon fields in the one-nucleon loop approximation, using the large \(N_C\) expansion to justify keeping only the leading-order contributions from nucleon-loop anomalies, which are fully determined by one-nucleon loop diagrams. The model is applied to compute the effective Lagrangian of the free deuteron field and its coupling to an external electromagnetic field, fitting the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments to fix free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: \(\varepsilon_{\rm D} = 2.225\,{\rm MeV}\) (experimental value fitted by the model).
- Electric quadrupole moment of the deuteron: \(Q_{\rm D} = 0.286\,{\rm fm}^2\) (experimental value used as input).
- Magnetic dipole moment of the deuteron: \(\mu_{\rm D} = 0.857\) (in nuclear magnetons, experimental value fitted).
- Phenomenological coupling constant: \(g_{\rm V} = 11.319\) (calculated at \(N_C = 3\)).
- Cut-off parameter: \(\Lambda_{\rm D} = 115.729\,{\rm MeV}\), corresponding to a spatial region \(1/\Lambda_{\rm D} \sim \rho_{\rm D} = 1.705\,{\rm fm}\), consistent with the effective range of the deuteron \(\rho_{\rm D} = (1.759\pm 0.005)\,{\rm fm}\).
- Relation between coupling constants: \(g_{\rm T} = -1.662\,g_{\rm V}\).
- Parameters \(a\) and \(b\) fitted to electromagnetic moments: \(a = -0.442\), \(b = -4.418\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier RFMD formulations (Ivanov et al., 1996-1998) by grounding the model in QCD first principles rather than a phenomenological sigma-model analogy, and corrects a Lorentz-violating version (Ivanov et al., 1998) that gave an unreliable astrophysical factor for solar proton burning. The RFMD contrasts with the potential model approach (PMA) and effective field theory (EFT) approaches (e.g., Kaplan et al., 1996-1998) by including anti-nucleon degrees of freedom and nucleon-loop anomalies from the Dirac sea, which are absent in those methods. The model enables a quantum field theoretic description of nuclei as elementary particles, following the ideas of Sakita and Goebal (1966) and Kim and Primakoff (1965), and can be extended to low-energy nuclear processes such as p + p → D + e⁺ + νₑ.
