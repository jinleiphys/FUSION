# Theory progress at Strange Quark Matter 2021

## Key claim
The paper reviews recent theory developments presented at the 19th International Conference on Strangeness in Quark Matter (SQM 2021), highlighting that while significant progress has been made across multiple areas of strange and heavy flavor physics, many open questions remain and new ones are emerging as experimental and theoretical understanding advances.

## Method
The author summarizes theory presentations from SQM 2021 covering four main areas: the QCD equation of state and phase diagram (including lattice QCD at finite baryon chemical potential, model calculations, and critical point searches); transport coefficients and medium effects (including heavy quark diffusion, quarkonium evolution, and bulk medium properties); hadronization mechanisms (including strangeness enhancement, charm hadron production, and hadronic afterburners); and polarization and spin phenomena in heavy ion collisions. The review synthesizes results from lattice QCD, effective field theories, transport models, and hydrodynamic simulations presented at the conference.

## Key numbers
- Lattice QCD can determine thermodynamic observables up to baryon chemical potential $\mu_B = 3.5 T$ using a reorganized expansion scheme, though singularities in the complex $\mu_B$ plane may cause expansions to fail at $\mu_B/T \gtrsim 1.5$
- Global angular momentum in 2.76 TeV LHC collisions at impact parameter $b = 7$ fm is $L = 10^7 \hbar$, generating vorticity of order $10^{21}$ s$^{-1}$
- Predictions indicate a maximum in $\Lambda$ polarization at collision energy $\sqrt{s} \approx 7.7$ GeV
- The ordering $P_{\Omega^-} > P_{\Xi^-} > P_{\Lambda}$ was found for hyperon polarization in 200 GeV RHIC collisions using AMPT calculations
- Lattice QCD calculations show that widths of $\Upsilon(1S)$, $\Upsilon(2S)$, and $\Upsilon(3S)$ increase significantly with temperature while masses are barely modified

## Context
This review extends the ongoing SQM conference series tradition of assessing theory progress in strangeness and heavy flavor physics, covering work presented alongside experimental advances reported by Bellini (2021). The paper identifies key open questions including: whether experimentally observed extrema in fluctuation observables as a function of beam energy indicate criticality; which physical picture (rope hadronization, core+corona, or statistical hadronization) best describes strangeness enhancement in small systems; and how to resolve the continued difficulty in simultaneously describing medium suppression ($R_{AA}$) and elliptic anisotropy ($v_2$) of high momentum probes. The review also highlights the need for complete spin transport theory or spin hydrodynamics to understand all aspects of spin in heavy ion collisions, and calls for detailed quantitative studies of singularities in the complex $\mu_B$ plane using finite-volume scaling analyses.
