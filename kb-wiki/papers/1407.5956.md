# b-CGC versus IP-Sat and high precision combined HERA data

> The Impact-Parameter dependent Color Glass Condensate (b-CGC) and Saturation (IP-Sat) dipole models incorporate key features of small-x physics properties and match smoothly to the perturbative QCD regime at large $Q^2$ for a given $x$. Although both models include saturation effects and depend on impact-parameter, the former is based on the non-linear BK equation, while the latter is based on DGLAP evolution. After confronting the models to the recently released high precision combined HERA data, we show that in both models, the typical impact-parameter probed in the total $γ^{*}p$ cross-section is about $b\approx 2÷3\,\text{GeV}^{-1}$ and the proton saturation scale is $Q_S<1$ GeV in HERA kinematics. We show that most features of inclusive DIS and exclusive diffractive data at HERA are correctly reproduced in both models. Nevertheless, the b-CGC and the IP-Sat models give significantly different predictions beyond the current HERA kinematics for the structure functions at very low x and high virtualities $Q^2$, and for the exclusive diffractive vector meson and DVCS production at high $t$.

## Key claim
Although both the b-CGC and IP-Sat dipole models correctly reproduce most features of inclusive DIS and exclusive diffractive data at HERA, they give significantly different predictions beyond current HERA kinematics for structure functions at very low x and high virtualities Q², and for exclusive diffractive vector meson and DVCS production at high t.

## Method
The b-CGC dipole model, based on the non-linear Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation, and the IP-Sat dipole model, based on DGLAP evolution with saturation via the Glauber-Mueller approximation, were each fitted to the high precision combined HERA reduced cross-section data. The models were then used to compute inclusive structure functions (F₂, F_L, F₂^{c\bar{c}}) and exclusive diffractive processes (J/Ψ, ρ, φ vector meson production and DVCS) for comparison with HERA data, and to make predictions beyond the fitted kinematic range.

## Key numbers
- The typical impact-parameter probed in the total γ*p cross-section is about b ≈ 2–3 GeV⁻¹ in both models.
- The proton saturation scale is Q_S < 1 GeV in HERA kinematics.
- In the b-CGC model, the extracted parameters from the new combined HERA fit are γ_s ≈ 0.65 and λ ≈ 0.20.
- In the b-CGC model, the saturation scale is defined at the value where the dipole amplitude N(x, r_S = √2/Q_S, b) = 1 − exp(−1/2) = 0.4.
- The b-CGC and IP-Sat models give similar saturation scales within the x-region x ∈ [10⁻², 10⁻⁵], but become significantly different at x < 10⁻⁵.
- In both models, the preferred value of light quark masses is m_u ≈ 10⁻² to 10⁻⁴ GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous fits of the b-CGC model (Rezaeian, 2008) and the IP-Sat model (Rezaeian, 2013) by confronting them with the high precision combined HERA data (H1 and ZEUS collaborations, 2009). The new b-CGC fit yields parameters (γ_s ≈ 0.65, λ ≈ 0.20) now compatible with perturbative expectations, in contrast to the old fit. The results enable improved predictions for final state observables in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, where the new b-CGC fit has already been shown to agree with recent LHC diffractive photoproduction data, unlike the old 2008 fit.
