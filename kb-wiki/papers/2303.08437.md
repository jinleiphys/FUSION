# The effect of non-equal emission times and space-time correlations on (anti-) nuclei production

> Light (anti-) nuclei are a powerful tool both in collider physics and astrophysics. In searches for new and exotic physics, the expected small astrophysical backgrounds at low energies make these antinuclei ideal probes for, e.g., dark matter. At the same time, their composite structure and small binding energies imply that they can be used in collider experiments to probe the hadronisation process and two-particle correlations. For the proper interpretation of such experimental studies, an improved theoretical understanding of (anti-) nuclei production in specific kinematic regions and detector setups is needed. In this work, we develop a coalescence framework for (anti-) deuteron production which accounts for both the emission volume and momentum correlations on an event-by-event basis. This framework goes beyond the equal-time approximation, which has been commonly assumed in femtoscopy experiments and (anti-) nucleus production models until now. Using PYTHIA~8 as an event generator, we find that the equal-time approximation leads to an error of O(10%) in low-energy processes like $Υ$ decays, while the errors are negligible at LHC energies. The framework introduced in this work paves the way for tuning event generators to (anti-) nuclei measurements.

## Key claim

The equal-time approximation commonly used in femtoscopy and coalescence models leads to an error of O(10%) in low-energy processes like Upsilon decays, while the errors are negligible at LHC energies; the authors develop a coalescence framework that accounts for both emission volume and momentum correlations on an event-by-event basis, going beyond this approximation.

## Method

The authors extend the Wigner function approach to coalescence by deriving a formalism that accounts for non-equal emission times of nucleons, showing that the effect shifts the spatial coordinate r by the classical distance qt/m that the first particle propagates before the second is produced. They implement this framework as an afterburner to PYTHIA 8, using either a Gaussian ansatz for the nucleon emission volume or the semi-classical space-time picture provided by PYTHIA itself, with the deuteron Wigner function approximated by a sum of two Gaussians (the phi0-fit) to handle negative regions of the phase-space distribution.

## Key numbers

- The equal-time approximation leads to an error of O(10%) in low-energy processes like Upsilon decays
- The Gaussian emission volume parameter sigma = (1.0 +/- 0.1) fm reproduces a wide range of experimental data on pp, e+e- and pN collisions
- The deuteron Wigner function parameters are Delta = 0.581, d1 = 3.979 fm, and d2 = 0.890 fm
- The deuteron charge radius is reproduced with d = 3.2 fm when using a single Gaussian wave function
- The formation time condition requires tau << 1/E_d, where 1/E_d is approximately 90 fm

## Context

This work extends the WiFunC model (Wigner Function with Correlations) introduced by Kachelriess (2019) and further developed in subsequent papers, which previously relied on the equal-time approximation. The framework connects to femtoscopy measurements by ALICE (2020) of baryonic emission sources in pp collisions at 13 TeV, allowing independent tuning of coalescence models. The approach enables using both femtoscopy and antideuteron measurements to tune QCD-inspired event generators like PYTHIA, particularly for small interacting systems where momentum correlations cannot be neglected.
