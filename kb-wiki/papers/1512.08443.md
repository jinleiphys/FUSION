# The Polyakov loop at next-to-next-to leading order

> We calculate the next-to-next-to-leading correction to the expectation value of the Polyakov loop or equivalently to the free energy of a static charge. This correction is of order $g^5$. We show that up to this order the free energy of the static charge is proportional to the quadratic Casimir operator of the corresponding representation. We also compare our perturbative result with the most recent lattice results in SU(3) gauge theory.

## Key claim

The next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) correction to the Polyakov loop expectation value, or equivalently to the free energy of a static charge, is of order \(g^5\); up to this order the free energy is proportional to the quadratic Casimir operator of the corresponding representation, and the perturbative result shows agreement with lattice results in SU(3) gauge theory when the scale dependence is properly handled.

## Method

The authors compute the NNLO correction to the Polyakov loop using both a direct diagrammatic expansion in QCD and an effective field theory approach based on electrostatic QCD (EQCD), which separates the scales \(T\) and \(m_D\). They evaluate the relevant self-energy contributions at scales \(T\) and \(m_D\) in Coulomb gauge and Feynman gauge, including the two-loop self-energy at the scale \(m_D\) and mixed-scale contributions from the running coupling and matching coefficients. The result is obtained by combining the EQCD calculation of the static correlator \(\langle \widetilde{A}_0^2 \rangle\) with the matching coefficients \(\mathcal{Z}_2\) and the Debye mass \(m_E^2\) at next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

* The NNLO correction to the Polyakov loop is of order \(g^5\).
* The Debye mass at leading order is \(m_D^2 = \frac{C_A + T_F n_f}{3} g^2 T^2\).
* The \(\mathcal{O}(g^5)\) contribution to the Polyakov loop is given by Eq. (Lg5): \(L|_{g^5} = \frac{3 C_R \alpha_s^2 m_D}{16\pi T} \left[ 3 C_A + \frac{4}{3} T_F n_f (1-4\ln 2) + 2\beta_0 \left( \gamma_E + \ln \frac{\mu}{4\pi T} \right) \right] - \frac{C_R C_A^2 \alpha_s^3 T}{m_D} \left( \frac{89}{48} - \frac{11}{12} \ln 2 + \frac{\pi^2}{12} \right) - \frac{C_R C_F n_f T_F \alpha_s^3 T}{2 m_D}\).
* The matching coefficient \(\mathcal{Z}_2\) at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)\) is \(\mathcal{Z}_2 = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi} \left[ \frac{11}{3} C_A + \frac{4}{3} T_F n_f (1-4\ln 2) + 2\beta_0 \left( \gamma_E + \ln \frac{\mu}{4\pi T} \right) \right]\).
* The NLO correction to \(m_E^2\) is \(m_E^2 = m_D^2 \left[ 1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi} \left( \frac{5}{3} C_A + \frac{4}{3} T_F n_f (1-4\ln 2) + 2\beta_0 \left( \gamma_E + \ln \frac{\mu}{4\pi T} \right) \right) \right] - 2 C_F T_F n_f \alpha_s^2 T^2\).

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculation of the Polyakov loop by Burnier et al. (2009) and Brambilla et al. (2010) to NNLO, reducing the scale dependence of the perturbative result. It confirms Casimir scaling up to \(\mathcal{O}(g^7)\), which is important for understanding lattice results on Polyakov loops in higher representations by Mykkanen et al. (2012) and Petreczky et al. (2015). The result enables more precise comparisons between perturbative QCD and lattice QCD calculations of the static quark free energy at high temperatures, and provides a foundation for further calculations at \(\mathcal{O}(g^6)\) and beyond.
