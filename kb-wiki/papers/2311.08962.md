# Constraints on Density Dependent MIT Bag Model Parameters for Quark and Hybrid Stars

> We compute the equation of state (EoS) of strange quark stars (SQSs) with the MIT Bag model using density dependent bag pressure, characterized by a Gaussian distribution function. The bag pressure's density dependence is controlled by three key parameters namely the asymptotic value ($B_{as}$), $ΔB(=B_0 - B_{as})$, and $β$. We explore various parameter combinations ($B_{as}$, $ΔB$, $β$) that adhere to the Bodmer-Witten conjecture, a criterion for the stability of SQSs. Our primary aim is to analyze the effects of these parameter variations on the structural properties of SQSs. However we find that none of the combinations can satisfy the NICER data for PSR J0030+0451 and the constraint on tidal deformability from GW170817. So it can be emphasized that this model cannot describe reasonable SQS configurations. We also extend our work to calculate structural properties of hybrid stars (HSs). With the density dependent bag model (DDBM), these astrophysical constraints are fulfilled by the HSs configurations within a very restricted range of the three parameters. The present work is the first to constrain the parameters of DDBM for both SQS and HSs using the recent astrophysical constraints on tidal deformabiity from GW170817 and that on mass-radius relationship from NICER data.

# Constraints on Density Dependent MIT Bag Model Parameters for Quark and Hybrid Stars

## Key claim

The density dependent MIT Bag model with a Gaussian form for the bag pressure cannot produce strange quark star configurations that simultaneously satisfy the Bodmer-Witten stability condition and the recent astrophysical constraints from NICER data for PSR J0030+0451 and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817; however, hybrid star configurations can satisfy all these constraints, but only within a very restricted range of the three model parameters (B_as, Delta B, and beta).

## Method

The authors computed the equation of state for strange quark matter using the MIT Bag model with a density dependent bag pressure B(rho) given by a Gaussian distribution function characterized by three parameters: the asymptotic value B_as, the difference Delta B = B_0 - B_as (where B_0 is the bag pressure at zero density), and beta (which controls the rate at which B(rho) decreases from B_0 to B_as with increasing density). For hybrid stars, they combined the NL3wrho6 relativistic mean field model for the hadronic phase with the same density dependent bag model for the quark phase, using Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark phase transition. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability for both strange quark stars and hybrid stars, then compared these results against observational constraints from NICER (PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620) and GW170817.

## Key numbers

- For strange quark stars, no combination of (B_as, Delta B, beta) that satisfies the Bodmer-Witten stability condition (matter energy per baryon less than or equal to 930 MeV) can satisfy the NICER data for PSR J0030+0451 (mass 1.34+0.15-0.16 solar masses, radius 12.71+1.14-1.19 km from Riley 2019; mass 1.44+0.15-0.14 solar masses, radius 13.02+1.24-1.06 km from Miller 2019) and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 (70 less than or equal to Lambda_1.4 less than or equal to 580).

- For hybrid stars, the allowed parameter range that satisfies all constraints is: B_as around 10 MeV fm^-3 (not 20 or 30 MeV fm^-3); Delta B between 550 and 850 MeV fm^-3; beta around 0.2 (not 0.1 or 0.3).

- For hybrid stars with B_as = 10 MeV fm^-3 and Delta B = 550 MeV fm^-3, the maximum mass increases with increasing beta; for beta = 0.2 the constraints are satisfied, while for beta = 0.1 the maximum mass constraint from PSR J0740+6620 (2.08 +/- 0.07 solar masses from Fonseca 2021) is violated and for beta = 0.3 the NICER data is violated.

- For hybrid stars with B_as = 10 MeV fm^-3 and beta = 0.2, the maximum mass increases with decreasing Delta B; the allowed range is Delta B = 550 to 850 MeV fm^-3, with values below 550 MeV fm^-3 violating NICER data and values above 850 MeV fm^-3 failing the maximum mass constraint.

- For hybrid stars with Delta B = 550 MeV fm^-3 and beta = 0.2, B_as = 10 MeV fm^-3 satisfies all constraints, while B_as = 20 and 30 MeV fm^-3 do not satisfy the maximum mass constraint.

- The bag pressure B(rho) equals the constant B case at approximately rho = 4.12 rho_0 for beta = 0.4 and at approximately 3.62 rho_0 for beta = 0.7 (with B_as = 20 MeV fm^-3 and Delta B = 50 MeV fm^-3).

## Context

This work extends the density dependent bag model approach of Burgio (2001, 2002), who originally proposed the Gaussian form for B(rho) in the context of hadron-quark phase transition, by testing the model against modern astrophysical constraints from NICER and gravitational wave observations. The authors find that while Burgio (2002) fixed parameters to match CERN-SPS transition energy densities, those values are not suitable for explaining strange quark star structure. This is the first study to constrain all three density dependent bag model parameters (B_as, Delta B, beta) for both strange quark stars and hybrid stars using the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 and mass-radius constraints from NICER data. The results enable future work to focus on the narrow parameter window identified for hybrid stars, and they demonstrate that the simplest density dependent bag model without repulsive or perturbative corrections cannot describe reasonable strange quark star configurations, consistent with earlier findings by Lopes (2020) that constant bag pressure models also fail for quark stars without such corrections.
