# Resonances in $Λd$ Scattering and the $Σ$-hypertriton

> Using separable $NN$ and $ΛN$-$ΣN$ potentials in the Faddeev equations, we have demonstrated that the predicted enhancement in the $Λd$ cross section near the $Σd$ threshold is associated with resonance poles in the scattering amplitude. The positions of these poles, on the second Riemann sheet of the complex energy plane, are determined by examining the eigenvalues of the kernel of the Faddeev equations. This suggests that for a certain class of $ΛN$-$ΣN$ potentials we can form a $Σ$-hypertriton with a width of about 8 MeV.

## Key claim

The enhancement observed in the Λd cross section near the Σd threshold is associated with resonance poles in the scattering amplitude on the second Riemann sheet of the complex energy plane, and for a certain class of ΛN-ΣN potentials these poles correspond to a Σ-hypertriton state with a width of about 8 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the YNN system using separable NN and ΛN-ΣN potentials, restricting the YN interactions to S-wave. They analytically continued the homogeneous integral equation for the Faddeev kernel onto the second Riemann sheet by rotating the contour of integration in momentum space, then searched for eigenvalues λn(E)=1 which correspond to poles of the scattering amplitude. The two-body input included a Yamaguchi potential for the 1S0 NN channel, a Phillips potential for the 3S1-3D1 NN channel, the SRW potential for the 1S0 YN channel, and the TGE-A, TGE-B, and TGE-C potentials for the 3S1 YN channel, with the coupling strength between ΛN and ΣN channels varied via a parameter R.

## Key numbers

- The TGE-B potential has a ΣN bound state at (2126.7 - 0i) MeV when ΛN-ΣN coupling is turned off, corresponding to a binding energy of 4.3 MeV
- With coupling included, the TGE-B resonance pole sits on the [bt] sheet at (2131.7 - 5.4i) MeV, just above the ΣN threshold
- The width of the Σ-hypertriton is about 8 MeV
- For the TGE-B potential, the two-body resonance width is approximately 5 MeV
- The peak in the π- spectrum from 4He(K-,π-) experiments is centered at BΣ+ = 4 ± 1 MeV with a width of about 10 ± 2 MeV
- Harada et al. predicted an A=4 ΣNNN bound state with BΣ+ = 4.6 MeV and Γ = 7.9 MeV using the SAP-1 approximation to the Nijmegen YN potential model D

## Context

This work extends the analysis of ΛN-ΣN coupling effects in hypernuclei previously studied by Afnan and Gibson (1990) for the hypertriton, and addresses the interpretation of narrow structure observed by Hayano et al. (1989, 1991) in the π- spectrum from stopped K- on 4He, which was interpreted as a bound 4ΣHe state. The authors build on the sheet-labeling convention of Pearce and Gibson (1989) and the eigenvalue method of Afnan (1991) to directly determine resonance pole positions. The results enable future calculations to connect three-body resonance structure to the properties of the underlying YN interaction, and are relevant to analysis of Λd scattering experiments and the 3H(K-,π-) reaction.
