# Toward a resolution of the NN controversy

> Lattice QCD calculations of two-nucleon interactions have been underway for about a decade, but still haven't reached the pion mass regime necessary for matching onto effective field theories and extrapolating to the physical point. Furthermore, results from different methods, including the use of the Luscher formalism with different types of operators, as well as the HALQCD potential method, do not agree even qualitatively at very heavy pion mass. We investigate the role that different operators employed in the literature may play on the extraction of spectra for use within the Luscher method. We first explore expectations from Effective Field Theory solved within a finite volume, for which the exact spectrum may be computed given different physical scenarios. We then present preliminary lattice QCD results for two-nucleon spectra calculated using different operators on a common lattice ensemble.

## Key claim

The discrepancies between different lattice QCD calculations of two-nucleon interactions likely arise from excited-state contamination in the extracted spectra, not from a failure of the Luscher formalism itself; the authors show that local hexaquark operators at the source can produce systematically incorrect spectra at intermediate times that mimic a bound state even when no physical bound state exists.

## Method

The authors constructed a discretized pionless effective field theory (EFT) with point-like non-relativistic nucleon fields interacting via delta-function interactions, tuned to reproduce two physical scenarios: one with no bound state and one with a deeply-bound state. They computed exact finite-volume spectra from the EFT and then generated correlation functions using three operator types: momentum-space operators with variational (GEVP) methods, local hexaquark operators at the source with momentum-projected sinks, and spatially displaced two-nucleon operators at the source with momentum-projected sinks. They also performed preliminary lattice QCD calculations on a single CLS ensemble with m_pi ~ 714 MeV, a ~ 0.086 fm, and L ~ 4.1 fm, comparing results from displaced operators and hexaquark operators against variational sLapH results from Horz (2020).

## Key numbers

- The lattice QCD ensemble used has m_pi ~ 714 MeV, a ~ 0.086 fm, and L ~ 4.1 fm.
- The EFT study used a box size of L = 12 lattice sites for the no-bound-state and deeply-bound-state scenarios.
- The Mainz collaboration found discretization effects on the order of ~30 MeV in the H-dibaryon system at an SU(3)-symmetric point with m_pi ~ 420 MeV (Green 2021).
- The EFT study used spatial displacements of Delta = 1 and Delta = 5 lattice sites for the displaced two-nucleon operators.
- The variational method used bases of 10 and 30 momentum-space operators in the EFT study.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing controversy between lattice QCD results from NPLQCD (2013, 2012), which found bound two-nucleon states at heavy pion masses using hexaquark operators, and results from variational calculations by Green (2021), Horz (2020), Francis (2018), and Amarasinghe (2021), which found unbound systems at similar pion masses. The EFT study extends the earlier work of Iritani (2017, 2018) on excited-state contamination and provides a controlled framework for understanding operator dependence. The preliminary lattice QCD results on a single ensemble enable direct comparison of methods while holding discretization effects fixed, and suggest that the hexaquark operator results from NPLQCD (2013, 2012) are likely incorrect, with the system showing no true bound state.
