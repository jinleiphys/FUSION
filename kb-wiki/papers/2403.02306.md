# On the study of new proxies for second order cumulants of conserved charges in heavy-ion collisions with EPOS4

> Proxies for cumulants of baryon number $B$, electric charge $Q$ and strangeness $S$ are usually measured in heavy-ion collisions via moments of net-number distribution of given hadronic species. Since these cumulants of conserved charges are expected to be sensitive to the existence of a critical point in the phase diagram of nuclear matter, it is crucial to ensure that the proxies used as substitutes are as close to them as possible. Hence, we use the EPOS4 framework to generate Au+Au collisions at several collision energies of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan. We compute $2^\text{nd}$ order net-cumulants of $π$, $K$ and $p$, for which experimental data has been published, as well as the corresponding conserved charge cumulants. We then compare them with proxies, defined in previous lattice QCD and Hadron Resonance Gas model studies, which are shown to reproduce more accurately their associated conserved charge cumulants. We investigate the impact of hadronic re-scatterings occurring in the late evolution of the system on these quantities, as well as the amount of signal actually originating from the bulk medium which endures a phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether new proxy ratios for second-order cumulants of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness, inspired by lattice QCD and Hadron Resonance Gas model studies, reproduce the corresponding conserved charge cumulants more accurately than the standard STAR proxies in Au+Au collisions simulated with EPOS4, but only for collision energies above 19.6 GeV where the model is applicable.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS4 event generator to simulate Au+Au collisions at five RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies (19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV) with a crossover equation of state and no critical point. They computed second-order net-cumulants (variances and covariances) of pions, kaons, protons, and lambdas within the same kinematic cuts as STAR data (pseudorapidity |eta| < 0.5, transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 1.6 GeV, distance of closest approach < 1 cm). They compared standard STAR proxy ratios with new proxy ratios defined in Bellwied (2019) and a newly proposed proxy for QB correlations, and compared all proxies against the actual conserved charge cumulants computed from all particles in the same phase space. The modular design of EPOS4 allowed them to compare results at hadronization time versus final-state distributions to quantify the impact of hadronic re-scatterings, and to separate contributions from the bulk medium (core) versus hard processes (corona).

## Key numbers

- Simulated 2.5 million Au+Au events at 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV; 2.0 million at 62.4 GeV; 1.5 million at 200 GeV
- Applied kinematic cuts: |eta| < 0.5, 0.4 < pT < 1.6 GeV, DCA < 1 cm, matching STAR analysis in STAR (2019)
- Used 20 bins of width Delta Npart = 20 for centrality determination, corresponding to approximately 5% centrality classes
- Initial time for core-corona separation: tau0 = 0.4 fm/c
- Energy density for hadronization: epsilon_H = 0.57 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of Bellwied (2019), which used the Hadron Resonance Gas model to construct improved proxies for conserved charge fluctuations, by testing those proxies in realistic heavy-ion collision simulations with EPOS4. It directly compares with experimental results from the STAR collaboration (STAR 2019) on net-pion, net-kaon, and net-proton cumulants at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies. The study enables future investigations of critical point signals by establishing which hadronic proxies most faithfully reflect conserved charge fluctuations, and by quantifying how much of the fluctuation signal originates from the bulk medium that would experience a phase transition, though the authors note that EPOS4 with a crossover equation of state serves only as a non-critical baseline.
