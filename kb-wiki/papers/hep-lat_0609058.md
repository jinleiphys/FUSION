# Lattice simulations of real-time quantum fields

> We investigate lattice simulations of scalar and nonabelian gauge fields in Minkowski space-time. For SU(2) gauge-theory expectation values of link variables in 3+1 dimensions are constructed by a stochastic process in an additional (5th) ``Langevin-time''. A sufficiently small Langevin step size and the use of a tilted real-time contour leads to converging results in general. All fixed point solutions are shown to fulfil the infinite hierarchy of Dyson-Schwinger identities, however, they are not unique without further constraints. For the nonabelian gauge theory the thermal equilibrium fixed point is only approached at intermediate Langevin-times. It becomes more stable if the complex time path is deformed towards Euclidean space-time. We analyze this behavior further using the real-time evolution of a quantum anharmonic oscillator, which is alternatively solved by diagonalizing its Hamiltonian. Without further optimization stochastic quantization can give accurate descriptions if the real-time extend of the lattice is small on the scale of the inverse temperature.

## Key claim

Stochastic quantization can accurately describe real-time quantum field dynamics on the lattice, but only if the real-time extent of the lattice is small compared to the inverse temperature; without this condition, the Langevin flow converges to one of multiple possible fixed points, and the correct physical solution cannot be selected a priori without additional constraints.

## Method

The authors simulate scalar field theory and SU(2) pure gauge theory in 3+1 Minkowski dimensions by introducing a fifth "Langevin-time" dimension and evolving the fields via a complex Langevin equation with a tilted real-time contour. For the gauge theory, the link variables are updated using a second-order discretized Langevin equation with real Gaussian noise, and the group manifold is extended from SU(2) to SL(2,C) during the stochastic process. The scalar anharmonic oscillator is also solved by Hamiltonian diagonalization for comparison.

## Key numbers

- For the scalar anharmonic oscillator, stochastic quantization gives accurate descriptions when the real-time extent of the lattice is small on the scale of the inverse temperature.
- For SU(2) gauge theory, the thermal equilibrium fixed point is only approached at intermediate Langevin-times; its stability increases when the complex time path is deformed towards Euclidean space-time.
- The Langevin step size must be "sufficiently small" and the real-time contour must be "tilted" to achieve converging results in general.
- The discretized Langevin equation uses a step size parameter epsilon (denoted ε) related to the Langevin-time increment by Δt₅²/2 → ε.

## Context

This work extends the real-time stochastic quantization approach previously applied to scalar fields by Berges (2005) to nonabelian gauge theories relevant for QCD, addressing the long-standing problem of direct Minkowski-space lattice simulations. It contrasts with Euclidean lattice methods that require analytic continuation and with reweighting approaches that suffer from sign and overlap problems. The paper shows that the fixed-point ambiguity is analogous to problems previously observed in Euclidean theories with complex actions by Ambjorn (1986) and Karsch (1985), and it enables future work on nonequilibrium dynamics such as early thermalization in QCD plasmas, provided the lattice size constraints are respected.
