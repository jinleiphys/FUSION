# Tripling Fluctuations and Peaked Sound Speed in Fermionic Matter

> A crossover involving three-fermion clusters is relevant to the hadron-quark crossover, which, if occurring in a neutron star, could naturally reproduce the dense-matter equation of state recently deduced from simultaneous observations of neutron-star masses and radii. To understand the crossover mechanism, we examine the role of tripling fluctuations induced by the formation of three-fermion clusters. The phase-shift representation of fluctuations manifests an interplay of bound and scattering states, leading to non-monotonic momentum distributions of baryon-like clusters and peaked sound speed at finite densities. We demonstrate them by applying our approach to a nonrelativistic system of one-dimensional three-color fermions analogous to the hadron-quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that tripling fluctuations from three-fermion clusters produce a non-monotonic momentum distribution of baryon-like states and a peaked sound speed in dense fermionic matter, but only under the condition that the phase-shift representation of the propagator enforces cancellation between bound-state and scattering-state contributions, which is essential for avoiding double-counting of constituent particles.

## Method

The authors generalize the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink pairing-fluctuation approach to three-body (tripling) fluctuations, using a phase-shift representation of the thermodynamic potential. They apply this formalism to a nonrelativistic one-dimensional system of three-color fermions with a contact-type three-body attractive interaction, where the three-body binding energy is fixed via renormalization. The momentum distributions of quark-like fermions and baryon-like three-body states are computed from the thermodynamic potential, and the isothermal sound speed is derived from the density susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- Three-body binding energy \(\mathcal{B}\) is fixed as a positive parameter; the coupling \(V(\Lambda)\) is renormalized as \(\frac{1}{V(\Lambda)} = -\frac{m}{2\sqrt{3}\pi} \ln\left( \frac{\mathcal{B} + \Lambda^2/m}{\mathcal{B}} \right)\).
- At zero temperature, the baryon distribution \(f_{\rm B}^{T=0}(K)\) reaches a maximum of 1 as \(K\) approaches the threshold momentum \(K_{\rm th} = 3k_{\rm F}\), where \(k_{\rm F} = \sqrt{2m\tilde{\mu}}\) is the quark Fermi momentum.
- The squared isothermal sound speed \(c_s^2/v_{\rm F}^2\) (normalized by Fermi velocity \(v_{\rm F} = \pi n/3m\)) exhibits a peak as a function of chemical potential \(\mu\) at temperatures \(T/\mathcal{B} = 0.2\) and other values shown; at high densities it approaches unity from below.
- The momentum shell thickness parameter is \(\Delta(K_{\rm th}) \equiv \sqrt{K_{\rm th}^2 + 2M_{\rm B}\mathcal{B}} - K_{\rm th}\), where \(M_{\rm B} = 3m\).

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter concept of McLerran and Reddy (2019) and the duality model of Kojo et al. (2024) by providing a microscopic treatment of three-fermion clustering rather than relying on phenomenological Pauli blocking. It generalizes prior studies of two-color QCD and isospin QCD where hadron-quark continuity was established by lattice simulations (Iida et al., 2024; PTEP, 2022). The approach is qualitatively consistent with quantum Monte Carlo simulations of one-dimensional three-component Fermi gases (PhysRevA.102.023313) and enables future application to systems closer to real QCD matter, as well as potential experimental realization in ultracold-atom systems.
