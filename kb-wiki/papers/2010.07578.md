# Probing negative-parity states of $^{24}$Mg probed via proton and alpha inelastic scattering

> [Background:] The band structure of the negative-parity states of $^{24}$Mg has not yet been clarified. The $K^π=0^-$, $K^π=1^-$, and $K^π=3^-$ bands have been suggested, but the assignments have been inconsistent between experiments and theories. [Purpose:] Negative-parity states of $^{24}$Mg are investigated by microscopic structure and reaction calculations via proton and alpha inelastic scattering to clarify the band assignment for the observed negative-parity spectra. [Method:] The structure of $^{24}$Mg was calculated using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics~(AMD). Proton and alpha inelastic reactions were calculated using microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with the AMD densities of $^{24}$Mg. [Results:] The member states of the $K^π=0^+$, $K^π=2^+$, $K^π=0^-$, $K^π=1^-$, and $K^π=3^-$ bands of $^{24}$Mg were obtained through the AMD result. In the MCC+AMD results for proton and alpha elastic and inelastic cross sections, reasonable agreements were obtained with existing data, except in the case of the $4^+_1$ state. [Conclusions:] The $3^-$ state of the $K^π=3^-$ band and the $1^-$ and $3^-$ states of the $K^π=0^-$ bands were assigned to the $3^-_1$(7.62 MeV), $1^-_1$(7.56 MeV), and $3^-_2$(8.36 MeV) states, respectively. The present AMD calculation is the first microscopic structure calculation to reproduce the energy ordering of the $K^π=0^-$, $K^π=1^-$, and $K^π=3^-$ bands of $^{24}$Mg.

## Key claim

The AMD calculation is the first microscopic structure calculation to reproduce the energy ordering of the Kπ=0−, Kπ=1−, and Kπ=3− bands of 24Mg, but the calculated band-head energies are systematically overestimated compared to experiment, and the theoretical E2-transition strengths tend to underestimate the experimental data, requiring renormalization factors of 1.19–2.5 to fit observed values.

## Method

The structure of 24Mg was calculated using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after parity and total-angular-momentum projections (VAP), using the MV1 central force and G3RS spin-orbit force. Proton and alpha inelastic scattering cross sections were calculated using microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations, where the nucleon-nucleus and alpha-nucleus potentials were constructed by folding the Melbourne g-matrix NN interaction with the AMD diagonal and transition densities of 24Mg, with the transition densities renormalized to fit experimental electromagnetic transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The 3−1 state at 7.62 MeV is assigned to the Kπ=3− band, the 1−1 state at 7.56 MeV and 3−2 state at 8.36 MeV are assigned to the Kπ=0− band
- Calculated ground state matter radius: 3.02 fm (experimental point-proton rms radius: 2.941(2) fm)
- Calculated electric quadrupole moment of 2+1 state: −15.1 e fm2 (experimental: −16.6(6) e fm2)
- Renormalization factors ftr for transition densities range from 1.19 to 2.5 across different states
- B(E2) for 2+1 → 0+1: experimental 88.4(4.1) e2 fm4, AMD 55.4 e2 fm4 (ftr = 1.26)
- B(E2) for 4+1 → 2+1: experimental 160(16) e2 fm4, AMD 72.8 e2 fm4 (ftr = 1.48)
- B(E3) for 3−1 (Kπ=3−) → 0+1: experimental 80 e2 fm6 from (e,e'), AMD 28 e2 fm6 (ftr = 1.68)
- B(E3) for 3−2 (Kπ=0−) → 0+1: experimental 226 e2 fm6 from (e,e'), AMD 89 e2 fm6 (ftr = 1.59)
- Deformation parameter β = 0.35 for the 0+1 ground state, β = 0.40 for the Kπ=0− and Kπ=1− bands

## Context

This work extends previous structure calculations using quasiparticle RPA (Nesterenko 2017, 2019) and AMD (Kimura 2012, Chiba 2019) that predicted low-lying isoscalar dipole excitations but gave inconsistent band assignments compared to the experimental band assignment from Fifield (1979). The MCC+AMD approach has been previously applied successfully to proton and alpha scattering off various p- and sd-shell nuclei (Kanada-En'yo 2019, 2020). The results enable future analyses of inelastic electron, proton, and alpha scattering data to resolve the long-standing ambiguity in negative-parity band assignments of 24Mg, though the systematic overestimation of band-head energies and underestimation of transition strengths indicate that higher-order effects beyond the present AMD model are needed.
