# Low energy reaction $K^-p\rightarrowО›О·$ and the negative parity $О›$ resonances

> The reaction $K^-p\rightarrowО›О·$ at low energies is studied with a chiral quark model approach. Good descriptions of the existing experimental data are obtained. It is found that $О›(1670)$ dominates the reaction around threshold. Furthermore, $u$- and $t$-channel backgrounds play crucial roles in this reaction as well. The contributions from the $D$-wave state $О›(1690)$ are negligibly small for its tiny coupling to $О·О›$. To understand the strong coupling properties of the low-lying negative parity $О›$ resonances extracted from the $\bar{K}N$ scattering, we further study their strong decays. It is found that these resonances are most likely mixed states between different configurations. Considering these low-lying negative parity $О›$ resonances as mixed three-quark states, we can reasonably understand both their strong decay properties from Particle Data Group and their strong coupling properties extracted from the $\bar{K}N$ scattering. As a byproduct, we also predict the strong decay properties of the missing $D$-wave state $|О›\frac{3}{2}^-\ >_3$ with a mass of $\sim1.8$ GeV. We suggest our experimental colleagues search it in the $ОЈ(1385)ПЂ$ and $ОЈПЂ$ channels.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-lying negative parity Lambda resonances, particularly Lambda(1670), are most likely mixed three-quark states between different SU(6) x O(3) configurations rather than pure states, and that this mixing can simultaneously explain both the strong decay properties from the Particle Data Group and the strong coupling properties extracted from antikaon-nucleon scattering. The authors hedge that the bowl structures in differential cross sections that might indicate a narrow exotic D-wave state cannot be confirmed due to large uncertainties in the present data.

## Method

The authors studied the K- p -> Lambda eta reaction at low energies using a chiral quark model approach, calculating s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel (vector and scalar exchange) amplitudes at tree level. They fitted the model parameters to experimental total and differential cross section data from the Crystal Ball Collaboration, extracting coupling strength parameters for each resonance relative to the SU(6) x O(3) symmetry limit. Separately, they calculated strong decay widths of the low-lying negative parity Lambda resonances using the same chiral quark model, treating them as mixed states between different quark model configurations with mixing angles determined by fitting the Particle Data Group decay data.

## Key numbers

- The coupling strength parameter C for Lambda(1670) S01 in the K- p -> Lambda eta reaction is 34.70, about 34 times larger than the SU(6) x O(3) symmetry limit value of 1.
- The coupling strength parameter C for Lambda(1405) S01 is 1.17, and for Lambda(1520) D03 is 1.18.
- The Breit-Wigner mass and width extracted for Lambda(1670) S01 are 1676.0 MeV and 35.0 MeV, compared to the PDG values of 1670 +/- 10 MeV and 25-50 MeV.
- The Breit-Wigner mass and width extracted for Lambda(1690) D03 are 1682.4 MeV and 70.0 MeV, compared to the PDG values of 1690 +/- 5 MeV and 50-70 MeV.
- The mixing angles for the S-wave states are theta_12 approximately 75 degrees, theta_13 approximately 50 degrees, and theta_23 approximately 125 degrees.
- As a mixed state, the predicted total decay width of Lambda(1670) S01 is 44.7 MeV, with branching ratios of 0.30 for N anti-K, 0.41 for Lambda eta, and 0.26 for Sigma pi.
- The predicted total decay width of Lambda(1405) S01 as a mixed state is approximately 53 MeV, in agreement with the PDG value of 50 +/- 2 MeV.
- The predicted partial decay width of Lambda(1800) S01 to N anti-K is 51.1 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 52 +/- 9 MeV from Alston (1977).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic study of K- p -> Sigma0 pi0, Lambda pi0, anti-K0 n reactions (Zhong 2013) to the K- p -> Lambda eta channel, which provides a cleaner probe of Lambda resonances due to isospin selection rules. The results contradict the pure constituent quark model classification in the SU(6) x O(3) limit, which cannot explain the weak coupling of Lambda(1670) to anti-K N and its strong coupling to eta Lambda. The analysis also addresses the work of Liu and Xie (2011, 2012), who found evidence for an exotic narrow D-wave resonance with mass approximately 1669 MeV and width approximately 1.5 MeV from the same reaction data, but the present authors cannot confirm this due to data uncertainties. The paper enables future experimental searches for the missing D-wave state |Lambda 3/2- >_3 with mass approximately 1.8 GeV in the Sigma(1385) pi and Sigma pi channels.
