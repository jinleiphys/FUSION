# Higgs boson production in partonic and electromagnetic interactions with heavy ions

> Higgs boson production in partonic and electromagnetic (photon-photon) interactions of light- and heavy-ions (A-A) at the LHC and future colliders is summarized. Parton-induced cross sections --including gluon-gluon, vector-boson fusion, and associated W, Z, t processes-- are computed at NNLO with MCFM 8.0 using nuclear parton distribution functions. Photon-photon cross sections are computed with MadGraph 5.0 for ultraperipheral A-A interactions with both ions surviving the collision. In the center-of-mass energy range $\sqrt{s} \approx 5$--100 TeV, the ratio of electromagnetic-to-partonic Higgs cross sections is $\rm R_{e.m./parton} \approx 10^{-5}$--10$^{-4}$. At FCC energies, this ratio is 10--100 times larger in A-A than in p-p thanks to the strong nuclear coherent $γ$ fluxes. The feasibility of Higgs boson measurements at LHC, HE-LHC, and FCC, in the most-favourable $H\toγγ,b\bar{b}$ decay channels in partonic and e.m. interactions respectively, is determined taking into account standard acceptance and efficiency losses on the final states, plus selection criteria to minimize the respective continuum backgrounds. Whereas $3σ$ evidence for partonic and e.m. Higgs production requires about $\times$35 and $\times$200 ($\times$7 and $\times$30) larger integrated luminosities than those expected for a nominal 1-month run at the LHC (HE-LHC), a $5σ$ observation of both production modes is warranted in just one month at the FCC.

## Key claim

Higgs boson production in heavy-ion collisions, via both partonic and electromagnetic (photon-photon) interactions, is feasible at future colliders but requires significantly larger integrated luminosities than nominal one-month runs at the LHC and HE-LHC; a 5-sigma observation of both production modes is warranted in just one month at the FCC.

## Method

Parton-induced Higgs cross sections (gluon-gluon fusion, vector-boson fusion, and associated W, Z, t processes) were computed at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) with MCFM 8.0 using CT10 proton PDFs and EPS09 nuclear PDFs, with renormalization and factorization scales fixed at mu_R = mu_F = m_H/2. Photon-photon cross sections for ultraperipheral A-A collisions were computed with MadGraph 5.0 by convolving Weizsacker-Williams equivalent photon fluxes of the ions with the elementary gamma-gamma to Higgs cross section, excluding nuclear overlap by imposing impact parameter cuts. The feasibility of measurements was determined for the H to gamma-gamma and H to b-bbar decay channels, accounting for acceptance and efficiency losses plus selection criteria to minimize continuum backgrounds.

## Key numbers

* Parton-induced Higgs cross section in Pb-Pb at LHC (5.5 TeV): approximately 500 nb.
* Parton-induced Higgs cross section in Pb-Pb at FCC (39 TeV): approximately 11.5 microbarn.
* Electromagnetic Higgs cross section in Pb-Pb at LHC: approximately 15 pb.
* Electromagnetic Higgs cross section in Pb-Pb at FCC: approximately 1.8 nb.
* Ratio of electromagnetic-to-partonic Higgs cross sections in A-A collisions over sqrt(s_NN) = 5-100 TeV: R_e.m./parton approximately 10^-5 to 10^-4.
* At FCC energies, the electromagnetic-to-partonic ratio is 10-100 times larger in A-A than in p-p.
* Expected Higgs bosons per month in Pb-Pb at LHC (5.5 TeV) in the diphoton channel: about 10.
* Expected Higgs bosons per month in Pb-Pb at FCC (39 TeV) in the diphoton channel: about 1500.
* Significance of diphoton signal at HL-LHC: 0.5 sigma (requires factor 35 larger luminosity for 3-sigma evidence).
* Significance of diphoton signal at HE-LHC: 1.2 sigma (requires factor 7 larger luminosity for 3-sigma evidence).
* Significance of diphoton signal at FCC with nominal luminosity: 5.5 sigma.
* For electromagnetic Higgs production, evidence at HL-LHC requires factor 200 larger luminosity; at HE-LHC requires factor 30 larger luminosity.
* At FCC, electromagnetic Higgs production yields about 200 Higgs per month (about 110 in the b-bbar channel).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by d'Enterria (2017) on parton-initiated Higgs cross sections in A-A collisions and by d'Enterria (2009) on ultraperipheral Higgs production, now including light-ion collisions and comparing both production modes. The results show that Higgs boson observation in heavy-ion collisions is out of reach at the HL-LHC with nominal luminosities but becomes possible at the HE-LHC for partonic production and at the FCC for both production modes, enabling independent measurements of the H-gamma coupling and the total Higgs width.
