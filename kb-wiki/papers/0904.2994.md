# Influence of entrance channels on formation of superheavy nuclei in massive fusion reactions

> Within the framework of the dinuclear system (DNS) model, the production cross sections of superheavy nuclei Hs (Z=108) and Z=112 combined with different reaction systems are analyzed systematically. It is found that the mass asymmetries and the reaction Q values of the combinations play a very important role on the formation cross sections of the evaporation residues. Both methods by solving the master equations along the mass asymmetry degree of freedom (1D) and along the proton and the neutron degrees of freedom (2D) are compared each other and with the available experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the mass asymmetry and reaction Q value of projectile-target combinations are the dominant entrance-channel factors controlling the production cross sections of superheavy evaporation residues, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the 1D master-equation approach overestimates fusion probabilities for systems with large mass asymmetries and large quadrupole deformations, so the 2D master-equation treatment is required for those cases.

## Method

The authors used the dinuclear system (DNS) model, solving master equations for the time evolution of the probability distribution along either the mass asymmetry degree of freedom (1D) or along both proton and neutron degrees of freedom (2D). The evaporation residue cross section was computed as a product of the transmission probability over the Coulomb barrier, the fusion probability (from the master equations), and the survival probability against fission during neutron evaporation, summed over partial waves up to J_max = 30. Calculations were performed for several reaction systems leading to the same compound nuclei (Hs, Z=108 and Z=112) to isolate entrance-channel effects.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U leading to Z=112, the 3n channel at an excitation energy of 35 MeV shows a slight decrease in cross section compared to the 1D calculation, with the 2D calculation giving smaller cross sections than the 1D master equations.
- For the system $^{136}$Xe+$^{136}$Xe leading to $^{272}$Hs, the upper limit cross sections for evaporation residues in the 1n–3n channels were observed experimentally to be $\sigma_{(1-3)n} \leq 4$ pb.
- The maximal angular momentum used in the partial-wave sum was $J_{max}=30$, since the fission barrier of the heavy nucleus disappears at high spin.

## Context

This work extends the DNS model calculations of Feng (2006, 2007, 2009) to systematically compare 1D and 2D master-equation approaches for superheavy element formation. It directly addresses the experimental results from Dubna (Oganessian, 2004b) and GSI (Hofmann, 2007) for $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U, and the recent upper-limit measurements for $^{136}$Xe+$^{136}$Xe (Oganessian, 2009). The findings enable more reliable predictions of optimal projectile-target combinations for synthesizing new isotopes of elements Z=108 and Z=112, particularly favoring the reactions $^{30}$Si+$^{252}$Cf (2n, 3n, 4n channels) and $^{36}$S+$^{250}$Cm (4n channel) for producing new Z=112 isotopes.
