# Vacuum Structure in QCD with Quark and Gluon Condensates

> We consider here the vacuum structure in QCD with both quark and gluon condensates and a variational ansatz for the ground state. The method is nonperturbative using only equal time algebra for the field operators. We then find that a constrained energy minimisation of the Hamiltonian leads to a QCD vacuum with both quark and gluon condensates for $α_s > α_c=0.62$. Pion decay constant and the charge radius of the pion seem to fix the QCD coupling constant $α_s$ as 1.28. The approach opens up possibilities of relating the mysterious vacuum structure with common place hadronic properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a QCD vacuum with both quark and gluon condensates becomes energetically preferred over the perturbative vacuum only when the strong coupling constant exceeds a critical value of \(\alpha_c = 0.62\), and that the specific value \(\alpha_s = 1.28\) simultaneously reproduces the pion decay constant and the pion charge radius.

## Method

The authors construct a variational ansatz for the QCD ground state using unitary Bogoliubov transformations on the perturbative vacuum, with gaussian trial functions for gluon condensates and a momentum-dependent ansatz for quark condensates. They evaluate the expectation value of the QCD Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge using equal-time field algebra, then perform a constrained energy minimization where the SVZ gluon condensate parameter and the pion decay constant are fixed to their experimental values. The remaining variational parameters are minimized as functions of the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s\).

## Key numbers

* Critical coupling for vacuum destabilization: \(\alpha_c = 0.62\); for \(\alpha_s \leq 0.62\) the perturbative vacuum remains stable.
* At \(\alpha_s = 1.28\), the pion charge radius is reproduced as \(R_{ch} \simeq 0.65\) fm, agreeing with the experimental value of 0.66 fm from Amendolia (1986).
* Energy density at \(\alpha_s = 1.4\) is approximately \(-55\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
* The gluon mass parameter \(m_G\) is around 300 MeV, constant across couplings.
* At \(\alpha_s = 1.28\), the bag pressure \((-\epsilon_0)^{1/4} = 140\) MeV.
* At \(\alpha_s = 1.28\), the number density of perturbative gluons in the condensate vacuum is \(N_G \simeq 0.233\)/fm\(^3\) and of quarks is \(N_q \simeq 0.085\)/fm\(^3\).
* The SVZ gluon condensate parameter is fixed to \(0.012\) GeV\(^4\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier variational approach applied to pure gluon condensates in SU(3) Yang-Mills fields (Mishra et al., Pramana 1991) and to quark condensates in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models (Mishra and Misra, 1993). It also builds on the phenomenological ansatz of Mishra and Misra (1993) where no energy minimization was performed. The method enables a direct connection between the QCD coupling constant and low-energy hadronic properties such as the pion decay constant and charge radius, and opens the possibility of relating vacuum structure to hadronic observables in a nonperturbative framework.
