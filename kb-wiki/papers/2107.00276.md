# Treating divergence in quark matter by using energy projectors

> We calculate gluon self-energy using quark energy projectors in a general quark-gluon plasma. By separating the quark field into a positive- and a negative-energy mode, the quark loop constructed with the same mode is always convergent, and the divergence appears only in the mixed loop with different modes and is medium independent. After removing the divergence in vacuum, we obtain the one-loop gluon self-energy at finite temperature, chemical potential and quarks mass without approximation. With the method of quark loop resummation, we calculate non-perturbatively the gluon Debye mass and thermodynamic potential. In the limit of small gluon momentum in comparison with temperature, chemical potential and quark mass, our calculation comes back to the known HTL/HDL results in literature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by using energy projectors to separate quark fields into positive- and negative-energy modes, the one-loop gluon self-energy at finite temperature, chemical potential, and quark mass can be calculated without approximation, with the divergence appearing only in the mixed loop (positive- and negative-energy modes) and being medium independent, thus removable as a vacuum effect.

## Method

The authors rewrite the QCD Lagrangian in Nambu-Gorkov space and separate quark fields into positive- and negative-energy modes using energy projectors. They calculate the one-loop gluon self-energy by decomposing the quark loop into three contributions: two pure-mode loops (positive-positive and negative-negative) and one mixed-mode loop (positive-negative). After removing the vacuum divergence from the mixed loop, they apply quark loop resummation to compute the gluon Debye mass and thermodynamic potential non-perturbatively.

## Key numbers

* Debye mass for massless quarks at finite temperature and chemical potential: \( m_D^2 = g^2\left(\frac{N_c}{3} + \frac{N_f}{6}\right)T^2 + g^2\sum_f\frac{\mu_f^2}{2\pi^2} \), matching known HTL/HDL results.
* Contribution from the negative-energy quark loop to the Debye mass: \( m_{--}^2 = \frac{g^2}{2} \left(\frac{T^2}{6} + \frac{\mu_f^2}{2\pi^2}\right) \) for massless quarks.
* Gluon and ghost loop contribution to the Debye mass: \( m_G^2 = \frac{N_c}{3}g^2T^2 \).
* For nonzero quark mass, the negative-energy quark loop contribution to \( \overline\Pi_{--}^{00} \) is given by \( -\frac{g^2 m_f^3}{8\pi^2 T} \int_1^\infty dx \, x\sqrt{x^2-1} \sum_{s=\pm} f_F(x_s)(1-2f_F(x_s)) + \mathcal{O}(|{\bf q}|/m_f) \).

## Context

This work extends the energy-projector method previously used in studies of color superconductivity at high baryon density (Hong 2004, Nardulli 2002, Anglani 2011) and in non-relativistic QCD for heavy quarks (Bodwin 1994, Eichten 1989, Isgur 1989). The method provides a systematic way to handle divergences in one-loop calculations without relying on the hard thermal loop (HTL) or hard dense loop (HDL) approximations of Bellac (1996) and Kapusta (2006), while recovering their results in the limit of small gluon momentum compared to temperature, chemical potential, and quark mass. This enables non-perturbative calculations of thermodynamic properties in quark matter for general conditions.
