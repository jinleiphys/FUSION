# $U_A(1)$ symmetry restoration in QCD with $N_f$ flavors

> Recently, there have been reports that the chirally restored phase of QCD is effectively symmetric under $U(N_f) \times U(N_f)$ rather than $SU(N_f)\times SU(N_f)$. We supplement their argument by including the contributions from topologically nontrivial gauge field configurations and discuss how the conclusions are modified. General statements are made concerning the particle spectrum of QCD with light $N_f$ flavors in the high temperature chirally restored phase.

## Key claim

The chirally restored phase of QCD is not necessarily symmetric under \(U(N_f) \times U(N_f)\); topologically nontrivial gauge field configurations contribute to two-point functions in the chiral limit for \(N_f = 2\), and for \(N_f \ge 3\) the effects vanish only in the exact chiral limit, but in the real world with a finite strange quark mass the breaking may be non-negligible.

## Method

The authors analyze the Euclidean QCD partition function by separating contributions from gauge field sectors with topological charge \(\nu = 0, \pm 1\). They compute the quark condensate and two-point correlation functions for scalar and pseudoscalar mesons (e.g., \(\sigma\) and \(\delta\)), using the index theorem to relate zero modes to topological charge and expanding in powers of the quark mass \(m\). The analysis is performed for general \(N_f\), with explicit formulas for \(N_f = 2\) and generalizations to higher \(N_f\).

## Key numbers

- For \(N_f = 2\), the topological (\(\nu = \pm 1\)) contribution to the difference between \(\sigma\) and \(\delta\) two-point functions remains nonzero even in the chiral limit (\(m \to 0\)).
- For \(N_f \ge 3\), the topological contribution to two-point functions is of order \(O(m^{N_f-2})\) and vanishes in the chiral limit.
- In the real world with \(SU(3)\) flavor, the strange quark mass \(m_s \sim 150\) MeV leads to a possibly non-negligible topological effect even at high temperature.
- The topological correction to the quark condensate \(\langle \bar{q} q \rangle\) starts at \(O(m^{N_f-1})\) for general \(N_f\).

## Context

This work extends and critiques the argument by Cohen (1995) that the chirally restored phase of QCD is effectively symmetric under \(U(N_f) \times U(N_f)\). The authors show that Cohen’s conclusion, which omitted contributions from topologically nontrivial gauge configurations, holds only for \(N_f \ge 3\) in the exact chiral limit. The paper builds on the instanton calculus of 't Hooft (1976) and the finite-temperature instanton formalism of Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981). It enables future quantitative studies of \(U_A(1)\) breaking at high temperature, depending on the density of topological configurations.
