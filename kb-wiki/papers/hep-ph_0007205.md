# On the masses, branching ratios, and full widths of heavy rho', rho'' and omega', omega'' resonances

> Based on the previous and recent fits of multiple data on the reactions of e^+e^- annihilation, tau lepton decay, and the reaction K^-p -> pi^+pi^- Lambda, the magnitude of the branching ratios and total widths of the isovector rho', rho'', and the isoscalar omega', omega'' resonances are calculated. Some topics on the spectroscopy of the rho(1450) and omega(1420) states are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper calculates branching ratios and total widths for the heavy vector mesons rho'(1450), rho''(1700), omega'(1420), and omega''(1600) using previously extracted coupling constants, but the results carry large uncertainties and the resonances are so wide that their visible peak positions are significantly shifted downward from their bare masses, complicating spectroscopic identification.

## Method

The authors take the masses and coupling constants previously extracted from fits to e+e- annihilation, tau lepton decay, and K-p -> pi+pi-Lambda data (Achasov and Kozhevnikov 1997, 1998) and compute partial widths and their errors. They then divide each partial width by the sum of central values to obtain branching ratios, incorporating new data on e+e- -> pi+pi-pi0 (CMD-2 1998, SND 1999), e+e- -> omega pi0 (SND 2000), and tau lepton decays (CLEO 2000) to update the resonance parameters.

## Key numbers

- For rho'(1450) from the pi+pi- channel: mass = 1370 (+90/-70) MeV, total width = 763 +/- 500 MeV, leptonic width = 6.4 (+1.2/-1.4) keV
- For rho''(1700) from the omega pi0 channel: mass = 1710 +/- 90 MeV, total width = 1886 +/- 613 MeV, leptonic width = 5.2 +/- 1.5 keV
- For rho''(1700) from the tau- -> (4pi)- nu_tau channel: mass = 1860 (+260/-160) MeV, total width = 3255 +/- 388 MeV, leptonic width = 9.3 +/- 0.6 keV
- For omega'(1420) from the pi+pi-pi0 channel: mass = 1430 (+110/-70) MeV, total width ~ 903 MeV, leptonic width = 144 (+94/-58) eV
- For omega''(1600) from the pi+pi-pi0 channel: mass = 1940 (+170/-130) MeV, total width ~ 14000 MeV, leptonic width = 109 (+58/-46) eV
- Estimated VP decay widths: Gamma(rho'_1 -> omega pi) ~ 280 MeV, Gamma(omega'_1 -> rho pi) ~ 820 MeV at mass 1400 MeV; Gamma(rho'_2 -> omega pi) ~ 880 MeV, Gamma(omega'_2 -> rho pi) ~ 2600 MeV at mass 1750 MeV
- Radial wave function at origin for omega'(1420) as 2^3S1 state: |R_S(0)|^2 = (25 +/- 10) x 10^-3 GeV^3, compared to (38 +/- 4) x 10^-3 GeV^3 for omega(782)

## Context

This work extends the unified approach of Achasov and Kozhevnikov (1997, 1998) that treats mixing via common decay modes among heavy resonances and the ground state rho(770) and omega(782). The results address the long-standing confusion about whether the rho(1300) state reported by LASS and the omega(1200) state reported by SND are distinct resonances or the same as rho(1450) and omega(1420) respectively, with the peak shift explained by large widths and rapid phase space growth. The findings enable future tests of the conventional q-qbar assignment for these states, particularly questioning whether rho''(1700) and omega''(1600) can be the 1^3D1 states predicted to be relatively narrow by Godfrey and Isgur (1985).
