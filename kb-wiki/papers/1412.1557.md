# The $η'N$ interaction from a chiral effective model and $η'$-$N$ bound state

> The $η'$ mass reduction in the nuclear medium is expected from the degeneracy of the pseudoscalar-singlet and octet mesons when chiral symmetry is manifest. In this study, we investigate the $η'N$ 2body interaction which is the foundation of the in-medium $η'$ properties using the linear sigma model as a chiral effective model. The $η'N$ interaction in the linear sigma model comes from the scalar meson exchange with U$_A$(1) symmetry effect and is found to be fairly strong attraction. Moreover, the $ηN$ transition is included in our calculation, and is important for the imaginary part of the $η'$-optical potential. The transition amplitude of $η'N$ to the $ηN$ channel is relatively small compared to that of elastic channel. From the analysis of the $η'N$ 2body system, we find a $η'N$ bound state with the binding energy $12.3-3.3i$MeV. We expect that this strongly attractive two body interaction leads to a deep and attractive optical potential.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the η'N two-body interaction, derived from a chiral effective model (linear sigma model), is strongly attractive and produces a η'N bound state with binding energy 12.3−3.3i MeV. The hedge is that this result depends on the assumption of 35% reduction of the quark condensate at normal nuclear density and on the natural renormalization scheme used to handle loop divergences.

## Method

The authors used the linear sigma model with three-flavor chiral symmetry and U_A(1) anomaly effects to compute the η'N scattering amplitude at tree level, where the interaction arises from scalar meson exchange. They then solved a coupled-channel scattering equation (including η'N, ηN, and πN channels) using the tree-level amplitude as a momentum-independent interaction kernel, with loop integrals renormalized via the natural renormalization scheme. The η'N bound state was identified by searching for a pole in the complex energy plane of the T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- η'N binding energy: 12.3−3.3i MeV (complex value, imaginary part corresponds to half-width)
- η'N scattering length: −1.91+0.24i fm
- η'N effective range: 0.24−7.6×10⁻³i fm
- η'N threshold energy: 1896.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of in-medium η' properties by Nagahiro (2006) and Sakai (2013), and is methodologically analogous to the analysis of the K̅N system where the Λ(1405) appears as a quasi-bound state (Hyodo 2012). The results enable predictions for η'-nucleus bound states and provide a microscopic foundation for the η' optical potential in nuclear matter, which is relevant for interpreting transparency ratio experiments (Nanova 2013) and pp→ppη' reactions (Moskal 2000, 2014).
