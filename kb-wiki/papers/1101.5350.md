# New solution to the puzzle of the long lifetime of 14C

> A new cluster model solution to the long standing nuclear structure problem of describing the anomalously long lifetime of 14C is presented. Related beta-decay data for 14O to states in 14N, gamma decay data between low lying positive parity states in 14N and the elastic and inelastic magnetic dipole electron scattering from 14N data are all shown to be very accurately described by the model. The shapes of the beta spectra for the A=14 system are also well reproduced by the model. The model invokes four-nucleon tetrahedral symmetric spatial correlations arising from three- and four-nucleon interactions which yields a high degree of SU(4) singlet structure for the clusters and a tetrahedral intrinsic shape for the doubly magic 16O ground state. The large quadrupole moment of the 14N ground state is obtained here for the first time and arises because of the almost 100% d-wave deuteron-like-hole cluster structure inherent in the model.

## Key claim

The anomalously long lifetime of 14C is explained by a cluster model invoking four-nucleon tetrahedral symmetric spatial correlations in the 16O reference state, where the large difference in the η coefficients between the 14C and 14O ground states (arising from electromagnetic spin-orbit effects) leads to the large ratio of Gamow-Teller matrix elements for the two mirror beta decays, with the caveat that the model requires renormalized axial and magnetic couplings to achieve agreement with data.

## Method

The author constructs wave functions for A=14 nuclei using a cluster model where the 16O ground state is treated as four alpha-like clusters arranged in a tetrahedral (Td) symmetry, with two-nucleon holes extracted either from the same cluster (yielding S- and D-states) or from different clusters (yielding P-states). Energy matrices are diagonalized for the T=0 (3×3) and T=1 (2×2) sectors using spin-orbit interactions including both nuclear and electromagnetic components, and the resulting wave function admixtures are used to compute beta-decay observables with renormalized axial and magnetic coupling constants.

## Context

This work extends the alpha-cluster model approach dating back to Wheeler (1937) and the author's own earlier work on quark-model-based spatial correlations (Robson, summarized in 2005), providing a solution to the long-standing problem of the 14C lifetime that had resisted explanation by conventional shell-model approaches using two p-shell holes in 16O. The model contradicts the findings of Towner and Hardy (2008) for the 14C beta-decay log(f_A t) value (9.04 vs. 8.72 from their PWBT shell model), while agreeing with their results for 14O decay, and enables future calculations of other observables in the A=14 system using the tetrahedral cluster framework.
