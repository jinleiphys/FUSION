# Toward CP-even Neutrino Beam

> The best method of measuring CP violating effect in neutrino oscillation experiments is to construct and use a neutrino beam made of an ideal mixture of $\barν_e$ and $ν_e$ of monochromatic lines. The conceptual design of such a beam is described, together with how to measure the CP-odd quantity. We propose to exploit an accelerated unstable hydrogen-like heavy ion in a storage ring, whose decay has both electron capture and bound beta decay with a comparable fraction.

## Key claim

A CP-even neutrino beam, a mixture of monochromatic \(\nu_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_e\) that yields equal numbers of \(\mu^+\) and \(\mu^-\) after oscillation if CP is conserved, can be realized by simultaneously using the electron-capture (EC) and bound-beta (\(b\beta\)) decays of accelerated hydrogen-like heavy ions in a storage ring, provided the ions are chosen such that the EC and \(b\beta\) branching ratios are comparable.

## Method

The authors propose to accelerate unstable hydrogen-like heavy ions (e.g., \(^{108}_{47}\mathrm{Ag}^{46+}\)) in a storage ring; these ions undergo both electron capture (producing monochromatic \(\nu_e\)) and bound beta decay (producing monochromatic \(\bar{\nu}_e\)). The neutrino energy is tuned by the Lorentz factor \(\gamma\) of the stored beam, and the optimal detection energy is chosen near 1000 MeV, above the muon production threshold but below multi-pion production. The CP-odd asymmetry is extracted from the difference between the appearance probabilities \(P(\nu_e \rightarrow \nu_\mu)\) and \(P(\bar{\nu}_e \rightarrow \bar{\nu}_\mu)\), measured at the first oscillation peak (\(L/E \simeq 517\ \mathrm{km}/1000\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).

## Key numbers

*   The best single-ion candidate is \(^{108}_{47}\mathrm{Ag}^{46+}\), with a half-life of 2.36 min; its monochromatic \(b\beta(1s)\) line has energy 1.671 MeV and branching ratio 0.0149, while its EC(1s) line has energy 1.897 MeV and branching ratio 0.0087.
*   For \(^{108}_{47}\mathrm{Ag}^{46+}\), the continuum beta (\(c\beta\)) decay has endpoint energy 1.649 MeV and branching ratio 0.9479.
*   The CP-evenness \(\eta\) for a \(^{108}_{47}\mathrm{Ag}^{46+}\) beam with an iron detector, selecting the four major lines and neglecting energy dependence of cross sections, is \(\eta \approx 0.09\); including the \(c\beta\) contribution integrated over an 8 keV interval below threshold gives \(\eta \approx 0\).
*   The optimal baseline-to-energy ratio for CP sensitivity is \(L/E \simeq 517\ \mathrm{km}/1000\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
*   For a multi-ion beam, the 1s \(b\beta\) event rate from \(^{122}_{48}\mathrm{Cd}^{48+}\) is larger by a factor \(\approx 12\) than that of \(^{108}_{47}\mathrm{Ag}^{46+}\), and the EC rate from \(^{152}_{70}\mathrm{Yb}\) is larger by \(\approx 600\) than that of \(^{108}_{47}\mathrm{Ag}^{46+}\).
*   The monochromatic neutrino energies for the multi-ion candidates are 3.0 MeV (1s \(b\beta\)) for \(^{122}_{48}\mathrm{Cd}^{48+}\) and 5.0 MeV (1s EC) for \(^{152}_{70}\mathrm{Yb}\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of monochromatic neutrino beams from electron capture, proposed by Sato (2000) and Bernabeu (2000), and the beta-beam concept of Zucchelli (2002), by combining both \(\nu_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_e\) from a single ion species to create a CP-even beam. It enables a direct measurement of the CP-violating phase \(\delta\) and the mixing angle \(\theta_{13}\) in neutrino oscillations, using a detector placed at the first oscillation peak. The authors note that measured EC rates in hydrogen-like ions (e.g., \(^{140}\mathrm{Pr}^{58+}\)) can be enhanced by angular momentum effects, which may further improve the feasibility of the proposed scheme.
