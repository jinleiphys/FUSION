# Vector Meson Production in Coherent Hadronic Interactions: An update on predictions for RHIC and LHC

> In this letter we update our predictions for the photoproduction of vector mesons in coherent $pp$ and $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies using the color dipole approach and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism. In particular, we present our predictions for the first run of the LHC at half energy and for the rapidity dependence of the ratio between the $J/Ψ$ and $ρ$ cross sections at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The authors update their previous predictions for vector meson photoproduction in coherent hadron-hadron collisions, now providing results for the first LHC runs at half energy (pp at 7 TeV, PbPb at 2.76 TeV) and for the rapidity dependence of the J/Ψ-to-ρ cross-section ratio at RHIC, using the Color Glass Condensate formalism with the Iancu-Itakura-Munier (IIM) saturation model for nuclear targets and the non-forward saturation model of Marquet, Peschanski, and Soyez (MPS) for proton targets.

## Method

The cross section for coherent vector meson photoproduction is computed by factorizing the equivalent photon flux of the hadron projectile and the photon-target production cross section, using the color dipole approach. The elementary dipole-target scattering amplitude is obtained from the IIM saturation model for photon-nucleus interactions and from the MPS non-forward saturation model for photon-proton interactions, with the dipole-nucleon cross section taken from the IIM model. The rapidity distribution is derived from the relation between photon energy and rapidity of the produced meson.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at LHC (√s = 7 TeV): central rapidity cross section dσ/dy(y=0) ≈ 6.5 nb for J/Ψ and 18 pb for Υ.
- For pp collisions at LHC (√s = 7 TeV): integrated cross section 63.70 nb for J/Ψ (event rate 637.0 s⁻¹) and 0.18 nb for Υ (event rate 1.80 s⁻¹), assuming design luminosity 10⁷ mb⁻¹s⁻¹.
- For AuAu collisions at RHIC: integrated cross section 609.7 mb for ρ (event rate 256.0 s⁻¹) and 0.51 mb for J/Ψ (event rate 0.20 s⁻¹), assuming luminosity 0.4 mb⁻¹s⁻¹.
- For PbPb collisions at LHC (√s = 2.76 TeV): central rapidity cross section dσ/dy(y=0) ≈ 470 mb for ρ and 3.8 mb for J/Ψ.
- For PbPb collisions at LHC (√s = 2.76 TeV): integrated cross section 4276 mb for ρ (event rate 1796.0 s⁻¹) and 20 mb for J/Ψ (event rate 8.40 s⁻¹), assuming design luminosity 0.42 mb⁻¹s⁻¹.
- Ratio of ρ to J/Ψ cross sections at RHIC central rapidity: dσ(ρ⁰)/dy / dσ(J/Ψ)/dy ≈ 1.2 × 10³.
- Predictions at √s = 7 TeV are almost a factor 2 smaller for J/Ψ and a factor 4 smaller for Υ compared to previous results for full LHC energy.
- Predictions for J/Ψ in AuAu at RHIC are ≈ 10% larger than previous results using the GBW model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of J/Ψ, Υ, and ρ production at RHIC and full LHC energies (Goncalves and Machado, 2009-2010) by providing predictions for the first LHC runs at reduced energies and for the rapidity-dependent J/Ψ-to-ρ ratio at RHIC, which is under analysis by the STAR collaboration. The results enable experimental comparisons at RHIC and LHC that can test saturation physics, particularly the different energy dependence predicted for light versus heavy vector mesons. The ratio observable is expected to have small sensitivity to corrections from mutual nuclear excitation (Nystrand, 2005).
