# Recent Results from Jefferson Lab

> Recent results on studies of the structure of nucleons and nuclei in the regime of strong interaction QCD are discussed. Use of high current polarized electron beams, polarized targets, and recoil polarimeters, in conjunction with modern spectrometers and detector instrumentation allow much more detailed studies of nucleon and nuclear structure than has been possible in the past. The CEBAF accelerator at Jefferson Lab was build to study the internal structure of hadrons in a regime where confinement is important and strong interaction QCD is the relevant theory. I discuss how the first experiments already make significant contributions towards an improved understanding of hadronic structure.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Jefferson Lab's first experiments are already making significant contributions to understanding hadronic structure in the intermediate-distance regime where confinement is important and strong interaction QCD is the relevant theory, but the author explicitly notes that "QCD has not been solved for processes at intermediate distance scales" and that "the connection to the fundamentals of QCD remains poorly established" in this region.

## Method

The paper reviews results from multiple experiments at the CEBAF accelerator (maximum energy ~6 GeV) using polarized electron beams, polarized targets (NH3, ND3, 3He), and recoil polarimeters with modern spectrometers and detector instrumentation. Measurements include elastic electron-nucleon scattering with double polarization techniques to extract form factors, inclusive and exclusive meson electroproduction to study baryon resonances, parity-violating electron scattering to probe strangeness contributions, and deuteron elastic scattering and photodisintegration to study nuclear structure at high momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- HAPPEX experiment at Q2 = 0.47 GeV2 measured GsE + 0.4 GsM = 0.023 ± 0.034 (stat) ± 0.022 (syst) ± 0.026 (GnE), showing small strangeness contributions
- Deuteron charge form factor GC(Q2) shows a zero crossing at Q2 = 0.7 GeV2
- For the Δ(1232) transition, |E1+/M1+| < 5% up to Q2 = 4 GeV2 from Hall C pπ0 production
- CLAS collected ~5 × 105 pη' events in photoproduction
- GDH integral measurements planned for Q2 range 0.1 – 2.0 GeV2
- Deuteron photodisintegration scaling (dσ/dt ∝ 1/sn-2 with n-2 = 11) observed at θcm near 90° for photon energies from 1 GeV to 4 GeV, but not at smaller angles

## Context

This review extends earlier work from Bonn, DESY, CEA, and SLAC on nucleon form factors, and addresses open problems identified in the quark model of Isgur (1980s) and the Bloom-Gilman duality observation (1970s). The results enable tests of chiral perturbation theory at low Q2, constrain models of the GDH sum rule evolution by Burkert and Ioffe (1990s) and Soffer (1990s), and provide data for the N* program searching for missing quark model states predicted by Isgur and others. The paper also looks ahead to measuring generalized parton distributions as proposed by Ji and Radyushkin (1990s).
