# Shape and blocking effects on odd-even mass differences and rotational motion of nuclei

> Nuclear shapes and odd-nucleon blockings strongly influence the odd-even differences of nuclear masses. When such effects are taken into account, the determination of the pairing strength is modified resulting in larger pair gaps. The modified pairing strength leads to an improved self-consistent description of moments of inertia and backbending frequencies, with no additional parameters.

# Shape and blocking effects on odd-even mass differences and rotational motion of nuclei

## Key claim
When shape effects (from deformed mean fields and deformation changes) and odd-nucleon blocking effects are taken into account, the pairing strength G determined from odd-even mass differences increases by about 5–10%, leading to larger pair gaps and an improved self-consistent description of both moments of inertia and backbending frequencies without additional parameters. The abstract glosses over the fact that the average gap method for determining pairing strengths does not have the proper particle-number and deformation dependence, and that each nucleus may require its own separate determination of G.

## Method
The authors used the deformed Woods-Saxon model with Strutinsky energy decomposition (macroscopic liquid-drop plus microscopic shell and pairing corrections) and the Lipkin-Nogami method with approximate particle-number projection, including both monopole and quadrupole pairing. They calculated theoretical odd-even mass differences using a five-point formula along isotopic chains for even-even rare-earth nuclei (Er, Yb, Hf, W isotopes), separating shape effects (from shell-correction and macroscopic deformation energies) and blocking effects (from odd-nucleon blocking in adjacent odd-particle systems), then adjusted the pairing strength by a constant factor F to reproduce experimental odd-even mass differences. The consistency was tested by calculating moments of inertia and backbending frequencies using the pairing-deformation self-consistent cranked shell model.

## Key numbers
- Shape effects are usually of the order of 100 to 200 keV for even-even Er, Yb, Hf and W isotopes.
- Blocking effects are usually about -200 to -400 keV for the rare-earth nuclei studied.
- Combined shape and blocking effects range mostly from -100 to -300 keV, or about 10% to 30% of the corresponding odd-even mass differences.
- The average scaling factor for neutrons is F_nu = 1.08 and for protons F_pi = 1.05, resulting in increases of Lipkin-Nogami pairing gaps by about 25% for neutrons and 15% for protons.
- For individual nuclei, non-average F values are mostly in the range of 1.05–1.10 for neutrons and 1.03–1.08 for protons.
- In 178Hf, the Delta+lambda_2 values from the average gap method over-estimated experimental odd-even mass differences by 166 keV (neutrons) and 190 keV (protons); in 180W by 98 keV (neutrons) and 125 keV (protons).
- Deformation changes due to G adjustments are very small: |Delta beta_2| < 0.003 and |Delta beta_4| <= 0.002 for nuclei that are not soft.
- Quadrupole pairing contribution to binding energies is usually less than 30 keV in magnitude.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Satula et al. (1998) on mean-field effects in odd-even mass differences from light- and medium-mass nuclei to heavy rare-earth deformed nuclei, showing that shape and blocking effects are significant (10–30% of odd-even mass differences) and cannot be neglected. The paper addresses the long-standing problem that cranking calculations with monopole pairing alone cannot simultaneously describe both moments of inertia and band-crossing frequencies, as noted by R. Bengtsson (1983) and T. Bengtsson (1989). The results establish a consistent relation between mass differences, moments of inertia, and excitation energies of high-seniority states, and agree with the need for adjusted pairing strength found in calculations of multi-quasiparticle state energies by Xu et al. (1998).
