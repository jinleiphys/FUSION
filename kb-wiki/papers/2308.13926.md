# The Schrödinger Representation and 3d Gauge Theories

> In this review we consider the Hamiltonian analysis of Yang-Mills theory and some variants of it in three spacetime dimensions using the Schrödinger representation. This representation, although technically more involved than the usual covariant formulation, may be better suited for some nonperturbative issues. Specifically for the Yang-Mills theory, we explain how to set up the Hamiltonian formulation in terms of manifestly gauge-invariant variables and set up an expansion scheme for solving the Schrödinger equation. We review the calculation of the string tension, the Casimir energy and the propagator mass and compare with the results from lattice simulations. The computation of the first set of corrections to the string tension, string breaking effects, extensions to the Yang-Mills-Chern-Simons theory and to the supersymmetric cases are also discussed. We also comment on how entanglement for the vacuum state can be formulated in terms of the BFK gluing formula. The review concludes with a discussion of the status and prospects of this approach.

## Key claim

The Schrödinger representation, while technically more involved than covariant perturbation theory, may be better suited for nonperturbative issues in 2+1 dimensional Yang-Mills theory, and the analysis yields quantitative results for the string tension and Casimir energy that compare favorably with lattice simulations, subject to certain approximations and caveats explained in detail.

## Method

The author sets up the Hamiltonian formulation of Yang-Mills theory in 2+1 dimensions using the Schrödinger representation, parametrizing the complex components of the gauge potentials as \(A = -\partial M M^{-1}\) and \(\bar{A} = M^{\dagger -1} \bar{\partial} M^\dagger\) where \(M\) is an \(SL(N,\mathbb{C})\) matrix. The gauge-invariant volume element on the space of fields modulo gauge transformations is calculated, and the Hamiltonian is expressed in terms of gauge-invariant variables, specifically a current \(J \sim \partial H H^{-1}\) with \(H = M^\dagger M\). A systematic expansion scheme for solving the Schrödinger equation is developed, and the vacuum wave function is used to compute the string tension and Casimir energy.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Feynman (1981) who analyzed Yang-Mills theory in 2+1 dimensions and argued that the space of gauge-invariant configurations might lead to a discrete spectrum and mass gap, and by Singer who showed the configuration space is not compact but has positive sectional curvature. The approach builds on developments after those works, specifically using an exact expression for the volume element of the configuration space from KKN1-KKN4 and KN5. The results enable analytic comparison with lattice simulations for nonperturbative quantities in 2+1 dimensional Yang-Mills theory, which is relevant for understanding the high-temperature limit of 4d Yang-Mills theory where the 3d mass gap becomes the magnetic mass controlling screening of magnetic fields in the gluon plasma, as discussed by Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe.
