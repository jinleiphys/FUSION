# New mechanism for the production of the extremely fast light particles in heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi energy domain

> Employing a four-body classical model, various mechanisms responsible for the production of fast light particles in heavy ion collisions at low and intermediate energies have been studied. It has been shown that at energies lower than 50 A MeV, light particles of velocities of more than two times higher than the projectile velocities are produced due to the acceleration of the target light-particles by the mean field of the incident nucleus. It has also been shown that precision experimental reaction research in normal and inverse kinematics is likely to provide vital information about which mechanism is dominant in the production of fast light particles.

## Key claim

At beam energies below 50 A MeV, light particles with velocities more than twice the projectile velocity are produced primarily by acceleration of target light particles in the mean field of the incident nucleus, rather than by the previously assumed mechanisms of projectile breakup or nucleon-nucleon collisions. This mechanism is claimed to dominate the high-energy portion of the pre-equilibrium particle spectrum only at energies per nucleon below the Fermi energy.

## Method

A four-body classical model was used, treating projectile and target nuclei as bound two-body systems composed of a heavy core and a light fragment moving in a Woods-Saxon potential. The model includes six pair potentials, dissipative forces between heavy cores, and an absorption probability for light particles traversing nuclear matter, with parameters from Perey (1974) and Gross (1978). Trajectories were calculated for 15 reaction channels, and the maximum energies of pre-equilibrium neutrons were computed as a function of beam energy for the reaction 20Ne + 165Ho.

## Key numbers

- At beam energies below the Fermi energy, the maximum neutron energy from the target acceleration mechanism reaches approximately 8 times the beam energy per nucleon (E_n,max ≈ 8E_0), corresponding to a velocity more than 2.5 times the beam velocity.
- For the "splashing out" mechanism from the projectile, the maximum neutron energy is approximately 3E_0.
- For nucleon-nucleon collisions, the maximum neutron energy is approximately 4E_0, but this mechanism is suppressed by Pauli blocking at low energies.
- The calculations were performed for the reaction 20Ne + 165Ho → n + X at beam energies from approximately 10 to 100 A MeV, with the Fermi energy indicated by an arrow in Figure 2.
- For the reactions 40Ar + 51V and 132Xe + 51V at 44 A MeV, the pre-equilibrium proton spectrum from the 132Xe-induced reaction extends several tens of MeV higher than that from the 40Ar-induced reaction.

## Context

This work extends the four-body classical model of Denikin and Zagrebaev (2002) to identify a new mechanism for fast light particle production, contradicting the earlier assumption that such particles originate primarily from the projectile nucleus via "splashing out" (Mohring, 1985; Zagrebaev, 1990) or from nucleon-nucleon collisions (Bondorf, 1980; Randrup, 1987). The paper proposes that comparing reactions in normal and inverse kinematics, such as 40Ar + 51V versus 132Xe + 51V, can experimentally distinguish which mechanism dominates, enabling future precision measurements (Penionzhkevich, 1998; Alba, 1994; Sapienza, 1998) to test the model's predictions.
