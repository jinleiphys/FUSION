# Relativistic Three-Body Scattering in a First Order Faddeev Formulation

> Relativistic Faddeev equations for three-body scattering at arbitrary energies are solved in first order in the two-body transition operator in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. Relativistic invariance is incorporated within the framework of Poincar{é} invariant quantum mechanics. Based on a Malfliet-Tjon type interaction, observables for elastic and breakup scattering are calculated and compared to the nonrelativistic ones.

# Relativistic Three-Body Scattering in a First Order Faddeev Formulation

## Key claim

Relativistic Faddeev equations for three-body scattering can be solved in first order in the two-body transition operator using momentum vectors without partial wave decomposition, and when phase-shift-equivalent interactions are used, the relativistic and nonrelativistic three-body cross sections are very close, suggesting that in a first-order calculation, relativistic effects are mostly contained in the two-body amplitude rather than in the three-body dynamics.

## Method

The authors solve the leading-order term of the Faddeev multiple scattering series within Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics, using momentum vectors directly without partial wave decomposition. A Malfliet-Tjon type interaction (short-range repulsive and intermediate-range attractive Yukawa forces) is employed, and the relativistic two-body t-matrix is constructed from a nonrelativistic one using the Coester-Piper-Serduke (CPS) method to ensure phase-shift equivalence. The Poincaré invariant transition amplitude of the two-body subsystem embedded in the three-particle Hilbert space is obtained via a first resolvent method.

## Key numbers

- The Malfliet-Tjon type interaction supports a bound state at E_d = -2.23 MeV.
- Total elastic cross sections are calculated for projectile kinetic energies from 10 MeV up to 1 GeV.
- Differential cross sections for elastic scattering are shown at 0.5 GeV projectile energy as a function of laboratory scattering angle.
- Inclusive breakup cross sections are calculated at 0.495 GeV laboratory projectile kinetic energy for emission angles of 18 degrees and 24 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Liu (2004) who solved Faddeev equations directly as functions of vector variables for scattering at intermediate energies, and applies it to relativistic three-boson scattering using Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics. The relativistic interaction is constructed via the Coester-Piper-Serduke (CPS) method to be phase-shift equivalent to the nonrelativistic one. The results enable future full solutions of the relativistic Faddeev equation, where additional relativistic effects beyond the first-order treatment are expected to appear.
