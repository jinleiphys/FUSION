# Dynamics aspect of subbarrier fusion reaction in light heavy ion systems

> Subbarrier fusion of the 7Li + 12C reaction is studied using an antisymmetrized molecular dynamics model (AMD) with an after burner, GEMINI. In AMD, 7Li shows an α+ t structure at its ground state and it is significantly deformed. Simulations are made near the Coulomb barrier energies, i.e., E_{cm} = 3 - 8 MeV. The total fusion cross section of the AMD + GEMINI calculations as a function of incident energy is compared to the experimental results and both are in good agreement at E_{cm} > 3 MeV. The cross section for the different residue channels of the AMD + GEMINI at E_{cm} = 5 MeV is also compared to the experimental results.

## Key claim

The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model combined with the GEMINI statistical decay code reproduces the experimental total fusion cross section for the 7Li + 12C reaction reasonably well at center-of-mass energies above 3 MeV, but underestimates it below that energy because the AMD formulation does not incorporate quantum tunneling through the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors used the AMD code of Ono et al. with the Gogny interaction to generate initial 7Li and 12C nuclei, then simulated 7Li + 12C collisions at center-of-mass energies from 3 to 8 MeV over impact parameters from 0 to 7 fm. The AMD calculations were run for 3000 fm/c at lower energies and 1000 fm/c at higher energies, after which clusters were identified using a coalescence technique with a 5 fm coordinate-space radius. The excited primary fragments were then cooled using the statistical decay code GEMINI (C++ version), and fusion events were defined by the emission of fragments with atomic number Z > 6.

## Key numbers

- The calculated binding energy of 7Li is 40.00 MeV (experimental: 39.24 MeV) and its root-mean-square radius is 3.02 fm (experimental: 2.43 fm), about 20% larger than experiment.
- The calculated binding energy of 12C is 92.24 MeV (experimental: 92.16 MeV) and its root-mean-square radius is 2.53 fm (experimental: 2.47 fm).
- Simulations were performed at center-of-mass energies from 3 to 8 MeV, with impact parameters from 0 to 7 fm; no fusion reactions were observed for impact parameters above 7 fm.
- Complete fusion (19F formation) occurs in about 80% of cases at lower incident energies and decreases to about 70% at higher energies.
- At E_cm = 5 MeV, the relative cross sections of major decay channels from AMD+GEMINI are compared to experimental results from Mukherjee (1996), with the 15N + alpha channel being the main discrepancy.

## Context

This work extends studies of subbarrier fusion reactions involving weakly bound and cluster-structured nuclei, motivated by debates over whether halo nuclei enhance or suppress fusion cross sections (Hussein 1992; Hagino 2000). The authors use AMD simulations to investigate how the well-developed alpha + triton cluster structure of 7Li and the 3-alpha structure of 12C affect fusion dynamics and exit channel distributions. The results enable further study of the suppression of the 15N + alpha channel in experimental data, which the authors note is not yet fully understood.
