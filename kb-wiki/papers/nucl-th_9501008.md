# The Charge Response of a Meson-Correlated Relativistic Fermi Gas

> The quasielastic longitudinal electromagnetic response function R_L is studied within the context of a model that extends our previous investigations of pionic correlations and currents. Four mesons are now employed (pion, rho, sigma and omega, via the Bonn potential) and the many-body dynamics are extended to the full random-phase approximation built upon a Hartree-Fock basis. Wherever possible the Lorentz covariance of the problem is respected. The first three energy-weighted moments of the reduced response are computed, namely, the zeroth moment (Coulomb sum rule), the first moment (related to the position of the quasielastic peak) and the second moment (related to the peak width or variance). We discuss how with a modest downward adjustment of the Fermi momentum it is possible to obtain the expected zeroth and second moments; this implies that the nuclear system has a lower density than that required when using the free relativistic Fermi gas and accordingly that the interaction effects are weaker than one might initially find.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by applying a "principle of invariance of the variance", allowing a modest downward adjustment of the Fermi momentum (by about 10% from 225 MeV/c), the meson-correlated relativistic Fermi gas model can reproduce the expected zeroth and second energy-weighted moments of the longitudinal charge response, implying that the nuclear system has a lower effective density than the free relativistic Fermi gas would suggest.

## Method

The authors extended their previous relativistic Fermi gas model by including four mesons (pion, rho, sigma, and omega) from the Bonn potential and computing the longitudinal electromagnetic response function R_L within the fully antisymmetrized random-phase approximation built upon a Hartree-Fock basis. They calculated the first three energy-weighted moments of the reduced response (Coulomb sum rule, mean energy, and variance) for momentum transfers between 300 MeV/c and 1 GeV/c, and then searched for a renormalized Fermi momentum that would preserve the variance (half-width) of the response.

## Key numbers

- The standard Fermi momentum used as reference is k_F = 225 MeV/c, chosen from analysis of carbon-12 data.
- The renormalized Fermi momentum values required to preserve the variance are always lower than 225 MeV/c by about 10%.
- The Hartree-Fock field induces a particle effective mass m_N*/m_N ranging from 0.77 at q=300 MeV/c to 0.86 at q=1000 MeV/c (hole effective mass is 0.67 m_N).
- The Fock self-energy of the rho meson alone would harden the charge response by about 20 MeV at q=500 MeV/c for k_F=225 MeV/c.
- The momentum range studied extends from 300 MeV/c to 1 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by Barbaro, De Pace, Donnelly, and Molinari (1990, 1993) that studied pionic correlations and currents in the relativistic Fermi gas model, now incorporating four mesons from the Bonn potential and full RPA dynamics. The finding that pionic effects are enhanced rather than suppressed by shorter-range nuclear forces contradicts the expectation from ground-state energy calculations where pion contributions are suppressed in spin-isospin saturated systems. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from electron scattering once meson-exchange currents and two-particle-two-hole excitations are included, and relate to polarized (p,n) experiments at LAMPF reanalyzed by Taddeucci (1994) that showed compatibility with pion-induced softening of the spin-longitudinal response.
