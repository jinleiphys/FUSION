# Mixed Species Charge and Baryon Balance Functions Studies with PYTHIA

> Mixed species charge and baryon balance functions are computed based on proton--proton (pp) collisions simulated with the PYTHIA8 model. Simulations are performed with selected values of the collision energy $\sqrt{s}$ and the Monash tune and the Ropes and Shoving modes of PYTHIA8 to explore whether such measurements provide useful new information and constraints on mechanisms of particle production in pp collisions. Charge balance functions are studied based on mixed pairs of pions, kaons, and protons, whereas baryon balance functions are computed for mixed low mass strange and non-strange baryons. Both charge and baryon balance functions of mixed particle pairs feature shapes and amplitudes that sensitively depend on the particle considered owing largely to the particle production mechanisms implemented in PYTHIA. The evolution of balance functions integrals with the longitudinal width of the acceptance are presented and one finds that sums of such integrals for a given reference particle obey expected sum rules for both charge and baryon balance functions. Additionally, both types of balance functions are found to evolve in shape and amplitude with increasing collision energy $\sqrt{s}$ and the PYTHIA tunes considered.

## Key claim

Mixed species charge and baryon balance functions computed from PYTHIA8 simulations of proton-proton collisions show sensitivity to the specific PYTHIA tune (Monash, Ropes, Shoving) and collision energy, but the paper hedges that these are exploratory simulations with ideal acceptance (full 4π coverage and no transverse momentum cuts) and that actual experimental measurements would face significant challenges, particularly for baryons involving neutrons or decay photons.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA8 with the Monash 2013 tune (with color reconnection) and the Ropes and Shoving modes to simulate minimum-bias proton-proton collisions at center-of-mass energies of 2.76, 5.02, and 13 TeV. They computed unified balance functions for mixed pairs of charged pions, kaons, and protons (charge balance functions) and for mixed low-mass strange and non-strange baryons (baryon balance functions), using the symmetric definition that averages particle-antiparticle and antiparticle-particle correlations. Weak decays of light hadrons were turned off to focus on primary particles, while short-lived resonances were allowed to decay.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 13 TeV with the Monash tune, the cumulative integral I^{π∓π±,s}(Δy) for π∓π± pairs saturates near unity for Δy > 3, while I^{K∓π±,s}(Δy) and I^{p̄π+,s}(Δy) contribute smaller fractions that sum to unity in the large acceptance limit (Δy → 20).
- The π∓π± balance function amplitude at Δy = 0 slowly rises with increasing √s from 2.76 to 13 TeV, while its width shows a modest reduction.
- The integral I^{pπ,s} at Δy → 20 decreases in magnitude with rising √s, compensating for the increase observed in I^{Kπ,s}.
- With the Ropes mode at √s = 13 TeV, the B^{pπ,s} balance function shows a relatively large increase in amplitude and integral at all Δy values compared to the Monash tune and Shoving mode.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of unified balance functions introduced by Pruneau (2022, 2023) to mixed-species correlations in proton-proton collisions, building on earlier charge balance function studies by Bass (2000), Pratt (2002), and Jeon (2002) that were developed for heavy-ion collisions and the quark-gluon plasma. The results enable future comparisons with other models such as EPOS4 (Werner, 2023) and hydrodynamical models, and provide baseline predictions for experimental measurements at the LHC, particularly for constraining particle production mechanisms in small collision systems.
