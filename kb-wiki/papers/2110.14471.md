# Gluon Parton Distribution of the Pion and Nucleon from Lattice QCD

> We present the $x$-dependent nucleon and pion gluon distribution from lattice QCD using the pseudo-PDF approach, on lattice ensembles with $2+1+1$ flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ), generated by MILC Collaboration. We use clover fermions for the valence action and momentum smearing to achieve pion boost momentum up to 2.56~GeV on three lattice spacings $a\approx 0.9, 0.12$ and 0.15~fm and three pion masses $M_π\approx 220$, 310 and 690~MeV. We compare our pion and preliminary nucleon gluon results with the determination by global fits.

# Gluon Parton Distribution of the Pion and Nucleon from Lattice QCD

## Key claim
The lattice-calculated gluon parton distribution functions (PDFs) for both the pion and nucleon are consistent with global-fit determinations in the large-x region (x > 0.3 for nucleon, x > 0.2 for pion), but show strong deviations in the small-x region (x < 0.3 for nucleon), likely due to limited Ioffe-time range (maximum ν less than 7) and increasing statistical errors at large ν.

## Method
The authors used the pseudo-PDF approach on lattice ensembles with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) generated by the MILC Collaboration, employing clover fermions for the valence action and momentum smearing. They extracted ground-state matrix elements using a two-state fit on two-point correlators and a two-sim fit on three-point correlators, then constructed reduced pseudo Ioffe-time distributions (pITDs) via a double ratio. The evolved pITDs were obtained using leading-order matching with the strong coupling constant, and the lightcone gluon PDFs were extracted by fitting the evolved pITDs with 2-parameter functional forms.

## Key numbers
- Pion boost momentum up to 2.56 GeV achieved on three lattice spacings a ≈ 0.09, 0.12, and 0.15 fm
- Three pion masses studied: Mπ ≈ 220, 310, and 690 MeV
- Lattice spacing values: a = 0.0888(8) fm (a09m310), 0.1184(10) fm (a12m220), 0.1207(11) fm (a12m310), 0.1510(20) fm (a15m310)
- Valence pion masses: 226.6(3) MeV (a12m220), 309.0(11) MeV (a12m310), 313.1(13) MeV (a09m310), 319.1(31) MeV (a15m310)
- Pion measurements: 731,200 (a12m220), 143,680 (a12m310), 21,600 (a15m310)
- Nucleon measurements: 145,296 (a09m310), 324,160 (a12m310), 21,600 (a15m310)
- Nucleon gluon PDF consistent with CT18 NNLO and NNPDF3.1 NNLO within one sigma for x > 0.3
- Pion gluon PDF consistent with JAM and xFitter NLO PDFs within one sigma for x > 0.2
- PDFs presented at μ² = 4 GeV² in the MSbar scheme

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous nucleon gluon PDF calculation on the a12m310 ensemble (Fan 2020) to multiple lattice spacings and pion masses, and presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the x-dependent pion gluon PDF. The results are compared with phenomenological global fits from CT18 NNLO, NNPDF3.1 NNLO for the nucleon, and xFitter (Novikov 2020) and JAM (Barry 2018, Cao 2021) for the pion. The study demonstrates weak dependence on lattice spacing and pion mass under current statistics, and enables future work with improved statistics and systematic control including quark PDF mixing and finite-ν effects.
