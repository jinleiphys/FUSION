# Systematic investigation on the superheavy nucleus formation in the reactions of $^{48}$Ca, $^{50}$Ti, $^{51}$V and $^{54}$Cr on actinide nuclei

> The synthesis of superheavy elements strongly relies on the competition of the quasifission and fusion fission dynamics in the fusion-evaporation reactions. The systematics on the formation of superheavy nuclei in the $^{48}$Ca, $^{50}$Ti, $^{51}$V and $^{54}$Cr induced fusion reactions on actinide nuclei $^{232}$Th, $^{231}$Pa, $^{238}$U, $^{237}$Np, $^{242,244}$Pu, $^{243}$Am, $^{245,248}$Cm, $^{249}$Bk, $^{249}$Cf has been thoroughly investigated with the dinuclear system model by including the cluster transfer and coupling to the dynamical evolution of the quadrupole deformation parameters. The uncertainties of the fusion-evaporation excitation functions with the mass models of FRDM2012, KTUY05, LDM1966, SkyHFB, WS4 are investigated and compared with the available experimental data from Dubna, GSI, Berkeley and RIKEN. The production cross sections, optimal evaporation channels and beam energies in the synthesis of superheavy elements Z = 119 and 120 were predicted and compared for the different mass models in the reactions of $^{50}\mathrm{Ti} + ^{249}\mathrm{Bk}$, $^{51}\mathrm{V} + ^{248}\mathrm{Cm}$, $^{54}\mathrm{Cr} + ^{243}\mathrm{Am}$, $^{50}\mathrm{Ti} + ^{249}\mathrm{Cf}$, $^{51}\mathrm{V} + ^{249}\mathrm{Bk}$, $^{54}\mathrm{Cr} + ^{248}\mathrm{Cm}$, respectively.

## Key claim

The production cross sections for superheavy elements Z = 119 and 120 depend strongly on the choice of nuclear mass model, with predictions varying by up to an order of magnitude; the optimal reaction systems, evaporation channels, and beam energies are therefore model-dependent and must be compared across multiple mass tables to guide experimental searches.

## Method

The authors employed the dinuclear system (DNS) model, which describes fusion-evaporation reactions in three stages (capture, fusion, and survival), and extended it to include cluster transfer (deuteron, triton, 3He, alpha) and the dynamical evolution of quadrupole deformation parameters. They systematically calculated excitation functions for 48Ca, 50Ti, 51V, and 54Cr projectiles incident on actinide targets (232Th, 231Pa, 238U, 237Np, 242,244Pu, 243Am, 245,248Cm, 249Bk, 249Cf) using five different nuclear mass models: FRDM2012, KTUY05, LDM1966, SkyHFB, and WS4. The calculated results were compared with available experimental data from Dubna, GSI, Berkeley, and RIKEN.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 48Ca + 248Cm → 296116*, the internal fusion barriers obtained with different mass models were: FRDM2012 = 14.9946 MeV, KTUY05 = 17.2527 MeV, LDM1966 = 15.2321 MeV, SkyHFB = 13.8170 MeV, and WS4 = 15.4945 MeV.
- The Businaro-Gallone (B.G.) point asymmetry parameters (eta) for the same reaction were: FRDM2012 at eta = -0.8176, KTUY05 at eta = -0.8446, LDM1966 at eta = -0.8176, SkyHFB at eta = -0.8581, and WS4 at eta = -0.8851.
- The Q-values for the reaction 50Ti + 249Cf ranged from -193.09 MeV (SkyHFB) to -196.66 MeV (WS4), and for 54Cr + 248Cm from -206.18 MeV (SkyHFB) to -208.48 MeV (WS4).
- The maximum angular momentum used in calculations was J_max = 30 hbar.
- The relaxation time for radial motion was tau_r = 5 × 10^(-22) s, and for angular momentum dissipation tau_J = 15 × 10^(-22) s.

## Context

This work extends the systematic application of the DNS model to superheavy element synthesis, building on prior studies by Feng (2007, 2009) and Chen (2023) that established the model framework. It directly addresses the sensitivity of cross-section predictions to nuclear mass inputs, as noted by Geng (2024), and provides a comprehensive comparison of five mass models (FRDM2012, KTUY05, LDM1966, SkyHFB, WS4) for reactions targeting elements Z = 119 and 120. The results enable experimental groups at Dubna, GSI, RIKEN, and other facilities to select optimal beam energies and evaporation channels for future synthesis attempts, following the recent successful synthesis of superheavy nuclei using a 50Ti beam at Berkeley (Gates, 2024).
