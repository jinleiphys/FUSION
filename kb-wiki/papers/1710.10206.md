# Nuclear pasta in hot dense matter and its implications for neutrino scattering

> We find that the abundance of large clusters of nucleons in neutron-rich matter at sub-nuclear density is greatly reduced by finite temperature effects when matter is close to beta-equilibrium. Large nuclei and exotic non-spherical nuclear configurations called pasta, favored in the vicinity of the transition to uniform matter at $T=0$, dissolve at relatively low temperature. For matter close to beta-equilibrium we find that the pasta melting temperature is $T_m^β\simeq 4\pm 1$~MeV for realistic equations of state. The mechanism for pasta dissolution is discussed, and in general $T_m^β$ is shown to be sensitive to the proton fraction. We find that coherent neutrino scattering from nuclei and pasta makes a modest contribution to the opacity under the conditions encountered in supernovae and neutron star mergers. Implications for neutrino signals from galactic supernovae are briefly discussed.

## Key claim
Finite temperature effects greatly reduce the abundance of large nucleon clusters and non-spherical "pasta" configurations in neutron-rich matter near beta-equilibrium, with a pasta melting temperature of T_m^beta = 4 +/- 1 MeV for realistic equations of state, meaning that the enhanced neutrino opacity from pasta assumed in prior simulations (Horowitz et al., 2016) is significantly smaller than previously thought.

## Method
The authors performed calculations of hot dense matter at finite temperature using realistic nuclear equations of state to determine the thermodynamic conditions under which nuclear pasta phases exist in proto-neutron stars. They computed the pasta melting temperature as a function of proton fraction and evaluated the coherent neutrino scattering opacity from both spherical nuclei and pasta structures, including the effect of Coulomb correlations between clusters that suppresses scattering for neutrinos with wavelengths larger than the inter-cluster distance.

## Key numbers
- Pasta melting temperature for matter near beta-equilibrium: T_m^beta = 4 +/- 1 MeV for realistic equations of state
- Density range where nuclear pasta phase exists: between 10^13 and 10^14 g/cm^3

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of nuclear pasta phases by Ravenhall, Pethick, and Wilson (1983) and Lattimer, Pethick, Ravenhall, and Lamb (1985), and directly addresses the recent finding by Horowitz et al. (2016) that enhanced neutrino opacity from pasta significantly impacts proto-neutron star evolution. The results show that the pasta phase dissolves at relatively low temperatures for the small electron fractions characteristic of beta-equilibrium, making the coherent neutrino scattering contribution modest rather than large. The finding that Coulomb correlations suppress scattering for long-wavelength neutrinos agrees with earlier work by Itoh (1975) and Itoh et al. (2004). This enables more accurate modeling of neutrino transport in supernovae and neutron star mergers, with implications for interpreting neutrino signals from galactic supernovae.
