# QCD saturation at the LHC: comparisons of models to p+p and A+A data and predictions for p+Pb collisions

> In a previous paper (arXiv:1011.1895), we showed that saturation models, constrained by e+p HERA data on inclusive and diffractive cross-sections, are in good agreement with p+p data at LHC in the soft sector. Particularly impressive was the agreement of saturation models with the multiplicity distribution as a function of $n_{\rm ch.}$. In this paper, we extend these studies further and consider the agreement of these models with data on bulk distributions in A+A collisions. We compare our results to data on central and forward particle production in d+Au collisions at RHIC and make predictions for inclusive distributions in p+Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

Saturation models (IP-Sat, b-CGC, rcBK) constrained by HERA e+p data provide good agreement with LHC p+p and A+A data in the soft sector, but the authors caution that theoretical uncertainties give "considerable elasticity of 20%-50% variability depending on the quantity studied," and only qualitative differences from theoretical projections can rule out models at present.

## Method

The authors use k-perp factorization to compute single inclusive gluon distributions in hadron-hadron collisions, where unintegrated gluon distributions are expressed in terms of dipole cross-sections measured in deep inelastic scattering at HERA. For A+A collisions, the dipole-nucleus cross-section is approximated using the IP-Sat model with a Glauber-based nuclear thickness function, and the multiplicity distribution is computed within the Glasma flux tube framework using a negative binomial distribution. The infrared divergence in the multiplicity distribution is regulated by replacing p-perp with m-perp = sqrt(p-perp^2 + m^2), with m varied between 0.2 and 0.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The parameter zeta = 0.155, extracted from a fit to p+p data at 0.9 TeV, gives the best fit for both p+p and A+A multiplicity distributions; this is a factor of two smaller than the lower end of the non-perturbative range (zeta ~ 0.3-1.5) computed from Yang-Mills equations
- For the IP-Sat model in A+A collisions, the normalization K-factor is 0.07 for mass term m = 0.4 GeV, fit to single inclusive distribution at one energy at eta = 0
- For p+p collisions, the IP-Sat model normalization uses A = 0.23, b0 = 5.77, and C = 0.32 with mass term m = 0.4 GeV; for rcBK model, A = 6.15, b0 = 5.14, and C = 0.76
- For p+A collisions, the IP-Sat model normalization uses A = 0.25 with b_max = 9.5 fm (fixed, not energy-dependent); for rcBK model, A = 0.032 with m = 0.4 GeV
- The nuclear modification factor R_pA for p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 4.4 TeV/nucleon approaches unity at p_perp ~ 5 GeV in both IP-Sat and rcBK models
- For p+Pb at sqrt(s) = 8.8 TeV/nucleon, the suppression persists to higher p_perp

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Tribedy and Venugopalan, 2010) which showed saturation models agree with p+p LHC data, now comparing to A+A data from RHIC and LHC and making predictions for p+Pb collisions at the LHC. The results are compared to related approaches by other groups (arXiv:1111.2312, arXiv:1007.2430) which use the b-CGC model and assume an additional gluon cascade mechanism for A+A energy dependence; the authors argue their IP-Sat model explains the faster energy dependence in A+A relative to p+p through larger saturation scales in nuclei without additional modifications. The work enables predictions for the upcoming p+Pb run at the LHC, particularly for the nuclear modification factor R_pA and inclusive distributions at various rapidities.
