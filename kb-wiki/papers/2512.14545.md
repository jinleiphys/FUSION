# Prediction of deformed halo nuclei $^{43,45}$Si from multiple criteria based on structure and reaction analyses

> Possible deformed neutron halos in silicon isotopes are investigated from both structure and reaction perspectives using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) combined with the Glauber model. The experimental neutron separation energies of silicon isotopes are well reproduced by the DRHBc theory. Multiple halo criteria are examined, including the global ones based on root-mean-square radii and density profiles, as well as the microscopic ones based on single-particle orbitals and their spatial distributions. Calculations employing different density functionals and pairing strengths consistently indicate the emergence of $p$-wave neutron halos in $^{43,45}$Si, accompanied by pronounced shape decoupling between the halo and the core. Moreover, the enhanced reaction cross sections and the narrow longitudinal momentum distributions of one-neutron removal residues provide additional evidence supporting the halo structures in $^{43,45}$Si.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the neutron-rich isotopes $^{43}$Si and $^{45}$Si exhibit deformed neutron halos, but the claim is conditional on multiple criteria from both structure and reaction analyses, and the authors note that $^{41}$Si does not qualify as a halo nucleus despite showing some reaction signatures, because its valence neutron does not decouple clearly from the core.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) to compute ground-state properties of silicon isotopes, using the density functional PC-PK1 with a pairing strength of -325 MeV fm$^3$ and a pairing window of 100 MeV, with additional calculations using PC-L3R, NL3$^*$, and NL-SH functionals. They then used the resulting nuclear densities as input to the Glauber model to evaluate reaction cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions for one-neutron removal reactions on a $^{12}$C target at 240 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The one-neutron separation energy $S_{1n}$ drops below 1 MeV for odd-$N$ silicon isotopes with $N \geq 29$, and becomes negative at $N=35$, marking the one-neutron drip line at $^{48}$Si.
- The DRHBc calculations reproduce experimental one-neutron separation energies with a root-mean-square deviation of $\sigma = 1.03$ MeV.
- For $^{43}$Si, the valence neutron occupies a $1/2^-$ orbital at $\epsilon = -1.22$ MeV with 58% $2p_{1/2}$ and 35% $1f_{7/2}$ components; the halo rms radius is $R_{n,\text{halo}} = 5.56$ fm compared to the core radius $R_{n,\text{core}} = 3.83$ fm, and the halo has quadrupole deformation $\beta_{2,\text{halo}} = -0.01$ while the core has $\beta_{2,\text{core}} = -0.37$.
- For $^{45}$Si, the valence neutron occupies a $1/2^-$ orbital at $\epsilon = -1.87$ MeV with 62% $2p_{1/2}$ component; the halo rms radius is $R_{n,\text{halo}} = 5.21$ fm compared to the core radius $R_{n,\text{core}} = 3.83$ fm, and the halo has $\beta_{2,\text{halo}} = 0.01$ while the core has $\beta_{2,\text{core}} = -0.31$.
- The longitudinal momentum distributions for $^{41,43,45,47}$Si have full widths at half maximum of about $50$ MeV/$c$, significantly smaller than the values ($\gtrsim 120$ MeV/$c$) for isotopes with $A \leq 39$.

## Context

This work extends previous DRHBc studies that predicted halos in $^{39}$Na (Zhang 2023), $^{42,44}$Mg (Zhou 2010, Li 2012), and $^{40,42}$Al (Zhang 2023, Zhang 2025), and builds on the combined DRHBc+Glauber approach that successfully described the known halo nucleus $^{37}$Mg (An 2024). The predictions for $^{43,45}$Si, if confirmed experimentally, would set a new record as the heaviest halo nuclei, surpassing the current heaviest known halo nucleus $^{37}$Mg (Kobayashi 2014, Takechi 2014). The authors note that these extremely neutron-rich silicon isotopes are becoming experimentally accessible.
