# Prospects for ditauonium discovery at colliders

> The feasibility of observing ditauonium, the bound state of two tau leptons, at $e^+e^-$ colliders (BES III at $\sqrt{s} = 3.78$ GeV, Belle II at $\sqrt{s} = 10.6$ GeV, a future super tau-charm factory (STCF) at $\sqrt{s} \approx 2m_τ$, and the FCC-ee at $\sqrt{s} = 91.2$ GeV) as well as in hadronic and photon-photon collisions at the LHC, is studied. Cross sections and expected yields for spin-0 para- ($\mathcal{T}_0$) and spin-1 ortho- ($\mathcal{T}_1$) ditauonium are presented for nine different production and decay processes. Para-ditauonium can be observed at the FCC-ee via photon fusion in its diphoton decay ($γγ\to\mathcal{T}_0\toγγ$). Ortho-ditauonium can be observed at STCF via $e^+e^-\to\mathcal{T}_1\toμ^+μ^-$, where a threshold scan with monochromatized beams can also provide a very precise extraction of the tau lepton mass with a $\mathcal{O}(25$ keV) uncertainty or less. Observing pp $\to \mathcal{T}_1(μ^+μ^-)+X$ at the LHC is possible by identifying its displaced vertex with a good control of the combinatorial dimuon background. In addition, we compute the rare decay branching fractions of ditauonium into quarkonium plus a photon.

## Key claim

Ditauonium, the QED bound state of two tau leptons, could be observed at current and future colliders through several production and decay channels, but the feasibility depends critically on the specific process, collider energy, and integrated luminosity, with the most promising channels being ortho-ditauonium at a super tau-charm factory (STCF) via resonant s-channel production and para-ditauonium at FCC-ee via two-photon fusion.

## Method

The authors computed cross sections and expected event yields for nine different production and decay processes of spin-0 para-ditauonium and spin-1 ortho-ditauonium, considering e+e- colliders (BES III at sqrt(s)=3.78 GeV, Belle II at sqrt(s)=10.6 GeV, a future STCF at sqrt(s)≈2m_tau, and FCC-ee at sqrt(s)=91.2 GeV) as well as hadronic and photon-photon collisions at the LHC. Signal and background events were generated with the HELAC-Onia 2.6.6 Monte Carlo code, and the authors estimated statistical significances and displaced-vertex yields for each channel.

## Key numbers

- Ditauonium ground state mass: 3553.6962 ± 0.2400 MeV, with binding energy -23.655 keV at NNLO accuracy
- Total decay widths: 23.84 meV for para-ditauonium (lifetime 27.60 fs) and 31.59 meV for ortho-ditauonium (lifetime 20.83 fs)
- Para-ditauonium branching fraction to diphotons: 77.72%; ortho-ditauonium branching fraction to dileptons: 20.37%
- Para-ditauonium via gamma-gamma to gamma-gamma at FCC-ee (sqrt(s)=91.2 GeV, 50 ab^-1): 5600 signal events, statistical significance 5 sigma
- Para-ditauonium via gamma-gamma to gamma-gamma at Belle II (sqrt(s)=10.6 GeV, 50 ab^-1): 750 signal events, statistical significance 3 sigma
- Ortho-ditauonium via e+e- to mu+mu- at STCF: threshold scan with monochromatized beams can provide tau lepton mass extraction with O(25 keV) uncertainty or less
- Para-ditauonium Dalitz decay branching fractions: 1.79% to e+e-gamma, 0.52% to mu+mu-gamma
- Para-ditauonium plus FSR photon at Belle II: 4 expected events

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous detailed spectroscopic studies of ditauonium (d'Enterria 2022) and first feasibility studies for its production via two-photon fusion (d'Enterria 2022). Ditauonium was originally proposed by Moffat (1975) and Avilez (1977, 1978), and only two leptonium states (positronium and muonium) have been observed to date. The paper enables future experimental searches at multiple facilities, with the most promising avenues being resonant ortho-ditauonium production at a future STCF for precision tau mass measurements, and para-ditauonium via photon fusion at FCC-ee for QED tests at smaller distances than other exotic atoms.
