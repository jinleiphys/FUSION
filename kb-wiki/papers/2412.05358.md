# $q_T$-slicing with multiple jets

> Modern collider phenomenology requires unprecedented precision for the theoretical predictions, for which slicing techniques provide an essential tool at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the strong coupling. The most popular slicing variable is based on the transverse momentum $q_T$ of a color-singlet final state, but its generalization to final states with jets is known to be very difficult. Here we propose two generalizations of $q_T$ that can be used for jet processes, providing proof of concept with an NLO slicing for $pp \to 2$ jets. We present factorization formulae that enable our approach to NNLO, calculate the NNLO collinear-soft function and demonstrate slicing at this order for $e^+e^- \to 2$ jets. One of these generalizations of $q_T$ only applies to planar Born processes, such as $pp \to 2$ jets, but offers a dramatic simplification of the soft function. We also discuss how our approach can directly be extended to obtain predictions for the fragmentation of hadrons. This presents a promising path for high-precision QCD calculations with multi-jet final states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes two generalizations of the transverse momentum \(q_T\) as slicing variables for jet processes, demonstrating proof-of-concept at NLO for \(pp \to 2\) jets and at NNLO for \(e^+e^- \to 2\) jets, but the approach is currently limited to planar Born processes (such as \(pp \to 2\) jets) for one generalization, and the full NNLO implementation for hadron colliders awaits the completion of the gluon WTA jet function.

## Method

The authors use a recoil-free jet axis (winner-take-all recombination scheme) to define a modified \(q_T\) variable that becomes nonzero even when radiation is emitted inside jets, enabling slicing. For planar Born processes, they use the azimuthal decorrelation \(\delta\phi\) (equivalent to the transverse momentum component \(q_x\) perpendicular to the scattering plane), while for general processes they use the total transverse momentum \(q_T\) of all jets and color-singlets. They derive factorization formulae in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the leading-power cross section at small \(q_T\) or \(\delta\phi\), and validate the slicing by comparing the sum of unresolved and resolved contributions to known fixed-order results from NLOJET++ (Nagy, 2001) for \(pp \to 2\) jets and to analytic NNLO results for \(e^+e^- \to 2\) jets.

## Key numbers

* For \(pp \to 2\) jets at NLO, the slicing using \(\delta\phi^{\rm cut}\) converges to the correct NLO correction \(\delta\sigma^{\rm NLO}\) (red line from NLOJET++) for small \(\delta\phi^{\rm cut}\), with jets defined using anti-\(k_T\) algorithm with radius \(R = 0.5\) and cuts \(p_{T,1} > 100\) GeV, \(p_{T,2} > 80\) GeV, \(|\eta_{1,2}| < 2\), with renormalization and factorization scales \(\mu_{R,F} = 2p_{T,1}\).
* For \(pp \to 2\) jets at NLO, the slicing using \(q_T^{\rm cut}\) (with WTA scheme) converges faster than \(\delta\phi^{\rm cut}\), as shown by fitting the residual error to \(a\, r_{\rm cut} \ln r_{\rm cut} + b\, r_{\rm cut}\) where \(r_{\rm cut} = \delta\phi^{\rm cut}\) or \(q_T^{\rm cut}/p_{T,1}\).
* For \(e^+e^- \to 2\) jets at NNLO, the slicing using \(\theta^{\rm cut}\) converges to the correct NNLO correction \(K_2\) (given in equation K12 of the paper) for small \(\theta^{\rm cut}\).
* The soft function for \(q_T\) with jets depends on the jet radius \(R\); the authors add finite terms up to \(\mathcal{O}(R^4)\) to achieve sub-percent accuracy on the cross section for \(R = 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends the \(q_T\)-slicing method, which is well-established for color-singlet production (Catani, 2007), to multi-jet final states where the standard \(q_T\) is unsuitable because radiation inside jets leaves \(q_T = 0\). It contrasts with the \(N\)-jettiness slicing approach (Boughezal, 2015; Gaunt, 2015), which has a more complicated soft function at NNLO (Bell, 2023; Agarwal, 2024), and with the recently introduced \(k_T\)-ness (Buonocore, 2022; Buonocore, 2023), which lacks a factorization formula. The results enable a path toward NNLO calculations for processes such as \(pp \to 3\) jets and \(pp \to Z + 2\) jets, and can be extended to fragmentation of hadrons by replacing one TMD jet function with a TMD fragmentation function.
