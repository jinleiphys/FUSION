# Resummation of in-medium ladder diagrams: s-wave effective range and p-wave interaction

> A recent work on the resummation of fermionic in-medium ladder diagrams to all orders is extended by considering the effective range correction in the s-wave interaction and a (spin-independent) p-wave contact-interaction. A two-component recursion generates the in-medium T-matrix at any order when off-shell terms spoil the factorization of multi-loop diagrams. The resummation to all orders is achieved in the form of a geometrical series for the particle-particle ladders, and through an arctangent-function for the combined particle-particle and hole-hole ladders. One finds that the effective range correction changes the results in the limit of large scattering length considerably, with the effect that the Bertsch parameter $ξ_n$ nearly doubles. Applications to the equation of state of neutron matter at low density are also discussed. For the p-wave contact-interaction the resummation to all orders is facilitated by decomposing tensorial loop-integrals with a transversal and a longitudinal projector. The enhanced attraction provided by the p-wave ladder series has its origin mainly in the coherent sum of Hartree and Fock contributions.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous resummation method for fermionic in-medium ladder diagrams by including the effective range correction in the s-wave interaction and a spin-independent p-wave contact-interaction, finding that the effective range correction nearly doubles the Bertsch parameter for neutron matter in the normal phase (from 0.507 to 0.876) when the combined particle-particle and hole-hole ladder series is resummed.

## Method

The author uses a two-component recursion to generate the in-medium T-matrix at any order when off-shell terms from the effective range correction spoil the direct factorization of multi-loop diagrams, achieving resummation to all orders as a geometrical series for particle-particle ladders and through an arctangent-function for combined particle-particle and hole-hole ladders. For the p-wave contact-interaction, tensorial loop-integrals are decomposed into transversal and longitudinal projectors, which decouple and are resummed separately through an arctangent-function or geometrical series.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter for combined particle-hole ladder series with effective range: xi_n(r_s != 0) = 0.876 (compared to xi_n(r_s = 0) = 0.507 without effective range)
- Bertsch parameter for particle-particle ladder series alone with effective range: xi_n^{(pp)}(r_s != 0) = 0.43 (compared to xi_n^{(pp)}(r_s = 0) = 0.237 without effective range)
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = (18.95 +/- 0.40) fm
- Neutron-neutron effective range: r_s = (2.75 +/- 0.11) fm
- Leading order effective range correction to energy per particle: bar E(k_f)^{(lo)} = a^2 r_s k_f^5 / 20 pi M

## Context

This work extends the resummation method of Kaiser (2012) for fermionic in-medium ladder diagrams, which previously achieved a Bertsch parameter of 0.507 for the normal phase using only a contact-interaction proportional to the scattering length. The inclusion of the effective range correction follows earlier work by Schafer et al. on particle-particle ladder series and by Schwenk and Pethick (who argued the effective range improves neutron matter descriptions), while the p-wave resummation addresses interactions relevant for nuclear systems. The results enable analytical comparisons with quantum Monte Carlo simulations of strongly-coupled fermionic many-body systems such as neutron matter at low density, and may be contained as special cases in the broader partial-wave framework of Lacour et al.
