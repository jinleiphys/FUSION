# Elastic magnetic electron scattering from deformed nuclei

> Magnetic form factors corresponding to elastic electron scattering from odd-A nuclei are presented. The calculations are carried out in plane-wave Born approximation. The one-body properties are obtained in a deformed self-consistent mean-field calculation based on a Skyrme HF+BCS method. Collective effects are also included in the cranking approximation. Results on several stable nuclei are compared with the available experimental information. It is shown that a deformed formalism improves the agreement with experiment in deformed nuclei, while reproducing equally well spherical nuclei by taking properly the spherical limit of the deformed model and the effect of nucleon-nucleon correlations. Thus, the capability of the model to describe magnetic form factors is demonstrated. This opens the door to explore also unstable nuclei of particular interest that could be measured in future experiments on electron-radioactive beam colliders.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a deformed self-consistent mean-field formalism, using Skyrme HF+BCS with the equal filling approximation, improves agreement with experimental elastic magnetic form factors for deformed odd-A nuclei while also reproducing spherical nuclei when the spherical limit of the deformed model is taken properly, including nucleon-nucleon correlation effects via natural orbitals.

## Method

The authors calculate magnetic form factors for elastic electron scattering from odd-A nuclei in plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA), using a deformed Skyrme HF+BCS mean-field method with the SLy4 interaction. Collective rotational effects are included in the cranking approximation, and nucleon-nucleon correlations are incorporated through natural orbitals obtained from the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model (CDFM). The calculations are compared with experimental data for nine stable nuclei, with the deformed formalism applied to both deformed and spherical cases by taking the spherical limit of the deformed model.

## Key numbers

- For $^{17}$O ($I^\pi=5/2^+$): calculated charge radius $r_{c,\text{th}} = 2.7429$ fm vs. experimental $r_{c,\text{exp}} = 2.6932(75)$ fm; deformed magnetic moment $\mu_{\text{th,def}} = -1.946\ \mu_N$ vs. $\mu_{\text{exp}} = -1.89379(9)\ \mu_N$
- For $^{25}$Mg ($I^\pi=5/2^+$): quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_p = 0.338$; deformed magnetic moment $\mu_{\text{th,def}} = -0.864\ \mu_N$ vs. $\mu_{\text{exp}} = -0.85545(8)\ \mu_N$, showing clear improvement over the spherical Schmidt value of $-1.913\ \mu_N$
- For $^{115}$In ($I^\pi=9/2^+$): $\beta_p = 0.090$; deformed magnetic moment $\mu_{\text{th,def}} = 5.968\ \mu_N$ vs. $\mu_{\text{exp}} = 5.5408(2)\ \mu_N$, improved over the spherical Schmidt value of $6.793\ \mu_N$
- For $^{39}$K ($I^\pi=3/2^+$): both spherical and deformed calculations overestimate the magnetic moment ($\mu_{\text{th,sph}} = +0.124\ \mu_N$, $\mu_{\text{th,def}} = +0.116\ \mu_N$ vs. $\mu_{\text{exp}} = +0.39157(3)\ \mu_N$)
- Charge radius agreement within less than 1% for medium-mass nuclei from $^{29}$Si to $^{115}$In
- Luminosities over $10^{27}$ cm$^{-2}$ s$^{-1}$ have been achieved at the SCRIT facility for $^{132}$Xe experiments

## Context

This work extends earlier deformed mean-field calculations of magnetic form factors by Elvira et al. (various years), Kovalenko et al. (various years), and Sarriguren et al. (various years), which demonstrated the need for deformation to account for data in $^{181}$Ta. The paper builds on the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model (CDFM) approach of Antonov et al. (1979, 1989, 1994) for incorporating nucleon-nucleon correlations through natural orbitals. The results enable future application to unstable nuclei that could be measured at electron-radioactive beam colliders such as ELISe at FAIR-GSI and SCRIT at RIKEN, where similar luminosities to those already achieved for stable $^{132}$Xe are expected.
