# Reanalysis of the most strange dibaryon within constituent quark models

> The most strange dibaryon $ΩΩ$ with quantum numbers $S=-6$, $I=0$, and $J^{P}=0^{+},~1^{-},~2^{+},~3^{-}$ is reanalyzed in the framework of quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) and chiral quark model (ChQM). The $ΩΩ$ dibaryon with $J^{P}=0^{+}$ is bound, and the one with other quantum numbers $J^{P}=1^{-},~2^{+},~3^{-}$ are all unbound in our calculation. The low-energy scattering phase shifts, the scattering length, and the effective range of the $ΩΩ$ dibaryon with $J^{P}=0^{+}$ also support the existence of such strange dibaryon. This dibaryon is showed to be a shallow bound state in QDCSM, while the binding energy becomes much larger in the ChQM by including the effect of the hidden-color channel coupling. And the scalar nonet meson-exchange in the ChQM also provides more attraction for the $ΩΩ$ system. Experimental search for such most strange dibaryon will provide much information for understanding the hadron-hadron interactions in different quark models.

# Reanalysis of the most strange dibaryon within constituent quark models

## Key claim
The $\Omega\Omega$ dibaryon with $J^{P}=0^{+}$ is a bound state in both the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) and the chiral quark model (ChQM), but the binding energy is model-dependent: it is a shallow bound state in QDCSM (consistent with lattice QCD results from the HAL QCD Collaboration), while it becomes a much deeper bound state in the $SU(2)$ and $SU(3)$ versions of ChQM when hidden-color channel coupling is included.

## Method
The authors performed a dynamic calculation using the resonating group method (RGM) to solve the six-quark system for the $\Omega\Omega$ dibaryon with quantum numbers $S=-6$, $I=0$, and $J^{P}=0^{+}, 1^{-}, 2^{+}, 3^{-}$ in three constituent quark models: QDCSM, $SU(2)$ ChQM, and $SU(3)$ ChQM. They computed effective potentials between two $\Omega$ baryons, binding energies from single-channel and hidden-color channel-coupled calculations, and low-energy scattering phase shifts from which scattering lengths and effective ranges were extracted using the Kohn-Hulthen-Kato variational method.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of $J^{P}=0^{+}$ $\Omega\Omega$ in QDCSM single channel: $-2.0$ MeV (shallow bound state).
- Binding energy of $J^{P}=0^{+}$ $\Omega\Omega$ in $SU(2)$ ChQM with hidden-color channel coupling: $-46.8$ MeV.
- Binding energy of $J^{P}=0^{+}$ $\Omega\Omega$ in $SU(3)$ ChQM with hidden-color channel coupling: $-103.3$ MeV.
- Scattering length $a_{0}$ for $J^{P}=0^{+}$ $\Omega\Omega$ in QDCSM: $4.1296$ fm; effective range $r_{0}$: $1.3977$ fm; binding energy from effective range relation: $-0.31$ MeV.
- Scattering length $a_{0}$ in $SU(2)$ ChQM: $1.4050$ fm; effective range $r_{0}$: $0.6813$ fm; binding energy from effective range relation: $-35.6$ MeV.
- Scattering length $a_{0}$ in $SU(3)$ ChQM: $0.87275$ fm; effective range $r_{0}$: $0.42850$ fm; binding energy from effective range relation: $-92.3$ MeV.
- Lattice QCD comparison from HAL QCD Collaboration: $a_{0}=4.6(6)(^{+1.2}_{-0.5})$ fm and $r_{0}=1.27(3)(^{+0.06}_{-0.03})$ fm at $m_{\pi}\simeq 146$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the $\Omega\Omega$ dibaryon, which was predicted by Kopeliovich in the flavor $SU(3)$ Skyrmion model and later investigated in QDCSM by Pang et al. and by the HAL QCD Collaboration using lattice QCD simulations with a nearly physical pion mass. The authors compare two competing mechanisms for intermediate-range attraction in baryon-baryon interactions: the $\sigma$ meson-exchange used in ChQM versus the quark delocalization and color screening used in QDCSM, which was previously shown to be equivalent to hidden-color channel coupling in the $NN$ and $N\Omega$ systems. The results enable experimental searches for the $\Omega\Omega$ dibaryon via pair-momentum correlation measurements in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, as suggested by Morita et al. and the STAR experiment's ongoing search for the $N\Omega$ bound state.
