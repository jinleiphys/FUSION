# Theory of Eta Photo- and Electroproduction

> We analyze the available data on eta photo- and electroproduction, around W~1535 MeV, in the framework of the effective Lagrangian approach, and extract, in a nearly model-independent fashion, the electrostrong amplitude for the gamma+N--> N*(1535)--> N+eta processes. Quark model approaches are shown to be quite inadequate to explain this property at all Q^2. In particular, at high Q^2, the extracted amplitude falls much slower than the predictions of the quark model, as a function of Q^2, a situation similar to the electroexcitation and decay of Delta(1232). A QCD explanation of these observations is urgently needed.

## Key claim

The effective Lagrangian approach extracts a nearly model-independent electrostrong amplitude for the gamma+N→N*(1535)→N+eta process, and this amplitude falls much slower with Q^2 than quark model predictions, a failure the authors claim holds at all Q^2, not just at high Q^2.

## Method

The authors analyze existing data on eta photo- and electroproduction near W≈1535 MeV using a tree-level effective Lagrangian approach (ELA) that includes Born terms, vector meson exchanges, and N* excitations. They fit angular distribution data from Mainz (real photons) and older electroproduction cross-section data to extract dressed (physical) parameters for the N*(1535) excitation and its decay to the N-eta channel. The extracted parameter ξ_i = sqrt(ζ_i Γ_η) A_{1/2}^i / Γ_T is compared with quark model predictions.

## Key numbers

- For photoproduction, the extracted electrostrong parameter for the proton is ξ_p = 2.20 ± 0.15 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-1}, while the quark model (Capstick and Roberts) gives 1.13 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-1}.
- For the neutron, ξ_n = -1.86 ± 0.20 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-1}, compared to the quark model value of -0.94 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-1}.
- The ratio A_{1/2}^n / A_{1/2}^p = -0.84 ± 0.15, which agrees with the quark model prediction of -0.83.
- The eta-nucleon pseudoscalar coupling constant is constrained to the range 0.2 ≤ g_η ≤ 6.2.
- Vector meson coupling parameters: λ_ρ g^ρ_v + λ_ω g^ω_v = 5.93 ± 0.82, and λ_ρ g^ρ_t + λ_ω g^ω_t = 17.50 ± 2.57.
- Vector meson electromagnetic decay amplitudes: λ_ρ = 1.06 ± 0.15, λ_ω = 0.31 ± 0.06.
- For the N→Δ magnetic dipole amplitude (M1), the K-matrix residue gives 290 ± 13 units, the ELA fit gives 285 ± 37 units, the quark model gives 206 units, and lattice QCD gives 210 ± 25 units.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach of the RPI group (Mukhopadhyay et al., 1995) to analyze N*(1535) excitation, and it contradicts quark model predictions from Koniuk and Isgur (1980), Close and Li (1990), Weber (1992), and Capstick and Roberts (1994). The failure of the quark model for the N*(1535) transition electricity at the photon point and at higher Q^2 is compared to the similar shortage of transition magnetism in the N→Δ(1232) transition, as seen in the work of Davidson and Mukhopadhyay (1995) and Leinweber, Draper, and Woloshyn (1995). The extracted parameters provide a benchmark for future QCD calculations, lattice gauge studies (Leinweber et al., 1995), and coupled-channel approaches (Bennhold and Tanabe, 1995; Sauermann et al., 1995).
