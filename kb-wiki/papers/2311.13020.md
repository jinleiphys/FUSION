# Understanding the Effect of Chiral NN Parametrization on Nuclear Shapes From an Ab Initio Perspective

> The ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model naturally describes nuclear deformation and collectivity, and is therefore well-suited to studying the dynamics and coexistence of shapes in atomic nuclei. For the first time, we analyze how these features in low-lying states of 6Li and 12C are impacted by the underlying realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. We find that the interaction parametrization has a notable but limited effect on collective shapes in the lowest 6Li and 12C states, while collective structures in the excited 2+ state of 12C are significantly more sensitive to the interaction parameters and exhibits emergent shape coexistence.

## Key claim

The parametrization of chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions has a notable but limited effect on collective shapes in the lowest states of 6Li and 12C, while collective structures in the excited 2+ state of 12C are significantly more sensitive to the interaction parameters and exhibit emergent shape coexistence, based on sampling low-energy constants within ±50% of their NNLOopt values.

## Method

The authors used the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) with an Sp(3,R) basis to compute wave functions for 6Li (1+ ground state and 3+ first excited state) and 12C (first and second excited 2+ states). They constructed model spaces containing the most dominant shapes (13 shapes for 6Li, 14 shapes for 12C) and independently varied each of the 14 low-energy constants (LECs) of the chiral NNLO nucleon-nucleon potential by drawing 300 uniformly distributed LEC combinations using a Latin hypercube design, bounded by ±50% of their NNLOopt values.

## Key numbers

- For 6Li, the prolate shape 0(2 0) contributes at least 70% in each 1+ and 3+ sample wave function across all 300 LEC combinations
- For 12C, the oblate 0(0 4) shape in the first excited 2I+ state can drop as low as 40% probability in the sample wave functions
- The von Neumann entropy of 12C samples peaks at S=0.58 for the 2I+ state and at S=0.65 for the 2II+ state
- Only 6Li samples with excitation energy Ex(3+) within ±1 MeV of the NNLOopt value were considered
- Only 12C samples with energy difference E(2II+) - E(2I+) within ±5 MeV of the NNLOopt value were considered

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio studies of nuclear collectivity using the SA-NCSM framework (Dytrych et al., 2020; Launey et al., 2021) and builds on the understanding that shape coexistence plays a driving role in light nuclei (Heyde and Wood, 2011; Garrett, 2020). The study is a first step toward understanding how the shape structures of light nuclei respond to comparatively large changes in the underlying nuclear forces, as previously investigated by Becker et al. (2023). The results enable future work to identify which specific parts of the nucleon-nucleon interaction are responsible for emergent collective phenomena and shape coexistence in atomic nuclei.
