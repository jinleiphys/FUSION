# Beyond mean-field description of the low-lying spectrum of 16O

> Starting from constrained Skyrme-mean-field calculations, the low-energy excitation spectrum of 16O is calculated by configuration mixing of particle-number and angular-momentum projected mean-field states in the framework of the Generator Coordinate Method. Without any adjustable parameters, this approach gives a very good description of those states and their transition moments that can be described with our restriction to axially and reflection-symmetric shapes. The structure of low-lying 0+ states is analyzed in terms of self-consistent 0p-0h, 2p-2h, and 4p-4h Hartree-Fock states.

## Key claim

Starting from constrained Skyrme-mean-field calculations with no adjustable parameters, the Generator Coordinate Method with particle-number and angular-momentum projection gives a very good description of those low-lying states in 16O that can be described with the restriction to axially and reflection-symmetric shapes, but cannot describe negative-parity states or configurations like α-12C.

## Method

Constrained Skyrme-mean-field calculations (using the SLy4 parametrization with a T=1 density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction) generated a set of BCS states along the axial quadrupole moment Q0. The Lipkin-Nogami method was used to preserve pairing correlations along the constraining path. These intrinsic states were then projected simultaneously on angular momentum and on neutron and proton particle numbers, and the resulting non-orthogonal projected states were mixed as a function of the quadrupole moment using the Generator Coordinate Method to obtain the final collective wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Calculated excitation energy of the 0+2 state: 6.03 MeV (experimental: 6.05 MeV)
- Calculated excitation energy of the 2+1 state: 7.09 MeV (experimental: 6.92 MeV)
- Calculated excitation energy of the 0+3 state: 12.45 MeV (experimental: 12.05 MeV)
- Calculated excitation energy of the 2+2 state: 13.18 MeV (experimental: 13.02 MeV)
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment Qs for the 2+1 state: -11.7 e fm2
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment Qs for the 2+2 state: -22.9 e fm2
- B(E2)↑ for the 2+1 → 0+1 transition: 38 e2 fm4 (experimental: 42 ± 1.4 e2 fm4)
- B(E2)↑ for the 2+1 → 0+2 transition: 241 e2 fm4 (experimental: 370 ± 4 e2 fm4)
- Monopole matrix element M(E0) for 0+2 → 0+1: 5.735 fm2 (experimental: 3.55 ± 0.21 fm2)
- Weight of the 0p-0h HF state in the 0+1 ground state: 0.737 (73.7%)
- Weight of the 4p-4h HF state in the 0+2 state: 0.212 (21.2%)
- Gain in energy from configuration mixing for the 0+1 state relative to the HF ground state: 2.3 MeV
- Proton rms radius of the 0+1 state: 2.75 fm (compared to 2.68 fm for the spherical HF state)

## Context

This work extends the method introduced by Valor et al. (2000) for configuration mixing of projected self-consistent mean-field states, applying it to the doubly-magic nucleus 16O. It builds on earlier mean-field calculations by Gir83, Auv84, and Zhe88 that showed correlations beyond Hartree-Fock are necessary, and on shell-model calculations by Hax90 and Wab92 that mixed (0+2+4)ħω excitations. The results demonstrate that symmetry restoration and configuration mixing are key to a quantitative description of low-energy states from a mean-field approach, and the analysis in terms of self-consistent np-nh Hartree-Fock states provides a bridge to shell-model interpretations. The method enables future inclusion of reflection-asymmetric configurations (like 12C+4He) and triaxial shapes, as well as projection on isospin and proton-neutron pairing correlations.
