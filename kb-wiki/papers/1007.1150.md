# Event Excess in the MiniBooNE Search for $\bar ν_μ\rightarrow \bar ν_e$ Oscillations

> The MiniBooNE experiment at Fermilab reports results from a search for $\bar ν_μ\rightarrow \bar ν_e$ oscillations, using a data sample corresponding to $5.66 \times 10^{20}$ protons on target. An excess of $20.9 \pm 14.0$ events is observed in the energy range $475<E_ν^{QE}<1250$ MeV, which, when constrained by the observed $\bar ν_μ$ events, has a probability for consistency with the background-only hypothesis of 0.5\%. On the other hand, fitting for $\barν_μ\rightarrow\barν_e$ oscillations, the best-fit point has a $χ^2$-probability of 8.7\%. The data are consistent with $\bar ν_μ\rightarrow \bar ν_e$ oscillations in the 0.1 to 1.0 eV$^2$ $Δm^2$ range and with the evidence for antineutrino oscillations from the Liquid Scintillator Neutrino Detector at Los Alamos National Laboratory.

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE experiment observes an excess of $\bar \nu_e$ candidate events above 475 MeV that is consistent with $\bar \nu_\mu \rightarrow \bar \nu_e$ oscillations in the 0.1 to 1.0 eV$^2$ $\Delta m^2$ range, but the background-only hypothesis cannot be ruled out at the 5-sigma level: the probability for consistency with background-only is 0.5% in the energy range $475<E_\nu^{QE}<1250$ MeV, while the best oscillation fit has a $\chi^2$-probability of 8.7%.

## Method

The analysis uses a sample of $5.66 \times 10^{20}$ protons on target from the Fermilab Booster, producing an antineutrino beam via 8 GeV protons on a beryllium target with a magnetic focusing horn. The MiniBooNE detector, a 40-foot diameter sphere filled with mineral oil and lined with 1520 photomultiplier tubes, identifies charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) events from $\bar \nu_e$ and $\bar \nu_\mu$ interactions. Oscillation parameters are extracted from a combined fit to the $\bar \nu_e/\nu_e$ CCQE and $\bar \nu_\mu/\nu_\mu$ CCQE event distributions using a covariance matrix that includes correlations between the two samples, with the $\bar \nu_\mu$ sample used to constrain systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Data sample: $5.66 \times 10^{20}$ protons on target
- Excess of $20.9 \pm 14.0$ events in the energy range $475<E_\nu^{QE}<1250$ MeV
- Probability for consistency with background-only hypothesis: 0.5% in the $475<E_\nu^{QE}<1250$ MeV range
- Best oscillation fit point: ($\Delta m^2$, $\sin^22\theta$) = (0.064 eV$^2$, 0.96) for $475<E_\nu^{QE}<3000$ MeV
- $\chi^2$-probability of best oscillation fit: 8.7% for $475<E_\nu^{QE}<1250$ MeV
- Total excess: $43.2 \pm 22.5$ events for $200<E_\nu^{QE}<3000$ MeV
- $\bar \nu_\mu$ CCQE sample: 24,771 data events, with 78% antineutrino and 22% neutrino content
- $\bar \nu_\mu$ flux peaks at 400 MeV with mean energy of 600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the MiniBooNE search for $\bar \nu_\mu \rightarrow \bar \nu_e$ oscillations with 1.7 times more protons on target than the initial antineutrino-mode results reported in MiniBooNE (2009). The observed excess is consistent with the LSND (2001) evidence for $\bar \nu_e$ appearance at the 3.8-sigma level, and the allowed regions overlap with the LSND allowed region in the 0.1 to 1.0 eV$^2$ $\Delta m^2$ range. The results enable further investigation of the LSND anomaly and motivate additional antineutrino running to approximately double the current statistics, as well as comparisons with the KARMEN (2002) and Bugey (1995) limits that constrain the same oscillation parameter space.
