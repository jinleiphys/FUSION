# Interference Effects in Relativistic Deuteron Electrodisintegration

> We extend the relativistic plane--wave impulse approximation formalism to incorporate a specific class of relativistic interference effects for use in describing inclusive electrodisintegration of $^2$H. The role of these ``exchange'' terms for the various response functions accessible in parity--conserving and --violating inclusive processes is investigated and shown, especially for the latter, to have important consequences for experiment. An extension to a simple quasi--deuteron model is also considered.

## Key claim

The paper extends the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) to include interference ("exchange") terms for inclusive deuteron electrodisintegration, showing that these exchange effects can have important consequences for parity-violating electron scattering even though they are often assumed to be suppressed at quasielastic kinematics.

## Method

The authors relax the PWIA assumption that the detected nucleon is the same as the one struck by the virtual photon, deriving a relativistic plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) for the deuteron that includes both direct (incoherent) and exchange (interference) contributions to the hadronic tensor. They evaluate the spin sums explicitly using a decomposition of single-nucleon operators into spin-flip and non-spin-flip parts, then integrate over the detected nucleon's quantum numbers to obtain inclusive response functions for both parity-conserving and parity-violating electron scattering.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron Fermi momentum is approximately 55 MeV/c, while the D-state contribution in momentum space becomes comparable to the S-state only around 250 MeV/c (more than 4 times the Fermi momentum)
- The ratio η_F ≡ p_F/M ranges from about 0.06 in the deuteron to 0.28 in heavy nuclei, where p_F is the Fermi momentum and M is the nucleon mass
- The parameter κ ≡ q/2M can be unity or larger for high momentum transfer, defining the regime where relativistic effects become important
- A factor of 3 appears in the denominator of the exchange contribution to the transverse response (R_T ∼ κ² {G_M^{T=0}² (D + E/3) + G_M^{T=1}² (D - E/3)}), which suppresses the role of exchange terms in the transverse channel compared to the longitudinal channel

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic PWBA calculations of Fabian and Arenhövel (1979, 1982) by including single-nucleon electromagnetic current terms to all orders in 1/M, rather than truncating at order 1/M. The paper also builds on the authors' own previous PWIA studies (Hadji, Caballero, Caballero_2) that tested the validity of the factorized PWIA for quasielastic scattering. The results are motivated by upcoming high-momentum-transfer parity-violating electron scattering experiments on few-body systems aimed at measuring strangeness form factors, and the formalism can be extended to a relativistic quasi-deuteron model for heavier nuclei.
