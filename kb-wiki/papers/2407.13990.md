# Exploring the first-order phase transition in neutron stars using the parity doublet model and NJL-type quark model

> We investigate the possibility and impacts of a first-order phase transition from hadronic matter to quark matter in neutron stars (NSs) using two specific models: the parity doublet model (PDM) for the hadronic phase and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model for the quark phase. By combining these models, we construct hybrid equations of state (EOSs) that capture the transition between the two phases. We explore the parameter space of both models to identify the conditions under which a first-order phase transition can occur and study its effects on NS properties. We identify the suitable parameter space and constrain the onset density of the first-order phase transition. For $m_0$ = 500 MeV -- the chiral invariant mass in PDM, the phase transition occurs between 1.9$n_0$ and 2.95$n_0$ and ends between 2.1$n_0$ and 3.6$n_0$. Increasing $m_0$ to 600 MeV shifts the phase transition to higher densities, occurring between 2.9$n_0$ and 4.1$n_0$ and ending between 3.4$n_0$ and 4.6$n_0$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a first-order phase transition from hadronic to quark matter in neutron stars is possible within the combined parity doublet model (PDM) and NJL-type quark model framework, but only when the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\) is below 700 MeV; for \(m_0 = 500\) MeV the transition occurs between \(1.9n_0\) and \(2.95n_0\), while for \(m_0 = 600\) MeV it shifts to between \(2.9n_0\) and \(4.1n_0\), with the exact onset density strongly dependent on the interplay between hadronic and quark matter parameters.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state (EOSs) by combining the parity doublet model (PDM) for hadronic matter with an NJL-type quark model including three flavors, U(1)\(_A\) anomaly effects, vector repulsion (\(g_V\)), and attractive diquark correlations (\(H\)). They apply the Maxwell construction to enforce equal pressure and chemical potential at the hadron-quark interface, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations, systematically varying \(m_0\) (500, 550, 600, 700, 800 MeV) and the NJL parameters \((H, g_V)\).

## Key numbers

* For \(m_0 = 500\) MeV, the first-order phase transition begins between \(1.9n_0\) and \(2.95n_0\) and ends between \(2.1n_0\) and \(3.6n_0\), where \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is the nuclear saturation density.
* For \(m_0 = 600\) MeV, the phase transition begins between \(2.9n_0\) and \(4.1n_0\) and ends between \(3.4n_0\) and \(4.6n_0\).
* For \(m_0 = 700\) and 800 MeV, the maximum neutron star mass in the pure hadronic PDM falls below \(2 M_\odot\), making a first-order phase transition impossible while satisfying the two-solar-mass constraint.
* The NJL-type model uses the Hatsuda-Kunihiro parameters: \(G\Lambda^2 = 1.835\), \(K\Lambda^5 = 9.29\) with \(\Lambda = 631.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by some of the same authors (Minamikawa et al., 2020-2021; Gao et al., 2022-2024) that assumed a crossover transition between hadronic and quark matter, now explicitly investigating the first-order phase transition scenario. The PDM approach is consistent with lattice QCD simulations (Aarts et al., 2015-2018) and skyrmion crystal approaches (Ma et al., 2013). The results enable future comparisons with multi-messenger observations from LIGO-VIRGO (GW170817) and NICER to constrain the onset density of deconfinement in neutron star interiors.
