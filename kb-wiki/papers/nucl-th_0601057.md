# The pi N -> pi pi N reaction around the N(1440) energy

> We study the pi N -> pi pi N reaction around the N(1440) mass-shell energy. Considering the total cross sections and invariant mass distributions, we discuss the role of N(1440) and its decay processes. The calculation is performed by extending our previous approach [Phys. Rev. C 69, 025206 (2004)] to this reaction, in which only the nucleon and Delta(1232) were considered as intermediate baryon states. The characteristics observed in the recent data for the pi- p -> pi0 pi0 n reaction obtained by Crystal Ball Collaboration (CBC), can be understood as a strong interference between the two decay processes: N(1440) -> pi Delta(1232) and N(1440) -> N(pi pi)_S. It is also found that the scalar-isoscalar pi pi rescattering effect in the NN*(pi pi)_S vertex, which corresponds to the propagation of sigma meson, seems to be necessary for explain ing the several observables of the pi N -> pi pi N reaction: the large asymmetric shape in the pi0-pi0 invariant mass distributions of the pi- p -> pi0 pi0 n reaction and the pi+ p -> pi+ pi+ n total cross section.

## Key claim

The observed characteristics of the $\pi^- p \rightarrow \pi^0 \pi^0 n$ reaction around the $N^*(1440)$ mass-shell energy can be understood as a strong interference between the $N^*(1440) \rightarrow \pi \Delta(1232)$ and $N^*(1440) \rightarrow N(\pi\pi)_S$ decay processes, with the scalar-isoscalar $\pi\pi$ rescattering effect (corresponding to $\sigma$ meson propagation) appearing necessary to explain the large asymmetric shape in the $\pi^0\pi^0$ invariant mass distributions and the $\pi^+ p \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^+ n$ total cross section.

## Method

The authors extended their previous chiral reduction formula approach (Kamano and Arima, 2004), which originally included only nucleon and $\Delta(1232)$ as intermediate baryon states, by adding the $N^*(1440)$ resonance with its $\pi N N^*$, $\pi \Delta N^*$, and $N N^* (\pi\pi)^{I=0}_{S\text{ wave}}$ interactions. They evaluated Green's functions using relativistic tree-level diagrams with phenomenological cut-off factors ($\Lambda_B = 850$ MeV for baryons, $\Lambda_M = 1700$ MeV for mesons) and incorporated $\pi\pi$ rescattering in the $I=J=0$ channel to model the scalar-isoscalar correlation.

## Key numbers

- $f_{\pi N N^*}(m_\pi^2) = 0.465$ and $f_{\pi \Delta N^*}(m_\pi^2) = 1.71$, extracted from the central values of $N^*(1440) \rightarrow \pi N$ and $N^*(1440) \rightarrow \pi \Delta$ decay widths listed in the Particle Data Group (2004).
- $N^*(1440) \rightarrow N(\pi\pi)^{I=0}_{S\text{ wave}}$ decay width of 26.25 MeV (central value from PDG 2004) used to constrain the ellipse of parameters $c^*_1$ and $c^*_2$.
- Cut-off parameters fixed at $\Lambda_B = 850$ MeV and $\Lambda_M = 1700$ MeV based on fits to total cross section data up to $T_\pi = 620$ MeV.
- Coupling constants $f_{\pi \Delta \Delta} = 0.4$ and $f_{\rho N \Delta} = 7.8$ selected from the allowed ranges ($0.4 \lesssim f_{\pi \Delta \Delta} \lesssim 0.8$, $3.5 \lesssim f_{\rho N \Delta} \lesssim 7.8$) based on fits to $\pi^\pm p \rightarrow \pi^\pm \pi^0 p$ channels.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral reduction formula approach (Kamano and Arima, 2004) which studied $\Delta(1232)$ contributions up to $T_\pi = 400$ MeV, by incorporating the $N^*(1440)$ resonance to address the energy region up to $T_\pi = 620$ MeV. The study directly confronts recent high-precision data from the Crystal Ball Collaboration (Prakhov et al., 2004; Crabb et al., 2003) for the $\pi^- p \rightarrow \pi^0 \pi^0 n$ reaction, and builds upon earlier theoretical work by Jena (1997) and Bernard et al. (1995) on the role of the $N^*(1440) \rightarrow N(\pi\pi)_{S\text{ wave}}$ decay. The results enable further investigation of the controversial $\sigma$ meson degree of freedom, which has been suggested as important for understanding the structure of $N^*(1440)$ itself (Krein et al., 2000; Dillig et al., 2004).
