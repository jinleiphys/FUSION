# Schwinger Model with a Dynamical Axion

> One of the major open puzzles in the Standard Model of particle physics is the strong CP problem: although Quantum Chromodynamics allows a CP-violating topological $θ$-term, experiments constrain its value to be extremely small. The Peccei--Quinn mechanism resolves this problem by promoting the $θ$-angle to a dynamical field-introducing the axion -- whose dynamics relax the effective angle $θ_\text{eff}$ to a CP-conserving minimum. Here, we investigate the resulting axion physics in a Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory (LGT) by coupling a quantized axion field to the massive Schwinger model with a topological $θ$-term. Using infinite matrix product state techniques, we compute the ground-state properties of the resulting theory and demonstrate that the axion dynamically relaxes $θ_\text{eff}$ to the minimum of the vacuum energy. Consequently, the ground-state energy becomes independent of $θ$, demonstrating the axion-mediated solution to the strong CP problem within a fully dynamical LGT. We further analyze CP restoration and extract the axion mass from the topological susceptibility and excitation spectrum. Our results provide a nonperturbative demonstration of axion dynamics in a quantum LGT amenable to investigation on modern quantum hardware.

# Schwinger Model with a Dynamical Axion

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a dynamical axion field coupled to the massive Schwinger model in 1+1D relaxes the effective topological angle to the CP-conserving minimum of the vacuum energy, making the ground-state energy independent of the bare theta parameter. However, this cancellation is only exact for integer spin truncations of the gauge field (s=1), while half-integer truncations (s=3/2) show deviations at low gauge coupling due to truncation-induced shifts of the potential minima.

## Method

The authors couple a quantized axion field to the massive Schwinger model in a Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory formulation, using the quantum link model (QLM) to truncate the infinite-dimensional gauge fields to finite spin representations s. They employ infinite matrix product state (iMPS) techniques with the infinite density matrix renormalization group (iDMRG) algorithm to compute ground-state properties, including the ground-state energy, axion field expectation value, electric field expectation value, topological susceptibility, and the energy gap to the first excited state via the MPS excitation ansatz.

## Key numbers

- Bond dimension of 30 used for iDMRG calculations, sufficient for one spatial dimension away from criticality
- Gauge coupling values studied: g² ∈ {1, 5, 10} with fermion mass m = 0.25 and minimal coupling κ = 2
- Spin truncations studied: s ∈ {1/2, 1, 3/2, 2, 5/2, 3}
- Axion decay constant fixed at f_a = 4π with local axion truncation to 32 levels per site
- For s=1 QLM: topological susceptibility χ = 0.0141 (g²=1), 0.0888 (g²=5), 0.2114 (g²=10)
- Axion mass m_a = 0.0594 (g²=1), 0.1489 (g²=5), 0.230 (g²=10)
- Energy gap to first excited state ΔE = 0.0656 (g²=1), 0.1486 (g²=5), 0.230 (g²=10)

## Context

This work extends the study of axion physics in the Schwinger model beyond effective potential descriptions, such as those in Ho et al. (2026), by treating the axion as a fully dynamical quantum field rather than minimizing an effective potential. It provides a nonperturbative demonstration of the Peccei-Quinn mechanism in a Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory amenable to quantum simulation, complementing Euclidean lattice QCD approaches that suffer from a sign problem at finite theta. The results enable future investigations of real-time axion dynamics on quantum hardware and establish the Schwinger model as a testbed for axion-gauge field dynamics.
