# Relativistic Green's function approach to charged-current neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering

> A relativistic Green's function approach to inclusive quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering is developed. The components of the hadron tensor are written in terms of the single-particle Green's function, which is expanded on the eigenfunctions of the nuclear optical potential, so that final state interactions are accounted for by means of a complex optical potential but without a loss of flux. Results for the (neutrino_mu, mu-) reaction on 16O and 12C target nuclei are presented and discussed. A reasonable agreement of the flux-averaged cross section on 12C with experimental data is achieved.

# Relativistic Green's function approach to charged-current neutrino-nucleus quasielastic scattering

## Key claim

The authors develop a relativistic Green's function formalism for inclusive quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering that treats final state interactions via a complex optical potential while conserving flux, achieving reasonable agreement with experimental data on 12C only after normalizing the calculated cross section to the data in arbitrary units. The approach overestimates the measured flux-averaged cross section on 12C, and the authors note that other processes beyond quasielastic scattering (such as excitation of discrete states) may affect the inclusive reaction at the low energies of the experiment.

## Method

The hadron tensor for inclusive quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering is expressed in terms of the single-particle Green's function, which is expanded in a biorthogonal basis of eigenfunctions of the complex nuclear optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate. The cross sections are calculated from a single-particle expression of the hadron tensor using relativistic bound state wave functions from Dirac-Hartree solutions and the EDAD1 energy-dependent complex phenomenological optical potential fitted to proton elastic scattering data. Only the first term of the spectral representation is included, neglecting the principal-value integral term estimated to be small in the kinematics explored.

## Key numbers

- Flux-averaged cross section for 12C(nu_mu, mu-) integrated over muon energy: 11.15 x 10^{-40} cm^2 (Green's function approach), compared to experimental value (10.6 +/- 0.3 +/- 1.8) x 10^{-40} cm^2 from the LSND collaboration
- Fermi constant G = 1.16639 x 10^{-11} MeV^{-2}
- Cabibbo angle: cos(theta_c) = 0.9749
- Axial coupling constant g_A = 1.267
- Axial mass M_A = 1.032 GeV
- Muon mass = 105.9 MeV
- Incident neutrino energies studied: 300, 500, and 1000 MeV for 16O target
- Optical potential energy range of validity: up to 1040 MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Green's function approach previously applied to inclusive electron scattering (Capuzzi et al., nonrelativistic; Meucci et al., relativistic) to charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions. The method treats final state interactions consistently with exclusive reactions like (e,e'p) and (gamma,p), which were successfully described by the same group using relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA). The approach contrasts with earlier calculations using the elementary particle model (Kim and Min), relativistic Fermi gas with RPA (Kim et al.), shell model (Hayes, Volpe), and continuum RPA (Kolbe et al.), all of which gave larger cross section values than the present Green's function result. The work enables consistent treatment of inclusive and exclusive neutrino-nucleus reactions within a single relativistic framework, though the authors note that the principal-value integral term and contributions from non-quasielastic processes remain to be included for a complete description.
