# A relativistic description of the A(pi+,K+)_ΛA reaction

> We investigate the A$(π^+,K^+){_Λ}$A reaction within a covariant model. We consider those amplitudes which are described by creation, propagation and decay into relevant channel of $N^*$(1650), $N^*$(1710), and $N^*$(1720) intermediate baryonic resonance states in the initial collision of the incoming pion with one of the target nucleons. The bound state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with appropriate scalar and vector potentials. Expressions for the reaction amplitudes are derived taking continuum particle wave function in the plane wave approximation. Numerical calculations are presented for reactions on $^{12}$C, $^{40}$Ca, $^{51}$V and $^{89}$Y target nuclei. The predictions of our model are in reasonable agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their fully covariant model, which treats baryons as Dirac particles and includes only s-channel resonance contributions from N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) intermediate states, provides a reasonable description of the A(π+,K+)ΛA reaction data, but they acknowledge that the omission of non-resonant background terms introduces an uncertainty of 10-20% in the results.

## Method

The authors calculate differential cross sections for the (π+,K+) reaction on 12C, 40Ca, 51V, and 89Y targets using a covariant model where the reaction proceeds via creation, propagation, and decay of intermediate N* baryon resonance states. Bound state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector potentials, while continuum particle wave functions are treated in the plane wave approximation. The elementary πN → KΛ process is described using effective Lagrangians with pseudoscalar or pseudovector couplings for spin-1/2 resonances and derivative couplings for the spin-3/2 resonance, with coupling constants taken from previous studies of K+Λ production in proton-proton collisions.

## Key numbers

- Incident pion lab momentum: 1.05 GeV for the 12C(π+,K+)12ΛC reaction
- Binding energy of the Λ s1/2 state in 12ΛC: 10.79 ± 0.11 MeV
- Binding energy of the Λ p3/2 state in 12ΛC: 0.10 ± 0.04 MeV
- Binding energy of the Λ s1/2 state in 51ΛV: 19.75 MeV
- Binding energy of the Λ s1/2 state in 89ΛY: 23.1 MeV
- Best experimental resolution achieved at KEK: approximately 1.6-1.7 MeV
- Momentum transfer in the (π+,K+) reaction: typically 300 MeV/c or more
- Coupling constants: g(N*(1650)Nπ) = 0.81, g(N*(1650)ΛK+) = 0.76; g(N*(1710)Nπ) = 1.04, g(N*(1710)ΛK+) = 6.12; g(N*(1720)Nπ) = 0.21, g(N*(1720)ΛK+) = 0.87

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations of the (π+,K+) reaction, which used Schrodinger equation solutions with Woods-Saxon or harmonic oscillator potentials, by providing a fully covariant treatment that retains the field theoretical structure of interaction vertices and treats baryons as Dirac particles. The authors note that at momentum transfers of 300 MeV/c or more, the lower component of the Dirac spinor is no longer negligible compared to its upper component, motivating the relativistic approach. The model builds on similar covariant descriptions of the A(p,K+)ΛB and A(γ,K+)ΛB reactions developed by Shyam et al. (2004, 2006, 2008), and the results enable future inclusion of distortion effects and core-excited states for more accurate spectroscopic studies of hypernuclei.
