# A percolation transition in Yang-Mills matter at finite number of colours

> We examine baryonic matter at quark chemical potential of the order of the confinement scale, $μ_q\sim \lqcd$. In this regime, quarks are supposed to be confined but baryons are close to the ``tightly packed limit'' where they nearly overlap in configuration space. We show that this system will exhibit a percolation phase transition {\em when varied in the number of colours} $N_c$: at high $N_c$, large distance correlations at quark level are possible even if the quarks are essentially confined. At low $N_c$, this does not happen. We discuss the relevance of this for dense nuclear matter, and argue that our results suggest a new ``phase transition'', varying $N_c$ at constant $μ_q$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that baryonic matter at quark chemical potential near the confinement scale (\(\mu_q \sim \Lambda_{QCD}\)) will exhibit a percolation phase transition when the number of colours \(N_c\) is varied, with the transition occurring at a critical \(N_c\) probably but not certainly higher than 3; this is distinct from deconfinement because the fraction of correlated quarks remains small even above the transition.

## Method

The authors model two neighbouring baryons as hard spheres of radius \(\Lambda_{QCD}^{-1}\) containing \(N_c\) quarks each, and define a link probability \(p\) that at least one quark-level correlation (via gluon exchange or quark flip) occurs between them. They compute \(p\) by integrating over quark positions with a transition amplitude \(F(y)\) that drops sharply above a range \(r_T\) and has strength \(\lambda/N_c\), then compare \(p\) to the known 3D bond-percolation threshold \(p_c\) for various lattices. The calculation assumes \(\alpha=2\) (gluon-exchange dominance) and uses two forms for \(F(y)\): a step function and a momentum-space \(\Theta\)-function propagator.

## Key numbers

- The critical link probability for percolation on a hexagonal-close-packed lattice is \(p_c \sim 0.12\); for simple-cubic it is \(p_c \sim 0.25\), and for body-centered-cubic it is \(p_c \sim 0.18\).
- The inter-baryon distance is fixed at \(2\Lambda_{QCD}^{-1}\) (approximately 2 fm in natural units).
- The critical number of colours is shown in contour plots for \(2 \le N_c \le 80\) as a function of \(\lambda\) and \(r_T\) (in \(\Lambda_{QCD}\) units); for \(r_T \sim \Lambda_{QCD}^{-1}\) and \(\lambda \sim 1\), the critical \(N_c\) is significantly larger than 3.
- The correction from correlations between quark pairs in the integral for \(p\) is approximately 3%, leaving the qualitative outcome unaffected.

## Context

This work extends the percolation picture of deconfinement (previously applied to high-temperature QCD) to the dense baryonic regime at \(\mu_q \sim \Lambda_{QCD}\), building on the quarkyonic matter conjecture of McLerran and collaborators (2007). It also connects to the large-\(N_c\) Skyrme crystal studies of Witten (1979) and to mean-field analyses showing \(N_c\) variation between \(N_c=3\) and \(N_c=10\) (Giacosa, 2010). The results suggest that applying the \(N_c \to \infty\) limit to dense baryonic systems should be done with caution, and they motivate future lattice studies using strong-coupling expansion or holographic techniques beyond the supergravity limit.
