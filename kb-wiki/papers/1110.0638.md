# Particle Production at Large Transverse Momentum with ALICE

> We present transverse momentum distributions of inclusive charged particles and identified hadrons in $pp$ and Pb--Pb collisions at $\rs= 2.76$ TeV, measured by ALICE at the LHC. The Pb--Pb data are presented in intervals of collision centrality and cover transverse momenta up to 50 GeV/$c$. Nuclear medium effects are studied in terms of the nuclear modification factor \raa. The results indicate a strong suppression of high-$p_T$ particles in Pb--Pb collisions, consistent with a large energy loss of hard-scattered partons in the hot, dense and long-lived medium created at the LHC. We compare the preliminary results for inclusive charged particles to previous results from RHIC and calculations from energy loss models. Furthermore, we compare the nuclear modification factors of inclusive charged particles to those of identified $π^0$, $π^{\pm}$, K$^0_s$, and $Λ$.

## Key claim

The strong suppression of high-transverse-momentum particles in central Pb–Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=2.76\) TeV is confirmed and quantified, but the paper notes that the new results based on a measured \(pp\) reference at the same energy are slightly (10–20%) below previously published ALICE results, though still within the earlier uncertainties.

## Method

Inclusive charged-particle tracks were reconstructed using the Inner Tracking System (ITS) and Time Projection Chamber (TPC) in ALICE, with a momentum resolution parametrized as \(\sigma_{p_T}/p_T \approx 0.002 \cdot p_T\). The nuclear modification factor \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) was constructed by dividing the Pb–Pb yield by the \(pp\) reference scaled by the mean number of nucleon-nucleon collisions \(\langle N_{\mathrm{coll}}\rangle\) from a Monte-Carlo Glauber model; the \(pp\) reference used measured data below \(p_T=6\) GeV/\(c\) and a modified Hagedorn function fit from \(6<p_T<40\) GeV/\(c\) extrapolated to 50 GeV/\(c\). Identified hadrons (charged pions, neutral pions via photon conversions, \(\mathrm{K}^0_s\), and \(\Lambda\)) were separated using TPC specific energy loss and topological reconstruction of secondary decay vertices.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive charged-particle \(p_T\) distributions in Pb–Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=2.76\) TeV are presented out to \(p_T=50\) GeV/\(c\) for several centrality intervals.
- The \(p_T\) resolution is \(\sigma_{p_T}/p_T \approx 0.002 \cdot p_T\), yielding about 10% at \(p_T=50\) GeV/\(c\).
- The uncertainty on the \(pp\) reference from parametrization and extrapolation reaches 20% at \(p_T=50\) GeV/\(c\).
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) in central (0–5%) Pb–Pb collisions shows a pronounced minimum at about \(p_T=6\)–8 GeV/\(c\) and then rises monotonically up to \(p_T=50\) GeV/\(c\).
- The new central \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) is slightly (10–20%) below the previously published ALICE result (Aamodt et al., 2011) but within those uncertainties.
- The \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) of \(\Lambda\) in peripheral and central collisions at LHC does not show the enhancement above unity reported by STAR at RHIC (lower collision energy).
- Strange particle \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) results extend out to \(p_T=16\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This paper presents the first LHC results on high-\(p_T\) particle production at a Quark Matter conference, extending the jet-quenching measurements previously performed at RHIC by experiments such as STAR and PHENIX. The data provide stronger constraints on energy-loss models (e.g., those by X.-N. Wang, Majumder, Renk, Horowitz) because the less steeply falling \(p_T\) spectrum at LHC increases sensitivity to the details of the parton energy-loss distribution \(P(\Delta E)\). The comparison of \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) for different hadron species (pions, kaons, \(\Lambda\)) enables studies of baryon-versus-meson suppression patterns and may help separate quark and gluon energy losses.
