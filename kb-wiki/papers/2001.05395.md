# Exploring the partonic phase at finite chemical potential in and out-of equilibrium

> We study the influence of the baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ on the properties of the Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) in and out-of equilibrium. The description of the QGP in equilibrium is based on the effective propagators and couplings from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) that is matched to reproduce the equation-of-state of the partonic system above the deconfinement temperature $T_c$ from lattice QCD. We study the transport coefficients such as the ratio of shear viscosity $η$ and bulk viscosity $ζ$ over entropy density $s$, i.e. $η/s$ and $ζ/s$ in the $(T,μ)$ plane and compare to other model results available at $μ_B =0$. The out-of equilibrium study of the QGP is performed within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach extended in the partonic sector by explicitly calculating the total and differential partonic scattering cross sections based on the DQPM and the evaluated at actual temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ in each individual space-time cell where partonic scattering takes place. The traces of their $μ_B$ dependences are investigated in different observables for symmetric Au+Au and asymmetric Cu+Au collisions such as rapidity and $m_T$- distributions and directed and elliptic flow coefficients $v_1, v_2$ in the energy range 7.7 GeV $\le \sqrt{s_{NN}}\le 200$ GeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the baryon chemical potential (mu_B) significantly influences the equilibrium and non-equilibrium properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), but this influence is demonstrated specifically through the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) matched to lattice QCD results, meaning the conclusions are model-dependent and rely on the DQPM's effective propagators and couplings.

## Method

The equilibrium QGP properties were computed using the DQPM, which provides effective propagators and couplings matched to reproduce the lattice QCD equation of state above the deconfinement temperature T_c. Transport coefficients (eta/s and zeta/s) were then calculated across the (T, mu_B) plane. For the out-of-equilibrium study, the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach was extended by explicitly calculating total and differential partonic scattering cross sections based on DQPM, evaluated at the actual temperature T and baryon chemical potential mu_B in each individual space-time cell where partonic scattering occurs. These cross sections were then used to simulate Au+Au and Cu+Au collisions at various beam energies.

## Key numbers

*   The study covers collision energies in the range 7.7 GeV <= sqrt(s_NN) <= 200 GeV for symmetric Au+Au and asymmetric Cu+Au collisions.
*   Transport coefficients eta/s and zeta/s were mapped across the (T, mu_B) plane, with comparisons to other model results available at mu_B = 0.
*   The mu_B dependence of partonic scattering cross sections was investigated through observables including rapidity distributions, m_T distributions, and directed flow v_1 and elliptic flow v_2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of QGP properties at zero baryon chemical potential (mu_B = 0) by incorporating finite mu_B effects into both equilibrium transport coefficients and non-equilibrium transport simulations. It builds upon the DQPM framework, which was previously matched to lattice QCD results, and integrates it into the PHSD transport approach to study heavy-ion collisions. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and provide a framework for interpreting how finite baryon density modifies QGP transport properties and flow observables.
