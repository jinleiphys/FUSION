# Continuum effects in neutron-drip-line oxygen isotopes

> The binding-energy pattern along the neutron-rich oxygen chain, governed by an interplay between shell effects and many-body correlations impacted by strong couplings to one- and two-neutron continuum, make these isotopes a unique testing ground for nuclear models. In this work, we investigate ground states and low-lying excited states of $^{23-28}$O using the complex-energy Gamow Shell Model and Density Matrix Renormalization Group method with a finite-range two-body interaction optimized to the bound states and resonances of $^{23-26}$O, assuming a core of $^{22}$O. Our results suggest that the ground-state of $^{28}$O has a threshold character, i.e., is very weakly bound or slightly unbound. We also predict narrow excited resonances in $^{25}$O and $^{27}$O. The inclusion of the large continuum space significantly impacts predicted binding energies of $^{26-28}$O. This implies that the careful treatment of neutron continuum is necessary prior to assessing the spectroscopic quality of effective interactions in this region.

# Continuum effects in neutron-drip-line oxygen isotopes

## Key claim

The ground state of ²⁸O has a threshold character, meaning it is either very weakly bound or slightly unbound, but the authors' model predicts it to be bound by about 500 keV, which is in apparent contradiction with current experimental evidence suggesting it is unbound.

## Method

The authors used the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) combined with the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) method, assuming a ²²O core and optimizing a finite-range Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) two-body interaction to the bound states and resonances of ²³⁻²⁶O. The GSM Hamiltonian included the Berggren ensemble of single-particle states (bound states, decaying resonances, and non-resonant scattering continuum) in the complex-energy plane, with the model space limited to the bound 1s₁/₂ and resonant 0d₃/₂ shells and associated scattering continua. Two optimization variants were performed: variant A included a ν0d₅/₂ hole configuration via a GSM-hole technique, while variant B did not.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of ²⁸O is calculated to be bound by about 500 keV in both variants A and B, in apparent contradiction with current experimental evidence.
- Including the continuum space adds about 2 MeV of binding energy to the ground state of ²⁸O compared to calculations without scattering states.
- Adding pf scattering continua further decreases the ground-state energy of ²⁸O by 0.55 MeV.
- The enlarged model space (including p₃/₂, p₁/₂, d₅/₂, f₇/₂, and f₅/₂ continua) results in increased binding of ²⁶O by about 200 keV and ²⁸O by about 350 keV due to continuum coupling.
- The predicted narrow excited Jπ = 5/2⁺ state in ²⁵O has a decay width of about 80 keV, while the Jπ = 1/2⁺ states in ²⁵O and ²⁷O have widths less than 10 keV.
- The ground-state energy of ²⁴O relative to ²²O is -6.35 MeV experimentally, compared to -6.34 MeV (variant A) and -6.29 MeV (variant B) in the calculation.
- The ground-state energy of ²⁶O relative to ²²O is -6.33 MeV experimentally, compared to -6.39 MeV (variant A) and -6.31 MeV (variant B).

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, such as those by Caurier et al. (1998) and Utsuno et al. (2001), which predicted ²⁸O to be two-neutron unstable, and continuum shell-model calculations by Volya and Zelevinsky (2005, 2006) that also yielded ²⁸O outside the two-neutron drip line. The results contrast with state-of-the-art calculations including chiral two- and three-body forces (Hergert et al., 2013, 2014; Bogner et al., 2014; Cipollone et al., 2013, 2015; Jansen et al., 2014; Epelbaum et al., 2014; Binder et al., 2014; Stroberg et al., 2017) that deviate from experiment for the heaviest known isotopes like ²⁶O in the absence of continuum couplings. The findings motivate further experimental and theoretical investigations to determine definitively whether ²⁸O is bound or unbound, and to assess the role of repulsive effective three-nucleon forces in this region.
