# Infrared and extended on-mass-shell renormalization of two-loop diagrams

> Using a toy model Lagrangian we demonstrate the application of both infrared and extended on-mass-shell renormalization schemes to multiloop diagrams by considering as an example a two-loop self-energy diagram. We show that in both cases the renormalized diagrams satisfy a straightforward power counting.

## Key claim

Using a toy model Lagrangian with scalar particles, the authors demonstrate that both the infrared (IR) renormalization scheme and the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme can be applied to two-loop self-energy diagrams, and that in both cases the renormalized diagrams satisfy a straightforward power counting. The key condition is that the subtraction of one-loop sub-diagrams plays an essential role in the renormalization of two-loop diagrams.

## Method

The authors consider a two-loop self-energy diagram for a scalar particle Ψ interacting with a lighter scalar particle π via a π²Ψ² interaction in a toy model Lagrangian. They apply dimensional regularization and use a dimensional counting analysis to separate the self-energy into three components based on how the integral behaves when the light mass M and the quantity p²-m² are taken to zero. For both the IR and EOMS renormalization schemes, they identify the subtraction terms needed to remove power-counting-violating contributions, including contributions from counterterms that arise from renormalizing the one-loop sub-diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The two-loop self-energy diagram is assigned the chiral order Q^(2n-5), where n is the number of space-time dimensions and Q represents small quantities such as the π mass M or small external momenta.
- In the EOMS scheme, subtraction terms up to and including order Q² are given by ΔF^EOMS = Δ_{0,0} + Δ_{1,0}(p²-m²) + Δ_{2,0}(p²-m²)² + Δ_{0,1}M², with explicit coefficients expressed in terms of Gamma functions and the mass m.
- In the IR scheme, additional subtraction terms beyond the EOMS ones include δ_{3,0}(p²-m²)³ and δ_{1,1}(p²-m²)M², with coefficients also given in terms of Gamma functions.
- The one-loop counterterm contribution in the IR scheme contains a term M²I_π ∼ Q⁴, which can be neglected in a calculation up to and including order Q³.

## Context

This work extends the reformulated IR renormalization scheme of Schindler (2003) to multiloop diagrams, building on the earlier IR regularization of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) and the EOMS scheme of Fuchs (2003). The paper demonstrates that the procedure can be applied iteratively to multiloop diagrams, and while calculations using the full baryon chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian are more involved, the general features of the renormalization program do not change. This enables consistent power counting in manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulations of chiral perturbation theory beyond one-loop order.
