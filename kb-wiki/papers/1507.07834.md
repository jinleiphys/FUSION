# Analytic solution of the Boltzmann equation in an expanding system

> For a massless gas with constant cross section in a homogeneous, isotropically expanding spacetime we reformulate the relativistic Boltzmann equation as a set of non-linear coupled moment equations. For a particular initial condition this set can be solved exactly, yielding the first analytical solution of the Boltzmann equation for an expanding system. The non-equilibrium behavior of this relativistic gas can be mapped onto that of a homogeneous, static non-relativistic gas of Maxwell molecules.

## Key claim

For a massless gas with constant cross section in a homogeneous, isotropically expanding spacetime, the relativistic Boltzmann equation can be reformulated as a set of non-linear coupled moment equations that, for a particular initial condition, can be solved exactly, yielding the first analytical solution of the full Boltzmann equation for an expanding system. The non-equilibrium behavior of this relativistic gas can be mapped onto that of a homogeneous, static non-relativistic gas of Maxwell molecules.

## Method

The authors considered a massless gas with constant, energy-independent total cross section in a Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker (FLRW) metric with zero global curvature. They used the method of moments to convert the relativistic Boltzmann equation into an infinite set of coupled nonlinear differential equations for the scalar moments of the distribution function. By introducing scaled moments and a scaled time variable, the evolution equations were simplified to a form identical to the Bobylev-Krook-Wu (BKW) equations for a homogeneous, static non-relativistic gas of Maxwell molecules, which admits an exact analytic solution of the Krook-Wu type.

## Key numbers

* The scaled moments are given by \(M_m(\tau) = \mathcal{K}(\tau)^{m-1}[m - (m-1)\mathcal{K}(\tau)]\) for \(m \geq 0\), where \(\mathcal{K}(\tau) = 1 - \frac{1}{4}\exp(-\tau/6)\) and \(\tau\) is a time variable accounting for the expansion.
* The analytical solution for the distribution function is \(f_k(\tau) = \lambda \exp\left(-\frac{u\cdot k}{\mathcal{K}(\tau)T(\tau)}\right) \left[ \frac{4\mathcal{K}(\tau)-3}{\mathcal{K}^4(\tau)} + \frac{u\cdot k}{T(\tau)}\left( \frac{1-\mathcal{K}(\tau)}{\mathcal{K}^5(\tau)}\right) \right]\).
* At the initial time \(\tau = 0\) (corresponding to \(\hat{t}_0\)), the initial condition is \(f_k(0) = \frac{256}{243} (k/T_0) \lambda \exp[-4k/(3T_0)] > 0\).
* For an expansion with \(a(\hat{t}) \sim \hat{t}^{1/2}\), the mean free path increases faster than the expansion rate, \(\lim_{\hat{t}\to \infty} \ell(\hat{t})\theta(\hat{t}) \to \infty\), and local equilibrium cannot be reached even at asymptotically large times.

## Context

This work extends the study of analytical solutions of the Boltzmann equation, which were previously known only for homogeneous systems (Bobylev 1976, Krook and Wu 1977, Ernst 1984), to an expanding system for the first time. It builds on recent progress using the Anderson-Witting equation (a relaxation time approximation) for expanding systems (Florkowski 2013, Denicol 2014, Noronha 2015, Hatta 2015) by solving the full nonlinear collision term. The results enable a deeper understanding of non-equilibrium phenomena in expanding dilute gases, such as the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions, and demonstrate that exact self-similar non-equilibrium solutions may not exist even in rapidly expanding systems.
