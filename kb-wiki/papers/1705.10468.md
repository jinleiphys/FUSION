# The neutron radiation capture process $n+p\rightarrow D+γ$ and $^1 S_0$ $np$ wave function

> Cross section of the neutron capture reaction $n+p\rightarrow d+γ$ at threshold was calculated with different realistic $^{3}S_{1}$ deuteron and $^{1}S_{0}$ $np$ scattering wave functions stemming from Nijmegen-II, JISP16, Paris, Idaho and Moscow (with forbidden states) potentials. It is found that this reaction with thermal neutrons may be described without a contribution of meson exchange currents.

# The neutron radiation capture process \(n+p\rightarrow D+\gamma\) and \(^1 S_0\) \(np\) wave function

**Authors:** Khokhlov, N. A.  
**Date:** 2017-05-30

## Key claim

The thermal neutron capture reaction \(n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma\) can be described without any contribution from meson exchange currents, provided that properly fitted local \(^{1}S_{0}\) and \(^{3}SD_{1}\) partial potentials are used. This contradicts the long-standing assumption that pion exchange currents are necessary to reproduce the cross section.

## Method

The cross section for \(n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma\) at threshold was calculated using the formula \(\sigma = (2\pi \alpha w^3)/(M_N q) |F|^2\), where the matrix element \(F\) involves an integral over the product of the \(^{1}S_{0}\) scattering wave function \(u_0(r)\) and the \(^{3}S_{1}\) deuteron wave function \(u(r)\). Wave functions were generated from five realistic \(NN\) potentials: Nijmegen-II, JISP16, Paris, Idaho, and Moscow (with forbidden states). Two Moscow-type potentials (Moscow14 and Moscow17-eD Inverse) were specifically fitted to reproduce the experimental \(np\) singlet scattering length and the thermal capture cross section.

## Key numbers

- Experimental cross section: \(\sigma(np\rightarrow d\gamma) = 334.2 \pm 0.5\) mb (thermal neutrons)
- Experimental \(np\) singlet scattering length: \(a_s = -23.7\) Fm
- Nijmegen-II potential: \(\sigma = 304\) mb, \(a_s = -23.6\) Fm
- Idaho potential: \(\sigma = 308\) mb, \(a_s = -23.7\) Fm
- Paris potential: \(\sigma = 188\) mb, \(a_s = -17.5\) Fm
- JISP16 potential: \(\sigma = 286\) mb, \(a_s = -22.4\) Fm
- Moscow14 potential: \(\sigma = 334\) mb, \(a_s = -23.7\) Fm
- Moscow17-eD Inverse potential: \(\sigma = 334\) mb, \(a_s = -23.7\) Fm
- Nucleon momentum for thermal neutrons: \(q \approx 1.745 \times 10^{-5}\) Fm\(^{-1}\)

## Context

This work directly challenges the conclusion of Mathiot (1984) and the textbook "Pions and Nuclei" that meson exchange currents are required to describe low-energy \(np\) capture and deuteron electrodisintegration. The Paris potential was used in Mathiot (1984) to establish that necessity, but the present calculation shows that Paris gives a cross section of only 188 mb, far below experiment. The author notes that relativistic effects (Arriaga, 2007) and other corrections (Leidemann, 1983) complicate the electrodisintegration case. The Moscow-type potentials fitted here simultaneously describe \(NN\) elastic scattering up to 2.7 GeV, the deuteron electromagnetic form factors, and the thermal capture cross section, suggesting that local potentials can absorb the effects traditionally attributed to exchange currents.
