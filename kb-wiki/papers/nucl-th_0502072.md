# Asymptotic normalization coefficients for mirror virtual nucleon decays in a microscopic cluster model

> It has been suggested recently (Phys. Rev. Lett. 91, 232501 (2003)) that charge symmetry of nucleon-nucleon interactions relates the Asymptotic Normalization Coefficients (ANCs) of proton and neutron virtual decays of mirror nuclei. This relation is given by a simple analytical formula which involves proton and neutron separation energies, charges of residual nuclei and the range of their strong interaction with the last nucleon. Relation between mirror ANCs, if understood properly, can be used to predict astrophysically relevant direct proton capture cross sections using neutron ANCs measured with stable beams. In this work, we calculate one-nucleon ANCs for several light mirror pairs, using microscopic two-, three- and four-cluster models, and compare the ratio of mirror ANCs to the predictions of the simple analytic formula. We also investigate mirror symmetry between other characteristics of mirror one-nucleon overlap integrals, namely, spectroscopic factors and single-particle ANCs.

## Key claim
The simple analytical formula relating mirror asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) via charge symmetry of nucleon-nucleon interactions (Timofeyuk 2003) is tested against microscopic cluster model calculations; the agreement varies across systems, with deviations up to 6-9% for weakly-bound states, and the formula's accuracy depends on the orbital angular momentum, binding energy, and channel spin.

## Method
The authors performed microscopic cluster model (MCM) calculations for one-nucleon overlap integrals in several light mirror pairs, using two-, three-, and four-cluster wave functions with the Volkov V2 and Minnesota (MN) effective nucleon-nucleon interactions including Coulomb and spin-orbit forces. They extracted ANCs from the asymptotic tails of the overlap integrals via the microscopic R-matrix approach, and compared the ratio of mirror ANCs to the analytical formula of Timofeyuk (2003) and to single-particle estimates. For each mirror pair, they varied the Majorana parameter m (V2) or parameter u (MN) to explore a range of theoretical separation energies covering experimental values.

## Key numbers
- For the $^8$B-$^8$Li mirror pair, the ratio ${\cal R}_{MCM} = (C_p/C_n)^2$ averaged over the V2 and MN potentials is 1.08, compared to the analytical value ${\cal R}_0 = 1.13 \pm 0.01$ and the single-particle value ${\cal R}_{s.p.} = 1.01 \pm 0.01$.
- For $^{12}$N-$^{12}$B, ${\cal R}_{MCM} = 1.29$ (V2) and 1.30 (MN), compared to ${\cal R}_0 = 1.38 \pm 0.02$ and ${\cal R}_{s.p.} = 1.30 \pm 0.02$.
- For $^{13}$N-$^{13}$C in the two-cluster model, ${\cal R}_{MCM} = 1.13$ (V2) and 1.14 (MN), agreeing with ${\cal R}_0$ within uncertainties.
- For $^{17}$F-$^{17}$O (1s state), ${\cal R}_{MCM}$ is 5-8% smaller than ${\cal R}_0$ for all proton separation energies considered; when the proton separation energy decreases to 0.13 MeV, ${\cal R}_{s.p.}$ overestimates ${\cal R}_{MCM}$ by about 9%.
- The experimentally determined ratio of mirror ANCs for $^8$B-$^8$Li is $1.08 \pm 0.15$, in excellent agreement with the average MCM ratio of 1.08.

## Context
This work extends the analytical relation for mirror ANCs proposed by Timofeyuk (2003) by testing it against microscopic cluster model calculations that include many-body effects and Coulomb-induced charge symmetry breaking. The results show that the simple formula works best for relatively strongly-bound p-shell nuclei like $^{13}$N-$^{13}$C, but deviates for weakly-bound s-states and for small channel-spin components. The calculations enable more reliable predictions of astrophysical proton-capture cross sections from neutron ANCs measured with stable beams, as demonstrated by the first experiment using this idea for $^8$B from $^8$Li ANCs (Trache et al. 2003). The MCM also reveals that mirror single-particle potential wells are not exactly identical, contradicting the assumption used in single-particle estimates.
