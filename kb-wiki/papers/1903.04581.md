# Ab initio optical potentials and nucleon scattering on medium mass nuclei

> We derive ab initio optical potentials from self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory and compute the elastic scattering of neutrons off oxygen and calcium isotopes. The comparison with scattering data is satisfactory at low scattering energies. The method is benchmarked against no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) calculations, showing that virtual excitations of the target are crucial to predict proper fragmentation and absorption at higher energies. This is a significant step toward deriving optical potentials for medium mass nuclei and complex many-body systems in general.

# Ab initio optical potentials and nucleon scattering on medium mass nuclei

## Key claim
The authors derive optical potentials from self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory and show that elastic neutron scattering on oxygen and calcium isotopes can be reproduced satisfactorily at low energies, but only when virtual excitations of the target are included; the method still lacks sufficient absorption at higher energies because doorway configurations beyond 2p1h are neglected.

## Method
The irreducible self-energy from SCGF theory, computed at the ADC(3) level in a harmonic oscillator basis up to Nmax=13, is transformed to momentum space and used as a non-local, energy-dependent optical potential in a one-body Schrödinger-like equation solved in momentum space. The method is benchmarked against no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) calculations for neutron scattering off 16O using both a NN-only chiral interaction (EM500, SRG-evolved) and a saturating NN+3N interaction (NNLOsat). Phase shifts and differential cross sections are computed and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- For 16O with the EM500-SRG interaction, the SCGF static self-energy phase shifts agree well with NCSM/RGM for the jπ=1/2+ and 5/2+ partial waves, with a discrepancy of about 1 MeV for the 3/2+ resonance energy.
- For 17O quasiparticle energies from NNLOsat, SCGF (full self-energy) gives -6.3, -5.5, and 0.5 MeV for the 5/2+, 1/2+ bound states and 3/2+ resonance, respectively; NCSMC extrapolated values are -3.4, -2.7, and 3.2 MeV.
- The 3/2+ single-particle resonance in 17O is computed at 0.91 MeV in the center-of-mass frame, close to the experimental value of 0.94 MeV.
- For 17O, the NNLOsat interaction predicts bound and resonant states with accuracy of less than or about 1 MeV for dominant quasiparticle and hole states.
- Differential cross sections are shown for neutron elastic scattering on 16O at 3.286 MeV and on 40Ca at 3.2 MeV neutron energy.
- The SCGF-ADC(3) calculation for 41Ca predicts only about 40 excitations below the neutron separation threshold, while more than 200 are observed experimentally (Nesaraja, 2016).

## Context
This work extends earlier SCGF studies of phase shifts (Barbieri, 2005) and analytical properties of optical models (Waldecker, 2011) that were limited to two-nucleon forces and could not compare meaningfully to experiment due to poor radii. The use of the saturating chiral interaction NNLOsat (Ekstrom, 2015), which reproduces binding energies and radii across oxygen (Lapoux, 2016) and calcium chains (Garcia, 2016), now enables comparison to scattering data. The method is benchmarked against NCSM/RGM calculations (Navratil, 2010) and new NCSMC calculations. The results show that the lack of absorption in ab initio optical potentials is directly linked to missing 3p2h and higher doorway configurations, identifying the next challenge for predictive theories at medium scattering energies.
