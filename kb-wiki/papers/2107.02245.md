# Hyperonic neutron stars: reconciliation between nuclear properties and NICER and LIGO/VIRGO results

> Using a extended version of the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD), I propose a new microscopic equation of state (EoS) able to correctly reproduce the main properties of symmetric nuclear matter at the saturation density, as well produce massive neutron stars and satisfactory results for the radius and the tidal parameter $Λ$. I show that even when hyperons are present, this EoS is able to reproduce at least 2.00 solar masses neutron star. The constraints about the radius of a $2.00M_\odot$ and the minimum mass that enable direct Urca effect are also checked.

# Hyperonic neutron stars: reconciliation between nuclear properties and NICER and LIGO/VIRGO results

## Key claim
The paper presents a new microscopic equation of state (EoS) within extended Quantum Hadrodynamics that can simultaneously satisfy all six main nuclear matter saturation constraints and produce neutron stars above 2.00 solar masses even when hyperons are present, while also meeting NICER and LIGO/VIRGO bounds on radius and tidal deformability. However, the model does not satisfy all constraints perfectly: it shows over-pressure relative to heavy-ion collision constraints for densities between 2 and 4.6 times saturation, fulfilling only 35% of the original constraint interval (or 85% if the upper limit is increased by 20%).

## Method
The author constructs a relativistic mean-field model based on the non-linear σωρ QHD Lagrangian, extended with a hidden strangeness φ vector meson coupling only to hyperons and a non-linear ω-ρ coupling term from the IUFSU model. Hyperon-meson coupling constants are fixed using SU(3) symmetry arguments with a single free parameter αv, constrained by the Λ⁰ hyperon potential depth UΛ = -28 MeV. The EoS is solved for beta-stable, charge-neutral matter including the full baryon octet and leptons as a free Fermi gas, with the BPS EoS used for the crust.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density n₀ = 0.156 fm⁻³ (constraint range: 0.148–0.170 fm⁻³)
- Effective nucleon mass M*/M = 0.69 (constraint range: 0.6–0.8)
- Incompressibility K = 256 MeV (constraint range: 220–260 MeV)
- Symmetry energy S₀ = 31.2 MeV (constraint range: 28.6–34.4 MeV)
- Slope of symmetry energy L = 74 MeV (constraint range: 36–86.8 MeV)
- Binding energy per baryon B/A = 16.2 MeV (constraint range: 15.8–16.5 MeV)
- Maximum neutron star mass with hyperons: 2.00 M⊙ (αv=1.00), 2.07 M⊙ (αv=0.75), 2.15 M⊙ (αv=0.50); without hyperons: 2.30 M⊙
- Radius of 1.4 M⊙ star: 12.96 km (constraint: < 13.32 km)
- Tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ = 527 (constraint: < 580)
- Radius of 2.00 M⊙ star: 11.27 km (αv=1.00), 12.07 km (αv=0.75), 12.28 km (αv=0.50); constraint: > 11.85 km
- Minimum mass for direct Urca process with hyperons: 1.50 M⊙ (αv=1.00), 1.52 M⊙ (αv=0.75), 1.55 M⊙ (αv=0.50); without hyperons: 1.73 M⊙ (constraint: > 1.50 M⊙)
- Symmetry energy at 2n₀: S(2n₀) = 52.4 MeV (constraint: 38–64 MeV)
- Pressure at 2n₀: p(2n₀) = 16.4 MeV/fm³ (constraint: 11.2–38.7 MeV/fm³)

## Context
This work addresses the "hyperon puzzle", the conflict between the softening of the EoS due to hyperon onset and the observational requirement of two-solar-mass neutron stars. It extends prior QHD parametrizations such as GM1 (Glendenning and Moszkowski, 1991) and NL3 (Lalazissis et al., 1997) which produce massive hyperonic stars but fail modern nuclear constraints, and IUFSU (Fattoyev et al., 2010) which satisfies nuclear properties but does not include hyperons. The model incorporates the φ meson coupling to hyperons, following earlier work by Lopes and Menezes (2012) and Weissenborn et al. (2012), and uses SU(3) symmetry to fix hyperon couplings with a single parameter, as in Lopes (2013). The results enable future studies of finite temperature effects, finite nuclei, magnetic fields, and pasta phases using this parametrization.
