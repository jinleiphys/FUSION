# Photoproduction of pion pairs at high energy and small angles

> We explore the photoproduction mechanisms for charged and neutral pion pairs off a heavy nucleus at threshold. We calculate the production of charged pairs in the Coulomb field of the nucleus in the Born approximation using explicit expressions for the differential cross sections and their connection with the total cross-section. The production of sigma mesons at threshold and their subsequent decay into pions is an important mechanism and is investigated quantitatively in a framework that can be used for both charged and neutral pions. Sigma mesons can be produced via electromagnetic (photon exchange) or strong (omega exchange) interactions. For neutral pions, another important production mechanism consists of the two-step process where charged pions are produced first and then charge exchange into two neutral pions. Differential and total cross sections are computed for each production mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper provides quantitative calculations for the photoproduction of charged and neutral pion pairs off heavy nuclei at threshold and small angles, but the results are explicitly computed for a lead-208 target at a photon beam energy of 5.5 GeV, with the interference angle between Coulomb and strong mechanisms set to 1 radian, meaning the numerical cross sections are specific to these conditions rather than general.

## Method

The authors calculate charged pion pair production in the Coulomb field of a nucleus using the Born approximation, deriving differential cross sections expressed via the total cross section of the process gamma gamma -> pi+ pi-. For neutral pion pairs, they compute production via sigma meson decay using a Breit-Wigner formalism for both electromagnetic (photon exchange) and strong (omega exchange) mechanisms, with the strong amplitude scaled from existing parametrizations for pi0 photoproduction. They also evaluate a two-step charge-exchange mechanism where charged pions are produced and then rescatter into neutral pions, using chiral theory expressions for the cross section of gamma gamma -> pi0 pi0.

## Key numbers

* The radiative decay width of the sigma meson is taken as Gamma(sigma -> gamma gamma) = 2.05 keV, adopted from Dai (2014).
* The partial width for sigma decay to pi0 pi0 is Gamma(sigma -> pi0 pi0) = 0.210 GeV, from Agaev (2018).
* The charge-exchange contribution to neutral pion pair production is approximately 2 orders of magnitude smaller than the Coulomb contribution in the threshold region.
* The strong amplitude for sigma meson photoproduction is scaled from the nucleon amplitude f_N(gamma N -> pi0 N) = L (E/k_0)^1.2 sin(theta) with L = 10 sqrt(microbarn) and k_0 = 1 GeV, from Browman (1974).
* The differential cross sections are computed for lead-208 nucleus at a photon beam energy of 5.5 GeV, with specific results shown for pion pair masses of 0.275 GeV, 0.52 GeV, and 0.75 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism for Primakoff production of single mesons, such as the pi0 photoproduction calculations by Miskimen (2011) and the PrimEx collaboration (2010), to the case of pion pair production. The calculations are directly motivated by upcoming experiments at Jefferson Lab Hall D, including the measurement of charged pion pair Primakoff production (CPPexp) and neutral pion pair Primakoff production (NPPexp), as well as the eta meson lifetime measurement (PrimexEta). The results enable quantitative background estimates for these experiments and provide the signal calculations for the pion pair measurements themselves.
