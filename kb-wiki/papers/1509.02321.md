# Electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments of odd nuclei near the magic ones in a self-consistent approach

> We present a model which describes the properties of odd-even nuclei with one nucleon more, or less, with respect to the magic number. In addition to the effects related to the unpaired nucleon, we consider those produced by the excitation of the closed shell core. By using a single particle basis generated with Hartree-Fock calculations, we describe the polarization of the doubly magic-core with Random Phase Approximation collective wave functions. In every step of the calculation, and for all the nuclei considered, we use the same finite-range nucleon-nucleon interaction. We apply our model to the evaluation of electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments of odd-even nuclei around oxygen, calcium, zirconium, tin and lead isotopes. Our Random Phase Approximation description of the polarization of the core improves the agreement with experimental data with respect to the predictions of the independent particle model. We compare our results with those obtained in first-order perturbation theory, with those produced by Hartree-Fock-Bogolioubov calculations and with those generated within the Landau-Migdal theory of finite Fermi systems. The results of our universal, self-consistent, and parameter free approach have the same quality of those obtained with phenomenological approaches where the various terms of the nucleon-nucleon interaction are adapted to reproduce some specific experimental data. A critical discussion on the validity of the model is presented.

## Key claim

The authors present a fully self-consistent, parameter-free model for electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments of odd-even nuclei near doubly-magic cores, where the same finite-range nucleon-nucleon interaction is used in all steps (Hartree-Fock, Random Phase Approximation, and moment evaluation). The model improves agreement with experimental data compared to the Independent Particle Model, but the abstract's claim of "same quality" as phenomenological approaches is qualified by the fact that the RPA description fails for the first 2+ states in light nuclei like 16O and 40Ca, where the experimental excitation energies are much lower and transition strengths much larger than the RPA predictions.

## Method

The authors generate single-particle wave functions via Hartree-Fock calculations using the Gogny D1S or D1M finite-range effective interactions. They describe the polarization of the doubly-magic core by solving the Random Phase Approximation equations for the 2+ and 1+ excitations of the core, then evaluate the electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments of the odd-even nucleus using Eq. (6) of the paper, which includes the unpaired nucleon contribution plus core-polarization terms mediated by the residual interaction and RPA propagator. For magnetic dipole moments, they include both the one-body operator and two-body meson exchange currents from charged-pion exchange (seagull and pionic diagrams), using bare electromagnetic operators without effective charges or free parameters.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Random Phase Approximation approach to odd-even nuclei, building on the Landau-Migdal theory of finite Fermi systems (Migdal, 1967) and its applications around 208Pb (Rinker and Speth, 1973; Bauer et al., 1973; Speth et al., 1977). It differs from earlier self-consistent FFS calculations (Borghardt et al., 2008, 2010; Tolokonnikov et al., 2011, 2012; Voitenkov et al., 2012) by using a finite-range Gogny interaction (D1S from Berger et al., 1991; D1M from Goriely et al., 2009) with parameters fixed from global Hartree-Fock fits (Chamel et al., 2007), avoiding free parameters for effective charges or cutoff energies. The model enables systematic, parameter-free predictions for electric quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments across a wide range of nuclei from oxygen to lead, though it fails for light nuclei where the first 2+ state has complex structure beyond one-particle-one-hole excitations.
