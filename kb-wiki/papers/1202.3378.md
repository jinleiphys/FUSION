# Deep Crustal Heating in a Multicomponent Accreted Neutron Star Crust

> A quasi-statistical equilibrium model is constructed to simulate the multicomponent composition of the crust of an accreting neutron star. The ashes of rp-process nucleosynthesis are driven by accretion through a series of electron captures, neutron emissions, and pycnonuclear fusions up to densities near the transition between the neutron star crust and core. A liquid droplet model which includes nuclear shell effects is used to provide nuclear masses far from stability. Reaction pathways are determined consistently with the nuclear mass model. The nuclear symmetry energy is an important uncertainty in the masses of the exotic nuclei in the inner crust and varying the symmetry energy changes the amount of deep crustal heating by as much as a factor of two.

# Deep Crustal Heating in a Multicomponent Accreted Neutron Star Crust

## Key claim

Varying the nuclear symmetry energy changes the amount of deep crustal heating by as much as a factor of two, but this result depends on the specific choice of the homogeneous equation of state used to model nuclear masses in the liquid droplet approach, and the actual heating rate is sensitive to the uncertain properties of exotic nuclei in the inner crust.

## Method

A quasi-statistical equilibrium (QSE) model was constructed to simulate the multicomponent composition of an accreting neutron star crust, starting from rp-process ashes at densities near 10^6 g/cm^3 and following electron captures, neutron emissions, and pycnonuclear fusions up to the crust-core transition. Nuclear masses were computed using a liquid droplet model with shell corrections, fitted to experimental mass data with an RMS deviation of about 1.2 MeV, and the reaction pathways were determined consistently with this mass model. The system was evolved by allowing reactions that lower the Gibbs energy per particle at constant pressure, with pycnonuclear fusion rates evaluated using S-factors from Beard (2010) and the multicomponent plasma formalism of Yakovlev (2006).

## Key numbers

- The RMS deviation of the mass formula from experimental data is about 1.2 MeV, compared to 2.6 MeV or more for a typical liquid droplet model.
- The impurity parameter <Q> shows large variation in the deepest regions of the crust, with average proton and neutron numbers changing significantly with density.
- The crust model covers densities from near 10^6 g/cm^3 up to approximately 10^14 g/cm^3, where the crust ends.
- Heating from electron captures is multiplied by 1/4 to account for neutrino losses, following Haensel (2008).
- The chunk size for QSE evolution is 1/100th of the total number density of all nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the single-component crust models of Haensel (1990) and the equilibrium crust calculations of Newton (2011) by providing one of the first multicomponent models that computes properties of the deepest regions of the accreted crust. The results are relevant for interpreting crust cooling observations of transiently accreting neutron stars such as KS 1731, MXB 1659, and SAX J1808, as studied by Shternin (2007), Brown (2009), and Heinke (2006), and may help address difficulties in superburst models identified by Brown (2004), Cumming (2006), Gupta (2007), and Cooper (2009). The model enables future work on the thermal conductivity and cooling of multicomponent crusts, and can be extended to include pasta phases and more realistic pycnonuclear fusion rates.
