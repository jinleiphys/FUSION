# Breaking rotational symmetry in two-flavor color superconductors

> The color superconductivity under flavor asymmetric conditions relevant to the compact star phenomenology is studied within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We focus on the effect of the deformation of the Fermi surfaces on the pairing properties and the energy budget of the superconducting state. We find that at finite flavor asymmetries the color superconducting BCS state is unstable towards spontaneous quadrupole deformation of the Fermi surfaces of the $d$ and $u$ quarks into ellipsoidal form. The ground state of the phase with deformed Fermi surfaces corresponds to a superposition of prolate and oblate deformed Fermi ellipsoids of $d$ and $u$ quarks.

# Breaking rotational symmetry in two-flavor color superconductors

## Key claim

The spatially homogeneous two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) BCS state is unstable towards spontaneous quadrupole deformation of the Fermi surfaces of up and down quarks into ellipsoidal form at finite flavor asymmetries, but this deformed-Fermi-surface (DFS) phase is not necessarily the true ground state, a careful comparison with the crystalline color superconducting (CCS) phase is required to determine which non-BCS phase is favored.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two flavors and three colors, assuming the chirally symmetric phase, to derive the thermodynamic potential and self-consistent gap equations for the 2SC phase at finite temperature. They introduce a Legendre expansion of the chemical potentials in terms of polar angle, keeping only the leading l=2 quadrupole terms, and treat the symmetric and antisymmetric deformation parameters ε_S and ε_A as variational parameters to minimize the free energy at fixed baryon density and flavor asymmetry α.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap in the flavor-symmetric undeformed state is Δ₀₀ = 40 MeV at baryon density ρ_B = 0.31 fm⁻³ and chemical potential μ = 320 MeV at T = 0.
- The free-energy difference between superconducting and normal states in the flavor-symmetric undeformed state is δF(α=0, ε_A=0) = 37 MeV fm⁻³.
- The minimum of the free energy for the DFS phase occurs at relative deformation parameter ε_A ≈ 0.25, with weak dependence on flavor asymmetry α.
- The DFS phase exists for flavor asymmetries α > 0.3 only with positive deformation parameter; for α ≤ 0.3 the BCS state with spherical Fermi surfaces is also possible.
- The coupling constant used is G₁ = 3.10861 GeV⁻² with G₁Λ² = 1.31, employing a three-dimensional momentum cutoff Λ.

## Context

This work extends a previous non-relativistic analysis of deformed Fermi surfaces (DFS) to relativistic systems with the specific interactions of color superconductors, building on the NJL model treatments of Berges (1998), Schwarz (1999), and Buballa (2001). The DFS phase is one of several proposed non-BCS phases for flavor-asymmetric quark matter in compact stars, alongside the Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrel (LOFF) or crystalline color superconducting (CCS) phase studied by Alford (2000), Bowers (2001, 2002), and Casalbuoni (2001, 2002), and the interior gap pairing (IGP) phase. The results enable future combined studies of DFS and CCS phases to determine the true ground state of two-flavor color superconductors under β-equilibrium conditions relevant to compact star phenomenology.
