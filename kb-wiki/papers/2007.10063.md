# Anisotropy fluctuation and correlation in central $α$-clustered $^{12}$C+$^{197}$Au collisions

> In the framework of a multi-phase transport model, fluctuation and correlation of the azimuthal anisotropies in $^{12}$C+$^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV are explored. Properties of the initial eccentricity and final harmonic flow fluctuation resulted from $^{12}$C+$^{197}$Au collisions with Woods-Saxon configuration and triangular $α$-clustering configurations of $^{12}$C are investigated via scaled variance, skewness and kurtosis. Comparisons are made between results from $α$-clustered configurations and Woods-Saxon configuration. The triangular flow fluctuation is found to have particular sensitivity in distinguishing triangular $α$-clustering structure of $^{12}$C. Furthermore, correlations between initial eccentricities and final flow harmonics are studied with strong multiplicity dependence observed for the correlation functions.

# Anisotropy fluctuation and correlation in central α-clustered ¹²C+¹⁹⁷Au collisions

## Key claim

Triangular flow fluctuation (scaled variance of v₃) shows particular sensitivity in distinguishing the triangular α-clustering structure of ¹²C from a Woods-Saxon configuration, but this sensitivity is strongest in high-multiplicity collisions and the actual experimental significance may be lower than the ideal simulated cases because the ground state of ¹²C could exist as a configuration mixing state in reality.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport model (AMPT) with string melting to simulate central ¹²C+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. Initial nucleon distributions in ¹²C were configured either with a standard Woods-Saxon profile or with three α clusters arranged in a triangular structure based on extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) calculations, with cluster centers at rc(α) = 1.8 fm and width σrc(α) = 0.1 fm. Event-by-event fluctuations of initial eccentricity εn and final flow harmonics vn were characterized via scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis, and correlations between initial and final anisotropies were quantified using the Pearson coefficient.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: √sNN = 200 GeV
- Triangular α-clustering parameters: rc(α) = 1.8 fm, σrc(α) = 0.1 fm
- Transverse momentum window for charged particles: 0.2 < pT < 6.0 GeV/c
- Rapidity window for charged particles: -1.0 < y < 1.0
- The ratio Rv₃/Rv₂ shows monotonic Ntrack dependence and differs in magnitude between Woods-Saxon and triangular α-clustering configurations, with greater sensitivity at large Ntrack
- Correlation coefficients Cvn,εn (n=2,3) show an increasing trend as a function of Ntrack, with the triangular configuration presenting stronger v₃-ε₃ correlation than Woods-Saxon especially at large Ntrack

## Context

This work extends prior studies on using harmonic flow to probe α-clustering structures in light nuclei, particularly the theoretical proposals by S. Zhang (2017, 2018) and Guo (2019, 2017) that intrinsic nuclear structure leads to quantitative differences in flow measurements. It also builds on the approach of using flow fluctuation ratios proposed by Piotr (2014) and Rybczynski (2018). The results enable experimental searches for α-clustering signatures in light-heavy collision systems such as ¹⁶O+¹⁹⁷Au or ¹⁶O+²⁰⁸Pb, and suggest that flow fluctuation measurements should be used alongside standard flow coefficients as complementary probes.
