# Soft modes in hot QCD matter

> The chiral crossover of QCD at finite temperature and vanishing baryon density turns into a second order phase transition if lighter than physical quark masses are considered. If this transition occurs sufficiently close to the physical point, its universal critical behaviour would largely control the physics of the QCD phase transition. We quantify the size of this region in QCD using functional approaches, both Dyson-Schwinger equations and the functional renormalisation group. The latter allows us to study both critical and non-critical effects on equal footing, facilitating a precise determination of the scaling regime. We find that the physical point is far away from the critical region. Importantly, we show that the physics of the chiral crossover is dominated by soft modes even far beyond the critical region. While scaling functions determine all thermodynamic properties of the system in the critical region, the order parameter potential is the relevant quantity away from it. We compute this potential in QCD using the functional renormalisation group and Dyson-Schwinger equations and provide a simple parametrisation for phenomenological applications.

## Key claim
The physical point of QCD is "far away" from the critical region of the chiral phase transition, with strictly critical behavior observed only for pion masses below approximately 2-5 MeV; however, soft modes (pions and sigma mesons) still dominate the physics of the chiral crossover even far beyond this critical region.

## Method
The authors used the functional renormalisation group (fRG) to compute the light chiral condensate over five orders of magnitude in pion mass (0.002 to 200 MeV), and extracted the effective critical exponent delta as a function of pion mass from scaling fits that include sub-leading corrections. They also computed the order parameter potential via Legendre transformation of the grand potential, and cross-checked results against Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) calculations. The ratio of sigma to pion masses was evaluated as a measure of soft-mode dominance.

## Key numbers
- Critical region defined by pion mass m_pi,crit approximately 2-5 MeV, based on where the effective exponent delta(m_pi) agrees with the actual critical exponent
- Critical exponents from fixed-point analysis: beta = 0.405, delta = 4.784, theta_H = 0.272
- Pion mass range studied: m_pi in [0.002, 200] MeV (five orders of magnitude)
- Critical region in temperature direction estimated as T - T_c less than or equal to 1-7 MeV
- At the pseudocritical temperature in the critical region, the ratio m_sigma/m_pi lies between 1 and approximately 2
- The leading scaling behavior requires m_pi less than or approximately 0.01 MeV for validity

## Context
This work extends previous fRG analyses by Fu (2019) and Braun (2020) that indicated a very small scaling region, and contrasts with lattice QCD studies by HotQCD (2019), Borsanyi (2020), Aarts (2020, 2023), Kotov (2021), and Ding (2023) that suggested the physical point could be in the critical scaling region. The computed order parameter potential provides input for beyond-Ginzburg-Landau analyses of the chiral crossover and for transport studies of heavy-ion collisions, building on the framework of Gao (2021).
