# Three Point Energy Correlators in the Collinear Limit: Symmetries, Dualities and Analytic Results

> Energy Correlators measure the energy deposited in multiple detectors as a function of the angles between the detectors. In this paper, we analytically compute the three particle correlator in the collinear limit in QCD for quark and gluon jets, and also in $\mathcal{N}=4$ super Yang-Mills theory. We find an intriguing duality between the integrals for the energy correlators and infrared finite Feynman parameter integrals, which maps the angles of the correlators to dual momentum variables. In $\mathcal{N}=4$, we use this duality to express our result as a rational sum of simple Feynman integrals (triangles and boxes). In QCD our result is expressed as a sum of the same transcendental functions, but with more complicated rational functions of cross ratio variables as coefficients. Our results represent the first analytic calculation of a three-prong jet substructure observable of phenomenological relevance for the LHC, revealing unexplored simplicity in the energy flow of QCD jets. They also provide valuable data for improving the understanding of the light-ray operator product expansion.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first analytic calculation of a three-prong jet substructure observable of phenomenological relevance for the LHC, specifically the three-point energy correlator (EEEC) in the collinear limit, but the results are derived at leading order in perturbation theory and apply only in the limit where all three angles between detectors are small, without assuming any hierarchy between those angles.

## Method

The authors compute the EEEC jet function by integrating the triple collinear splitting functions for QCD (quark and gluon jets) and N=4 super Yang-Mills against the EEEC measurement function over the triple collinear phase space, using timelike collinear factorization. They exploit a duality that maps the energy correlator integrals to infrared-finite Feynman parameter integrals, where the angles of the correlators become dual momentum variables, allowing the results to be expressed in terms of polylogarithmic functions of a complex variable encoding two conformal cross ratios.

## Key numbers

* The EEEC is defined with three delta functions: δ(x₁ - (1-cosθ_ij)/2), δ(x₂ - (1-cosθ_jk)/2), δ(x₃ - (1-cosθ_kl)/2), where x₁, x₂, x₃ ≪ 1 in the collinear limit.
* The collinear phase space includes a factor Θ(-Δ)(-Δ)^{-1/2-ε} where Δ = (ξ₃s₁₂ - ξ₁s₂₃ - ξ₂s₁₃)² - 4ξ₁ξ₂s₁₃s₂₃, and the measurement function automatically ensures Δ ≥ 0 via Heron's formula.
* The Mandelstam invariants in the collinear limit satisfy s_ij = ξ_i ξ_j Q² θ_ij²/4 = ξ_i ξ_j Q² x_k.
* The area of the triangle formed by the three detectors is given by iA = (z - z̄) = i√(-r₁² - (-1+r₂)² + 2r₁(1+r₂)), where r₁ = x₁/x₃ = zz̄ and r₂ = x₂/x₃ = (1-z)(1-z̄).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of energy correlators beyond the two-point Energy-Energy Correlator (EEC), which was previously computed at NLO in QCD by Dixon et al. (2018) and Luo et al. (2019), and whose collinear limit was studied by Dixon et al. (2019) and Kologlu et al. (2019). The results provide the first analytic calculation of a multi-particle (three-prong) correlation within a jet, enabling phenomenological applications at the LHC and providing data to improve the understanding of the light-ray operator product expansion in Lorentzian signature.
