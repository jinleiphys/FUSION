# Indication of asymptotic scaling in the reactions $dd\to p^3$H, $dd\to n^3$He and $dp\to dp$

> It is shown that the differential cross sections of the reactions $dd\to ^3He n$ and $dd\to ^3H p$ measured at c.m.s.scattering angle$θ_{cm}=60^\circ$ in the interval of the deuteron beam energy 0.5 - 1.2 GeV demonstrate the scaling behaviour,$dσ/d t\sim s^{-22}$, which follows from constituent quark counting rules. It is found also that the differential cross section of the elastic $dp\to dp$ scattering at $θ_{cm}=125^\circ-135^\circ$ follows the scaling regime $\sim s^{-16}$ at beam energies 0.5 - 5 GeV. These data are parameterized here using the Reggeon exchange.

## Key claim

The differential cross sections of the reactions \(dd\to n^3He\) and \(dd\to p^3H\) at \(\theta_{cm}=60^\circ\) and the elastic \(dp\to dp\) scattering at \(\theta_{cm}=125^\circ-135^\circ\) follow the scaling behavior predicted by constituent counting rules, but the number of experimental points in the scaling region is too small (5 or 6) to make a definite conclusion, and the \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for the \(dp\to dp\) data is rather high (4.3), possibly due to systematic errors from different experiments.

## Method

The author analyzed existing experimental data for the reactions \(dd\to n^3He\) and \(dd\to p^3H\) from SATURNE at beam energies 0.3-1.25 GeV and for elastic \(dp\to dp\) scattering from several experiments at beam energies 0.5-5 GeV. The data were fitted to the scaling form \(d\sigma/dt \sim s^{-n}\) predicted by constituent counting rules, and also parameterized using a Reggeon exchange model with an effective nucleon Regge trajectory and a parameterized vertex function containing a nucleon pole term and a structureless term.

## Key numbers

- For \(dd\to n^3He\) and \(dd\to p^3H\) at \(\theta_{cm}=60^\circ\) and beam energies 0.5-1.25 GeV, the data follow \(d\sigma/dt \sim s^{-22}\) with \(\chi^2_{n.d.f.}=1.18\).
- For elastic \(dp\to dp\) at \(\theta_{cm}=125^\circ-135^\circ\) and beam energies 0.5-5 GeV, the data follow \(d\sigma/dt \sim s^{-16}\) with \(\chi^2_{n.d.f.}=4.3\).
- In the one-nucleon exchange model for \(dd\to p^3H\) at \(T_d=0.8\) GeV and \(\theta_{cm}=90^\circ\), the relative momenta are \(q_{pn}=0.8\) GeV/c and \(q_{Nd}=1.0\) GeV/c.
- For the \(dd\to n^3He\) reaction at \(\theta_{cm}=60^\circ\) and beam energies 0.5-1.25 GeV, the internal momenta in the deuteron are \(q_{pn}=0.55-0.85\) GeV/c and in the \(^3He\) (\(^3H\)) nuclei are \(q_{Nd}=0.60-0.94\) GeV/c, corresponding to NN-distances less than 0.35 fm.

## Context

This work extends the observation of constituent counting rule scaling from electromagnetic processes on the deuteron, such as the deuteron electromagnetic form factor measured at SLAC (Arnold et al.) and JLab (Alexa et al.) and the \(\gamma d\to pn\) photodisintegration reaction (Napolitano, Freedman, Beltz, Bochna, Schulte, Mizarata), to hadron-nucleus reactions with deuterons and \(^3He\) (\(^3H\)) nuclei. The scaling behavior sets in at energies around 1 GeV and large scattering angles where meson-exchange models fail and quark degrees of freedom become important, and the author suggests that new, more detailed data from a single experiment covering the full energy range are necessary to confirm the observation and to probe multi-quark configurations in light nuclei.
