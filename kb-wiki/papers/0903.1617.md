# The Inclusive-Exclusive Connection and the Neutron Negative Central Charge Density

> We find an interpretation of the recent finding that the central charge density of the neutron is negative by using models of generalized parton distributions at zero skewness to relate the behavior of deep inelastic scattering quark distributions, evaluated at large values of Bjorken x, to the transverse charge density evaluated at small distances. The key physical input of these models is the Drell-Yan-West relation We find that the d quarks dominate the neutron structure function for large values of Bjorken x, where the large longitudinal momentum of the struck quark has a significant impact on determining the center-of-momentum of the system, and thus the "center" of the nucleon in the transverse position plane.

# The Inclusive-Exclusive Connection and the Neutron Negative Central Charge Density

## Key claim

The negative central charge density of the neutron, obtained from model-independent transverse charge density calculations using measured form factors, can be interpreted through generalized parton distributions (GPDs) as arising from the dominance of negatively charged d quarks at high Bjorken x, which become localized near the center of transverse momentum due to the kinematic definition of the nucleon's center in the infinite momentum frame.

## Method

The authors compute the transverse charge density of the neutron as the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the Dirac form factor F1, using recent parameterizations of measured form factors from Kelly (2004) and Bradford et al. (2006). They then employ models of generalized parton distributions at zero skewness from Diehl et al. (2005), Guidal et al. (2005), and Ahmad et al. (2007) to decompose the charge density into contributions from different x regions, exploiting the Drell-Yan-West relation that connects high-x behavior of structure functions to high-Q2 behavior of form factors and thus to small transverse distances b.

## Key numbers

- The neutron Dirac form factor F1 is negative for all values of Q2, which directly yields a negative central charge density at b=0 via the Fourier transform relation.
- For the proton, the half-maximum width of the transverse charge density decreases from approximately 0.5 fm at x approximately 0.1 to approximately 0.12 fm at x approximately 0.8.
- Above x=0.5, the ratio d_n(x)/u_n(x) in the neutron is at least three, with d quarks dominating the parton distribution at large x.
- The GPDs of Ahmad et al. (2007) use an initial scale of Q0^2 = 0.094 GeV^2 with the constraint that the nucleon consists of three quarks.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent determination of the neutron transverse charge density by Miller (2007), which surprisingly found a negative central density, contradicting expectations from the pion cloud picture (Carlson, Brown, and Miller) and from one-gluon exchange correlations (Isgur and Karl) that predicted a positive central density. The analysis uses the inclusive-exclusive connection via GPDs to show that the negative central density is a natural consequence of the definition of the transverse center of momentum in the infinite momentum frame, where high-x d quarks dominate and become localized at small b. This enables future experimental tests at Jefferson Laboratory's 12 GeV upgrade to probe the x and b dependence of parton distributions.
