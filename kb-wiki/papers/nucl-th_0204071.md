# Extraction of the n structure function $F_2^n$ from inclusive scattering data on composite nuclei

> We consider a generalized convolution, linking Structure Functions (SF) $F^N_2$ for nucleons, $F^A_2$ for a physical nucleus and $f^{PN,A}$ for a nucleus, composed of point-nucleons. In order to extract $F_2^n$ we employ data on $F_2^{p,A}$ and the computed $f^{PN,A}$. Only for $Q^2\approx 3.5 {\rm GeV}^2$ do data permit the extraction of $F_2^A(x,3.5)$ over a sufficiently wide $x$-range. Applying Mellin transforms, the above relation between SF turns into an algebraic one, which one solves for the Mellin transform of the unknown $F_2^n$. We present inversion methods leading to the desired $F_2^n$, all using a parametrization for $C(x,Q^2)=F_2^n(x,Q^2)/F_2^p(x,Q^2)$. Imposing motivated constraints, the simplest parametrization leaves one free parameter $C(x=1,Q^2)$. For $Q^2= 3.5 {\rm GeV}^2$ its average over several targets and different methods is $<C(1,3.5)>=0.54\pm0.03$. We argue that for the investigated $Q^2$, $C(x\to 1,3.5)$ is determined by the nucleon-elastic ($NE$) part of SF. The calculated value is near the extracted one and both are close to the SU(6) limit 2/3.

## Key claim

The authors extract the neutron structure function ratio \(C(x,Q^2)=F_2^n(x,Q^2)/F_2^p(x,Q^2)\) from inclusive electron scattering data on deuterium, carbon, and iron, but only for \(Q^2\approx 3.5\ \text{GeV}^2\) because that is the only momentum transfer where data permit extraction over a sufficiently wide \(x\)-range. The extracted value \(C(1,3.5)=0.54\pm0.03\) is argued to be determined by the nucleon-elastic part of the structure functions and is close to the SU(6) limit of 2/3.

## Method

The authors use a generalized convolution relation linking nuclear structure functions \(F_2^A\) to nucleon structure functions \(F_2^N\) via a point-nucleon structure function \(f^{PN,A}\) computed from a non-perturbative Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith theory. They apply Mellin transforms to convert the convolution into an algebraic equation, then solve for the Mellin transform of the unknown \(F_2^n\). The extraction uses a parametrization \(C(x,Q^2)=\sum_k d_k (1-x)^k\) with constraints from the low-\(x\) behavior and the primitive approximation \(F_2^{n,pr}=2F_2^D-F_2^p\), leaving one free parameter \(C(1,Q^2)\) determined by minimizing variances between computed and extracted quantities.

## Key numbers

- \(\langle C(1,3.5)\rangle = 0.54 \pm 0.03\) averaged over deuterium, carbon, and iron targets using multiple extraction methods at \(Q^2=3.5\ \text{GeV}^2\)
- The nucleon-elastic calculation gives \(C(x=1,3.5)\approx 0.61\) and \(C(x=1,5.0)\approx 0.56\) from static nucleon form factors
- The SU(6) symmetry limit for \(C(1)\) is 2/3; other quark model predictions cited are 3/7 and 1/4
- The Gottfried sum rule evaluated at \(Q^2=3.5\ \text{GeV}^2\) gives \(S_G(3.5)=0.251\), close to the recent value \(S_G(4.0)=0.256\pm0.026\)
- The pion threshold for \(Q^2=3.5\ \text{GeV}^2\) is \(x_{thr}(3.5)\approx 0.93\)

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to extract \(F_2^n\) from deuterium data using the impulse approximation (Bodek et al., 1979; Melnitchouk and Thomas, 1996) and from proposed \(A=3\) experiments (Afnan et al., 2000; Pace et al., 2000). The authors use existing JLab data on deuterium, carbon, and iron (Arrington et al., 1999; Niculescu et al., 2000) and EMC ratios from Gomez et al. (1994). The results enable comparison with global parton distribution function fits (Lai et al., 1995; Barone et al., 2000) and motivate future inclusive scattering experiments on deuterium and \(^4\)He with a 6 GeV beam to sharpen the extracted neutron structure function.
