# An Augmented QCD Phase Portrait: Mapping Quark-Hadron Deconfinement for Hot, Dense, Rotating Matter under Magnetic Field

> The quark-hadron transition that happens in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is expected to be influenced by the effects of rotation and magnetic field, both present due to the geometry of a generic non-head-on impact. We augment the conventional $T$--$μ_B$ planar phase diagram for QCD matter by extending it to a multi-dimensional domain spanned by temperature $T$, baryon chemical potential $μ_B$, external magnetic field $B$ and angular velocity $ω$. Using two independent approaches, one from a rapid rise in entropy density and another dealing with a dip in the squared speed of sound, we identify deconfinement in the framework of a modified statistical hadronization model. We find that the deconfinement temperature $T_C(μ_B,~ω,~eB)$ decreases nearly monotonically with increasing $μ_B,~ω$ and $eB$ with the most prominent drop (by nearly $40$ to $50$ MeV) in $T_C$ occurring when all the three quasi-control (via collision energy and centrality) parameters are simultaneously tuned to finite values that are typically achievable in present and upcoming heavy-ion colliders.

## Key claim

The deconfinement temperature \(T_C(\mu_B, \omega, eB)\) decreases nearly monotonically with increasing baryon chemical potential, angular velocity, and magnetic field, with the most prominent drop of 40 to 50 MeV occurring when all three parameters are simultaneously tuned to finite values achievable in present and upcoming heavy-ion colliders; this combined effect can produce a downward shift comparable to that from extremely large rotation alone without magnetic field.

## Method

The authors employed a modified hadron resonance gas (statistical hadronization) model incorporating Landau quantization for charged particles under a strong magnetic field and a causality-bound cylindrical geometry for rotation. They computed the entropy density and squared speed of sound from the free energy density, then identified deconfinement using two independent criteria: a rapid rise in scaled entropy density (\(s/T^3 = 4\) to 7) and a dip in the squared speed of sound. The model included all hadrons up to 1.5 GeV mass (excluding spin-3/2 particles) from the THERMUS-V3.0 particle list, with fixed system radius \(R = 12.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (2.5 fm) and radial coordinate \(r = 3\) GeV\(^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

* Deconfinement temperature drop of 40 to 50 MeV when \(\mu_B\), \(\omega\), and \(eB\) are simultaneously tuned to finite values achievable in colliders.
* Angular velocity \(\omega \approx (9\pm1)\times10^{21}\) s\(^{-1}\) \(\sim 0.03\) fm\(^{-1}\) \(\sim 0.006\) GeV from RHIC collaboration; higher theoretical estimates yield \(\omega \sim 0.1\) fm\(^{-1}\) \(\sim 0.02\) GeV.
* Magnetic field \(B \sim 10^{19}\) Gauss corresponding to \(eB \sim 6 m_{\pi}^2 \sim 0.12\) GeV\(^2\).
* System radius \(R = 12.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (2.5 fm), fixed radial coordinate \(r = 3\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* Scaled entropy density criterion: \(s/T^3 = 4\) (lower edge) to 7 (upper edge) for deconfinement bands; central value \(s/T^3 = 5.5\) used for surface plots.
* Magnetic field values studied: \(eB = 0\) and \(eB = 0.15\) GeV\(^2\).
* Angular velocity values studied: \(\omega = 0\) GeV and \(\omega = 0.02\) GeV.
* At \(\mu_B \approx 0.5\) GeV, the drop in \(T_C\) reaches near 0.1 GeV when both magnetic field and angular velocity are high.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the QCD phase diagram that considered baryon chemical potential, angular velocity, and magnetic field either separately or pairwise (Fukushima 2016, Liu 2017, Fujimoto 2021, Jiang 2016) by examining the combined interplay of all three parameters simultaneously. The results enable future phenomenological interpretation of chemical freeze-out data from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, LHC, NICA, and FAIR, potentially allowing the extraction of magnetic field and rotational motion magnitudes from experimental measurements, complementing existing uses of freeze-out data as "thermometer" and "baryometer" (Andronic 2018).
