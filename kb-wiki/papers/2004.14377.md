# Mixed phase transition from hypernuclear matter to deconfined quark matter fulfilling mass-radius constraints of neutron stars

> A recent solution of the hyperon puzzle by a first order phase transition to color superconducting quark matter is revisited in order to replace the Maxwell construction by an interpolation method which describes a mixed phase. To do this, we apply for the first time the finite-range polynomial interpolation method for constructing a transition between hadronic and quark matter phases to the situation that is characterized in the literature as the reconfinement problem. For the description of the hadronic phase the lowest order constrained variational method is used while for the quark phase the nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with constant (model nlNJLA) and with density-dependent (model nlNJLB) parameters is employed. Applying the replacement interpolation method to both quark matter models results in a hybrid equation of state that allows a coexistence of nuclear matter, hypernuclear matter and quark matter in a mixed phase between the pure hadronic and quark phases which can also be realized in the structure of the corresponding hybrid star sequences. The predicted hybrid stars fulfill the constraints on the mass-radius relation for neutron stars obtained from recent observations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that replacing the Maxwell construction with a finite-range polynomial interpolation method (the replacement interpolation method, RIM) for the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star interiors yields hybrid star sequences that satisfy the observed mass-radius constraints, but only when the interpolation parameter \(\Delta_P\) is chosen to handle the "reconfinement problem" where the hadronic pressure would otherwise exceed the quark matter pressure at high densities.

## Method
The authors used the lowest order constrained variational method with hyperons (LOCVY) to describe the hadronic phase and the nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model for the color superconducting quark matter phase, with two parameter schemes: constant parameters (model nlNJLA) and density-dependent parameters (model nlNJLB). They applied the replacement interpolation method (RIM), which replaces the Maxwell construction with a parabolic interpolation between the hadronic and quark matter equations of state in a finite chemical potential interval, with the free parameter \(\Delta_P\) controlling the pressure offset at the critical chemical potential and mimicking the effect of surface tension in a mixed pasta phase.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear saturation density is \(\rho_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), above which hyperonic degrees of freedom may appear.
- The critical surface tension above which the mixed phase reduces to the Maxwell construction is \(\sigma_c \sim 60\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\).
- A value of \(\Delta_P \approx 0.05-0.07\) corresponds to vanishing surface tension and thus a Glendenning construction.
- For model nlNJLA, the interpolation method was applied with \(\eta = 0.12\) and three values of \(\Delta_P = -2.5\%\), \(-3.5\%\), and \(-5\%\) to handle the reconfinement problem.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Shahrbaf et al., 2019, referred to as paper I) which used a Maxwell construction for the phase transition from hypernuclear matter to color superconducting quark matter. The present paper replaces the Maxwell construction with the replacement interpolation method (RIM) developed by Ayriyan et al. (2017) and Abgaryan et al. (2018), which mimics the effects of a pasta phase with finite surface tension. This approach addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperon softening of the equation of state prevents reaching observed neutron star masses of about \(2 M_\odot\), and enables more realistic hybrid star models that include a mixed phase of nuclear matter, hypernuclear matter, and quark matter.
