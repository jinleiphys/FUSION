# Nuclear dependence of $R=\frac{σ_L}{σ_T}$ and Callan-Gross relation in nuclei

> The electromagnetic nuclear structure functions $F_{1A} (x,Q^2)$, $F_{2A} (x,Q^2)$ and $F_{LA} (x,Q^2)$ have been calculated using a microscopic model of nucleus to study the nuclear medium effects on the ratio $R_A(x,Q^2)=\frac{σ_{LA} (x,Q^2)}{σ_{TA} (x,Q^2)} = \frac{F_{LA} (x,Q^2)}{2xF_{1A} (x,Q^2)}$ and the Callan-Gross relation(CGR) in nuclei. The nuclear medium effects due to the Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic contribution and shadowing have been taken into account. The theoretical results for the nuclear dependence of $R_{A} (x,Q^2)$ and its impact on CGR have been presented and compared with the available experimental data on the various nuclear targets. The predictions have been made for $R_{A} (x,Q^2)$ in the kinematic region of $x$ and $Q^2$ for some nuclei relevant for the future experiments to be performed at the JLab.

## Key claim

The authors claim that nuclear medium effects (Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic contributions, and shadowing) modify the ratio \(R_A(x,Q^2)\) relative to the free nucleon case, but the magnitude of this modification is small and shows almost no dependence on the mass number \(A\), with a crossover point \(x_o\) between 0.35 and 0.55 where \(R_A\) changes from being smaller than \(R_N\) to larger than \(R_N\) depending on \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear structure functions \(F_{1A}\), \(F_{2A}\), and \(F_{LA}\) using a field-theoretical model based on the Lehmann representation for the relativistic nucleon propagator, incorporating a spectral function that accounts for Fermi motion, binding energy, and nucleon correlations via a local density approximation. They added contributions from virtual pion and rho mesons using dressed meson propagators, applied target mass corrections at the nucleon level using the prescription of Schienbein et al. (2007), and included shadowing following Kulagin et al. (2004). The free nucleon parton distribution functions were taken from the CTEQ collaboration at next-to-leading order, and the free nucleon ratio \(R_N\) was parameterized using the fit of Whitlow et al. (1991).

## Key numbers

- The crossover point \(x_o\) where \(R_A(x,Q^2)\) transitions from below to above \(R_N(x,Q^2)\) lies between 0.35 and 0.55 for \(2 < Q^2 < 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- The kinematic region studied for JLab experiments is \(1 < Q^2 < 5\) GeV\(^2\) and \(0.1 < x < 0.6\).
- The model reproduces JLab data for \(Q^2 \ge 2\) GeV\(^2\) without a cut on center-of-mass energy \(W\), but overestimates data at \(Q^2 \le 2\) GeV\(^2\) for \(x \ge 0.5\).
- With a cut on \(W \ge 1.4\) GeV, the model satisfactorily explains JLab data in the entire region \(1 < Q^2 < 5\) GeV\(^2\) and \(0.5 < x < 0.8\).
- The difference \(R_A(x,Q^2) - R_D(x,Q^2)\) is negative for \(x < 0.45\) and changes sign for \(x > 0.45\), with almost no dependence on \(A\) in the absence of Coulomb effects.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear medium effects in deep inelastic scattering, particularly the EMC effect discovered by Aubert et al. (1983) and Bodek et al. (1983), by focusing on the longitudinal-to-transverse cross section ratio \(R_A\) and the Callan-Gross relation in nuclei. It builds on the reanalysis of world data by Guzey et al. (2012) and Solvignon et al. (2009), which suggested a nontrivial nuclear dependence of \(R_A\), and provides predictions for the kinematic region of a proposed JLab experiment (Covrig et al.) on carbon, copper, and gold targets. The results enable more precise extraction of nuclear structure functions via Rosenbluth separation and test the validity of the Callan-Gross relation in the nuclear medium.
