# Constraining the quadrupole deformation of atomic nuclei with relativistic nuclear collisions

> Preliminary data by the STAR collaboration at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider shows that the elliptic flow, $v_2$, and the average transverse momentum, $\langle p_t \rangle$, of final-state hadrons produced in high-multiplicity $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions are negatively correlated. This observation brings experimental evidence of a significant prolate deformation, $β\approx 0.3$, in the colliding $^{238}$U nuclei. I show that a quantitative description of this new phenomenon can be achieved within the hydrodynamic framework of heavy-ion collisions, and that thus such kind of data in the context of high-energy nuclear experiments can help constrain the quadrupole deformation of the colliding species.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the negative correlation between elliptic flow ($v_2$) and average transverse momentum ($\langle p_t \rangle$) observed in preliminary STAR data for high-multiplicity $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions can be quantitatively described within the hydrodynamic framework, but only after implementing a more realistic initial-state predictor for $\langle p_t \rangle$ (initial energy $E_0$ rather than system size $R$) and including statistical fluctuations from finite particle number.

## Method

The author uses the TRENTo model to generate initial entropy density profiles for $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions at top RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV), with nuclei modeled as deformed Woods-Saxon distributions with prolate deformation parameter $\beta=0.3$. The initial energy $E_0$ is computed from the entropy density using a conformal QCD equation of state, and the distribution of $\langle p_t \rangle$ is obtained from $E_0/S$ via a linear scaling relation with a phenomenological parameter $\kappa_0\approx0.4$ calibrated to STAR data on dynamical $\langle p_t \rangle$ fluctuations. Statistical fluctuations from finite particle number ($N\approx1000$) are included by smearing the $\langle p_t \rangle$ distribution with a Gaussian of width 1.5 times the dynamical fluctuation width.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameter of $^{238}$U: $\beta=0.286$ from Raman (2001), rounded to $\beta=0.3$ in the calculation
- Relative dynamical fluctuation of $\langle p_t \rangle$ in central collisions at RHIC: $\sigma_{\rm dynamical}(\langle p_t \rangle)/\langle\langle p_t \rangle\rangle = 0.012$ (STAR, Adam 2019)
- Relative fluctuation of $E_0/S$ in TRENTo calculation: approximately 0.03, yielding $\kappa_0\approx0.4$
- Statistical fluctuation of $\langle p_t \rangle$ from finite particle number: $\sigma_{\rm stat}=0.01$ GeV, with relative fluctuation $\sigma_{\rm stat}/\langle\langle p_t \rangle\rangle = 0.18$ using $\langle\langle p_t \rangle\rangle=0.57$ GeV (Adams 2005)
- Ratio of statistical to dynamical fluctuations: $\sigma_{\rm stat}(\langle p_t \rangle)/\langle\langle p_t \rangle\rangle = 1.5 \times \sigma_{\rm dynamical}(\langle p_t \rangle)/\langle\langle p_t \rangle\rangle$

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Giacalone (2019), which first proposed using $\langle p_t \rangle$ as a tool to access nuclear deformation in heavy-ion collisions, by improving the predictor for $\langle p_t \rangle$ from system size $R$ to initial energy $E_0$ and including statistical fluctuations. The paper builds on prior observations of nuclear deformation signatures in comparisons of $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au and $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions at RHIC (Adamczyk 2015) and $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb versus $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe collisions at LHC (Acharya 2018, Sirunyan 2019, Aad 2019). The results enable future quantitative comparisons with STAR data on the $v_2$–$\langle p_t \rangle$ correlation, potentially constraining the quadrupole deformation of colliding nuclei from high-energy experiments.
