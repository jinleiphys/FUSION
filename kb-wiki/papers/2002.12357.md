# $β$ decay of even-A nuclei within the interacting boson model based on nuclear density functional theory

> We compute the $β$-decay $ft$-values within the frameworks of the energy density functional (EDF) and the interacting boson model (IBM). Based on the constrained mean-field calculation with the Gogny-D1M EDF, the IBM Hamiltonian for an even-even nucleus and essential ingredients of the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM) for describing the neighboring odd-odd nucleus are determined in a microscopic way. Only the boson-fermion and residual neutron-proton interaction strengths are determined empirically. The Gamow-Teller (GT) and Fermi (F) transition rates needed to compute the $β$-decay $ft$-values are obtained without any additional parameter or quenching of the $g_A$ factor. The observed $\ft$ values for the $β^+$ decays of the even-even Ba into odd-odd Cs nuclei, and of the odd-odd Cs to the even-even Xe nuclei, with mass $A\approx 130$ are reasonably well described. The predicted GT and F transition rates represent a sensitive test of the quality of the IBM and IBFFM wave functions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that β-decay log(ft) values for even-A nuclei in the A≈130 mass region can be computed using the interacting boson model (IBM) and interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM) with input from Gogny-D1M energy density functional (EDF) mean-field calculations, but only the boson-fermion and residual neutron-proton interaction strengths are determined empirically, while the Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition rates are obtained without any additional parameter or quenching of the gA factor.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny-D1M EDF to obtain potential energy surfaces for even-even 124-132Xe and 124-132Ba nuclei, then mapped these onto the expectation value of the IBM-2 Hamiltonian in the boson coherent state to determine the IBM parameters. Single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for unpaired nucleons were computed from Gogny-D1M HFB calculations constrained to zero deformation, and the IBFFM Hamiltonian for odd-odd 124-132Cs nuclei was built by coupling the IBM core with fermion degrees of freedom, with boson-fermion strengths fitted to reproduce experimental low-energy spectra in neighboring odd-A nuclei and residual neutron-proton interaction strengths (uD=0.7 MeV, uT=0.02 MeV) taken from prior work.

## Key numbers

* For 124Ba → 124Cs 0+1 → 0+1 decay: predicted log(ft) = 6.134, experimental log(ft) = 6.01(10)
* For 124Ba → 124Cs 0+1 → 1+1 decay: predicted log(ft) = 4.837, experimental log(ft) = 5.2(3)
* For 126Ba → 126Cs 0+1 → 0+1 decay: predicted log(ft) = 6.348, experimental log(ft) = 6.44(17)
* For 126Ba → 126Cs 0+1 → 1+1 decay: predicted log(ft) = 4.744, experimental log(ft) = 5.36(9)
* For 128Ba → 128Cs 0+1 → 1+1 decay: predicted log(ft) = 5.201, experimental log(ft) = 5.471(9)
* For 124Cs → 124Xe 1+1 → 0+1 decay: predicted log(ft) = 7.452, experimental log(ft) = 5.10(7)
* Residual neutron-proton interaction strengths: uD = 0.7 MeV and uT = 0.02 MeV
* Free nucleon value gA = 1.2701(25) used without quenching

## Context

This work extends the EDF-based IBM approach previously applied to odd-A β decays in Nomura (2020) to even-A nuclei, which is required for computing ββ decay nuclear matrix elements. The IBFFM framework for β decays had previously been applied only to 124Ba in Brant (2006) and to two-neutrino ββ decays from Te to Xe isotopes in Yoshida (2013), both using fully phenomenological Hamiltonians; the present work provides a more microscopic determination of the Hamiltonian parameters from Gogny-D1M EDF calculations. The results enable systematic studies of β decay and ββ decay nuclear matrix elements across isotopic chains, though the method shows limitations for higher-lying non-yrast states and for Cs→Xe decays where the 1+1 → 0+1 log(ft) values are considerably overestimated.
