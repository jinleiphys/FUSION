# Rotation catalyzed chiral magnetovortical instability

> We demonstrate that a background rotation significantly catalyzes the chiral magnetovortical instability in chiral magnetohydrodynamics. The rotation splits the linearly polarized Alfven wave into two circularly polarized magneto-Coriolis waves, one of which exhibits a lower frequency than the original Alfven wave. We find that this low-frequency magneto-Coriolis wave is always unstable in the presence of even a weak chiral vortical effect. This instability may enable new dynamo mechanism applicable to various rotating chiral plasmas.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a background rotation significantly catalyzes the chiral magnetovortical instability (CMVI), such that the instability can occur for any finite value of the chiral vortical effect (CVE) coefficient, unlike the non-rotating case where the onset condition requires the CVE coefficient to exceed a threshold value of one (in dimensionless units).

## Method

The authors perform a linear stability analysis of non-relativistic chiral magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) in a co-rotating reference frame, where the Coriolis force appears. They derive the dispersion relations for magneto-Coriolis (MC) waves coupled to the chiral vortical effect and the chiral magnetic effect, solving for the eigenfrequencies of left-handed and right-handed circularly polarized modes. The analysis is complemented by numerical simulations of the nonlinear evolution, coupling the mode equations to the chiral anomaly equation with a chirality-flipping term.

## Key numbers

- The onset condition for CMVI without rotation is given by $\xi_\omega > \sqrt{\rho}$, where $\xi_\omega$ is the CVE coefficient and $\rho$ is the mass density (this is the threshold the abstract glosses over).
- For the slow magneto-Coriolis wave with $\chi=-1, \lambda=-1$ and $\cos\theta>0$, instability occurs for all wavenumbers $k>0$ when the dimensionless CVE coefficient satisfies $\xi_\omega' \geq 1$.
- For the same mode with $0 < \xi_\omega' < 1$, instability is confined to wavenumbers $0 < k < 2\Omega \xi_\omega / [B_0 (1 - \xi_\omega^{\prime 2})]$.
- For the fast magneto-Coriolis wave with $\chi=-1, \lambda=+1$ and $\cos\theta>0$, instability requires $\xi_\omega' > 1$ and wavenumbers $k > 2\Omega \xi_\omega / [B_0 (\xi_\omega^{\prime 2} - 1)]$.
- The chiral plasma instability (CPI) from the chiral magnetic effect has the same unstable condition with rotation as without: right-hand waves ($\lambda=-1$) with wavenumber $k < \xi_B$.

## Context

This work extends the chiral magnetovortical instability first identified in non-rotating plasmas by the same group (PhysRevD.109.L121302), where the onset required $\xi_\omega > \sqrt{\rho}$. The paper shows that rotation splits the linearly polarized Alfven wave into two circularly polarized magneto-Coriolis waves, a phenomenon known from conventional rotating MHD (Lehnert 1954, Chandrasekhar 1955, Acheson 1973). The rotation-catalyzed CMVI may enable new dynamo mechanisms in rotating chiral plasmas such as those in the early Universe, supernovae, neutron stars, and heavy-ion collisions, where the CVE coefficient is typically expected to be small.
