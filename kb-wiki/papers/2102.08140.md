# Diffusion coefficient matrix of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

> We study the diffusion properties of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) and evaluate the diffusion coefficient matrix for the baryon ($B$), strange ($S$) and electric ($Q$) charges - $κ_{qq'}$ ($q,q' = B, S, Q$) and show their dependence on temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$. The non-perturbative nature of the sQGP is evaluated within the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM) which is matched to reproduce the equation of state of the partonic matter above the deconfinement temperature $T_c$ from lattice QCD. The calculation of diffusion coefficients is based on two methods: i) the Chapman-Enskog method for the linearized Boltzmann equation, which allows to explore non-equilibrium corrections for the phase-space distribution function in leading order of the Knudsen numbers as well as ii) the relaxation time approximation (RTA). In this work we explore the differences between the two methods. We find a good agreement with the available lattice QCD data in case of the electric charge diffusion coefficient (or electric conductivity) at vanishing baryon chemical potential as well as a qualitative agreement with the recent predictions from the holographic approach for all diagonal components of the diffusion coefficient matrix. The knowledge of the diffusion coefficient matrix is also of special interest for more accurate hydrodynamic simulations.

## Key claim

The paper evaluates the full diffusion coefficient matrix for baryon (B), strange (S), and electric (Q) charges in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma using the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model, finding good agreement with lattice QCD data for electric charge diffusion at vanishing baryon chemical potential and qualitative agreement with holographic predictions for all diagonal components, but only under the assumption that the phase transition remains a rapid crossover for baryon chemical potentials up to 0.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors compute the diffusion coefficient matrix κ_{qq'} (q,q' = B, S, Q) using two kinetic theory methods applied to the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM): the Chapman-Enskog method for the linearized Boltzmann equation in leading order of the Knudsen number, and the relaxation time approximation (RTA). The DQPM describes the QGP in terms of dynamical quasiparticles (quarks and gluons) with temperature- and baryon-chemical-potential-dependent masses and widths, matched to reproduce the lattice QCD equation of state above the deconfinement temperature T_c. Cross sections for elastic binary processes are derived from the DQPM and used as input for both methods.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature at zero baryon chemical potential is T_c(μ_B = 0) ≈ 0.158 GeV
- The baryon chemical potential range studied is μ_B ≤ 0.5 GeV, where the phase transition is a rapid crossover
- The DQPM coupling parameterization uses coefficients d = 169.934, e = -0.178434, and f = 1.14631
- The strange quark dynamical mass is m_s(T,μ_B) = m_{u/d}(T,μ_B) + Δm with Δm = 30 MeV
- The parameter α in T_c(μ_B) = T_c(0)√(1 - α μ_B^2) is α = 0.974 GeV^{-2}
- The magnetic cut-off parameter c_m = 14.4 is used in the width formula

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the diffusion coefficient matrix for a hadron gas and a simple QGP model by Greif et al. (2017) and Fotakis et al. (2019), and combines them with the DQPM framework developed by Soloveva et al. (2019) for transport coefficients. The results enable more accurate hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions that include coupled diffusion of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge, particularly relevant for the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC and future FAIR and NICA experiments.
