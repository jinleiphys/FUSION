# Centrality dependence of inclusive prompt photon production in d+Au, Au+Au, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions

> We calculate the centrality dependence of the midrapidity nuclear modification for inclusive prompt photon production in d+Au and Au+Au collisions at RHIC and in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. Our results, using the recent spatially dependent nuclear PDF set EPS09s, are consistent with the existing high-pT data from the PHENIX and CMS collaborations. The good agreement even in the case of nucleus+nucleus collisions suggests that the high-pT direct photon production is not significantly altered by the strongly interacting medium produced in such collisions. We find the centrality dependence of the nuclear modifications generally rather weak but perhaps measurable at low pT.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the centrality dependence of inclusive prompt photon production in heavy-ion collisions can be described using spatially dependent nuclear PDFs (EPS09s) without requiring any additional modifications from the strongly interacting medium, but the authors hedge that the net medium-effect on the fragmentation component is not exactly known and could involve competing processes that may cancel.

## Method
The authors performed next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations using the INCNLO package with CTEQ6.6M free proton PDFs and EPS09s spatially dependent nuclear PDFs, combined with BFG set II photonic fragmentation functions. Centrality classes were defined as impact parameter intervals using the optical Glauber model, and the nuclear modification factor R_AB^gamma was computed for d+Au and Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) and p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5.0 TeV and 2.76 TeV respectively).

## Key numbers
- For d+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV): the isospin effect dominates at p_T > 10 GeV/c, with suppression of order 5% at p_T ~ 12-20 GeV/c
- For Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV): enhancement of order 5% observed in the region 4 GeV/c < p_T < 9 GeV/c in central collisions, despite isospin suppression
- For Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV): suppression of order 10% around p_T ~ 100 GeV/c due to isospin effect
- For p+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5.0 TeV): isospin effect is less than 5% in the p_T region considered and negligible at p_T < 30 GeV/c
- In p+Pb collisions, the low p_T suppression in most central collisions (0-20%) is about twice that in most peripheral collisions (60-80%)
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections used: 42 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, 64 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, and 70 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.0 TeV

## Context
This work extends the spatially dependent nuclear PDF approach (EPS09s) developed by Helenius, Eskola, and Paukkunen (2012) to prompt photon production in multiple collision systems. It provides a baseline calculation against which possible medium modifications of fragmentation photons can be compared, particularly for nucleus-nucleus collisions where parton energy loss, jet-to-photon conversion, and medium-induced radiation may occur. The results enable future global nPDF fits to include photon data from Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions, and suggest that p+Pb collisions at the LHC offer the cleanest environment for measuring nPDF spatial dependence without QCD-medium complications.
