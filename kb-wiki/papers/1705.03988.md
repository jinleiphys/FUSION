# Parity partners in the baryon resonance spectrum

> We describe a calculation of the spectrum of flavour-SU(3) octet and decuplet baryons, their parity partners, and the radial excitations of these systems, made using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction as the foundation for the relevant few-body equations. Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking generates nonpointlike diquarks within these baryons and hence, using the contact interaction, flavour-antitriplet scalar, pseudoscalar and vector, and flavour-sextet axial-vector quark-quark correlations can all play an active role. The model yields reasonable masses for all systems studied, and Faddeev amplitudes for ground states and associated parity partners that sketch a realistic picture of their internal structure: ground-state, even parity baryons are constituted, almost exclusively, from like-parity diquark correlations; but orbital angular momentum plays an important role in the rest-frame wave functions of odd-parity baryons, whose Faddeev amplitudes are dominated by odd-parity diquarks.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction can yield reasonable masses for flavour-SU(3) octet and decuplet baryons, their parity partners, and radial excitations, but only after introducing an ad-hoc spin-orbit repulsion parameter (g_DB) in the Faddeev equations for odd-parity octet baryons, because without this adjustment the parity partners are too light and dominated by like-parity diquark correlations.

## Method

The authors solved Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equations for baryons, using a rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations with a vector-vector contact interaction. Diquark correlation masses and amplitudes were obtained from Bethe-Salpeter equations in scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector channels, with spin-orbit repulsion factors adjusted to reproduce the a1-rho mass splitting and to reverse the meson-diquark mass ordering for pseudoscalar and vector diquarks. The Faddeev kernels employed a static approximation for the exchanged quark, and a new parameter g_DB was introduced to suppress unequal-parity diquark contributions in the Faddeev equations for positive-parity baryons and enhance them for negative-parity baryons.

## Context

This work extends the contact-interaction approach of Roberts et al. (2011) and Chen et al. (2012) for baryon spectroscopy by including parity partners and radial excitations, and by incorporating pseudoscalar and vector diquark correlations that were previously omitted. The results contradict simple constituent quark model descriptions where parity partners are L=1 orbital excitations (e.g., DeRujula 1975, Godfrey 1985), instead supporting a picture where dynamical chiral symmetry breaking generates the mass splittings. The computed masses are intended as dressed-quark cores that require subsequent reduction from meson-baryon final-state interactions (as in Eichmann 2008, Segovia 2014) to match experimental values, enabling future studies of baryon structure with more realistic Faddeev kernels.
