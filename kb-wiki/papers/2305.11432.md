# Chiral anomaly in a (1+1)-dimensional Floquet system under high-frequency electric fields

> We investigate the chiral anomaly in a Floquet system under a time-periodic electric field in (1+1) dimensions. Using the van~Vleck high-frequency expansion, we analytically calculate the chiral current and the pseudo-scalar condensate for massless/massive fermions and how they are balanced with the topological charge. In the high-frequency limit, we find that finite-mass effects are suppressed and the topological charge is dominated by the chirality production. Our calculations show that the information about the chiral anomaly is stored not in the static Floquet Hamiltonian but in the periodic kick operator. The computational steps are useful as the theoretical foundation for higher-dimensional generalization.

## Key claim

The chiral anomaly in a (1+1)-dimensional Floquet system under high-frequency electric fields is not encoded in the static Floquet Hamiltonian but instead resides entirely in the periodic kick operator, and the anomaly relation can be understood as a sum rule between chirality production and the pseudo-scalar condensate, with the relative contributions depending on the frequency regime.

## Method

The authors used the van Vleck high-frequency expansion within Floquet theory to analytically compute the chiral current and pseudo-scalar condensate for both massless Weyl and massive Dirac fermions in (1+1) dimensions under a time-periodic electric field. They solved the recursive equations for the Floquet Hamiltonian and kick operator to arbitrary orders, and for the Weyl case they performed an exact resummation of the series to obtain closed-form expressions. The chiral anomaly relation was then examined by separately evaluating the divergence of the chiral current and the mass-dependent pseudo-scalar condensate term.

## Key numbers

- For massless Weyl fermions, the chiral anomaly relation is $\partial_\mu J_{\rm R}^\mu = -\partial_\mu J_{\rm L}^\mu = eE/(2\pi)$ in (1+1) dimensions.
- For massive Dirac fermions, the anomaly relation becomes $\partial_\mu J_{5}^\mu = eE/\pi + 2mP$, where $P$ is the pseudo-scalar condensate.
- In the slow limit, the pair production number is $N_{\rm pair}(t,x) \simeq \frac{1}{2\pi} \int^t {\rm d}t' eE(t',x) \exp(-\pi m^2/|eE(t,x)|)$.
- In the slow limit, the chirality production is $\partial_\mu J_5^\mu \simeq (eE/\pi) \exp(-\pi m^2/|eE|)$ and the pseudo-scalar condensate is $P \simeq -(eE/\pi)(1 - \exp(-\pi m^2/|eE|))/(2m)$.
- The van Vleck expansion for Weyl fermions converges when $T/L < 1$, where $T$ is the period and $L$ is the typical length scale of the electric field variation.

## Context

This work extends the study of the chiral anomaly from static or slowly varying fields (where the Schwinger mechanism dominates, as in Copinger (2018) and Fukushima (2010)) to rapidly oscillating, high-frequency fields using Floquet theory. It provides the first application of Floquet theory to the chiral anomaly and to the massive chiral anomaly under periodic driving. The results enable future higher-dimensional generalizations and offer a theoretical foundation for probing the chiral anomaly in systems such as Weyl semimetals, where residual mass effects are unavoidable, and in heavy-ion collision experiments where transient magnetic fields create effectively (1+1)-dimensional dynamics.
