# Modeling the Impact Parameter Dependence of the nPDFs With EKS98 and EPS09 Global Fits

> So far the nuclear PDFs (nPDFs) in the global DGLAP fits have been taken to be spatially independent. In this work, using the $A$-dependence of the globally fitted sets EPS09 and EKS98, we have determined the spatial dependence of the nPDFs in terms of powers of the nuclear thickness functions. New spatially dependent nPDF sets EPS09s (NLO, LO, error sets) and EKS98s (LO) are released. As an application, we consider the nuclear modification factor $R_{dAu}^{π^0}$ at midrapidity for neutral pion production in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC in NLO. Comparison with the PHENIX data in different centrality classes is also shown. In addition, predictions for the corresponding nuclear modification factor $R_{pPb}^{π^0}$ in proton-lead collisions at the LHC are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a method to incorporate impact-parameter (spatial) dependence into existing globally fitted nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) by assuming the spatial dependence can be expressed as a power series of the nuclear thickness function, with the condition that the spatial average reproduces the original spatially independent nPDFs.

## Method

The authors parameterize the spatially dependent nuclear modification \(r_i^A(x,Q^2,\mathbf{s})\) as a fourth-order power series in the nuclear thickness function \(T_A(\mathbf{s})\), with \(A\)-independent coefficients \(c_j^i(x,Q^2)\) determined by fitting the spatial average to the \(A\)-dependence of the existing EKS98 and EPS09 global fits. The resulting spatially dependent nPDF sets, called EPS09s (available at NLO, LO, and with error sets) and EKS98s (LO), are then used in next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AB}^{\pi^0}\) for neutral pion production in deuteron-gold (d+Au) and proton-lead (p+Pb) collisions, with centrality classes defined via the optical Glauber model.

## Key numbers

* The gluon modification \(R_g^A(x,Q^2)\) is shown at fixed \(x=0.001\) and \(Q^2=1.69\) GeV\(^2\) as a function of nuclear mass number \(A\), with fits reproducing the EPS09NLO and EPS09LO central sets for nuclei with \(A \ge 16\).
* The nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm dAu}^{\pi^0}\) is computed for d+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV at midrapidity (\(y=0\)) in four centrality bins: 0-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, and 60-88%.
* Predictions for \(R_{\rm pPb}^{\pi^0}\) in p+Pb collisions are given at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.0\) TeV at \(y=0\) for different centrality classes.

## Context

This work extends the spatially independent global nPDF analyses EKS98 (Eskola et al., 1998) and EPS09 (Eskola et al., 2009) by introducing a spatial dependence, enabling consistent calculations of hard-process cross sections in different centrality classes. The new EPS09s and EKS98s sets allow comparison with centrality-dependent data from PHENIX at RHIC (Adler et al., 2006) and provide predictions for p+Pb collisions at the LHC, enabling future tests of nuclear shadowing and parton energy loss models in a spatially resolved framework.
