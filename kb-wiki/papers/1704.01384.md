# Reactions induced by $^9$Be in a four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels framework

> We investigate the elastic scattering of $^9$Be on $^{208}$Pb at beam energies above (50 MeV) and below (40 MeV) the Coulomb barrier. The reaction is described within a four-body framework using the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method. The $^9$Be projectile states are generated using the analytical Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates. Our calculations confirm the importance of continuum effects at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that continuum (breakup) effects are important for elastic scattering of the weakly-bound, Borromean nucleus \(^9\)Be on \(^{208}\)Pb even at energies below the Coulomb barrier (40 MeV), not only above it (50 MeV), as shown by four-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations.

## Method

The authors describe the elastic scattering of \(^9\)Be on \(^{208}\)Pb using a four-body CDCC framework, treating \(^9\)Be as a three-body projectile (\(\alpha + \alpha + n\)) plus a structureless target. The projectile bound and continuum states are generated using the analytical Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis in hyperspherical coordinates, with the ground state and low-energy resonances fixed to experimental values. Coupled equations are solved including projectile-target interaction multipole couplings to all orders.

## Key numbers

- The \(^9\)Be nucleus has a binding energy of 1.5736 MeV below the \(\alpha + \alpha + n\) threshold.
- Elastic scattering cross sections (relative to Rutherford) are calculated at beam energies of 50 MeV (above the Coulomb barrier) and 40 MeV (below the Coulomb barrier).
- The model space includes projectile states with \(j^\pi = 3/2^\pm, 1/2^\pm, 5/2^\pm\).

## Context

This work extends previous two-body projectile models for \(^9\)Be (Pandit, 2011) and three-body projectile models (Descouvemont, 2015) by using a four-body CDCC method with an analytical THO basis. The results confirm that breakup effects are important even at sub-barrier energies, supporting the reliability of the method for reactions induced by three-body projectiles with more than one charged particle. The approach enables more accurate descriptions of reactions involving weakly-bound Borromean nuclei such as \(^6\)He and \(^{11}\)Li.
