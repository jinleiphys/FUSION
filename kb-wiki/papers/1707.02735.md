# Chiral phase transition in linear sigma model with non-extensive statistical mechanics

> From the non-extensive statistical mechanics, we investigate the chiral phase transition at finite temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ in the framework of the linear sigma model. The corresponding non-extensive distribution, based on Tsallis' statistics, is characterized by a dimensionless non-extensive parameter, $q$, and the results in the usual Boltzmann-Gibbs case are recovered when $q\to 1$. The thermodynamics of the linear sigma model and its correspodning phase diagram are analysed. At high temperature region, the critical temperature $T_c$ is shown to decrease with increasing $q$ from the phase diagram in the $(T,~μ)$ plane. However, larger values of $q$ causes the rise of $T_c$ at low temperature but high chemical potential. Moreover, it is found that $μ$ different from zero corresponds to a first-order phase transition while $μ=0$ to a crossover one. The critical endpoint (CEP) carries higher chemical potential but lower temperature with $q$ increasing due to the non-extensive effects.

# Chiral phase transition in linear sigma model with non-extensive statistical mechanics

## Key claim

The critical temperature of the chiral phase transition decreases with increasing non-extensive parameter q at high temperature, but larger q causes the critical temperature to rise at low temperature and high chemical potential; the critical endpoint shifts to higher chemical potential and lower temperature as q increases, but only for systems near the phase boundary are the effects of non-extensive statistics significant.

## Method

The authors apply Tsallis non-extensive statistical mechanics to the linear sigma model with two-flavor quarks in the mean-field approximation, replacing the Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential distribution with a q-exponential distribution characterized by a dimensionless non-extensive parameter q. They compute the grand canonical potential, solve the gap equation for the constituent quark mass M = gσ_V, and determine the phase diagram in the (T, μ) plane for q values of 1.1, 1.05, 0.95, and the baseline q = 1. The susceptibility χ = -∂M/∂T|_μ is also evaluated to locate the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is f_π = 93 MeV, the pion mass is m_π = 138 MeV, and the sigma mass is m_σ = 600 MeV; the coupling constant λ^2 is fixed as 20, and the quark-meson coupling g ≈ 3.3.
- At μ = 0 and T = 148 MeV, the thermodynamical potential minimum shifts to smaller constituent quark mass as q increases from 0.95 to 1.1.
- At T = 40 MeV and μ = 286.8 MeV, the potential gap between local and global vacuum increases as q increases.
- The critical temperature T_c at μ = 0 decreases for larger q, as shown by the peak of susceptibility χ moving to lower temperature.
- The critical endpoint (CEP) carries higher chemical potential but lower temperature with increasing q, consistent with the q-NJL model results of Rozynek and Wilk (2009).

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model studies of Scavenius et al. (2001) from the Boltzmann-Gibbs case (q = 1) to the non-extensive Tsallis framework, and generalizes the finite-temperature but zero-chemical-potential calculations of Ishihara (2015) to include finite baryon chemical potential. The results are compared with the non-extensive Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (q-NJL model) of Rozynek and Wilk (2009), showing agreement on the CEP behavior. The work enables future comparison with experimental data to constrain the physical interpretation of the Tsallis parameter q and to guide searches for the critical endpoint in heavy-ion collisions.
