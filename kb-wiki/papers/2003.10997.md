# QCD Challenges from pp to A-A Collisions

> This paper is a write-up of the ideas that were presented, developed and discussed at the third International Workshop on QCD Challenges from pp to A-A, which took place in August 2019 in Lund, Sweden. The goal of the workshop was to focus on some of the open questions in the field and try to come up with concrete suggestions for how to make progress on both the experimental and theoretical sides. The paper gives a brief introduction to each topic and then summarizes the primary results.

## Key claim

This paper summarizes discussions from the third International Workshop on QCD Challenges from pp to A-A (August 2019, Lund, Sweden) and does not present a single new result; instead it identifies open questions and concrete suggestions for progress on both experimental and theoretical sides regarding whether QGP-like effects observed in small collision systems require a deconfined medium explanation or can arise from alternative mechanisms such as string-based models.

## Method

The workshop assigned each participant to one of five topical groups (initial state, QGP-like effects in small systems, jet quenching, hadronization, heavy quarks), where participants prepared posters and discussed open questions and concrete ideas for making progress. The write-up organizes each topic into a self-contained section summarizing the main ideas and discussions from the final plenary presentations.

## Key numbers

Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This paper extends the ongoing debate about whether collective phenomena observed in small systems (pp, pA) at the LHC and RHIC require QGP formation or can be explained by alternative mechanisms such as the Color-Glass Condensate framework (Gelis 2010), string interactions (Bierlich 2017), or parton escape mechanisms (He 2015). It builds upon prior observations of long-range correlations (Dusling 2009, Abelev 2012, CMS 2012), strangeness enhancement (ALICE 2017), and flow in small systems (Zhao 2017). The workshop aims to enable discrimination between hydrodynamic, CGC-based, and string-based approaches through specific proposed measurements including multiplicity distributions, higher-harmonic flow coefficients, HBT radii, and correlations between high-pT triggers and soft particle production.
