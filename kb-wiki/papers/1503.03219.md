# High-spin structures of $^{77,79,81,83}$As isotopes

> In the present work we report comprehensive set of shell model calculations for arsenic isotopes. We performed shell model calculations with two recent effective interactions JUN45 and jj44b. The overall results for the energy levels and magnetic moments are in rather good agreement with the available experimental data. We have also reported competition of proton- and neutron-pair breakings analysis to identify which nucleon pairs are broken to obtain the total angular momentum of the calculated states. Further theoretical development is needed by enlarging model space by including $π0f_{7/2}$ and $ν1d_{5/2}$ orbitals.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using the JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions in the restricted \(f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}\) valence space reproduce the overall energy levels and magnetic moments of \(^{77,79,81,83}\)As reasonably well, but the authors explicitly state that further theoretical development is needed by enlarging the model space to include the \(\pi 0f_{7/2}\) and \(\nu 1d_{5/2}\) orbitals, particularly to describe neutron excitations across the \(N=50\) shell.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations for the odd-even arsenic isotopes \(^{77,79,81,83}\)As using the full \(f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}\) valence space above a \(^{56}\)Ni core, with no truncation. Two recent effective interactions were employed: JUN45, derived from the Bonn-C potential with empirical adjustments in the \(A=63-69\) region, and jj44b, obtained from a fit to binding and excitation energies of nuclei with \(Z=28-30\) and \(N=48-50\). The calculations were carried out using the shell-model codes ANTOINE and NuShellX, and the results were compared with available experimental data for energy levels, magnetic moments, \(B(E2)\) and \(B(M1)\) transition strengths, and quadrupole moments.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{77}\)As, the JUN45 interaction predicts the \(1/2_2^-\) level at 411 keV and jj44b at 1181 keV, while the experimental value is 2195 keV.
- For \(^{79}\)As, the JUN45 interaction predicts the \(9/2^+\) level 656 keV higher than experiment; jj44b predicts it 1011 keV higher.
- For \(^{81}\)As, the JUN45 and jj44b interactions predict the \(21/2_1^-\) level higher by 734 keV and 882 keV, respectively, than the experimental result.
- For \(^{83}\)As, the \(9/2_1^-\) and \(11/2_1^-\) levels are lowered by 132 keV and 141 keV in JUN45, while both levels are higher by 165 keV and 173 keV in jj44b.
- The experimental magnetic moment of the \(3/2_1^-\) state in \(^{77}\)As is +1.2946(13) \(\mu_N\); JUN45 gives +1.941/+1.358 \(\mu_N\) and jj44b gives +1.477/+1.034 \(\mu_N\) (with free/effective \(g_s\) factors).
- The experimental \(B(E2)\) for the \(5/2_1^+ \rightarrow 9/2_1^+\) transition in \(^{77}\)As is 80(16) W.u.; JUN45 gives 19.39/29.78 W.u. and jj44b gives 23.24/38.70 W.u. (with two sets of effective charges).
- For the \(^{79}\)As ground state, the JUN45 interaction predicts a \(\pi(p_{3/2}^1)\) configuration with 12.7% probability; jj44b predicts 10.1%.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of neutron-rich nuclei near the \(^{56}\)Ni region, particularly following the systematics of low-lying yrast states in As isotopes reported by Porquet et al. (2011), who suggested that the \(f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}\) space is insufficient to explain quadrupole excitations in \(^{81}\)As. The calculations also complement projected shell-model results for \(^{67-79}\)As reported by Preeti et al. (2013). The present study enables future work by identifying the need to include the \(\pi 0f_{7/2}\) and \(\nu 1d_{5/2}\) orbitals to simultaneously describe proton excitations across \(Z=28\) and neutron excitations across \(N=50\), and by providing predicted electromagnetic properties for transitions where no experimental data yet exist.
