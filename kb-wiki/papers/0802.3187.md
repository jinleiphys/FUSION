# Neutrality of the color-flavor-locked phase in a Dyson-Schwinger approach

> The role of neutrality constraints for the phase structure of QCD at non-vanishing chemical potentials is studied within a self-consistent truncation scheme for the Dyson-Schwinger equation of the quark propagator in Landau gauge. We find the (approximate) color-flavor-locked phase to be energetically preferred at all potentially relevant densities and for physical values of the quark masses. We furthermore observe the impossibility to define this phase by residual global symmetries and discuss the role of chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The authors find that the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase is energetically preferred at all potentially relevant densities for physical values of the quark masses, even after imposing neutrality constraints; however, they observe that a finite strange-quark mass breaks the CFL symmetry completely, so the phase can only be defined by a continuity argument from the chiral limit rather than by residual global symmetries.

## Method

The authors solve a truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the quark propagator in Landau gauge within a Nambu-Gor'kov formalism, including both normal self-energies and gap functions with full energy and momentum dependence. They impose color neutrality by adjusting color chemical potentials μ₃ and μ₈, and enforce electrical neutrality and β-equilibrium by including a charge chemical potential μ_Q and massless electrons. The running coupling α_s(k²) is taken from DSE studies of the Yang-Mills sector, and the strange-quark mass is varied while up- and down-quark masses are set to zero.

## Key numbers

- The CFL phase persists up to a renormalized strange-quark mass of approximately 135 MeV at μ = 400 MeV, at which point pairing of green strange-quarks with blue down-quarks becomes ungapped.
- The uSC phase exists between approximately 180 MeV and 190 MeV renormalized strange-quark mass, and the 2SC phase appears in the window of approximately 190-200 MeV at μ = 400 MeV.
- The electron chemical potential μ_e exceeds the size of the largest gaps in the gCFL, uSC, and 2SC phases, and is non-vanishing even in the fully gapped CFL phase.
- The critical strange-quark mass for the CFL-to-gCFL transition is slightly shifted to higher values when imposing neutrality constraints compared to the analysis without neutrality in Nickel (2006).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DSE study (Nickel, 2006) by incorporating neutrality constraints (color and electrical neutrality plus β-equilibrium) into the CFL phase analysis. It contrasts with NJL-type model investigations (Ruster, 2005; Steiner, 2002; Abuki, 2005) which find that the CFL phase is automatically electrically neutral with μ_Q = 0; the authors show that energy-dependent gap functions allow for non-vanishing electron chemical potential even in the gapped CFL phase. The results enable future work on whether the gapless phases found at larger strange-quark masses are stable or give way to inhomogeneous phases, as suggested by studies of imaginary Meissner masses (Huang, 2004; Casalbuoni, 2004).
