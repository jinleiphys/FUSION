# A Case for the Chiral Magnetic Effect in mid-peripheral Au-Au Collisions $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV

> The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is predicted for mid-peripheral Au-Au collisions $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV at RHIC. However many backgrounds can give signals that make the measurement hard to interpret. The STAR experiment has made a measurement that makes it possible to separate out the background from the signal. An event shape analysis is the key for controlling the effects of Transverse Momentum Conservation (TMC) in the Au-Au collisions. LHC Pb-Pb collision $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV has made event plane measurements from which we calculate the CME and make predictions about what the event shape analysis should give.

## Key claim

The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is consistent with same-sign charge pair correlations measured by STAR in mid-peripheral Au-Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, but only after separating the dominant background from Transverse Momentum Conservation (TMC) using an event shape analysis; the CME signal is extracted as a constant offset independent of the event-by-event azimuthal anisotropy (v2).

## Method

The analysis uses STAR data for same-sign charge pairs in the centrality range 50-60%, employing the two-particle correlators γ = ⟨cos(φ1+φ2-2ΨRP)⟩ and δ = ⟨cos(φ1-φ2)⟩. An event shape analysis splits the TPC tracks into two sub-events: one half determines the reaction plane, the other half calculates γ and v2 = ⟨cos2(φ-ΨRP)⟩. The TMC background varies linearly with v2 (zero at v2=0, negative for positive v2, positive for negative v2), while the CME is a constant offset independent of v2. A three-parameter fit to γ, δ, and the ratio CME/γ extracts the CME amplitude, TMC amplitude, and the scaling factor ScaleT that relates TMC in δ to its contribution in γ.

## Key numbers

* CME amplitude for same-sign pairs at 50-60% centrality in Au-Au √sNN=200 GeV: -0.0002625 (+0.0000638 / -0.0000845)
* TMC amplitude for same-sign pairs at 50-60% centrality in Au-Au √sNN=200 GeV: -0.0006460 (+0.0001195 / -0.0001295)
* ScaleT (fraction of TMC in δ that appears in γ) for same-sign pairs at 50-60% centrality in Au-Au √sNN=200 GeV: 0.2836 (+0.1601 / -0.1296)
* Measured γ for same-sign pairs at 50-60% centrality in Au-Au √sNN=200 GeV: -0.00045 ± 0.00005
* Measured δ for same-sign pairs at 50-60% centrality in Au-Au √sNN=200 GeV: -0.00038 ± 0.00010
* v2 for 50-60% centrality in Au-Au √sNN=200 GeV: 0.06
* Ratio of pure CME to γ at measured v2=0.06 for same-sign pairs: 0.60 ± 0.15
* For LHC Pb-Pb √sNN=2.76 TeV at 50-60% centrality: γ = -0.0004, δ = 0.00125, v2 = 0.105
* CME amplitude extracted for LHC Pb-Pb √sNN=2.76 TeV at 50-60% centrality: -0.00048 (with no TMC term) or -0.000304 (with ScaleT = 0.2836)

## Context

This work extends the CME search by STAR (STAR Collaboration, 2009) by introducing an event shape analysis to control the TMC background derived by Koch et al. (2010). It contradicts the simple expectation from Koch et al. (2010) that ScaleT should equal v2 (0.06), finding instead ScaleT = 0.2836, attributed to a suppression of near-side same-sign pairs from glasma flux tubes (Longacre, 2011). The analysis enables predictions for ALICE Pb-Pb data at √sNN=2.76 TeV, where the sign change of δ indicates a significant jet contribution, and provides a testable event shape dependence that could confirm or falsify the CME interpretation.
