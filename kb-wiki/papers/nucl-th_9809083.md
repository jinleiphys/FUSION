# Subbarrier heavy ion fusion enhanced by nucleon transfer and subbarrier fusion of nuclei far from the line of β-stability

> We discuss a model for the description of subbarrier fusion of heavy ions which takes into account the coupling to the low-energy surface vibrational states and to the few-nucleon transfer with arbitrary reaction Q-value. The fusion reactions ^{28,30}Si+^{58,62,64}Ni, ^{40}Ca+^{90,96}Zr, ^{28}S+^{94,100}Mo, ^{16,18,20,22,24}O+^{58}Ni and ^{28}Si+^{124,126,128,130,132}Sn are analyzed in detail. The model describes rather well the experimental fusion cross section and mean angular momentum for reactions between nuclei near the β-stability line. It is shown that these quantities are significantly enhanced by few-nucleon transfer with large positive Q-value. A shape independent parameterization of the heavy ion potential at distances smaller then the touching point is proposed.

## Key claim

The model describes subbarrier fusion cross sections and mean angular momenta well for reactions between nuclei near the beta-stability line, but the enhancement from few-nucleon transfer is significant only when the transfer reaction has a large positive Q-value; the model also predicts strong enhancement for reactions involving neutron-rich nuclei far from stability.

## Method

The model couples low-energy surface vibrational states (using the same approach as the CCFUS code) with few-nucleon transfer channels treated in the DWBA approximation. The transmission coefficient for barrier penetration is computed using the WKB approximation with the Landau method of complex classical paths, splitting the action into three terms: tunneling before transfer, the nucleon transfer process itself, and tunneling after transfer. The distance at which transfer occurs is determined by minimizing the total action, and the ion-ion potential at distances smaller than the touching point uses a shape-independent quadratic parameterization matched to the Krappe-Nix-Sierk potential.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr, the Q-values for 2-neutron and 4-neutron transfer are 5.526 MeV and 9.637 MeV respectively; the reaction $^{40}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr has negative Q-values for neutron transfer.
- For the reaction $^{24}$O+$^{58}$Ni, the 1-neutron transfer Q-value is 5.29 MeV; for $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr the 1-neutron transfer Q-value is 0.508 MeV.
- The parameter $\delta$ in the transfer action was taken as 0.7 fm for reactions with Si, Ca, Zr, Mo, and Sn isotopes, and as 1.2 fm for reactions with oxygen isotopes.
- The nuclear radius parameter $r_0$ in the KNS potential was varied slightly from the recommended 1.18 fm; values used are listed in Table 1 of the paper.

## Context

This work extends the CCFUS model (Landowne, 1980s) by treating nucleon transfer with arbitrary Q-values using a semiclassical method that avoids a free coupling constant, unlike earlier phenomenological treatments (e.g., Corradi 1993). The model successfully describes the large subbarrier fusion enhancement observed for $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr (Stefanini, 1998) which could not be explained by vibrational couplings alone. It enables predictions for fusion reactions with radioactive beams involving neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, such as $^{24}$O+$^{58}$Ni and $^{28}$Si+$^{132}$Sn, where large positive Q-values for neutron transfer are expected to produce strong subbarrier enhancement.
