# Radial Excitations

> In this contribution I review recent experimental developments in the spectroscopy of higher-mass mesons, especially candidate radial excitations discussed at the WHS99 meeting in Frascati.

# Radial Excitations

## Key claim
This review argues that identifying radially excited $q\bar q$ mesons is essential for isolating non-$q\bar q$ exotica such as hybrids and glueballs, but cautions that the experimental masses of candidate radial excitations appear to be about 0.1–0.2 GeV lower than the Godfrey-Isgur quark model predictions, and that many reported states may involve multiple overlapping resonances or mixing between quarkonium and hybrid basis states.

## Method
The paper surveys experimental results presented at the WHS99 meeting in Frascati, focusing on Crystal Barrel $p\bar p$ annihilation data in flight analyzed by Bugg and collaborators across final states including $\pi\pi$, $\eta\eta$, $\eta\eta'$, $3\pi^o$, $\eta\pi^o$, $\eta'\pi^o$, and $\eta\pi^o\pi^o$. It also reviews CLEO $\tau$ hadronic decay results for the $a_1(1700)$, VES and E852 data on higher-mass mesons, and L3 $\gamma\gamma$ collision data. Theoretical comparisons are made using the $^3$P$_0$ decay model and flux-tube hybrid model predictions from Barnes et al. (1997) and Close and Page (1995).

- $\rho_5(2335)$: I=1, $J^{PC}=5^{--}$, mass $2335(20)$ MeV, width $150(35)$ MeV, seen in $\pi^+\pi^-$ from Crystal Barrel
- $f_4(2025)$: I=0, $J^{PC}=4^{++}$, mass $2025(15)$ MeV, width $180(15)$ MeV from Crystal Barrel
- $a_1(1700)$ candidate: I=1, $J^{PC}=1^{++}$, reported in $\tau^- \to \nu_\tau \pi^-\pi^o\pi^o$ at CLEO; theoretical $q\bar q$ total width 246 MeV, hybrid total width $\approx 300$ MeV
- $\pi(1800)$: theoretical $q\bar q$ partial widths: $\rho\pi$ 31 MeV, $\rho\omega$ 73 MeV, $f_0(1300)\pi$ 7 MeV; hybrid predictions: $\rho\pi$ 30 MeV, $\rho\omega$ 0 MeV, $f_0(1300)\pi$ 170 MeV

- Crystal Barrel $a_2$ candidate: mass $\approx 1670(20)$ MeV, width $\approx 280(70)$ MeV in $\eta\pi^o$

## Context
This review extends the program of identifying conventional $q\bar q$ states as a prerequisite for isolating hybrids and glueballs, following the flux-tube model predictions of Close and Page (1995) and the bag model. It builds on the Godfrey-Isgur quark model (1985) for mass predictions and the $^3$P$_0$ decay model calculations of Barnes et al. (1997). The paper enables future experimental work to measure branching fractions of candidate radial excitations, particularly for the 2P multiplet where nonexotic hybrid partners of the $\pi_1(1600)$ exotic are expected, and for the 3P and 4P multiplets where the lightest tensor glueball is predicted near 2.2–2.3 GeV by lattice gauge theory.
