# Angular dependence in transverse momentum dependent diffractive parton distributions at small-$x$

> We discuss the angular dependence of the recently proposed transverse momentum dependent quark and gluon diffractive parton distributions at small-$x$. We introduce the difractive versions of the Sivers function and the elliptic gluon Wigner distribution and evaluate them in simple models with gluon saturation and study their geometric scaling properties. We also show that the diffractive version of the linearly polarized gluon distribution identically vanishes to leading order. These distributions enrich the physics opportunities for measuring semi-inclsuive diffractive DIS processes at the future electron-ion colliders.

# Angular dependence in transverse momentum dependent diffractive parton distributions at small-$x$

## Key claim
The paper introduces and computes diffractive versions of the Sivers function and elliptic gluon Wigner distribution, showing that these angular-dependent transverse momentum dependent diffractive parton distributions (TMD DPDFs) exhibit approximate modified geometric scaling only in limited kinematic regimes, with scaling explicitly violated at large transverse momentum; the diffractive linearly polarized gluon distribution identically vanishes to leading order.

## Method
The authors evaluate quark and gluon TMD DPDFs at small-$x$ within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework using the dipole S-matrix formalism, starting from the operator definitions of TMD DPDFs in terms of Wilson lines. They compute the angular correlations by parameterizing the dipole S-matrices with azimuthal-angle-dependent terms (elliptic and spin-dependent odderon contributions) and performing the angular integrals analytically, then study the resulting distributions numerically using a simple Gaussian saturation model.

## Key numbers
- The azimuthally symmetric quark TMD DPDF scales as $\Gamma^q \propto (1-\beta)$ and the gluon as $\Gamma^g \propto (1-\beta)^2$ as $\beta\to 1$.
- The elliptic quark distribution $\Gamma_\epsilon^q$ and the elliptic gluon distribution $\Gamma_\epsilon^g$ both scale as $\propto (1-\beta)$ as $\beta\to 1$.
- The quark Sivers DPDF contribution $\Gamma_{S_\perp}^q$ scales as $\sim (1-\beta)^0$ (constant) as $\beta\to 1$.
- The modified geometric scaling variable is $k_\perp/\widetilde{Q}_{(a)s}$ where $\widetilde{Q}_{(a)s} \equiv \sqrt{1-\beta} Q_{(a)s}$, with $Q_s$ and $Q_{as}$ ($Q_{as}^2 = \frac{N_c}{C_F} Q_s^2$) the quark and gluon saturation scales respectively.
- The elliptic gluon distribution $\Gamma_\epsilon^g$ does not vanish as $k_\perp \to 0$, contrary to the expectation that $\cos 2\phi_k$ angular dependence should vanish as $k_\perp^2$.

## Context
This work extends the recently proposed TMD DPDF framework of Hatta and Yuan (2022) by computing the angular-dependent correlations that arise from the transverse momentum transfer $\Delta_\perp$ and nucleon spin $S_\perp$, analogous to the angular structure of Wigner distributions studied in Hagiwara et al. (2016) and Yao et al. (2018). The results enable future measurements of semi-inclusive diffractive DIS at the Electron-Ion Collider, providing access to spin-dependent odderon physics and gluon saturation dynamics that complement the non-diffractive TMD studies of Metz and Zhou (2011) and the exclusive diffractive studies of Boussarie et al. (2019).
