# Current status and desired accuracy of the isotopic production cross-sections relevant to astrophysics of cosmic rays II. Fluorine to Silicon (and updated LiBeB)

> High-precision cosmic-ray data from ongoing and recent past experiments (Voyager, ACE-CRIS, PAMELA, ATIC, CREAM, NUCLEON, AMS-02, CALET, DAMPE) are being released in the tens of MeV/n to multi-TeV/n energy range. Astrophysical and dark matter interpretations of these data are limited by the precision of nuclear production cross-sections. In Paper I, PRC 98, 034611 (2018), we set up a procedure to rank nuclear reactions whose desired measurements will enable us to fully exploit currently available data on CR Li to N ($Z=3-7$) species. Here we extend these rankings to O up to Si nuclei ($Z=8-14$), also updating our results on the LiBeB species. We also highlight how comprehensive new high precision nuclear data, that could e.g. be obtained at the SPS at CERN, would be a game-changer for the determination of key astrophysical quantities (diffusion coefficient, halo size of the Galaxy) and indirect searches for dark matter signatures.

## Key claim

The paper extends the ranking of nuclear production cross-section measurements needed to exploit current cosmic-ray data from oxygen to silicon (Z=8-14), updating previous results for lithium, beryllium, and boron, and finds that comprehensive new high-precision nuclear data from facilities such as the Super Proton Synchrotron at CERN would be transformative for determining astrophysical quantities like the diffusion coefficient and Galactic halo size, as well as for indirect dark matter searches.

## Method

The authors used the 1D semianalytical propagation model in the USINE package with a purely diffusive configuration (SLIM) and a broken-power-law diffusion coefficient, employing species-dependent broken power laws for cosmic-ray source spectra. They considered two parameterizations from the GALPROP code (OPT12 and OPT22) for isotopic production cross sections, and a third mixed parameterization that renormalizes the most important reactions for LiBeB and F to newer nuclear data. The ranking coefficients f_abc were calculated at 10 GeV/n to identify the most important production reactions a+b to c for each cosmic-ray species.

## Key numbers

- The precision of nuclear production cross sections reaches only 10-20% on average, while AMS-02 cosmic-ray data achieve unprecedented few-percentage precision
- At 10 GeV/n, fluorine is a quasipure secondary species, while Ne, Mg, and Si are predominantly of primary origin, and Na and Al are mixed species
- Direct (1-step) production dominates fragmentation production, but 2-step production contributes up to 20% and more-than-2-step production contributes up to 10% for some species
- The break in cosmic-ray spectra occurs at magnetic rigidity R approximately 300 GV for primary, secondary, and intermediate nuclei
- AMS-02 data indicate a cutoff in the positron spectrum at approximately 350 GeV

## Context

This paper extends the ranking procedure established in Paper I (Génolini et al., 2018, Physical Review C 98, 034611), which covered lithium to nitrogen (Z=3-7), to oxygen through silicon (Z=8-14) while updating results for LiBeB species. The work enables experimental teams to focus on a small number of crucial nuclear reactions rather than measuring thousands of relevant reactions, and the F/Si ratio is identified as a heavier analog to the B/C ratio used for determining cosmic-ray propagation parameters. The results are largely independent of the specific cosmic-ray model implementation as long as the model reproduces standard observables.
