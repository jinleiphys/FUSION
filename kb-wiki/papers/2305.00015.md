# Determination of the neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb from ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> Emergent bulk properties of matter governed by the strong nuclear force give rise to physical phenomena across vastly different scales, ranging from the shape of atomic nuclei to the masses and radii of neutron stars. They can be accessed on Earth by measuring the spatial extent of the outer skin made of neutrons that characterises the surface of heavy nuclei. The isotope $^{208}$Pb, owing to its simple structure and neutron excess, has been in this context the target of many dedicated efforts. Here, we determine the neutron skin from measurements of particle distributions and their collective flow in $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb collisions at ultrarelativistic energy performed at the Large Hadron Collider, which are sensitive to the overall size of the colliding $^{208}$Pb ions. By means of state-of-the-art global analysis tools within the hydrodynamic model of heavy-ion collisions, we infer a neutron skin $Δr_{np}=0.217\pm0.058$ fm, consistent with nuclear theory predictions, and competitive in accuracy with a recent determination from parity-violating asymmetries in polarised electron scattering. We establish thus a new experimental method to systematically measure neutron distributions in the ground state of atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors determine the neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb from ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collision data using a hydrodynamic model, obtaining a value of $\Delta r_{np}=0.217\pm0.058$ fm that is consistent with both nuclear theory predictions and the PREX result, but the extraction depends on the specific combination of observables used and the model assumptions for energy deposition.

## Method

The authors simulate $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb collisions at LHC energies using a hydrodynamic model with a T$_{\text{R}}$ENTo-type initial condition, where the neutron skin is introduced by varying the neutron diffuseness parameter in a two-parameter Fermi distribution while keeping the half-width neutron radius fixed. They perform a Bayesian global analysis with 26 model parameters, using 3000 design points for Gaussian Process emulation, and constrain the neutron skin by fitting to 653 data points from $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb collisions and one data point from proton-$^{208}$Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.217 \pm 0.058$ fm, corresponding to a point-like rms neutron radius $r_n = 5.653 \pm 0.058$ fm
- Slope parameter of the symmetry energy: $L = 79 \pm 39$ MeV, representing the first collider-based constraint on this parameter
- Without including the total $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb and $p$+$^{208}$Pb cross sections: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.31 \pm 0.10$ fm
- Using exclusively the cross section data points: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.03 \pm 0.12$ fm
- Without the $\rho_2$ observable: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.243 \pm 0.059$ fm
- Using only $p_T$-integrated observables (233 ALICE data points): $\Delta r_{np} = 0.216 \pm 0.057$ fm

## Context

This work establishes a new experimental method for measuring neutron distributions in atomic nuclei using ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, complementing the parity-violating electron scattering approach used by the PREX collaboration (2021) and the ab initio nuclear theory calculations of Hu et al. (2021). The result is consistent with both previous determinations and enables future systematic measurements of neutron skins across the nuclear chart, particularly with planned collisions of $^{48}$Ca and other ions at the high-luminosity LHC, which could be compared with the CREX measurement of $^{48}$Ca.
