# Jets as precision probes in electron-nucleus collisions at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We discuss the prospects of using jets as precision probes in electron-nucleus collisions at the future Electron-Ion Collider. Jets produced in deep-inelastic scattering can be calibrated by a measurement of the scattered electron. Such electron-jet "tag and probe" measurements call for an approach that is orthogonal to most HERA jet measurements as well as previous studies of jets at the future EIC. We present observables such as the electron-jet momentum balance, azimuthal correlations and jet substructure, which can provide constraints on the parton transport coefficient in nuclei. We compare simulations and analytical calculations and provide estimates of the expected medium effects. Implications for detector design at the future EIC are discussed.

## Key claim

Jets produced in deep-inelastic scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider can be calibrated by the scattered electron on an event-by-event basis, enabling electron-jet "tag and probe" measurements that provide constraints on the parton transport coefficient in nuclei, but only for events with inelasticity \(y>0.1\) due to the kinematic resolution limitations of the electron-only method.

## Method

The authors used Pythia8 to simulate neutral-current DIS events in electron-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=89\) GeV (20 GeV electron, 100 GeV proton), applying EPPS16 nuclear PDFs for lead to approximate electron-nucleus collisions. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-\(k_{\mathrm{T}}\) algorithm with radius parameter \(R=1.0\) in the laboratory frame, requiring \(Q^{2}>1\) GeV\(^{2}\), \(W^{2}>10\) GeV\(^{2}\), and \(0.1<y<0.85\). The analysis compared Pythia8 simulations with analytical calculations from Liu et al. (2018) for observables including electron-jet transverse momentum balance, azimuthal correlations, and jet substructure.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: \(\sqrt{s}=89\) GeV (20 GeV electron, 100 GeV proton)
- Jet reconstruction uses anti-\(k_{\mathrm{T}}\) algorithm with \(R=1.0\)
- Kinematic selection: \(Q^{2}>1\) GeV\(^{2}\), \(W^{2}>10\) GeV\(^{2}\), \(0.1<y<0.85\)
- Minimum jet transverse momentum: \(\pTjet \ge 4\) GeV/c (following HERA and RHIC precedents)
- Transverse momentum spectra reach up to \(\pT\approx 35\) GeV/c for 10 fb\(^{-1}\) integrated luminosity
- Average \(x=0.11\) and average \(\nu=1.1\) TeV for the kinematic selection \(10<\pTelectron<15\) GeV/c
- For struck-quark and beam-remnant separation: \(Q^{2}>25\) GeV\(^{2}\) required at low-\(x\) (0.008–0.01); \(Q^{2}>100\) GeV\(^{2}\) required for \(\pTelectron\) 10–30 GeV/c
- Azimuthal cut to suppress beam remnant jets: \(|\phi^{jet}-\phi^e-\pi|<0.4\)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Liu et al. (2018) for electron-jet correlations in DIS to practical experimental implementation at the future EIC, and differs from most HERA jet measurements (which used the Breit frame and suppressed the leading-order DIS process) by working in the laboratory frame. It also differs from recent work by Aschenauer et al. (2019) that focused on jet photoproduction in electron-proton collisions. The results enable nuclear tomography via azimuthal correlations, providing a clean channel to explore the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) in cold nuclear matter, which may also illuminate studies of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions.
