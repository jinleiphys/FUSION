# Deep Inelastic Electropion Production

> This paper is devoted to a study of possible scaling laws, and their logarithmic corrections, occurring in deep inelastic electropion production. Both the exclusive and semi-exclusive processes are considered. Scaling laws, originally motivated from PCAC and current algebra considerations are examined, first in the framework of the parton model and QCD peturbation theory and then from the more formal perspective of the operator product expansion and asymptotic freedom, (as expressed through the renormalization group). We emphasize that these processes allow scaling to be probed for the full amplitude rather than just its absorbtive part (as is the case in the conventional structure functions). Because of this it is not possible to give a formal derivation of scaling for deep inelastic electropion production processes even if one believes that they are unambiguously sensitive to the light cone behavior of the operator product. The origin of this is shown to be related to its behavior near $x\approx 0$. Investigations, both theoretical and experimental, of these processes is therefore strongly encouraged.

## Key claim

The paper argues that deep inelastic electropion production allows scaling to be probed for the full amplitude rather than just its absorptive part (as in conventional structure functions), but a formal derivation of scaling cannot be given even if the process is sensitive to light cone behavior, because of complications related to the amplitude's behavior near x ≈ 0.

## Method

The authors analyze deep inelastic exclusive and semi-exclusive pion production from nucleons using three complementary approaches: current algebra with PCAC, the quark-parton model, and the operator product expansion with renormalization group methods. They derive scaling laws and calculate leading logarithmic corrections to the amplitude using diagrammatic methods analogous to the Altarelli-Parisi evolution equations, working in the light cone gauge with ladder diagrams. The analysis is restricted to the kinematic region where the momentum transfer t and Bjorken x are fixed and finite while q² → -∞, with x not too close to unity.

## Key numbers

- The Cornell experiment measured d²σ/dt dW'² at two values of √s = 2.66 GeV and 3.14 GeV at the same x; the scaling prediction implies spectra should differ by a normalization factor of (3.14/2.66)⁴ ≈ 2.41
- The minimum momentum transfer t_min ≈ -x²M²/(1-x) in the deep inelastic limit
- The anomalous dimension exponent for moments of the amplitude is d_n = (C_F/β)(1 + 4∑_{j=2}^{n+2} 1/j - 2/((n+2)(n+3)))
- The running coupling constant α_s(k²_⊥) ≈ 4π/(β ln k²_⊥) with β = (11 - 2n_f/3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' own earlier current algebra analyses from the 1970s (Dombey and Shann 1972; West 1993) and a thesis by Calogeracos (1981), which predicted scaling behavior that was verified by coarse data from Bebek et al. (1973, 1975) at Cornell. The paper contrasts the theoretical footing of deep inelastic electropion production with the well-established operator product expansion treatment of conventional structure functions, and with other hadronic processes like Drell-Yan or jet production where unknown soft infrared contributions require pragmatic recipes. The authors encourage new experiments at HERA, CEBAF, and a possible SLAC upgrade, noting that the results also apply to deep inelastic electroproduction of ρ mesons, K mesons, and real photons.
