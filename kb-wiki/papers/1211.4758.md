# Pairing and realistic shell-model interactions

> This paper starts with a brief historical overview of pairing in nuclei, which fulfills the purpose of properly framing the main subject. This concerns the pairing properties of a realistic shell-model effective interaction which has proved very successful in describing nuclei around doubly magic 132Sn. We focus attention on the two nuclei 134Te and 134Sn with two valence protons and neutrons, respectively. Our study brings out the key role of one particle-one hole excitations in producing a significant difference between proton and neutron pairing in this region.

# Pairing and realistic shell-model interactions

## Key claim

The realistic shell-model effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the Vlow-k renormalization approach reproduces the large difference between proton and neutron pairing gaps in the 132Sn region, but this difference arises not from the bare interaction itself but from the different role played by one particle-one hole (1p1h) core polarization contributions for protons versus neutrons.

## Method

The authors derived a shell-model effective interaction from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, renormalized using the Vlow-k approach with a cutoff momentum Λ, and then computed the effective interaction via a Q-box folded-diagram expansion including second-order diagrams (Vlow-k, two core polarization diagrams V1p1h and V2p2h, and a ladder diagram). They applied this interaction to calculate the spectra of 134Te and 134Sn, assuming doubly magic 132Sn as a closed core, with valence protons occupying five orbitals in the 50-82 shell and neutrons occupying six orbitals in the 82-126 shell. The analysis focused on the J=0 matrix elements and the role of 1p1h core polarization for different valence configurations.

## Key numbers

- The first 2+ state in 134Te lies at 1.28 MeV experimentally, while in 134Sn it drops to 726 keV, making it the lowest first-excited 2+ level observed in a semi-magic even-even nucleus.
- The J=0 matrix element of Vlow-k for the (νf7/2)2 configuration is shifted down by only 250 keV when including all 1p1h contributions.
- For the (πg7/2)2 configuration, the J=0 matrix element of V1p1h is -0.554 MeV, compared to -0.248 MeV for Vlow-k, showing the dominant role of core polarization for protons.
- For the (νh9/2)2 configuration, the J=0 matrix element of V1p1h is -0.723 MeV, compared to -0.098 MeV for Vlow-k, more than half an order of magnitude larger.
- For the (πg7/2)2 configuration, the J=0 matrix element of V1p1h is -0.554 MeV, more than twice that of Vlow-k (-0.248 MeV).
- The pairing constant G for protons ranges from 0.18 to 0.24 MeV (excluding the s1/2 orbit at 0.56 MeV), while for neutrons it ranges from 0.13 to 0.26 MeV.
- The J=0 diagonal matrix element of Veff for (νf7/2)2 is -0.654 MeV; for (νh9/2)2 it is -1.114 MeV; for (πg7/2)2 it is -0.967 MeV; for (πh11/2)2 it is -1.143 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the program of realistic shell-model calculations using effective interactions derived from free nucleon-nucleon potentials, as reviewed by Coraggio et al. (2009). It addresses a debate in the literature: earlier work by Terasaki et al. (2002) and Shimizu et al. (2004) had interpreted the low 2+ energy in 134Sn as evidence for a quenching of the N=82 shell closure, while a first mass measurement by Fogelberg et al. (1999) cast doubt on the doubly magic nature of 132Sn. A high-precision Penning trap measurement by Dworschak et al. (2008) later restored the neutron-shell gap at N=82. The present results show that the realistic effective interaction accounts for the reduced neutron pairing without invoking shell-structure modifications, and the analysis of the microscopic origin of the pairing component enables future studies of how core polarization determines pairing properties in different mass regions.
