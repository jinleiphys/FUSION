# EIC Physics from An All-Silicon Tracking Detector

> The proposed electron-ion collider has a rich physics program to study the internal structure of protons and heavy nuclei. This program will impose strict requirements on detector design. This paper explores how these requirements can be satisfied using an all-silicon tracking detector, by consideration of three representative probes: heavy flavor hadrons, jets, and exclusive vector mesons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an all-silicon tracking detector can satisfy the physics requirements for the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) for heavy flavor hadrons, jets, and exclusive vector mesons, but only with a 3.0 T magnetic field and only over the pseudorapidity range \(-2.0 < \eta < 3.0\); at 1.4 T, no pseudorapidity range meets the requirements over the full momentum range.

## Method

The authors designed a cylindrical all-silicon tracker with 6 barrel layers and 5 disks on each side, modeled after ALICE ITS2-upgrade staves with 10×10 µm² pixel MAPS sensors, and implemented it in GEANT4 within the Fun4All framework. They simulated charged pions, electrons, muons, and protons from the nominal interaction point over momenta 0–30 GeV/c and pseudorapidity \(|\eta|<4\) using 3.0 T (BeAST) and 1.4 T (BaBar) solenoidal field maps, then extracted momentum resolution, distance-of-closest-approach (DCA) resolution, angular resolution, and tracking efficiency from truth-vs-reconstructed comparisons. For physics projections, they used PYTHIA v6.4 to generate e+p events at multiple beam energies and applied the detector performance parameters to study charm-hadron reconstruction, secondary vertexing, and heavy-flavor production.

## Key numbers

- Tracker dimensions: radius 43.2 cm, length 242 cm along z; innermost barrel layer at radius 3.30 cm, outermost at 43.23 cm
- Material budget per stave: 0.3% X₀; total barrel and disk stave material < 5% X₀
- Sensor pixel pitch: 10×10 µm², silicon thickness 50 µm, point resolution 10/√12 µm
- Momentum resolution parametrized as dp/p = A·p ⊕ B; at 3.0 T and \(0<\eta<0.5\), A = 0.13%/(GeV/c) and B = 0.33% (from fits shown in Fig. 7)
- At 3.0 T, the tracker satisfies physics requirements over 0 < p < 30 GeV/c for \(-2.0 < \eta < 3.0\); at 1.4 T, no η range satisfies requirements over the full momentum range
- pT threshold for reaching outer layer (r ≥ 43.23 cm): 195 MeV/c at 3.0 T, 90 MeV/c at 1.4 T; for reaching third barrel layer (r ≥ 21.00 cm): 95 MeV/c at 3.0 T, 44 MeV/c at 1.4 T
- Primary vertex resolution: ~25 µm at track multiplicity ~5 in 18×275 GeV e+p collisions with Q² > 1 GeV²
- Adding a 10 µm-pixel silicon disk at z = -180 cm improves momentum resolution in the backward region by a factor of two or more for high-momentum particles

## Context

This work extends prior EIC detector concept studies, including a notional all-silicon detector extending to 75 cm radius (Klein, 2020) and earlier heavy-flavor measurement studies at the EIC (Aschenauer, 2017; Chudakov, 2016). The all-silicon tracker is compared against hybrid designs combining silicon pixels with gas detectors (TPC, MPGD), and the paper shows the compact all-silicon design (43.2 cm radius) leaves more space for outer PID detectors. The results enable detailed performance projections for heavy-flavor hadron production to constrain gluon PDFs, gluon TMDs, gluon helicity distributions, and heavy-quark hadronization studies at the EIC, building on HERA charm structure function measurements (Abramowicz, 1900rp) and COMPASS gluon helicity measurements (Adolph, 2012).
