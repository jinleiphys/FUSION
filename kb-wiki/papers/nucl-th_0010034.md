# GDH2000 Convenor's Report: Spin Polarizabilities

> The subject of low energy polarized Compton scattering from the proton, which is characterized by phenomenological spin-polarizabilities, is introduced and connection is made to new theoretical and experimental developments which were reported to this meeting.

## Key claim

The decomposition of the GDH sum rule integrand into intrinsic amplitudes and spin-polarizabilities is more important than the final sum rule value itself, but the interpretation of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχpt) results for spin-polarizabilities is presently in dispute due to ambiguity over whether certain diagrams with a nucleon pole and a pion loop renormalizing an electromagnetic vertex are one-particle-reducible and should be discarded.

## Method

The report surveys theoretical calculations of spin-polarizabilities using HBχpt at orders p^3 and p^4, and compares them to values extracted via dispersive analysis of real Compton scattering (RCS) data using fixed-t dispersion relations with subtraction constants fitted from experiment. For virtual Compton scattering (VCS), generalized (q-dependent) polarizabilities are extracted from MAMI electron scattering data after subtracting Bethe-Heitler and Born contributions, and compared to HBχpt and other model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Electric polarizability of the proton (new TAPS/LARA measurement): α_E^p = (12.24 ± 0.24 ± 0.54) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Magnetic polarizability of the proton (new TAPS/LARA measurement): β_M^p = (1.57 ∓ 0.24 ∓ 0.54) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Forward spin polarizability γ_π (LEGS global analysis, pion pole subtracted): (15.7 ± 2.3 ± 2.8 ± 2.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Forward spin polarizability γ_π (new TAPS data): (7.4 ± 2.3) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Forward spin polarizability γ_0 (MAID analysis sum rule): -0.80 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Use of the SAID E_0+ multipole leads to a 20 μb (~10%) change in the GDH sum rule value and a 0.75 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ (~100%) shift in the forward spin polarizability
- Generalized polarizabilities from MAMI VCS experiment (q=0.6 GeV): P_LL - P_TT/ε = 23.7 ± 2.2 ± 0.6 ± 4.3 GeV⁻²; P_LT = -5.0 ± 0.8 ± 1.1 ± 1.4 GeV⁻²

## Context

This convenor's report summarizes the session on low-energy aspects of the GDH sum rule at the GDH2000 workshop, extending the well-known spin-independent polarizabilities (α_E, β_M) measured at SAL and MAMI to the spin-polarizability sector. It connects the theoretical framework of HBχpt calculations by Bernard, Kaiser, Schmidt and Meissner (1993) and by Hemmert and McGovern to experimental results from the LEGS group, the TAPS/LARA collaboration, and the MAMI VCS experiment by Roche et al. The work enables future direct measurements of spin-polarizabilities via polarized Compton scattering (p(γ,γ)p) and extension of the dispersive analysis to virtual Compton scattering at higher Q².
