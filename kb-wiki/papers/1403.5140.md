# $D$ and $D^{\ast}$ meson mixing in spin-isospin correlated cold nuclear matter

> We propose to study the mass spectrum of the heavy-light mesons to probe the structure of the spin-isospin correlation in the nuclear medium. We point out that the spin-isospin correlation in the nuclear medium generates a mixing among the heavy-light mesons carrying different spins and isospins such as $D^+$, $D^0$, $D^{\ast +}$, and $D^{\ast 0}$ mesons. We use two types of correlations motivated by the skyrmion crystal and the chiral density wave as typical examples to obtain the mass splitting caused by the mixing. Our result shows that the structure of the mixing reflects the pattern of the correlation, i.e., the remaining symmetry. Furthermore, the magnitude of the mass modification provides information of the strength of the correlation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the mass spectrum of heavy-light mesons (specifically $D$ and $D^{\ast}$ mesons) can serve as a probe of spin-isospin correlations in cold nuclear matter, but the actual mixing and mass splitting patterns depend on the specific symmetry-breaking pattern of the correlation (e.g., skyrmion crystal vs. chiral density wave), and the magnitude of the mass modification is of the same order as the correlation strength parameter $\alpha$.

## Method

The authors construct a heavy-light meson effective Lagrangian in the heavy quark limit, including a pion coupling term with coupling constant $g_\pi = 0.56$ determined from $D^{\ast} \to D \pi$ decay. They introduce position-dependent pion condensates $\langle \alpha_{\perp}^{iA} \rangle$ representing spin-isospin correlations, then compute the inverse propagator matrix for the eight states ($D^+$, $D^0$, $D^{\ast+}$, $D^{\ast0}$ with spin degrees of freedom). The mass spectrum is obtained by solving $\det \Delta = 0$ for two specific correlation patterns: Pattern I (hedgehog/skyrmion crystal) where $\langle \alpha_{\perp}^{iA} \rangle = \alpha \delta^{iA}$, and Pattern II (chiral density wave) where $\langle \alpha_{\perp}^{iA} \rangle = \alpha \delta^{i3} \delta^{A3}$. The analysis is performed both in the heavy quark limit and including the vacuum mass difference between $D$ and $D^{\ast}$ mesons.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant $g_\pi = 0.56$ (determined from $D^{\ast} \to D \pi$ decay)
- Vacuum masses used: $m_D \simeq 1870$ MeV and $m_{D^\ast} \simeq 2010$ MeV (empirical values)
- For Pattern I in the heavy quark limit: six states have mass shift $2k_0 = g_\pi \alpha$ and two states have $2k_0 = -3 g_\pi \alpha$
- For Pattern II in the heavy quark limit: four states have $2k_0 = g_\pi \alpha$ and four states have $2k_0 = -g_\pi \alpha$
- The chiral density wave phase is expected above $2.4 \rho_0$ (normal nuclear density)
- In the Skyrme model with vector mesons, the pion $p$-wave condensation size becomes on the order of a few 100 MeV at about normal nuclear density $\rho_0$

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy-light mesons in nuclear matter (previously studied by various authors cited as "Dmeosn") by explicitly addressing the mixing between $D$ and $D^{\ast}$ mesons carrying different spins, which had not been previously stated in the literature. The spin-isospin correlations considered are motivated by the skyrmion crystal (Park and Vento, 2009) and the chiral density wave phase (cited as "CDW"), with the Skyrme model calculation including vector meson effects by Ma et al. (2013) providing density estimates. The work enables future concrete model calculations that explicitly yield the density dependence of the correlation strength $\alpha$, and can be extended to bottomed mesons, anticharmed mesons, and mixings between different heavy-quark multiplets such as $D_1$ and $D^{\ast}$.
