# A Relativistic Many-Body Analysis of the Electric Dipole Moment of $^{223}$Rn

> We report the results of our {\it ab initio} relativistic many-body calculations of the electric dipole moment (EDM) $d_A$ arising from the electron-nucleus tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) interaction, the interaction of the nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) with the atomic electrons and the electric dipole polarizability $α_d$ for $^{223}$Rn. Our relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA) results are substantially larger than those of lower-order relativistic many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) and the results based on the relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) method with single and double excitations (CCSD) are the most accurate to date for all the three properties that we have considered. We obtain $d_A = 4.85(6) \times 10^{-20} < σ> C_T \ |e| \ cm$ from T-PT interaction, $d_A=2.89(4) \times 10^{-17} {S/(|e|\ fm^3)}$ from NSM interaction and $α_d=35.27(9) \ ea_0^3$. The former two results in combination with the measured value of $^{223}$Rn EDM, when it becomes available, could yield the best limits for the T-PT coupling constant, EDMs and chromo-EDMs of quarks and $θ_{QCD}$ parameter, and would thereby shed light on leptoquark and supersymmetric models that predict CP violation.

## Key claim

The authors report that their relativistic coupled-cluster method with single and double excitations (CCSD) yields the most accurate calculations to date for the electric dipole moment (EDM) of $^{223}$Rn arising from both the tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) interaction and the nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) interaction, but these results are contingent on the eventual availability of a measured EDM value for this isotope.

## Method

The authors performed four-component relativistic many-body calculations using the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian with a Fermi nuclear charge distribution. They applied a hierarchy of methods: Dirac-Fock (DF), second-order and third-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT(2), MBPT(3)), random-phase approximation (RPA), linearized coupled-cluster with single and double excitations (LCCSD), and full CCSD. The EDM and electric dipole polarizability were computed as expectation values of the dipole operator using the first-order perturbed wave function, with uncertainties estimated from triple excitations, Breit interaction, and QED corrections.

## Key numbers

- EDM from T-PT interaction: $d_A = 4.85(6) \times 10^{-20} \langle \sigma \rangle C_T \ |e| \ cm$ (CCSD result)
- EDM from NSM interaction: $d_A = 2.89(4) \times 10^{-17} \ {S/(|e|\ fm^3)}$ (CCSD result)
- Electric dipole polarizability: $\alpha_d = 35.27(9) \ ea_0^3$ (CCSD result)
- DF value for $\alpha_d$: 34.42 $ea_0^3$; RPA value: 35.00 $ea_0^3$
- The $^{223}$Rn EDMs are about 9 times larger than the authors' previously reported results for $^{129}$Xe
- The best limit for a diamagnetic atom EDM from $^{199}$Hg is $d_A < 3.1 \times 10^{-29} \ |e| cm$ (Griffith et al.)
- $^{223}$Rn EDM is estimated to be a factor of 400 to 600 times larger than that of $^{199}$Hg (Octen et al.)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of $^{223}$Rn EDM by Dzuba et al. (2002, 2009) who used relativistic RPA, and provides the first CCSD-level treatment for this isotope. The results improve upon earlier calculations of the electric dipole polarizability by Runeberg et al., Nash et al., Nakajima et al., Roos et al., and Dilip et al., which showed poor agreement with each other. The computed EDM values, when combined with future experimental measurements of $^{223}$Rn EDM (projected sensitivity $10^{-26}$ to $10^{-27} \ |e| cm$), could yield improved limits for the T-PT coupling constant, quark EDMs and chromo-EDMs, and the $\theta_{QCD}$ parameter, thereby testing leptoquark and supersymmetric models.
