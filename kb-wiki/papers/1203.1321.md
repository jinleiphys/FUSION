# Origin of ΔI=1/2 Rule for Kaon Decays: QCD Infrared Fixed Point

> We replace ordinary chiral SU(3)_L * SU(3)_R perturbation theory CHPT_3 by a new theory CHPT_sigma based on a low-energy expansion about an infrared fixed point alpha_IR for 3-flavor QCD. At alpha_IR, the quark condensate <bar{q}q>_vac =\= 0 induces nine Nambu-Goldstone bosons: pi, K, eta and a 0++ QCD dilaton sigma. Physically, sigma appears as the f_0(500) resonance, a pole at a complex mass with real part < m_K. The ΔI = 1/2 rule for nonleptonic K-decays is then a consequence of CHPT_sigma, with a K_S-sigma coupling fixed by data for K_S^0 --> gamma gamma and gamma gamma --> pi pi. We estimate R_IR ~ 5 for the nonperturbative Drell-Yan ratio R = sigma(e+e- --> hadrons)/sigma(e+e- --> mu+mu-) at alpha_IR.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the long-standing ΔI = 1/2 rule for nonleptonic kaon decays can be explained as a leading-order consequence of a new chiral-scale perturbation theory (χPTσ) based on an expansion about an infrared fixed point αIR for 3-flavor QCD, rather than requiring an unnaturally large enhancement of the octet coupling g8 relative to g27 as in standard χPT3.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral-scale perturbation theory χPTσ that includes a QCD dilaton σ (identified with the f0(500) resonance) as a ninth Nambu-Goldstone boson alongside π, K, η, based on the hypothesis that the quark condensate induces scale symmetry breaking at an infrared fixed point αIR where the QCD β-function vanishes. They add effective weak operators to this Lagrangian and compute the leading-order K_S-σ mixing amplitude, using data for K_S0 → γγ and γγ → ππ to fix the K_Sσ coupling and thereby determine the dilaton-pole contribution to K → ππ decays.

## Key numbers

- f0(500) complex pole mass: m_f0 = 441 − i 272 MeV (from Caprini et al. 2006)
- Experimental I=0 ππ amplitude: |A0|_expt. = 0.33 keV (PDG)
- Dilaton-pole amplitude estimate: |A_σ-pole| ≈ 0.34 keV
- K_Sσ coupling estimate: |g_K_Sσ| ≈ 4.4 × 10^3 keV^2 (to about 30% precision)
- σππ coupling magnitude: |g_σππ| = 3.31^{+0.35}_{-0.15} GeV (from Kaminski et al. 2011)
- Dilaton width estimate: Γ_σππ ∼ 250 MeV (from m_σ^3/(16π F_σ^2))
- Estimated Drell-Yan ratio at αIR: R_IR ≈ 5
- f0(500) → γγ width: Γ(f0 → γγ) = 1.98^{+0.30}_{-0.24} keV (from Oller et al. 2008)
- Dilaton decay constant estimate: F_σ ∼ −100 MeV (from NN scattering, with ∼70% uncertainty)
- Constraint on combination of parameters: 1½ ≲ |2 + (1−c₁)β′| ≲ 6

## Context

This work extends the original idea of Crewther (1970) and Ellis (1970) that the chiral condensate may also be a scale condensate, and replaces standard chiral perturbation theory χPT3 (Gasser and Leutwyler 1985) with a new theory χPTσ that includes the f0(500) as a dilaton. It contradicts the conventional view that the ΔI = 1/2 rule requires an unnaturally large octet enhancement (|g8/g27| ≈ 22), and instead explains the factor of 22 as arising from a dilaton-pole diagram in leading order. The approach enables predictions for the nonperturbative Drell-Yan ratio R_IR ≈ 5 at the infrared fixed point, and opens the possibility of relating low-energy kaon decay amplitudes to high-energy quantities characteristic of massless QCD at αIR.
