# Relativistic effective interaction for nuclei, giant resonances, and neutron stars

> Nuclear effective interactions are useful tools in astrophysical applications especially if one can guide the extrapolations to the extremes regions of isospin and density that are required to simulate dense, neutron-rich systems. Isospin extrapolations may be constrained in the laboratory by measuring the neutron skin thickness of a heavy nucleus, such as 208Pb. Similarly, future observations of massive neutron stars will constrain the extrapolations to the high-density domain. In this contribution we introduce a new relativistic effective interaction that is simultaneously constrained by the properties of finite nuclei, their collective excitations, and neutron-star properties. By adjusting two of the empirical parameters of the theory, one can efficiently tune the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb and the maximum neutron star mass. We illustrate this procedure in response to the recent interpretation of X-ray observations by Steiner, Lattimer, and Brown that suggests that the FSUGold effective interaction predicts neutron star radii that are too large and a maximum stellar mass that is too small. The new effective interaction is fitted to a neutron skin thickness in 208Pb of only R_n - R_p = 0.16 fm and a moderately large maximum neutron star mass of 1.94 Msun.

## Key claim
The authors introduce a new relativistic effective interaction (IU-FSU) that is simultaneously constrained by finite-nuclei properties, collective excitations, and neutron-star observations, specifically tuned to produce a neutron skin thickness in 208Pb of 0.16 fm and a maximum neutron star mass of 1.94 solar masses. The claim is conditional on the interpretation of X-ray observations by Steiner, Lattimer, and Brown (2010), which suggested that the earlier FSUGold interaction predicted neutron star radii that were too large and a maximum mass that was too small.

## Method
The authors start from the FSUGold parameter set and adjust two empirical parameters: the omega-meson self-coupling (zeta) is reduced from 0.06 to 0.03 to stiffen the high-density equation of state and increase the maximum neutron star mass, while the isoscalar-isovector coupling (Lambda_v) is increased from 0.030 to 0.046 to soften the symmetry energy and reduce the neutron skin thickness in 208Pb. The isoscalar parameters (g_s, g_v, kappa, lambda) are then refitted to maintain saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter at a density of 0.148 fm^-3, an energy per nucleon of -16.30 MeV, and an incompressibility coefficient of 230 MeV. Ground-state properties of closed-shell nuclei are computed in a relativistic mean-field approximation, collective excitations are calculated using relativistic random-phase approximation, and neutron star structure is obtained by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers
- Neutron skin thickness in 208Pb: R_n - R_p = 0.16 fm (IU-FSU prediction)
- Maximum neutron star mass: 1.94 solar masses (IU-FSU prediction)
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: 0.148 fm^-3 (FSU) and 0.155 fm^-3 (IU-FSU)
- Incompressibility coefficient: K = 230.0 MeV (FSU) and K = 231.2 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J = 32.59 MeV (FSU) and J = 31.30 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Slope of symmetry energy at saturation: L = 60.5 MeV (FSU) and L = 47.2 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Binding energy per nucleon in symmetric nuclear matter: -16.30 MeV (FSU) and -16.40 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Centroid energy of GMR in 208Pb: 14.17 MeV (IU-FSU), compared to experimental 14.17 +/- 0.28 MeV
- Centroid energy of GMR in 90Zr: 17.87 MeV (IU-FSU), compared to experimental 17.89 +/- 0.20 MeV
- Peak energy of IVGDR in 208Pb: 13.24 MeV (IU-FSU), compared to experimental 13.30 +/- 0.10 MeV
- Stellar radius for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 12.49 km (IU-FSU prediction)
- Maximum neutron star mass for NL3: 2.78 solar masses
- Maximum neutron star mass for FSU: 1.72 solar masses

## Context
This work extends the line of relativistic effective interactions developed by Mueller and Serot (1996), Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005) (FSUGold), and Lalazissis, Raman, and Ring (1996, 1999) (NL3). The IU-FSU interaction is motivated by the observational analysis of Steiner, Lattimer, and Brown (2010), which suggested that FSUGold predicted neutron star radii that were too large and a maximum mass that was too small, while NL3 predicted a maximum mass of 2.78 solar masses that was too large. The new interaction enables simultaneous consistency with finite-nuclei properties, giant resonance data, and neutron-star observations, and provides a framework for tuning the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb and maximum neutron star mass by adjusting only two parameters. Future measurements from the Lead Radius Experiment (PREx) and improved X-ray observations of neutron stars will further constrain the isovector interaction and high-density equation of state.
