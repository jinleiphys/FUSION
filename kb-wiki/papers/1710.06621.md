# Quasiparticle and $γ$-band structures in $^{156}$Dy

> Excited band structures recently observed in $^{156}$Dy are investigated using the microscopic triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach and the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) based on the rotating mean-field. It is demonstrated that new observed excited bands, tracking the ground-state band, are the $γ$-bands based on the excited two-quasineutron configurations as conjectured in the experimental work.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the recently observed excited bands in \(^{156}\)Dy, which track the ground-state band, are \(\gamma\)-bands built on excited two-quasineutron configurations, as conjectured in the experimental work. This interpretation is supported by both the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) and the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) based on the rotating mean-field, though the TPSM calculation overestimates the spread of the spectrum, likely due to the assumption of fixed deformation and pair gaps.

## Method

The authors extended the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) basis to include four-neutron and four-proton quasiparticle configurations for the first time, projecting good angular-momentum states from triaxial intrinsic states using a three-dimensional projection operator. They diagonalized a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian within this basis, using deformation parameters \(\epsilon=0.278\) and \(\epsilon'=0.105\) (corresponding to \(\beta=0.29\) and \(\gamma=20.6^\circ\)), and compared the resulting energies, alignments, and \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities with experimental data. Additionally, they performed cranked shell model (CSM) calculations with an axial Nilsson potential and monopole pairing, and applied the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) to the rotating mean-field to describe the \(\gamma\)-vibrations.

## Key numbers

*   The band-head of the \(\gamma\)-band is at an excitation energy of 0.98 MeV from the ground-state, and the \(\gamma\gamma\)-band lies at 2.13 MeV.
*   The ground-state band is crossed by the signature \(\alpha=0\) branch of the two-quasineutron aligned configuration (the s-band) at spin I=14.
*   The CSM crossing frequency between the ground-state band and the s-band is \(\hbar \omega = 0.23\) MeV, which underestimates the experimental value of \(\hbar \omega = 0.28\) MeV.
*   The calculated \(B(E2)\) values for the ground-state band show dips at spin I=18\(\hbar\) and I=24\(\hbar\), due to crossings with two-proton and four-neutron aligned configurations, respectively.
*   The staggering parameter \(S(I)\) for the \(\gamma\)-band is small at low spin and increases above I=10, with even-spin states lying below odd-spin states, a pattern reproduced by the TPSM calculations.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Majola et al. (2014) on high-spin band structures in \(^{156}\)Dy, providing a theoretical foundation for the proposed \(\gamma\)-bands built on quasiparticle excitations. It builds on the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach developed by Sheikh et al. (1999) and the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) applied to rotating nuclei by Shimizu et al. (1982, 1983, 1984) and others. The results enable further studies of \(\gamma\)-vibrational bands in transitional and deformed nuclei, particularly at high spins where multi-quasiparticle configurations become important, and suggest that g-factor measurements are needed to probe the intrinsic structures of the observed bands.
