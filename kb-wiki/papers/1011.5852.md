# Convergence of a renormalization group approach to dimer-dimer scattering

> We study the convergence of a functional renormalisation group technique by looking at the ratio between the fermion-fermion scattering length and the dimer-dimer scattering length for a system of nonrelativistic fermions. We find that in a systematic expansion in powers of the fields there is a rapid convergence of the result that agrees with know exact results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a functional renormalisation group (ERG) expansion in powers of fields and gradients converges rapidly for the dimer-dimer scattering length, yielding a result that agrees with the exact Schrödinger-equation value, provided all local four-body terms are included and the cutoff parameter is chosen near unity.

## Method

The authors apply a functional renormalisation group to a system of nonrelativistic fermions with a zero-range interaction, bosonised via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a dimer field. They parametrise the scale-dependent effective action as a local expansion in fermion and dimer fields, including boson self-energy, dimer-dimer coupling, fermion-dimer coupling, and two four-body terms, and solve the resulting coupled differential equations for the couplings as a function of the RG scale \(k\). The evolution is integrated from a large initial scale down to \(k=0\), using both a sharp cutoff (Litim 2001) and a smooth error-function cutoff, with a relative scale parameter \(c_B\) controlling the bosonic regulator.

## Key numbers

* The exact ratio of dimer-dimer to fermion-fermion scattering lengths is \(a_B = 0.6 a_F\) (Shlyapnikov 1994).
* The mean-field (boson-loop neglected) result gives \(a_B = 2 a_F\).
* With the full four-body truncation, the authors obtain \(a_B/a_F = 0.58 \pm 0.02\), in excellent agreement with the exact value.
* The fixed-point value for the dimensionless dimer-dimer coupling in the minimal truncation is \(c_0 = 0.0656565\); with three-body term added it becomes \(c_0 = 0.037878\); with all four-body terms it is \(c_0 = 0.0356522\).
* The anomalous dimension for the three-body coupling \(\lambda\) is \(\eta_\lambda \approx 3.10355\), compared to the exact result \(4.32244\) (Griesshammer 2004, Birse 2006, Weidenmüller 2006).
* The lowest anomalous dimension for the four-fermion sector is \(4.19149\), compared to the numerical value \(5.0184\) (Stecher and Greene 2009).

## Context

This work extends the functional RG approach of Krippa (2004), Diehl (2007), and Birse (2008) to the four-body sector, where previous mean-field treatments gave only \(a_B = 2a_F\) (Haussmann 1993). The paper shows that including all local four-body terms (dimer-dimer, fermion-dimer, and two mixed four-fermion/dimer couplings) yields convergence to the exact Schrödinger result of Shlyapnikov (1994). The method is designed to be extendable to many-body systems at finite density, where the exact few-body solution is not available.
