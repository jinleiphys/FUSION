# Neutron star properties in density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory

> With the equations of state provided by the newly developed density dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory for hadronic matter, the properties of the static and $β$-equilibrium neutron stars without hyperons are studied for the first time, and compared to the predictions of the relativistic mean field (RMF) models and recent observational data. The influences of Fock terms on properties of asymmetric nuclear matter at high densities are discussed in details. Because of the significant contributions from the $σ$- and $ω$-exchange terms to the symmetry energy, large proton fractions in neutron stars are predicted by the DDRHF calculations, which strongly affect the cooling process of the star. The critical mass about 1.45 $M_\odot$, close to the limit 1.5 $M_\odot$ determined by the modern soft X-ray data analysis, is obtained by DDRHF with the effective interactions PKO2 and PKO3 for the occurrence of direct Urca process in neutron stars. The maximum masses of neutron stars given by the DDRHF calculations lie between 2.45 M$_\odot$ and 2.49 M$_\odot$, which are in reasonable agreement with high pulsar mass $2.08 \pm 0.19 M_\odot$ from PSR B1516+02B. It is also found that the mass-radius relations of neutron stars determined by DDRHF are consistent with the observational data from thermal radiation measurement in the isolated neutron star RX J1856, QPOs frequency limits in LMXBs 4U 0614+09 and 4U 1636-536, and redshift determined in LMXBs EXO 0748-676.

## Key claim

The density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory, applied for the first time to static, β-equilibrium neutron stars without hyperons, predicts maximum neutron star masses between 2.45 and 2.49 solar masses and a critical mass of about 1.45 solar masses for the onset of the direct Urca process, with the latter being close to the 1.5 solar mass limit from soft X-ray data analysis, but these results depend on the specific effective interaction (PKO1, PKO2, or PKO3) and the exclusion of hyperon degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations for static, spherically symmetric neutron stars using equations of state (EoS) from the DDRHF theory, which includes Fock exchange terms from σ, ω, ρ, and π meson exchanges with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings. The neutron star matter was modeled as β-equilibrium nuclear matter composed of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons (no hyperons), with the BPS and BBP models providing the EoS at low densities (ρ_b < 0.08 fm⁻³). The DDRHF results were compared to those from relativistic mean field (RMF) models (including nonlinear self-coupling interactions GL-97, NL1, NL3, NLSH, TM1, PK1 and density-dependent ones TW99, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, PKDD) and to observational data from pulsars and low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs).

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses from DDRHF calculations: between 2.45 M_⊙ and 2.49 M_⊙, compared to the observed pulsar mass 2.08 ± 0.19 M_⊙ from PSR B1516+02B
- Critical mass for direct Urca process onset in DDRHF with PKO2 and PKO3: about 1.45 M_⊙, close to the limit 1.5 M_⊙ from modern soft X-ray data analysis
- Saturation density ρ_0 from DDRHF: 0.1510–0.1530 fm⁻³ (PKO1: 0.1520, PKO2: 0.1510, PKO3: 0.1530)
- Binding energy per particle at saturation from DDRHF: −15.996 to −16.041 MeV
- Incompressibility K from DDRHF: 249.6–262.5 MeV (PKO1: 250.2, PKO2: 249.6, PKO3: 262.5)
- Symmetry energy J at saturation from DDRHF: 32.49–34.37 MeV (PKO1: 34.371, PKO2: 32.492, PKO3: 32.987)
- Scalar mass M_S*/M at saturation from DDRHF: 0.586–0.603 (PKO1: 0.5900, PKO2: 0.6025, PKO3: 0.5862)

## Context

This work extends the DDRHF theory, previously developed for nuclear matter and finite nuclei by Long et al. (2006, 2007, 2008), to the study of neutron star properties for the first time, contrasting with the extensive RMF-based neutron star calculations by Hofmann et al. (2001) and Ban et al. (2004) that neglected Fock terms. The results enable comparisons with observational constraints from pulsar mass measurements (e.g., PSR B1516+02B by Freire et al. 2007), thermal radiation from RX J1856 (Trumper et al. 2003), QPO frequency limits in LMXBs 4U 0614+09 and 4U 1636-536 (Miller et al. 2004, Barret et al. 2005), and redshift measurements in EXO 0748-676 (Cottam et al. 2002, Ozel et al. 2006), while also providing a baseline for future studies including hyperons or other exotic degrees of freedom.
