# Response Function of Hot Nuclear Matter

> We investigate the response function of hot nuclear matter to a small isovector external field using a simplified Skyrme interaction reproducing the value of the symmetry energy coefficient. We consider values of the momentum transfer corresponding to the dipole oscillation in heavy nuclei. We find that while at zero temperature the particle hole interaction is almost repulsive enough to have a sharp (zero sound type) collective oscillation, such is no longer the case at temperatures of a few MeV. As a result a broadening of the dipole resonance occurs, leading to its quasi disappearence by the time the temperature reaches 5 MeV. The sensivity of the temperature evolution of the width when modifying the residual interaction strength is also examined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the rapid broadening of the giant dipole resonance with temperature in hot nuclei arises because the isovector particle-hole residual interaction, while almost strong enough to produce a sharp zero-sound collective mode at zero temperature, becomes insufficient to sustain such a mode at temperatures of a few MeV, leading to a quasi-disappearance of the resonance by 5 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculated the retarded response function of symmetric nuclear matter to a small isovector external field within the random phase approximation (RPA), using a simplified Skyrme interaction (with parameters \(t_0\), \(t_3\), and \(x_0\)) fitted to reproduce the nuclear matter saturation density, binding energy, and symmetry energy. They evaluated the unperturbed Lindhard function at finite temperature and solved the RPA dispersion relation for a momentum transfer \(q = \pi/(2R)\) corresponding to the dipole oscillation in lead-208 (\(R = 6.7\) fm). The strength function \(S(\omega)\) was then computed from the imaginary part of the RPA response function.

## Key numbers

*   The momentum transfer used to model the dipole resonance in lead-208 was \(q = 0.23\) fm\(^{-1}\), derived from a nuclear radius \(R = 6.7\) fm.
*   The residual interaction strength parameter was \(V_0 = 203\) MeV fm\(^3\), obtained from the Skyrme parameters that reproduce a symmetry energy coefficient \(a_{\tau} = 30\) MeV.
*   At zero temperature, the peak in the strength function was located at \(\omega = 14\) MeV, in close agreement with the observed giant dipole resonance energy.
*   The collective oscillation was found to have quasi-disappeared by the time the temperature reached 5 MeV.
*   A weaker residual interaction with \(V_0 = 100\) MeV fm\(^3\) was also examined, showing a slower evolution of the width with temperature.

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA calculations of giant resonances in hot nuclei, specifically addressing the discrepancy between the rapid width increase found in some early RPA calculations using oscillator functions (Vautherin and Vinh Mau, 1984) and the negligible increase found in self-consistent RPA calculations including continuum states (Sagawa and Bertsch, 1984). The paper provides a transparent analytical explanation for this sensitivity, showing that the temperature evolution of the width depends critically on how close the residual interaction is to the threshold for zero sound. The results enable a better understanding of the damping mechanisms of the giant dipole resonance and suggest that the coupling to two particle-two hole states, as noted by Adachi and Yoshida (1984), remains important for a complete description.
