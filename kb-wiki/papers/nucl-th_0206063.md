# Effects of the difference between the charge and matter deformations on fusion reactions of unstable nuclei

> Relativistic mean field calculations suggest that the charge and matter deformations significantly differ in some of the unstable neutron and proton rich nuclei. We discuss the effects of the difference on the fusion reactions induced by them at energies near and below the Coulomb barrier by taking the $^{19,25,37}$Na + $^{208}$Pb reactions as examples. We also discuss whether one can probe the difference by the so called fusion barrier distribution analysis.

## Key claim

Relativistic mean field calculations suggest that the charge and matter deformations can significantly differ for some unstable neutron- and proton-rich nuclei, and this difference can be probed through fusion reactions near and below the Coulomb barrier, but only for certain isotopes (e.g., \(^{19,37}\)Na) and not for others (e.g., \(^{25}\)Na) where the effect is compensated by differences in charge and matter radii.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations with NLSH parameters (ignoring pairing correlations) to obtain the quadrupole deformation parameters and root-mean-square radii for the proton, neutron, matter, and charge distributions of Na isotopes. They then used a coupled-channels formalism in the no-Coriolis (rotating frame) approximation, including the ground-state rotational band of the deformed odd-mass Na projectile (up to \(I_{\text{max}} = 7/2\)) and assuming \(^{208}\)Pb as a structureless target, to calculate fusion cross sections and barrier distributions for \(^{19,25,37}\)Na + \(^{208}\)Pb reactions.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{19,25,37}\)Na + \(^{208}\)Pb, the potential parameters used were \((V_0, a_0) = (114.2\ \text{MeV}, 1.222\ \text{fm})\).
- The fusion barrier distribution was calculated using a point-difference formula with \(\Delta E = 2\ \text{MeV}\) in the center-of-mass energy.
- The moment of inertia was assumed as \(\mathcal{J} \approx \mathcal{J}_{\text{rig}}/2\) for the main calculations, and also tested with \(\mathcal{J} \approx \mathcal{J}_{\text{rig}}/3\) for \(^{19,37}\)Na + \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The rigid-body moment of inertia was given by \(\mathcal{J}_{\text{rig}} = \frac{2}{5} A M R^2 (1 + \frac{1}{3}\delta)\), with \(\delta\) related to the quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta_2\) via \(\delta = 0.945\beta_2[1 - \frac{4}{3}\pi^2(a_0/R_P)^2] + 0.34\beta_2^2\).

## Context

This work extends the use of heavy-ion fusion reactions below and near the Coulomb barrier as a probe of nuclear shapes, previously established for stable nuclei (e.g., by Balantekin and Takigawa (1998) and Dasgupta et al. (1998)), to unstable nuclei where charge and matter deformations may differ. The authors use RMF calculations (following Gambhir et al. (1990) and Sugahara and Toki (1994)) to predict deformations for Na isotopes, and apply the fusion barrier distribution method (introduced by Rowley et al. (1991)) to assess whether the difference can be experimentally observed. The results suggest that \(^{19,37}\)Na + \(^{208}\)Pb reactions are promising candidates, while \(^{25}\)Na + \(^{208}\)Pb is not due to compensating effects, and the authors note that including pairing correlations (as in Patra (1993)) would improve the reliability of the deformation parameters.
