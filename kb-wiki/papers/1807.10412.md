# Production mechanism of neutron-rich nuclei around $N=126$ in multi-nucleon transfer reaction $^{132}$Sn + $^{208}$Pb

> Time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach in three dimensions is employed to study the multi-nucleon transfer reaction $^{132}$Sn + $^{208}$Pb at various incident energies above the Coulomb barrier. Probabilities for different transfer channels are calculated by using particle-number projection method. The results indicate that neutron stripping (transfer from the projectile to the target) and proton pick-up (transfer from the target to the projectile) are favored. Deexcitation of the primary fragments are treated by using the state-of-art statistical code GEMINI++. Primary and final production cross sections of the target-like fragments (with $Z=77$ to $Z=87$) are investigated. The results reveal that fission decay of heavy nuclei plays an important role in the deexcitation process of nuclei with $Z>82$. It is also found that the final production cross sections of neutron-rich (deficient) nuclei slightly (strongly) depend on the incident energy.

# Production mechanism of neutron-rich nuclei around $N=126$ in multi-nucleon transfer reaction $^{132}$Sn + $^{208}$Pb

## Key claim
Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations combined with the GEMINI++ statistical deexcitation code predict that neutron stripping (transfer from projectile to target) and proton pick-up (transfer from target to projectile) are favored in the $^{132}$Sn + $^{208}$Pb reaction, and that the final production cross sections of neutron-rich nuclei depend only slightly on incident energy, while those of neutron-deficient nuclei depend strongly on it. The authors note that mean-field models underestimate distribution widths due to the lack of nucleon-nucleon correlations, and that pairing effects should be included as a next step.

## Method
Three-dimensional TDHF calculations with the Skyrme SLy6 parametrization were performed for $^{132}$Sn + $^{208}$Pb at three center-of-mass energies (470, 550, and 800 MeV) above the Coulomb barrier, using the Sky3D code. Probabilities for different neutron and proton transfer channels were obtained via the particle-number projection method applied to the TDHF final wave functions. Primary fragments were then deexcited using the statistical code GEMINI++, with excitation energies and angular momenta taken from the TDHF results, to obtain final production cross sections for target-like fragments with atomic numbers Z=77 to Z=87.

## Key numbers
- Incident energies: $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 470$ MeV (1.2 times the Bass barrier), 550 MeV (1.4 times), and 800 MeV (2.0 times); the Bass barrier is approximately 400 MeV.
- Impact parameter ranges: $b_{\text{max}} = 10$ fm (470 MeV), 12 fm (550 MeV), and 13 fm (800 MeV), with interval $\Delta b = 1$ fm.
- Total kinetic energy of outgoing fragments in deep-inelastic collisions is kept around 340 MeV for all three energies, about 30 MeV above the Viola systematics.
- For Z=77 at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 470$ MeV, deexcitation mode fractions are: neutron evaporation 99.88%, light charged particle emission 0.1%, fission 0.02%; for Z=87 at the same energy: neutron evaporation 70%, light charged particle emission 0.8%, fission 29.2%.
- For Z=77 at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 800$ MeV: neutron evaporation 16.7%, light charged particle emission 75.9%, fission 7.4%; for Z=87 at 800 MeV: neutron evaporation 0%, light charged particle emission 27.8%, fission 72.2%.
- The largest cross section of francium (Z=87) at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 800$ MeV is larger than those at the two lower energies by 2 to 3 orders of magnitude.

## Context
This work extends microscopic TDHF calculations of multi-nucleon transfer reactions, previously applied to asymmetric systems by Wangning (2016) and others, to the specific case of $^{132}$Sn + $^{208}$Pb aimed at producing neutron-rich nuclei near the $N=126$ shell closure. The combination of TDHF with GEMINI++ follows the approach used by Sekizawa (2017), Umar (2017), and Li (2018). The results are compared with predictions from the dinuclear system model of Feng (2017) and Zhu (2017), and the observed weak energy dependence of neutron-rich yields and strong energy dependence of neutron-deficient yields is consistent with those earlier DNS calculations. The work enables experimental guidance for producing neutron-rich nuclei around $N=126$, the last waiting point of the astrophysical r-process, and identifies the need to include pairing correlations in future TDHF studies.
