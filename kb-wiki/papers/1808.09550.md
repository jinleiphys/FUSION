# Novel physics opportunities at the HESR-Collider with PANDA at FAIR

> Exciting new scientific opportunities are presented for the PANDA detector at the High Energy Storage Ring in the redefined $\bar{\text{p}} \text{p}(A)$ collider mode, HESR-C, at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) in Europe. The high luminosity, $L \sim 10^{31}$ cm$^{-2}$ s$^{-1}$, and a wide range of intermediate and high energies, $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$ up to 30 GeV for $\bar{\text{p}} \text{p}(A)$ collisions will allow to explore a wide range of exciting topics in QCD, including the study of the production of excited open charm and bottom states, nuclear bound states containing heavy (anti)quarks, the interplay of hard and soft physics in the dilepton production, and the exploration of the regime where gluons -- but not quarks -- experience strong interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a future collider mode of the HESR ring (HESR-C) with the PANDA detector at FAIR, operating at center-of-mass energies up to 30 GeV and luminosities up to 5 × 10³¹ cm⁻² s⁻¹, would enable unique studies of heavy quark physics and QCD that are not accessible at LHC energies, though the authors acknowledge that some proposed measurements (such as hidden beauty resonance production) may be too low-rate to observe without higher luminosity upgrades.

## Method

The authors present a conceptual design for converting the HESR fixed-target ring into a symmetric proton-antiproton collider, requiring a second proton injection line, the RESR storage ring for antiproton accumulation, and an 8 MeV electron cooler. They then perform rate estimates for heavy quark production using perturbative QCD calculations with threshold resummation, and conduct (2+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with a time-dependent equation of state that interpolates between pure Yang-Mills lattice QCD and full (2+1)-flavor QCD to model the pure glue scenario in small collision systems.

## Key numbers

- Luminosity: L ∼ 10³¹ cm⁻² s⁻¹, with a conservative startup estimate of 4 × 10³⁰ cm⁻² s⁻¹ and a possible upgrade to 5 × 10³¹ cm⁻² s⁻¹ at √s ≈ 30 GeV
- Center-of-mass energy: √s up to 30 GeV for pp collisions, √s_NN up to 19 GeV for pA collisions
- Bottom pair production cross section: σ_bb = 1.8 × 10⁻² μb at √s = 30 GeV, with 30% relative uncertainty
- Charm pair production cross section: σ_cc = 30 μb
- Yearly event rates at L = 4 × 10³⁰ cm⁻² s⁻¹: N_bb = 10⁶, N_cc = 10⁹
- Yearly events with two cc pairs: N_cc,cc = 10⁶
- Expected events per year with b b c c: about 10³
- For bbq baryon observation: need luminosity of 10³³ cm⁻² s⁻¹ to produce ∼10² b b b b pairs per year
- Critical temperature for pure glue matter: T_c = 270 MeV from lattice QCD
- Initial temperature in hydrodynamic simulations: 273 MeV in central cell
- Hard-sphere initial radius: R = 0.6 fm
- Quark equilibration times studied: τ_* = 0, 1, 5, 10 fm/c, and infinite (pure gluodynamic)

## Context

This paper extends the physics case for the PANDA experiment at FAIR beyond the fixed-target mode (√s < 6 GeV) into a proposed collider mode, building on earlier design studies by Bradamante (2005), Lehrach (2005), and Stocker (2015). The work complements heavy quark spectroscopy programs at LHC and e⁺e⁻ colliders by offering access to near-threshold production of b-quark states, where the authors argue that pentaquark and tetraquark formation probabilities are enhanced relative to LHC energies. The pure glue scenario extends ideas by Van Hove (1974) and Shuryak (1992) about two-step equilibration of quark-gluon plasma, and the hydrodynamic modeling uses lattice QCD equations of state from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2012, 2013). The paper enables future experimental proposals for the HESR-C collider configuration and motivates detector modifications for PANDA.
