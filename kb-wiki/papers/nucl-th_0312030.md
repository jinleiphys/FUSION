# The complete experiment for $e^{\mp}N$ elastic scattering in presence of two-photon exchange

> We propose possible methods to measure the nucleon electromagnetic form factors in presence of two-photon exchange. Using a general parametrization of the spin structure of the matrix element for elastic $eN$-scattering, in terms of three independent complex amplitudes, we show that the measurements of the differential cross section for electron and positron nucleon elastic scattering, in the same kinematical conditions, allows to extract the nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The same is correct for the polarization method, with the measurement of the $P_{x,z}$ components of the final nucleon polarization (for the scattering of longitudinally polarized electrons and positrons). An alternative way, in absence of positron beam, is to measure a definite set of T-odd polarization observables, which includes three different experiments or only T-even observables, with five independent experiments. In both cases, the ratio $G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)$ is related to quantities of the order of $α$, which requires different polarization experiments of very high accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that nucleon electromagnetic form factors can be extracted in the presence of two-photon exchange contributions, but only if one measures specific combinations of observables; the ratio \(G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)\) is related to quantities of order \(\alpha\) (the fine-structure constant), requiring polarization experiments of very high accuracy.

## Method

The authors use a general parametrization of the spin structure of the elastic \(eN\)-scattering matrix element in terms of three independent complex amplitudes, based on P-invariance and relativistic invariance. They analyze how C-invariance and crossing symmetry constrain the amplitudes for electron and positron scattering, and derive expressions for differential cross sections and polarization observables (including T-odd and T-even quantities) in the presence of one-photon and two-photon exchange interference. The formalism is applied to propose three distinct experimental strategies: (1) comparing \(e^-N\) and \(e^+N\) scattering cross sections and polarizations, (2) measuring T-odd polarization observables in \(e^-N\) scattering alone, and (3) measuring a set of five T-even polarization observables in \(e^-N\) scattering.

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)\) is related to quantities of order \(\alpha\) (the fine-structure constant), requiring percent-level precision in polarization experiments.
* The asymmetry \({\cal A}^{(e)}\) from transversally polarized leptons is proportional to \(m_e/m \simeq 10^{-3}\), where \(m_e\) is the electron mass and \(m\) is the nucleon mass.
* The cross section at HERA energies is larger than at JLab by a factor of \(22\) to \(49\), based on the \(E^2\)-dependence of \(d\sigma/dQ^2\) at fixed \(Q^2\).
* Two-photon contributions are expected to become observable in \(eN\)-scattering at \(Q^2 \simeq 10\) GeV\(^2\), based on analysis of deuteron data up to \(Q^2 = 6\) GeV\(^2\) from JLab (Al99, Ab99).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of two-photon exchange effects in electron-deuteron scattering (Rekalo 1999) to the nucleon case, and addresses the discrepancy between Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer measurements of the proton form factor ratio (Ar75, Jo00). The paper provides a model-independent framework for extracting the physical form factors \(G_E(Q^2)\) and \(G_M(Q^2)\) despite two-photon contamination, and suggests that the discrepancy noted by Br03 could be resolved through dedicated experiments. The proposed methods enable future measurements at JLab or HERA to determine the nucleon electromagnetic form factors without relying on assumptions about the two-photon mechanism.
