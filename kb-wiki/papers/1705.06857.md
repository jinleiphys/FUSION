# Constraining the $\bar{K}N$ interaction from the $1S$ level shift of kaonic deuterium

> Motivated by the precise measurement of the $1S$ level shift of kaonic hydrogen, we perform accurate three-body calculations for the spectrum of kaonic deuterium using a realistic antikaon-nucleon ($KN$) interaction. In order to describe both short- and long-range behavior of the kaonic atomic states, we solve the three-body Schrödinger equation with a superposition of a large number of correlated Gaussian basis functions covering distances up to several hundreds of fm. Transition energies between $1S$, $2P$ and $2S$ states are determined with high precision. The complex energy shift of the $1S$ level of kaonic deuterium is found to be $670-i508$ eV. The sensitivity of this level shift with respect to the isospin $I=1$ component of the $KN$ interaction is examined. It is pointed out that an experimental determination of the kaonic deuterium level shift within an uncertainty of 25\% will provide a constraint for the $I=1$ component of the $KN$ interaction significantly stronger than that from kaonic hydrogen.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the 1S level shift of kaonic deuterium is 670-i508 eV, and they argue that an experimental determination of this shift within 25% uncertainty would provide a significantly stronger constraint on the isospin I=1 component of the antikaon-nucleon interaction than existing kaonic hydrogen data, because the I=1 component is currently only weakly constrained (the control parameter beta can range from -0.17 to 1.08 within SIDDHARTA constraints).

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrodinger equation for the K- p n and anti-K0 n n coupled channels using a variational method with correlated Gaussian basis functions covering distances from 0.1 fm to several hundred fm. They employed the Kyoto antikaon-nucleon potential, which is based on chiral SU(3) effective field theory and reproduces low-energy antikaon-nucleon data with chi-squared per degree of freedom approximately 1, and the Minnesota potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The energy dependence of the antikaon-nucleon potential was set to zero at threshold, which was justified by showing that self-consistent and fixed-energy calculations give identical results for kaonic hydrogen.

## Key numbers

- The 1S level shift of kaonic deuterium is predicted as Delta E - i Gamma/2 = (670 - i 508) eV, corresponding to (Delta E, Gamma) = (670, 1016) eV using the Kyoto antikaon-nucleon potential
- The 2P state energy remains unchanged at -2.602 keV with decay width less than 1 eV, confirming it is unaffected by the strong interaction
- The 2S state shift is an order of magnitude smaller than the 1S shift, at -2.517 - i 0.067 keV
- Varying the I=1 strength parameter beta from -0.17 to 1.08 changes the kaonic deuterium shift Delta E by 170 eV (from 506 to 676 eV) while Gamma remains around 1 keV
- The planned experiments at J-PARC E57 and SIDDHARTA-2 expect precisions of 60 eV and 30 eV respectively for the shift, and 140 eV and 70 eV for the width
- Two-nucleon absorption contributes only about 10 eV to the width, based on the BNL bubble chamber measurement of (1.2 plus or minus 0.1) percent for the ratio of two-nucleon to single-nucleon absorption

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of kaonic deuterium that used approximate two-body treatments (Meissner 2006, Gal 2007, Doring 2011) and Faddeev calculations with separable potentials (Bahaoui 2003, recent works by Revai and Dolinska). The authors use the Kyoto antikaon-nucleon potential (Hyodo and Weise 2012) which reproduces the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data (283 plus or minus 36 plus or minus 6 eV shift, 541 plus or minus 89 plus or minus 22 eV width). The results enable improved constraints on the I=1 component of the antikaon-nucleon interaction, which remains poorly determined from kaonic hydrogen alone, and will be directly testable by the upcoming J-PARC E57 and SIDDHARTA-2 experiments.
