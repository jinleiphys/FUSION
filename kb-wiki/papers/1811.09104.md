# Nucleon Parton Distribution Amplitude: A scalar diquark picture

> We report progress on the development of Perturbative Integral Representation Ansätze to compute the nucleon Faddeev Wave function, using an explicit quark-diquark picture. Our formalism is able to handle non-pointlike diquark and to mimic the strong, dynamical correlations observed when solving the Faddeev Equation. We then project the wave function in order to compute the leading-twist Parton Distribution Amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper reports progress on computing the nucleon leading-twist parton distribution amplitude (PDA) using a scalar diquark picture within a Perturbative Integral Representation Ansatz (PTIR) framework, but the results are preliminary and limited to the scalar diquark component only, with the axial-vector diquark contributions and the first radial excitation (Roper resonance) left for future work.

## Method

The authors model the nucleon Faddeev amplitude using a quark-diquark picture with scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations, then compute Mellin moments of the leading-twist PDA by projecting the Faddeev wave function. They employ PTIR to model the quark propagator, diquark correlation vertex, diquark propagator, and quark-diquark Faddeev amplitude, using algebraic forms with parameters fitted to reproduce the first four Chebychev moments from realistic numerical solutions of the Faddeev equation (Segovia 2015). The scalar diquark PDA is computed analytically for the case where the quark mass equals the diquark correlation scale, and the full scalar diquark contribution to the nucleon PDA is obtained by convolution with the quark-diquark amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The scalar diquark PDA parameters (in units of nucleon mass): quark mass M = 2/5, diquark mass M0 = 9/10, diquark correlation scale Λ_Γ = 3/5, Faddeev amplitude scale Λ0 = 6/5.
- In the limit y = M²/K² ≫ 1 (K² ≪ Λ_Γ²), the scalar diquark PDA approaches the asymptotic form φ0(hat x2, hat x3) → 6 hat x2 hat x3.
- In the limit y ≪ 1 (K² ≫ Λ_Γ²), the scalar diquark PDA approaches φ(hat x2, hat x3) → 1, corresponding to a pointlike particle.
- The endpoint behavior of the scalar diquark PDA is linear, independent of y.

## Context

This work extends the continuum QCD approach to nucleon structure using PTIR, which has been successfully applied to meson PDAs, PDFs, and GPDs (Chang 2013, Mezrag 2014, Mezrag 2016). It builds on the borromean picture of the nucleon from Faddeev equation studies (Segovia 2015) and improves upon the authors' previous work (Mezrag 2017). The results enable future computation of the full nucleon PDA including axial-vector diquark contributions and extension to the Roper resonance, providing input for exclusive processes at large momentum transfer.
