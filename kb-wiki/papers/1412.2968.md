# Hydrodynamic transport coefficients for the non-conformal quark-gluon plasma from holography

> In this paper we obtain holographic formulas for the transport coefficients $κ$ and $τ_π$ present in the second-order derivative expansion of relativistic hydrodynamics in curved spacetime associated with a non-conformal strongly coupled plasma described holographically by an Einstein+Scalar action in the bulk. We compute these coefficients as functions of the temperature in a bottom-up non-conformal model that is tuned to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryon chemical potential. We directly compute, besides the speed of sound, 6 other transport coefficients that appear at second-order in the derivative expansion. We also give an estimate for the temperature dependence of 11 other transport coefficients taking into account the simplest contribution from non-conformal effects that appear near the QCD crossover phase transition. Using these results, we construct an Israel-Stewart-like theory in flat spacetime containing 13 of these 17 transport coefficients that should be suitable for phenomenological applications in the context of numerical hydrodynamic simulations of the strongly-coupled, non-conformal quark-gluon plasma. Using several different approximations, we give parametrizations for the temperature dependence of all the second-order transport coefficients that appear in this theory in a format that can be easily implemented in existing numerical hydrodynamic codes.

## Key claim

The paper derives holographic formulas for the second-order hydrodynamic transport coefficients κ and τπ in a non-conformal strongly coupled plasma, but the direct numerical computation is performed only for 6 of the 17 second-order coefficients; the temperature dependence of the remaining 11 coefficients is estimated using the simplest non-conformal contributions near the QCD crossover, not from a full holographic calculation.

## Method

The authors use a bottom-up Einstein-Scalar holographic model in 5-dimensional asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime, tuned to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryon chemical potential. They compute the second-order transport coefficients κ and τπ via Kubo formulas derived from the retarded Green's function of the stress-energy tensor, solving the linearized equation of motion for a metric perturbation in the small-frequency, small-momentum limit. The temperature dependence of 6 second-order coefficients (including the speed of sound) is obtained directly from the holographic model, while the remaining 11 coefficients are estimated by incorporating the simplest non-conformal corrections near the crossover transition.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 1/(4π) (the universal holographic bound), used as the baseline for the strongly coupled plasma.
- Speed of sound squared c_s² = dP/dε, computed as a function of temperature from the holographic model tuned to lattice QCD.
- The second-order coefficient κ is computed via the Kubo formula κ = -lim_{q→0} lim_{ω→0} ∂²G_R^{xy,xy}(ω,q)/∂q², with explicit temperature-dependent results shown in figures.
- The relaxation time τπ is computed via τπ = (1/(2η)) [lim_{q→0} lim_{ω→0} ∂²G_R^{xy,xy}(ω,q)/∂ω² - κ + T dκ/dT], with temperature-dependent results shown in figures.
- The paper provides parametrizations (fitting functions) for the temperature dependence of all 13 second-order transport coefficients that appear in the Israel-Stewart-like theory, suitable for implementation in numerical hydrodynamic codes.

## Context

This work extends the conformal second-order hydrodynamic framework of Baier et al. (2008) to non-conformal plasmas, using the gradient expansion formalism developed by Romatschke (2010) for curved spacetime. It provides the first holographic computation of the full set of second-order transport coefficients for a non-conformal QGP-like system, enabling more realistic numerical hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions that include temperature-dependent bulk viscosity and second-order corrections near the QCD crossover phase transition. The results can be directly implemented in existing numerical codes to improve phenomenological modeling of the quark-gluon plasma evolution.
