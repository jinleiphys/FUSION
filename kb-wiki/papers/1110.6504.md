# Formation of Heavy Meson Bound States by Two Nucleon Pick-up Reactions

> We develop a model to evaluate the formation rate of the heavy mesic nuclei in the two nucleon pick-up reactions, and apply it to the $^6$Li target cases for the formation of heavy meson-$α$ bound states, as examples. The existence of the quasi-deuteron in the target nucleus is assumed in this model. It is found that the mesic nuclei formation in the recoilless kinematics is possible even for heavier mesons than nucleon in the two nucleon pick-up reactions. We find the formation rate of the meson-$α$ bound states can be around half of the elementary cross sections at the recoilless kinematics with small distortions.

## Key claim

The authors develop a model for forming heavy meson-nucleus bound states via two-nucleon pick-up reactions, finding that recoilless kinematics is possible for mesons heavier than the nucleon, but only under the assumption that a quasi-deuteron exists in the target nucleus and that the target has a simple cluster structure (such as the alpha-plus-deuteron configuration in 6Li).

## Method

The authors use an effective number approach, where the formation cross section is factorized into an elementary meson-production cross section on a deuteron target and an effective number Neff that encodes the nuclear structure and reaction kinematics. They model 6Li as a bound state of an alpha particle and a deuteron, solving the Schrodinger equation with a Woods-Saxon potential to obtain the deuteron-alpha relative wavefunction, and solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a complex optical potential to obtain the meson-alpha bound state wavefunctions. The effective number is then calculated as the overlap integral of the incident and emitted particle plane waves with the deuteron and meson wavefunctions, evaluated as a function of momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

* The two-nucleon separation energy from 6Li is fixed to S2N = 1.47 MeV, derived from the mass difference (M_alpha + M_d) - M_6Li.
* For the phi meson (mass 1020 MeV), three optical potential parameter sets are used: (V0, W0) = (-34.7, -7.5) MeV based on KEK E325 data (mass shift -3.4%, width 15 MeV); (-250, -5) MeV; and (-250, -20) MeV.
* For the shallow potential (-34.7, -7.5) MeV, the 1s phi-alpha bound state has binding energy 0.76 MeV and width 2.9 MeV.
* For the deep potential (-250, -5) MeV, the 2s state has binding energy 14.5 MeV and width 2.8 MeV; the 1s state has binding energy 131.5 MeV and width 8.1 MeV.
* The maximum effective number for the 2s bound state formation is Neff = 0.606, occurring at incident photon momentum p_gamma = 1.59 GeV/c (proton kinetic energy Tp = 2.66 GeV), meaning the formation cross section can be about half of the elementary cross section.
* The Woods-Saxon potential parameters for the deuteron-alpha relative wavefunction are R = 2.0 fm, a = 0.5 fm, and U0 = -75 MeV, with a normalization factor of 0.73 applied to match the momentum distribution from Ref. [6Li].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of meson-nucleus bound states formed via one-nucleon pick-up reactions (such as (d,3He) for pionic atoms, and (gamma,p) and (pi,N) reactions) to heavier mesons (eta-prime, phi, D mesons) where recoilless kinematics is impossible with one-nucleon pick-up. The two-nucleon pick-up reactions (gamma,d) and (p,3He) are proposed as alternatives, building on an earlier experimental attempt to observe eta-mesic states via the 27Al(p,3He) reaction at COSY-GEM (Budzanowski et al.). The model is designed to be simple and applicable to other two-nucleon pick-up reactions such as (pi,d), but requires target nuclei with a large quasi-deuteron component; the 6Li target is chosen for its well-known alpha-deuteron cluster structure. The results provide a guide for future experiments to extract meson binding energies and widths from the predicted simple spectral shapes.
