# Negative Parity N* Resonances in an Extended GBE

> In this paper, we calculate the masses and mixing angles of L=1 negative parity N* resonances in an extended GBE (Goldstone-boson-exchange model) with harmonic-oscillator wave functions. By using those mixing angles, we get their photoproduction amplitudes, and compare them with experimental data and the results of OPE (one-pion-exchange model), OPsE (only pseudoscalar meson exchange model), and OGE (one-gluon-exchange model). We find that the extended GBE gives right internal wave functions. It is essential to extend GBE to include not only pseudoscalar meson exchanges but also vector meson exchanges.

## Key claim

The extended Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) model, which includes both pseudoscalar and vector meson exchanges, yields mixing angles and photoproduction amplitudes for L=1 negative parity N* resonances that agree with experimental data, thereby resolving Isgur's critique that earlier one-pion-exchange (OPE) models gave incorrect internal wave functions.

## Method

The authors calculated masses and mixing angles for the four L=1 negative parity N* resonances (N(1520), N(1700), N(1535), N(1650)) using harmonic-oscillator wave functions and a hyperfine interaction from the extended GBE model that includes pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson exchanges. They then used these mixing angles to compute photoproduction amplitudes in the Breit frame, comparing the results against experimental data and against predictions from the one-pion-exchange (OPE), only-pseudoscalar-meson-exchange (OPsE), and one-gluon-exchange (OGE) models.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angles for the extended GBE at κ=1.2: θ_{1/2-} = -38° to -23°, θ_{3/2-} = 5° to 8° (compared to OPE: θ_{1/2-}=25.5°, θ_{3/2-}=-52.7°; OGE: θ_{1/2-}=-32°, θ_{3/2-}=6°; experimental: θ_{1/2-}=-32°, θ_{3/2-}=10°)
- Sum of χ² for twelve photoproduction amplitudes: extended GBE = 7.1–9.2, OPsE = 50–59, OGE = 11, OPE = 80
- Fitted masses for five L=1 N* resonances at κ=1.2: D13(1520) = 1562 MeV, D13(1700) = 1704 MeV, S11(1535) = 1543 MeV, S11(1650) = 1649 MeV, D15(1675) = 1662 MeV (experimental ranges from PDG: 1515–1530, 1650–1750, 1520–1555, 1640–1680, 1670–1685 MeV respectively)
- Quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 340 MeV; effective quark mass for photoproduction m* = 437 MeV

## Context

This work directly addresses Isgur's critique (2000) that the one-pion-exchange model gave wrong baryon internal wave functions, particularly for the N(1535) and N(1650) states where OPE predicted mixing angles (θ_{1/2-}=±13°) that could not explain the anomalously large Nη branching ratio of N(1535). The authors extend the earlier GBE model of Glozman and Riska (1996) by including vector meson exchanges as proposed by Wagenbrunn et al. (2001), and show that this extended GBE gives mixing angles close to those of the one-gluon-exchange model of Isgur and Karl (1977) and to experimental values, with a χ² sum of about 8 compared to 80 for OPE. This enables future work to use the extended GBE for reliable calculations of baryon decay amplitudes and internal structure.
