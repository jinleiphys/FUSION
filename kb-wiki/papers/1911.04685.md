# Pion Beta Decay and CKM Unitarity

> Pion beta decay, $π^+\to π^0 e^+ν(γ)$, provides a theoretically clean $\pm 0.3\%$ determination of the CKM matrix element $V_{\text{ud}}$. That value falls short of super-allowed nuclear beta decays where an order of magnitude better precision already exists. We advocate a new strategy for utilizing pion beta decay, based on its role in determining ${V_{\text{us}} / V_{\text{ud}}}$ via the ratio $R_V=Γ\left(K\to πl ν(γ)\right)/Γ\left(π^+\to π^0 e^+ν(γ)\right)$. $R_V$ measures $ { f_+^K(0) |V_{\text{us}}| / f_+^π(0) |V_{\text{ud}}| }$ and is insensitive to the Fermi constant and some radiative corrections. Its dependence on the ratio of form factors may also prove useful for lattice gauge theory calculations. Employing a lattice based value ${ f_+^K(0) / f_+^π(0) } = 0.970(2)$, we find $V_{\text{us}}/V_{\text{ud}}=0.22918(88)$ compared to $V_{\text{us}}/V_{\text{ud}}=0.23131(45)$ obtained from $R_A=Γ(K\to μν(γ))/Γ(π\to μν(γ))$. Those vector and axial-vector based $V_{\text{us}}/V_{\text{ud}}$ values exhibit a 2.2$σ$ discrepancy. That tension may be relieved by a shift in the lattice $ { f_+^K(0) / f_+^π(0) } $ towards the consistency range 0.961(4), changes in experimental input or new physics. Other implications of $R_V$ and $R_A$ are also discussed.

## Key claim

Pion beta decay provides a theoretically clean ±0.3% determination of Vud, but the paper argues its real value lies in forming the ratio R_V = Γ(K→πlν(γ))/Γ(π⁺→π⁰e⁺ν(γ)) to determine Vus/Vud, which is insensitive to the Fermi constant and some radiative corrections; the resulting Vus/Vud = 0.22910(91) from R_V using a lattice form factor ratio of 0.970(2) exhibits a 2.2σ discrepancy with the value 0.23131(45) obtained from the axial-vector ratio R_A.

## Method

The authors construct the ratio R_V of the K_L → πeν(γ) decay rate to the π⁺ → π⁰e⁺ν(γ) decay rate, where common short-distance electroweak and QED radiative corrections cancel. They extract Vus/Vud from the experimental R_V value by equating it to the theoretical expression containing the ratio of hadronic form factors f₊ᴷ(0)/f₊ᵖ(0) and the phase space integrals I_K and I_π. They then compare the resulting Vus/Vud with the value obtained from the analogous axial-vector ratio R_A = Γ(K→μν(γ))/Γ(π→μν(γ)), which uses the ratio of decay constants f_K⁺/f_π⁺.

## Key numbers

- Pion beta decay branching ratio: 1.038(6)×10⁻⁸
- Pion beta decay rate: Γ(π⁺→π⁰e⁺ν(γ)) = 0.3988(23) s⁻¹
- Vud from pion beta decay alone: |Vud| = 0.9739(29)
- Experimental value of R_V: 1.9884(115)(93)×10⁷
- From R_V with lattice f₊ᴷ(0)/f₊ᵖ(0) = 0.970(2): Vus/Vud = 0.22910(91)
- From R_A with lattice f_K⁺/f_π⁺ = 1.1932(19): Vus/Vud = 0.23131(45)
- Lattice form factor ratio needed for consistency: f₊ᴷ(0)/f₊ᵖ(0) = 0.961(4)
- Average of all K_l3 modes: f₊ᴷ(0)Vus / f₊ᵖ(0)Vud = 0.22223(64)(40)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of testing CKM unitarity using semileptonic decays, following the dispersion relation approach of Seng (2018) and the CMS calculation of Czarnecki (2019) that found 2-3σ deviations from unitarity in super-allowed nuclear beta decays. The paper introduces R_V as a vector-current analog to the well-established axial-vector ratio R_A from Marciano (2004), providing a complementary probe that cancels different systematic uncertainties. The results call for new experimental measurements of pion beta decay with factor 2-3 improvement in precision, and encourage lattice gauge theory studies to resolve the 2.2σ tension between the vector and axial-vector determinations of Vus/Vud.
