# Electrical conductivity and Hall conductivity of hot and dense quark gluon plasma in a magnetic field: a quasi particle approach

> We estimate here the electrical and Hall conductivity using a quasiparticle approach for quark matter. We use a Boltzmann kinetic approach in presence of external magnetic field. We confront the results of model calculations with Lattice QCD simulations for vanishing magnetic field. In general electrical conductivity decreases with magnetic field. The Hall conductivity on the other hand can show a non monotonic behaviour with magnetic field due to an intricate interplay of behaviour of relaxation time and strength of the magnetic field. We argue for vanishing quark chemical potential Hall conductivity vanishes and quark gluon plasma with finite quark chemical potential can show Hall effect. Both electrical conductivity and Hall conductivity increases with increasing quark chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper estimates electrical and Hall conductivity of quark-gluon plasma using a quasiparticle approach, finding that while electrical conductivity generally decreases with magnetic field, Hall conductivity can show non-monotonic behavior due to the interplay between relaxation time and magnetic field strength; the authors argue that Hall conductivity vanishes at zero quark chemical potential and only appears at finite chemical potential.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation for a uniform, static medium with transverse electric and magnetic fields. They employ two distinct quasiparticle models: QPM I, which uses temperature-dependent thermal masses for quarks and gluons with relaxation times derived from perturbative QCD, and QPM II (effective fugacity quasiparticle model), which modifies the equilibrium distribution functions through effective fugacities fitted to lattice QCD thermodynamics. Electrical and Hall conductivities are then computed from the resulting out-of-equilibrium distribution functions using Eqs. (15) and (16).

- Normalized electrical conductivity σ^{el}/T in QPM I is consistent with lattice QCD data from Amato et al., while QPM II results are consistent with lattice data from Gupta et al. (Fig. 1, no explicit numerical values given for the comparison)
- Relaxation time in QPM II is order of magnitude larger than in QPM I (Fig. 2, no explicit numerical values given)
- QCD transition temperature taken as T_c = 200 MeV
- Strange quark mass set to 80 MeV; light quark masses taken as 0.1 times the strange quark mass
- Fitting parameters for QPM II fugacities: gluon a_g = 0.803 ± 0.009, b_g = 1.837 ± 0.039, a'_g = 0.978 ± 0.007, b'_g = 0.942 ± 0.035; quark a_q = 0.810 ± 0.010, b_q = 1.721 ± 0.040, a'_q = 0.960 ± 0.007, b'_q = 0.846 ± 0.033
- The crossover temperature for fugacity parameterization: x_{q,g} ≡ T_{q,g}/T_c ∼ 1.70 for gluons and 1.68 for quarks

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in quark-gluon plasma, particularly electrical conductivity calculations using quasiparticle models (Plumari et al., Cassing et al.) and perturbative QCD approaches (Feng et al., 2017). The authors note that while Feng et al. (2017) estimated electrical and Hall conductivity using perturbative QCD, the strongly coupled nature of QGP formed in heavy-ion collisions motivates the quasiparticle approach used here. The study also builds on the authors' previous work on Hall conductivity in hadronic matter using the hadron resonance gas model (hmhall). This work enables more realistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon density, particularly relevant for future experiments at FAIR and NICA.
