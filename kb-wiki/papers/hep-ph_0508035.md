# Polymer Chains and Baryons in a Strongly Coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Recently there was a significant change of views on physical properties and underlying dynamics of Quark-Gluon Plasma at $T=170-350 MeV$, produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. Instead of being a gas of $q,g$ quasiparticles, a near-perfect liquid is observed. Also, precisely in this temperature interval, the interaction deduced from lattice studies is strong enough to support multiple binary bound states. This work is the first variational study of {\em multibody} bound states. We will consider: (i) ``polymer chains'' of the type $\bar q g g ..g q$; (ii) baryons $(qqq)$; (iii) closed (3-)chains of gluons $(ggg)$. We found that chains (i) form in exactly the same $T$ range as binary states, with the same binding {\em per bond}. The binding and $T$-range for diquarks, baryons and closed 3-chains are also established. We point out that the presence of chains, or possibly even a chain network, may drastically change the transport properties of matter, such as charm diffusion or jet energy loss. We further suggest that it seems to exist only for $T=(1-1.5)T_c$ and thus there may be a ``latent period'' for charm/jet quenching in RHIC collisions, while matter cools down to such $T$.

# Polymer Chains and Baryons in a Strongly Coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

This work is the first variational study of multibody bound states in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) at temperatures T = 170-350 MeV, showing that polymer chains of the form $\bar q g g ... g q$ form in exactly the same temperature range as binary bound states, with the same binding per bond, but the authors caution that these multibody states likely provide only a few percent correction to thermal properties unless unincluded effects increase binding by about a factor of two.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations using a parameterized temperature-dependent potential extracted from lattice data, with a trial wavefunction having asymptotic Coulomb behavior at short distances and free-particle behavior at large distances. For polymer chains, they proved that in a string-like interaction picture the problem splits into (n-1) independent two-body Schroedinger equations, giving the same binding energy per bond as mesonic states. For baryons and trigluon closed chains, they used a three-body trial wavefunction constructed as a product of pairwise diquark-type wavefunctions, with the potential strength adjusted by the appropriate Casimir factor (1/2 for diquark channels, 1 for meson/trigluon channels).

## Key numbers

- Meson ($\bar q q$) binding energy at $T_c$: -1.45 $T_c$, melting temperature 2.1 $T_c$
- Diquark ($qq$) binding energy at $T_c$: -0.13 $T_c$, melting temperature 1.4 $T_c$
- Baryon ($qqq$) binding energy at $T_c$: -1.10 $T_c$, melting temperature 1.6 $T_c$
- Trigluon closed chain ($ggg$) binding energy at $T_c$: -7.64 $T_c$, melting temperature 2.6 $T_c$
- Polymer chains have binding energy per bond equal to meson binding (-1.45 $T_c$ at $T_c$), with total binding $(N-1) \times E_b$ for an N-element chain
- The temperature range for existence of these multibody bound states is T = (1-1.5) $T_c$
- Quasiparticle mass used in calculations: $m^* = 800$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Shuryak and Zahed (2004) that the interaction in sQGP is strong enough to preserve meson-like bound states up to about 2 $T_c$, and that multiple binary colored bound states should exist in the same temperature domain. The authors build on lattice-based binary potentials from Shuryak and Zahed (2004) and apply them to multibody problems for the first time. The results suggest that polymer chains and baryons may drastically change transport properties such as charm diffusion and jet energy loss, and the authors propose a possible "latent period" for jet quenching in RHIC collisions while matter cools from initial temperatures of about 2 $T_c$ down to the polymer-dominated regime near 1.5 $T_c$, consistent with the timing proposed by Pantuev (2005) for switching on jet quenching.
