# J-Psi Suppression in Hadron-Nucleus Collisions

> We examine the production of J/$Ψ$ mesons in high energy hadron-nucleus collisions using models that account for both the initial state modification of parton distributions in nuclei and the final state interaction of the produced $c\bar c$ pairs. We show that, at the energies of current fixed target experiments, J/$Ψ$ production through quark-antiquark annihilation gives the largest contribution at $x_F > 0.5$, while gluon-gluon fusion dominates the production at smaller $x_F$. The observed J/$Ψ$ suppression at large $x_F$ is directly connected to nuclear shadowing in deeply inelastic scattering. We find that a $6\sim 8$mb $c\bar c$-nucleon cross section is needed to explain the data on J/$Ψ$ suppression if a simple incoherent multiple scattering formalism is used for the final state interactions.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of J/Ψ production at large Feynman xF in hadron-nucleus collisions can be explained by combining initial-state modification of parton distributions in nuclei (particularly shadowing of sea quarks) with final-state interactions of the produced cc̅ pairs, but the relative importance of these mechanisms depends on xF because quark-antiquark annihilation (not gluon-gluon fusion) dominates production at xF > 0.5.

## Method

The authors calculated the J/Ψ production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions using leading-order QCD parton model expressions, summing contributions from light quark-antiquark annihilation and gluon-gluon fusion. They parametrized nuclear quark distributions by fitting to NMC deep inelastic scattering data on structure function ratios, and adopted two different parametrizations (SET 1 and SET 2) for nuclear gluon distributions to bracket the uncertainty. For final-state interactions, they implemented two models: a simple incoherent multiple scattering (SIMS) formalism with either constant or color-transparency-dependent cc̅-nucleon cross sections, and a new model where multiple soft scatterings increase the cc̅ invariant mass, shifting some pairs above the open charm threshold.

## Key numbers

- A 6–8 mb effective cc̅-nucleon cross section is needed to fit the E772 data when using the SIMS formalism with constant cross section.
- For the multiple soft scattering model, a single parameter ε = 0.3 GeV² (where E²_ms = ε A^(1/3)) gives a good fit to both normalization and A-dependence of the E772 data at √s = 40 GeV.
- The charm quark mass is taken as m_c = 1.5 GeV and the open charm threshold mass as m' = 1.85 GeV.
- The fraction of cc̅ production from gluon-gluon fusion dominates for xF < 0.5, while quark-antiquark annihilation dominates for xF > 0.5 in proton-deuteron collisions at fixed-target energies.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of J/Ψ suppression observed by the E772 collaboration (Alde et al., 1991) at √s = 40 GeV and the NA3 collaboration (Badier et al., 1983) at √s = 20 GeV, which had been found to conflict with color transparency predictions (Brodsky and Mueller, 1988; Hüfner and Simbel, 1991). The authors connect the large-xF suppression to nuclear shadowing measured in deep inelastic scattering by the NMC and E665 collaborations, and show that a model incorporating both initial-state parton shadowing and final-state multiple soft scattering can fit the data across multiple nuclear targets. This enables future work to disentangle initial-state and final-state nuclear effects in heavy quarkonium production and to constrain nuclear gluon distributions.
