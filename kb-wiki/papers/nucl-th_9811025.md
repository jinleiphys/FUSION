# Radiative Muon Capture and Induced Pseudoscalar Coupling Constant in Nuclear Matter

> The recent TRIUMF experiment for $μ^- p \to n ν_μ γ$ gave a surprising result that the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant $g_P$ was larger than the value obtained from $μ^- p \to n ν_μ$ experiment as much as 44 %. Reexamining contribution of the axial vector current in electromagnetic interaction, we found an additional term to the matrix element which was used to extract the $g_P$ value from the measured photon energy spectrum. This additional term, which plays a key role to restore the reliability of $g_P (- 0.88 m_μ^2) = 6.77 g_A (0)$, is shown to affect the $g_P$ quenching problems in nucleus.

# Radiative Muon Capture and Induced Pseudoscalar Coupling Constant in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The authors identify a missing "Seagull term" (ΔM_e) in the transition amplitude used to extract the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant g_P from radiative muon capture (RMC) on a proton. Including this term restores the PCAC-based value of g_P(-0.88 m_μ^2) = 6.77 g_A(0), resolving the 44% discrepancy between the TRIUMF RMC result (ĝ_P = 9.8 ± 0.7 ± 0.3) and the ordinary muon capture (OMC) value, provided the missing term is accounted for in the analysis.

## Method

The authors construct a radiative axial current using a linear sigma model with an additional chiral-invariant Lagrangian term to reproduce g_A = 1.26, then apply minimal coupling to introduce the photon field. They derive the full relativistic amplitude for RMC on a proton, identifying a fourth term (ΔM_e) in the axial current expression that was omitted in the earlier Beder-Fearing formalism (Fearing 1980, Beder and Fearing 1987). The photon energy spectrum is calculated for both free protons and nuclear matter, with nuclear matter treated via relativistic mean field theory (Serot and Walecka 1986) using a Fermi gas model with effective nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

- The TRIUMF experimental result for the normalized induced pseudoscalar coupling constant: ĝ_P = 9.8 ± 0.7 ± 0.3 (Jonkmans et al. 1996)
- The PCAC-based theoretical value: g_P(-0.88 m_μ^2) = 6.77 g_A(0)
- Number of RMC photons observed for k ≥ 60 MeV: 279 ± 26
- Number of photons from the solid curve (ĝ_P = 9.8, no ΔM_e term): 299
- Number of photons from the authors' result (ĝ_P = 6.77, with ΔM_e term): 273
- The authors' calculation uses g_A = -1.25, g_V = 1.0, g_M = 3.71, κ_p = 1.79, κ_n = -1.91

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy between the TRIUMF RMC result (Jonkmans et al. 1996) and the PCAC prediction confirmed by OMC (Bardin et al. 1981). The authors extend their earlier work (Cheon and Cheoun 1997, 1998) by identifying a missing term in the Beder-Fearing formalism (Fearing 1980, Beder and Fearing 1987) used to extract g_P from the RMC photon spectrum. The analysis enables reinterpretation of g_P quenching in nuclei, where previous studies (Hasinoff 1996) reported quenching in medium-heavy and heavy nuclei but enhancement in light nuclei. The authors' pseudovector coupling scheme predicts smaller quenching than the usual pseudoscalar coupling scheme, with results that are nearly independent of effective nucleon mass in nuclear matter.
