# Model selection for $K^+Σ^-$ photoproduction within isobar model

> We utilise an isobar model to investigate the $K^+ Σ^-$ photoproduction off a neutron in the resonance region. Except for the Born terms, we include high-spin (spin-3/2 and spin-5/2) nucleon resonances in the consistent formalism together with a few $Δ$ and kaon resonances to achieve an acceptable agreement with data. Interestingly, we reveal that no hyperon resonances are needed to achieve a reasonable description of data. On the other hand the $N(1720)3/2^+$ resonance was found to be very important for correct description of data. The free parameters of the model were fitted to experimental data from the LEPS and CLAS Collaborations on either differential cross sections or photon beam asymmetry. The novel feature of the fitting procedure is the use of a regularization method, the Least Absolute Shrinkage Selection Operator, and information criteria in order to choose the best fit.

## Key claim

The paper claims that no hyperon resonances are needed to achieve a reasonable description of $K^+\Sigma^-$ photoproduction data, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific set of nucleon, $\Delta$, and kaon resonances included in the isobar model and on the use of a regularization method (LASSO) to select the minimal set of contributing resonances.

## Method

The authors constructed an isobar model at tree level using an effective Lagrangian, including Born terms, $t$-channel kaon resonances ($K^*$ and $K_1$), $s$-channel nucleon and $\Delta$ resonances up to spin-5/2, and $u$-channel hyperon resonances. Free parameters (coupling constants and hadronic form factor cutoffs) were fitted to 674 data points on differential cross sections and photon beam asymmetry from the LEPS and CLAS collaborations using two approaches: a standard $\chi^2$ minimization with the Minuit library, and a regularized fit employing the Least Absolute Shrinkage Selection Operator (LASSO) with a penalty term, where the optimal regularization parameter $\lambda$ was selected using Akaike and Bayesian information criteria.

## Key numbers

- The best fit using Minuit alone (fit M) achieved $\chi^2/\text{n.d.f.} = 2.39$ with 25 free parameters and 14 resonances.
- The best fit using the LASSO method (fit L) achieved $\chi^2/\text{n.d.f.} = 3.2$ (corresponding to $\chi^2 = 3.42$ relative to the data) with 17 free parameters and 9 resonances.
- The main coupling constant $g_{K^+\Sigma^- n}$ was constrained to the range $\sqrt{2}\cdot 0.8 \leq g_{K^+\Sigma^- n}/\sqrt{4\pi} \leq \sqrt{2}\cdot 1.3$ based on broken SU(3) symmetry.
- The hadronic form factor cutoff parameters were constrained between 0.5 and 3.0 GeV; the fit M yielded $\Lambda_{\rm bgr}=0.87$ GeV and $\Lambda_{N}=1.45$ GeV.
- The LASSO scan used $\lambda$ values from 3.0 down to 0 in steps of 0.2 or 0.1; the information criteria showed minima around $\lambda \approx 2.5$ (underfitting) and $\lambda \approx 1$ (preferred fit).
- The experimental data covered photon lab energies $E_\gamma^{lab}$ from 1.15 to 3.55 GeV for differential cross sections and from 1.1345 to 2.276 GeV for beam asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends the isobar model analysis previously applied to $K^+\Lambda$ photoproduction by Skoupil (2016) to the $K^+\Sigma^-$ channel off a neutron, using new CLAS beam asymmetry data (CLAS, 2021) alongside older LEPS and CLAS cross section data. The novel use of LASSO regularization and information criteria for model selection in kaon photoproduction follows the approach of Landay (recent) in pion photoproduction studies. The results enable future coupled-channel analyses to distinguish between nucleon and hyperon resonance contributions in strangeness production, and they challenge the expectation from the $K^+\Lambda$ channel where hyperon resonances were found significant by Skoupil (2016).
