# New Gogny interaction suitable for astrophysical applications

> The D1 family of parametrizations of the Gogny interaction commonly suffers from a rather soft neutron matter equation of state that leads to maximal masses of neutron stars well below the observational value of two solar masses. We propose a reparametrization scheme that preserves the good properties of the Gogny force but allows one to tune the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which, in turn, modifies the predictions for the maximum stellar mass. The scheme works well for D1M, and leads to a new parameter set, dubbed D1M*. In the neutron-star domain, D1M* predicts a maximal mass of two solar masses and global properties of the star in harmony with those obtained with the SLy4 Skyrme interaction. By means of a set of selected calculations in finite nuclei, we check that D1M* performs comparably well to D1M in several aspects of nuclear structure in nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors propose a reparametrization of the D1M Gogny interaction, called D1M*, that tunes the density dependence of the symmetry energy to produce neutron stars with maximum masses of two solar masses, while preserving the quality of D1M for finite nuclei. The key hedge is that the scheme works specifically for D1M but not for D1S or D1N, which are "too far from the 2 M⊙ target."

## Method

The authors modified the eight finite-range parameters (Wi, Bi, Hi, Mi) of the Gogny interaction while keeping the zero-range term, spin-orbit term, and Gaussian ranges fixed to the D1M values. They constrained the new parameters to reproduce D1M's saturation density, energy per particle, compressibility, effective mass, symmetry energy at 0.1 fm⁻³, and pairing properties, leaving a single free parameter (B₁) to adjust the slope L of the symmetry energy. The zero-range strength t₃ was then slightly readjusted to optimize nuclear mass predictions.

## Key numbers

- D1M* predicts a maximum neutron star mass of 2.0 M⊙ with a radius of 10.2 km
- D1M* predicts a canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star with a radius of 11.6 km
- The symmetry energy slope L changes from 24.83 MeV (D1M) to 43.18 MeV (D1M*)
- The symmetry energy at saturation E_sym(ρ₀) is 30.25 MeV for D1M* (vs. 28.55 MeV for D1M)
- The symmetry energy at 0.1 fm⁻³ is 23.82 MeV for D1M* (vs. 23.80 MeV for D1M)
- D1M* reproduces 620 even-even nuclear binding energies with a root-mean-square deviation of 1.34 MeV (vs. 1.36 MeV for D1M using the same method)
- For pulsar A of PSR J0737-3039 (mass 1.34 M⊙), D1M* predicts a moment of inertia of 1.29 × 10⁴⁵ g cm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the D1 family of Gogny interactions (D1S, D1N, D1M) which previously failed to reach the observed 2 M⊙ neutron star masses (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). The new D1M* force achieves maximum masses comparable to the SLy4 Skyrme interaction used in the Douchin-Haensel unified neutron star equation of state (2001). The approach is similar to families of Skyrme and relativistic mean field parametrizations (SAMi-J, KDE0-J, FSU-TAMU) that tune the symmetry energy slope while preserving nuclear properties. The work enables future unified neutron star equation of state calculations using a finite-range interaction that can describe pairing without divergences.
