# Coulomb corrections to photon and dilepton production in high energy pA collisions

> We consider particle production in high energy pA collisions. In addition to the coherent interactions with the nuclear color field, we take into account coherent interactions with the nuclear electromagnetic Coulomb field. Employing the dipole model, we sum up the leading multiple color and electromagnetic interactions and derive inclusive cross sections for photon and dilepton production. We found that the Coulomb corrections are up to $10\%$ at $\sqrt{s}=200$~GeV per nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Coulomb (electromagnetic) corrections to photon and dilepton production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions can reach up to 10% at sqrt(s)=200 GeV per nucleon, but this effect is largest only at small photon transverse momenta and small dilepton invariant masses, and it diminishes as collision energy increases because the QCD saturation momentum grows as a power of energy while the QED contribution grows only logarithmically.

## Method

The author employs the dipole model of high-energy scattering, summing leading multiple color and electromagnetic interactions via the Glauber-Mueller formalism. The proton-nucleus scattering amplitude is factorized into strong (QCD) and electromagnetic (QED) contributions, with the strong amplitude being purely imaginary (double-gluon exchange) and the electromagnetic amplitude being purely real (single-photon exchange). A "cylindrical nucleus" model is used to compute impact parameter integrals analytically, and the dipole-nucleus total cross section separates into a sum of QCD and QED terms. The inclusive cross sections for photon and dilepton production are then derived using light-cone wave functions and the dipole scattering formalism.

## Key numbers

* Coulomb correction to photon production in p-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV per nucleon: up to 7% at small photon transverse momentum k1.
* Coulomb correction to dilepton (e+e-) production in p-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV per nucleon: up to 10% at invariant mass M ~ 200 MeV.
* The QED contribution to the total dipole-nucleus cross section is suppressed relative to the QCD term by a factor of order (r * alpha * Z / R_A)^2 * log(s), where r is dipole size, Z=79 for gold, R_A is nuclear radius.
* For gold nucleus (A=197, Z=79), the smallest suppression factor for the Coulomb correction is about 0.1 at sqrt(s)=200 GeV.
* The saturation momentum parameter used is Q_s^2 ~ 0.16 * A^(1/3) GeV^2 with anomalous dimension gamma ~ 0.63.
* The QED contribution to the b-integrated dilepton cross section scales as 1/ell^4 * (alpha*Z)^2 * ln(s/(4*m_ell^2*m_N*R_A)).

## Context

This work extends the dipole model treatment of particle production in high-energy pA collisions, previously applied to photon production without electromagnetic corrections by Jalilian-Marian (2005), by incorporating coherent electromagnetic interactions alongside the coherent color field. The paper notes that the QED parameter alpha*Z is of the same order as the QCD parameter alpha_s^2 * A^(1/3) for heavy nuclei, making electromagnetic effects non-negligible. The results enable more accurate extraction of prompt photon yields from dilepton spectra in pA collisions, and suggest that the standard formula dN^(ell+ell-)/dM = (2*alpha)/(3*pi*M) * N^gamma overestimates the photon yield by about 10% due to Coulomb corrections. The analysis is limited to pA collisions, and the author notes that extending it to AA collisions requires further analytical investigation.
