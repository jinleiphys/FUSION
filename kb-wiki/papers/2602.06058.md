# "Observations on the possible electromagnetic nature of nucleon interactions and pions" -- historical manuscript from 1969 by B. W. Ninham and C. Pask

> This manuscript presents an historical perspective prepared by Barry Ninham and Colin Pask in 1969 on the connection between quantum electrodynamics theory and nucleon interactions. A new theory of strong interactions based on electromagnetic considerations is proposed. Energy and force range magnitudes are correctly given. A new theory of the pion emerges and the pion mass and lifetime are calculated. No strong interaction coupling constant is required.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that strong nucleon interactions and pions can be explained entirely by electromagnetic fluctuation forces (Casimir-type forces) and a high-temperature electron-positron plasma, without invoking any strong interaction coupling constant; the authors explicitly state they "do not claim any great accuracy" and present the work as "rough ideas" that "could lead to an interesting quantitative theory if correctly interpreted."

## Method

The authors model nucleons as semi-infinite planes of nuclear material and apply Casimir's formula for the electromagnetic interaction energy between perfectly reflecting surfaces. They identify the Klein-Gordon equation for pions with a plasma dielectric function, setting the pion mass equal to the plasma frequency of an electron-positron pair gas. The equivalent temperature of the Casimir energy in a gap of 1 fm is used to compute the equilibrium electron-positron number density via blackbody formulas from Landau and Lifshitz, from which the pion mass is derived; the neutral pion lifetime is estimated using Ninham's earlier plasma lifetime calculations for plasmon decay into electron-positron pairs.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass calculated as approximately 220 electron masses (m_π ≈ 220 m_e), compared to the experimental value of about 270 m_e.
* Neutral pion lifetime estimated as ≤ 1.5×10⁻¹⁷ s, compared to the experimental value of (0.84 ± 0.1)×10⁻¹⁶ s.
* Attractive electromagnetic energy between two nucleons at a distance of 1 fm computed as approximately 10 MeV.
* A crude balancing of Casimir attraction against Coulomb repulsion between two protons gives a typical nuclear distance d ∼ 5 fm with corresponding energy ∼ 1/5 MeV.
* The equivalent temperature in a 1 fm gap is kT ∼ 140 m_e c².

## Context

This 1969 manuscript by Ninham and Pask extends the line of thought that Feynman believed there should be a connection between electromagnetic theory and nuclear interactions, a connection Feynman never found. The work builds on Casimir's (1948) derivation of forces between perfectly reflecting plates and on Landau and Lifshitz's statistical physics treatment of electron-positron pair equilibrium at high temperatures. The authors note that their approach contrasts with the standard meson cloud model (Galloway) and with current algebra approaches that could not account for neutral pion decay (Beg). The paper enables a semiclassical electromagnetic alternative to QCD-based descriptions of nuclear forces, later developed further by Ninham and Boström (2003, 2014) and by Ninham, Brevik, and Boström (2022) for muon lifetimes.
