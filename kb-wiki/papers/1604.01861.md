# Couplings between the $ρ$ and $D$- and $D^\ast$-mesons

> We compute couplings between the $ρ$-meson and $D$- and $D^\ast$-mesons - $D^{(\ast)}ρD^{(\ast)}$ - that are relevant to phenomenological meson-exchange models used to analyse nucleon-$D$-meson scattering and explore the possibility of exotic charmed nuclei. Our framework is built from elements constrained by Dyson-Schwinger equation studies in QCD, and therefore expresses a consistent, simultaneous description of light- and heavy-quarks and the states they constitute, We find that all interactions, including the three independent $D^{\ast} ρ\,D^{\ast}$ couplings, differ markedly amongst themselves in strength and also in range, as measured by their evolution with $ρ$-meson virtuality. As a consequence, it appears that no single coupling strength or parametrization can realistically be employed in the study of interactions between $D^{(\ast)}$-mesons and matter.

## Key claim

The five independent couplings between the ρ-meson and D- and D*-mesons differ markedly in both strength and range as a function of ρ-meson virtuality, implying that no single coupling strength or parametrization can realistically be employed in studies of interactions between D(*)-mesons and matter, contrary to the leading-order heavy-quark symmetry prediction of degenerate couplings.

## Method

The authors compute the Poincaré-invariant amplitudes for DρD, D*ρD, and D*ρD* couplings using a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework with dressed quark propagators and simplified Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for the mesons. The quark propagators are parametrized to reproduce DSE solutions, with light quarks exhibiting dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the charm quark treated with a momentum-independent mass of 1.32 GeV. The meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are Gaussian forms with width parameters fixed by requiring agreement with experimental leptonic decay constants.

## Key numbers

- gDρD(0) = 6.37 (dimensionless coupling at zero ρ-meson virtuality)
- gD*ρD(0) = 22.6 (dimensionless coupling at zero ρ-meson virtuality)
- g1D*ρD*(0) = 10.5, g2D*ρD*(0) = 51.5, g3D*ρD*(0) = 16.8 (three independent D*ρD* couplings at zero virtuality)
- gD*πD = 18.7+2.5-1.4 (computed within same framework, compared with experimental value 17.9±1.9)
- fD = 0.206±0.009 GeV, fD* = 0.245±0.020 GeV, fρ = 0.22 GeV (leptonic decay constants used to fix meson width parameters)
- ωD = 1.08±0.1 GeV, ωD* = 0.81±0.15 GeV, ωρ = 0.66 GeV (meson width parameters in Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes)
- ℓD = 0.183 fm, ℓD* = 0.244 fm, ℓρ = 0.352 fm (inverse width parameters as length scales)
- gDρD(0) ≈ 6.4 < gD*π0D ≈ 13 < gD*ρD(0) ≈ 23 (comparison of couplings at zero virtuality)

## Context

This work extends the earlier DSE study of the DρD coupling by El-Bennich et al. (2011), which found SU(4) flavor symmetry broken at the 300-400% level and that DρD form factors are softer than common monopole parametrizations used by Haidenbauer et al. (2010). The computed couplings are relevant for phenomenological meson-exchange models used to analyze nucleon-D-meson scattering and explore exotic charmed nuclei, such as the X(3872) state. The results enable improved predictions for charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions and for the proposed PANDA program at FAIR, where previous studies by Torres et al. (2014) used momentum-independent couplings based on SU(4) symmetry.
