# Higher derivative holography and temperature dependence of QGP viscosities

> Recent Bayesian analyses of heavy ion collision data have established a non-trivial temperature dependence of the shear and bulk viscosity per entropy. Motivated by this, we consider higher derivative corrections to realistic, bottom-up holographic models of quark-gluon plasma based on five-dimensional Einstein-dilaton theories and determine the dilaton potentials in the higher derivative terms by matching the Bayesian analyses. A byproduct of our analysis is the bulk viscosity that follows from the holographic V-QCD theory. Higher derivative corrections when treated perturbatively lead to tension with existing data. We investigate possible resolutions.

## Key claim
The paper shows that higher-derivative (Riemann-squared) corrections to holographic Einstein-dilaton models can be tuned to match the temperature dependence of shear viscosity per entropy from Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion data, but only if the corrections are large enough to invalidate the perturbative treatment; this tension suggests that finite-coupling corrections are essential for describing QGP transport, and that a single Riemann-squared term cannot simultaneously fit both shear and bulk viscosity.

## Method
The authors consider a five-dimensional Einstein-dilaton action with a Riemann-squared correction term \(\ell^2 \beta G(\Phi) R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} R^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\), where \(G(\Phi)\) is a dilaton-dependent coupling. They derive analytic expressions for shear viscosity per entropy \(\eta/s\) and bulk viscosity per entropy \(\zeta/s\) in the presence of this correction, generalizing prior work. Using the V-QCD holographic model (with fixed dilaton potentials from lattice and perturbative QCD matching), they treat the correction perturbatively and attempt to determine \(G(\Phi)\) by requiring \(\eta/s\) to match the Trajectum Bayesian posterior. They then compute the resulting \(\zeta/s\) and compare it to the same Bayesian band.

## Key numbers
- The Trajectum Bayesian analysis gives \(\overline{\eta/s} = 0.191^{+0.051}_{-0.048}\) as the average shear viscosity per entropy in the range \(0.15\,\text{GeV} < T < 0.3\,\text{GeV}\), with slope \((\eta/s)_{\text{slope}} = -0.34^{+0.66}_{-0.61}\,\text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- The Trajectum bulk viscosity parameters are \(d = 0.074^{+0.048}_{-0.044}\) (peak height) and \(d \times e = 0.0068^{+0.0033}_{-0.0029}\,\text{GeV}\) (area under peak), with peak temperature \(T_\zeta = 0.330^{+0.098}_{-0.100}\,\text{GeV}\).
- The shear viscosity per entropy at the two-derivative level is the universal KSS value \(1/(4\pi) \approx 0.08\); the Trajectum mean \(\overline{\eta/s} = 0.191\) is about 2.4 times larger.
- The Riemann-squared correction to \(\eta/s\) is given by \(\frac{1}{4\pi}\left[1 + \frac{2}{3}\beta\ell^2\left(-G(\Phi_h)V(\Phi_h) + \frac{9}{8}\partial_\Phi G(\Phi_h)\partial_\Phi V(\Phi_h)\right)\right]\).
- The confinement-preserving IR asymptotics of \(G(\Phi)\) require \(G(\Phi) \sim G_{IR} e^{\gamma\Phi}\Phi^\delta\) with \(\gamma \leq -4/3\).

## Context
This work extends the program of using bottom-up holographic QCD models (ihQCD by Gursoy et al. (2008), V-QCD by Jarvinen et al. (2011)) to describe quark-gluon plasma transport, by adding higher-derivative corrections beyond the supergravity approximation. It directly confronts the temperature-dependent \(\eta/s\) and \(\zeta/s\) profiles from Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion data (Trajectum by Nijs et al. (2020-2023) and Giacalone et al. (2023); JETSCAPE by JETSCAPE Collaboration (2020)). Prior work by Cremonini (2012) studied higher-curvature corrections to shear transport in Einstein-dilaton theories but lacked reliable data to constrain them. The results here show that perturbative Riemann-squared corrections cannot simultaneously match both viscosities, motivating future work with non-perturbative treatments or additional correction types (e.g., \(R^2\) and \(R_{\mu\nu}^2\) terms) to resolve the tension.
