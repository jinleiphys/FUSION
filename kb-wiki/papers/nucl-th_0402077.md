# Neutrino neutral reaction on 4He, effects of final state interaction and realistic NN force

> The inelastic neutral reaction of neutrino on 4He is calculated microscopically, including full final state interaction among the four nucleons. The calculation is performed using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method and the hyperspherical-harmonic effective interaction approach (EIHH), with a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. A detailed energy dependent calculation is given in the impulse approximation. With respect to previous calculations, this work predicts an increased reaction cross-section by 10%-30% for neutrino temperature up to 15 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper predicts an increased inelastic neutrino-⁴He neutral reaction cross-section by 10%-30% for neutrino temperatures up to 15 MeV compared to previous calculations, but this result is obtained in the impulse approximation (one-body currents only) and the authors estimate that many-body currents, which are not included, could contribute about 10% error to the cross-section.

## Method

The authors performed a microscopic ab-initio calculation of the inelastic neutral reaction of neutrino on ⁴He using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method combined with the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method, with the realistic AV8' nucleon-nucleon potential. The LIT method converts the scattering problem into a bound-state-like problem, and the EIHH method solves the resulting equations by expanding wave functions in four-body antisymmetrized hyperspherical harmonics basis functions. The calculation was done in the impulse approximation, taking into account only one-body weak currents, and the response functions were shifted by 2.4 MeV to correct for the under-binding of ⁴He (calculated binding energy 25.19 MeV versus experimental threshold).

## Key numbers

- Calculated ⁴He binding energy with AV8' force: 25.19 MeV (discrepancy of approximately 2.4 MeV from experimental inelastic reaction threshold)
- Calculated triton binding energy: 7.76 MeV
- Temperature averaged inclusive inelastic cross-section at T=10 MeV: 7.85 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (this work) versus 6.78 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (Woosley et al., 1990)
- Temperature averaged inclusive inelastic cross-section at T=12 MeV: 2.05 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (this work) versus 1.63 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (Woosley et al., 1990)
- Temperature averaged inclusive inelastic cross-section at T=16 MeV: 8.52 × 10⁻⁴² cm²
- Energy transfer cross-section at T=10 MeV: 2.30 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² MeV
- Energy transfer cross-section at T=16 MeV: 2.84 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² MeV
- Fit parameters for energy transfer cross-section: α = 0.64 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² MeV, T₀ = 2.05 MeV, β = 4.46
- Numerical accuracy of the calculation: about 1% (relative error in sum-rule of response functions below 1%)
- Estimated total error: about 10%, mainly due to neglected many-body currents

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Woosley et al. (1990) and Bruenn and Haxton (1991) by providing the first realistic microscopic calculation of the inelastic neutrino-⁴He cross-section that includes full final state interaction among the four nucleons. The method had been previously used successfully to calculate photoabsorption cross sections of up to six-body nuclei (Barnea et al., 2002). The results enable more accurate hydrodynamic simulations of core-collapse supernovae, particularly for the neutrino-driven shock revival mechanism, and can improve predictions for nucleosynthesis of rare A=7 lithium and beryllium isotopes in the inner helium shell.
