# Temperature dependent BCS equations with continuum coupling

> The temperature dependent BCS equations are modified in order to include the contribution of the continuum single particle states. The influence of the continuum upon the critical temperature corresponding to the phase transition from a superfluid to a normal state and upon the behaviour of the excitation energy and of the entropy is discussed.

# Temperature dependent BCS equations with continuum coupling

## Key claim

The paper extends the temperature-dependent BCS (TBCS) equations to include the contribution of continuum single-particle states via the continuum level density, and finds that the widths of resonant states significantly reduce pairing correlations and lower the critical temperature compared to calculations that treat resonances as quasibound states. The effect is most relevant for nuclei far from stability where the Fermi level lies close to the continuum threshold.

## Method

The authors modify the standard TBCS gap and particle-number equations by replacing the bound-state level density with the total level density, which sums the bound spectrum and the continuum level density defined as the derivative of the phase shift with respect to energy. They apply the formalism to the neutron-rich isotope 84Ni using a Hartree-Fock single-particle spectrum from the Skyrme III interaction, with resonant energies and widths extracted from phase-shift analysis. The continuum contribution is evaluated numerically using the continuum level density, and the results are compared to the case where resonant states are treated as quasibound states (zero width).

## Key numbers

- Gap at zero temperature: Δ(0) = 0.955 MeV for 84Ni with continuum coupling included.
- Critical temperature with continuum coupling: Tc = 0.524 MeV, which is about 3.8% smaller than the value predicted by the constant level-density relation Tc = 0.57Δ(0).
- Critical temperature when resonance widths are neglected: Tc = 0.722 MeV, only 1% larger than the constant level-density approximation.
- Excitation energy at critical temperature: 2.185 MeV.
- Entropy at critical temperature: 5.708.
- Level-density parameter with continuum coupling: a = 3.958 MeV−1; when widths are neglected: a = 4.143 MeV−1.
- Pairing strength used: G = 25/A MeV, with A = 84 for 84Ni.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature BCS with continuum coupling developed by Sandulescu, Liotta, and Wyss (1997) and Sandulescu, Nguyen Van Giai, and Liotta (1998) to finite temperature. It contrasts with earlier temperature-dependent BCS calculations by Sano and Yamasaki (1963) and others that used a constant single-particle level density and neglected the continuum, which is adequate for beta-stable nuclei but insufficient for nuclei near the drip line. The formalism enables future studies of superfluid properties in exotic nuclei where the Fermi level lies close to the continuum threshold, and it provides a framework for treating the competition between pairing correlations and particle emission in hot, weakly-bound nuclei.
