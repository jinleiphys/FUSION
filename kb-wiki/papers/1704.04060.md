# Latest results from the EbyE NLO EKRT model

> We review the results from the event-by-event next-to-leading order perturbative QCD + saturation + viscous hydrodynamics (EbyE NLO EKRT) model. With a simultaneous analysis of LHC and RHIC bulk observables we systematically constrain the QCD matter shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio eta/s(T), and test the initial state computation. In particular, we study the centrality dependences of hadronic multiplicities, pT spectra, flow coefficients, relative elliptic flow fluctuations, and various flow-correlations in 2.76 and 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. Overall, our results match remarkably well with the LHC and RHIC measurements, and predictions for the 5.02 TeV LHC run are in an excellent agreement with the data. We probe the applicability of hydrodynamics via the average Knudsen numbers in the space-time evolution of the system and viscous corrections on the freeze-out surface.

## Key claim

The EbyE NLO EKRT model simultaneously describes bulk observables at both LHC and RHIC energies, and the authors identify two best-fit parametrizations for the temperature-dependent shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio eta/s(T), but note that further systematic tests of hydrodynamic validity are still needed, especially for more complicated correlators.

## Method

The model combines next-to-leading order perturbative QCD minijet production with a local saturation condition in the transverse plane to generate event-by-event initial energy density profiles, which are then evolved using 2+1 D second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics. The initial state fluctuations are obtained by sampling nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon density and assigning a Gaussian gluon thickness function of width 0.43 fm around each nucleon. The authors test multiple parametrizations of eta/s(T) against centrality dependences of charged hadron multiplicities, pT spectra, flow coefficients v_n{2}, relative elliptic flow fluctuations, and various flow correlations in 2.76 and 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian gluon thickness function width is set to sigma = 0.43 fm, based on Chekanov (2004)
- The constant formation time for starting hydro simulations is tau_0 = 0.2 fm, corresponding to 1/p_sat^min
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature is T_dec = 100 MeV; chemical decoupling temperature is T_chem = 175 MeV
- The parameter beta controlling minimum E_T in rapidity window Delta y is fixed to 0.8
- Average Knudsen numbers <Kn> are of order 1 for centralities up to 50%, indicating hydrodynamic validity in that range
- The ratio of flow coefficients v_n{2} between 5.023 TeV and 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions shows a slight increase with increasing harmonic number n

## Context

This work extends the EKRT model framework developed by Eskola (1999) and Niemi (2015) by providing a comprehensive multi-energy and multi-observable analysis that constrains eta/s(T). The results are consistent with the Bayesian analysis of Bernhard (2016). The model's predictive power for 5.02 TeV LHC collisions, demonstrated in Niemi (2015), is confirmed by excellent agreement with ALICE data. The work enables further systematic studies of dissipative phenomena and tests of hydrodynamic validity for more complicated correlators such as C_{2,3,5}, where the model currently underestimates STAR data.
