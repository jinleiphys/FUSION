# Near-threshold $ω$-meson production in proton-proton collisions: With or without resonance excitations ?

> We present results for the $p p \to p p ω$ reaction studied by considering two different scenarios: with and without the inclusion of nucleon resonance excitations. The recently measured angular distribution by the COSY-TOF Collaboration at an excess energy of $Q = 173$ MeV and the energy dependence of the total cross section data for $π^- p \to ωn$ are used to calibrate the model parameters. The inclusion of nucleon resonances improves the theoretical prediction for the energy dependence of the total cross section in $pp \to ppω$ at excess energies $Q < 31$ MeV. However, it still underestimates the data by about a factor of two, and remains a problem in understanding the reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

Including nucleon resonance excitations improves the theoretical prediction for the energy dependence of the total cross section in $pp \to pp\omega$ at excess energies below 31 MeV, but the model still underestimates the SATURNE data by about a factor of two, indicating a remaining problem in understanding the reaction mechanism.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic hadronic model with the reaction amplitude calculated in the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA), including both initial and final state interactions for the protons. They considered two scenarios: one without nucleon resonance excitations and one including four specific resonances ($S_{11}(1535)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $D_{13}(1700)$, $P_{13}(1720)$). The model parameters were calibrated using the COSY-TOF angular distribution data at excess energy $Q = 173$ MeV and the energy dependence of the total cross section for $\pi^- p \to \omega n$.

## Key numbers

- The tensor-to-vector coupling constant ratio at the $\omega NN$ vertex was determined as $\kappa_\omega = -2.0$ (without resonances) and $\kappa_\omega = -0.5$ (with resonances), using $g_{\omega NN} = +9.0$.
- The cutoff parameter at the $\omega NN$ vertex was set to $\Lambda_N = 1.1$ GeV in the resonance-included scenario.
- The cutoff parameter for the $\omega \rho \pi$ vertex form factor (dipole form) was $\Lambda_\rho = 850$ MeV.
- The coupling constant for $P_{11}(1710)$ to $N\sigma$ was adjusted to $g_{P_{11}N\sigma} = -4.3$ to reproduce the $pp \to pp\omega$ total cross section at $Q = 173$ MeV.
- The model with resonances still underestimates the SATURNE data for $pp \to pp\omega$ at excess energies $Q < 31$ MeV by about a factor of two.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of near-threshold $\omega$ production in proton-proton collisions, using the COSY-TOF angular distribution data at $Q = 173$ MeV (COSY-TOF Collaboration) and the SATURNE total cross section data for $Q < 31$ MeV (Hibou et al., 2000). The authors compare their results to the analysis of Wilkin (1999) used in the SATURNE data interpretation. The inclusion of resonances improves consistency with the $\pi^- p \to \omega n$ reaction data, but the persistent factor-of-two discrepancy at low excess energies indicates that further refinements to the reaction mechanism are needed, possibly including additional resonance contributions or improved treatment of mesonic currents.
