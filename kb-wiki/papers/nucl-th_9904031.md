# The N N -> NN pi+ Reaction near Threshold in a Chiral Power Counting Approach

> Power-counting arguments are used to organize the interactions contributing to the N N -> d pi, p n pi reactions near threshold. We estimate the contributions from the three formally leading mechanisms: the Weinberg-Tomozawa (WT) term, the impulse term, and the $Δ$-excitation mechanism. Sub-leading but potentially large mechanisms, including $S$-wave pion-rescattering, the Galilean correction to the WT term, and short-ranged contributions are also examined. The WT term is shown to be numerically the largest, and the other contributions are found to approximately cancel. Similarly to the reaction p p -> p p pi0, the computed cross sections are considerably smaller than the data. We discuss possible origins of this discrepancy.

## Key claim

The authors use chiral power counting to organize contributions to the $NN \rightarrow d\pi$ and $NN \rightarrow pn\pi$ reactions near threshold, finding that the Weinberg-Tomozawa term is numerically dominant but that the sum of all leading and sub-leading mechanisms still underpredicts the measured cross sections by approximately a factor of 2, similar to the long-standing discrepancy in $pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0$.

## Method

The authors split the reaction amplitude into a kernel (irreducible diagrams involving momenta of order $\sqrt{m_N m_\pi}$) and initial/final-state interactions encoded in wavefunctions from realistic $NN$ potentials. They evaluate the leading-order contributions (Weinberg-Tomozawa term, impulse term, and $\Delta$-excitation mechanism) and several sub-leading mechanisms ($S$-wave pion rescattering, Galilean correction to the WT term, and short-range contributions modeled by $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho-\omega$ meson exchange) using the chiral Lagrangian with explicit nucleon, delta, and pion degrees of freedom, adapting the power counting to the threshold kinematics where typical momenta are $\sqrt{m_N m_\pi}$ rather than $m_\pi$.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order contributions (WT, IA, $\Delta$) are all of order ${\cal O}(f_{\pi}^{-3} \sqrt{m_\pi /M})$ in the chiral power counting.
- The pion decay constant used is $f_{\pi}=93$ MeV.
- The delta-nucleon mass difference is $\delta = m_\Delta - m_N$, numerically of order $m_\pi$ (approximately $2m_\pi$).
- The typical momentum in the kernel is $p_{\rm typ} \sim \sqrt{m_N m_\pi}$.
- The nucleon propagator scales as $\sim 1/m_\pi$, the delta propagator as $\sim -1/m_\pi$, and the pion propagator as $\sim 1/(m_\pi m_N)$.
- The cross section for $pp \rightarrow d\pi^+$ near threshold is $\sigma_{pp}= \alpha C_0^2 \eta + {\cal O}(\eta^3)$, where $\eta = q/m_\pi$ and $q$ is the maximum pion momentum.
- The cross section for $np \rightarrow d\pi^0$ near threshold is $\sigma_{np}= (\alpha/2)\eta + {\cal O}(\eta^3)$.
- For $pp \rightarrow pn\pi^+$, the measured energy dependence is $|a_1|^2 \propto \eta^{3.2}$.
- The leading-order calculation for $pp\rightarrow pp\pi^0$ underpredicts data by a factor of approximately 5.

## Context

This work extends the chiral power-counting approach of Cohen et al. (1996) from the $pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0$ reaction to the charged-pion channels $NN \rightarrow d\pi$ and $NN \rightarrow pn\pi$, which have traditionally been considered better understood. The authors build on earlier calculations by Koltun and Reitan (1966) who found the Weinberg-Tomozawa term dominated the deuteron channel, and on more recent work by van Kolck et al. (1996) showing that heavy-meson exchanges ($\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho-\omega$) are relevant for $pp\pi^0$. The results enable systematic comparison with new threshold data from TRIUMF, COSY, and IUCF, and highlight that the theoretical uncertainties from delta contributions, $S$-wave rescattering, and short-range mechanisms must be resolved before agreement with experiment can be achieved.
