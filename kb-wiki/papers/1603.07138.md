# Isospin dynamics on neck fragmentation in isotopic nuclear reactions

> The neck dynamics in Fermi-energy heavy-ion collisions, to probe the nuclear symmetry energy in the domain of sub-saturation densities, is investigated within an isospin dependent transport model. The single and double ratios of neutron/proton from free nucleons and light clusters (complex particles) in the isotopic reactions are analyzed systematically. Isospin effects of particles produced from the neck fragmentations are explored, which are constrained within the midrapidities ($|y/y_{proj}|<$0.3) and azimuthal angles (70$^{o}\sim$110$^{o}$, 250$^{o}\sim$290$^{o}$) in semiperipheral nuclear collisions. It is found that the ratios of the energetic isospin particles strongly depend on the stiffness of nuclear symmetry energy and the effects increase with softening the symmetry energy, which would be a nice probe for extracting the symmetry energy below the normal density in experimentally. A flat structure appears at the tail spectra from the double ratio distributions. The neutron to proton ratio of light intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) with charged number Z$\leq$8 is related to the density dependence of symmetry energy with less sensitivity in comparison to the isospin ratios of nucleons and light particles.

# Isospin dynamics on neck fragmentation in isotopic nuclear reactions

## Key claim
The single and double neutron/proton ratios from free nucleons and light clusters produced in neck fragmentations at Fermi energies (35 MeV/nucleon) depend strongly on the stiffness of the nuclear symmetry energy, with effects increasing when the symmetry energy is softened, but this sensitivity is constrained to particles emitted within midrapidities (|y/y_proj| < 0.3) and specific azimuthal angles (70°–110°, 250°–290°) in semiperipheral collisions, and the neutron-to-proton ratio of light intermediate mass fragments (Z ≤ 8) shows less sensitivity than the ratios of nucleons and light particles.

## Method
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, combined with the GEMINI statistical decay code for de-excitation of primary fragments, was used to simulate semiperipheral collisions of isotopic Sn+Ni and Sn+Sn systems at 35 MeV/nucleon with impact parameters of 6–8 fm. Particles from neck fragmentation were selected by midrapidity cuts (|y/y_proj| < 0.3), and single and double neutron/proton ratios were computed for free nucleons, "gas-phase" nucleons (including hydrogen and helium isotopes), and light intermediate mass fragments with charged number Z ≤ 8, using soft (γ_s = 0.5), linear (γ_s = 1), and hard (γ_s = 2) forms of the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 31.5 MeV for all three stiffness cases (soft, linear, hard), crossing at ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Compression modulus of isospin symmetric nuclear matter: K = 230 MeV.
- Beam energy: 35 MeV/nucleon for all reactions.
- Impact parameter range for semicentral collisions: 6–8 fm.
- Symmetry energy effect on single n/p ratios: close to 50% due to longer isospin relaxation time at Fermi energies compared to high-density observables.
- Double neutron/proton ratios show variation of 16%–18% due to symmetry energy stiffness, roughly twice the variation seen in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.
- Coalescence parameters for primary fragment construction: relative momentum P₀ = 200 MeV/c, relative distance R₀ = 3 fm.
- Midrapidity selection: |y/y_proj| < 0.3; azimuthal selection: 70°–110° and 250°–290°.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy constraints from high-density observables (e.g., π⁻/π⁺, K⁰/K⁺, Σ⁻/Σ⁺ ratios) by Feng (2012, 2013) into the sub-saturation density domain accessible at Fermi energies. The results are consistent with earlier IBUU transport model calculations by Li (1997) for pre-equilibrium neutron/proton ratios. The double ratio technique follows experimental measurements at NSCL by Colonna (2014) for Sn isotopic reactions at 50 and 120 MeV/nucleon, and the IMF data are compared with CHIMERA detector measurements by Fichera (2012). The findings enable experimental extraction of the density dependence of symmetry energy below normal nuclear density using isotopic reaction pairs, with the double ratio method reducing Coulomb and systematic uncertainties.
