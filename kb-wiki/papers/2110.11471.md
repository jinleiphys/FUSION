# Cumulants: It's More Than You Think

> Cumulants of baryon number are given considerable attention in analyses of heavy-ion collision experiments as possible signatures of the QCD critical point. In this work, we show that the values of the lowest three cumulants can also be utilized to recover information about the isothermal speed of sound and its logarithmic derivative with respect to the baryon number density. This result provides a new method for obtaining information about fundamental properties of nuclear matter studied in heavy-ion collisions, with consequences for both the search for the QCD critical point and neutron star studies. While the approximations and the model comparison we considered apply to experiments at low energies, the approach itself can be used at any collision energy provided that measurements of cumulants of baryon number distribution as well as their temperature dependence are available.

## Key claim
The lowest three cumulants of baryon number can be used to recover information about the isothermal speed of sound and its logarithmic derivative with respect to baryon number density, but the approximations are validated only for low collision energies where the ratio T/μ_B is small; the approach itself is general provided measurements of cumulants and their temperature dependence are available.

## Method
The authors derive analytic relations connecting the first three cumulants of net baryon number to the isothermal speed of sound c_T^2 and its logarithmic derivative (d ln c_T^2 / d ln n_B)_T, using thermodynamic identities. They test the validity of these approximations by comparing exact and approximate expressions in two nuclear matter models: the Walecka model (describing the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition) and a relativistic vector density functional (VDF) model (with an additional QGP-like phase transition). They then apply the approximate formulas to experimental net-proton cumulant data from STAR and HADES collisions at 0-5% centrality, using freeze-out parameters from the literature.

## Key numbers
- The isothermal speed of sound approximation c_T^2 ≈ T κ_1 / (μ_B κ_2) is valid for T ≲ 100 MeV and μ_B ≳ 600 MeV, based on model comparisons.
- The logarithmic derivative approximation (d ln c_T^2 / d ln n_B)_T + c_T^2 ≈ 1 - κ_3 κ_1 / κ_2^2 is valid for μ_B ≳ 200 MeV, with the exception of the Walecka model at T = 200 MeV.
- In the VDF model, the QGP-like critical point is placed at T_c = 100 MeV and n_c = 3 n_0, with n_0 = 0.160 fm^{-3}; the T=0 spinodal boundaries are at n_B = 2.5 n_0 (left) and n_B = 3.32 n_0 (right).
- For the lowest STAR energy (√s_NN = 7.7 GeV), c_T^2 is nearly constant as a function of n_B; for the HADES energy (√s = 2.4 GeV), c_T^2 is small but has a large slope with n_B.
- The pseudocritical temperature at zero baryon chemical potential from lattice QCD is T_0 = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV (Bazavov 2018).

## Context
This work extends prior attempts to estimate the speed of sound in heavy-ion collisions (Gardim 2019, Steinheimer 2012) by proposing a new method based on baryon number cumulants, which are traditionally studied as signatures of the QCD critical point (Asakawa 2009, Stephanov 2011). The results suggest that at low collision energies (STAR and HADES), cumulants may be more sensitive to hadronic physics and the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition than to the QCD critical point. The approach enables future extraction of c_T^2 at higher energies if temperature-dependent cumulant measurements become available, and calls for dynamical simulations to assess the reliability of the method.
