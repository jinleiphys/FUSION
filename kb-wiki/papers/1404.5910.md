# Reversing a heavy-ion collision

> We introduce a novel approach to study the longitudinal hydrodynamic expansion of the quark-gluon fluid created in heavy-ion collisions. It consists of two steps: First, we apply the maximum entropy method to reconstruct the freeze-out surface from experimentally measured particle distribution. We then take the output of the reconstruction as the "initial" condition to evolve the system back in time by solving the 1+1 ideal hydrodynamic equations analytically, using the method of Khalatnikov and Landau. We find an approximate Bjorken-like plateau in the energy density vs rapidity profile at the early times, which shrinks with time as the boundary shocks propagate inward. In Bjorken frame, the fluid velocity is close to zero within the plateau, as in the Bjorken solution, but increases outside the plateau. The results carry implications for fully numerical hydrodynamic simulations as well as models of heavy-ion collisions based on gauge-gravity duality.

## Key claim
The authors introduce a novel two-step approach to reconstruct the longitudinal expansion history of quark-gluon fluid in heavy-ion collisions by applying the maximum entropy method to invert the Cooper-Frye freeze-out formula and then evolving the system backward in time using analytic solutions to 1+1 ideal hydrodynamics. The key hedge is that this exploratory study neglects transverse expansion, assumes constant speed of sound, and uses ideal hydrodynamics, so the results provide qualitative insights rather than quantitative precision.

## Method
The authors first apply the maximum entropy method (MEM) to deconvolve the Cooper-Frye freeze-out integral transform, reconstructing the freeze-out surface (proper time τ_f(α) and flow profile η_f(α) as functions of fluid rapidity α) from experimentally measured pion rapidity distributions. They then use the reconstructed freeze-out surface as Cauchy initial conditions on the isothermal surface T = T_f to solve the Khalatnikov equation analytically, evolving the system backward in time to obtain the temperature and flow profiles at earlier proper times.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature assumed: T_f = 140 MeV
- Transverse momentum bin: p_⊥ = 0.55 GeV with width 0.1 GeV
- Central collision system: Au-Au at √s = 200 GeV
- Reconstructed combination A_⊥τ_f(0) ≈ (1.4-1.5) × 10^4 fm^3
- Flow deviation from Bjorken boost-invariance: |(α-η_f)/η_f| of order 10%-20% for all α considered
- Early-time profiles shown at τ = 0.1τ_f(0), 0.2τ_f(0), and 0.4τ_f(0)

## Context
This work extends the Bjorken (1983) boost-invariant model by reconstructing realistic longitudinal flow beyond the idealized plateau assumption. It contrasts with fully numerical hydrodynamic simulations such as those by Hirano (2001), Satarov (2006), Bozek (2009), and Schenke (2010), which rely on trial-and-error tuning of initial condition ansatze with several free parameters. The approach enables direct determination of early-time longitudinal profiles from experimental data, providing constraints for initial conditions in numerical simulations and for holographic models of heavy-ion collisions based on gauge-gravity duality.
