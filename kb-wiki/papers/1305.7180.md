# Transport coefficients from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for $SU(3)_f$

> We calculate the shear $η(T)$ and bulk viscosities $ζ(T)$ as well as the electric conductivity $σ_e(T)$ and heat conductivity $κ(T)$ within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for 3 flavors as a function of temperature as well as the entropy density $s(T)$, pressure $P(T)$ and speed of sound $c_s^2(T)$. We compare the results with other models such as the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) and confront these results with lattice QCD data whenever available. We find the NJL model to have a limited predictive power for the thermodynamic variables and various transport coefficients above the critical temperature whereas the PNJL model and DQPM show acceptable results for the quantities of interest.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model has limited predictive power for thermodynamic variables and transport coefficients above the critical temperature, whereas the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) show acceptable results when compared to lattice QCD data.

## Method

The authors computed shear viscosity η(T), bulk viscosity ζ(T), electric conductivity σ_e(T), and heat conductivity κ(T) within the SU(3)_f Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model as functions of temperature. They also calculated the entropy density s(T), pressure P(T), and speed of sound c_s^2(T) from the NJL model and compared these results with those from the PNJL model and the DQPM, as well as with available lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 200 MeV defined as the mean Mott temperature in the NJL model
- NJL model parameters: cutoff Λ = 602.3 MeV, coupling GΛ^2 = 1.835, KΛ^5 = 12.36, bare light quark mass m_{0q} = 5.5 MeV, bare strange quark mass m_{0s} = 140.7 MeV
- DQPM critical temperature T_c = 158 MeV (from Wuppertal-Budapest LQCD Collaboration)
- PNJL critical temperature T_c ≈ 185 MeV (from HotQCD LQCD Collaboration)
- DQPM running coupling parameters: λ = 2.42, T_s/T_c = 0.56, with coupling modification parameters T^* = 1.19 T_c and γ = 3.1
- DQPM width parameter c = 14.4 related to a magnetic cutoff
- PNJL Polyakov loop potential parameters: a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = −2.47, a_2 = 15.2, b_3 = −1.75, T_0 = 270 MeV (modified to 210 MeV for comparison with NJL)

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model calculations of quark and antiquark elastic scattering and hadronization cross sections by Rehberg (1996) and transport simulations by Marty (2012) to the systematic calculation of equilibrium transport coefficients. The results are compared with the PNJL model developed by Ratti (2007) and Costa (2008, 2010) and the DQPM by Peshier (2004, 2005) and Cassing (2009), which is used in the parton-hadron string dynamics (PHSD) approach by Cassing (2008) and Bratkovskaya (2011). The findings enable comparison of different effective QCD models for use in transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
