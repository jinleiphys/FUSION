# Proton-Proton On Shell Optical Potential at High Energies and the Hollownes Effect

> We analyze the usefulness of the optical potential as suggested by the double spectral Mandelstam representation at very high energies, such as in the proton-proton scattering at ISR and the LHC. Its particular meaning regarding the interpretation of the scattering data up to the maximum available measured energies is discussed. Our analysis reconstructs 3D dynamics from the effective transverse 2D impact parameter representation and suggests that besides the onset of gray nucleons at the LHC there appears an inelasticity depletion (hollowness) which precludes convolution models at the attometer scale.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the on-shell optical potential, reconstructed from proton-proton scattering data via an Abel inversion of the inelasticity profile, reveals a "hollowness" effect at LHC energies where the imaginary part of the potential has a dip at the origin rather than a maximum, but this effect is less evident in the 2D impact parameter representation than in the full 3D reconstruction.

## Method

The authors use the MBP2 parameterization of the Barger-Phillips type (Fagundes et al., 2013) fitted to differential cross sections at six energies from ISR to LHC, then compute the inelastic profile function n_in(b) and its derivative. They invert the Abel-type integral equation relating the 3D on-shell optical potential to the 2D impact parameter inelasticity profile, obtaining Im W(r,s) directly from the scattering data without solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key numbers

- Energies analyzed: sqrt(s) = 23.4, 30.5, 44.6, 52.8, 62.0, and 7000 GeV (fitted), plus an extrapolation to 14000 GeV
- The MBP2 fits produce chi^2/d.o.f. ~ 1.2-1.7
- The hollowness effect (3D depletion at the origin) sets in at 0.5-1 TeV, below LHC energies
- The maximum of the imaginary part of the on-shell optical potential occurs at approximately r = 1 fm at LHC energies
- The mean squared radius of Im W(r) follows <r^2> = (3/2)<b^2> and displays logarithmic growth with energy

## Context

This work extends the optical model approach from neutron-nucleus scattering (Fernbach, 1949; Serber, 1964) and the Mandelstam representation definition of the optical potential (Cornwall and Ruderman, 1962) to high-energy proton-proton scattering. It contradicts the naive expectation that central collisions produce the most inelasticity, showing instead that the inelasticity becomes maximal at finite impact parameter. The hollowness effect goes beyond the "gray nucleon" picture suggested by Dremin (2015) and precludes simple convolution models of the proton structure at attometer scales. The results enable future studies of inelasticity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.
