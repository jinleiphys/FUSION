# The role of nucleon recoil in low-energy antikaon-deuteron scattering

> The effect of the nucleon recoil for antikaon-deuteron scattering is investigated in the framework of effective field theory. In particular, we concentrate on the calculation of the nucleon recoil effect for the double-scattering process. It is shown that the leading correction to the static term that emerges at order xi^{1/2} with xi=M_K/m_N vanishes due to a complete cancellation of individually large contributions. The resulting recoil effect in this process is found to be of order of 10-15% as compared to the static term. We also briefly discuss the application of the method in the calculations of the multiple-scattering diagrams.

## Key claim

The leading nucleon recoil correction to the static (fixed-center) approximation for antikaon-deuteron double scattering, which naively appears at order ξ^(1/2) with ξ = M_K/m_N, vanishes exactly due to a complete cancellation of individually large contributions; the actual recoil effect is therefore of order 10-15% relative to the static term, not the larger magnitude one might expect from the mass ratio alone.

## Method

The authors employ a non-relativistic effective field theory (EFT) framework to compute the antikaon-deuteron scattering amplitude, focusing on the double-scattering diagrams. They apply the perturbative uniform expansion method of Mohr et al. to systematically expand the loop integrals in half-integer and integer powers of the small parameter ξ = M_K/m_N, separating the integration into low-momentum, high-momentum, and intermediate regimes. The cancellation at order ξ^(1/2) is demonstrated analytically for both isoscalar and isovector antikaon-nucleon interactions, using the orthogonality of the deuteron bound-state and continuum NN wave functions and Pauli principle arguments.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is ξ = M_K/m_N ≈ 0.5, where M_K is the kaon mass and m_N the nucleon mass.
- The leading recoil correction to the static term vanishes at order ξ^(1/2); the surviving recoil effect is of order 10-15% compared to the static term.
- The S-wave antikaon-nucleon scattering lengths b_0, b_1 are of order 1 fm (large and strongly absorptive).
- The expectation value ⟨1/r⟩ ≈ 0.5 fm^(-1) for the deuteron wave function, giving the expansion parameter b·⟨1/r⟩ ≈ 1 for the multiple-scattering series.
- The total two-nucleon absorption rate in K^-d scattering is (1.22 ± 0.09)%.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nucleon recoil effects previously studied for pion-deuteron scattering by Kolybasov et al. and Fäldt, and within EFT by Baru et al. and Lensky et al., where similar cancellations of the leading √(M_π/m_N) correction were found. The paper provides a systematic EFT framework for evaluating recoil corrections in antikaon-deuteron scattering, which is needed for the analysis of upcoming kaonic deuterium data from the DEAR/SIDDHARTA collaboration. This enables a model-independent extraction of the isoscalar and isovector antikaon-nucleon scattering lengths b_0 and b_1 from combined kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium measurements.
