# Quark-photon vertex in confining models

> We compute the one-loop quark-photon vertex and the quark magnetic moment in three different models with an infrared-modified gluon propagator, namely: the massive (Curci-Ferrari-like) model, the Gribov-Zwanziger model, and the Refined Gribov-Zwanziger model. We show results for the $F_2$ form factor and analyze the role played by the mass parameters associated with the confined gluon in these models. Using the framework of the Constituent Quark Model, we further construct the observable proton magnetic moment including the effects of QCD interactions generated by the different confining models. Our results show that there is no qualitative observable difference stemming from the presence of complex-conjugated poles. On the other hand, quantitative differences between the various confining models can be sizable, so that observable constraints would in principle be possible, provided one has sufficient information about the constituent quark mass and the QCD running coupling.

## Key claim

The paper computes the one-loop quark-photon vertex and quark magnetic moment in three confining models (massive/Curci-Ferrari-like, Gribov-Zwanziger, and Refined Gribov-Zwanziger) and finds that while complex-conjugated poles in the gluon propagator produce no qualitative observable difference, quantitative differences between models can be sizable enough to allow observable constraints, provided one has sufficient information about the constituent quark mass and the QCD running coupling.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop QCD contribution to the quark-photon vertex form factor F2 in Euclidean space, using a standard Feynman parameter integration procedure generalized to complex mass parameters. The gluon propagator was replaced by the modified forms from three confining models: the massive (Curci-Ferrari-like) model with a real gluon mass, the Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) model with a pair of complex-conjugated poles parameterized by the Gribov mass gamma, and the Refined Gribov-Zwanziger (RGZ) model with three independent mass parameters (M, m, lambda) yielding complex poles and residues. The resulting quark anomalous magnetic moment was then used within the Constituent Quark Model to construct the observable proton magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- For the perturbative QCD case at zero momentum transfer, the normalized form factor is F2_bar(0) = 1/2, corresponding to F2(0) = CF * (alpha_s / (2*pi))
- For the massive model at small gluon-to-quark mass ratio a -> 0: F2_bar(0) = 1/2 - (pi/2)*a^(1/2) + O(a)
- For the GZ model at small Gribov-to-quark mass ratio a -> 0: F2_bar(0) = 1/2 - (pi/2)*(sqrt(2)/2)*a^(1/2) + O(a)
- For the massive model at large a -> infinity: F2_bar(0) = 1/(3a) + O(1/a^2)
- For the GZ model at large a -> infinity: F2_bar(0) = (12*Log[a] - 25)/(12*a^2) + O(1/a^3), lacking the inverse linear term present in the massive model
- For the RGZ model with a = b -> 0 and kappa > 1/3: F2_bar(0) > 1/2, with leading term 1/2 + (pi*sqrt(2)*(3*kappa - 1)/8)*a^(1/2)
- The GZ model yields larger F2_bar(0) than the massive model for a < 0.177 (corresponding to m_g = gamma approximately 153 MeV for m_q = 363 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Gribov-Zwanziger and Refined Gribov-Zwanziger theories, which have mostly focused on nonphysical correlation functions (Capri 2015, Mintz 2017, Mintz 2018, Capri 2018), by computing an actual observable: the quark anomalous magnetic moment and the proton magnetic moment via the Constituent Quark Model. The results enable future constraints on confining model parameters using high-precision measurements of nucleon magnetic moments from the Particle Data Group (2022), and could be compared with Lattice QCD computations of the quark-photon vertex (Leutnant 2018) when full momentum-dependent form factors become available.
