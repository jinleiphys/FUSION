# Entanglement in selected Binary Tree States: Dicke/Total spin states, particle number projected BCS states

> Binary Tree States (BTS) are states whose decomposition on a quantum register basis formed by a set of qubits can be made sequentially. Such states sometimes appear naturally in many-body systems treated in Fock space when a global symmetry is imposed, like the total spin or particle number symmetries. Examples are the Dicke states, the eigenstates of the total spin for a set of particles having individual spin $1/2$, or states obtained by projecting a BCS states onto particle number, also called projected BCS in small superfluid systems. Starting from a BTS state described on the set of $n$ qubits or orbitals, the entanglement entropy of any subset of $ k$ qubits is analyzed. Specifically, a practical method is developed to access the $k$ qubits/particles von Neumann entanglement entropy of the subsystem of interest. Properties of these entropies are discussed, including scaling properties, upper bounds, or how these entropies correlate with fluctuations. Illustrations are given for the Dicke state and the projected BCS states.

## Key claim

The paper develops a practical method to compute the von Neumann entanglement entropy of any subset of k qubits for Binary Tree States (BTS), which include Dicke states and particle-number-projected BCS states, and shows that the entanglement entropy is strongly correlated with particle-number fluctuations in the subsystem, with a Gaussian asymptotic limit that becomes accurate once fluctuations exceed about 0.5 particle units.

## Method

The author exploits the Schmidt decomposition of BTS states when the total qubit register is split into two subspaces, showing that the reduced density matrix becomes diagonal in the basis of subsystem BTS states with coefficients given by hypergeometric probabilities (for Dicke states) or more general coefficients computed via generating functions. For projected BCS states, the coefficients are obtained numerically using a Fomenko integration technique on the generating function H_S(φ) = ∏ (1 + |x_i|² e^{-iφ}) over the relevant subsystem, where the x_i parameters are derived from BCS pairing theory with a given pairing gap Δ and Fermi energy λ.

## Key numbers

- For Dicke states with n=100 qubits, the one-qubit entropy S₁ depends only on the ratio K/n and equals S₁ = -(K/n) log₂(K/n) - (1 - K/n) log₂(1 - K/n)
- The upper bound for the k-qubit entropy in Dicke states is S_A ≤ log₂(k+1) when 0 ≤ k ≤ K ≤ n/2
- For the maximally entangled Dicke state with K=n/2 and n=100, the approximate maximum entropy is S^{max}_k = ½ log₂[πe/2] + ½ log₂[k(n-k)/(n-1)], giving S^{max}_₁ ≈ 1.047, S^{max}_₂ ≈ 1.55, S^{max}_₃ ≈ 1.84, and S^{max}_₄ ≈ 2.05
- For projected BCS states with n=49 equidistant levels, the Gaussian asymptotic limit S̃_k = ½ log₂(2πe σ²_N) becomes accurate when the fluctuation √σ²_N exceeds approximately 0.5 particle units

## Context

This work extends the empirical finding of Lacroix (2022) that entanglement entropy of k interacting neutrinos shows simple scaling properties, providing a general framework for BTS states that appear in quantum chemistry (total spin conservation) and nuclear physics (superfluidity via projected quasiparticle states). The method generalizes the Schmidt decomposition known for Dicke states from Mor18 and Sto03, and connects to recent quantum computing algorithms for building such states (Kha23, Dut23). The results enable systematic computation of multipartite entanglement in small superfluid systems described by projected BCS states, which had not been previously characterized.
