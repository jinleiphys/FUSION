# Shell effects in quasi-fission for calcium induced reactions forming thorium isotopes

> Quantum shell effects induce an asymmetric fission mode in actinides, which disappears in neutron deficient isotopes. Quasi-fission, characterized by a significant mass transfer in heavy ion collisions at low-energies, is expected to be affected by similar shell effects. This is studied in 40-56Ca+176Yb reactions with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach. All reactions exhibit a mass equilibration process that stops when a heavy fragment with Z~54 protons is formed. Unlike the fission of thorium compound nuclei, quasi-fission does not exhibit a transition to symmetric modes in neutron deficient systems. This observation is interpreted in terms of potential energy surfaces that show a persistence of an asymmetric valley with an increasing barrier preventing its population in fission of the most neutron deficient thorium isotopes.

# Shell effects in quasi-fission for calcium induced reactions forming thorium isotopes

## Key claim
Unlike the fission of thorium compound nuclei, which exhibits a transition from asymmetric to symmetric modes in neutron deficient isotopes, quasi-fission in 40-56Ca+176Yb reactions does not show such a transition; the asymmetric valley persists in the potential energy surfaces of neutron deficient thorium isotopes, but an increasing barrier prevents its population in fission.

## Method
Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations with the SLy4d Skyrme energy density functional were performed for 40-56Ca+176Yb collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 172 MeV, covering even mass numbers from 40 to 56. Four initial orientations of the deformed 176Yb target (0, 45, 90, and 135 degrees) were considered, and quasi-fission outcomes were analyzed for a range of orbital angular momenta with step ΔL=2ℏ. Static potential energy surfaces of even thorium isotopes from 216Th to 232Th were computed using constrained Hartree-Fock with BCS pairing to interpret the dynamics.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy of 172 MeV corresponds to 10-15% above the Coulomb barrier for all reactions studied
- Contact times of 15-20 zeptoseconds (1 zs = 10^-21 s) are required for mass equilibration to stop
- Heavy fragments form with Z ≈ 54 protons, corresponding to light fragments with Z ≈ 36
- 480 TDHF calculations were compiled in total across all systems and orientations
- Mesh size of Δx = 0.8 fm on a 72×72×(28/2) Cartesian grid
- Initial distance between centers of mass is 22.6 fm
- Calculations stop when fragment centers of mass are separated by 26 fm

## Context
This work extends previous comparisons between fission and quasi-fission in 226Th (Simenel 2021, Lee 2024b) by systematically varying the neutron number of the projectile across the asymmetric-to-symmetric fission transition known from experiments on thorium isotopes (Schmidt 2000, Bockstiegel 2008, Chatillon 2019). The results contradict the expectation that quasi-fission would mirror the fission transition, and instead show that the asymmetric valley persists in quasi-fission even for the most neutron deficient systems. This enables future experimental tests with radioactive beams to verify whether the predicted persistence of asymmetric quasi-fission holds, and clarifies the role of entrance channel dynamics versus compound nucleus properties in determining fragment asymmetries.
