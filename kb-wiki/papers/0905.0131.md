# Role of nonlinear vector meson interactions in hyperon stars

> The extended nonlinear model has been applied to construct neutron star matter equation of state. In the case of neutron star matter with non-zero strangeness the extension of the vector meson sector by the inclusion of nonlinear mixed terms results in the stiffening of the equation of state and accordingly in the higher value of the maximum neutron star mass.

# Role of nonlinear vector meson interactions in hyperon stars

## Key claim

The inclusion of nonlinear mixed vector meson couplings (specifically between ω, ρ, and φ mesons) in an extended SU(3) chiral model stiffens the equation of state for neutron star matter containing hyperons, leading to higher maximum neutron star masses than models without such couplings. The effect is conditional on the presence of nonzero strangeness in the stellar matter.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field model based on quantum hadrodynamics and chiral SU(3) symmetry, including the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ, Ξ) and scalar (σ, σ*) and vector (ω, ρ, φ) mesons. The vector meson sector is extended beyond the standard Walecka model by adding nonlinear self-interactions and mixed couplings between ω, ρ, and φ fields, with coupling constants derived from the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. The equations of state for neutron star matter are then computed in the mean-field approximation, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain stellar masses.

## Key numbers

- The FSUGold model, which includes nonlinear isoscalar meson self-interactions and mixed isoscalar-isovector coupling, yields a maximum neutron star mass of M_max = 1.72 M_⊙.
- The compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter in the FSUGold model is K_v = 230 MeV.
- The neutron skin thickness in ²⁰⁸Pb predicted by the FSUGold model is R_n − R_p = 0.21 fm.
- The neutron star in EXO 0748-676 has a mass M ≥ 2.10 ± 0.28 M_⊙ and radius R ≥ 13.8 ± 1.8 km.
- The neutron star in the LMXB 4U 1636-536 has a mass of 2.0 ± 0.1 M_⊙.
- The isolated neutron star RX J1856.5-3754 has a radius R > 12 km.

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear Walecka model approach developed by Boguta and Bodmer (1977) and the TM1/TM2 models of Sugahara and Toki (1994) by incorporating a broader spectrum of nonlinear vector meson couplings motivated by chiral SU(3) symmetry. It builds on the FSUGold model of Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005), which achieved a softer equation of state and a neutron skin thickness of 0.21 fm in ²⁰⁸Pb, but here the focus is on the role of strangeness-bearing hyperons. The inclusion of mixed ω-ρ-φ couplings allows the model to simultaneously address the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the stiffening of the high-density equation of state, enabling predictions for maximum neutron star masses that can be compared with observational constraints from systems such as EXO 0748-676 and 4U 1636-536.
