# Lectures on Field Theory and the Standard Model: A Symmetry-Oriented Approach

> The standard model of particle physics represents the cornerstone of our understanding of the microscopic world. In these lectures we review its contents and structure, with a particular emphasis on the central role played by symmetries and their realization. This is not intended to be an exhaustive review but a discussion of selected topics that we find interesting, with the specific aim of clarifying some subtle points and potential misunderstandings. A number of more technical topics are discussed in separated boxes interspersed throughout the text.

## Key claim

These lectures present a symmetry-oriented review of the standard model and quantum field theory, explicitly clarifying that the text is not an exhaustive review but a discussion of selected topics aimed at clarifying subtle points and potential misunderstandings, with more technical material relegated to separate boxes.

## Method

The authors review the structure of the standard model of particle physics by emphasizing the central role played by symmetries and their realizations, including global symmetries, gauge symmetries, and spontaneous symmetry breaking. They discuss effective field theory as a foundational framework, using dimensional analysis to classify operators and distinguish renormalizable from nonrenormalizable theories, and illustrate these concepts with examples including general relativity as an effective theory and chiral perturbation theory for low-energy QCD.

## Key numbers

* Planck scale: \(\Lambda_{\rm Pl} = 2.4 \times 10^{18}\) GeV, derived from the gravitational constant \(\kappa \equiv \sqrt{8\pi G_N}\) which has dimensions \([\kappa] = E^{-1}\)
* The Planck scale is about \(10^{14}\) times the center-of-mass energy at which the LHC currently operates
* The QCD confinement scale \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) is the energy threshold below which quarks and gluons become confined into hadrons and chiral perturbation theory becomes the appropriate effective description

## Context

This work extends the pedagogical tradition of quantum field theory textbooks such as those by Peskin, Weinberg, and Schwartz, as well as the authors' own previous book (Alvarez-Gaume and Vazquez-Mozo). It is written for the 2022 European School for High Energy Physics and aims to clarify common misconceptions about renormalizability, effective field theories, and the quantization of gravity. The lectures enable students to understand the modern EFT perspective, where nonrenormalizable theories are no longer dismissed but interpreted as effective descriptions valid below some physical cutoff scale.
