# Sigma-omega meson coupling and properties of nuclei and nuclear matter

> We have constructed a Lagrangian model (SIG-OM) with coupling of sigma and omega mesons in the relativistic mean-field theory. Properties of finite nuclei and nuclear matter have been explored with the new Lagrangian model. The study shows that with SIG-OM an excellent description of binding energies and charge radii of nuclei over a large range of isospin is achieved. With an incompressibility of nuclear matter K=265 MeV, it is also able to describe the breathing-mode isoscalar giant monopole resonance energies appropriately. It is shown that the high-density behaviour of the equation of state of nuclear and neutron matter with the sigma-omega coupling is significantly softer than the non-linear scalar coupling model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that adding a sigma-omega meson coupling term (σ²ω²) to the standard relativistic mean-field Lagrangian with σ³+σ⁴ nonlinear terms can simultaneously achieve excellent descriptions of binding energies and charge radii across a broad isospin range, appropriate breathing-mode giant monopole resonance energies, and a significantly softer high-density equation of state than the conventional nonlinear scalar coupling model, but the incompressibility of nuclear matter (K=265.2 MeV) is slightly lower than NL3 (K=271.6 MeV), and the asymmetry energy (37.0 MeV) remains above the empirical value of approximately 33 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a new Lagrangian model (SIG-OM) within relativistic mean-field theory by adding a σ²ω² coupling term to the standard Lagrangian containing σ³+σ⁴ nonlinear scalar self-couplings. They fitted the parameters to binding energies and charge radii of eight spherical nuclei (¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹¹⁶Sn, ¹²⁴Sn, ¹³²Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb) using the oscillator expansion method for solving the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations, with BCS pairing for open-shell nuclei and a harmonic oscillator center-of-mass correction.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility K = 265.2 MeV for SIG-OM, compared to 271.6 MeV for NL3 and 354.9 MeV for NL-SH
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.149 fm⁻³ for SIG-OM (0.146 for NL-SH, 0.148 for NL3)
- Binding energy per nucleon a_v = −16.30 MeV for SIG-OM (−16.33 for NL-SH, −16.24 for NL3)
- Effective mass m* = 0.622 at saturation for SIG-OM (0.597 for NL-SH, 0.595 for NL3)
- Asymmetry energy a₄ = 37.0 MeV for SIG-OM (36.1 for NL-SH, 37.4 for NL3)
- Binding energy of ¹⁰⁰Sn: −826.0 MeV with SIG-OM versus experimental −824.5 MeV (NL-SH: −830.6, NL3: −829.2)
- Binding energy of ²⁰⁸Pb: −1638.3 MeV with SIG-OM versus experimental −1636.7 MeV (NL-SH: −1640.4, NL3: −1639.6)
- Charge radius of ²⁰⁸Pb: 5.506 fm with SIG-OM versus experimental 5.503 fm (NL-SH: 5.509, NL3: 5.523)
- GMR energy for ⁹⁰Zr: 18.2 MeV with SIG-OM versus experimental 17.81±0.30 MeV (NL3: 16.9, NL-SH: 19.5)
- GMR energy for ¹²⁰Sn: 16.2 MeV with SIG-OM versus experimental 15.52±0.15 MeV (NL3: 15.0, NL-SH: 16.7)
- GMR energy for ²⁰⁸Pb: 14.1 MeV with SIG-OM versus experimental 13.96±0.28 MeV (NL3: 13.0, NL-SH: 15.0)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Boguta and Bodmer (1977) and the subsequent NL-SH force of Sharma et al. (1993) and NL3 force of Lalazissis et al. (1997), which used only σ³+σ⁴ nonlinear terms and produced stiff equations of state. The SIG-OM model introduces a σ-ω coupling that softens the high-density equation of state, addressing the known incompatibility of stiff RMF models with neutron star mass observations, while maintaining good descriptions of finite nuclei properties including the problematic ¹⁰⁰Sn binding energy and Pb isotope charge radii. This enables further investigation of surface properties and shell effects in nuclei far from stability, and provides a softer equation of state for neutron star structure calculations that remains compatible with recent observations of neutron stars near 2 solar masses.
