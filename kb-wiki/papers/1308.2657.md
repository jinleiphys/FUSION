# Large-N Structure of Tetraquark Mesons

> Following Weinberg's argument that narrow tetraquark mesons are not precluded in large N_c QCD, we explore the flow of N_c factors needed for the consistency of this picture, and show that they must arise in a novel way, not simply through the usual combinatoric counting.

# Large-N Structure of Tetraquark Mesons

## Key claim
Narrow tetraquark mesons can exist in large N_c QCD, but only if the required N_c factors arise through a novel, nonperturbative mechanism, not through the usual combinatoric counting of color factors and coupling constants. The existence of such states depends on the noncommutativity of the local limit and the large N_c limit.

## Method
The paper analyzes the N_c scaling of the two-point correlation function of color-singlet quartic quark operators (products of two quark bilinears) and its connected and disconnected components. By examining the nonlocal generalization of these operators, where the two bilinears are separated in spacetime, the author shows that a Gaussian profile in the separation with a specific N_c-dependent exponent can produce the required 1/√N_c suppression to yield a tetraquark decay constant f_Q = O(N_c^{1/2}) and a decay width of O(1/N_c).

## Key numbers
- Tetraquark decay constant f_Q = O(N_c^{1/2}) (same scaling as ordinary meson decay constants)
- Tetraquark decay width = O(1/N_c) (same scaling as ordinary meson widths)
- Connected two-point function of quartic operators is O(N_c^1), while the disconnected piece is O(N_c^2)
- The Gaussian profile exponent m = 1/3 gives the required 1/√N_c suppression when integrated over 3 spatial dimensions
- Meson-meson scattering amplitudes are O(1/N_c)
- Meson decay constant f_B = O(N_c^{1/2})

## Context
This work extends the argument of Weinberg (2013) that narrow tetraquarks are not precluded in large N_c QCD, and addresses the subsequent finding by Knecht and Peris (2013) that some tetraquarks mix with mesons at O(N_c^0). The paper proposes a resolution to the apparent contradiction in N_c counting between the operator norm and the required decay constant scaling. The mechanism is nonperturbative in N_c counting, unlike the standard 't Hooft (1974) counting that explains ordinary mesons, glueballs, hybrid mesons (Cohen, 1998), baryons (Witten, 1979), and baryon resonances (Cohen, 2003). The result enables further study of tetraquark phenomenology and meson-tetraquark mixing at O(N_c^0).
