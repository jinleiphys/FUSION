# New family of models for incompressible quantum liquids in $d\geq 2$

> Through Haldane's construction, the fractional quantum Hall states on a two-sphere was shown to be the ground states of {\it one-dimensional} SU(2) spin Hamiltonians. In this Letter we generalize this construction to obtain a new class of SU(N) spin Hamiltonians. These Hamiltonians describes center-of-mass-position conserving pair hopping fermions in space dimension $d\ge 2$. We conjecture that the excitations of these models posses fractional fermion number.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new class of solvable SU(N) spin Hamiltonians in spatial dimension d ≥ 2 whose ground states are incompressible quantum liquids, generalizing Haldane's one-dimensional SU(2) construction; they conjecture that the excitations of these models possess fractional fermion number.

## Method

The authors generalize Haldane's pseudopotential Hamiltonian for the fractional quantum Hall effect on a two-sphere, which maps to a one-dimensional SU(2) spin Hamiltonian, to SU(3) spins whose weight space forms a two-dimensional triangular lattice. They construct a positive-definite Hamiltonian that projects pairs of SU(3) spins onto specific multiplets, prove that the unique singlet ground state is given by an SU(3) coherent-state wavefunction (a Slater determinant raised to the third power), and demonstrate the presence of an excitation gap using the single-mode approximation. The Hamiltonian is shown to be a center-of-mass-position conserving pair hopping model in two dimensions.

## Key numbers

- For the SU(3) model with m=3 and p an odd integer, the filling factor f = d(p)/d(mp) equals 1/m² = 1/9 in the thermodynamic (p → ∞) limit, where d(p) = (p+1)(p+2)/2.
- The hopping range scales as p^(1/2) for p from 30 to 600, while the linear dimension of the lattice scales as p, as shown in a log-log plot.
- For p=1, the single-spin Hilbert space is d(3)=10 dimensional and there are d(1)=3 spins; for p=3, the single-particle Hilbert space is 55 dimensional and there are d(3)=10 particles.

## Context

This work extends Haldane's (1983) pseudopotential construction for the fractional quantum Hall effect on a two-sphere, which was previously shown by Lee (2004) and Seidel (2005, 2006) to map to one-dimensional many-body insulators on a torus. The authors provide an affirmative answer to the question of whether a many-body insulator can exist without symmetry breaking in spatial dimension d ≥ 2, a problem motivated by Anderson's spin liquid proposal. The construction can be generalized to SU(N) for higher-dimensional incompressible quantum liquids, with the SU(4) model applicable to the four-dimensional quantum Hall effect proposed by Zhang and Hu (2001).
