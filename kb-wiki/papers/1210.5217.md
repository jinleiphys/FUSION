# Dijet production, collision centrality and backgrounds in high-energy p-p collisions

> Two aspects of high-energy \pp collisions share common phenomenological elements: (a) A correlation between jet production and \pp centrality is suggested by the transverse partonic structure of hadrons inferred from deep-inelastic scattering data. (b) The {\em underlying event} (UE) is defined as the final-state particles complementary to a triggered high-energy dijet. An observable common to both topics is variation of so-called {\em transverse multiplicity} $N_\perp$ with a $p_{t,trig}$ dijet trigger. We test assumptions associated with \pp collision centrality and the UE. We determine the nature of the UE and explore the relation between jet production and \pp centrality. We use the {\em two-component model} (TCM) of spectra and correlations derived from 200 GeV \pp collisions to construct a simulated particle distribution on $(p_t,n_{ch})$ to predict the $N_\perp$ response to $p_{t,trig}$. The $p_t$ spectrum TCM combined in this analysis with measured minimum-bias \pp angular correlations suggests that the UE includes a substantial contribution from the triggered dijet in addition to the contribution from projectile fragmentation (beam-beam remnants). The jet contribution to $N_\perp$ may represent a universal large-angle base common to all dijets that extends across $2π$ azimuth. The analysis further suggests that \pp centrality is not controlled significantly by $p_{t,trig}$ but may be correlated to some extent with an imposed $n_{ch}$ condition, depending on the role of fluctuations. Future correlation studies may better determine the role of \pp centrality. These results may have implications for ongoing RHIC analysis and LHC searches for physics beyond the standard model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "underlying event" (UE) in high-energy p-p collisions, conventionally assumed to be distinct from a triggered dijet, actually includes a substantial contribution from that same triggered dijet, and that p-p collision centrality is not significantly controlled by the trigger transverse momentum condition \(p_{t,trig}\) but may correlate with an imposed charged-particle multiplicity condition depending on fluctuations.

## Method

The author uses the two-component model (TCM) of \(p_t\) spectra and angular correlations derived from 200 GeV p-p collisions to construct a simulated particle distribution on the two-dimensional space of transverse momentum \(p_t\) and event charged-particle multiplicity \(n_{ch}\). This simulation predicts the response of transverse multiplicity \(N_\perp\) to a dijet trigger condition \(p_{t,trig}\). The TCM is then applied to published CDF underlying-event data at 1.8 TeV and 0.9 TeV, decomposing the \(N_\perp(p_t)\) spectrum into soft (projectile dissociation) and hard (jet-related) components, and the minimum-bias event distribution on \(n_{ch}\) is separated into soft events (no jets) and hard events (at least one jet) using the observed jet production rate vs \(n_{ch}\).

## Key numbers

* The hard-component fragment integral within \(2\pi\) azimuth and \(\Delta\eta = 2\) for the 1.8 TeV triggered data is \(n_h = 4 \pm 0.5\), corresponding to a coefficient \(N_{\perp,h} = 1.3\).
* The nonjet soft density for the 1.8 TeV triggered data is \(dn_s/d\eta \approx 5 \pm 1\), compared with the NSD soft multiplicity density \(dn_s/d\eta \approx 4\) measured at 1.8 TeV.
* The plateau value of transverse multiplicity \(N_\perp\) from CDF data at 1.8 TeV is approximately 2.3.
* The ratio of the hard-component maximum value in the triggered 1.8 TeV data to that in the untriggered 200 GeV data is about 40, compared with a nominal factor of 25 derived from jet frequency and azimuth acceptance differences.
* The ratio at \(y_t \approx 3.3\) (\(p_t \approx 2\) GeV/c) between the \(N_\perp(p_t)\) spectrum and the expected soft spectrum component is a factor of 20.

## Context

This work extends the two-component model analysis of p-p \(p_t\) spectra from Trainor (2008) and the pQCD description of jet fragmentation from Trainor (2010), and it directly tests the proposal by Frankfurt, Strikman, and Weiss (2011) that jet production correlates with p-p collision centrality via the transverse partonic structure of the proton. The analysis provides an alternative interpretation of CDF underlying-event measurements (Rick, 2002; Under, 2005) by showing that the transverse multiplicity \(N_\perp\) includes a substantial jet-related component rather than being purely a measure of nonjet activity. This work enables future correlation studies to better determine the role of p-p centrality and has implications for ongoing RHIC analysis and LHC searches for physics beyond the standard model.
