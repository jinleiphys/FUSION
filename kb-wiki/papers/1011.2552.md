# Baryonic Bound State of Vortices in Multicomponent Superconductors

> We construct a bound state of three 1/3-quantized Josephson coupled vortices in three-component superconductors with intrinsic Josephson couplings, which may be relevant with regard to iron-based superconductors. We find a Y-shaped junction of three domain walls connecting the three vortices, resembling the baryonic bound state of three quarks in QCD. The appearance of the Y-junction (but not a Delta-junction) implies that in both cases of superconductors and QCD, the bound state is described by a genuine three-body interaction (but not by the sum of two-body interactions). We also discuss a confinement/deconfinement phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a bound state of three 1/3-quantized Josephson coupled vortices in three-component superconductors with intrinsic Josephson couplings, finding a Y-shaped junction of three domain walls connecting the vortices. The authors claim this resembles the baryonic bound state of three quarks in QCD, but the result is conditional on the specific choice of positive Josephson couplings (γ_{ij} > 0) and the numerical simulation fixing the positions of the three vortices, which then collapse to form a single integer vortex due to wall tension.

## Method

The authors use the Ginzburg-Landau free energy for three-component superconductors with intrinsic Josephson couplings, decomposing the winding of three vortex types (1,0,0), (0,1,0), and (0,0,1) into gauge and global phase rotations to show each carries 1/3 of a flux quantum. They perform numerical relaxation simulations with parameters ℏ = c = 2m = 2e = v = λ/2 = 1 and γ = 0.02 to obtain the energy density, Josephson energy density, and magnetic field configuration, and they derive an effective sine-Gordon model for the phase differences to calculate domain wall tensions and the critical temperature for confinement/deconfinement.

## Key numbers

* The three vortices each carry magnetic flux Φ₀/3, where Φ₀ = hc/2e is the unit flux quantum.
* The numerical simulation uses parameters ℏ = c = 2m = 2e = v = λ/2 = 1 and γ = 0.02.
* The tension of each domain wall in the symmetric case (v₁ = v₂ = v₃ ≡ v, γ₁₂ = γ₂₃ = γ₃₁ ≡ γ) is T_dw = 8 ℏ v² √(2γ / 3m).
* The critical temperature for deconfinement is T_crit = (16 ξ ℏ v² / (k_B ln 2π)) √(2γ / 3m), where ξ is the larger of the coherence length and penetration depth.

## Context

This work extends the study of fractional vortex confinement in multicomponent superconductors, building on the mesonic bound state of two half-quantized vortices in two-gap superconductors studied by Goryo et al. (2007) and the earlier work on half-quantized vortices by Babaev (2002) and domain wall attachments by Tanaka (2001) and Gurevich (2003). The results enable experimental testing of baryonic vortex states in iron-based superconductors and suggest a method to determine intrinsic Josephson couplings from the shape of the Y-junction, while also providing an analog system to study the color confinement problem in QCD.
